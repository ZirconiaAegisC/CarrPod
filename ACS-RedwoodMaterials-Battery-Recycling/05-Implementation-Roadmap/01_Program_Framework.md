## 11.1 Program Objectives

The ACS engagement with Redwood Materials is structured to achieve five principal objectives:

1. **Due Diligence Validation:** Confirm the technical and commercial viability of Redwood's recycling, materials production, and energy storage operations through independent assessment of process technology, unit economics, and partnership architecture.
2. **Capital Formation Support:** Provide analytical foundation for potential debt or equity investment, including financial modeling, market analysis, and risk assessment.
3. **Strategic Advisory:** Identify opportunities for operational optimization, partnership expansion, and go-to-market acceleration across all three business lines.
4. **Policy Engagement:** Support Redwood's engagement with federal and state policy stakeholders concerning IRA implementation, DOE loan finalization, and critical minerals policy.
5. **Governance & Reporting:** Establish monitoring frameworks for ongoing portfolio oversight and performance measurement.

## 11.3 Governance Framework

**Steering Committee:** Monthly review with Redwood senior management (CEO, COO, SVP Business Development) and ACS program leadership.

**Working Group Cadence:** Weekly status calls per workstream; biweekly cross-workstream integration meetings.

**Reporting:** Monthly progress dashboard covering milestone attainment, risk register updates, and budget-to-actual variance.

**Escalation Protocol:** Material findings (adverse technical results, partnership risk, regulatory change) escalated to Steering Committee within 48 hours.

## 11.4 Risk Register — Top Tier

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| DOE loan disbursement delay | Medium | High | Parallel commercial financing workstream; public-market preparation |
| Lithium / nickel price decline | Medium | Medium | CAM production margin hedge; toll-processing fee structures |
| CAM production ramp delays | Medium | High | Phased commissioning plan; toll-processing fallback revenue |
| Charleston construction cost overrun | Medium | Medium | Fixed-price EPC contracting; contingency reserve |
| Key-person dependency (Straubel) | Low | High | Succession planning; executive bench depth |
| Chinese competition dumping recycled material | Medium | Medium | IRA domestic content advantages; customer switching cost lock-in |
| Second-life battery safety / liability | Low | Medium | UL 9540A testing; contractual liability allocation |
