# Boston Metal — Industry Partnerships and Strategic Alliances

## ArcelorMittal Partnership

ArcelorMittal, the world's second-largest steel producer with approximately 70 million tonnes of annual crude steel production, led Boston Metal's Series C funding round with a $120 million investment in 2023. This investment is the largest single commitment by a legacy steel producer into a direct electrification technology for ironmaking.

The strategic logic for ArcelorMittal is multifaceted:
- **Technology optionality**: ArcelorMittal maintains a portfolio of decarbonization pathways including hydrogen-based DRI (HYBRIT collaboration in Europe), carbon capture (Carbalyst project in Ghent), and now MOE through Boston Metal.
- **Geographic diversification**: MOE is particularly suited to regions with low-cost renewable electricity but without access to high-grade DRI pellets — notably Brazil, India, and parts of Africa.
- **Blast furnace replacement timeline**: The average blast furnace reline cycle is 15–20 years. As reline decisions approach, MOE offers a potential alternative to rebuilding carbon-intensive infrastructure.
- **Ore grade flexibility**: ArcelorMittal operates mines producing a range of ore grades, not all suitable for hydrogen-DRI. MOE provides a decarbonization pathway for lower-grade ore assets.

## Breakthrough Energy Ventures

Breakthrough Energy Ventures (BEV), founded by Bill Gates, participated in Boston Metal's Series B and subsequent funding rounds. BEV's mandate is to invest in technologies with the potential to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by at least 500 million tonnes annually — a criterion that Boston Metal's technology meets.

BEV's involvement extends beyond capital:
- Policy advocacy for green steel procurement standards
- Introduction to potential offtake partners across its network
- Technical due diligence capabilities through its fellows program
- Access to the broader Breakthrough Energy ecosystem, including the Catalyst program for first-of-a-kind commercial deployments

## Microsoft Climate Innovation Fund

Microsoft's Climate Innovation Fund invested in Boston Metal as part of its commitment to becoming carbon negative by 2030. The investment is strategic for Microsoft:
- Steel is a major embedded carbon component in data center construction
- Microsoft operates over 200 data centers globally, each requiring thousands of tonnes of steel
- Green steel procurement is a component of Microsoft's scope 3 emissions reduction strategy
- The investment provides potential for offtake agreements once commercial production begins

Microsoft's commitment to purchasing green steel provides demand-side certainty that accelerates technology deployment and helps achieve the learning-curve cost reductions essential for competitiveness with conventional steel.

## International Finance Corporation (IFC)

The IFC, the private-sector arm of the World Bank Group, invested in Boston Metal's later funding rounds. The IFC's involvement reflects its mandate to support sustainable industrialization in developing economies:
- Green steel production supports economic development without locking in high-emissions infrastructure
- The IFC's presence provides political risk mitigation for projects in emerging markets
- IFC environmental and social performance standards provide a framework for project development

## Academic Roots — MIT

Boston Metal was founded based on research conducted in Professor Donald Sadoway's laboratory at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. The foundational patent, US Patent 8,765,262 ("Electrolytic production of metals using consumable anodes"), was filed in 2006, and subsequent improvements have been patented over the following two decades.

MIT's Technology Licensing Office executed an exclusive license to Boston Metal for the core MOE patents. Professor Sadoway remains involved as a scientific advisor, and Boston Metal maintains active research collaborations with MIT's Department of Materials Science and Engineering.

The MIT connection provides not only the core intellectual property but also a pipeline of highly trained electrochemical engineers. Many of Boston Metal's senior technical staff are MIT alumni from Sadoway's research group.

## Supply Chain Partnerships

### Iron Ore Suppliers

Boston Metal has engaged with major iron ore producers to secure feedstocks for its demonstration and commercial plants. Because MOE accepts all ore grades, the negotiating position is favorable — unlike hydrogen-DRI projects that are dependent on a limited supply of high-grade pellets.

### Renewable Energy Providers

The energy intensity of MOE (3.5–4.0 MWh per tonne of iron) means that electricity cost is the dominant operating expense. Boston Metal has established relationships with renewable energy developers in Brazil, where industrial electricity prices from hydropower contracts are among the world's lowest at approximately $25–35 per MWh.

### Engineering and Construction

Boston Metal has partnered with engineering, procurement, and construction (EPC) firms specializing in industrial electrochemistry and high-temperature processing for the design and construction of its commercial cells and balance-of-plant systems. These partnerships leverage existing expertise from the aluminum smelting and electric arc furnace industries.

## Government and Policy Engagement

Boston Metal actively engages with policymakers in the United States and internationally:
- U.S. Department of Energy Industrial Demonstrations Program funding
- Advocacy for green steel procurement standards in federal infrastructure spending
- Engagement with the EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM), which creates a price advantage for low-emissions steel imported into the European market
- Partnership with the Brazilian government on industrial decarbonization incentives

The company has testified before the U.S. Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources on the topic of industrial decarbonization and has been cited in multiple DOE reports on pathways to net-zero industrial emissions.
