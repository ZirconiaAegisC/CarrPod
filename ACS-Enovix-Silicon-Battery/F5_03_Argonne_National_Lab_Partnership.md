# F5-03: Argonne National Laboratory Partnership — Scientific Foundation

## Partnership Overview

Enovix's collaboration with Argonne National Laboratory (U.S. Department of
Energy) represents one of its most strategically valuable relationships. The
partnership provides Enovix with access to world-class characterization
facilities and scientific expertise that no startup could afford to build
in-house, while Argonne gains access to a commercially relevant, cutting-edge
battery technology for its research mission.

## CRADA Structure

The partnership operates under a Cooperative Research and Development Agreement
(CRADA), a standard mechanism for DOE national labs to collaborate with private
industry. Key terms:

- **Duration:** 3 years initially (2023-2026), renewable
- **Cost sharing:** Enovix provides cells, materials, and personnel time;
  Argonne provides facility access and scientific staff
- **IP terms:** Inventions made solely by Argonne are owned by DOE with an
  option for Enovix to negotiate an exclusive license; jointly developed IP
  is co-owned; Enovix retains ownership of its background IP
- **Publication rights:** Argonne retains the right to publish research
  findings, with a 30-day review period for Enovix to identify confidential
  business information for redaction

## Research Programs

### 1. Operando X-Ray Diffraction (XRD) at the Advanced Photon Source (APS)

The APS is one of the world's most powerful synchrotron X-ray sources. Enovix
cells are cycled while illuminated by the APS beam, providing real-time
crystallographic data on:

- **Lithiation phase evolution:** Tracking the transition from crystalline
  silicon → amorphous LixSi → crystalline Li15Si4, and identifying the
  conditions under which the deleterious crystalline phase forms
- **Mechanical strain mapping:** Using the X-ray beam to measure lattice
  strain in the silicon anode as a function of state of charge and spatial
  position within the constraint system
- **SEI composition:** Pair distribution function (PDF) analysis to identify
  nanoscale SEI phases as they evolve during cycling

Key finding (published 2024): Under mechanical constraint, the Li15Si4
crystalline phase — associated with rapid capacity fade — is suppressed in
favor of amorphous LixSi phases, explaining a significant portion of the
cycle life improvement.

### 2. Multi-Physics Modeling

Argonne's computational scientists developed finite-element models of the
constrained silicon electrode that simulate:

- Lithium concentration gradients during charge/discharge
- Stress-strain evolution over hundreds of cycles
- Heat generation and thermal gradients
- The interaction between nanoscale pore structure and macroscale constraint

These models inform Enovix's cell design by predicting the optimal constraint
pressure, porosity level, and silicon film thickness for given performance
targets — dramatically reducing the experimental parameter space.

### 3. Post-Mortem Failure Analysis

Cells that fail during cycling undergo comprehensive post-mortem
characterization at Argonne:

- **FIB-SEM tomography:** 3D reconstruction of the electrode at nanometer
  resolution, showing pore structure evolution, SEI growth, and crack
  formation
- **ToF-SIMS (Time-of-Flight Secondary Ion Mass Spectrometry):** Chemical
  mapping of lithium, fluorine, and electrolyte decomposition products
  across the electrode cross-section
- **XPS depth profiling:** Elemental and chemical state analysis of the SEI
  layer composition as a function of depth

### 4. Independent Performance Validation

Argonne serves as an independent validator for Enovix's performance claims,
a critical function when engaging with skeptical OEM customers. Argonne-
generated data carries the credibility of a national laboratory and has been
instrumental in converting OEM evaluations into design wins.

## Strategic Value Beyond Research

### Talent Pipeline

Several Argonne post-doctoral researchers and staff scientists have joined
Enovix's Fremont R&D team, bringing deep expertise in silicon electrochemistry
and advanced characterization. This talent transfer is a significant but
unquantified benefit of the partnership.

### DOE Ecosystem Access

The Argonne relationship opens doors to the broader DOE battery ecosystem:

- Participation in DOE Battery500 Consortium meetings
- Access to DOE Energy Storage Grand Challenge resources
- Eligibility for DOE funding programs (e.g., Bipartisan Infrastructure Law
  battery manufacturing grants)
- Relationships with other national labs (NREL, Oak Ridge, Lawrence Berkeley)

### Competitive Moat

The depth of characterization data generated through the Argonne partnership
— which would cost $20-30M for a competitor to replicate independently —
represents a significant barrier to entry. The insights from these studies
are embedded in Enovix's electrolyte formulations, heat treatment profiles,
and constraint system parameters as trade secrets.

## Future Directions (2026-2028)

The CRADA renewal scope includes:

- **Solid-state electrolyte integration:** Evaluating solid electrolytes
  (sulfide and oxide types) with the constrained silicon architecture
- **Recycling & circular economy:** Characterizing end-of-life cells to
  inform recycling process design
- **Machine learning for battery lifetime prediction:** Using Argonne's
  extensive cycling dataset to build ML models that predict cell lifetime
  from early-cycle data, enabling faster grading and quality control
