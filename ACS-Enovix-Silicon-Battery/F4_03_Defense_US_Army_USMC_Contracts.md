# F4-03: Defense Contracts — US Army, USMC & Grinergy Partnership

## Defense Sector Entry

Enovix has strategically pursued U.S. Department of Defense (DoD) contracts as
a parallel revenue stream that provides:

1. Non-dilutive funding for technology development
2. Rigorous third-party validation of performance claims
3. A beachhead market with extreme performance requirements and high price
   tolerance — ideal for a novel, premium-priced battery technology

## Grinergy Partnership: US Army $2M Contract

### Program Overview

In 2025, Enovix, in partnership with Grinergy (a defense-focused power systems
integrator), was awarded a $2 million contract by the U.S. Army to develop and
demonstrate silicon-anode battery packs for dismounted soldier applications.

**Program Name:** Soldier Power Battery Pack (SPBP) — Next Generation
**Awarding Office:** U.S. Army Contracting Command - Aberdeen Proving Ground
**Contract Type:** Other Transaction Authority (OTA) — enables rapid
  prototyping without full FAR/DFARS compliance
**Period of Performance:** 18 months (2025-2026)
**Scope:** Deliver 50 prototype battery packs meeting or exceeding the
  performance specifications of the current Conformal Wearable Battery (CWB)

### Technical Requirements

The Army's dismounted soldier power requirements are among the most demanding
in any battery application:

| Requirement | Current CWB (Li-ion) | SPBP Target (Enovix) |
|---|---|---|
| Energy density (pack level) | 200 Wh/kg | >300 Wh/kg |
| Energy per pack | 150 Wh | >250 Wh |
| Weight | 0.75 kg | <0.85 kg |
| Operating temperature | -20°C to +50°C | -30°C to +55°C |
| Water immersion | 1m / 30 min | 2m / 60 min |
| Ballistic survivability | None | MIL-STD-810H |
| Recharge time | 4-6 hours | <2 hours (80%) |
| Unit cost | ~$600 | <$1,200 (acceptable at 2x current for ~70% more energy) |

### Why Enovix Won

The key differentiator was energy density at the pack level. The CWB uses
conventional 18650 cells with graphite anodes. Enovix's Gen2 cells, with
~40% higher energy density at cell level, enable a pack that delivers >300
Wh/kg — a 50% improvement over the current system. For a dismounted soldier
carrying 15-20 lbs of batteries for a 72-hour mission, a 50% weight reduction
or 50% runtime extension is a capability game-changer.

### Technology Transfer Implications

The Grinergy contract has validated Enovix's cells in a demanding real-world
environment, generating data that supports both additional defense contracts
and commercial OEM conversations. Specifically:

- MIL-STD-810H environmental testing (shock, vibration, temperature, altitude)
- Ballistic survivability testing (cells remained functional after projectile
  impact due to rigid housing)
- Extended temperature cycling (-20°C to +55°C) with better-than-expected
  performance, validating the constraint system's thermal stability

## USMC (United States Marine Corps) Contracts

### Program Details

Enovix has engaged with the USMC through the Marine Corps Warfighting
Laboratory (MCWL) and Marine Corps Systems Command (MCSC) for potential
applications including:

1. **Man-portable C4ISR systems:** Radios, tactical computers, and sensor
   packages requiring lightweight, high-energy batteries for extended
   operations in austere environments

2. **Unmanned systems:** Small UAS (Group 1-2 drones) where battery energy
   density directly determines endurance; the USMC's RQ-20 Puma and RQ-11
   Raven programs are potential insertion points

3. **Expeditionary energy:** Forward operating base power systems where
   battery weight affects transportability via helicopter and V-22 Osprey

### Status

As of Q4 2025, USMC engagement is at the "sources sought" and preliminary
testing phase. Enovix has delivered samples to Naval Surface Warfare Center
Crane Division for independent evaluation. No contract value has been publicly
disclosed.

## Defense Market Economics

### Price Tolerance

Defense batteries command 3-5x the ASP of commercial equivalents due to:

- Low-volume production runs (hundreds to thousands, not millions)
- MIL-SPEC testing and documentation overhead
- ITAR and cybersecurity compliance
- Ruggedization and specialized packaging

For Enovix, this means defense contracts can be profitable at current
manufacturing costs ($6/cell) when commercial smartphones require $4-5/cell
for sustainable margins. Defense thus serves as an economic bridge during the
manufacturing ramp.

### Revenue Contribution

| Year | Defense Revenue (Est.) | % of Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| 2025 | $2-4M | 10-15% |
| 2026 | $5-10M | 8-12% |
| 2027 | $10-20M | 5-8% |

Defense revenue is strategically important as a % of revenue in the early
years but will diminish in significance as commercial smartphone revenue
ramps into the hundreds of millions.

## Strategic Value Beyond Revenue

DoD contracts provide:

- **Independent validation:** MIL-SPEC testing by government labs carries
  more credibility with risk-averse commercial OEMs than vendor self-reported
  data
- **Extreme environment data:** Soldiers and Marines use equipment in
  conditions (Arctic, desert, high-altitude) that exceed commercial
  specifications — this data feeds into Gen3 automotive development
- **Supply chain security narrative:** The U.S. government's concern about
  Chinese dominance of the battery supply chain creates tailwinds for
  domestically developed battery technologies, even if final assembly is
  in Malaysia
