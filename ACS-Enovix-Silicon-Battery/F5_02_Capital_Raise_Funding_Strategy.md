# F5-02: Capital Raising & Funding Strategy — $350M+ Journey

## Historical Funding Timeline

Enovix has raised over $350 million since inception through a combination of
private venture funding, SPAC merger proceeds, PIPE investments, and
government grants. This capital has funded the development of the 3D silicon
architecture from lab concept to commercial production.

### Funding Rounds

| Round | Year | Amount ($M) | Key Participants |
|---|---|---|---|
| Series A/B | 2016-2018 | ~$45 | Eclipse Ventures, Intel Capital |
| Series C | 2019 | ~$60 | T.J. Rodgers, Cypress Semiconductor |
| Series D | 2020 | ~$90 | T. Rowe Price, Fidelity |
| SPAC Merger (ENVX) | 2021 | ~$405 | Oppenheimer SPAC (WSHC) + PIPE |
| Government Grants | 2022-2025 | ~$12 | DOE, US Army, USMC |

**Note on SPAC proceeds:** The $405M figure includes $345M from the SPAC
trust plus $175M PIPE. Redemptions reduced net proceeds. After transaction
costs and debt repayment, ~$300M was added to the balance sheet.

### Government Funding

In addition to defense contracts (revenue), Enovix has received non-dilutive
government grants:

- **DOE Advanced Manufacturing Office:** $5M for silicon anode manufacturing
  process development (2022)
- **US Army xTechSearch:** $1.5M prize competition award (2023)
- **NSF SBIR Phase II:** $1.2M for electrolyte development (2022)
- **California Energy Commission:** $3M for Fremont pilot line expansion (2023)

These grants validate the technology from a technical perspective independent
of commercial interests.

## Capital Allocation Priorities (2025-2027)

Management's stated capital allocation framework:

### 1. Fab-2 Construction (~$150M, 60% of remaining cash)

The single largest planned investment. Phased approach (F3-02) is designed to
conserve cash: 2-line Tranche 1 can be funded without additional raise, while
Tranche 2 and 3 are gated on commercial traction.

### 2. Fremont R&D (~$40M/year, 35% of annual spend)

Continued investment in Gen3 automotive development, next-generation cell
designs, and the Argonne collaboration. Management has stated they will
maintain R&D intensity (20-25% of revenue plus incremental investment) even
as revenue ramps, reflecting the technology-driven nature of the business.

### 3. Working Capital (~$15-20M)

Inventory (raw materials, WIP, finished goods) and receivables will grow as
commercial volume increases. The cash conversion cycle is expected to be
neutral to slightly negative in the ramp phase (OEMs pay in 30-60 days,
suppliers require 15-30 days).

### 4. Strategic Reserves (~$50M)

Minimum cash balance maintained for operational flexibility, to avoid forced
capital raises at unfavorable valuations, and to provide negotiating leverage
with OEMs.

## Future Financing Strategy

### Timing Considerations

Enovix is likely to require additional capital in the 2027-2028 timeframe to
complete Fab-2 and potentially begin Fab-3 planning for automotive scale. The
timing and form of this raise will be influenced by:

1. **Commercial progress:** If ENVX secures 2-3 Tier 1 smartphone design wins
   and revenue is tracking toward $200M+ in 2027, the company can raise from
   a position of strength.

2. **Stock price:** Current ENVX valuation (~$1.5-2B market cap) makes equity
   dilution painful but manageable. A stock price recovery to $20+ would make
   a secondary offering much more attractive.

3. **Strategic investors:** A smartphone OEM or automotive partner may invest
   directly (strategic PIPE), providing capital plus commercial validation.
   Several Asian OEMs have active corporate venture arms with battery
   technology mandates.

### Financing Options

| Option | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|---|---|---|
| Public equity offering | Deepest pool of capital | Dilutive at current valuation |
| Strategic PIPE | Customer validation + capital | May limit commercial flexibility |
| Convertible debt | Lower dilution, interest tax shield | Balance sheet leverage, conversion risk |
| Equipment lease financing | Preserves equity, tax-efficient | Higher effective cost of capital |
| DOE ATVM loan | Low interest, long tenor | Slow, bureaucratic, domestic content requirements |
| Project finance (Fab-2) | Non-recourse, off-balance-sheet | Complex, requires guaranteed offtake |

### Most Likely Scenario

A combination of (a) equipment lease financing for Fab-2 tools, (b) a modest
convertible note issuance in 2027, and (c) potential strategic PIPE from an
OEM customer if a major design win materializes. Pure equity is viewed as a
last resort.

## Dilution Analysis

At current ~$2 share price and 160M shares outstanding, every $100M raised
in equity adds ~50M shares (~31% dilution). Management is acutely aware of
this and has prioritized non-dilutive and minimally dilutive financing
options.

The board (which includes T.J. Rodgers, a vocal opponent of excessive
dilution) has set a target of raising no more than $100M in pure equity
over the next 3 years, with the balance of capital needs met through
debt, equipment financing, and customer-funded NRE.
