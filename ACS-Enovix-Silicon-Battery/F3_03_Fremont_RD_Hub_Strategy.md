# F3-03: Fremont R&D Hub — Innovation Engine Strategy

## Dual-Site Operating Model

Enovix operates a "two-site" model: Fremont, California as the R&D and
technology development hub, and Penang, Malaysia as the high-volume
manufacturing center. This separation — common in semiconductor but rare
in battery manufacturing — reflects the precision nature of Enovix's 3D
silicon architecture, which shares more DNA with semiconductor fabrication
than traditional battery slurry coating.

## Fremont Facility Overview

### Capabilities

- Pilot production line: 1 MWh/year capacity (approximately 200,000 cells),
  used for process development, material qualification, and customer samples
- Advanced materials lab: XRD, XPS, SEM/TEM, GC-MS for electrode and
  electrolyte characterization
- Electrochemical testing: 500+ formation and cycling channels across
  temperature-controlled chambers (-20°C to +80°C)
- Mechanical testing: Instron load frames for housing/constraint system
  validation; CT scanner for non-destructive 3D imaging of cycled cells
- Safety lab: ARC (accelerating rate calorimetry), nail penetration, crush,
  and overcharge testing per UL/IEC standards

### Team Composition

- Total Fremont headcount: ~180 (Q4 2025)
- PhD-level researchers: ~45 across materials science, electrochemistry,
  mechanical engineering, and process engineering
- Patent portfolio: 120+ granted patents, 80+ pending applications
  (core IP in constrained electrode architecture and silicon anode
  processing)

## Key R&D Programs

### 1. Gen3 Architecture: Automotive-Grade Silicon Cells

The Fremont team is developing Gen3 — an evolution of the constrained
silicon platform targeting automotive qualification. Key objectives:

- Calendar life extension from ~5-8 years to >12 years through advanced
  electrolyte formulations (LiFSI-dominant salts, dual-additive systems)
- High-temperature stability: operation at 60°C continuous (current Gen2
  limit is ~45°C) via ceramic-coated separators and cathode surface coatings
- Low-temperature performance: -20°C discharge capability (current limit
  is ~0°C) through electrolyte co-solvent engineering (ethyl acetate,
  methyl butyrate)
- Cost reduction: cathode formulation shift from NMC 811 to NMC 955 or
  lithium-rich manganese oxide (LRMO) to reduce cobalt content

### 2. Gen2 Continuous Improvement

- Electrolyte longevity: testing 50+ additive formulations to push Gen2
  cycle life from 1,000 to 1,500 cycles
- Manufacturing simplification: evaluating single-step silicon deposition
  (eliminating separate heat treatment) via in-situ heated PVD
- Housing cost reduction: evaluating aluminum alloys and rigid polymers as
  lower-cost alternatives to stainless steel

### 3. Next-Generation Cell Designs

- **Ultra-thin form factors:** 2.5-3.0 mm cell height for foldable phones
  and ultrabooks, requiring re-engineering of the constraint system for
  reduced cross-section
- **Large-format prismatic:** Scaling the 3D architecture to automotive-
  scale prismatic cells (50-100 Ah) while maintaining mechanical constraint
  integrity — a significant engineering challenge due to larger thermal
  gradients and constraint force scaling
- **Bipolar stacking:** Series-connected cells within a single housing,
  increasing pack voltage without external interconnects, targeting 12-48V
  module-level voltage

### 4. Argonne National Lab Collaboration

Enovix's partnership with Argonne (see F5-03) is managed through the Fremont
facility. Argonne provides:

- Advanced Photon Source (APS) synchrotron beamtime for operando XRD of
  silicon lithiation
- High-performance computing for multi-physics modeling of constrained
  electrode degradation
- Independent performance validation supporting OEM qualification

## Technology Transfer to Malaysia

A formal technology transfer process governs the transition of processes from
Fremont (pilot) to Penang (high-volume):

1. **Stage 0:** Concept feasibility (Fremont, 100-1,000 cells)
2. **Stage 1:** Process development (Fremont, 1,000-10,000 cells)
3. **Stage 2:** Pilot qualification (Fremont, 10,000-50,000 cells)
4. **Stage 3:** Manufacturing validation (Fab-1 Penang, 50,000-200,000 cells)
5. **Stage 4:** Production release (Fab-1 or Fab-2, >200,000 cells)

Each stage gate requires documented process capability (CpK >1.0),
yield (>70%), and performance data against target specifications.

## Intellectual Property Strategy

Enovix's IP strategy leverages the Fremont location's proximity to Silicon
Valley's legal and venture ecosystem:

- Core constraint architecture patents filed in US, EU, China, Korea, Japan
- Trade secret protection for electrolyte formulations, heat treatment
  profiles, and manufacturing process parameters
- Defensive publishing on non-core improvements to prevent competitor
  patenting

The Fremont team generates approximately 20-25 invention disclosures per
quarter, with 8-12 converting to filed patent applications.
