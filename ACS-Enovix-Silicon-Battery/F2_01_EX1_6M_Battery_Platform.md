# F2-01: EX1-6M Battery Platform — Gen2 Product Definition

## Platform Overview

The EX1-6M is Enovix's first commercial product under the Gen2 architecture.
Designated "6M" for its 6 MWh annual production capacity target per line, the
EX1-6M is purpose-built for the smartphone and premium consumer electronics
segment — the highest value-per-watt-hour market available for silicon anode
batteries.

## Physical Specifications

### Form Factor

Unlike cylindrical (18650, 21700) or pouch cells that dominate automotive, the
EX1-6M uses a prismatic form factor optimized for smartphone cavities:

- Nominal dimensions: 4.5 mm × 65 mm × 85 mm (customizable within ±15%)
- Nominal capacity: 4,500-5,000 mAh (single cell)
- Nominal voltage: 3.7V (standard Li-ion range)
- Energy density: 900-950 Wh/L at cell level
- Weight: ~55-60 grams

For comparison, a flagship smartphone battery (e.g., Samsung Galaxy S-series)
typically provides 4,500-5,000 mAh in a 5.0-5.5 mm thick pouch cell at ~700
Wh/L. The EX1-6M delivers equivalent capacity in a thinner package or ~25%
more capacity in the same volume.

### Electrical Performance

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| Charge voltage (max) | 4.35V |
| Discharge cut-off | 3.0V |
| Standard charge rate | 0.5C |
| Fast charge rate | 2C (80% in ~25 min) |
| Max continuous discharge | 3C |
| DC internal resistance | <25 mΩ (fresh) |
| Cycle life (to 80% SOH) | >500 cycles at 1C/1C |

### Safety Certifications

The EX1-6M has passed standard UL 1642 and IEC 62133 safety testing including:
nail penetration, overcharge to 12V, 130°C hot box, and short circuit. The
rigid housing provides inherent crush resistance that pouch cells lack.

## Target Customers

### Smartphone OEM Sampling Program

Enovix initiated a sampling program with 5-8 "Tier 1" smartphone manufacturers
in mid-2025. These OEMs are evaluating EX1-6M cells for:

1. **Thinner flagship phones:** Using the 4.5 mm cell height to reduce device
   thickness by 0.5-1.0 mm while maintaining battery life

2. **Extended runtime SKUs:** Deploying higher-capacity cells in "Pro" or
   "Max" variants without increasing device footprint

3. **Foldable devices:** The rigid prismatic form factor offers mechanical
   advantages over pouch cells in hinged designs

### Penetration Timeline

- 2025 H2: Sampling and qualification (3-6 month OEM cycles)
- 2026 H1: Design wins and initial purchase orders
- 2026 H2: First commercial smartphone launch with EX1-6M
- 2027: Volume ramp to >10M units/year across multiple OEMs

## Competitive Positioning vs. Established Incumbents

### Samsung SDI, LG Energy Solution, ATL (Amperex Technology Ltd.)

These three suppliers dominate the premium smartphone battery market. Their
NMC-graphite cells achieve ~700-750 Wh/L. Enovix's 900-950 Wh/L represents a
~25-30% step-improvement — compelling enough to justify qualification effort
by OEMs seeking differentiation.

### Price Premium Strategy

At initial commercial scale (11.5M units/year from Fab-1), EX1-6M cells are
priced at approximately $8-12/cell vs. $4-6 for comparable graphite cells.
This 2x premium is justified by the energy density advantage and is absorbed
into the bill of materials for $800+ flagship smartphones. As yields improve
and Fab-2 volume comes online, target ASP is $6-8/cell at >50M units/year,
bringing the premium closer to 1.3-1.5x.

## Design Win Economics

A single flagship smartphone model ships 20-50M units per year. Winning one
design slot at a major OEM represents $100-400M in annual revenue potential
at $8-10/cell. This "lumpy" revenue model — high concentration, high value per
win — defines Enovix's near-term commercial strategy.
