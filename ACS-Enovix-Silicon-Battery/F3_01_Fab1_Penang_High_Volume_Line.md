# F3-01: Fab-1 Penang — High-Volume Gen2 Manufacturing Line

## Facility Overview

Fab-1, located in the Batu Kawan Industrial Park, Penang, Malaysia, is
Enovix's first high-volume manufacturing facility and the global launch
site for Gen2 production. The facility was inaugurated in March 2025
following an 18-month construction and equipment installation phase.

### Physical Specifications

- Total site area: ~200,000 sq ft (~18,600 sq m)
- Cleanroom classification: ISO Class 7 (10,000) for electrode fabrication;
  ISO Class 8 (100,000) for assembly and formation
- Installed power capacity: 15 MW (expandable to 25 MW)
- Water treatment: On-site deionized water plant (500 L/min capacity)
- Workforce: ~500 employees at target utilization (Q4 2025: ~320)

### Line Configuration

Fab-1 houses one Gen2 high-volume line with an annual nameplate capacity of
11.5 million units. The line is designed with a modular architecture — each
of the nine process stations (F2-02) can be individually scaled by adding
parallel modules without disrupting adjacent stations.

## Production Ramp Status

### Q1-Q4 2025 Ramp Data

| Quarter | Units Produced | Yield (Grade A) | OEE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 2025 | ~85,000 | 42% | 38% |
| Q2 2025 | ~310,000 | 58% | 51% |
| Q3 2025 | ~720,000 | 67% | 63% |
| Q4 2025 | ~1,350,000 | 78% | 72% |
| **FY2025 Total** | **~2,465,000** | — | — |

The ramp trajectory shows a classic S-curve: slow initial progress as process
parameters were tuned (Q1-Q2), accelerating improvement as operators gained
experience and equipment was optimized (Q3), and approaching steady-state
performance by Q4.

### First Commercial Shipments

In Q3 2025, Enovix shipped its first commercial production units to OEM
customers — a milestone that marked the transition from pre-revenue R&D
to commercial operations. These initial shipments went to 3-4 smartphone
OEMs undergoing qualification testing.

### Key Lessons from the Fab-1 Ramp

1. **PVD deposition uniformity** was the primary yield bottleneck. Non-
   uniform silicon film thickness (±15% initially) caused cell-to-cell
   capacity variation. Solution: redesigned magnetron target configuration
   and in-situ thickness monitoring reduced variation to ±5%.

2. **Formation cycling throughput** was initially constrained by the number
   of formation channels. Cycle time was reduced from 18 hours to 12 hours
   by optimizing charge protocols, and additional channels were installed
   in Q3 2025, doubling throughput.

3. **Supply chain localization:** Initial dependence on imported materials
   (separators from Japan, electrolyte from Korea) caused lead-time issues.
   By Q4 2025, 60% of materials were sourced from Southeast Asian suppliers,
   reducing logistics risk and cost.

## Penang Ecosystem Advantages

### Why Malaysia?

- **Established semiconductor/electronics workforce:** Penang has a 50-year
  history as a global electronics manufacturing hub (Intel, AMD, Keysight,
  etc.), providing an experienced technical workforce for precision
  manufacturing

- **Cost competitiveness:** Direct labor costs ~60-70% lower than Fremont;
  total manufacturing cost ~40% lower when including utilities, facilities,
  and logistics

- **Government incentives:** Malaysia Investment Development Authority (MIDA)
  granted Pioneer Status with 10-year tax holiday and investment tax
  allowances for the Enovix facility

- **Proximity to smartphone supply chain:** Major EMS (electronics
  manufacturing services) providers and smartphone assembly plants in
  Malaysia, Vietnam, and Thailand are within 2-4 hours flight time

### Workforce Development

Enovix partnered with the Penang Skills Development Centre (PSDC) to create
a customized training program for battery manufacturing technicians. The
program graduates ~50 technicians per cohort with skills in vacuum systems,
precision metrology, and lithium-ion cell formation — domain expertise not
previously available in Malaysia's electronics-centric workforce.
