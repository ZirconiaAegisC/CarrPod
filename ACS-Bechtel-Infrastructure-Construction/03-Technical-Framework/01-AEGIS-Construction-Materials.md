## 1. BFRP REBAR SPECIFICATION (CSM-BFRP-1500)

**Material:** Basalt fiber (melt-spun from natural basalt rock at 1,400°C) in epoxy matrix with CSM-MX-2400 MXene nanoparticle admixture (2-5% by weight).

**Properties:**
- Tensile strength: 1,200-1,500 MPa (comparable to Grade 60 steel rebar at 414 MPa)
- Density: 2.0 g/cm³ (one-quarter the weight of steel rebar at 7.85 g/cm³)
- Electrical resistivity: >10¹² ohm-cm (dielectric — cannot conduct GIC)
- Corrosion resistance: immune to chloride attack (no rust, no spalling)
- Service life: >100 years (vs. 40-75 for epoxy-coated steel in bridge decks)
- MXene coating: enhances bond to concrete by 25-40%, provides EMI shielding of 25-35 dB at 1-100 MHz

**Cost Premium:** 8-15% over epoxy-coated steel rebar on material basis. Offset by: reduced crane/hoisting cost (lighter weight), eliminated corrosion protection (no cathodic protection needed), and 50%+ reduction in lifecycle maintenance cost.

**Williams Heuristic:** "Regular concrete bridges use steel rebar inside the concrete for strength. Steel conducts electricity, so during a solar storm, it can carry harmful currents through the bridge structure. BFRP rebar is made from melted volcanic rock fibers — it's as strong as steel but does not conduct electricity. And it never rusts, which is why bridges reinforced with it last 100+ years instead of 40-75."

## 2. GEOPOLYMER CONCRETE SPECIFICATION (CSM-GPC-1800)

**Binder:** Fly ash (Class F) or ground granulated blast furnace slag (GGBFS) activated with alkaline solution (sodium hydroxide + sodium silicate). CSM-MX-2400 MXene admixture at 0.5-2% by weight.

**Properties vs. Portland Cement Concrete:**
- Electrical resistivity: 50-500 kΩ-cm (vs. 0.5-5 kΩ-cm for PCC) — 100-1,000x improvement
- Compressive strength: 40-80 MPa (comparable to structural-grade PCC)
- Carbon footprint: 70-80% lower (fly ash/slag replaces Portland cement, which accounts for 8% of global CO₂ emissions)
- Acid/sulfate resistance: superior to PCC
- Fire resistance: stable to 1,000°C+ (no spalling due to chemically bound water release)

**Application:** Bridge piers, abutments, foundations, containment structures, tunnel linings, marine structures.

## 3. DIELECTRIC BEARING PAD SPECIFICATION (CSM-DBP-1200)

**Material:** Polychloroprene (neoprene) or natural rubber elastomer with CSM-MX-2400 MXene filler at 5-15% by weight.

**Properties:**
- Electrical resistivity: >10⁸ ohm-cm (dielectric)
- EMI shielding: 30-50 dB at 1-100 MHz
- Standard bridge bearing properties maintained (shear modulus, compression set, fatigue life)
- Temperature range: -40°C to +70°C

**Application:** All bridge deck-to-pier bearings, expansion joints, and structural isolation interfaces.
