## Technology Platform

### SCC55 Architecture
The SCC55 composite consists of a tuned porous carbon scaffold that hosts amorphous silicon within its pore structure. The carbon matrix provides structural integrity, electronic conductivity, and a framework for stable solid-electrolyte interphase (SEI) formation. Silicon domains are engineered at the nanoscale to minimize volumetric expansion stress while maximizing reversible lithium storage capacity.

### Key Technical Advantages
1. **Volume Buffering**: The porous carbon scaffold accommodates silicon expansion (up to 300%) without particle fracture
2. **Stable SEI**: Engineered carbon surface chemistry promotes formation of a thin, stable SEI layer
3. **Drop-in Integration**: Compatible with existing graphite anode slurry mixing, coating, and calendaring processes
4. **Cycle Life**: Demonstrated >1,000 cycles at commercially relevant areal capacities
5. **First-Cycle Efficiency**: >90% FCE, competitive with graphite while delivering superior capacity

### Competitive Moat
Group14 holds over 100 granted and pending patents covering carbon scaffold synthesis, silicon infiltration methods, and composite particle architectures. The proprietary chemical vapor deposition (CVD) process for silicon incorporation into the carbon matrix represents a core trade-secret manufacturing advantage.

## Strategic Thesis

Group14 is positioned to capture significant share of the silicon anode transition due to:
1. First-mover advantage in commercial-scale production (BAM-1 operational, BAM-2 scaling)
2. Drop-in compatibility minimizing customer switching costs
3. Deep strategic partnerships with both automotive OEMs (Porsche) and materials suppliers (SK)
4. Strong IP portfolio protecting core carbon scaffold technology
5. DOE backing validating national strategic importance

The primary execution risk is the BAM-2 scale-up — transitioning from 100 tons/yr to 10,000 tons/yr represents a two-order-of-magnitude manufacturing challenge. Competitor Sila Nano's Moses Lake facility targets similar scale, creating a race to capture the silicon anode market.
