CSMDropBox — Staging, Duplicates, and Document Management

The Librarian's Corner of the Aegis Archive. Featuring Mork, Chester, and Zirconia.

THE QUICK PITCH

A knowledge base of six hundred and fifty-two directories generates a lot of files. CSMDropBox is the managed staging area — documents being reviewed before promotion, duplicates being compared, format conversions bridging Markdown and PDF. The Duplicates subdirectory holds comparison tools and merge workflows. Nothing in CSMDropBox is final. Everything is in transit. The directory is not glamorous — no SiblingFrequency episode will ever be set here — but without it, the versioning discipline that underpins every CSM document's credibility would drown in unmanaged copies. Step into the document office.

THE CONVERSATION

A document management office. Monitors display diff tools. A scanner hums. Mork is narrating version changes. Chester is sorting duplicate PDFs. Zirconia stands at the filing cabinets.

ZIRCONIA: Welcome to CSMDropBox. I am Zirconia, director of information architecture, and these are Mork and Chester. The tradition of archival science — from the Library of Alexandria to modern digital preservation — provides the framework. CSMDropBox applies it to a knowledge base where a single changed decimal point in a material property can cascade through hundreds of documents. Mork, the workflow.

MORK: Three states: Draft, Revision, Duplicate. Each has a workflow with automated steps and human approval gates. The validation suite — link checker, readability auditor, dependency graph regenerator — runs on every document before promotion. A document that fails validation does not leave CSMDropBox. The validation suite is the immune system.

CHESTER: The Duplicates subdirectory. Every document exists in multiple formats. When a revision occurs, all formats are regenerated and compared. The merge workflow resolves conflicts. The canonical source is authoritative. For sure.
