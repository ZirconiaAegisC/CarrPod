# Glossary of Technical Terms

**Date:** 15 July 2026

## A-D

- **Anodizing:** Electrochemical surface treatment process that thickens the natural oxide layer on aluminum and titanium alloys, improving corrosion resistance and enabling color finishes. Apple uses anodizing extensively on aluminum and titanium device enclosures.
- **ASHRAE TC 9.9:** American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers Technical Committee 9.9, responsible for mission-critical facilities, technology spaces, and electronic equipment thermal guidelines.
- **BECCS:** Biomass Energy with Carbon Capture and Storage. A carbon-negative energy pathway combining biogenic fuel combustion with geological or mineral CO2 sequestration.
- **CDU:** Coolant Distribution Unit. The interface between facility-level chilled water or condenser water loops and rack-level liquid cooling circuits, providing fluid conditioning, pumping, and temperature control.
- **Ceramming:** The controlled heat-treatment process by which a glass is partially crystallized to form a glass-ceramic, involving a nucleation stage at lower temperature followed by crystal growth at higher temperature.
- **CFD:** Computational Fluid Dynamics. A numerical simulation methodology for solving fluid flow and heat transfer equations, used extensively to model data center airflow and temperature distributions.
- **Ceramic Shield:** Apple's trademarked glass-ceramic composite material for iPhone display covers, produced in partnership with Corning. Incorporates nanoscale ceramic crystallites within a glass matrix to improve drop performance and scratch resistance.
- **CII:** Carbon Intensity Indicator. A measure of CO2 emissions per unit of economic output, typically expressed in grams CO2 equivalent per U.S. dollar of revenue.
- **CNC:** Computer Numerical Control. A subtractive manufacturing process using computer-controlled machine tools. Apple uses CNC milling extensively for aluminum and titanium unibody enclosure production.
- **Compressive Stress (CS):** The surface-layer compressive stress introduced in glass through ion exchange, measured in megapascals (MPa). Higher CS improves resistance to fracture from surface flaws.
- **Corning Incorporated:** Apple's primary glass and glass-ceramic technology partner, recipient of Apple Advanced Manufacturing Fund investments totaling over $450 million since 2017.
- **CRAC/CRAH:** Computer Room Air Conditioner / Computer Room Air Handler. Precision cooling units for data centers that condition and circulate air through raised-floor or overhead distribution systems.
- **DAC:** Direct Air Capture. A technology for extracting carbon dioxide directly from ambient air using chemical sorbents or solvents.
- **Dielectric Fluid:** An electrically insulating liquid used in immersion cooling systems. Single-phase dielectric fluids remain in liquid state at operating temperatures; two-phase fluids boil at the chip surface, utilizing latent heat of vaporization.
- **DTC:** Direct-to-Chip Cooling. A liquid cooling approach that circulates coolant through cold plates attached directly to processor or memory module surfaces.
- **Dual-Ion Exchange:** A glass strengthening process involving two sequential ion-exchange baths, typically with different salt compositions, bath temperatures, or dwell times to optimize surface compressive stress and case depth.
- **Case Depth (DOL):** Depth of Layer. The depth from the glass surface to the point where compressive stress transitions to tensile stress, measured in microns.
- **FMEA:** Failure Mode and Effects Analysis. A systematic methodology for identifying potential failure modes in a product or process, assessing their severity, occurrence likelihood, and detectability, and prioritizing corrective actions.
- **HHI:** Herfindahl-Hirschman Index. A measure of market or supply concentration calculated as the sum of squared market shares of all participants, ranging from near-zero (highly fragmented) to 10,000 (monopoly).

## E-L

- **ESG:** Environmental, Social, and Governance. A framework for evaluating corporate sustainability and ethical performance, used by investors and rating agencies.
- **FBAR:** Film Bulk Acoustic Resonator. A radio-frequency filter technology manufactured by Broadcom for wireless communications applications.
- **FOM:** Figure of Merit. A dimensionless performance metric used to compare heat transfer fluids or cooling system architectures.
- **Fusion-Draw Process:** Corning's proprietary glass sheet manufacturing process that produces ultra-thin, pristine glass surfaces without grinding or polishing.
- **GWP:** Global Warming Potential. A measure of the heat-trapping capacity of a greenhouse gas relative to carbon dioxide over a specified time horizon (typically 100 years).
- **IP68:** An ingress protection rating under IEC standard 60529 indicating dust-tight construction (6) and protection against continuous immersion in water beyond 1 meter depth (8).
- **Knoop Hardness:** A microhardness measurement technique using an elongated pyramidal diamond indenter. Used to characterize scratch resistance of Ceramic Shield and competing display cover materials.
- **KPI:** Key Performance Indicator. A quantifiable measure used to evaluate success against defined objectives.
- **KOH:** Potassium Hydroxide. Used in alkaline etch processes for glass surface treatment, including post-ion-exchange surface layer removal to eliminate handling-induced flaws.
- **LCA:** Life Cycle Assessment. A methodology for evaluating environmental impacts across all stages of a product's life, from raw material extraction through manufacturing, use, and end-of-life.
- **LIBS:** Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy. An analytical technique using laser ablation to determine elemental composition; applied to alloy sorting in aluminum recycling operations.
- **LTL:** Liquid-to-Liquid. A heat exchanger configuration in which both primary and secondary circuits use liquid working fluids.
- **MRV:** Measurement, Reporting, and Verification. A protocol framework for quantifying, documenting, and independently auditing greenhouse gas emissions reductions or removals.

## M-R

- **OCP:** Open Compute Project. An industry consortium that develops and publishes open-source reference designs for data center hardware, including servers, racks, and cooling systems.
- **PPA:** Power Purchase Agreement. A long-term contract between an electricity generator and a purchaser, commonly used by Apple and its suppliers to procure renewable energy.
- **PP&E:** Property, Plant, and Equipment. A balance sheet line item representing long-term physical assets, including data center buildings, manufacturing equipment, and tooling.
- **PUE:** Power Usage Effectiveness. The ratio of total facility energy consumption to IT equipment energy consumption. PUE = total facility power / IT equipment power. Ideal value approaches 1.0. Apple's fleet average PUE is a key metric in the Implementation Framework.
- **PVD:** Physical Vapor Deposition. A vacuum coating process used to apply thin-film metallic or ceramic coatings to device enclosures and components.
- **PVT:** Pressure, Volume, Temperature. A thermodynamic framework for characterizing fluid behavior, relevant to two-phase immersion cooling system design.
- **PFAS:** Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances. A class of fluorinated organic compounds, some of which are used in dielectric cooling fluids. Increasingly subject to environmental regulation in the U.S. and EU, driving interest in PFAS-free dielectric fluid alternatives.
- **REC:** Renewable Energy Certificate. A tradable instrument representing the environmental attributes of one megawatt-hour of renewable electricity generation.
- **RDHx:** Rear-Door Heat Exchanger. A liquid-to-air heat exchanger mounted on the rear of a server rack, capturing exhaust air heat into a liquid cooling loop.
- **RTD:** Resistance Temperature Detector. A precision temperature sensor utilizing the predictable change in electrical resistance of a metal (typically platinum) with temperature. Class A RTDs achieve accuracy of +/-0.15degC at 0degC.

## S-Z

- **SBTi:** Science Based Targets initiative. A partnership organization that validates corporate emissions reduction targets against climate science benchmarks.
- **Scope 1, 2, 3 Emissions:** Greenhouse gas emissions categorized by source. Scope 1: direct emissions from owned or controlled sources. Scope 2: indirect emissions from purchased electricity, steam, heating, and cooling. Scope 3: all other indirect emissions in the value chain.
- **SPIC:** Single-Phase Immersion Cooling. A data center cooling method in which IT equipment is submerged in a dielectric fluid that remains in the liquid phase, removing heat through sensible heat gain and natural or forced convection.
- **Ti-6Al-4V:** Titanium alloy Grade 5, composed of 90% titanium, 6% aluminum, and 4% vanadium. Used by Apple for iPhone 15 Pro and 16 Pro enclosures.
- **TPIC:** Two-Phase Immersion Cooling. A cooling method utilizing a dielectric fluid that boils at the heat source surface, removing heat through latent heat of vaporization, then condenses on a heat exchanger surface and returns to the fluid pool.
- **TRL:** Technology Readiness Level. A scale from 1 (basic principles observed) to 9 (actual system proven in operational environment) used to assess technology maturity.
- **TDP:** Thermal Design Power. The maximum sustained power that a processor or component is designed to dissipate under realistic workload conditions.
- **Unibody:** A manufacturing approach in which a device enclosure is machined from a single block of material (typically aluminum or titanium), providing structural rigidity and precise dimensional control.
- **Vickers Hardness:** An indentation hardness test using a square-based pyramidal diamond indenter. Used alongside Knoop for characterizing display cover material mechanical properties.
- **WUE:** Water Usage Effectiveness. The ratio of data center water consumption to IT equipment energy consumption, expressed in liters per kilowatt-hour (L/kWh).
- **Zero Waste:** An operational target in which 100% of waste generated by a facility is diverted from landfill through reuse, recycling, composting, or waste-to-energy conversion.
- **24/7 CFE:** 24/7 Carbon-Free Energy. An energy procurement paradigm in which each hour of electricity consumption is matched with carbon-free generation on the same grid within the same hour, going beyond annual REC-based renewable energy matching.
