# Ampaire Product Portfolio — Eco Caravan Hybrid-Electric Aircraft

## Platform Overview

The Eco Caravan is Ampaire's flagship product: a hybrid-electric conversion
of the Cessna 208B Grand Caravan, one of the most widely operated regional
utility aircraft in the world. The conversion replaces the standard Pratt and
Whitney PT6A-114A turboprop engine with Ampaire's AMP-H570 hybrid-electric
powertrain while retaining the Grand Caravan's airframe, systems, and
operating characteristics. First flight was achieved in November 2022,
marking a critical milestone on the path to FAA Supplemental Type Certificate
(STC) approval.

## Why the Cessna Grand Caravan

The Grand Caravan was selected as Ampaire's initial conversion platform based
on a deliberate set of criteria:

### Installed Base

Over 3000 Cessna 208-series aircraft have been delivered since the type's
introduction in 1984, with approximately 2000 Grand Caravans currently in
service worldwide. This large installed base provides:

- A substantial addressable market for conversions
- Established maintenance infrastructure and parts supply chain
- Widespread pilot familiarity with the type's handling characteristics
- Existing route networks operated by Caravan-equipped airlines

### Mission Profile

The Grand Caravan's typical mission profile aligns well with hybrid-electric
propulsion economics:

- **Stage length**: Average mission lengths of 50 to 250 nautical miles —
  within the sweet spot where battery-electric boost provides maximum fuel
  savings
- **Payload**: 9 to 14 passengers or equivalent cargo, with typical load
  factors of 60 to 80 percent on regional routes
- **Altitude**: Operations at 8000 to 15000 feet, avoiding the energy penalty
  of climbing to higher altitudes where turbine efficiency advantages
  diminish for short missions
- **Utilization**: 600 to 1200 flight hours annually per aircraft, providing
  rapid payback on fuel savings

### Regulatory Pathway

The Grand Caravan is certified under FAA Part 23 (normal category) with an
established modification history. Ampaire's STC pathway — modifying an
existing certified aircraft rather than certifying a new type — is
significantly faster and less costly than a new Type Certificate. The FAA
has accepted the Grand Caravan STC basis under the agency's evolving
framework for electric and hybrid-electric propulsion certification.

## Performance Specifications

### Target Performance (Eco Caravan with AMP-H570)

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Seating | 9 to 11 passengers |
| Payload | 1200 to 1500 kg |
| Maximum takeoff weight | 3970 kg (8750 lb) |
| Cruise speed | 165 to 175 knots TAS |
| Electric-only range | 50 to 80 nm |
| Hybrid range (with reserves) | 400 to 500 nm |
| Fuel consumption (150 nm mission) | 50 to 70 percent less than baseline |
| Takeoff distance (MTOW, SL, ISA) | 650 to 750 m |
| Landing distance | 550 to 600 m |
| Service ceiling | 15000 to 18000 ft |
| Rate of climb | 800 to 1000 fpm |

### Comparison with Baseline Grand Caravan

The Eco Caravan matches or exceeds the baseline Caravan's payload and range
for the regional missions that constitute the majority of Caravan operations
while reducing fuel consumption by half or more. The electric propulsion
system adds approximately 200 to 300 kg to the aircraft's empty weight,
primarily from the battery system. This weight increase is partially offset
by the lighter automotive-derived engine compared with the PT6A, resulting
in a net empty weight increase of approximately 150 to 250 kg — within the
aircraft's structural margins as validated during the STC process.

## Cabin and Systems

### Cockpit Modifications

The Eco Caravan cockpit retains the Grand Caravan's Garmin G1000 NXi avionics
suite with additional displays and controls for the hybrid powertrain:

- Power management display showing power flow, battery state of charge,
  range extender status, and energy consumption trends
- Simplified power control: a single power lever commands total propulsive
  power; the power management system automatically allocates power between
  battery and range extender based on phase of flight and system status
- Annunciator panel providing hybrid system caution and warning indications
- Backup instruments for critical hybrid system parameters in the event of
  primary display failure

### Passenger Cabin

The passenger cabin is unchanged from the baseline Grand Caravan, preserving
the existing interior configuration, seating, and cargo capability.
Ampaire's conversion focuses exclusively on the propulsion system, minimizing
the scope of interior modifications that would add certification complexity
and cost. Battery modules are installed in under-floor compartments and in
a modified aft baggage area, with no intrusion into the passenger cabin
space.

### Environmental Control

The aircraft's environmental control system (cabin heat, windshield defrost)
is modified to use electric heating elements rather than engine bleed air,
since the automotive range extender does not provide the high-volume bleed
air supply that the PT6A turbine generates. This change improves cabin
heating efficiency — electric heating delivers heat directly to the cabin
without the thermodynamic losses of bleeding compressed air from the engine —
and enables cabin heating during electric-only ground operations.

## Certification Status

The Eco Caravan's certification pathway follows the FAA STC process:

1. **Project specific certification plan (PSCP)**: Submitted to and accepted
   by the FAA, establishing the certification basis including applicable
   airworthiness standards and means of compliance
2. **Ground testing**: Powertrain ground testing completed, including
   endurance testing, failure mode testing, and electromagnetic compatibility
   testing
3. **Flight testing**: Test aircraft accumulating flight hours toward the
   required test program; first flight achieved November 2022
4. **Conformity**: Production-conforming aircraft and systems undergoing
   conformity inspection
5. **STC issuance**: Target 2024 to 2025 for FAA STC approval

Post-STC, Ampaire will pursue supplemental validation from other civil
aviation authorities — including EASA (Europe), Transport Canada, and ANAC
(Brazil) — to enable Eco Caravan operations in additional markets.

## Production and Delivery

Initial Eco Caravan conversions will be performed at Ampaire's Hawthorne,
California facility, with a capacity of approximately 12 to 24 aircraft per
year. Ampaire is establishing partnerships with existing Cessna-authorized
maintenance facilities to create a network of conversion centers that can
scale production as demand grows. The conversion process, described in detail
in the manufacturing documentation, is designed for a 6 to 8 week turnaround
from induction of the donor aircraft to delivery of the completed Eco Caravan.
