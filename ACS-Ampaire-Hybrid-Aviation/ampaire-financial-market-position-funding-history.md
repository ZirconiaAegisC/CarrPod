# Ampaire Financial and Market Position — Funding and Capital Structure

## Funding History

Ampaire has raised over 50 million USD in equity and grant funding since its
founding in 2016, positioning the company as one of the leading capitalized
startups in the emerging hybrid-electric aviation sector. The funding
trajectory reflects a deliberate strategy of leveraging government grants for
early-stage technology development, transitioning to venture capital for
commercialization, and accessing strategic corporate investment for market
access and scale-up.

### Seed and Early Rounds

Ampaire was founded in 2016 by CEO Kevin Noertker and a team of aerospace
engineers with backgrounds at Northrop Grumman, SpaceX, and other aerospace
and technology companies. Initial funding came from angel investors and
early-stage venture capital, supplemented by government grants including:

- **NASA Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR)**: Multiple awards
  supporting hybrid-electric propulsion technology development
- **US Department of Energy**: Grants supporting battery technology
  development for aviation applications
- **California Energy Commission**: Funding for electric aviation
  infrastructure development

Government grants provided non-dilutive capital for technology risk reduction,
allowing Ampaire to achieve key technical milestones before raising larger
equity rounds at more favorable valuations.

### Series A (Approximately 2018-2019)

The Series A round raised approximately 10 to 15 million USD, led by venture
capital firms with expertise in aerospace and clean technology. This round
funded:

- Construction and flight testing of the Electric EEL technology demonstrator
- Initial design and engineering of the AMP-H570 powertrain
- Launch of partnerships with Mokulele Airlines and other early customers
- Expansion of the engineering team from approximately 10 to 40 employees

### Growth Rounds (2020-2022)

Subsequent funding rounds raised approximately 35 to 45 million USD in
aggregate, attracting:

- **Venture capital**: Existing investors increasing their positions and new
  investors entering the syndicate
- **Strategic corporate investors**: Surf Air Mobility, the regional air
  mobility platform, acquired a majority stake in Ampaire through a
  combination of equity investment and operational partnership
- **Industry partners**: Funds from partners in the aviation and energy
  sectors interested in the hybrid-electric propulsion market

These rounds funded the transition from technology demonstration to product
development, including the Eco Caravan certification program, establishment
of the Hawthorne conversion facility, and expansion of the customer pipeline.

### Surf Air Mobility Investment

Surf Air Mobility's investment in Ampaire represents a strategic milestone.
Surf Air, a publicly traded regional air mobility company, operates a network
of scheduled and on-demand regional flights and has announced plans to
electrify its fleet. The investment:

- Provides Ampaire with access to Surf Air's operational platform for
  real-world deployment of Eco Caravan aircraft
- Creates a captive launch customer that can absorb initial production
  capacity
- Positions Ampaire within a larger corporate structure that offers potential
  for synergies in aircraft operations, maintenance, and financing
- Provides Surf Air investors with exposure to the hybrid-electric technology
  development value creation

The terms of Surf Air's majority stake and the governance relationship between
the companies are not publicly disclosed in detail, but the partnership
represents a model where an operating platform and a technology developer
align to accelerate commercial deployment.

## Grant and Government Funding

Ampaire has been particularly successful in securing government funding,
reflecting the alignment between its technology development and government
priorities for sustainable aviation:

- **NASA Electrified Powertrain Flight Demonstration (EPFD) program**:
  Collaboration with NASA on hybrid-electric propulsion technology
  development and flight demonstration
- **FAA Continuous Lower Energy, Emissions, and Noise (CLEEN) program**:
  Partnership supporting technology development for reduced environmental
  impact
- **Department of Defense**: Interest in hybrid-electric propulsion for
  military applications including reduced acoustic signature, reduced fuel
  logistics requirements, and operational flexibility

Government funding provides approximately 10 to 20 percent of Ampaire's
total funding, serving as non-dilutive capital that supports technology
development while reducing the equity capital required for certification.

## Revenue Model

### Aircraft Conversions

The primary revenue stream is Eco Caravan conversions, priced at
approximately 800000 to 1.2 million USD per conversion for customer-provided
aircraft. For Ampaire-acquired and converted aircraft, the sale price includes
the donor aircraft cost plus conversion, typically 2.5 to 3.5 million USD
for a mid-time airframe with fresh conversion.

### Recurring Revenue

Ampaire's recurring revenue streams include:

- **Battery replacements**: Battery packs sold for replacement at end of
  life, generating recurring revenue every 5 to 8 years per aircraft
- **Powertrain component overhauls**: Motor bearing replacement, power
  electronics refurbishment, range extender engine overhaul — generating
  recurring maintenance revenue over the aircraft's life
- **Software and data services**: Power management system software updates,
  operational data analytics, and fleet management services offered on a
  subscription basis
- **Training**: Initial and recurrent pilot and mechanic training for the
  hybrid powertrain system

At steady state, recurring revenue is projected to represent 30 to 40
percent of total revenue, providing the financial characteristics of an
aerospace aftermarket business.

### Technology Licensing

Ampaire has indicated interest in licensing its hybrid-electric propulsion
technology for application to other aircraft types beyond the Grand Caravan.
Potential licensing revenue includes:

- **Engineering fees**: Non-recurring engineering for adapting the AMP-H570
  powertrain to other aircraft types
- **Royalties**: Per-aircraft royalties on conversions performed by licensees
- **Component sales**: Sale of Ampaire-produced powertrain components to
  licensees performing conversions under Ampaire's STC

Licensing expands Ampaire's addressable market beyond the conversions it can
perform in-house, enabling faster market penetration with lower capital
investment.

## Financial Trajectory and Capital Requirements

### Path to Profitability

Ampaire's path to profitability depends on:

- Achieving STC issuance to begin revenue-generating deliveries
- Ramping production to a rate that covers fixed operating costs —
  estimated at 20 to 30 conversions annually at Hawthorne plus initial
  partner center contribution
- Achieving target gross margins of 25 to 35 percent on conversions
  through production efficiency and supply chain cost reduction
- Building recurring revenue to a level that provides a stable base
  independent of conversion volume

At full-rate production (60 to 120 aircraft annually), Ampaire would
generate estimated annual revenue of 80 to 180 million USD at an estimated
gross margin of 30 to 40 percent, providing the foundation for sustained
profitability and reinvestment in technology development.

### Additional Capital Requirements

Ampaire is likely to require one to two additional funding rounds before
achieving cash flow breakeven:

- **Bridge to STC**: A round funding the final certification activities and
  initial production ramp — estimated 20 to 40 million USD
- **Scale-up round**: A round funding production capacity expansion and
  market development — estimated 50 to 100 million USD

Sources of additional capital may include existing investors, strategic
corporate partners, government loan guarantees (such as the Department of
Energy's Title XVII program for clean energy technology), and potentially
public markets. Surf Air Mobility's status as a public company provides a
potential pathway to public market access for Ampaire, though no specific
plans for an IPO or SPAC transaction have been publicly announced.

### Exit Scenarios

Longer-term exit scenarios for investors include:

- **Initial public offering**: Ampaire goes public as a standalone company,
  providing liquidity to investors and access to growth capital from public
  markets
- **Acquisition by strategic buyer**: A larger aerospace company — Textron
  (parent of Cessna), an engine manufacturer, or an aerospace systems
  supplier — acquires Ampaire to integrate hybrid-electric propulsion into
  its product portfolio
- **Merger with Surf Air Mobility**: Full integration into Surf Air's
  corporate structure, with Ampaire shareholders receiving Surf Air equity
- **Continued private operation**: Ampaire operates as a privately held
  company generating returns to shareholders through dividends from
  profitable operations

The most probable exit scenario will depend on Ampaire's execution against
its certification and production targets, the development of the broader
electric aviation market, and the strategic priorities of its major
shareholders including Surf Air Mobility.
