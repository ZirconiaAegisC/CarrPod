# DOCUMENT 08: TITLE VI — NUCLEAR WORKFORCE PIPELINE

## TITLE VI — NUCLEAR WORKFORCE PIPELINE

### SECTION 601. NUCLEAR SCIENCE AND ENGINEERING SCHOLARSHIP PROGRAM.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy, through the Office of Nuclear Energy, shall establish a Nuclear Science and Engineering Scholarship Program to fund undergraduate and graduate education for students pursuing degrees in nuclear engineering, health physics, radiochemistry, nuclear materials science, and related disciplines.

**(b) Scholarship Structure.** —

(1) UNDERGRADUATE SCHOLARSHIPS. — Not fewer than 3,000 four-year scholarships of up to $25,000 per year for tuition, fees, books, and living expenses.

(2) GRADUATE FELLOWSHIPS. — Not fewer than 1,500 three-year graduate fellowships of up to $35,000 per year, plus full tuition and fees.

(3) POSTDOCTORAL FELLOWSHIPS. — Not fewer than 500 two-year postdoctoral fellowships of up to $75,000 per year for research in advanced reactor design, fusion energy, nuclear fuel cycles, and nuclear safety.

**(c) Service Commitment.** — Recipients of scholarships under this section shall commit to employment in a nuclear energy-related occupation for a period of not fewer than two years following completion of the funded degree program. The Secretary may waive the service commitment in cases where the recipient demonstrates that, despite good-faith effort, suitable nuclear energy employment was not available.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for scholarships, fellowships, and related program administration under this section.

### SECTION 602. UNIVERSITY REACTOR INFRASTRUCTURE UPGRADES.

**(a) University Reactor Infrastructure Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a competitive grant program for universities operating research and training reactors to upgrade reactor infrastructure, instrumentation, and safety systems.

**(b) Grant Purposes.** — Grants may be used for:

(1) reactor control system modernization, including digital instrumentation and control upgrades;

(2) irradiated materials handling and examination facilities;

(3) radiation detection and measurement laboratory equipment;

(4) reactor cooling and safety system upgrades required to maintain or obtain Nuclear Regulatory Commission operating licenses;

(5) facilities and equipment for advanced reactor and fusion energy research; and

(6) establishment of new university research reactors at institutions that currently lack such capabilities, with priority for Minority-Serving Institutions and institutions in states without existing university reactors.

**(c) Grant Amounts.** — Grants under this section shall not exceed $20,000,000 per institution, with a federal cost share not to exceed 80 percent. The non-federal share may include in-kind contributions.

**(d) Target.** — Not fewer than 25 university reactors shall receive infrastructure upgrade grants over the ten-year authorization period.

**(e) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 603. FUSION WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT INITIATIVE.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy, through the Office of Fusion Energy Sciences, shall establish a Fusion Workforce Development Initiative to address the critical shortage of trained fusion scientists, engineers, and technicians identified in Finding 17 of this Act.

**(b) Program Components.** —

(1) FUSION ENERGY CENTERS OF EXCELLENCE. — Designation and funding of not fewer than 15 university-based Fusion Energy Centers of Excellence integrating fusion science, plasma physics, materials science, and fusion engineering disciplines.

(2) FUSION TECHNICIAN TRAINING. — Not fewer than 10 community and technical college programs offering two-year associate degrees and one-year certificates in fusion technology operations, maintenance, and safety.

(3) FUSION INDUSTRY INTERNSHIPS. — A matching program under which the federal government reimburses fusion energy companies for 50 percent of intern wages and benefits, up to $15,000 per intern per year, for internships of not fewer than 10 weeks duration.

(4) FUSION POSTDOCTORAL ACCELERATOR. — Not fewer than 200 two-year postdoctoral positions at national laboratories and fusion companies focused on applied fusion technology development, including magnet systems, plasma-facing materials, tritium breeding, and balance-of-plant engineering.

(5) INTERNATIONAL FUSION WORKFORCE EXCHANGE. — A bilateral exchange program with ITER partner nations enabling United States fusion scientists and engineers to gain operational experience at international fusion facilities.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 604. NUCLEAR TECHNICIAN CERTIFICATION EXPANSION.

**(a) National Nuclear Technician Certification.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Nuclear Regulatory Commission and the Nuclear Energy Institute, shall develop and implement a National Nuclear Technician Certification (NNTC) program establishing uniform competency standards for nuclear operations, maintenance, radiation protection, and instrumentation and control technicians.

**(b) Certification Tracks.** — The NNTC shall include the following certification tracks:

(1) NUCLEAR OPERATIONS TECHNICIAN. — Covering reactor operations fundamentals, plant systems, emergency procedures, and human performance.

(2) NUCLEAR MAINTENANCE TECHNICIAN. — Covering mechanical, electrical, and instrumentation and control maintenance for nuclear facilities, including advanced reactor-specific competencies.

(3) RADIATION PROTECTION TECHNICIAN. — Covering radiological monitoring, dosimetry, contamination control, and emergency response.

(4) NUCLEAR FUEL CYCLE TECHNICIAN. — Covering uranium enrichment, fuel fabrication, HALEU production, and spent fuel management.

**(c) Training Program Accreditation.** — The Secretary shall establish a national accreditation system for nuclear technician training programs at community and technical colleges, with initial accreditation of not fewer than 25 programs by December 31, 2029.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $300,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 605. HALEU AND ADVANCED FUEL CYCLE TRAINING.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a HALEU and Advanced Fuel Cycle Training Program in partnership with national laboratories, including Idaho National Laboratory, Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, to train the workforce necessary for domestic HALEU production, advanced fuel fabrication, and fuel cycle facility operations.

**(b) Program Activities.** —

(1) not fewer than 500 trainees annually in HALEU production operations, including centrifugal enrichment, downblending, and deconversion;

(2) not fewer than 300 trainees annually in advanced fuel fabrication for TRISO, metallic, and molten salt reactor fuels;

(3) nuclear criticality safety training for advanced fuel cycle facilities;

(4) spent fuel and advanced reactor waste management technician training; and

(5) virtual reality and simulation-based training modules for fuel cycle facility operations.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.
