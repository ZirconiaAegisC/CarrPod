## SECTION 1203. ECONOMIC IMPACT PROJECTIONS AND REPORTING.

**(a) Macroeconomic Impact.** — The following projections are based on analysis by the Department of Energy, the Council of Economic Advisers, and labor market modeling:

| Metric | Projection |
|--------|-----------|
| Total federal investment authorized | $50 billion |
| Cumulative worker earnings catalyzed (10 years) | $200 billion |
| GDP contribution from workforce gains (cumulative) | $350 billion |
| Workers trained and certified | 2,000,000+ |
| New registered apprenticeships | 500,000 |
| Veterans placed in clean energy jobs | 100,000 |
| Fossil fuel workers transitioned | 200,000 |
| Clean Energy Corps members (annual peak) | 30,000 |
| Fiscal multiplier (estimated) | 3.5x-4.0x |
| Private sector co-investment | $100 billion |

**(b) Sectoral Employment Projections (2035).**

| Sector | Projected Employment |
|--------|---------------------|
| Solar installation and maintenance | 350,000 |
| Wind turbine service and manufacturing | 180,000 |
| Battery and energy storage manufacturing | 150,000 |
| Grid modernization and transmission | 140,000 |
| Nuclear energy operations and construction | 95,000 |
| Semiconductor and advanced manufacturing | 130,000 |
| Carbon removal and DAC operations | 60,000 |
| Green hydrogen production | 55,000 |
| Electric vehicle and charging infrastructure | 200,000 |
| Energy efficiency and building performance | 220,000 |
| Sustainable materials manufacturing | 85,000 |
| Geothermal drilling and operations | 40,000 |
| Bridge inspection, coating, and cathodic protection | 35,000 |
| Clean Energy Corps | 30,000 |
| Other clean energy occupations | 230,000 |
| **TOTAL** | **2,000,000** |

**(c) Fiscal Impact of Tax Credits.** —

| Credit Program | 10-Year Revenue Cost ($B) |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| Apprenticeship hiring credit (Sec. 45AA) | 1.5 |
| Clean energy worker hiring credit (Sec. 45BB) | 2.0 |
| Small business transition credit (Sec. 45CC) | 0.5 |
| Worker relocation deduction (Sec. 199B) | 0.3 |
| Wage subsidy program (Sec. 903) | 1.5 |
| Total tax expenditure | 5.8 |

**(d) Return on Investment.** — The $50 billion federal investment is projected to generate $200 billion in cumulative worker earnings and $350 billion in GDP contribution, representing a 7:1 ratio of GDP contribution to federal investment. Reductions in unemployment insurance, SNAP, and other federal safety net expenditures attributable to program participants are projected to offset not less than $15 billion of program costs.

**(e) Long-Term Competitiveness Impact.** — The global clean energy market is projected to exceed $2 trillion annually by 2035. A trained domestic workforce at the scale authorized by this Act positions the United States as the global leader in clean energy manufacturing, installation, and services, with projected clean energy export revenues of $250-400 billion annually by 2040.
