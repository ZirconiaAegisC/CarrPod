# 119TH CONGRESS — WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND GREEN JOBS ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 04: TITLE II — COMMUNITY COLLEGE MANUFACTURING CENTERS

---

## TITLE II — COMMUNITY COLLEGE MANUFACTURING CENTERS

### SECTION 201. CLEAN MANUFACTURING CENTER OF EXCELLENCE PROGRAM.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Secretary of Education, shall establish a Clean Manufacturing Center of Excellence (CMCE) Program to designate and fund not fewer than 200 centers at community and technical colleges across all 50 states, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, Guam, the United States Virgin Islands, American Samoa, and the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands.

**(b) Selection Criteria.** — In designating Centers, the Secretaries shall consider:

(1) demonstrated partnerships with clean energy, semiconductor, battery, nuclear, and advanced manufacturing employers within the region;

(2) alignment of proposed training programs with regional labor market demand as documented by state and local workforce development boards;

(3) capacity and commitment to serve students from energy transition communities, underrepresented populations, and veterans;

(4) articulation agreements with four-year institutions enabling credit transfer and stackable credential pathways;

(5) proposed curriculum aligned with the National Clean Energy Apprenticeship Corps standards developed under section 105; and

(6) commitments from regional employers to interview, hire, or provide paid internships to Center graduates.

**(c) Center Requirements.** — Each CMCE shall:

(1) offer at least three credential or degree programs in clean energy or advanced manufacturing occupations;

(2) maintain industry-standard equipment and laboratories relevant to the technologies taught;

(3) employ at least one full-time industry liaison responsible for employer partnerships, internship coordination, and job placement;

(4) provide comprehensive student support services, including academic advising, career counseling, financial aid navigation, and wraparound supports such as childcare referrals and transportation assistance; and

(5) submit annual performance reports to the Department of Energy detailing enrollment, completion, credential attainment, job placement rates, and employer satisfaction.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants to Centers under this section, with not fewer than $1,500,000 annually in base operating support per designated Center.

### SECTION 202. EQUIPMENT MODERNIZATION GRANTS.

**(a) Purpose.** — The Secretary of Education, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish an Equipment Modernization Grant Program to upgrade laboratory and workshop equipment at community and technical colleges for clean energy and advanced manufacturing training programs.

**(b) Grant Amounts.** — Grants under this section shall be awarded in amounts not to exceed $5,000,000 per campus, with a required non-federal cost share of not less than 25 percent, which may include in-kind contributions of equipment, software, or instructional materials from industry partners.

**(c) Eligible Equipment.** — Grant funds may be used for acquisition of:

(1) solar photovoltaic training arrays and installation simulators;

(2) wind turbine nacelle and blade service simulators;

(3) battery manufacturing and testing equipment;

(4) electric vehicle and charging station service bays;

(5) semiconductor cleanroom equipment suitable for educational use;

(6) industrial robotics and automation training cells;

(7) advanced manufacturing equipment including CNC machines, 3D printers, and computer-aided design workstations;

(8) grid modernization and protective relay testing equipment;

(9) virtual and augmented reality training systems for hazardous or high-cost environments; and

(10) learning management systems and digital credentialing platforms.

**(d) Priority.** — In awarding grants, the Secretary shall give priority to institutions serving energy transition communities, Tribal Colleges and Universities, and Minority-Serving Institutions.

**(e) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 203. INDUSTRY PARTNERSHIP CURRICULUM DEVELOPMENT.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Education shall establish a competitive grant program to fund partnerships between community and technical colleges and clean energy, semiconductor, and advanced manufacturing employers for the co-development of industry-validated curriculum.

**(b) Eligible Partnerships.** — Grants may be awarded to consortia consisting of at least one community or technical college and at least three regional employers in clean energy or advanced manufacturing industries.

**(c) Grant Activities.** — Grant funds may be used for:

(1) collaborative curriculum design and development incorporating employer-defined competency requirements;

(2) development of competency-based and accelerated training pathways enabling students to earn credentials in 6 to 18 months;

(3) creation of open educational resources freely available to other institutions;

(4) development of bilingual and multilingual training materials to serve diverse learner populations; and

(5) integration of work-based learning, including paid internships, cooperative education, and clinical placements.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 204. FACULTY TRAINING AND INDUSTRY EXCHANGE.

**(a) Faculty Fellowship Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Clean Energy Faculty Fellowship Program providing paid summer fellowships of 8 to 12 weeks for community and technical college faculty to work in clean energy and advanced manufacturing companies, national laboratories, or federal research facilities.

**(b) Fellowship Stipend.** — Fellows shall receive a stipend of $15,000 per fellowship period and reimbursement for travel and housing expenses.

**(c) Industry Instructor Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an Industry Instructor Loan Program under which clean energy and advanced manufacturing companies may place experienced technicians and engineers as adjunct instructors at partner community and technical colleges, with the federal government reimbursing the company for 50 percent of the employee's salary and benefits during the teaching assignment, up to $75,000 per instructor per academic year.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 205. CREDENTIALING AND CERTIFICATION ALIGNMENT.

**(a) National Clean Energy Credential Clearinghouse.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Education, shall establish and maintain a National Clean Energy Credential Clearinghouse providing:

(1) a publicly accessible registry of all industry-recognized credentials, certifications, and licenses applicable to clean energy and advanced manufacturing occupations;

(2) crosswalks mapping credentials to registered apprenticeship programs, associate and bachelor's degree programs, and military occupational specialties;

(3) recognition criteria for credentials to qualify for federal hiring preferences, contractor qualification requirements, and tax credit apprenticeship compliance; and

(4) credit-for-prior-learning frameworks enabling students to receive academic credit for industry credentials, military training, and verified work experience.

**(b) State Alignment Grants.** — The Secretary of Education shall award matching grants to states to align community and technical college curricula with the National Clean Energy Credential Clearinghouse and to establish statewide articulation agreements ensuring seamless transfer of credits between institutions.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.
