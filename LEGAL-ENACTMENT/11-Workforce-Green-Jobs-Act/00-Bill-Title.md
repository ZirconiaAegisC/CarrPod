## A BILL

To establish a comprehensive national workforce development and training infrastructure for the clean energy, advanced manufacturing, critical infrastructure, and emerging technology sectors, to authorize apprenticeship expansion programs, community college manufacturing centers, veteran transition programs, fossil fuel worker bridge programs, K-12 STEM pipeline initiatives, nuclear and semiconductor workforce pipelines, a Clean Energy Corps, green jobs tax credits, and tribal workforce development programs, and for other purposes.

### SECTION 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSES.

**(a) Findings.** — Congress finds the findings set forth in the Findings Document of this Act to be accurate and material to the purposes of this legislation.

**(b) Purposes.** — The purposes of this Act are to —

(1) train and deploy not fewer than 2,000,000 American workers into clean energy, advanced manufacturing, critical infrastructure, and emerging technology occupations by calendar year 2036;

(2) establish a permanent national workforce development infrastructure that connects K-12 education, community colleges, apprenticeship programs, and employers across all 50 states, territories, and Tribal nations;

(3) ensure that no American worker or community is left behind in the transition to a clean energy economy, with explicit bridges for fossil fuel workers, veterans, and historically underserved populations;

(4) create not fewer than 500,000 new registered apprenticeships in clean energy, semiconductor manufacturing, nuclear energy, battery production, carbon removal, and related fields;

(5) achieve a labor force participation rate in the clean energy sector that reflects the demographic diversity of the United States workforce; and

(6) catalyze not less than $200 billion in cumulative worker earnings and economic output through the training, certification, and deployment programs authorized by this Act.

### SECTION 3. DEFINITIONS.

In this Act:

**(1) CLEAN ENERGY OCCUPATION.** — The term "clean energy occupation" means employment in the design, construction, manufacturing, installation, operation, maintenance, or decommissioning of technologies and infrastructure for renewable energy generation, energy storage, grid modernization, nuclear energy, carbon management, clean manufacturing, electric transportation, sustainable buildings, and other activities that reduce or avoid greenhouse gas emissions.

**(2) REGISTERED APPRENTICESHIP.** — The term "registered apprenticeship" means an apprenticeship program registered with the Department of Labor under the National Apprenticeship Act (29 U.S.C. 50 et seq.) or a State Apprenticeship Agency recognized by the Department of Labor.

**(3) ENERGY TRANSITION COMMUNITY.** — The term "energy transition community" means a county, parish, census tract, or Tribal area in which employment in coal mining, coal-fired power generation, oil and gas extraction, or petroleum refining accounted for not less than 1 percent of total employment or not fewer than 250 jobs as of January 1, 2020, or in which a coal mine or coal-fired power plant has closed or been announced for closure on or after January 1, 2015.

**(4) QUALIFIED VETERAN.** — The term "qualified veteran" means an individual who served on active duty in the Armed Forces and was discharged or released under conditions other than dishonorable.

**(5) CLEAN ENERGY CORPS.** — The term "Clean Energy Corps" means the national service program established under Title VIII of this Act, modeled on the Civilian Conservation Corps of 1933 through 1942.
