## TITLE XI — FUNDING AND APPROPRIATIONS

### SECTION 1101. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

**(a) In General.** — There are authorized to be appropriated to carry out Titles I through X of this Act a total of $50,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036, to remain available until expended unless otherwise specified.

**(b) Annual Authorization.** — The authorization of appropriations under subsection (a) shall be apportioned by fiscal year as follows:

| Fiscal Year | Authorization ($ billions) |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| 2027 | 2.0 |
| 2028 | 3.5 |
| 2029 | 5.0 |
| 2030 | 6.0 |
| 2031 | 6.5 |
| 2032 | 6.5 |
| 2033 | 6.5 |
| 2034 | 5.5 |
| 2035 | 5.0 |
| 2036 | 3.5 |
| **TOTAL** | **50.0** |

**(c) Rationale.** — The authorization schedule features a ramp-up in early years to allow for program rulemaking, grant competition processes, and institutional capacity building; a sustained peak during the middle years corresponding to maximum enrollment in apprenticeship, training, and service programs; and a gradual ramp-down as workforce pipelines achieve maturity.

### SECTION 1103. OVERSIGHT AND REPORTING.

**(a) Annual Workforce Report.** — Not later than March 31 of each year, the Secretary of Labor shall submit to Congress a comprehensive report detailing:

(1) obligations and expenditures under each title;

(2) enrollment, completion, placement, and earnings outcomes for all training, apprenticeship, and service programs;

(3) demographic composition of program participants, disaggregated by occupation, geography, and program type;

(4) employer satisfaction and hiring metrics; and

(5) progress toward the 2,000,000-worker training target and 500,000-apprentice target.

**(b) GAO Review.** — The Comptroller General shall conduct independent evaluations of program effectiveness at years 3, 5, and 8 after enactment.

**(c) Public Dashboard.** — The Secretary of Labor shall maintain a publicly accessible online dashboard providing real-time data on all metrics specified in subsection (a).

### SECTION 1104. PROGRAM EVALUATION AND METRICS.

**(a) Key Performance Indicators.** — Program effectiveness shall be evaluated based on:

(1) job placement rate at 6 and 12 months post-completion;

(2) median earnings at 12 and 36 months post-completion;

(3) apprentice and trainee completion rates;

(4) employer return-on-investment and satisfaction scores;

(5) reduction in occupational skills gap as measured by employer surveys; and

(6) participant demographic diversity relative to local labor force composition.

**(b) Sunset.** — Authority to provide new grants and tax credits under this Act shall expire on September 30, 2036, except that obligations incurred before such date shall remain in effect.

## TITLE XII — IMPLEMENTATION AND ECONOMIC IMPACT

### SECTION 1201. IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE.

**(a) Phase I — Program Establishment (FY 2027-2028).**

- Not later than 90 days after enactment: publish Federal Register notices for all grant and tax credit programs;
- Not later than 180 days after enactment: designate first 50 Clean Manufacturing Centers of Excellence;
- Not later than 270 days after enactment: publish final rules for Clean Energy Apprenticeship Standards;
- Not later than 365 days after enactment: first apprentice cohorts enrolled; first Clean Energy Corps members deployed;
- Not later than December 31, 2028: 15 Tribal Clean Energy Training Centers operating; 25 RTCOs staffed.

**(b) Phase II — Early Deployment (FY 2029-2031).**

- Not later than FY 2029: 50,000 new apprentices registered; 10,000 Corps members deployed;
- Not later than FY 2030: 100 Centers of Excellence operating; 25,000 veterans placed;
- Not later than FY 2031: 250,000 cumulative apprentices registered; 15 university fusion centers designated.

**(c) Phase III — Scale-Up (FY 2032-2034).**

- Not later than FY 2033: 150 Centers of Excellence operating;
- Not later than FY 2033: 30,000 Corps members deployed annually;
- Not later than FY 2034: 500,000 cumulative apprentices registered; 50 fast-track semiconductor programs operating.

**(d) Phase IV — Full Capacity (FY 2035-2036).**

- Not later than FY 2035: 200 Centers of Excellence operating;
- Not later than FY 2036: 2,000,000 cumulative workers trained; 100,000 veterans placed; 500,000 apprentices completed.

### SECTION 1202. MILESTONE ACCOUNTABILITY.

The Secretary of Labor shall submit quarterly progress reports on all milestones. If any milestone is projected to be missed, a corrective action plan shall be submitted to Congress not later than 90 days before the milestone deadline.
