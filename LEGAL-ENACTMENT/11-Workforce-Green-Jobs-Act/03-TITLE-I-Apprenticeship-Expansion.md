# 119TH CONGRESS — WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND GREEN JOBS ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 03: TITLE I — APPRENTICESHIP EXPANSION

---

## TITLE I — APPRENTICESHIP EXPANSION

### SECTION 101. NATIONAL CLEAN ENERGY APPRENTICESHIP CORPS.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish a National Clean Energy Apprenticeship Corps (NCEAC) to recruit, train, and register not fewer than 500,000 apprentices in clean energy occupations over the ten-year period beginning on the date of enactment of this Act.

**(b) Functions.** — The NCEAC shall:

(1) establish national recruitment targets by occupation, geography, and demographic group, with specific targets for veterans, energy transition community workers, women, and individuals from historically underrepresented populations;

(2) develop model apprenticeship standards for not fewer than 25 clean energy occupations, including:
- Solar photovoltaic installer and designer;
- Wind turbine service technician;
- Battery and energy storage manufacturing technician;
- Electric vehicle and charging infrastructure technician;
- Grid modernization and smart grid electrician;
- Bridge inspection, coating, and cathodic protection technician;
- Direct air capture and carbon removal facility operator;
- Green hydrogen production and fueling technician;
- Geothermal drilling and heat pump installer;
- Industrial heat pump and electrification technician;
- Nuclear operations technician;
- Semiconductor fabrication equipment technician;
- Lithium processing and battery materials technician;
- Transmission and distribution line worker;
- Energy efficiency and building performance auditor; and
- Electric aircraft and advanced air mobility mechanic;

(3) operate a national online portal providing a single point of entry for prospective apprentices to identify programs, apply to openings, and track progress toward journeyworker status; and

(4) provide technical assistance to employers, labor organizations, educational institutions, and state apprenticeship agencies in establishing, registering, and operating apprenticeship programs.

**(c) Reporting.** — Not later than March 1 of each year, the Secretary of Labor shall submit to Congress a report detailing apprentice enrollment, completion, placement, earnings outcomes, and demographic composition of the NCEAC, disaggregated by occupation, state, and apprenticeship program.

### SECTION 102. REGISTERED APPRENTICESHIP TAX CREDIT FOR CLEAN ENERGY EMPLOYERS.

**(a) In General.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

**"SEC. 45AA. CLEAN ENERGY REGISTERED APPRENTICESHIP CREDIT.**

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the clean energy registered apprenticeship credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to the sum of the applicable credit amounts for each qualified apprentice employed by the taxpayer during such taxable year.

"(b) Applicable Credit Amount. —

"(1) FIRST-YEAR CREDIT. — $6,000 per qualified apprentice for the first apprenticeship year.

"(2) SECOND-YEAR CREDIT. — $4,500 per qualified apprentice for the second apprenticeship year.

"(3) THIRD-YEAR CREDIT. — $3,000 per qualified apprentice for the third apprenticeship year.

"(4) COMPLETION BONUS. — $5,000 for each qualified apprentice who completes the registered apprenticeship program and attains journeyworker status during the taxable year, provided the apprentice is retained in employment by the taxpayer for not fewer than 180 days following completion.

"(c) Qualified Apprentice. — For purposes of this section, the term 'qualified apprentice' means an individual who:

"(1) is enrolled in a registered apprenticeship program in a clean energy occupation;

"(2) is employed by the taxpayer in work directly related to such occupation; and

"(3) is not a dependent of the taxpayer.

"(d) Limitations. —

"(1) MAXIMUM CREDIT PER EMPLOYER. — The credit allowed under this section shall not exceed $500,000 per taxpayer per taxable year.

"(2) COORDINATION WITH WORK OPPORTUNITY CREDIT. — No credit shall be allowed under this section for any apprentice with respect to whom a credit is allowed under section 51 for the same taxable year.

"(e) Sunset. — This section shall not apply to apprentices who begin their apprenticeship program after December 31, 2036."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendment made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 103. PRE-APPRENTICESHIP PIPELINE PROGRAM.

**(a) Authorization.** — The Secretary of Labor shall establish a Pre-Apprenticeship Pipeline Grant Program to prepare individuals from underrepresented populations for entry into registered apprenticeship programs in clean energy occupations.

**(b) Eligible Entities.** — Grants under this section may be awarded to:

(1) community-based organizations with demonstrated experience serving populations underrepresented in the construction and manufacturing trades;

(2) labor organizations and labor-management partnerships;

(3) public workforce development boards established under the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (29 U.S.C. 3111 et seq.);

(4) community and technical colleges;

(5) nonprofit organizations with a mission of workforce development; and

(6) consortia of any of the above.

**(c) Grant Activities.** — Grant funds may be used for:

(1) outreach and recruitment targeting women, people of color, justice-involved individuals, individuals with disabilities, and residents of energy transition communities;

(2) basic skills remediation in mathematics, reading comprehension, and workplace communication;

(3) introductory technical training in clean energy technologies;

(4) job readiness coaching, including attendance, punctuality, and professional conduct;

(5) supportive services, including transportation, childcare, tools, and work clothing; and

(6) direct transition support into registered apprenticeship programs.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 104. APPRENTICESHIP COMPLETION INCENTIVE GRANTS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Labor shall establish a competitive grant program providing incentive payments to registered apprenticeship programs in clean energy occupations that achieve specified completion rates, diversity benchmarks, and journeyworker placement targets.

**(b) Incentive Structure.** —

(1) Programs achieving an overall completion rate of 60 percent or higher shall receive $1,500 per completer.

(2) Programs in which not less than 20 percent of completers are women shall receive an additional $2,000 per female completer.

(3) Programs in which not less than 25 percent of completers are from populations underrepresented in the relevant occupation shall receive an additional $2,000 per completer from underrepresented populations.

(4) Programs that place not fewer than 85 percent of completers into journeyworker employment within 90 days of completion shall receive $1,000 per placed completer.

**(c) Use of Incentive Funds.** — Grantees shall use incentive funds for program improvement, including instructor training, equipment acquisition, supportive services for apprentices, and expansion of program capacity.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 105. INDUSTRY-RECOGNIZED APPRENTICESHIP STANDARDS.

**(a) Clean Energy Standards Board.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall convene a Clean Energy Apprenticeship Standards Board composed of representatives of clean energy employers, labor organizations, educational institutions, and workforce development experts.

**(b) Functions.** — The Board shall:

(1) develop model apprenticeship standards for each clean energy occupation identified in section 101(b)(2) that include minimum on-the-job training hours, related technical instruction requirements, competency benchmarks, and progression schedules;

(2) review and update standards not less than every three years to reflect technological change and evolving industry practice;

(3) establish reciprocity agreements enabling apprentices to transfer between registered programs without loss of progress; and

(4) publish national competency certifications recognized across state lines.

**(c) Interoperability.** — Industry-recognized credentials developed under this section shall be designed for interoperability with state licensure requirements, federal contractor qualifications, and Department of Defense SkillBridge equivalencies.
