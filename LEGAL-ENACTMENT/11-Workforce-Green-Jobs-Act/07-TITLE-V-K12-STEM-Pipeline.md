# 119TH CONGRESS — WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND GREEN JOBS ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 07: TITLE V — K-12 STEM PIPELINE INITIATIVE

---

## TITLE V — K-12 STEM PIPELINE INITIATIVE

### SECTION 501. CLEAN ENERGY CAREER TECHNICAL EDUCATION GRANTS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Education, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish a Clean Energy Career Technical Education (CTE) Grant Program for local educational agencies, area career and technical education schools, and educational service agencies.

**(b) Grant Activities.** — Grant funds may be used for:

(1) development and implementation of clean energy CTE programs of study aligned with registered apprenticeship programs under Title I and the National Clean Energy Credential Clearinghouse under section 205;

(2) acquisition of clean energy training equipment, including solar training arrays, wind turbine simulators, electric vehicle service bays, robotics training cells, and energy efficiency diagnostic tools;

(3) dual enrollment and concurrent enrollment partnerships with community and technical colleges enabling high school students to earn college credit and industry credentials while enrolled in secondary education;

(4) work-based learning experiences, including internships, job shadowing, and pre-apprenticeships with clean energy employers; and

(5) student support services, including transportation to training sites, purchase of required safety equipment and tools, and exam fees for industry certification tests.

**(c) Priority.** — The Secretary shall give priority to applications from:

(1) local educational agencies serving energy transition communities;

(2) local educational agencies in which not less than 40 percent of students are eligible for free or reduced-price lunch under the Richard B. Russell National School Lunch Act;

(3) local educational agencies serving students on or near Tribal lands; and

(4) consortia of local educational agencies with business and industry partners.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 502. STEM TEACHER TRAINING IN CLEAN TECHNOLOGIES.

**(a) Clean Energy STEM Teacher Corps.** — The Secretary of Education, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy and the National Science Foundation, shall establish a Clean Energy STEM Teacher Corps to provide training, professional development, and classroom resources for K-12 teachers in clean energy science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

**(b) Program Components.** —

(1) SUMMER INSTITUTES. — Not fewer than 10 regionally distributed summer institutes of two to four weeks duration providing hands-on training in clean energy technologies, curriculum development, and laboratory instruction methods. Participating teachers shall receive a stipend of $3,000 and reimbursement for travel and housing.

(2) ONLINE PROFESSIONAL LEARNING COMMUNITY. — A national online platform providing asynchronous training modules, lesson plans, laboratory exercises, and peer collaboration tools for clean energy STEM educators.

(3) CLASSROOM GRANT PROGRAM. — Grants of up to $5,000 per teacher for acquisition of clean energy demonstration equipment, laboratory supplies, and classroom materials.

(4) NATIONAL LAB TEACHER RESEARCH EXPERIENCES. — Not fewer than 500 eight-week summer research experiences annually at Department of Energy national laboratories for K-12 STEM teachers, with a stipend of $10,000 per teacher.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 503. HIGH SCHOOL PRE-APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAMS.

**(a) Clean Energy Pre-Apprenticeship Pathway.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Education, shall establish a Clean Energy Pre-Apprenticeship Pathway Program enabling high school juniors and seniors to begin registered apprenticeship programs in clean energy occupations while completing secondary education.

**(b) Program Design.** —

(1) Participating students shall complete not fewer than 450 hours of related technical instruction aligned with the corresponding registered apprenticeship program standards;

(2) Participating students shall complete not fewer than 300 hours of paid on-the-job training with an approved clean energy employer during the academic year and summer periods;

(3) Hours completed through the Pre-Apprenticeship Pathway shall be credited toward the on-the-job training and related technical instruction requirements of the corresponding registered apprenticeship program; and

(4) Students who complete the Pre-Apprenticeship Pathway and graduate from high school shall be guaranteed admission to the partner registered apprenticeship program.

**(c) Employer Participation Incentive.** — Employers participating in the Pre-Apprenticeship Pathway Program shall be eligible for a credit under section 102 with respect to qualifying pre-apprenticeship participants, at the rate applicable to first-year apprentices.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 504. SUMMER YOUTH CLEAN ENERGY CORPS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish a Summer Youth Clean Energy Corps to provide paid summer employment, training, and mentorship to not fewer than 50,000 youth aged 16 to 21 annually in clean energy and environmental conservation projects.

**(b) Program Structure.** —

(1) Participants shall be employed for a period of not fewer than 8 weeks and not more than 12 weeks during the summer months.

(2) Participants shall be paid not less than the higher of the federal, state, or local minimum wage.

(3) Participants shall work in supervised crews of not more than 12 members under the direction of a trained crew leader.

(4) At least 20 percent of program hours shall be dedicated to education, training, and career exploration activities.

**(c) Project Types.** — Corps projects shall include:

(1) installation of solar panels and energy efficiency upgrades on public buildings, schools, and low-income housing;

(2) weatherization and building envelope improvements;

(3) tree planting and urban greening for heat island mitigation;

(4) trail construction and maintenance on public lands;

(5) wildfire fuel reduction and forest resilience projects;

(6) coastal and wetland restoration;

(7) community resilience and infrastructure improvement projects; and

(8) energy auditing and data collection in underserved communities.

**(d) Priority Populations.** — Not fewer than 40 percent of participants shall be youth from households with income at or below 200 percent of the federal poverty level, from energy transition communities, or from communities experiencing persistently high youth unemployment.

**(e) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 505. CLEAN ENERGY MAKER SPACES AND LABS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Education shall establish a Clean Energy Maker Space and Lab Grant Program providing funding for the establishment of hands-on clean energy learning laboratories at middle schools and high schools.

**(b) Eligible Equipment and Activities.** — Grant funds may be used for:

(1) design, construction, and outfitting of maker spaces equipped with 3D printers, laser cutters, electronics workbenches, and computer-aided design stations;

(2) small-scale clean energy demonstration systems, including solar panels, wind turbines, battery storage systems, and hydrogen fuel cells suitable for educational use;

(3) robotics and automation training equipment;

(4) energy efficiency and building science diagnostic tools; and

(5) curriculum development and instructor training for maker space-based clean energy education.

**(c) Matching Requirement.** — The federal share of costs under this section shall not exceed 75 percent, with the non-federal share provided by state, local, or private sources.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.
