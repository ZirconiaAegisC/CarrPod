# DOCUMENT 06: TITLE IV — FOSSIL FUEL WORKER BRIDGE PROGRAM

## TITLE IV — FOSSIL FUEL WORKER BRIDGE PROGRAM

### SECTION 401. ENERGY TRANSITION ADJUSTMENT ASSISTANCE.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Labor shall establish an Energy Transition Adjustment Assistance (ETAA) Program for workers dislocated from employment in coal mining, coal-fired power generation, oil and gas extraction, petroleum refining, and related supply chain industries.

**(b) Eligibility.** — An individual is eligible for ETAA benefits if:

(1) the individual was employed for not fewer than 12 of the 24 months preceding separation in an occupation classified under NAICS codes 211 (oil and gas extraction), 2121 (coal mining), 213111 (drilling oil and gas wells), 213113 (support activities for coal mining), 221112 (fossil fuel electric power generation), 32411 (petroleum refineries), or 486 (pipeline transportation);

(2) the individual's separation was due to facility closure, mass layoff, or reduction in operations related to market decline, regulatory action, or employer transition away from fossil fuel activities; and

(3) the individual is enrolled in or has completed a training program in a clean energy occupation approved by the Secretary of Labor.

**(c) Benefits.** — Eligible individuals shall receive:

(1) WAGE INSURANCE. — For a period of up to three years following reemployment, a wage insurance payment equal to 50 percent of the difference between the individual's pre-separation weekly wage and the individual's reemployment weekly wage, up to a maximum payment of $15,000 per year.

(2) TRAINING ALLOWANCE. — During periods of full-time training in an approved clean energy occupation program, a training allowance equal to 70 percent of the individual's pre-separation weekly wage, up to a maximum of $800 per week, for a period not to exceed 24 months.

(3) RELOCATION ALLOWANCE. — A lump-sum relocation allowance of up to $10,000 for moves of not fewer than 50 miles to accept verified clean energy employment.

(4) HEALTH INSURANCE BRIDGE. — Continuation of employer-sponsored health insurance or purchase of coverage through the Health Insurance Marketplace, with the federal government paying 85 percent of premium costs for up to 36 months following separation.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessary for ETAA benefits under this section, estimated at $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

### SECTION 402. WAGE INSURANCE AND BRIDGE INCOME PROGRAM.

**(a) Purpose.** — The wage insurance program established under section 401(c)(1) is designed to ensure that no worker faces an income cliff when transitioning from fossil fuel employment to clean energy employment, thereby removing the primary financial barrier to voluntary workforce transition.

**(b) Benefit Computation.** —

(1) The pre-separation weekly wage shall be calculated as the average of the highest four consecutive quarters of earnings in the 24 months preceding separation.

(2) The wage insurance payment shall be calculated quarterly based on verified reemployment earnings, with payments made monthly.

(3) If the individual's reemployment wage equals or exceeds the pre-separation wage at any point during the three-year benefit period, wage insurance payments shall cease.

**(c) Hardship Bridge Payments.** — For individuals who exhaust training allowances under section 401(c)(2) and have not yet obtained reemployment, the Secretary may authorize additional bridge payments of up to $400 per week for a period not to exceed six months, provided the individual demonstrates active job search as verified by the applicable state workforce agency.

### SECTION 403. COAL AND OIL COMMUNITY REINVESTMENT FUND.

**(a) Establishment.** — There is established in the Treasury of the United States a Coal and Oil Community Reinvestment Fund (CRF), to be administered by the Secretary of Commerce in coordination with the Secretary of Energy.

**(b) Purposes.** — The CRF shall provide grants, loans, and loan guarantees for:

(1) economic diversification planning and implementation in counties, municipalities, and Tribal areas classified as energy transition communities;

(2) small business development and entrepreneurship support in energy transition communities, with priority for businesses owned by former fossil fuel workers;

(3) clean energy manufacturing facility siting and infrastructure development in energy transition communities;

(4) brownfield remediation and site preparation for redevelopment of former mine lands, power plant sites, and industrial facilities for clean energy uses;

(5) broadband, transportation, water, and other enabling infrastructure necessary to attract clean energy employers to energy transition communities; and

(6) community health, education, and quality-of-life investments to address the social impacts of economic transition.

**(c) Allocation.** — Of amounts appropriated to the CRF:

(1) not less than 40 percent shall be reserved for projects in counties with poverty rates exceeding the national average and median household income below the national median;

(2) not less than 10 percent shall be reserved for projects on Tribal lands; and

(3) not less than 25 percent shall be allocated through state-administered block grants to provide flexible funding for locally determined priorities.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the CRF.

### SECTION 404. SKILLS MAPPING AND CERTIFICATION CROSSWALK.

**(a) National Skills Crosswalk Initiative.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy and the Secretary of the Interior, shall develop and maintain a National Fossil Fuel to Clean Energy Skills Crosswalk.

**(b) Contents.** — The Crosswalk shall:

(1) map each significant occupational category in coal mining, coal power generation, oil and gas extraction, and petroleum refining to one or more clean energy occupations for which the incumbent's existing skills satisfy 50 percent or more of the required competencies;

(2) identify the gap training required for each mapped pathway, including estimated duration and cost;

(3) list approved training providers for each gap training program;

(4) incorporate voluntary self-assessment tools enabling workers to receive personalized career pathway recommendations; and

(5) be updated not less than annually to reflect changes in technology and labor market demand.

**(c) Certification Portability.** — The Secretary of Labor shall work with state licensing boards, industry certification bodies, and labor organizations to establish reciprocity agreements ensuring that certifications, licenses, and training completed by fossil fuel workers are recognized for purposes of qualification for clean energy employment.

### SECTION 405. REGIONAL TRANSITION COORDINATION OFFICES.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Commerce, shall establish not fewer than 25 Regional Transition Coordination Offices (RTCOs) in energy transition communities.

**(b) Functions.** — Each RTCO shall:

(1) serve as the single point of contact for workers and employers accessing ETAA benefits, CRF programs, and related federal, state, and local transition assistance;

(2) provide career counseling, skills assessment, and training navigation services to dislocated fossil fuel workers;

(3) coordinate economic development planning and CRF project implementation with state, local, and Tribal governments;

(4) maintain a real-time dashboard of local clean energy job openings, training program availability, and reemployment outcomes; and

(5) convene quarterly stakeholder meetings with employers, labor organizations, educational institutions, and community organizations.

**(c) Staffing.** — Each RTCO shall be led by a director appointed by the Secretary of Labor and shall employ not fewer than five full-time staff, including at least one economic development specialist, one workforce counselor, and one community outreach coordinator.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for RTCO operations.
