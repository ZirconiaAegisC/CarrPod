# 119TH CONGRESS — WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND GREEN JOBS ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 11: TITLE IX — GREEN JOBS TAX CREDIT

## TITLE IX — GREEN JOBS TAX CREDIT

### SECTION 901. EMPLOYER CLEAN ENERGY HIRING TAX CREDIT.

**(a) In General.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

**"SEC. 45BB. CLEAN ENERGY WORKER HIRING CREDIT.**

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the clean energy worker hiring credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to 30 percent of the qualified first-year wages paid or incurred by the taxpayer during the taxable year to each qualified clean energy worker.

"(b) Qualified Clean Energy Worker. — For purposes of this section:

"(1) IN GENERAL. — The term 'qualified clean energy worker' means any individual who:

"(A) is employed by the taxpayer in a full-time position directly engaged in clean energy manufacturing, installation, service, operations, maintenance, or project development;

"(B) begins employment with the taxpayer after December 31, 2026, and before January 1, 2037;

"(C) performs substantially all of their services for the taxpayer within the United States or a possession of the United States; and

"(D) is not a dependent of the taxpayer.

"(2) CLEAN ENERGY ACTIVITIES. — For purposes of paragraph (1)(A), the term 'clean energy' means the activities specified in the Clean Energy Occupations Priority List published by the Secretary of Labor under section 303(c), and shall include at minimum:

"(A) renewable energy generation, including solar, wind, geothermal, and hydropower;

"(B) energy storage manufacturing and installation;

"(C) electric grid modernization and smart grid technologies;

"(D) nuclear energy, including advanced reactors and fusion;

"(E) carbon dioxide removal and utilization;

"(F) green hydrogen production, storage, and distribution;

"(G) electric vehicle and charging infrastructure manufacturing and installation;

"(H) semiconductor and advanced manufacturing;

"(I) battery and critical minerals processing and recycling;

"(J) energy efficiency and building decarbonization;

"(K) sustainable aviation fuel and clean transportation fuels; and

"(L) sustainable building and infrastructure materials manufacturing.

"(c) Qualified First-Year Wages. — For purposes of this section, the term 'qualified first-year wages' means wages paid or incurred by the taxpayer during the first 12 months of employment to a qualified clean energy worker, not to exceed $50,000 per worker.

"(d) Credit Amount. — The credit allowed under subsection (a) with respect to any qualified clean energy worker shall not exceed $15,000 ($50,000 x 30 percent).

"(e) Limitations. —

"(1) MAXIMUM CREDIT. — The credit allowed under this section shall not exceed $1,000,000 per taxpayer per taxable year.

"(2) RETENTION REQUIREMENT. — No credit shall be allowed with respect to any worker who is not employed by the taxpayer on the last day of the taxable year. In the case of a worker who is not so employed, the credit otherwise allowable shall be allowed if the worker voluntarily separated or was terminated for cause.

"(3) COORDINATION WITH OTHER CREDITS. — No credit shall be allowed under this section for any worker with respect to whom a credit is allowed under section 45AA, section 51, or section 1396 for the same taxable year.

"(f) Regulations. — The Secretary shall issue such regulations as may be necessary to carry out the purposes of this section.

"(g) Sunset. — This section shall not apply to workers who begin employment after December 31, 2036."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendment made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 902. WORKER RELOCATION AND TRAINING DEDUCTION.

**(a) Deduction Allowance.** — Part VI of subchapter B of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

**"SEC. 199B. CLEAN ENERGY WORKER RELOCATION AND TRAINING EXPENSES.**

"(a) Allowance of Deduction. — In the case of an individual, there shall be allowed as a deduction an amount equal to the qualified clean energy relocation and training expenses paid or incurred by the taxpayer during the taxable year.

"(b) Qualified Expenses. — For purposes of this section:

"(1) RELOCATION EXPENSES. — The term 'qualified relocation expenses' means amounts paid or incurred for moving household goods and personal effects, and for travel (including lodging) for the taxpayer and members of the taxpayer's household, from the taxpayer's former residence to a new residence, provided the move is for the purpose of beginning employment in a clean energy occupation and the distance between the new place of employment and the former residence is not fewer than 50 miles greater than the distance between the former residence and the former place of employment.

"(2) TRAINING EXPENSES. — The term 'qualified training expenses' means amounts paid or incurred for tuition, fees, books, supplies, and equipment required for enrollment or attendance at an educational institution or training program in a clean energy occupation, provided the training is required for or directly related to the taxpayer's employment in a clean energy occupation.

"(c) Dollar Limitation. — The deduction allowed under this section shall not exceed $10,000 per taxable year.

"(d) Sunset. — This section shall not apply to expenses paid or incurred after December 31, 2036."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendment made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 903. CLEAN ENERGY APPRENTICESHIP WAGE SUBSIDY.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Labor shall establish a Clean Energy Apprenticeship Wage Subsidy Program providing direct payments to employers to offset a portion of apprentice wages during the first year of a registered apprenticeship program in a clean energy occupation.

**(b) Subsidy Amount.** — The wage subsidy shall equal:

(1) 50 percent of wages paid to the apprentice during the first six months of the apprenticeship, up to a maximum subsidy of $12,500 per apprentice; and

(2) 25 percent of wages paid to the apprentice during months 7 through 12 of the apprenticeship, up to a maximum additional subsidy of $6,250.

**(c) Employer Eligibility.** — To be eligible for the wage subsidy, an employer shall:

(1) employ the apprentice under a registered apprenticeship program in a clean energy occupation;

(2) pay the apprentice not less than the prevailing wage for the occupation in the local area;

(3) provide the apprentice with health insurance benefits meeting the minimum value standard under the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act; and

(4) certify that the apprentice is not replacing a regular employee who was laid off or terminated within the 90 days preceding the apprentice's start date.

**(d) Priority.** — In the event that demand for wage subsidies exceeds available funding, the Secretary shall give priority to:

(1) small business concerns with fewer than 500 employees;

(2) employers located in energy transition communities; and

(3) employers hiring apprentices from populations underrepresented in the relevant clean energy occupation.

**(e) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the wage subsidy program under this section.

### SECTION 904. SMALL BUSINESS GREEN TRANSITION CREDIT.

**(a) Credit Established.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding after section 45BB (as added by section 901) the following new section:

**"SEC. 45CC. SMALL BUSINESS CLEAN ENERGY TRANSITION CREDIT.**

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the small business clean energy transition credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to 40 percent of the qualified transition expenses paid or incurred by an eligible small business during the taxable year.

"(b) Eligible Small Business. — For purposes of this section, the term 'eligible small business' means any employer that:

"(1) employed not more than 100 full-time equivalent employees in the preceding taxable year;

"(2) is transitioning its primary business activity from a non-clean-energy activity to a clean energy activity, as determined by the Secretary of Labor; and

"(3) has not previously claimed the credit under this section.

"(c) Qualified Transition Expenses. — For purposes of this section, the term 'qualified transition expenses' means amounts paid or incurred for:

"(1) employee retraining and certification in clean energy occupations;

"(2) clean energy equipment and tools for retrained employees;

"(3) clean energy certification and licensing fees;

"(4) business development, marketing, and client transition costs related to the change in business activity; and

"(5) consulting, legal, and accounting fees directly related to the business transition.

"(d) Dollar Limitation. — The credit allowed under this section shall not exceed $100,000 per taxpayer.

"(e) Sunset. — This section shall not apply to expenses paid or incurred after December 31, 2036."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendment made by this section shall apply to taxable years beginning after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 905. PREVAILING WAGE AND BENEFITS REQUIREMENTS.

**(a) Requirement.** — No credit shall be allowed under sections 45AA, 45BB, or 45CC with respect to any worker, and no wage subsidy payment shall be made under section 903, unless:

(1) the worker is paid not less than the prevailing wage for the occupation in the local area, as determined by the Secretary of Labor in accordance with the Davis-Bacon Act (40 U.S.C. 276a et seq.); and

(2) the employer provides, or contributes toward the cost of, health insurance benefits for the worker that meet the minimum value standard under section 36B(c)(2)(C)(ii) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

**(b) Enforcement.** — The Secretary of Labor shall have authority to audit employer compliance with subsection (a), assess penalties for noncompliance, and recover credits and subsidies improperly claimed. Penalties shall equal 200 percent of credits or subsidies claimed in violation, plus interest.
