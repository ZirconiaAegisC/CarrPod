# DOCUMENT 01: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND GREEN JOBS ACT (WDGJA) OF 2026

**Prepared for the Committee on Education and the Workforce, Committee on Energy and Commerce, Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, and Committee on Veterans' Affairs**

---

### I. OVERVIEW

The Workforce Development and Green Jobs Act of 2026 (WDGJA) establishes the most comprehensive national workforce training and deployment infrastructure in United States history, specifically designed to prepare the American labor force for the industries of the clean energy, advanced manufacturing, and critical infrastructure economy. The Act authorizes $50 billion in federal investment over a ten-year period, projects the training and certification of more than 2,000,000 American workers, and targets the creation of not fewer than 500,000 new registered apprenticeships across the clean energy economy.

### II. PROBLEM STATEMENT

The United States faces an acute and accelerating shortage of skilled workers in the industries that will define the 21st-century economy. The Department of Energy's 2024 United States Energy and Employment Report documented that clean energy employers across every technology category reported difficulty hiring qualified workers, with shortages most severe in solar installation (89 percent of firms), wind turbine service (83 percent), battery manufacturing (78 percent), and grid modernization (76 percent). The CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 committed $52 billion to domestic semiconductor manufacturing, yet the Semiconductor Industry Association estimates a shortfall of 67,000 technicians and engineers by 2030. The Advanced Nuclear Energy Deployment Act and Carbon Removal and Industrial Decarbonization Act create demand for nuclear engineers, DAC operators, and green cement workers at a scale that current training pipelines cannot meet.

Simultaneously, approximately 1.7 million American workers are employed in fossil fuel industries facing decline, and more than 200,000 service members separate from active duty each year. Both populations possess transferable skills that, with modest additional training, can fill clean energy workforce gaps. The Workforce Development and Green Jobs Act bridges these supply and demand fundamentals through a coordinated national system of training, credentialing, placement, and income support that leaves no worker or community behind.

### IV. KEY PROVISIONS

**Title I — Apprenticeship Expansion.** Establishes a National Clean Energy Apprenticeship Corps targeting 500,000 new registered apprenticeships over ten years. Creates a tax credit of $5,000 per apprentice per year for employers hiring registered apprentices in clean energy occupations. Authorizes $3 billion in pre-apprenticeship pipeline grants through the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) system to prepare underrepresented populations for apprenticeship entry.

**Title II — Community College Manufacturing Centers.** Authorizes $6 billion to establish not fewer than 200 Clean Manufacturing Centers of Excellence at community and technical colleges across all 50 states. Provides equipment modernization grants of up to $5 million per campus, industry-driven curriculum development partnerships, and faculty training exchange programs with clean energy manufacturers.

**Title III — Veteran Transition Program.** Creates the Clean Energy Veterans Corps to recruit, train, and place 100,000 veterans in clean energy occupations over ten years. Expands GI Bill benefits to cover clean technology certification programs at community colleges and registered apprenticeship programs. Establishes DOD SkillBridge partnerships with clean energy employers and authorizes veteran-owned clean energy business startup grants of up to $75,000.

**Title IV — Fossil Fuel Worker Bridge Program.** Provides Energy Transition Adjustment Assistance, including up to three years of wage insurance covering 80 percent of the gap between previous and new wages, up to $15,000 annually. Establishes a $5 billion Coal and Oil Community Reinvestment Fund for economic diversification projects. Creates Regional Transition Coordination Offices in every state with energy transition communities.

**Title V — K-12 STEM Pipeline.** Authorizes $5 billion for clean energy Career Technical Education (CTE) grants to school districts, STEM teacher training in clean technologies, high school pre-apprenticeship programs with articulation agreements to registered apprenticeships, and a Summer Youth Clean Energy Corps engaging 50,000 students annually.

**Title VI — Nuclear Workforce Pipeline.** Funds 5,000 four-year nuclear science and engineering scholarships, university research reactor upgrades at 25 campuses, a dedicated fusion workforce development initiative, and accelerated nuclear technician certification programs. Authorizes HALEU fuel cycle training partnerships with national laboratories.

**Title VII — Semiconductor Workforce.** Coordinates with CHIPS Act workforce provisions to create semiconductor technician fast-track training programs at 50 community colleges, advanced packaging and assembly workforce development initiatives, university microelectronics research hubs, and standardized semiconductor equipment maintenance certification.

**Title VIII — Clean Energy Corps.** Establishes a national service program modeled on the Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC), deploying 30,000 Corps members annually to build clean energy, energy efficiency, and resilience projects in underserved communities. Corps members receive educational benefits equivalent to the AmeriCorps Segal Education Award, plus stackable industry-recognized credentials.

**Title IX — Green Jobs Tax Credit.** Provides a hiring credit of 30 percent of the first $50,000 of wages for each new clean energy worker (maximum $15,000 per worker), a worker relocation and training expense deduction, and apprenticeship wage subsidies of 50 percent of apprentice wages for the first year.

**Title X — Tribal Workforce Development.** Authorizes $3 billion for Tribal Clean Energy Training Centers at Tribal Colleges and Universities, Indian energy preference hiring incentives, and Tribal energy sovereignty workforce grants for renewable and microgrid projects on Tribal lands.

### VI. IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE

| Phase | Years | Key Milestones |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| Phase I — Program Establishment | FY 2027-2028 | Rulemaking, guidance, initial grant solicitations, first Centers of Excellence designated |
| Phase II — Early Deployment | FY 2028-2030 | 50 Centers operating, 50,000 new apprentices, first Clean Energy Corps cohort deployed |
| Phase III — Scale-Up | FY 2030-2033 | 150 Centers operating, 250,000 new apprentices, 15,000 Corps members annually |
| Phase IV — Full Capacity | FY 2033-2036 | 200+ Centers, 500,000 apprentices, 30,000 Corps members, 2 million total trained |

### VII. FUNDING MECHANISM

The $50 billion authorization shall be funded through a combination of general revenues, recapture of certain fossil fuel subsidies identified by GAO, and a 0.25 percent surcharge on federal oil and gas royalty rates. Green Jobs Tax Credits are estimated to reduce federal revenues by $3 billion over ten years, which shall be treated as an offset within the $50 billion authorization.
