# DOCUMENT 05: TITLE III — VETERAN TRANSITION PROGRAM

## TITLE III — VETERAN TRANSITION PROGRAM

### SECTION 301. CLEAN ENERGY VETERANS CORPS.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Veterans Affairs and the Secretary of Defense, shall establish a Clean Energy Veterans Corps (CEVC) to recruit, train, and place not fewer than 100,000 qualified veterans in clean energy occupations over the ten-year period beginning on the date of enactment of this Act.

**(b) Functions.** — The CEVC shall:

(1) operate a dedicated veterans' recruitment portal listing clean energy apprenticeship, training, and employment opportunities with military occupational specialty (MOS) crosswalk mappings to clean energy occupations;

(2) provide one-on-one transition counseling connecting separating service members and veterans to clean energy career pathways matched to their military training and experience;

(3) fund not fewer than 25 regionally distributed Veterans Clean Energy Training Centers co-located with military installations or in communities with high veteran populations;

(4) provide relocation assistance of up to $10,000 per veteran for moves to accept clean energy employment in areas with demonstrated labor shortages; and

(5) track longitudinal employment outcomes for all CEVC participants for not fewer than five years following program completion.

**(c) Annual Targets.** — The CEVC shall achieve the following minimum annual placement targets:

| 2027 | 2,500 |
| 2028 | 5,000 |
| 2029 | 7,500 |
| 2030 | 10,000 |

| 2032 | 12,000 |
| 2033 | 13,000 |
| 2034 | 13,000 |

### SECTION 302. GI BILL EXPANSION FOR CLEAN TECHNOLOGY CERTIFICATION.

**(a) Covered Programs.** — Section 3314 of title 38, United States Code, is amended to include, as programs of education eligible for educational assistance under chapter 33:

(1) certificate and industry-recognized credential programs in clean energy occupations offered by community and technical colleges, including programs of fewer than 15 credit hours or less than one semester in duration, provided such programs have been approved by the State Approving Agency;

(2) registered apprenticeship programs in clean energy occupations, with monthly housing allowance equivalent to the rate payable under section 3313(c)(1)(B); and

(3) on-the-job training and pre-apprenticeship programs in clean energy occupations that are registered with the Department of Labor and approved by the State Approving Agency.

**(b) Licensing and Certification Reimbursement.** — Section 3315A of title 38, United States Code, is amended to increase the maximum reimbursement for licensing and certification tests in clean energy occupations to $4,000 per test, and to authorize reimbursement for up to four tests per eligible individual per calendar year.

**(c) Effective Date.** — The amendments made by this section shall take effect on the first day of the first academic year beginning after the date of enactment of this Act.

### SECTION 303. DOD SKILLBRIDGE CLEAN ENERGY PARTNERSHIPS.

**(a) Clean Energy SkillBridge Initiative.** — The Secretary of Defense, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish a Clean Energy SkillBridge Initiative to expand the number of Department of Defense-approved SkillBridge opportunities with clean energy employers to not fewer than 500 partner employers by December 31, 2028, and not fewer than 1,500 partner employers by December 31, 2031.

**(b) Partner Employer Incentives.** — Eligible clean energy employers participating in the SkillBridge program may receive:

(1) technical assistance from the Department of Labor in developing SkillBridge training plans that satisfy program requirements;

(2) expedited approval processing, with a target of 30 days from application to approval for employers offering training in occupations identified on the Clean Energy Occupations Priority List published under subsection (c); and

(3) reimbursement of up to 50 percent of the cost of required safety equipment, tools, and training materials provided to SkillBridge participants, not to exceed $2,500 per participant.

**(c) Clean Energy Occupations Priority List.** — Not later than 90 days after enactment, the Secretary of Labor shall publish and biennially update a Clean Energy Occupations Priority List identifying the occupations and geographic regions with the most acute clean energy workforce shortages, which shall serve as the prioritization framework for the Clean Energy SkillBridge Initiative.

### SECTION 304. VETERAN-OWNED CLEAN ENERGY BUSINESS GRANTS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Administrator of the Small Business Administration, shall establish a grant program to support qualified veterans in establishing and expanding clean energy and energy resilience businesses.

**(b) Grant Types.** —

(1) STARTUP GRANTS. — Grants of up to $75,000 for veteran-owned small businesses in the startup phase (fewer than two years in operation) engaged in clean energy installation, service, manufacturing, or consulting.

(2) EXPANSION GRANTS. — Grants of up to $150,000 for veteran-owned small businesses with more than two years of operating history seeking to expand into clean energy markets or add clean energy product or service lines.

(3) FEDERAL CONTRACT READINESS GRANTS. — Grants of up to $50,000 for veteran-owned clean energy businesses seeking certification and capability development to compete for federal contracts under the Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business (SDVOSB) and Veteran-Owned Small Business (VOSB) programs.

**(c) Technical Assistance.** — Grantees shall be eligible for technical assistance through Small Business Development Centers, Procurement Technical Assistance Centers, and the DOE Office of Small and Disadvantaged Business Utilization.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 305. MILITARY BASE TRANSITION PILOT PROGRAM.

**(a) Purpose.** — The Secretary of Defense, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy and the Secretary of Labor, shall establish a pilot program at not fewer than five military installations scheduled for closure, realignment, or significant force reduction to demonstrate the feasibility of repurposing installation facilities and infrastructure for clean energy workforce training and manufacturing.

**(b) Eligible Activities.** — Pilot program funds may be used for:

(1) conversion of installation buildings and infrastructure into clean energy training facilities, manufacturing incubators, and technology demonstration sites;

(2) partnership agreements with community colleges, universities, and clean energy employers to operate training programs on installation property;

(3) installation of clean energy generation, storage, and microgrid systems to serve as training platforms and provide energy resilience to retained installation functions; and

(4) environmental remediation necessary to enable civilian reuse of installation property for clean energy purposes.

**(c) Selection Criteria.** — Installations shall be selected for the pilot program based on:

(1) proximity to energy transition communities with significant veteran populations;

(2) existing infrastructure suitable for conversion to training and manufacturing use;

(3) demonstrated interest from clean energy employers, educational institutions, and state and local governments; and

(4) potential to serve as replicable models for additional installations.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $300,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for the pilot program under this section.
