# 119TH CONGRESS — WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND GREEN JOBS ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 09: TITLE VII — SEMICONDUCTOR WORKFORCE

---

## TITLE VII — SEMICONDUCTOR WORKFORCE

### SECTION 701. CHIPS ACT WORKFORCE COORDINATION.

**(a) Findings and Purpose.** — Congress finds that the CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 (Public Law 117-167) included workforce development provisions but did not establish dedicated funding or institutional infrastructure at the scale required to meet the projected shortfall of 67,000 semiconductor technicians and engineers by 2030. The purpose of this section is to complement and amplify CHIPS Act workforce investments with a dedicated semiconductor technician training pipeline.

**(b) Coordination Mandate.** — The Secretary of Commerce, acting through the CHIPS Program Office, shall coordinate with the Secretary of Labor to ensure that:

(1) semiconductor workforce training programs funded under this Title are aligned with the workforce needs of CHIPS Act incentive recipients and the broader domestic semiconductor industry;

(2) workforce development plans required of CHIPS Act applicants include preferences for graduates of Clean Manufacturing Centers of Excellence designated under section 201; and

(3) the Clean Energy Apprenticeship Standards Board established under section 105 develops semiconductor fabrication technician apprenticeship standards within 180 days of enactment.

**(c) Workforce Data Collection.** — The CHIPS Program Office shall require all CHIPS Act funding recipients to report annually on:

(1) total employment by occupational category, disaggregated by construction and ongoing operations;

(2) number of registered apprentices and pre-apprentices employed;

(3) partnerships with community and technical colleges, including number of students enrolled in partnership programs and hired upon completion; and

(4) veteran hiring metrics and participation in DOD SkillBridge.

### SECTION 702. SEMICONDUCTOR TECHNICIAN FAST-TRACK TRAINING.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Commerce, shall establish a Semiconductor Technician Fast-Track Training Program at not fewer than 50 community and technical colleges in proximity to semiconductor fabrication facilities under construction or in operation.

**(b) Program Design.** —

(1) FAST-TRACK CERTIFICATES. — Programs shall offer stackable certificates in semiconductor fabrication technology that can be completed in 12 to 24 weeks of full-time study, including:

- Semiconductor cleanroom protocols and contamination control;
- Wafer handling and fabrication equipment operation;
- Photolithography and etch process fundamentals;
- Chemical mechanical planarization;
- Metrology and inspection techniques;
- Vacuum systems and gas handling;
- Semiconductor equipment maintenance and troubleshooting; and
- Workplace safety and hazardous materials management.

(2) EMPLOYER COMMITMENT. — Each fast-track program shall have a partnership agreement with at least one semiconductor employer committing to interview all program completers and to hire a specified minimum percentage of program graduates.

(3) SIMULATED TRAINING ENVIRONMENTS. — Programs shall be equipped with semiconductor equipment simulators, virtual reality training modules, and scaled-down process equipment suitable for educational use.

**(c) Tuition Coverage.** — Tuition and fees for fast-track programs shall be covered at 100 percent for enrolled students, with funds provided through a combination of federal grants, state contributions, and employer co-investment.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 703. ADVANCED PACKAGING AND ASSEMBLY WORKFORCE.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Commerce, in coordination with the Secretary of Labor, shall establish an Advanced Packaging and Assembly Workforce Development Program to train technicians in the specialized skills required for heterogeneous integration, chiplets, 2.5D and 3D packaging, and advanced assembly technologies.

**(b) Training Centers.** — Not fewer than 10 regionally distributed Advanced Packaging Training Centers shall be established at community and technical colleges co-located with advanced packaging facilities or in regions identified by the CHIPS Program Office as National Advanced Packaging Manufacturing Program priority areas.

**(c) Curriculum.** — Training programs shall cover:

(1) wafer bumping and redistribution layer processing;

(2) through-silicon via formation and interconnection;

(3) die attach, wire bonding, and flip-chip assembly;

(4) underfill and encapsulation materials and processes;

(5) advanced packaging metrology and defect inspection;

(6) thermal management for high-density packages; and

(7) reliability testing and failure analysis.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 704. UNIVERSITY MICROELECTRONICS RESEARCH HUBS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The National Science Foundation, in coordination with the Secretary of Commerce and the Secretary of Defense, shall establish not fewer than 10 University Microelectronics Research Hubs to integrate semiconductor workforce development with cutting-edge research in microelectronics design, fabrication, and packaging.

**(b) Hub Functions.** — Each Hub shall:

(1) conduct collaborative research involving faculty, graduate students, and undergraduate students in partnership with semiconductor companies and national laboratories;

(2) offer semiconductor design and fabrication "tape-out" courses enabling students to design, fabricate, and test integrated circuits through multi-project wafer services;

(3) provide undergraduate research experiences and summer internship programs with semiconductor industry partners;

(4) maintain relationships with community college semiconductor programs in the region to create articulation pathways and shared use of specialized equipment; and

(5) serve as nodes in the National Semiconductor Technology Center network established under the CHIPS and Science Act.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for research hubs under this section.

### SECTION 705. SEMICONDUCTOR EQUIPMENT MAINTENANCE CERTIFICATION.

**(a) Purpose.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with SEMI (the semiconductor equipment and materials industry association) and semiconductor equipment manufacturers, shall develop a standardized Semiconductor Equipment Maintenance Certification (SEMC) recognized across all domestic semiconductor fabrication facilities.

**(b) Certification Levels.** —

(1) SEMC LEVEL I — ENTRY. — Covering fundamental cleanroom practices, basic tool operation, preventive maintenance procedures, and safety protocols. Requiring approximately 200 hours of training.

(2) SEMC LEVEL II — SPECIALIST. — Covering equipment-specific troubleshooting, calibration, repair procedures, and process qualification for specific tool categories (lithography, etch, deposition, CMP, metrology). Requiring approximately 400 hours of additional training and 1,000 hours of on-the-job experience.

(3) SEMC LEVEL III — MASTER. — Covering advanced diagnostics, predictive maintenance, equipment modification, and training of Level I and II technicians. Requiring Level II certification, 2,000 hours of on-the-job experience, and completion of a capstone project.

**(c) Portability.** — The SEMC shall be recognized by all federal agencies for purposes of federal contractor qualification, and the Secretary shall encourage all CHIPS Act funding recipients to recognize the SEMC as either a requirement or a preference for equipment maintenance hiring.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $100,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2029 for development and initial implementation of the SEMC program.
