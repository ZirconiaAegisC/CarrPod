# 119TH CONGRESS — WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND GREEN JOBS ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 10: TITLE VIII — CLEAN ENERGY CORPS

---

## TITLE VIII — CLEAN ENERGY CORPS

### SECTION 801. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE CLEAN ENERGY CORPS.

**(a) Establishment.** — There is established within the Corporation for National and Community Service (AmeriCorps) a Clean Energy Corps, modeled on the Civilian Conservation Corps of 1933 through 1942, to deploy Corps members in service projects that advance clean energy deployment, energy efficiency, environmental conservation, and community resilience.

**(b) Mission.** — The mission of the Clean Energy Corps is to:

(1) accelerate the deployment of clean energy, energy efficiency, and climate resilience projects in underserved communities;

(2) provide Corps members with industry-recognized training, credentials, and work experience that prepare them for careers in the clean energy economy;

(3) engage young adults from diverse backgrounds in paid national service that builds civic engagement, leadership skills, and environmental stewardship; and

(4) strengthen community resilience to climate change impacts while reducing energy costs for low-income households.

**(c) Corps Capacity.** — The Clean Energy Corps shall have the capacity to deploy:

(1) 10,000 Corps members annually, beginning in fiscal year 2028;

(2) 20,000 Corps members annually, beginning in fiscal year 2030; and

(3) 30,000 Corps members annually, beginning in fiscal year 2032.

### SECTION 802. CORPS MEMBER RECRUITMENT AND TRAINING.

**(a) Eligibility.** — An individual is eligible to serve as a Corps member if the individual:

(1) is not less than 18 years of age and not more than 35 years of age at the time of enrollment, except that veterans of any age shall be eligible;

(2) is a United States citizen, a national, or a lawful permanent resident alien;

(3) has a high school diploma or recognized equivalent; and

(4) passes a background check administered by the Corporation for National and Community Service.

**(b) Recruitment Priorities.** — In recruiting Corps members, the Corporation shall prioritize:

(1) residents of energy transition communities;

(2) residents of persistent poverty counties and Qualified Opportunity Zones;

(3) individuals from populations underrepresented in the clean energy workforce, including women, people of color, and individuals with disabilities;

(4) justice-involved individuals and individuals in recovery from substance use disorders; and

(5) veterans and military family members.

**(c) Training.** — Each Corps member shall receive:

(1) a minimum of 120 hours of classroom and hands-on technical training during the service term, aligned with industry-recognized credentials in fields including solar installation, energy efficiency, weatherization, and environmental restoration;

(2) safety training meeting OSHA 10 or OSHA 30 standards;

(3) leadership development, financial literacy, and career readiness training;

(4) first aid and CPR certification; and

(5) not fewer than 40 hours of environmental and climate science education.

**(d) Corps Member Compensation.** —

(1) LIVING ALLOWANCE. — Corps members shall receive a living allowance of not less than $18,000 per year of full-time service, with upward adjustments of not less than 15 percent in areas with a cost of living exceeding the national average by 10 percent or more.

(2) HOUSING. — The Corporation shall provide Corps members with housing or a supplemental housing allowance when service projects are not within commuting distance of the member's residence.

(3) HEALTH CARE. — Corps members shall be enrolled in a health benefits plan meeting minimum essential coverage standards under the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act.

(4) CHILDCARE. — Corps members with dependent children shall receive a childcare subsidy covering not less than 80 percent of the cost of licensed childcare.

### SECTION 803. PROJECT PORTFOLIO AND DEPLOYMENT PRIORITIES.

**(a) Project Categories.** — Clean Energy Corps projects shall include:

(1) installation of solar photovoltaic and solar thermal systems on public buildings, schools, community centers, and low-income housing;

(2) weatherization and energy efficiency retrofits for low-income households, including air sealing, insulation, and HVAC upgrades;

(3) energy auditing and building performance assessment for municipal, school district, and nonprofit facilities;

(4) tree planting and urban forestry for heat island mitigation, stormwater management, and carbon sequestration;

(5) wildfire risk reduction through fuel management, defensible space creation, and forest thinning;

(6) coastal and inland wetland restoration, living shoreline construction, and flood mitigation;

(7) trail construction and maintenance, park infrastructure improvement, and public land restoration;

(8) community resilience hub construction, including backup power, emergency shelter upgrades, and communications infrastructure;

(9) brownfield assessment and remediation preparation for clean energy redevelopment; and

(10) electric vehicle charging infrastructure installation at public facilities and multi-family housing.

**(b) Project Selection Criteria.** — Projects shall be selected based on:

(1) measurable benefits to underserved communities, including energy cost savings, job creation, and environmental quality improvement;

(2) alignment with state, local, and Tribal climate and energy plans;

(3) partnership commitments from local governments, nonprofit organizations, utilities, or community-based organizations;

(4) training and credentialing opportunities for Corps members; and

(5) potential for long-term community impact beyond the service term.

**(c) Geographic Distribution.** — Not fewer than 40 percent of Corps members and project funding shall be deployed in energy transition communities, persistent poverty counties, and Tribal lands.

### SECTION 804. CORPS MEMBER EDUCATIONAL BENEFITS.

**(a) Education Award.** — Upon successful completion of a term of service of not fewer than 1,700 hours, a Corps member shall receive an education award equal to the maximum Pell Grant award for the award year in which the term of service is completed, to be used for:

(1) tuition and fees at an institution of higher education;

(2) repayment of qualified student loans; or

(3) enrollment in an approved apprenticeship program or industry-recognized credential program.

**(b) Credential Attainment.** — In addition to the education award, the Corporation shall pay the cost of one industry-recognized certification examination per term of service, including NABCEP solar installer certification, BPI building analyst certification, or equivalent certifications approved by the Secretary of Energy.

**(c) Non-Competitive Eligibility.** — Corps members who complete a term of service shall be granted non-competitive eligibility for federal civil service employment for a period of one year following completion of service, consistent with the provisions applicable to Peace Corps and AmeriCorps VISTA alumni under 5 CFR 315.605.

### SECTION 805. STATE AND LOCAL PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENTS.

**(a) Partnership Program.** — The Corporation shall establish a Clean Energy Corps State Partnership Program under which states may apply for authority to administer Clean Energy Corps projects within their jurisdiction.

**(b) State Plans.** — A state applying for partnership authority shall submit a State Clean Energy Corps Plan that:

(1) identifies the state agency responsible for program administration;

(2) describes the process for project solicitation, selection, and oversight;

(3) provides for matching funds of not less than 25 percent of federal project costs, which may include in-kind contributions;

(4) establishes coordination with state workforce development boards, community colleges, and clean energy employers; and

(5) commits to performance reporting and participant outcome tracking.

**(c) Direct Federal Partnerships.** — The Corporation may also enter into direct partnership agreements with Tribal governments, local governments, and nonprofit organizations for Clean Energy Corps project implementation.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this Title.
