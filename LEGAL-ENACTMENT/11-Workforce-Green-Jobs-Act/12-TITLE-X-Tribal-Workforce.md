# 119TH CONGRESS — WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND GREEN JOBS ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 12: TITLE X — TRIBAL WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT

---

## TITLE X — TRIBAL WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT

### SECTION 1001. TRIBAL CLEAN ENERGY TRAINING CENTERS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy, through the Office of Indian Energy Policy and Programs, shall establish a Tribal Clean Energy Training Center Program to fund the construction, renovation, and operation of clean energy training facilities at Tribal Colleges and Universities (TCUs) and on Tribal lands.

**(b) Center Functions.** — Each Tribal Clean Energy Training Center shall:

(1) offer certificate and degree programs in clean energy occupations relevant to Tribal energy needs, including solar photovoltaic installation, wind turbine maintenance, microgrid design and operations, energy efficiency and weatherization, and biomass and bioenergy systems;

(2) provide hands-on training using real-world clean energy installations, including renewable energy systems serving Tribal buildings and homes;

(3) offer entrepreneurship and small business development training for Tribal members seeking to establish clean energy businesses;

(4) incorporate Traditional Ecological Knowledge (TEK) and culturally appropriate instructional approaches in curriculum design and delivery; and

(5) provide student support services including academic advising, financial aid navigation, childcare, and transportation assistance.

**(c) Center Targets.** — Not fewer than 15 Tribal Clean Energy Training Centers shall be operational by December 31, 2030, with priority for:

(1) TCUs and Tribal communities located on lands with high renewable energy technical potential as assessed by the National Renewable Energy Laboratory;

(2) Tribal communities in which not fewer than 10 percent of households lack access to electricity or experience energy burden exceeding 6 percent of household income; and

(3) Tribal communities located in energy transition communities, including areas affected by coal mine and coal power plant closures.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 1002. TRIBAL COLLEGE AND UNIVERSITY PARTNERSHIPS.

**(a) Partnership Program.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Secretary of Education and the National Science Foundation, shall establish a TCU Clean Energy Partnership Program providing grants for partnerships between TCUs and:

(1) research universities with established clean energy programs;

(2) Department of Energy national laboratories;

(3) clean energy companies and utilities; and

(4) other TCUs to form consortia for shared curriculum, equipment, and faculty resources.

**(b) Partnership Activities.** — Grants may be used for:

(1) collaborative curriculum development and faculty exchange programs;

(2) shared use of laboratory, workshop, and field training equipment;

(3) joint research projects addressing Tribal energy challenges, including remote community electrification, microgrid resilience, and energy-water-food nexus issues;

(4) student internship and research experience programs at partner institutions, national laboratories, and clean energy companies; and

(5) distance learning and virtual laboratory infrastructure enabling TCU students to participate in courses and research at partner institutions.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 1003. NATIVE AMERICAN CLEAN ENERGY EMPLOYMENT PREFERENCE.

**(a) Indian Preference in Clean Energy Projects.** — Section 703(i) of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 (42 U.S.C. 2000e-2(i)) is amended to add, after the existing provision concerning Indian preference on or near an Indian reservation, the following additional preference:

"Any clean energy project that receives federal funding, tax credits, or federal land use authorizations, and that is located on or adjacent to Indian lands, as defined in 25 U.S.C. 3501, shall, to the greatest extent feasible, give preference in employment and contracting to Indians (as defined in 25 U.S.C. 5304(d)) who are members of the Tribe on whose land the project is located."

**(b) Tribal Employment Plans.** — Any applicant for federal clean energy funding, tax credits, permits, or authorizations for a project on or adjacent to Indian lands shall submit, as part of the application, a Tribal Employment Plan that:

(1) describes the specific steps the applicant will take to recruit, train, and employ Tribal members;

(2) sets numerical targets for Tribal employment, disaggregated by occupational category;

(3) describes partnerships with TCUs, Tribal workforce development programs, and Tribal employment rights offices; and

(4) provides for annual reporting on Tribal employment outcomes.

**(c) Tribal Hire Tax Credit.** — An employer operating a clean energy project on or adjacent to Indian lands shall be eligible for a credit against tax equal to $7,500 for each qualified Tribal member employed in a full-time position on the project during the taxable year, in addition to any credit allowed under section 901.

### SECTION 1004. TRIBAL ENERGY SOVEREIGNTY WORKFORCE GRANTS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy, through the Office of Indian Energy Policy and Programs, shall establish a Tribal Energy Sovereignty Workforce Grant Program providing funding directly to Tribal governments for workforce development activities related to Tribal energy projects.

**(b) Eligible Activities.** — Grants may be used for:

(1) tribal clean energy workforce needs assessments and planning;

(2) scholarships and training stipends for Tribal members enrolled in clean energy certificate, apprenticeship, and degree programs;

(3) development and delivery of Tribal-specific clean energy training curricula;

(4) purchase and installation of clean energy training equipment;

(5) Tribal energy workforce coordinators and career counselors;

(6) stipends for Tribal elders and knowledge holders contributing Traditional Ecological Knowledge to clean energy training programs; and

(7) Tribal clean energy job fairs, career expos, and employer engagement activities.

**(c) Application Simplification.** — The Secretary shall develop a streamlined application process for Tribal governments, including:

(1) a single common application form for all Tribal clean energy workforce programs administered by the Department of Energy;

(2) acceptance of Tribal government resolutions or ordinances in lieu of certain federal grant documentation requirements where consistent with law;

(3) technical assistance from the Office of Indian Energy in application preparation; and

(4) a target of not more than 90 days from application submission to grant award decision.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $800,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for grants under this section.

### SECTION 1005. CULTURAL PRESERVATION AND GREEN JOB INTEGRATION.

**(a) Findings.** — Congress finds that:

(1) Tribal communities possess deep Traditional Ecological Knowledge relevant to sustainable energy systems, land stewardship, and climate adaptation;

(2) clean energy workforce programs on Tribal lands are most successful when they integrate cultural values, Tribal sovereignty, and community-defined priorities; and

(3) workforce programs should support, not undermine, the retention of Tribal languages, cultural practices, and intergenerational knowledge transfer.

**(b) Cultural Integration Grants.** — The Secretary of Energy shall award grants to TCUs and Tribal governments for:

(1) integration of Traditional Ecological Knowledge into clean energy curriculum and training materials;

(2) development of bilingual and Tribal language clean energy training materials;

(3) Tribal language immersion programs that incorporate clean energy and STEM content;

(4) cultural mentorship programs pairing Tribal clean energy students with Tribal elders and knowledge holders; and

(5) documentation and preservation of Traditional Ecological Knowledge relevant to sustainable energy and environmental stewardship.

**(c) Cultural Review.** — All Tribally focused workforce programs under this Act shall be subject to review by the Tribal Advisory Committee established by the Department of Energy under 42 U.S.C. 7144e, and program designs, materials, and practices shall be modified in response to Committee recommendations.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.
