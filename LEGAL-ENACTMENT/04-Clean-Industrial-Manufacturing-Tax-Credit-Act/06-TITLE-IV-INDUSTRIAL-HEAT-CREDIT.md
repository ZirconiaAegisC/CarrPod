# TITLE IV — INDUSTRIAL HEAT DECARBONIZATION INVESTMENT TAX CREDIT

## SECTION 401. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Industrial Heat Decarbonization
Investment Tax Credit Act."

## SECTION 402. INDUSTRIAL HEAT DECARBONIZATION CREDIT ESTABLISHED

**(a) In General.** Section 48 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986
is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

"(i) Industrial Heat Decarbonization Property.

"(1) In General. For purposes of subsection (a), the energy percentage
for energy property described in paragraph (2) shall be:

"(A) 30 percent, in the case of property placed in service before
January 1, 2033, and

"(B) 20 percent, in the case of property placed in service after
December 31, 2032, and before January 1, 2037.

"(2) Qualified Industrial Heat Decarbonization Property. For purposes
of this subsection, the term 'qualified industrial heat decarbonization
property' means property:

"(A) that is an industrial facility or component thereof that provides
process heat for an industrial or manufacturing process;

"(B) that replaces or avoids the use of fossil fuel combustion as the
source of such process heat;

"(C) that is one or more of the following technologies:

"(i) Thermal energy storage systems, including but not limited to
carbon-block thermal batteries (as demonstrated by Antora Energy),
molten-salt thermal storage, or solid-media thermal storage;

"(ii) Industrial electric boilers, including electrode boilers and
resistive heating systems;

"(iii) Industrial heat pumps capable of delivering process heat
temperatures of not less than 150 degrees Celsius;

"(iv) Linear generators capable of operating on hydrogen, ammonia,
biogas, or blends thereof (as demonstrated by Mainspring Energy);

"(v) Electric arc or induction furnaces replacing fossil-fuel-fired
furnaces;

"(vi) Mechanical vapor recompression systems;

"(vii) Any other innovative industrial heating technology that the
Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Energy, determines
achieves a lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions reduction of not less
than 60 percent compared to the displaced fossil fuel heating system;
and

"(D) that is placed in service at a qualified industrial facility
located in the United States.

"(3) Prevailing Wage and Apprenticeship Requirements. The requirements
of subsection (a)(9) (relating to prevailing wages) and subsection
(a)(10) (relating to apprenticeship) shall apply to any credit claimed
under this subsection at the 30 percent rate. In the case of a
taxpayer that does not satisfy such requirements, the energy percentage
shall be reduced to 6 percent (in lieu of 30 percent) or 4 percent
(in lieu of 20 percent).

"(4) Domestic Content Bonus. The energy percentage shall be increased
by 10 percentage points for property certified as meeting the domestic
content requirements applicable to the energy credit under section 48.

"(5) Energy Communities Bonus. The energy percentage shall be increased
by 10 percentage points for property placed in service in an energy
community, as defined in section 45(b)(11)(B).

"(6) Low-Income Community Bonus. The energy percentage shall be
increased by 10 percentage points for property placed in service in
a facility located in a low-income community or on Indian land.

"(7) Maximum Credit Rate. The aggregate energy percentage under this
subsection shall not exceed 60 percent.

"(8) Qualified Progress Expenditures. Rules similar to the rules of
subsection (b) of section 46 (as in effect on the day before the date
of enactment of the Revenue Reconciliation Act of 1990) shall apply
for purposes of this subsection.

"(9) Coordination with Other Credits. The credit under this subsection
shall be allowed in addition to any credit allowable under section 45V
or section 48C, provided that property that qualifies for a credit
under this subsection shall not be treated as energy property for
any other purpose of this section.

"(10) Termination. No credit shall be allowed under this subsection
for property placed in service after December 31, 2036."

**(b) Conforming Amendment.** Section 48(a)(3)(A) is amended by adding
at the end the following new clause:

"(viii) qualified industrial heat decarbonization property described
in subsection (i)(2)."

## SECTION 403. ELIGIBLE TECHNOLOGIES AND EQUIPMENT

**(a) Thermal Energy Storage Batteries.** Equipment that stores
electrical energy from renewable sources as thermal energy in
graphite or carbon blocks, refractory brick, molten salt, or other
solid or liquid media, and that discharges such thermal energy as
process heat at temperatures of not less than 800 degrees Celsius,
including the carbon-block thermal battery technology commercialized
by Antora Energy.

**(b) Pumped Thermal Energy Storage.** Equipment that stores
electrical energy as thermal energy in molten salt and chilled
antifreeze for later reconversion to electricity or process heat,
including systems developed by Malta (a subsidiary of Alphabet's
Moonshot Factory).

**(c) Industrial Electric Boilers.** Electrode or resistive boilers
that produce steam or hot water for industrial processes, replacing
natural gas or fuel oil boilers, capable of output temperatures of
not less than 200 degrees Celsius.

**(d) Industrial Heat Pumps.** Mechanical or absorption heat pump
systems that upgrade low-temperature waste heat or ambient heat to
process temperatures of not less than 150 degrees Celsius, including
high-temperature heat pumps supplied by OEMs such as Siemens Energy,
MAN Energy Solutions, and Mitsubishi Heavy Industries.

**(e) Fuel-Flexible Linear Generators.** Free-piston linear generators
that produce electricity and heat from hydrogen, ammonia, biogas,
renewable natural gas, or blends thereof, without spark plugs or
complex mechanical systems, including the linear generator technology
commercialized by Mainspring Energy.

**(f) Electric Arc Furnaces.** Direct-current or alternating-current
electric arc furnaces used for steelmaking, ferroalloy production,
or other high-temperature metallurgical processes, replacing
blast furnaces or other coke-based or gas-fired smelting operations.

## SECTION 404. QUALIFYING INDUSTRIAL FACILITY

**(a) Definition.** For purposes of this title, the term "qualified
industrial facility" means a facility:

(1) that is classified under NAICS codes 31–33 (Manufacturing) or
is a mining, quarrying, or oil and gas extraction facility classified
under NAICS code 21;

(2) that consumed not less than 50 billion British thermal units of
process heat energy in the most recent calendar year before the date
of application, or is a new facility with an anticipated annual
process heat consumption of not less than 50 billion Btu; and

(3) for which the installation of qualified industrial heat
decarbonization property replaces or avoids fossil fuel combustion
that would otherwise supply the process heat.

**(b) Small and Medium Enterprise Set-Aside.** Not less than 15 percent
of the total credit allocation under this title shall be reserved for
facilities with fewer than 500 employees.

## SECTION 405. VERIFICATION AND METERING

**(a) Metering Requirements.** Qualified facilities shall install and
maintain thermal energy metering, electrical submetering, and
continuous emissions monitoring systems sufficient to demonstrate
the displacement of fossil fuel combustion and the quantity of
process heat delivered by qualified property.

**(b) Third-Party Verification.** Claims for credits under this title
shall be accompanied by a certification from a qualified independent
engineer verifying that:

(1) The qualified property meets the specifications of section 402.

(2) The annual thermal energy delivered by the qualified property
is not less than 80 percent of the design nameplate capacity.

(3) Fossil fuel consumption at the facility has been reduced in
proportion to the thermal energy delivered by qualified property.

## SECTION 406. INTERACTION WITH SECTION 48C CREDITS

A taxpayer may claim a credit under section 48C for the manufacture
of components of qualified industrial heat decarbonization property
and a credit under this title for the installation and use of such
property in a qualified facility, provided that no component cost
is counted for both credits.

## SECTION 407. TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE FOR INDUSTRIAL FACILITIES

**(a) In General.** The Secretary of Energy, through the Industrial
Efficiency and Decarbonization Office, shall establish a technical
assistance program to assist industrial facility operators in
identifying, evaluating, and deploying industrial heat decarbonization
technologies.

**(b) Funding.** There is authorized to be appropriated $250 million
for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for the program under subsection (a).

---

*This title amends section 48 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.
The Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Energy, shall
issue implementing regulations not later than 180 days after
enactment, including specifications for thermal metering requirements
and third-party verification protocols.*
