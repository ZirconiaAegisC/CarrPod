# FINDINGS AND DECLARATIONS

## SECTION 2. CONGRESSIONAL FINDINGS AND DECLARATIONS OF POLICY

### SUBSECTION A. FINDINGS

The Congress makes the following findings:

**(1) Industrial Sector Emissions.** The industrial sector is responsible
for approximately 30 percent of total United States greenhouse gas
emissions, making it the single largest emitting sector when accounting
for both direct process emissions and energy-related combustion emissions.
Without targeted intervention, the Energy Information Administration
projects industrial emissions will remain flat or increase through 2050.

**(2) Cement Sector Impact.** Cement production accounts for approximately
8 percent of global anthropogenic carbon dioxide emissions. The United
States is the world's fourth-largest cement producer, with approximately
100 million metric tons of annual production across 96 facilities in
34 states. Conventional Portland cement manufacturing releases
approximately 0.9 metric tons of CO2 per metric ton of cement produced,
roughly 60 percent from process chemistry (calcination of limestone)
and 40 percent from fuel combustion.

**(3) Steel Sector Impact.** Iron and steel production accounts for
approximately 7 to 9 percent of global carbon dioxide emissions. The
United States produces approximately 80 million metric tons of crude
steel annually, with approximately 30 percent from integrated blast
furnace-basic oxygen furnace (BF-BOF) routes emitting approximately
2.0 metric tons of CO2 per metric ton of steel, and approximately
70 percent from electric arc furnace (EAF) routes emitting approximately
0.4 metric tons of CO2 per metric ton.

**(4) Success of Prior Manufacturing Tax Credits.** The advanced
manufacturing production credit established under section 45X of the
Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (as added by section 13501 of the
Inflation Reduction Act of 2022) has successfully accelerated domestic
manufacturing of solar photovoltaic components, wind turbine components,
battery cells and modules, and critical minerals processing. Since
enactment, domestic solar module manufacturing capacity has increased
by over 400 percent, and announced battery manufacturing investments
exceed $130 billion. This demonstrates that production tax credits
are an effective policy instrument for catalyzing private investment
in clean energy manufacturing supply chains.

**(5) Technology Maturity and Commercial Readiness.** Multiple
transformational clean industrial technologies have reached commercial
demonstration stage and are prepared for deployment at scale, including:

- (A) Molten oxide electrolysis for iron production without process
  carbon emissions, demonstrated by Boston Metal at pilot scale and
  progressing toward commercial deployment;
- (B) Electrochemical cement production pathways that avoid limestone
  calcination, demonstrated by Sublime Systems at pilot scale;
- (C) Carbon-negative ordinary Portland cement utilizing carbon-free
  calcium silicate rocks, demonstrated by Brimstone at pilot scale;
- (D) Advanced alkaline and proton exchange membrane electrolyzers
  for green hydrogen production, manufactured domestically by Electric
  Hydrogen, Verdagy, Hystar, and Sunfire;
- (E) Carbon dioxide injection and mineralization in concrete, deployed
  commercially by CarbonCure Technologies at over 700 plants globally;
- (F) Thermal energy storage batteries for industrial process heat,
  demonstrated by Antora Energy and Malta at pilot and commercial scale;
- (G) Fuel-flexible linear generators capable of operating on hydrogen
  and ammonia, demonstrated by Mainspring Energy;
- (H) Continuous carbon fiber composite manufacturing for lightweight
  structural components, demonstrated by Arris Composites;
- (I) Metal additive manufacturing for complex geometries with reduced
  material waste, demonstrated by Velo3D and Relativity Space.

**(6) Carbon Leakage Risk.** Without comparable domestic incentives,
United States heavy industry faces significant risk of carbon leakage,
in which production and associated emissions shift to jurisdictions
with less stringent environmental standards, including China (which
produces approximately 55 percent of global steel and 60 percent of
global cement) and India (the second-largest cement and steel producer).
The European Union Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism, which will begin
full implementation in 2026, will impose carbon costs on imports from
jurisdictions without equivalent carbon pricing, threatening the
competitiveness of United States exports unless domestic producers can
demonstrate equivalent emissions performance.

**(7) Workforce and Community Considerations.** The transition to clean
industrial manufacturing will affect approximately 150,000 workers
directly employed in traditional steel, cement, chemical, and aluminum
manufacturing facilities, as well as tens of thousands of indirect
workers in related supply chains and the communities that depend on
these facilities for their economic base. A just transition requires
federal support for facility conversion, worker retraining, and
community economic diversification.

**(8) National Security Implications.** Domestic production of green
steel, low-carbon cement, and clean hydrogen strengthens national
security by reducing dependence on foreign supply chains for critical
industrial materials, ensuring manufacturing capacity for defense
applications, and enhancing energy security through reduced fossil
fuel consumption in industrial processes.

### SUBSECTION B. DECLARATIONS OF POLICY

It is the policy of the United States that:

**(1)** The Federal Government shall establish production tax credits
and investment tax credits for clean industrial manufacturing sufficient
to achieve a 30 percent reduction in industrial sector greenhouse gas
emissions by 2035.

**(2)** The Federal Government shall prioritize domestic manufacturing
of clean industrial technologies and components, including electrolyzers,
thermal storage systems, electric furnaces, and advanced manufacturing
equipment, through the expansion of the advanced manufacturing production
credit under section 45X of the Internal Revenue Code.

**(3)** The Federal Government shall support the conversion of existing
fossil-fuel-dependent industrial facilities to clean production pathways
and shall provide assistance to workers and communities affected by the
industrial transition.

**(4)** Federal procurement and federally funded infrastructure projects
shall, to the maximum extent practicable, utilize domestically manufactured
green steel, low-carbon cement, and other clean industrial materials.

**(5)** The Secretary of the Treasury, in coordination with the Secretary
of Energy, the Secretary of Commerce, the Secretary of Labor, and the
Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency, shall administer
the provisions of this Act in a manner that maximizes emissions reductions,
promotes equitable access to credit programs, and ensures rigorous
verification and accountability.

### SUBSECTION C. SENSE OF CONGRESS

It is the sense of Congress that:

**(1)** The United States should lead the global transition to clean
industrial manufacturing and should establish a model for other nations
to follow in decarbonizing heavy industry.

**(2)** The tax credits established by this Act should be designed to
be durable, predictable, and technology-neutral within defined
performance thresholds, providing the investment certainty necessary
for capital-intensive industrial facilities with multi-decade operational
lifetimes.

**(3)** The Department of Energy, in coordination with the National
Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine, should conduct and
publish biennial assessments of progress in industrial decarbonization
technologies and make recommendations for adjustments to credit rates
and eligibility thresholds as technologies mature.

**(4)** The United States Trade Representative should pursue international
agreements that establish common standards for measuring and verifying
the carbon intensity of traded industrial goods and that prevent carbon
leakage through coordinated border adjustment mechanisms.

---

*These findings are based on data from the Environmental Protection Agency
Inventory of U.S. Greenhouse Gas Emissions and Sinks, the Department of
Energy Industrial Decarbonization Roadmap (DOE/EE-2635, September 2022),
the International Energy Agency Net Zero by 2050 Roadmap, and the United
States Geological Survey Minerals Yearbook.*
