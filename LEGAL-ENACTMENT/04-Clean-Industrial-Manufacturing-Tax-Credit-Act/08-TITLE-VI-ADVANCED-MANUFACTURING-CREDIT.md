# TITLE VI — ADVANCED INDUSTRIAL MANUFACTURING CREDIT EXPANSION

## SECTION 601. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Advanced Industrial Manufacturing
Credit Expansion Act of 2026."

## SECTION 602. EXPANSION OF ADVANCED MANUFACTURING PRODUCTION CREDIT
(SECTION 45X)

**(a) Additional Eligible Components.** Section 45X(b) of the Internal
Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new
paragraphs:

"(12) Advanced Composite Materials.

"(A) In General. Continuous carbon fiber-reinforced thermoplastic
composite components manufactured through automated production
processes, including those produced using additive molding technology
as demonstrated by Arris Composites.

"(B) Credit Amount. The credit amount shall be an amount equal to
15 percent of the cost of goods sold for qualified advanced composite
materials produced by the taxpayer and sold to an unrelated person.

"(13) Additive Manufacturing Equipment and Components.

"(A) In General. Metal powders, alloys, and feedstock materials
specifically formulated for additive manufacturing, and components
produced through industrial-scale metal additive manufacturing
processes, including those produced using laser powder bed fusion
or directed energy deposition systems manufactured by Velo3D,
EOS, SLM Solutions, or equivalent manufacturers.

"(B) Credit Amount. The credit amount for qualified additive
manufacturing feedstock materials shall be 10 percent of the cost
of production. The credit amount for qualified additive manufactured
components shall be 15 percent of the cost of goods sold.

"(14) Nano-Engineered Coatings and Surface Treatments.

"(A) In General. Nano-engineered chemical coatings, corrosion-resistant
surface treatments, and advanced functional coatings that reduce
material usage, extend product lifetime, or improve energy efficiency
of industrial equipment, including those manufactured by PPG Industries
and equivalent manufacturers.

"(B) Credit Amount. The credit amount shall be 10 percent of the cost
of goods sold for qualified nano-engineered coatings produced by the
taxpayer and sold to an unrelated person.

"(15) Energy Storage Media for Industrial Heat.

"(A) In General. Graphite blocks, refractory bricks, phase-change
materials, and other thermal storage media specifically designed
for industrial thermal energy storage systems operating at
temperatures of not less than 500 degrees Celsius.

"(B) Credit Amount. The credit amount shall be 15 percent of the
cost of production for qualified energy storage media.

"(16) Electrolyzer Components.

"(A) In General. Membrane electrode assemblies, bipolar plates,
porous transport layers, catalyst-coated membranes, and stack
assembly components for proton exchange membrane, alkaline, and
solid oxide electrolyzers.

"(B) Credit Amount. The credit amount shall be the sum of:

"(i) $50 per kilowatt of electrolyzer capacity for membrane electrode
assemblies.

"(ii) $20 per kilowatt of electrolyzer capacity for bipolar plates.

"(iii) $15 per kilowatt of electrolyzer capacity for porous transport
layers.

"(iv) 10 percent of the cost of production for other qualified
electrolyzer components.

"(17) High-Efficiency Electric Motors and Generators.

"(A) In General. Permanent magnet motors, synchronous reluctance
motors, and high-speed generators with efficiencies exceeding
IE4 classification for use in industrial applications.

"(B) Credit Amount. The credit amount shall be $10 per kilowatt
of rated capacity for qualified motors and generators."

**(b) Extension of Credit Period.** Section 45X(a)(3)(B) is amended
by striking "2030" and inserting "2036."

**(c) Phase-Out.** Section 45X(a)(3)(B) is further amended by adding:

"In the case of eligible components described in paragraphs (12)
through (17) of subsection (b), the applicable percentage shall
be 100 percent for components sold before January 1, 2033,
75 percent for components sold in calendar years 2033 and 2034,
50 percent for components sold in calendar years 2035, and
25 percent for components sold in calendar year 2036."

## SECTION 603. ELIGIBLE COMPANIES AND FACILITIES

The following entities represent illustrative examples of those
that may qualify for credits under the expanded section 45X:

**(a) Arris Composites.** Developer of additive molding technology
that combines continuous carbon fiber reinforcement with thermoplastic
matrices to produce lightweight structural components for aerospace,
automotive, and industrial applications, with a manufacturing
facility in Berkeley, California.

**(b) Velo3D.** Manufacturer of advanced metal additive manufacturing
systems utilizing Sapphire laser powder bed fusion technology capable
of printing complex geometries in titanium, Inconel, aluminum, and
other high-performance alloys, with headquarters in Fremont, California.

**(c) Relativity Space.** Developer of the Stargate metal additive
manufacturing platform, utilizing directed energy deposition and
laser powder bed fusion for large-format aerospace components, with
a manufacturing facility in Long Beach, California.

**(d) PPG Industries.** Manufacturer of nano-engineered coatings,
including electrocoat, powder coat, and liquid coating systems that
reduce material usage and improve corrosion resistance for automotive
and industrial applications.

**(e) 6K Additive.** Producer of advanced metal powders for additive
manufacturing using the UniMelt microwave plasma platform to produce
spherical metal powders from certified chemistry barstock, scrap, and
revert.

**(f) Fortify.** Developer of digital composite manufacturing systems
combining magnetic alignment of reinforcing fibers with digital light
processing for high-performance composite components.

**(g) Seurat Technologies.** Developer of area-printing metal additive
manufacturing technology utilizing pulsed laser projection for
high-throughput production of metal components.

## SECTION 604. INDUSTRIAL AUTOMATION AND PROCESS CONTROL CREDIT

**(a) In General.** Section 48D of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986
(relating to the advanced manufacturing investment credit for
semiconductors) is amended by adding at the end the following new
subsection:

"(j) Application to Industrial Automation Equipment.

"(1) In General. The credit under this section shall also be available
for qualified industrial automation property placed in service at a
qualified manufacturing facility.

"(2) Qualified Industrial Automation Property. The term includes:

"(A) Industrial robots and collaborative robots for material handling,
assembly, welding, and finishing operations.

"(B) Machine vision and automated inspection systems for quality
control.

"(C) Digital twin and simulation platforms for manufacturing process
optimization.

"(D) Predictive maintenance systems utilizing Internet of Things
sensors and machine learning algorithms.

"(E) Automated guided vehicles and autonomous mobile robots for
in-plant logistics.

"(F) Supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) and
manufacturing execution systems (MES) that enable real-time process
optimization and energy management.

"(3) Credit Rate. The credit shall be 25 percent of the qualified
investment in qualified industrial automation property."

## SECTION 605. CRITICAL MINERALS PROCESSING FOR INDUSTRIAL APPLICATIONS

**(a) Expansion of Section 45X(c).** Section 45X(c) is amended by
adding at the end the following new paragraph:

"(11) Industrial Decarbonization Materials. The term 'applicable
critical mineral' includes:

"(A) Graphite and carbon materials for thermal energy storage systems
and electric arc furnace electrodes.

"(B) Bauxite and alumina for non-metallurgical applications, including
refractory materials for high-temperature industrial furnaces.

"(C) Rare earth elements for high-efficiency electric motors and
generators used in industrial applications.

"(D) Lithium, nickel, cobalt, and manganese used in industrial
thermal storage batteries."

## SECTION 606. TECHNICAL EDUCATION AND WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT

**(a) Credit for Workforce Training Partnerships.** A credit of
$5,000 per qualified apprentice or trainee shall be allowed against
tax for taxpayers that:

(1) Partner with a community college, technical school, or registered
apprenticeship program to train workers in additive manufacturing,
advanced composites fabrication, electrolyzer manufacturing, or
industrial automation;

(2) Pay such apprentice or trainee not less than 150 percent of the
applicable minimum wage during the training period; and

(3) Employ such apprentice or trainee for not less than 12 months
following completion of training.

**(b) Limitation.** The credit under this section shall not exceed
$500,000 per taxpayer per taxable year.

## SECTION 607. REPORTING ON ADVANCED MANUFACTURING DEPLOYMENT

**(a)** The Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Commerce
and the Secretary of Energy, shall submit an annual report to Congress
on the status of advanced manufacturing deployment in the United States,
including metrics on additive manufacturing adoption, advanced composites
production, and nano-engineered materials manufacturing.

**(b)** The Secretary shall maintain a publicly accessible database of
eligible components and qualified manufacturers for purposes of this
title.

---

*This title amends sections 45X and 48D of the Internal Revenue Code
of 1986. The Secretary shall issue implementing regulations not later
than 180 days after enactment, including detailed product category
definitions and credit calculation methodologies for the new eligible
components added by section 602.*
