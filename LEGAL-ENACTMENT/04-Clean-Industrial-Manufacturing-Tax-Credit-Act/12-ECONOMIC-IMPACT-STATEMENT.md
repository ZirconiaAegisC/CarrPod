# ECONOMIC IMPACT STATEMENT

## CLEAN INDUSTRIAL MANUFACTURING TAX CREDIT ACT OF 2026

### I. INTRODUCTION AND METHODOLOGY

This Economic Impact Statement is prepared in accordance with the
requirements of the Congressional Budget and Impoundment Control Act
of 1974 and the rules of the Committee on Finance of the Senate and
the Committee on Ways and Means of the House of Representatives. The
analysis presented herein utilizes the Department of Energy's Annual
Energy Outlook 2023 reference case, the Environmental Protection Agency's
Inventory of U.S. Greenhouse Gas Emissions and Sinks, and the National
Energy Modeling System industrial module, supplemented by industry-specific
data from the United States Geological Survey, the American Iron and Steel
Institute, the Portland Cement Association, and the International Energy
Agency.

### II. DIRECT FISCAL IMPACT

**(a) Revenue Estimates.** The Joint Committee on Taxation estimates the
following ten-year revenue effects of the CIMTCA (fiscal years 2027–2036,
in billions of nominal dollars):

| Provision | FY2027–2031 | FY2032–2036 | 10-Year Total |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| Title I — Green Steel PTC | $3.5 | $8.9 | $12.4 |
| Title II — Low-Carbon Cement PTC | $2.1 | $5.6 | $7.7 |
| Title III — Clean Hydrogen Expansion | $2.8 | $7.4 | $10.2 |
| Title IV — Industrial Heat ITC | $1.4 | $3.9 | $5.3 |
| Title V — Carbon Capture Expansion | $2.2 | $6.1 | $8.3 |
| Title VI — Advanced Manufacturing | $1.2 | $3.3 | $4.5 |
| **Total Tax Credits** | **$13.2** | **$35.2** | **$48.4** |
| Title VII — Transition Grants | $3.5 | $1.5 | $5.0 |
| Title VIII — Buy America Admin | $0.1 | $0.1 | $0.2 |
| **Total Outlays** | **$16.8** | **$36.8** | **$53.6** |

**(b) Offsetting Revenues.** The CIMTCA is projected to generate offsetting
federal revenues through:

(1) Increased payroll tax receipts from approximately 500,000 new
manufacturing and construction jobs: $15.2 billion over ten years.

(2) Increased corporate income tax receipts from profitable clean
manufacturing operations: $8.6 billion over ten years.

(3) Reduced expenditures on climate-related disaster relief and
adaptation: estimated $5.2 billion in avoided costs over ten years.

(4) Net ten-year revenue effect: approximately negative $24.6 billion.

### III. EMPLOYMENT IMPACT

**(a) Direct Employment.** The CIMTCA is projected to create or sustain
the following direct employment:

| Sector | Construction Phase (Years 2–5) | Operational Phase (Years 5–10) |
|--------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Green Steel Production | 85,000 | 45,000 |
| Low-Carbon Cement | 55,000 | 35,000 |
| Clean Hydrogen Production | 65,000 | 30,000 |
| Industrial Heat Systems | 40,000 | 20,000 |
| Carbon Capture Facilities | 50,000 | 25,000 |
| Advanced Manufacturing | 30,000 | 25,000 |
| Electrolyzer Manufacturing | 25,000 | 20,000 |
| Total Direct | 350,000 | 200,000 |

**(b) Indirect and Induced Employment.** Using the Department of
Commerce Regional Input-Output Modeling System (RIMS II), the
multiplier effect of direct employment is projected to generate an
additional 350,000 indirect and induced jobs in supply chains,
transportation, services, and local economies, for a total employment
impact of approximately 500,000 to 550,000 jobs at peak deployment.

**(c) Geographic Distribution.** The employment impact is projected
to be concentrated in:

(1) Industrial Midwest (Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Michigan, Pennsylvania):
   approximately 30 percent of direct jobs.

(2) Gulf Coast (Texas, Louisiana, Alabama): approximately 20 percent
   of direct jobs, primarily in hydrogen and carbon capture.

(3) Appalachia (West Virginia, Kentucky, western Pennsylvania):
   approximately 15 percent of direct jobs, primarily in transition
   assistance and facility conversion.

(4) Western States (California, Colorado, Nevada): approximately 15
   percent of direct jobs, primarily in advanced manufacturing and
   clean hydrogen.

(5) Southeast and other regions: approximately 20 percent.

### IV. INDUSTRIAL OUTPUT AND GDP IMPACT

**(a) Industrial Output.** The CIMTCA is projected to increase domestic
industrial output as follows:

(1) Green steel production: 25 million metric tons annually by 2035,
   representing approximately 30 percent of domestic production.

(2) Low-carbon cement and concrete: 40 million metric tons annually
   by 2035, representing approximately 40 percent of domestic production.

(3) Clean hydrogen production: 10 million metric tons annually by 2035.

(4) Cumulative incremental industrial output over ten years:
   $200 billion (in constant 2026 dollars).

**(b) GDP Impact.** The Council of Economic Advisers estimates that the
CIMTCA will increase gross domestic product by:

(1) 0.15 percent of GDP in 2030 (approximately $40 billion).

(2) 0.35 percent of GDP in 2035 (approximately $95 billion).

(3) A cumulative GDP increase of approximately $550 billion over the
   ten-year credit window (discounted to present value).

### V. EMISSIONS REDUCTION IMPACT

**(a) Direct Emissions Reductions.** Based on lifecycle assessment
modeling, the CIMTCA is projected to achieve:

| Year | Annual CO2 Avoided (Mmt) | Cumulative CO2 Avoided (Mmt) |
|------|-------------------------|------------------------------|
| 2028 | 10 | 10 |
| 2030 | 50 | 110 |
| 2032 | 90 | 360 |
| 2035 | 150 | 820 |
| 2040 (post-credit) | 180 | 1,520 |

**(b) Sectoral Breakdown of 2035 Reductions.**

(1) Steel sector: 55 million metric tons CO2 annually.

(2) Cement sector: 40 million metric tons CO2 annually.

(3) Hydrogen (displacing gray H2): 25 million metric tons CO2 annually.

(4) Industrial heat decarbonization: 20 million metric tons CO2 annually.

(5) Carbon capture and utilization: 10 million metric tons CO2 annually.

**(c) Social Cost of Carbon.** Using the Environmental Protection Agency's
interim social cost of carbon estimate of $190 per metric ton (2020 dollars,
3.0 percent discount rate), the cumulative emissions reductions of
1.5 gigatons CO2 by 2040 represent avoided climate damages of approximately
$285 billion, exceeding the total fiscal cost of the Act.

### VI. COMPETITIVENESS AND TRADE IMPACT

**(a) Carbon Leakage Prevention.** By 2030, the European Union Carbon
Border Adjustment Mechanism will fully apply carbon costs to imports of
steel, cement, aluminum, fertilizers, electricity, and hydrogen from
countries without equivalent carbon pricing. The CIMTCA enables United
States producers to meet or exceed EU CBAM emissions benchmarks,
preserving approximately $12 billion in annual export value for affected
sectors.

**(b) Export Competitiveness.** Domestic producers of green steel and
low-carbon cement will be positioned to:

(1) Capture a share of the global low-carbon materials market, projected
to reach $300 billion annually by 2035.

(2) Supply growing demand from corporate buyers with net-zero commitments
under the First Movers Coalition and similar initiatives.

(3) Compete with subsidized clean industrial production in the European
Union, China, India, and other jurisdictions that are implementing
comparable industrial decarbonization incentives.

**(c) Import Substitution.** The CIMTCA is projected to reduce imports
of carbon-intensive steel and cement by approximately 40 percent by 2035,
improving the United States trade balance in industrial goods by an
estimated $15 billion annually.

### VII. COMMUNITY AND DISTRIBUTIONAL IMPACT

**(a) Energy Communities.** Approximately 40 percent of projected
facility locations are in counties classified as energy communities
under section 45(b)(11)(B) of the Internal Revenue Code, applying
the brownfield site, fossil fuel employment, and coal closure criteria.

**(b) Wages.** Average annual wages for direct manufacturing jobs
created under the CIMTCA are projected to be $75,000 to $95,000
(in 2026 dollars), significantly exceeding the national median wage.

**(c) Environmental Justice.** Emissions reductions from the CIMTCA
are projected to disproportionately benefit communities historically
overburdened by industrial air pollution, as conventional steel and
cement facilities are concentrated in environmental justice communities.
The cumulative reduction in criteria air pollutants (SO2, NOx, PM2.5)
is projected to yield $8 billion in annual public health benefits by
2035.

### VIII. SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

The projections in this statement are subject to uncertainty. Key
variables and their sensitivity ranges are:

| Variable | Baseline | Low Case | High Case |
|----------|----------|----------|-----------|
| Technology learning rate | 15%/yr | 10%/yr | 20%/yr |
| Green steel adoption rate | 30% by 2035 | 20% by 2035 | 45% by 2035 |
| Clean hydrogen cost ($/kg) | $3.00 by 2030 | $4.50 by 2030 | $2.00 by 2030 |
| Carbon price trajectory (EU ETS) | €90/tCO2 | €60/tCO2 | €120/tCO2 |
| Interest rates (10-year Treasury) | 3.5% | 5.0% | 2.5% |

Under the low case, cumulative emissions reductions reach 1.0 gigaton
CO2 by 2040. Under the high case, cumulative reductions exceed
2.0 gigatons CO2 by 2040.

### IX. CONCLUSION

The Clean Industrial Manufacturing Tax Credit Act of 2026 is projected
to generate substantial net economic benefits for the United States,
including 500,000 new manufacturing jobs, $200 billion in incremental
industrial output, and 1.5 gigatons of cumulative CO2 emissions
reductions by 2040. The social cost of carbon avoided exceeds the
fiscal cost of the Act by a factor of approximately five to one,
and the Act's provisions for domestic procurement, community
transition, and workforce development ensure that the economic
benefits are broadly distributed.
