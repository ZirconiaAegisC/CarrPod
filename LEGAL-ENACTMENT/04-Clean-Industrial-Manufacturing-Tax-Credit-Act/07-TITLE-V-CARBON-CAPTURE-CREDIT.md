# TITLE V — CARBON CAPTURE AND UTILIZATION CREDIT EXPANSION

## SECTION 501. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Carbon Capture, Utilization, and
Storage Credit Expansion Act of 2026."

## SECTION 502. AMENDMENTS TO SECTION 45Q

**(a) Increase in Credit Amounts.** Section 45Q(a)(2) of the Internal
Revenue Code of 1986 is amended:

(1) In subparagraph (A), by striking "$36" and inserting "$85."

(2) In subparagraph (B), by striking "$26" and inserting "$60."

(3) By adding at the end the following new subparagraph:

"(E) Direct Air Capture. In the case of qualified carbon oxide that is
captured from the ambient air using direct air capture technology
and that is disposed of in secure geological storage, the credit
amount shall be $180 per metric ton.

"(F) Direct Air Capture Utilization. In the case of qualified carbon
oxide captured from the ambient air using direct air capture technology
and utilized as described in paragraph (5) or (6), the credit amount
shall be $130 per metric ton."

**(b) Extension of Commence Construction Date.** Section 45Q(d)(1) is
amended by striking "January 1, 2033" and inserting "January 1, 2038."

**(c) Reduction of Capture Thresholds.** Section 45Q(d) is amended:

(1) In paragraph (2), by striking subparagraphs (A) through (D) and
inserting the following:

"(A) 1,000 metric tons of qualified carbon oxide in the case of a direct
air capture facility.

"(B) 12,500 metric tons of qualified carbon oxide in the case of an
electricity generating facility that is not a direct air capture facility.

"(C) 12,500 metric tons of qualified carbon oxide in the case of any
other facility."

(2) In paragraph (7), by striking "25,000" and inserting "12,500."

**(d) Direct Pay and Transferability.** Section 45Q is amended by
adding at the end the following new subsection:

"(i) Direct Pay for Direct Air Capture. In the case of a direct air
capture facility, the taxpayer may elect to receive direct payment
under section 6417 in lieu of claiming the credit under this section,
regardless of the taxpayer's status as a tax-exempt entity."

## SECTION 503. CO2 UTILIZATION AND MINERALIZATION BONUS

**(a) In General.** Section 45Q is further amended by adding after
subsection (i) (as added by section 502(d)) the following new
subsection:

"(j) CO2 Utilization and Mineralization Bonus.

"(1) Additional Credit. In the case of qualified carbon oxide that is
utilized in a manner that results in permanent sequestration through
mineralization in concrete, aggregates, or other durable products,
the credit amount determined under subsection (a) shall be increased
by $25 per metric ton.

"(2) Permanent Sequestration. For purposes of this subsection,
sequestration shall be considered permanent if independent
third-party life cycle assessment demonstrates that the carbon
oxide will remain sequestered for not less than 1,000 years under
expected use and disposal conditions.

"(3) Eligible Utilization Pathways. Eligible pathways include:

"(A) CO2 injection into fresh concrete during mixing, as demonstrated
by CarbonCure Technologies and CarbonBuilt.

"(B) Production of synthetic carbonate aggregates from captured CO2,
as demonstrated by Blue Planet Systems.

"(C) Mineralization of CO2 in alkaline industrial wastes, including
steel slag, cement kiln dust, and coal combustion residuals.

"(D) Production of precipitated calcium carbonate or magnesium
carbonate for use in paper, plastics, paints, and building materials.

"(E) CO2 curing of precast concrete products, achieving equivalent or
superior strength while permanently sequestering CO2.

"(F) Any other mineralization or utilization pathway that the
Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Energy, determines
results in permanent sequestration."

## SECTION 504. DIRECT AIR CAPTURE HUB PROGRAM

**(a) Establishment.** The Secretary of Energy shall establish a
program to support the development of four geographically distributed
regional direct air capture hubs, each with an annual capture capacity
of not less than 1 million metric tons of CO2.

**(b) Eligible Entities.** Entities eligible to participate in the
hub program include:

(1) Climeworks, which operates the world's first commercial DAC
facility (Orca) in Iceland and plans United States deployments using
solid sorbent technology.

(2) Heirloom Carbon Technologies, which utilizes limestone-based cyclic
calcination-carbonation DAC technology with planned facilities in the
United States.

(3) Carbon Engineering (now a subsidiary of Occidental Petroleum),
which has developed liquid-solvent DAC technology and is constructing
the Stratos facility in Ector County, Texas.

(4) Global Thermostat, utilizing solid amine-based sorbents for DAC.

(5) Any other entity demonstrating a technically viable and
economically scalable DAC technology.

**(c) Funding.** There is authorized to be appropriated $3.5 billion
for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for the DAC hub program.

## SECTION 505. INDUSTRIAL POINT-SOURCE CAPTURE ELIGIBILITY

**(a) Covered Facilities.** The credit under section 45Q (as amended
by this title) shall be available for qualified carbon oxide captured
at the following categories of industrial facilities:

(1) Cement manufacturing plants.

(2) Iron and steel manufacturing facilities.

(3) Petroleum refineries.

(4) Chemical manufacturing plants, including ammonia, ethylene, and
methanol production.

(5) Pulp and paper mills.

(6) Glass manufacturing facilities.

(7) Ethanol production facilities.

(8) Hydrogen production facilities utilizing steam methane reforming.

**(b) Integration with Clean Production Credits.** A taxpayer may
claim credits under both section 45Q and section 45AA or 45BB for
the same facility, provided that no single ton of CO2 is credited
more than once.

## SECTION 506. MONITORING, REPORTING, AND VERIFICATION

**(a) Requirements.** Any facility claiming the credit under section
45Q shall meet the monitoring, reporting, and verification requirements
established by the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency
under subpart RR or subpart UU of 40 C.F.R. Part 98, or successor
regulations.

**(b) Lifecycle Assessment for Utilization.** For credits claimed
under section 45Q(j) (CO2 utilization and mineralization bonus), the
taxpayer shall submit a lifecycle assessment conducted in accordance
with ISO 14040 and ISO 14044 demonstrating the net carbon dioxide
removal or avoidance, including emissions associated with capture,
transport, and the utilization process.

**(c) Independent Verification.** All claims for credits exceeding
$1 million in a single taxable year shall be verified by an independent
third-party qualified under standards developed by the Secretary in
consultation with the Administrator of the Environmental Protection
Agency.

## SECTION 507. PREVAILING WAGE AND APPRENTICESHIP

The prevailing wage and apprenticeship requirements of section 45Q(h)
shall apply to credits claimed under this title. For direct air capture
facilities, such requirements shall apply to both construction and
ongoing operations and maintenance.

## SECTION 508. ANNUAL REPORT ON CARBON CAPTURE DEPLOYMENT

**(a) Report Required.** The Secretary, in consultation with the
Secretary of Energy and the Administrator of the Environmental
Protection Agency, shall submit an annual report to Congress on:

(1) The total volume of CO2 captured, utilized, and sequestered at
qualified facilities.

(2) The total credits claimed under section 45Q.

(3) The number and status of direct air capture facilities under
construction and in operation.

(4) An assessment of the permanence of CO2 sequestration through
utilization pathways.

(5) Recommendations for adjustments to credit rates and eligibility
thresholds.

**(b) Public Database.** The Secretary shall maintain a publicly
accessible database of qualified facilities claiming credits under
section 45Q, including capture volumes, utilization pathways, and
storage locations.

---

*This title amends section 45Q of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986
and establishes complementary programs for direct air capture and CO2
utilization. Implementing regulations shall be issued not later than
180 days after enactment, including detailed rules for utilization-based
credit claims and direct air capture credit eligibility.*
