# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

## CLEAN INDUSTRIAL MANUFACTURING TAX CREDIT ACT OF 2026

### I. OVERVIEW

The Clean Industrial Manufacturing Tax Credit Act of 2026 (CIMTCA) establishes
a comprehensive framework of production tax credits (PTCs) and investment tax
credits (ITCs) designed to accelerate the decarbonization of American heavy
industry. The Act authorizes $50 billion in tax credits over a ten-year period
and targets a 30 percent reduction in greenhouse gas emissions from the
industrial sector by 2035, relative to 2022 baseline levels.

### II. SCOPE AND COVERED SECTORS

The Act covers the following industrial subsectors, which collectively account
for the substantial majority of United States industrial emissions:

1. **Iron and Steel Production** (Title I): Green Steel Production Tax Credit
2. **Cement and Concrete Manufacturing** (Title II): Low-Carbon Cement PTC
3. **Hydrogen Production** (Title III): Clean Hydrogen Credit Expansion
4. **Industrial Process Heat** (Title IV): Industrial Heat Decarbonization ITC
5. **Carbon Capture and Utilization** (Title V): Section 45Q Expansion
6. **Advanced Manufacturing** (Title VI): Section 45X Manufacturing Credit Expansion
7. **Workforce and Community Transition** (Title VII): Transition Assistance Program
8. **Domestic Procurement** (Title VIII): Buy America Requirements
9. **Implementation** (Title IX): Administrative Provisions
10. **Appropriations** (Title X): Authorization of Funds

### III. CREDIT STRUCTURE AND AMOUNTS

| Credit Program | Tier 1 Rate | Tier 2 Rate | Estimated Annual Outlay |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|------------------------|
| Green Steel PTC (Title I) | $150/metric ton | $75/metric ton | $3.2 billion |
| Low-Carbon Cement PTC (Title II) | $50/metric ton | $25/metric ton | $1.8 billion |
| Clean Hydrogen Expansion (Title III) | $3.00/kg + $1.00/kg | $1.00/kg | $2.5 billion |
| Industrial Heat ITC (Title IV) | 30% ITC | 20% ITC | $1.2 billion |
| Carbon Capture Expansion (Title V) | $180/DAC / $85/point-source | $60/utilization | $2.0 billion |
| Advanced Manufacturing (Title VI) | Per-unit credit | N/A | $1.0 billion |
| **TOTAL AUTHORIZED** | | | **$50 billion (10-year)** |

### IV. ELIGIBLE TECHNOLOGIES AND COMPANIES

The Act is technology-neutral within performance thresholds and supports
deployment of the following demonstrated technologies:

**Green Steel**: Molten oxide electrolysis (Boston Metal), hydrogen direct
reduced iron (H2 Green Steel, SSAB HYBRIT), electric arc furnace with
renewable power

**Low-Carbon Cement**: Electrochemical cement production (Sublime Systems),
carbon-negative ordinary Portland cement (Brimstone), carbonate mineralization
(Fortera), CO2-injected concrete (CarbonCure Technologies)

**Clean Hydrogen**: Proton exchange membrane electrolysis (Electric Hydrogen,
Hystar), alkaline electrolysis (Verdagy, Sunfire), solid oxide electrolysis

**Industrial Heat**: Thermal energy storage batteries (Antora Energy, Malta),
electric boilers, industrial heat pumps, fuel-flexible linear generators
(Mainspring Energy)

**Carbon Capture**: Direct air capture (Climeworks, Heirloom Carbon,
Carbon Engineering), industrial point-source capture, CO2 mineralization
and utilization (CarbonCure, Blue Planet)

**Advanced Manufacturing**: Continuous carbon fiber composites (Arris
Composites), metal additive manufacturing (Velo3D, Relativity Space),
nano-engineered surface coatings (PPG Industries)

### V. EMISSIONS REDUCTION TARGETS

The Act establishes the following benchmarks, to be reported annually by
the Secretary of the Treasury in consultation with the Secretary of Energy:

- **2028**: First qualifying facilities operational; 10 million metric tons
  CO2e avoided annually
- **2030**: 50 million metric tons CO2e avoided; 15% of domestic steel and
  cement production from qualifying clean facilities
- **2035**: 150 million metric tons CO2e avoided; 30% reduction in industrial
  sector emissions from 2022 baseline
- **2040**: Cumulative 1.5 gigatons CO2e avoided across all creditable
  activities

### VI. ECONOMIC AND EMPLOYMENT IMPACT

Based on modeling conducted by the Department of Energy and the Council of
Economic Advisers, the Act is projected to yield:

- **500,000 direct and indirect manufacturing jobs** created over the
  ten-year credit window, including construction trades, plant operations,
  engineering, and supply chain positions
- **$200 billion in cumulative incremental industrial output** from
  domestic clean manufacturing facilities
- **$35 billion in annual export revenue** by 2035 from globally
  competitive low-carbon industrial products
- **Avoidance of carbon border adjustment penalties** under the European
  Union Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (EU CBAM) and similar regimes,
  preserving approximately $12 billion annually in export value

### VII. FISCAL ANALYSIS

The ten-year authorization of $50 billion is structured as nonrefundable
tax credits with a five-year carryforward and transferability provisions
consistent with sections 6417 and 6418 of the Internal Revenue Code
(as added by the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022). The Joint Committee
on Taxation estimates a net revenue impact of $48.7 billion over the
scoring window, offset in part by increased payroll tax receipts from
new manufacturing employment and reduced expenditures on climate
adaptation and disaster relief.

### VIII. RELATIONSHIP TO EXISTING LAW

CIMTCA amends and expands upon provisions established in:

- Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (Pub. L. 117-169), particularly
  sections 45V (Clean Hydrogen), 45Q (Carbon Capture), 45X (Advanced
  Manufacturing), and 48C (Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit)
- Energy Policy Act of 2005 (Pub. L. 109-58)
- Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act of 2021 (Pub. L. 117-58)
- Buy America Act (41 U.S.C. §§ 8301–8305)

### IX. COORDINATION WITH INTERNATIONAL FRAMEWORKS

The Act is designed to complement and integrate with:

- European Union Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (EU CBAM,
  Regulation 2023/956)
- United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC)
  Paris Agreement nationally determined contributions
- First Movers Coalition industrial decarbonization commitments
- Industrial Deep Decarbonisation Initiative (IDDI) green procurement
  pledges
