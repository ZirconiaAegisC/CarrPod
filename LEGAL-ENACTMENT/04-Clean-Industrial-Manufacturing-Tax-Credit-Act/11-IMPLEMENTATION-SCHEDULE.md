# TITLE IX — IMPLEMENTATION AND ADMINISTRATION

## SECTION 901. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Clean Industrial Manufacturing Tax
Credit Implementation Act."

## SECTION 902. TREASURY GUIDANCE AND RULEMAKING SCHEDULE

**(a) Phase 1 — Initial Guidance (Year 1).** Not later than 180 days
after the date of enactment, the Secretary of the Treasury shall issue:

(1) Proposed regulations implementing the green steel production
credit under section 45AA, including detailed methodologies for carbon
intensity determination on a cradle-to-gate lifecycle basis, the
establishment of a qualified verifier accreditation program, and
procedures for claiming the credit.

(2) Proposed regulations implementing the low-carbon cement production
credit under section 45BB, including regional baseline carbon intensity
values for each of the four regions (Northeast, South, Midwest, and
West), methodologies for carbon intensity reduction calculations,
and guidelines for credit allocation between cement and concrete
producers.

(3) Interim guidance on the clean hydrogen production credit
amendments under Title III, including the domestic electrolyzer
manufacturing bonus credit and the industrial heat end-use bonus.

(4) Proposed regulations implementing the industrial heat
decarbonization investment credit under section 48(i), including
a list of pre-qualified technologies and equipment categories, and
thermal metering and verification standards.

(5) Interim guidance on the amendments to the carbon capture credit
under section 45Q, including the direct air capture credit rate and
the CO2 utilization and mineralization bonus credit.

(6) Proposed regulations implementing the advanced industrial
manufacturing credit expansions under section 45X(b)(12)–(17),
including product category definitions, credit calculation
methodologies, and domestic content certification procedures.

**(b) Phase 2 — Final Regulations (Year 2).** Not later than 360 days
after the date of enactment, the Secretary shall issue final
regulations for all credits established or amended by this Act.

**(c) Phase 3 — Program Operation (Years 2–5).** During the period
beginning on the date on which final regulations are issued and
ending 5 years after the date of enactment:

(1) First qualified facilities shall become eligible to claim credits
under Titles I through VI.

(2) The Secretary of Energy shall issue the first awards under the
DAC Hub Program (Title V) and the Industrial Facility Conversion
Grant Program (Title VII).

(3) The Secretary of Labor shall establish and begin administering
the Workforce Retraining and Transition Program (Title VII).

(4) The Secretary of Commerce shall establish the Clean Industrial
Materials Database (Title VIII).

(5) The Buy America clean industrial procurement requirements
(Title VIII) shall begin phase-in, with full compliance required
by January 1, 2029.

**(d) Phase 4 — Full Deployment (Years 5–10).** During the period
beginning 5 years after the date of enactment and ending December 31,
2036:

(1) Full-scale commercial deployment of green steel, low-carbon cement,
clean hydrogen, and industrial heat decarbonization technologies shall
occur across all regions of the United States.

(2) Annual credit claims under all titles shall reach steady-state
levels.

(3) The Interagency Industrial Transition Task Force (Title VII)
shall submit annual assessments of program effectiveness.

(4) The Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Energy, shall
conduct a comprehensive review of credit rates and eligibility
thresholds to inform potential legislative adjustments.

## SECTION 903. INTERAGENCY COORDINATION

**(a) Clean Industrial Manufacturing Interagency Working Group.**
There is established a Clean Industrial Manufacturing Interagency
Working Group, co-chaired by the Secretary of the Treasury and the
Secretary of Energy, to coordinate the implementation of this Act.

**(b) Membership.** The Working Group shall include representatives
from:

(1) The Department of the Treasury, including the Internal Revenue
Service.

(2) The Department of Energy, including the Office of Clean Energy
Demonstrations, the Industrial Efficiency and Decarbonization Office,
and the Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technologies Office.

(3) The Department of Commerce, including the National Institute of
Standards and Technology and the Economic Development Administration.

(4) The Department of Labor.

(5) The Environmental Protection Agency.

(6) The Office of Management and Budget.

(7) The Council on Environmental Quality.

**(c) Functions.** The Working Group shall:

(1) Develop and maintain consistent methodologies for carbon intensity
determination across all titles of this Act.

(2) Coordinate the development of qualified verifier accreditation
standards.

(3) Identify and resolve interagency conflicts or gaps in program
implementation.

(4) Prepare the annual report required under section 906.

## SECTION 904. CARBON INTENSITY METHODOLOGY STANDARDIZATION

**(a) Development.** The Administrator of the Environmental Protection
Agency, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy and the Secretary
of the Treasury, shall develop standardized methodologies for
determining the carbon intensity of industrial products on a
cradle-to-gate lifecycle basis.

**(b) Requirements.** The methodologies shall:

(1) Be consistent with ISO 14040, ISO 14044, and ISO 14067, or
successor international standards.

(2) Account for Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (purchased energy), and
upstream Scope 3 (raw material extraction and transportation)
emissions.

(3) Include both process emissions (from chemical reactions) and
combustion emissions.

(4) Provide for the use of both supplier-specific data and
industry-average data where supplier-specific data is unavailable.

(5) Be applicable to steel, cement, concrete, hydrogen, chemicals,
aluminum, and other industrial products as may be determined by
the Administrator.

(6) Be published and made publicly available not later than 12 months
after the date of enactment.

**(c) Periodic Review.** The Administrator shall review and update the
methodologies not less frequently than once every 3 years.

## SECTION 905. QUALIFIED VERIFIER ACCREDITATION PROGRAM

**(a) Establishment.** The Secretary, in consultation with the
Secretary of Energy and the Administrator of the Environmental
Protection Agency, shall establish a program for the accreditation
of qualified independent third-party verifiers.

**(b) Qualification.** To be accredited, a verifier must demonstrate:

(1) Professional engineering licensure or equivalent technical
qualifications in industrial process engineering, chemical engineering,
environmental engineering, or a related field.

(2) Experience with lifecycle assessment methodologies and industrial
process emissions quantification.

(3) Independence from the taxpayer for whom verification is performed.

(4) Compliance with continuing education requirements.

**(c) Registry.** The Secretary shall maintain a public registry of
accredited verifiers.

## SECTION 906. ANNUAL REPORTING TO CONGRESS

**(a) Comprehensive Report.** Not later than June 30 of each calendar
year, the Secretary, in coordination with the Clean Industrial
Manufacturing Interagency Working Group, shall submit to the Committee
on Finance of the Senate and the Committee on Ways and Means of the
House of Representatives a comprehensive report on the implementation
of this Act, including:

(1) Total credits claimed under each title, by credit rate tier, by
State, and by technology pathway.

(2) Volumes of green steel, low-carbon cement, clean hydrogen, and
other qualifying products produced, by facility and by State.

(3) Aggregate greenhouse gas emissions reductions achieved, calculated
on a lifecycle basis relative to conventional production pathways.

(4) Number of qualified facilities by title, by State, and by
technology pathway.

(5) Status of the Interagency Industrial Transition Task Force
activities and the number of workers and communities receiving
assistance under Title VII.

(6) Assessment of domestic manufacturing capacity for qualifying
technologies and components, including electrolyzers, thermal storage
systems, and advanced manufacturing equipment.

(7) Analysis of carbon leakage risk and the competitiveness of United
States clean industrial products in international markets.

(8) Recommendations for legislative or administrative adjustments
to credit rates, eligibility thresholds, or program design.

**(b) Public Availability.** The report shall be made publicly
available, with confidential business information redacted.

## SECTION 907. PROGRAM INTEGRITY AND ANTI-FRAUD MEASURES

**(a) Penalties for Fraudulent Claims.** Any person who knowingly
makes a false statement or submits a false claim for a credit under
this Act shall be subject to:

(1) A civil penalty equal to 200 percent of the amount of the
improperly claimed credit.

(2) Disqualification from claiming any credit under this Act for a
period of not less than 5 years.

(3) Criminal penalties as provided under sections 286 and 287 of
title 18, United States Code (conspiracy to defraud the Government
and false claims).

**(b) Audit Authority.** The Secretary shall have authority to audit
any claim for a credit under this Act, including the authority to:

(1) Examine books, records, and data of the taxpayer and any related
person.

(2) Conduct on-site inspections of qualified facilities.

(3) Require production of third-party verification reports, metering
data, and carbon intensity calculations.

**(c) Whistleblower Provisions.** Section 7623 of the Internal Revenue
Code of 1986 shall apply with respect to violations of this Act.

## SECTION 908. SUNSET AND REVIEW

**(a) Sunset.** The authority to claim credits under Titles I, II, IV,
V, and VI shall expire for products produced or property placed in
service after December 31, 2036, unless reauthorized by Congress.

**(b) Comprehensive Review.** Not later than December 31, 2034, the
Government Accountability Office shall submit to Congress a
comprehensive review of the effectiveness of this Act in achieving
its stated objectives, including recommendations for extension,
modification, or termination of the Act's provisions.

*This title establishes the administrative framework for implementation
of the Clean Industrial Manufacturing Tax Credit Act of 2026. All
references to the "Secretary" in this title refer to the Secretary
of the Treasury unless otherwise specified.*
