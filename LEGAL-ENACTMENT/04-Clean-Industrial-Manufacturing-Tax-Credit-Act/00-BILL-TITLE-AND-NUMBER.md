# CLEAN INDUSTRIAL MANUFACTURING TAX CREDIT ACT OF 2026

## SHORT TITLE

This Act may be cited as the "Clean Industrial Manufacturing Tax Credit Act of 2026"
or the "CIMTCA of 2026."

## LEGISLATIVE PURPOSE

To amend the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 to establish production tax credits
and investment tax credits for clean industrial manufacturing, to accelerate
the decarbonization of heavy industry sectors including steel, cement, chemicals,
aluminum, and hydrogen production, to expand existing credits under sections 45V,
45Q, and 45X, to establish a transition assistance program for fossil-fuel-dependent
industrial facilities and communities, to require domestic procurement preferences
for federally funded infrastructure projects, and for other purposes.

## CONGRESSIONAL FINDINGS IN BRIEF

The Congress finds that the industrial sector accounts for approximately 30 percent
of United States greenhouse gas emissions; that cement production alone contributes
8 percent of global carbon dioxide emissions and steel production contributes 7 to 9
percent; that the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 successfully demonstrated the
efficacy of production tax credits in accelerating domestic clean energy manufacturing;
that the United States possesses substantial competitive advantages in clean industrial
technologies including molten oxide electrolysis for steel, electrochemical cement
production, and advanced electrolyzer manufacturing; and that without targeted federal
incentives, the United States risks carbon leakage to jurisdictions with less stringent
environmental standards.

## EFFECTIVE DATE

Except as otherwise provided in specific titles, this Act shall take effect on the
first day of the first taxable year beginning after the date of enactment.

## SEVERABILITY

If any provision of this Act, or the application thereof to any person or circumstance,
is held invalid, the remainder of this Act and the application of such provision to
other persons or circumstances shall not be affected thereby.
