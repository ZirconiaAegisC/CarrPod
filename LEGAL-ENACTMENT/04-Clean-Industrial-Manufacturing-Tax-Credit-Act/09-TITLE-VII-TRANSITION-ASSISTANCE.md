# TITLE VII — INDUSTRIAL TRANSITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM

## SECTION 701. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Industrial Transition and Community
Resilience Act."

## SECTION 702. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE

**(a) Findings.** Congress finds that:

(1) The transition from traditional fossil-fuel-dependent industrial
processes to clean manufacturing pathways will affect workers and
communities that have historically depended on integrated steel mills,
cement kilns, chemical plants, and related facilities for employment
and economic activity.

(2) Approximately 400 blast furnaces and basic oxygen furnaces and
approximately 100 integrated cement plants operate in the United States,
directly employing approximately 150,000 workers, with an additional
500,000 indirect jobs in supply chains and dependent communities.

(3) Communities that host these facilities, particularly in the
industrial Midwest, Appalachia, and the Gulf Coast, face significant
economic disruption if facilities close rather than convert, and
require federal support for workforce retraining and economic
diversification.

(4) The experience of previous industrial transitions, including the
decline of coal mining and coal-fired power generation, demonstrates
that proactive federal investment in worker retraining, community
economic development, and infrastructure repurposing significantly
reduces the social and economic costs of transition.

**(b) Purpose.** The purpose of this title is to:

(1) Provide financial assistance for the conversion of existing fossil
fuel-dependent industrial facilities to clean production pathways.

(2) Establish workforce retraining and apprenticeship programs for
workers affected by the industrial transition.

(3) Support economic diversification in communities dependent on
traditional heavy industry.

(4) Ensure that the benefits of clean industrial manufacturing are
broadly shared across regions and demographic groups.

## SECTION 703. INDUSTRIAL FACILITY CONVERSION GRANT PROGRAM

**(a) Establishment.** The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with
the Secretary of Commerce, shall establish a grant program to support
the conversion of existing fossil-fuel-dependent industrial facilities
to clean production pathways.

**(b) Eligible Conversion Activities.** Grants may be used for:

(1) Conversion of integrated blast furnace-basic oxygen furnace steel
mills to hydrogen direct reduced iron-electric arc furnace operations.

(2) Conversion of conventional limestone-based cement kilns to
electrochemical cement production, carbon-negative feedstock
processing, or supplementary cementitious materials-based production.

(3) Conversion of steam methane reforming hydrogen plants to
electrolytic hydrogen production.

(4) Replacement of coal-fired or natural-gas-fired boilers and
furnaces with electric boilers, thermal storage batteries, or
hydrogen-fired equipment.

(5) Installation of carbon capture and utilization or storage
equipment at existing industrial facilities where complete conversion
is technically infeasible.

(6) Site remediation and environmental cleanup associated with
facility conversion.

**(c) Grant Amounts.** Grants may cover up to:

(1) 50 percent of eligible conversion costs for facilities with
more than 500 employees.

(2) 65 percent of eligible conversion costs for facilities with
100 to 500 employees.

(3) 80 percent of eligible conversion costs for facilities with
fewer than 100 employees.

**(d) Priority.** The Secretary shall give priority to applications
that:

(1) Demonstrate a binding commitment to retain or rehire existing
workers at prevailing wages.

(2) Are located in energy communities as defined in section
45(b)(11)(B) of the Internal Revenue Code.

(3) Include a community benefits agreement negotiated with local
labor organizations and community groups.

(4) Demonstrate potential for cost-effective emissions reductions.

**(e) Funding.** There is authorized to be appropriated $5 billion for
the Industrial Facility Conversion Grant Program for fiscal years 2027
through 2031.

## SECTION 704. WORKFORCE RETRAINING AND TRANSITION PROGRAM

**(a) Establishment.** The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with
the Secretary of Energy, shall establish a program to provide:

(1) Grants to States, local workforce development boards, community
colleges, and technical schools to develop and administer retraining
programs for workers affected by the industrial transition.

(2) Wage replacement and income support payments to workers enrolled
in approved retraining programs, at a rate of not less than 70 percent
of the worker's average weekly wage for the preceding 12 months, for
a period not to exceed 24 months.

(3) Relocation assistance for workers who accept employment at
qualified clean industrial facilities more than 50 miles from their
current residence.

(4) Health care continuation coverage for workers enrolled in
retraining programs.

**(b) Eligible Workers.** Workers are eligible if they:

(1) Were employed at a fossil-fuel-dependent industrial facility for
not less than 12 of the preceding 24 months.

(2) Were separated from employment due to facility closure,
conversion, or workforce reduction related to the transition to clean
industrial manufacturing.

(3) Are enrolled in or have completed an approved retraining program.

**(c) Funding.** There is authorized to be appropriated $2 billion for
the Workforce Retraining and Transition Program for fiscal years 2027
through 2031.

## SECTION 705. COMMUNITY ECONOMIC DIVERSIFICATION GRANTS

**(a) Establishment.** The Secretary of Commerce, through the Economic
Development Administration, shall establish a program of grants to
communities that are economically dependent on traditional heavy
industry to support economic diversification.

**(b) Eligible Activities.** Grants may be used for:

(1) Planning and feasibility studies for economic diversification.

(2) Infrastructure improvements to support new industries, including
broadband, transportation, water, and energy infrastructure.

(3) Site preparation and development for new industrial facilities.

(4) Small business development and entrepreneurship programs.

(5) Tourism, recreation, and outdoor industry development.

(6) Redevelopment of former industrial sites for new uses.

**(c) Eligible Communities.** A community is eligible if:

(1) It is located within 25 miles of a fossil-fuel-dependent
industrial facility that has closed or undergone conversion within
the preceding 5 years or is scheduled to close or convert within the
following 3 years.

(2) Not less than 10 percent of local employment or local tax revenue
was attributable to such facility in any of the 5 years preceding the
application.

**(d) Funding.** There is authorized to be appropriated $1.5 billion
for the Community Economic Diversification Grant Program for fiscal
years 2027 through 2031.

## SECTION 706. BROWNFIELD REDEVELOPMENT FOR CLEAN INDUSTRIAL SITES

**(a) In General.** The Administrator of the Environmental Protection
Agency shall establish a program of grants, revolving loans, and
technical assistance for the assessment, cleanup, and redevelopment
of former fossil-fuel-dependent industrial sites for clean industrial
manufacturing uses.

**(b) Eligibility.** Sites are eligible if they:

(1) Were formerly used for steelmaking, cement production, chemical
manufacturing, or other heavy industrial processes that relied on
fossil fuel combustion or feedstocks.

(2) Are planned for redevelopment as facilities qualifying for
credits under Titles I through VI of this Act.

**(c) Funding.** There is authorized to be appropriated $500 million
for fiscal years 2027 through 2031.

## SECTION 707. INTERAGENCY TRANSITION TASK FORCE

**(a) Establishment.** The President shall establish an Interagency
Industrial Transition Task Force, co-chaired by the Secretary of
Energy and the Secretary of Labor, to coordinate federal transition
assistance programs and ensure timely and effective delivery of
benefits to affected workers and communities.

**(b) Membership.** The Task Force shall include representatives from
the Departments of Energy, Labor, Commerce, the Treasury, and
Transportation, the Environmental Protection Agency, the Small
Business Administration, and the Appalachian Regional Commission.

**(c) Reporting.** The Task Force shall submit an annual report to
Congress on the status of industrial transition, the number of workers
and communities receiving assistance, and recommendations for
program improvements.

## SECTION 708. WAGE AND BENEFIT STANDARDS

**(a)** All workers employed in construction, conversion, or operation
of facilities receiving assistance under this title shall be paid wages
at rates not less than the prevailing rates for similar work in the
locality as determined by the Secretary of Labor in accordance with
the Davis-Bacon Act.

**(b)** Recipients of grants exceeding $1 million under this title shall
submit a plan for ensuring that not less than 15 percent of labor hours
are performed by qualified apprentices.

**(c)** Recipients of grants exceeding $5 million shall negotiate or
enter into a project labor agreement with one or more labor organizations.

---

*This title establishes the industrial transition assistance programs
authorized by this Act. The Secretary of Energy, Secretary of Labor,
and Secretary of Commerce shall jointly issue implementing regulations
not later than 180 days after enactment.*
