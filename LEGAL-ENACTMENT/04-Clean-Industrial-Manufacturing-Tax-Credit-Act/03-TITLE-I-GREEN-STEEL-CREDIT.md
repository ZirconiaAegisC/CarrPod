# TITLE I — GREEN STEEL PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT

## SECTION 101. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Green Steel Production Tax Credit Act."

## SECTION 102. GREEN STEEL PRODUCTION CREDIT ESTABLISHED

**(a) In General.** Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1
of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end
the following new section:

**"SEC. 45AA. GREEN STEEL PRODUCTION CREDIT.**

"(a) Allowance of Credit. For purposes of section 38, the green steel
production credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to the product of:

"(1) the applicable amount per qualified metric ton of green steel
produced by the taxpayer during such taxable year at a qualified
facility, multiplied by

"(2) the applicable percentage for such facility for such taxable year.

"(b) Applicable Amount. For purposes of this section:

"(1) Tier 1 Credit. The applicable amount shall be $150 per qualified
metric ton of green steel that is produced with a carbon intensity of
less than or equal to 0.5 metric tons of CO2-equivalent per metric ton
of crude steel, determined on a cradle-to-gate lifecycle basis.

"(2) Tier 2 Credit. The applicable amount shall be $75 per qualified
metric ton of green steel that is produced with a carbon intensity
greater than 0.5 but less than or equal to 1.0 metric tons of
CO2-equivalent per metric ton of crude steel, determined on a
cradle-to-gate lifecycle basis.

"(3) Inflation Adjustment. The dollar amounts in paragraphs (1) and (2)
shall be adjusted for inflation for taxable years beginning after
December 31, 2027, by substituting the CPI-U adjustment factor for
the base year of 2026.

"(c) Qualified Facility. For purposes of this section, the term
'qualified facility' means a manufacturing facility located in the
United States or a territory or possession of the United States that
produces crude steel using one or more of the following production
pathways:

"(1) Molten oxide electrolysis (MOE) of iron ore using renewable
electricity, without coke, coal, or natural gas reductants.

"(2) Hydrogen direct reduced iron (H2-DRI) utilizing green hydrogen
with a carbon intensity of not more than 2.0 kilograms of
CO2-equivalent per kilogram of hydrogen, followed by processing in
an electric arc furnace powered by renewable electricity.

"(3) Electrochemical reduction of iron ore in an alkaline or acidic
medium at low temperature, without coke or coal inputs.

"(4) Electric arc furnace operations utilizing 100 percent recycled
scrap steel and powered by renewable electricity, provided that the
facility demonstrates a carbon intensity of not more than 0.5 metric
tons of CO2-equivalent per metric ton of crude steel.

"(5) Any other innovative production pathway that the Secretary, in
consultation with the Secretary of Energy, determines achieves a
carbon intensity of not more than the applicable threshold under
subsection (b)(1) or (b)(2) and that does not utilize coal or coke
as a chemical reductant.

"(d) Carbon Intensity Determination. For purposes of this section,
carbon intensity shall be determined on a cradle-to-gate lifecycle
basis in accordance with:

"(1) The Greenhouse Gas Protocol Product Life Cycle Accounting and
Reporting Standard, or a successor standard recognized by the
Secretary.

"(2) Methodologies approved by the Department of Energy for
industrial product carbon intensity, including the Argonne National
Laboratory GREET model or successor models.

"(3) Emissions accounting that includes all Scope 1 (direct process
and combustion), Scope 2 (purchased electricity and heat), and
upstream Scope 3 (raw material extraction and transport) emissions
attributable to production.

"(e) Prevailing Wage and Apprenticeship Requirements. The credit
allowed under this section shall be subject to the prevailing wage
requirements of section 45(b)(7) and the apprenticeship requirements
of section 45(b)(8), applied with respect to the construction,
alteration, or repair of a qualified facility and the operation of
such facility.

"(f) Domestic Content Requirement. To qualify for the Tier 1 credit
rate, a facility must certify that not less than 60 percent of the
components and subcomponents utilized in the production process were
produced in the United States. The Secretary may waive this requirement
for specific components that are not produced in the United States in
sufficient quantities or of satisfactory quality.

"(g) Transferability. The credit allowed under this section shall be
transferable in accordance with section 6418.

"(h) Direct Pay. In lieu of claiming the credit against tax, a
tax-exempt entity, State or local government, Indian tribal government,
or other eligible entity may elect direct payment under section 6417.

"(i) Recapture. Rules similar to the rules of section 50(a) shall
apply with respect to any credit allowed under this section, provided
that recapture shall apply if a qualified facility fails to maintain
the required carbon intensity threshold for a continuous period of
24 months.

"(j) Termination. This section shall not apply to steel produced after
December 31, 2036."

**(b) Conforming Amendments.**

(1) Section 38(b) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by
striking "plus" at the end of paragraph (40), by striking the period
at the end of paragraph (41) and inserting ", plus", and by adding at
the end the following new paragraph:

"(42) the green steel production credit determined under section 45AA(a)."

(2) The table of sections for subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of
chapter 1 is amended by adding after the item relating to section 45Z
the following new item:

"Sec. 45AA. Green steel production credit."

## SECTION 103. ELIGIBLE COMPANIES AND FACILITIES

The following existing and planned facilities and technology providers
are identified as illustrative examples of entities that may qualify for
the credit under this title, subject to satisfaction of all statutory
requirements:

**(a) Boston Metal.** Molten oxide electrolysis technology for direct
iron production without carbon emissions, with a planned commercial-scale
facility capable of producing 100,000 metric tons of green steel annually.

**(b) H2 Green Steel.** Hydrogen direct reduced iron technology with
integrated electric arc furnace, utilizing green hydrogen produced
on-site from renewable-powered electrolysis.

**(c) SSAB HYBRIT.** Hydrogen-based direct reduction of iron ore
utilizing fossil-free hydrogen, with a demonstrated pilot facility
in Lulea, Sweden, and feasibility studies for United States deployment.

**(d) Electra.** Low-temperature electrochemical iron production from
low-grade ores at 60 degrees Celsius, enabling use of intermittent
renewable electricity.

**(e) Nucor Corporation.** Existing electric arc furnace operations
powered by power purchase agreements for renewable electricity, capable
of achieving Tier 1 carbon intensity thresholds with full renewable
power procurement.

## SECTION 104. LABOR STANDARDS

**(a) Prevailing Wages.** All laborers and mechanics employed by
contractors or subcontractors in the construction, alteration, or
repair of a qualified facility shall be paid wages at rates not less
than the prevailing rates for similar work in the locality as
determined by the Secretary of Labor in accordance with the
Davis-Bacon Act (40 U.S.C. 3141 et seq.).

**(b) Registered Apprenticeship.** Qualified facilities shall ensure
that not less than 15 percent of the total labor hours performed in the
construction of the facility are performed by qualified apprentices
enrolled in a registered apprenticeship program.

**(c) Good-Faith Exception.** The Secretary shall prescribe regulations
providing a good-faith effort exception for taxpayers that demonstrate
that they requested qualified apprentices from registered apprenticeship
programs and such request was denied for reasons other than the refusal
of the taxpayer to comply with established program standards.

## SECTION 105. RECORDKEEPING AND VERIFICATION

**(a) Metering and Monitoring.** Qualified facilities shall install and
maintain continuous emissions monitoring systems, electricity metering
with source certification, and mass-balance tracking systems sufficient
to verify compliance with carbon intensity thresholds.

**(b) Third-Party Verification.** Claims for the credit under this
title shall be accompanied by an independent third-party verification
report prepared by a qualified engineer or verifier accredited by a
recognized standards body.

**(c) Penalties for Fraud.** Any taxpayer that knowingly claims a
credit to which it is not entitled under this title shall be liable
for a penalty equal to 200 percent of the amount of the improperly
claimed credit, in addition to repayment of the credit amount and
interest.

## SECTION 106. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

**(a) Annual Report.** Not later than April 15 of each calendar year,
the Secretary shall submit to the Committee on Finance of the Senate
and the Committee on Ways and Means of the House of Representatives a
report detailing the total credits claimed under this title, the volume
of green steel produced, the average carbon intensity of creditable
production, and the geographic distribution of qualified facilities.

**(b) Public Transparency.** The Secretary shall publish annually
aggregated data on credits claimed, production volumes, and emissions
reductions achieved, in a manner that does not disclose confidential
business information.

## SECTION 107. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS

There are authorized to be appropriated to the Secretary such sums as
may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this title, including
for the development of lifecycle analysis methodologies, third-party
verification protocols, and continuous emissions monitoring standards.
