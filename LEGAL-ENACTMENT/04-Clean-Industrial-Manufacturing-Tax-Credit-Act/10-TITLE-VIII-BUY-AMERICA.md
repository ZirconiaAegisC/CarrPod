# TITLE VIII — BUY AMERICA CLEAN INDUSTRIAL PROCUREMENT REQUIREMENTS

## SECTION 801. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Buy America Clean Industrial
Procurement Act of 2026."

## SECTION 802. FINDINGS

Congress finds that:

(1) The Federal Government is the largest single purchaser of
construction materials in the United States, with annual procurement
of steel, cement, asphalt, glass, and other industrial materials
exceeding $100 billion through direct procurement and federally funded
infrastructure projects.

(2) The Buy America Act (41 U.S.C. §§ 8301–8305) and the Build
America, Buy America Act (sections 70901–70953 of the Infrastructure
Investment and Jobs Act of 2021) have successfully increased domestic
manufacturing of iron, steel, and manufactured products for federally
funded projects.

(3) Extending Buy America preferences to green steel, low-carbon
cement, and clean hydrogen produced through the tax credits
established by this Act will create a reliable market for clean
industrial products, accelerating private investment in clean
manufacturing capacity and achieving economies of scale that reduce
costs for all purchasers.

(4) Federal procurement preferences for clean industrial materials
will send a strong demand signal to domestic manufacturers,
complementing the supply-side incentives provided by the production
and investment tax credits in Titles I through VI.

(5) Coordination of federal procurement policy with the tax credit
framework established by this Act will ensure that taxpayer-funded
incentives support production that directly supplies taxpayer-funded
infrastructure projects.

## SECTION 803. APPLICATION OF BUY AMERICA PREFERENCES TO CLEAN
INDUSTRIAL MATERIALS

**(a) In General.** Chapter 83 of title 41, United States Code, is
amended by adding at the end the following new section:

"§ 8306. Clean industrial materials

"(a) Requirement. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, not
later than January 1, 2029, all iron, steel, cement, concrete, and
hydrogen purchased for use in any project, program, or activity
funded in whole or in part by the Federal Government shall be
produced at a facility that meets the applicable clean production
standards.

"(b) Clean Production Standards. For purposes of this section:

"(1) Iron and steel shall be considered to meet clean production
standards if produced at a facility that qualifies for the green
steel production credit under section 45AA of the Internal Revenue
Code of 1986.

"(2) Cement and concrete shall be considered to meet clean production
standards if produced at a facility that qualifies for the low-carbon
cement production credit under section 45BB of the Internal Revenue
Code of 1986.

"(3) Hydrogen shall be considered to meet clean production standards
if produced at a facility that qualifies for the clean hydrogen
production credit under section 45V of the Internal Revenue Code of
1986 at the 1.0 kilogram of CO2-equivalent per kilogram of hydrogen
emission rate or lower.

"(c) Applicability. The requirements of subsection (a) shall apply to:

"(1) All projects, programs, or activities funded under the
Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act of 2021 (Public Law 117-58).

"(2) All projects, programs, or activities funded under the
Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (Public Law 117-169).

"(3) All direct federal procurement of construction materials for
federal buildings, facilities, and infrastructure.

"(4) All projects, programs, or activities funded under the Federal-Aid
Highway Act, the Airport and Airway Improvement Act, and the Federal
Transit Act.

"(d) Waivers.

"(1) In general. The head of a Federal agency may waive the
requirements of subsection (a) with respect to a particular project,
program, or activity if the head of the agency finds that:

"(A) applying subsection (a) would be inconsistent with the public
interest;

"(B) clean industrial materials produced in the United States are
not produced in sufficient and reasonably available quantities or
of satisfactory quality; or

"(C) inclusion of clean industrial materials produced in the United
States will increase the cost of the overall project by more than
25 percent.

"(2) Public notice and comment. Not later than 30 days before issuing
a waiver under paragraph (1), the head of the agency shall publish in
the Federal Register a detailed written explanation of the proposed
waiver and provide a period of not less than 15 days for public
comment.

"(3) Annual report. Each agency shall submit an annual report to
Congress listing all waivers issued under this subsection, including
a detailed justification for each waiver.

"(e) Coordination with Build America, Buy America Act. The
requirements of this section shall apply in addition to, and shall
not supersede or modify, the requirements of sections 70911 through
70917 of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act of 2021
(Public Law 117-58)."

**(b) Clerical Amendment.** The table of sections for chapter 83 of
title 41, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end the
following:

"8306. Clean industrial materials."

## SECTION 804. GREEN PROCUREMENT PREFERENCE FOR CLEAN INDUSTRIAL
PRODUCTS

**(a) Evaluation Preference.** In any procurement of iron, steel,
cement, concrete, or hydrogen by a Federal agency, the agency shall
apply an evaluation preference of not less than 10 percent to bids
or offers for products that are produced at a facility that qualifies
for the green steel production credit under section 45AA of the
Internal Revenue Code of 1986 or the low-carbon cement production
credit under section 45BB of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

**(b) Bid Adjustment.** The evaluation preference shall be applied by
reducing the bid price of the qualifying offer by not less than
10 percent for purposes of bid evaluation only, without affecting the
actual contract price.

**(c) Reporting.** Each Federal agency shall include in its annual
procurement report a summary of procurements in which the evaluation
preference under this section was applied and the resulting selection
of clean industrial products.

## SECTION 805. CLEAN INDUSTRIAL MATERIALS DATABASE

**(a) Establishment.** The Secretary of Commerce, through the National
Institute of Standards and Technology, shall establish and maintain a
publicly accessible database of facilities and products that meet the
clean production standards described in section 8306(b) of title 41,
United States Code (as added by section 803).

**(b) Content.** The database shall include:

(1) A list of qualified facilities by product category (steel, cement,
hydrogen) and geographic location.

(2) Certified carbon intensity data for each qualified facility and
product.

(3) Production capacity, lead times, and contact information for
procurement officers.

(4) Independent third-party verification reports.

**(c) Funding.** There is authorized to be appropriated $25 million
for the establishment and maintenance of the database for fiscal years
2027 through 2031.

## SECTION 806. FEDERAL-STATE PARTNERSHIP FOR CLEAN PROCUREMENT

**(a) State Incentive Program.** The Secretary of Commerce shall
establish a competitive grant program to provide financial and
technical assistance to States, local governments, and tribal
governments that adopt procurement preferences for clean industrial
materials consistent with the standards established in this title.

**(b) Matching Requirement.** Federal funds under this section shall
not exceed 75 percent of the cost of activities funded.

**(c) Authorization.** There is authorized to be appropriated
$100 million for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for the program under
this section.
