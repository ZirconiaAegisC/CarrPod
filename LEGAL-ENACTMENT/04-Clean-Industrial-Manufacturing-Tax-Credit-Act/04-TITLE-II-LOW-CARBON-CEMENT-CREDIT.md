# TITLE II — LOW-CARBON CEMENT PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT

## SECTION 201. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Low-Carbon Cement Production Tax
Credit Act."

## SECTION 202. LOW-CARBON CEMENT PRODUCTION CREDIT ESTABLISHED

**(a) In General.** Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1
of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end
the following new section:

**"SEC. 45BB. LOW-CARBON CEMENT PRODUCTION CREDIT.**

"(a) Allowance of Credit. For purposes of section 38, the low-carbon
cement production credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to
the product of:

"(1) the applicable amount per qualified metric ton of low-carbon
cement produced by the taxpayer during such taxable year at a
qualified facility, multiplied by

"(2) the applicable percentage for such facility for such taxable year.

"(b) Applicable Amount. For purposes of this section:

"(1) Tier 1 Credit. The applicable amount shall be $50 per qualified
metric ton of cementitious product that is produced with a carbon
intensity reduction of greater than 60 percent compared to the
regional industry baseline for ordinary Portland cement, determined
on a cradle-to-gate lifecycle basis.

"(2) Tier 2 Credit. The applicable amount shall be $25 per qualified
metric ton of cementitious product that is produced with a carbon
intensity reduction of greater than 30 percent but less than or equal
to 60 percent compared to the regional industry baseline for ordinary
Portland cement, determined on a cradle-to-gate lifecycle basis.

"(3) Regional Baseline. For purposes of this subsection, the regional
industry baseline for ordinary Portland cement shall be determined by
the Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Energy and the
Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency, for each of four
regions (Northeast, South, Midwest, and West), and shall reflect the
average carbon intensity of ordinary Portland cement produced in each
region in 2022, which shall be not less than 0.85 metric tons of
CO2-equivalent per metric ton of cementitious product.

"(4) Inflation Adjustment. The dollar amounts in paragraphs (1) and (2)
shall be adjusted for inflation in taxable years beginning after
December 31, 2027, consistent with the adjustment methodology under
section 45AA(b)(3).

"(c) Eligible Technologies. For purposes of this section, the following
production pathways and technologies are eligible:

"(1) Electrochemical Cement. Cement produced through an electrochemical
process that avoids the calcination of limestone, including but not
limited to the ambient-temperature electrolytic process demonstrated
by Sublime Systems, in which calcium-bearing minerals are dissolved and
converted to cementitious compounds without process CO2 emissions.

"(2) Carbon-Negative Ordinary Portland Cement. Cement meeting ASTM C150
specifications for ordinary Portland cement that is produced from
calcium silicate feedstocks other than limestone, including
carbon-free calcium silicate rocks that do not release process CO2
during calcination, as demonstrated by Brimstone.

"(3) Carbonate Mineralization Cement. Cement or supplementary
cementitious materials produced through carbonate mineralization
pathways that consume CO2 during production, including those
demonstrated by Fortera and Blue Planet Systems.

"(4) CO2-Injected Concrete. Concrete produced in which captured carbon
dioxide is injected during mixing, where the CO2 permanently mineralizes
within the concrete matrix, as demonstrated by CarbonCure Technologies
and CarbonBuilt.

"(5) Blended Cements. Cements incorporating greater than 35 percent
supplementary cementitious materials, including calcined clays,
natural pozzolans, ground glass, and steel slag, that achieve Tier 2
carbon intensity reductions.

"(6) Carbon-Cured Concrete. Concrete cured using waste CO2 instead of
steam or water, resulting in permanent sequestration of CO2 while
achieving equivalent or superior compressive strength.

"(7) Other Innovative Pathways. Any other cement or concrete production
pathway that the Secretary, in consultation with the Secretary of Energy,
determines achieves the applicable carbon intensity reduction threshold
under subsection (b)(1) or (b)(2).

"(d) Qualified Facility. The term 'qualified facility' means a
manufacturing facility located in the United States or a territory
or possession of the United States that produces cementitious products
using eligible technologies described in subsection (c) and that meets
the carbon intensity reduction thresholds described in subsection (b).

"(e) Carbon Intensity Determination. Carbon intensity shall be
determined in accordance with section 45AA(d), with additional
accounting for:

"(1) Process emissions from calcination and other chemical reactions.

"(2) Thermal energy inputs, including fuel combustion and purchased heat.

"(3) Electricity consumption, including renewable energy procurement.

"(4) Upstream emissions from raw material extraction, beneficiation,
and transportation.

"(5) Carbonation of concrete products during their service life and
at end of life, where scientifically documented.

"(f) Concrete Credit Allocation. Where cement is used to produce
concrete, the credit shall be allocated between the cement producer
and the concrete producer based on their respective contributions to
the carbon intensity reduction, as prescribed by the Secretary in
regulations.

"(g) Prevailing Wage and Apprenticeship. The requirements of section
45AA(e) shall apply to credits claimed under this section.

"(h) Transferability and Direct Pay. The provisions of section
45AA(g) and (h) shall apply to credits under this section.

"(i) Recapture. The provisions of section 45AA(i) shall apply with
respect to credits under this section.

"(j) Termination. This section shall not apply to cementitious
products produced after December 31, 2036."

**(b) Conforming Amendments.** Section 38(b) of the Internal Revenue
Code of 1986 is further amended by adding after paragraph (42) (as
added by section 102(b)) the following new paragraph:

"(43) the low-carbon cement production credit determined under
section 45BB(a)."

## SECTION 203. IDENTIFIED QUALIFYING TECHNOLOGIES AND FACILITIES

The following entities and technologies are illustrative of those
that may qualify for credits under this title:

**(a) Sublime Systems.** Electrochemical cement production utilizing
ambient-temperature electrolysis to produce cementitious materials
without limestone calcination, with a pilot facility in Somerville,
Massachusetts, and a planned commercial-scale facility.

**(b) Brimstone.** Production of ASTM C150-compliant ordinary Portland
cement from carbon-free calcium silicate rocks, yielding a
carbon-negative product with negative process emissions.

**(c) Fortera Corporation.** Carbonate mineralization technology
producing cementitious materials with 60 to 70 percent lower CO2
emissions compared to conventional Portland cement.

**(d) CarbonCure Technologies.** CO2 injection systems installed at
over 700 concrete plants globally, mineralizing captured CO2 within
fresh concrete and reducing cement content through strength enhancement.

**(e) Blue Planet Systems.** Production of synthetic limestone aggregate
from captured CO2, yielding carbon-negative aggregate for use in concrete.

**(f) Ecocem and Solidia Technologies.** Low-carbon cement formulations
utilizing alternative chemistries and carbon-curing processes that
reduce overall carbon intensity.

## SECTION 204. SUPPLEMENTARY CEMENTITIOUS MATERIALS INCENTIVE

**(a) In General.** In addition to the credit under section 45BB, a
supplementary cementitious materials production credit of $15 per
metric ton shall be allowed for the domestic production of:

(1) Calcined clays processed for use as supplementary cementitious
materials in blended cements.

(2) Ground glass pozzolans produced from post-consumer recycled glass.

(3) Processed steel slag meeting specifications for use in concrete.

(4) Natural pozzolans mined and processed in the United States.

**(b) Qualification.** To qualify, supplementary cementitious materials
must be produced at a facility located in the United States and must
meet ASTM C618, ASTM C989, or successor standard specifications for
use in concrete, as certified by a qualified independent laboratory.

## SECTION 205. PERFORMANCE STANDARDS AND TESTING

**(a) Strength and Durability.** No credit shall be allowed for cement
or concrete that does not meet applicable ASTM International or AASHTO
standards for compressive strength, durability, workability, and other
performance criteria for its intended application.

**(b) Independent Testing.** Carbon intensity claims shall be verified
by an independent laboratory accredited by the AASHTO Accreditation
Program or an equivalent program recognized by the Secretary.

**(c) Continuous Monitoring.** Facilities qualifying for the Tier 1
credit shall install and maintain continuous emissions monitoring
systems and production mass-balance tracking systems.

## SECTION 206. ENVIRONMENTAL PRODUCT DECLARATIONS

**(a) Requirement.** Producers claiming credits under this title shall
develop and publish Type III environmental product declarations in
accordance with ISO 14025 and applicable product category rules.

**(b) Registry.** The Secretary, in consultation with the Administrator
of the Environmental Protection Agency, shall establish or designate a
national registry for environmental product declarations for cementitious
products, which shall be publicly accessible.

## SECTION 207. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS

There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessary
to carry out this title, including development of regional baselines,
lifecycle analysis methodologies specific to cement and concrete, and
testing infrastructure for novel cementitious materials.

---

*This title establishes a new section 45BB of the Internal Revenue Code
of 1986. The Secretary shall issue implementing regulations not later than
210 days after enactment, including detailed regional baseline carbon
intensity values for each of the four regions identified in section 202(b)(3).*
