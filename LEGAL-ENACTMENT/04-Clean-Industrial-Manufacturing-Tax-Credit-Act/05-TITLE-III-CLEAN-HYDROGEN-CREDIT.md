# TITLE III — CLEAN HYDROGEN PRODUCTION CREDIT EXPANSION

## SECTION 301. SHORT TITLE

This title may be cited as the "Clean Hydrogen Production and
Electrolyzer Manufacturing Credit Expansion Act."

## SECTION 302. AMENDMENTS TO SECTION 45V

**(a) Extension of Credit Period.** Section 45V(a)(2)(A)(ii) of the
Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by striking "10-year period"
and inserting "15-year period."

**(b) Modification of Emission Rates.** Section 45V(b)(2) is amended:

(1) In subparagraph (A), by striking "4.0" and inserting "3.5."

(2) In subparagraph (B), by striking "2.5" and inserting "2.0."

(3) In subparagraph (C), by striking "1.5" and inserting "1.0."

(4) In subparagraph (D), by striking "0.45" and inserting "0.2."

**(c) Inflation Adjustment Amendment.** Section 45V(b)(4) is amended
by striking "section 45V(b)(2)(A), (B), and (C)" and inserting
"section 45V(b)(2)(A), (B), (C), and (D)(ii)."

## SECTION 303. DOMESTIC ELECTROLYZER MANUFACTURING BONUS CREDIT

**(a) In General.** Section 45V is amended by adding at the end the
following new subsection:

"(i) Domestic Electrolyzer Manufacturing Bonus. In the case of a
qualified clean hydrogen production facility that places in service
electrolyzer stacks or modules manufactured in the United States, the
applicable amount under subsection (a) shall be increased by an
additional credit determined under paragraph (2).

"(2) Additional Credit Amount. The additional credit amount is $1.00
per kilogram of qualified clean hydrogen produced during the taxable
year, multiplied by the applicable percentage under subsection (b)(4).

"(3) Domestic Manufacturing Requirement. For purposes of this
subsection, an electrolyzer stack or module shall be treated as
manufactured in the United States if not less than 60 percent of
its total components and subcomponents (by value) were mined,
produced, or manufactured in the United States.

"(4) Phase-Down. The additional credit amount under paragraph (2)
shall phase down by 25 percent for electrolyzer stacks and modules
placed in service after December 31, 2030, and by an additional
25 percent for electrolyzer stacks and modules placed in service
after December 31, 2032.

"(5) Termination. No additional credit shall be allowed under this
subsection for electrolyzer stacks or modules placed in service after
December 31, 2034."

**(b) Eligible Domestic Manufacturers.** Electrolyzer manufacturers
with existing or planned domestic manufacturing facilities include:

(1) Electric Hydrogen, with a gigafactory in Devens, Massachusetts,
producing 100-megawatt proton exchange membrane electrolyzer stacks.

(2) Verdagy, with facilities in Moss Landing, California, producing
advanced alkaline electrolyzers with large active areas and dynamic
operating ranges.

(3) Hystar, with planned United States manufacturing for ultra-efficient
proton exchange membrane electrolyzers.

(4) Sunfire, with planned manufacturing for high-temperature solid
oxide electrolysis stacks.

(5) Plug Power, with a gigafactory in Rochester, New York, producing
proton exchange membrane electrolyzer stacks and membrane electrode
assemblies.

(6) Nel Hydrogen, with a manufacturing facility in Wallingford,
Connecticut, producing alkaline and proton exchange membrane stacks.

(7) Bloom Energy, with manufacturing capacity in Newark, Delaware,
and Fremont, California, for solid oxide electrolyzer cells and stacks.

(8) Ohmium International, with manufacturing in India and planned
expansion to the United States.

## SECTION 304. INDUSTRIAL HEAT END-USE BONUS

**(a) Additional Credit.** Section 45V is further amended by adding
after subsection (i) (as added by section 303) the following new
subsection:

"(j) Industrial Heat End-Use Bonus.

"(1) In General. In the case of qualified clean hydrogen sold or used
as a feedstock or fuel for industrial process heat in a sector for
which hydrogen is the technically and economically preferred
decarbonization pathway, the applicable amount under subsection (a)
shall be increased by an additional $0.50 per kilogram, multiplied
by the applicable percentage.

"(2) Eligible End-Uses. Qualified end-uses include:

"(A) Direct reduced iron production for green steel manufacturing.

"(B) High-temperature process heat in glass, ceramics, and metals
processing.

"(C) Chemical manufacturing feedstocks, including ammonia and
methanol production, where hydrogen replaces fossil-derived syngas.

"(D) Industrial furnace and kiln fuel, where electrification is
technically infeasible or economically prohibitive.

"(3) Certification. The taxpayer must demonstrate through independent
third-party verification that the hydrogen is used for an eligible
end-use and that no lower-emission alternative pathway is technically
and economically feasible for the specific application."

## SECTION 305. QUALIFYING CLEAN HYDROGEN DEFINITION REFINEMENTS

**(a) Hourly Matching.** Effective for hydrogen produced after
December 31, 2028, the Secretary shall require that qualified clean
hydrogen be produced using electricity for which the taxpayer
demonstrates hourly matching of renewable energy generation and
electrolyzer consumption, consistent with the three-pillar framework
of additionality, temporal correlation, and geographic deliverability.

**(b) Transition Rule.** For hydrogen produced before January 1, 2029,
annual matching shall be accepted; for hydrogen produced in 2029 and
2030, monthly matching shall be accepted; for hydrogen produced after
December 31, 2030, hourly matching shall apply unless the Secretary
determines that tracking infrastructure is not yet available.

**(c) Additionality.** Electricity used to produce qualified clean
hydrogen shall be generated by a facility placed in service not
earlier than 36 months before the electrolyzer facility is placed
in service, or shall be from a facility that has undergone a
significant incremental investment in repowering.

## SECTION 306. EXPANSION OF QUALIFYING ADVANCED ENERGY PROJECT
CREDIT FOR HYDROGEN INFRASTRUCTURE

**(a) In General.** Section 48C of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986
is amended by adding hydrogen transmission and distribution
infrastructure to the definition of "qualifying advanced energy
project," including:

(1) Hydrogen pipelines, compression, and storage.

(2) Hydrogen refueling stations for heavy-duty transportation.

(3) Geological storage facilities for hydrogen.

(4) Ammonia cracking and hydrogen purification equipment.

**(b) Allocation.** Of the total amount of section 48C credits
authorized, not less than $5 billion shall be reserved for hydrogen
infrastructure projects described in subsection (a).

## SECTION 307. CLEAN HYDROGEN PRODUCTION AND INFRASTRUCTURE GRANTS

**(a) Establishment.** The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with
the Secretary of the Treasury, shall establish a grant program to
support the deployment of clean hydrogen production facilities and
associated infrastructure in underserved and energy-transition
communities.

**(b) Authorization.** There is authorized to be appropriated
$2 billion for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for
grants under this section.

## SECTION 308. REPORTING AND ACCOUNTABILITY

**(a) Annual Hydrogen Credit Report.** The Secretary, in consultation
with the Secretary of Energy, shall submit an annual report to Congress
detailing:

(1) Total credits claimed under section 45V, including bonus credits.

(2) Kilograms of qualified clean hydrogen produced by production
pathway and by State.

(3) Lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions of hydrogen produced at each
qualified facility.

(4) The number of qualified facilities utilizing domestically
manufactured electrolyzers and the domestic content percentages
achieved.

**(b) Technology Assessment.** The Secretary of Energy shall conduct
and publish a biennial assessment of electrolyzer manufacturing
capacity and supply chain readiness in the United States.

---

*This title amends section 45V of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986
and establishes complementary provisions for hydrogen infrastructure
and electrolyzer manufacturing. Regulations shall be issued not later
than 180 days after enactment, including detailed rules for the hourly
matching and additionality requirements of sections 304 and 305.*
