================================================================================
              TITLE V — AGRICULTURAL WATER EFFICIENCY
================================================================================

  Authorization: $10,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 501. IRRIGATION MODERNIZATION GRANTS.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Agriculture shall establish
      a competitive grant program to fund on-farm and irrigation district
      water delivery system modernization, including:
        (1) Conversion from flood and furrow irrigation to pressurized
            sprinkler or micro-irrigation systems;
        (2) Lining or piping of open earthen canals to eliminate seepage
            losses;
        (3) Automation and remote control of irrigation delivery gates
            and turnout structures;
        (4) Installation of tailwater recovery and recirculation systems;
        (5) Laser leveling and precision land grading;
        (6) Soil moisture sensor networks and variable rate irrigation.

  (b) COST SHARE. — The Federal share shall not exceed:
        (1) 50 percent for irrigation districts and large agricultural
            operations with annual gross revenues exceeding $1,000,000;
        (2) 75 percent for small and mid-sized operations with annual
            gross revenues between $100,000 and $1,000,000;
        (3) 90 percent for beginning farmers, ranchers, and operations
            with annual gross revenues below $100,000.

  (c) WATER SAVINGS QUANTIFICATION. — All grant recipients shall install
      water measurement devices at delivery and return flow points and
      report water savings to the Secretary on an annual basis.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $4,000,000,000 is authorized for the program
      established under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 502. PRECISION AGRICULTURE WATER TECHNOLOGY DEPLOYMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) TECHNOLOGY INCENTIVE PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Agriculture shall
      establish a program providing cost-share payments for adoption of
      precision agriculture water technologies, including:
        (1) Drone and satellite-based crop water stress detection using
            thermal and multispectral imaging;
        (2) Soil moisture sensing networks with wireless data transmission;
        (3) Variable rate irrigation systems with GPS-guided control;
        (4) Weather station networks for microclimate-based irrigation
            scheduling;
        (5) Artificial water deficit and regulated deficit irrigation
            strategies for permanent crops.

  (b) TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE. — The Natural Resources Conservation Service
      shall expand the Conservation Technical Assistance program to provide
      dedicated water efficiency specialists in each State experiencing
      severe, extreme, or exceptional drought as classified by the United
      States Drought Monitor.

  (c) DEMONSTRATION FARMS. — The Secretary shall establish a network of
      100 Agricultural Water Efficiency Demonstration Farms distributed
      across major irrigated agricultural regions to:
        (1) Showcase integrated water efficiency technologies;
        (2) Provide hands-on training for farmers and irrigation managers;
        (3) Publish annual water use, yield, and economic return data.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 503. CROP TRANSITION ASSISTANCE FOR DROUGHT-AFFECTED REGIONS.
================================================================================

  (a) TRANSITION PAYMENTS. — The Secretary of Agriculture shall establish a
      program providing multi-year transition payments to agricultural
      producers in the Colorado River Basin and other critically drought-
      affected regions who:
        (1) Voluntarily convert from high-water-use crops to low-water-use
            alternative crops;
        (2) Transition from irrigated to dryland farming;
        (3) Voluntarily and permanently retire irrigated acreage with
            associated water rights dedicated to instream flow or system
            conservation.

  (b) CROP RESEARCH AND EXTENSION. — The Agricultural Research Service
      and land-grant university extension services shall expand research
      programs on:
        (1) Breeding and development of drought-tolerant crop varieties
            suited to arid and semi-arid regions;
        (2) Crop water productivity optimization under deficit irrigation;
        (3) Alternative cropping systems including agroforestry and
            perennial grain systems.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 504. REGENERATIVE AGRICULTURE SOIL WATER RETENTION.
================================================================================

  (a) SOIL HEALTH WATER RETENTION PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Agriculture
      shall expand the Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP) and
      the Conservation Stewardship Program (CSP) to specifically incentivize
      practices that improve soil water-holding capacity, including:
        (1) Cover cropping and continuous living cover;
        (2) No-till and reduced-till farming systems;
        (3) Compost and organic matter application at agronomic rates;
        (4) Agroforestry, windbreaks, and riparian buffer restoration;
        (5) Managed intensive rotational grazing.

  (b) QUANTIFICATION. — The Secretary shall develop standardized
      methodologies to quantify the water retention benefits of
      regenerative agricultural practices, expressed as acre-inches
      of additional soil water-holding capacity.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 505. LIVESTOCK WATER SUPPLY RESILIENCE.
================================================================================

  (a) WATER SUPPLY DEVELOPMENT GRANTS. — The Secretary of Agriculture shall
      provide grants for:
        (1) Development of off-stream livestock watering systems to reduce
            riparian area degradation;
        (2) Installation of solar-powered stock wells and pumping systems;
        (3) Construction of stock ponds, water catchment systems, and
            emergency water storage;
        (4) Pipeline and distribution systems for rangeland livestock
            watering.

  (b) DROUGHT PREPAREDNESS PLANNING. — The Secretary shall provide technical
      assistance to livestock producers for development of drought
      preparedness plans addressing:
        (1) Stocking rate flexibility and adaptive management triggers;
        (2) Alternative water supply identification;
        (3) Feed reserve and alternative forage strategies.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
