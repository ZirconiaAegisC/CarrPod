================================================================================
              TITLE I — LEAD PIPE ELIMINATION AND SAFE
              DRINKING WATER INFRASTRUCTURE
================================================================================

  Authorization: $45,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 101. LEAD SERVICE LINE INVENTORY AND REPLACEMENT MANDATE.
================================================================================

  (a) INVENTORY REQUIREMENT. — Not later than 18 months after the date of
      enactment of this Act, every community water system shall complete and
      submit to the Administrator a comprehensive inventory of all lead
      service lines within its distribution system, including the material
      composition and location of each service line.

  (b) REPLACEMENT MANDATE. — Not later than 10 years after the date of
      enactment of this Act, every community water system shall replace
      all lead service lines identified in the inventory, with priority
      given to:
        (1) Service lines serving schools, childcare facilities, and
            healthcare facilities;
        (2) Service lines in disadvantaged communities;
        (3) Service lines with the highest measured lead concentrations;
        (4) Service lines in communities where children under age 6
            constitute more than 10 percent of the population.

  (c) FULL REPLACEMENT REQUIREMENT. — Replacement of lead service lines
      under this section shall include replacement of the entire service
      line from the water main to the building inlet, including both the
      publicly owned and privately owned portions. Partial replacement
      of lead service lines is prohibited.

  (d) NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS. — Community water systems replacing lead
      service lines under this section shall provide written notification
      to affected residents not less than 45 days before commencing
      replacement, including information on the health risks of lead
      exposure, the replacement schedule, the provision of point-of-use
      water filters, and post-replacement water testing.

  (e) PENALTIES FOR NONCOMPLIANCE. — The Administrator may assess civil
      penalties not to exceed $25,000 per day against any community water
      system that fails to meet the requirements of this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 102. LOW-INCOME HOUSEHOLD ASSISTANCE PROGRAM.
================================================================================

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Administrator shall establish a grant program
      to provide financial assistance to low-income households for the
      replacement of the privately owned portion of lead service lines.

  (b) ELIGIBILITY. — Households with annual income at or below 200 percent
      of the Federal poverty level shall be eligible for 100 percent cost
      coverage of private-side lead service line replacement.

  (c) POINT-OF-USE FILTER PROVISION. — For households awaiting service line
      replacement, the Administrator shall provide certified point-of-use
      water filters meeting NSF/ANSI Standard 53 for lead reduction, with
      filter replacement cartridges provided at no cost to the household
      at intervals not to exceed 6 months.

================================================================================
SECTION 103. STATE REVOLVING FUND CAPITALIZATION GRANTS.
================================================================================

  (a) SUPPLEMENTAL CAPITALIZATION. — In addition to amounts authorized
      under section 1452 of the Safe Drinking Water Act (42 U.S.C. 300j-12),
      there is authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 for each of
      fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for capitalization grants to State
      drinking water treatment revolving loan funds, of which:

        (1) 49 percent shall be used for principal forgiveness loans or
            grants to disadvantaged communities;
        (2) 30 percent shall be available for lead service line replacement;
        (3) 15 percent shall be available for emerging contaminant treatment;
        (4) 6 percent shall be available for distribution system upgrades.

  (b) BUY AMERICA REQUIREMENT. — All iron and steel products, manufactured
      products, and construction materials used in projects funded under
      this section shall be produced in the United States, consistent with
      the requirements of the Build America, Buy America Act (Title IX of
      Division G of Public Law 117-58).

================================================================================
SECTION 104. SCHOOL AND CHILDCARE FACILITY LEAD REMEDIATION.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall establish a competitive grant
      program to fund lead remediation in public schools, charter schools,
      and licensed childcare facilities, including:
        (1) Replacement of lead-containing plumbing fixtures, pipes, and
            solder within the facility;
        (2) Installation of certified point-of-use filtration systems;
        (3) Ongoing water quality testing.

  (b) PRIORITY. — Priority for grants under this section shall be given to:
        (1) Schools and childcare facilities located in disadvantaged
            communities;
        (2) Facilities serving children under age 6;
        (3) Facilities built before 1988, when the Safe Drinking Water Act
            lead ban took full effect.

  (c) TESTING AND REPORTING. — All schools and childcare facilities receiving
      grants under this section shall test drinking water outlets for lead
      annually and publicly report results.

================================================================================
SECTION 105. LEAD TESTING AND COMPLIANCE STANDARDS.
================================================================================

  (a) STRENGTHENED LEAD AND COPPER RULE. — The Administrator shall, not
      later than 12 months after the date of enactment of this Act,
      promulgate revisions to the Lead and Copper Rule (40 CFR Part 141
      Subpart I) to:
        (1) Require a lead action level of 5 parts per billion;
        (2) Mandate replacement of all lead service lines within 10 years
            of the effective date of the revised rule;
        (3) Require more frequent and comprehensive tap water sampling;
        (4) Improve public notification and transparency requirements.

  (b) WATER QUALITY TESTING INVENTORY. — The Administrator shall maintain
      a publicly accessible, searchable online database of lead and copper
      testing results for every community water system in the United States,
      updated at least quarterly.

================================================================================
SECTION 106. WORKFORCE TRAINING FOR LEAD PIPE REPLACEMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) REGISTERED APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Labor, in
      consultation with the Administrator, shall establish a national
      registered apprenticeship program for lead service line replacement
      workers, in coordination with labor unions and community colleges.

  (b) GRANTS FOR TRAINING. — There is authorized $500,000,000 for grants
      to community colleges, technical schools, labor-management training
      programs, and other qualified training providers for:
        (1) Curriculum development and instructor training;
        (2) Equipment and facility modernization;
        (3) Pre-apprenticeship programs targeting underrepresented
            communities and veterans.

  (c) LOCAL HIRING PREFERENCE. — To the maximum extent practicable, water
      systems receiving funding under this title shall prioritize hiring
      residents of the communities in which lead service line replacement
      is being performed.
