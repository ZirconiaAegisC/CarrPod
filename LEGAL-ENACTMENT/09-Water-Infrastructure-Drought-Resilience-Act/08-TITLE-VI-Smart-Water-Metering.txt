================================================================================
              TITLE VI — SMART WATER METERING AND LEAK
              DETECTION INFRASTRUCTURE
================================================================================

  Authorization: Subsumed within administrative allocations

================================================================================
SECTION 601. NATIONAL SMART METERING INITIATIVE.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall establish a competitive grant
      program to fund deployment of advanced metering infrastructure (AMI)
      in community water systems across the United States, including:
        (1) Smart water meters with two-way communication capability;
        (2) Fixed-network data collection systems;
        (3) Meter data management software and analytics platforms;
        (4) Customer-facing water use portals and leak notification
            applications;
        (5) Integration with existing utility billing and customer
            information systems.

  (b) DEPLOYMENT TARGETS. — Each community water system serving more than
      10,000 persons shall achieve:
        (1) Not less than 50 percent AMI coverage by December 31, 2031;
        (2) Not less than 100 percent AMI coverage by December 31, 2036.

  (c) CYBERSECURITY REQUIREMENTS. — All AMI systems funded under this
      section shall comply with the National Institute of Standards and
      Technology (NIST) Framework for Improving Critical Infrastructure
      Cybersecurity and shall incorporate:
        (1) End-to-end encryption of meter data transmissions;
        (2) Role-based access control and multi-factor authentication;
        (3) Secure firmware update mechanisms;
        (4) Intrusion detection and anomaly monitoring.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for the program
      established under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 602. ADVANCED LEAK DETECTION TECHNOLOGY DEPLOYMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) TECHNOLOGY DEPLOYMENT FUND. — The Administrator shall establish a fund
      to accelerate deployment of advanced leak detection technologies
      including:
        (1) Acoustic leak detection sensors and correlators;
        (2) Satellite-based synthetic aperture radar leak detection;
        (3) In-pipe inspection technologies including tethered and
            free-swimming sensors;
        (4) Pressure transient monitoring and analysis systems;
        (5) Distributed fiber optic acoustic and temperature sensing;
        (6) Unmanned aerial vehicle-based thermal and multispectral
            leak detection.

  (b) PRIORITY. — Priority for funding under this section shall be given to
      water systems with:
        (1) Non-revenue water exceeding 25 percent of total production;
        (2) Annual main break rates exceeding 25 per 100 miles of pipe;
        (3) Infrastructure age averaging greater than 50 years.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for the fund established
      under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 603. REAL-TIME DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM MONITORING.
================================================================================

  (a) MONITORING REQUIREMENT. — Not later than 5 years after enactment, every
      community water system serving more than 50,000 persons shall deploy
      a real-time distribution system monitoring network that includes:
        (1) Continuous pressure monitoring at representative locations
            throughout the distribution system;
        (2) Continuous flow monitoring at district metered area boundaries;
        (3) Water quality monitoring for chlorine residual, turbidity,
            pH, and conductivity at critical control points;
        (4) Integrated supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA)
            system with alarm and notification capabilities.

  (b) DATA SHARING. — The Administrator shall establish a voluntary national
      database of anonymized distribution system performance metrics,
      including water loss rates, pressure management data, and pipe failure
      statistics, to support benchmarking and best practice identification.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 604. NON-REVENUE WATER REDUCTION TARGETS.
================================================================================

  (a) STATE WATER LOSS AUDITING REQUIREMENT. — Not later than 24 months after
      enactment, each State shall require all community water systems serving
      more than 3,300 persons to:
        (1) Conduct annual water audits using the American Water Works
            Association (AWWA) M36 water audit methodology;
        (2) Submit validated water audit results to the State;
        (3) Publish annual water audit summary data.

  (b) NATIONAL TARGETS. — The Administrator shall establish national
      non-revenue water reduction targets, including:
        (1) All community water systems exceeding 25 percent non-revenue
            water shall reduce to no more than 20 percent within 5 years;
        (2) All community water systems exceeding 15 percent non-revenue
            water shall reduce to no more than 12 percent within 10 years.

  (c) TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE. — The Administrator shall provide technical
      assistance to small water systems serving fewer than 10,000 persons
      for water loss auditing, leak detection, and water loss control
      planning.

================================================================================
SECTION 605. DATA STANDARDIZATION AND INTEROPERABILITY.
================================================================================

  (a) NATIONAL STANDARDS. — The Administrator, in coordination with the
      National Institute of Standards and Technology, shall develop:
        (1) Data format and communication protocol standards for water
            metering and distribution system monitoring equipment;
        (2) Interoperability requirements for vendor-proprietary systems;
        (3) Open data standards for water utility performance reporting.

  (b) INTEROPERABILITY CERTIFICATION. — The Administrator shall establish a
      voluntary certification program for water technology products that
      demonstrate compliance with the interoperability standards developed
      under subsection (a).
