SECTION 1001. IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE AND MILESTONES.
================================================================================

  (a) YEAR 1 (FISCAL YEAR 2027):

    (1) QUARTER 1 (Oct-Dec):
        — Establishment of interagency implementation task force
        — Promulgation of initial program regulations and guidance
        — Publication of Notices of Funding Opportunity for Title I lead
          service line inventory and replacement grants
        — Initiation of Tribal water rights settlement consultations

    (2) QUARTER 2 (Jan-Mar):
        — First grant awards under Title I State revolving fund
          capitalization
        — Publication of draft revisions to the Lead and Copper Rule
        — Initiation of PFAS destruction technology research grants
        — Colorado River Basin conservation program framework published

    (3) QUARTER 3 (Apr-Jun):
        — First awards under Title IV Western Drought Resilience programs
        — Title III PFAS monitoring grants awarded to water systems
          exceeding current health advisory levels
        — Title VI smart water metering Notice of Funding Opportunity
          published
        — National Water Recycling Implementation Plan guidance issued
          to States

    (4) QUARTER 4 (Jul-Sep):
        — Title II water recycling demonstration project applications
          due
        — Title VII desalination research center designations announced
        — Title V agricultural water efficiency first grant round
        — Annual report to Congress submitted

  (b) YEAR 2 (FISCAL YEAR 2028):

    (1) QUARTER 1-2:
        — Revised Lead and Copper Rule final rule published
        — All community water systems serving more than 50,000 persons
          shall have submitted lead service line inventories
        — Title VIII Tribal water infrastructure grants first round
        — Five direct potable reuse demonstration projects selected

    (2) QUARTER 3-4:
        — PFAS National Primary Drinking Water Regulation published
        — Title IV groundwater monitoring network expansion contracts
          awarded
        — Title VII desalination demonstration facility site selection
          complete
        — Interstate water banking framework agreement completed for
          Colorado River Basin

  (c) YEAR 3 (FISCAL YEAR 2029):

    — All community water systems shall have completed lead service line
      inventories
    — At least 15 percent of identified lead service lines replaced
    — First desalination demonstration facility construction commenced
    — State water recycling implementation plans submitted and reviewed
    — Smart metering deployments in at least 50 water systems initiated

  (d) YEAR 5 (FISCAL YEAR 2031):

    — At least 40 percent of lead service lines replaced nationwide
    — First direct potable reuse facility operational
    — All community water systems serving more than 50,000 persons shall
      have deployed real-time distribution system monitoring
    — Energy-efficient desalination cost targets benchmark assessment
      completed
    — Midpoint program evaluation by Government Accountability Office

  (e) YEAR 10 (FISCAL YEAR 2036):

    — 100 percent of lead service lines replaced nationwide
    — Municipal wastewater reuse rate of not less than 10 percent
    — PFAS remediation completed at all priority sites
    — Colorado River Basin system conservation target of 3 million
      acre-feet annual savings achieved
    — Agricultural water use efficiency improvement of 20 percent
      in the Colorado River Basin
    — Comprehensive program evaluation and sunset review
    — Final report to Congress

================================================================================
SECTION 1002. ANNUAL REPORT TO CONGRESS.
================================================================================

  (a) REQUIREMENT. — Not later than September 30 of each fiscal year, the
      Secretary of the Interior and the Administrator shall jointly submit
      to the appropriate congressional committees a comprehensive report
      on the implementation of this Act.

  (b) CONTENTS. — Each report submitted under subsection (a) shall include:

    (1) A detailed accounting of all funds appropriated, obligated, and
        expended during the preceding fiscal year, disaggregated by title,
        program, and State;

    (2) Quantitative performance metrics including:
        (A) Number and location of lead service lines replaced;
        (B) Volume of water recycled through new facilities;
        (C) Number of PFAS sites assessed and remediated;
        (D) Acre-feet of water conserved through Title IV programs;
        (E) Irrigated acres converted to high-efficiency systems;
        (F) Number of smart meters deployed;
        (G) Desalination research and demonstration milestones achieved;
        (H) Tribal communities with new or improved water infrastructure;

    (3) An assessment of progress toward statutory targets and milestones;

    (4) Identification of any statutory, regulatory, or administrative
        barriers impeding program implementation;

    (5) Recommendations for legislative or administrative actions to
        improve program effectiveness;

    (6) An analysis of environmental justice outcomes, including the
        distribution of benefits to disadvantaged communities.

================================================================================
SECTION 1003. COMPTROLLER GENERAL AUDITS.
================================================================================

  (a) BIENNIAL AUDIT. — The Comptroller General of the United States shall
      conduct a comprehensive audit of the programs authorized under this
      Act not less frequently than once every 2 years.

  (b) SCOPE. — Each audit conducted under subsection (a) shall evaluate:

    (1) The efficiency and effectiveness of program administration;

    (2) Compliance with statutory requirements, including Buy America
        provisions and environmental justice mandates;

    (3) The accuracy of performance metrics reported to Congress;

    (4) The adequacy of internal controls to prevent waste, fraud,
        and abuse;

    (5) Whether the programs are achieving their stated objectives
        in a cost-effective manner.

  (c) PUBLIC AVAILABILITY. — All audits conducted under this section shall
      be made publicly available on the Government Accountability Office
      website within 30 days of transmission to Congress.

SECTION 1004. ECONOMIC IMPACT ASSESSMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) INDEPENDENT ASSESSMENT. — Not later than 36 months after enactment,
      and every 3 years thereafter, the Secretary shall contract with an
      independent entity, such as the National Academies of Sciences,
      Engineering, and Medicine, to conduct a comprehensive economic
      impact assessment.

  (b) ASSESSMENT SCOPE. — The assessment shall evaluate:

    (1) Direct, indirect, and induced economic impacts of investments
        made under each title, including:
        (A) Gross domestic product contributions;
        (B) Employment impacts disaggregated by occupation, geography,
            and demographic characteristics;
        (C) Wage and income effects;
        (D) State and local tax revenue impacts;

    (2) Public health benefits and cost savings from lead service line
        elimination, including avoided:
        (A) Healthcare costs;
        (B) Special education expenditures;
        (C) Lost lifetime earnings due to cognitive impairment;
        (D) Criminal justice system costs;

    (3) Economic value of water saved through recycling, conservation,
        and efficiency programs;

    (4) Regional economic effects of drought resilience investments
        compared with costs of water shortage-driven economic disruption;

    (5) Return on investment analysis for each major program category.

  (c) ECONOMIC IMPACT BENCHMARKS. — The assessment shall evaluate progress
      against the following benchmarks:

    (1) Creation of not fewer than 350,000 jobs attributable to this Act
        by fiscal year 2036;

    (2) Cumulative GDP contribution attributable to this Act of not less
        than $180 billion over the period of fiscal years 2027-2036;

    (3) Annual public health cost savings attributable to lead service
        line elimination of not less than $50 billion by fiscal year 2036;

    (4) Economic value of water loss reduction of not less than
        $5 billion annually by fiscal year 2036.

================================================================================
SECTION 1005. BUY AMERICA REQUIREMENTS.
================================================================================

  (a) DOMESTIC CONTENT REQUIREMENT. — All iron and steel products,
      manufactured products, and construction materials used in projects
      funded under this Act shall be produced in the United States,
      consistent with the requirements of the Build America, Buy America
      Act (Title IX of Division G of Public Law 117-58).

  (b) WAIVER PROCESS. — The head of the relevant Federal agency may waive
      the requirements of subsection (a) only upon a written determination,
      published in the Federal Register for public comment, that:

    (1) Applying the requirement would be inconsistent with the public
        interest;

    (2) Iron, steel, manufactured products, or construction materials
        of satisfactory quality and sufficient quantity are not produced
        in the United States; or

    (3) Inclusion of domestic material would increase the cost of the
        overall project by more than 25 percent.

  (c) REPORTING. — Any waiver issued under subsection (b) shall be reported
      to the appropriate congressional committees within 15 days of issuance
      and included in the annual report required under section 1002.
