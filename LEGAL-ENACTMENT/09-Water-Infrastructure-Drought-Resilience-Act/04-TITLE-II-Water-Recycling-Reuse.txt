================================================================================
              TITLE II — WATER RECYCLING AND REUSE
================================================================================

  Authorization: $20,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 201. NATIONAL WATER RECYCLING TARGETS.
================================================================================

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT OF TARGETS. — Congress establishes the following national
      targets for municipal wastewater recycling and beneficial reuse:
        (1) Not less than 10 percent of total municipal wastewater flow
            recycled for beneficial reuse by December 31, 2036.
        (2) Not less than 25 percent of total municipal wastewater flow
            recycled for beneficial reuse by December 31, 2046.
        (3) Not less than 50 percent of total municipal wastewater flow
            recycled for beneficial reuse by December 31, 2056.

  (b) STATE IMPLEMENTATION PLANS. — Not later than 24 months after the date
      of enactment of this Act, each State shall submit to the Administrator
      a water recycling implementation plan that identifies:
        (1) Existing wastewater treatment capacity and current recycling rates;
        (2) Projected water demand and supply deficits through 2056;
        (3) Priority recycling projects and associated cost estimates;
        (4) Regulatory and institutional barriers to recycling expansion.

  (c) FEDERAL TECHNICAL STANDARDS. — The Administrator, in consultation with
      the Secretary of Health and Human Services, shall promulgate Federal
      technical and public health standards for:
        (1) Indirect potable reuse through groundwater recharge;
        (2) Direct potable reuse treatment train requirements;
        (3) Industrial and agricultural non-potable reuse quality standards;
        (4) Monitoring and reporting requirements for recycled water systems.

================================================================================
SECTION 202. DIRECT POTABLE REUSE DEMONSTRATION PROJECTS.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Administrator shall establish a competitive
      grant program to fund a minimum of 20 direct potable reuse demonstration
      projects distributed across diverse geographic, climatic, and
      hydrogeologic regions of the United States.

  (b) PROJECT REQUIREMENTS. — Each demonstration project shall:
        (1) Employ a full advanced treatment train including microfiltration
            or ultrafiltration, reverse osmosis, and advanced oxidation;
        (2) Include a comprehensive water quality monitoring program testing
            for at least 250 constituents including pathogens, trace organic
            compounds, pharmaceuticals, PFAS, and disinfection byproducts;
        (3) Incorporate public education and community engagement components;
        (4) Operate for a minimum period of 5 years with full monitoring.

  (c) ISRAEL MODEL BENCHMARKING. — The Administrator shall, within 18 months
      of enactment, produce a comprehensive report analyzing Israel's national
      water recycling program, including regulatory frameworks, treatment
      technologies, public acceptance strategies, agricultural reuse practices,
      and economic models, and shall incorporate findings into United States
      program design.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $5,000,000,000 is authorized for the program established
      under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 203. INDUSTRIAL AND AGRICULTURAL REUSE INCENTIVES.
================================================================================

  (a) INDUSTRIAL WATER REUSE CREDIT. — The Administrator shall establish a
      tax credit of $0.50 per 1,000 gallons of recycled water used by
      industrial facilities in lieu of potable water supplies, up to a maximum
      credit of $5,000,000 per facility per year.

  (b) AGRICULTURAL REUSE PIPELINE GRANTS. — The Secretary of Agriculture shall
      establish a grant program to fund the construction of distribution
      pipelines connecting municipal water recycling facilities to agricultural
      users, with priority given to:
        (1) Pipelines connecting treatment plants in urban areas to
            agricultural areas in water-stressed basins;
        (2) Projects that reduce groundwater extraction in critically
            overdrafted basins;
        (3) Projects providing drought-resilient supply to operations
            producing high-value specialty crops.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for grants under
      subsection (b).

================================================================================
SECTION 204. ADVANCED TREATMENT TECHNOLOGY DEPLOYMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) TECHNOLOGY ACCELERATION FUND. — The Administrator shall establish a fund
      to accelerate deployment of advanced water treatment technologies
      including:
        (1) Ceramic membrane filtration systems;
        (2) Forward osmosis and pressure-retarded osmosis;
        (3) Ultraviolet advanced oxidation processes;
        (4) Granular activated carbon and ion exchange systems;
        (5) Biological activated carbon filtration;
        (6) Membrane bioreactor optimization.

  (b) COST SHARING. — The Federal share of project costs under this section
      shall not exceed 75 percent for non-disadvantaged communities and may
      be up to 100 percent for disadvantaged communities.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for the fund established
      under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 205. INTERNATIONAL BEST PRACTICES AND TECHNOLOGY TRANSFER.
================================================================================

  (a) INTERNATIONAL COOPERATION. — The Administrator shall establish a
      water recycling technology exchange program with Israel, Singapore,
      Australia, the European Union, and other nations identified as having
      advanced water recycling infrastructure.

  (b) ACTIVITIES. — The exchange program shall include:
        (1) Biennial technical exchange conferences;
        (2) Exchange of scientists, engineers, and regulatory officials;
        (3) Joint research and development projects;
        (4) Publication of translated technical standards and guidance.

================================================================================
SECTION 206. PUBLIC ACCEPTANCE AND EDUCATION CAMPAIGN.
================================================================================

  (a) NATIONAL CAMPAIGN. — The Administrator, in coordination with the
      Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, shall conduct a national
      public education campaign to:
        (1) Communicate the safety and reliability of recycled water;
        (2) Address public misperceptions and concerns;
        (3) Highlight successful water recycling programs in the
            United States and internationally;
        (4) Explain the advanced treatment technologies that ensure
            recycled water quality meets or exceeds drinking water
            standards.

  (b) COMMUNITY-SPECIFIC OUTREACH. — Grant recipients under this title
      shall include community-specific public engagement activities,
      including public meetings, facility tours, educational curricula,
      and transparent water quality data reporting.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for the campaign
      established under this section.
