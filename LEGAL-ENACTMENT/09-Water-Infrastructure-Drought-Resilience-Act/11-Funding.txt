================================================================================
              TITLE IX — FUNDING AND APPROPRIATIONS
================================================================================

================================================================================
SECTION 901. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.
================================================================================

  (a) TOTAL AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated to carry
      out this Act $120,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036.

  (b) ANNUAL AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated
      $12,000,000,000 for each of fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

  (c) AVAILABILITY. — Amounts appropriated under this section shall remain
      available until expended.

================================================================================
SECTION 902. ALLOCATION AMONG TITLES.
================================================================================

  (a) ALLOCATION. — Of the amounts authorized under section 901:

      (1) TITLE I (Lead Pipe Elimination): ....................... $45,000,000,000
      (2) TITLE II (Water Recycling and Reuse): .................. $20,000,000,000
      (3) TITLE III (PFAS Remediation): .......................... $15,000,000,000
      (4) TITLE IV (Western Drought Resilience): ................. $25,000,000,000
      (5) TITLE V (Agricultural Water Efficiency): ............... $10,000,000,000
      (6) TITLE VI (Smart Water Metering): ....................... Subsumed below
      (7) TITLE VII (Desalination R&D): .......................... Subsumed below
      (8) TITLE VIII (Tribal Water Rights): ...................... $5,000,000,000
      (9) Section 902(b) Reserve: ................................ $5,000,000,000

  (b) RESERVE FUND. — Amounts not specifically allocated to Titles I through
      VIII under subsection (a) shall be deposited in a Reserve Fund
      administered by the Secretary of the Treasury and shall be available
      for:
        (1) Title VI and Title VII program allocations as specified in
            this Act;
        (2) Program administration, technical assistance, and oversight;
        (3) Emergency supplemental allocations upon determination by the
            Secretary of the Treasury, in consultation with the appropriate
            congressional committees, that additional funding is necessary
            to meet program objectives.

  (c) REPROGRAMMING. — Not more than 5 percent of the amount allocated to any
      title under subsection (a) may be reprogrammed to another title upon
      30 days written notification to and approval by the appropriate
      congressional committees.

================================================================================
SECTION 903. OVERSIGHT, ACCOUNTABILITY, AND AUDIT.
================================================================================

  (a) INSPECTOR GENERAL AUDITS. — The Inspector General of each department
      or agency receiving appropriations under this Act shall conduct annual
      audits of all programs, grants, and expenditures to:
        (1) Verify compliance with statutory requirements;
        (2) Detect and prevent waste, fraud, and abuse;
        (3) Evaluate program effectiveness and efficiency.

  (b) RECIPIENT REPORTING. — Each recipient of funding exceeding $1,000,000
      under this Act shall submit to the relevant agency an annual report
      detailing:
        (1) Funds expended in the preceding fiscal year;
        (2) Project milestones achieved;
        (3) Performance metrics including water saved, lead lines replaced,
            PFAS sites remediated, and other applicable metrics;
        (4) Jobs created or retained as a result of Federal funding.

  (c) PUBLIC TRANSPARENCY PORTAL. — The Administrator shall establish and
      maintain a publicly accessible website that provides:
        (1) Real-time or quarterly data on all grant and contract awards
            made under this Act, including recipient name, project location,
            award amount, and project description;
        (2) Annual progress reports for each title;
        (3) Searchable database of performance metrics.

  (d) WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTIONS. — Employees, contractors, and grantees
      who report waste, fraud, or abuse related to funds appropriated under
      this Act shall be entitled to the protections of the Whistleblower
      Protection Act (5 U.S.C. 2302(b)(8)) and section 1107 of the
      National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2018.

================================================================================
SECTION 904. MATCHING FUND REQUIREMENTS.
================================================================================

  (a) STATE AND LOCAL MATCH. — Except as provided in subsection (b), the
      Federal share of project costs under this Act shall not exceed:
        (1) 80 percent for projects in disadvantaged communities;
        (2) 65 percent for projects in communities with median household
            income at or above 200 percent of the Federal poverty level;
        (3) 50 percent for all other projects.

  (b) WAIVER. — The Federal share may be increased to 100 percent for:
        (1) Projects serving Tribal communities;
        (2) Projects serving communities where the median household income
            is less than 80 percent of the State median;
        (3) Emergency appropriations authorized under section 406 or
            section 805.

  (c) IN-KIND CONTRIBUTIONS. — Non-Federal matching requirements under this
      section may be met through in-kind contributions of labor, materials,
      equipment, or land, as determined by the relevant Secretary.

================================================================================
SECTION 905. SUNSET PROVISIONS.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM SUNSET. — The authority to award new grants and enter into new
      contracts under this Act shall expire on September 30, 2036.

  (b) EXTENSION AUTHORITY. — The Secretary may extend the authority under
      subsection (a) for an additional 5-year period upon:
        (1) Completion of a comprehensive program evaluation by the
            Government Accountability Office;
        (2) Submission of a written justification to the appropriate
            congressional committees not later than June 30, 2036;
        (3) Passage of a joint resolution of Congress approving the
            extension.

  (c) CONTINUING OBLIGATIONS. — Expiration of authority under this section
      shall not affect the validity of contracts, grants, or loan guarantees
      entered into prior to the expiration date.
