================================================================================
              TITLE IV — WESTERN DROUGHT RESILIENCE
================================================================================

  Authorization: $25,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 401. COLORADO RIVER BASIN CONSERVATION PROGRAM.
================================================================================

  (a) VOLUNTARY SYSTEM CONSERVATION PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall establish
      a permanent, fully funded System Conservation Program within the
      Colorado River Basin to provide compensation for voluntary, verifiable
      reductions in water consumption by agricultural, municipal, industrial,
      and Tribal water users.

  (b) COMPENSATION RATES. — The Secretary shall establish compensation rates
      for conserved water based on:
        (1) Regional market prices for water rights transfers;
        (2) The economic value of agricultural production foregone;
        (3) Environmental and recreational co-benefits;
        (4) The value of avoided shortage declaration impacts.

  (c) PRIORITY BASIN. — Not less than 60 percent of funds authorized under
      this section shall be allocated to the Lower Colorado River Basin,
      with priority for conservation in Arizona, California, and Nevada.

  (d) QUANTIFICATION AND VERIFICATION. — All conserved water under this
      section shall be quantified using best available science, satellite
      evapotranspiration data, and direct flow measurement, and shall be
      verified by the Bureau of Reclamation or an approved third-party
      verifier.

  (e) AUTHORIZATION. — $8,000,000,000 is authorized for the Conservation
      Program established under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 402. GROUNDWATER MANAGEMENT AND AQUIFER RECHARGE.
================================================================================

  (a) MANAGED AQUIFER RECHARGE GRANTS. — The Secretary shall award grants to
      States, water districts, irrigation districts, and groundwater
      sustainability agencies for:
        (1) Construction of infiltration basins and injection wells;
        (2) Aquifer storage and recovery (ASR) well systems;
        (3) Conveyance infrastructure connecting surface water supplies
            to recharge facilities;
        (4) Monitoring well networks to verify recharge effectiveness.

  (b) CRITICAL AQUIFER PROTECTION. — The Secretary shall identify and
      prioritize aquifers that are:
        (1) Designated as critically overdrafted by the relevant State;
        (2) Serving as the primary drinking water source for a population
            greater than 100,000;
        (3) Demonstrating declines in average water level of more than
            2 feet per year.

  (c) GROUNDWATER DATA PROGRAM. — The United States Geological Survey, in
      coordination with State geological surveys, shall establish a National
      Groundwater Monitoring Network expansion program to deploy 5,000
      additional monitoring wells across priority aquifers, with real-time
      data transmission and public access.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $5,000,000,000 is authorized for programs established
      under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 403. INTERSTATE WATER BANKING AND TRANSFER AGREEMENTS.
================================================================================

  (a) COLORADO RIVER WATER BANK. — The Secretary shall facilitate the
      establishment of a Colorado River Basin Water Bank to enable:
        (1) Temporary, voluntary leasing of water rights among States
            and water users within the Basin;
        (2) Storage and accounting of conserved water for subsequent use
            or transfer;
        (3) Emergency water supply allocations during shortage declarations.

  (b) INTERSTATE COMPACT FACILITATION. — The Secretary shall provide technical
      and legal support for negotiations toward updated interstate water
      compacts reflecting current hydrologic realities, including the Colorado
      River Compact of 1922, the Rio Grande Compact, and the Republican River
      Compact.

  (c) PROHIBITION ON FEDERAL WATER RIGHTS PREEMPTION. — Nothing in this
      section shall be construed to preempt, modify, or abrogate State water
      law, interstate water compacts, or decreed water rights, including
      Tribal water rights.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for activities under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 404. DROUGHT EARLY WARNING SYSTEM EXPANSION.
================================================================================

  (a) NATIONAL INTEGRATED DROUGHT INFORMATION SYSTEM (NIDIS) EXPANSION. —
      The Director of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration
      shall expand NIDIS to include:
        (1) Sub-seasonal to seasonal drought forecasting at a spatial
            resolution of 1 kilometer or finer;
        (2) Soil moisture monitoring network expansion to 5,000 stations;
        (3) Snowpack and snow water equivalent monitoring network expansion;
        (4) Satellite-based evapotranspiration and groundwater monitoring;
        (5) Integration of paleoclimate and tree-ring reconstructions into
            drought risk assessments.

  (b) INTERAGENCY COORDINATION. — The Director shall establish an interagency
      Drought Monitoring and Prediction Council comprising representatives
      from NOAA, USGS, USDA, the Bureau of Reclamation, the Army Corps of
      Engineers, and the Federal Emergency Management Agency.

  (c) STATE AND LOCAL PARTNERSHIPS. — The Director shall provide grants to
      States, Tribes, and regional authorities to develop and maintain
      locally relevant drought monitoring and decision-support tools.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,500,000,000 is authorized for programs established
      under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 405. WATER-EFFICIENT LANDSCAPING AND URBAN CONSERVATION.
================================================================================

  (a) TURF REMOVAL AND XERISCAPING PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall establish
      a grant program providing financial incentives to residential,
      commercial, and institutional property owners for:
        (1) Removal of non-functional turf grass and replacement with
            drought-tolerant landscaping;
        (2) Installation of high-efficiency drip irrigation systems;
        (3) Rainwater harvesting and graywater systems for landscape use.

  (b) URBAN WATER CONSERVATION GRANTS. — The Administrator shall award
      grants to municipalities for:
        (1) Distribution system leak detection and repair programs;
        (2) Water-efficient plumbing fixture rebate programs;
        (3) Public water conservation education and outreach;
        (4) Water rate structure reform to incentivize conservation.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 406. RURAL COMMUNITY DROUGHT ASSISTANCE.
================================================================================

  (a) EMERGENCY WATER SUPPLY PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall establish a
      program to provide emergency water supply assistance to rural
      communities experiencing drought-related water shortages, including:
        (1) Temporary water hauling and distribution;
        (2) Emergency well drilling and deepening;
        (3) Emergency interties and pipeline connections to alternative
            water sources;
        (4) Mobile water treatment units.

  (b) LONG-TERM RESILIENCE PLANNING. — The Secretary of Agriculture shall
      provide technical assistance and grants to rural communities for
      development of long-term drought resilience plans addressing:
        (1) Source water diversification;
        (2) Demand management and conservation;
        (3) Infrastructure modernization;
        (4) Cooperative water supply agreements.

  (c) COLONIAS ASSISTANCE. — Not less than 10 percent of funds authorized
      under this section shall be allocated to colonias and other rural
      communities along the United States-Mexico border lacking basic
      water and wastewater infrastructure.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $6,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
