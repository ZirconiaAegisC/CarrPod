================================================================================
              TITLE VIII — TRIBAL WATER RIGHTS AND
              INFRASTRUCTURE
================================================================================

  Authorization: $5,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 801. TRIBAL WATER RIGHTS SETTLEMENT FUND.
================================================================================

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is established in the Treasury of the United
      States a Tribal Water Rights Settlement Fund to provide funding for
      implementation of approved and pending Tribal water rights settlement
      agreements.

  (b) AUTHORIZED SETTLEMENTS. — The Secretary shall prioritize completion
      and funding of:
        (1) Navajo Nation water rights settlement for the San Juan River
            Basin and Little Colorado River Basin;
        (2) Confederated Salish and Kootenai Tribes water compact;
        (3) Hualapai Tribe water rights settlement;
        (4) White Mountain Apache Tribe water rights quantification;
        (5) All other pending settlements for which the Secretary has
            determined negotiation is substantially complete.

  (c) ADDITIONAL NEGOTIATIONS. — The Secretary shall initiate or accelerate
      water rights settlement negotiations with all federally recognized
      Tribes that currently lack quantified and enforceable water rights,
      prioritizing those Tribes:
        (1) Located in basins experiencing severe or extreme drought;
        (2) Whose water sources are threatened by groundwater depletion,
            climate change, or upstream diversions;
        (3) Whose reservation lands lack access to reliable drinking water.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,500,000,000 is authorized for the Fund established
      under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 802. INDIAN RESERVATION WATER INFRASTRUCTURE PROGRAM.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Administrator, in coordination with the Indian
      Health Service, shall establish a dedicated grant program for:
        (1) Construction, rehabilitation, and expansion of drinking water
            treatment and distribution systems on Indian reservations;
        (2) Construction and rehabilitation of wastewater collection and
            treatment facilities;
        (3) Development of new water supply sources including wells,
            springs, and surface water diversions;
        (4) Installation of household water and wastewater service
            connections;
        (5) Water quality testing laboratory equipment and training.

  (b) OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE. — Not less than 20 percent of grants
      awarded under this section shall be available for ongoing operation
      and maintenance costs, recognizing that many Tribal water systems
      lack the ratepayer base to generate sufficient revenue for
      sustainable operations.

  (c) SELF-DETERMINATION. — To the maximum extent practicable, projects
      funded under this section shall be planned, designed, constructed,
      and operated by Tribal entities through self-determination contracts
      and self-governance compacts under the Indian Self-Determination
      and Education Assistance Act (25 U.S.C. 5301 et seq.).

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,500,000,000 is authorized for the program
      established under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 803. TRIBAL CONSULTATION AND CONSENT REQUIREMENTS.
================================================================================

  (a) CONSULTATION POLICY. — All Federal agencies implementing programs under
      this title shall adhere to the Presidential Memorandum on Tribal
      Consultation and Strengthening Nation-to-Nation Relationships of
      January 26, 2021, and shall:
        (1) Engage in meaningful government-to-government consultation
            with affected Tribes prior to undertaking any action that may
            significantly affect Tribal water resources;
        (2) Provide timely, accessible information regarding proposed
            actions affecting Tribal water resources;
        (3) Document and respond to Tribal concerns and recommendations;
        (4) Seek free, prior, and informed consent where actions would
            directly affect Tribal water rights, sacred sites, or cultural
            resources.

  (b) TRIBAL WATER COMMISSION. — The Secretary shall establish a permanent
      Tribal Water Advisory Commission comprising representatives of not
      fewer than 15 federally recognized Tribes from diverse geographic
      regions, to advise on implementation of this title.

================================================================================
SECTION 804. TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE AND CAPACITY BUILDING.
================================================================================

  (a) TRIBAL WATER TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE CENTER. — The Secretary shall
      establish, through a cooperative agreement with a Tribal college
      or university or a consortium thereof, a Tribal Water Technical
      Assistance Center to provide:
        (1) Engineering and planning support for water infrastructure
            projects;
        (2) Training and certification programs for Tribal water system
            operators;
        (3) Assistance with grant writing, financial management, and
            regulatory compliance;
        (4) Hydrologic and water resources assessment services;
        (5) Climate adaptation planning.

  (b) TRIBAL WATER SCHOLARSHIPS. — The Secretary, in coordination with the
      Secretary of Education, shall establish a scholarship program for
      Tribal members pursuing degrees in water resources engineering,
      hydrology, environmental science, and related fields.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 805. EMERGENCY WATER SUPPLY FOR TRIBAL COMMUNITIES.
================================================================================

  (a) EMERGENCY DECLARATION. — The Secretary shall maintain authority to
      declare a Tribal Water Emergency for any Indian reservation where:
        (1) Drinking water supply has been interrupted for more than
            7 consecutive days;
        (2) Water quality testing indicates contamination exceeding
            maximum contaminant levels established under the Safe Drinking
            Water Act;
        (3) Drought conditions classified as D3 (extreme) or D4
            (exceptional) by the United States Drought Monitor persist
            for more than 90 days.

  (b) EMERGENCY ASSISTANCE. — Upon declaration of a Tribal Water Emergency,
      the Secretary shall provide, within 72 hours:
        (1) Emergency drinking water delivery;
        (2) Mobile water treatment units;
        (3) Emergency well drilling and pump repair services;
        (4) Point-of-use water filtration systems.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
