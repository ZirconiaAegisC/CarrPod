================================================================================
              TITLE III — PFAS REMEDIATION AND EMERGING
              CONTAMINANT RESPONSE
================================================================================

  Authorization: $15,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 301. NATIONAL PRIMARY DRINKING WATER REGULATION FOR PFAS.
================================================================================

  (a) RULEMAKING REQUIREMENT. — Not later than 18 months after the date of
      enactment of this Act, the Administrator shall promulgate a national
      primary drinking water regulation for PFAS under section 1412 of the
      Safe Drinking Water Act (42 U.S.C. 300g-1) that establishes:

        (1) Maximum contaminant levels (MCLs) for perfluorooctanoic acid
            (PFOA) and perfluorooctane sulfonic acid (PFOS) at not more
            than 4 parts per trillion each;

        (2) A hazard index approach for mixtures of perfluorohexane
            sulfonic acid (PFHxS), hexafluoropropylene oxide dimer acid
            (HFPO-DA, commonly known as GenX), perfluorononanoic acid
            (PFNA), and perfluorobutane sulfonic acid (PFBS);

        (3) Monitoring requirements for all community water systems serving
            more than 3,300 persons, and a representative sampling program
            for systems serving fewer than 3,300 persons;

        (4) Public notification requirements for any exceedance of MCLs
            established under this subsection.

  (b) ADDITIONAL PFAS. — The Administrator shall, not less frequently than
      every 5 years, evaluate additional PFAS compounds for potential
      regulation and shall establish MCLs for such compounds where the
      Administrator determines that:
        (1) The compound may have an adverse effect on human health;
        (2) The compound is known to occur or is likely to occur in public
            water systems with a frequency and at levels of public health
            concern.

================================================================================
SECTION 302. PFAS DESTRUCTION TECHNOLOGY RESEARCH AND DEPLOYMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) RESEARCH PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall establish a research program
      to evaluate and advance PFAS destruction technologies including:
        (1) Supercritical water oxidation;
        (2) Electrochemical oxidation;
        (3) Plasma-based destruction;
        (4) Sonochemical degradation;
        (5) Photocatalytic degradation;
        (6) Thermal destruction and incineration with emissions monitoring;
        (7) Biodegradation pathways and enzyme engineering.

  (b) DEMONSTRATION GRANTS. — The Administrator shall award competitive grants
      for full-scale demonstration of PFAS destruction technologies at:
        (1) Contaminated groundwater sites;
        (2) Wastewater treatment plants;
        (3) Landfill leachate treatment facilities;
        (4) Industrial discharge points;
        (5) Drinking water treatment plants.

  (c) TECHNOLOGY CERTIFICATION. — Not later than 36 months after enactment,
      the Administrator shall establish a certification and verification
      program to validate the performance of PFAS destruction technologies
      based on:
        (1) Destruction efficiency of greater than 99.99 percent for
            regulated PFAS compounds;
        (2) Absence of toxic byproducts of incomplete destruction;
        (3) Energy efficiency and lifecycle cost analysis;
        (4) Applicability to diverse waste matrices.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for the research and
      demonstration programs established under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 303. CONTAMINATED SITE REMEDIATION FUND.
================================================================================

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Administrator shall establish a PFAS Site
      Remediation Fund to provide grants to States, local governments,
      and water utilities for the investigation, assessment, and remediation
      of PFAS-contaminated sites, including:
        (1) Military installations where aqueous film-forming foam (AFFF)
            was used for firefighting training and operations;
        (2) Airports and fire training facilities;
        (3) Industrial manufacturing facilities producing or using PFAS;
        (4) Biosolids application sites;
        (5) Landfills and leachate management facilities.

  (b) PRIORITY. — The Administrator shall prioritize grants for sites that:
        (1) Are located in or adjacent to disadvantaged communities;
        (2) Present an imminent and substantial endangerment to public health;
        (3) Contaminate or threaten to contaminate drinking water sources
            serving more than 10,000 persons.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $7,000,000,000 is authorized for the Fund established
      under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 304. DRINKING WATER UTILITY PFAS TREATMENT GRANTS.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall award grants to community
      water systems and non-transient non-community water systems for
      capital costs associated with installation of PFAS treatment
      technologies including granular activated carbon, ion exchange,
      reverse osmosis, and nanofiltration systems.

  (b) SMALL SYSTEM SUPPORT. — Not less than 40 percent of amounts made
      available under this section shall be awarded to water systems
      serving fewer than 10,000 persons.

  (c) PRIORITY. — The Administrator shall give priority to applications
      from water systems where PFAS levels in finished drinking water
      exceed the MCLs established under section 301 by a factor of 10 or
      more.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,500,000,000 is authorized for the program
      established under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 305. PFAS SOURCE REDUCTION AND INDUSTRIAL DISCHARGE STANDARDS.
================================================================================

  (a) EFFLUENT LIMITATION GUIDELINES. — Not later than 24 months after
      enactment, the Administrator shall promulgate effluent limitation
      guidelines and pretreatment standards under sections 301, 304, and
      307 of the Federal Water Pollution Control Act (33 U.S.C. 1311,
      1314, and 1317) for PFAS discharges from:
        (1) Organic chemicals, plastics, and synthetic fibers manufacturing;
        (2) Metal finishing and electroplating operations;
        (3) Pulp, paper, and paperboard manufacturing;
        (4) Textile mills and carpet manufacturing;
        (5) Landfill leachate discharges.

  (b) AQUEOUS FILM-FORMING FOAM PHASE-OUT. — Not later than 12 months after
      enactment, the Administrator shall prohibit the manufacture, import,
      processing, and distribution of aqueous film-forming foam containing
      intentionally added PFAS, except where no fluorine-free alternative
      has been certified by the Administrator for the specific firefighting
      application.

================================================================================
SECTION 306. BIOMONITORING AND HEALTH IMPACT STUDIES.
================================================================================

  (a) NATIONAL PFAS BIOMONITORING PROGRAM. — The Director of the Centers for
      Disease Control and Prevention, in coordination with the Administrator,
      shall establish and maintain a national PFAS biomonitoring program to:
        (1) Measure PFAS concentrations in representative samples of the
            United States population, stratified by geography, age,
            occupation, and exposure risk;
        (2) Track temporal trends in PFAS body burden;
        (3) Identify communities with elevated PFAS exposure.

  (b) HEALTH IMPACT RESEARCH. — The National Institutes of Health shall
      expand research on the health effects of PFAS exposure, including:
        (1) Carcinogenicity and cancer epidemiology;
        (2) Immune system effects including vaccine response;
        (3) Developmental and reproductive toxicity;
        (4) Endocrine disruption;
        (5) Metabolic effects including obesity and diabetes;
        (6) Interactions between PFAS mixtures and co-exposures.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,500,000,000 is authorized for programs established
      under this section.
