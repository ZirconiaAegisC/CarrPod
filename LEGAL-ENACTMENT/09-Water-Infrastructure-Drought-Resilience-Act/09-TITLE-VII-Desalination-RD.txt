================================================================================
              TITLE VII — DESALINATION RESEARCH AND
              DEVELOPMENT
================================================================================

  Authorization: Subsumed within administrative allocations

================================================================================
SECTION 701. ADVANCED MEMBRANE AND MATERIALS RESEARCH.
================================================================================

  (a) RESEARCH PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the
      Administrator, shall establish an Advanced Desalination Materials
      Research Program to fund development of:
        (1) Biomimetic and aquaporin-based membranes offering 50 percent
            or greater permeability improvement over current thin-film
            composite reverse osmosis membranes;
        (2) Graphene oxide, carbon nanotube, and other nanostructured
            membranes with enhanced fouling resistance;
        (3) Chlorine-tolerant membrane materials eliminating the need for
            dechlorination prior to reverse osmosis treatment;
        (4) High-selectivity membranes for selective removal of specific
            contaminants including boron, arsenic, and nitrate;
        (5) Membrane distillation and forward osmosis draw solutions with
            reduced energy requirements.

  (b) RESEARCH CENTERS. — The Secretary shall establish at least 5 university-
      based National Desalination Research Centers, each focused on a distinct
      aspect of desalination science and technology:
        (1) Novel membrane materials and fabrication;
        (2) Energy recovery and process intensification;
        (3) Brine management and mineral recovery;
        (4) Renewable energy integration and hybrid systems;
        (5) Environmental monitoring and impact mitigation.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 702. BRACKISH WATER DESALINATION DEMONSTRATION FACILITIES.
================================================================================

  (a) DEMONSTRATION PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall fund the design,
      construction, and operation of not fewer than 10 brackish water
      desalination demonstration facilities distributed across diverse
      hydrogeologic settings, including:
        (1) Deep brackish aquifers in the Ogallala Aquifer region;
        (2) Coastal brackish aquifers in California, Texas, and Florida;
        (3) Brackish surface waters in the Colorado River Basin;
        (4) Agricultural drainage waters in the San Joaquin Valley.

  (b) FACILITY REQUIREMENTS. — Each demonstration facility shall:
        (1) Have a design capacity of at least 5 million gallons per day;
        (2) Achieve a water recovery rate of at least 90 percent;
        (3) Incorporate zero liquid discharge or beneficial brine reuse;
        (4) Use renewable energy for not less than 75 percent of power
            requirements;
        (5) Include a comprehensive environmental monitoring program.

  (c) KNOWLEDGE SHARING. — The Secretary shall establish a publicly
      accessible database documenting the design, construction, operational,
      and economic performance of all demonstration facilities funded under
      this section.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 703. BRINE MANAGEMENT AND ZERO LIQUID DISCHARGE.
================================================================================

  (a) RESEARCH AND DEMONSTRATION. — The Secretary shall fund research,
      development, and demonstration of technologies for brine minimizations
      and beneficial use, including:
        (1) High-efficiency brine concentrators and crystallizers;
        (2) Electrodialysis reversal and membrane capacitive deionization
            for brine volume reduction;
        (3) Selective mineral recovery from brine including lithium,
            magnesium, and rare earth elements;
        (4) Brine-to-hydrogen conversion through electrolysis;
        (5) Salinity gradient energy recovery;
        (6) Beneficial brine use in aquaculture and agriculture.

  (b) OCEAN DISCHARGE MITIGATION. — The Administrator shall, in coordination
      with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, develop
      updated guidance for brine discharge to marine environments based on:
        (1) Best available science on marine ecosystem impacts of elevated
            salinity and brine plume dispersion modeling;
        (2) Diffuser design standards to maximize mixing and minimize
            benthic impacts;
        (3) Site-specific environmental monitoring requirements.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 704. RENEWABLE ENERGY-POWERED DESALINATION.
================================================================================

  (a) INTEGRATION PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with
      the Secretary, shall establish a program to demonstrate the integration
      of desalination facilities with renewable energy sources, including:
        (1) Solar photovoltaic-powered reverse osmosis with battery storage;
        (2) Concentrating solar power with thermal desalination;
        (3) Offshore wind-powered seawater reverse osmosis;
        (4) Geothermal-powered multi-effect distillation.

  (b) LOAD MANAGEMENT. — Each project funded under this section shall
      demonstrate the ability to:
        (1) Operate under variable renewable energy input conditions;
        (2) Manage desalination production as a flexible load for grid
            balancing;
        (3) Integrate energy storage to enable continuous water production.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 705. ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT MITIGATION.
================================================================================

  (a) INTAKE TECHNOLOGY RESEARCH. — The Administrator shall fund research
      on seawater intake technologies that minimize impingement and
      entrainment of marine organisms, including:
        (1) Subsurface slant well and beach gallery intakes;
        (2) Wedge-wire passive screening systems;
        (3) Variable-speed pumping to enable organism avoidance behavior.

  (b) ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING STANDARDS. — The Administrator, in
      coordination with NOAA, shall promulgate standardized environmental
      monitoring protocols for desalination facilities, including:
        (1) Source water quality characterization;
        (2) Brine discharge plume monitoring;
        (3) Entrainment and impingement mortality assessments;
        (4) Benthic community monitoring.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 706. COST REDUCTION AND COMMERCIALIZATION.
================================================================================

  (a) COST TARGETS. — The Secretary of Energy shall establish program targets
      for achieving:
        (1) Seawater desalination at an energy consumption of not more than
            2.0 kilowatt-hours per cubic meter of water produced by 2031;
        (2) Brackish water desalination at an energy consumption of not
            more than 1.0 kilowatt-hours per cubic meter by 2031;
        (3) Total levelized cost of seawater desalination not exceeding
            $1.50 per cubic meter by 2031.

  (b) COMMERCIALIZATION PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall provide grants,
      loan guarantees, and technical assistance to support commercialization
      of innovative desalination technologies that demonstrate:
        (1) A credible pathway to meeting the cost and energy targets
            specified in subsection (a);
        (2) Verified performance at pilot scale of not less than
            100,000 gallons per day;
        (3) Compliance with environmental requirements under this title.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
