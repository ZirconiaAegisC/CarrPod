# TITLE V — PORTS AND WATERWAYS

---

## SUBTITLE A — PORT ELECTRIFICATION AND MODERNIZATION

**§ 501. Findings and Purpose.**

(a) FINDINGS.—The Congress finds that:

(1) United States ports and inland waterways support approximately 30.8
million jobs and generate approximately $5.4 trillion in annual economic
activity, representing approximately 26 percent of Gross Domestic Product
and providing the primary conduit for international trade.

(2) The American Society of Civil Engineers 2025 Infrastructure Report
Card awarded the Nation's port infrastructure a grade of C+, identifying
chronic underinvestment in port maintenance, modernization, and
environmental mitigation.

(3) Marine cargo handling equipment, including ship-to-shore container
cranes, rubber-tired gantry cranes, yard tractors, and forklifts, is
predominantly diesel-powered, contributing substantial emissions of
nitrogen oxides, particulate matter, sulfur oxides, and greenhouse
gases in port-adjacent communities that experience disproportionately
high rates of respiratory illness and cancer.

(4) Steel sheet piling, H-piles, pipe piles, bearing piles, and other
steel structural elements in marine environments are subject to
accelerated corrosion due to chloride exposure in the splash, tidal,
submerged, and atmospheric zones, with unprotected steel in the splash
zone experiencing corrosion rates of 5 to 15 mils per year, requiring
significant structural remediation within 20 to 30 years of installation.

(5) The Harbor Maintenance Trust Fund collects approximately $1.9
billion annually from the Harbor Maintenance Tax on imported cargo, yet
annual disbursements for harbor maintenance dredging have historically
averaged only 65 percent of the full-use level, resulting in a compounded
dredging backlog exceeding $30 billion and channel depth restrictions
that increase shipping costs and reduce port competitiveness.

(b) PURPOSE.—The purpose of this Title is to authorize port electrification
and emissions reduction programs, mandate cathodic protection for maritime
steel structures, reform Harbor Maintenance Trust Fund disbursement
processes, and establish enhanced port security requirements.

**§ 502. Port Electrification Program.**

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Secretary of Transportation, acting through the
Maritime Administrator, shall establish a Port Electrification Grant Program.

(b) ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES.—Grants under this section may be used for:

(1) Electrification of ship-to-shore container cranes, including
conversion from diesel to electric drive and installation of regenerative
braking energy recovery systems;
(2) Electrification of rubber-tired gantry cranes, yard tractors, reach
stackers, top handlers, forklifts, and other cargo handling equipment;
(3) Installation of shore power (cold ironing) infrastructure to supply
electrical power to vessels at berth, including substations, cable
management systems, and vessel connection interfaces meeting IEC/IEEE
80005-1 standards for high-voltage shore connection;
(4) Installation of on-terminal renewable energy generation, including
solar photovoltaic arrays on warehouse and terminal roofs, and battery
energy storage systems for peak shaving and grid stability;
(5) Construction or upgrade of electrical distribution infrastructure
within port terminals sufficient to support the additional electrical
load from electrified cargo handling equipment and shore power systems;
(6) Development of hydrogen fueling infrastructure for fuel-cell-powered
cargo handling equipment and drayage trucks.

(c) FEDERAL COST SHARE.—The Federal share of project costs under this
section shall not exceed:

(1) 80 percent for projects at ports located in nonattainment areas
for the National Ambient Air Quality Standards for ozone or particulate
matter;
(2) 65 percent for projects at all other ports.

(d) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated $12,000,000,000
for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this section.

**§ 503. Cathodic Protection for Maritime Steel Structures.**

(a) MANDATORY CATHODIC PROTECTION.—All new steel sheet piling, H-piles,
pipe piles, bearing piles, and other structural steel elements installed
in marine, estuarine, or coastal environments after the date that is three
years after enactment of this Act shall be protected by a cathodic
protection system designed, installed, and commissioned in accordance
with NACE SP0176-2023 or any successor standard.

(b) EXISTING STRUCTURES.—Steel structural elements in marine environments
installed before the effective date of subsection (a) shall be retrofitted
with cathodic protection systems not later than December 31, 2036, unless
the structure owner demonstrates to the satisfaction of the FIPA
Administrator that:

(1) The structure has a remaining service life of less than 15 years;
(2) Corrosion rate measurements demonstrate that the structure is not
experiencing active corrosion exceeding 2 mils per year; or
(3) Alternative corrosion protection measures provide equivalent
protection.

(c) SYSTEM DESIGN REQUIREMENTS.—Cathodic protection systems shall be
designed to meet the following criteria:

(1) For impressed current cathodic protection (ICCP) systems: a
minimum design life of 25 years for anodes and reference electrodes,
with potential monitoring at intervals not exceeding three months;
(2) For galvanic anode cathodic protection (GACP) systems: anode
mass calculated to provide protection for not less than 20 years at
the design current density for the structure and environment;
(3) Polarized potential of -850 millivolts or more negative relative
to a copper/copper sulfate reference electrode (CSE), or a 100
millivolt polarization shift, maintained throughout the structure;
(4) Remote monitoring capability with automatic alarm notification
when protection potentials fall outside established criteria.

(d) INSPECTION REQUIREMENT.—Cathodic protection systems on maritime
structures shall be inspected by a NACE Cathodic Protection Specialist
(CPS) or equivalent certified professional at intervals not to exceed
12 months. Inspection reports shall be maintained as permanent records
and transmitted to the FIPA Administrator.

(e) GRANTS FOR RETROFIT.—There is authorized to be appropriated
$750,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for
grants to port authorities and other owners of maritime steel structures
for the cost of cathodic protection system design, installation, and
commissioning, with a Federal cost share of 65 percent.

**§ 504. Harbor Maintenance and Channel Dredging Reform.**

(a) FULL-USE DISBURSEMENT.—Notwithstanding any other provision of law,
beginning with fiscal year 2027, the Secretary of the Army, acting through
the Chief of Engineers, shall obligate from the Harbor Maintenance Trust
Fund, for authorized harbor maintenance activities and dredged material
disposal, an amount equal to the full-use level, defined as 100 percent
of the total amount required to maintain the authorized width and depth
of all Federal navigation channels and harbors.

(b) ANNUAL ALLOCATION.—Of the amounts made available from the Harbor
Maintenance Trust Fund each fiscal year:

(1) 13 percent shall be allocated to emerging harbor projects at small
and medium ports with total annual tonnage of less than 5 million tons;
(2) 10 percent shall be reserved for donor ports and energy transfer
ports, defined as ports where annual Harbor Maintenance Tax collections
significantly exceed Federal expenditures on navigation maintenance at
that port;
(3) 5 percent shall be reserved for Great Lakes navigation projects;
(4) The remaining 72 percent shall be allocated among all authorized
harbor maintenance projects on a priority basis reflecting the economic
significance of the waterway to national and regional commerce.

(c) BENEFICIAL USE OF DREDGED MATERIAL.—The Chief of Engineers shall,
to the maximum extent practicable, prioritize beneficial use of dredged
material for:

(1) Beach nourishment and shoreline protection;
(2) Wetland and habitat restoration;
(3) Construction fill and land reclamation;
(4) Levee and flood protection system construction and maintenance.

(d) REPORTING REQUIREMENT.—The Secretary of the Army shall submit to
Congress an annual report describing HMTF receipts, obligations, and the
status of the harbor maintenance dredging backlog, including estimates of
the economic impact of channel depth restrictions on port throughput.

**§ 505. Port Security Enhancement.**

(a) SECURITY ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENT.—Each port authority operating a
facility designated as a strategic seaport by the Department of Defense
or classified as a Tier 1 security asset by the Department of Homeland
Security shall conduct a comprehensive security assessment at intervals
not to exceed three years.

(b) SECURITY GRANT PROGRAM.—The Secretary of Homeland Security, acting
through the Federal Emergency Management Agency, shall administer a Port
Security Enhancement Grant Program, authorized at $1,500,000,000 for the
period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036, for:

(1) Perimeter security systems, including fencing, lighting, intrusion
detection, and video surveillance;
(2) Access control systems, including biometric authentication and
automated license plate recognition;
(3) Waterside security, including underwater detection systems, radar
surveillance, and patrol vessel procurement;
(4) Cybersecurity enhancements for port operating systems, terminal
operating systems, and industrial control systems;
(5) Emergency response equipment and training.

**§ 506. Inland Waterway Infrastructure.**

(a) LOCK AND DAM REHABILITATION.—The Secretary of the Army, acting through
the Chief of Engineers, shall prioritize rehabilitation of locks and dams
on the inland waterway system that have exceeded their 50-year design life.

(b) INLAND WATERWAY TRUST FUND.—There is established in the Treasury an
Inland Waterway Infrastructure Trust Fund, funded through a per-gallon fee
on diesel fuel used in commercial navigation on the inland waterway system,
for the purpose of financing inland waterway construction and major
rehabilitation projects.

(c) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated $6,000,000,000
for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for inland waterway
infrastructure projects.
