# CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION ACT OF 2026
## IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE

---

### § 701. Phased Implementation Structure.

The programs and requirements established under this Act shall be
implemented in three phases, as set forth in this section. The FIPA
Administrator shall publish detailed implementation guidance for each
phase not later than 180 days after enactment.

---

## PHASE I — ASSESSMENT AND STANDARDS ESTABLISHMENT
### (Years 1–2: Fiscal Years 2027–2028)

**1. Institutional Establishment.**

(a) The Federal Infrastructure Protection Authority (FIPA) shall be
established and fully staffed to minimum operational capacity not later
than 180 days after enactment.

(b) The FIPA Administrator shall promulgate interim final regulations
establishing the National Infrastructure Protection Rating (NIPR)
methodology not later than 270 days after enactment.

(c) State Infrastructure Protection Offices (SIPOs) shall be established
in each State not later than 365 days after enactment as a condition of
eligibility for funds under this Act.

(d) The seven-member Board of Directors of the National Infrastructure
Protection Bank shall be nominated by the President not later than 120
days after enactment and confirmed by the Senate not later than 270 days
after enactment.

**2. Condition Assessment Activities.**

(a) Comprehensive condition assessment of all bridges in the National
Bridge Inventory shall be completed not later than 24 months after
enactment, establishing baseline Bridge Health Index scores for all
structures.

(b) Each community water system serving more than 50,000 persons shall
complete and submit its initial Leak Reduction Plan not later than
December 31, 2028.

(c) Lead service line inventories shall be completed and submitted to
the EPA Administrator not later than December 31, 2028.

(d) High-Fire-Threat District mapping shall be completed and published
by the Secretary of Energy not later than June 30, 2028.

(e) Each covered data center shall complete its initial PUE baseline
measurement and submit a compliance plan to the FIPA Administrator not
later than December 31, 2028.

**3. Regulatory and Standards Development.**

(a) The FIPA Administrator shall promulgate final regulations for:

(1) NIPR methodology and rating categories — 270 days after enactment;
(2) Bridge Condition Assessment protocol — 360 days after enactment;
(3) Lifecycle protective coating specifications — 360 days after enactment;
(4) Mandatory inspection intervals for covered infrastructure — 365 days
after enactment;
(5) Professional certification requirements for infrastructure protection
inspectors and assessors — 18 months after enactment.

(b) The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with FERC, NERC, and NARUC,
shall promulgate: (1) model interconnection standards for distributed
energy resources — 18 months after enactment; (2) DCD system technical
requirements — 18 months after enactment; (3) weather sensor network
specifications — 12 months after enactment.

(c) The EPA Administrator shall promulgate: (1) advanced leak detection
technology performance standards — 18 months after enactment; (2) water
recycling and reuse quality standards — 24 months after enactment.

**4. Program Funding Initiation.**

(a) Initial Infrastructure Protection Bond issuance of $50,000,000,000
by June 30, 2027, to capitalize the National Infrastructure Protection
Bank and provide initial funding for Phase I activities.

(b) First-round grant solicitations for:
(1) Bridge Fleet Protection Fund — issued by September 30, 2027;
(2) Grid Resilience Grants — issued by September 30, 2027;
(3) Water Infrastructure Protection Grants — issued by December 31, 2027;
(4) Port Electrification Grants — issued by March 31, 2028.

## PHASE II — PRIORITY PROJECTS AND ACCELERATED DEPLOYMENT
### (Years 3–5: Fiscal Years 2029–2031)

**1. Bridge Infrastructure.**

(a) Lifecycle protective coating application shall commence on the 500
highest-priority steel bridges identified through the Phase I condition
assessment, with priority given to: (1) structures with a BHI below 80;
(2) structures in marine or heavy-deicing environments; (3) structures
carrying average daily traffic exceeding 50,000 vehicles.

(b) All new steel bridge construction initiated after December 31, 2028,
shall be specified with lifecycle protective coating systems meeting the
requirements of section 102(c).

(c) Bridge emergency response plans shall be completed and submitted by
each State not later than December 31, 2029.

**2. Electrical Grid.**

(a) DCD system deployment shall be completed on all distribution feeders
in Tier 1 HFTD areas not later than December 31, 2031.

(b) Covered conductor replacement and fast-acting fault interruption
deployment shall reach 40 percent completion in Tier 1 HFTD areas not
later than December 31, 2031.

(c) Weather sensor network deployment shall achieve the required density
in Tier 1 HFTD areas not later than December 31, 2030.

(d) Initial microgrid projects serving critical facilities in Tier 1
HFTD areas shall achieve commercial operation not later than December
31, 2031.

**3. Water Systems.**

(a) Lead service line replacement shall reach 50 percent completion
nationally not later than December 31, 2029, and 75 percent completion
not later than December 31, 2031.

(b) Advanced leak detection systems shall be deployed in all community
water systems serving more than 100,000 persons not later than December
31, 2031.

(c) Each community water system serving more than 10,000 persons shall
have backup power generation installed or arranged not later than
December 31, 2030.

**4. Data Centers.**

(a) All new covered data centers shall achieve compliance with PUE targets
in effect as of the construction commencement date.

(b) Existing covered data centers shall submit retrofit compliance plans
to the FIPA Administrator not later than December 31, 2029.

**5. Ports and Waterways.**

(a) Cathodic protection system design and procurement shall be completed
for all priority maritime steel structures, defined as structures located
in highly corrosive environments and with remaining service life exceeding
20 years, not later than December 31, 2030.

(b) Harbor Maintenance Trust Fund full-use disbursement shall be achieved
by fiscal year 2029 and maintained thereafter.

(c) Port electrification projects approved in the first two funding rounds
shall reach 40 percent completion not later than December 31, 2031.

## PHASE III — FULL DEPLOYMENT AND LONG-TERM SUSTAINMENT
### (Years 6–10: Fiscal Years 2032–2036)

**1. Bridge Infrastructure.**

(a) Lifecycle protective coating application shall be completed on all
steel bridges in the National Bridge Inventory ranked in the top 50
percent of the corrosion mitigation priority list.

(b) All major rehabilitation of existing steel bridges receiving Federal
funds shall incorporate lifecycle protective coating systems.

(c) Bridge condition assessment intervals of 12 or 24 months, as
applicable, shall be fully established for all bridges.

(d) The three-year rolling average Bridge Health Index of the National
Bridge Inventory shall demonstrate a positive trend, with a target of a
5-point improvement over Phase I baseline.

**2. Electrical Grid.**

(a) Undergrounding of not less than 85 percent of distribution circuit
miles in Tier 1 HFTD areas shall be completed by December 31, 2036.

(b) Covered conductor replacement and fast-acting fault interruption shall
be completed in all Tier 1 and Tier 2 HFTD areas by December 31, 2036.

(c) DCD system deployment shall be completed in all Tier 2 HFTD areas.

(d) Microgrid projects serving critical facilities in all designated
high-risk areas shall achieve commercial operation.

**3. Water Systems.**

(a) Lead service line elimination shall be substantially completed, with
100 percent of public-side and 95 percent of private-side lead service
lines replaced, not later than December 31, 2036.

(b) Community water systems serving more than 50,000 persons shall achieve
their non-revenue water reduction targets.

(c) Water recycling and reuse projects approved in the first four funding
rounds shall achieve commercial operation.

**4. Data Centers.**

(a) All existing covered data centers shall achieve compliance with the
applicable PUE targets established under section 303(b).

(b) Waste heat recovery measures shall be implemented at all new covered
data centers where the feasibility assessment demonstrates a positive
net present value.

**5. Ports and Waterways.**

(a) Cathodic protection retrofits for existing maritime steel structures
shall be substantially completed by December 31, 2036.

(b) Port electrification projects shall achieve 80 percent completion
across all funded projects.

(c) Inland waterway lock and dam rehabilitation projects shall be
substantially completed on structures prioritized in Phase I.

## § 702. Performance Metrics and Reporting.

(a) QUARTERLY PERFORMANCE DASHBOARD.—The FIPA Administrator shall maintain
a publicly accessible, real-time performance dashboard that tracks, for
each program established under this Act:

(1) Total funds appropriated, obligated, and expended;
(2) Number of projects initiated, in progress, and completed;
(3) Miles of distribution lines undergrounded and conductors replaced;
(4) Number of lead service lines replaced;
(5) Bridge Health Index trends for covered bridges;
(6) PUE compliance status for covered data centers;
(7) Harbor maintenance dredging backlog status;
(8) Jobs created and retained, by program and State.

(b) GAO REVIEW.—The Comptroller General of the United States shall conduct
a comprehensive review of the implementation of this Act not later than
December 31, 2031, and shall submit to Congress a report containing
findings and recommendations regarding program effectiveness, fund
management, and any statutory modifications necessary to achieve the
purposes of this Act.

(c) SUNSET REVIEW.—The provisions of this Act authorizing new appropriations
shall terminate on September 30, 2036. Not later than January 31, 2035, the
FIPA Administrator shall submit to Congress a comprehensive report evaluating
program outcomes and recommending whether authorization should be extended,
modified, or allowed to expire.
