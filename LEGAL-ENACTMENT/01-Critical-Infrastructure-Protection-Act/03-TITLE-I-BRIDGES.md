# TITLE I — PROTECTION OF BRIDGE INFRASTRUCTURE

---

## SUBTITLE A — BRIDGE FLEET PROTECTION PROGRAM

**§ 101. Findings and Purpose.**

(a) FINDINGS.—The Congress finds that:

(1) The National Bridge Inventory, maintained by the Federal Highway
Administration, contains approximately 618,000 bridges, of which
approximately 42,000 are classified as structurally deficient and
approximately 220,000 are rated in fair condition with advancing
deterioration that requires intervention to prevent reclassification.

(2) The predominant deterioration mechanism for steel bridge structures
is atmospheric corrosion of structural steel elements, which accounts
for approximately 72 percent of all structurally deficient classifications
for steel bridges.

(3) Lifecycle protective coating systems, specifically thermal spray zinc
(TSZ) duplex systems applied in accordance with SSPC-CS 23.00/AWS C2.23M/NACE
No. 12 and AMPP/NACE SP21412-2023, can extend the service life of steel bridge
structural elements to match or exceed the 100-year design life of the bridge
structure, eliminating the need for repeated recoating cycles that generate
hazardous waste, cause traffic disruption, and consume public funds.

(4) The deployment of lifecycle protective coating systems on all new and
rehabilitated steel bridge construction, combined with a program of condition
assessment at biennial intervals, represents the most cost-effective strategy
for reducing the $13.6 billion annual corrosion cost to bridge infrastructure.

(b) PURPOSE.—The purpose of this Title is:

(1) To establish a comprehensive national program for the protection of
bridge infrastructure through the deployment of lifecycle protective coating
systems, mandatory condition assessment, and prioritized rehabilitation of
bridges at risk of structural deficiency due to corrosion.

(2) To reduce the annual economic cost of bridge corrosion by not less than
50 percent within 20 years of enactment.

(3) To establish a dedicated and sustainable funding mechanism for bridge
protection through the Bridge Fleet Protection Fund.

**§ 102. Bridge Fleet Protection Program.**

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Administrator of the Federal Highway Administration,
in consultation with the Administrator of the Federal Infrastructure
Protection Authority, shall establish and administer a program to be known
as the Bridge Fleet Protection Program (BFPP).

(b) PROGRAM COMPONENTS.—The BFPP shall consist of the following components:

(1) MANDATORY CONDITION ASSESSMENT.—Each bridge in the National Bridge
Inventory shall undergo a comprehensive condition assessment at intervals
not to exceed 24 months, conducted in accordance with the Bridge Condition
Assessment protocol established under section 103.

(2) LIFECYCLE COATING SPECIFICATION.—All new steel bridge construction and
all major rehabilitation of existing steel bridges receiving Federal funds
shall be specified with lifecycle protective coating systems meeting the
requirements of section 102(c).

(3) CORROSION MITIGATION PRIORITIZATION.—The FHWA Administrator shall
develop and maintain a priority ranking of all steel bridges in the National
Bridge Inventory based on corrosion severity, remaining service life as
affected by corrosion, traffic volume, detour length, and strategic
importance, and shall direct BFPP funds to the highest-priority structures.

(4) RESEARCH AND DEMONSTRATION.—The FHWA Administrator shall administer a
program of research and field demonstration projects to validate the
performance of lifecycle protective coating systems under diverse
environmental conditions, in coordination with the National Cooperative
Highway Research Program.

(c) LIFECYCLE COATING REQUIREMENTS.—

(1) THERMAL SPRAY ZINC DUPLEX SYSTEMS.—For all new steel bridge
construction initiated after the date that is three years after the date
of enactment of this Act, and for all major rehabilitation of existing
steel bridges initiated after the date that is five years after enactment,
the specified corrosion protection system shall be a thermal spray zinc
duplex system consisting of:

(A) A metallized zinc or zinc-aluminum alloy coating applied in
accordance with SSPC-CS 23.00/AWS C2.23M/NACE No. 12, with a minimum
thickness of 12 mils (300 microns) for structural steel in
atmospheric exposure categories C3 through C5 as defined in ISO 9223;
(B) A sealer coating applied within 24 hours of metallizing;
(C) An intermediate epoxy or urethane tie coat; and
(D) A polysiloxane or aliphatic polyurethane finish coat providing
ultraviolet resistance and color retention.

(2) ALTERNATIVE SYSTEMS.—The FIPA Administrator may approve alternative
protective coating systems for specific applications upon a showing by
clear and convincing evidence that the alternative system will provide
equivalent corrosion protection for the full design service life of the
bridge structure under the specific environmental exposure conditions
of the installation location.

(3) EXEMPTIONS.—Non-structural architectural steel elements not
affecting load-bearing capacity and bridges with an anticipated remaining
service life of less than 15 years may be exempted from the requirements
of paragraph (1) by determination of the FHWA Division Administrator for
the State in which the bridge is located.

(d) COATING APPLICATION QUALITY ASSURANCE.—

(1) INSPECTOR CERTIFICATION.—All inspection of lifecycle protective
coating application shall be performed by inspectors holding current
AMPP Certified Coating Inspector (CIP) Level 3 certification or
equivalent certification approved by the FIPA Administrator.

(2) HOLD-POINT INSPECTIONS.—The coating application specification shall
require mandatory hold-point inspections at each of the following stages:
surface preparation completion, metallized coating thickness verification,
sealer application, and finish coat application.

(3) DOCUMENTATION.—Complete coating application records, including surface
preparation profiles, ambient condition readings, wet and dry film
thickness measurements, adhesion test results, and inspector sign-offs,
shall be maintained in the bridge permanent record and transmitted to the
FHWA National Bridge Inventory database.

**§ 103. Bridge Condition Assessment Requirements.**

(a) ASSESSMENT INTERVAL.—Each bridge in the National Bridge Inventory shall
undergo a comprehensive Bridge Condition Assessment (BCA) at intervals not
to exceed 24 months. Bridges with a Bridge Health Index below 75 shall
undergo BCA at intervals not to exceed 12 months.

(b) BRIDGE HEALTH INDEX.—The FHWA Administrator, in consultation with the
FIPA Administrator and the American Association of State Highway and
Transportation Officials (AASHTO), shall establish a uniform Bridge Health
Index (BHI) methodology that:

(1) Assigns a numerical rating on a scale of 0 to 100 for each primary
structural element group, including deck, superstructure, and substructure;
(2) Weights each element group according to its structural criticality
and replacement cost;
(3) Computes an aggregate BHI as the weighted average of element group
ratings;
(4) Translates element-level condition states into remaining service life
estimates based on documented deterioration rates; and
(5) Integrates with and updates the existing National Bridge Inventory
condition rating system.

(c) CORROSION-SPECIFIC ASSESSMENT.—The BCA protocol shall include a dedicated
Corrosion Condition Assessment (CCA) component that:

(1) Documents visible corrosion severity on a standardized 0-to-4 scale for
each accessible steel element;
(2) Measures coating thickness at representative locations using calibrated
electromagnetic or eddy-current gauges;
(3) Records coating adhesion using pull-off adhesion testing per ASTM D4541
at locations exhibiting visible distress;
(4) Documents environmental exposure conditions including proximity to
marine environments, deicing chemical application rates, and atmospheric
corrosivity category per ISO 9223; and
(5) Computes a projected coating remaining service life based on measured
thickness and documented deterioration rates for the coating type and
exposure environment.

(d) ASSESSMENT DATA REPORTING.—All BCA and CCA data shall be submitted to
the FHWA in a standardized electronic format within 30 days of the assessment
date and shall be incorporated into the National Bridge Inventory database
and the National Infrastructure Protection Rating registry established under
section 701.

(e) PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE.—A State that fails to complete required BCA
assessments within the prescribed intervals shall be subject to a withholding
of 25 percent of its BFPP allocation for the following fiscal year until
compliance is achieved.

**§ 104. Bridge Fleet Protection Fund.**

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established in the Treasury of the United States
a revolving fund to be known as the Bridge Fleet Protection Fund (Fund).

(b) FUNDING SOURCES.—The Fund shall consist of:

(1) Amounts authorized to be appropriated under section 601;
(2) Transfers from the Highway Trust Fund as authorized under section 104(d);
(3) Proceeds from Infrastructure Protection Bonds issued under section 602;
(4) Repayments of loans made under section 104(e);
(5) Gifts, bequests, and donations from private sources; and
(6) Interest earned on obligations of the Fund.

(c) AUTHORIZED USES.—Amounts in the Fund shall be available, without further
appropriation, for the following purposes:

(1) Grants to States for lifecycle coating application on bridges within
the State's portion of the National Bridge Inventory, in accordance with
the allocation formula established under section 105;
(2) Grants to States for bridge condition assessment activities;
(3) Loans and loan guarantees to States and bridge authorities for bridge
protection projects;
(4) Research and demonstration projects under section 106;
(5) Administrative expenses of the BFPP, not to exceed 2 percent of annual
Fund disbursements;
(6) Emergency bridge protection for bridges damaged by natural disasters,
accidents, or acts of terrorism.

(d) HIGHWAY TRUST FUND TRANSFERS.—There is authorized to be transferred from
the Highway Trust Fund (other than the Mass Transit Account) to the Bridge
Fleet Protection Fund the amount of $8,000,000,000 for each of fiscal years
2027 through 2036.

(e) LOAN PROGRAM.—

(1) The FHWA Administrator may make secured loans and loan guarantees to
States, bridge authorities, and other eligible entities for bridge
protection projects, with interest rates set at 50 percent of the Treasury
rate for obligations of comparable maturity.
(2) The Federal share of a loan made under this subsection shall not
exceed 80 percent of the reasonably anticipated eligible project costs.
(3) The FHWA Administrator shall issue regulations establishing the
eligibility criteria, application procedures, and underwriting standards
for loans under this subsection within 18 months of enactment.

**§ 105. Funding Allocation Formula.**

(a) TOTAL ANNUAL ALLOCATION.—Of the amounts made available for grants under
section 104(c)(1) for each fiscal year:

(1) 40 percent shall be apportioned among the States in proportion to the
total deck area of steel bridges in each State's portion of the National
Bridge Inventory;
(2) 30 percent shall be apportioned among the States in proportion to the
number of bridges in each State classified as structurally deficient due
to corrosion-related deterioration;
(3) 20 percent shall be apportioned among the States in proportion to
vehicle-miles traveled on bridges with a BHI below 85; and
(4) 10 percent shall be available for discretionary allocation by the
FHWA Administrator for high-priority projects, emergency needs, and
projects demonstrating innovative coating technologies.

(b) STATE MATCHING REQUIREMENT.—The Federal share of project costs for
grants made under section 104(c)(1) shall be 80 percent. Matching funds
may include the value of in-kind contributions of labor, materials, and
equipment used for the project.

(c) SMALL-STATE MINIMUM.—Each State shall receive not less than 0.5
percent of the total annual BFPP grant allocation, irrespective of the
formula allocation produced under subsection (a).

(d) TERRITORIAL SET-ASIDE.—Not more than 2 percent of total annual BFPP
grant allocations shall be set aside for bridges located in the territories
and possessions of the United States and on Tribal lands.

**§ 106. Research and Demonstration Projects.**

(a) IN GENERAL.—The FHWA Administrator, in coordination with the NCHRP
and the FIPA Administrator, shall establish a program of research and
field demonstration projects to:

(1) Validate the predicted service life of TSZ duplex coating systems
across the range of environmental exposure conditions present in the
United States, including marine, deicing chemical, industrial, and
arid environments;
(2) Develop and validate accelerated test protocols for predicting
long-term coating performance;
(3) Evaluate emerging coating technologies, including nano-enhanced
primers, self-healing coatings, and smart coatings with embedded
corrosion sensors;
(4) Develop standard specifications for coating application under
adverse environmental conditions, including high humidity, low
temperature, and limited access environments;
(5) Quantify the lifecycle cost differential between TSZ duplex
systems and conventional coating systems through long-term monitoring
of paired demonstration installations;
(6) Evaluate robotic and automated surface preparation and coating
application technologies for bridge structural steel.

(b) DEMONSTRATION SITES.—The FHWA Administrator shall select not fewer
than 20 demonstration bridge sites, distributed across all FHWA regions
and representing diverse environmental exposure conditions. Each
demonstration site shall be instrumented for long-term monitoring of
coating condition, corrosion rate, and environmental exposure parameters.

(c) REPORTING.—The FHWA Administrator shall submit to Congress an annual
report on the progress and findings of the research and demonstration
program, including recommendations for revisions to coating specifications
based on field performance data.

## SUBTITLE B — BRIDGE SECURITY AND RESILIENCE

**§ 111. Bridge Vulnerability Assessments.**

(a) The Secretary of Homeland Security, in coordination with the FHWA
Administrator, shall develop and maintain a methodology for assessing
the vulnerability of bridges to intentional damage, including terrorist
attack, vessel collision, and cyberattack on bridge control systems.

(b) Each bridge authority operating a bridge classified as a Tier 1 or
Tier 2 security asset under the Transportation Security Administration's
surface transportation security methodology shall complete a vulnerability
assessment at intervals not to exceed three years.

**§ 112. Bridge Emergency Response Planning.**

Each State shall develop and maintain a Bridge Emergency Response Plan
that identifies contingency traffic routing, emergency repair contracting
procedures, and public communication protocols for use in the event of
an unplanned bridge closure. Plans shall be updated at intervals not to
exceed five years and shall be coordinated with adjacent States for
bridges carrying interstate traffic.
