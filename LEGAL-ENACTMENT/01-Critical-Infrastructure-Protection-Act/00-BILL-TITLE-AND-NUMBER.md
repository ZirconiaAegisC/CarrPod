# A BILL

**To protect, rehabilitate, and modernize the critical infrastructure of the United
States, including bridges, electrical transmission and distribution systems, data
centers, water supply and treatment systems, ports and waterways, and pipeline
networks; to establish a National Infrastructure Protection Rating system; to
authorize appropriations for infrastructure protection programs; to create the
Federal Infrastructure Protection Authority; and for other purposes.**

*Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of
America in Congress assembled,*

## SECTION 2. FINDINGS AND DECLARATIONS.

The Congress finds and declares the following:

(1) The American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) 2025 Report Card for America's
Infrastructure assigned the nation's infrastructure a cumulative grade of C−,
identifying an aggregate investment gap of approximately $2.6 trillion over the
next decade.

(2) Corrosion of steel bridge components costs the United States economy an
estimated $13.6 billion annually in direct costs, according to the NACE
International Corrosion Cost Study (IMPACT Report, 2023), with indirect costs
from traffic disruption, supply chain delays, and environmental degradation
exceeding an additional $25 billion per year.

(3) The Bay Area Toll Authority (BATA) bridge fleet, comprising seven major
toll bridges in the San Francisco Bay Area, exhibits a weighted average Bridge
Health Index of 85.7 out of 100, with the Richmond–San Rafael Bridge rated at
73.1 and requiring an estimated $1.1 billion in rehabilitation over the next
15 years, per the BATA 2024 Capital Improvement Program.

(4) Deferred maintenance obligations across the California State University
(CSU) system total approximately $8.6 billion, with an additional $9.1 billion
in deferred maintenance across the University of California (UC) system,
representing systemic underinvestment in public infrastructure assets that
jeopardize operational continuity and public safety.

(5) Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E) has incurred wildfire mitigation
costs exceeding $73 billion through its Chapter 11 reorganization and ongoing
system hardening programs, demonstrating the catastrophic financial consequence
of deferred infrastructure protection in the electrical transmission and
distribution sector.

(6) The National Cooperative Highway Research Program Report 398 (NCHRP 398)
documents that bridge coating systems utilizing thermal spray zinc (TSZ)
duplex systems with organic topcoats can extend structural steel service life
by 75 to 100 years with minimal maintenance, representing a transformative
lifecycle cost reduction compared to conventional liquid-applied coating
systems that require full recoating at 15- to 25-year intervals.

(7) The United States water infrastructure system contains approximately 2.2
million miles of underground pipes, of which an estimated 35 percent are beyond
their design service life, resulting in approximately 240,000 water main breaks
annually and non-revenue water losses exceeding $7.6 billion per year.

(8) United States ports and inland waterways support approximately 30.8 million
jobs and generate approximately $5.4 trillion in annual economic activity, yet
harbor maintenance dredging is funded at less than 65 percent of the full-use
level authorized under the Water Resources Reform and Development Act.

(9) Data center electricity consumption in the United States is projected to
reach 35 gigawatts by 2030, representing approximately 8 percent of total
national electricity demand, with cooling infrastructure accounting for up to
40 percent of facility energy consumption, creating an urgent need for
efficiency standards and advanced cooling technologies.

(10) The Federal Government has a constitutional responsibility under Article I,
Section 8 to provide for the common defense and general welfare, which includes
the protection and maintenance of critical infrastructure assets essential to
national security, economic prosperity, and public health and safety.

## SECTION 3. DEFINITIONS.

In this Act:

(1) ADMINISTRATOR.—The term "Administrator" means the Administrator of the
Federal Infrastructure Protection Authority (FIPA), established under section 605.

(2) CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE.—The term "critical infrastructure" means systems
and assets, whether physical or virtual, so vital to the United States that the
incapacity or destruction of such systems and assets would have a debilitating
impact on national security, national economic security, national public health
or safety, or any combination thereof.

(3) BRIDGE HEALTH INDEX.—The term "Bridge Health Index" or "BHI" means the
numerical rating system established under section 103(b) for assessing the
condition of bridge structural elements on a scale of 0 to 100.

(4) LIFECYCLE PROTECTIVE COATING.—The term "lifecycle protective coating" means
a coating system designed and specified to provide corrosion protection for the
full design service life of the structure to which it is applied, including
thermal spray zinc (TSZ) duplex systems, high-ratio inorganic zinc-rich primers,
and other systems certified under section 703.

(5) INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION RATING.—The term "Infrastructure Protection
Rating" or "IPR" means the rating assigned to covered infrastructure assets
under the National Infrastructure Protection Rating system established under
section 701.

(6) POWER USAGE EFFECTIVENESS.—The term "Power Usage Effectiveness" or "PUE"
means the ratio of total facility energy consumption to information technology
equipment energy consumption, as defined by The Green Grid consortium metric.

(7) STATE.—The term "State" means each of the several States, the District of
Columbia, each territory or possession of the United States, and each federally
recognized Indian Tribe.

(8) COVERED ENTITY.—The term "covered entity" means any State agency,
municipality, public utility, port authority, water district, bridge authority,
transit agency, or private owner or operator of critical infrastructure
receiving Federal funds under this Act.
