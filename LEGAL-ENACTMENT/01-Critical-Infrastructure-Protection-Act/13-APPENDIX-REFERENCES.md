# CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION ACT OF 2026
## APPENDIX A — REFERENCES AND SOURCE DOCUMENTS

---

**IMPORTANT NOTE:** The following references are incorporated throughout the
Findings, Declarations, and substantive provisions of this Act. These
documents constitute the evidentiary and technical foundation upon which
the Congress has based its legislative determinations. Inclusion in this
appendix does not constitute formal incorporation by reference of the
entirety of each document into the statutory text; specific provisions
incorporated by reference are identified in the sections where they appear.

---

### I. BRIDGE INFRASTRUCTURE REFERENCES

**ASCE 2025 Report Card for America's Infrastructure.**
American Society of Civil Engineers. Published March 2025.
Available at: https://infrastructurereportcard.org
Comprehensive assessment of 17 infrastructure categories. Assigns
cumulative grade of C−, identifies $2.6 trillion investment gap
over 10 years. Bridges category awarded grade C, with 42,000
structurally deficient bridges identified.

**BATA 2024 Capital Improvement Program (CIP).**
Bay Area Toll Authority, Metropolitan Transportation Commission.
Adopted June 2024. San Francisco, California.
Ten-year capital program document for the seven-bridge BATA toll
fleet. Contains Bridge Health Index scores, condition assessments,
and detailed project cost estimates. Documents Richmond–San Rafael
Bridge BHI of 73.1 and $1.1 billion estimated rehabilitation cost
over 15 years.

**Caltrans 2023 Bridge Inspection Records.**
California Department of Transportation, Division of Engineering
Services, Structure Maintenance and Investigations.
Provides condition ratings for approximately 25,000 bridges on the
State highway system and locally owned bridges.

**NACE International Corrosion Cost Study (IMPACT Report).**
NACE International (now AMPP). 2016, with 2023 update.
Houston, Texas: AMPP.
Comprehensive study quantifying the total annual direct cost of
corrosion in the United States at $452 billion (2.5% of GDP).
Highway bridge corrosion cost estimated at $13.6 billion annually.

**National Bridge Inventory (NBI).**
Federal Highway Administration, United States Department of
Transportation. Updated annually.
Available at: https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/bridge/nbi.cfm
Contains condition data for approximately 618,000 bridges.

**NCHRP Report 398: Durability of Bridge Coatings.**
National Cooperative Highway Research Program.
Transportation Research Board, National Research Council.
Washington, D.C.: National Academy Press, 1998.
Definitive study documenting the 75–100 year service life achievable
with thermal spray zinc duplex coating systems. Established the
lifecycle cost-effectiveness of metallized coating systems for
bridge structural steel.

### II. ELECTRICAL GRID REFERENCES

**Department of Energy 2023 Grid Resilience and Wildfire Mitigation Study.**
United States Department of Energy, Office of Electricity.
Washington, D.C., 2023.
Quantifies the cost of grid hardening measures, analyzes
undergrounding cost-effectiveness, and projects wildfire risk
expansion under climate scenarios.

**Department of Energy 2024 Quadrennial Energy Review.**
United States Department of Energy.
Washington, D.C., 2024.
Documents the age profile of electrical transmission and distribution
infrastructure. Finds 70% of transmission lines and power transformers
are 25 years or older.

**IEEE Standard 2748-2023.**
Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers.
"Standard for Covered Conductor for Overhead Transmission and
Distribution Lines."
Specifies performance requirements for covered conductor in wildfire
mitigation applications.

**NERC Long-Term Reliability Assessment.**
North American Electric Reliability Corporation.
Published annually. Atlanta, Georgia.
Contains assessments of transmission and generation adequacy,
identifies regions at risk of resource shortfall, and recommends
infrastructure investment levels.

**PG&E Chapter 11 Bankruptcy Proceeding.**
In re PG&E Corporation and Pacific Gas and Electric Company,
Case No. 19-30088, United States Bankruptcy Court for the Northern
District of California. Filed January 29, 2019; confirmed June 20,
2020.
Documents wildfire liabilities exceeding $30 billion in allowed
claims, including $13.5 billion to fire victims, $11 billion to
insurance subrogation claimants, and $1 billion to government
entities. Total wildfire mitigation costs and settlements exceed
$73 billion.

**National Interagency Fire Center (NIFC) Wildland Fire Statistics.**
Boise, Idaho. Updated annually.
Available at: https://www.nifc.gov/fire-information/statistics

**ASHRAE Thermal Guidelines for Data Processing Environments, 5th Edition.**
American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning
Engineers. Atlanta, Georgia: ASHRAE, 2021.

humidity ranges for data center equipment.

**ISO/IEC 30134-2:2016.**
International Organization for Standardization.
"Information Technology — Data Centres — Key Performance Indicators —
Part 2: Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)."
Standardized methodology for PUE measurement and calculation.

**Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory 2023 Study: Advanced Cooling
Technologies for Data Centers.**
Berkeley, California: LBNL, 2023.
Quantifies energy savings potential of immersion cooling and other
advanced cooling technologies. Projects 25 TWh annual savings by
2035.

**The Green Grid Consortium.**
"PUE: A Comprehensive Examination of the Metric." White Paper No. 49,
2012. Updated 2019.

**FERC 2024 Data Center Electricity Demand Projections.**
Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, Office of Energy Policy and
Innovation. Washington, D.C., 2024.

**EPA 2023 Drinking Water Infrastructure Needs Survey and Assessment.**
United States Environmental Protection Agency, Office of Water.

**AWWA 2023 "Buried No Longer: Confronting America's Water

**CDC Childhood Lead Poisoning Prevention Program.**

### V. PORTS AND WATERWAYS REFERENCES

**ASCE 2025 Report Card: Ports.**
American Society of Civil Engineers. Assigns grade C+ to United States
port infrastructure. Documents congestion delays and maintenance
dredging backlog.

**Water Resources Reform and Development Act of 2014 (WRRDA).**
Public Law 113-121. Enacted June 10, 2014.
Title II: Harbor Maintenance Trust Fund Reform. Authorized incremental
increases toward full-use HMTF expenditure.

**WRDA 2024.**
Public Law 118-XXX. Water Resources Development Act of 2024.
Continues HMTF reform, modifies cost-sharing for navigation projects.

**USACE Navigation Data Center.**
United States Army Corps of Engineers, Institute for Water Resources.
Alexandria, Virginia.
Available at: https://www.navigationdatacenter.us
Database of waterborne commerce, lock performance, and dredging
statistics.

Establishes atmospheric corrosivity categories C1 through CX.

in marine environments.
