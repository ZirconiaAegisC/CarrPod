# TITLE VI — FUNDING AND FINANCING

---

## SUBTITLE A — AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS

**§ 601. Authorization of Appropriations.**

(a) IN GENERAL.—There is authorized to be appropriated for the period of
fiscal years 2027 through 2036, out of any money in the Treasury not
otherwise appropriated, the aggregate amount of $500,000,000,000 to carry
out the provisions of this Act, allocated as follows:

(1) BRIDGE FLEET PROTECTION FUND (Title I) — $120,000,000,000.

(2) ELECTRICAL GRID RESILIENCE (Title II) — $145,000,000,000.

(3) DATA CENTER INFRASTRUCTURE (Title III) — $18,000,000,000.

(4) WATER SYSTEM INFRASTRUCTURE (Title IV) — $125,500,000,000.

(5) PORTS AND WATERWAYS (Title V) — $20,250,000,000.

(6) FEDERAL INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION AUTHORITY
(administrative operations) — $6,000,000,000.

(7) RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS — $5,250,000,000.

(8) STATE ENFORCEMENT GRANTS (Title IX) — $3,000,000,000.

(9) WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT (Title VIII) — $12,000,000,000.

(10) CONTINGENCY AND EMERGENCY RESPONSE RESERVE — $45,000,000,000.

(b) AVAILABILITY.—Amounts appropriated under this section shall remain
available until expended.

(c) TRANSFER AUTHORITY.—The President may transfer not more than 5 percent
of the amounts appropriated for any title of this Act to any other title
upon a determination that such transfer is necessary to meet unanticipated
priority needs, provided that the President notifies the Committees on
Appropriations of the House of Representatives and the Senate not less
than 30 days before exercising such transfer authority.

(d) SUPPLEMENT NOT SUPPLANT.—Amounts appropriated under this Act shall be
used to supplement and not supplant other Federal, State, and local funds
made available for infrastructure protection and maintenance.

(e) ANNUAL REPORT.—The President shall submit to the Congress, not later
than February 1 of each year, a report describing the allocation,
obligation, and expenditure of funds appropriated under this Act during
the preceding fiscal year.

**§ 602. Infrastructure Protection Bonds.**

(a) ISSUANCE AUTHORITY.—The Secretary of the Treasury is authorized to
issue bonds, to be known as Infrastructure Protection Bonds, in such
amounts as may be necessary to fund the programs authorized under this
Act, subject to the limitation that the aggregate principal amount of
such bonds outstanding at any time shall not exceed $350,000,000,000.

(b) TERMS AND CONDITIONS.—Infrastructure Protection Bonds shall be:

(1) Issued in such denominations and forms as the Secretary may prescribe;
(2) Subject to such terms and conditions as the Secretary may determine;
and
(3) Issued at such times as the Secretary determines to be advantageous
to the Treasury, consistent with debt management policy.

(c) INTEREST.—Infrastructure Protection Bonds shall bear interest at rates
determined by the Secretary, taking into consideration current market
yields on outstanding Treasury obligations of comparable maturity, plus
a reasonable increment to reflect the use of proceeds for revenue-generating
infrastructure investments.

(d) REPAYMENT.—Infrastructure Protection Bonds shall be repaid from:

(1) Federal excise taxes on imported goods and materials, as
authorized under section 602(e);
(2) A portion of revenues from the Harbor Maintenance Tax, dedicated
to bond service under section 504;
(3) Loan repayments received under the National Infrastructure
Protection Bank loan programs;
(4) Cost recovery from responsible parties for infrastructure damage;
(5) General revenues of the United States, to the extent that other
dedicated revenue sources are insufficient.

(e) INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION SURCHARGE.—There is hereby imposed on the
importation of goods and materials into the customs territory of the
United States a surcharge of 0.1 percent ad valorem, the proceeds of
which shall be dedicated to the repayment of Infrastructure Protection
Bonds. This surcharge shall expire when all bonds issued under this
section have been fully retired.

(f) BOND TRUST FUND.—There is established in the Treasury a trust fund
to be known as the Infrastructure Protection Bond Trust Fund, into which
shall be deposited all revenues dedicated to bond service. The Secretary
shall manage the Trust Fund to ensure timely payment of bond principal
and interest.

**§ 603. Public-Private Partnership Framework.**

(a) DEFINITION.—In this section, the term "public-private partnership" or
"P3" means a contractual arrangement between a public-sector entity and a
private-sector entity for the design, construction, financing,
maintenance, or operation of an infrastructure project, under which the
private-sector entity assumes substantial financial, technical, and
operational risk in exchange for a return on investment derived from
project revenues, availability payments, or a combination thereof.

(b) P3 ELIGIBILITY.—Infrastructure projects receiving Federal funding under
this Act shall be eligible for delivery through a public-private partnership
if:

(1) The project has a total capital cost exceeding $100,000,000;
(2) The project sponsor has completed a value-for-money analysis
demonstrating that P3 delivery is likely to achieve lower lifecycle
cost, faster project delivery, more efficient risk allocation, or
superior long-term asset condition compared to conventional public-
sector delivery;
(3) The P3 agreement includes provisions for: (A) lifecycle asset
management through the full term of the agreement; (B) performance-
based payment mechanisms tied to asset condition, availability, and
service quality; (C) handback requirements specifying the minimum
asset condition at the end of the agreement term; and (D) transparent
financial reporting requirements, including annual disclosure of
actual project costs, revenues, and returns.

(c) FEDERAL P3 ASSISTANCE OFFICE.—There is established within the
Department of the Treasury a Federal P3 Assistance Office, which shall:

(1) Provide technical assistance to State and local governments in
structuring, procuring, and managing P3 agreements;
(2) Maintain a publicly accessible database of P3 project documentation,
performance data, and lessons learned;
(3) Develop and publish model P3 contract provisions addressing risk
allocation, dispute resolution, change management, default and
termination, and handback requirements;
(4) Administer a P3 predevelopment grant program providing grants of
up to $5,000,000 per project for feasibility studies, value-for-money
analyses, financial and legal advisory services, and transaction
structuring costs.

(d) BUY AMERICA AND PREVAILING WAGE REQUIREMENTS.—Public-private partnership
agreements involving projects that receive Federal funds under this Act or
that are undertaken on Federal property shall be subject to the Buy America
requirements of section 803 and the prevailing wage requirements of the
Davis-Bacon Act (40 U.S.C. 3141 et seq.).

**§ 604. State Matching Fund Requirements.**

(a) MATCHING FUND OBLIGATION.—Except as otherwise provided in this Act, the
Federal share of project costs under this Act shall be 80 percent for any
project, and the State or local recipient shall provide not less than 20
percent of project costs from non-Federal sources.

(b) REDUCED MATCHING FOR DISADVANTAGED COMMUNITIES.—

(1) For projects serving disadvantaged communities, the Federal share may
be increased to 90 percent.

(2) For projects serving persistent poverty counties, as defined by the
Secretary of Agriculture, the Federal share may be increased to 95 percent.

(3) For projects serving Tribal communities, the Federal share may be
increased to 100 percent.

(c) IN-KIND CONTRIBUTIONS.—The non-Federal matching requirement may be
satisfied through:

(1) Cash contributions from State, local, or private sources;
(2) The value of real property, materials, and equipment contributed
to the project;
(3) The value of labor contributed to the project, valued at the
prevailing wage rate for the locality;
(4) Administrative costs directly attributable to project delivery.

(d) STATE INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION OFFICES.—As a condition of receiving
funds under this Act, each State shall establish a State Infrastructure
Protection Office (SIPO) within the State department of transportation,
public utility commission, or other appropriate agency. The SIPO shall:

(1) Coordinate with the FIPA Administrator on the implementation of
this Act;
(2) Maintain an inventory of all covered infrastructure assets within
the State;
(3) Administer State-level grant and loan programs funded under this Act;
(4) Submit annual reports to the FIPA Administrator on the condition
of covered infrastructure assets and the status of funded projects.

**§ 605. National Infrastructure Protection Bank.**

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—There is established a government corporation to be known
as the National Infrastructure Protection Bank (Bank), which shall be an
independent establishment of the executive branch.

(b) BOARD OF DIRECTORS.—The Bank shall be governed by a Board of Directors
consisting of seven members appointed by the President, by and with the
advice and consent of the Senate, for terms of seven years. Board members
shall have demonstrated expertise in infrastructure finance, engineering,
project delivery, or public administration.

(c) POWERS AND FUNCTIONS.—The Bank shall:

(1) Make direct loans and loan guarantees to eligible entities for
infrastructure protection projects authorized under this Act at
interest rates not exceeding the Treasury rate for obligations of
comparable maturity;

(2) Provide credit enhancement, including letters of credit and
debt service reserve funds, for infrastructure protection bonds
issued by State and local governments;

(3) Purchase or guarantee infrastructure protection bonds in the
secondary market;

(4) Provide technical assistance to project sponsors in structuring
financing packages;

(5) Maintain a revolving loan fund capitalized at $50,000,000,000
from the proceeds of Infrastructure Protection Bonds and appropriations;

(d) ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA.—The Bank shall establish eligibility criteria,
creditworthiness standards, and underwriting procedures for its financial
assistance programs, including:

(1) A minimum project size of $25,000,000 for direct loans;
(2) A requirement that the project has completed environmental review
under the National Environmental Policy Act;
(3) A requirement that the project sponsor demonstrates the financial
capacity to repay the loan; and
(4) A requirement that the project meets or exceeds the applicable National
Infrastructure Protection Rating standards established under section 701.

(e) FEDERAL CREDIT SUBSIDY.—The Bank shall receive an annual appropriation
for the Federal credit subsidy cost of direct loans and loan guarantees,
calculated in accordance with the Federal Credit Reform Act of 1990.

(f) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated $50,000,000,000
for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to capitalize the Bank's
revolving loan fund and to provide for the Federal credit subsidy costs of
Bank financial assistance programs.
