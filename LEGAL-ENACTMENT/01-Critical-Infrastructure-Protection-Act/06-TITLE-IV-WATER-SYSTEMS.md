# TITLE IV — WATER SYSTEM INFRASTRUCTURE

---

## SUBTITLE A — NATIONAL WATER INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION PROGRAM

**§ 401. Findings and Purpose.**

(a) FINDINGS.—The Congress finds that:

(1) The United States water infrastructure system contains approximately
2.2 million miles of underground drinking water distribution pipes and
approximately 1.8 million miles of wastewater collection pipes, of which
an estimated 35 percent are beyond their design service life.

(2) The Environmental Protection Agency's 2023 Drinking Water
Infrastructure Needs Survey and Assessment identified $472.6 billion in
capital improvement needs for drinking water infrastructure over the
20-year period from 2023 through 2043, including $312.4 billion for
transmission and distribution system replacement and rehabilitation.

(3) Approximately 240,000 water main breaks occur annually in the United
States, resulting in the loss of approximately 2.1 trillion gallons of
treated drinking water with an economic value of approximately $7.6
billion per year at average municipal water rates, plus indirect costs
from service disruption, property damage, traffic disruption, and
emergency repair mobilization.

(4) The American Water Works Association's 2023 "Buried No Longer" report
estimates that restoring and expanding existing buried drinking water
infrastructure will require approximately $1 trillion over 25 years,
with the cost of inaction—including emergency repairs, premature
replacement, water loss, and health impacts—exceeding the cost of planned
rehabilitation by a factor of three to four.

(5) The EPA estimates that 9.2 million lead service lines remain in
service across the United States, serving approximately 22 million people,
and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention has determined that
there is no safe level of lead exposure, with neurological and
developmental impacts on children imposing estimated lifetime economic
costs of $50 billion per year.

(6) Advanced leak detection technologies, including acoustic correlators,
satellite-based synthetic aperture radar interferometry, fiber-optic
distributed temperature and acoustic sensing, and in-pipe free-swimming
inspection platforms, can detect leaks at flow rates as low as 0.5 gallons
per minute and locate them within 6 feet of actual position, enabling a
transition from reactive "break-and-repair" asset management to proactive
condition-based maintenance.

(b) PURPOSE.—The purpose of this Title is to authorize a comprehensive
national program for the replacement, rehabilitation, and protection of
water infrastructure, eliminate lead service lines, deploy advanced leak
detection and condition assessment technologies, promote water recycling
and reuse, and establish mandatory resilience standards for water systems.

**§ 402. Water Infrastructure Protection Grants.**

(a) ESTABLISHMENT.—The Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency
shall establish a Water Infrastructure Protection Grant Program.

(b) ELIGIBLE USES.—Grants under this section may be used for:

(1) Replacement, rehabilitation, or structural lining of distribution
mains and transmission mains;
(2) Replacement or rehabilitation of service lines from the water main
to the property line;
(3) Replacement, rehabilitation, or structural lining of wastewater
collection pipes, force mains, and interceptors;
(4) Rehabilitation of water storage tanks, pump stations, treatment plant
structural components, and other above-ground water infrastructure assets;
(5) Deployment of advanced metering infrastructure for leak detection
and pressure management;
(6) Installation of supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA)
systems for real-time monitoring and control of water distribution
and treatment operations;
(7) Development of asset management plans, hydraulic models, and condition
assessment methodologies;
(8) Emergency repairs to restore water service following natural disasters,
system failures, or cybersecurity incidents.

(c) FEDERAL COST SHARE.—

(1) The Federal share of project costs under this section shall be:

(A) 80 percent for disadvantaged communities as defined by the State
based on median household income, unemployment rate, and population
decline criteria;
(B) 65 percent for communities with a population of less than 50,000;
(C) 50 percent for all other eligible entities.

(2) In-kind contributions of labor, materials, equipment, and administrative
costs may be credited toward the non-Federal matching requirement.

(d) SET-ASIDES FOR DISADVANTAGED COMMUNITIES.—Not less than 40 percent of
funds appropriated under this section shall be allocated to projects
serving disadvantaged communities.

(e) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.—There is authorized to be appropriated
$75,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry
out this section.

**§ 403. Lead Service Line Elimination.**

(a) INVENTORY REQUIREMENT.—Not later than December 31, 2028, each community
water system and each nontransient noncommunity water system shall complete
and submit to the EPA Administrator a comprehensive inventory of all lead
service lines, including:

(1) The material composition of the public-side and private-side portions
of each service line;
(2) The geographic coordinates of each service line connection;
(3) The year of original installation, if known;
(4) The results of any tap water sampling for lead conducted at the
service location.

(b) REPLACEMENT MANDATE.—

(1) PUBLIC-SIDE SERVICE LINES.—Not later than December 31, 2031, each
water system shall complete replacement of all lead service lines, or
portions thereof, under the control of the water system.

(2) PRIVATE-SIDE SERVICE LINES.—Not later than December 31, 2036, each
water system shall complete replacement of all lead service lines, or
portions thereof, not under the control of the water system, provided
that the water system has offered replacement at no direct cost to the
property owner.

(3) PARTIAL REPLACEMENT PROHIBITION.—Except in emergency circumstances
presenting an imminent threat to public health or safety, no water system
shall conduct partial replacement of a lead service line without replacing
the entire service line from the water main to the building interior
plumbing connection in a single coordinated operation.

(4) NOTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS.—A water system conducting lead service line
replacement shall provide written notification to the occupants of the
served property not less than 45 days before commencing replacement, shall
provide pitcher filters or point-of-use filtration devices certified for
lead reduction under NSF/ANSI Standard 53 for use during the replacement
period and for not less than six months thereafter, and shall conduct
post-replacement tap water sampling for lead at intervals of 1 day, 30
days, and 6 months after replacement.

(c) FUNDING.—There is authorized to be appropriated $45,000,000,000 for the
period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for lead service line replacement
activities under this section, of which not less than 49 percent shall be
provided as grants and principal forgiveness loans to disadvantaged
communities.

**§ 404. Advanced Leak Detection and Condition Assessment.**

(a) LEAK DETECTION DEPLOYMENT PROGRAM.—The EPA Administrator shall establish
a competitive grant program for the deployment of advanced leak detection
technologies by community water systems.

(b) ELIGIBLE TECHNOLOGIES.—Grants may be used for:

(1) Permanent acoustic leak detection sensor networks;
(2) Satellite-based synthetic aperture radar (SAR) leak detection surveys;
(3) Fiber-optic distributed temperature sensing (DTS) and distributed
acoustic sensing (DAS) systems;
(4) In-pipe inspection platforms, including free-swimming, tethered,
and robotic systems capable of assessing pipe wall condition;
(5) Drone-based thermal imaging and multispectral leak detection;
(6) Advanced pressure management systems incorporating real-time
hydraulic modeling.

(c) LEAK REDUCTION TARGETS.—Each community water system serving more than
50,000 persons shall develop and submit to the EPA Administrator a Leak
Reduction Plan that:

(1) Establishes a baseline non-revenue water percentage;
(2) Sets annual leak reduction targets that together achieve not less than
a 50 percent reduction in non-revenue water within 10 years of plan
submission; and
(3) Documents methods for measuring and verifying leak reduction.

(d) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 for
the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this section.

**§ 405. Water Recycling and Reuse.**

(a) LARGE-SCALE WATER RECYCLING GRANTS.—The Secretary of the Interior, acting
through the Commissioner of Reclamation, shall establish a competitive grant
program for the planning, design, and construction of large-scale water
recycling and reuse projects.

(b) ELIGIBLE PROJECTS.—Grants may be used for:

(1) Advanced wastewater treatment facilities producing recycled water
suitable for: (A) agricultural irrigation; (B) landscape irrigation;
(C) groundwater recharge through direct injection or surface spreading;
(D) industrial process water; (E) environmental restoration flows; and
(F) indirect or direct potable reuse, subject to compliance with all
applicable Federal and State drinking water standards;

(2) Distribution systems for recycled water, including transmission
pipelines, pump stations, storage reservoirs, and turnouts;

(3) Brine management and concentrate disposal systems;

(4) Environmental mitigation measures required for project permitting.

(c) FEDERAL COST SHARE.—The Federal share of project costs shall not
exceed 25 percent of total project costs. The non-Federal share may be
provided in the form of cash contributions or in-kind services, materials,
or property.

(d) WATER-STRESSED REGION PRIORITY.—Not less than 60 percent of funds
appropriated under this section shall be allocated to projects located in
regions classified by the EPA as experiencing high or extremely high water
stress based on the Water Stress Index methodology.

(e) AUTHORIZATION.—There is authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 for
the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this section.

**§ 406. Emergency Water System Resilience.**

(a) RESILIENCE ASSESSMENT REQUIREMENT.—Each community water system serving
more than 3,300 persons shall complete a resilience assessment that
evaluates:

(1) Vulnerability to natural hazards, including earthquakes, floods,
hurricanes, tornadoes, wildfires, landslides, and drought;
(2) Vulnerability to malevolent acts and terrorism;
(3) Vulnerability to cybersecurity threats;
(4) Resilience of electric power supply, including backup generation
capacity and fuel supply arrangements;
(5) Resilience of chemical supply chains for water treatment;
(6) Redundancy of critical system components and availability of
mutual aid arrangements.

(b) EMERGENCY RESPONSE PLAN.—Each community water system required to
complete a resilience assessment under subsection (a) shall prepare or
update an emergency response plan that addresses the findings of the
assessment and identifies specific actions to mitigate identified
vulnerabilities.

(c) EMERGENCY POWER REQUIREMENT.—Each community water system serving more
than 10,000 persons shall install or arrange for on-site backup power
generation sufficient to maintain minimum water treatment and distribution
pressure for not less than 72 hours without external fuel resupply.

(d) GRANTS.—The EPA Administrator shall establish a Water System Resilience
Grant Program, authorized at $2,500,000,000 for the period of fiscal years
2027 through 2036, to assist community water systems with the costs of
resilience assessments, emergency response planning, and implementation of
resilience measures identified through the assessment process.
