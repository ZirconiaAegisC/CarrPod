# TITLE VII — REGULATORY STANDARDS

---

## SUBTITLE A — NATIONAL INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION RATING SYSTEM

**§ 701. Establishment of NIPR System.**

(a) IN GENERAL.—The Administrator of the Federal Infrastructure Protection
Authority shall establish a uniform rating system, to be known as the
National Infrastructure Protection Rating (NIPR) system, for the assessment
and classification of the condition, vulnerability, and protection status
of all covered infrastructure assets in the United States.

(b) NIPR RATING CATEGORIES.—The NIPR system shall assign to each covered
infrastructure asset a numerical rating on a scale of 1 to 5, defined as
follows:

(1) NIPR-1 — OPTIMAL.—The asset is in excellent condition. All protective
systems are fully functional. No deferred maintenance exists. The asset
meets or exceeds all design standards and is projected to achieve its
full design service life without extraordinary maintenance expenditure.

(2) NIPR-2 — ADEQUATE.—The asset is in good condition. Protective systems
are functional with minor deficiencies identified and scheduled for
correction. Deferred maintenance is limited to non-structural elements.
The asset is projected to achieve its design service life with planned
maintenance.

(3) NIPR-3 — ATTENTION REQUIRED.—The asset exhibits significant
deterioration of protective systems or structural elements. Deferred
maintenance has accumulated to a level that, if not addressed within
five years, will result in a reduction in service life, an increase in
lifecycle cost, or both. Intervention is required on a scheduled basis.

(4) NIPR-4 — CRITICAL.—The asset exhibits advanced deterioration.
Protective systems have substantially failed. Structural elements
exhibit active corrosion, cracking, spalling, or section loss. The
asset is at risk of service disruption or loss of functionality within
three years without substantial rehabilitation.

(5) NIPR-5 — IMMINENT FAILURE.—The asset is at risk of catastrophic
failure, complete loss of functionality, or presents an imminent threat
to public safety. Emergency intervention is required.

(c) ASSET CLASSES.—The NIPR system shall be applied to the following classes
of covered infrastructure assets:

(1) Bridges and bridge structural elements;
(2) Electrical transmission towers, poles, and substation structures;
(3) Electrical distribution poles, crossarms, and associated structural
components;
(4) Water distribution mains, transmission mains, and service lines;
(5) Water storage tanks, pump stations, and treatment plant structural
components;
(6) Maritime steel structures, including sheet piling, bearing piles,
docks, piers, and wharves;
(7) Port cargo handling equipment structural and mechanical systems;
(8) Pipeline transmission and distribution infrastructure;
(9) Data center structural, electrical, and cooling infrastructure;
(10) Any other asset class designated by the FIPA Administrator.

(d) RATING METHODOLOGY.—

(1) The NIPR methodology shall incorporate: (A) physical condition data
from inspections and assessments; (B) remaining service life projections
based on documented deterioration rates and environmental exposure
conditions; (C) the consequence of failure, including public safety,
economic, and national security impacts; (D) the vulnerability of the
asset to natural hazards, malevolent acts, and cyber threats; and (E)
the adequacy of existing protective systems and maintenance programs.

(2) The methodology shall weight physical condition at not less than
50 percent of the overall rating, with remaining service life, failure
consequence, vulnerability, and protection adequacy each receiving
weight scores that together constitute the remaining 50 percent.

(3) The methodology shall include quantitative thresholds for each
rating level, designed to minimize inter-rater variability and
ensure consistent application across asset classes and geographic
regions.

(e) RATING PUBLICATION.—The FIPA Administrator shall maintain a publicly
accessible electronic registry of NIPR ratings for all covered
infrastructure assets, updated within 30 days of each assessment.
Individual asset ratings shall be published at a level of detail that does
not disclose vulnerabilities that could be exploited for malevolent
purposes, as determined by the FIPA Administrator in consultation with the
Secretary of Homeland Security.

**§ 702. Mandatory Inspection Intervals.**

(a) GENERAL REQUIREMENT.—Each owner or operator of a covered infrastructure
asset shall conduct a NIPR assessment at the following intervals:

(1) NIPR-1 (Optimal) — Not to exceed 48 months;
(2) NIPR-2 (Adequate) — Not to exceed 36 months;
(3) NIPR-3 (Attention Required) — Not to exceed 18 months;
(4) NIPR-4 (Critical) — Not to exceed 12 months;
(5) NIPR-5 (Imminent Failure) — Continuous monitoring until remediation.

(b) BRIDGE-SPECIFIC INTERVALS.—Notwithstanding subsection (a), bridge
assessments shall be conducted at intervals not to exceed 24 months for
BHI of 75 or above, and not to exceed 12 months for BHI below 75.

(c) POST-EVENT INSPECTIONS.—Following any natural disaster, accident, or
malevolent act that may have affected the condition of a covered
infrastructure asset, the owner or operator shall conduct a NIPR
assessment within 30 days.

(d) INSPECTION PERSONNEL QUALIFICATIONS.—All NIPR assessments shall be
conducted or directly supervised by personnel holding a valid
Infrastructure Protection Assessor (IPA) certification issued under
section 704, or an equivalent State certification approved by the FIPA
Administrator.

(e) ELECTRONIC REPORTING.—NIPR assessment results shall be submitted to
the FIPA Administrator in a standardized electronic format within 30 days
of assessment completion.

**§ 703. Material and Coating Standards.**

(a) ADOPTION OF CONSENSUS STANDARDS.—The following consensus standards are
incorporated by reference into the regulatory requirements of this Act:

(1) SSPC-CS 23.00/AWS C2.23M/NACE No. 12, "Specification for the
Application of Thermal Spray Coatings (Metallizing) of Aluminum, Zinc, and
Their Alloys and Composites for the Corrosion Protection of Steel";

(2) AMPP/NACE SP21412-2023, "Standard Practice for the Application of
Thermal Spray Zinc (TSZ) Duplex Coating Systems for the Corrosion
Protection of Steel Bridges and Other Steel Structures";

(3) ISO 12944, Parts 1 through 9, "Paints and Varnishes — Corrosion
Protection of Steel Structures by Protective Paint Systems";

(4) ISO 9223, "Corrosion of Metals and Alloys — Corrosivity of
Atmospheres — Classification, Determination, and Estimation";

(5) NACE SP0176-2023, "Corrosion Control of Steel Fixed Offshore
Structures Associated with Petroleum Production";

(6) ASTM D4541, "Standard Test Method for Pull-Off Strength of Coatings
Using Portable Adhesion Testers";

(7) ASTM D4417, "Standard Test Methods for Field Measurement of Surface
Profile of Blast Cleaned Steel";

(8) SSPC-PA 2, "Procedure for Determining Conformance to Dry Coating
Thickness Requirements";

(9) NACE SP0187, "Design Considerations for Corrosion Control of
Reinforcing Steel in Concrete";

(10) ASTM A780, "Standard Practice for Repair of Damaged and
Uncoated Areas of Hot-Dip Galvanized Coatings."

(b) ALTERNATIVE STANDARDS.—The FIPA Administrator may approve alternative
material and coating standards upon a determination that such standards
provide protection equivalent to or exceeding the protection provided by
the standards listed in subsection (a).

(c) PERIODIC REVIEW.—The FIPA Administrator shall review and, as
appropriate, update the standards incorporated under this section at
intervals not to exceed five years, to reflect advances in materials
science, corrosion engineering, and protective coating technology.

(d) MATERIAL QUALIFICATION TESTING.—Materials specified for protective
coating systems on covered infrastructure assets shall be qualified
through testing in accordance with the applicable standard and through
pre-construction qualification testing as prescribed by the FIPA
Administrator, including: (1) accelerated corrosion testing per ASTM
B117, ASTM G85, or ISO 12944-6; (2) adhesion testing per ASTM D4541;
(3) weathering resistance per ASTM G154 or ISO 16474; and (4) chemical
resistance testing relevant to the anticipated service environment.

(e) MANUFACTURER CERTIFICATION.—Manufacturers of protective coating materials
used on covered infrastructure assets shall certify that their products meet
the applicable standards and shall maintain quality control documentation
sufficient to demonstrate batch-to-batch consistency. The FIPA Administrator
may require independent third-party testing of coating materials at any time.

**§ 704. Professional Certification Programs.**

(a) INFRASTRUCTURE PROTECTION ASSESSOR (IPA) CERTIFICATION.—

(1) The FIPA Administrator shall establish a professional certification
program for Infrastructure Protection Assessors, which shall include:
(A) a written examination covering the NIPR methodology, asset-specific
deterioration mechanisms, inspection techniques, and reporting
requirements; (B) a practical field examination demonstrating competence
in condition assessment procedures; and (C) continuing education
requirements of not less than 24 hours of approved coursework every
two years.

(2) The FIPA Administrator may recognize equivalent certifications
issued by States, professional societies, or international bodies upon
a determination that the certification requirements are substantially
equivalent to those established under this subsection.

(b) COATING INSPECTOR CERTIFICATION.—The FIPA Administrator shall recognize
the AMPP Certified Coating Inspector (CIP) program at Levels 1, 2, and 3
as meeting the qualification requirements for coating application
inspection under this Act. The FIPA Administrator may recognize equivalent
certification programs upon a determination of substantial equivalence.

(c) CATHODIC PROTECTION CERTIFICATION.—The FIPA Administrator shall
recognize the AMPP Cathodic Protection Specialist (CPS), Cathodic
Protection Technologist (CP3), and Cathodic Protection Technician (CP2)
certifications as meeting the qualification requirements for cathodic
protection system design, installation, and inspection under this Act.

(d) TRANSITION PERIOD.—During the period of 36 months following the
effective date of this section, the FIPA Administrator shall accept,
in lieu of certification under this section, documentation demonstrating
not less than 10 years of professional experience in the relevant
assessment or inspection discipline and completion of a bridge program
recognized by the FIPA Administrator.

**§ 705. Data Standards and Interoperability.**

(a) The FIPA Administrator shall establish data standards for the electronic
submission of all inspection reports, NIPR ratings, and compliance
documentation required under this Act. Data standards shall:

(1) Be machine-readable and interoperable across Federal, State, and
local government information systems;
(2) Include standardized data dictionaries for asset attributes,
condition metrics, and rating classifications;
(3) Include geographic information system (GIS) data standards for
spatial representation of infrastructure assets and conditions;
(4) Comply with the Federal Geographic Data Committee's National
Spatial Data Infrastructure framework standards.

(b) The FIPA Administrator shall develop and maintain application
programming interfaces (APIs) enabling automated submission and retrieval
of NIPR data by authorized State and local agencies, asset owners, and
researchers.
