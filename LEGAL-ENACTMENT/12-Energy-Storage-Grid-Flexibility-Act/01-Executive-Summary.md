## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT (ESGFA) OF 2026

**Prepared for the Committee on Energy and Commerce, Committee on Ways and Means, Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, and the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission**

---

### I. OVERVIEW

The Energy Storage and Grid Flexibility Act of 2026 (ESGFA) establishes the statutory framework to deploy 600 gigawatts of energy storage capacity in the United States by 2035, build a complete domestic supply chain for every major storage technology, integrate storage as a grid asset across transmission and distribution systems, and implement national standards for virtual power plants and grid flexibility markets. The Act authorizes $80 billion in federal investment over ten years and projects $320 billion in total economic activity.

### II. PROBLEM STATEMENT

The United States electric grid faces an unprecedented storage deficit. The Department of Energy's 2024 "Pathways to Commercial Liftoff: Long Duration Energy Storage" report identified that reaching a net-zero grid by 2035 requires 600 to 700 GW of energy storage capacity — compared to approximately 30 GW deployed as of 2026. The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 established a standalone storage investment tax credit under Section 48 of the Internal Revenue Code, which has successfully stimulated rapid deployment of lithium-ion battery storage. However, lithium-ion batteries alone cannot solve the multi-day storage gap: periods of low renewable generation lasting 24 to 100+ hours require long-duration storage technologies — iron-air batteries, flow batteries, zinc hybrid batteries, thermal storage, gravity storage, and pumped hydro — that remain pre-commercial or early-commercial.

Simultaneously, the domestic battery manufacturing supply chain remains heavily dependent on China for lithium processing (65 percent of global capacity), graphite (nearly 100 percent of battery-grade synthetic graphite), and cathode and anode production. The CHIPS Act and IRA manufacturing credits have begun to address this dependency, but additional targeted support for long-duration storage technologies and grid flexibility mechanisms is required to achieve energy sovereignty.

### IV. KEY PROVISIONS

**Title I — Long-Duration Storage Deployment.** Establishes a $15 billion LDES Deployment Program providing direct investment support, offtake agreements, and loan guarantees for iron-air (Form Energy), flow battery (ESS Inc.), zinc hybrid (Eos Energy, e-Zinc), and other 10+ hour storage technologies. Includes $5 billion in federal purchase commitments as anchor buyer to accelerate cost reductions.

**Title II — Short-Duration Battery Manufacturing.** Creates a Domestic Lithium-Ion Gigafactory Program for cell and pack manufacturing, a Solid-State Battery Manufacturing Initiative (QuantumScape, Solid Power), and a zinc-based battery production tax credit. Authorizes $5 billion for battery component supply chain facilities including cathode, anode, separator, and electrolyte production.

**Title III — Thermal Storage for Industry.** Authorizes $8 billion for industrial thermal storage deployment, including thermal battery systems (Antora Energy, Rondo Energy), high-temperature phase-change materials, and molten-salt storage for industrial process heat. Creates a "Heat-as-a-Service" pilot program enabling manufacturers to purchase thermal energy without upfront capital expenditure.

**Title IV — Gravity and Pumped Storage.** Authorizes $8 billion for pumped storage hydropower (Malta/PHES), gravity-based storage (Energy Vault), and underground pumped storage using abandoned mines. Establishes a streamlined federal permitting process for closed-loop pumped storage and a Mine-to-Storage Repurposing Program converting former coal mines into gravity-based storage assets in energy transition communities.

**Title V — V2G and EV Battery Integration.** Creates a V2G Infrastructure Program providing $5 billion for bidirectional charging deployment, smart charging standards, and electric school bus fleet V2G pilots. Establishes a second-life battery utilization framework for EV batteries repurposed as stationary grid storage.

**Title VI — Storage Tax Credit Extension.** Expands Section 48 ITC for standalone storage to maintain the 30 percent rate through 2036. Expands Section 45X advanced manufacturing production credit to cover long-duration storage components including iron-air electrodes, flow battery membranes, and thermal storage media. Creates a new Long-Duration Storage Production Tax Credit of $0.03 per kilowatt-hour discharged.

**Title VII — Storage R&D.** Authorizes $10 billion for next-generation storage research, including solid-state electrolytes, sodium-ion batteries, metal-air batteries, organic flow batteries, and superconducting magnetic energy storage. Establishes an Advanced Battery Materials Innovation Hub and a Grid-Scale Storage Testing and Validation Center at Idaho National Laboratory.

**Title VIII — Grid Flexibility Market Design.** Mandates full FERC Order 2222 implementation across all regional transmission organizations by December 31, 2028. Establishes national standards for distributed energy resource aggregation, time-of-use and real-time pricing, and interconnection queue reform to reduce average interconnection wait times from 4 years to 18 months.

**Title IX — Virtual Power Plant Framework.** Creates a National VPP Deployment Program targeting 80 GW of aggregated distributed energy resources by 2035. Establishes VPP aggregator certification standards, cybersecurity requirements, and a residential storage-plus-solar VPP incentive providing upfront rebates of $500/kWh for behind-the-meter batteries enrolled in utility VPP programs.

**Title X — Transmission-Storage Co-Location.** Authorizes storage-as-transmission pilot projects and transmission congestion relief through strategic storage siting. Creates co-located renewable-plus-storage investment incentives and interconnection cost allocation reform.
