# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 12: TITLE X — TRANSMISSION-STORAGE CO-LOCATION

---

## TITLE X — TRANSMISSION-STORAGE CO-LOCATION

### SECTION 1001. STORAGE-AS-TRANSMISSION PILOT PROGRAM.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Storage-as-Transmission Pilot Program to demonstrate the use of energy storage systems to defer, reduce, or avoid conventional transmission infrastructure investments.

**(b) Pilot Selection.** — The Secretary shall select not fewer than 10 pilot projects representing diverse grid conditions, including:

(1) transmission-constrained load pockets where storage can defer or avoid new transmission line construction;

(2) areas with high renewable energy curtailment where storage can increase effective transmission capacity;

(3) urban areas where storage can defer substation and transformer upgrades at lower cost and with less community disruption than conventional upgrades; and

(4) remote and islanded communities where storage can reduce dependence on expensive and emissions-intensive diesel generation.

**(c) Cost Recovery.** — Participating utilities and transmission owners shall be permitted to recover the capital and operating costs of storage-as-transmission assets through transmission rates, subject to FERC or state public utility commission approval and a requirement that storage assets provide transmission service not fewer than 80 percent of operating hours.

**(d) Performance Reporting.** — Each pilot project shall report detailed operational data, cost data, and a comparison of actual costs to the estimated costs of the transmission upgrade that was deferred or avoided.

**(e) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for the Pilot Program under this section.

### SECTION 1002. TRANSMISSION CONGESTION RELIEF THROUGH STORAGE.

**(a) Congestion Relief Program.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with FERC, shall establish a Transmission Congestion Relief Through Storage Program providing grants for strategically sited energy storage projects that reduce transmission congestion and associated congestion costs.

**(b) Siting Process.** — The Secretary shall:

(1) in coordination with RTOs and ISOs, identify the 50 most congested transmission corridors or nodes in the United States, as measured by annual congestion costs and renewable curtailment hours;

(2) conduct a storage siting analysis for each identified location to determine optimal storage size, technology, and dispatch strategy for congestion relief; and

(3) issue competitive solicitations for storage projects at the identified locations.

**(c) Shared Savings.** — Storage projects funded under this section shall be eligible for congestion revenue rights or financial transmission rights equivalent to the estimated congestion cost savings attributable to the storage project, as determined by the relevant RTO/ISO market monitor.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 1003. CO-LOCATED RENEWABLE-STORAGE INCENTIVES.

**(a) Co-Location Incentive.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Co-Located Renewable-Storage Incentive Program providing:

(1) capital grants covering not more than 10 percent of the cost of energy storage systems co-located and sharing an interconnection point with renewable energy generation facilities;

(2) an additional 5 percent grant bonus for storage systems co-located with renewable generation on previously disturbed lands, including brownfields, landfills, and former mine sites; and

(3) technical assistance for project developers in optimizing co-located renewable-storage project design for shared interconnection, shared operations and maintenance, and maximized combined revenue.

**(b) Shared Interconnection Priority.** — Co-located renewable-storage projects shall be eligible for the fast-track interconnection pathway established under section 804(b)(4), and RTOs/ISOs shall develop shared interconnection study procedures enabling renewable and storage projects to be studied as a combined resource.

**(c) Hybrid Resource Participation.** — FERC shall, not later than 180 days after enactment, ensure that each RTO/ISO tariff provides for participation of hybrid resources — defined as a single resource combining generation and storage at a single point of interconnection — on terms that recognize the operational flexibility and reliability value of such hybrid resources.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 1004. INTERCONNECTION COST ALLOCATION REFORM.

**(a) Purpose.** — Congress finds that the current practice of assigning the full cost of network upgrades to the next interconnecting project ("first-mover pays") creates inequitable cost burdens, disincentivizes storage and renewable development, and is inconsistent with the principle that transmission infrastructure benefits all network users.

**(b) Cost Allocation Principles.** — FERC shall, not later than 180 days after enactment, initiate a rulemaking to reform interconnection cost allocation based on the following principles:

(1) network upgrade costs shall be allocated in proportion to benefits received by all network users, not solely the interconnecting project;

(2) projects that demonstrably reduce overall system costs, including storage projects that reduce peak loading on constrained transmission elements, shall receive interconnection cost credits reflecting such system benefits;

(3) interconnection customers shall not be required to fund network upgrades that are needed due to transmission owner deferred maintenance, aging infrastructure, or long-standing underinvestment; and

(4) interconnection customers shall be provided with transparent, itemized cost estimates and the right to independently verify the necessity and cost reasonableness of assigned network upgrades.

**(c) Storage-Specific Cost Allocation.** — The FERC rulemaking shall specifically address the allocation of network upgrade costs for energy storage projects, recognizing that storage can both charge (increasing load on upstream transmission) and discharge (reducing net load), and that the net impact of storage on transmission loading should determine cost allocation.

### SECTION 1005. NATIONAL TRANSMISSION-STORAGE PLANNING.

**(a) Integrated Planning Process.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with FERC, shall facilitate a National Transmission-Storage Integrated Planning Process that:

(1) identifies the optimal mix of transmission expansion and energy storage deployment to achieve a net-zero electricity grid by 2035 at lowest total system cost;

(2) evaluates all major long-distance transmission corridors and proposes priority corridors where storage co-location would maximize transmission utilization and defer additional transmission investment;

(3) assesses the potential for energy storage to serve as a "transmission non-wires alternative" for each of the National Interest Electric Transmission Corridors designated under section 216(a) of the Federal Power Act; and

(4) publishes a National Transmission-Storage Blueprint updated every three years.

**(b) Interregional Storage Sharing.** — The planning process shall evaluate mechanisms for interregional sharing of energy storage resources, including the potential for storage located in one RTO/ISO region to provide services to adjacent regions through coordinated dispatch and market integration.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $300,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for the planning process and blueprint under this section.
