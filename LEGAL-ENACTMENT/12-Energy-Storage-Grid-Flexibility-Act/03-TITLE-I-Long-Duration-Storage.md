# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 03: TITLE I — LONG-DURATION STORAGE DEPLOYMENT

---

## TITLE I — LONG-DURATION STORAGE DEPLOYMENT

### SECTION 101. LONG-DURATION ENERGY STORAGE DEPLOYMENT PROGRAM.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Long-Duration Energy Storage Deployment Program (LDES Program) to accelerate the commercial deployment of energy storage systems with a rated discharge duration of not fewer than 10 hours.

**(b) Program Components.** — The LDES Program shall include:

(1) DIRECT INVESTMENT SUPPORT. — Competitive grants, loans, and loan guarantees covering not more than 60 percent of capital costs for LDES projects, including:

- iron-air battery manufacturing facilities and utility-scale storage installations;
- flow battery production facilities using iron, zinc-bromine, vanadium, and organic electrolytes;
- zinc hybrid cathode and zinc-air battery manufacturing and deployment;
- pumped heat energy storage (PHES/Carnot battery) facilities; and
- compressed air energy storage in above-ground and underground formations.

(2) FEDERAL OFFTAKE AGREEMENTS. — The Secretary shall enter into long-term power purchase and offtake agreements, acting as anchor buyer, for not fewer than 10,000 megawatt-hours of annual LDES discharge capacity, providing demand certainty to accelerate manufacturing scale-up and cost reduction.

(3) STATE PARTNERSHIP GRANTS. — Matching grants to states establishing LDES procurement mandates, storage portfolio standards, or LDES-specific clean peak standards.

(4) UTILITY INCENTIVE PROGRAM. — Cost-share payments to electric utilities for LDES demonstration and deployment projects, with priority for projects in:

- regions with high renewable energy penetration;
- energy transition communities hosting retired coal or natural gas generating facilities;
- areas identified by NERC as facing resource adequacy risk; and
- Tribal lands and remote communities with high energy costs.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $10,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the LDES Program under this section.

### SECTION 102. LDES COST-REDUCTION PURCHASE COMMITMENTS.

**(a) Long-Duration Storage Cost-Reduction Initiative.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Long-Duration Storage Cost-Reduction Initiative modeled on the successful Department of Defense and NASA launch services purchase commitments that enabled the commercial space industry.

**(b) Purchase Commitments.** — The Secretary shall enter into advance purchase commitments for LDES systems at declining price points, with the following targets:

| Year | Purchase Volume (MWh) | Target Price ($/kWh) |
|------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 2028 | 500 | $150 |
| 2029 | 1,000 | $130 |
| 2030 | 2,000 | $110 |
| 2031 | 5,000 | $90 |
| 2032 | 10,000 | $70 |
| 2033 | 20,000 | $55 |
| 2034 | 50,000 | $50 |
| 2035 | 100,000 | $45 |
| 2036 | 150,000 | $40 |

**(c) Eligible Technologies.** — Purchase commitments shall be awarded across not fewer than three distinct LDES technology categories, with not less than 20 percent of commitment volume reserved for technologies that store energy in non-electrochemical forms, including thermal, mechanical, and gravitational storage.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for advance purchase commitments under this section.

### SECTION 103. IRON-AIR BATTERY MANUFACTURING INITIATIVE.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an Iron-Air Battery Manufacturing Initiative to support domestic production of iron-air electrodes, cells, modules, and balance-of-system components at not fewer than five manufacturing facilities.

**(b) Eligible Support.** — The Initiative shall provide:

(1) construction and equipment grants covering up to 50 percent of capital costs for iron-air battery manufacturing facilities;

(2) loan guarantees for up to 80 percent of project debt for qualified iron-air manufacturing projects; and

(3) production incentive payments of $10 per kilowatt-hour of rated storage capacity for domestically manufactured iron-air battery systems through December 31, 2036.

**(c) Domestic Content Requirement.** — To qualify for support under this section, not less than 70 percent of components by value in the iron-air battery system shall be mined, produced, or manufactured in the United States.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Initiative under this section.

### SECTION 104. FLOW BATTERY PRODUCTION SCALE-UP.

**(a) Flow Battery Manufacturing Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Flow Battery Manufacturing Program providing:

(1) grants for construction and expansion of flow battery production facilities for iron flow (ESS Inc.), zinc-bromine (Redflow), vanadium redox, and organic flow battery technologies;

(2) production tax credits of $12 per kilowatt-hour of rated storage capacity for domestically manufactured flow battery systems;

(3) grants for domestic production of flow battery electrolyte chemicals, membranes, bipolar plates, and pumps; and

(4) technical assistance to manufacturers in achieving scale production and automated assembly.

**(b) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 105. LDES DEMONSTRATION PROJECTS.

**(a) Demonstration Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall fund not fewer than 20 LDES demonstration projects representing diverse technologies, applications, and geographies, including:

(1) iron-air battery utility-scale storage at retired coal plant sites, demonstrating multi-day discharge capability for resource adequacy during extended low-renewable periods;

(2) flow battery systems providing behind-the-meter storage for industrial facilities with high energy demand and critical process loads;

(3) zinc hybrid and zinc-air battery community-scale storage for grid resilience in wildfire-prone and hurricane-prone regions;

(4) thermal battery (carbon block) industrial heat storage demonstrating displacement of natural gas-fired process heat with renewably charged thermal storage; and

(5) pumped heat energy storage (Carnot battery) co-located with wind and solar generation in regions with high renewable curtailment.

**(b) Reporting.** — Each demonstration project shall submit detailed operational data, cost data, and lessons learned to the Department of Energy for public dissemination.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for demonstration projects under this section.
