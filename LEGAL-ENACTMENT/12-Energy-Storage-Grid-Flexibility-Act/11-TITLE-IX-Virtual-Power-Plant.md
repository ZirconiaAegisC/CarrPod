# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 11: TITLE IX — VIRTUAL POWER PLANT FRAMEWORK

---

## TITLE IX — VIRTUAL POWER PLANT FRAMEWORK

### SECTION 901. NATIONAL VPP DEPLOYMENT PROGRAM.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a National Virtual Power Plant (VPP) Deployment Program targeting the aggregation of 80 gigawatts of distributed energy resources, including behind-the-meter batteries, rooftop solar, smart thermostats, electric water heaters, and controllable EV chargers, into VPPs operating in wholesale and retail electricity markets by December 31, 2035.

**(b) Program Components.** —

(1) VPP PILOT GRANTS. — Competitive grants to utilities, aggregators, and technology providers for VPP pilot projects in not fewer than 25 states, with each pilot aggregating not fewer than 10 MW of DERs.

(2) VPP READINESS ASSESSMENTS. — Technical assistance and matching funding for states and large municipal utilities to conduct VPP readiness assessments evaluating DER penetration, market rules, communication infrastructure, and utility operational readiness.

(3) VPP INTERCONNECTION ACCELERATION. — Grants to distribution utilities for upgrading distribution automation, advanced metering infrastructure, and distribution management systems to support real-time VPP dispatch and coordination.

(4) VPP PERFORMANCE INCENTIVES. — Performance-based payments to VPP operators achieving specified levels of peak load reduction, renewable integration, and grid reliability improvement.

**(c) Priority Communities.** — Not fewer than 30 percent of VPP program resources shall be deployed in:

(1) energy transition communities;

(2) communities with annual energy burden exceeding 6 percent of median household income;

(3) communities identified by the Council on Environmental Quality as disadvantaged under the Climate and Economic Justice Screening Tool; and

(4) Tribal lands.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 902. VPP AGGREGATOR CERTIFICATION AND STANDARDS.

**(a) Certification Program.** — FERC shall establish a VPP Aggregator Certification Program requiring aggregators to demonstrate:

(1) technical capability to communicate with, dispatch, and verify the response of individual DERs within an aggregation;

(2) financial responsibility, including bonding or parent guarantee requirements proportional to the aggregator's market participation volume;

(3) cybersecurity measures meeting NIST Cybersecurity Framework standards;

(4) data privacy practices compliant with the VPP Data Privacy Standard established under section 905; and

(5) customer enrollment practices including plain-language disclosure of participation terms, compensation, and the right to opt out.

**(b) Aggregator Registry.** — The Secretary of Energy shall maintain a public registry of certified VPP aggregators, including their service territories, DER portfolios, and historical performance metrics.

**(c) Small Aggregator Streamlined Certification.** — FERC shall establish a streamlined certification pathway for small aggregators with portfolios of fewer than 5,000 DERs, with reduced financial responsibility requirements and simplified application procedures.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $100,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2029 for certification program development and administration.

### SECTION 903. RESIDENTIAL STORAGE AND SOLAR-VPP INCENTIVE.

**(a) Residential VPP Storage Rebate.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Residential VPP Storage Rebate Program providing:

(1) an upfront rebate of $500 per kilowatt-hour of usable storage capacity, up to a maximum of $7,500 per household, for behind-the-meter battery storage systems enrolled in a certified VPP program;

(2) an additional rebate of $250 per kilowatt-hour, up to $3,750, for battery storage systems paired with rooftop solar photovoltaic systems with capacity of not less than 4 kilowatts; and

(3) an additional $1,000 rebate for low-income households, defined as households with income at or below 80 percent of area median income.

**(b) VPP Enrollment Requirement.** — To qualify for the rebate, the battery system must:

(1) be enrolled in a certified VPP program for a minimum of five years;

(2) grant the VPP aggregator authority to dispatch the battery during not more than 60 grid events per calendar year, with each event not exceeding 4 hours;

(3) reserve not less than 20 percent of battery capacity for customer backup power at all times; and

(4) be enrolled in a retail electricity rate with time-varying pricing or receive compensation for grid services at rates approved by the state public utility commission.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Rebate Program under this section.

### SECTION 904. UTILITY VPP PROCUREMENT MANDATE.

**(a) Mandate.** — Not later than December 31, 2030, each electric utility serving more than 200,000 customers shall:

(1) conduct a VPP potential assessment identifying the technical and economic potential for VPP deployment within its service territory;

(2) file for approval with its state public utility commission a VPP procurement plan that:

- establishes targets for VPP capacity procurement, with an initial target of not less than 3 percent of system peak demand by 2030, increasing to not less than 10 percent by 2035;
- provides for competitive procurement of VPP services from certified aggregators;
- ensures equitable access to VPP participation for low-income customers, renters, and customers in multifamily housing; and
- includes an implementation schedule with annual milestones; and

(3) annually report on VPP deployment progress to the state commission and the Department of Energy.

**(b) State Commission Authority.** — State public utility commissions shall have authority to approve, modify, or reject utility VPP procurement plans. The Secretary of Energy shall provide technical assistance and matching grants to state commissions for VPP plan review and oversight.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for state commission capacity building under this section.

### SECTION 905. VPP CYBERSECURITY AND DATA PRIVACY.

**(a) Cybersecurity Standards.** — The Secretary of Energy, through the Office of Cybersecurity, Energy Security, and Emergency Response (CESER), shall develop and publish mandatory cybersecurity standards for VPP systems, including:

(1) secure communication protocols between DERs, aggregators, distribution utilities, and market operators;

(2) authentication and access control requirements including multi-factor authentication for administrative access;

(3) intrusion detection and security event monitoring requirements;

(4) incident response and reporting requirements, including mandatory notification of the Electricity Information Sharing and Analysis Center (E-ISAC) within 24 hours of detection of a cybersecurity incident; and

(5) supply chain security requirements for VPP hardware and software, including requirements for software bills of materials (SBOMs) and vulnerability disclosure programs.

**(b) Data Privacy Standard.** — The Secretary shall develop and publish, in coordination with the Federal Trade Commission, a VPP Data Privacy Standard that:

(1) limits the collection of customer energy usage data to the minimum necessary for VPP operation;

(2) requires affirmative opt-in consent from customers before energy usage data is shared with third parties;

(3) prohibits the sale of VPP customer data for purposes unrelated to grid services; and

(4) requires annual privacy audits by an independent third party for VPP aggregators with more than 10,000 enrolled customers.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for standards development, testing, and enforcement under this section.
