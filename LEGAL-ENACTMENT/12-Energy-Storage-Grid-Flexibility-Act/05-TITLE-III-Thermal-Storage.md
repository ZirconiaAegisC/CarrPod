# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 05: TITLE III — THERMAL STORAGE FOR INDUSTRY

## TITLE III — THERMAL STORAGE FOR INDUSTRY

### SECTION 301. INDUSTRIAL THERMAL STORAGE DEPLOYMENT PROGRAM.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an Industrial Thermal Storage Deployment Program to displace fossil fuel combustion for industrial process heat through deployment of thermal battery systems, high-temperature heat storage, and electrified thermal processes.

**(b) Program Components.** —

(1) THERMAL BATTERY GRANTS. — Competitive grants covering not more than 50 percent of capital costs for installation of thermal battery systems at industrial facilities, including:

- carbon block resistive heating systems (such as those developed by Antora Energy) capable of delivering heat at temperatures exceeding 1,000 degrees Celsius for cement kilns, steel reheat furnaces, glass melting, and chemical processing;
- refractory brick-based thermal storage systems (such as those developed by Rondo Energy) for industrial steam and hot air production; and
- molten salt and liquid metal thermal storage for high-temperature continuous process applications.

(2) FOSSIL FUEL BOILER REPLACEMENT. — Grants and loan guarantees for replacement of natural gas, coal, and oil-fired industrial boilers with electrified thermal storage systems, with priority for facilities in environmental justice communities.

(3) THERMAL ENERGY NETWORKS. — Grants for design and construction of thermal energy networks connecting multiple industrial facilities to shared thermal storage resources, district heating and cooling systems, and industrial heat recovery infrastructure.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 302. THERMAL BATTERY MANUFACTURING CREDIT.

**(a) Credit Established.** — Section 45X of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding thermal battery components to the list of eligible components in subsection (b), with the following credit amounts:

(1) SOLID THERMAL STORAGE MEDIA. — $15 per kilowatt-hour-equivalent of thermal storage capacity for carbon blocks, refractory bricks, ceramics, and other solid thermal storage media produced in the United States.

(2) THERMAL BATTERY MODULES. — $20 per kilowatt-hour-equivalent of thermal storage capacity for integrated thermal battery modules produced in the United States.

(3) ELECTRIC THERMAL HEATERS. — $5 per kilowatt of rated heating capacity for electric resistance heaters, electrode boilers, and thermal fluid heaters designed for thermal battery integration.

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendment made by this section shall apply to components produced after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 303. INDUSTRIAL HEAT-AS-A-SERVICE PILOT PROGRAM.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an Industrial Heat-as-a-Service (HaaS) Pilot Program under which thermal storage system providers may offer industrial heat to manufacturing facilities under power purchase agreement or heat services agreement structures, eliminating the upfront capital expenditure barrier to adoption.

**(b) Program Structure.** —

(1) The Secretary shall provide partial loan guarantees covering up to 80 percent of the capital cost of thermal storage assets owned by HaaS providers.

(2) The Secretary shall establish a thermal heat price index providing transparent benchmark pricing for renewably sourced industrial heat.

(3) The Secretary shall develop standardized HaaS contract templates to reduce transaction costs and accelerate adoption.

**(c) Pilot Scale.** — The HaaS Pilot shall support not fewer than 50 industrial facilities across not fewer than 10 states by December 31, 2030.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for the Pilot Program under this section.

### SECTION 304. THERMAL STORAGE FOR DISTRICT ENERGY SYSTEMS.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a District Energy Thermal Storage Program providing grants for integration of large-scale thermal storage into district heating and cooling systems serving universities, medical centers, downtown business districts, military installations, and municipal complexes.

**(b) Eligible Projects.** — District thermal storage projects may include:

(1) chilled water thermal energy storage tanks providing peak cooling load shifting for district cooling systems;

(2) hot water and steam thermal storage integrated with electric boilers and heat pumps for district heating;

(3) seasonal borehole thermal energy storage (BTES) and aquifer thermal energy storage (ATES) for long-duration seasonal heat storage; and

(4) ice storage systems for commercial building peak load management.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 305. HIGH-TEMPERATURE THERMAL STORAGE RESEARCH.

**(a) Research Program.** — The Secretary of Energy, through the Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy (ARPA-E) and the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy, shall fund high-temperature thermal storage research addressing:

(1) phase-change materials with melting points between 500 and 1,500 degrees Celsius, including molten salts, liquid metals, and metal alloys;

(2) thermochemical energy storage using reversible chemical reactions with energy densities exceeding 500 kJ/kg;

(3) ultra-high-temperature materials for thermal battery containment, including ceramics, refractory metals, and composite insulation;

(4) thermophotovoltaic and thermionic energy conversion for direct electricity production from stored thermal energy; and

(5) advanced heat exchangers for thermal battery charge and discharge with effectiveness exceeding 90 percent.

**(b) Research Centers.** — The Secretary shall establish not fewer than three High-Temperature Thermal Storage Research Centers at DOE national laboratories or universities.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for research under this section.
