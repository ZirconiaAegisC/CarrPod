# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 06: TITLE IV — GRAVITY AND PUMPED STORAGE

---

## TITLE IV — GRAVITY AND PUMPED STORAGE

### SECTION 401. PUMPED STORAGE HYDROPOWER STREAMLINING.

**(a) Permitting Reform.** — The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, in coordination with the Department of Energy, the Army Corps of Engineers, the Environmental Protection Agency, and the Department of the Interior, shall establish a streamlined permitting process for closed-loop pumped storage hydropower projects that:

(1) are not located on existing natural water bodies and do not involve new dams on free-flowing river reaches;

(2) use water recycled between upper and lower reservoirs with minimal consumptive water use; and

(3) are located on previously disturbed lands, including former mine sites, brownfield sites, or lands adjacent to existing energy infrastructure.

**(b) Permitting Timeline.** — The streamlined process shall include:

(1) a single lead federal agency responsible for coordinating all federal permits, licenses, and authorizations;

(2) a target timeline of not more than two years from application to final decision on all federal permits;

(3) concurrent rather than sequential environmental review under the National Environmental Policy Act (42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.) by all participating agencies; and

(4) a preliminary permit process of not more than 180 days.

**(c) Pre-Approved Zones.** — The Secretary of Energy shall identify and designate not fewer than 100 pre-approved pumped storage zones on federal, state, and private lands where:

(1) topographic conditions are suitable for closed-loop pumped storage;

(2) environmental impacts are expected to be minimal based on desktop and GIS analysis;

(3) transmission interconnection is available or planned within 10 miles; and

(4) community support has been documented through local government resolutions.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for permitting reform, pre-approved zone designation, and agency capacity building under this section.

### SECTION 402. GRAVITY-BASED STORAGE DEPLOYMENT FUND.

**(a) Fund Established.** — There is established in the Treasury the Gravity-Based Storage Deployment Fund, to be administered by the Secretary of Energy.

**(b) Eligible Technologies.** — The Fund shall provide grants, loans, and loan guarantees for:

(1) MULTI-ARMED CRANE GRAVITY STORAGE. — Systems lifting and lowering composite or concrete blocks to store and release gravitational potential energy, such as those developed by Energy Vault Holdings, Inc.

(2) RAIL-BASED GRAVITY STORAGE. — Systems using electric locomotives to move weighted rail cars up and down inclined tracks, such as those developed by ARES (Advanced Rail Energy Storage).

(3) MINE SHAFT GRAVITY STORAGE. — Systems using existing mine shafts, either vertical or inclined, to raise and lower weights for energy storage.

(4) UNDERWATER GRAVITY STORAGE. — Systems using buoyant vessels or spheres that are submerged and released to store and recover energy in offshore or deep lake environments.

**(c) Block Material Sourcing.** — Gravity storage systems receiving Fund support shall prioritize block materials made from:

(1) coal combustion residuals (coal ash) from impoundments identified for closure;

(2) mine tailings and waste rock;

(3) decommissioned wind turbine blades and other composite waste streams;

(4) construction and demolition debris; and

(5) locally sourced soil and aggregate.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Fund under this section.

### SECTION 403. UNDERGROUND PUMPED STORAGE EXPLORATION.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy, through the U.S. Geological Survey and the National Renewable Energy Laboratory, shall conduct a national assessment of underground pumped storage hydropower potential, including:

(1) utilization of abandoned underground mines as lower reservoirs;

(2) utilization of deep aquifer systems as lower reservoirs;

(3) construction of purpose-built underground caverns as lower reservoirs; and

(4) assessment of groundwater impacts, subsidence risk, and long-term geotechnical stability.

**(b) Pilot Projects.** — The Secretary shall fund not fewer than three underground pumped storage pilot projects at sites representing diverse geologic conditions, including:

(1) an abandoned hard-rock mine in a metal mining district;

(2) an abandoned coal mine in an energy transition community; and

(3) a purpose-built underground cavern in a sedimentary basin.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 404. MINE-TO-STORAGE REPURPOSING PROGRAM.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Secretary of the Interior and the Secretary of Labor, shall establish a Mine-to-Storage Repurposing Program to convert retired coal, metal, and mineral mine sites into:

(1) pumped storage hydropower facilities using former open-pit mines as reservoirs;

(2) gravity-based storage systems using former mine infrastructure and materials;

(3) compressed air energy storage in former underground mine workings; and

(4) solar-plus-storage facilities on reclaimed mine lands.

**(b) Community Benefits.** — Projects receiving support under this section shall:

(1) enter into community benefits agreements with affected local governments and Tribal nations;

(2) prioritize hiring of former mine workers and residents of the affected community;

(3) make payments in lieu of taxes not less than the property tax revenue generated by the mine site at peak operations; and

(4) contribute not less than 0.5 percent of annual gross revenue to a community trust fund for economic diversification.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 405. WATER RIGHTS AND ENVIRONMENTAL COMPLIANCE FOR PHES.

**(a) Water Rights Facilitation.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Secretary of the Interior and state water resource agencies, shall:

(1) develop model water rights agreements for closed-loop pumped storage projects;

(2) provide technical assistance to project developers in navigating state water rights processes; and

(3) establish a PHES Water Rights Mediation Program providing neutral third-party facilitation of water rights conflicts.

**(b) Environmental Performance Standards.** — Closed-loop pumped storage projects receiving federal support shall meet the following minimum standards:

(1) water consumption not exceeding 5 percent of reservoir volume per year from evaporation and seepage;

(2) reservoir liners or natural geologic containment adequate to prevent groundwater contamination;

(3) fish and wildlife mitigation plans approved by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service; and

(4) minimum bypass flows or other measures sufficient to protect downstream aquatic ecosystems if any natural water body is used for initial fill or periodic makeup water.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for programs under this section.
