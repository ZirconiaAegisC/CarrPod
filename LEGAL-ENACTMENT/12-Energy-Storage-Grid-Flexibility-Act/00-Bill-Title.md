## A BILL

To accelerate the deployment of long-duration and short-duration energy storage technologies, to establish manufacturing and supply chain programs for domestic battery production, to authorize thermal storage for industrial applications, gravity-based and pumped hydro storage expansion, vehicle-to-grid and electric vehicle battery integration, energy storage tax credit extensions and expansions, next-generation storage research and development, grid flexibility market design standards, a virtual power plant framework, and transmission-storage co-location programs, and for other purposes.

### SECTION 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSES.

**(a) Findings.** — Congress finds the findings set forth in the Findings Document of this Act to be accurate and material to the purposes of this legislation.

**(b) Purposes.** — The purposes of this Act are to —

(1) deploy not less than 600 gigawatts of energy storage capacity within the United States by calendar year 2035;

(2) establish a complete domestic supply chain for lithium-ion, solid-state, flow, zinc-based, iron-air, thermal, and gravity-based storage technologies, from raw materials through manufacturing to recycling;

(3) integrate energy storage as a transmission and distribution grid asset to defer or avoid not less than $15 billion in transmission infrastructure costs;

(4) accelerate the implementation of Federal Energy Regulatory Commission Order 2222 and establish national standards for distributed energy resource aggregation, virtual power plants, and grid flexibility markets;

(5) reduce the installed cost of long-duration energy storage to not more than $50 per kilowatt-hour by 2035;

(6) create not fewer than 200,000 domestic jobs in energy storage manufacturing, installation, operations, and recycling; and

(7) ensure that the benefits of energy storage deployment flow to all communities, including energy transition communities, low-income households, and Tribal nations.

### SECTION 3. DEFINITIONS.

In this Act:

**(1) ENERGY STORAGE SYSTEM.** — The term "energy storage system" means equipment or facilities capable of absorbing electrical, thermal, mechanical, or chemical energy, storing such energy for a period of time, and delivering the stored energy as electricity, thermal energy, or mechanical work, including batteries, flow batteries, pumped storage hydropower, compressed air energy storage, gravity-based storage, thermal storage, flywheels, and hydrogen-based storage when used for electricity grid services.

**(2) LONG-DURATION ENERGY STORAGE (LDES).** — The term "long-duration energy storage" means an energy storage system with a rated discharge duration of not fewer than 10 hours at rated power output.

**(3) VIRTUAL POWER PLANT (VPP).** — The term "virtual power plant" means a cloud-based aggregation of distributed energy resources, including rooftop solar, behind-the-meter batteries, electric vehicles, smart thermostats, and controllable loads, that are coordinated to provide grid services equivalent to a conventional power plant.

**(4) STANDALONE ENERGY STORAGE.** — The term "standalone energy storage" means an energy storage system that is not an integral part of a qualified facility for which a production tax credit or investment tax credit is claimed under any other provision of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

**(5) GRID FLEXIBILITY.** — The term "grid flexibility" means the capability of the electric grid to maintain reliable service under changing conditions by modulating supply, demand, and storage resources across temporal and spatial scales.
