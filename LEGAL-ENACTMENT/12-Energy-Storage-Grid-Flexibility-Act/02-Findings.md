# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 02: CONGRESSIONAL FINDINGS

---

## CONGRESSIONAL FINDINGS

### PURSUANT TO THE ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026

---

**The Congress finds the following:**

### PART A — GRID STORAGE DEFICIT AND CLIMATE IMPERATIVE

**Finding 1.** The Department of Energy, in its 2024 "Pathways to Commercial Liftoff: Long Duration Energy Storage" report, found that achieving a net-zero electricity grid by 2035 requires between 600 and 700 gigawatts of installed energy storage capacity. As of the date of enactment, the United States has approximately 30 gigawatts of installed storage capacity, representing a deployment gap exceeding 570 gigawatts.

**Finding 2.** The National Renewable Energy Laboratory's 2024 "Storage Futures Study" concluded that in scenarios achieving 90 percent or greater renewable electricity penetration, seasonal and multi-day energy storage capacity of 100 to 600 hours becomes essential to maintain grid reliability during extended periods of low renewable generation.

**Finding 3.** The North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC), in its 2025 Long-Term Reliability Assessment, identified energy storage adequacy as a "high-risk" dimension of grid reliability, noting that the retirement of dispatchable thermal generation without corresponding deployment of multi-day storage resources threatens resource adequacy across every regional transmission organization.

### PART B — LONG-DURATION STORAGE TECHNOLOGY READINESS

**Finding 4.** Form Energy, Inc., based in Somerville, Massachusetts, has developed an iron-air battery capable of discharging at rated power for 100 hours at a projected cost of less than $20 per kilowatt-hour at scale. Form Energy's first commercial manufacturing facility in Weirton, West Virginia, commenced production in 2024, with initial utility contracts with Great River Energy, Georgia Power, and Xcel Energy totaling over 5 gigawatt-hours of capacity. The iron-air battery represents a United States-invented technology with global export potential.

**Finding 5.** ESS Inc., based in Wilsonville, Oregon, has developed an iron flow battery using abundant iron, salt, and water electrolytes, with a rated discharge duration of 6 to 12 hours and an operational life exceeding 25 years without capacity degradation. ESS Inc. has deployed systems for San Diego Gas & Electric, Portland General Electric, and the Sacramento Municipal Utility District.

**Finding 6.** Eos Energy Enterprises has developed a zinc hybrid cathode battery with a discharge duration of 3 to 12 hours, manufactured at its facility in Turtle Creek, Pennsylvania. Eos batteries use Earth-abundant zinc and operate at ambient temperature without thermal runaway risk, making them suitable for urban and distributed deployment.

**Finding 7.** e-Zinc, based in Toronto, Canada, has developed a zinc-air long-duration energy storage system capable of 24 to 72 hours of discharge, with zinc particles serving as the storage medium in a reversible electrochemical cycle.

**Finding 8.** Redflow Limited, based in Brisbane, Australia, has developed a zinc-bromine flow battery with a rated discharge duration of 6 to 12 hours, tolerant of 100 percent depth of discharge and high ambient temperatures, suitable for remote, industrial, and grid-scale applications.

### PART C — THERMAL STORAGE TECHNOLOGY

**Finding 9.** Antora Energy, based in Sunnyvale, California, has developed a thermal battery using solid carbon blocks heated to temperatures exceeding 1,500 degrees Celsius by renewable electricity, capable of storing thermal energy for days and discharging on demand as industrial process heat or, via thermophotovoltaic cells, as electricity, at costs competitive with fossil fuel-based industrial heat.

**Finding 10.** Rondo Energy has deployed a thermal battery at Calgren Renewable Fuels in Pixley, California, using electric resistance heating of refractory brick to store heat at temperatures up to 1,500 degrees Celsius, with discharge as industrial steam or hot air for cement, chemical, and food processing applications.

**Finding 11.** Malta Inc. has developed a pumped heat energy storage (PHES) system, also known as a "Carnot battery," using a reversible heat pump and heat engine to store electricity as thermal energy in molten salt and chilled antifreeze, with a rated discharge duration of 10 to 200 hours and projected round-trip efficiency of 55 to 65 percent.

### PART D — GRAVITY AND PUMPED STORAGE

**Finding 12.** Energy Vault Holdings, Inc. has developed a gravity-based energy storage system using a multi-armed crane to lift and lower 30-metric-ton composite blocks made from locally sourced materials including coal ash, mine tailings, and decommissioned wind turbine blades. Energy Vault's first commercial system in Rudong, China, is connected to the grid and rated at 25 MW/100 MWh. Projects in the United States, including in Texas and California, are in development.

**Finding 13.** The United States currently has approximately 22 gigawatts of pumped storage hydropower capacity, with an additional 50+ gigawatts of identified closed-loop pumped storage potential according to the National Renewable Energy Laboratory. The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission has issued preliminary permits for more than 60 GW of proposed pumped storage projects, yet no new pumped storage facility has been completed in the United States since 1995, due primarily to permitting timelines exceeding 10 years.

### PART E — DOMESTIC BATTERY SUPPLY CHAIN VULNERABILITY

**Finding 14.** The Department of Energy's 2024 "America's Strategy to Secure the Supply Chain for a Robust Clean Energy Transition" identified that China controls approximately 65 percent of global lithium processing capacity, nearly 100 percent of battery-grade synthetic graphite production, 78 percent of cathode production, and 71 percent of anode production. The concentration of battery supply chains in a single country represents a critical national security vulnerability.

**Finding 15.** The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 Section 45X advanced manufacturing production credit and Section 48C qualifying advanced energy project credit have stimulated announcements of more than 50 new battery manufacturing facilities in the United States. However, the DOE Liftoff report found that announced manufacturing capacity for long-duration storage technologies — flow batteries, metal-air batteries, and thermal storage systems — remains less than 5 percent of the projected 2035 demand.

### PART F — GRID FLEXIBILITY AND MARKET DESIGN

**Finding 16.** FERC Order 2222, issued September 17, 2020, directed regional transmission organizations and independent system operators to remove barriers to participation of distributed energy resource aggregations in wholesale electricity markets. As of the date of enactment, full compliance with Order 2222 remains incomplete in several RTOs/ISOs, with distributed resources unable to participate on equal terms with conventional generation.

**Finding 17.** The Brattle Group, in its 2024 study "The Real Value of Virtual Power Plants," estimated that a national VPP deployment of 80 GW of aggregated distributed energy resources could save consumers $15-35 billion in avoided grid infrastructure costs over a ten-year period, while providing equivalent resource adequacy to 80 GW of peaking generation capacity.

**Finding 18.** Interconnection queues across the United States contain over 2,000 GW of proposed generation and storage projects, with average interconnection study wait times exceeding 4 years in multiple RTOs, according to Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory's 2025 interconnection queue analysis. Energy storage projects are disproportionately affected by interconnection delays, as storage revenue models require timely market access.

### PART G — V2G AND DISTRIBUTED STORAGE

**Finding 19.** The United States electric vehicle fleet is projected to exceed 40 million vehicles by 2035, with a collective battery capacity exceeding 3,000 GWh. The Electric Power Research Institute estimates that managed charging and vehicle-to-grid (V2G) integration could provide 500 to 750 GW of flexible demand and supply capacity, equivalent to the entire current United States generating fleet.

**Finding 20.** School bus electrification under the EPA Clean School Bus Program, funded at $5 billion through the IIJA, provides a unique opportunity for V2G deployment: school buses operate on predictable schedules, are parked during peak solar generation hours in summer months, and serve communities that experience disproportionate air quality burdens from diesel emissions.

### PART H — ECONOMIC AND EMPLOYMENT IMPACT

**Finding 21.** The energy storage industry employed approximately 90,000 workers in 2024, according to the DOE USEER report, with employment growing at 12 percent annually. The American Clean Power Association projects that achieving 600 GW of storage by 2035 will require a storage workforce of not fewer than 350,000 workers.

**Finding 22.** The global market for energy storage technologies is projected to exceed $500 billion annually by 2035, according to Bloomberg New Energy Finance, with the market for long-duration storage alone projected at $150 to $250 billion. United States early-mover advantage in iron-air, flow battery, thermal storage, and gravity storage technologies positions domestic firms to capture a leading share of global export markets.
