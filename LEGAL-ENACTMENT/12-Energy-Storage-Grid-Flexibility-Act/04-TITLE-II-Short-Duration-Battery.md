# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 04: TITLE II — SHORT-DURATION BATTERY MANUFACTURING

---

## TITLE II — SHORT-DURATION BATTERY MANUFACTURING

### SECTION 201. DOMESTIC LITHIUM-ION GIGAFACTORY PROGRAM.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Domestic Lithium-Ion Gigafactory Program to accelerate the construction and expansion of lithium-ion battery cell, module, and pack manufacturing facilities in the United States.

**(b) Program Components.** —

(1) GIGAFACTORY CONSTRUCTION GRANTS. — Grants covering up to 30 percent of capital costs for new lithium-ion battery manufacturing facilities with an annual production capacity of not less than 5 GWh.

(2) PRODUCTION INCENTIVE PAYMENTS. — Production incentive payments of $5 per kilowatt-hour of rated storage capacity for lithium-ion battery cells manufactured in the United States, provided that the cells contain not less than 60 percent domestically sourced critical minerals by value, increasing to 80 percent by 2030.

(3) EQUIPMENT MODERNIZATION LOANS. — Low-interest loans for modernization of existing battery manufacturing facilities to increase automation, improve energy efficiency, and reduce manufacturing defect rates.

(4) CRITICAL MINERALS PROCESSING PARTNERSHIPS. — Cost-share grants for co-location of lithium hydroxide, nickel sulfate, cobalt sulfate, and manganese sulfate processing facilities with gigafactory sites.

**(c) Priority Facilities.** — Priority for support shall be given to:

(1) gigafactories located in energy transition communities;

(2) facilities that commit to registered apprenticeship programs for construction and operations workforce; and

(3) facilities that achieve zero-liquid-discharge water management and minimum 50 percent recycled water use.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Gigafactory Program under this section.

### SECTION 202. SOLID-STATE BATTERY MANUFACTURING INITIATIVE.

**(a) Initiative Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Solid-State Battery Manufacturing Initiative to accelerate the transition from laboratory-scale solid-state battery production to commercial-scale manufacturing.

**(b) Components.** —

(1) PILOT MANUFACTURING FACILITIES. — Grants for not fewer than three solid-state battery pilot manufacturing facilities, with capacity of not less than 100 MWh annually per facility, producing cells using sulfide, oxide, or polymer solid electrolytes.

(2) MANUFACTURING PROCESS DEVELOPMENT. — Research and development grants for solid-state battery manufacturing processes, including dry electrode coating, solid electrolyte thin-film deposition, and lithium metal anode handling.

(3) SOLID-STATE BATTERY TESTING AND VALIDATION. — Establishment of a Solid-State Battery Testing Center at a Department of Energy national laboratory providing independent performance, safety, and durability testing for solid-state battery cells and modules.

(4) WORKFORCE TRAINING. — Partnership with Clean Manufacturing Centers of Excellence to develop solid-state battery manufacturing technician training curricula and certificate programs.

**(c) Company Partnerships.** — The Initiative shall engage with United States solid-state battery developers including QuantumScape Corporation, Solid Power, Inc., Factorial Energy, and SES AI Corporation, as well as automotive original equipment manufacturers and battery cell manufacturers investing in solid-state technology.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Initiative under this section.

### SECTION 203. ZINC-BASED BATTERY PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT.

**(a) Credit Established.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

**"SEC. 45DD. ZINC-BASED BATTERY PRODUCTION CREDIT.**

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the zinc-based battery production credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to $15 per kilowatt-hour of rated capacity of qualified zinc-based battery cells produced by the taxpayer during such taxable year.

"(b) Qualified Zinc-Based Battery Cell. — The term 'qualified zinc-based battery cell' means a battery cell that:

"(1) uses zinc as the primary active material in the anode or cathode;

"(2) is produced in the United States or a possession of the United States;

"(3) has a rated cycle life of not fewer than 3,000 cycles to 80 percent depth of discharge; and

"(4) is designed and manufactured for stationary energy storage applications.

"(c) Domestic Content Requirement. — The credit amount under subsection (a) shall be reduced by 50 percent if less than 60 percent of the components of the qualified zinc-based battery cell (by value) are mined, produced, or manufactured in the United States.

"(d) Sunset. — This section shall not apply to cells produced after December 31, 2036."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendment made by this section shall apply to cells produced after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 204. BATTERY COMPONENT SUPPLY CHAIN RESILIENCE.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Battery Component Supply Chain Resilience Program to onshore the production of battery components for which the United States is currently dependent on imports.

**(b) Targeted Components.** — The Program shall provide grants, loans, and loan guarantees for domestic production of:

(1) CATHODE ACTIVE MATERIALS. — Lithium iron phosphate (LFP), lithium nickel manganese cobalt oxide (NMC), and lithium manganese iron phosphate (LMFP) cathode powder production facilities;

(2) ANODE ACTIVE MATERIALS. — Synthetic and natural graphite anode powder production facilities, and silicon anode material production;

(3) SEPARATORS. — Polyolefin and ceramic-coated separator film production;

(4) ELECTROLYTES. — Lithium hexafluorophosphate (LiPF6), lithium bis(fluorosulfonyl)imide (LiFSI), and advanced electrolyte solvent and additive production;

(5) CURRENT COLLECTORS. — Ultra-thin copper and aluminum foil production for battery electrodes;

(6) BATTERY BINDERS. — Polyvinylidene fluoride (PVDF) and aqueous binder production; and

(7) CAN AND PACKAGING. — Cylindrical, prismatic, and pouch cell can, lid, and terminal production.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $4,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 205. BATTERY WORKFORCE TRAINING FOR MANUFACTURING.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish a Battery Manufacturing Workforce Training Program in alignment with Title I and Title II of the Workforce Development and Green Jobs Act of 2026.

**(b) Program Components.** —

(1) training grants to community and technical colleges for battery manufacturing technician programs, including electrode coating, cell assembly, formation and aging, and quality control;

(2) registered apprenticeship standards for battery manufacturing occupations, including cell assembly technician, formation technician, quality control inspector, and battery testing technician;

(3) employer-provided on-the-job training reimbursement of 50 percent of wages for the first six months of employment for new battery manufacturing workers; and

(4) development of virtual reality and simulation-based training modules for battery manufacturing cleanroom and dry-room environments.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.
