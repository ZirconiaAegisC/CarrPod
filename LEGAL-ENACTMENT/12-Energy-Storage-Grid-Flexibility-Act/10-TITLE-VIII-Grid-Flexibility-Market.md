# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 10: TITLE VIII — GRID FLEXIBILITY MARKET DESIGN

---

## TITLE VIII — GRID FLEXIBILITY MARKET DESIGN

### SECTION 801. FERC ORDER 2222 IMPLEMENTATION ACCELERATION.

**(a) Congressional Finding.** — Congress finds that full and timely implementation of FERC Order 2222, issued September 17, 2020 (175 FERC 61,061), is essential to enable distributed energy resource aggregations to participate in wholesale electricity markets on equal terms with conventional generation resources, and that delays in implementation are impeding the deployment of energy storage and grid flexibility resources.

**(b) Implementation Mandate.** —

(1) Not later than December 31, 2027, each regional transmission organization (RTO) and independent system operator (ISO) shall have filed with FERC, and FERC shall have approved, tariff provisions fully complying with Order 2222.

(2) Not later than June 30, 2028, each RTO/ISO shall have operational systems, market platforms, and metering and telemetry requirements enabling distributed energy resource aggregations to register, bid, schedule, settle, and be measured and verified on equal terms with other market participants.

(3) If any RTO/ISO fails to meet the deadlines in paragraphs (1) or (2), FERC shall convene a technical conference within 60 days, issue a show cause order, and, if necessary, impose penalty assessments of not more than $50,000 per day until compliance is achieved.

**(c) Minimum Participation Threshold.** — FERC shall establish a maximum aggregation size threshold of not more than 100 kilowatts for distributed energy resource aggregations to participate in RTO/ISO markets, ensuring that residential and small commercial storage and solar-plus-storage systems are eligible for aggregation.

### SECTION 802. DISTRIBUTED ENERGY RESOURCE AGGREGATION STANDARDS.

**(a) National DER Aggregation Standards.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with FERC and the National Institute of Standards and Technology, shall develop and publish national standards for distributed energy resource aggregation covering:

(1) registration and qualification of DER aggregators;

(2) communication protocols between DERs, aggregators, distribution utilities, and RTO/ISO market platforms, including IEEE 2030.5 (Smart Energy Profile 2.0) and OpenADR 2.0;

(3) metering, telemetry, and measurement and verification requirements for aggregated DERs providing wholesale market services;

(4) distribution utility coordination, ensuring that DER dispatch for wholesale market services does not violate distribution system operating limits; and

(5) dispute resolution procedures between aggregators, distribution utilities, and RTOs/ISOs.

**(b) Aggregator Certification.** — No entity shall participate as a DER aggregator in RTO/ISO markets unless certified by FERC as meeting the national DER aggregation standards. FERC shall establish a streamlined certification process with a target timeline of not more than 90 days from application.

**(c) State Coordination.** — The Secretary of Energy shall provide technical assistance and matching grants to state public utility commissions to align state DER interconnection, net metering, and distributed storage policies with the national DER aggregation standards.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 803. TIME-OF-USE AND REAL-TIME PRICING MANDATES.

**(a) Dynamic Pricing Mandate.** — Not later than December 31, 2029, each electric utility serving more than 500,000 customers shall offer to all residential and small commercial customers the option of:

(1) a time-of-use rate with at least three distinct price periods (off-peak, mid-peak, on-peak);

(2) a real-time pricing rate reflecting day-ahead or real-time wholesale electricity market prices; and

(3) a critical peak pricing rate providing discounts during non-peak periods in exchange for higher prices during a limited number of critical peak events per year.

**(b) Customer Protections.** — Utilities offering dynamic pricing rates shall:

(1) provide clear, plain-language disclosure of rate structures, including historical price data enabling customers to estimate bills under each rate option;

(2) offer an opt-out provision enabling customers to return to standard flat-rate service within one billing cycle;

(3) ensure that low-income customers enrolled in bill assistance programs are held harmless, with total annual bills under dynamic rates not exceeding projected bills under the standard flat rate;

(4) provide automated notifications through text, email, or app alert not fewer than 24 hours in advance of critical peak pricing events; and

(5) make available, at no cost to the customer, interval usage data enabling customers to shift consumption.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $300,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for state public utility commission implementation support, customer education, and low-income bill protection under this section.

### SECTION 804. INTERCONNECTION QUEUE REFORM.

**(a) Purpose.** — Congress finds that interconnection queue backlogs, with over 2,000 GW of projects awaiting study and average wait times exceeding four years, represent a critical barrier to deployment of energy storage and renewable generation, and that fundamental reform of interconnection procedures is essential to meeting the 2035 clean electricity targets.

**(b) FERC Rulemaking.** — Not later than 180 days after enactment, FERC shall initiate a rulemaking to implement:

(1) a "connect and manage" interconnection approach that permits storage and renewable generation projects to interconnect subject to curtailment during grid congestion, rather than requiring network upgrades as a precondition to interconnection;

(2) cluster study reforms reducing the average interconnection study timeline to not more than 18 months from interconnection request to interconnection agreement;

(3) commercial readiness criteria, including site control and financial commitment milestones, to remove speculative projects from interconnection queues; and

(4) a fast-track interconnection pathway for projects of 20 MW or less that can be accommodated on existing distribution or sub-transmission infrastructure without network upgrades.

**(c) Storage-Specific Interconnection.** — The FERC rulemaking shall include storage-specific interconnection provisions:

(1) recognition of energy storage as a generation resource with a defined maximum charge rate and maximum discharge rate;

(2) modeling of storage dispatch behavior in interconnection studies based on expected market participation rather than worst-case assumptions; and

(3) allowance for storage projects to interconnect as either generation-only, load-only, or combined generation-and-load resources.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2029 for FERC and RTO/ISO interconnection process reform implementation.

### SECTION 805. REGIONAL MARKET INTEGRATION.

**(a) Inter-Regional Transmission Planning.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with FERC, shall facilitate inter-regional transmission planning processes that:

(1) identify and quantify the value of transmission capacity between adjacent RTO/ISO regions for energy storage resource sharing, renewable energy balancing, and resilience;

(2) develop cost allocation methodologies for inter-regional transmission projects; and

(3) establish minimum inter-regional transfer capability targets of not less than 15 percent of coincident peak demand between each pair of adjacent RTO/ISO regions.

**(b) Energy Imbalance Market Expansion.** — The Secretary shall provide technical assistance and matching grants to utilities and balancing authorities outside the Western Energy Imbalance Market and the Southeast Energy Exchange Market to join or establish organized real-time energy imbalance markets that enable efficient integration of variable renewable generation and energy storage.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for programs under this section.
