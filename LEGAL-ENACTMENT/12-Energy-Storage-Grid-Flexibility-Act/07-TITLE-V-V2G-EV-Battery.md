# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 07: TITLE V — V2G AND EV BATTERY INTEGRATION

---

## TITLE V — VEHICLE-TO-GRID AND EV BATTERY INTEGRATION

### SECTION 501. VEHICLE-TO-GRID INFRASTRUCTURE PROGRAM.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Secretary of Transportation, shall establish a Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) Infrastructure Program to accelerate deployment of bidirectional charging infrastructure enabling electric vehicles to discharge electricity to the grid, buildings, and homes.

**(b) Program Components.** —

(1) BIDIRECTIONAL CHARGER REBATES. — Rebates covering 50 percent of the cost of bidirectional chargers, up to $2,500 per residential charger and $15,000 per commercial or fleet charger, for chargers certified under the V2G interoperability standard developed under section 503.

(2) UTILITY V2G INTEGRATION GRANTS. — Matching grants to electric utilities for V2G pilot programs, including:

- dynamic pricing signals incentivizing EV owners to discharge during peak demand periods;
- automated V2G dispatch platforms integrated with utility distribution management systems;
- V2G-enabled vehicle-to-building backup power for critical facilities; and
- V2G aggregation for wholesale market participation under FERC Order 2222.

(3) FLEET V2G DEPLOYMENT. — Grants to state and local governments, school districts, and transit agencies for deployment of bidirectional charging infrastructure and V2G integration for public fleets, with priority for fleets serving disadvantaged communities.

**(c) Interoperability Mandate.** — Beginning January 1, 2029, all electric vehicle supply equipment receiving federal funds or installed in federal facilities shall support bidirectional power flow compliant with the V2G interoperability standard established under section 503.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 502. EV BATTERY SECOND-LIFE UTILIZATION.

**(a) Second-Life Battery Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an EV Battery Second-Life Utilization Program to develop the technical standards, safety protocols, and market mechanisms for repurposing electric vehicle batteries that have degraded below 80 percent of original capacity for stationary grid storage applications.

**(b) Program Activities.** —

(1) BATTERY STATE-OF-HEALTH ASSESSMENT. — Development of standardized rapid testing protocols for assessing used EV battery state of health, remaining useful life, and suitability for second-life storage applications.

(2) SECOND-LIFE BATTERY PACK RE-MANUFACTURING. — Grants for facilities that disassemble, test, recombine, and certify used EV battery modules and packs for stationary storage.

(3) SECOND-LIFE STORAGE PERFORMANCE WARRANTY PROGRAM. — A federal backstop warranty program providing partial coverage for second-life battery storage system performance, reducing financial risk for adopters and accelerating market development.

(4) SAFETY STANDARDS. — Development and publication of safety standards for second-life battery storage installation, operation, and decommissioning, addressing fire risk, thermal management, and end-of-life protocols.

**(c) Procurement Target.** — Not later than fiscal year 2030, federal agencies shall procure not less than 100 MWh of stationary energy storage capacity using second-life EV batteries, increasing to 500 MWh by fiscal year 2035.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.

### SECTION 503. SMART CHARGING STANDARDS AND INTEROPERABILITY.

**(a) V2G Interoperability Standard.** — The Secretary of Energy, through the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), shall develop and publish, not later than 12 months after enactment, a national V2G interoperability standard addressing:

(1) communication protocols between vehicles, chargers, and grid operators;

(2) cybersecurity requirements for bidirectional power flow, including authentication, encryption, and intrusion detection;

(3) grid interconnection requirements, including anti-islanding, power quality, and ride-through capabilities;

(4) data privacy standards for vehicle owner charging and discharging data; and

(5) minimum performance and efficiency requirements for bidirectional chargers.

**(b) Smart Charging Mandate.** — Beginning January 1, 2030, all new electric vehicle supply equipment sold in the United States shall be capable of smart charging, defined as the ability to modulate charging rate in response to external price, grid condition, or greenhouse gas emission intensity signals.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2029 for standards development and testing under this section.

### SECTION 504. SCHOOL BUS FLEET V2G PILOT PROGRAM.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency, shall establish a School Bus Fleet V2G Pilot Program deploying bidirectional charging infrastructure and V2G services at not fewer than 500 school districts across all 50 states.

**(b) Pilot Features.** —

(1) installation of bidirectional chargers at school bus depots, with preference for chargers manufactured in the United States;

(2) V2G dispatch software enabling automated participation in wholesale electricity markets and utility demand response programs;

(3) integration with on-site solar photovoltaic systems to maximize renewable energy utilization and minimize charging costs;

(4) emergency backup power capability enabling school buses to serve as mobile power sources for community shelters, cooling centers, and emergency response operations; and

(5) revenue-sharing agreements ensuring that a minimum of 50 percent of V2G revenue accrues to the school district.

**(c) Demonstration Requirement.** — Pilot program participants shall collect and publicly report data on V2G utilization rates, revenue generation, battery degradation, and operational lessons learned.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Pilot Program under this section.

### SECTION 505. EV BATTERY RECYCLING AND CIRCULAR ECONOMY.

**(a) Program Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an EV Battery Recycling and Circular Economy Program to onshore the complete battery recycling value chain and reduce dependence on virgin critical mineral extraction.

**(b) Program Components.** —

(1) recycling facility grants covering up to 50 percent of capital costs for facilities using hydrometallurgical, pyrometallurgical, or direct recycling processes to recover lithium, cobalt, nickel, manganese, and graphite from end-of-life EV batteries;

(2) recycling efficiency standards requiring minimum recovery rates of 90 percent for cobalt, nickel, and copper, 70 percent for lithium, and 70 percent for graphite by 2030;

(3) battery design-for-recycling labeling requirements including QR codes or RFID tags enabling automated sorting and providing chemistry, state-of-health, and safe handling information to recyclers; and

(4) a Battery Recycling Content Credit providing $5 per kilogram of recycled battery-grade lithium carbonate equivalent, $3 per kilogram of recycled nickel, and $2 per kilogram of recycled cobalt produced in the United States.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Program under this section.
