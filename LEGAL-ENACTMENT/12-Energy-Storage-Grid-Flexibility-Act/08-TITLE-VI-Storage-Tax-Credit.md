# 119TH CONGRESS — ENERGY STORAGE AND GRID FLEXIBILITY ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 08: TITLE VI — STORAGE TAX CREDIT EXTENSION

## TITLE VI — STORAGE TAX CREDIT EXTENSION

### SECTION 601. MODIFICATION OF SECTION 48 ITC FOR STANDALONE STORAGE.

**(a) Extension of 30 Percent Rate.** — Section 48(a)(2)(A)(i) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended to extend the 30 percent energy credit rate for energy storage technology (as defined in section 48(c)(2)) through December 31, 2036, for property placed in service before January 1, 2037.

**(b) Standalone Storage Clarification.** — Section 48(c)(2) is amended to explicitly include within the definition of energy storage technology:

(1) electrochemical battery storage systems of any chemistry, including lithium-ion, sodium-ion, flow batteries, metal-air batteries, and solid-state batteries;

(2) thermal energy storage systems, including those using solid media, phase-change materials, or molten salts;

(3) mechanical storage systems, including pumped storage hydropower, compressed air energy storage, gravity-based storage, and flywheels;

(4) hydrogen storage systems when used for electricity grid services, including electrolyzers, hydrogen storage vessels, and fuel cells or turbines for re-electrification; and

(5) any combination of the technologies listed in paragraphs (1) through (4) with renewable energy generation, demand response controls, and power conversion equipment.

**(c) Minimum Capacity Threshold.** — The Act further amends section 48(c)(2) to remove any minimum capacity threshold for the standalone storage ITC, making the credit available for residential, commercial, and community-scale storage systems of any size.

**(d) Effective Date.** — The amendments made by this section shall apply to property placed in service after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 602. SECTION 45X EXPANSION FOR STORAGE COMPONENTS.

**(a) Expanded Eligible Components.** — Section 45X(b) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended to add the following eligible components with corresponding credit amounts:

**(1) IRON-AIR BATTERY ELECTRODES.** — $10 per kilowatt-hour of rated storage capacity for iron-air battery electrodes produced in the United States.

**(2) FLOW BATTERY MEMBRANES.** — $25 per square meter for ion-exchange membranes designed for flow battery applications, produced in the United States.

**(3) FLOW BATTERY ELECTROLYTES.** — $5 per liter for vanadium, iron, zinc-bromine, and organic flow battery electrolyte solutions produced in the United States.

**(4) THERMAL STORAGE MEDIA.** — $10 per kilowatt-hour-equivalent for solid thermal storage media produced in the United States, including refractory bricks, carbon blocks, ceramic pellets, and phase-change materials.

**(5) GRAVITY STORAGE BLOCKS.** — $3 per metric ton for composite blocks manufactured for gravity-based energy storage systems using not less than 50 percent recycled or waste materials by mass.

**(6) COMPRESSED AIR STORAGE VESSELS.** — $15 per kilowatt-hour-equivalent for above-ground compressed air energy storage pressure vessels produced in the United States.

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendments made by this section shall apply to components produced after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 603. LONG-DURATION STORAGE PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT.

**(a) Credit Established.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

**"SEC. 45EE. LONG-DURATION ENERGY STORAGE PRODUCTION CREDIT.**

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the long-duration energy storage production credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to $0.03 per kilowatt-hour of electricity discharged by a qualified long-duration energy storage facility during such taxable year.

"(b) Qualified Long-Duration Energy Storage Facility. —

"(1) IN GENERAL. — The term 'qualified long-duration energy storage facility' means a facility that:

"(A) is owned by the taxpayer;

"(B) is located in the United States or a possession of the United States;

"(C) has a rated discharge duration of not fewer than 10 hours at rated power output; and

"(D) is originally placed in service after December 31, 2026, and before January 1, 2037.

"(2) ROUND-TRIP EFFICIENCY REQUIREMENT. — A facility shall have a round-trip efficiency of not less than 40 percent to qualify for the full credit. Facilities with round-trip efficiency between 30 and 40 percent shall receive a credit equal to the product of the credit amount in subsection (a) and the ratio of the facility's round-trip efficiency to 40 percent.

"(c) Duration of Credit. — The credit shall be allowable for the 10-year period beginning on the date the facility is originally placed in service.

"(d) Prevailing Wage and Apprenticeship Requirements. — The credit shall be subject to the prevailing wage and apprenticeship requirements of section 48(a)(10) and section 48(a)(11), respectively.

"(e) Sunset. — This section shall not apply to facilities placed in service after December 31, 2036."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendment made by this section shall apply to facilities placed in service after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 604. STORAGE-AS-TRANSMISSION ASSET CLASSIFICATION.

**(a) FERC Rulemaking.** — Not later than 180 days after enactment, the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission shall initiate a rulemaking to:

(1) establish a regulatory classification for energy storage systems deployed as transmission assets, including cost recovery through transmission rates;

(2) define criteria under which an energy storage system qualifies for dual-use classification, providing both transmission services and wholesale market services with transparent cost allocation; and

(3) establish interconnection procedures specific to transmission-connected storage.

**(b) Cost Recovery.** — Energy storage systems classified as transmission assets under the rulemaking established by subsection (a) shall be eligible for cost recovery through transmission rates, with costs allocated in proportion to benefits to transmission customers.

### SECTION 605. PREVAILING WAGE AND DOMESTIC CONTENT FOR STORAGE CREDITS.

**(a) Prevailing Wage Requirement.** — The enhanced credit rates provided under this Title shall be available only to projects meeting the prevailing wage requirements of section 48(a)(10) and the apprenticeship requirements of section 48(a)(11) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended by the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022.

**(b) Domestic Content Bonus.** — An additional credit of 10 percent shall be available for energy storage projects using not less than 55 percent domestically produced steel, iron, and manufactured products, consistent with the domestic content bonus credit framework established in section 48(a)(14).

**(c) Energy Community Bonus.** — An additional credit of 10 percent shall be available for energy storage projects located in energy communities, as defined in sections 48(a)(14)(B) and 45(b)(11)(B) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

**(d) Low-Income Community Bonus.** — An additional credit of 10 or 20 percent shall be available for energy storage projects located in or serving low-income communities, consistent with the low-income communities bonus credit program established under section 48(e).
