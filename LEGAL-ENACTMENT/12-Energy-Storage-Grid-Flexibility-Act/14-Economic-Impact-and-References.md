**(e) Grid Reliability and Consumer Benefit.** — The deployment of 600 GW of storage, 80 GW of VPP aggregated DERs, and grid flexibility market reforms is projected to:

(1) reduce annual wholesale electricity costs by $15-25 billion through peak shaving, congestion relief, and reduced renewable curtailment;

(2) avoid or defer $15-30 billion in transmission and distribution infrastructure investment;

(3) reduce the frequency and duration of customer outages by 30-50 percent in participating regions; and

(4) reduce annual CO2 emissions by an additional 400-600 million metric tons by enabling higher renewable energy penetration and displacing natural gas peaking generation.

**(f) Energy Security and Trade Impact.** — Domestic manufacturing of storage components is projected to:

(1) reduce United States dependence on imported batteries and components from 75 percent in 2026 to less than 20 percent by 2035;

(2) establish United States firms as leaders in the global long-duration storage market projected at $150-250 billion annually by 2035; and

(3) generate United States energy storage export revenues of $50-100 billion annually by 2040.

**(33)** Nothing in this Act shall be construed to limit, supersede, or preempt any state law that establishes more stringent energy storage safety, siting, environmental, or consumer protection requirements.

**(35)** The provisions of this Act shall be implemented consistent with Executive Order 14096 (Environmental Justice) and Executive Order 14057 (Federal Sustainability).
