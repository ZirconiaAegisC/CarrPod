# DOCUMENT 09: TITLE VII — STORAGE RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT

## TITLE VII — STORAGE RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT

### SECTION 701. NEXT-GENERATION STORAGE RESEARCH INITIATIVE.

**(a) Initiative Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Next-Generation Energy Storage Research Initiative to fund high-risk, high-reward research on energy storage technologies with the potential to achieve transformational improvements in cost, performance, duration, safety, and material sustainability.

**(b) Research Priority Areas.** — The Initiative shall prioritize research in:

(1) SOLID-STATE ELECTROLYTES. — Discovery, synthesis, and characterization of solid electrolyte materials with ionic conductivity exceeding 10 mS/cm at room temperature, electrochemical stability windows exceeding 5 volts, and compatibility with lithium metal anodes.

(2) SODIUM-ION BATTERIES. — Development of sodium-ion cathode and anode materials enabling energy densities exceeding 200 Wh/kg at costs below $50/kWh, leveraging abundant domestic sodium resources.

(3) METAL-AIR BATTERIES. — Research on rechargeable zinc-air, aluminum-air, and lithium-air batteries with practical energy densities exceeding 500 Wh/kg and cycle lives exceeding 1,000 cycles.

(4) ORGANIC FLOW BATTERIES. — Development of organic redox-active molecules for flow batteries, including quinones, viologens, and TEMPO derivatives, synthesized from abundant feedstocks and optimized for stability exceeding 10,000 cycles.

(5) SUPERCAPACITORS AND HYBRID DEVICES. — Nanostructured electrode materials for supercapacitors achieving energy densities exceeding 50 Wh/kg while retaining power densities exceeding 10 kW/kg.

(6) SUPERCONDUCTING MAGNETIC ENERGY STORAGE (SMES). — Research on high-temperature superconducting materials for SMES systems capable of multi-hour discharge at grid scale.

(7) HYDROGEN STORAGE MATERIALS. — Solid-state hydrogen storage materials, including metal hydrides, complex hydrides, and liquid organic hydrogen carriers, achieving volumetric hydrogen densities exceeding 50 kg H2/m3.

**(c) Funding Mechanism.** — The Initiative shall be administered through ARPA-E, the DOE Office of Science, and the DOE Office of Electricity.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Initiative under this section.

### SECTION 702. ADVANCED BATTERY MATERIALS INNOVATION HUB.

**(a) Hub Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an Advanced Battery Materials Innovation Hub, modeled on the Joint Center for Energy Storage Research (JCESR), as an Energy Innovation Hub under section 319 of the Energy Policy Act of 2005 (42 U.S.C. 16199).

**(b) Mission.** — The Hub shall pursue a five-year mission to discover, develop, and demonstrate battery materials that eliminate or minimize the use of critical materials subject to supply chain risk, including cobalt, nickel, and graphite, while achieving or exceeding the performance of current state-of-the-art lithium-ion batteries.

**(c) Hub Components.** —

(1) a central research facility at a Department of Energy national laboratory or university;

(2) participation by not fewer than five national laboratories, ten universities, and ten industrial partners;

(3) a materials acceleration platform integrating high-throughput computation, automated synthesis, and machine learning-driven characterization; and

(4) a technology transfer office responsible for licensing Hub-developed intellectual property to United States manufacturers.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Hub under this section.

### SECTION 703. GRID-SCALE STORAGE TESTING AND VALIDATION.

**(a) Testing Center Established.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Grid-Scale Storage Testing and Validation Center at the Idaho National Laboratory, with satellite facilities at Sandia National Laboratories and Pacific Northwest National Laboratory.

**(b) Functions.** — The Testing Center shall:

(1) provide independent, standardized performance testing for energy storage systems of all technologies at scales from kilowatt-hours to hundreds of megawatt-hours;

(2) conduct accelerated life testing to validate manufacturer degradation and cycle life claims;

(3) perform safety testing, including thermal runaway propagation, fire suppression effectiveness, and failure mode analysis;

(4) publish performance ratings enabling utility and developer comparison of competing storage technologies on a standardized basis;

(5) operate a grid simulator capable of subjecting storage systems to actual recorded grid events and extreme contingency scenarios; and

(6) maintain a public database of all test results.

**(c) Pre-Commercial Testing Access.** — The Testing Center shall provide subsidized testing access to United States-based storage technology startups and small businesses, with fees not exceeding 10 percent of the full cost of testing.

**(d) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 for the Testing Center under this section.

### SECTION 704. STORAGE SAFETY AND STANDARDS DEVELOPMENT.

**(a) Safety Standards Program.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the National Fire Protection Association, Underwriters Laboratories, and state and local fire marshals, shall develop and publish:

(1) model building and fire codes for energy storage system installation, addressing siting, ventilation, fire suppression, and emergency responder access;

(2) standardized safety testing protocols for thermal runaway propagation, off-gassing toxicity, and fire suppression system effectiveness; and

(3) an energy storage incident reporting system and public database modeled on the National Transportation Safety Board aviation incident reporting system.

**(b) First Responder Training.** — The Secretary shall fund development and delivery of energy storage safety training for not fewer than 50,000 firefighters, hazardous materials responders, and emergency medical technicians across all 50 states.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031 for programs under this section.

### SECTION 705. STORAGE-DURATION BENCHMARKING AND PROCUREMENT.

**(a) Storage Duration Classification System.** — The Secretary of Energy shall develop and publish a standardized storage duration classification system, including:

(1) SHORT-DURATION. — Systems rated for 1 to 4 hours of discharge at rated power;

(2) MEDIUM-DURATION. — Systems rated for 4 to 10 hours;

(3) LONG-DURATION. — Systems rated for 10 to 100 hours; and

(4) MULTI-DAY/SEASONAL. — Systems rated for more than 100 hours.

**(b) Utility Procurement Guidance.** — The Secretary shall publish model procurement frameworks for electric utilities to evaluate and acquire energy storage across all duration classes, incorporating:

(1) resource adequacy value assessment methodologies;

(2) transmission deferral value quantification;

(3) environmental and community benefit scoring; and

(4) total lifecycle cost analysis including decommissioning and recycling.

**(c) Authorization of Appropriations.** — There are authorized to be appropriated $100,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2029 for programs under this section.
