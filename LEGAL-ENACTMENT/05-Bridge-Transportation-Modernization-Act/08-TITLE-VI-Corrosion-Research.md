                TITLE VI — CORROSION RESEARCH AND INNOVATION

================================================================================

SECTION 601. ADVANCED COATING RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM.

    (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary, acting through the
    Turner-Fairbank Highway Research Center, shall establish an
    Advanced Coating Research and Development Program to accelerate
    the development, validation, and deployment of next-generation
    bridge corrosion protection technologies.

    (b) RESEARCH PRIORITIES. — The Program shall fund competitive
    research grants in the following priority areas:

        (1) Thermal spray coating process optimization, including
        cold spray techniques, high-velocity oxygen fuel (HVOF)
        application methods, and robotic/automated application
        systems for shop and field use;

        (2) Zinc-aluminum-magnesium alloy coatings with enhanced
        galvanic protection and reduced zinc consumption;

        (3) Nanoparticle-modified sealers and topcoats with
        improved UV resistance, chemical resistance, and
        mechanical durability;

        (4) Accelerated test methods that accurately replicate
        60-year field performance of duplex coating systems in
        under 5,000 hours of laboratory testing;

        (5) Field performance monitoring of TSZ duplex coatings
        using electrochemical impedance spectroscopy, scanning
        Kelvin probe, and embedded corrosion sensors; and

        (6) Lifecycle assessment and environmental product
        declarations for bridge coating systems, including
        comparative analysis of TSZ duplex versus conventional
        paint systems over a 100-year service life.

    (c) TESTING FACILITY. — The Secretary shall establish or
    designate a National Bridge Coating Test Facility capable of:

        (1) Accelerated cyclic corrosion testing per ASTM D5894;

        (2) Outdoor exposure testing at multiple sites
        representing the range of U.S. climatic and environmental
        conditions (marine, coastal, northern deicing, southern
        humid, arid, and industrial);

        (3) Full-scale structural member testing with applied
        loading during corrosion exposure; and

        (4) Nondestructive evaluation of coating condition
        using advanced techniques.

    (d) FUNDING. — From amounts authorized under section 801,
    $2,500,000,000 is authorized for the Program for the period
    of fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

SECTION 602. GRAPHENE-ENHANCED PROTECTIVE SYSTEMS RESEARCH.

    (a) RESEARCH PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall establish a
    dedicated research initiative on graphene-enhanced protective
    systems for bridge infrastructure, including:

        (1) Graphene nanoplatelet (GNP) and graphene oxide (GO)
        additives for epoxy and polyurethane coatings to enhance
        barrier properties, reduce water and oxygen permeability,
        and improve mechanical durability;

        (2) Graphene-modified zinc-rich primers with enhanced
        electrical conductivity for improved cathodic protection
        efficiency at reduced zinc loading;

        (3) Graphene-reinforced fiber-reinforced polymer (FRP)
        composite wraps and laminates for column strengthening
        and corrosion repair; and

        (4) Assessment of long-term durability, environmental
        stability, and health and safety aspects of graphene-
        enhanced materials in infrastructure applications.

    (b) PUBLIC-PRIVATE PARTNERSHIPS. — The Secretary is authorized
    to enter into cooperative research and development agreements
    (CRADAs) with private-sector manufacturers of graphene and
    graphene-enhanced materials to accelerate technology transfer
    and commercialization.

    (c) FIELD DEMONSTRATION. — Not later than 5 years after
    enactment, the Secretary shall initiate field demonstration
    projects on a minimum of 5 bridges to evaluate the performance
    of graphene-enhanced protective systems in comparison with
    conventional and TSZ duplex coating systems.

    (d) FUNDING. — From amounts authorized under section 801,
    $750,000,000 is authorized for graphene-enhanced protective
    systems research for the period of fiscal years 2027 through
    2036.

SECTION 603. SELF-HEALING COATING TECHNOLOGY DEVELOPMENT.

    (a) RESEARCH OBJECTIVES. — The Secretary shall fund research
    and development of self-healing coating technologies for bridge
    steel, including:

        (1) Microencapsulated healing agents (drying oils,
        siloxanes, or epoxy resins) embedded in coating matrices
        that release upon mechanical damage or corrosion initiation;

        (2) Vascular network systems that deliver healing agents
        to damage sites through embedded microchannels;

        (3) Shape-memory polymer coatings that close scratches
        and cracks upon thermal or optical activation;

        (4) pH-sensitive and ion-sensitive release mechanisms
        that trigger healing agent delivery specifically at
        sites of active corrosion; and

        (5) Integration of self-healing functionality with TSZ
        duplex coating systems for extended maintenance-free
        service life.

    (b) PERFORMANCE TARGETS. — Self-healing coating systems
    developed under this section shall be designed to achieve,
    under standardized laboratory testing:

        (1) Autonomous healing of scratches up to 2 mm in width
        within 72 hours at ambient temperature;

        (2) Restoration of at least 90 percent of original
        corrosion protection (as measured by electrochemical
        impedance spectroscopy) after healing;

        (3) Multiple healing cycles (minimum of 3) at the same
        damage location;

        (4) Compatibility with standard surface preparation
        and application methods used in bridge coating practice;
        and

        (5) Projected service life extension of at least 20
        years beyond the base coating system.

    (c) FUNDING. — From amounts authorized under section 801,
    $750,000,000 is authorized for self-healing coating
    technology development for the period of fiscal years 2027
    through 2036.

SECTION 604. PREDICTIVE CORROSION ANALYTICS PROGRAM.

    (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary shall establish a
    Predictive Corrosion Analytics Program to develop and deploy
    data-driven tools for forecasting bridge corrosion progression
    and optimizing maintenance interventions.

    (b) PROGRAM ELEMENTS. — The Program shall include:

        (1) Development of a National Bridge Corrosion Database
        integrating:

            (A) Historical NBI condition data;

            (B) Coating system type, age, and condition data
            from State bridge management systems;

            (C) Environmental exposure data (temperature,
            relative humidity, precipitation, chloride deposition
            rate, sulfur dioxide concentration) from NOAA and
            EPA monitoring networks;

            (D) Traffic data, including deicing chemical
            application rates and types; and

            (E) Corrosion rate measurements from SHM sensors
            and field testing programs;

        (2) Development of physics-based and data-driven corrosion
        progression models for:

            (A) Atmospheric corrosion of coated and uncoated
            steel;

            (B) Chloride-induced corrosion of reinforced concrete;

            (C) Crevice corrosion at bolted and riveted connections;

            (D) Galvanic corrosion at dissimilar metal interfaces;
            and

            (E) Microbially influenced corrosion in submerged
            and waterline environments;

        (3) Development of a Bridge Corrosion Risk Index (BCRI)
        that integrates structure age, design details, coating
        system, environmental exposure, and inspection history to
        predict the probability and severity of future corrosion
        damage;

        (4) Integration of corrosion prediction models with
        AASHTOWare Bridge Management to optimize maintenance,
        repair, and rehabilitation scheduling based on predicted
        corrosion progression; and

        (5) Development of a public-facing Corrosion Risk Map
        showing BCRI values for all bridges on the National
        Bridge Inventory, updated annually.

    (c) MACHINE LEARNING INTEGRATION. — The Secretary shall
    support research into the application of machine learning
    and computer vision techniques for:

        (1) Automated analysis of bridge inspection photographs
        to detect, classify, and quantify visible corrosion;

        (2) Pattern recognition in SHM corrosion sensor data
        for early detection of coating degradation and corrosion
        initiation;

        (3) Optimization of coating maintenance scheduling
        based on predicted condition progression; and

        (4) Identification of bridge design details, materials,
        and environments associated with accelerated corrosion.

    (d) FUNDING. — From amounts authorized under section 801,
    $1,000,000,000 is authorized for the Predictive Corrosion
    Analytics Program for the period of fiscal years 2027 through
    2036.
