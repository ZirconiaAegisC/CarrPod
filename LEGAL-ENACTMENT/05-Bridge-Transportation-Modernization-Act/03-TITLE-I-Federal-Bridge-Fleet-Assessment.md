              TITLE I — FEDERAL BRIDGE FLEET ASSESSMENT

================================================================================

SECTION 101. MANDATORY NATIONAL BRIDGE CONDITION INVENTORY.

    (a) IN GENERAL. — Not later than 24 months after the date of
    enactment of this Act, the Secretary of Transportation, acting
    through the Administrator of the Federal Highway Administration
    (FHWA), shall complete a comprehensive National Bridge Condition
    Inventory of every bridge listed on the National Bridge Inventory
    as of the date of enactment.

    (b) INVENTORY ELEMENTS. — The National Bridge Condition Inventory
    shall include, for each bridge:

        (1) A current condition rating for the deck, superstructure,
        and substructure using the 0–9 National Bridge Inventory
        (NBI) rating scale, performed by a National Highway
        Institute-certified bridge inspector within the preceding
        12 months;

        (2) A National Infrastructure Performance Rating (NIPR),
        as established under section 102, that integrates structural
        condition, load capacity, seismic vulnerability, scour
        criticality, and remaining service life into a single
        composite index;

        (3) A corrosion condition assessment performed by a
        NACE/AMPP-certified corrosion technologist or senior
        corrosion technologist, including measurement of coating
        thickness, assessment of coating adhesion, identification
        of active corrosion sites, and evaluation of cathodic
        protection system effectiveness where installed;

        (4) High-resolution photographic documentation of all
        structural elements, with particular emphasis on known
        corrosion sites, fatigue-sensitive details, and previously
        repaired areas;

        (5) A digital Bridge Information Model (BrIM) meeting the
        minimum requirements established under section 501 for all
        bridges with a deck area exceeding 10,000 square feet or
        a span length exceeding 200 feet;

        (6) A lifecycle cost analysis comparing rehabilitation
        versus replacement for bridges rated 4 or below on the
        NBI scale for any principal structural element;

        (7) A seismic vulnerability classification consistent with
        the requirements of section 301 for all bridges located in
        Seismic Design Categories C, D, E, or F as defined by
        AASHTO LRFD Bridge Design Specifications; and

        (8) An estimate of the remaining service life for each
        bridge, computed using deterioration models calibrated to
        local environmental conditions and maintenance history.

    (c) DATA COLLECTION STANDARDS. — The Secretary shall, within
    180 days of enactment, promulgate uniform data collection
    standards, inspection protocols, and quality assurance procedures
    for the National Bridge Condition Inventory. Such standards shall
    be developed in consultation with AASHTO, NACE/AMPP, and the
    National Highway Institute.

    (d) STATE RESPONSIBILITIES. — Each State department of
    transportation shall be responsible for the collection and
    submission of inventory data for bridges within its jurisdiction.
    The Secretary shall withhold up to 5 percent of a State's
    Federal-aid highway apportionment under 23 U.S.C. 104 for
    failure to submit complete inventory data by the deadline
    established in subsection (a).

    (e) TRIBAL AND TERRITORIAL BRIDGES. — The Secretary shall
    conduct or contract for the inventory of bridges on tribal
    lands and in U.S. territories, using funds appropriated under
    this Title.

SECTION 102. NATIONAL INFRASTRUCTURE PERFORMANCE RATING SYSTEM.

    (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — Not later than 12 months after the date
    of enactment, the Secretary shall establish a National
    Infrastructure Performance Rating (NIPR) system for bridges
    that integrates the following factors into a single composite
    numeric index on a 0–100 scale:

        (1) Structural condition (weighted at 35 percent), based
        on NBI deck, superstructure, and substructure ratings.

        (2) Load capacity (weighted at 15 percent), based on load
        rating factor relative to the AASHTO HL-93 design load.

        (3) Seismic vulnerability (weighted at 15 percent), based
        on the seismic demand-to-capacity ratio determined in
        accordance with section 301.

        (4) Scour criticality (weighted at 10 percent), based on
        the NBI scour rating and hydraulic vulnerability assessment.

        (5) Remaining service life (weighted at 15 percent), based
        on deterioration modeling and corrosion assessment data.

        (6) Traffic criticality (weighted at 10 percent), based on
        average daily traffic, detour length, and functional
        classification.

    (b) RATING CATEGORIES. — Bridges shall be classified into the
    following NIPR categories:

        (1) NIPR-A (Excellent): NIPR score 85–100. No action required
        beyond routine inspection.

        (2) NIPR-B (Good): NIPR score 70–84. Routine maintenance
        recommended within standard cycles.

        (3) NIPR-C (Fair): NIPR score 50–69. Rehabilitation
        recommended within five years; structural monitoring sensors
        required under section 502(b).

        (4) NIPR-D (Poor): NIPR score 30–49. Rehabilitation or
        replacement required within three years; enhanced monitoring
        required; load posting may be required.

        (5) NIPR-F (Critical): NIPR score below 30. Immediate
        action required; bridge shall be closed or subject to
        emergency load restrictions pending repair or replacement.

    (c) PUBLIC AVAILABILITY. — NIPR scores for all bridges shall
    be published annually on a publicly accessible website maintained
    by FHWA, in a format that enables comparison, mapping, and
    trend analysis.

    (d) INTEGRATION WITH NBI. — The NIPR system shall supplement,
    not replace, the existing National Bridge Inventory condition
    rating system. NBI condition data shall continue to be collected
    and reported as required under 23 U.S.C. 144.

SECTION 103. ACCELERATED TWO-YEAR INSPECTION CYCLE.

    (a) INSPECTION INTERVAL. — Notwithstanding 23 CFR 650.311, the
    maximum interval between routine bridge inspections for any
    bridge rated NIPR-C or below (NIPR score below 70) shall be
    24 months.

    (b) ENHANCED INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS. — For bridges rated
    NIPR-D or below, the biennial inspection shall include:

        (1) Hands-on inspection of all fracture-critical members;

        (2) Ultrasonic testing of all pins and hangers in
        non-redundant steel tension members;

        (3) Half-cell potential mapping of all concrete deck and
        substructure surfaces showing signs of chloride contamination;

        (4) Coating thickness measurements at a minimum of 30 test
        locations per bridge; and

        (5) Underwater inspection of all substructure elements in
        water depths exceeding 2 feet.

    (c) INSPECTOR QUALIFICATIONS. — Inspections conducted under
    this section shall be led by a Team Leader certified by the
    National Highway Institute and meeting the requirements of
    23 CFR 650.309, with additional corrosion assessment training
    as established under section 701 of this Act.

    (d) REPORTING. — Inspection reports shall be entered into the
    National Bridge Inventory within 30 days of inspection completion.

SECTION 104. BRIDGE DATA TRANSPARENCY AND PUBLIC ACCESS.

    (a) PUBLIC PORTAL. — The Secretary shall maintain a publicly
    accessible, searchable online portal containing:

        (1) Current NIPR scores and NBI condition ratings for all
        bridges;

        (2) Inspection report summaries in plain-language format;

        (3) Bridge age, design type, average daily traffic, and
        detour length;

        (4) Status of planned or active rehabilitation projects;

        (5) Any load postings or restrictions currently in effect;
        and

        (6) Historical condition trend data for each bridge.

    (b) DATA FORMAT. — All data shall be published in machine-
    readable formats with open data licenses to facilitate analysis
    by researchers, planning agencies, and the public.

    (c) ANNUAL REPORT TO CONGRESS. — The Secretary shall submit
    an annual report to the Committee on Transportation and
    Infrastructure of the House of Representatives and the Committee
    on Environment and Public Works of the Senate summarizing:

        (1) The number and percentage of bridges in each NIPR
        category, nationally and by State;

        (2) Trends in bridge condition over the preceding five
        years; and

        (3) The status of programs and expenditures under this Act.

SECTION 105. STATE REPORTING REQUIREMENTS AND COMPLIANCE.

    (a) STATE BRIDGE MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS. — Each State shall maintain
    a bridge management system compatible with the AASHTOWare Bridge
    Management (BrM) software or an equivalent system approved by
    the Secretary.

    (b) ANNUAL STATE REPORT. — Each State shall submit to the
    Secretary, not later than September 30 of each year, a State
    Bridge Condition Report that includes:

        (1) Verification of inspection completion for all bridges
        within the State;

        (2) A prioritized list of bridge rehabilitation, retrofit,
        and replacement needs, ranked by NIPR score;

        (3) Certification of compliance with inspection interval
        requirements under section 103;

        (4) A summary of bridge load postings and restrictions
        in effect; and

        (5) A five-year capital program for bridge infrastructure.

    (c) PENALTIES FOR NONCOMPLIANCE. — The Secretary may withhold
    up to 10 percent of a State's apportionment under the Bridge
    Formula Program (23 U.S.C. 119) for failure to comply with the
    reporting requirements of this section.
