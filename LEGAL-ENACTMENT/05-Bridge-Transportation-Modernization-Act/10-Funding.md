              TITLE VIII — AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS

================================================================================

SECTION 801. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    (a) IN GENERAL. — There is authorized to be appropriated from
    the Highway Trust Fund (other than the Mass Transit Account)
    for the programs established under this Act a total of
    $200,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027 through
    2036, to remain available until expended.

    (b) ANNUAL AUTHORIZATION. — Of the amount authorized under
    subsection (a), the following annual amounts are authorized
    to be appropriated:

        FY2027 ........................... $12,000,000,000
        FY2028 ........................... $16,000,000,000
        FY2029 ........................... $20,000,000,000
        FY2030 ........................... $22,000,000,000
        FY2031 ........................... $24,000,000,000
        FY2032 ........................... $24,000,000,000
        FY2033 ........................... $22,000,000,000
        FY2034 ........................... $22,000,000,000
        FY2035 ........................... $20,000,000,000
        FY2036 ........................... $18,000,000,000

    The ramp-up in early years reflects program establishment,
    workforce development, completion of the National Bridge
    Condition Inventory, and initial seismic assessments.
    The ramp-down in later years reflects program maturity
    and transition to steady-state operations.

SECTION 802. ALLOCATION AMONG PROGRAM CATEGORIES.

    (a) ALLOCATION FORMULA. — Of amounts appropriated under
    section 801, the Secretary shall allocate funds as follows:

        (1) BRIDGE FLEET ASSESSMENT (TITLE I) — $5,000,000,000
            (2.5 percent). For completion of the National Bridge
            Condition Inventory, development and maintenance of
            the NIPR system, enhanced inspections, and the
            public data portal.

        (2) ADVANCED COATING SYSTEMS (TITLE II) — $30,000,000,000
            (15.0 percent). For the incremental cost of TSZ duplex
            coating specification, application, inspection, and
            warranty on Federal-aid bridge projects; State TSZ
            transition planning and specification development; and
            coating workforce training.

        (3) SEISMIC RETROFIT (TITLE III) — $50,000,000,000
            (25.0 percent). For the National Seismic Retrofit
            Priority Fund, seismic vulnerability assessments,
            retrofit design and construction, and the accelerated
            program for high-seismic-zone bridges.

        (4) CATHODIC PROTECTION (TITLE IV) — $15,000,000,000
            (7.5 percent). For ICCP system design, installation,
            and monitoring on federally funded concrete bridges;
            State ICCP deployment grants; and technical assistance.

        (5) DIGITAL TWIN AND MONITORING (TITLE V) — $20,000,000,000
            (10.0 percent). For BrIM development, SHM sensor
            procurement, installation, and data management, the
            National Bridge Digital Twin Data Platform, and
            cybersecurity.

        (6) CORROSION RESEARCH (TITLE VI) — $5,000,000,000
            (2.5 percent). For competitive research grants in
            advanced coatings, graphene-enhanced systems,
            self-healing coatings, and predictive corrosion
            analytics, and operation of the National Bridge
            Coating Test Facility.

        (7) WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT (TITLE VII) — $5,000,000,000
            (2.5 percent). For the National Bridge Inspector
            Training Pipeline, Coatings Applicator Certification
            Program, NACE/AMPP partnership, and community college
            and apprenticeship grants.

        (8) BRIDGE REHABILITATION GRANTS — $70,000,000,000
            (35.0 percent). For competitive and formula-based
            grants to States for:

            (A) Bridge rehabilitation, repair, and replacement
            projects on bridges rated NIPR-C or below;

            (B) The incremental cost of compliance with the
            coating, cathodic protection, seismic, and monitoring
            requirements of this Act beyond standard project scope;

            (C) Emergency bridge repair following natural disasters,
            floods, vessel collision, or other events;

            (D) Bridge removal and replacement where rehabilitation
            is not cost-effective as determined by lifecycle cost
            analysis; and

            (E) Planning, preliminary engineering, and environmental
            compliance for bridge projects consistent with the
            requirements of this Act.

    (b) FLEXIBILITY. — The Secretary may transfer up to 10 percent
    of the amount allocated to any program category under subsection
    (a) to any other program category upon a determination that
    such transfer is necessary to meet program objectives, provided
    that the Secretary notifies the Committees on Transportation
    and Infrastructure and Environment and Public Works not less
    than 60 days before any such transfer.

    (c) ADMINISTRATIVE COSTS. — Not more than 2 percent of the
    total amount authorized under section 801 may be used for
    Federal administrative costs, including FHWA staffing, program
    management, grant processing, and oversight activities.

SECTION 803. STATE MATCHING REQUIREMENTS.

    (a) FEDERAL SHARE. — Except as otherwise provided in this Act,
    the Federal share of the cost of a project funded under this
    Act shall not exceed:

        (1) 90 percent for projects on the Interstate System;

        (2) 80 percent for all other projects.

    (b) INCREASED FEDERAL SHARE. — The Federal share may be
    increased to:

        (1) 95 percent for projects on Indian reservation roads
    or park roads and parkways;

        (2) 100 percent for emergency relief projects authorized
    under 23 U.S.C. 125;

        (3) 95 percent for projects where the State demonstrates
    to the satisfaction of the Secretary that the State cannot
    meet the matching requirement due to fiscal hardship, as
    defined by criteria published by the Secretary within 180
    days of enactment.

    (c) NON-FEDERAL SHARE. — The non-Federal share may be provided
    from State, local, or private sources, including in-kind
    contributions of materials, services, or rights-of-way, as
    determined by the Secretary.

    (d) MAINTENANCE OF EFFORT. — As a condition of receiving funds
    under this Act, each State shall certify that it will maintain
    its aggregate expenditures on bridge infrastructure from
    State and local sources at a level not less than the average
    annual level of such expenditures for the 3 fiscal years
    preceding the date of enactment.

SECTION 804. OVERSIGHT AND ACCOUNTABILITY.

    (a) BIENNIAL GAO AUDITS. — The Comptroller General of the
    United States shall conduct biennial audits of expenditures
    under this Act, including:

        (1) Verification that funds are being expended in
        accordance with the allocations and purposes specified
        in this Title;

        (2) Assessment of progress toward the goal of reducing
        structurally deficient bridges by 75 percent;

        (3) Evaluation of the effectiveness of mandatory coating,
        cathodic protection, seismic, and monitoring requirements
        in improving bridge condition and extending service life;
        and

        (4) Recommendations for program improvements.

    (b) INSPECTOR GENERAL OVERSIGHT. — The Inspector General of
    the Department of Transportation shall:

        (1) Investigate allegations of fraud, waste, or abuse
        in the expenditure of funds under this Act;

        (2) Conduct annual reviews of a representative sample
        of projects funded under this Act to confirm compliance
        with program requirements; and

        (3) Report findings to the Secretary and to the
        Committees on Transportation and Infrastructure and
        Environment and Public Works.

    (c) PERFORMANCE METRICS. — Within 180 days of enactment, the
    Secretary shall publish a set of performance metrics to be
    used in evaluating the effectiveness of programs under this
    Act, including:

        (1) Annual change in the number and percentage of
        bridges in each NIPR category;

        (2) Average NBI condition rating for bridge decks,
        superstructures, and substructures, nationally and by
        State;

        (3) Number of bridges receiving TSZ duplex coating,
        ICCP systems, SHM sensors, and seismic retrofit under
        this Act;

        (4) Service life extension (in years) achieved per
        dollar of program expenditure;

        (5) Number of bridge inspectors, coatings applicators,
        and corrosion professionals trained and certified under
        Title VII; and

        (6) Reduction in annual direct bridge corrosion costs,
        as estimated through NACE IMPACT study methodology.

    (d) SUNSET AND REAUTHORIZATION. — The authorization of
    appropriations under this Act shall expire on September 30,
    2036. Not later than June 30, 2035, the Secretary shall submit
    to Congress a comprehensive report on the implementation and
    effectiveness of this Act, together with recommendations
    regarding reauthorization.
