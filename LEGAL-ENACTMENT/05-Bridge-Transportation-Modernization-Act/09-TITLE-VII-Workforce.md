SECTION 701. NATIONAL BRIDGE INSPECTOR TRAINING PIPELINE.

    (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

        (1) An estimated 30 percent of the Nation's certified
        bridge inspection workforce is eligible for retirement
        within five years of the date of enactment of this Act;

        (2) The National Highway Institute (NHI) bridge inspection
        training courses produce approximately 500 newly certified
        bridge inspectors annually, significantly fewer than the
        number required to replace retiring inspectors and support
        the expanded inspection requirements of this Act; and

        (3) A robust pipeline of qualified bridge inspectors is
        essential to the successful implementation of the
        accelerated inspection cycle mandated under section 103
        and the condition assessment requirements of Title I.

    (b) PIPELINE PROGRAM. — The Secretary, in coordination with the
    Secretary of Labor, shall establish a National Bridge Inspector
    Training Pipeline Program to:

        (1) Expand the capacity of NHI bridge inspection training
        courses (FHWA-NHI-130055, 130056, 130078, and successors)
        to train no fewer than 2,000 new bridge inspectors per
        year by fiscal year 2030;

        (2) Develop a specialized Corrosion Assessment for Bridge
        Inspectors curriculum, in partnership with NACE/AMPP,
        covering:

            (A) Visual identification of coating degradation
            and active corrosion on steel and concrete elements;

            (B) Use of coating thickness gauges, adhesion testers,
            and half-cell potential measurement equipment;

            (C) Interpretation of corrosion monitoring data from
            SHM sensors and ICCP systems; and

            (D) Documentation of corrosion findings in inspection
            reports consistent with NBI requirements;

        (3) Establish a Bridge Inspector Apprenticeship Program,
        registered with the Department of Labor, combining
        classroom instruction, field training under a certified
        Team Leader, and competency-based assessments;

        (4) Provide scholarships and tuition reimbursement for
        individuals pursuing NHI certification, with priority
        given to veterans, individuals from underrepresented
        communities, and residents of rural areas; and

        (5) Develop and maintain a National Bridge Inspector
        Registry tracking certifications, continuing education,
        and inspection assignments.

    (c) CONTINUING EDUCATION. — Beginning 2 years after enactment,
    all bridge inspectors conducting inspections under 23 U.S.C. 144
    shall complete a minimum of 24 hours of approved continuing
    education every 2 years, including at least 8 hours specific
    to corrosion recognition and assessment.

    (d) FUNDING. — From amounts authorized under section 801,
    $2,000,000,000 is authorized for the National Bridge Inspector
    Training Pipeline for the period of fiscal years 2027 through
    2036.

SECTION 702. COATINGS APPLICATOR CERTIFICATION PROGRAM.

    (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary, in partnership
    with NACE/AMPP, SSPC, and the International Union of Painters
    and Allied Trades (IUPAT), shall establish a National Bridge
    Coatings Applicator Certification Program.

    (b) CERTIFICATION LEVELS. — The Program shall offer three
    certification levels:

        (1) Level I — Bridge Coatings Applicator: Certified to
        perform surface preparation and coating application on
        bridge steel under the direct supervision of a Level II
        or Level III certified applicator. Requires completion
        of 80 hours of classroom and hands-on training, passage
        of a written examination, and documentation of 1,000
        hours of supervised field experience.

        (2) Level II — Senior Bridge Coatings Applicator:
        Certified to supervise coating application crews
        and perform quality control testing. Requires Level I
        certification, an additional 120 hours of training,
        passage of written and practical examinations, and
        documentation of 3,000 hours of field experience in
        bridge coating application.

        (3) Level III — Bridge Coatings Specialist (Thermal
        Spray): Certified to plan, supervise, and inspect
        thermal spray zinc application on bridge structures.
        Requires Level II certification, an additional 160
        hours of specialized TSZ training, passage of written
        and practical examinations, and documentation of 2,000
        hours of hands-on TSZ application experience.

    (c) REQUIRED CERTIFICATION. — Effective October 1, 2029, all
    thermal spray zinc application on Federal-aid bridge projects
    shall be performed by or under the direct supervision of
    personnel holding a minimum of Level II certification, with
    a Level III Bridge Coatings Specialist (Thermal Spray) present
    at the job site at all times during zinc application operations.

    (d) TRAINING FACILITIES. — The Secretary shall provide grants
    to establish or expand TSZ applicator training facilities,
    including mobile training units capable of delivering hands-on
    instruction at bridge project sites and in underserved regions.

    (e) FUNDING. — From amounts authorized under section 801,
    $1,500,000,000 is authorized for the Coatings Applicator
    Certification Program for the period of fiscal years 2027
    through 2036.

SECTION 703. NACE/AMPP PARTNERSHIP AND ACCREDITATION.

    (a) FORMAL PARTNERSHIP. — The Secretary shall enter into a
    memorandum of understanding with the Association for Materials
    Protection and Performance (AMPP, formerly NACE International)
    to:

        (1) Develop and maintain corrosion-related training
        curricula for bridge inspectors, coatings applicators,
        and cathodic protection technicians;

        (2) Establish and maintain a Bridge Corrosion Professional
        certification track within the AMPP certification framework;

        (3) Develop standardized corrosion assessment protocols
        for bridge inspection consistent with the requirements
        of this Act;

        (4) Provide technical review and guidance on the
        implementation of Title II (Advanced Coating Mandate)
        and Title IV (Cathodic Protection);

        (5) Maintain a registry of AMPP-certified corrosion
        professionals qualified to perform bridge corrosion
        assessments, coating inspections, and ICCP system
        evaluations; and

        (6) Conduct an annual State of Bridge Corrosion report,
        summarizing national trends, technology developments,
        and workforce data.

    (b) ACCREDITATION. — The Secretary shall recognize AMPP
    Bridge Corrosion Professional certifications as meeting or
    exceeding the corrosion assessment qualifications required
    for bridge inspections conducted under this Act and under
    23 U.S.C. 144.

    (c) FUNDING. — From amounts authorized under section 801,
    $500,000,000 is authorized for the NACE/AMPP partnership
    and accreditation activities for the period of fiscal years
    2027 through 2036.

SECTION 704. COMMUNITY COLLEGE AND APPRENTICESHIP GRANTS.

    (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Secretary, in coordination with the
    Secretary of Labor and the Secretary of Education, shall
    establish a competitive grant program for community colleges,
    technical schools, and labor-management apprenticeship programs
    to develop and expand bridge infrastructure workforce training
    programs.

    (b) ELIGIBLE ACTIVITIES. — Grant funds may be used for:

        (1) Curriculum development for bridge inspection, coatings
        application, structural monitoring system installation
        and maintenance, ICCP system maintenance, nondestructive
        testing, and bridge construction trades;

        (2) Acquisition of training equipment, including thermal
        spray systems, coating inspection instruments, ultrasonic
        testing equipment, SHM sensor calibration equipment, and
        simulation software;

        (3) Instructor training and certification;

        (4) Student scholarships, stipends, and transportation
        assistance, with priority for students from economically
        disadvantaged backgrounds;

        (5) Establishment of apprenticeship programs registered
        with the Department of Labor's Office of Apprenticeship;
        and

        (6) Development of bridge infrastructure career pathways
        in secondary schools, including dual-enrollment programs
        and pre-apprenticeship programs.

    (c) GRANT PRIORITIES. — In awarding grants, the Secretary shall
    give priority to:

        (1) Programs located in States with high concentrations of
        NIPR-D and NIPR-F bridges;

        (2) Programs serving rural areas with limited access to
        workforce training;

        (3) Programs with demonstrated partnerships with State
        departments of transportation, contractors, or labor
        organizations; and

        (4) Programs that include commitments to hire graduates
        from participating employers.

    (d) FUNDING. — From amounts authorized under section 801,
    $1,000,000,000 is authorized for community college and
    apprenticeship grants for the period of fiscal years 2027
    through 2036.
