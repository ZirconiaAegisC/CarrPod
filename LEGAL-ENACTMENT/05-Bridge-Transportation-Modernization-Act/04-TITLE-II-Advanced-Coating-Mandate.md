              TITLE II — ADVANCED COATING MANDATE

================================================================================

SECTION 201. REQUIREMENT FOR THERMAL SPRAY ZINC DUPLEX COATING SYSTEMS.

    (a) MANDATORY APPLICATION. — Effective for all Federal-aid
    highway bridge projects advertised for bid on or after
    October 1, 2028, all structural steel bridge elements shall
    be protected by a thermal spray zinc (TSZ) duplex coating
    system that complies with the requirements of this Title.

    (b) SCOPE OF APPLICATION. — The requirement of subsection (a)
    applies to:

        (1) All structural steel in new bridge construction
        receiving Federal-aid highway funds;

        (2) All structural steel in bridge rehabilitation projects
        receiving Federal-aid highway funds where existing coating
        is removed to bare metal on more than 25 percent of the
        total steel surface area;

        (3) All bridge steel replacement members or new steel
        added during widening, strengthening, or seismic retrofit
        projects; and

        (4) All structural steel in bridge decks, superstructures,
        and substructures, including girders, stringers, floor
        beams, truss members, arch ribs, tie girders, hangers,
        bearings, expansion joints, and railings.

    (c) DEFINITION. — For purposes of this Title, a "thermal spray
    zinc duplex coating system" means a corrosion protection system
    consisting of:

        (1) Surface preparation to SSPC-SP 10 (Near-White Metal
        Blast Cleaning) standard, achieving a minimum surface
        profile of 2.5 mils angular anchor profile;

        (2) Application of thermal-sprayed zinc in accordance with
        AWS C2.18 or SSPC-CS 23.00/AWS C2.23/NACE No. 12,
        achieving a minimum dry film thickness of 8 mils on all
        surfaces, with 12 mils required in marine or deicing salt
        exposure zones;

        (3) Application of a penetrating sealer conforming to
        AASHTO Duplex Coating Guidelines Section 4.3 within 4
        hours of zinc application;

        (4) Application of an epoxy intermediate coat achieving
        a minimum dry film thickness of 3 mils; and

        (5) Application of an aliphatic polyurethane or polysiloxane
        topcoat achieving a minimum dry film thickness of 2 mils.

    (d) ALTERNATIVE SYSTEMS. — A State department of transportation
    may petition the Secretary for approval of an alternative
    corrosion protection system that demonstrates, through
    accelerated laboratory testing (ASTM B117, ASTM G85, and
    ASTM D5894) and a minimum of 10 years of field performance
    data, a projected service life equal to or greater than 60
    years in the relevant exposure environment.

    (e) METALLIZING ALTERNATIVES. — Thermal spray coatings using
    zinc-aluminum alloy (85% Zn / 15% Al) or aluminum-magnesium
    alloy may be used in lieu of pure zinc subject to approval
    by the Secretary based on documented performance in comparable
    environments.

SECTION 202. PERFORMANCE STANDARDS AND LIFE-CYCLE REQUIREMENTS.

    (a) DESIGN SERVICE LIFE. — TSZ duplex coating systems applied
    under this Title shall be designed and specified to achieve a
    minimum service life of 60 years before the first major
    maintenance recoating is required.

    (b) PERFORMANCE VERIFICATION. — Coating system performance
    shall be verified through:

        (1) Adhesion testing in accordance with ASTM D4541
        (pull-off adhesion), achieving a minimum of 750 psi on
        test coupons and 500 psi on production surfaces;

        (2) Dry film thickness verification at a minimum frequency
        of one measurement per 10 square feet of surface area,
        using a calibrated magnetic or eddy-current gauge;

        (3) Holiday detection (low-voltage wet sponge or
        high-voltage DC) on 100 percent of sealed and topcoated
        surfaces, with all detected holidays repaired and
        retested;

        (4) Bend testing of witness panels in accordance with
        ASTM D522 to verify coating flexibility; and

        (5) Salt spray resistance testing of production witness
        panels in accordance with ASTM B117 for a minimum of
        3,000 hours with no blistering, rusting, or loss of
        adhesion exceeding a rating of 8 per ASTM D610 and
        ASTM D714.

    (c) INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS. — All TSZ duplex coating
    application shall be inspected by a NACE/AMPP-certified
    Coatings Inspector (Level 2 or higher) who is independent
    of the coating applicator. Inspection shall include:

        (1) Verification of ambient conditions (temperature,
        relative humidity, dew point, wind speed) at the start
        of each shift and whenever conditions change by more
        than 5 degrees Fahrenheit or 10 percent relative humidity;

        (2) Verification of surface cleanliness and profile
        before zinc application;

        (3) Continuous monitoring of zinc application parameters,
        including spray distance, angle, traverse rate, and wire
        feed rate for arc spray systems; and

        (4) Documentation of all inspection findings in a daily
        quality control report retained in the bridge permanent
        records.

    (d) WARRANTY. — The Secretary shall require, as a condition
    of Federal-aid participation, a minimum 10-year materials and
    workmanship warranty for TSZ duplex coating systems from the
    coating applicator.

SECTION 203. COATING SYSTEM QUALIFICATION AND CERTIFICATION.

    (a) SYSTEM QUALIFICATION. — Before any TSZ duplex coating
    system may be specified on a Federal-aid bridge project, the
    manufacturer or applicator shall submit to the Secretary a
    qualification package demonstrating:

        (1) Conformity of the proposed zinc wire or powder to
        ASTM B833 (for wire) or ASTM B899 (for powder);

        (2) Conformity of the proposed sealer, intermediate coat,
        and topcoat materials to AASHTO Duplex Coating Guidelines,
        Appendix B Material Specifications;

        (3) Results of cyclic corrosion testing (ASTM D5894) for
        a minimum of 5,040 hours (30 cycles) demonstrating no
        underfilm corrosion at the scribe exceeding 2 mm; and

        (4) A minimum of three independent references documenting
        successful application and satisfactory performance of
        the proposed coating system on bridge structures after a
        minimum of five years of service.

    (b) APPLICATOR CERTIFICATION. — Any contractor applying TSZ
    duplex coating systems on Federal-aid bridge projects shall:

        (1) Employ a minimum of one NACE/AMPP Senior Certified
        Coatings Inspector (Level 3) or SSPC Protective Coatings
        Specialist on site full-time during all coating operations;

        (2) Employ thermal spray applicators holding current
        certification from the AWS Certified Welder program
        (thermal spray endorsement) or an equivalent program
        approved by the Secretary; and

        (3) Maintain documented quality control procedures
        conforming to SSPC-QP 6, "Standard Procedure for
        Evaluating the Qualifications of Contractors Who Apply
        Thermal Spray Coatings to Steel Structures."

SECTION 204. COMPLIANCE DEADLINES FOR FEDERALLY FUNDED BRIDGE STEEL.

    (a) NEW CONSTRUCTION. — Effective October 1, 2028, all
    Federal-aid bridge construction projects shall specify
    TSZ duplex coating on all structural steel.

    (b) MAJOR REHABILITATION. — Effective October 1, 2029,
    all Federal-aid bridge rehabilitation projects involving
    removal of existing coating to bare metal on more than
    25 percent of the total steel surface area shall apply
    TSZ duplex coating to the stripped surfaces.

    (c) EXISTING COATING FAILURE. — Effective October 1, 2030,
    any Federal-aid bridge maintenance project addressing
    coating failure on more than 10 percent of a bridge's
    steel surface area shall apply TSZ duplex coating to the
    affected areas and to any contiguous steel surfaces where
    the existing coating is projected, through adhesion testing
    and visual assessment, to fail within five years.

SECTION 205. EXEMPTIONS AND WAIVER PROCESS.

    (a) EXEMPT CATEGORIES. — The requirements of this Title shall
    not apply to:

        (1) Weathering steel (ASTM A709 Grade 50W, A588, or
        equivalent) used in environments where FHWA Technical
        Advisory T 5140.22 deems uncoated weathering steel
        acceptable;

        (2) Stainless steel bridge elements (ASTM A709 Grade
        50CR or equivalent);

        (3) Bridge elements located in arid environments where
        the mean annual relative humidity is below 60 percent
        and deicing chemicals are not applied;

        (4) Temporary bridges or detour structures with a
        design service life of less than 5 years; and

        (5) Bridge elements with a remaining design service life
        of less than 10 years, as documented in a bridge
        management plan approved by the Secretary, provided
        the bridge is scheduled for replacement within that period.

    (b) WAIVER PETITION. — A State department of transportation
    may petition the Secretary for a waiver from TSZ duplex
    coating requirements for a specific project on the grounds of:

        (1) Demonstrated infeasibility due to site constraints
        or access limitations that preclude safe and effective
        TSZ application;

        (2) Documented availability of an alternative coating
        system that meets the performance requirements of
        section 202(a) as verified by section 201(d); or

        (3) Exceptional circumstances where the cost of TSZ
        duplex coating exceeds 20 percent of the total project
        cost, provided the State submits a lifecycle cost
        analysis demonstrating that the alternative coating
        system has a lower 60-year net present value cost.

    (c) WAIVER REVIEW. — The Secretary shall publish all waiver
    petitions and determinations on a publicly accessible website
    and shall submit a semiannual report to the Committees on
    Transportation and Infrastructure and Environment and Public
    Works summarizing all waiver activity.
