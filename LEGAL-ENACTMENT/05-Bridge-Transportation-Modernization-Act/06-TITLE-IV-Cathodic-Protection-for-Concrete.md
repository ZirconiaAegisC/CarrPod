           TITLE IV — CATHODIC PROTECTION FOR CONCRETE BRIDGES

================================================================================

SECTION 401. IMPRESSED CURRENT CATHODIC PROTECTION MANDATE.

    (a) MANDATORY APPLICATION. — Effective for all Federal-aid
    highway bridge projects advertised for bid on or after
    October 1, 2029, impressed current cathodic protection (ICCP)
    shall be provided for all concrete bridge substructure elements
    and bridge decks in the following environments:

        (1) Bridges located in States where the average annual
        application of deicing salts exceeds 5 tons per lane-mile;

        (2) Bridges located within 3 miles of a marine coastline,
        including tidal estuaries and saltwater bays;

        (3) Bridges where chloride ion content at the depth of
        the outermost reinforcing steel exceeds 0.05 percent by
        weight of concrete (approximately 1.2 pounds per cubic
        yard), as determined by testing in accordance with
        AASHTO T 260; and

        (4) Bridges where half-cell potential testing in
        accordance with ASTM C876 indicates a greater than 90
        percent probability of active corrosion at any test
        location on the substructure or deck.

    (b) NEW CONSTRUCTION. — For new bridge construction in
    environments described in subsection (a), ICCP system anodes
    and wiring shall be installed during construction. The system's
    transformer-rectifier units shall be energized within 6 months
    of structure completion.

    (c) EXISTING BRIDGE REHABILITATION. — For bridge rehabilitation
    projects in environments described in subsection (a) involving
    deck replacement or substructure repair exceeding 25 percent
    of the total surface area, an ICCP system shall be installed
    and energized as part of the rehabilitation contract.

    (d) SYSTEM DESIGN STANDARDS. — ICCP systems shall be designed
    in accordance with NACE SP0290, "Standard Practice for
    Impressed Current Cathodic Protection of Reinforcing Steel in
    Atmospherically Exposed Concrete Structures," and shall meet
    the following performance criteria:

        (1) The system shall be designed to deliver a minimum
        current density of 0.5 milliamperes per square foot of
        reinforcing steel surface area at all points within the
        protected zone;

        (2) The design life of buried or embedded anodes shall
        be not less than 40 years;

        (3) Transformer-rectifier units shall be equipped with
        remote monitoring capability and shall be accessible for
        maintenance without lane closures; and

        (4) The system shall incorporate reference electrodes
        (silver/silver chloride or manganese dioxide) at a
        minimum density of one reference electrode per 500
        square feet of protected surface area for ongoing
        performance verification.

    (e) COMPLIANCE DEADLINE. — For existing bridges in environments
    described in subsection (a) that are not scheduled for
    rehabilitation within 8 years of enactment, ICCP systems shall
    be retrofitted no later than October 1, 2034, unless the
    Secretary grants an extension based on a State-submitted plan
    demonstrating that rehabilitation or replacement is scheduled
    within a reasonable period.

SECTION 402. ELIGIBLE SUBSTRUCTURE AND SUPERSTRUCTURE ELEMENTS.

    (a) SUBSTRUCTURE. — ICCP systems shall protect, at a minimum,
    the following substructure elements:

        (1) Columns, piers, and pier caps from the top of footing
        to the bottom of superstructure;

        (2) Abutment stems, wingwalls, and backwalls;

        (3) Pile bents and pile caps in tidal or splash zones;

        (4) Pier walls and diaphragm walls; and

        (5) Any other concrete element within 15 feet of the
        roadway surface, within the tidal zone, or within the
        atmospheric marine spray zone.

    (b) BRIDGE DECKS. — ICCP shall be provided for bridge decks
    where:

        (1) The deck has been replaced or overlaid within the
        preceding 5 years and active corrosion was documented
        before replacement;

        (2) Half-cell potential mapping indicates active corrosion
        at more than 10 percent of tested locations; or

        (3) Chloride ion content at the depth of the top mat of
        reinforcing steel exceeds 0.05 percent by weight of
        concrete at more than 20 percent of tested locations.

    (c) ANODE TYPE SELECTION. — The selection of anode type
    (mixed metal oxide titanium mesh, conductive coating, arc-sprayed
    zinc, or discrete anode) shall be based on a life-cycle cost
    analysis considering installation cost, design life, maintenance
    requirements, and compatibility with the structure geometry.

SECTION 403. PERFORMANCE MONITORING AND REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.

    (a) ANNUAL MONITORING. — Each ICCP system installed under
    this Title shall be inspected and tested annually to verify:

        (1) Continuity of electrical circuit to anodes and
        reference electrodes;

        (2) Transformer-rectifier output voltage and current
        within design range;

        (3) Instant-off potential at each reference electrode
        location, measured in accordance with NACE TM0294; and

        (4) Adherence to the 100 mV polarization decay criterion
        or the E-log I criterion as specified in NACE SP0290.

    (b) REMOTE MONITORING. — All ICCP systems on bridges with an
    average daily traffic exceeding 20,000 vehicles shall be
    equipped with remote monitoring and data telemetry systems
    enabling real-time performance data transmission to the
    State bridge management system.

    (c) ANNUAL REPORTING. — Each State shall include in its annual
    report to the Secretary under section 105 of this Act:

        (1) A summary of all ICCP systems in operation within
        the State, including installation date, protected area,
        and current operational status;

        (2) Results of annual monitoring testing, including
        identification of any systems failing to meet performance
        criteria and the corrective actions taken; and

        (3) Energy consumption and maintenance cost data for all
        operating ICCP systems.

SECTION 404. STATE IMPLEMENTATION GRANTS.

    (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall establish a
    competitive grant program to assist States in the initial
    deployment of ICCP systems, including:

        (1) Engineering design and specification development;

        (2) Pilot installation on State-identified priority
        structures;

        (3) Training of State maintenance personnel in ICCP
        system operation and troubleshooting; and

        (4) Acquisition of monitoring and testing equipment.

    (b) FEDERAL SHARE. — The Federal share of grant-funded ICCP
    deployment activities under this section shall not exceed
    80 percent, except that the Secretary may increase the Federal
    share to 90 percent for projects on the Interstate System or
    on State-owned bridges classified as NIPR-D or NIPR-F.

    (c) OREGON DOT MODEL DISSEMINATION. — The Secretary shall
    document the Oregon Department of Transportation's experience
    in protecting 15 concrete bridges through ICCP at a cost of
    approximately $150,000,000—representing a projected savings
    of over $850,000,000 compared to replacement—and shall
    disseminate technical guidance based on this experience to
    all State departments of transportation.

    (d) TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE. — The Secretary shall provide technical
    assistance to States through the FHWA Resource Center and the
    Turner-Fairbank Highway Research Center for ICCP system design,
    specification development, construction inspection, and
    performance evaluation.
