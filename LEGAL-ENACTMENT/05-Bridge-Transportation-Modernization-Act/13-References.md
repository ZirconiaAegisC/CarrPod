                           REFERENCES AND AUTHORITIES

================================================================================

    The following references, standards, and data sources inform
    the findings, mandates, and technical requirements of this Act.
    Inclusion in this list does not constitute incorporation by
    reference into law except where explicitly cited in the text
    of the Act.

A.  BRIDGE CONDITION AND INFRASTRUCTURE ASSESSMENT

    A.1  American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE). 2025 Report
         Card for America's Infrastructure: Bridges. Reston, VA:
         ASCE, 2025. Assigns U.S. bridges a grade of "C+" and
         identifies 46,000 structurally deficient bridges.

    A.2  Federal Highway Administration. National Bridge Inventory
         (NBI) Database. Washington, DC: U.S. Department of
         Transportation. Annual bridge condition data for
         approximately 617,000 bridges. Accessible at the
         FHWA Long-Term Bridge Performance Program portal.

    A.3  Federal Highway Administration. FHWA HIF-22-004,
         Corrosion Protection of Steel Bridges. Washington, DC:
         U.S. Department of Transportation, 2022. Comprehensive
         technical guidance on coating selection, surface
         preparation, application, and inspection for steel
         bridge corrosion protection.

    A.4  Federal Highway Administration. 23 CFR Part 650,
         Subpart C — National Bridge Inspection Standards (NBIS).
         Federal Register. Establishes inspection frequency,
         inspector qualifications, and reporting requirements.

    A.5  Federal Highway Administration. Recording and Coding
         Guide for the Structure Inventory and Appraisal of the
         Nation's Bridges. Report No. FHWA-PD-96-001. Washington,
         DC, 1995 (with updates). Defines the NBI 0–9 condition
         rating scale used to classify bridges as structurally
         deficient.

B.  THERMAL SPRAY ZINC DUPLEX COATING SYSTEMS

    B.1  NCHRP Report 398. Durability of Thermal Spray Coatings
         for Corrosion Protection of Steel Bridges. Washington,
         DC: Transportation Research Board, National Research
         Council, 1998. Foundational research documenting 60–100
         year service life potential for TSZ duplex coatings.

    B.2  AASHTO. Duplex Coating Guidelines for Steel Bridges,
         2026 Edition. Washington, DC: American Association of
         State Highway and Transportation Officials. Current
         standard for TSZ duplex coating specification including
         surface preparation, zinc application parameters, sealer
         selection, topcoat requirements, and quality control.

    B.3  Norwegian Public Roads Administration (Statens vegvesen).
         Thermal Spray Zinc Coating Experience on Norwegian Bridges,
         1965–2025. Oslo, Norway: NPRA Bridge Division. Documents
         60-year performance of TSZ duplex coatings on 62
         Norwegian bridges in marine and cold-climate environments.

    B.4  AWS C2.18-93 (R2008). Guide for the Protection of Steel
         with Thermal Sprayed Coatings of Aluminum and Zinc and
         Their Alloys and Composites. Miami, FL: American Welding
         Society. Application standards for thermal spray coatings.

    B.5  SSPC-CS 23.00/AWS C2.23/NACE No. 12. Specification for
         the Application of Thermal Spray Coatings (Metallizing)
         of Aluminum, Zinc, and Their Alloys and Composites for
         the Corrosion Protection of Steel. Pittsburgh, PA: SSPC;
         Miami, FL: AWS; Houston, TX: NACE International. Joint
         industry standard for thermal spray coating application.

    B.6  ASTM B833. Standard Specification for Zinc and Zinc
         Alloy Wire for Thermal Spraying (Metallizing). West
         Conshohocken, PA: ASTM International. Material standard
         for zinc wire feedstock.

    B.7  ASTM B899. Standard Terminology Relating to Non-Ferrous
         Metals and Alloys. Includes zinc powder specifications
         for thermal spray. West Conshohocken, PA: ASTM International.

    B.8  ASTM D4541. Standard Test Method for Pull-Off Strength
         of Coatings Using Portable Adhesion Testers. West
         Conshohocken, PA: ASTM International. Adhesion testing
         standard referenced in performance verification.

    B.9  ASTM B117. Standard Practice for Operating Salt Spray
         (Fog) Apparatus. West Conshohocken, PA: ASTM International.

    B.10 ASTM G85. Standard Practice for Modified Salt Spray (Fog)
         Testing. West Conshohocken, PA: ASTM International.

    B.11 ASTM D5894. Standard Practice for Cyclic Salt Fog/UV
         Exposure of Painted Metal. West Conshohocken, PA: ASTM
         International. Cyclic corrosion testing for qualification.

    B.12 SSPC-SP 10. Near-White Metal Blast Cleaning. Pittsburgh,
         PA: SSPC — The Society for Protective Coatings. Surface
         preparation standard for TSZ duplex coating application.

    B.13 SSPC-QP 6. Standard Procedure for Evaluating the
         Qualifications of Contractors Who Apply Thermal Spray
         Coatings to Steel Structures. Pittsburgh, PA: SSPC.

C.  CORROSION ECONOMICS

    C.1  NACE International. International Measures of Prevention,
         Application, and Economics of Corrosion Technologies
         (IMPACT) Study. Houston, TX: NACE International, 2016.
         Estimates $13.6 billion annual direct bridge corrosion
         cost in the United States. Direct and indirect corrosion
         costs across all sectors total approximately $450 billion
         annually (2.7% of GDP).

    C.2  Koch, G.H., M.P.H. Brongers, N.G. Thompson, Y.P. Virmani,
         and J.H. Payer. Corrosion Costs and Preventive Strategies
         in the United States. FHWA-RD-01-156. Washington, DC:
         Federal Highway Administration, 2002.

C.3  FHWA. Highway Bridge Replacement and Rehabilitation Program,
         Annual Report. Washington, DC: U.S. DOT. Tracks bridge
         rehabilitation backlog and funding levels.

D.  CATHODIC PROTECTION OF CONCRETE BRIDGES

    D.1  NACE SP0290 (formerly RP0290). Standard Practice:
         Impressed Current Cathodic Protection of Reinforcing
         Steel in Atmospherically Exposed Concrete Structures.
         Houston, TX: AMPP, current edition. Primary design
         standard for ICCP systems on bridge structures.

    D.2  NACE TM0294. Testing of Embeddable Impressed Current
         Anodes for Use in Cathodic Protection of Atmospherically
         Exposed Steel-Reinforced Concrete. Houston, TX: AMPP.
         Testing and monitoring standard for ICCP anodes.

    D.3  ASTM C876. Standard Test Method for Corrosion Potentials
         of Uncoated Reinforcing Steel in Concrete. West
         Conshohocken, PA: ASTM International. Half-cell potential
         mapping standard for corrosion activity assessment.

    D.4  AASHTO T 260. Standard Method of Test for Sampling and
         Testing for Chloride Ion in Concrete and Concrete Raw
         Materials. Washington, DC: AASHTO. Chloride content
         testing standard.

    D.5  Oregon Department of Transportation. ICCP Bridge
         Protection Program: 15-Year Performance Review.
         Salem, OR: ODOT Bridge Engineering Section, 2024.
         Documents protection of 15 bridges at $150 million cost
         versus estimated $1 billion in replacement costs.

    D.6  Sohanghpurwala, A.A., and W.T. Scannell. Long-Term
         Performance of Impressed Current Cathodic Protection
         Systems on Reinforced Concrete Bridges. FHWA-HRT-11-032.
         Washington, DC: FHWA, 2011.

E.  SEISMIC DESIGN AND RETROFIT

    E.1  AASHTO. LRFD Bridge Design Specifications, 10th Edition.
         Washington, DC: AASHTO, 2024. Current standard for
         bridge design including seismic provisions and Seismic
         Design Category classification.

    E.2  AASHTO. Guide Specifications for LRFD Seismic Bridge
         Design, 3rd Edition. Washington, DC: AASHTO, 2023.
         Performance-based seismic design provisions.

    E.3  United States Geological Survey. National Seismic Hazard
         Model (NSHM), 2023 Update. Reston, VA: USGS Earthquake
         Hazards Program. Ground motion data for seismic hazard
         assessment at bridge sites.

    E.4  California Department of Transportation (Caltrans).
         Seismic Retrofit Program Summary. Sacramento, CA:
         Caltrans Division of Engineering Services, 2024.
         Documents retrofit of over 2,200 bridges since 1989
         at a program cost of $15 billion.

    E.5  Bay Area Toll Authority (BATA). Capital Improvement
         Program, Fiscal Year 2025. San Francisco, CA: BATA/MTC.
         Documents $2.3 billion CIP for 10 toll bridge structures,
         including $163 million San Mateo-Hayward substructure
         and $68 million Richmond-San Rafael repainting.

    E.6  FHWA. Seismic Retrofitting Manual for Highway Structures:
         Part 1 — Bridges. FHWA-HRT-06-032. Washington, DC, 2006.

    E.7  FHWA. Seismic Design and Retrofit of Bridges. Training
         course FHWA-NHI-130093 and successors. Washington, DC:
         National Highway Institute.

F.  BRIDGE INFORMATION MODELING AND STRUCTURAL HEALTH MONITORING

    F.1  FHWA. Bridge Information Modeling Standardization.
         FHWA-HIF-17-019 through HIF-17-022. Washington, DC, 2017.

    F.2  buildingSMART International. Industry Foundation Classes
         (IFC) for Bridges. International standard for BrIM data
         exchange.

    F.3  FHWA. Long-Term Bridge Performance (LTBP) Program.
         Washington, DC: Turner-Fairbank Highway Research Center.
         Multi-decade research program on bridge deterioration
         and performance.

    F.4  NIST. Framework for Improving Critical Infrastructure
         Cybersecurity, Version 2.0. Gaithersburg, MD: National
         Institute of Standards and Technology, 2024. Cybersecurity
         framework referenced for the Digital Twin Platform.

G.  WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT

    G.1  FHWA. Bridge Inspector's Reference Manual (BIRM).
         FHWA-NHI-03-001 through 03-003. Washington, DC: National
         Highway Institute, 2002 (with updates). Primary training
         reference for bridge inspectors.

    G.2  AMPP. Coatings Inspector Program (CIP) Level 1, 2, and 3.
         Houston, TX: Association for Materials Protection and
         Performance. Industry-standard coating inspection
         certification program.

    G.3  SSPC. Protective Coatings Specialist (PCS) Certification.
         Pittsburgh, PA: SSPC — The Society for Protective Coatings.

H.  ECONOMIC ANALYSIS METHODOLOGY

    H.1  Congressional Budget Office. The Macroeconomic and
         Budgetary Effects of Federal Investment. Washington, DC:
         CBO, 2016. Documents GDP multipliers for infrastructure
         spending (range: 0.4–2.5 depending on economic conditions).

    H.2  Office of Management and Budget. Circular A-94: Guidelines
         and Discount Rates for Benefit-Cost Analysis of Federal
         Programs. Washington, DC: OMB (current edition). Standard
         methodology for Federal benefit-cost analysis.

    H.3  Office of Management and Budget. Circular A-4: Regulatory
         Analysis. Washington, DC: OMB, 2003 (with 2023 update).
         Guidelines for regulatory impact analysis.

    H.4  U.S. Department of Commerce, Bureau of Economic Analysis.
         RIMS II Input-Output Model. Washington, DC: BEA.
         Regional economic multiplier model used for employment
         impact estimates.
