                    TITLE I — DOMESTIC LITHIUM MINING
================================================================================

    SEC. 101. EXPEDITED PERMITTING FOR DOMESTIC LITHIUM MINES.

        (a) DESIGNATION OF COVERED PROJECTS. — The following lithium
        mining projects located within the United States are hereby
        designated as Covered Projects under Title 41 of the Fixing
        America's Surface Transportation Act (42 U.S.C. 4370m et seq.,
        commonly known as "FAST-41"):

            (1) The Thacker Pass lithium mine and processing facility
                in Humboldt County, Nevada (Lithium Americas Corp.);

            (2) The Rhyolite Ridge lithium-boron project in Esmeralda
                County, Nevada (ioneer Ltd.);

            (3) The Clayton Valley lithium brine extraction projects
                in Esmeralda County, Nevada;

            (4) Any new lithium mine or mine expansion project located
                within the United States with projected annual
                production capacity exceeding ten thousand (10,000)
                metric tons of lithium carbonate equivalent.

        (b) LEAD AGENCY COORDINATION. — The Bureau of Land Management,
        in consultation with the United States Forest Service, the
        Environmental Protection Agency, the Army Corps of Engineers,
        the United States Fish and Wildlife Service, and applicable
        State and tribal authorities, shall serve as the lead Federal
        agency for environmental review and permitting of Covered
        Projects under this section.

        (c) PERMITTING TIMELINES. —

            (1) ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT STATEMENT. — For any Covered
                Project requiring an Environmental Impact Statement
                under the National Environmental Policy Act of 1969
                (42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.), the lead agency shall
                complete the draft Environmental Impact Statement
                within eighteen (18) months and the final Environmental
                Impact Statement and Record of Decision within
                twenty-four (24) months from the date of formal
                application acceptance.

            (2) ENVIRONMENTAL ASSESSMENT. — For any Covered Project
                qualifying for an Environmental Assessment, the lead
                agency shall complete the Environmental Assessment
                and issue a Finding of No Significant Impact or
                determine that an Environmental Impact Statement is
                required within twelve (12) months.

            (3) CATASTROPHIC DELAY REMEDY. — If the lead agency fails
                to meet the deadlines established in this subsection,
                the project proponent may petition the Critical
                Minerals Permitting Council established under Section
                801 of this Act for a mandatory scheduling order.

        (d) CONCURRENT REVIEW. — To the maximum extent practicable,
        all Federal, State, and local agency reviews shall be conducted
        concurrently rather than sequentially. The lead agency shall
        establish a joint review schedule within sixty (60) days of
        application acceptance.

        (e) JUDICIAL REVIEW. —

            (1) Any civil action seeking judicial review of a final
                agency action concerning a Covered Project shall be
                filed not later than one hundred twenty (120) days
                after the date of the final agency action.

            (2) The United States Court of Appeals for the District
                of Columbia Circuit shall have exclusive jurisdiction
                over such civil actions.

            (3) The court shall endeavor to hear and render a final
                determination on any such civil action not later than
                one hundred eighty (180) days after the date on which
                the action is filed.

    SEC. 102. MINING ROYALTY FRAMEWORK.

        (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — Effective as of the date of enactment of
        this Act, any new lithium mining operation conducted on Federal
        lands shall be subject to a gross royalty on the value of
        lithium-bearing mineral production at the following rates:

            (1) BRINE AND SEDIMENTARY DEPOSITS. — A royalty of five
                percent (5%) of gross value for lithium produced from
                brine, clay, or sedimentary deposits located on
                Federal lands.

            (2) HARD ROCK DEPOSITS. — A royalty of eight percent (8%)
                of gross value for lithium produced from hard rock
                (pegmatite) deposits located on Federal lands.

        (b) ROYALTY DISTRIBUTION. — Revenues collected under this
        section shall be distributed as follows:

            (1) Forty-eight percent (48%) to the State in which the
                production occurs;

            (2) Forty-two percent (42%) to the Critical Minerals
                Reclamation and Community Fund established under
                subsection (c) of this section;

            (3) Ten percent (10%) to the Treasury of the United States
                for deficit reduction.

        (c) CRITICAL MINERALS RECLAMATION AND COMMUNITY FUND. —

            (1) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is hereby established in the
                Treasury of the United States a revolving fund to be
                known as the Critical Minerals Reclamation and
                Community Fund.

            (2) USES. — Amounts in the Fund shall be available,
                without further appropriation, for:
                    (A) Environmental remediation and reclamation of
                        abandoned mine lands;
                    (B) Water quality monitoring and restoration in
                        mining-affected watersheds;
                    (C) Community infrastructure investments in
                        mining-impacted counties;
                    (D) Tribal community development grants;
                    (E) Workforce development and training programs
                        for critical mineral industries.

        (d) EXISTING OPERATIONS. — Mining operations that commenced
        commercial production prior to the date of enactment of this
        Act shall be subject to the royalty rates established under
        this section beginning with the first full calendar quarter
        that commences after the date that is five (5) years following
        the date of enactment.

    SEC. 103. ENVIRONMENTAL AND RECLAMATION STANDARDS.

        (a) RECLAMATION PLANS. — As a condition of receiving any
        Federal permit for lithium mining operations, the operator
        shall submit and obtain approval for a comprehensive
        reclamation plan that includes:

            (1) Pre-mining environmental baseline surveys for water
                quality, air quality, soil conditions, wildlife habitat,
                and cultural resources;

            (2) Progressive reclamation requirements to ensure that
                disturbed areas are reclaimed concurrently with active
                mining operations to the maximum extent practicable;

            (3) Post-mining land use plans developed in consultation
                with State, local, and tribal authorities;

            (4) Reclamation performance bonds or other financial
                assurance mechanisms in amounts sufficient to
                guarantee completion of all required reclamation.

        (b) WATER QUALITY. — All lithium mining operations shall
        comply with applicable State and Federal water quality
        standards. Operations employing brine extraction methods
        shall be required to:

            (1) Monitor and report groundwater levels, quality, and
                aquifer characteristics on a quarterly basis;

            (2) Develop and implement an aquifer management plan
                to prevent unacceptable drawdown or contamination;

            (3) Submit to the relevant State agency and the United
                States Geological Survey all monitoring data.

        (c) TAILINGS AND WASTE MANAGEMENT. — Operators shall design,
        construct, and maintain all tailings storage facilities and
        waste rock disposal areas in accordance with best available
        technology standards. Dry-stacking tailings management shall
        be required for all new operations where technically feasible.

        (d) AIR QUALITY. — All lithium processing facilities shall
        install and operate best available control technology for
        particulate matter, sulfur dioxide, and any other criteria
        pollutant identified by the Environmental Protection Agency.

        (e) GREENHOUSE GAS EMISSIONS. — Operators shall report
        annual greenhouse gas emissions from mining and processing
        operations to the Environmental Protection Agency. The
        Secretary of Energy shall, within two (2) years of enactment,
        publish best practice guidance for minimizing greenhouse gas
        emissions from lithium mining and processing operations.

    SEC. 104. TRIBAL CONSULTATION AND COMMUNITY BENEFIT AGREEMENTS.

        (a) TRIBAL CONSULTATION REQUIREMENTS. —

            (1) For any Covered Project that may affect lands of
                cultural, religious, or historical significance to
                an Indian Tribe, or that may affect treaty rights
                or reserved rights, the lead Federal agency and the
                project proponent shall engage in government-to-
                government consultation with the affected Indian
                Tribe in accordance with Executive Order 13175
                (65 Fed. Reg. 67249) and the Department of the
                Interior's Tribal Consultation Policy.

            (2) Consultation shall commence not later than sixty (60)
                days after the filing of a formal permit application
                and shall continue throughout the permitting and
                operational phases of the project.

            (3) The lead Federal agency shall prepare and publish a
                Tribal Consultation Record documenting the
                consultation process, issues raised, and the
                agency's response to each issue.

        (b) COMMUNITY BENEFIT AGREEMENTS. —

            (1) As a condition of receiving any Federal loan guarantee,
                grant, or tax benefit under this Act, a project
                proponent shall negotiate a Community Benefit
                Agreement with the governing body of each county
                in which the proposed mining or processing operation
                is located, and with each affected Indian Tribe.

            (2) Community Benefit Agreements shall address, at a
                minimum:
                    (A) Local hiring preferences and workforce
                        training commitments;
                    (B) Infrastructure investments to mitigate
                        project impacts on local communities;
                    (C) Revenue-sharing arrangements;
                    (D) Environmental monitoring and community
                        health provisions;
                    (E) Post-closure economic transition planning.

        (c) ENFORCEMENT. — Community Benefit Agreements executed under
        this section shall be enforceable as contracts in the Federal
        district court for the district in which the project is located.
