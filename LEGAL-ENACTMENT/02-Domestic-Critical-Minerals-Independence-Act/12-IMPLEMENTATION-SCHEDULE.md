                    TITLE X — IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================

    SEC. 1001. CRITICAL MINERALS SUPPLY CHAIN OFFICE.

        (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is hereby established within the
        Department of Energy the Critical Minerals Supply Chain
        Office to coordinate the implementation of this Act across
        all Federal departments and agencies.

        (b) DIRECTOR. — The Office shall be headed by a Director,
        appointed by the President with the advice and consent of
        the Senate, who shall report directly to the Secretary of
        Energy.

        (c) DUTIES. — The Director shall:

            (1) Coordinate all Federal activities related to the
                development of domestic critical mineral supply
                chains;

            (2) Develop and maintain the National Critical Minerals
                Strategy, to be updated every three (3) years;

            (3) Administer grant, loan, and incentive programs
                established under this Act;

            (4) Serve as the primary liaison between the Federal
                Government and private sector entities engaged in
                critical mineral activities;

            (5) Prepare the annual reports required under Section
                1002 of this Title.

        (d) ADVISORY COMMITTEE. — The Director shall convene a
        Critical Minerals Supply Chain Advisory Committee with
        membership from industry, labor, environmental organizations,
        tribal governments, academia, and State and local governments
        to provide guidance on program implementation.

    SEC. 1002. ANNUAL REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.

        (a) ANNUAL REPORT. — Not later than March 31 of each year
        beginning with the first full calendar year after the date
        of enactment, the Director of the Critical Minerals Supply
        Chain Office shall submit to Congress a comprehensive annual
        report on the implementation of this Act.

        (b) CONTENTS. — Each annual report shall include:

            (1) DOMESTIC PRODUCTION. — Data on domestic lithium
                extraction volumes, processing throughput, and
                manufacturing output, including year-over-year
                changes and projections;

            (2) IMPORT DEPENDENCE. — Updated assessment of United
                States import dependence for lithium and other
                critical battery minerals;

            (3) MARKET ANALYSIS. — Current and projected lithium
                supply, demand, and pricing, including global
                market share data by nation;

            (4) PROGRAM STATUS. — Status of each grant, loan, and
                incentive program established under this Act,
                including obligations, expenditures, and outcomes;

            (5) PERMITTING PROGRESS. — Status of covered project
                permitting, including timelines and compliance;

            (6) RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT. — Summary of research
                and development outcomes and technology milestones
                achieved;

            (7) INTERNATIONAL ENGAGEMENT. — Summary of Critical
                Minerals Agreement negotiations, partnership
                activities, and diversification progress;

            (8) RECOMMENDATIONS. — Recommendations for statutory,
                regulatory, or programmatic changes to improve
                program effectiveness.

        (c) PUBLIC AVAILABILITY. — The annual report shall be made
        publicly available on the Department of Energy website not
        later than thirty (30) days after submission to Congress.

    SEC. 1003. INSPECTOR GENERAL OVERSIGHT.

        (a) AUDITS. — The Inspector General of the Department of
        Energy shall conduct biennial audits of the programs and
        activities authorized under this Act.

        (b) FOCUS AREAS. — Audits shall assess:

            (1) Compliance with statutory and regulatory requirements;

            (2) Effectiveness of financial controls and prevention
                of fraud, waste, and abuse;

            (3) Achievement of program objectives and performance
                metrics;

            (4) Environmental compliance and monitoring;

            (5) Domestic content and recycled content verification
                processes.

        (c) REPORT. — The Inspector General shall submit audit reports
        to Congress and shall make such reports publicly available.

    SEC. 1004. IMPLEMENTATION PHASES AND MILESTONES.

        (a) PHASE ONE: YEARS 1-2 (2027-2028) — FOUNDATION.

            Milestone 1.1: Critical Minerals Supply Chain Office
                fully operational with Director confirmed (Q2 2027).

            Milestone 1.2: All advisory committees and interagency
                working groups convened (Q3 2027).

            Milestone 1.3: FAST-41 expansion regulations promulgated
                and Critical Minerals Permitting Council convened
                (Q4 2027).

            Milestone 1.4: Initial DLE research and development
                grants awarded; not fewer than ten (10) projects
                funded (Q4 2027).

            Milestone 1.5: National Battery Collection Network
                design completed and State Permitting Assistance
                Program operational (Q2 2028).

            Milestone 1.6: Bilateral Critical Minerals Agreement
                negotiations commenced with Australia, Canada,
                Chile, and Argentina (Q3 2028).

            Milestone 1.7: Strategic Lithium Stockpile initial
                acquisition authority exercised; first procurement
                contracts awarded (Q4 2028).

        (b) PHASE TWO: YEARS 3-5 (2029-2031) — BUILD-OUT.

            Milestone 2.1: Not fewer than two (2) new domestic
                lithium mining operations permitted and under
                construction (Q2 2029).

            Milestone 2.2: Smackover Formation regional DLE
                demonstration facility under construction;
                Salton Sea DLE projects at not fewer than two (2)
                geothermal facilities under construction (Q4 2029).

            Milestone 2.3: Not fewer than two (2) domestic lithium
                hydroxide conversion facilities under construction
                or commissioned (Q2 2030).

            Milestone 2.4: Smackover DLE demonstration facility
                operational; minimum production capacity of five
                thousand (5,000) metric tons LCE per year (Q4 2030).

            Milestone 2.5: National Battery Collection Network
                operational in all ten (10) EPA regions (Q4 2030).

            Milestone 2.6: Strategic Lithium Stockpile reaches
                ninety-day (90-day) supply target (Q4 2030).

            Milestone 2.7: First cathode active material
                manufacturing facility using domestically sourced
                lithium commissioned (Q2 2031).

            Milestone 2.8: Not fewer than three (3) commercial-scale
                battery recycling facilities operational (Q4 2031).

        (c) PHASE THREE: YEARS 6-10 (2032-2036) — SUPPLY CHAIN
            MATURITY.

            Milestone 3.1: Domestic lithium mine production reaches
                not less than two hundred thousand (200,000) metric
                tons LCE per year (Q4 2032).

            Milestone 3.2: Domestic lithium hydroxide conversion
                capacity reaches not less than one hundred twenty-
                five thousand (125,000) metric tons per year (Q4
                2033).

            Milestone 3.3: Strategic Lithium Stockpile reaches
                one hundred twenty-day (120-day) supply target
                (Q4 2033).

            Milestone 3.4: Domestic cathode active material
                manufacturing capacity reaches three hundred
                thousand (300,000) metric tons per year (Q4 2034).

            Milestone 3.5: Recycled content mandates under Section
                403 come fully into force (Q1 2035).

            Milestone 3.6: Domestic lithium supply chain
                independence achieved: not less than eighty-five
                percent (85%) of annual domestic lithium demand
                satisfied by domestically extracted, processed,
                manufactured, and recycled materials (Q4 2035).

            Milestone 3.7: Strategic Lithium Stockpile reaches
                full one hundred eighty-day (180-day) supply target
                (Q4 2035).

            Milestone 3.8: Comprehensive decennial implementation
                review completed; reauthorization recommendations
                submitted to Congress (Q2 2036).

        (d) CONTINGENCY PROVISIONS. — If at any annual review the
        Director determines that the United States is not on track
        to achieve a milestone by its target date, the Director shall
        submit to Congress within sixty (60) days:

            (1) An analysis of the causes of delay;

            (2) A remediation plan to achieve the milestone;

            (3) Identification of any additional authorities or
                appropriations required;

            (4) Revised milestone dates if necessary.

================================================================================

    SEC. 1005. SUNSET AND REAUTHORIZATION.

        (a) SUNSET. — Except as otherwise provided in this Act, the
        authorities granted under this Act shall terminate on
        September 30, 2036.

        (b) REAUTHORIZATION REVIEW. — Not later than June 30, 2035,
        the Comptroller General of the United States shall submit
        to Congress a comprehensive evaluation of the implementation
        and effectiveness of this Act, together with recommendations
        regarding reauthorization.

        (c) SAVINGS CLAUSE. — Termination of authorities under this
        Act shall not affect any contract, grant, loan guarantee, or
        other obligation entered into prior to the termination date.
