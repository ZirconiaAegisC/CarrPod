================================================================================
              TITLE III — HARBOR DEEPENING AND
              NAVIGATION CHANNEL IMPROVEMENTS
================================================================================

  Authorization: $20,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 301. HARBOR DEEPENING PRIORITIZATION.
================================================================================

  (a) PRIORITY LIST. — The Secretary shall, not later than 12 months after
      the date of enactment of this Act, publish a prioritized list of
      harbor deepening projects to be undertaken under this title, based on:

    (1) Current and projected vessel draft requirements, with projects
        to achieve a depth of not less than 50 feet mean lower low water
        (MLLW) receiving highest priority;

    (2) Tonnage and value of cargo handled, with nationally significant
        ports receiving priority;

    (3) Beneficial cost ratio, expressed as the ratio of transportation
        cost savings and economic benefits to project construction cost;

    (4) Supply chain criticality, including the port's role in energy
        imports and exports, military logistics, and agricultural
        commodity exports;

    (5) State of readiness, including completed feasibility studies and
        environmental documentation.

  (b) PRIORITY PROJECTS. — The following projects shall be included in the
      priority list developed under subsection (a):

    (1) Port of Savannah, Georgia: deepening from 47 feet to 52 feet;
    (2) Port of Charleston, South Carolina: deepening to 54 feet;
    (3) Port of Mobile, Alabama: deepening to 52 feet;
    (4) Port of Houston, Texas: deepening the Houston Ship Channel to
        50 feet;
    (5) Port of New York and New Jersey: deepening Ambrose Channel to
        55 feet;
    (6) Port of Long Beach-Los Angeles: deepening to accommodate vessels
        exceeding 55-foot draft;
    (7) Port of Seattle-Tacoma: deepening to 53 feet;
    (8) Port of Norfolk, Virginia: deepening to 55 feet.

  (c) PROJECT ACCELERATION. — For projects on the priority list, the
      Secretary shall:

    (1) Complete feasibility studies within 2 years;
    (2) Complete environmental review and permitting within 3 years;
    (3) Commence construction within 4 years;
    (4) Achieve project completion within 10 years.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $12,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 302. NAVIGATION CHANNEL WIDENING.
================================================================================

  (a) WIDENING REQUIREMENT. — For each harbor deepening project authorized
      under section 301, the Secretary shall evaluate and, where
      economically justified, execute channel widening to a width of not
      less than 1,000 feet in straight reaches and 1,400 feet in turning
      basins and bendway sections.

  (b) TWO-WAY TRAFFIC CAPABILITY. — Channel widening projects shall, to
      the maximum extent economically justified, provide for simultaneous
      two-way passage of the design vessel without tidal or daylight
      restrictions.

  (c) WIDENING BENEFITS. — In evaluating the economic justification for
      channel widening, the Secretary shall consider:

    (1) Vessel transit time savings and port throughput capacity increase;
    (2) Vessel collision and grounding risk reduction;
    (3) Accommodation of larger and wider-beam vessel designs;
    (4) Reduction in pilotage and tug assist requirements;
    (5) National security and military vessel passage requirements.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 303. BENEFICIAL USE OF DREDGED MATERIAL.
================================================================================

  (a) BENEFICIAL USE REQUIREMENT. — Not less than 60 percent of all
      dredged material from projects authorized under this title shall
      be placed for beneficial use, including:

    (1) Beach nourishment and dune construction;
    (2) Wetland, marsh, and island creation and restoration;
    (3) Levee, berm, and flood protection structure construction;
    (4) Habitat restoration and enhancement (bird nesting islands,
        fish habitat, oyster reef substrate);
    (5) Brownfield redevelopment and port expansion fill.

  (b) REGIONAL SEDIMENT MANAGEMENT. — The Secretary shall establish
      Regional Sediment Management Plans for each Corps of Engineers
      coastal division that:

    (1) Identify beneficial use placement opportunities for dredged
        material from navigation projects within the region;

    (2) Coordinate dredging schedules with beneficial use project
        construction schedules;

    (3) Quantify the environmental and economic benefits of beneficial
        use compared with offshore or confined disposal.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 304. DREDGING FLEET MODERNIZATION.
================================================================================

  (a) FLEET ASSESSMENT. — The Secretary shall, not later than 12 months
      after enactment, complete an assessment of the Army Corps of
      Engineers hopper dredge, cutterhead dredge, and support vessel
      fleet, quantifying:

    (1) Fleet capacity in cubic yards per year relative to projected
        annual dredging demand;

    (2) Average vessel age, maintenance status, and remaining service
        life;

    (3) Technological obsolescence relative to current best available
        dredging technology;

    (4) Domestic dredging industry capacity and capability to
        supplement Corps fleet operations.

  (b) FLEET ACQUISITION. — The Secretary is authorized to acquire:

    (1) Not fewer than 4 new or replacement trailing suction hopper
        dredges of not less than 8,000 cubic yard hopper capacity;

    (2) Not fewer than 3 new or replacement cutterhead suction dredges
        with a dredging depth capability of not less than 60 feet;

    (3) Associated support vessels including survey vessels, tender
        vessels, and pipeline deployment vessels.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 305. INTERAGENCY COORDINATION AND PERMITTING REFORM.
================================================================================

  (a) LEAD AGENCY DESIGNATION. — The Secretary is designated as the lead
      Federal agency for environmental review and permitting of navigation
      improvement projects authorized under this title.

  (b) CONCURRENT REVIEW. — All Federal, State, and local agency reviews
      and permit decisions shall be conducted concurrently rather than
      sequentially, with a target of completing all environmental review
      and permitting within 3 years of initiation.

  (c) PERMITTING DASHBOARD. — The Secretary shall maintain a publicly
      accessible online permitting dashboard for each project authorized
      under this title, displaying the status of each required permit,
      review, and approval.
