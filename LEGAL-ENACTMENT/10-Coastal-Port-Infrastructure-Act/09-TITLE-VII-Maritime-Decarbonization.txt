SECTION 701. ALTERNATIVE MARITIME FUEL INFRASTRUCTURE.
================================================================================

  (a) FUEL INFRASTRUCTURE GRANTS. — The Secretary of Transportation, through
      the Maritime Administration, shall establish a competitive grant
      program to fund:

    (1) Green methanol production, storage, and bunkering facilities at
        United States seaports, with a target of methanol bunkering
        capacity at not fewer than 10 ports by 2031;

    (2) Green ammonia storage, handling, and bunkering infrastructure,
        including cryogenic storage, ammonia-to-hydrogen crackers,
        and safety systems compliant with international codes;

    (3) Liquid hydrogen storage and bunkering infrastructure at ports
        designated as green shipping corridor hubs;

    (4) Battery charging and swapping infrastructure for electric harbor
        craft, ferries, and short-sea vessels;

    (5) On-site renewable hydrogen production through electrolysis
        powered by dedicated renewable energy generation.

  (b) FUEL STANDARD. — All alternative fuels dispensed at facilities
      funded under this section shall:

    (1) Achieve lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions reductions of not
        less than 70 percent compared with conventional marine distillate
        fuel, as determined under a methodology developed by the
        Administrator;

    (2) Be produced in compliance with applicable environmental,
        safety, and labor standards, including Build America, Buy
        America requirements.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

SECTION 702. VESSEL ELECTRIFICATION AND HYBRIDIZATION.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT AND LOAN PROGRAM. — The Maritime Administration shall
      establish a program providing grants and loan guarantees for:

    (1) Construction and retrofit of battery-electric and hybrid-electric
        harbor craft including tugboats, pilot boats, crew transfer
        vessels, and workboats;

    (2) Construction and retrofit of battery-electric and hybrid-electric
        ferries for passenger and vehicle transport;

    (3) Design and construction of fully electric short-sea container
        vessels for routes not exceeding 200 nautical miles;

    (4) Fuel cell integration for auxiliary power and hotel loads on
        ocean-going vessels.

  (b) DOMESTIC SHIPYARD INCENTIVE. — Grants and loan guarantees under this
      section shall be available exclusively for vessels constructed or
      retrofitted in United States shipyards.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

SECTION 703. GREEN SHIPPING CORRIDORS.
================================================================================

  (a) CORRIDOR DESIGNATION. — The Secretary of Transportation, in
      coordination with the Secretary of State, shall:

    (1) Designate not fewer than 5 international green shipping corridors
        connecting United States ports to major trading partner ports,
        prioritizing corridors with:

        (A) High vessel traffic volumes and emissions;
        (B) Proximity to alternative fuel production capacity;
        (C) Availability of port-side bunkering infrastructure;
        (D) Cooperation agreements with partner nations.

    (2) Establish domestic green shipping corridors connecting major
        ports within the United States, including:

        (A) Los Angeles-Long Beach to Honolulu;
        (B) Seattle-Tacoma to Anchorage;
        (C) New York-New Jersey to San Juan, Puerto Rico;
        (D) Houston to Tampa and Miami.

  (b) CORRIDOR REQUIREMENTS. — Each green shipping corridor shall:

    (1) Be served exclusively by zero-emission vessels by 2035, with
        interim targets of 15 percent by 2031;

    (2) Provide full alternative fuel bunkering infrastructure at
        all designated corridor ports;

    (3) Achieve total elimination of greenhouse gas emissions and
        significant reduction of criteria pollutant emissions along
        the corridor.

  (c) INTERNATIONAL COOPERATION. — The Secretary of State shall pursue
      bilateral and multilateral agreements with trading partner nations
      to establish international green shipping corridors consistent with
      the Clydebank Declaration and the International Maritime
      Organization's greenhouse gas strategy.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

SECTION 704. MARITIME EMISSIONS MONITORING AND REPORTING.
================================================================================

  (a) MONITORING REQUIREMENT. — Not later than 24 months after enactment,
      the Administrator shall promulgate regulations requiring:

    (1) All vessels calling at United States ports to report annual
        carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, black carbon, sulfur
        oxide, and nitrogen oxide emissions;

    (2) Real-time emissions monitoring on vessels exceeding 5,000 gross
        tonnage using continuous emissions monitoring systems or
        equivalent technology;

    (3) Public disclosure of vessel-level emissions data through an
        online registry.

  (b) FUEL LIFECYCLE ACCOUNTING. — The Administrator shall develop a
      well-to-wake lifecycle greenhouse gas accounting methodology for
      maritime fuels consistent with the International Maritime
      Organization's guidelines on lifecycle GHG intensity of marine
      fuels.

SECTION 705. RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT FOR ZERO-EMISSION VESSELS.
================================================================================

  (a) RESEARCH PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the
      Maritime Administration, shall establish a Zero-Emission Vessel
      Research and Development Program funding:

    (1) Advanced battery chemistries with energy densities exceeding
        500 watt-hours per kilogram for marine applications;

    (2) High-temperature proton exchange membrane and solid oxide fuel
        cell systems for maritime use, including maritime fuel
        processing and reformer systems;

    (3) Ammonia-fueled internal combustion engines and ammonia fuel
        cell systems;

    (4) Onboard carbon capture systems for residual fossil fuel vessel
        emissions during the transition period;

    (5) Wind-assisted propulsion technologies including rotor sails,
        rigid wing sails, and kite propulsion;

    (6) Air lubrication and hull friction reduction technologies.

  (b) DEMONSTRATION VESSEL. — The Maritime Administration shall fund the
      design, construction, and operation of a United States-flagged
      zero-emission demonstration vessel of not less than 500 TEU
      capacity, to serve as a test platform for integrated zero-emission
      propulsion technologies.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
