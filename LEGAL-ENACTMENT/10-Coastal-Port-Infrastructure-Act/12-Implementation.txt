SECTION 1001. IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE AND MILESTONES.
================================================================================

  (a) YEAR 1 (FISCAL YEAR 2027):

    (1) QUARTER 1 (Oct-Dec):
        — Establishment of interagency coastal infrastructure task force
        — National Coastal Vulnerability Assessment initiated
        — Harbor deepening priority list published
        — Notices of Funding Opportunity for Title I sea wall grants
          and Title IV coastal ecosystem restoration published
        — Port Resilience Hub designation criteria published

    (2) QUARTER 2 (Jan-Mar):
        — First awards under Title II shore power infrastructure grants
        — Title III harbor deepening feasibility studies commenced for
          priority ports
        — Title IV wetland restoration project selection completed
        — Title VI offshore wind port grant applications accepted
        — Risk Rating 3.0 implementation plan published

    (3) QUARTER 3 (Apr-Jun):
        — Title I urban flood barrier feasibility studies initiated
        — Title II zero-emission cargo handling equipment first grants
          awarded
        — Title VII green shipping corridor designations announced
        — Title VIII flood mapping update program launched
        — Dredging fleet assessment completed

    (4) QUARTER 4 (Jul-Sep):
        — Title III dredging fleet acquisition contracts awarded
        — Title V resilience master plan guidance issued
        — Title VII alternative fuel infrastructure first round awards
        — First annual report to Congress submitted
        — Coastal flood mapping program initial data release

  (b) YEAR 2 (FISCAL YEAR 2028):

    (1) QUARTER 1-2:
        — National Coastal Vulnerability Assessment completed
        — Title I prioritized sea wall construction commenced
        — Five offshore wind staging port projects selected
        — Green shipping corridor international agreements negotiated
        — Community Rating System reform rulemaking completed

    (2) QUARTER 3-4:
        — Title III first harbor deepening construction commenced
        — Title IV oyster reef restoration projects initiated
        — Title V storm surge protection projects commenced at
          Resilience Hubs
        — Title VII zero-emission vessel demonstration design contract
          awarded
        — Risk Rating 3.0 premium updates implemented

  (c) YEAR 3 (FISCAL YEAR 2029):

    — At least 25 percent of major port shore power installations
      completed
    — First green shipping corridor operational
    — Maritime emissions monitoring regulations promulgated
    — Managed retreat pilot programs initiated in high-risk communities
    — Blue carbon registry operational

  (d) YEAR 5 (FISCAL YEAR 2031):

    — At least 50 percent of priority harbor deepening projects
      under construction
    — At least 50 shore power berths operational nationwide
    — First zero-emission demonstration vessel deployed
    — Green port certification program fully launched
    — Midpoint program evaluation completed by GAO

  (e) YEAR 10 (FISCAL YEAR 2036):

    — All priority harbor deepening projects completed
    — Mandatory cold ironing effective at all major ports
    — Zero-emission cargo handling equipment mandate in effect
    — All green shipping corridors fully operational
    — Net zero coastal wetland loss achieved
    — Comprehensive program evaluation and sunset review
    — Final report to Congress

================================================================================
SECTION 1002. ANNUAL REPORT TO CONGRESS.
================================================================================

  (a) REQUIREMENT. — Not later than September 30 of each fiscal year, the
      Secretary of Transportation, in coordination with the Secretaries
      of the Army, Homeland Security, the Interior, and Energy, and the
      Administrators of NOAA and EPA, shall submit to the appropriate
      congressional committees a comprehensive report.

  (b) CONTENTS. — Each report under subsection (a) shall include:

    (1) Detailed accounting of all funds appropriated, obligated, and
        expended during the preceding fiscal year, disaggregated by
        title, program, and State;

    (2) Quantitative performance metrics including:
        (A) Linear feet of sea wall and coastal protection constructed;
        (B) Shore power berths completed and vessel connections logged;
        (C) Cargo handling equipment electrified;
        (D) Cubic yards of harbor deepening dredging completed;
        (E) Acres of coastal wetlands restored or created;
        (F) Alternative fuel bunkering events completed at United States
            ports;
        (G) Vessel emissions reductions measured in metric tons of
            carbon dioxide equivalent;
        (H) NFIP policies adjusted under Risk Rating 3.0;

    (3) Assessment of progress toward statutory targets and milestones;

    (4) Identification of regulatory, administrative, or statutory
        barriers impeding program implementation;

    (5) Recommendations for legislative or administrative actions;

    (6) Environmental justice outcomes analysis, including distribution
        of benefits to port-adjacent communities.

================================================================================
SECTION 1003. COMPTROLLER GENERAL AUDITS.
================================================================================

  (a) BIENNIAL AUDIT. — The Comptroller General shall conduct a
      comprehensive audit of programs authorized under this Act not less
      frequently than once every 2 years.

  (b) SCOPE. — Each audit shall evaluate:

    (1) Efficiency and effectiveness of program administration;
    (2) Compliance with statutory requirements, including Buy America
        provisions, environmental review requirements, and environmental
        justice mandates;
    (3) Accuracy of performance metrics reported to Congress;
    (4) Adequacy of internal controls preventing waste, fraud, and abuse;
    (5) Whether programs achieve stated objectives cost-effectively.

  (c) PUBLIC AVAILABILITY. — All audits shall be publicly available on the
      GAO website within 30 days of transmission to Congress.

================================================================================
SECTION 1004. ECONOMIC IMPACT ASSESSMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) INDEPENDENT ASSESSMENT. — Not later than 36 months after enactment and
      every 3 years thereafter, the Secretary of Transportation shall
      contract with an independent entity to conduct a comprehensive
      economic impact assessment.

  (b) ASSESSMENT SCOPE. — The assessment shall evaluate:

    (1) Direct, indirect, and induced economic impacts of investments,
        including GDP contributions, employment impacts by occupation and
        geography, wage and income effects, and State and local tax
        revenue impacts;

    (2) Economic value of property and infrastructure protected from
        sea level rise and storm surge;

    (3) Transportation cost savings from harbor deepening and port
        efficiency improvements;

    (4) Supply chain resilience benefits and avoided congestion costs;

    (5) Public health benefits and cost savings from port emissions
        reductions in port-adjacent communities;

    (6) Return on investment analysis for each major program category.

  (c) ECONOMIC BENCHMARKS. — The assessment shall evaluate progress against:

    (1) Creation of not fewer than 250,000 jobs attributable to this Act
        by fiscal year 2036;
    (2) Cumulative GDP contribution of not less than $150 billion;
    (3) Protection of not less than $500 billion in coastal property
        value from chronic flooding;
    (4) Port congestion cost reduction of at least $50 billion;
    (5) Greenhouse gas emissions reduction of 60 percent in port
        operations relative to 2025 baseline.

================================================================================
SECTION 1005. BUY AMERICA REQUIREMENTS.
================================================================================

  (a) DOMESTIC CONTENT. — All iron and steel products, manufactured
      products, and construction materials used in projects funded under
      this Act shall be produced in the United States, consistent with
      the Build America, Buy America Act (Title IX of Division G of
      Public Law 117-58).

  (b) WAIVER PROCESS. — The head of the relevant Federal agency may waive
      requirements of subsection (a) only upon a written determination,
      published in the Federal Register for public comment, that:

    (1) Applying the requirement would be inconsistent with the public
        interest;
    (2) Materials of satisfactory quality and sufficient quantity are not
        produced in the United States; or
    (3) Inclusion of domestic material would increase overall project
        cost by more than 25 percent.

  (c) REPORTING. — Any waiver issued under subsection (b) shall be reported
      to the appropriate congressional committees within 15 days of issuance
      and included in the annual report required under section 1002.

  (d) DOMESTIC SHIPBUILDING PREFERENCE. — For any project involving vessel
      construction or retrofit, grants and loan guarantees shall be
      conditioned on construction or retrofit occurring in a United
      States shipyard, consistent with the Jones Act (46 U.S.C. 55102).
