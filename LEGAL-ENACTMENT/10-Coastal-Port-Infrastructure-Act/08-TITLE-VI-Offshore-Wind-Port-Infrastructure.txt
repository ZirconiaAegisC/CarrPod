================================================================================
              TITLE VI — OFFSHORE WIND PORT
              INFRASTRUCTURE
================================================================================

  Authorization: Subsumed within administrative allocations

================================================================================
SECTION 601. OFFSHORE WIND STAGING AND ASSEMBLY PORTS.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Transportation, through the
      Maritime Administration, shall establish a competitive grant program
      to fund development of ports and waterfront facilities for offshore
      wind turbine staging, assembly, manufacturing, and deployment,
      including:

    (1) Heavy-lift wharves and quaysides with bearing capacities of not
        less than 5,000 pounds per square foot for turbine component
        staging;

    (2) Laydown areas of not less than 50 acres with unobstructed access
        to the wharf face;

    (3) Deepening of approach channels and berth pockets to accommodate
        wind turbine installation vessels (WTIVs) and heavy-lift
        transport vessels;

    (4) Onshore assembly halls for turbine nacelle integration, tower
        section assembly, and blade handling;

    (5) Navigational aids, mooring systems, and vessel traffic management
        for installation vessel operations.

  (b) NATIONAL NETWORK. — The Secretary shall designate a national network
      of offshore wind staging ports sufficient to support the deployment
      of not less than 30 gigawatts of offshore wind energy capacity by
      2030 and 110 gigawatts by 2050, distributed along the Atlantic,
      Pacific, and Gulf coasts.

  (c) PRIORITY PORTS. — Priority shall be given to ports that:

    (1) Are located within 100 nautical miles of designated offshore
        wind lease areas;

    (2) Possess or commit to developing heavy-lift crane capacity of
        not less than 800 metric tons;

    (3) Include plans for manufacturing and fabrication facilities
        within the port complex.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 602. SPECIALIZED VESSEL AND CRANE INFRASTRUCTURE.
================================================================================

  (a) WTIV INCENTIVES. — The Secretary of Transportation shall establish
      a program providing loan guarantees and grants to support:

    (1) Construction of United States-flagged wind turbine installation
        vessels compliant with the Jones Act (46 U.S.C. 55102);

    (2) Construction of United States-flagged service operation vessels
        (SOVs) and crew transfer vessels (CTVs) for offshore wind
        operations and maintenance;

    (3) Construction of United States-flagged cable-lay vessels and
        rock placement vessels for offshore wind.

  (b) HEAVY-LIFT CRANE PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall provide grants to
      ports for acquisition and installation of heavy-lift cranes with:

    (1) Lifting capacity of not less than 1,000 metric tons;
    (2) Outreach sufficient to load and unload turbine components
        from installation vessels;
    (3) Mobile or crawler configurations permitting flexible port
        yard layouts.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 603. SUBMARINE CABLE LANDING FACILITIES.
================================================================================

  (a) CABLE LANDING INFRASTRUCTURE. — The Secretary of Energy, in
      coordination with the Secretary of Transportation, shall establish
      a grant program for:

    (1) Construction of submarine power cable landing points and
        horizontal directional drilling duct banks at designated
        offshore wind ports;

    (2) Onshore cable transition facilities, substations, and
        interconnection points for offshore wind export cables;

    (3) Cable storage yards, jointing facilities, and cable
        deployment equipment.

  (b) INTERCONNECTION COORDINATION. — The Federal Energy Regulatory
      Commission shall establish an expedited interconnection review
      process for offshore wind projects utilizing ports funded under
      this title.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 604. WORKFORCE TRAINING FOR OFFSHORE WIND LOGISTICS.
================================================================================

  (a) TRAINING PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with
      the Secretary of Transportation, shall establish:

    (1) Registered apprenticeship programs for offshore wind port
        logistics occupations including heavy-lift crane operators,
        stevedores, vessel crew, and terminal managers;

    (2) Curriculum development grants to maritime academies, community
        colleges, and technical schools for offshore wind logistics
        programs;

    (3) Pre-apprenticeship programs targeting underrepresented
        communities in port-adjacent neighborhoods.

  (b) SAFETY STANDARDS. — The Secretary of Labor, through the Occupational
      Safety and Health Administration, shall develop safety standards
      specific to offshore wind port operations addressing:

    (1) Heavy-lift operations and rigging;
    (2) Blade and tower component handling;
    (3) Working at height and over water;
    (4) Hazardous energy control and electrical safety.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 605. ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING FOR OFFSHORE WIND.
================================================================================

  (a) MONITORING PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall establish a
      comprehensive environmental monitoring program for offshore
      wind development areas and associated port infrastructure,
      addressing:

    (1) Marine mammal presence, distribution, and behavioral response
        to construction and operation noise;
    (2) Avian and bat collision risk and population-level impacts;
    (3) Benthic habitat changes from turbine foundation installation
        and cable burial;
    (4) Fisheries displacement and reef effects of turbine foundations;
    (5) Cumulative effects of multiple wind energy areas on migratory
        species.

  (b) PUBLIC DATA. — All monitoring data shall be made publicly available
      through an online data portal within 90 days of collection.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
