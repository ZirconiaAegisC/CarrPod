================================================================================
              TITLE I — SEA WALL AND COASTAL ARMORING
              INFRASTRUCTURE
================================================================================

  Authorization: $30,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 101. NATIONAL COASTAL VULNERABILITY ASSESSMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) COMPREHENSIVE ASSESSMENT. — Not later than 24 months after the date
      of enactment of this Act, the Administrator, in coordination with the
      United States Army Corps of Engineers and the Federal Emergency
      Management Agency, shall complete a National Coastal Vulnerability
      Assessment that:

    (1) Maps and quantifies coastal flood risk under multiple sea level
        rise scenarios (0.5 meters, 1.0 meters, and 2.0 meters) for all
        coastal counties and municipalities;

    (2) Identifies critical infrastructure at risk, including ports,
        airports, energy facilities, wastewater treatment plants,
        transportation corridors, hospitals, and military installations;

    (3) Estimates the replacement cost and economic value of at-risk
        property and infrastructure;

    (4) Identifies communities facing disproportionate risk due to
        socioeconomic vulnerability, historic disinvestment, and
        limited adaptive capacity;

    (5) Prioritizes locations for coastal protection investment based
        on risk exposure, population density, economic value protected
        per dollar invested, and environmental justice considerations.

  (b) PUBLIC ACCESS. — The Assessment shall be made publicly available
      through an interactive online mapping platform updated not less
      frequently than every 5 years.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for the Assessment
      under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 102. SEA WALL AND LEVEE CONSTRUCTION GRANTS.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Secretary, in coordination with the Administrator,
      shall establish a competitive grant program providing Federal cost-
      share funding for:

    (1) Construction, rehabilitation, and elevation of sea walls,
        levees, floodwalls, and storm surge barriers;

    (2) Tide gates, pump stations, and drainage infrastructure for
        management of interior flooding behind flood defense structures;

    (3) Elevation, relocation, or floodproofing of critical public
        infrastructure including water and wastewater treatment plants,
        electrical substations, and emergency response facilities.

  (b) DESIGN STANDARDS. — All projects funded under this section shall:

    (1) Be designed to withstand a 100-year storm event under 2050 sea
        level rise projections at minimum;

    (2) Incorporate sea level rise adaptation allowances permitting future
        heightening or retrofitting without complete reconstruction;

    (3) Include monitoring instrumentation measuring structural
        performance, water levels, and wave conditions.

  (c) URBAN COASTAL FLOOD BARRIER PROGRAM. — Not less than 30 percent of
      funds authorized under this section shall be reserved for projects
      protecting urban areas with populations exceeding 100,000 and
      aggregate at-risk property value exceeding $10 billion, including:

    (1) New York-New Jersey Harbor storm surge barrier system;
    (2) Miami-Dade County coastal flood protection system;
    (3) Galveston Bay and Houston Ship Channel surge barrier;
    (4) Charleston Peninsula flood protection system;
    (5) Norfolk and Hampton Roads regional flood protection.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $15,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 103. LIVING SHORELINE AND HYBRID COASTAL PROTECTION.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Administrator, in coordination with the
      Secretary, shall establish a Living Shoreline and Hybrid Coastal
      Protection Program providing grants for projects that integrate
      natural and nature-based features with traditional engineered
      structures, including:

    (1) Oyster reef breakwaters and artificial reef structures attenuating
        wave energy while providing marine habitat;

    (2) Vegetated berms, marsh terraces, and sills combining sediment
        trapping with structural protection;

    (3) Mangrove and salt marsh restoration in the intertidal zone
        fronting engineered protection structures;

    (4) Beach and dune systems with buried structural cores providing
        backup protection during extreme events.

  (b) DEMONSTRATION PROJECTS. — The Administrator shall fund not fewer
      than 50 living shoreline demonstration projects distributed across
      the Atlantic, Gulf, and Pacific coasts and the Great Lakes.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $5,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 104. URBAN COASTAL FLOOD BARRIER SYSTEMS.
================================================================================

  (a) CORPS OF ENGINEERS AUTHORITY. — The Secretary is directed to
      expedite feasibility studies, environmental review, and project
      authorization for urban coastal flood barrier systems at locations
      identified in the National Coastal Vulnerability Assessment.

  (b) ACCELERATED DELIVERY. — For projects with an estimated total cost
      exceeding $5 billion, the Secretary may use continuing contracts
      authority under section 902 of the Water Resources Development
      Act of 1986 (33 U.S.C. 2280) to accelerate project delivery.

  (c) LOCAL COST SHARE. — The non-Federal cost share for projects under
      this section shall not exceed 35 percent of total project cost,
      and may be reduced to 10 percent for disadvantaged communities
      as defined in this Act.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $7,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 105. BEACH NOURISHMENT AND DUNE RESTORATION.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary shall expand the existing
      Federal beach nourishment program to:

    (1) Increase the authorized Federal cost share for periodic
        nourishment from 50 percent to 65 percent;

    (2) Fund initial construction of engineered dune systems providing
        storm surge attenuation and wildlife habitat;

    (3) Establish regional sediment management plans identifying sand
        resources, sediment transport pathways, and nourishment needs
        for each coastal sediment cell.

  (b) SAND SOURCE MAPPING. — The United States Geological Survey, in
      coordination with the Bureau of Ocean Energy Management, shall
      conduct a comprehensive mapping of offshore sand resources
      suitable for beach nourishment along the Atlantic, Gulf, and
      Pacific coasts.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 106. COASTAL MONITORING AND EARLY WARNING.
================================================================================

  (a) SYSTEM EXPANSION. — The Administrator shall expand the National
      Water Level Observation Network and the Physical Oceanographic
      Real-Time System to:

    (1) Deploy not fewer than 500 additional water level and wave
        monitoring stations in high-risk coastal locations;

    (2) Integrate coastal camera and satellite remote sensing for real-
        time shoreline change and flood extent monitoring;

    (3) Develop sub-seasonal to seasonal coastal flood prediction
        capabilities with a spatial resolution of 50 meters or finer.

  (b) EARLY WARNING. — The Administrator, in coordination with FEMA,
      shall develop a national coastal flood early warning system
      providing emergency managers and the public with actionable
      flood forecasts not less than 72 hours in advance.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
