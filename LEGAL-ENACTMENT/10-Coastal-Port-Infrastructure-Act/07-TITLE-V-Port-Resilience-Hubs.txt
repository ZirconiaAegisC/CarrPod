================================================================================
              TITLE V — PORT RESILIENCE HUBS
================================================================================

  Authorization: Subsumed within administrative allocations

================================================================================
SECTION 501. PORT RESILIENCE HUB DESIGNATION.
================================================================================

  (a) DESIGNATION CRITERIA. — The Secretary of Homeland Security, in
      coordination with the Secretary of Transportation, shall designate
      not fewer than 15 United States seaports as Port Resilience Hubs
      based on:

    (1) Annual cargo tonnage and economic significance, including the
        port's role in energy imports and exports;

    (2) Role in Department of Defense strategic sealift and military
        deployment operations;

    (3) Vulnerability to sea level rise, storm surge, seismic events,
        and other natural hazards;

    (4) Criticality to regional and national supply chain resilience,
        including provision of essential goods during emergencies;

    (5) Geographic distribution ensuring resilience hub coverage on
        each coast.

  (b) RESILIENCE PLANNING. — Each designated Port Resilience Hub shall
      develop, not later than 24 months after designation, a comprehensive
      port resilience master plan addressing:

    (1) Physical protection from sea level rise and storm surge;
    (2) Emergency power and energy resilience;
    (3) Supply chain continuity and cargo diversion capabilities;
    (4) Cybersecurity of operational technology and information
        systems;
    (5) Community integration and equitable recovery.

================================================================================
SECTION 502. STORM SURGE AND FLOOD PROTECTION.
================================================================================

  (a) CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE ELEVATION. — The Secretary of Homeland
      Security shall provide grants for:

    (1) Elevation of electrical substations, emergency generators,
        and fuel storage above the 500-year flood elevation;

    (2) Floodproofing of warehouse, terminal, and intermodal yard
        structures;

    (3) Installation of deployable flood barrier systems at wharf
        edges and terminal entries;

    (4) Pump station and drainage system upgrades sufficient to manage
        the 500-year storm event under 2075 sea level rise projections.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 503. EMERGENCY POWER AND BACKUP SYSTEMS.
================================================================================

  (a) POWER RESILIENCE REQUIREMENT. — Each designated Port Resilience Hub
      shall maintain emergency power capability sufficient to:

    (1) Power all critical port operations including container handling
        equipment, lighting, security systems, and communications for
        not less than 7 consecutive days without grid power;

    (2) Provide cold ironing power to vessels carrying essential cargo
        during emergencies;

    (3) Provide power for emergency response staging, medical triage,
        and community shelter operations, where port facilities are
        designated as community emergency response sites.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 504. SUPPLY CHAIN RESILIENCE AND REDUNDANCY.
================================================================================

  (a) CARGO DIVERSION NETWORK. — The Secretary of Transportation shall
      establish a National Port Cargo Diversion Network enabling
      coordinated rerouting of cargo among designated Resilience Hubs
      during disruptions, including:

    (1) Real-time port capacity and congestion data sharing;
    (2) Inter-port agreements for priority berthing during emergencies;
    (3) Coordinated chassis, container, and intermodal equipment
        repositioning;
    (4) Expedited customs and inspection clearance for diverted cargo.

  (b) STRATEGIC CARGO RESERVE. — The Secretary of Transportation, in
      coordination with the Department of Homeland Security, shall
      establish a Strategic Cargo Reserve Program maintaining stocks
      of critical goods — including medical supplies, food, energy
      products, and construction materials — at designated Resilience
      Hubs for distribution during national emergencies.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 505. CYBERSECURITY FOR PORT INFRASTRUCTURE.
================================================================================

  (a) CYBERSECURITY REQUIREMENTS. — Each designated Port Resilience Hub
      shall:

    (1) Implement the National Institute of Standards and Technology
        (NIST) Cybersecurity Framework for port operational technology
        (OT) and information technology (IT) systems;

    (2) Conduct annual cybersecurity assessments and penetration testing
        performed by qualified third-party assessors;

    (3) Deploy network segmentation between OT and IT systems to prevent
        lateral movement of cyber threats;

    (4) Maintain an incident response plan tested through annual tabletop
        exercises and biennial live exercises.

  (b) CYBERSECURITY GRANTS. — The Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security
      Agency (CISA) shall provide technical assistance and grants for
      implementation of cybersecurity measures at all United States
      seaports, with priority for designated Resilience Hubs.

  (c) INFORMATION SHARING. — The Secretary of Homeland Security shall
      establish a Port Cybersecurity Information Sharing and Analysis
      Center (PC-ISAC) providing:

    (1) Real-time threat intelligence sharing among ports;
    (2) Vulnerability disclosure and coordinated remediation;
    (3) Best practices and technical guidance for maritime OT security.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
