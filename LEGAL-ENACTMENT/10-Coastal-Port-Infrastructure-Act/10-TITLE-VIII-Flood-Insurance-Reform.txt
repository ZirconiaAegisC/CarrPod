================================================================================
              TITLE VIII — FLOOD INSURANCE REFORM
================================================================================

  Authorization: Subsumed within administrative allocations

================================================================================
SECTION 801. NATIONAL FLOOD INSURANCE PROGRAM MODERNIZATION.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM REFORM OBJECTIVES. — The Administrator of the Federal
      Emergency Management Agency shall implement reforms to the
      National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) to:

    (1) Ensure long-term fiscal solvency while maintaining affordability
        for policyholders of limited means;

    (2) Accurately reflect current and projected flood risk in premium
        pricing through full implementation of Risk Rating 3.0;

    (3) Incentivize community-level and property-level flood risk
        reduction measures;

    (4) Reduce losses to the NFIP from repetitive loss properties,
        which account for a disproportionate share of claims;

    (5) Provide mechanisms for orderly transition of properties facing
        unavoidable inundation out of the program.

  (b) REPETITIVE LOSS MITIGATION. — The Administrator shall:

    (1) Identify all repetitive loss and severe repetitive loss
        properties in the NFIP portfolio;

    (2) Provide enhanced mitigation assistance for such properties,
        including buyouts, elevations, and floodproofing;

    (3) Establish a goal of reducing the number of severe repetitive
        loss properties by 75 percent over 10 years.

  (c) DEBT RESTRUCTURING. — The Secretary of the Treasury is authorized
      to restructure the NFIP's existing debt, including provision for
      partial debt cancellation upon certification by the Administrator
      that program reforms under this title are fiscally sustainable.

================================================================================
SECTION 802. RISK RATING 3.0 IMPLEMENTATION AND AFFORDABILITY.
================================================================================

  (a) FULL IMPLEMENTATION. — The Administrator shall complete full
      implementation of the Risk Rating 3.0 pricing methodology for
      all NFIP policies, ensuring that premiums reflect:

    (1) Individual property flood risk, including distance to coast
        and waterbody, flood frequency, foundation type, and elevation;

    (2) Future flood risk projections incorporating sea level rise,
        increased precipitation intensity, and other climate change
        factors;

    (3) Replacement cost value of insured structures.

  (b) AFFORDABILITY PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall establish an
      affordability program providing means-tested premium assistance
      for:

    (1) Policyholders with household income at or below 80 percent
        of area median income;

    (2) Policyholders whose premiums under Risk Rating 3.0 exceed
        5 percent of annual household income;

    (3) Communities where 30 percent or more of households qualify
        for affordability assistance.

  (c) PREMIUM CAP. — No policyholder shall experience an annual premium
      increase exceeding 15 percent as a result of Risk Rating 3.0
      implementation.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for the affordability
      program under this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 803. COMMUNITY RATING SYSTEM ENHANCEMENTS.
================================================================================

  (a) CRS EXPANSION. — The Administrator shall reform the Community Rating
      System (CRS) to:

    (1) Provide enhanced premium discounts for communities undertaking
        significant flood risk reduction investments, including sea
        walls, levees, living shorelines, and stormwater management;

    (2) Create a new CRS class (Class 1+) providing premium discounts
        of not less than 50 percent for communities achieving net zero
        growth in flood exposure through comprehensive floodplain
        management;

    (3) Incorporate community-level climate adaptation planning and
        implementation into CRS credit calculations;

    (4) Provide technical assistance to disadvantaged communities
        seeking to improve their CRS classification.

  (b) OPEN SPACE PRESERVATION. — The Administrator shall provide CRS
      credits for:

    (1) Acquisition and preservation of flood-prone lands as open space;
    (2) Restoration of natural floodplain functions, including wetland
        restoration and stream daylighting;
    (3) Relocation of structures from high-risk flood zones.

================================================================================
SECTION 804. COASTAL FLOOD MAPPING UPDATE.
================================================================================

  (a) MAPPING PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall, in coordination with the
      Administrator of NOAA, undertake a comprehensive update of coastal
      flood hazard maps to:

    (1) Incorporate updated sea level rise projections for 2050 and 2100;
    (2) Map future flood zones under multiple sea level rise scenarios
        for transparency and planning purposes;
    (3) Map erosion hazard zones and projected shoreline change;
    (4) Incorporate wave runup, overtopping, and coastal storm surge
        modeling for all coastal counties;
    (5) Update maps at intervals not exceeding 5 years.

  (b) MAPPING MODERNIZATION. — The Administrator shall:

    (1) Transition from static paper maps to dynamic, interactive online
        mapping platforms with parcel-level granularity;
    (2) Integrate LiDAR topographic data and bathymetric data at the
        highest available resolution;
    (3) Make all flood hazard data publicly available through open
        application programming interfaces (APIs).

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 805. MANAGED RETREAT AND RELOCATION ASSISTANCE.
================================================================================

  (a) RELOCATION PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall establish a voluntary
      managed retreat and relocation assistance program for communities
      where:

    (1) Chronic tidal flooding exceeds 26 days per year;
    (2) Shoreline erosion rates exceed 10 feet per year;
    (3) Repeated NFIP claims demonstrate economic infeasibility of
        continued occupancy.

  (b) ASSISTANCE PACKAGES. — The program shall provide:

    (1) Buyout offers at pre-flood risk fair market value;
    (2) Relocation assistance covering moving expenses, temporary
        housing, and transition counseling;
    (3) Priority access to affordable replacement housing in lower-risk
        areas;
    (4) Community planning grants for neighborhood-scale relocation
        preserving social networks and community identity.

  (c) VOLUNTARY PARTICIPATION. — Participation in the relocation program
      shall be entirely voluntary, and no property owner shall be required
      to accept a buyout offer or relocate.

  (d) LAND USE RESTRICTIONS. — Properties acquired through the program
      shall be deed-restricted as open space, conservation, or public
      access in perpetuity.

  (e) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
