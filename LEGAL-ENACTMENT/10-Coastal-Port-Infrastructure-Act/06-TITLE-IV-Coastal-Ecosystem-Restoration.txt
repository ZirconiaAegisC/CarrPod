================================================================================
              TITLE IV — COASTAL ECOSYSTEM RESTORATION
================================================================================

  Authorization: $15,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 401. WETLAND RESTORATION AND CREATION.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Administrator, in coordination with the
      Secretary and the Director of the United States Fish and Wildlife
      Service, shall establish a Coastal Wetland Restoration Program to:

    (1) Restore, enhance, and create tidal wetlands, including salt
        marshes, brackish marshes, and freshwater tidal wetlands;

    (2) Reconnect wetlands isolated by roads, levees, and other
        infrastructure to restore natural tidal hydrology;

    (3) Apply thin-layer sediment placement to increase wetland
        elevation and combat subsidence-induced wetland loss;

    (4) Remove or modify tide gates, culverts, and other water control
        structures impeding natural tidal exchange.

  (b) PRIORITY REGIONS. — Not less than 60 percent of funds under this
      section shall be allocated to:

    (1) The Mississippi River Delta and Louisiana coastal zone, which
        accounts for approximately 90 percent of coastal wetland loss
        in the contiguous United States;

    (2) The Chesapeake Bay watershed;
    (3) San Francisco Bay and the Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta;
    (4) Puget Sound;
    (5) The Everglades and Florida coastal wetlands.

  (c) ANNUAL ACREAGE TARGETS. — The Administrator shall establish targets
      for net annual wetland acreage gain, with a goal of achieving net
      zero coastal wetland loss by 2036 and net annual gain of not less
      than 50,000 acres by 2046.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION. — $6,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 402. OYSTER REEF AND SHELLFISH HABITAT RESTORATION.
================================================================================

  (a) RESTORATION PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall establish a national
      oyster reef restoration program to:

    (1) Restore oyster reef habitat in each major estuary along the
        Atlantic, Gulf, and Pacific coasts;

    (2) Provide substrate material including oyster shell, limestone,
        concrete reef balls, and engineered reef structures;

    (3) Deploy hatchery-reared oyster spat on suitable substrate;

    (4) Monitor reef development, water quality improvement, and
        associated fisheries enhancement.

  (b) ECOSYSTEM SERVICES VALUATION. — The Administrator shall quantify
      the ecosystem services provided by restored oyster reefs, including:

    (1) Nitrogen removal and water quality improvement;
    (2) Shoreline stabilization and erosion reduction;
    (3) Fisheries enhancement and habitat provision;
    (4) Carbon burial in reef-associated sediments.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 403. SEAGRASS AND KELP FOREST RESTORATION.
================================================================================

  (a) RESTORATION PROGRAM. — The Administrator shall establish a seagrass
      and macroalgae restoration program to:

    (1) Restore eelgrass (Zostera marina) beds along the Atlantic and
        Pacific coasts suffering from wasting disease, nutrient loading,
        and physical disturbance;

    (2) Restore turtle grass (Thalassia testudinum) and other seagrass
        species in Florida, Gulf of Mexico, and Caribbean waters;

    (3) Restore giant kelp (Macrocystis pyrifera) and bull kelp (Nereocystis
        luetkeana) forests along the California, Oregon, and Washington
        coasts affected by marine heatwaves and sea urchin overgrazing.

  (b) NURSERY AND PLANTING. — The Administrator shall fund the establishment
      of seagrass and kelp nurseries capable of producing not fewer than
      50 million transplant units annually for restoration planting.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $3,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 404. COASTAL LAND ACQUISITION AND CONSERVATION.
================================================================================

  (a) ACQUISITION FUND. — The Secretary of the Interior, through the
      United States Fish and Wildlife Service, shall establish a Coastal
      Conservation Land Acquisition Fund to:

    (1) Acquire coastal lands from willing sellers for inclusion in the
        National Wildlife Refuge System, State wildlife management areas,
        or other conservation holdings;

    (2) Acquire conservation easements on privately owned coastal lands
        that provide significant habitat, flood storage, or water quality
        benefits;

    (3) Fund State and local government acquisition of coastal lands
        for conservation, flood risk reduction, and public access.

  (b) PRIORITY. — Priority for acquisitions shall be given to:

    (1) Lands providing natural flood protection to adjacent communities;
    (2) Lands within the migratory corridors of federally listed threatened
        and endangered species;
    (3) Lands adjacent to existing conservation holdings, creating larger
        contiguous habitat blocks;
    (4) Lands identified as high-priority for climate resilience in
        State Wildlife Action Plans.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 405. BLUE CARBON ECOSYSTEM PRESERVATION.
================================================================================

  (a) BLUE CARBON INITIATIVE. — The Administrator, in coordination with the
      Secretary of Energy, shall establish a Blue Carbon Ecosystem
      Preservation Initiative to:

    (1) Quantify carbon stocks and sequestration rates in United States
        coastal blue carbon ecosystems, including salt marshes, mangroves,
        seagrass meadows, and kelp forests;

    (2) Develop standardized protocols for measurement, verification,
        and reporting of blue carbon;

    (3) Create a national blue carbon inventory and registry;

    (4) Explore mechanisms for blue carbon crediting in compliance and
        voluntary carbon markets.

  (b) CARBON SEQUESTRATION TARGET. — The Initiative shall establish a
      target of sequestering not less than 50 million metric tons of
      carbon dioxide equivalent annually in United States coastal blue
      carbon ecosystems by 2036.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $1,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.
