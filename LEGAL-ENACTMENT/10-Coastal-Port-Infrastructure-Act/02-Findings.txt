================================================================================
                          FINDINGS AND DECLARATIONS
                        COASTAL AND PORT INFRASTRUCTURE
                        PROTECTION ACT OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 101. FINDINGS.

  Congress finds the following:

    (1) Approximately 40 percent of the United States population, or more
        than 130 million Americans, resides in coastal shoreline counties
        that constitute less than 10 percent of the nation's land area,
        according to the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration
        and the United States Census Bureau.

    (2) Coastal counties generate approximately $9.5 trillion in goods
        and services annually, representing roughly 45 percent of the
        national gross domestic product, and support more than 60 million
        jobs.

    (3) Global mean sea level has risen approximately 10 to 12 inches
        since 1880, with the rate of rise accelerating: approximately
        4 inches of the total rise has occurred since 1993, indicating
        a rate approximately double that of the preceding century.

    (4) The National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration projects an
        additional 10 to 12 inches of sea level rise along United States
        coastlines by 2050, with higher scenarios reaching 18 inches or
        more in subsiding regions such as the Gulf Coast.

    (5) The United States coastline extends approximately 95,000 miles,
        encompassing diverse geographic and ecological settings, including
        barrier islands, estuaries, salt marshes, mangroves, coral reefs,
        rocky shores, and bluffs.

    (6) An estimated $1 trillion in coastal real estate value is at risk
        of chronic tidal flooding by 2050 under intermediate sea level
        rise scenarios, as documented in peer-reviewed research published
        by the Union of Concerned Scientists.

    (7) More than 300,000 residential and commercial properties in the
        contiguous United States, with a collective market value exceeding
        $135 billion, are projected to experience chronic inundation
        defined as 26 or more flooding events per year by 2045.

    (8) Flood damage to coastal properties already exceeds $20 billion
        annually, and the Federal Emergency Management Agency projects
        this cost to double by 2050 absent significant adaptation
        investment.

    (9) United States seaports handle approximately 99 percent of
        overseas trade by volume and 65 percent by value, supporting
        over 30 million jobs and $5.4 trillion in annual economic
        activity, according to the American Association of Port
        Authorities.

    (10) The supply chain disruptions of 2021 through 2023, during which
         port congestion at major United States container ports including
         Los Angeles, Long Beach, Savannah, and New York-New Jersey
         caused cumulative economic losses exceeding $20 billion,
         demonstrated the acute vulnerability of the nation's port
         infrastructure to demand surges and operational constraints.

    (11) The average age of major United States port infrastructure,
         including wharves, piers, navigation channels, and cargo handling
         systems, exceeds 50 years; the American Society of Civil Engineers
         awarded United States port infrastructure a grade of C-plus in
         its 2021 Report Card.

    (12) The new generation of ultra-large container vessels exceeding
         24,000 twenty-foot equivalent unit (TEU) capacity, deployed by
         global shipping lines including Maersk, MSC, and CMA CGM,
         requires channel depths of 50 feet or greater and widths of
         1,000 feet or greater; fewer than 10 United States ports
         currently meet these dimensional requirements.

    (13) The international maritime shipping sector accounts for
         approximately 3 percent of global anthropogenic greenhouse gas
         emissions, equivalent to the annual emissions of Germany, and
         the International Maritime Organization has adopted a revised
         strategy targeting net-zero greenhouse gas emissions from
         international shipping by or around 2050.

    (14) Port-related air pollution disproportionately affects
         neighboring communities, which are predominantly low-income
         communities and communities of color, through emissions of
         particulate matter, nitrogen oxides, sulfur oxides, and
         diesel exhaust from ships at berth, cargo handling equipment,
         and drayage trucks.

    (15) Coastal ecosystems — including salt marshes, mangroves, seagrass
         meadows, and oyster reefs — provide an estimated $100 billion
         annually in ecosystem services including storm surge attenuation,
         fishery habitat, carbon sequestration, water quality improvement,
         and recreational opportunities, yet the United States loses
         approximately 80,000 acres of coastal wetlands annually.

    (16) Vessels operating in United States waters, including ocean-going
         container ships, cruise ships, tankers, bulk carriers, and
         harbor craft, emitted approximately 40 million metric tons
         of carbon dioxide equivalent in 2024, representing a growing
         share of national transportation emissions.

    (17) Global shipping companies including Maersk and Carnival
         Corporation have publicly announced fleet emission reduction
         targets and investments in alternative fuel vessels, creating
         an opportunity for United States ports to attract green
         shipping activity through infrastructure investment.

    (18) The National Flood Insurance Program, with approximately
         5 million policies in force and $1.3 trillion in coverage,
         carries an accumulated debt to the United States Treasury of
         approximately $20.5 billion as of 2026, and requires structural
         reform to ensure long-term solvency while maintaining
         affordability for policyholders.

SECTION 102. DECLARATIONS OF POLICY.

  Congress declares that it is the policy of the United States to:

    (1) Protect coastal communities and critical infrastructure from
        accelerating sea level rise, storm surge, and chronic tidal
        flooding through strategic investment in sea walls, levees,
        flood barriers, living shorelines, and hybrid coastal protection.

    (2) Modernize United States port infrastructure to accommodate the
        current and future global shipping fleet, improve operational
        efficiency, enhance supply chain resilience, and maintain the
        nation's competitive position in international trade.

    (3) Electrify port operations and deploy zero-emission cargo handling
        equipment to eliminate port-related air pollution, improve public
        health in port-adjacent communities, and reduce greenhouse gas
        emissions.

    (4) Accelerate the maritime energy transition through investment in
        alternative fuel infrastructure, green shipping corridors, vessel
        electrification, and zero-emission vessel technology.

    (5) Restore and protect coastal ecosystems for their intrinsic
        ecological value, their critical role in attenuating storm surge
        and buffering coastal communities, and their capacity as blue
        carbon sinks to sequester atmospheric carbon dioxide.

    (6) Reform the National Flood Insurance Program to ensure long-term
        fiscal solvency, accurately reflect climate risk in premium
        pricing, maintain affordability for policyholders of limited
        means, and provide transition assistance for communities facing
        unavoidable inundation.

    (7) Prioritize environmental justice by ensuring that disadvantaged
        communities receive not less than 40 percent of the overall
        benefits of Federal coastal infrastructure investments and that
        port-adjacent communities benefit directly from emissions
        reduction investments.

    (8) Apply Buy America requirements to all materials, equipment,
        and components procured under this Act, supporting domestic
        manufacturing of steel, concrete, electrical equipment, and
        specialized maritime infrastructure.
