================================================================================
              TITLE II — PORT ELECTRIFICATION AND
              EMISSIONS REDUCTION
================================================================================

  Authorization: $25,000,000,000 over 10 fiscal years

================================================================================
SECTION 201. SHORE POWER AND COLD IRONING INFRASTRUCTURE.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Transportation, through the Maritime
      Administration, shall establish a competitive grant program to fund
      installation of shore power and cold ironing infrastructure at United
      States seaports, including:

    (1) High-voltage electrical substations and cable management systems
        at container, cruise, and roll-on/roll-off vessel berths;

    (2) Frequency conversion equipment to accommodate vessel electrical
        systems operating at 50 Hz or 60 Hz;

    (3) Electrical grid interconnection and capacity upgrades at host
        ports sufficient to power multiple vessels simultaneously;

    (4) Standardized shore connection interfaces compliant with
        international standards including IEC/IEEE 80005 series.

  (b) MANDATORY COLD IRONING. — Not later than January 1, 2032, all
      container vessels with a gross tonnage exceeding 5,000 and all
      cruise vessels calling at United States ports shall connect to
      shore power for the duration of their berth stay, except where:

    (1) The berth call duration is less than 2 hours;
    (2) The vessel utilizes an alternative zero-emission technology
        certified by the Administrator to be functionally equivalent
        to shore power connection;
    (3) The port has not yet completed shore power installation at
        the berth despite having an approved plan.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $10,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 202. ZERO-EMISSION CARGO HANDLING EQUIPMENT.
================================================================================

  (a) EQUIPMENT REPLACEMENT PROGRAM. — The Administrator of the
      Environmental Protection Agency, in coordination with the
      Secretary of Transportation, shall establish a grant program
      providing cost-share funding for replacement of diesel-powered
      cargo handling equipment with zero-emission alternatives,
      including:

    (1) Battery-electric container cranes, rubber-tired gantry cranes,
        and rail-mounted gantry cranes;
    (2) Battery-electric and hydrogen fuel cell top handlers, reach
        stackers, and forklifts;
    (3) Electric or hydrogen yard tractors and terminal trucks;
    (4) Electric conveyor and automated stacking crane systems.

  (b) FLEET TRANSITION REQUIREMENT. — Not later than January 1, 2036,
      all cargo handling equipment operating at ports receiving more
      than 1 million TEU annually shall be zero-emission equipment,
      except where the Secretary determines that zero-emission equipment
      is not commercially available for a specific application.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $7,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 203. PORT MICROGRIDS AND RENEWABLE ENERGY.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with
      the Secretary of Transportation, shall establish a grant program
      to fund:

    (1) Port microgrid systems integrating on-site renewable energy
        generation, battery energy storage, and intelligent load
        management;

    (2) Solar photovoltaic installations on port warehouse roofs,
        parking structures, and brownfield sites;

    (3) Behind-the-meter energy storage systems providing peak demand
        management, grid services, and resilience during outages;

    (4) Substation upgrades and transmission interconnections capable
        of supporting full port electrification loads.

  (b) RESILIENCE REQUIREMENT. — All microgrid systems funded under this
      section shall be capable of island-mode operation during grid
      outages for a minimum of 72 hours at full operational load.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $5,000,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 204. DRAYAGE TRUCK ELECTRIFICATION PROGRAM.
================================================================================

  (a) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Administrator of the Environmental Protection
      Agency shall establish a dedicated grant program for:

    (1) Purchase of Class 8 battery-electric and hydrogen fuel cell
        drayage trucks by port-serving motor carriers;

    (2) Installation of high-power charging and hydrogen refueling
        infrastructure on port property and at adjacent truck depots;

    (3) Scrappage and replacement of pre-2010 model year drayage trucks.

  (b) ZERO-EMISSION DRAYAGE MANDATE. — Not later than January 1, 2035,
      all drayage trucks operating at ports receiving more than 1 million
      TEU annually shall be zero-emission vehicles.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $2,500,000,000 is authorized for programs under
      this section.

================================================================================
SECTION 205. PORT AIR QUALITY MONITORING AND COMPLIANCE.
================================================================================

  (a) MONITORING NETWORK. — The Administrator shall require each port
      receiving more than $10 million in cumulative funding under this
      title to deploy a network of at least 5 real-time air quality
      monitoring stations, including:

    (1) Continuous monitoring of fine particulate matter (PM2.5),
        nitrogen dioxide (NO2), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and black carbon;

    (2) Real-time public reporting through a publicly accessible
        web-based dashboard;

    (3) Fenceline monitoring at port-adjacent residential areas.

  (b) HEALTH IMPACT REPORTING. — Each port shall submit to the
      Administrator an annual report quantifying port-related air
      emissions and associated health impact metrics, including
      estimated asthma exacerbations, lost work and school days,
      and premature mortality attributable to port air pollution.

================================================================================
SECTION 206. GREEN PORT CERTIFICATION PROGRAM.
================================================================================

  (a) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Transportation shall
      establish a voluntary Green Port Certification Program that
      recognizes ports demonstrating excellence in:

    (1) Emissions reductions and air quality improvement;
    (2) Shore power deployment and utilization;
    (3) Renewable energy integration and energy efficiency;
    (4) Water quality management and stormwater treatment;
    (5) Community engagement and environmental justice;
    (6) Sustainable supply chain management.

  (b) CERTIFICATION BENEFITS. — Green Port Certification shall entitle
      the certified port to:

    (1) Preferential consideration in competitive grant programs
        authorized under this Act;
    (2) Use of a Green Port Certification designation in marketing
        and commercial materials;
    (3) Eligibility for reduced Federal cost-share requirements on
        qualifying projects.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION. — $500,000,000 is authorized for the program
      established under this section.
