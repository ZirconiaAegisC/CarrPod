================================================================================
                      TITLE II — AI COMPUTE INFRASTRUCTURE
                      CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 201. SHORT TITLE.

  This title may be cited as the "National AI Compute Infrastructure Act of
  2026."

SECTION 202. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE.

  (a) FINDINGS. — Congress finds that:

    (1) Artificial intelligence has emerged as a general-purpose technology
        of transformative economic and military significance, with projected
        global economic impact exceeding $15 trillion by 2030 according to
        industry analyses.

    (2) The development, training, and deployment of frontier artificial
        intelligence models requires access to computing infrastructure of
        extraordinary scale and cost, with leading AI training clusters
        costing in excess of $1 billion per deployment and consuming
        electrical power equivalent to a medium-sized city.

    (3) A single corporation—Nvidia Corporation—commands approximately 80
        to 90 percent of the global market for AI training accelerators,
        with its CUDA software ecosystem functioning as a de facto monopoly
        platform for AI development. Nvidia's 2025 fiscal year revenue
        exceeded $130 billion, driven primarily by AI chip demand, and the
        company's market valuation exceeded $3 trillion, reflecting the
        extraordinary concentration of AI compute supply.

    (4) Access to frontier AI compute resources is increasingly concentrated
        in a small number of large technology corporations—including
        Alphabet Inc. (Google), Microsoft Corporation, Amazon.com Inc.,
        and Meta Platforms Inc.—limiting the ability of academic
        researchers, small businesses, and government agencies to
        participate in foundational AI research and development.

    (5) The People's Republic of China has established a national strategy
        for AI dominance, investing an estimated $150 billion through state-
        directed funds, and has pursued aggressive acquisition of advanced
        semiconductor manufacturing equipment and AI chips through third-
        party intermediaries despite United States export controls.

  (b) PURPOSE. — The purpose of this title is to establish a National AI
      Compute Reserve accessible to government agencies, academic
      researchers, and qualifying domestic entities; to fund the acquisition
      of advanced AI computing infrastructure; to democratize access to AI
      compute resources; and to ensure that the United States maintains
      global leadership in artificial intelligence research and development.

SECTION 203. NATIONAL AI COMPUTE RESERVE.

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the
      Director of the National Science Foundation and the Director of the
      Office of Science and Technology Policy, shall establish a National
      AI Compute Reserve (in this title referred to as the "Reserve").

  (b) FUNCTIONS. — The Reserve shall:

    (1) Acquire, operate, and maintain advanced AI computing systems of
        DGX SuperPOD-class or equivalent capability, including associated
        networking, storage, and power infrastructure;

    (2) Allocate compute hours to Federal agencies, national laboratories,
        accredited universities, and qualified domestic research
        institutions;

    (3) Establish tiered access models providing free compute allocations
        to academic researchers, cost-recovery allocations to government
        agencies, and market-rate allocations to commercial entities;

    (4) Ensure geographic distribution of compute capacity across not fewer
        than five regional nodes within the United States; and

    (5) Develop and publish benchmarks for AI compute utilization, energy
        efficiency, and research output productivity.

  (c) PROCUREMENT REQUIREMENTS. — In acquiring computing systems under this
      section, the Secretary of Energy shall:

    (1) Procure systems manufactured by United States-headquartered
        companies to the maximum extent practicable;

    (2) Require that AI accelerator chips in procured systems are
        manufactured, packaged, and tested in the United States or in
        allied nations within ten years of enactment;

    (3) Prioritize systems incorporating domestically packaged advanced
        semiconductors; and

    (4) Ensure interoperability with existing national laboratory and
        Department of Defense computing infrastructure.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $15,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 204. ACADEMIC AI COMPUTE GRANT PROGRAM.

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Director of the National Science Foundation shall
      establish a competitive grant program to provide computing resources
      and funding to accredited United States universities and colleges for
      artificial intelligence research and education.

  (b) ELIGIBLE USES. — Grant funds may be used for:

    (1) Acquisition of AI computing hardware, including GPU clusters,
        AI accelerators, and associated infrastructure;

    (2) Cloud computing credits for AI research workloads;

    (3) Development of AI curricula and training programs;

    (4) Establishment of on-campus AI research computing centers; and

    (5) Collaborative AI research projects with national laboratories,
        nonprofit research organizations, and industry partners.

  (c) PREFERENCE. — In awarding grants, the Director shall give preference
      to applications from:

    (1) Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs) and Minority-
        Serving Institutions (MSIs);

    (2) Institutions in States without existing significant AI compute
        infrastructure;

    (3) Institutions located in proximity to semiconductor manufacturing
        clusters; and

    (4) Consortia of multiple institutions pooling resources.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $3,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 205. NATIONAL AI RESEARCH CLOUD.

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Director of the National Science Foundation, in
      coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish a National
      AI Research Cloud providing on-demand access to advanced computing
      resources, curated datasets, pre-trained models, and collaborative
      research tools.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $2,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 206. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.

  Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment, and annually
  thereafter, the Secretary of Energy and the Director of the National
  Science Foundation shall jointly submit to the appropriate congressional
  committees a report detailing:

    (1) The total compute capacity of the Reserve;

    (2) Allocation of compute hours by sector;

    (3) Research outputs attributable to Reserve resources;

    (4) Energy consumption and efficiency metrics; and

    (5) Recommendations for improving United States AI compute
        competitiveness.
