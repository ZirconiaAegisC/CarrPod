================================================================================
                      TITLE I — CHIPS ACT 2.0
                      EXPANDED SEMICONDUCTOR MANUFACTURING SUPPORT
                      CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 101. SHORT TITLE.

  This title may be cited as the "CHIPS and Science Act Expansion and
  Extension Act of 2026."

SECTION 102. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE.

  (a) FINDINGS. — Congress finds that:

    (1) The CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 (Public Law 117-167) authorized
        $52.7 billion for semiconductor manufacturing incentives, research
        and development, and related programs, catalyzing approximately
        $450 billion in private sector semiconductor investment announcements
        through 2026, including TSMC's $65 billion commitment to construct
        three advanced fabrication facilities in Phoenix, Arizona (3 nanometer
        and 4 nanometer process nodes), and Intel Corporation's $20 billion
        investment in a mega-fabrication site in New Albany, Ohio.

    (2) The initial CHIPS Act funding has proven insufficient to fully
        address the scope of semiconductor supply chain vulnerability, with
        the Department of Commerce reporting that qualified applications for
        CHIPS incentives exceeded available funding by a factor of
        approximately three to one.

    (3) The semiconductor equipment and materials supply chain remains
        highly concentrated, with the five largest semiconductor equipment
        manufacturers—Applied Materials, ASML, Lam Research, Tokyo Electron,
        and KLA Corporation—together controlling approximately 70 percent of
        the global market, and critical input materials such as ultra-high
        purity silicon, photoresists, and specialty chemicals sourced from
        a limited number of foreign suppliers.

    (4) Advanced semiconductor packaging, which is essential to modern chip
        performance including artificial intelligence accelerators, high-
        performance computing processors, and advanced memory, is
        predominantly conducted in Asia, with Taiwan and South Korea
        accounting for over 90 percent of global advanced packaging capacity
        as of 2025.

  (b) PURPOSE. — The purpose of this title is to double the Federal
      investment in domestic semiconductor manufacturing, to extend support
      to semiconductor equipment, materials, and advanced packaging sectors,
      and to provide enhanced research and development incentives to ensure
      enduring United States leadership in semiconductor technology.

SECTION 103. AUTHORIZATION OF SUPPLEMENTAL APPROPRIATIONS.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — There is authorized to be appropriated to the Secretary
      of Commerce $100,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out the programs authorized under this title.

  (b) ALLOCATION. — Of the amounts authorized under subsection (a):

    (1) $55,000,000,000 shall be available for semiconductor fabrication
        facility incentives pursuant to section 104;

    (2) $15,000,000,000 shall be available for semiconductor equipment and
        materials manufacturing incentives pursuant to section 105;

    (3) $15,000,000,000 shall be available for advanced packaging subsidy
        programs pursuant to section 106;

    (4) $10,000,000,000 shall be available for research and development
        tax credit enhancements pursuant to section 107; and

    (5) $5,000,000,000 shall be available for State and local incentive
        coordination and semiconductor infrastructure pursuant to
        section 108.

SECTION 104. SEMICONDUCTOR FABRICATION FACILITY INCENTIVES.

  (a) PROGRAM EXPANSION. — The Secretary of Commerce shall expand the
      semiconductor fabrication facility incentive program established
      under section 9902 of the William M. (Mac) Thornberry National
      Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2021, as amended by the
      CHIPS Act of 2022, to provide additional grants, cooperative
      agreements, loans, and loan guarantees for the construction,
      expansion, and modernization of semiconductor fabrication
      facilities in the United States.

  (b) PRIORITY CONSIDERATIONS. — The Secretary shall give priority to
      projects producing advanced logic (7nm and below), advanced memory,
      integrating domestic packaging, and including workforce development
      and energy efficiency commitments.

  (c) INVESTMENT TAX CREDIT. — The advanced manufacturing investment
      credit under 26 U.S.C. 48D is extended through December 31, 2036,
      at 25 percent for facilities commencing construction by 2032.

  (d) DIRECT FUNDING AGREEMENTS. — The Secretary may enter into direct
      funding agreements, structured as milestone-based grants, with
      semiconductor fabrication facility applicants, consistent with
      the model established for the CHIPS Act awards to TSMC Arizona,
      Intel, Samsung, and Micron.

SECTION 105. SEMICONDUCTOR EQUIPMENT AND MATERIALS MANUFACTURING.

  (a) NEW PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall establish grants and loan
      guarantees for domestic manufacturing of semiconductor equipment
      (deposition, etch, lithography, metrology, CMP, etc.), critical
      materials (silicon wafers, photoresists, photomasks, specialty
      chemicals, high-purity gases), and equipment components and
      subsystems.

  (b) GOAL. — The Secretary shall establish a goal that by 2035, not
      less than 30 percent of semiconductor manufacturing equipment
      installed in United States fabrication facilities shall be
      manufactured in the United States.

SECTION 106. ADVANCED PACKAGING SUBSIDY PROGRAM.

  The Secretary of Commerce shall, in coordination with the Advanced
  Packaging Incentive Program established under Title IV of this Act,
  ensure that semiconductor fabrication facility incentives under this
  title include requirements and incentives for co-located or proximate
  advanced packaging capability.

SECTION 107. RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT TAX CREDIT ENHANCEMENT.

  (a) INCREASED RATE. — The credit rate for qualified research expenses
      related to semiconductor design, manufacturing, advanced packaging,
      and equipment shall be increased to 40 percent for taxable years
      beginning after December 31, 2026 and before January 1, 2037,
      covering transistor architectures, materials, manufacturing
      processes, EDA software, and equipment innovation.

SECTION 108. STATE AND LOCAL INCENTIVE COORDINATION.

  (a) FEDERAL-STATE PARTNERSHIP FUND. — The Secretary shall establish a
      fund providing matching grants to States, territories, and Tribal
      governments for semiconductor manufacturing incentives including
      property tax abatements, infrastructure improvements, workforce
      training, site preparation, and expedited permitting.

SECTION 109. SEMICONDUCTOR MANUFACTURING SECURITY.

  (a) TECHNOLOGY SECURITY. — All facilities receiving Federal incentives
      shall comply with technology security requirements established by
      the Secretary of Commerce in consultation with the Secretary of
      Defense and the Director of National Intelligence.

  (b) PROHIBITED SHARING. — No recipient may transfer semiconductor
      manufacturing process technology developed with Federal support
      to foreign adversary entities without explicit authorization.

  (c) CYBERSECURITY. — Recipients shall implement cybersecurity measures
      consistent with NIST Special Publication 800-171 or successor
      framework.
