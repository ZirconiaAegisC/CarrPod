================================================================================
                     TITLE XI — ECONOMIC IMPACT ASSESSMENT
                     CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 1101. CONGRESSIONAL BUDGET OFFICE SCORING REQUIREMENT.

  The Director of the Congressional Budget Office shall, not later than
  60 days after the date of introduction of this Act, provide a formal
  cost estimate and ten-year economic impact projection consistent with
  section 402 of the Congressional Budget Act of 1974.

SECTION 1102. DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE ECONOMIC IMPACT ANALYSIS.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — The Secretary of Commerce, in coordination with the
      Secretary of Labor, the Secretary of the Treasury, and the Director
      of the Office of Management and Budget, shall prepare and publish
      an Economic Impact Analysis of this Act not later than 180 days
      after enactment and updated biennially thereafter.

  (b) PROJECTED ECONOMIC BENEFITS. — Based on preliminary analysis and
      modeling of analogous industrial policy interventions including the
      CHIPS and Science Act of 2022, the Department of Commerce projects
      the following economic outcomes from full implementation of this Act:

    (1) DOMESTIC SEMICONDUCTOR MANUFACTURING CAPACITY. — Increase of
        United States share of global advanced semiconductor manufacturing
        capacity from approximately 12 percent in 2024 to no less than
        30 percent by 2035, representing a structural reversal of the
        three-decade decline in domestic manufacturing share.

    (2) SEMICONDUCTOR INDUSTRY EMPLOYMENT. — Creation of approximately
        500,000 direct and indirect domestic jobs, comprising:

        (A) Approximately 125,000 direct semiconductor fabrication,
            packaging, equipment, and materials manufacturing jobs;

        (B) Approximately 175,000 indirect jobs in the semiconductor
            supply chain, construction, and related services;

        (C) Approximately 200,000 induced jobs in communities hosting
            semiconductor manufacturing clusters through multiplier
            effects.

    (3) GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT GROWTH. — Cumulative contribution of
        approximately $500 billion to United States gross domestic
        product over the ten-year authorization period, with annual GDP
        contribution growing from approximately $20 billion in fiscal
        year 2028 to approximately $80 billion in fiscal year 2036.

    (4) REGIONAL ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT. — Catalytic economic
        transformation of regions hosting major semiconductor
        manufacturing clusters, including:

        (A) Maricopa County, Arizona: TSMC fab campus buildout
            supporting an estimated 50,000 direct and indirect jobs,
            $120 billion cumulative regional GDP contribution;

        (B) Central Ohio: Intel mega-fab campus buildout supporting
            an estimated 80,000 direct and indirect jobs at full
            buildout, $200 billion cumulative regional GDP contribution;

        (C) Central Texas: Samsung Austin/Taylor campus expansion
            supporting an estimated 40,000 jobs, $100 billion regional
            GDP contribution;

        (D) Upstate New York: Micron Clay facility and associated
            ecosystem supporting an estimated 50,000 jobs, $120
            billion regional GDP contribution;

        (E) Idaho: Micron Boise expansion supporting 20,000 jobs,
            $50 billion regional GDP contribution.

    (5) ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE INDUSTRY SECURITY. — Protection and
        enablement of the domestic artificial intelligence industry,
        which independent analysts project to represent a global market
        of $1.5 trillion to $4.4 trillion by 2035, by:

        (A) Ensuring domestic manufacturing of AI accelerator chips;

        (B) Providing domestic advanced packaging capacity for
            leading-edge AI chip architectures;

        (C) Democratizing access to AI compute infrastructure for
            research and small business innovation;

        (D) Maintaining United States leadership in AI semiconductor
            design through a secure and resilient supply chain.

    (6) RARE EARTH SUPPLY CHAIN RESILIENCE. — Reduction of United States
        dependency on the People's Republic of China for rare earth
        permanent magnets from approximately 90 percent to no more than
        30 percent by 2035, with domestic and allied-sourced rare earth
        magnet supply sufficient to meet defense and essential civilian
        requirements.

    (7) ENERGY SECTOR BENEFITS. — Acceleration of advanced nuclear and
        Small Modular Reactor deployment for industrial applications,
        with semiconductor manufacturing serving as the anchor tenant
        for new nuclear capacity that subsequently enables broader
        grid decarbonization and industrial electrification.

    (8) TAX REVENUE GENERATION. — Estimated cumulative Federal, State,
        and local tax revenues of approximately $80 billion to $120
        billion over the ten-year period, partially offsetting the
        Federal investment, from direct and indirect economic activity
        generated by programs authorized under this Act.

    (9) TECHNOLOGICAL SPILLOVER EFFECTS. — The semiconductor
        manufacturing ecosystem established under this Act is projected
        to generate substantial technological spillover benefits
        including:

        (A) Acceleration of materials science, photonics, quantum
            computing, and advanced manufacturing technologies;

        (B) Creation of a durable domestic innovation ecosystem
            attracting and retaining scientific and engineering talent;

        (C) Establishment of manufacturing process expertise
            transferable to pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, advanced
            materials, and clean energy manufacturing.

    (10) NATIONAL SECURITY BENEFIT. — Reduction of the estimated
         probability of catastrophic supply chain disruption from
         geopolitical conflict in the Taiwan Strait, quantified
         through avoided expected economic loss of between $1 trillion
         and $3 trillion, substantially exceeding the total authorization
         under this Act.
