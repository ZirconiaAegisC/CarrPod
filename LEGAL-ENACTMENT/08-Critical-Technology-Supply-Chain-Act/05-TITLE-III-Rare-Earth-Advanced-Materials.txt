================================================================================
                     TITLE III — RARE EARTH AND ADVANCED MATERIALS
                     CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 301. SHORT TITLE.

  This title may be cited as the "Domestic Critical Materials Production and
  Processing Act of 2026."

SECTION 302. FINDINGS.

  Congress finds that:

    (1) The People's Republic of China currently controls approximately 60
        percent of global rare earth element mining production and 90 percent
        of rare earth permanent magnet manufacturing capacity, creating a
        near-monopoly position in materials essential to defense systems,
        electric vehicles, wind turbines, medical imaging, consumer
        electronics, and precision-guided munitions.

    (2) China's Ministry of Commerce imposed formal export restrictions on
        gallium and germanium in August 2023, followed by expanded controls
        on graphite in December 2023 and antimony in August 2024, asserting
        leverage over materials critical to semiconductor manufacturing,
        infrared optics, fiber optics, battery production, and flame
        retardants.

    (3) The Mountain Pass rare earth mine and processing facility in
        California, operated by MP Materials Corp, represents the only
        operating rare earth mining and primary processing facility in the
        Western Hemisphere, yet the facility currently ships concentrated
        rare earth ore to China for final separation and magnet production.

    (4) Lynas Rare Earths Ltd., an Australian corporation, has received
        $258 million in Department of Defense funding to establish a heavy
        rare earth separation facility in Texas; however, the facility
        remains under construction and the supply chain from mine to magnet
        remains incomplete within allied territory.

    (5) The Department of Energy's Critical Materials Assessment has
        identified rare earth elements (neodymium, praseodymium, dysprosium,
        terbium), gallium, germanium, graphite, cobalt, and lithium as
        materials facing high supply risk critical to energy, defense, and
        technology sectors.

SECTION 303. DOMESTIC RARE EARTH PERMANENT MAGNET PRODUCTION.

  (a) DIRECT LOAN AND LOAN GUARANTEE PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Energy
      shall establish a program of direct loans and loan guarantees for
      projects related to:

    (1) Domestic rare earth element mining, beneficiation, and separation;

    (2) Domestic rare earth metal and alloy production;

    (3) Domestic rare earth permanent magnet manufacturing, including
        neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) sintered and bonded magnets;

    (4) Magnet recycling and urban mining facilities recovering rare earth
        elements from end-of-life products;

    (5) Research and development into rare earth-free or rare earth-reduced
        magnet technologies.

  (b) MAXIMUM LOAN AMOUNTS. — The total amount of loans and loan guarantees
      outstanding under this section shall not exceed $10,000,000,000 at any
      one time.

  (c) PREFERENCE FOR VERTICAL INTEGRATION. — In evaluating applications
      under this section, the Secretary shall give preference to projects
      that demonstrate vertical integration across mining, separation,
      metal production, alloy production, and magnet manufacturing within
      the United States or allied nations.

  (d) MOUNTAIN PASS EXPANSION SUPPORT. — The Secretary of Defense, in
      coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall negotiate and execute
      a Defense Production Act Title III agreement with MP Materials Corp
      for the establishment of a fully integrated rare earth permanent magnet
      production line, from separated oxides through finished sintered
      magnets, at the Mountain Pass, California facility.

  (e) LYNAS TEXAS FACILITY COMPLETION. — The Secretary of Defense is
      authorized to provide additional funding, not to exceed $500,000,000,
      to accelerate completion of the Lynas Rare Earths heavy rare earth
      separation and processing facility in Texas, and to support upstream
      mine development at the Mount Weld operation in Western Australia for
      supply to the Texas facility.

SECTION 304. GALLIUM, GERMANIUM, AND GRAPHITE DOMESTIC PROCESSING.

  (a) PROCESSING FACILITY INCENTIVES. — The Secretary of Commerce shall
      establish a grant program to incentivize the construction and operation
      of domestic processing facilities for:

    (1) High-purity gallium (99.9999 percent or greater) for compound
        semiconductor manufacturing;

    (2) Germanium metal and germanium tetrachloride for fiber optic and
        infrared optics manufacturing;

    (3) Battery-grade spherical purified graphite for lithium-ion battery
        anode material;

    (4) Isotropic and extruded graphite for semiconductor crystal growth
        furnaces and other industrial applications.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $3,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 305. NATIONAL DEFENSE STOCKPILE EXPANSION.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — The Secretary of Defense shall expand the National
      Defense Stockpile to include minimum inventory levels of:

    (1) Rare earth oxides and metals sufficient to supply domestic defense
        manufacturing requirements for not fewer than five years;

    (2) Gallium, germanium, graphite, cobalt, and lithium sufficient for
        not fewer than three years of defense and essential civilian demand;

    (3) Completed rare earth permanent magnets in standard military
        specification configurations.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $2,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2031 to carry out this section.

SECTION 306. CRITICAL MATERIALS RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT.

  (a) DEPARTMENT OF ENERGY PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Energy shall
      establish a Critical Materials Innovation Hub focused on:

    (1) Alternative magnet materials with reduced or eliminated rare earth
        content;

    (2) Advanced separation technologies, including bio-extraction and
        continuous ion exchange;

    (3) Recycling and recovery technologies for rare earth elements,
        gallium, germanium, and graphite from end-of-life products;

    (4) Substitutes for critical materials in semiconductor manufacturing
        processes.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $500,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.
