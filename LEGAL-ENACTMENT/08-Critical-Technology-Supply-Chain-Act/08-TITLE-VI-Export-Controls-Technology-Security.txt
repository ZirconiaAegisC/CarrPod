================================================================================
                 TITLE VI — EXPORT CONTROLS AND TECHNOLOGY SECURITY
                 CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 601. SHORT TITLE.

  This title may be cited as the "Critical Technology Export Control and
  Investment Security Act of 2026."

SECTION 602. FINDINGS.

  Congress finds that:

    (1) The U.S. export control system requires modernization to address
        adversary acquisition of advanced semiconductor technology and
        AI capabilities.

    (2) The October 2022 and October 2023 BIS interim final rules on AI
        chips and semiconductor equipment demonstrated effectiveness,
        yet third-party transshipment circumvention persists.

    (3) CFIUS, as strengthened by FIRRMA, has effectively mitigated
        risks from foreign acquisitions but lacks jurisdiction over
        outbound investment and greenfield technology transfer.

    (4) The PRC has invested in excess of $50 billion through its
        National IC Industry Investment Fund to acquire semiconductor
        technology through investment, espionage, and forced transfer.

SECTION 603. EXPANSION OF BIS ENTITY LIST AUTHORITIES.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — Section 744.11 of the Export Administration Regulations
      (15 C.F.R. 744.11) is affirmed and expanded as follows:

  (b) SEMICONDUCTOR ENTITY LIST. — The Secretary of Commerce shall maintain
      a dedicated Semiconductor Restricted Entity List, a subset of the
      Entity List, comprising foreign persons and entities determined to
      pose an unacceptable risk of diversion of advanced semiconductors,
      semiconductor manufacturing equipment, electronic design automation
      software, or related technology to foreign adversaries.

  (c) CRITERIA FOR DESIGNATION. — In making Entity List designations under
      this section, the Secretary shall consider:

    (1) Whether the entity is involved in semiconductor manufacturing for
        the People's Liberation Army or Chinese military-civil fusion
        programs;

    (2) Whether the entity has procured United States-origin items on
        behalf of designated entities through third-party intermediaries;

    (3) Whether the entity is located in, or operates through, a
        jurisdiction identified by the Secretary as a transshipment hub
        for controlled technology;

    (4) Whether the entity is subject to ownership or control, directly
        or indirectly, by a foreign adversary government or military
        organization.

SECTION 604. AI CHIP EXPORT CONTROL FRAMEWORK.

  (a) PERFORMANCE-BASED THRESHOLDS. — The Secretary of Commerce shall
      maintain export control thresholds based on:

    (1) Aggregate bidirectional chip-to-chip data transfer rate, measured
        in terabytes per second;

    (2) Aggregate processing performance, measured in TOPS (trillions of
        operations per second) at a specified precision; and

    (3) Chip-to-chip interconnect density, measured in gigatransfers per
        second per millimeter of die edge.

  (b) DYNAMIC ADJUSTMENT. — The Secretary shall review and adjust thresholds
      under subsection (a) not less frequently than annually to maintain
      controls at a level consistent with advancing technology and evolving
      national security requirements.

  (c) PRESUMPTION OF CONTROL. — For purposes of the EAR, all advanced AI
      accelerator integrated circuits with aggregate bidirectional
      interconnect bandwidth exceeding 600 gigabytes per second shall be
      presumed subject to export control licensing requirements when
      destined for Macau or the People's Republic of China, displacing
      the de minimis rule for such items.

  (d) WORLDWIDE CONTROLS. — Items subject to control under this section
      that are manufactured outside the United States but incorporate
      United States-origin technology, software, or equipment subject to
      the EAR shall remain subject to United States export jurisdiction
      consistent with the foreign direct product rule framework.

SECTION 605. SEMICONDUCTOR MANUFACTURING EQUIPMENT RESTRICTIONS.

  (a) CRITICAL EQUIPMENT LIST. — The Secretary of Commerce shall publish
      and maintain a Critical Semiconductor Manufacturing Equipment List
      identifying categories of equipment, components, and associated
      software for which exports to Macau or the People's Republic of China
      require a license, which shall be subject to a presumption of denial.

  (b) EQUIPMENT CATEGORIES. — The list shall include EUV lithography
      equipment, DUV immersion lithography below 40nm pitch, ALD and
      selective epitaxial growth equipment, advanced etch equipment,
      e-beam and x-ray metrology for sub-10nm nodes, and EDA software
      for gate-all-around or backside power delivery design.

  (c) MAINTENANCE AND SERVICE RESTRICTIONS. — The authorization required
      for the export of controlled equipment shall include authorization
      for provision of maintenance, repair, service, spare parts, and
      software updates, except as specifically licensed by the Secretary.

SECTION 606. CFIUS EXPANSION.

  (a) COVERED TRANSACTIONS. — Section 721(a)(4) of the Defense Production
      Act (50 U.S.C. 4565(a)(4)) is expanded to include any transaction
      involving U.S. businesses developing or manufacturing advanced
      semiconductors, equipment, or packaging; greenfield fab construction
      by foreign adversary-linked entities; and joint ventures or
      licensing providing foreign adversary access to semiconductor
      process technology or design methodology.

  (b) OUTBOUND INVESTMENT. — The Secretary of the Treasury is authorized
      to review and prohibit U.S. person investments in semiconductor
      manufacturing, advanced packaging, or AI accelerator design
      entities in foreign adversary countries where such investment
      poses national security risk.

SECTION 607. ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES.

  (a) ADDITIONAL RESOURCES. — There is authorized to be appropriated
      $1,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2031 to
      the Bureau of Industry and Security for enforcement of export controls
      under this title, including:

    (1) Expansion of the Office of Export Enforcement field offices;

    (2) Enhanced end-use monitoring and verification capabilities;

    (3) Interagency task forces for semiconductor diversion investigations.
