================================================================================
                    TITLE X — IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE
                    CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 1001. IMPLEMENTATION PHASES AND MILESTONES.

  (a) PHASE I — IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Fiscal Years 2027-2028). —

    (1) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — Not later than 180 days after the date
        of enactment, the Secretary of Commerce shall issue a Notice of
        Funding Opportunity for CHIPS Act 2.0 semiconductor fabrication
        facility incentives, advanced packaging incentives, and
        semiconductor equipment and materials manufacturing incentives.

    (2) AI COMPUTE RESERVE. — Not later than 270 days after enactment,
        the Secretary of Energy shall publish the site selection criteria
        and procurement plan for the National AI Compute Reserve.

    (3) RARE EARTH PROGRAM. — Not later than 180 days after enactment,
        the Secretary of Energy shall issue a solicitation for rare earth
        permanent magnet production loan guarantees and begin negotiation
        of the Mountain Pass Defense Production Act agreement.

    (4) WORKFORCE GRANTS. — Not later than 120 days after enactment, the
        Director of the National Science Foundation shall issue a
        solicitation for university microelectronics education program
        grants and designate the first five Semiconductor Manufacturing
        Centers of Excellence.

    (5) EXPORT CONTROL UPDATES. — Not later than 90 days after enactment,
        the Secretary of Commerce shall finalize and publish the Critical
        Semiconductor Manufacturing Equipment List and the Semiconductor
        Restricted Entity List.

    (6) ALLIED FRAMEWORK. — Not later than 180 days after enactment, the
        Secretary of State shall formally open negotiations with the
        European Union, Japan, and the Republic of Korea for the
        Quadrilateral Semiconductor Alliance Treaty.

    (7) ENERGY CORRIDORS. — Not later than 180 days after enactment, the
        Secretary of Energy shall designate the first three Semiconductor
        Energy Corridors.

    (8) CHIPS ACT NOTIFICATION. — Not later than 60 days after enactment,
        the Secretary of Commerce shall notify existing CHIPS Act
        applicants of the availability of supplemental funding and the
        expanded scope of the program.

  (b) PHASE II — PROGRAM RAMP (Fiscal Years 2029-2031). —

    (1) FABRICATION FACILITIES. — Award and commence construction of not
        fewer than 10 additional semiconductor fabrication facility
        projects beyond those funded under the original CHIPS Act.

    (2) AI COMPUTE. — Complete procurement and installation of the first
        National AI Compute Reserve node, achieving not fewer than 50,000
        AI accelerator equivalent compute units.

    (3) RARE EARTHS. — Achieve domestic rare earth separation capacity
        of not fewer than 20,000 metric tons of rare earth oxide
        equivalent per year. Commence domestic sintered NdFeB magnet
        production of not fewer than 5,000 metric tons per year.

    (4) ADVANCED PACKAGING. — Award advanced packaging incentives for not
        fewer than five facilities and commence construction of not fewer
        than three.

    (5) WORKFORCE. — Enroll not fewer than 50,000 students in
        semiconductor-related degree and certificate programs funded
        under this Act. Achieve H-1B semiconductor talent visa utilization
        of not fewer than 8,000 visas per year.

    (6) ENERGY. — Commence construction of not fewer than two
        Semiconductor Energy Corridor transmission projects and not
        fewer than one SMR co-location project at a semiconductor
        manufacturing cluster.

    (7) ALLIED. — Conclude the Quadrilateral Semiconductor Alliance Treaty
        and establish the shared supply chain mapping platform.

  (c) PHASE III — SCALING (Fiscal Years 2032-2034). —

    (1) FABRICATION FACILITIES. — Achieve United States advanced
        semiconductor manufacturing share of not fewer than 25 percent
        of global capacity at process nodes of 7 nanometers and below.

    (2) AI COMPUTE. — Achieve National AI Compute Reserve capacity of not
        fewer than 200,000 AI accelerator equivalent compute units across
        not fewer than five regional nodes.

    (3) RARE EARTHS. — Achieve domestic sintered NdFeB magnet production
        of not fewer than 15,000 metric tons per year. Complete
        construction of domestic gallium, germanium, and graphite
        processing facilities.

    (4) ADVANCED PACKAGING. — Achieve commercial operation of not fewer
        than seven domestic advanced packaging facilities covering all
        four primary technology domains (CoWoS, EMIB, Foveros, and hybrid
        bonding).

    (5) WORKFORCE. — Achieve cumulative semiconductor workforce placement
        of not fewer than 200,000 trained workers.

    (6) ENERGY. — Achieve commercial operation of not fewer than two
        SMR facilities co-located with semiconductor manufacturing
        clusters. Complete not fewer than four Semiconductor Energy
        Corridor transmission projects.

  (d) PHASE IV — SUSTAINMENT (Fiscal Years 2035-2036). —

    (1) Meet or exceed the 30 percent United States share of global
        advanced semiconductor manufacturing capacity target.

    (2) Achieve commercial operation of not fewer than 10 domestic
        advanced packaging facilities.

    (3) Achieve domestic NdFeB magnet production of not fewer than
        20,000 metric tons per year.

    (4) Conduct comprehensive program review and prepare final report
        to Congress with recommendations for any continuing programs.

SECTION 1002. ANNUAL REPORT TO CONGRESS.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — Not later than March 1 of each year from 2028 through
      2037, the Secretary of Commerce shall submit to the appropriate
      congressional committees a report on the implementation of this Act.

  (b) REPORT CONTENTS. — Each report shall include:

    (1) Status of each program element against established milestones;

    (2) Aggregate and project-level expenditure data;

    (3) Metrics on manufacturing capacity added, jobs created, and
        supply chain resilience improved;

    (4) Assessment of technology security and export control
        effectiveness;

    (5) Recommendations for legislative or administrative action to
        improve program effectiveness.

SECTION 1003. COMPTROLLER GENERAL AUDITS.

  (a) INITIAL AUDIT. — Not later than three years after the date of
      enactment, the Comptroller General shall submit an audit assessing:

    (1) Efficiency and effectiveness of program administration;

    (2) Adequacy of internal controls and fraud prevention measures;

    (3) Accuracy of performance data reported by the Department of
        Commerce;

    (4) Progress toward statutory goals and milestones.

  (b) BIENNIAL AUDITS. — Biennially thereafter through fiscal year 2037,
      the Comptroller General shall conduct follow-up audits and provide
      recommendations for improvement.

SECTION 1004. PRESIDENTIAL DETERMINATION OF CONTINUING NEED.

  Not later than June 30, 2035, the President shall transmit to Congress
  a determination of whether the authorities and appropriations under
  this Act should be extended beyond fiscal year 2036, accompanied by a
  detailed justification.
