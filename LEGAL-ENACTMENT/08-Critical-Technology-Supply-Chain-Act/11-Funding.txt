                       TITLE IX — FUNDING AND APPROPRIATIONS
                       CTSCRA OF 2026

SECTION 901. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — There are authorized to be appropriated to carry out
      this Act, in addition to amounts otherwise authorized by law, a
      total of $200,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036.

  (b) ANNUAL FUNDING PROFILE. — The annual appropriation profile shall
      follow a front-loaded investment curve with a ramp-up period from
      fiscal years 2027-2028, peak investment from fiscal years 2029-2033,
      and sustained investment through fiscal year 2036:

    Fiscal Year 2027: $12,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2028: $18,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2029: $24,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2030: $26,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2031: $26,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2032: $24,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2033: $22,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2034: $18,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2035: $16,000,000,000
    Fiscal Year 2036: $14,000,000,000

SECTION 903. OVERSIGHT AND ACCOUNTABILITY.

  (a) INSPECTOR GENERAL. — The Inspector General of the Department of
      Commerce shall conduct annual audits of all programs authorized
      under this Act and submit findings to the appropriate congressional
      committees.

  (b) GAO REVIEW. — The Comptroller General of the United States shall
      conduct a comprehensive review of the implementation of this Act
      not later than three years after the date of enactment, and
      biennially thereafter.

  (c) RECIPIENT REPORTING. — Each recipient of Federal financial assistance
      under this Act shall submit annual reports to the Secretary of
      Commerce detailing:

    (1) Amount of Federal assistance received and expended;

    (2) Project milestones achieved and timeline status;

    (3) Jobs created, disaggregated by occupation category and
        demographic characteristics;

    (4) Domestic and foreign supply chain dependencies identified;

    (5) Technology security measures implemented.

  (d) CLAWBACK PROVISIONS. — The Secretary of Commerce shall include in
      all agreements for Federal financial assistance under this Act
      provisions for the recapture of funds in the event of:

    (1) Material failure to meet project milestones without reasonable
        justification;

    (2) Unauthorized technology transfer to foreign adversary entities;

    (3) Material misrepresentation in the application for assistance;

    (4) Cessation of covered manufacturing operations within ten years
        of project completion without Department approval.

  (e) PUBLIC TRANSPARENCY. — The Secretary shall maintain a publicly
      accessible online dashboard displaying, for each awarded project:
      recipient name, location, award amount, project status, jobs
      created, and milestone achievement, updated not less frequently
      than quarterly.

SECTION 904. SUNSET PROVISIONS.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — The authority to enter into new obligations under this
      Act shall expire on September 30, 2036.

  (b) CONTINUING OBLIGATIONS. — Obligations entered into before the
      expiration date under subsection (a) shall continue in effect
      according to their terms.

  (c) UNEXPENDED FUNDS. — Any funds appropriated under this Act not
      obligated by September 30, 2038, shall be returned to the
      general fund of the Treasury.
