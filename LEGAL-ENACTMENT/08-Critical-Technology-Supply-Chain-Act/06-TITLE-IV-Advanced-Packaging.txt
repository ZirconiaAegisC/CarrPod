================================================================================
                    TITLE IV — ADVANCED PACKAGING MANUFACTURING
                    CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 401. SHORT TITLE.

  This title may be cited as the "Domestic Advanced Semiconductor Packaging
  Act of 2026."

SECTION 402. FINDINGS.

  Congress finds that:

    (1) Advanced semiconductor packaging technologies—including Chip-on-
        Wafer-on-Substrate (CoWoS), Embedded Multi-Die Interconnect Bridge
        (EMIB), Intel Foveros three-dimensional stacking, and hybrid
        bonding—have become essential to the performance, power efficiency,
        and functional density of modern semiconductor products, including
        artificial intelligence accelerators, high-performance computing
        processors, advanced graphics processors, and mobile system-on-chip
        designs.

    (2) As of 2025, approximately 90 percent of global advanced packaging
        capacity is concentrated in Asia, with Taiwan Semiconductor
        Manufacturing Company dominating CoWoS production (used by Nvidia
        for its AI GPU products), Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix leading
        in High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) packaging, and ASE Technology
        Holding operating the world's largest independent packaging and
        test facility in Kaohsiung, Taiwan.

    (3) The CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 included provisions for advanced
        packaging under the National Advanced Packaging Manufacturing
        Program, but funding for actual packaging facility construction
        was limited, and as of 2026, the only major domestic advanced
        packaging facility announcements are Intel's Foveros packaging
        site in New Mexico and a planned Samsung advanced packaging
        facility in Texas.

    (4) Artificial intelligence chip demand is accelerating advanced
        packaging requirements, with Nvidia's next-generation GPU
        architectures requiring more complex CoWoS configurations,
        Intel's roadmap projecting increased reliance on EMIB and Foveros
        Direct, and AMD projecting increased chiplet-based designs
        requiring advanced interconnect technologies.

SECTION 403. ADVANCED PACKAGING FACILITY INCENTIVE PROGRAM.

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Commerce shall establish within the
      CHIPS Program Office an Advanced Packaging Incentive Program to provide
      grants, cooperative agreements, loans, and loan guarantees to support
      the construction, expansion, and modernization of advanced
      semiconductor packaging and test facilities in the United States.

  (b) ELIGIBLE TECHNOLOGIES. — Projects eligible for support under this
      section shall involve one or more of the following technologies:

    (1) Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate (CoWoS) and variants (CoWoS-S, CoWoS-R,
        CoWoS-L) for high-performance computing applications;

    (2) Embedded Multi-Die Interconnect Bridge (EMIB) technology;

    (3) Three-dimensional die stacking, including Foveros, Foveros Omni,
        and Foveros Direct technologies;

    (4) Hybrid bonding, including copper-to-copper direct bond interconnect
        with sub-10-micron pitch;

    (5) High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM) die stacking and integration;

    (6) Fan-out wafer-level packaging (FOWLP) and fan-out panel-level
        packaging (FOPLP);

    (7) Silicon interposer and bridge technologies;

    (8) System-in-Package (SiP) and heterogeneous integration technologies.

  (c) FACILITY TARGETS. — The Secretary shall establish a goal of not fewer
      than 10 advanced packaging facilities operating at commercial scale
      in the United States by December 31, 2035.

  (d) NATIONAL SECURITY PACKAGING MANDATE. — Not later than January 1, 2033,
      all advanced semiconductor components procured by the Department of
      Defense and the Intelligence Community shall be packaged and tested
      either in the United States or in a facility located in a formal
      allied nation as designated under Title VII of this Act.

  (e) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $20,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 404. ADVANCED PACKAGING RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT CONSORTIA.

  (a) PUBLIC-PRIVATE CONSORTIA. — The Secretary of Commerce, acting through
      the Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology
      (NIST), shall establish not fewer than three public-private advanced
      packaging research and development consortia, each focused on a
      different advanced packaging technology domain.

  (b) CONSORTIA FOCUS AREAS. — The consortia shall address:

    (1) Substrate and interposer technology, including glass core substrates
        and advanced organic substrates;

    (2) Thermal management for high-power-density three-dimensional
        integrated packages;

    (3) Design automation and electronic design automation (EDA) tools for
        heterogeneous integration;

    (4) Metrology and inspection for advanced packaging processes;

    (5) materials for advanced packaging, including underfill, thermal
        interface materials, and microbump metallurgy;

    (6) Reliability testing and qualification standards for advanced
        packages in military, aerospace, and automotive applications.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $2,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 405. ADVANCED PACKAGING WORKFORCE INITIATIVE.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the
      Secretary of Commerce, shall establish a grant program to support
      workforce training for advanced semiconductor packaging technicians,
      process engineers, and related occupations.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $500,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section, from amounts authorized
      under Title V of this Act.
