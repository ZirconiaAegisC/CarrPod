================================================================================
                    TITLE V — SEMICONDUCTOR WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT
                    CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 501. SHORT TITLE.

  This title may be cited as the "Semiconductor Workforce for American
  Competitiveness Act of 2026."

SECTION 502. FINDINGS.

  Congress finds that:

    (1) The Semiconductor Industry Association estimates that the United
        States semiconductor industry will require an additional 115,000
        workers by 2030 to staff planned fabrication facilities, advanced
        packaging facilities, and related operations, yet current graduation
        rates in relevant science and engineering disciplines project a
        shortfall of approximately 67,000 workers at current trends.

    (2) Semiconductor manufacturing requires a uniquely interdisciplinary
        workforce spanning electrical engineering, materials science,
        chemical engineering, mechanical engineering, industrial
        engineering, and specialized technician skills in cleanroom
        operations, vacuum systems, plasma processing, photolithography,
        metrology, and chemical handling.

    (3) The H-1B visa program, which provides essential talent recruitment
        pathways for the semiconductor industry, remains oversubscribed,
        with annual demand exceeding the statutory cap of 85,000 visas by
        a factor of more than three, and processing delays measured in
        months creating uncertainty for employers and employees.

    (4) Veterans of the United States Armed Forces possess technical skills
        highly transferable to semiconductor manufacturing, including
        experience with complex equipment operation, maintenance protocols,
        cleanroom discipline, safety procedures, and team-based operational
        environments.

SECTION 503. UNIVERSITY MICROELECTRONICS EDUCATION PROGRAMS.

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Director of the National Science Foundation shall
      establish a competitive grant program to expand and enhance
      microelectronics and semiconductor manufacturing education at
      accredited United States universities and colleges.

  (b) PROGRAM COMPONENTS. — Grants under this section may support:

    (1) Establishment of new undergraduate and graduate degree programs
        in semiconductor engineering, microelectronics manufacturing,
        and related disciplines;

    (2) Expansion of cleanroom laboratory facilities for hands-on
        semiconductor fabrication instruction;

    (3) Development of semiconductor-focused curricula incorporating
        industry-standard electronic design automation tools;

    (4) Faculty recruitment and retention in semiconductor-related fields;

    (5) Scholarship and fellowship programs for students pursuing
        semiconductor-related degrees, with preference for students
        from underrepresented groups;

    (6) Cooperative education and internship programs in partnership with
        domestic semiconductor manufacturers.

  (c) CENTERS OF EXCELLENCE. — The Director shall designate not fewer than
      10 university-based Semiconductor Manufacturing Centers of
      Excellence, geographically distributed to align with semiconductor
      manufacturing clusters, each receiving enhanced funding to serve
      as regional hubs for semiconductor education, research, and
      workforce development.

  (d) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $5,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 504. H-1B VISA REFORM FOR SEMICONDUCTOR SECTOR.

  (a) SEMICONDUCTOR TALENT VISA CATEGORY. — Not later than 180 days after
      the date of enactment, the Secretary of Homeland Security shall
      establish a dedicated H-1B visa sub-allocation of not fewer than
      10,000 visas per fiscal year exclusively for positions in the
      semiconductor manufacturing, semiconductor equipment, semiconductor
      materials, and advanced packaging sectors.

  (b) EXPEDITED PROCESSING. — The Secretary of Homeland Security shall
      establish premium processing service standards guaranteeing
      adjudication within 15 calendar days for H-1B petitions in the
      semiconductor talent sub-allocation.

  (c) DUAL INTENT CLARIFICATION. — Petitions for semiconductor talent
      sub-allocation visas shall not be denied on the basis of immigrant
      intent, and beneficiaries shall be eligible for employment-based
      immigrant visa processing concurrent with H-1B status.

  (d) ANNUAL REPORT. — The Secretary of Homeland Security shall report
      annually to Congress on the utilization of the semiconductor talent
      sub-allocation, including the number of petitions filed, approved,
      and denied, countries of origin of beneficiaries, sponsoring
      employers, and average processing times.

SECTION 505. VETERAN RETRAINING AND TRANSITION PROGRAMS.

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the
      Secretary of Veterans Affairs and the Secretary of Defense, shall
      establish a Semiconductor Veterans Transition Program to recruit,
      train, and place separating service members and veterans into
      semiconductor manufacturing careers.

  (b) PROGRAM ELEMENTS. — The program shall include:

    (1) Semiconductor manufacturing skills assessment and gap analysis
        for military occupational specialties;

    (2) Accelerated training programs at community colleges near military
        installations and semiconductor manufacturing clusters;

    (3) Paid apprenticeship programs with domestic semiconductor
        manufacturers;

    (4) Relocation assistance for veterans accepting semiconductor
        manufacturing positions; and

    (5) Recognition of military training and experience for semiconductor
        industry certification and credentialing purposes.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $1,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 506. COMMUNITY COLLEGE TECHNICIAN PROGRAMS.

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Education, in coordination with
      the Secretary of Commerce, shall establish a grant program for
      community and technical colleges to develop and expand semiconductor
      manufacturing technician training programs.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $4,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.
