================================================================================
                 TITLE VII — ALLIED SEMICONDUCTOR COOPERATION
                 CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 701. SHORT TITLE.

  This title may be cited as the "Allied Semiconductor Partnership and
  Supply Chain Coordination Act of 2026."

SECTION 702. FINDINGS.

  Congress finds that:

    (1) No single nation possesses the complete semiconductor supply
        chain from raw materials through finished products; achieving
        supply chain resilience requires structured cooperation among
        the United States and its allies and partners.

    (2) The United States, European Union, Japan, and Republic of Korea
        collectively represent the democratic world's semiconductor
        design, equipment, materials, and manufacturing capabilities,
        encompassing firms including Nvidia, Intel, AMD, Qualcomm,
        Broadcom, ASML, Tokyo Electron, Samsung Electronics, SK Hynix,
        and leading research institutions.

    (3) The European Union enacted the European Chips Act in 2023,
        authorizing 43 billion euros for semiconductor manufacturing,
        research, and supply chain resilience; Japan has committed over
        $13 billion to support domestic semiconductor manufacturing,
        including subsidies for TSMC's Kumamoto fabrication facility and
        the Rapidus 2-nanometer consortium; and the Republic of Korea
        has enacted the K-Chips Act providing substantial tax incentives
        for semiconductor investment, including Samsung's planned $230
        billion semiconductor cluster in Yongin.

    (4) Joint allied semiconductor cooperation can reduce redundant
        investment, share research and development costs, strengthen
        collective export control enforcement, and create a resilient
        semiconductor supply chain less dependent on any single geography.

SECTION 703. US-EU-JAPAN-KOREA SEMICONDUCTOR ALLIANCE FRAMEWORK.

  (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The President, acting through the Secretary of State
      and the Secretary of Commerce, shall seek to negotiate and conclude a
      Quadrilateral Semiconductor Alliance Treaty or equivalent executive
      agreement among the United States, the European Union, Japan, and the
      Republic of Korea (in this title referred to as the "Alliance").

  (b) ALLIANCE OBJECTIVES. — The Alliance shall pursue:

    (1) Coordinated semiconductor manufacturing investment to ensure
        geographic diversification of advanced fabrication capacity;

    (2) Shared early warning and rapid response mechanisms for supply
        chain disruptions affecting semiconductor or critical material
        flows;

    (3) Joint research and development programs in advanced semiconductor
        technology, including sub-2-nanometer process nodes, advanced
        packaging, new materials, and next-generation lithography;

    (4) Coordinated export controls on semiconductor technology, equipment,
        and materials to prevent diversion to foreign adversaries;

    (5) Mutual recognition of trusted foundry, packaging, and test
        facility certifications to enable secure allied supply chains;

    (6) Joint workforce development and talent exchange programs.

  (c) ANNUAL ALLIED SEMICONDUCTOR SUMMIT. — The Secretary of Commerce shall
      convene an annual Allied Semiconductor Supply Chain Summit with
      ministerial-level representatives of Alliance members to review
      progress, coordinate investment strategies, and address emerging
      supply chain vulnerabilities.

SECTION 704. JOINT RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMS.

  (a) ALLIED SEMICONDUCTOR RESEARCH FUND. — The Secretary of Commerce shall
      establish the Allied Semiconductor Research Fund to co-finance joint
      research and development projects with Alliance member nations.

  (b) ELIGIBLE PROJECTS. — The Fund may support:

    (1) Joint development of sub-2-nanometer transistor architectures,
        including gate-all-around and complementary FET technologies;

    (2) Advanced lithography research, including High-NA EUV and beyond;

    (3) Three-dimensional heterogeneous integration and advanced packaging;

    (4) Wide-bandgap and ultra-wide-bandgap semiconductor materials for
        power electronics and radio-frequency applications;

    (5) Cryogenic computing and superconducting digital logic;

    (6) Chiplet interface standards and open chiplet ecosystem development.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $5,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.

SECTION 705. GLOBAL SUPPLY CHAIN MAPPING AND TRANSPARENCY.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — The Secretary of Commerce, in coordination with Alliance
      members, shall establish a secure, shared information platform for:

    (1) Mapping the complete allied semiconductor supply chain from raw
        materials through finished products;

    (2) Identifying single points of failure and critical dependencies;

    (3) Tracking inventories and production capacities of critical
        semiconductor inputs;

    (4) Sharing early warning indicators of supply disruptions.

  (b) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $500,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2031 to carry out this section.

SECTION 706. MUTUAL INVESTMENT FACILITATION.

  (a) IN GENERAL. — The Secretary of Commerce shall establish an Allied
      Semiconductor Investment Office to facilitate cross-border
      investment among Alliance members in semiconductor manufacturing,
      equipment, materials, and packaging facilities.

  (b) FUNCTIONS. — The Office shall:

    (1) Maintain a database of investment opportunities and site
        readiness information across Alliance member locations;

    (2) Coordinate regulatory review processes to expedite allied
        semiconductor investments;

    (3) Facilitate consortia of allied companies for large-scale
        semiconductor investment projects;

    (4) Provide technical assistance to Alliance members developing
        semiconductor investment incentive frameworks.

  (c) AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be
      appropriated $500,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
      through 2036 to carry out this section.
