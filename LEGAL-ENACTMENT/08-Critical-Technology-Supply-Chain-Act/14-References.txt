================================================================================
                    TITLE XII — REFERENCES AND AUTHORITIES
                    CTSCRA OF 2026
================================================================================

SECTION 1201. STATUTORY REFERENCES.

  This Act draws upon and amends, as applicable, the following Acts:

    (1) CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 (Public Law 117-167, Div. A),
        $52.7B semiconductor manufacturing incentive and R&D framework.

    (2) National Defense Authorization Act, FY 2021 (Public Law 116-283,
        Title XCIX), initial authorization for CHIPS incentives.

    (3) Energy Act of 2020 (Public Law 116-260, Div. Z, §7002),
        critical minerals definition and DOE research program.

    (4) Defense Production Act of 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4501 et seq.), Title
        III domestic industrial base authorities, as amended.

    (5) Export Control Reform Act of 2018 (50 U.S.C. 4801 et seq.),
        statutory authority for EAR administered by BIS.

    (6) Foreign Investment Risk Review Modernization Act of 2018
        (FIRRMA), CFIUS jurisdiction and authority expansion.

    (7) Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (Public Law 117-169), advanced
        manufacturing tax credits and critical mineral provisions.

    (8) Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act of 2021 (Public Law
        117-58), grid modernization and transmission provisions.

    (9) Atomic Energy Act of 1954 (42 U.S.C. 2011 et seq.), nuclear
        reactor and SMR licensing framework.

    (10) Immigration and Nationality Act (8 U.S.C. 1101 et seq.),
         H-1B visa provisions under §214(g).

SECTION 1202. AGENCY AND INDUSTRY REPORTS.

  This Act references the following foundational reports:

    (1) SIA, "2025 State of the U.S. Semiconductor Industry," March
        2025, providing manufacturing capacity and employment data.

    (2) SIA and BCG, "Emerging Resilience in the Semiconductor Supply
        Chain," April 2024, analyzing CHIPS Act impact and remaining
        vulnerabilities.

    (3) BIS, "Assessment of the Critical Supply Chains Supporting the
        U.S. ICT Industry," February 2024.

    (4) NIST, "Strategic Plan for the National Semiconductor Technology
        Center," 2024, providing R&D roadmap.

    (5) CHIPS Program Office, "Notice of Funding Opportunity: Commercial
        Fabrication Facilities," 88 Fed. Reg. 12566 (Feb. 28, 2023).

    (6) CHIPS Program Office, "Vision for Success," February 2024,
        articulating 20% leading-edge logic target by 2030.

    (7) DOE, "Critical Materials Assessment," 2023 and 2025 eds.,
        identifying rare earths, gallium, germanium, graphite as
        high supply risk materials.

    (8) DOE, "America's Strategy to Secure the Supply Chain for a Robust
        Clean Energy Transition," February 2023 (EO 14017 response).

    (9) DoD, "Annual Report on Military and Security Developments
        Involving the PRC," 2024 and 2025 editions.

    (10) ODNI, "Annual Threat Assessment," 2025, documenting adversarial
         technology acquisition efforts.

    (11) NSCAI, "Final Report," March 2021, recommending national AI
         compute infrastructure.

    (12) PCAST, "Revitalizing the U.S. Semiconductor Ecosystem,"
         September 2024.

    (13) GAO, "Semiconductor Supply Chain: Policy Options to Improve
         Resilience," GAO-24-106876, April 2024.

SECTION 1203. INDUSTRY INVESTMENT ANNOUNCEMENTS.

  This Act references the following private-sector commitments that
  are contingent upon, or accelerated by, Federal support:

    (1) TSMC Arizona Corp., $65B for three fabs (3nm/4nm) in Phoenix,
        AZ, announced December 2022 and expanded April 2024.

    (2) Intel Corp., $20B+ Ohio mega-fab campus (New Albany), announced
        January 2022, with potential for eight fabs exceeding $100B.

    (3) Samsung Electronics, ~$40B for fabs in Taylor and Austin, TX,
        announced November 2021 and updated 2024.

    (4) Micron Technology, up to $100B for memory fabs in Boise, ID and
        Clay, NY, announced September 2022-October 2023.

    (5) MP Materials Corp, Mountain Pass, CA, rare earth mining,
        separation, and magnet manufacturing (DPA Title III).

    (6) Lynas Rare Earths Ltd., Seadrift, TX, heavy rare earth
        separation facility ($258M DoD funding).

    (7) SK Hynix, West Lafayette, IN, advanced packaging and HBM
        facility (~$3.9B, announced 2024).
