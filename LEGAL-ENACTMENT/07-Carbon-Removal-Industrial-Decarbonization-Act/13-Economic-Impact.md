### SECTION 1007. COST REDUCTION TRAJECTORY.

| Cumulative Global DAC Deployment (Mt CO2) | Projected DAC Cost ($/ton CO2) |
|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| 0.1 (current) | $600-1,000 |
| 1 | $350-500 |
| 10 | $200-300 |
| 100 | $130-180 |
| 1,000 | $80-120 |

Target cost of $100 per ton CO2 is projected to be achievable at approximately 500 Mt cumulative deployment, consistent with analysis by the DOE Earthshots Initiative and the National Academies.

**(b) Trade Impact Projections.**

(1) Imports of covered goods are projected to decline by 5-15 percent upon CBAM implementation, with the largest declines in goods from countries with high emission intensities and low carbon prices;

(2) Domestic production of covered goods, particularly cement and steel, is projected to increase by 3-8 percent as the carbon cost advantage of domestic producers is recognized;

(3) Carbon leakage is projected to be reduced by 80-95 percent for covered sectors, consistent with modeling by Resources for the Future and the OECD; and

(4) WTO dispute risk is assessed as moderate, and the provisions of section 804 regarding international cooperation and equivalence determinations are designed to mitigate legal risk.

### SECTION 1009. RETURN ON INVESTMENT.

**(a) Direct Economic Return.** — The $80 billion in federal investment is projected to catalyze $320 billion in private investment and generate $400 billion in total industrial output, representing a 5:1 ratio of industrial output to federal investment.

**(b) Climate Benefit.** — The cumulative carbon dioxide removed through 2050 under the trajectory enabled by this Act is projected to exceed 15 gigatons of CO2. At the federal Interagency Working Group social cost of carbon of $190 per metric ton (2020 dollars, 2 percent near-term discount rate), the monetized climate benefit exceeds $2.8 trillion.

**(c) Health Co-Benefits.** — Reduction in criteria air pollutant emissions from industrial sources — including sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and fine particulate matter — is projected to generate annual health benefits of $15-25 billion by 2035, through reduced rates of asthma, cardiovascular disease, and premature mortality, consistent with EPA and Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health co-benefits analysis methodologies.

**(d) Energy Security.** — Reduction in industrial sector fossil fuel consumption is projected to reduce United States natural gas demand by 3-5 trillion cubic feet per year by 2035, equivalent to approximately 10 percent of current total United States natural gas consumption, enhancing energy security and reducing exposure to global energy price volatility.

**(e) Technology Export Opportunity.** — The global market for carbon removal and clean industrial technologies is projected to exceed $1 trillion per year by 2050. United States early-mover advantage under this Act positions domestic firms to capture a significant share of global export markets for DAC equipment, green cement and steel technologies, thermal batteries, and MRV services, projected at $100-200 billion per year in export revenue by 2040.
