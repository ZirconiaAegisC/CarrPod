# 119TH CONGRESS — CARBON REMOVAL AND INDUSTRIAL DECARBONIZATION ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 12: IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE

---

## TITLE X, SUBTITLE A — IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE AND MILESTONES

### SECTION 1001. IMPLEMENTATION PHASES AND TIMELINE.

**(a) Phase I — Program Establishment (Fiscal Years 2027-2028)**

The Secretary of Energy and other implementing agencies shall accomplish the following milestones during Phase I:

**(1) Rulemaking and Program Design (FY 2027).**
- Not later than 90 days after enactment: publish Federal Register notices for all grant, loan, and tax credit programs established under this Act;
- Not later than 180 days after enactment: publish draft regulations for the National MRV Standards under section 601;
- Not later than 270 days after enactment: publish final program guidance documents for Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park competitive solicitation, DAC Hub expansion solicitation, Industrial Capture Retrofit Program, and Industrial Boiler and Furnace Electrification Program;
- Not later than 365 days after enactment: publish final National MRV Standards;
- Not later than 365 days after enactment: Treasury shall publish regulations implementing expanded Section 45Q credit rates, new green cement and steel production tax credits, and thermal battery investment tax credit.

**(2) CBAM Implementation (FY 2027-2028).**
- Not later than 180 days after enactment: Treasury shall publish proposed regulations for the U.S. CBAM;
- Not later than 365 days after enactment: Treasury shall publish final CBAM regulations;
- Not later than January 1, 2028: CBAM reporting requirements enter into force (transitional period);
- Not later than January 1, 2029: CBAM certificate purchase requirement enters into force.

**(3) Initial Solicitations (FY 2028).**
- Not later than March 31, 2028: issue first competitive solicitation for DAC Hub expansion (Hubs 5-7);
- Not later than June 30, 2028: issue first competitive solicitation for Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks;
- Not later than September 30, 2028: issue first competitive solicitation for Industrial Capture Retrofit Program grants;
- Not later than December 31, 2028: issue first competitive solicitation for Industrial Boiler and Furnace Electrification grants.

**(b) Phase II — Early Deployment (Fiscal Years 2029-2031)**

Phase II milestones shall include:

**(1) DAC Hub Construction (FY 2029-2031).**
- Not later than March 31, 2029: award funding for DAC Hubs 5 through 7;
- Not later than September 30, 2029: award funding for DAC Hubs 8 through 10;
- Not later than December 31, 2030: DAC Hubs 5 through 7 commence initial operations at not less than 10 percent of design capacity;
- Not later than December 31, 2031: DAC Hubs 8 through 10 commence initial operations at not less than 10 percent of design capacity;
- Cumulative DAC operational capacity target: 2,000,000 metric tons CO2 per year by December 31, 2031.

**(2) Industrial Park Construction (FY 2029-2031).**
- Not later than June 30, 2029: award first five Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park grants;
- Not later than December 31, 2029: award remaining Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park grants;
- Not later than December 31, 2030: first three Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks commence construction;
- Not later than December 31, 2031: first Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park achieves initial operations with at least two tenant facilities.

**(3) MRV System Deployment (FY 2029-2031).**
- Not later than January 1, 2030: Digital MRV platform operational with initial 200 ground-based CO2 monitoring stations;
- Not later than January 1, 2031: Federal Carbon Credit Registry operational and issuing credits;
- Not later than December 31, 2031: 500 ground-based monitoring stations deployed and satellite-based CO2 verification validated.

**(4) Facility Retrofits (FY 2029-2031).**
- Not later than December 31, 2031: not fewer than 15 industrial carbon capture retrofit projects under construction;
- Not later than December 31, 2031: not fewer than five cement or steel legacy plant transition projects under construction.

**(c) Phase III — Scale-Up (Fiscal Years 2032-2034)**

Phase III milestones shall include:

**(1) Carbon Removal Capacity Expansion.**
- Cumulative DAC operational capacity target: 10,000,000 metric tons CO2 per year by December 31, 2032;
- Cumulative DAC operational capacity target: 50,000,000 metric tons CO2 per year by December 31, 2033;
- Cumulative DAC operational capacity target: 200,000,000 metric tons CO2 per year by December 31, 2034;
- Cumulative point-source capture capacity target: 100,000,000 metric tons CO2 per year by December 31, 2034.

**(2) Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks.**
- Not later than December 31, 2032: all ten Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks under construction;
- Not later than December 31, 2034: not fewer than seven Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks at full or partial operations;
- Not later than December 31, 2034: CCUS Backbone Network connecting not fewer than 12 facilities to not fewer than three geologic storage sites.

**(3) Federal Procurement.**
- Not later than fiscal year 2032: federal procurement of not less than 1,000,000 metric tons of verified carbon removal credits;
- Not later than fiscal year 2034: federal procurement of not less than 5,000,000 metric tons of verified carbon removal credits;
- Not later than fiscal year 2034: not less than 25 percent of federal cement and steel procurement from qualified green cement and green steel sources.

**(4) CBAM Full Implementation.**
- Not later than January 1, 2032: CBAM fully operational with certificate purchase requirement for all covered goods;
- Not later than January 1, 2033: first CBAM sectoral expansion review completed.

**(d) Phase IV — Full Capacity (Fiscal Years 2035-2036)**

Phase IV milestones shall include:

**(1) Carbon Removal Target.**
- Not later than December 31, 2035: cumulative United States carbon dioxide removal capacity reaches or exceeds 1,000,000,000 metric tons CO2 per year.

**(2) Industrial Emissions Reduction.**
- Not later than December 31, 2035: United States industrial sector greenhouse gas emissions reduced by not less than 50 percent from 2022 baseline levels.

**(3) Green Materials Procurement.**
- Not later than fiscal year 2035: 100 percent of federal cement and steel procurement from qualified green cement and green steel sources, with limited national security and unavailability exceptions.

**(4) Program Review.**
- Not later than June 30, 2035: comprehensive program review and sunset evaluation submitted to Congress;
- Not later than September 30, 2036: reauthorization legislation considered by relevant committees.

---

### SECTION 1002. MILESTONE ACCOUNTABILITY.

**(a) Quarterly Progress Reports.** — The Secretary of Energy shall submit quarterly progress reports to the relevant committees of Congress on progress toward each Phase I through Phase IV milestone.

**(b) Milestone Adjustments.** — If the Secretary determines that any milestone specified in this section is unlikely to be achieved on schedule, the Secretary shall submit to Congress, not later than 90 days before the milestone deadline, a detailed explanation of the causes of delay and a corrective action plan.

**(c) GAO Milestone Audits.** — The Comptroller General shall, not later than two years and five years after enactment, conduct independent audits of progress toward the milestones established in this section.

---

### SECTION 1003. INTERAGENCY COORDINATION.

**(a) Carbon Removal Interagency Working Group.** — There is established a Carbon Removal Interagency Working Group, co-chaired by the Secretary of Energy and the Director of the Office of Science and Technology Policy, and including representatives from:

(1) the Department of Energy;
(2) the Environmental Protection Agency;
(3) the Department of the Treasury;
(4) the Department of Commerce;
(5) the Department of the Interior;
(6) the Department of Transportation;
(7) the Department of Labor;
(8) the Department of State;
(9) the Department of Agriculture;
(10) the Council on Environmental Quality; and
(11) the Office of Management and Budget.

**(b) Functions.** — The Working Group shall:

(1) coordinate implementation of all titles of this Act;
(2) resolve interagency disputes or conflicting requirements;
(3) identify and address cross-cutting challenges; and
(4) meet not less frequently than quarterly.
