# 119TH CONGRESS — CARBON REMOVAL AND INDUSTRIAL DECARBONIZATION ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 04: TITLE II — POINT-SOURCE CARBON CAPTURE

---

## TITLE II — POINT-SOURCE CARBON CAPTURE

### SECTION 201. EXPANSION OF SECTION 45Q FOR INDUSTRIAL POINT-SOURCE CAPTURE.

**(a) Increased Credit Rate.** — Section 45Q(b)(1)(A) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by striking the existing rate and inserting "$100 per metric ton" for qualified carbon oxide captured at an industrial facility using carbon capture equipment and disposed of in secure geologic storage.

**(b) Industrial Facility Definition.** — For purposes of this section, the term "industrial facility" means:

(1) a cement manufacturing plant classified under NAICS 327310;

(2) an iron and steel mill classified under NAICS 331110 or 331111;

(3) a chemical manufacturing facility classified under NAICS 325;

(4) a petroleum refinery classified under NAICS 324110;

(5) a pulp and paper mill classified under NAICS 322;

(6) a glass manufacturing facility classified under NAICS 3272;

(7) a hydrogen production facility utilizing steam methane reforming or autothermal reforming; and

(8) any other manufacturing facility with annual CO2 process or combustion emissions exceeding 25,000 metric tons.

**(c) Prohibition on Enhanced Oil Recovery Usage.** — Carbon dioxide captured at facilities claiming the increased credit rate under this section shall not be used for enhanced oil recovery or any other form of hydrocarbon extraction. Carbon oxide utilized for such purposes shall be eligible only for the utilization credit rate established under section 45Q(b)(1)(A)(ii), and such credit shall not be increased by this section.

**(d) Direct Pay and Transferability.** — Facilities qualifying under this section shall be eligible for the direct pay election under section 6417 and the transferability provisions of section 6418 of the Internal Revenue Code.

**(e) Commence-Construction Window.** — The commence-construction deadline under section 45Q(d)(1) is extended to January 1, 2035, for facilities claiming credits under this section.

### SECTION 202. CEMENT, STEEL, AND CHEMICAL FACILITY CAPTURE RETROFIT PROGRAM.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an Industrial Carbon Capture Retrofit Program to provide financial assistance for the installation of carbon capture equipment at existing industrial facilities.

**(b) Eligible Recipients.** — Grants, loans, and loan guarantees under this section may be provided to:

(1) owners and operators of cement manufacturing facilities;

(2) owners and operators of iron and steel manufacturing facilities;

(3) owners and operators of chemical manufacturing facilities producing ammonia, ethylene, methanol, or hydrogen; and

(4) owners and operators of petroleum refineries that commit to a binding schedule for facility transition to production of zero-carbon fuels or materials.

**(c) Federal Cost Share.** — The federal cost share for projects under this section shall not exceed:

(1) 50 percent of total project costs for retrofit of existing facilities; and

(2) 60 percent of total project costs for facilities located in environmental justice communities, as defined by EPA's EJScreen tool or successor methodology, or in energy transition communities.

**(d) Project Selection Criteria.** — The Secretary shall prioritize projects that:

(1) demonstrate the largest absolute quantity of CO2 captured per dollar of federal investment;

(2) include binding commitments for permanent geologic storage, not enhanced oil recovery;

(3) are located in communities with high cumulative environmental burdens;

(4) include labor peace agreements, project labor agreements, or neutrality agreements with labor organizations;

(5) commit to local hiring and workforce training; and

(6) provide for community benefit agreements with local governments and community organizations.

**(e) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $15,000,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036, of which:

(1) not less than $5,000,000,000 shall be for cement facility capture retrofits;

(2) not less than $4,000,000,000 shall be for iron and steel facility capture retrofits;

(3) not less than $3,000,000,000 shall be for chemical manufacturing facility capture retrofits; and

(4) not more than $3,000,000,000 shall be for refinery and other industrial capture retrofits.

### SECTION 203. CARBON DIOXIDE PIPELINE TRANSPORTATION INFRASTRUCTURE.

**(a) National CO2 Pipeline Program.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Secretary of Transportation, shall establish a Carbon Dioxide Pipeline Transportation Infrastructure Program.

**(b) Program Activities.** — The Program shall:

(1) provide grants for the planning, design, permitting, and construction of carbon dioxide trunk pipelines connecting capture facilities to geologic storage sites or utilization facilities;

(2) provide grants for pipeline lateral connections from individual capture facilities to trunk pipelines;

(3) provide federal siting authority through the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission for CO2 pipelines that cross state boundaries or federal lands;

(4) establish pipeline safety standards for CO2 transportation, including requirements for leak detection, automatic shutoff valves, and community notification; and

(5) develop a National CO2 Pipeline Map identifying optimal pipeline corridors based on capture facility locations, storage site availability, and environmental constraints.

**(c) Federal Cost Share.** — For projects under this section, the federal cost share shall not exceed:

(1) 80 percent for planning, design, and permitting costs; and

(2) 50 percent for construction costs.

**(d) Siting Authority.** — Section 7 of the Natural Gas Act (15 U.S.C. 717f) is amended by adding at the end the following:

"(i) CARBON DIOXIDE TRANSPORTATION. — The Commission shall have authority, upon application by any person, to issue certificates of public convenience and necessity for the construction and operation of interstate carbon dioxide pipelines for the purpose of transporting carbon dioxide from capture facilities to geologic storage sites or utilization facilities. The provisions of subsections (c) through (h) shall apply to certificates for carbon dioxide pipelines in the same manner as for natural gas pipelines."

**(e) Community Engagement.** — Before issuance of a certificate for a carbon dioxide pipeline, the Commission shall require:

(1) at least two public hearings in each affected county or parish;

(2) consultation with Tribal Nations whose lands or treaty rights may be affected;

(3) an environmental justice analysis; and

(4) an emergency response plan.

### SECTION 204. CARBON CAPTURE EQUIPMENT MANUFACTURING TAX CREDIT.

**(a) In General.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

"SEC. 45BB. CARBON CAPTURE EQUIPMENT MANUFACTURING CREDIT.

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the carbon capture equipment manufacturing credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to 30 percent of the qualified investment for such taxable year with respect to any qualified manufacturing facility.

"(b) Qualified Manufacturing Facility. — The term 'qualified manufacturing facility' means a facility that manufactures, in the United States, components used in carbon capture equipment, including sorbents, solvents, membranes, compressors, contactors, heat exchangers, and related components.

"(c) Qualified Investment. — The term 'qualified investment' means, with respect to any qualified manufacturing facility, the basis of any property placed in service by the taxpayer during the taxable year which is part of such facility.

"(d) Prevailing Wage and Apprenticeship Requirements. — Rules similar to the rules of section 45(b)(7) shall apply."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendments made by this section shall apply to property placed in service after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 205. ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE SAFEGUARDS FOR CAPTURE FACILITIES.

**(a) Cumulative Impact Analysis.** — No federal financial assistance may be provided for a carbon capture facility under this Act unless the applicant has completed a cumulative impact analysis that:

(1) inventories existing sources of pollution in the affected area;

(2) models the facility's projected emissions of criteria air pollutants, hazardous air pollutants, and greenhouse gases;

(3) assesses the net change in local air quality resulting from the facility's operation; and

(4) is made publicly available and subject to a public comment period of not fewer than 60 days.

**(b) Air Quality Monitoring.** — Recipients of federal financial assistance under this title shall install and operate continuous air quality monitoring equipment at the facility boundary and shall report monitoring data to EPA and the relevant state environmental agency on a real-time basis.

**(c) Community Advisory Boards.** — Each facility receiving federal financial assistance under this title shall establish a Community Advisory Board composed of residents of communities located within three miles of the facility. The Board shall have authority to:

(1) review and comment on the facility's operating plans;

(2) request inspections by state or federal environmental authorities; and

(3) receive and disseminate information about the facility's environmental performance.
