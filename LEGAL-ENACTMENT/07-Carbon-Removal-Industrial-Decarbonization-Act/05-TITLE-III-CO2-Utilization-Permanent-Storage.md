# DOCUMENT 05: TITLE III — CO2 UTILIZATION AND PERMANENT STORAGE

## TITLE III — CO2 UTILIZATION AND PERMANENT STORAGE

### SECTION 301. CO2 MINERALIZATION IN CONCRETE PRODUCTS CREDIT.

**(a) In General.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

"SEC. 45CC. CARBON DIOXIDE MINERALIZATION IN CONCRETE CREDIT.

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the carbon dioxide mineralization in concrete credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to $50 per metric ton of qualified carbon oxide that is mineralized by the taxpayer in concrete during such taxable year.

"(b) Qualifying Conditions. — To qualify for the credit under this section, the taxpayer must demonstrate:

"(1) that the carbon oxide used is anthropogenic in origin and captured from an industrial source, direct air capture facility, or other qualifying source under section 45Q;

"(2) that the carbon oxide is permanently mineralized within the concrete matrix, with verification that the carbon oxide is chemically bound as stable carbonate minerals;

"(3) that the concrete product meets or exceeds applicable ASTM International standards for compressive strength, durability, and workability, including ASTM C39 for compressive strength and ASTM C1202 for chloride permeability; and

"(4) that the mineralization process does not result in a net increase in other environmental pollutants.

"(c) Permanence. — Carbon oxide mineralized in concrete shall be considered permanently stored for purposes of this section, with an expected storage duration of not less than 100 years under normal conditions of concrete use and end-of-life disposal.

"(d) Domestic Production. — The credit under this section shall apply only to concrete produced at facilities located within the United States or its territories.

"(e) Prevailing Wage. — Rules similar to the requirements of section 45(b)(7) relating to prevailing wage requirements shall apply."

**(b) Funding Limitation.** — The aggregate amount of credits allowed under this section shall not exceed $2,000,000,000 for any calendar year, as certified by the Secretary of the Treasury in consultation with the Secretary of Energy.

### SECTION 302. PERMANENT GEOLOGIC STORAGE VIA MINERAL CARBONATION.

**(a) Carbfix Method Deployment Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a program to accelerate deployment of permanent geologic carbon dioxide storage through in-situ mineral carbonation based on the Carbfix method, in which carbon dioxide dissolved in water is injected into reactive basaltic rock formations where it rapidly precipitates as stable carbonate minerals.

**(b) Program Activities.** — The program shall:

(1) conduct detailed geologic characterization of basaltic formations in the United States, including the Columbia River Basalt Group, the Deccan Traps-analog formations, and other identified mafic and ultramafic rock formations;

(2) fund not fewer than three large-scale demonstration projects of not less than 100,000 tons CO2 per year each, demonstrating in-situ carbon mineralization in different geologic settings;

(3) establish best practices and standard operating procedures for mineral carbonation injection, monitoring, and verification; and

(4) create a public database of basaltic formation characteristics, injection performance data, and mineralization rates.

**(c) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $1,500,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

### SECTION 303. EPA CLASS VI WELL PERMITTING REFORM.

**(a) Permitting Timeline.** — Section 1421 of the Safe Drinking Water Act (42 U.S.C. 300h) is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

"(g) CLASS VI WELL PERMITTING. —

"(1) TIMELINE. — The Administrator shall approve or deny a complete application for a Class VI injection well permit under this section not later than 18 months after the date on which the application is determined to be complete.

"(2) COMPLETENESS DETERMINATION. — The Administrator shall determine whether an application is complete not later than 90 days after its submission.

"(3) DEFINED TIMELINES FOR APPLICATION REVIEW STAGES. — The Administrator shall establish by regulation defined timelines for each stage of the Class VI permit review process, including geologic review, engineering review, and public participation.

"(4) STATE PRIMACY. — The Administrator shall establish a streamlined process for States to apply for and receive primary enforcement authority (primacy) for Class VI injection wells under section 1422, and shall provide technical and financial assistance to States seeking Class VI primacy.

"(5) PERMITTING CAPACITY. — There is authorized to be appropriated $200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to increase EPA and state Class VI permitting capacity, including hiring and training of permitting staff, acquisition of modeling and review tools, and development of digital application processing systems."

**(b) State Primacy Grants.** — The Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency shall establish a competitive grant program to assist States in applying for and implementing Class VI injection well primary enforcement authority. Grants may be used for:

(1) development of state regulatory frameworks;

(2) hiring and training of state permitting and enforcement personnel;

(3) procurement of geologic modeling software and data;

(4) development of state-level public participation processes; and

(5) other activities necessary to assume Class VI primacy.

### SECTION 304. CARBON UTILIZATION RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Carbon Utilization Research and Development Program to accelerate innovation in pathways that convert captured carbon dioxide into valuable products.

**(b) Priority Research Areas.** — The Program shall provide grants for research in the following areas:

(1) CO2-derived building materials, including carbon-cured concrete, carbon-negative aggregates, and carbonate-based wallboard;

(2) CO2-derived chemicals and polymers, including polycarbonates, polyurethanes, and carbon fibers;

(3) CO2-derived fuels, including sustainable aviation fuel, methanol, and synthetic natural gas produced using zero-carbon hydrogen;

(4) CO2-derived carbon black, graphite, and advanced carbon materials;

(5) CO2-derived agricultural products, including soil amendments and controlled-release fertilizers; and

(6) biological CO2 conversion pathways, including algae-based and microbial systems.

**(c) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $1,000,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

### SECTION 305. LIABILITY FRAMEWORK FOR GEOLOGIC STORAGE SITES.

**(a) In General.** — Title VI of the Clean Air Act (42 U.S.C. 7671 et seq.) is amended by adding at the end the following:

"SEC. 620. GEOLOGIC CARBON DIOXIDE STORAGE LIABILITY.

"(a) Post-Closure Stewardship. — Upon issuance of a certificate of project completion by the Administrator for a closed geologic carbon dioxide storage site, the Federal Government shall assume title to the stored carbon dioxide and shall assume long-term liability for the site, provided that:

"(1) the site has operated in compliance with its EPA-issued permit for the duration of injection operations;

"(2) post-injection monitoring over a period of not less than ten years demonstrates that the stored carbon dioxide is permanently contained and that no material leakage has occurred;

"(3) the operator has met all financial assurance requirements, including contributions to the Geologic Storage Trust Fund established under subsection (c); and

"(4) the operator has plugged all injection and monitoring wells and completed site closure in accordance with the approved closure plan.

"(b) Exceptions. — The Federal Government shall not assume liability for damages caused by the operator's gross negligence, intentional misconduct, or violation of any material term of its permit.

"(c) Geologic Storage Trust Fund. — There is established in the Treasury of the United States a Geologic Storage Trust Fund, to be administered by the Administrator, for the purpose of funding long-term monitoring, remediation, and emergency response for closed geologic storage sites. Operators of Class VI injection wells shall pay into the fund a fee of $0.05 per metric ton of carbon dioxide injected.

"(d) Long-Term Monitoring. — The Administrator shall establish a long-term monitoring program for closed geologic storage sites, which shall include satellite-based surface deformation monitoring, groundwater monitoring, and soil gas monitoring, with monitoring frequency and methods to be determined by the Administrator based on site-specific risk assessment."
