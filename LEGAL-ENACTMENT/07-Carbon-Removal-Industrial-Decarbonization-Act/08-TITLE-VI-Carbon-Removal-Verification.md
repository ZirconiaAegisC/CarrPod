# 119TH CONGRESS — CARBON REMOVAL AND INDUSTRIAL DECARBONIZATION ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 08: TITLE VI — CARBON REMOVAL VERIFICATION

---

## TITLE VI — CARBON REMOVAL VERIFICATION

### SECTION 601. NATIONAL MEASUREMENT, REPORTING, AND VERIFICATION STANDARDS.

**(a) Establishment of National MRV Standards.** — Not later than 18 months after the date of enactment of this Act, the Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency, the Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology, and the Administrator of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, shall establish by rule National Measurement, Reporting, and Verification (MRV) Standards for carbon dioxide removal.

**(b) Standard Requirements.** — The National MRV Standards shall include:

(1) **Measurement Protocols.** — Standardized methodologies for quantifying the mass of carbon dioxide captured from ambient air or point sources, including:
- (A) continuous emissions monitoring systems for point-source capture facilities;
- (B) mass balance accounting for direct air capture facilities;
- (C) sorbent and solvent loading and regeneration monitoring; and
- (D) energy consumption and emissions accounting for capture facility operations.

(2) **Reporting Requirements.** — Standardized formats and frequencies for reporting of:
- (A) gross CO2 captured;
- (B) net CO2 removed, accounting for all facility emissions including energy consumption;
- (C) CO2 transported, utilized, and stored;
- (D) energy consumption by source and type;
- (E) water consumption and discharge;
- (F) criteria air pollutant emissions; and
- (G) on a frequency of not less than monthly, with annual independent verification.

(3) **Verification Protocols.** — Independent third-party verification requirements including:
- (A) qualification and accreditation standards for third-party verifiers;
- (B) conflict-of-interest prohibitions for verifiers;
- (C) minimum scope and frequency of verification activities;
- (D) materiality thresholds for reporting variances; and
- (E) requirements for public disclosure of verification reports.

(4) **Storage Permanence.** — Methodologies for quantification of expected carbon storage duration, which shall:
- (A) require demonstration of expected storage permanence of not less than 100 years for quantification of removal credits;
- (B) provide for upward adjustment of credited removal quantities when expected permanence exceeds 1,000 years; and
- (C) require modeling and monitoring of leakage risk for all storage pathways.

**(c) Periodic Review.** — The National MRV Standards shall be reviewed and updated by the Secretary not less frequently than once every five years to incorporate advances in measurement science, remote sensing, and modeling capabilities.

### SECTION 602. DIGITAL MRV INFRASTRUCTURE USING SENSORS AND SATELLITES.

**(a) Digital MRV Program.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Administrator of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration and the Director of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, shall establish a Digital Measurement, Reporting, and Verification Program.

**(b) Program Components.** — The Program shall:

(1) deploy a national network of ground-based atmospheric CO2 concentration sensors, including at locations upwind and downwind of carbon removal facilities and storage sites, with not fewer than 500 monitoring stations by 2030;

(2) develop and validate satellite-based methods for detection and quantification of CO2 plumes from point sources and verification of CO2 flux changes at carbon removal facilities, leveraging the NASA Orbiting Carbon Observatory and successor missions;

(3) establish a publicly accessible digital platform that integrates ground-based sensor data, satellite observations, facility-reported data, and atmospheric transport model outputs to provide independent verification of reported carbon removal quantities;

(4) fund research and development in advanced monitoring technologies, including:
- (A) low-cost distributed CO2 sensor networks;
- (B) drone-based and aircraft-based CO2 flux measurement systems;
- (C) machine learning and data assimilation methods for CO2 flux estimation; and
- (D) isotopic tracer methods for distinguishing anthropogenic and natural CO2 sources; and

(5) provide open-source reference implementations of MRV algorithms and data processing pipelines.

**(c) Data Quality Standards.** — The Program shall establish data quality objectives for all monitoring data used for carbon credit quantification, including:
- (1) accuracy requirements for CO2 concentration measurements;
- (2) frequency and spatial resolution requirements;
- (3) data completeness thresholds; and
- (4) uncertainty quantification requirements.

**(d) Interagency Coordination.** — The Secretary shall establish a Digital MRV Interagency Working Group including representatives from the Department of Energy, EPA, NASA, NOAA, USGS, and NIST to coordinate federal digital MRV activities.

### SECTION 603. FEDERAL CARBON CREDIT REGISTRY.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish and maintain a Federal Carbon Credit Registry.

**(b) Registry Functions.** — The Registry shall:

(1) assign unique serial numbers to each metric ton of verified carbon dioxide removal;

(2) track the issuance, transfer, ownership, and retirement of carbon removal credits;

(3) maintain a public record of all credit transactions;

(4) prevent double-counting of credits across registries and programs; and

(5) interoperate with international carbon credit registries, including those established under Article 6 of the Paris Agreement.

**(c) Registry Participation.** — All carbon removal credits that receive federal financial support under Titles I through V of this Act, or that are used to comply with any federal carbon reduction requirement, shall be registered on the Federal Carbon Credit Registry.

**(d) Public Access.** — The Registry shall maintain a publicly accessible website providing:
- (1) real-time data on total credits issued, outstanding, and retired;
- (2) credit issuance history by project;
- (3) verification reports for each project; and
- (4) downloadable datasets in open formats.

**(e) Coordination with Voluntary Markets.** — The Secretary shall establish standards for the recognition of credits issued by voluntary carbon market registries, provided that such credits meet the National MRV Standards established under section 601.

### SECTION 604. FRAUD PREVENTION AND ENFORCEMENT.

**(a) Criminal Penalties.** — Section 741 of title 18, United States Code, is amended by adding at the end:

"Whoever knowingly makes any false statement, representation, or certification, or knowingly falsifies, conceals, or covers up any material fact, in connection with the issuance, transfer, or retirement of any carbon removal credit under federal law, or in any report or verification required under federal carbon removal programs, shall be fined under this title or imprisoned not more than 10 years, or both. Each metric ton of carbon dioxide removal that is fraudulently represented shall constitute a separate offense."

**(b) Civil Penalties.** — The Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency may assess a civil penalty of not more than $1,000,000 per violation against any person who knowingly submits false or misleading information in connection with the measurement, reporting, or verification of carbon dioxide removal.

**(c) Whistleblower Protection.** — Section 21F of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (15 U.S.C. 78u-6) is amended to provide whistleblower awards and protections for individuals who report fraudulent carbon credit issuance or trading to the Securities and Exchange Commission, the Environmental Protection Agency, or the Department of Energy.

**(d) MRV Enforcement Office.** — There is established within the Department of Energy an Office of MRV Enforcement, which shall have authority to:
- (1) investigate potential violations of MRV standards and carbon credit fraud;
- (2) audit verification reports and supporting data;
- (3) refer cases for criminal prosecution to the Department of Justice; and
- (4) impose civil penalties for violations.

---

### SECTION 605. INTERNATIONAL MRV COOPERATION AND STANDARDS ALIGNMENT.

**(a) Bilateral MRV Agreements.** — The Secretary of State, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, is authorized to negotiate bilateral and multilateral agreements for mutual recognition of carbon removal measurement, reporting, and verification standards.

**(b) International Standards Development.** — The Secretary of Energy shall direct the Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology to participate actively in international standards-setting bodies, including the International Organization for Standardization (ISO), to promote adoption of rigorous, science-based MRV standards consistent with the National MRV Standards established under section 601.

**(c) Article 6 Cooperation.** — The Secretary of State shall ensure that carbon removal credits generated in the United States and certified under this Act meet or exceed the requirements for internationally transferred mitigation outcomes under Article 6 of the Paris Agreement.

**(d) Developing Country MRV Assistance.** — The Secretary of State, in coordination with the Administrator of the United States Agency for International Development, shall establish a technical assistance program to support developing countries in building MRV capacity, deploying ground-based and satellite monitoring infrastructure, and establishing carbon credit registries.

*End of Title VI*
