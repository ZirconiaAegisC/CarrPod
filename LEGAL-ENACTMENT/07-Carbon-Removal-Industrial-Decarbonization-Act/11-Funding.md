## TITLE IX — FUNDING AND APPROPRIATIONS

### SECTION 901. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

**(a) In General.** — There are authorized to be appropriated to carry out Titles I through VII of this Act a total of $80,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036, to remain available until expended unless otherwise specified.

| 2031        | 10.0                      |
| 2032        | 11.0                      |
| 2033        | 11.0                      |
| 2034        | 10.0                      |
| 2035        | 8.0                       |

### SECTION 903. OVERSIGHT, REPORTING, AND GAO AUDIT REQUIREMENTS.

**(a) Annual Report.** — Not later than March 31 of each year, the Secretary of Energy shall submit to Congress a report detailing:

(1) obligations and expenditures under each title of this Act during the preceding fiscal year;

(2) project-level data on carbon removal quantities, costs, and technology performance for all federally supported projects;

(3) progress toward the 2035 carbon removal and emission reduction targets established in section 2;

(4) job creation, workforce development, and community benefit metrics; and

(5) programmatic recommendations for the subsequent fiscal year.

**(b) GAO Review.** — The Comptroller General of the United States shall:

(1) not later than two years after the date of enactment, submit a report assessing the effectiveness of program implementation, identifying any barriers to program execution, and recommending corrective actions;

(2) not later than five years after the date of enactment, submit a comprehensive evaluation of the Act's effectiveness in achieving its stated purposes, including an independent analysis of carbon removal quantities, cost reductions achieved, jobs created, and environmental outcomes; and

(3) annually audit the Federal Carbon Credit Registry established under section 603 for accuracy, completeness, and absence of double-counting.

**(c) Inspector General.** — The Inspector General of the Department of Energy shall conduct periodic audits of grant and loan programs under this Act to detect and prevent fraud, waste, and abuse.

**(d) Public Dashboard.** — The Secretary of Energy shall maintain a publicly accessible online dashboard providing real-time data on:

(1) appropriations, obligations, and expenditures by program;

(2) project locations, technologies, and performance;

(3) carbon removal quantities by project and in aggregate; and

(4) employment and community benefit metrics.

---

### SECTION 904. SUNSET AND PROGRAM REVIEW PROVISIONS.

**(a) Program Sunset.** — The authority to provide new grants, loans, and loan guarantees under Titles I, II, IV, V, and VII of this Act shall expire on September 30, 2036, except that:

(1) obligations incurred before such date shall remain in effect until completed or terminated in accordance with their terms; and

(2) monitoring, verification, and enforcement activities under Title VI shall continue for the duration of the relevant storage permanence period.

**(b) Tax Credit Sunset.** — Tax credits established or expanded under this Act shall apply to property placed in service or production occurring before the applicable sunset dates specified in each credit's statutory language, provided that property the construction of which begins before such date shall remain eligible for the credit upon placement in service.

**(c) Reauthorization Review.** — Not later than January 1, 2034, the Secretary of Energy shall submit to Congress a comprehensive reauthorization proposal that:

(1) evaluates the effectiveness of each program in achieving its objectives;

(2) recommends whether each program should be reauthorized, modified, or terminated;

(3) provides updated cost estimates for program continuation; and

(4) makes recommendations for new or expanded programs based on technological developments, market conditions, and evolving climate science.
