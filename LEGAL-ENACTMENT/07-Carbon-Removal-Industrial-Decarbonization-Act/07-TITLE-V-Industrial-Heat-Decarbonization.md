# 119TH CONGRESS — CARBON REMOVAL AND INDUSTRIAL DECARBONIZATION ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 07: TITLE V — INDUSTRIAL HEAT DECARBONIZATION

---

## TITLE V — INDUSTRIAL HEAT DECARBONIZATION

### SECTION 501. THERMAL BATTERY DEPLOYMENT INVESTMENT TAX CREDIT.

**(a) In General.** — Subpart E of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

"SEC. 48F. THERMAL BATTERY INVESTMENT CREDIT.

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 46, the thermal battery investment credit for any taxable year is an amount equal to 30 percent of the basis of any qualified thermal battery property placed in service by the taxpayer during such taxable year.

"(b) Qualified Thermal Battery Property. — The term 'qualified thermal battery property' means equipment that:

"(1) stores electricity as heat in solid storage media, including but not limited to carbon blocks, graphite, ceramic bricks, sand, crushed rock, or other refractory materials;

"(2) achieves a storage temperature of not less than 1,000 degrees Celsius when fully charged;

"(3) is capable of discharging thermal energy for industrial process heat applications, including but not limited to steam generation, hot air or gas production, kiln heating, furnace heating, and other industrial heating processes;

"(4) achieves a round-trip efficiency, defined as the ratio of useful heat delivered to electric energy input, of not less than 85 percent; and

"(5) is designed for a useful life of not less than 20 years.

"(c) Eligible Applications. — Qualified thermal battery property must be deployed in an industrial facility or clean industrial manufacturing park, and must displace fossil fuel combustion for process heat that would otherwise be supplied by natural gas, coal, fuel oil, or other fossil fuels.

"(d) Prevailing Wage and Apprenticeship. — The increased credit rate under subsection (a) shall apply only if the taxpayer satisfies the prevailing wage requirements of section 45(b)(7) and the apprenticeship requirements of section 45(b)(8), applied with respect to the construction, alteration, or repair of the property.

"(e) Direct Pay and Transferability. — The taxpayer may elect to receive a direct payment in lieu of the credit under section 6417 or may transfer the credit under section 6418."

**(b) Bonus Credit.** — The credit rate under subsection (a) shall be increased by 10 percentage points for property placed in service in an environmental justice community as defined by the EPA EJScreen tool, for a total credit rate of 40 percent.

**(c) Domestic Content.** — The credit rate shall be reduced to 20 percent for property that does not meet domestic content requirements requiring that not less than 55 percent of components by value are manufactured in the United States.

**(d) Effective Date.** — The credit shall apply to property placed in service after December 31, 2026, and before January 1, 2037.

### SECTION 502. LINEAR GENERATOR AND CLEAN DISTRIBUTED POWER CREDIT.

**(a) In General.** — Section 48F of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as added by section 501 of this Act, is amended by adding at the end the following new subsection:

"(f) Linear Generator Credit. — In addition to the credit under subsection (a), the energy credit under section 48 shall apply at a rate of 30 percent to qualified linear generator property.

"(g) Qualified Linear Generator Property. — The term 'qualified linear generator property' means equipment that:

"(1) generates electricity through free-piston linear motion directly driving electromagnetic conversion without intermediate rotary motion;

"(2) is capable of operating on multiple fuel types, including natural gas with an efficiency of not less than 45 percent, hydrogen with near-zero criteria pollutant emissions, and biogas or ammonia;

"(3) is designed for flexible operation including rapid start-up, load following, and high part-load efficiency;

"(4) achieves nitrogen oxide emissions of not more than 0.07 grams per brake horsepower-hour when operating on natural gas; and

"(5) is deployed at an industrial facility, clean industrial manufacturing park, or distributed energy system."

### SECTION 503. INDUSTRIAL BOILER AND FURNACE ELECTRIFICATION PROGRAM.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an Industrial Boiler and Furnace Electrification Program to provide grants for the replacement of fossil fuel-fired industrial boilers, furnaces, kilns, ovens, and other process heating equipment with electric alternatives.

**(b) Eligible Equipment.** — Grants may be used for procurement and installation of:

(1) electric resistance heaters and furnaces;

(2) electric induction heating systems;

(3) electric arc furnaces;

(4) electric infrared and ultraviolet curing systems;

(5) electric heat pumps for low and medium-temperature process heat;

(6) electrode boilers for steam generation;

(7) electric kilns for cement, ceramics, and refractories manufacturing; and

(8) thermal battery systems integrated with electric process heating equipment.

**(c) Application Priority.** — The Secretary shall prioritize applications that:

(1) demonstrate the largest absolute reduction in on-site fossil fuel combustion per dollar of federal grant;

(2) are located in environmental justice communities with high cumulative pollution burdens;

(3) commit to powering electric process heat equipment with zero-carbon electricity through on-site generation, power purchase agreements, or utility green tariff programs;

(4) include binding commitments for local hiring and prevailing wage payment; and

(5) provide worker retraining for employees transitioning from fossil fuel-fired equipment to electric systems.

**(d) Federal Cost Share.** — The federal cost share for projects under this section shall not exceed:

(1) 50 percent of total eligible project costs; or

(2) 60 percent for facilities located in environmental justice communities.

**(e) Technical Assistance.** — The Secretary, through the Industrial Assessment Centers program and the Department of Energy national laboratories, shall provide no-cost technical assistance to industrial facilities for the assessment of electrification potential, including analysis of process heat requirements, electric infrastructure needs, and economic feasibility.

**(f) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

### SECTION 504. INDUSTRIAL HEAT TRANSFORMATION FUND.

**(a) Establishment.** — There is established in the Treasury of the United States an Industrial Heat Transformation Fund, to be administered by the Secretary of Energy.

**(b) Purpose.** — The Fund shall provide grants, direct loans, and loan guarantees for projects that reduce or eliminate greenhouse gas emissions from industrial process heating through:

(1) electrification of process heat equipment;

(2) deployment of thermal energy storage systems for industrial applications;

(3) installation of zero-carbon on-site power generation for industrial heat supply;

(4) fuel switching from coal, oil, or natural gas to green hydrogen, sustainable biomass, or other zero-carbon fuels; and

(5) industrial heat recovery and efficiency improvement projects.

**(c) Funding Mechanisms.** — The Fund shall allocate its resources as follows:

(1) not less than 40 percent for direct grants to eligible projects;

(2) not less than 30 percent for direct loans and loan guarantees; and

(3) not more than 30 percent for other forms of credit support and financial assistance.

**(d) Eligibility.** — Eligibility for Fund assistance shall be limited to:

(1) owners and operators of industrial facilities classified under NAICS sectors 31 through 33 (manufacturing);

(2) developers of clean industrial manufacturing parks; and

(3) consortia of industrial facility operators, technology providers, and financial institutions.

**(e) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $10,000,000,000 to carry out this section and section 503 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

---

### SECTION 505. PROCESS HEAT INNOVATION AND DEMONSTRATION PROJECTS.

**(a) Industrial Heat Innovation Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish an Industrial Heat Innovation Program to fund research, development, and demonstration of advanced process heat technologies.

**(b) Priority Technology Areas.** — The Program shall fund projects in the following areas:

(1) ultra-high-temperature heat pumps capable of delivering process heat above 200 degrees Celsius;

(2) advanced thermal storage materials with melting points above 1,500 degrees Celsius for high-temperature industrial heat supply;

(3) hydrogen combustion systems for kilns, furnaces, and reactors that achieve ultra-low nitrogen oxide emissions without flue gas treatment;

(4) electric plasma heating and microwave processing systems for industrial applications;

(5) supercritical CO2 power cycles for waste heat recovery and conversion; and

(6) digital control and optimization systems for electrified process heat management.

**(c) Demonstration Projects.** — The Secretary shall fund not fewer than ten full-scale industrial heat decarbonization demonstration projects across diverse industrial sectors, including cement, steel, chemicals, food processing, glass, and pulp and paper.

**(d) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.
