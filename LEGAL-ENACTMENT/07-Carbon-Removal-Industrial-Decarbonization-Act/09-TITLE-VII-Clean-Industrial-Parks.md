# 119TH CONGRESS — CARBON REMOVAL AND INDUSTRIAL DECARBONIZATION ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 09: TITLE VII — CLEAN INDUSTRIAL PARKS

---

## TITLE VII — CLEAN INDUSTRIAL PARKS

### SECTION 701. ESTABLISHMENT OF CLEAN INDUSTRIAL MANUFACTURING PARKS.

**(a) Program Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park Program to provide financial and technical assistance for the development of geographically co-located industrial facilities sharing common clean energy and carbon management infrastructure.

**(b) Number of Parks.** — The Secretary shall select not fewer than ten Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks for funding under this section.

**(c) Park Requirements.** — Each Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park shall:

(1) include not fewer than two distinct industrial manufacturing facilities representing not fewer than two distinct NAICS manufacturing subsectors;

(2) include not fewer than one carbon dioxide removal facility or carbon capture facility;

(3) be served by shared infrastructure including CO2 pipeline connections to geologic storage sites or utilization facilities;

(4) be served by shared green hydrogen production and distribution infrastructure, where technically and economically feasible;

(5) have access to dedicated zero-carbon electricity generation or firm grid-connected renewable energy supply;

(6) include on-site or adjacent workforce training facilities; and

(7) be developed under a master plan that incorporates environmental justice principles, community engagement, and cumulative impact assessment.

**(d) Selection Criteria.** — In selecting Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks, the Secretary shall prioritize proposals that:

(1) are located in or near environmental justice communities, energy transition communities, or communities with unemployment rates exceeding the national average;

(2) demonstrate strong support from state and local governments, including commitments to expedited permitting, infrastructure improvements, and tax incentives;

(3) include commitments from industrial tenants to locate or expand manufacturing operations within the park;

(4) utilize domestic supply chains for construction materials and manufacturing equipment;

(5) incorporate circular economy principles, including industrial symbiosis where waste or byproduct streams from one facility serve as inputs for another; and

(6) include binding commitments for local hiring, prevailing wage, and apprenticeship utilization.

**(e) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036, of which:

(1) not less than $3,000,000,000 shall be for capital grants for park infrastructure development;

(2) not less than $1,000,000,000 shall be for park planning, design, environmental review, and permitting; and

(3) not more than $1,000,000,000 shall be for technical assistance, workforce training, and program management.

### SECTION 702. SHARED INFRASTRUCTURE FINANCING AND DEVELOPMENT.

**(a) Shared Infrastructure Program.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Shared Industrial Infrastructure Financing Program to provide loans and loan guarantees for common-use infrastructure at Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks.

**(b) Eligible Infrastructure.** — The Program shall provide financing for:

(1) CO2 gathering pipelines, compression stations, and injection infrastructure;

(2) green hydrogen production facilities, including electrolyzers, renewable energy integration, hydrogen storage, and distribution pipelines;

(3) electric transmission and distribution infrastructure, including substations and microgrids;

(4) water treatment and distribution infrastructure;

(5) industrial wastewater treatment and resource recovery facilities;

(6) shared logistics infrastructure including rail spurs, barge terminals, and material handling equipment; and

(7) shared monitoring, control, and data systems for park operations.

**(c) Financing Terms.** — Loans under this section shall:

(1) have a term of not more than 30 years;

(2) bear interest at a rate not less than the rate for United States Treasury obligations of comparable maturity; and

(3) provide for a federal financing share of not more than 80 percent of eligible project costs.

**(d) Loan Guarantees.** — The Secretary may guarantee up to 90 percent of loans made by private lenders for shared infrastructure at Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks.

### SECTION 703. GREEN HYDROGEN PIPELINE AND CCUS BACKBONE NETWORKS.

**(a) National Green Hydrogen Pipeline Program.** — The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Secretary of Transportation, shall establish a program to support development of regional hydrogen pipeline networks connecting Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks, hydrogen production facilities, and industrial end-users.

**(b) Program Activities.** — The Program shall:

(1) provide grants for hydrogen pipeline feasibility studies, routing, and design;

(2) provide grants for construction of hydrogen trunk pipelines and lateral connections;

(3) develop hydrogen pipeline safety standards and best practices, incorporating lessons from existing natural gas pipeline standards; and

(4) coordinate with the Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs program established under section 40314 of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.

**(c) CCUS Backbone Network.** — The Secretary shall develop a Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS) Backbone Network connecting carbon capture facilities at Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks and other industrial facilities to geologic storage sites.

**(d) Network Development.** — The CCUS Backbone Network shall:

(1) connect not fewer than 20 carbon capture facilities to not fewer than five geologic storage sites by 2035;

(2) provide open-access transportation service for captured carbon dioxide at regulated rates;

(3) be designed with excess capacity to accommodate future carbon capture projects; and

(4) be developed through competitive procurement of design-build-operate contracts.

**(e) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $3,000,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

### SECTION 704. RENEWABLE ENERGY INTEGRATION FOR INDUSTRIAL PARKS.

**(a) Clean Energy Requirements.** — Each Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park shall, by not later than calendar year 2035, be supplied by not less than 90 percent zero-carbon electricity on an annual basis.

**(b) On-Site Generation Incentives.** — The Secretary of Energy shall provide supplemental grants of up to 10 percent of total park infrastructure costs for Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks that include on-site zero-carbon electricity generation capacity equal to not less than 50 percent of park electricity demand.

**(c) Virtual Power Purchase Agreements.** — The Secretary shall provide technical assistance to Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks and their industrial tenants in negotiating virtual power purchase agreements with renewable energy developers.

**(d) Energy Storage Integration.** — Clean Industrial Manufacturing Parks shall be eligible for additional grants of up to 5 percent of total park infrastructure costs for deployment of battery energy storage systems or thermal energy storage systems with capacity equal to not less than four hours of average park electricity demand.

---

### SECTION 705. CLEAN INDUSTRIAL PARK SELECTION AND GOVERNANCE.

**(a) Selection Process.** — The Secretary of Energy shall conduct a competitive solicitation for Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park proposals not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act.

**(b) Application Requirements.** — Applications shall include:

(1) a master plan for the proposed park;

(2) letters of intent or binding commitments from prospective industrial tenants;

(3) environmental review documentation;

(4) a community benefits plan;

(5) a workforce development plan;

(6) a financing plan demonstrating the ability to secure matching funds; and

(7) governance documents for park management and shared infrastructure operation.

**(c) Governance Requirements.** — Each Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park shall:

(1) establish a park management entity with authority to own, operate, and maintain shared infrastructure;

(2) establish a park governance board including representatives of industrial tenants, local government, labor organizations, and community organizations; and

(3) adopt by-laws providing for transparent decision-making, financial accountability, and equitable allocation of shared infrastructure costs.

**(d) Reporting Requirements.** — Each Clean Industrial Manufacturing Park shall submit to the Secretary an annual report detailing:

(1) park occupancy and employment;

(2) greenhouse gas emissions by facility and in aggregate;

(3) shared infrastructure utilization and performance;

(4) energy consumption by source; and

(5) compliance with community benefits and workforce commitments.
