# 119TH CONGRESS — CARBON REMOVAL AND INDUSTRIAL DECARBONIZATION ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 03: TITLE I — DIRECT AIR CAPTURE SCALE-UP

---

## TITLE I — DIRECT AIR CAPTURE SCALE-UP

### SECTION 101. EXPANSION OF SECTION 45Q TAX CREDIT FOR DIRECT AIR CAPTURE.

**(a) In General.** — Section 45Q of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (26 U.S.C. 45Q) is amended as follows:

**(1) Increased Credit Rate for Direct Air Capture.** — Subsection (b)(1)(B) is amended by striking "$180" and inserting "$250" for qualified carbon oxide captured using direct air capture equipment and disposed of in secure geologic storage.

**(2) Definition of Direct Air Capture Equipment.** — Subsection (d) is amended by adding at the end the following new paragraph:

"(6) DIRECT AIR CAPTURE EQUIPMENT. — The term 'direct air capture equipment' means any equipment, facility, or process that separates carbon dioxide from ambient air using chemical sorbents, liquid solvents, solid adsorbents, electrochemical processes, or other physical or chemical means, and produces a concentrated stream of carbon dioxide for utilization or permanent geologic storage that shall not be used for enhanced oil recovery or any form of hydrocarbon production."

**(3) Prevailing Wage and Apprenticeship Requirements.** — The increased credit rate under paragraph (1) shall apply only to facilities that satisfy the prevailing wage requirements of subsection (f) and the apprenticeship requirements of subsection (g).

**(4) Direct Pay Election.** — Qualified facilities claiming the credit under this section shall be eligible for the direct pay election under section 6417 of the Internal Revenue Code for the full credit amount.

**(b) Effective Date.** — The amendments made by this section shall apply to facilities placed in service after December 31, 2026.

### SECTION 102. DIRECT AIR CAPTURE HUB PROGRAM EXPANSION.

**(a) Authorization and Purpose.** — The Secretary of Energy shall expand the Regional Direct Air Capture Hubs program established under section 4104 of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (42 U.S.C. 16371 note; Public Law 117-58) as follows:

**(b) Number of Hubs.** — Not fewer than ten Regional Direct Air Capture Hubs shall be established, expanding from the initial four hubs currently authorized.

**(c) Geographic Distribution.** — The Secretary shall ensure that DAC Hubs are geographically distributed across the United States, with not fewer than:

(1) two hubs located in the Gulf Coast region, proximate to existing geologic storage resources and industrial infrastructure;

(2) two hubs located in the Western United States, including one proximate to basaltic formations suitable for in-situ mineralization;

(3) two hubs located in the Midwest, proximate to agricultural and industrial carbon dioxide sources and sinks;

(4) one hub located in the Appalachian region, serving as an economic transition anchor for fossil energy communities;

(5) one hub located in the Great Plains region, leveraging wind energy resources for zero-carbon DAC operations;

(6) one hub located in the Pacific Northwest, proximate to the Columbia River Basalt Group; and

(7) one hub located in Alaska, addressing permafrost thaw mitigation and leveraging natural gas infrastructure for carbon transport.

**(d) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $30,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this section, of which:

(1) not less than $20,000,000,000 shall be for direct grants to DAC Hub project developers;

(2) not less than $5,000,000,000 shall be for shared infrastructure including CO2 pipeline laterals, geologic characterization, and injection infrastructure;

(3) not less than $3,000,000,000 shall be for technology development, testing, and demonstration at DAC Hub sites; and

(4) not more than $2,000,000,000 shall be for program administration, technical assistance, and environmental review.

**(e) Federal Cost Share.** — The federal cost share for DAC Hub projects shall not exceed:

(1) 80 percent for Phase I (initial design, site characterization, environmental review, and permitting);

(2) 60 percent for Phase II (construction and commissioning); and

(3) 50 percent for Phase III (operations and monitoring for a period of not more than five years).

**(f) Hub Capacity Requirements.** — Each DAC Hub shall have planned capture capacity of not less than 1,000,000 metric tons of CO2 per year at full operational capacity.

**(g) Community Benefits Plans.** — Each applicant for DAC Hub funding shall submit a Community Benefits Plan demonstrating:

(1) engagement with local communities, labor unions, and Tribal Nations;

(2) commitments to local hiring, including hiring from environmental justice communities and energy transition communities;

(3) prevailing wage and apprenticeship utilization plans; and

(4) environmental justice impact assessments.

**(h) Technology Diversity.** — The Secretary shall ensure that the portfolio of funded DAC Hubs includes not fewer than three distinct technological approaches to direct air capture, including solid sorbent-based systems, liquid solvent-based systems, and electrochemical or mineralization-based approaches.

### SECTION 103. FEDERAL PROCUREMENT COMMITMENTS FOR CARBON REMOVAL.

**(a) Carbon Removal Procurement Program.** — Not later than 180 days after the date of enactment of this Act, the Administrator of General Services, in coordination with the Secretary of Defense and the heads of other Federal agencies, shall establish a Carbon Removal Procurement Program.

**(b) Procurement Targets.** — The Program shall establish binding procurement targets as follows:

(1) for calendar year 2028: not less than 100,000 metric tons of verified carbon dioxide removal;

(2) for calendar year 2030: not less than 1,000,000 metric tons of verified carbon dioxide removal;

(3) for calendar year 2035: not less than 10,000,000 metric tons of verified carbon dioxide removal; and

(4) for calendar year 2040: not less than 50,000,000 metric tons of verified carbon dioxide removal.

**(c) Advance Purchase Commitments.** — The Program is authorized to enter into advance purchase commitments for carbon removal credits of up to 15 years' duration, at prices not exceeding the social cost of carbon as determined by the Interagency Working Group on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases.

**(d) Quality Standards.** — All carbon removal credits procured under this section shall meet the measurement, reporting, and verification standards established under Title VI of this Act and shall represent permanent storage of not less than 1,000 years' expected duration.

**(e) Domestic Priority.** — Not less than 75 percent of carbon removal credits procured under this section shall be sourced from facilities located within the United States or its territories.

### SECTION 104. DAC TECHNOLOGY DEVELOPMENT AND COMMERCIALIZATION GRANTS.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Direct Air Capture Technology Development and Commercialization Grant Program to accelerate cost reduction pathways for DAC technologies.

**(b) Grant Categories.** — The Program shall provide grants in the following categories:

(1) Advanced Sorbent and Solvent Development — for research and development of novel capture materials with higher selectivity, faster kinetics, lower regeneration energy requirements, and longer operational lifetimes;

(2) Modular DAC Systems — for development and demonstration of standardized, containerized DAC units capable of being manufactured at scale and deployed rapidly;

(3) Energy Integration — for projects integrating DAC systems with zero-carbon energy sources, including geothermal, nuclear, and renewable energy with storage;

(4) Manufacturing Scale-Up — for grants to domestic manufacturers of DAC components, including contactors, fans, pumps, heat exchangers, and control systems; and

(5) Pilot and Demonstration — for projects at the 100 to 10,000 ton per year scale bridging laboratory research to commercial deployment.

**(c) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $2,000,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036, to remain available until expended.

---

### SECTION 105. WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT FOR CARBON REMOVAL INDUSTRIES.

**(a) DAC Workforce Training Program.** — The Secretary of Labor, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish a Direct Air Capture and Carbon Removal Workforce Training Program.

**(b) Program Activities.** — The Program shall provide grants to:

(1) community colleges, technical schools, and labor-management training partnerships to develop curriculum for DAC facility operations, maintenance, and construction;

(2) apprenticeship programs registered with the Department of Labor that train workers in carbon removal technology installation, operation, and monitoring;

(3) university-based research centers focused on carbon removal science and engineering; and

(4) programs that recruit and train workers from environmental justice communities, energy transition communities, and communities with high unemployment rates.

**(c) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $500,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.
