# 119TH CONGRESS — CARBON REMOVAL AND INDUSTRIAL DECARBONIZATION ACT OF 2026
# DOCUMENT 06: TITLE IV — GREEN CEMENT AND STEEL DEPLOYMENT

---

## TITLE IV — GREEN CEMENT AND STEEL DEPLOYMENT

### SECTION 401. GREEN CEMENT PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT.

**(a) In General.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new section:

"SEC. 45DD. GREEN CEMENT PRODUCTION CREDIT.

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the green cement production credit for any taxable year is $40 per metric ton of qualified green cement produced by the taxpayer in the United States during such taxable year.

"(b) Qualified Green Cement. — The term 'qualified green cement' means cementitious material:

"(1) that meets or exceeds the performance standards of ASTM C150 for portland cement, ASTM C595 for blended hydraulic cement, or ASTM C1157 for hydraulic cement, as applicable;

"(2) that achieves lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions of not more than 0.25 metric tons of CO2-equivalent per metric ton of cementitious material, representing at least a 70 percent reduction relative to the United States industry average of approximately 0.84 metric tons of CO2 per metric ton of ordinary portland cement;

"(3) for which lifecycle emissions have been verified by an independent third party in accordance with standards established by the Secretary of Energy under this section; and

"(4) that is produced at a facility located within the United States or its territories.

"(c) Lifecycle Emissions Calculation. — The Secretary of Energy, in consultation with the Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency, shall establish a standardized methodology for calculating lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions of cementitious materials, which shall include:

"(1) process emissions from calcination and chemical reactions;

"(2) combustion emissions from fuel use;

"(3) indirect emissions from electricity consumption;

"(4) emissions from transportation of raw materials and finished products; and

"(5) credit for carbon dioxide uptake through recarbonation over the product's life cycle.

"(d) Technology-Neutral Standard. — The credit under this section shall be available for any cement production pathway that meets the lifecycle emission threshold, including but not limited to:

"(1) calcium silicate-based cement produced from non-carbonate raw materials, such as the process demonstrated by Brimstone Energy;

"(2) cement produced via electrochemical processes, such as the process demonstrated by Sublime Systems;

"(3) blended cements using calcined clays, natural pozzolans, or other supplementary cementitious materials that substantially reduce the clinker-to-cement ratio;

"(4) cement produced using carbon capture and storage on conventional kilns, provided that lifecycle emissions meet the threshold; and

"(5) other innovative cement production pathways that meet the emissions performance standard.

"(e) Phase-Down of Credit. — The credit rate under subsection (a) shall begin phasing down in the sixth calendar year after the year in which United States production of green cement first exceeds 10,000,000 metric tons per year, at a rate of 10 percent per year thereafter, provided however that the credit rate shall not be reduced below $10 per ton."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The credit shall apply to green cement produced after December 31, 2027.

### SECTION 402. GREEN STEEL PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT.

**(a) In General.** — Subpart D of part IV of subchapter A of chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended by section 401 of this Act, is further amended by adding at the end the following new section:

"SEC. 45EE. GREEN STEEL PRODUCTION CREDIT.

"(a) Allowance of Credit. — For purposes of section 38, the green steel production credit for any taxable year is $85 per metric ton of qualified green steel produced by the taxpayer in the United States during such taxable year.

"(b) Qualified Green Steel. — The term 'qualified green steel' means crude steel or hot-rolled steel:

"(1) that meets or exceeds applicable ASTM International standards for steel products;

"(2) that achieves lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions of not more than 0.50 metric tons of CO2-equivalent per metric ton of crude steel, representing at least a 70 percent reduction relative to approximately 1.85 metric tons of CO2 per metric ton for conventional blast furnace-basic oxygen furnace production; and

"(3) for which lifecycle emissions have been verified by an independent third party in accordance with standards established by the Secretary of Energy.

"(c) Eligible Production Pathways. — The credit shall be available for steel produced via:

"(1) direct reduced iron (DRI) using green hydrogen, followed by electric arc furnace steelmaking powered by zero-carbon electricity;

"(2) molten oxide electrolysis, such as the process demonstrated by Boston Metal;

"(3) hydrogen plasma smelting reduction;

"(4) conventional electric arc furnace steelmaking using 100 percent recycled scrap and zero-carbon electricity; and

"(5) other innovative steelmaking pathways that meet the emissions performance standard.

"(d) Phase-Down of Credit. — The credit rate shall begin phasing down in the sixth calendar year after the year in which United States production of green steel first exceeds 15,000,000 metric tons per year, at a rate of 10 percent per year thereafter, provided however that the credit rate shall not be reduced below $20 per ton."

**(b) Effective Date.** — The credit shall apply to green steel produced after December 31, 2027.

### SECTION 403. FEDERAL PROCUREMENT PREFERENCES FOR LOW-CARBON MATERIALS.

**(a) Buy Clean America Program.** — Not later than one year after the date of enactment of this Act, the Administrator of General Services, in coordination with the Secretary of Transportation and the Secretary of Defense, shall establish a Buy Clean America Program for federal procurement of construction materials.

**(b) Procurement Standards.** — The Program shall require that, for any federally funded construction project with a total estimated cost exceeding $25,000,000:

(1) not less than 50 percent of cement and concrete products procured shall be qualified green cement as defined in section 401, beginning in fiscal year 2030;

(2) not less than 50 percent of structural steel products procured shall be qualified green steel as defined in section 402, beginning in fiscal year 2031; and

(3) beginning in fiscal year 2035, all cement, concrete, and structural steel products procured shall be qualified green cement and qualified green steel respectively, with limited exceptions for national security and unavailability.

**(c) Environmental Product Declarations.** — Not later than fiscal year 2029, all bidders on federal construction contracts exceeding $25,000,000 shall be required to submit facility-specific Type III Environmental Product Declarations for cement, concrete, and steel products.

**(d) Buy Clean Task Force.** — There is established an interagency Buy Clean Task Force, co-chaired by the Administrator of General Services and the Federal Chief Sustainability Officer, to coordinate implementation of federal low-carbon procurement policies across all agencies.

### SECTION 404. LEGACY PLANT TRANSITION GRANT PROGRAM.

**(a) Establishment.** — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a Legacy Industrial Plant Transition Grant Program to provide financial assistance to owners and operators of existing cement and steel manufacturing facilities for retooling, retrofitting, or replacing carbon-intensive production equipment with low-carbon alternatives.

**(b) Eligible Activities.** — Grants may be used for:

(1) conversion of cement kilns from limestone-based raw material to non-carbonate raw materials;

(2) installation of carbon capture equipment on existing cement kilns;

(3) conversion of blast furnaces to direct reduced iron or electric arc furnace steelmaking;

(4) installation of electrolytic steelmaking equipment;

(5) installation of on-site zero-carbon electricity generation and storage; and

(6) workforce retraining for employees transitioning from carbon-intensive to low-carbon production processes.

**(c) Grant Amounts.** — Individual grants shall not exceed the lesser of $500,000,000 or 50 percent of total eligible project costs.

**(d) Worker and Community Transition.** — As a condition of receiving a grant under this section, recipients shall submit a Worker and Community Transition Plan that:

(1) provides for continuation of employment at not less than prevailing wages for affected employees during and after the transition period;

(2) provides for retraining of affected employees for new production processes;

(3) provides for continuation of health care and retirement benefits during the transition period; and

(4) provides for community impact mitigation including maintenance of local tax base and support for affected local businesses.

**(e) Funding.** — There is authorized to be appropriated $5,000,000,000 to carry out this section for fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

### SECTION 405. BUY CLEAN AMERICA PERFORMANCE STANDARDS.

**(a) Material Carbon Intensity Standards.** — The Administrator of the Environmental Protection Agency, in coordination with the Secretary of Energy, shall establish by regulation maximum greenhouse gas intensity standards for construction materials procured with federal funds, including:

(1) cement and concrete products;

(2) structural and reinforcing steel;

(3) flat glass;

(4) asphalt and asphalt mixtures; and

(5) mineral wool insulation.

**(b) Progressive Stringency.** — The standards established under subsection (a) shall be set at the best-performing 20 percent of domestic production for each material category upon initial establishment, and shall be progressively tightened to the best-performing 10 percent of domestic production by 2035.

**(c) State and Local Procurement.** — The Secretary of Energy shall provide technical assistance and incentive grants to state and local governments that adopt procurement standards consistent with or more stringent than the federal Buy Clean America standards.
