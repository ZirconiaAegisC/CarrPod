### V. COMPANY AND TECHNOLOGY REFERENCES

**(21) Climeworks AG**
- Orca DAC Facility, Hellisheidi, Iceland: 4,000 tons CO2/year capacity, commissioned 2021
- Mammoth DAC Facility, Hellisheidi, Iceland: 36,000 tons CO2/year capacity, commissioned 2024
- Technology: Solid sorbent-based direct air capture with geothermal-powered regeneration and Carbfix method geologic storage
- Reference for current DAC technology readiness level and deployment trajectory in Finding 9

**(22) Carbon Engineering Ltd. (acquired by Occidental Petroleum, 2023)**
- STRATOS DAC Facility, Ector County, Texas: 500,000 tons CO2/year capacity, under construction, expected operations 2028
- Technology: Liquid solvent-based (potassium hydroxide) direct air capture with high-temperature calcination regeneration
- Reference for large-scale DAC deployment costs and timelines in Finding 9

**(23) Heirloom Carbon Technologies, Inc.**
- Technology: Limestone-based passive direct air capture using accelerated mineral carbonation cycles
- Reference for technology diversity in DAC approaches in Section 102(h)

**(24) CarbonCure Technologies, Inc.**
- CO2 mineralization in concrete technology deployed at 750+ concrete plants worldwide
- Technology: Injection of captured CO2 into fresh concrete during mixing, where it reacts with calcium ions to form nano-sized calcium carbonate minerals permanently bound in the concrete matrix
- Reference for Section 301 (CO2 Mineralization in Concrete Products Credit) and Title III CO2 utilization provisions

**(25) Brimstone Energy, Inc.**
- Calcium silicate-based cement produced from non-carbonate raw materials without limestone calcination
- Technology: Use of calcium silicate rocks (e.g., basalt, wollastonite) as cement feedstock, eliminating process CO2 emissions from carbonate decomposition
- Reference for Section 401 (Green Cement Production Tax Credit) technology pathway

**(26) Sublime Systems, Inc.**
- Electrochemical cement production using electricity to drive mineral dissolution and precipitation at ambient temperature
- Technology: Electrochemical process producing reactive calcium silicate cementitious material without high-temperature kilns or limestone calcination
- Reference for Section 401 (Green Cement Production Tax Credit) technology pathway

**(27) Boston Metal, Inc.**
- Molten oxide electrolysis (MOE) for green steel production
- Technology: Direct electrolytic reduction of iron ore in molten oxide electrolyte, producing pure liquid iron and oxygen, with zero direct CO2 process emissions when powered by zero-carbon electricity
- Reference for Section 402 (Green Steel Production Tax Credit) electrolytic steelmaking pathway

**(28) Antora Energy, Inc.**
- Thermal battery technology using solid carbon blocks heated to >1,500 degrees Celsius by renewable electricity
- Technology: Resistive heating of solid carbon blocks, with thermal energy stored as sensible heat and discharged via radiative heat transfer for industrial process heat applications
- Reference for Section 501 (Thermal Battery Deployment Investment Tax Credit) technology parameters

**(29) Mainspring Energy, Inc.**
- Linear generator technology for clean distributed power generation
- Technology: Free-piston linear motion directly driving electromagnetic generation, capable of operating on natural gas, hydrogen, biogas, or ammonia with high efficiency and ultra-low criteria pollutant emissions
- Reference for Section 502 (Linear Generator and Clean Distributed Power Credit) technology parameters

**(30) Carbfix hf.**
- In-situ mineral carbonation method for permanent CO2 storage
- Technology: Dissolution of CO2 in water and injection into reactive basaltic formations, where CO2 mineralizes into stable carbonate minerals within approximately two years
- Commercial operations at Hellisheidi, Iceland since 2014; cumulative injection exceeds 100,000 tons CO2
- Reference for Section 302 (Permanent Geologic Storage via Mineral Carbonation) and Carbfix Method Deployment Program

### VII. CONSISTENCY AND INTERPRETATION

**(35)** Nothing in this Act shall be construed to limit, supersede, or preempt any State law or regulation that establishes more stringent greenhouse gas emission reduction, carbon removal, or environmental justice requirements than those established in this Act.

**(36)** If any provision of this Act, or the application of such provision to any person or circumstance, is held to be unconstitutional, the remainder of this Act and the application of such provision to other persons or circumstances shall not be affected.

**(37)** The provisions of this Act shall be interpreted in a manner consistent with the obligations of the United States under applicable international agreements, including the Marrakesh Agreement Establishing the World Trade Organization and the Paris Agreement.
