SECTION 701. NUCLEAR ENERGY WORKFORCE TRAINING PROGRAM.

     (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

          (1) the current nuclear energy workforce is insufficient
          to support the construction and operation of the advanced
          nuclear facilities contemplated by this Act, with the
          Department of Energy's 2024 United States Energy and
          Employment Report documenting significant shortages of
          qualified nuclear engineers, health physicists, reactor
          operators, welders, electricians, and other skilled trades;

          (2) the Nuclear Energy Institute estimates that deploying
          50 gigawatts of new nuclear capacity by 2040 will require
          approximately 200,000 construction workers and 50,000
          permanent operations and maintenance personnel;

          (3) workforce development investments must begin years in
          advance of construction to ensure a qualified workforce is
          available when projects commence.

     (b) WORKFORCE TRAINING PROGRAM. — The Secretary, in coordination
     with the Secretary of Labor and the Secretary of Education, shall
     establish a comprehensive Nuclear Energy Workforce Training
     Program consisting of the following elements:

          (1) NUCLEAR WORKFORCE GRANTS. — Competitive grants to
          community colleges, technical schools, labor unions, and
          other eligible training providers for the development and
          delivery of nuclear energy workforce training programs,
          including:

               (A) nuclear construction trades programs, including
               nuclear-grade welding, pipefitting, electrical work,
               concrete placement, and quality assurance inspection;

               (B) nuclear operations training programs, including
               reactor operator, senior reactor operator, and
               non-licensed operator training;

               (C) radiation protection and health physics technician
               training;

               (D) nuclear quality assurance and quality control
               training;

               (E) advanced manufacturing training for nuclear
               component fabrication.

          (2) REGISTERED APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAMS. — Support for the
          establishment of registered apprenticeship programs in
          nuclear energy, in partnership with the Department of
          Labor's Office of Apprenticeship, labor unions, and
          nuclear employers.

          (3) PRE-APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAMS. — Support for pre-
          apprenticeship programs that prepare individuals from
          underrepresented communities for entry into nuclear
          registered apprenticeship programs, with priority for
          programs located in energy communities and communities
          hosting nuclear facilities.

          (4) WORKFORCE INNOVATION GRANTS. — Competitive grants for
          innovative workforce development approaches, including
          virtual reality and augmented reality training, modular
          skills certification, and competency-based training models.

     (c) INDUSTRY PARTNERSHIPS. — Grants under this section shall
     require partnership with an employer or industry association
     in the nuclear energy sector, ensuring that training programs
     are aligned with actual workforce demand and that graduates
     have a clear pathway to employment.

     (d) AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated
     $2,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry
     out this section.

SECTION 702. UNIVERSITY NUCLEAR ENGINEERING PROGRAM EXPANSION.

     (a) NUCLEAR ENGINEERING FACULTY GRANTS. — The Secretary, acting
     through the Office of Nuclear Energy, shall establish a grant
     program to expand the capacity of university nuclear engineering
     programs, including:

          (1) grants for the establishment of new nuclear engineering
          degree programs at institutions of higher education that do
          not currently offer such programs;

          (2) grants for the expansion of existing nuclear engineering
          programs at minority-serving institutions, including
          Historically Black Colleges and Universities, Tribal
          Colleges and Universities, and Hispanic-Serving Institutions;

          (3) grants for the recruitment and retention of nuclear
          engineering faculty, including endowed chairs and
          professorships.

     (b) GRADUATE FELLOWSHIPS. — The Secretary shall establish a
     Nuclear Energy Graduate Fellowship Program, providing not fewer
     than 500 fellowships per year for doctoral and master's students
     in nuclear engineering, nuclear materials science, health physics,
     radiochemistry, fusion science and engineering, and related
     fields.

     (c) UNDERGRADUATE SCHOLARSHIPS. — The Secretary shall establish
     a Nuclear Energy Undergraduate Scholarship Program, providing
     not fewer than 2,000 scholarships per year for undergraduate
     students pursuing degrees in nuclear engineering, nuclear
     energy technology, or related fields, with priority for students
     from underrepresented communities and energy communities.

     (d) UNIVERSITY RESEARCH REACTOR INFRASTRUCTURE. — The Secretary
     shall establish a program to support the upgrade, expansion, or
     establishment of university research and training reactors,
     including:

          (1) the conversion of existing university research reactors
          from high-enriched uranium to low-enriched uranium fuel;

          (2) the establishment of new university research reactors,
          including advanced reactor designs at reduced scale suitable
          for educational and research purposes;

          (3) the upgrade of instrumentation, control systems, and
          safety systems at existing university research reactors.

     (e) AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated
     $1,500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out
     this section.

SECTION 703. LABOR UNION PARTNERSHIP AND APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAMS.

     (a) LABOR PARTNERSHIP REQUIREMENT. — As a condition of receiving
     any grant, cost-share award, loan guarantee, or other financial
     assistance under titles I, II, III, or VI of this Act in an
     amount exceeding $50,000,000, an applicant shall demonstrate
     that it has:

          (1) entered into a project labor agreement with one or more
          labor organizations representing workers in the geographic
          area of the project, consistent with Executive Order 14063
          (87 FR 7363, February 9, 2022);

          (2) committed to utilizing registered apprenticeship
          programs for not less than 15 percent of total project
          labor hours;

          (3) established a plan for recruiting and retaining workers
          from local communities, energy communities, and
          underrepresented populations.

     (b) UNION TRAINING PARTNERSHIPS. — The Secretary shall establish
     a grant program to support labor union training partnerships that:

          (1) develop and deliver nuclear-specific construction and
          operations training at union training centers;

          (2) establish portable credentialing and certification
          programs that are recognized across the nuclear industry;

          (3) support the participation of labor unions in the
          development and review of nuclear construction codes and
          standards.

     (c) AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated
     $500,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out
     this section.

SECTION 704. TRANSITION ASSISTANCE FOR FOSSIL FUEL WORKERS.

     (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

          (1) the transition from fossil fuel generation to clean
          energy generation is affecting communities and workers
          that have relied on coal, oil, and natural gas production
          and electricity generation for generations;

          (2) workers in fossil fuel industries possess skills that
          are directly transferable to nuclear energy construction
          and operations, including welding, pipefitting, heavy
          equipment operation, industrial process control, and
          power plant operations;

          (3) targeted transition assistance can enable fossil fuel
          workers to transition to nuclear energy careers without
          loss of income, benefits, or community.

     (b) TRANSITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM. — The Secretary, in
     coordination with the Secretary of Labor and the Secretary of
     Commerce, shall establish a Fossil Fuel to Nuclear Energy
     Worker Transition Program consisting of:

          (1) WAGE REPLACEMENT. — Wage replacement payments for
          eligible displaced fossil fuel workers enrolled in
          nuclear energy training programs, equal to the difference
          between the worker's previous wage and any training
          stipend or part-time employment income, for a period of
          up to 3 years while the worker completes training;

          (2) SKILLS ASSESSMENT AND GAP TRAINING. — Skills assessment
          and gap training programs that evaluate the skills of
          displaced fossil fuel workers against nuclear energy job
          requirements and provide targeted training to close
          identified skill gaps;

          (3) RELOCATION ASSISTANCE. — Relocation assistance for
          displaced fossil fuel workers who accept employment at
          nuclear energy facilities located more than 50 miles from
          their current residence;

          (4) HEALTHCARE AND RETIREMENT BRIDGE. — Continuation of
          employer-sponsored healthcare coverage and retirement
          contributions for eligible displaced workers during the
          training and transition period.

     (c) ELIGIBLE WORKERS. — For purposes of this section, an eligible
     displaced fossil fuel worker is an individual who:

          (1) was employed for not less than 5 years at a coal mine,
          coal-fired power plant, natural gas-fired power plant, or
          oil-fired power plant;

          (2) was separated from such employment due to plant closure,
          unit retirement, or mass layoff occurring after January 1,
          2020;

          (3) enrolls in a training program approved under section 701
          or 702 of this Act.

     (d) AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated
     $750,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out
     this section.

SECTION 705. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS FOR TITLE VII.

     There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this title

          (1) For section 701 (Workforce Training Program):
          $2,000,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (2) For section 702 (University Nuclear Engineering):
          $1,500,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (3) For section 703 (Labor Union Partnerships):
          $500,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (4) For section 704 (Fossil Fuel Worker Transition):
          $750,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (5) For the remaining programs and activities authorized
          by this title: $250,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.
