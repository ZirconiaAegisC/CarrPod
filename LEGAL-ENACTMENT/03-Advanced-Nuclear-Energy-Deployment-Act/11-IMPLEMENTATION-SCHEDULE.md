                IMPLEMENTATION SCHEDULE AND MILESTONES

             IMPLEMENTING THE ADVANCED NUCLEAR ENERGY
                   DEPLOYMENT ACT OF 2026 (ANEDA)

================================================================================

SECTION 901. INTERAGENCY NUCLEAR DEPLOYMENT TASK FORCE.

     (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is established an Interagency Nuclear
     Deployment Task Force (the "Task Force"), co-chaired by the
     Secretary of Energy and the Chairman of the Nuclear Regulatory
     Commission, or their designees.

     (b) MEMBERSHIP. — The Task Force shall include senior
     representatives from:

          (1) the Department of Energy, including the Office of
          Nuclear Energy, the Office of Fusion Energy Sciences,
          the Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations, the Loan
          Programs Office, and the National Nuclear Security
          Administration;

          (2) the Nuclear Regulatory Commission;

          (3) the Department of State;

          (4) the Department of Defense;

          (5) the Department of Commerce;

          (6) the Department of Labor;

          (7) the Department of the Treasury;

          (8) the Environmental Protection Agency;

          (9) the Council on Environmental Quality;

          (10) the Office of Science and Technology Policy;

          (11) the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission;

          (12) the Department of the Interior;

          (13) the Department of Transportation.

     (c) RESPONSIBILITIES. — The Task Force shall:

          (1) coordinate the implementation of this Act across all
          relevant Federal agencies;

          (2) identify and resolve interagency conflicts, delays,
          or obstacles to the deployment of advanced nuclear energy
          technologies;

          (3) track progress against the milestones established under
          this section and section 902;

          (4) serve as the primary point of contact for advanced
          reactor developers, fusion energy companies, fuel suppliers,
          and other stakeholders interacting with multiple Federal
          agencies;

          (5) convene quarterly meetings to review progress and
          address emerging issues.

     (d) CONVENING. — The Task Force shall convene its first meeting
     not later than 90 days after the date of enactment of this Act.

SECTION 902. IMPLEMENTATION MILESTONES AND TIMELINE.

     (a) PHASE I: FOUNDATION PERIOD (YEARS 1-3: 2027-2029).

          YEAR 1 (Calendar Year 2027):

               (1) NRC publishes final Part 53 rule for advanced
               reactor licensing (per section 501).

               (2) NRC issues interim fusion regulatory guidance
               (per section 301(d)).

               (3) DOE issues first funding opportunity announcements
               for ARDP expansion, HALEU enrichment, SMR cost-share,
               and workforce training.

               (4) DOE establishes HALEU Consortium (per section 403).

               (5) Interagency Task Force holds first quarterly meeting.

               (6) DOE completes Clean Firm Power Study (per section
               605(b)).

               (7) FERC convenes technical conference on capacity
               market reforms (per section 605(c)).

               (8) DOE publishes environmental justice screening
               protocol (per section 802(a)).

          YEAR 2 (Calendar Year 2028):

               (1) NRC issues first standard design approvals under
               expedited pathway (per section 502).

               (2) DOE awards first HALEU enrichment construction
               contracts.

               (3) First coal-to-nuclear transition grant awards
               announced.

               (4) DOE awards first SMR manufacturing supply chain
               grants.

               (5) First university nuclear engineering expansion
               grants awarded.

               (6) Centrus American Centrifuge Plant expands HALEU
               production to not less than 5 metric tons per year.

               (7) DOE completes selection of five additional ARDP
               demonstration projects.

          YEAR 3 (Calendar Year 2029):

               (1) NRC Fusion Energy Division publishes proposed
               fusion regulatory framework for public comment.

               (2) First coal-to-nuclear early site permit applications
               submitted to NRC.

               (3) ARPA-E Fusion Program announces first milestone-
               based award recipients.

               (4) Construction commences on first domestic HALEU
               enrichment facility under this Act.

               (5) DOE Fusion Pilot Plant Program issues request for
               proposals.

               (6) TRISO-X fuel fabrication facility in Oak Ridge,
               Tennessee, commences commercial operations.

     (b) PHASE II: ACCELERATION PERIOD (YEARS 3-7: 2029-2033).

          YEAR 4 (Calendar Year 2030):

               (1) TerraPower Natrium demonstration reactor commences
               operation in Kemmerer, Wyoming (per existing schedule).

               (2) First fusion pilot plant milestone-based awards
               issued.

               (3) NRC issues first combined license for a multi-module
               SMR facility.

               (4) Kairos Power Hermes test reactor completes
               commissioning and begins operational testing.

               (5) First community benefit agreements certified under
               Title VIII.

          YEAR 5 (Calendar Year 2031):

               (1) Domestic HALEU enrichment production reaches not
               less than 20 metric tons per year.

               (2) Prohibition on HALEU imports from covered foreign
               nations takes effect (per section 404(a)).

               (3) First SMR modules delivered to a construction site
               for installation.

               (4) DOE submits report to Congress on domestic HALEU
               production competitiveness.

          YEARS 6-7 (Calendar Years 2032-2033):

               (1) NRC issues final fusion regulatory framework.

               (2) First commercial SMR facility commences operation.

               (3) Construction commences on second and third SMR
               deployment projects.

               (4) Not less than 10 gigawatts of advanced nuclear
               capacity under construction.

               (5) X-energy Xe-100 first-of-a-kind commercial unit
               commences operation at Seadrift, Texas.

     (c) PHASE III: SCALE PERIOD (YEARS 7-15: 2033-2041).

          YEARS 7-10 (Calendar Years 2033-2036):

               (1) SMR manufacturing and deployment achieves steady-
               state production rate of not less than 5 GW per year.

               (2) First fusion pilot plant achieves net energy gain
               and delivers electricity to the grid.

               (3) Domestic HALEU enrichment reaches not less than
               50 metric tons per year.

               (4) Multiple Generation IV advanced reactor designs
               in commercial operation.

               (5) Coal-to-nuclear transition projects online at
               not fewer than 10 former coal plant sites.

          YEARS 11-15 (Calendar Years 2037-2041):

               (1) National target of 50 GW of new nuclear capacity
               in commercial operation achieved.

               (2) First commercial fusion power plant in operation,
               delivering electricity at commercially competitive
               rates.

               (3) United States nuclear energy exports reach not
               less than $50,000,000,000 in cumulative value.

               (4) Domestic nuclear fuel supply chain fully independent
               of foreign sources.

               (5) Nuclear energy workforce meets or exceeds projected
               demand of 200,000 construction and 50,000 permanent
               positions.

SECTION 903. ANNUAL REPORTS TO CONGRESS.

     (a) ANNUAL REPORT. — Not later than January 31 of each calendar
     year beginning in 2028, the Secretary, in coordination with the
     Commission and the Task Force, shall submit to the appropriate
     committees of Congress a report on the implementation of this
     Act, including:

          (1) progress against the milestones established under
          section 902;

          (2) a summary of Federal financial assistance provided
          under each title of this Act, including the number of
          awards, the amount of funding obligated and expended, and
          the status of funded projects;

          (3) an assessment of the status of domestic HALEU production
          and fuel fabrication capabilities;

          (4) an assessment of the status of NRC licensing reform
          implementation, including staffing levels, review timelines,
          and the number of applications received, accepted for review,
          and acted upon;

          (5) an assessment of the status of fusion energy regulatory
          framework development;

          (6) an assessment of workforce development progress,
          including the number of individuals trained, certified, and
          employed through programs under Title VII;

          (7) an assessment of environmental justice and community
          engagement activities under Title VIII;

          (8) identification of obstacles, delays, or challenges
          in implementation and recommendations for addressing
          such obstacles.

     (b) GAO OVERSIGHT. — Not later than 3 years after the date of
     enactment of this Act, and every 3 years thereafter, the
     Comptroller General of the United States shall submit to Congress
     a report evaluating the implementation of this Act, including an
     assessment of:

          (1) the effectiveness of Federal financial assistance in
          accelerating advanced nuclear deployment;

          (2) the adequacy and efficiency of the NRC's regulatory
          framework for advanced reactors and fusion systems;

          (3) the status of the domestic HALEU supply chain and any
          remaining vulnerabilities;

          (4) the effectiveness of workforce development and community
          engagement programs;

          (5) the projected trajectory of advanced nuclear deployment
          relative to the 50 GW by 2040 target;

          (6) any recommendations for legislative or administrative
          action to improve implementation.

SECTION 904. SEVERABILITY.

     If any provision of this Act, or the application of such provision
     to any person or circumstance, is held to be unconstitutional or
     otherwise invalid, the remainder of this Act, and the application
     of such provision to other persons or circumstances, shall not be
     affected thereby.
