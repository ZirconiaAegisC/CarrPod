               TITLE IV — DOMESTIC HALEU FUEL SUPPLY CHAIN

================================================================================

SECTION 401. DOMESTIC HALEU ENRICHMENT CAPABILITY ESTABLISHMENT.

     (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

          (1) high-assay low-enriched uranium (HALEU), defined as
          uranium enriched to greater than 5 percent and less than
          20 percent in the uranium-235 isotope, is the required fuel
          form for the majority of advanced reactor designs currently
          under development in the United States, including many small
          modular reactors, molten salt reactors, sodium-cooled fast
          reactors, and high-temperature gas-cooled reactors;

          (2) as of the date of enactment of this Act, there is no
          commercial-scale HALEU enrichment capability in operation
          within the United States;

          (3) the Russian Federation, through its state-owned nuclear
          enterprise TENEX, currently controls a dominant share of
          global HALEU enrichment capacity, creating a critical
          national security vulnerability and supply chain risk for
          the domestic deployment of advanced nuclear technologies;

          (4) the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (Public Law 117-169)
          appropriated $700,000,000 for the HALEU Availability Program,
          but analysis by the Department of Energy indicates that
          establishing a commercially sustainable domestic HALEU
          supply chain requires substantially greater investment.

     (b) DOMESTIC HALEU ENRICHMENT PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall
     establish and carry out a program to support the construction
     and operation of domestic commercial-scale HALEU enrichment
     facilities using centrifuge technology.

     (c) PROGRAM ELEMENTS. — The program shall include:

          (1) competitive awards for the construction of not fewer
          than two domestic HALEU enrichment facilities, with a
          combined annual production capacity of not less than 20
          metric tons of HALEU by December 31, 2031, and not less
          than 50 metric tons by December 31, 2036;

          (2) technical and financial support for the licensing of
          HALEU enrichment facilities by the Commission, including
          preparation of license applications, environmental reports,
          and safety analysis reports;

          (3) off-take agreements or other contractual mechanisms
          to provide demand certainty to HALEU producers during the
          initial years of commercial operation;

          (4) coordination with the National Nuclear Security
          Administration to ensure that commercial HALEU enrichment
          does not adversely affect national security or
          nonproliferation objectives.

     (d) CENTRUS AMERICAN CENTRIFUGE. — The Secretary shall prioritize
     support for the deployment of American Centrifuge technology,
     developed by Centrus Energy Corp. under the Department of Energy's
     HALEU Demonstration Program, at the American Centrifuge Plant in
     Piketon, Ohio, which began HALEU production operations in
     October 2023 and has demonstrated the capability to produce
     HALEU at the rate of approximately 900 kilograms per year using
     a cascade of 16 AC-100M centrifuges.

     (e) ADDITIONAL ENRICHMENT TECHNOLOGIES. — The Secretary may also
     support alternative domestic enrichment technologies, including
     laser enrichment and advanced gas centrifuge designs, provided
     that such technologies are owned and controlled by United States
     entities and comply with all applicable nonproliferation and
     export control requirements.

     (f) NONPROLIFERATION. — All enrichment activities supported under
     this section shall be subject to:

          (1) International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) safeguards,
          including the United States-IAEA Voluntary Offer Agreement
          and the Additional Protocol;

          (2) physical protection requirements in accordance with
          Commission regulations at 10 CFR part 73;

          (3) export control requirements under the Atomic Energy Act
          of 1954 and the Export Administration Regulations.

SECTION 402. DOWNBLENDING AND FUEL FABRICATION PROGRAMS.

     (a) HALEU DOWNBLENDING. — The Secretary, acting through the
     National Nuclear Security Administration, shall establish a
     program to downblend surplus highly enriched uranium (HEU) from
     the national security enterprise into HALEU for use in advanced
     reactors, consistent with national security requirements and
     commitments.

     (b) FUEL FABRICATION. — The Secretary shall establish a program
     to support the construction and operation of domestic HALEU fuel
     fabrication facilities, including:

          (1) facilities for the fabrication of TRISO fuel particles
          and fuel forms (compacts and pebbles);

          (2) facilities for the fabrication of metallic fuel for
          sodium-cooled fast reactors;

          (3) facilities for the fabrication of molten salt reactor
          fuel, including liquid fuel salt preparation and solid fuel
          element fabrication;

          (4) facilities for the fabrication of advanced light-water
          reactor fuel with increased enrichment and burnup limits.

     (c) FUEL QUALIFICATION. — The Secretary, in coordination with the
     Commission, shall establish a fuel qualification program providing
     for the irradiation testing, post-irradiation examination, and
     regulatory acceptance of advanced fuel forms produced from
     domestic HALEU, utilizing test reactor capacity at the Advanced
     Test Reactor at Idaho National Laboratory, the High Flux Isotope
     Reactor at Oak Ridge National Laboratory, and the Transient
     Reactor Test Facility at Idaho National Laboratory.

SECTION 403. HALEU AVAILABILITY AND PRICING PROGRAM.

     (a) HALEU CONSORTIUM. — The Secretary shall establish a HALEU
     Consortium, comprised of advanced reactor developers, HALEU
     producers, fuel fabricators, and end-users, for the purpose of:

          (1) aggregating demand for HALEU to provide market certainty
          to domestic producers;

          (2) establishing standardized HALEU product specifications
          across advanced reactor designs;

          (3) coordinating fuel cycle logistics, including
          transportation, storage, and waste management.

     (b) PRICING MECHANISM. — The Secretary shall establish a HALEU
     pricing mechanism under which the Department of Energy may:

          (1) purchase HALEU from domestic producers at prices
          sufficient to ensure commercial viability;

          (2) sell or transfer HALEU to advanced reactor developers
          at prices comparable to the international market price for
          HALEU, adjusted to reflect the higher costs of domestic
          production during the initial market formation period;

          (3) utilize the difference between purchase and sale prices
          as a subsidy to bridge the cost gap between domestic and
          international HALEU until domestic production achieves
          economies of scale.

     (c) MARKET TRANSITION. — The pricing mechanism under subsection
     (b) shall be phased out as domestic HALEU production achieves
     commercial viability, with the Secretary required to submit a
     report to Congress not later than December 31, 2035, assessing
     the competitiveness of domestic HALEU production and recommending
     whether the pricing mechanism should be continued, modified, or
     terminated.

SECTION 404. PROHIBITION ON RELIANCE ON FOREIGN ADVERSARIES.

     (a) PROHIBITION. — Beginning January 1, 2032, no advanced nuclear
     reactor licensed by the Commission may be fueled with HALEU
     enriched in, or supplied by, a covered foreign nation, unless the
     Secretary certifies that no domestic source of HALEU is available
     in sufficient quantity and at a commercially reasonable price.

     (b) COVERED FOREIGN NATION DEFINED. — For purposes of this
     section, the term "covered foreign nation" means:

          (1) the Russian Federation;

          (2) the People's Republic of China;

          (3) the Democratic People's Republic of Korea;

          (4) the Islamic Republic of Iran;

          (5) any other country the Secretary of State, in
          consultation with the Secretary of Energy and the Director
          of National Intelligence, determines to be a foreign
          adversary with respect to nuclear fuel supply.

     (c) WAIVER AUTHORITY. — The Secretary may waive the prohibition
     under subsection (a) on a case-by-case basis if the Secretary
     determines that:

          (1) the waiver is necessary to prevent a disruption in
          the supply of electricity that would threaten grid
          reliability or public health and safety;

          (2) no domestic source of HALEU can meet the need within
          the required timeframe; and

          (3) the waiver is consistent with the national security
          interests of the United States.

     (d) CONGRESSIONAL NOTIFICATION. — Not later than 30 days before
     issuing a waiver under subsection (c), the Secretary shall
     notify the appropriate committees of Congress of the proposed
     waiver and the basis therefor.

SECTION 405. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS FOR TITLE IV.

     There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this title
     the following amounts:

          (1) For section 401 (Domestic HALEU Enrichment):
          $2,500,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (2) For section 402 (Downblending and Fuel Fabrication):
          $1,500,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (3) For section 403 (HALEU Availability and Pricing):
          $750,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (4) For section 404 (Prohibition Enforcement and
          Administration): $50,000,000 for FY2027-FY2031.

          (5) For the remaining programs and activities authorized
          by this title: $200,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.
