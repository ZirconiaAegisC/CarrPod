                  TITLE I — SMALL MODULAR REACTOR DEPLOYMENT

================================================================================

SECTION 101. STREAMLINED NRC LICENSING FOR STANDARDIZED SMR DESIGNS.

     (a) PURPOSE. — The purpose of this section is to establish an
     accelerated and predictable licensing pathway for small modular
     reactor designs that are standardized, factory-fabricated, and
     incorporate passive and inherent safety features.

     (b) STANDARD DESIGN APPROVAL TIMELINE. — Not later than 180 days
     after the date of enactment of this Act, the Commission shall
     promulgate regulations establishing a mandatory 24-month review
     timeline for applications for standard design approval of SMR
     designs that:

          (1) have a rated electric capacity of not more than 300
          megawatts per module;

          (2) are designed for factory fabrication and assembly;

          (3) incorporate passive safety systems that do not require
          operator action or external power for safe shutdown and
          decay heat removal for a period of not less than 72 hours
          following a design-basis accident;

          (4) utilize fuel forms, enrichment levels, and burnup limits
          that are within the existing regulatory experience base of
          the Commission or for which the applicant has provided
          sufficient qualification data to support the safety case.

     (c) MODULAR DESIGN CERTIFICATION. — The Commission shall, within
     270 days of enactment, issue guidance permitting an applicant to
     submit a single combined application covering design certification,
     early site permit, and construction permit or combined license
     for a multi-module SMR facility, enabling parallel rather than
     sequential review of interdependent licensing actions.

     (d) FACTORY FABRICATION INSPECTION. — The Commission shall establish,
     in coordination with the American Society of Mechanical Engineers
     and the National Board of Boiler and Pressure Vessel Inspectors,
     a framework for in-factory inspection and quality assurance
     certification of SMR modules, reducing the need for on-site
     inspection of factory-fabricated components.

SECTION 102. PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT FOR SMR-GENERATED ELECTRICITY.

     (a) CREDIT ESTABLISHED. — Section 45 of the Internal Revenue Code
     of 1986 is amended by adding at the end the following new
     subsection:

          "(AA) ADVANCED SMALL MODULAR REACTOR PRODUCTION CREDIT. —

               "(1) IN GENERAL. — For purposes of subsection (a), a
               qualified advanced small modular reactor facility shall
               be treated as a qualified facility described in
               paragraph (__) of subsection (d).

               "(2) CREDIT AMOUNT. — The credit determined under this
               subsection for any taxable year is equal to the product
               of —

                    "(A) 3 cents, multiplied by

                    "(B) the kilowatt-hours of electricity produced by
                    the taxpayer at a qualified advanced small modular
                    reactor facility during the 10-year period beginning
                    on the date the facility is originally placed in
                    service and sold by the taxpayer to an unrelated
                    person during such taxable year.

               "(3) QUALIFIED FACILITY. — The term 'qualified advanced
               small modular reactor facility' means an advanced nuclear
               reactor facility with a rated electric capacity of not
               more than 300 megawatts per module that —

                    "(A) is placed in service after December 31, 2026,
                    and before January 1, 2041;

                    "(B) is manufactured in the United States with not
                    less than 60 percent domestic content by value; and

                    "(C) meets the prevailing wage and apprenticeship
                    requirements of section 45(b)(7)."

     (b) EFFECTIVE DATE. — The amendments made by this section shall
     apply to facilities placed in service after December 31, 2026.

SECTION 103. DOE COST-SHARE PROGRAM FOR FIRST-OF-A-KIND SMR DEPLOYMENTS.

     (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary shall establish a cost-share
     program to support the deployment of first-of-a-kind small modular
     reactor projects in the United States.

     (b) FEDERAL SHARE. — The Federal share of total project costs under
     this section shall not exceed 50 percent, except that the Secretary
     may increase the Federal share to not more than 60 percent for
     projects that:

          (1) are sited at a retiring or retired coal or natural gas
          power plant;

          (2) commit to using union labor for construction and operations;

          (3) are located in an energy community as defined by the
          Interagency Working Group on Coal and Power Plant Communities
          and Economic Revitalization; or

          (4) demonstrate use of a domestic HALEU or TRISO fuel supply
          chain.

     (c) SELECTION CRITERIA. — In selecting projects for cost-share
     awards, the Secretary shall prioritize applications based on:

          (1) technical merit and probability of successful completion;

          (2) commercial viability and projected levelized cost of
          electricity;

          (3) strength of community support and host agreements;

          (4) contribution to domestic manufacturing and supply chains;

          (5) workforce development commitments and labor partnerships.

     (d) AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated
     $8,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036
     to carry out this section.

SECTION 104. COAL-TO-NUCLEAR TRANSITION PROGRAM.

     (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

          (1) there are more than 200 operating coal-fired power plants
          in the United States, many of which are projected to retire
          over the next two decades;

          (2) retired and retiring coal plant sites offer existing
          transmission interconnection, water rights, skilled
          workforces, and community acceptance for nuclear facilities;

          (3) a Department of Energy study published in 2022 identified
          157 operating and 237 retired coal plant sites as potentially
          suitable for advanced nuclear reactor deployment.

     (b) PROGRAM ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary shall establish a
     Coal-to-Nuclear Transition Program to provide technical assistance,
     regulatory guidance, and financial support to utilities, rural
     electric cooperatives, and independent power producers seeking to
     site advanced nuclear reactors at existing or retired coal plant
     locations.

     (c) GRANTS AND TECHNICAL ASSISTANCE. — The program shall include:

          (1) grants for site characterization, environmental assessment,
          and preliminary engineering for coal-to-nuclear transition
          projects;

          (2) technical assistance for NRC licensing, including
          preparation of early site permit and combined license
          applications;

          (3) workforce transition planning and retraining assistance
          for coal plant workers, coordinated with Title VII of this
          Act;

          (4) expedited environmental review under the National
          Environmental Policy Act, with the existing coal plant
          environmental impact statement serving as the baseline for
          the nuclear facility review.

     (d) PRIORITY CONSIDERATION. — The Secretary shall give priority
     consideration to coal-to-nuclear projects that:

          (1) are located in energy communities with high unemployment
          or economic distress resulting from coal plant closures;

          (2) commit to hiring former coal plant workers;

          (3) utilize SMR designs that have received Commission design
          certification or standard design approval.

     (e) AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated
     $3,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036
     to carry out this section.

SECTION 105. SMR MANUFACTURING AND SUPPLY CHAIN DEVELOPMENT.

     (a) MANUFACTURING GRANTS. — The Secretary, acting through the
     Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations and in coordination with
     the Department of Commerce, shall establish a grant program to
     support the development of domestic manufacturing capacity for
     SMR components, modules, and systems, including:

          (1) reactor pressure vessels and internals;

          (2) nuclear-grade instrumentation and control systems;

          (3) steam turbine generators optimized for SMR steam
          conditions;

          (4) safety-related electrical and mechanical equipment;

          (5) factory production facilities for modular SMR assembly.

     (b) SUPPLY CHAIN CONSORTIUM. — The Secretary shall facilitate the
     formation of an SMR Supply Chain Consortium, bringing together
     reactor developers, component manufacturers, engineering and
     construction firms, and labor organizations to identify supply
     chain gaps, establish common standards, and achieve economies of
     scale in SMR production.

     (c) DOMESTIC CONTENT PREFERENCE. — In awarding grants and entering
     into contracts under this section, the Secretary shall apply a
     preference for goods, products, and materials produced or
     manufactured in the United States, consistent with the Buy
     American Act (41 U.S.C. 8301 et seq.).

SECTION 106. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS FOR TITLE I.

          $8,000,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (2) For section 104 (Coal-to-Nuclear Transition):
          $3,000,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (3) For section 105 (Manufacturing and Supply Chain):
          $4,000,000,000 for FY2027-FY2031.

          (5) For SMR deployment grants to states, Tribes, and
          local governments for siting and infrastructure preparation:
          $2,000,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.

          (6) For the remaining programs and activities authorized
          by this title: $7,500,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.
