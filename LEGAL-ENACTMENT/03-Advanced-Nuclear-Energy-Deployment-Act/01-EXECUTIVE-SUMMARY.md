                       EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

          ADVANCED NUCLEAR ENERGY DEPLOYMENT ACT OF 2026
                            (ANEDA)

================================================================================

I.   OVERVIEW AND PURPOSE

     The Advanced Nuclear Energy Deployment Act of 2026 (ANEDA)
     establishes a comprehensive statutory framework to accelerate the
     deployment of advanced nuclear energy technologies across the
     United States. The Act authorizes total appropriations of
     $100,000,000,000 over a fifteen-year period (Fiscal Years 2027
     through 2041) and establishes a national goal of bringing not less
     than 50 gigawatts of new nuclear generating capacity into
     commercial operation by calendar year 2040.

     ANEDA addresses the full nuclear energy lifecycle — from fuel
     supply chain security through reactor licensing reform to
     deployment incentives, workforce development, and environmental
     justice — across three technology classes: small modular reactors
     (SMRs), Generation IV advanced fission reactors, and commercial
     fusion energy systems.

III. NATIONAL DEPLOYMENT TARGETS

     (a) 2027-2029 (Foundation Period): NRC regulatory reform fully
         implemented; HALEU enrichment capability of not less than
         5 metric tons per year established domestically; at least two
         first-of-a-kind advanced reactor construction permits issued.

     (b) 2030-2035 (Acceleration Period): First commercial SMR
         deployments in operation; TerraPower Natrium demonstration
         reactor online (per 2030 target); Kairos Power Hermes
         demonstration reactor online (per 2027 target); not less
         than 10 GW of advanced nuclear capacity under construction.

     (c) 2036-2040 (Scale Period): Not less than 50 GW of new nuclear
         capacity in commercial operation; first fusion pilot plant
         achieving net energy gain; domestic HALEU supply chain fully
         independent of foreign sources.

IV.  KEY POLICY INSTRUMENTS

     (a) PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT (PTC): $0.03 per kilowatt-hour for SMR
         electricity during first ten years of commercial operation;
         $0.05 per kilowatt-hour for fusion-generated electricity
         during first fifteen years of commercial operation.

     (b) INVESTMENT TAX CREDIT (ITC): 30 percent investment tax credit
         for qualifying advanced nuclear and fusion facilities that
         commence construction before January 1, 2036.

     (c) LOAN GUARANTEES: Expansion of the Department of Energy Title
         XVII Innovative Clean Energy Loan Guarantee Program by
         $50,000,000,000 in additional loan guarantee authority,
         dedicated to advanced nuclear and fusion projects.

     (d) DOE COST-SHARE PROGRAM: Federal cost-share of up to 50 percent
         of total project costs for first-of-a-kind advanced reactor
         deployments, with priority for projects utilizing domestic
         supply chains and union labor.

V.   NRC MODERNIZATION

     (a) Codification and full implementation of 10 CFR Part 53
         (Licensing of Advanced Nuclear Reactors), including
         technology-inclusive, risk-informed, and performance-based
         regulatory framework.

     (b) Establishment of expedited review pathways (target: 24-month
         review period) for standardized reactor designs that have
         received design certification from a cognizant regulatory
         authority of a qualifying allied nation.

     (c) Authorization for Commission to increase staffing by not less
         than 500 full-time equivalent positions dedicated to advanced
         reactor licensing review.

     (d) Establishment of a Fusion Energy Division within the NRC,
         responsible for developing and implementing a technology-
         appropriate regulatory framework for commercial fusion energy
         systems by January 1, 2029.

VI.  DOMESTIC FUEL SUPPLY SECURITY

     (a) $5,000,000,000 authorization for domestic HALEU enrichment,
         downblending, and fuel fabrication infrastructure, with the
         express goal of ending reliance on Russian-origin HALEU
         supply by December 31, 2031.

     (b) Establishment of a HALEU Availability Program within the
         Department of Energy to ensure that advanced reactor
         developers have access to HALEU at commercially reasonable
         prices during the initial deployment period.

VII. WORKFORCE AND COMMUNITY PROVISIONS

     (a) $5,000,000,000 authorization for nuclear workforce training,
         university nuclear engineering programs, union apprenticeship
         partnerships, and transition assistance for workers
         dislocated from fossil fuel industries.

     (b) Mandatory community benefit agreements for projects receiving
         federal financial assistance exceeding $50,000,000.

     (c) Tribal consultation and free, prior, and informed consent
         requirements for projects affecting tribal lands or resources.

VIII. ECONOMIC AND STRATEGIC IMPACT

     The Act is projected to support approximately 200,000 construction
     jobs and 50,000 permanent operations and maintenance jobs over
     the fifteen-year deployment period. The global market for advanced
     nuclear and fusion technologies is estimated at $1,000,000,000,000
     over the next three decades, and ANEDA positions the United States
     to capture a substantial share of this export market while
     strengthening domestic energy security, grid reliability, and
     industrial competitiveness.
