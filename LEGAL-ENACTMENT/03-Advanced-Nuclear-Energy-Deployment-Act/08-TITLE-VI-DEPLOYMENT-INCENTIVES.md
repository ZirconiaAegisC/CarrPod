SECTION 601. INVESTMENT TAX CREDIT FOR ADVANCED NUCLEAR FACILITIES.

     (a) CREDIT ESTABLISHED. — Section 48 of the Internal Revenue Code
     of 1986 is amended to include an energy credit for qualified
     advanced nuclear energy facilities.

     (b) CREDIT RATE. — The energy credit determined under this section
     for any qualified advanced nuclear energy facility shall be equal
     to 30 percent of the qualified investment in such facility.

     (c) QUALIFYING FACILITIES. — A facility qualifies for the credit
     under this section if:

          (1) the facility is an advanced nuclear reactor, as defined
          in section 4(a) of this Act, or a fusion energy system, as
          defined in section 4(c) of this Act;

          (2) construction of the facility commences before
          January 1, 2036;

          (3) the facility is placed in service before January 1, 2046;

          (4) the facility is located in the United States;

          (5) the taxpayer satisfies the prevailing wage requirements
          of section 48(a)(10) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986
          during the construction period and, with respect to any
          alteration or repair of the facility, during the 5-year
          period beginning on the date the facility is placed in
          service;

          (6) the taxpayer satisfies the apprenticeship requirements
          of section 48(a)(11) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

     (d) BONUS CREDIT. — The credit rate under subsection (b) shall
     be increased —

          (1) by 10 percentage points for facilities that are sited
          in an energy community as defined by the Interagency
          Working Group on Coal and Power Plant Communities and
          Economic Revitalization;

          (2) by 10 percentage points for facilities that meet
          domestic content requirements, defined as not less than
          55 percent of the components (by cost) being mined,
          produced, or manufactured in the United States;

          (3) by 2 percentage points for each of the preceding
          subparagraphs (1) and (2) met, up to a maximum additional
          20 percentage points, resulting in a maximum credit rate
          of 50 percent.

     (e) DIRECT PAY OPTION. — A taxpayer eligible for the credit under
     this section may elect to receive a direct payment from the
     Secretary of the Treasury in lieu of the credit, in an amount
     equal to the credit that would otherwise be allowable, in
     accordance with section 6417 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

     (f) TRANSFERABILITY. — The credit under this section shall be
     transferable in accordance with section 6418 of the Internal
     Revenue Code of 1986.

SECTION 602. PRODUCTION TAX CREDIT FOR NUCLEAR-GENERATED ELECTRICITY.

     (a) EXISTING NUCLEAR PTC. — Section 45J of the Internal Revenue
     Code of 1986 (relating to the advanced nuclear power facility
     production credit, enacted by the Energy Policy Act of 2005) is
     amended as follows:

          (1) IN GENERAL. — The 6,000 megawatt national capacity
          limitation under subsection (b) is repealed.

          (2) CREDIT AMOUNT. — The credit amount under subsection (b)
          is increased from 1.8 cents to 2.5 cents per kilowatt-hour,
          adjusted for inflation in accordance with subsection (d).

          (3) EXTENSION. — The requirement that the facility be placed
          in service before January 1, 2021, is extended to facilities
          placed in service before January 1, 2041.

     (b) COORDINATION WITH SMR AND FUSION PTCS. — A facility shall be
     eligible for a tax credit under only one of section 45J of the
     Internal Revenue Code of 1986, section 102 of this Act (SMR PTC),
     or section 302 of this Act (Fusion PTC), as elected by the
     taxpayer at the time the facility is placed in service.

SECTION 603. EXPANSION OF TITLE XVII LOAN GUARANTEE PROGRAM.

     (a) ADDITIONAL LOAN GUARANTEE AUTHORITY. — Section 1702 of the
     Energy Policy Act of 2005 (42 U.S.C. 16512) is amended by adding
     at the end the following:

          "(p) ADVANCED NUCLEAR AND FUSION ENERGY LOAN GUARANTEES. —

               "(1) IN GENERAL. — In addition to the authority provided
               under subsections (a) through (o), the Secretary may
               make guarantees under this section for projects that
               employ advanced nuclear reactor or fusion energy
               technologies, in a total amount not to exceed
               $50,000,000,000.

               "(2) CREDIT SUBSIDY COST. — There is authorized to be
               appropriated for the cost (as defined in section 502(5)
               of the Federal Credit Reform Act of 1990 (2 U.S.C.
               661a(5))) of guarantees made under this subsection
               $25,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2041.

               "(3) SUBORDINATION. — The Secretary may, on a case-by-
               case basis, enter into subordination agreements with
               other creditors providing financing for a project
               receiving a guarantee under this subsection, provided
               that the subordination is consistent with the purposes
               of this title and does not materially increase the risk
               of loss to the Federal Government.

               "(4) ELIGIBLE PROJECTS. — Projects eligible for
               guarantees under this subsection include:

                    "(A) construction of advanced nuclear reactors,
                    including small modular reactors and Generation IV
                    advanced reactor designs;

                    "(B) construction of commercial fusion energy
                    facilities;

                    "(C) construction of HALEU enrichment, downblending,
                    and fuel fabrication facilities;

                    "(D) construction of facilities for the
                    manufacturing of advanced reactor components and
                    modules;

                    "(E) first-of-a-kind deployments of advanced
                    reactor technologies at retired or retiring
                    fossil fuel plant sites."

     (b) STREAMLINED APPLICATION PROCESS. — Not later than 180 days
     after the date of enactment of this Act, the Secretary shall
     revise the regulations, policies, and procedures governing the
     Title XVII loan guarantee program to:

          (1) reduce the application processing time to not more than
          12 months from the date of submission of a complete
          application;

          (2) reduce application fees for first-of-a-kind projects and
          projects with total costs of less than $5,000,000,000;

          (3) provide for a pre-application consultation process that
          enables applicants to receive timely feedback on project
          eligibility, creditworthiness, and environmental review
          requirements.

SECTION 604. STATE CLEAN ENERGY STANDARD INCLUSION.

     (a) FEDERAL POLICY. — It is the policy of the United States that
     existing and new nuclear energy generation, including advanced
     nuclear and fusion generation, shall qualify as clean, zero-
     emission generation for purposes of any Federal clean energy
     standard, renewable portfolio standard, or similar electricity
     sector policy.

     (b) STATE INCENTIVE PROGRAMS. — The Secretary shall provide
     technical assistance to states, public utility commissions, and
     regional transmission organizations seeking to:

          (1) include nuclear energy, including advanced nuclear and
          fusion, in state-level clean energy standards, renewable
          portfolio standards, and emissions trading programs;

          (2) establish mechanisms for valuing the reliability, fuel
          diversity, and decarbonization attributes of nuclear
          generation;

          (3) implement policies that compensate nuclear generators
          for the zero-emission, firm, and dispatchable attributes
          of their electricity.

     (c) VOLUNTARY CLEAN ENERGY MARKET. — The Secretary, in
     coordination with the Environmental Protection Agency, shall
     support the development of voluntary markets for clean energy
     attributes and certificates that fully recognize and value the
     unique attributes of nuclear-generated electricity, including
     24/7 availability, high capacity factor, and zero-emission
     characteristics.

SECTION 605. CAPACITY PAYMENT MECHANISM FOR CLEAN FIRM POWER.

     (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

          (1) the reliable and affordable operation of the electric
          grid requires a mix of generating resources, including
          variable renewable resources, energy storage, and firm
          dispatchable resources;

          (2) current wholesale electricity market designs do not
          adequately compensate firm, dispatchable, zero-carbon
          resources for their contribution to resource adequacy and
          grid reliability;

          (3) the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) has
          initiated proceedings and issued orders aimed at improving
          the valuation of reliability attributes in organized
          wholesale electricity markets, but additional action is
          needed.

     (b) CLEAN FIRM POWER STUDY. — Not later than 180 days after the
     date of enactment of this Act, the Secretary, in consultation
     with the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, shall complete
     and submit to Congress a study on mechanisms for compensating
     clean firm power resources for their reliability and
     decarbonization attributes, including:

          (1) analysis of existing compensation mechanisms and their
          adequacy;

          (2) evaluation of capacity market reforms, reliability
          must-run arrangements, and clean firm power standards;

          (3) assessment of international models, including the United
          Kingdom's Contracts for Difference program and capacity
          market, and France's regulated nuclear tariff;

          (4) recommendations for legislative or regulatory action
          to ensure adequate compensation for clean firm power
          resources.

     (c) FERC TECHNICAL CONFERENCE. — Not later than 270 days after
     the date of enactment of this Act, the Federal Energy Regulatory
     Commission shall convene a technical conference to consider the
     findings of the study required under subsection (b) and to explore
     mechanisms for ensuring that organized wholesale electricity
     markets appropriately value the reliability, firmness, and fuel
     diversity attributes of nuclear generation.

SECTION 606. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS FOR TITLE VI.

     There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this title
     (excluding sections 601 and 602, which are funded through tax
     expenditures, and section 603, which is funded through credit
     subsidy appropriations and loan guarantee authority) the
     following amounts:

          (1) For section 604 (State Clean Energy Standard Technical
          Assistance): $100,000,000 for FY2027-FY2031.

          (2) For section 605 (Clean Firm Power Study and
          Implementation): $50,000,000 for FY2027-FY2029.

          (3) For the remaining programs and activities authorized
          by this title: $100,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.
