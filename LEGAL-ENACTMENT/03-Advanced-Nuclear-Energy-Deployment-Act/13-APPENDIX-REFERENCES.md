================================================================================
                             APPENDIX

         REFERENCES AND SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION FOR THE
          ADVANCED NUCLEAR ENERGY DEPLOYMENT ACT OF 2026

================================================================================

This Appendix identifies the principal statutes, legislative history,
regulatory dockets, agency programs, industry reports, and real-world
project data upon which the findings, declarations, and provisions of
the Advanced Nuclear Energy Deployment Act of 2026 are based.

================================================================================

PART I — STATUTES AND PUBLIC LAWS

     1.   Atomic Energy Act of 1954, as amended, 42 U.S.C. §§ 2011
          et seq. — Foundational statute governing civilian and military
          nuclear activities in the United States; establishes the NRC
          and the licensing framework for nuclear facilities.

     2.   Energy Reorganization Act of 1974, Pub. L. No. 93-438, 88
          Stat. 1233 (1974), codified as amended at 42 U.S.C. §§ 5801
          et seq. — Abolished the Atomic Energy Commission; created the
          Nuclear Regulatory Commission and the Energy Research and
          Development Administration (predecessor to DOE).

     3.   Energy Policy Act of 1992, Pub. L. No. 102-486, 106 Stat.
          2776 (1992) — Reformed NRC licensing process; established
          combined construction and operating license (COL) pathway
          under 10 CFR Part 52.

     4.   Energy Policy Act of 2005, Pub. L. No. 109-58, 119 Stat. 594
          (2005) — Established nuclear production tax credit (Section
          45J of the IRC); created Title XVII loan guarantee program;
          authorized standby support for new nuclear plants; established
          Next Generation Nuclear Plant (NGNP) program.

     5.   Energy Act of 2020, Pub. L. No. 116-260, Division Z, 134
          Stat. 2418 (2020) — Established the Advanced Reactor
          Demonstration Program (ARDP); authorized the National Reactor
          Innovation Center (NRIC); directed DOE to establish HALEU
          availability program.

     6.   Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, Pub. L. No. 117-58,
          135 Stat. 429 (2021) — Authorized $6 billion for Civil Nuclear
          Credit Program to prevent premature retirement of existing
          nuclear plants; provided funding for advanced reactor
          demonstrations.

     7.   Inflation Reduction Act of 2022, Pub. L. No. 117-169, 136
          Stat. 1818 (2022) — Appropriated $700,000,000 for HALEU
          availability program; established nuclear PTC under Section
          45U of the IRC; established technology-neutral clean
          electricity PTC and ITC; established prevailing wage and
          apprenticeship requirements for clean energy tax credits.

     8.   ADVANCE Act of 2024 (enacted as part of the Fire Grants and
          Safety Act), Pub. L. No. 118-67, 138 Stat. ____ (2024) —
          Directed NRC to modernize licensing for advanced reactors;
          reduced NRC fees for advanced reactor applicants;
          established expedited procedures for combined licenses;
          authorized NRC international coordination; directed
          Commission to establish fusion energy regulatory framework.

     9.   CHIPS and Science Act of 2022, Pub. L. No. 117-167, 136
          Stat. 1366 (2022) — Authorized DOE Office of Science fusion
          energy research programs; authorized fusion materials and
          technology programs.

PART II — REGULATORY PROCEEDINGS AND NRC DOCKETS

     10.  10 CFR Part 53 — "Licensing of Advanced Nuclear Reactors."
          Proposed rulemaking, 89 FR ____ (2024). Technology-inclusive,
          risk-informed, performance-based licensing framework for
          advanced reactors. NRC RIN 3150-AK64.

     11.  NRC Docket No. 50-603. Kairos Power LLC, Hermes Test Reactor.
          Construction Permit No. CPPR-1, issued December 12, 2023. The
          first non-light-water reactor construction permit issued by
          the NRC in more than 50 years. Fluoride salt-cooled, TRISO
          fueled, 35 MW thermal test reactor in Oak Ridge, TN.

     12.  NRC Docket No. 52-048. NuScale Power, LLC. NuScale Standard
          Plant Design Certification. Technical Report No. TR-0816-
          51110, submitted July 12, 2016. Standard Design Approval
          issued August 28, 2020. Final Design Certification Rule
          published January 19, 2023 (88 FR 3277). First SMR design
          certified by the NRC.

     13.  NRC Docket No. 52-049. NuScale Power, LLC. Combined License
          Application for the Carbon Free Power Project, Idaho National
          Laboratory site. Submitted 2024 (as modified). First SMR COL
          application under NRC review.

     14.  NRC Docket No. 99902087. TerraPower, LLC. Early
          engagement/regulatory interaction for Natrium reactor design.
          Pre-application review initiated. Sodium-cooled fast reactor
          with integral molten salt energy storage.

     15.  NRC Docket No. 99902067. X-energy, LLC. Pre-application
          regulatory interaction for Xe-100 reactor design. High-
          temperature gas-cooled pebble bed reactor, TRISO fuel.

     16.  NUREG/CR-7272, "Guidance for Developing Principal Design
          Criteria for Non-Light Water Reactors" (2023). Key output
          of the DOE-NRC Licensing Modernization Project.

     17.  NRC Policy Statement on Safety Goals for the Operation of
          Nuclear Power Plants, 51 FR 30028 (August 21, 1986).

     18.  NRC SECY-23-0001, "Options for Licensing and Regulating
          Fusion Energy Systems" (January 3, 2023). NRC staff paper
          recommending a distinct regulatory framework for fusion.

PART III — DEPARTMENT OF ENERGY PROGRAMS AND REPORTS

     19.  Advanced Reactor Demonstration Program (ARDP), DOE Office of
          Clean Energy Demonstrations (2021-present). Three award tracks:
          Advanced Reactor Demonstrations (TerraPower Natrium,
          X-energy Xe-100); Risk Reduction for Future Demonstrations
          (five awards including Kairos Hermes); and Advanced Reactor
          Concepts 2020 (five awards).

     20.  DOE/EIA, Annual Energy Outlook 2025, "Electricity Market
          Module" and "Levelized Cost of Electricity" analysis.

     21.  DOE, "Pathways to Commercial Liftoff: Advanced Nuclear,"
          Liftoff Reports (2023, updated 2024). Found that 5-10 GW of
          committed orders are needed to unlock the manufacturing
          learning curve for SMRs; identified HALEU supply as critical
          bottleneck.

     22.  DOE, "Safety of Fusion Energy Systems," DOE Fusion Energy
          Sciences Advisory Committee (FESAC) Report (2023). Concluded
          that fusion presents fundamentally different safety and
          security characteristics than fission and supports a
          distinct regulatory framework.

     23.  DOE, "Bold Decadal Vision for Commercial Fusion Energy,"
          White House Fusion Summit (March 2022). Announced goal of
          a fusion pilot plant by the early 2030s.

     24.  DOE, Fusion Energy Sciences Advisory Committee (FESAC),
          "Powering the Future: Fusion and Plasmas" (2020). Long-range
          strategic plan for fusion energy.

     25.  DOE, "Investigating Benefits and Challenges of Converting
          Retiring Coal Plants into Nuclear Plants" (2022). Identified
          157 operating and 237 retired coal plant sites as potentially
          suitable for nuclear reactor siting.

     26.  DOE, Nuclear Energy Advisory Committee (NEAC), "Advanced
          Reactor Supply Chain Assessment" (2023). Identified needs
          for domestic HALEU and TRISO fuel supply chain.

     27.  DOE, "United States Energy and Employment Report" (2024).
          Documented nuclear workforce shortages and employment trends.

PART IV — REAL-WORLD PROJECT DATA (ADVANCED NUCLEAR AND FUSION)

     28.  NuScale Power Corporation (NYSE: SMR). VOYGR SMR: 77 MWe
          per module, up to 12 modules (924 MWe total). NRC Design
          Certification approved January 2023. Public company; market
          capitalization ~$2B. First customer: Standard Power (Ohio/
          Pennsylvania data centers, 24 modules announced 2024).

     29.  TerraPower LLC (private; investors: Bill Gates, SK Group,
          others). Natrium Reactor: 345 MWe sodium-cooled fast reactor
          with 1 GWh molten-salt energy storage. DOE ARDP award
          $2B. Demonstration plant: Kemmerer, Wyoming (retiring coal
          plant site). Target operational date: 2030.

     30.  Kairos Power LLC (private; investors: DOE, others). Hermes
          Test Reactor: 35 MWt, TRISO fuel, FLiBe coolant. NRC
          construction permit issued December 2023 — first non-light-
          water permit in 50+ years. Oak Ridge, TN. Pathway to
          commercial KP-FHR design. Target Hermes operation: 2027.

     31.  X-energy, LLC (private; investors: DOE, Ares Management).
          Xe-100: 80 MWe per unit, 4-unit plant (320 MWe total)
          modular HTGR, TRISO pebble bed fuel. DOE ARDP award. First
          commercial deployment: Dow Seadrift, TX industrial site.
          TRISO-X fuel facility under construction in Oak Ridge, TN.

     32.  Helion Energy, Inc. (private; investors: Sam Altman, others).
          First fusion power purchase agreement executed with Microsoft
          Corporation (May 2023), targeting 50+ MWe delivery by 2029.
          Trenta and Polaris prototype systems. Capital raised: >$500M.

     33.  Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS) (private; investors: Bill
          Gates, Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Eni, Temasek, others).
          SPARC net-energy tokamak under construction in Devens, MA.
          HTS magnet technology: 18 magnets, field strength >20 tesla.
          Capital raised: >$2B. Target SPARC operation: late 2020s.

     34.  TAE Technologies, Inc. (private; investors: Google, Venrock,
          others). Norman prototype: field-reversed configuration,
          neutral beam injection only, plasma temperatures >75M°C.
          Next device: Copernicus. Hydrogen-boron (p-B11) fuel target
          (aneutronic fusion). Capital raised: >$1.2B.

     35.  General Fusion, Inc. (private; investors: Jeff Bezos, Temasek,
          governments of Canada, UK). Magnetized target fusion (MTF).
          Demonstration facility: Culham, UK, with UKAEA partnership.
          Target completion: 2027. Capital raised: >$300M.

     36.  Fusion Industry Association. "The Global Fusion Industry in
          2024" (2024). Documented $15.2B total private fusion
          investment globally; 40+ private fusion companies; $900M in
          new funding in prior 12 months.

     37.  National Ignition Facility (NIF), Lawrence Livermore National
          Laboratory. Achieved fusion ignition and energy gain on
          December 5, 2022 (target yield: 3.15 MJ from 2.05 MJ laser
          input); repeated achievement on July 30, 2023 (higher yield);
          additional repetitions reported through 2024-2025.

     38.  Centrus Energy Corp. (NYSE American: LEU). HALEU Demonstration
          Program at American Centrifuge Plant, Piketon, OH. Began
          production operations October 2023. First U.S. commercial
          HALEU production in decades. 16 AC-100M centrifuge cascade;
          capacity ~900 kg/yr HALEU at 19.75% U-235.

     39.  Google LLC. Announced nuclear PPA with Kairos Power for
          advanced SMR electricity to power data centers (2024). Part
          of trend of major technology companies contracting for 24/7
          carbon-free nuclear energy.

     40.  Global fusion private capital: $15.2B total as of 2026
          (Fusion Industry Association, "The Global Fusion Industry
          in 2025" update), representing a more than threefold increase
          from approximately $4.8B in 2022.

PART V — ACADEMIC AND INSTITUTIONAL RESEARCH

     41.  Massachusetts Institute of Technology, "The Future of Nuclear
          Energy in a Carbon-Constrained World" (2018). Found that
          achieving deep decarbonization is significantly less costly
          when clean firm power including nuclear is available.

     42.  MIT Energy Initiative, "The Future of Nuclear Power" (2003,
          updated 2009). Foundational analysis of nuclear energy's role
          in decarbonization.

     43.  National Renewable Energy Laboratory, "Electrification Futures
          Study" (2018). Multi-year analysis of scenarios for deep
          decarbonization of the U.S. electricity system.

     44.  Electric Power Research Institute (EPRI), "U.S. National
          Electrification Assessment" (2018). Found that clean firm
          resources substantially reduce the cost of achieving deep
          decarbonization.

     45.  Princeton University, Net-Zero America Project (2021).
          Multiple pathways analysis showing significant role for
          nuclear in least-cost decarbonization pathways.

     46.  Nuclear Energy Institute, "Advanced Reactor Roadmap" (2023)
          and annual workforce reports.

     47.  Breakthrough Institute, "How to Make Nuclear Cheap" (2023).
          Analysis of cost reduction pathways for advanced nuclear
          technologies.

     48.  Nuclear Innovation Alliance, "Part 53 Rulemaking: A New
          Framework for Licensing Advanced Reactors" (2023-2024).

     49.  Good Energy Collective, "Community Engagement for Advanced
          Nuclear: Best Practices and Policy Recommendations" (2023).

     50.  Clean Air Task Force, "Advanced Nuclear Energy: Need, Status,
          and Policy Recommendations" (2024).

PART VI — INTERNATIONAL PRECEDENTS AND COMPARATIVE POLICIES

     51.  United Kingdom, Nuclear Energy (Financing) Act 2022 (c. 15).
          Established Regulated Asset Base (RAB) financing model for
          new nuclear projects, reducing cost of capital.

     52.  United Kingdom, Energy Act 2023 (c. 52). Established UK
          Industrial Fusion Solutions; authorized fusion regulatory
          framework separate from fission.

     53.  Canada, Canadian Nuclear Safety Commission REGDOC-1.1.5,
          "Supplemental Information for Small Modular Reactor
          Proponents" (2023). Risk-informed approach.

     54.  European Union, Complementary Delegated Act on nuclear
          energy under the EU Taxonomy Regulation (2022). Classified
          nuclear energy as sustainable for purposes of EU sustainable
          finance framework.

     55.  International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA), Nuclear
          Harmonization and Standardization Initiative (NHSI)(2023-
          present).

     56.  IAEA, "Advances in Small Modular Reactor Technology
          Developments" (2024 edition).

     57.  IAEA, "Fusion Energy: A Review of Regulatory Frameworks,"
          forthcoming (2025).

     58.  OECD Nuclear Energy Agency, "The NEA Small Modular Reactor
          Dashboard" (2024).

     59.  Generation IV International Forum (GIF). Annual Reports and
          Technology Roadmaps.
