       TITLE VIII — ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE AND COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT

================================================================================

SECTION 801. COMMUNITY BENEFIT AGREEMENT REQUIREMENTS.

     (a) REQUIREMENT. — As a condition of receiving any grant, cost-
     share award, loan guarantee, or other financial assistance under
     this Act in an amount exceeding $50,000,000, an applicant shall
     negotiate and execute a community benefit agreement with the host
     community or communities.

     (b) AGREEMENT ELEMENTS. — A community benefit agreement under this
     section shall include, at minimum:

          (1) a description of the community benefits to be provided,
          which may include:

               (A) direct financial payments to the host community,
               including payments in lieu of taxes;

               (B) local hiring commitments, including specific metrics
               for the percentage of construction and operations jobs
               to be filled by residents of the host community;

               (C) local procurement commitments, including specific
               metrics for the percentage of goods and services to be
               sourced from businesses located in the host community
               or region;

               (D) investments in community infrastructure, including
               schools, roads, emergency services, healthcare
               facilities, and broadband access;

               (E) investments in affordable housing to accommodate
               construction and operations workforce;

               (F) environmental monitoring and mitigation commitments,
               including real-time public radiation monitoring;

               (G) educational partnerships, including STEM education
               programs, scholarships, and internship opportunities
               for students from the host community;

          (2) mechanisms for community oversight and enforcement of
          the agreement, including the establishment of a community
          advisory board with authority to review and comment on
          facility operations and community impacts;

          (3) dispute resolution procedures, including access to
          mediation and arbitration.

     (c) NEGOTIATION PROCESS. — The negotiation of a community benefit
     agreement under this section shall:

          (1) involve representatives of the host community selected
          through a transparent and inclusive process, including
          representatives of environmental justice communities,
          Tribal Nations (where applicable), local governments,
          labor organizations, and community-based organizations;

          (2) provide for independent technical assistance to the
          community, funded by the project applicant, to enable
          informed and effective participation in negotiations;

          (3) be completed and the agreement publicly disclosed not
          later than 180 days before the applicant submits an
          application for a construction permit or combined license
          to the Commission.

     (d) SECRETARIAL CERTIFICATION. — The Secretary shall certify that
     a community benefit agreement meets the requirements of this
     section before the applicant may receive financial assistance
     under this Act. The Secretary may not certify an agreement that
     fails to provide meaningful, enforceable benefits to the host
     community.

SECTION 802. ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE SCREENING AND MITIGATION.

     (a) ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE SCREENING. — Not later than 180 days
     after the date of enactment of this Act, the Secretary shall,
     in consultation with the Administrator of the Environmental
     Protection Agency and the Chair of the Council on Environmental
     Quality, develop and publish an environmental justice screening
     protocol for advanced nuclear energy facilities. The protocol
     shall incorporate:

          (1) the White House Council on Environmental Quality's
          Climate and Economic Justice Screening Tool (CEJST) or
          successor tool;

          (2) EPA's EJScreen environmental justice mapping and
          screening tool;

          (3) state and local environmental justice screening tools,
          where available;

          (4) analysis of cumulative environmental and health impacts
          on communities hosting or proximate to nuclear facilities.

     (b) MITIGATION REQUIREMENTS. — For any proposed advanced nuclear
     facility that would be located in or within five miles of an
     environmental justice community, as identified by the screening
     protocol under subsection (a), the applicant shall:

          (1) prepare an environmental justice impact assessment
          identifying and quantifying the potential environmental,
          health, and socioeconomic impacts of the proposed facility
          on the affected environmental justice community;

          (2) develop and commit to implement an environmental justice
          mitigation plan specifying the measures the applicant will
          take to avoid, minimize, or mitigate identified impacts;

          (3) provide for meaningful public participation by the
          affected environmental justice community in the development
          of the impact assessment and mitigation plan, including:

               (A) public meetings held at times and locations
               accessible to community members;

               (B) translation of key documents into languages spoken
               by significant segments of the community;

               (C) provision of technical assistance to enable
               informed community participation.

     (c) SECRETARIAL REVIEW. — The Secretary shall review and approve
     or disapprove the environmental justice impact assessment and
     mitigation plan before the applicant may receive financial
     assistance under this Act.

SECTION 803. TRIBAL CONSULTATION AND CONSENT PROTOCOLS.

     (a) TRIBAL CONSULTATION. — For any project receiving financial
     assistance under this Act that may affect Tribal lands, resources,
     or rights, the applicant and the relevant Federal agency shall
     engage in meaningful government-to-government consultation with
     the affected Tribal Nation or Nations, consistent with:

          (1) Executive Order 13175 (65 FR 67249, November 9, 2000),
          "Consultation and Coordination with Indian Tribal
          Governments";

          (2) the Presidential Memorandum of January 26, 2021, "Tribal
          Consultation and Strengthening Nation-to-Nation
          Relationships";

          (3) the Department of Energy's American Indian and Alaska
          Native Tribal Government Policy (DOE Order 144.1).

     (b) CONSENT PROTOCOLS. — For any project receiving financial
     assistance under this Act that would be located on Tribal lands,
     including lands held in trust by the United States for the
     benefit of a Tribal Nation, the applicant shall obtain the free,
     prior, and informed consent of the governing body of the affected
     Tribal Nation before:

          (1) submitting an application for an early site permit,
          construction permit, or combined license to the Commission;

          (2) commencing any site characterization or other
          pre-construction activities on Tribal lands.

     (c) TRIBAL BENEFIT AGREEMENTS. — For any project receiving
     financial assistance under this Act that may affect Tribal
     interests, the applicant shall negotiate in good faith with
     the affected Tribal Nation to enter into a tribal benefit
     agreement providing for:

          (1) employment and contracting preferences for Tribal
          members and Tribally-owned businesses;

          (2) revenue sharing or other financial benefits;

          (3) protection of cultural and sacred sites;

          (4) environmental monitoring and emergency preparedness
          coordination;

          (5) ongoing consultation and communication mechanisms.

SECTION 804. LOCAL HIRING AND PROCUREMENT PREFERENCES.

     (a) LOCAL HIRING. — Each recipient of financial assistance under
     this Act in an amount exceeding $50,000,000 shall:

          (1) establish a goal of filling not less than 40 percent of
          construction jobs on the funded project with individuals
          who reside within 50 miles of the project site, or within
          the same county as the project site;

          (2) establish a goal of filling not less than 60 percent of
          permanent operations and maintenance jobs on the funded
          project with individuals who reside within 50 miles of the
          project site, or within the same county as the project site,
          within 3 years after the project commences commercial
          operation;

          (3) develop and implement a local hiring plan describing the
          specific actions the applicant will take to achieve the goals
          under paragraphs (1) and (2), including partnerships with
          local workforce development boards, community colleges, and
          labor organizations.

     (b) LOCAL PROCUREMENT. — Each recipient of financial assistance
     under this Act in an amount exceeding $50,000,000 shall:

          (1) establish a goal of sourcing not less than 25 percent
          of goods and services for the funded project from businesses
          located within 100 miles of the project site, or within the
          same state as the project site;

          (2) develop and implement a local procurement plan describing
          the specific actions the applicant will take to achieve the
          goal under paragraph (1), including outreach to and capacity
          building for local small businesses, minority-owned
          businesses, women-owned businesses, and veteran-owned
          businesses.

     (c) REPORTING. — Each recipient shall report annually to the
     Secretary on progress toward the goals established under
     subsections (a) and (b). The Secretary shall make such reports
     publicly available.

SECTION 805. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION AND TRANSPARENCY REQUIREMENTS.

     (a) PUBLIC INFORMATION REPOSITORY. — Each recipient of financial
     assistance under this Act in an amount exceeding $50,000,000 shall
     establish and maintain a publicly accessible website that includes:

          (1) all license applications, environmental reports, safety
          analysis reports, and other documents submitted to the
          Commission;

          (2) the community benefit agreement, environmental justice
          impact assessment, and mitigation plan;

          (3) environmental monitoring data and reports;

          (4) emergency preparedness and response plans;

          (5) progress reports on local hiring, local procurement, and
          other community commitments;

          (6) contact information for community liaison personnel.

     (b) PUBLIC HEARINGS. — For each major Federal action under this
     Act, including the issuance of early site permits, construction
     permits, and combined licenses, and the provision of Federal
     financial assistance, the responsible Federal agency shall hold
     not fewer than two public hearings in the host community, at
     times and locations accessible to community members, before
     making a final decision.

     (c) TRANSLATION. — Key documents, including the community benefit
     agreement, environmental justice impact assessment, and summary
     of license applications, shall be translated into languages spoken
     by not less than 5 percent of the population of the host community,
     as determined by the most recent American Community Survey data.

SECTION 806. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS FOR TITLE VIII.

     There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this title
     the following amounts:

          (1) For section 801 (Community Benefit Agreement Technical
          Assistance and Oversight): $500,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.

          (2) For section 802 (Environmental Justice Screening and
          Mitigation): $250,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (3) For section 803 (Tribal Consultation): $250,000,000 for
          FY2027-FY2036.

          (4) For section 804 (Local Hiring and Procurement Support):
          $500,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.

          (5) For section 805 (Public Participation):
          $100,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (6) For the remaining programs and activities authorized
          by this title: $900,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.
