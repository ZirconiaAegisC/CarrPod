                                  A BILL

    To establish a comprehensive framework for the accelerated deployment
    of advanced nuclear energy technologies in the United States,
    including small modular reactors, Generation IV advanced reactors,
    and commercial fusion energy systems; to modernize the licensing and
    regulatory framework of the Nuclear Regulatory Commission; to secure
    a domestic supply chain for high-assay low-enriched uranium; to
    provide deployment incentives for clean firm baseload power; to
    develop the nuclear energy workforce; to ensure environmental justice
    and community engagement in nuclear deployment; and for other
    purposes.

    SECTION 4. DEFINITIONS.

      (a) ADVANCED NUCLEAR REACTOR. — The term "advanced nuclear reactor"
      means a nuclear fission reactor, including a small modular reactor,
      that uses innovative technologies, materials, or designs and
      provides enhanced safety features, improved economics, reduced
      waste, or increased proliferation resistance compared to
      commercial light-water reactors operating as of the date of
      enactment of this Act.

      (b) SMALL MODULAR REACTOR. — The term "small modular reactor"
      means an advanced nuclear reactor with a rated electric capacity
      of not more than 300 megawatts per module, designed for factory
      fabrication and modular deployment.

      (c) FUSION ENERGY SYSTEM. — The term "fusion energy system" means
      a device or facility capable of producing net energy through
      nuclear fusion reactions and delivering such energy for commercial
      electricity generation or industrial heat applications.

      (d) HIGH-ASSAY LOW-ENRICHED URANIUM (HALEU). — The term
      "high-assay low-enriched uranium" means uranium having an assay
      of the uranium-235 isotope that is greater than 5 weight percent
      and less than 20 weight percent.

      (e) TRISO FUEL. — The term "TRISO fuel" means tristructural
      isotropic particle fuel, consisting of a fuel kernel coated with
      layers of carbon and silicon carbide, designed to retain fission
      products at high temperatures.

      (f) SECRETARY. — The term "Secretary" means the Secretary of
      Energy.

      (g) COMMISSION. — The term "Commission" means the Nuclear
      Regulatory Commission.

      (h) CLEAN FIRM POWER. — The term "clean firm power" means
      electricity generation that is dispatchable, available on demand,
      and produces zero or near-zero greenhouse gas emissions during
      operations.
