SECTION 201. ADVANCED REACTOR DEMONSTRATION PROGRAM EXPANSION.

     (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that the Department of Energy's
     Advanced Reactor Demonstration Program (ARDP), established under
     section 2001 of the Energy Act of 2020 (42 U.S.C. 16271), has
     successfully selected and is supporting the deployment of multiple
     first-of-a-kind Generation IV reactor designs in the United States,
     including:

          (1) the TerraPower Natrium sodium-cooled fast reactor with
          integrated molten-salt energy storage, sited at a retiring
          coal facility in Kemmerer, Wyoming, with a target operational
          date of 2030;

          (2) the X-energy Xe-100 high-temperature gas-cooled pebble
          bed reactor, utilizing TRISO fuel, sited at a Dow Chemical
          industrial facility in Seadrift, Texas.

     (b) ARDP EXPANSION. — The Secretary shall expand the Advanced
     Reactor Demonstration Program to support not fewer than five
     additional advanced reactor demonstration projects by December 31,
     2032, representing a diversity of Generation IV technology pathways
     including:

          (1) molten salt reactors using liquid fuel or solid fuel
          with liquid salt coolant (such as the Kairos Power Hermes
          design);

          (2) sodium-cooled fast reactors with integrated energy
          storage;

          (3) high-temperature gas-cooled reactors for industrial
          process heat applications;

          (4) lead-cooled fast reactors;

          (5) microreactors with a rated capacity of less than 20
          megawatts for remote community, mining, and military
          applications.

     (c) FUNDING. — There is authorized to be appropriated
     $8,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry
     out the ARDP expansion under this section.

SECTION 202. MOLTEN SALT REACTOR DEMONSTRATION AND DEPLOYMENT.

     (a) KAIROS POWER HERMES PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall provide
     continued funding and technical support for the Kairos Power
     Hermes molten salt reactor demonstration program, including:

          (1) the Hermes reduced-scale test reactor (construction
          permit issued by the Commission in December 2023, the first
          non-light-water reactor construction permit in the
          United States in more than 50 years);

          (2) the Hermes 2 two-unit demonstration plant;

          (3) the KP-FHR commercial-scale fluoride salt-cooled
          high-temperature reactor.

     (b) MOLTEN SALT REACTOR RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT. — The Secretary,
     acting through the Office of Nuclear Energy, shall establish a
     Molten Salt Reactor Research and Development Program to address:

          (1) material compatibility and corrosion challenges for
          structural materials exposed to molten fluoride and chloride
          salts at temperatures exceeding 600 degrees Celsius;

          (2) online fuel processing and fission product removal for
          liquid-fueled molten salt reactor designs;

          (3) tritium management, capture, and sequestration in
          fluoride-salt-cooled systems;

          (4) qualification and licensing of advanced materials,
          including Alloy 617, Alloy 709, and silicon carbide
          composites, for high-temperature reactor service.

     (c) FUEL SALT AND COOLANT SALT SUPPLY CHAIN. — The Secretary
     shall establish a program to ensure a reliable domestic supply
     of high-purity fluoride and chloride salts, including enriched
     lithium-7, beryllium fluoride, and sodium fluoroborate, in
     quantities sufficient to support commercial molten salt reactor
     deployment.

SECTION 203. SODIUM-COOLED FAST REACTOR DEPLOYMENT.

     (a) TERRAPOWER NATRIUM PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall provide
     continued funding and technical support for the TerraPower
     Natrium sodium-cooled fast reactor demonstration program,
     including:

          (1) completion of construction and commissioning of the
          Natrium demonstration reactor at the Kemmerer, Wyoming site;

          (2) qualification of the metallic fuel cycle, including
          advanced cladding materials compatible with liquid sodium
          coolant;

          (3) demonstration of the integrated molten-salt thermal
          energy storage system, which enables the reactor to
          operate at a steady thermal output while varying electric
          output to complement variable renewable generation.

     (b) SODIUM FAST REACTOR RESEARCH. — The Secretary shall continue
     and expand research and development activities supporting the
     deployment of sodium-cooled fast reactors, including:

          (1) advanced instrumentation and inspection technologies
          for opaque liquid metal coolants;

          (2) sodium-water reaction mitigation and steam generator
          design optimization;

          (3) passive decay heat removal systems for pool-type
          sodium-cooled reactors;

          (4) advanced fuel cycle technologies, including pyroprocessing
          and metallic fuel fabrication, conducted in a manner
          consistent with United States nonproliferation policy.

SECTION 204. HIGH-TEMPERATURE GAS-COOLED REACTOR DEPLOYMENT.

     (a) X-ENERGY XE-100 PROGRAM. — The Secretary shall provide
     continued funding and technical support for the X-energy Xe-100
     high-temperature gas-cooled reactor demonstration program,
     including:

          (1) the first commercial Xe-100 four-unit plant at the
          Dow Seadrift, Texas industrial facility;

          (2) expansion of the TRISO-X fuel fabrication facility
          in Oak Ridge, Tennessee, the Nation's first commercial-
          scale TRISO fuel production facility.

     (b) HTGR INDUSTRIAL APPLICATIONS. — The Secretary, in coordination
     with the Department of Commerce and the Industrial Technology
     Innovation Advisory Committee, shall establish a program to
     demonstrate the use of high-temperature gas-cooled reactors for
     industrial process heat applications, including:

          (1) hydrogen production via high-temperature steam
          electrolysis or thermochemical water splitting;

          (2) petrochemical refining and ammonia production;

          (3) direct air capture of carbon dioxide using HTGR-
          supplied heat and electricity;

          (4) synthetic fuel production;

          (5) desalination of seawater.

     (c) INDUSTRIAL PARTNERSHIPS. — The Secretary shall solicit and
     support partnerships between HTGR developers and industrial
     end-users, with the goal of establishing not fewer than three
     commercial HTGR industrial heat projects by December 31, 2035.

SECTION 205. TRISO FUEL PARTICLE SUPPLY CHAIN ESTABLISHMENT.

     (a) DOMESTIC TRISO PRODUCTION. — The Secretary shall establish a
     program to ensure a reliable and commercially viable domestic
     supply chain for TRISO fuel particles, including:

          (1) high-density uranium oxycarbide (UCO) kernel production;

          (2) chemical vapor deposition of pyrolytic carbon and
          silicon carbide coating layers;

          (3) fuel particle quality control and qualification testing,
          including irradiation testing at the Advanced Test Reactor
          at Idaho National Laboratory;

          (4) compact and pebble fuel form fabrication.

     (b) PRODUCTION TARGETS. — The program shall aim to establish
     domestic TRISO production capacity of not less than 10 metric
     tons of heavy metal per year by December 31, 2031, and not less
     than 50 metric tons per year by December 31, 2036.

     (c) AUTHORIZATION. — There is authorized to be appropriated
     $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry
     out this section.

SECTION 206. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS FOR TITLE II.

     There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this title
     the following amounts:

          (1) For section 201 (ARDP Expansion): $8,000,000,000 for
          FY2027-FY2036.

          (2) For section 202 (Molten Salt Reactor R&D): $2,500,000,000
          for FY2027-FY2036.

          (3) For section 203 (Sodium Fast Reactor R&D): $2,000,000,000
          for FY2027-FY2036.

          (4) For section 204 (HTGR Industrial Applications):
          $2,000,000,000 for FY2027-FY2036.

          (5) For section 205 (TRISO Fuel Supply): $3,000,000,000
          for FY2027-FY2036.

          (6) For the remaining programs and activities authorized
          by this title: $2,500,000,000 for FY2027-FY2041.
