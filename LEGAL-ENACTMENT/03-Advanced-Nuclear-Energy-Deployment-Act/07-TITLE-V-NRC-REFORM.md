           TITLE V — NUCLEAR REGULATORY COMMISSION REFORM

================================================================================

SECTION 501. ADVANCED REACTOR LICENSING FRAMEWORK CODIFICATION.

     (a) CODIFICATION OF 10 CFR PART 53. — Not later than December 31,
     2027, the Commission shall complete the rulemaking for and
     promulgate as a final rule 10 CFR Part 53, "Licensing of Advanced
     Nuclear Reactors," which shall establish a technology-inclusive,
     risk-informed, and performance-based regulatory framework for
     the licensing of advanced nuclear reactors.

     (b) FRAMEWORK REQUIREMENTS. — The Part 53 framework shall include
     the following elements:

          (1) a licensing process that is applicable to any reactor
          technology, without requiring a new or amended rule for
          each technology class;

          (2) risk-informed, performance-based safety criteria that
          specify the safety functions and performance standards
          that a facility must achieve, rather than prescribing how
          the facility must be designed;

          (3) a graded approach under which the scope and depth of
          regulatory review and the stringency of regulatory
          requirements are calibrated to the radiological risk
          posed by the facility, determined using a quantitative
          health objective that is consistent with the Commission's
          Safety Goal Policy Statement;

          (4) provisions for the use of probabilistic risk assessment
          as a primary analytical tool in licensing decisions,
          supplemented by deterministic analyses and defense-in-depth
          considerations;

          (5) streamlined environmental review provisions that
          leverage generic environmental impact statements for reactor
          technologies and site types, reducing the scope and duration
          of project-specific environmental reviews;

          (6) provisions for the use of codes, standards, and
          qualification programs developed by standards development
          organizations and industry groups, subject to Commission
          endorsement, in satisfaction of regulatory requirements.

     (c) CONGRESSIONAL OVERSIGHT. — Not later than 90 days after the
     date of enactment of this Act, and every 180 days thereafter until
     the Part 53 final rule is promulgated, the Commission shall submit
     to the appropriate committees of Congress a report on the status
     of the Part 53 rulemaking, including any obstacles to timely
     completion and the Commission's plan for addressing such obstacles.

SECTION 502. EXPEDITED REVIEW PATHWAYS FOR STANDARDIZED DESIGNS.

     (a) EXPEDITED DESIGN CERTIFICATION. — The Commission shall
     establish an expedited review pathway for applications for design
     certification of standardized advanced reactor designs that:

          (1) have a rated capacity of not more than 500 megawatts
          thermal;

          (2) incorporate passive safety features that do not require
          operator action or offsite power for safe shutdown and
          decay heat removal;

          (3) utilize fuel forms and reactor technologies for which
          sufficient technical data exists to support regulatory
          decision-making;

          (4) have been previously licensed or certified by the
          regulatory authority of a qualifying allied nation, as
          determined by the Commission in consultation with the
          Secretary of State.

     (b) REVIEW TIMELINE. — For applications meeting the criteria of
     subsection (a), the Commission shall:

          (1) complete its acceptance review within 90 days of docketing;

          (2) issue a safety evaluation report within 18 months of
          docketing;

          (3) issue a final decision within 24 months of docketing.

     (c) COMBINED LICENSE REFORM. — The Commission shall amend its
     regulations to permit an applicant for a combined license under
     10 CFR Part 52 to reference a design certification application
     that is concurrently under review, enabling parallel rather than
     sequential resolution of design and site-specific issues.

     (d) EARLY SITE PERMIT ENHANCEMENTS. — The Commission shall revise
     its early site permit regulations to allow for:

          (1) generic early site permits that address site suitability
          for multiple reactor designs;

          (2) early site permits that are valid for a period of not
          less than 40 years, extendable in 20-year increments;

          (3) early site permits that may be issued before a specific
          reactor design has been selected, based on bounding
          environmental and safety analyses.

SECTION 503. TECHNOLOGY-INCLUSIVE RISK ASSESSMENT METHODOLOGY.

     (a) LICENSING MODERNIZATION PROJECT. — The Commission shall
     incorporate into its regulatory framework the products of the
     Licensing Modernization Project, including:

          (1) technology-inclusive, risk-informed, and performance-based
          guidance for non-light-water reactor licensing, as published
          in NUREG/CR-7272, "Guidance for Developing Principal Design
          Criteria for Non-Light Water Reactors";

          (2) the Licensing Modernization Project methodology for
          identifying licensing basis events, classifying structures,
          systems, and components, and determining appropriate
          special treatment requirements.

     (b) PROBABILISTIC RISK ASSESSMENT. — The Commission shall require
     that every application for a construction permit, operating
     license, or combined license for an advanced reactor include a
     facility-specific probabilistic risk assessment. The Commission
     shall use the probabilistic risk assessment as a primary input
     to its safety evaluation, including for the identification and
     classification of licensing basis events and the determination
     of the adequacy of the applicant's proposed safety case.

     (c) QUANTITATIVE HEALTH OBJECTIVES. — The Commission shall adopt
     quantitative health objectives applicable to advanced reactors,
     specifying the acceptable levels of risk to members of the public,
     expressed in terms of individual early fatality risk and
     individual latent cancer fatality risk, consistent with the
     Commission's Safety Goal Policy Statement (51 FR 30028, August 21,
     1986) and the Commission's Quantitative Health Objectives
     (54 FR 30028, August 21, 1986).

SECTION 504. NRC STAFFING AND RESOURCE ENHANCEMENT.

     (a) STAFFING INCREASE. — The Commission is authorized to increase
     its full-time equivalent staffing level by not fewer than 500
     positions, as compared to the staffing level as of September 30,
     2026, with such additional positions dedicated to:

          (1) advanced reactor licensing review (minimum 250 positions);

          (2) fusion energy regulatory framework development (minimum
          50 positions);

          (3) HALEU facility licensing and inspection (minimum 50
          positions);

          (4) international regulatory coordination and harmonization
          (minimum 25 positions);

          (5) research and development to support advanced reactor
          regulatory decision-making (minimum 125 positions).

     (b) COMPENSATION AND RECRUITMENT. — The Commission shall establish:

          (1) special pay rates, recruitment bonuses, and retention
          incentives for positions requiring expertise in advanced
          reactor technologies, probabilistic risk assessment, or
          other skills that are in high demand in the private sector;

          (2) an Advanced Reactor Fellowship Program, in collaboration
          with universities and national laboratories, to develop a
          pipeline of qualified regulatory professionals with expertise
          in advanced nuclear technologies.

     (c) CONTRACTOR SUPPORT. — The Commission may utilize contractor
     support, including from Department of Energy national laboratories,
     to supplement its in-house technical capabilities for the review
     of advanced reactor applications, provided that the Commission
     retains responsibility for all regulatory decisions.

SECTION 505. INTERNATIONAL REGULATORY HARMONIZATION.

     (a) BILATERAL COOPERATION. — The Commission, in coordination with
     the Secretary of State, shall seek to establish bilateral
     arrangements with the regulatory authorities of Canada, the United
     Kingdom, France, Japan, the Republic of Korea, and other qualifying
     allied nations for the mutual acceptance of reactor design reviews,
     certifications, and approvals.

     (b) MULTILATERAL FORA. — The Commission shall actively participate
     in multilateral regulatory cooperation initiatives, including:

          (1) the International Atomic Energy Agency's Nuclear
          Harmonization and Standardization Initiative (NHSI);

          (2) the Multinational Design Evaluation Programme (MDEP);

          (3) the Generation IV International Forum (GIF) Risk and
          Safety Working Group.

     (c) CODE AND STANDARD HARMONIZATION. — The Commission shall
     coordinate with the American Society of Mechanical Engineers,
     the American Nuclear Society, and the International Organization
     for Standardization to develop common codes and standards for
     advanced reactor design, fabrication, construction, and testing,
     reducing the need for technology-specific or country-specific
     qualification programs.

SECTION 506. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS FOR TITLE V.

     There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this title
     the following amounts:

          (1) For section 501 (Part 53 Codification and Completion):
          $100,000,000 for FY2027-FY2029.

          (2) For section 502 (Expedited Review Pathways):
          $50,000,000 for FY2027-FY2031.

          (3) For section 503 (Risk Assessment Methodology):
          $100,000,000 for FY2027-FY2031.

          (4) For section 504 (Staffing and Resource Enhancement):
          $2,000,000,000 for FY2027-FY2031.

          (5) For section 505 (International Harmonization):
          $50,000,000 for FY2027-FY2031.

          (6) For the remaining programs and activities authorized
          by this title: $200,000,000 for FY2027-FY2031.
