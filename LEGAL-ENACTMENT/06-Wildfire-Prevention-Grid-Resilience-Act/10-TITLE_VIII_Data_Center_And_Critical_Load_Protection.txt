        TITLE VIII — DATA CENTER AND CRITICAL LOAD PROTECTION

================================================================================

SEC. 801. GRID INTEGRATION STANDARDS.

    (a) FINDINGS. — Congress finds that:
        (1) The United States is experiencing unprecedented growth in
            data center electricity demand, with a projected 4.6 GW
            pipeline of new data center load seeking interconnection,
            representing approximately $500 billion in capital
            investment;
        (2) A significant portion of this load growth is concentrated
            in regions of the Western United States that overlap with
            High Fire Threat Districts;
        (3) Data center outages caused by wildfire-related grid
            disruptions pose risks to national economic security,
            financial systems, cloud computing infrastructure,
            artificial intelligence training workloads, and
            government digital services.

    (b) INTERCONNECTION STANDARDS. — Within 365 days of enactment,
    FERC, in consultation with the North American Electric Reliability
    Corporation (NERC), shall promulgate mandatory grid integration
    standards for new large-load interconnections in High Fire Threat
    Districts, including:

        (1) MINIMUM REDUNDANCY. — Data centers with a connected load
            exceeding 50 MW shall be served by not fewer than two
            independent transmission supply paths, with each path
            capable of carrying not less than 100 percent of the
            facility's peak demand.

        (2) BEHIND-METER RESILIENCE. — Data centers shall maintain
            behind-meter generation or storage resources capable of
            sustaining not less than 24 hours of operation at 50
            percent of peak load without grid power.

        (3) DEMAND RESPONSE CAPABILITY. — Data centers shall be
            capable of reducing load by not less than 30 percent
            within 10 minutes of receiving a demand response signal
            from the balancing authority or utility.

        (4) SITING RESTRICTIONS. — New data center facilities shall
            not be sited in Tier 3 (Extreme) High Fire Threat
            Districts unless the applicant demonstrates that no
            feasible alternative site exists and that comprehensive
            wildfire mitigation measures are incorporated into the
            facility design.

    (c) INTERCONNECTION QUEUE PRIORITY. — FERC shall direct
    transmission providers to establish a fast-track interconnection
    process for behind-meter storage and generation resources that
    reduce the net grid load of data center facilities in High Fire
    Threat Districts.

SEC. 802. BEHIND-METER STORAGE REQUIREMENTS.

    (a) MINIMUM STORAGE MANDATE. — Effective January 1, 2029, any
    new data center with a connected load exceeding 25 MW that
    interconnects to the grid within a High Fire Threat District
    shall install behind-meter battery energy storage with:

        (1) A rated power capacity of not less than 25 percent of
            the facility's peak demand;
        (2) A discharge duration of not less than 4 hours at rated
            power;
        (3) Black-start and islanding capability to support critical
            facility loads during grid outages;
        (4) Fire suppression systems compliant with NFPA 855.

    (b) FEDERAL INVESTMENT TAX CREDIT ENHANCEMENT. — Behind-meter
    storage systems meeting the requirements of subsection (a) and
    installed at data center facilities shall be eligible for a 10
    percent adder to the Energy Investment Tax Credit under section
    48 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986.

    (c) CO-LOCATED GENERATION. — Data centers that install behind-
    meter generation resources sufficient to meet 100 percent of
    their peak demand during grid outages shall be eligible for an
    additional 10 percent federal investment tax credit adder.

SEC. 803. DEMAND RESPONSE MANDATES.

    (a) PARTICIPATION REQUIREMENT. — Each data center facility with
    a connected load exceeding 25 MW and located in a High Fire
    Threat District shall:
        (1) Register as a demand response resource with the
            relevant balancing authority;
        (2) Maintain automated demand response capability compliant
            with OpenADR 2.0b or successor standard;
        (3) Participate in demand response events when called upon,
            subject to a maximum of 200 hours of curtailment per
            calendar year.

    (b) COMPENSATION. — Data centers participating in demand response
    shall be compensated at prevailing wholesale market prices for
    the capacity and energy value of their load reductions, or at
    rates established by the relevant state regulatory authority for
    vertically integrated utilities.

    (c) CRITICAL LOAD EXEMPTION. — Data center loads designated as
    Critical Infrastructure under Presidential Policy Directive 21
    (PPD-21) or serving essential government functions may be
    exempted from demand response participation upon certification
    by the Secretary of Homeland Security.

SEC. 804. CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE RESILIENCE PLANNING.

    (a) RESILIENCE PLANS. — Within 18 months of enactment, each data
    center operator with facilities exceeding 25 MW of connected load
    in High Fire Threat Districts shall submit to the Department of
    Energy a Wildfire Resilience Plan that addresses:

        (1) Identification of wildfire risks to the facility's grid
            supply and on-site infrastructure;
        (2) Redundant power supply arrangements and fuel supply
            agreements for backup generation;
        (3) Procedures for orderly load shedding and facility
            shutdown in the event of a forecasted PSPS event or
            wildfire approaching the facility;
        (4) Coordination protocols with local emergency management
            agencies and the serving electric utility;
        (5) Employee safety and evacuation procedures during
            wildfire events.

    (b) INFORMATION SHARING. — Data center operators shall share
    their Wildfire Resilience Plans with the serving electric
    utility, the state energy office, and the relevant county
    emergency management agency.

    (c) ANNUAL EXERCISES. — Data center operators shall conduct an
    annual tabletop or functional exercise of their Wildfire
    Resilience Plan, with results reported to the Department of
    Energy.

SEC. 805. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    (a) There is authorized to be appropriated $5 billion for the
    period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this Title,
    allocated as follows:
        (1) $3 billion for behind-meter storage and generation
            investment tax credit enhancements;
        (2) $1 billion for grid integration infrastructure
            supporting redundant supply paths to critical data
            center facilities;
        (3) $1 billion for research, standards development, and
            program administration.
