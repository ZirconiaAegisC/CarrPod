             TITLE IV — PUBLIC SAFETY POWER SHUTOFF REFORM

================================================================================

SEC. 401. NATIONAL PSPS STANDARDS.

    (a) MINIMUM STANDARDS. — Not later than 365 days after enactment,
    the Secretary of Energy, in consultation with FERC and the National
    Association of Regulatory Utility Commissioners (NARUC), shall
    promulgate national minimum standards for Public Safety Power
    Shutoff (PSPS) events. Such standards shall, at minimum, require:

        (1) RISK THRESHOLD. — PSPS events shall be initiated only when
        forecasted weather conditions meet or exceed established
        thresholds for wind speed, relative humidity, fuel moisture,
        and fire danger rating that create a high probability of
        utility-infrastructure-caused wildfire ignition.

        (2) GEOGRAPHIC TARGETING. — PSPS de-energization shall be
        limited to the minimum geographic area and the minimum number
        of circuits necessary to mitigate the identified wildfire risk.
        Utilities shall deploy sectionalizing devices, automated
        switches, and circuit reconfiguration to minimize the number
        of customers affected by each PSPS event.

        (3) ADVANCE NOTIFICATION. — Utilities shall provide:
            (A) 48-hour advance notice to state and local emergency
                management agencies of potential PSPS activation;
            (B) 24-hour advance notice to all potentially affected
                customers, delivered through at least three
                communication channels including text message, email,
                and automated voice call;
            (C) Real-time updates during the PSPS event at intervals
                not exceeding 4 hours.

        (4) RESTORATION. — Utilities shall deploy field patrol
        resources before, during, and after PSPS events to enable
        restoration of service within 24 hours of the cessation of
        the fire-weather conditions that triggered the event, weather
        and daylight permitting.

        (5) POST-EVENT REPORTING. — Within 30 days of each PSPS event,
        the utility shall file a public report with the relevant state
        regulatory authority and the Department of Energy detailing:
            (A) The meteorological conditions that triggered the
                event;
            (B) The geographic area and number of circuits and
                customers affected;
            (C) The duration of the de-energization;
            (D) Whether any ignitions occurred on de-energized
                circuits during the event window;
            (E) An assessment of whether the geographic scope and
                duration of the event were proportionate to the
                wildfire risk identified.

    (b) PG&E PSPS PERFORMANCE. — PG&E's PSPS program, which has
    achieved a 95% reduction in wildfire risk compared to pre-2020
    protocols through enhanced weather modeling, circuit
    sectionalizing, and customer notification systems, shall serve
    as a benchmark for the national standards developed under this
    section.

SEC. 402. MEDICALLY VULNERABLE CUSTOMER PROTECTIONS.

    (a) REGISTRY. — Each utility subject to PSPS standards shall
    maintain a confidential registry of customers who are medically
    vulnerable to power loss, including:
        (1) Individuals who rely on electric-powered medical devices,
            including but not limited to ventilators, oxygen
            concentrators, dialysis machines, and power wheelchairs;
        (2) Individuals with medical conditions for which loss of
            heating or cooling poses a significant health risk;
        (3) Individuals who require refrigerated medications.

    (b) SPECIAL NOTIFICATION. — Utilities shall provide medically
    vulnerable registered customers with:
        (1) Direct outreach by utility personnel via telephone not
            less than 48 hours before a planned PSPS event;
        (2) Priority restoration of service upon cessation of fire-
            weather conditions;
        (3) Information on available backup power resources and
            relocation options.

    (c) COMPENSATION. — Utilities shall compensate medically
    vulnerable customers at a rate of $100 per day for each full
    24-hour period of PSPS-caused power loss, with payments made
    within 30 days of the end of the PSPS event.

SEC. 403. BACKUP POWER GRANT PROGRAM.

    (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is established a Backup Power Grant
    Program, administered by the Department of Energy, to provide
    grants to:
        (1) Medically vulnerable customers for the purchase of
            portable backup batteries, portable generators, or
            permanently installed battery storage systems;
        (2) Critical community facilities, including hospitals,
            fire stations, police stations, emergency shelters,
            and water and wastewater treatment plants, for the
            installation of permanent backup power systems.

    (b) PRIORITY. — Grant priority shall be given to:
        (1) Medically vulnerable customers in census tracts where
            the median household income is below 80 percent of the
            state median;
        (2) Critical facilities located in High Fire Threat
            Districts that serve as designated community refuge
            points during PSPS events.

    (c) SOLAR-PLUS-STORAGE PREFERENCE. — For permanent backup
    power installations at critical community facilities, the
    Program shall provide a 25 percent grant premium for systems
    that combine solar photovoltaic generation with battery
    storage capable of islanding from the grid and providing not
    less than 72 hours of autonomous operation.

SEC. 404. POST-EVENT REPORTING AND TRANSPARENCY.

    (a) PUBLIC DATABASE. — The Secretary shall maintain a publicly
    accessible database of all PSPS events occurring in the United
    States, updated within 30 days of each event, containing at
    minimum:
        (1) The date, duration, and geographic extent of each event;
        (2) The number of customers affected;
        (3) The meteorological conditions at initiation and
            termination;
        (4) Whether any wildfire ignitions occurred on de-energized
            or adjacent circuits during the event.

    (b) ANNUAL ANALYSIS. — The Secretary shall publish an annual
    analysis of national PSPS trends, including:
        (1) Year-over-year changes in PSPS frequency, geographic
            scope, and customer impact;
        (2) Correlation between PSPS events and wildfire ignition
            outcomes;
        (3) Identification of best practices and areas requiring
            improvement.

SEC. 405. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    (a) There is authorized to be appropriated $5 billion for the
    period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this
    Title, allocated as follows:
        (1) $3.0 billion for backup power grants to medically
            vulnerable customers and critical facilities;
        (2) $1.0 billion for state and local PSPS preparedness and
            community resilience grants;
        (3) $1.0 billion for program administration, PSPS database
            maintenance, and technical assistance.
