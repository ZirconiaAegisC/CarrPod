SEC. 901. TOTAL AUTHORIZATION.

    (a) There is authorized to be appropriated to carry out this Act
    a total of $150,000,000,000 for the period of fiscal years 2027
    through 2036.

    (b) Funds authorized under this Act shall remain available until
    expended, subject to the program completion and sunset provisions
    of Title X.

    (c) Amounts authorized under this Act are in addition to any
    other amounts authorized for wildfire prevention, grid resilience,
    or related purposes under any other provision of law.

    (b) PROGRAM RESERVE. — The Secretary may reserve not more than
    3 percent of each program area's annual appropriation for
    program administration, technical assistance, and oversight.

SEC. 903. STATE MATCHING REQUIREMENTS.

    (a) STATES. — As a condition of receiving federal cost-share
    funds under Titles I, II, V, VI, and VII, each participating
    state shall provide a contribution of not less than 10 percent
    of the non-federal share of project costs within that state,
    which may be satisfied through:
        (1) Direct state appropriations;
        (2) State infrastructure bank loans or loan guarantees;
        (3) Ratepayer-backed securitization bonds issued by a
            state infrastructure authority;
        (4) Tax credits or other financial incentives provided
            by the state to the participating utility.

    (b) WAIVER. — The Secretary may waive or reduce the state
    matching requirement under subsection (a) for:
        (1) States that have experienced a major disaster
            declaration under the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief
            and Emergency Assistance Act within the preceding 24
            months;
        (2) States where the median household income is below 75
            percent of the national median as determined by the
            Bureau of the Census;
        (3) Projects located on tribal lands.

    (c) TRIBAL PROJECTS. — Projects located on federally recognized
    tribal lands shall be exempt from state matching requirements
    and shall be eligible for a federal cost-share of not less than
    90 percent.

SEC. 904. OVERSIGHT AND AUDIT.

    (a) INSPECTOR GENERAL. — The Inspector General of the Department
    of Energy shall conduct an annual audit of expenditures under
    this Act and shall report findings to the Committee on Energy and
    Commerce of the House of Representatives and the Committee on
    Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate.

    (b) GAO REVIEW. — The Comptroller General of the United States
    shall conduct a comprehensive review of the programs established
    under this Act not later than 5 years after enactment and every
    5 years thereafter, assessing:
        (1) The effectiveness of each program in achieving its
            stated wildfire prevention and grid resilience
            objectives;
        (2) The efficiency of fund allocation and expenditure;
        (3) The adequacy of program oversight and accountability
            mechanisms;
        (4) Recommendations for legislative or administrative
            improvements.

    (c) RECAPTURE AUTHORITY. — The Secretary may recapture funds
    from any grant recipient that:
        (1) Fails to comply with program requirements after notice
            and a 90-day opportunity to cure;
        (2) Engages in fraud, waste, or abuse in connection with
            Program funds;
        (3) Fails to achieve substantial completion of funded work
            within timelines established in the grant agreement.

    (d) FRAUD REPORTING. — The Secretary shall establish and
    publicize a mechanism for reporting suspected fraud, waste, or
    abuse in programs funded under this Act, including a toll-free
    hotline and a secure web-based reporting portal.

SEC. 905. SUPPLEMENT NOT SUPPLANT.

    (a) Funds provided under this Act shall supplement, and not
    supplant, state and utility expenditures for wildfire prevention
    and grid resilience that would otherwise be made in the absence
    of this Act.

    (b) As a condition of receiving federal funds under this Act,
    each recipient utility shall certify annually that federal funds
    have not been used to reduce the utility's own wildfire
    prevention expenditures below the average annual level of such
    expenditures during the three fiscal years preceding enactment.
