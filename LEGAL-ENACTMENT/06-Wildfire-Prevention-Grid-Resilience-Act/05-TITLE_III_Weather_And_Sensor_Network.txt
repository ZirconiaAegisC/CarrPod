          TITLE III — NATIONAL WEATHER AND SENSOR NETWORK
                 FOR WILDFIRE SITUATIONAL AWARENESS

================================================================================

SEC. 301. FEDERAL GRANT PROGRAM FOR WEATHER STATIONS.

    (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is established within the Department of
    Energy, in coordination with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric
    Administration (NOAA), a National Wildfire Weather and Sensor
    Network Grant Program ("the Weather Network Program").

    (b) DEPLOYMENT TARGET. — The Weather Network Program shall provide
    federal grants to eligible utilities and state agencies for the
    deployment of not fewer than 10,000 wildfire-weather monitoring
    stations across high-fire-risk regions of the United States over
    the ten-year authorization period.

    (c) STATION SPECIFICATIONS. — Each weather station shall, at
    minimum, measure and report at 10-minute intervals:
        (1) Wind speed and wind direction at 10-meter height;
        (2) Temperature and relative humidity;
        (3) Fuel moisture content, measured directly or calculated
            from meteorological inputs using the National Fire Danger
            Rating System (NFDRS) methodology;
        (4) Solar radiation;
        (5) Precipitation;
        (6) Soil moisture at two depths.

    (d) PG&E WEATHER NETWORK MODEL. — The PG&E weather station network,
    comprising 1,627 stations providing coverage across 70,000 square
    miles of high-fire-risk territory at an average density of one
    station per 43 square miles, shall serve as the reference design
    for the national network. The Secretary shall:
        (1) Adopt PG&E's station siting methodology, which positions
            stations to capture microclimate variability across
            complex terrain including ridgelines, canyons, and
            wildland-urban interface transition zones;
        (2) Require participating entities to maintain a minimum
            station density calibrated to local fire risk as
            determined by the Wildfire Hazard Potential Index.

SEC. 302. HIGH-DEFINITION CAMERA AND DETECTION NETWORK.

    (a) CAMERA NETWORK. — The Weather Network Program shall include
    grants for the deployment of a national network of high-definition
    pan-tilt-zoom (PTZ) cameras equipped with automated wildfire
    detection capability.

    (b) DEPLOYMENT DENSITY. — Participating entities shall deploy
    cameras at a minimum density sufficient to provide overlapping
    visual coverage of all Tier 2 and Tier 3 High Fire Threat
    Districts within their service territories, with priority given
    to corridors that provide line-of-sight coverage of electric
    transmission and distribution rights-of-way.

    (c) DETECTION CAPABILITIES. — Camera systems shall incorporate
    automated detection algorithms capable of identifying:
        (1) Smoke plumes within 60 seconds of visibility;
        (2) Flame front location and estimated rate of spread;
        (3) Changes in vegetation condition indicative of elevated
            fire risk.

    (d) PG&E CAMERA NETWORK REFERENCE. — PG&E's network of 706 HD
    cameras with automated detection, providing real-time visual
    monitoring of high-fire-risk corridors, shall serve as the
    reference implementation. The Secretary shall document and
    disseminate PG&E's camera siting methodology, detection
    algorithm performance characteristics, and operational
    integration with utility control centers and fire agency
    emergency operations centers.

SEC. 303. DATA SHARING AND INTEGRATION WITH NOAA.

    (a) DATA INTEGRATION. — All weather station and camera data
    collected under this Title shall be transmitted in real time to:
        (1) The deploying utility's control center;
        (2) NOAA's National Weather Service forecast offices
            serving the relevant geographic area;
        (3) The National Interagency Fire Center (NIFC);
        (4) State and local fire agencies with jurisdiction;
        (5) A centralized national data repository maintained by
            the Department of Energy.

    (b) NOAA INTEGRATION. — The Secretary of Commerce, acting through
    the Administrator of NOAA, shall:
        (1) Incorporate data from the Weather Network Program into
            operational weather forecast models, including the High-
            Resolution Rapid Refresh (HRRR) model;
        (2) Use network data to improve the Spatial Fire Danger
            Index and other fire-weather decision-support products;
        (3) Ensure that Weather Network Program data is accessible
            through the NOAA Big Data Program and the National
            Centers for Environmental Information.

    (c) DATA STANDARDS. — All data generated by the Weather Network
    Program shall conform to open data format standards established
    by the Federal Geographic Data Committee (FGDC) and shall be
    made publicly available, except for camera imagery within 30
    minutes of an active fire event, which may be restricted to
    authorized emergency response personnel.

SEC. 304. STANDARDS FOR METEOROLOGICAL DATA COLLECTION.

    (a) CALIBRATION. — All weather stations shall be calibrated to
    NIST-traceable standards upon installation and at intervals not
    exceeding 12 months thereafter.

    (b) QUALITY ASSURANCE. — The Secretary shall establish automated
    data quality assurance procedures, including range checks,
    temporal consistency checks, and spatial consistency checks
    against neighboring stations, with flagged data excluded from
    operational fire-weather calculations.

    (c) TRAINING. — The Secretary shall provide a training program
    for utility meteorologists and operations personnel on the use
    of Weather Network data for fire-risk assessment, PSPS decision-
    making, and operational planning.

SEC. 305. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    (a) There is authorized to be appropriated $10 billion for the
    period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this Title,
    allocated as follows:
        (1) $6.0 billion for weather station procurement, installation,
            and maintenance cost-share grants;
        (2) $3.0 billion for HD camera procurement, installation, and
            detection algorithm licensing;
        (3) $1.0 billion for NOAA data integration, the national data
            repository, and program administration.
