               TITLE VII — TRANSMISSION LINE HARDENING

================================================================================

SEC. 701. STRUCTURAL UPGRADE STANDARDS.

    (a) MINIMUM STANDARDS. — Within 365 days of enactment, the
    Secretary of Energy, in consultation with the American Society of
    Civil Engineers (ASCE) and the IEEE Power and Energy Society, shall
    promulgate enhanced structural standards for electric transmission
    and distribution lines in High Fire Threat Districts. Such standards
    shall require:

        (1) WIND LOADING. — Structures shall be designed to withstand
            the 99th percentile three-second gust wind speed for the
            location, as determined by ASCE 7-22 or successor standard,
            with a minimum design wind speed of 90 mph in all High
            Fire Threat Districts.

        (2) FIRE RESISTANCE. — Poles and towers shall maintain
            structural integrity for not less than 60 minutes when
            exposed to a surface fire with flame lengths of up to 8
            feet, equivalent to the Fire Resistant Design for Utility
            Poles (FIRE-RES-UP) standard.

        (3) CONDUCTOR ATTACHMENT. — Insulators, crossarms, and
            conductor attachment hardware shall be designed with a
            safety factor of not less than 2.5 under maximum
            combined wind, ice, and thermal loading conditions.

        (4) GUYING AND ANCHORING. — Guyed structures in High Fire
            Threat Districts shall utilize non-conductive guy
            insulators rated for not less than 50 kV impulse
            withstand and anchors designed for the site-specific
            soil conditions.

    (b) SEISMIC CONSIDERATIONS. — Transmission structures in High Fire
    Threat Districts located in Seismic Design Categories D, E, or F
    shall be designed to ASCE 7-22 seismic provisions, accounting for
    the potential for cascading failures initiated by seismic events
    that could produce wildfire ignitions.

SEC. 702. COMPOSITE AND FIRE-RESISTANT MATERIALS.

    (a) COMPOSITE POLE STANDARDS. — The Secretary shall establish a
    program to accelerate the deployment of composite utility poles
    in High Fire Threat Districts. Composite poles deployed under this
    Title shall:

        (1) Achieve a fire-resistance rating of not less than 60
            minutes when tested in accordance with ASTM E119 or an
            equivalent fire-endurance test standard;

        (2) Maintain not less than 85 percent of original bending
            strength after exposure to a standardized wildfire
            simulation protocol;

        (3) Exhibit Class A flame-spread index when tested in
            accordance with ASTM E84;

        (4) Have a design service life of not less than 60 years
            in the intended deployment environment.

    (b) COST-SHARE FOR COMPOSITE POLES. — Utilities replacing wood
    poles with composite poles in High Fire Threat Districts shall
    be eligible for a federal cost-share of 50 percent of the
    incremental cost of composite poles relative to equivalent-
    class wood poles.

    (c) FIRE-RESISTANT COATINGS. — The Secretary shall fund research,
    development, testing, and field validation of fire-resistant
    coatings and wraps for application to existing wood poles in
    High Fire Threat Districts, with the objective of achieving fire-
    resistance performance equivalent to composite poles at a cost
    not exceeding 30 percent of the cost of pole replacement.

SEC. 703. COVERED CONDUCTOR DEPLOYMENT.

    (a) DEFINITION. — For purposes of this Title, "covered conductor"
    means an overhead conductor with a covering of cross-linked
    polyethylene, ethylene propylene rubber, or equivalent polymeric
    material that provides:
        (1) Protection against phase-to-phase and phase-to-ground
            faults from incidental conductor contact with vegetation,
            wildlife, or wind-blown debris;
        (2) Electrical tracking resistance sufficient to prevent
            arc initiation under contaminated surface conditions;
        (3) Ultraviolet and thermal degradation resistance for not
            less than 40 years of service life.

    (b) DEPLOYMENT REQUIREMENT. — Not later than December 31, 2032,
    every electric utility operating distribution circuits within
    High Fire Threat Districts shall deploy covered conductor on not
    less than 75 percent of overhead circuit miles within such
    districts, with priority given to circuits:
        (1) With the highest historical ignition frequency;
        (2) Traversing the most severe terrain for vegetation
            management access;
        (3) Serving critical facilities.

    (c) COST-SHARE. — Utilities shall be eligible for a federal
    cost-share of 50 percent of the cost of covered conductor
    deployment, including associated hardware, installation labor,
    and project management.

SEC. 704. INSPECTION AND MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS.

    (a) ENHANCED INSPECTION CYCLE. — Utilities shall conduct detailed
    inspections of all transmission and distribution structures in
    High Fire Threat Districts at intervals not exceeding:
        (1) 3 years for steel and concrete transmission structures;
        (2) 2 years for wood transmission structures;
        (3) 1 year for all distribution poles and structures,
            whether wood, steel, concrete, or composite.

    (b) INSPECTION METHODOLOGY. — Inspections shall include, at
    minimum:
        (1) Visual inspection by qualified personnel;
        (2) Infrared thermography of all splices, connectors, and
            terminations;
        (3) Non-destructive evaluation of wood pole condition at
            the ground line, including resistograph or sonic
            tomography testing;
        (4) Drone-based inspection of overhead conductors and
            hardware, with high-resolution imagery archived for
            comparison with future inspections.

    (c) CORRECTIVE ACTION. — Conditions identified during
    inspections that could lead to conductor contact, structural
    failure, or equipment ignition shall be corrected within:
        (1) 30 days for conditions rated as presenting an
            immediate fire risk;
        (2) 90 days for conditions rated as presenting an elevated
            fire risk;
        (3) 365 days for conditions rated as presenting a routine
            fire risk.

SEC. 705. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    (a) There is authorized to be appropriated $10 billion for the
    period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this Title,
    allocated as follows:
        (1) $4 billion for composite pole and fire-resistant
            material deployment;
        (2) $4 billion for covered conductor deployment;
        (3) $1 billion for enhanced inspection and maintenance
            programs;
        (4) $1 billion for research, standards development, and
            fire-resistant coating technology.
