    (b) QUARTERLY STATUS REVIEWS. — The Secretary shall conduct
    quarterly program status reviews for each Title and shall
    publish a summary report on the Department's public website
    within 30 days of each quarter-end.

    (c) DELAY CORRECTIVE ACTION. — Any program that has not
    achieved its milestone under subsection (a) within 180 days
    of the specified deadline shall trigger a mandatory review
    by the Secretary, with findings and a corrective action plan
    submitted to the relevant congressional committees within
    90 days.

SEC. 1002. ANNUAL REPORTS TO CONGRESS.

    (a) REPORT CONTENTS. — Not later than March 31 of each year,
    the Secretary shall submit to the Committee on Energy and
    Commerce of the House of Representatives and the Committee on
    Energy and Natural Resources of the Senate an annual report
    on the implementation of this Act, which shall include:

        (1) A summary of expenditures and obligations for each
            program area during the preceding fiscal year;
        (2) Progress toward each milestone identified in section
            1001(a);
        (3) Outcome metrics including, at minimum:
            (A) Miles of line undergrounded;
            (B) Number of DCD devices deployed and ignitions
                prevented;
            (C) Number of weather stations and cameras installed;
            (D) PSPS events, customer-hours of outage, and
                medically vulnerable customers served;
            (E) Megawatts of storage deployed and microgrids
                operational;
            (F) Acres of vegetation managed and miles of covered
                conductor deployed;
            (G) Number of data center facilities in compliance
                with Title VIII requirements;
        (4) An independent evaluation of program effectiveness
            conducted by a qualified third-party organization;
        (5) Recommendations for legislative or administrative
            improvements.

    (b) PUBLIC AVAILABILITY. — Annual reports shall be made
    available to the public on the Department of Energy's website
    within 7 days of submission to Congress.

SEC. 1003. PROGRAM EVALUATION AND SUNSET REVIEW.

    (a) MID-TERM EVALUATION. — Not later than September 30, 2031,
    the Secretary shall contract with the National Academies of
    Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine to conduct an independent
    mid-term evaluation of all programs established under this Act,
    which shall address:
        (1) Program effectiveness in reducing wildfire ignition
            risk from electric infrastructure;
        (2) Cost-effectiveness of each program element relative
            to alternatives;
        (3) Equity of program benefits across communities of
            varying income levels and geographic regions;
        (4) Adequacy of program funding levels to achieve stated
            objectives;
        (5) Recommendations for the second five years of program
            implementation.

    (b) SUNSET REVIEW. — Not later than September 30, 2035, the
    Secretary shall submit to Congress a sunset review assessing
    whether each program authorized under this Act should be
    reauthorized, modified, or terminated, based on:
        (1) Demonstrated effectiveness;
        (2) Continued need as measured by residual wildfire risk;
        (3) Lessons learned from program implementation;
        (4) Stakeholder input from utilities, state regulators,
            fire agencies, and affected communities.

    (c) REAUTHORIZATION. — Programs authorized under this Act
    shall terminate on September 30, 2036, unless reauthorized
    by Act of Congress prior to such date.
