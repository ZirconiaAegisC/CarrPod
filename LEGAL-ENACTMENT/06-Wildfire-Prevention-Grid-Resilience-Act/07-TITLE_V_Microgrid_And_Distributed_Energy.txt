           TITLE V — MICROGRID AND DISTRIBUTED ENERGY
                     RESOURCES FOR RESILIENCE

================================================================================

SEC. 501. COMMUNITY MICROGRID DEVELOPMENT FUND.

    (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is established within the Department of
    Energy a Community Microgrid Development Fund ("the Microgrid Fund")
    to provide grants, loans, and loan guarantees for the planning,
    design, and construction of community microgrids in wildfire-prone
    regions.

    (b) ELIGIBLE PROJECTS. — The Microgrid Fund shall support projects
    that:
        (1) Serve a defined geographic area containing critical
            community facilities including, at minimum, one or more
            of the following: a hospital or medical clinic, a fire
            station, a police station, an emergency shelter, a
            school, a grocery store, or a water or wastewater
            treatment facility;
        (2) Are capable of islanding from the main grid and
            operating autonomously for not less than 72 consecutive
            hours at full design load;
        (3) Incorporate distributed generation from one or more
            sources, including solar photovoltaic, fuel cells, small
            wind, or combined heat and power systems;
        (4) Include battery energy storage sufficient to balance
            generation and load during islanded operation;
        (5) Utilize grid-forming inverter technology capable of
            establishing and maintaining voltage and frequency
            reference during islanded operation without reliance
            on the main grid.

    (c) GRANT AMOUNTS. — Grants under this section shall cover:
        (1) Up to 80 percent of eligible planning and feasibility
            study costs, not to exceed $2 million per project;
        (2) Up to 60 percent of eligible design and construction
            costs, not to exceed $50 million per project.

    (d) TRIBAL AND DISADVANTAGED COMMUNITY SET-ASIDE. — Not less than
    25 percent of Microgrid Fund resources shall be reserved for
    projects located in or serving:
        (1) Federally recognized tribal lands;
        (2) Census tracts where the median household income is below
            65 percent of the state median;
        (3) Communities identified as disadvantaged pursuant to state
            environmental justice screening tools.

SEC. 502. UTILITY-SCALE BATTERY STORAGE FOR RESILIENCE.

    (a) RESILIENCE STORAGE PROGRAM. — There is established a Utility-
    Scale Battery Storage for Resilience Program ("Storage Program")
    to provide federal cost-sharing for the deployment of battery
    energy storage systems (BESS) that enhance grid resilience in
    wildfire-prone regions.

    (b) ELIGIBLE STORAGE SYSTEMS. — The Storage Program shall provide
    cost-sharing for BESS installations that:
        (1) Have a rated capacity of not less than 10 MW and a
            duration of not less than 4 hours at rated power;
        (2) Are sited at substations, distribution switching centers,
            or other grid nodes serving High Fire Threat Districts;
        (3) Are capable of providing backup power to designated
            critical facilities and community resilience hubs during
            grid outages through pre-established islanding schemes;
        (4) Utilize lithium-iron-phosphate (LFP) battery chemistry
            or alternative non-flammable battery chemistry approved
            by the Secretary;
        (5) Comply with NFPA 855 standards for energy storage system
            installation and fire protection.

    (c) FEDERAL COST-SHARE. — The federal cost-share for eligible
    BESS installations shall be 50 percent of total installed cost,
    including engineering, procurement, construction, commissioning,
    and grid interconnection.

    (d) PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS. — BESS installations receiving
    federal cost-sharing shall maintain not less than 95 percent
    availability during wildfire season (June 1 through November 30)
    and shall be tested under islanded operation conditions not less
    than twice per calendar year.

SEC. 503. SOLAR-PLUS-STORAGE FOR CRITICAL FACILITIES.

    (a) PROGRAM. — There is established a Solar-Plus-Storage for
    Critical Facilities Program to provide federal grants for the
    installation of solar photovoltaic generation paired with
    battery storage at critical community facilities in wildfire-
    prone regions.

    (b) ELIGIBLE FACILITIES. — Critical facilities eligible for
    grants under this section include:
        (1) Hospitals and medical clinics;
        (2) Fire stations and emergency operations centers;
        (3) Police stations and public safety facilities;
        (4) Public schools designated as emergency shelters;
        (5) Water and wastewater treatment and pumping facilities;
        (6) Grocery stores and pharmacies in food deserts;
        (7) Community centers serving as cooling or heating centers;
        (8) Cellular communications tower sites serving rural areas.

    (c) SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS. — Solar-plus-storage systems funded
    under this section shall:
        (1) Provide not less than 72 hours of autonomous operation
            for essential facility loads;
        (2) Include automatic transfer switch capability for
            seamless transition between grid-connected and islanded
            modes;
        (3) Comply with applicable National Electrical Code (NEC)
            requirements for energy storage systems.

    (d) GRANT AMOUNT. — Grants shall cover up to 75 percent of
    total installed system cost, not to exceed $5 million per
    facility, with priority given to facilities in census tracts
    where the median household income is below 80 percent of the
    state median.

SEC. 504. GRID-FORMING INVERTER STANDARDS.

    (a) STANDARDS DEVELOPMENT. — The Secretary, in coordination with
    NIST and the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers
    (IEEE), shall, within 18 months of enactment, develop and publish
    mandatory performance standards for grid-forming inverters deployed
    in microgrid and energy storage applications under this Title.

    (b) STANDARD REQUIREMENTS. — Grid-forming inverter standards shall
    address, at minimum:
        (1) Voltage and frequency regulation capability during
            islanded operation;
        (2) Fault current contribution sufficient to operate
            protective devices;
        (3) Black-start capability;
        (4) Seamless transition between grid-following and grid-
            forming modes;
        (5) Cybersecurity requirements;
        (6) Interoperability with utility SCADA and microgrid
            controllers.

SEC. 505. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    (a) There is authorized to be appropriated $40 billion for the
    period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this Title,
    allocated as follows:
        (1) $10 billion for the Community Microgrid Development Fund;
        (2) $20 billion for the Utility-Scale Battery Storage for
            Resilience Program;
        (3) $8 billion for the Solar-Plus-Storage for Critical
            Facilities Program;
        (4) $2 billion for research, standards development, and
            program administration.
