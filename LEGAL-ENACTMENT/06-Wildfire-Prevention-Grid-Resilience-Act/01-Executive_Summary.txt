                         EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

       WILDFIRE PREVENTION AND GRID RESILIENCE ACT OF 2026
                        (WPGRA of 2026)

                     TOTAL AUTHORIZATION:
        $150,000,000,000 OVER TEN FISCAL YEARS (FY2027–FY2036)

================================================================================

I.   PROBLEM STATEMENT

     The United States confronts an escalating wildfire crisis driven by
     climate change, prolonged drought, and aging electric infrastructure.
     Annual wildfire costs to the U.S. economy range from $20 billion to
     $50 billion, encompassing direct fire suppression expenditures,
     property loss, business interruption, health impacts from smoke
     exposure, watershed degradation, and carbon release from burning
     forests. Electric utility infrastructure has been identified as the
     ignition source for multiple catastrophic wildfires, including the
     2018 Camp Fire ($16.5 billion in damages, 85 fatalities), the 2017
     Northern California fire siege ($14 billion in insured losses), the
     2020 Labor Day fires in Oregon and Washington, and the 2021 Dixie
     Fire. In California alone, seven of the twenty most destructive
     wildfires in state history have involved utility equipment as a
     causal or contributing factor.

     The Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E), serving approximately
     16 million people across 70,000 square miles of Northern and Central
     California, operates in the nation's highest wildfire risk territory.
     PG&E's experience—both its bankruptcy following the 2017–2018 fires
     and its subsequent transformation into an industry leader in wildfire
     prevention—provides a comprehensive operational template for national
     wildfire mitigation policy. With $24.9 billion in operating revenue
     for Fiscal Year 2025 and a $73 billion five-year capital investment
     plan, PG&E has demonstrated that systematic infrastructure investment
     can dramatically reduce wildfire ignition risk while maintaining
     reliable electric service.

     PG&E's performance metrics since 2021:
       • 1,241 miles of power lines undergrounded in high-fire-threat
         districts
       • 3rd consecutive year (2023, 2024, 2025) with zero major utility-
         caused wildfires
       • Deployment of 2,000 Downed Conductor Detection (DCD) devices,
         preventing 45 confirmed ignitions
       • Installation of 1,627 weather stations and 706 high-definition
         AI-enabled cameras providing real-time situational awareness
       • Public Safety Power Shutoff (PSPS) events achieving 95% risk
         reduction compared to pre-2020 operations
       • Enhanced Powerline Safety Settings (EPSS) achieving 69%
         reduction in ignitions on enabled circuits

     These results establish that a comprehensive, technology-enabled,
     infrastructure-intensive approach to wildfire prevention is both
     operationally feasible and quantitatively effective. The WPGRA
     codifies PG&E's demonstrated best practices into a national framework
     and provides the federal investment necessary to replicate these
     results across all high-risk utility territories.

II.  PROGRAM OVERVIEW

     The WPGRA establishes a coordinated federal-state-utility partnership
     organized under the Department of Energy (DOE) with designated
     implementation roles for the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission
     (FERC), the U.S. Forest Service (USFS), the Environmental Protection
     Agency (EPA), the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration
     (NOAA), and the Department of the Interior (DOI). The Act authorizes
     $150 billion in total federal expenditures over ten fiscal years
     (FY2027 through FY2036), allocated across eight programmatic titles:

          TITLE I:   Undergrounding ($60 billion) — Federal cost-share
                     for 10,000 miles of high-fire-threat line
                     undergrounding at approximately $3 million per mile,
                     with expedited CEQA and NEPA review.

          TITLE II:  Downed Conductor Detection ($10 billion) — Mandatory
                     deployment of DCD technology on all high-fire-threat
                     distribution and sub-transmission circuits.

          TITLE III: Weather and Sensor Network ($10 billion) — Federal
                     grants for deployment of 10,000 weather stations
                     and HD camera networks with automated detection.

          TITLE IV:  PSPS Reform ($5 billion) — National standards for
                     Public Safety Power Shutoffs, medically vulnerable
                     customer compensation, and backup power grants.

          TITLE V:   Microgrid and Distributed Energy ($40 billion) —
                     Community microgrid development, utility-scale
                     battery storage for resilience hubs, solar-plus-
                     storage for critical facilities.

          TITLE VI:  Vegetation Management ($20 billion) — Enhanced
                     clearance standards, technology-enabled risk
                     mapping, workforce training, prescribed fire
                     acceleration.

          TITLE VII: Transmission Hardening ($10 billion) — Structural
                     upgrades, composite poles, covered conductor
                     deployment, fire-resistant materials.

          TITLE VIII: Data Center Protection ($5 billion) — Grid
                     integration standards, behind-meter storage
                     requirements, demand response mandates for the
                     4.6 GW data center load pipeline.

III. ECONOMIC IMPACT

     The WPGRA is projected to generate approximately 500,000 direct and
     indirect jobs over the ten-year authorization period, with sustained
     employment in utility construction, manufacturing of grid components,
     forestry and vegetation management, sensor and technology production,
     and energy storage installation. The Act is projected to avoid $300
     billion in wildfire-related costs over a twenty-year horizon through
     the prevention of catastrophic fire events, reduction in suppression
     expenditures, preservation of property values, avoidance of business
     interruption, and protection of watershed and air quality resources.

     The WPGRA directly supports the nation's 4.6 GW data center load
     growth pipeline by ensuring reliable, wildfire-resilient electric
     service to critical digital infrastructure, protecting an estimated
     $500 billion in data center capital investment currently planned
     across high-fire-risk regions of the Western United States.

IV.  CONCLUSION

     The Wildfire Prevention and Grid Resilience Act of 2026 represents
     the most comprehensive federal investment in electric grid wildfire
     safety in United States history. The Act draws upon the demonstrated
     operational success of PG&E's multi-layered wildfire mitigation
     program while establishing the oversight, standards, and funding
     necessary to extend these protections to every American community
     served by electric infrastructure in wildfire-prone regions.
