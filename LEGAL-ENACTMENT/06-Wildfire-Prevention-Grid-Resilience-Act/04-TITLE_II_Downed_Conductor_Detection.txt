               TITLE II — DOWNED CONDUCTOR DETECTION
                        AND RAPID SHUTOFF

================================================================================

SEC. 201. MANDATORY DCD DEPLOYMENT.

    (a) DEPLOYMENT REQUIREMENT. — Not later than December 31, 2030, every
    electric utility operating distribution or sub-transmission circuits
    within any area designated as a High Fire Threat District by the
    relevant state regulatory authority shall deploy Downed Conductor
    Detection (DCD) technology on all circuits within such districts.

    (b) DEFINITION OF DCD TECHNOLOGY. — For purposes of this Title, the
    term "Downed Conductor Detection" means an integrated system capable
    of detecting a high-impedance fault or broken conductor condition and
    automatically de-energizing the affected circuit segment within 200
    milliseconds of fault detection, utilizing one or more of the
    following technical approaches:
        (1) Sub-cycle traveling-wave fault detection;
        (2) Voltage and current signature analysis with machine-learning
            classification of fault type;
        (3) Distributed line sensor networks with real-time
            communication to substation protection relays;
        (4) Equivalent or superior technologies as certified by the
            Secretary of Energy.

    (c) PG&E DCD MODEL. — The DCD deployment model developed and
    implemented by PG&E, comprising approximately 2,000 devices installed
    on high-fire-risk circuits as of 2025 and having prevented 45
    confirmed ignitions through automatic de-energization, shall serve
    as the reference implementation for the national DCD program. The
    Secretary shall:
        (1) Publish technical specifications based on PG&E's DCD
            system architecture and performance data;
        (2) Establish a knowledge-transfer program enabling PG&E
            engineering personnel to provide technical assistance to
            deploying utilities.

SEC. 202. FEDERAL PROCUREMENT AND TECHNICAL STANDARDS.

    (a) PERFORMANCE STANDARDS. — Within 270 days of enactment, the
    Secretary, in consultation with the National Institute of Standards
    and Technology (NIST) and the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission
    (FERC), shall promulgate mandatory minimum performance standards
    for DCD systems deployed under this Title, including:
        (1) Maximum fault detection time not to exceed 200 milliseconds;
        (2) Maximum circuit de-energization time not to exceed 500
            milliseconds from fault detection;
        (3) False positive rate not to exceed 5 percent of total
            de-energization events, measured annually;
        (4) Communication latency between field devices and substation
            protection systems not to exceed 50 milliseconds;
        (5) Cybersecurity requirements consistent with NIST Special
            Publication 800-82, Revision 3, or successor guidance;
        (6) Interoperability with utility SCADA, ADMS, and OMS
            platforms through open-standard protocols.

    (b) NATIONAL PROCUREMENT CONTRACT. — The General Services
    Administration (GSA) shall, within 365 days of enactment, negotiate
    and award one or more government-wide acquisition contracts for DCD
    equipment meeting the standards promulgated under subsection (a).
    All federal cost-share funds disbursed for DCD deployment shall
    require procurement of equipment through such GSA contracts, unless
    the Secretary grants a waiver based on specific technical or
    operational requirements of the requesting utility.

    (c) DOMESTIC MANUFACTURING PREFERENCE. — DCD equipment procured
    with federal funds under this Title shall comply with Buy America
    requirements under 49 U.S.C. 5323(j), with the Secretary authorized
    to grant waivers where domestic manufacturing capacity is
    insufficient to meet program demand within required timelines.

SEC. 203. DCD PERFORMANCE METRICS.

    (a) METRICS. — Each utility deploying DCD under this Title shall
    report to the Secretary, on a quarterly basis:
        (1) Total number of DCD devices deployed and operational;
        (2) Circuit miles protected by DCD devices;
        (3) Number of DCD-initiated de-energization events;
        (4) Number of confirmed ignitions prevented, as determined by
            post-event inspection;
        (5) Number of false positive de-energizations;
        (6) Mean time to restoration following DCD-initiated
            de-energization.

    (b) PUBLIC DATABASE. — The Secretary shall maintain a publicly
    accessible database of DCD performance metrics, aggregated by
    utility and by state, updated quarterly, with redaction of
    information that could compromise grid security.

    (c) PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT. — Utilities whose DCD systems fail to
    meet the performance standards established under section 202(a) for
    any two consecutive quarters shall be required to submit a
    corrective action plan to the Secretary within 90 days and shall
    be ineligible for enhanced federal cost-share rates until
    compliance is restored.

SEC. 204. INTEROPERABILITY REQUIREMENTS.

    (a) DATA SHARING. — DCD systems deployed under this Title shall be
    capable of sharing fault-detection data with:
        (1) The deploying utility's Energy Management System (EMS) and
            Distribution Management System (DMS);
        (2) Adjacent utilities' control centers, through secure
            inter-utility communication protocols, for coordinated
            response to multi-circuit fault events;
        (3) State and federal emergency operations centers during
            declared wildfire emergencies.

    (b) COMMUNICATIONS RESILIENCE. — DCD field devices shall be
    equipped with dual-path communications capability, including at
    minimum one terrestrial path (fiber optic or licensed microwave)
    and one satellite or cellular backup path, to ensure continued
    operability during wildfire events that may damage or disable
    primary communications infrastructure.

    (c) EMERGENCY OVERRIDE. — DCD systems shall include a secure
    manual override capability accessible only to authorized utility
    operations personnel, subject to the following conditions:
        (1) Override events shall be logged with timestamp, operator
            identity, and justification;
        (2) Any circuit segment subject to override shall remain in
            Enhanced Powerline Safety Settings (EPSS) mode, with
            sensitivity increased by not less than 50 percent above
            normal operating parameters;
        (3) Aggregate override events shall be reported quarterly
            to the relevant state regulatory authority.

SEC. 205. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    (a) There is authorized to be appropriated $10 billion for the
    period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this Title,
    allocated as follows:
        (1) $7.5 billion for DCD equipment procurement and
            installation cost-share grants to eligible utilities;
        (2) $1.5 billion for research, development, testing, and
            evaluation of next-generation DCD technologies;
        (3) $1.0 billion for program administration, technical
            assistance, and the national DCD performance database.
