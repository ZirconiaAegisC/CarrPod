             TITLE VI — VEGETATION MANAGEMENT AND
                       FUELS REDUCTION

================================================================================

SEC. 601. ENHANCED CLEARANCE STANDARDS.

    (a) MINIMUM CLEARANCE. — Within 365 days of enactment, the
    Secretary of Energy, in consultation with the U.S. Forest Service,
    shall promulgate enhanced minimum vegetation clearance standards
    for electric transmission and distribution rights-of-way in areas
    designated as High Fire Threat Districts. Such standards shall, at
    minimum, require:

        (1) TRANSMISSION LINES (>100 kV). — Minimum clearance of 15
            feet in all directions from energized conductors under
            worst-case sag and sway conditions, including consideration
            of conductor blow-out under the 99th percentile wind speed
            for the location.

        (2) DISTRIBUTION LINES (<100 kV). — Minimum clearance of 10
            feet in all directions from energized conductors under
            worst-case conditions, with 4 feet of clearance from
            communication attachments.

        (3) HAZARD TREES. — Removal of all dead, dying, or diseased
            trees within striking distance of conductors, as
            determined by a certified arborist or professional
            forester, regardless of whether such trees are within
            the utility's easement.

        (4) SURFACE FUELS. — Removal or treatment of surface fuels
            within utility rights-of-way to achieve a fuel loading
            of not more than 3 tons per acre of fine fuels in
            High Fire Threat Districts.

    (b) INSPECTION CYCLE. — Utilities shall conduct patrol
    inspections of vegetation conditions on all high-fire-threat
    circuits at intervals not exceeding:
        (1) 6 months for circuits in Tier 3 (Extreme) High Fire
            Threat Districts;
        (2) 12 months for circuits in Tier 2 (Elevated) High Fire
            Threat Districts.

    (c) PG&E VEGETATION MANAGEMENT REFERENCE. — PG&E's vegetation
    management program, which includes enhanced clearances exceeding
    state minimums and annual patrol inspection of all high-fire-
    threat circuits, shall inform the national standards developed
    under this section.

SEC. 602. TECHNOLOGY-ENABLED RISK MAPPING.

    (a) LIDAR MAPPING PROGRAM. — There is established a LIDAR-Based
    Vegetation Risk Mapping Program to provide federal grants to
    utilities for aerial and ground-based LIDAR scanning of vegetation
    within and adjacent to electric transmission and distribution
    rights-of-way in High Fire Threat Districts.

    (b) MAPPING REQUIREMENTS. — LIDAR data collected under this
    section shall:
        (1) Achieve a minimum point density of 30 points per square
            meter for aerial scans and 500 points per square meter for
            mobile ground-based scans;
        (2) Be collected at intervals not exceeding 24 months;
        (3) Be processed to identify:
            (A) Vegetation encroachment within clearance zones;
            (B) Hazard trees with height exceeding strike distance
                to conductors;
            (C) Surface fuel loading and continuity;
            (D) Terrain slope and aspect affecting fire behavior.

    (c) DATA INTEGRATION. — LIDAR data shall be integrated with:
        (1) Utility GIS and asset management systems;
        (2) The National Wildfire Hazard Potential map maintained by
            the U.S. Forest Service;
        (3) State and local community wildfire protection plans.

    (d) PREDICTIVE ANALYTICS. — The Secretary shall fund research and
    development of predictive vegetation-risk models that combine
    LIDAR data, satellite imagery, weather data, and fire-history
    data to forecast vegetation-related fire risk at a circuit-
    segment level and enable risk-based prioritization of vegetation
    management work.

SEC. 603. WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT AND TRAINING.

    (a) VEGETATION MANAGEMENT WORKFORCE PROGRAM. — There is
    established a Vegetation Management Workforce Development Program
    to provide grants to:
        (1) Community colleges and trade schools for the development
            of utility vegetation management and arboriculture
            training programs;
        (2) Nonprofit organizations and workforce development boards
            for pre-apprenticeship and apprenticeship programs in
            utility vegetation management;
        (3) Tribal governments for forestry workforce development
            on tribal lands within or adjacent to utility rights-of-way.

    (b) CERTIFICATION. — The Secretary, in consultation with the
    International Society of Arboriculture (ISA) and the Utility
    Arborist Association (UAA), shall develop a national Utility
    Vegetation Management Specialist certification program.

    (c) VETERANS PREFERENCE. — The Vegetation Management Workforce
    Development Program shall include a set-aside of not less than 10
    percent of grant funds for programs that recruit and train
    military veterans for utility vegetation management careers.

SEC. 604. PRESCRIBED FIRE ACCELERATION.

    (a) UTILITY RIGHT-OF-WAY PRESCRIBED FIRE PROGRAM. — There is
    established a grant program to support the use of prescribed fire
    by qualified burn practitioners within and adjacent to electric
    utility rights-of-way in High Fire Threat Districts for the
    purpose of:
        (1) Reducing surface and ladder fuel loading;
        (2) Creating and maintaining fuel breaks along utility
            corridors;
        (3) Restoring fire-adapted ecosystem conditions.

    (b) LIABILITY PROTECTION. — Certified prescribed burn managers
    conducting burns under this section in accordance with an
    approved burn plan shall be entitled to liability protection
    equivalent to that provided under applicable state prescribed
    fire statutes.

    (c) COORDINATION. — Prescribed fire projects funded under this
    section shall be coordinated with:
        (1) The U.S. Forest Service and Bureau of Land Management
            for federal lands;
        (2) State forestry agencies for state and private lands;
        (3) Local fire safe councils and prescribed burn
            associations.

SEC. 605. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    (a) There is authorized to be appropriated $20 billion for the
    period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036 to carry out this Title,
    allocated as follows:
        (1) $10 billion for vegetation management federal cost-share;
        (2) $4 billion for LIDAR mapping and predictive analytics;
        (3) $3 billion for workforce development and training;
        (4) $3 billion for prescribed fire acceleration and fuels
            reduction on and adjacent to utility rights-of-way.
