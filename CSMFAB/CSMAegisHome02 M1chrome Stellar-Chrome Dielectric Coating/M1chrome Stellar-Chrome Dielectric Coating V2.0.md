## 1. The Stellar-Chrome Concept
YInMn Blue is the primary color of the Aegis system — but many architectural and automotive applications require a metallic chrome aesthetic. Standard chrome uses electrodeposited metallic chromium — a highly conductive GIC susceptor.

Stellar-Chrome achieves the optical appearance of chrome while maintaining complete dielectric isolation:

**6-Layer Stellar-Chrome Stack:**
1. MAX Phase Ti₃AlC₂ primer (0.5 mm) — machinable base, mechanical adhesion, electrically isolated via PEEK undercoat
2. ZrO₂ base coat (50 μm, flash sintered at 1150°C) — white reflective base
3. MXene Ti₃C₂Tₓ FSS tiles (50 μm, 3×3 cm discontinuous) — 92 dB EMI, metallic sheen
4. SiO₂ optical spacer (200 nm) — constructive interference for silver-metallic color
5. ZrO₂ cap coat (30 nm) — durability, prevents MXene oxidation
6. QD-YInMn Blue topcoat (500 nm) — UV/NIR rejection superimposed on chrome appearance

**Visual result:** Silver-chrome appearance in visible light + 85% NIR rejection + 92 dB EMI SE.

## 2. Thermal Performance Under X-Class Solar Flare
For an X-class flare delivering 1 MW/m² NIR flux: 
- Standard chrome: absorbs 95% = 950 kW/m² → surface vaporization
- Stellar-Chrome: reflects 85% = 850 kW/m² rejected → 150 kW/m² absorbed → 37% heat load reduction vs chrome (37% of chrome's damage)
