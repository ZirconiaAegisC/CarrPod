## Material 9 — Basalt Fiber

**Source:** Natural volcanic basalt rock | **Class:** Inorganic mineral fiber | **Key Property:** Non-conductive, non-magnetic

### 9.1 Physical Properties
- Tensile strength: 3,000–4,840 MPa (manufacturing-grade to premium)
- Elastic modulus: 89–95 GPa
- Density: 2.65–2.80 g/cm³
- Dielectric constant: 6.4–7.2 at 1 MHz — excellent insulator
- Operating temperature: -200°C to +700°C continuous (vs. glass fiber: max 350°C)
- **Electrical resistivity: >10^12 Ω·m — zero GIC coupling**
- Color: dark brown/black (raw fiber); can be coated

### 9.2 How Basalt Fiber is Made

**Raw material:** Any volcanic basaltic rock (SiO2 45–55%, Al2O3 12–17%, CaO 8–11%, MgO 6–10%, FeOx 9–15%)
**Abundance:** Basalt is the **most common rock on Earth's surface** — essentially free

**Manufacturing process:**
1. Mine basalt rock (quarry cost $2–$5/ton)
2. Crush to aggregate ($1–$2/ton processing)
3. Melt in tank furnace at 1350–1450°C (same furnace as glass fiber)
4. Draw through platinum-rhodium bushing tips (200–400 tips per bushing)
5. Apply sizing (coupling agent, typically silane-based)
6. Wind on bobbins

**Critical difference from glass fiber:** No additives (no boron, no rare earths) — just rock.
**This makes basalt fiber potentially the cheapest performance fiber with domestic rock supply.**

### 9.4 In-House Production of Basalt Fiber — HIGHLY FEASIBLE

**The single most impactful in-house production opportunity in the CSMFAB portfolio.**

**Equipment:**
- Basalt rock crusher and grinder: $15,000–$40,000
- Electric resistance tank furnace (1450°C, 2-ton): $85,000–$180,000
- Platinum-rhodium bushings (key component): $40,000–$80,000 per bushing
- Sizing applicator and winding machines: $25,000–$50,000
- **Total capital investment: $165,000–$350,000**

**Raw material costs:**
- Local basalt rock (quarried): $2–$5/ton (essentially free)
- Na2CO3 flux (optional, improves meltability): $0.30/kg
- Silane coupling agent: $3.50/kg (100g per 100 kg fiber)

**In-house cost estimate:**
- Energy: 4 kWh/kg fiber × $0.08/kWh = $0.32/kg

- **Savings: 60–85%**

**Feasibility Rating: 5/5** — same process as glass fiber (proven industrial tech); domestic rock = zero raw material cost. This is the CSM portfolio's crown-jewel in-house production project.

**U.S. domestic basalt sources with verified fiber-quality composition:**
- Columbia River Basalt (Oregon/Washington): excellent Fe, Ti balance
- Snake River Plain (Idaho): high alumina grades preferred
- Hawaii (Mauna Loa): ultra-clean oceanic basalt

## Material 10 — Elium® Thermoplastic Acrylic Resin (Arkema)

**Chemical class:** Liquid thermoplastic acrylic resin (methyl methacrylate base) | **Key property:** Recyclable at end of life

### 10.1 Why Elium® Is Central to the Phoenix Protocol

Standard fiber-reinforced composites use thermoset epoxy or polyester — once cured, irreversible.
Elium® is a **thermoplastic resin that processes identically to thermoset** but can be:
- Dissolved by gentle thermal treatment at 350°C → monomer recovered (100% yield)
- Fiber recovered simultaneously (95% basalt fiber yield)
- Re-polymerized and re-used — **true circular composite manufacturing**

### 10.2 Chemical Composition and Properties

- Base monomer: methyl methacrylate (MMA) — same as Plexiglas
- Additives: initiator, chain transfer agent, rheology modifier
- Liquid viscosity: 100–300 mPa·s (compatible with infusion, VARTM, RTM)
- Cured Tg: 80–110°C (depending on cure profile)
- Tensile modulus (neat resin): 3.2 GPa
- Elongation at break: 6–12%
- Cure time: 30–120 minutes at room temperature (initiated by peroxide or UV)

**Composite mechanical properties (with 60% basalt FVF):**
- Tensile strength: 1,000–1,100 MPa (fiber direction)
- Modulus: 55–65 GPa

### 10.3 Raw Material: Methyl Methacrylate (MMA)

**MMA commercial supply:**
- Rohm (Evonik): Dominant German producer
- Mitsubishi Chemical: Japan
- Trinseo: USA
- **MMA price 2026: $1.80–$2.80/kg (bulk liquid)**

**MMA synthesis:**
1. **ACH (Acetone Cyanohydrin) process** (conventional): Acetone + HCN → methacrylamide → MMA
2. **C2 (Lucite Alpha) process** (modern, lower cost): Ethylene + CO + methanol → methyl propanoate → MMA
3. **Bio-based MMA:** From sugarcane-derived lactic acid or bio-propylene (2025 pilot — Arkema, Mitsubishi)

**Can CSM produce MMA in-house?** Not economically — MMA plant requires $50M+ capital.
**Action: Establish long-term supply agreement with Arkema/Trinseo at volume pricing.**

|---|---|---|

**In-house Elium® formulation from MMA:**
MMA + peroxide initiator (0.5–2%) + chain transfer agent → Elium-equivalent
Equipment: mixing vessel + cold storage; minimal. Cost: **$4–$6/kg** (vs. $8.50–$18 commercial)
**Feasibility Rating: 4/5** — well-documented MMA polymerization; initiator sourced easily.

### 10.5 VARTM Processing of Basalt/Elium®

**Vacuum-Assisted Resin Transfer Molding (VARTM):**
- Dry basalt preform placed in mold
- Vacuum applied; Elium® drawn in under atmospheric pressure
- Single-sided tooling only (open mold sufficient)
- Cure at room temperature: no heated press required
- **Equipment: $15,000–$35,000** (molds, vacuum pump, infusion lines)
- Production rate: 2–8 parts/day per mold (depending on part complexity)

**Products using BFRP/Elium®:** Virtually all structural CSMFAB products (001, 003, 004, 005, 006, 007, 009, 011–014, 019, 024, 028–040, 050, CSMIC 105, CSMMECH 001/003)

## Material 11 — UHMWPE (Ultra-High Molecular Weight Polyethylene) — Dielectric Rope

**Chemical class:** Polyethylene, Mw >3.5 million g/mol | **Brand names:** Dyneema® (DSM), Spectra® (Honeywell)

### 11.1 Physical Properties
- Tensile strength: 3,500–4,250 MPa (fiber, SK99 grade)
- Density: 0.97 g/cm³ (floats on water)
- **Electrical resistivity: >10^14 Ω·m — perfect GIC insulator**
- UV resistance: moderate (requires UV stabilizer)
- Cut resistance: 15× steel at equal weight

### 11.2 Raw Material Source

**Precursor:** Ethylene gas (C2H4) — from petroleum refining or bioethanol dehydration
**Polymerization:** Ziegler-Natta or metallocene catalysis → UHMWPE
**Gel spinning (fiber production):** UHMWPE dissolved in solvent → extruded → solvent extracted → oriented

**Bio-based UHMWPE (emerging, 2025):**
- Sugarcane bioethanol → ethylene → UHMWPE
- Braskem (Brazil) "Green PE" — from 100% sugarcane; carbon-negative
- Price premium: +$1.50–$3.00/kg over petroleum UHMWPE

### 11.3 Commercial Pricing

| Grade | Price/kg (2026) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| UHMWPE granules (bulk) | $4.50–$7.50 | Sheet/rod production |
| Dyneema® SK75 fiber | $18–$28 | Rope, ballistic |
| Dyneema® SK99 fiber | $35–$55 | Highest tenacity |
| Spectra® 1000 | $25–$40 | Honeywell equivalent |
| Braskem Green PE | $7.00–$10.00 | Bio-based granules |

### 11.4 In-House Processing: Rope Braiding

Gel-spinning fiber from UHMWPE granules requires $1–5M specialized equipment — not feasible in-house.
**Action: Purchase Dyneema® SK75/SK99 fiber at volume pricing and braid in-house.**

**Rope braiding equipment:** $12,000–$35,000 (16-carrier horizontal braider)
In-house braiding adds: $1.20–$2.50/m of rope (labor + amortization)
**Total rope cost: $4–$8/m (12mm diameter) vs. $12–$20/m finished product**

**Products:** 017 (rope kit), 019 (forklift rigging), 050 (drone harness)

## Material 12 — Aerogel Systems

**Types used in CSMFAB:**
1. Hydrophobic silica aerogel (baseline, historical)
2. **Polyimide-silica hybrid aerogel (V2.0 specification** — λ=0.010 W/m·K, 650°C service)
3. Cellulose nanocrystal (CNC) aerogel (bio-based, non-toxic, for Embark pet-safe products)

### 12.1 Silica Aerogel — The Original

**Raw materials:**
- Tetraethyl orthosilicate (TEOS) or sodium silicate (water glass): $2–$4/kg
- Methanol or ethanol: $0.60–$1.20/kg
- MTMS (methyltrimethoxysilane): hydrophobizing agent $8–$15/kg
- HF or NH4F catalyst (trace)

**Sol-gel synthesis:**
TEOS + ethanol + water + acid/base → silica alcogel → atmospheric drying (with MTMS) → aerogel

**Commercial aerogel costs:**
- Cabot Enova® particle aerogel: $5–$8/kg (bulk)
- Aspen Aerogels Pyrogel® XT (blanket form): $120–$180/m² (10mm thickness)
- Aspen Aerogels Pyrogel® XT-E (polyimide-silica): $200–$280/m²
- Nano Hi-Tech (China): $35–$65/m² (lower performance)

### 12.2 Polyimide-Silica Hybrid Aerogel — V2.0 Critical Material

**Synthesis route:**
1. Silica sol (TEOS-derived) + polyimide precursors (PMDA-ODA or BPDA-ODA) mixed
2. Gelation → polyimide crosslinks reinforce silica network
3. Supercritical CO2 drying → hybrid aerogel
4. Or: ambient-pressure drying (with progressive solvent exchange) — 70% energy savings

**Performance gains over pure silica aerogel:**
- λ: 0.010 vs. 0.015 W/m·K
- Max service: 650°C vs. 600°C
- Flexural strength: 3-4× (less brittle in handling)

**Commercial hybrid aerogel: $200–$280/m²** (Aspen Aerogels, 2026)

**In-house synthesis feasibility:**
- TEOS: $4/kg (bulk chemical) | Polyimide precursor (PMDA): $25–$40/kg
- Synthesis equipment: gel casting setup, supercritical CO2 dryer
- Supercritical dryer (30L capacity): $35,000–$80,000 — **major capital cost**
- Or: ambient-pressure dryer (modified) with TMCS solvent exchange: $8,000–$15,000

In-house cost estimate: **$55–$85/m²** (30mm blanket) vs. $200–$280/m² commercial
**Savings: 65-75%**
**Feasibility Rating: 3/5** — supercritical dryer is expensive but shared with entire aerogel product line. Ambient-pressure route is more accessible.

### 12.3 CNC (Cellulose Nanocrystal) Aerogel

**Raw material:** Wood pulp, cotton, agricultural waste (any cellulose source)
- Acid hydrolysis with H2SO4 → CNC suspension → freeze-dry or CO2 dry
- Bio-based, non-toxic, carbon-negative
- Performance: λ=0.018 W/m·K (slightly less than silica hybrid); excellent for comfort/pet-safe applications

**Commercial:** $120–$200/m² (limited commercial production; Oak Ridge ORNL technology)
**In-house:** Agricultural waste + H2SO4 treatment + freeze dryer = **$25–$45/m²**
Freeze dryer capital: $15,000–$40,000
**Feasibility Rating: 4/5** — simple processing; agricultural waste is free or nearly free.

**Products using aerogel:** 003, 004, 006, 007, 008, 011, 012, 014, 024, 028, 030, 036, 037

## Material 13 — PVDF-TrFE Piezoelectric Polymer Film

**Full name:** Poly(vinylidene fluoride-trifluoroethylene) | **Class:** Ferroelectric semicrystalline polymer

### 13.1 Physical/Electrical Properties
- d33: -38 to -50 pC/N (longitudinal piezoelectric coefficient)
- d31: +21 to +28 pC/N
- Curie temperature: 55–65°C (PVDF-TrFE-CFE grade for electrocaloric: 80°C)
- Dielectric constant: 8–12 at 1 kHz
- Operating range: -40°C to +100°C

### 13.2 Synthesis and Raw Materials

**Precursors:**
- VDF monomer (vinylidene fluoride): from vinylidene chloride via fluorination or from 1,1,1-trifluoro-2-chloroethane. $3–$5/kg (industrial)
- TrFE monomer (trifluoroethylene): from acetylene + HF; $8–$15/kg
- Optionally: CTFE or HFP for terpolymer grades

**Synthesis:**
Bulk or suspension polymerization in pressure reactor → PVDF-TrFE copolymer → solution cast into film → stretch/pole to align ferroelectric domains

**Commercial suppliers:**
- Piezotech/Arkema (France): $180–$280/kg film resin; $250–$450/m² film
- Measurement Specialties (TE Connectivity): $200–$350/m² film
- Solvay Solef: PVDF base resin $8–$12/kg (not PVDF-TrFE)

### 13.3 In-House Production

Fluorinated monomers require specialized pressure equipment and HF handling — **high safety barrier**.
**Recommendation: Purchase PVDF-TrFE resin from Arkema/Piezotech and cast films in-house.**

Film casting equipment: $25,000–$50,000 (film casting line, stretching frame, poling electrodes)
In-house cost: **$80–$120/m²** (from commercial resin) vs. $250–$450/m² fully commercial film
**Feasibility Rating: 3/5** for film casting in-house (from purchased resin).

**Products:** 004 (electrocaloric cooling), 024 (footwear harvesting), 026 (pedal harvesting), 028, 038, MAG (Bio-Sync)

## Material 14 — h-BN (Hexagonal Boron Nitride)

**Formula:** BN hexagonal | **Class:** 2D van der Waals ceramic / thermal conductor + electrical insulator

### 14.1 Properties
- Thermal conductivity: 250–400 W/m·K (in-plane basal)
- Electrical resistivity: >10^14 Ω·m
- Dielectric strength: 6 kV/mm
- Max service: 900°C in air; 2000°C in inert atmosphere
- Lubricity: similar to graphite (hexagonal structure)

### 14.2 Synthesis

**Routes:**
1. B2O3 + NH3 at 800–1000°C → BN (most common commercial)
2. BCl3 + NH3 → BN (gas-phase CVD)
3. Boric acid + urea → at 800°C → h-BN (simple, low-cost)

**Raw materials:**
- Boric acid (H3BO3): Borax (Na2B4O7) + H2SO4 → H3BO3; $0.80–$1.50/kg
- Urea: nitrogen fertilizer $0.35–$0.60/kg
- Or: NH3 gas $0.25–$0.50/kg

### 14.3 Commercial Pricing

| Grade | Price/kg (2026) |
|---|---|
| Powder (1-5 µm) | $28–$45/kg |
| Nanoplatelets (<200 nm) | $80–$150/kg |
| Film/coating (spray) | $15–$25/m² |

### 14.4 In-House Synthesis

**Boric acid + urea method:**
Equipment: tube furnace (1000°C, NH3 atmosphere available or urea provides N-source)
In-house cost: **$8–$15/kg** from commodity boric acid + urea
**Feasibility Rating: 4/5** — simple synthesis; uses inexpensive commodity precursors.

**Used in:** 013 (wiring insulation dielectric filler), CSMMECH 001 (ceramic-coated wiring)
