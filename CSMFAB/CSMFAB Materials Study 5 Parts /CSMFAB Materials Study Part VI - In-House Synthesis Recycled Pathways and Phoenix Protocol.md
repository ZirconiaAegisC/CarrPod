## Part E — Materials for Specific In-House Production — Step-by-Step Guides

### E.1 ZrB2 Powder via SHS — Detailed Protocol

**Batch size:** 5 kg of ZrB2 powder per run

**Materials:**
- Zirconium metal powder (99.5%, -100 mesh): 4.28 kg → Source: APC Pure Chemicals, $45/kg
- Crystalline boron powder (95%, -325 mesh): 1.30 kg → Source: Noah Technologies, $30/kg
- Argon gas (industrial, 99.99%): 50L cylinder → $35/cylinder
- Graphite susceptor (existing): shared equipment

**Procedure:**
1. Weigh Zr:B in 1:2.05 molar ratio (5% B excess to compensate for B evaporation)
2. Blend in ball mill under argon: 4 hours, 300 rpm
3. Press blended powder into cylindrical pellets at 100 MPa (hydraulic press)
4. Load pellets into argon-purged furnace boat (graphite-lined alumina crucible)
5. Purge furnace 3× with argon
6. Heat to 700°C at 10°C/min — hold 10 min (purge volatile surface oxides)
7. Heat to 1000°C at 15°C/min — SHS ignition typically occurs at 950-1050°C
8. Monitor: exotherm reaches 2200°C for ~30 seconds → reaction completes
9. Cool under argon to <200°C; no cooling rate restriction (product is stable)
10. Remove product; mill to target particle size (3-5 µm for SPS sintering)
11. XRD verification: confirm ZrB2 phase (no residual ZrO2 or B peaks)

**Cost per batch:**
- Zr powder: $45/kg × 4.28 = $192.60
- B powder: $30/kg × 1.30 = $39.00
- Argon: $35
- Milling media wear: $8
- Energy (furnace, 3h): $12
- Labor (4h @ $25/hr): $100
- **Total: $386.60 per 5 kg = $77.32/kg — vs. $280+ commercial**

### E.2 CoAl2O4 — Detailed Protocol

**Batch size:** 2 kg CoAl2O4

**Materials:**
- Co(NO3)2·6H2O: 1,740 g → $18/kg; needed 3.13 kg → $56.34
- Al(NO3)3·9H2O: 6,530 g → $4/kg; needed 6.53 kg → $26.12
- NH4OH (28%): 2L → $3.50/L → $7.00
- Deionized water: 20L (in-house RO)
- NaOH for pH adjustment: 50g → $0.10

**Procedure:**
1. Dissolve Co(NO3)2 and Al(NO3)3 in 10L DI water (molar ratio Co:Al = 1:2)
2. Prepare NH4OH solution (dilute 1:2 with DI water) in separate vessel
3. Add Co-Al nitrate solution dropwise to NH4OH with stirring → blue-green precipitate
4. Maintain pH 9–10 throughout addition
5. Filter precipitate; wash 3× with DI water; filter
6. Dry at 120°C overnight
7. Calcine at 900°C for 4h in air (ceramic furnace — shared)
8. Cool; grind in mortar to pass 100-mesh sieve
9. Color check: deep royal blue (indicates correct CoAl2O4 phase)
10. XRD confirmation if batching critical product

**Cost per batch:**
- Co(NO3)2: $56.34
- Al(NO3)3: $26.12
- NH4OH: $7.00
- Energy, DI water, consumables: $12
- Labor (6h at $25): $150
- **Total: $251.46 per 2 kg = $125.73/kg raw cost + scale-up savings**
At 50-kg batches: **~$35/kg** (vs. $55-100 commercial)

### E.3 Silica Aerogel Blanket — Ambient Pressure Route

**Target:** 1 m² × 10mm blanket, λ = 0.015 W/m·K

**Materials per m²:**
- TEOS (tetraethyl orthosilicate): 380 mL → $3.50/100mL → $13.30
- Ethanol (95%): 1.2 L → $1.20/L → $1.44
- HCl (catalyst, 0.1M): 25 mL → $0.15
- NH4OH (base catalyst, 5%): 25 mL → $0.20
- MTMS (hydrophobizing agent): 35 mL → $8/100mL → $2.80
- HMDSO (surface treatment, optional): 15 mL → $6/100mL → $0.90

**Procedure (simplified ambient-pressure route):**
1. Prepare base TEOS sol: TEOS + ethanol + HCl + DI water → stir 30 min
2. Add NH4OH → gelation occurs in 5-20 min; pour into flat mold (1m × 1m × 12mm)
3. Age gel 24h in sealed mold at 60°C
4. Solvent exchange: replace pore water with ethanol (3×), then hexane (1×)
5. Add MTMS in hexane → surface modification 2h (prevents capillary collapse)
6. Evaporate hexane slowly at 60°C for 12h
7. Final drying at 150°C for 2h
8. Result: flexible, hydrophobic aerogel blanket

**Cost per m² batch:**
- TEOS: $13.30
- Ethanol: $1.44
- MTMS: $2.80
- NH4OH, HCl, other: $1.20
- HMDSO: $0.90
- Energy (oven): $2.50
- Labor (8h @$25): $200 (first run; drops to $45 with experience)
- Mold (amort.): $5
- **First-run cost: ~$227/m²; steady-state: $72/m²** (vs. $120-280/m² commercial)
