## Material 15 — YInMn Blue Pigment (Yttrium Indium Manganese Oxide)

**Formula:** YIn0.7Mn0.3O3 — nominal | **Discovery:** Oregon State University, 2009 (M. Smith / Mas Subramanian)
**Key property:** NIR-reflective "cool" blue; SRI=115; non-toxic; inorganic stability

### 15.1 Why YInMn Blue is the Most Important CSMFAB Surface Material

Used on **35 of 43 CSMFAB products** as primary exterior coating.
Its unique crystal field absorption (Mn³⁺ in trigonal bipyramidal coordination, ~590 nm orange absorption) gives:
- 85-92% NIR reflectance (700–2500 nm)
- SRI=115 (vs. 10-25 for standard dark colors)
- Temperature differential: up to 70°C cooler than dark surfaces under equivalent sun/fire exposure
- Complete chemical stability (inorganic, no UV fade)

### 15.2 Chemical Composition and Precursors

**Synthesis formula (solid-state method):**
Y2O3 + In2O3 + Mn2O3 → YInMnO3 (calcined at 1200-1300°C in air)

**Precursor elements and sources:**

**Yttrium (Y):**
- Rare earth element (see Part V for full supply chain)
- From bastnäsite or monazite ore → rare earth separation → Y2O3
- Commercial Y2O3: $35–$55/kg

**Indium (In):**
- CRITICAL MINERAL — primarily recovered as by-product of zinc smelting
- Zinc ore deposits: China (Yunnan), Canada, South Korea, Peru
- Price extremely volatile: $150–$350/kg (2026); historically $80–$800/kg range
- Annual world production: ~700 metric tons — very limited
- This is the portfolio's single most supply-chain-vulnerable critical mineral

**Manganese (Mn):**
- Abundant: South Africa (#1), Australia, Brazil, Ukraine
- Mn2O3 or MnCO3 precursor: $1.20–$2.50/kg — cheap and abundant!
- No supply risk

### 15.3 YInMn Synthesis Routes

**Route A — Solid-state calcination (current commercial standard):**
Mix Y2O3 + In2O3 + Mn2O3 in stoichiometric ratio → ball-mill blend → press pellets → fire at 1200°C, 12h

**Route B — Hydrothermal synthesis (V2.0 advance, 2025):**
Y(NO3)3 + In(NO3)3 + Mn(NO3)2 dissolved in water → add NaOH → coprecipitate hydroxides → hydrothermal reactor at 250°C, 24h → wash → calcine at 900°C
- Key advantage: lower calcination temperature (900°C vs. 1300°C) → lower energy
- Equipment: autoclave reactor ($8,000–$25,000, 10L capacity)

**Route C — Indium-sparing formulas (V2.0 critical research priority):**
Partial substitution of Ga, Fe, or Al for In → reduces Indium content by 30-60%
Formula: Y(In0.4Ga0.3Mn0.3)O3 → still blue-range, 70-80% NIR reflectance at 40% less In
Formulation under development — potential to reduce In cost sensitivity dramatically

### 15.5 In-House Synthesis — STRONGLY RECOMMENDED

**Equipment for hydrothermal route:**
- Autoclave reaction vessels (2× 10L): $20,000
- Ball mill for precursor mixing: $5,000
- Calcination kiln (1100°C): $12,000 (shared with ceramics program)
- Particle characterization: $8,000

**In-house cost breakdown:**
- Y2O3: $45/kg × 0.52 kg per kg YInMn = $23.40
- In2O3: $200/kg (refined In oxide, not metal) × 0.62 kg per kg YInMn = $124.00
- Mn2O3: $2.50/kg × 0.18 kg = $0.45

**Wait — commercial is CHEAPER currently?** Yes, because Shepherd Color has In supply contracts at $120–$140/kg (vs. spot $200–$350/kg for oxide). Their volume leverage is not yet matchable.

**In-house strategy:** Research indium-sparing formulas aggressively. With 40% less indium:
In-house cost falls to **$85-100/kg** — now competitive with buy price.
**Feasibility Rating: 3/5** for standard formula; **4/5 for indium-sparing R&D version**

- Yttrium also recovered: $38 processing vs. $35–$55/kg virgin Y2O3

## Material 16 — Cobalt Aluminate Spinel (CoAl2O4)

**Formula:** CoAl2O4 | **Color:** Deep blue (complementary to YInMn) | **Class:** Spinel structure oxide

### 16.1 Properties

- SRI: 98 (slightly below YInMn's 115 but still excellent)
- NIR reflectance: 70–80%
- Max service: >1400°C (higher than YInMn's ~1200°C limit)
- Contains no Indium — no critical mineral vulnerability
- Absorbs 1060 nm Nd:YAG wavelength selectively — laser countermeasure function
- Cost: significantly lower than YInMn

### 16.2 Synthesis

**Spinel synthesis (co-precipitation or sol-gel):**
Co(NO3)2 + Al(NO3)3 in aqueous solution → add NH3 → Co-Al hydroxide co-precipitate → calcine at 800-1000°C → CoAl2O4 spinel

**Precursors:**
- Co(NO3)2 or CoSO4: cobalt from DRC/Congo mining; price $28–$45/kg Co metal equivalent
- Al(NO3)3: from alumina $2/kg → nitrate conversion is straightforward
- NH3: commodity

### 16.3 Commercial Pricing

- Standard ceramic grade: $55–$85/kg
- Pigment grade (fine, heat-stable): $70–$100/kg
- Heubach (Germany), Ferro Corp., Shepherd Color all supply

### 16.4 In-House Synthesis

Co-precipitation is simple bench chemistry. Equipment: pH-controlled reactor $5,000, calcination furnace (shared).
In-house cost: **$22–$38/kg** (vs. $55–$100 commercial)
**Feasibility Rating: 5/5** — simplest functional coating synthesis in portfolio.

**Products:** 001, 010, 011, 016, 021 (interior walls/Schumann surfaces), CSMIC 105 cross-arms

## Material 17 — MXene (Ti3C2Tx) Electromagnetic Shielding

**Full chemical name:** Ti3C2Tx where T = surface functional groups (OH, O, F)
**Class:** 2D transition metal carbide | **Discovery:** Drexel University, 2011

### 17.1 The New Crown Jewel of EMI Shielding

SE = 92 dB at 45 µm thickness (2024) — surpasses all other flexible EMI materials.
Used in 18 CSMFAB products; currently 2026's most expensive portfolio material at $2,000/kg.

**But:** Price projected to fall 70-80% by 2028-2030 as production scales.

### 17.2 Synthesis Chemistry

**Step 1 — MAX Phase precursor (Ti3AlC2):** (same as Material 6)
**Step 2 — Selective etching of Al layer:**

Method A (LiF/HCl — minimally intensive fluorine system):
Ti3AlC2 + LiF + HCl (12M) at 35°C for 24h → Ti3C2Tx multilayer clay

Method B (HF etching — original Drexel method):
Ti3AlC2 + concentrated HF (48%) → Ti3C2Tx
*Fluoride waste stream requires neutralization treatment*

Method C (TMAOH — non-fluorine, 2024):**
Ti3AlC2 + tetramethylammonium hydroxide → Ti3C2Tx (emerging; avoids HF)

**Step 3 — Delamination:**
Multilayer clay + sonication in water → single-layer/few-layer MXene colloidal solution

**Step 4 — Spray/filter into film or coating:**
- Sprayed onto substrate: 50-100 µm → 75+ dB SE
- Vacuum-filtered membrane: freestanding film

### 17.3 Raw Materials for MXene Synthesis

- Ti3AlC2 MAX phase: $80–$420/kg commercial (or in-house $35-55/kg — see Material 6)
- LiF: $8–$15/kg (lithium fluoride salt)
- HCl (12M): $0.30/kg bulk hydrochloric acid
- TMAOH (tetramethylammonium hydroxide): $25–$45/kg
- DI water: $0.001/L (in-house RO system)

**From in-house MAX phase at $35–$55/kg:**
Conversion yield: ~80% (Ti3AlC2 → Ti3C2Tx by mass after etching)
Chemical costs per kg MXene: $35–$55 (MAX) + $12 (LiF, HCl) + $8 (delamination) = **$55–$75/kg**

**This is the most transformative in-house opportunity in the portfolio:**
- Current commercial MXene: $1,500–$2,500/kg
- In-house from in-house MAX phase: $55–$75/kg
- **Savings: 96–97%**

### 17.4 Commercial Acquisition (Until In-House Scales)

| Supplier | Form | Price/kg (2026) |
|---|---|---|
| Drexel MXene/Murata partnership | Colloidal suspension | $2,000–$2,500 |
| American Elements | Multilayer powder | $1,500–$2,000 |
| 2D Materials Inc. | Research quality | $1,800–$2,800 |
| Taiyo Yuden / TDK (Japan) | Industrial trial | $800–$1,200 |

### 17.5 MXene Tile Pattern — Minimizing Usage

Key design insight: MXene provides GHz EMI shielding even in **discontinuous tile patterns**.
Islands of 3×3 cm MXene separated by 1mm gaps block GHz frequencies while maintaining GIC immunity.
This design reduces MXene content by **40-60%** vs. continuous coating —directly proportional cost savings.

**Example: CSMFAB 029 Neural-Grip Wheel Wrap:**
- Full coverage: 0.05 kg MXene × $2,000 = $100
- Tile pattern: 0.02 kg × $2,000 = $40
- At in-house price ($65/kg): 0.02 kg = $1.30 — effectively negligible

**Feasibility Rating: 5/5 for in-house (with in-house MAX phase program already running)**

**Products:** 001, 011, 019, 021, 024, 029, 030, 032, 034, 037, 038, 050, CSMMECH 001

## Material 18 — Magnetorheological Fluid (LORD MRF-140CG)

**Type:** Iron particle suspension in hydrocarbon carrier | **Supplier monopoly: LORD Corporation (Parker Hannifin)**

### 18.1 Composition and Properties

- Carbonyl iron particles: 7-10 µm diameter, 80-85 vol.%
- Carrier: hydrocarbon oil (mineral or synthetic)
- Stabilizer: fumed silica, surfactants
- Yield stress: 80 kPa at 250 kA/m (2025 specification)
- Off-state viscosity: 0.28 Pa·s at 40°C
- Operating range: -40°C to +130°C

### 18.2 Making MR Fluid In-House

**Carbonyl iron powder (CIP) synthesis:**
Iron pentacarbonyl (Fe(CO)5) vapor decomposition at 250-300°C → spherical carbonyl iron particles
- Fe(CO)5 source: from iron + CO at high pressure in autoclave
- High-pressure equipment (200 bar CO): $85,000–$150,000
- Safety: CO gas handling requires extreme precautions

**Alternative CIP source:** BASF produces CIP at $8–$15/kg (Carbonyl Iron Powder)
— Purchase CIP from BASF, formulate fluid in-house

**In-house MRF formulation from CIP:**
1. BASF CIP ($12/kg) + hydrocarbon oil ($2/kg) + fumed silica ($4/kg) + dispersant ($8/kg)
2. Mix in high-shear mixer; test rheology
3. Fill into product bladders

### 18.3 Commercial vs. In-House

| Option | Cost/kg | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| LORD MRF-140CG commercial | $250–$300/kg | Certified, guaranteed performance |
| In-house from BASF CIP | $25–$40/kg | Requires rheology qualification |

**In-house savings: 85-90%**
**Feasibility Rating: 4/5** — straightforward formulation; quality matching requires R&D effort.
Critical R&D task: Match LORD's specific particle size distribution (7-10 µm) and surface treatment.

**Products:** 024, 029, 030, 031, 035, MAG (Bio-Sync)

## Material 19 — Shear-Thickening Fluid (STF) — Thorax-Calm Seatbelt

**Composition:** SiO2 nanoparticles (200 nm) in polyethylene glycol (PEG-400)

### 19.1 How STF Works

At rest (low shear): particles dispersed, fluid flows normally (η₀ = 0.8 Pa·s)
At impact (high shear γ̇ > 200 s⁻¹): particles jam in hydroclusters → η rises to 85+ Pa·s
Effect: impact-activated stiffening — seatbelt cover becomes rigid pad in crash

### 19.2 Raw Materials

- **Fumed SiO2 (200 nm):** Cabot Aerosil® 200, or Evonik Aerosil®
  - Commercial: $5–$8/kg
  - Volume (10t/yr): $3.50–$5/kg
  - In-house synthesis (Stöber process from TEOS): **$1.80–$3.00/kg** — saves 40-60%
- **PEG-400 (polyethylene glycol):** commodity chemical $1.20–$2.50/kg
  - Sourced from Dow Chemical, Clariant — no in-house benefit

**STF formulation:**
60 vol.% SiO2 in PEG-400 → sonicate to disperse → fill and seal in TPU capsule

**In-house STF total cost: $12–$18/kg** (vs. no established commercial STF product for this use;
custom-mixed to order)

**Products:** 033 (Thorax-Calm seatbelt), 029 (Neural-Grip inner layer variant)

## Material 20 — WO3 Electrochromic Film (Stellar-Tint Window)

**Formula:** WO3 tungsten trioxide | **Application:** Variable-tint smart window film

### 20.1 Working Principle

WO3 + xLi+ + xe⁻ ↔ LixWO3 (blue-colored, absorbing) + Li+ removal (clear)
- Electrical switching at 1-2 V drives lithium intercalation/deintercalation
- Switching time: <3 seconds (2025 grade)
- Cycle life: 50,000+ cycles (150-year daily use estimate)

### 20.2 Raw Materials

**Tungsten source:**
- Primary mineral: **Scheelite (CaWO4)** or **Wolframite ((Fe,Mn)WO4)**
- Major producers: China (85% world supply), Russia, Canada, Vietnam
- **Supply risk: HIGH** (China dominance)
- WO3 commercial: $35–$55/kg tungsten oxide

**WO3 synthesis:**
- Ammonium paratungstate (APT) → calcine at 600°C in air → WO3
- APT from ore processing → $28–$42/kg

**Alternative: Hydrogen tungsten bronze (HxWO3)** — electrodeposited WO3 film
- Dissolve WO3 in H2O2/H2O → ammonium tungstate solution → spin-coat → anneal

### 20.3 Commercial Window Film Pricing

- WO3 nanoparticle ink: $250–$450/kg
- Roll-to-roll coated film (LiPON/WO3/PEDOT stack): $35–$65/m² (at scale; 2026 emerging market)
- Sage Glass (Saint-Gobain): $35–$100/m² (installed, not just film)

### 20.4 In-House Thin-Film Deposition

WO3 electrochromic layer deposition via:
- Sol-gel spin coating (simplest): dissolve APT in water → coat → bake
- Physical vapor deposition (sputtering): higher quality, more capital

**In-house sol-gel cost: $8–$15/m²** WO3 layer (from APT reagent)
Combined PEDOT:PSS + AgNW transparent conductor: $12–$25/m²
Total Stellar-Tint in-house: **$35–$55/m²** (vs. $60–$180/m² commercial specialty)
**Feasibility Rating: 3/5** — sol-gel coating is accessible; roll-to-roll requires $150K+ equipment.

**Products:** 034 (Stellar-Tint window film)

## Material 21 — PEDOT:PSS Transparent Conductor (Window Film)

**Full name:** Poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene) polystyrene sulfonate
**Function:** ITO-free transparent electrode for electrochromic and EMI shielding applications

### 21.1 Properties and Why It Replaces ITO

- Sheet resistance: 50–500 Ω/□ (controllable by formulation)
- Optical transmittance: 88–95%
- Solution-processable: spray, spin-coat, blade-coat, inkjet
- Acid treatment (H2SO4, formic acid) can reduce Rs to 15 Ω/□ — nearly ITO-equivalent
- Contains no Indium (unlike ITO) — zero critical mineral vulnerability
- Mechanical flexibility: survives 1000 bend cycles at 3mm radius

### 21.2 Synthesis

- EDOT monomer + PSS counter-polymer → oxidative polymerization in water
- Heraeus Clevios™ brand; Ossila Ltd. (research grade)
- Commercial PEDOT:PSS dispersion: $80–$180/kg (at 1-2% solid concentration)
- Solid content basis: $8,000–$18,000/kg film — mitigated by very thin layer usage

### 21.3 AgNW (Silver Nanowire) Enhancement

- AgNW + PEDOT:PSS hybrid achieves: 92% transmittance + 35 dB SE at 1 GHz
- AgNW synthesis: silver nitrate + polyvinylpyrrolidone (PVP) in ethylene glycol at 170°C
  → Ag nanowires 50-200 nm diameter × 5-30 µm length
- Commercial AgNW ink: $800–$2,500/kg (silver content-dependent)
- In-house polyol synthesis: **$120–$250/kg** (from AgNO3 $580/kg × 30% Ag content in wire)

**Products:** 034 (Stellar-Tint), 011 (building window film)
