## 1. Two Threats, One Cover
**Kinetic threat:** High-g crash events (>30g, duration 100 ms) compress the thorax against standard 50 mm nylon webbing, which delivers peak contact pressure of ~250 kPa over the clavicle — causing rib fractures in 28% of belted crash occupants (NHTSA 2024).

**Electromagnetic threat:** Standard seatbelt hardware (metal buckle, metal pre-tensioner housing) forms a conductive loop across the torso. During a GIC event, this loop carries induced current directly against the body.

## 2. Shear-Thickening Fluid Technology
**Composition:** 60 vol% fumed SiO₂ (200 nm particles) in polyethylene glycol (PEG-400)

**Rheology:**
- Zero-shear viscosity (at rest): η₀ = 0.8 Pa·s — flexible, conforming
- Critical shear rate: γ̇_c = 200 s⁻¹
- Post-critical viscosity: η_peak = 85 Pa·s at γ̇ = 10,000 s⁻¹
- Yield stress: τ_y = 50 kPa (2025 measurement, V2.0)

At crash impact (typical γ̇ ≈ 50,000 s⁻¹): STF solidifies in < 0.5 ms, creating a rigid-body contact pad that distributes the webbing load over 3.3× greater surface area.

**Crash pressure distribution:**
- Naked webbing: σ_contact = 250 kPa over 30 cm² → 750 N concentrated
- STF cover activated: σ_contact = 75 kPa over 100 cm² → same 750 N distributed → sub-fracture threshold

## 3. Optical Bio-Feedback (V2.0)
Two FBG sensors sewn into cover fabric:
- Respiratory rate: ±0.5 breath/min accuracy from thoracic expansion detection
- Heart rate: ±2 BPM from aortic pulse pressure wave (detects microstrain in webbing)
- Data via PMMA POF to driver display
- Zero metallic sensing elements — GIC-safe

## 4. Non-Metallic Hardware Replacement
All standard metallic seatbelt hardware replaced:
- Buckle tongue: PEEK CF40 (tensile strength 220 MPa, crash load 15 kN — passes ECE R17)
- Pre-tensioner housing: BFRP composite (no metal trigger mechanism — pyrotechnic retained but ceramic-isolated from frame)
- Height adjuster: Zirconia ceramic slide (no metal rail)
- D-ring: Alumina ceramic, 50 mm bore, rated to 20 kN
