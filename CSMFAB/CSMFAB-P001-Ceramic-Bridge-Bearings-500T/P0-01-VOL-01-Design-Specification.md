## 2. FUNCTION
Replaces conventional steel-laminated elastomeric bridge bearings with a dielectric ceramic assembly that breaks the geomagnetically induced current (GIC) path through steel bridge structures. Prevents magnetostrictive fatigue crack propagation in bridge girders during G5-class geomagnetic storms.

## 4. DIELECTRIC CURRENT-PATH BREAK
Steel bridge bearings create a continuous conductive path between the superstructure and substructure. GIC flows through this path, causing localized heating (I²R), magnetostrictive vibration, and accelerated fatigue crack propagation. The ZTA ceramic bearing replaces the steel-elastomer assembly, achieving an open-circuit resistance >10⁸ Ω·m — functionally infinite at GIC frequencies (DC-0.01 Hz).

## 5. FLASH SINTERING PROCESS
