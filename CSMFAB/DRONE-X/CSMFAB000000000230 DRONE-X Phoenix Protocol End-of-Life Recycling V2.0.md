## 1. Phoenix Protocol Overview

The Phoenix Protocol ensures that every DRONE-X airframe and its constituent materials are recovered, separated, and reintroduced into the manufacturing supply chain at end-of-life. The target recovery rate is 92% by mass — exceeding the EU WEEE Directive target of 85% for electronic equipment.

| Material Class | % of Total Mass | Recovery Method | Recovery Rate |
|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| BFRP (basalt/ELIUM) | 39.7% | Thermosolvolysis → fiber/acrylic separation | 95% |
| Aluminum/copper (motor windings) | 12.3% | Hydrometallurgy (D2EHPA solvent extraction) | 98% |
| Steel (bearings, hardware) | 6.2% | Magnetic separation | 99% |
| Neodymium magnets (N52SH) | 2.1% | Demagnetization + hydrogen decrepitation | 90% |
| Electronic PCBs | 4.8% | Shredding → density separation → smelting | 85% |
| LiPo battery | 15.4% | Cryogenic shred → hydrometallurgical Co/Li recovery | 88% |
| Silicone/polymer misc | 5.5% | Pyrolysis → energy recovery | 70% |
| Ti₃C₂Tₓ MXene coating | 0.4% | Solvent wash → flake recovery | 80% |
| Other (connectors, wire insulation, etc.) | 13.6% | Mixed waste processing | 80% |
| **Weighted Total Recovery** | **100%** | | **92.1%** |

## 2. BFRP Thermosolvolysis Process

The ELIUM acrylic thermoplastic matrix is depolymerized using a low-temperature thermosolvolysis process that preserves basalt fiber integrity:

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Solvent | Methyl methacrylate (MMA) monomer + 5% water |
| Temperature | 180°C |
| Pressure | 0.5 MPa (autoclave) |
| Duration | 4 hours |
| Catalyst | Potassium persulfate (0.1 wt%) |
| Fiber recovery | Continuous basalt fiber, 92% tensile strength retention |
| MMA recovery | 88% by distillation |
| Energy consumption | 4.8 MJ/kg BFRP processed |

Recovered basalt fiber is re-compounded with virgin ELIUM resin at 30:70 recovered:virgin ratio for new DRONE-X frame production.

## 3. D2EHPA Hydrometallurgy for Motor Winding Recovery

T-Motor F2306 stator windings contain high-purity OFHC copper. Recovery uses selective solvent extraction:

| Step | Reagent | Duration | Product |
|------|---------|----------|---------|
| 1. Leach | 2M H₂SO₄, 60°C, 2h | — | CuSO₄ + Al₂(SO₄)₃ solution |
| 2. Extract | 20% D2EHPA in kerosene, pH 2.5 | — | Organic phase: Al³⁺ |
| 3. Strip | 2M H₂SO₄ | — | Al₂(SO₄)₃ for reuse |
| 4. Electrowin | CuSO₄ electrolyte, 2.0V, 200 A/m² | 6h | 99.9% Cu cathode |

## 4. Neodymium Magnet Recovery

N52SH magnets from motors follow the hydrogen decrepitation (HD) process:

| Step | Process | Conditions |
|------|---------|------------|
| Demagnetize | Heat to 350°C (above Curie temp 310°C), slow cool | 2h cycle |
| Hydrogen decrepitation | H₂ atmosphere, 1 atm, room temperature | 4h, magnet self-pulverizes to < 500 µm |
| Dehydrogenation | Vacuum, 600°C | 2h |
| Jet milling | N₂ atmosphere | d₅₀ = 3–5 µm |
| Re-sintering | 1080°C, 1h, Ar atmosphere | New magnet production |

## 5. LiPo Battery Recycling

| Step | Process | Product |
|------|---------|---------|
| 1. Discharge | 0.1C to 0V in salt water bath (48h) | Safe for processing |
| 2. Cryogenic shred | -196°C (LN₂), hammer mill | < 5 mm fragments |
| 3. Magnetic separation | Remove steel casing | Clean Cu/Al foil fraction |
| 4. Leach | 2M H₂SO₄ + 5% H₂O₂, 70°C, 3h | Co²⁺, Ni²⁺, Mn²⁺, Li⁺ solution |
| 5. Precipitation | pH adjustment cascade | Li₂CO₃, Co(OH)₂, Ni(OH)₂, MnCO₃ |

Recovery rates: Co 92%, Ni 90%, Li 85%, Mn 88% — meeting EU Battery Regulation 2023/1542 targets for 2027.
