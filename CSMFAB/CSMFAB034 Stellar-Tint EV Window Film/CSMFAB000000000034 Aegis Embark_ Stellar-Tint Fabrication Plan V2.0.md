## 1. The ITO Problem
Traditional "smart glass" uses Indium Tin Oxide (ITO) as the transparent conductor — but ITO is a metallic conductor (σ ≈ 10⁶ S/m). In a vehicle window frame forming a loop of ~0.8 m × 0.4 m = 0.32 m² aperture, the ITO layer during a GIC event:
$$I_{GIC} = \frac{E_{geo} \times L}{R_{ITO}} = \frac{20 \text{ V/km} \times 0.8}{R_{sheet}}$$

For R_sheet = 10 Ω/□ (typical ITO): I ≈ 1.6 mA circulating in the window — small but measurable, and sufficient to damage electrochromic layers over repeated events.

**V2.0 solution: PEDOT:PSS + AgNW hybrid conductor**
- Sheet resistance: 15 Ω/□ (slightly higher than ITO but sufficient for electrochromism)
- Optical transmittance: 92% (vs 87% for ITO equivalent)
- EMI SE: 35 dB at 1 GHz (AgNW mesh resonance)
- Chemical stability: excellent in humid environments (unlike pure PEDOT:PSS)
- No indium — removes critical mineral supply risk

The Stellar-Tint incorporates a tunable notch at 480 nm using a cholesteric liquid crystal layer:

This makes the Stellar-Tint the first window film that actively manages the driver's circadian health.
