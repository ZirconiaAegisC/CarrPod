## 1. Load Case Summary

The AMSR-UBSCLS spear experiences 5 distinct load cases:

| Load Case | Phase | Duration | Max Load |
|---|---|---|---|
| LC1: Smart Rope Pull | Surface contact | 0.04 s | 50,040 N per rope |
| LC2: Hydrodynamic drag | Underwater ascent | 1-4 s | 4,063 N |
| LC3: Transonic buffet | Mach 0.8-1.2 | 0.5-2 s | Variable, ±1500 N |
| LC4: Second stage firing | Post-apogee | 28 s | 2754 N axial |
| LC5: Centrifugal (spin) | Entire flight | continuous | Blades: 1.44 MPa |

**Critical load case: LC1 — Smart Rope Pull at surface**

### 1.1 LC1 — Smart Rope Pull Analysis

For n=6 vessels, F_rope_max = 50,040 N each, all attached to spear base (aft 50mm).
Total axial force applied to spear: F_total = 6 * 50040 = 300,240 N

Average axial stress at spear cross-section:
```
sigma_axial = F_total / A_cross = 300240 / (pi * (0.04)^2 - pi * (0.036)^2)
            = 300240 / (5.03e-3 - 4.07e-3)
            = 300240 / 9.6e-4 = 312.75 MPa
```

ZrB2-SiC tensile strength: 850 MPa
**Safety factor: 850/313 = 2.72** — adequate for a single-use system.

For hollow shell (outer R=40mm, inner R=36mm, wall t=4mm, L=640mm):

Peak bending stress at attachment point (if ropes not perfectly centered):
Assume 1mm misalignment: e = 0.001 m
Bending moment: M = F_total * e = 300240 * 0.001 = 300 N·m
Bending stress: sigma_bend = M * R / I = 300 * 0.040 / (pi/64 * (0.08^4 - 0.072^4)) = 54.8 MPa

Combined: sigma_total = 313 + 55 = 368 MPa < 850 MPa → **Safety factor 2.31.**

### 1.2 LC2 — Hydrodynamic Drag Loading

Max drag force (underwater at 22 m/s): 4063 N (Vol 03)
This is a compressive axial load on the spear.
ZrB2-SiC compressive strength: 1800 MPa >> any achieved stress.

### 1.3 LC3 — Transonic Buffet

Oscillatory loading at Mach 0.8-1.2 from shock-boundary layer interaction.
Frequency: typically 50-200 Hz (structural resonance risk)

Natural frequency of spear:
```
f_n = 0.561 / L^2 * sqrt(E*I / (rho*A)) = 0.561/0.64^2 * sqrt(250e9*1.1e-6/(6090*9.6e-4))
    = 1.369 * sqrt(2.75e5/5.85) = 1.369 * sqrt(47009) = 297 Hz
```

Buffet frequency range (50-200 Hz) does not overlap with structural natural frequency (297 Hz):
**No resonance risk. KNbO3-BaTiO3 dampers provide additional margin.**

## 2. Fatigue Analysis

For a ceramic material (ZrB2-SiC), cyclic fatigue does not apply in the same way as metals.
Ceramics fail by:
1. **Quasi-static fracture** when stress exceeds fracture toughness locally
2. **Slow crack growth** under sustained load (stress corrosion in water)

**Stress corrosion in seawater:**
ZrB2-SiC: no water-reaction mechanism. Stress corrosion not applicable (inorganic, non-oxide).
**No fatigue limit concern.**

## 3. FEA Summary (Simplified Beam Model)

For Scale 3 (Small, 10 kg) spear with 6-rope pull:

| Location | Axial Stress (MPa) | Shear Stress (MPa) | Safety Factor |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rope attachment (aft) | 313 + 55 = 368 | 8 | 2.31 |
| Mid-span | 85 | 22 | 10.0 |
| Nose cone join | 12 | 15 | 51 |
| Screw blade root | 1.44 | 0.9 | 590 |

**All regions have safety factor > 2.0. The rope attachment zone is limiting.**

**V2.0 design modification:** Thicken rope attachment flange wall from 4mm to 7mm.
New safety factor at attachment: 850 / (312.75 * 4/7) = 4.05 — improved.
