## 1. The Critical Infrastructure GIC Threat

The 1989 Quebec blackout provides the engineering benchmark: a G5 storm destroyed 7 bulk power transformers in 90 seconds when GICs saturated their cores. Each transformer costs $5–10M and takes 12–18 months to manufacture and deliver. The US grid has approximately 2,000 bulk power transformers — replacement stock: ~50 units.

A Carrington-scale repeat would destroy an estimated 300–400 bulk transformers in North America, with recovery time of 4–10 YEARS. This is not hyperbole — it is the NERC (North American Electric Reliability Corporation) assessed scenario.
