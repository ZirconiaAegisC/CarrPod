# 3D Generation Prompt: Centrifugal Speed Governor Assembly
**Carrington Storm Motors / Safe Pod Engineering Company | Version 1.0 | June 2026**

## Component Identity
**Name:** ASB-1 Centrifugal Flyweight Speed Governor
**Function:** Passive mechanical speed limiter. As the spiral body spins faster, three weighted flyweight arms swing outward under centrifugal force, linked by push rods to pitch-adjusting levers on the blade roots. This increases aerodynamic drag proportionally to speed, limiting the maximum spin rate to ~4,500 RPM = ~87 mph tip speed. No electronics required.
**Mass target:** 8g | **Material:** PLA printed (arms + hub) + 3 steel fishing sinker weights (1g each)

## Precise Dimensions
- Hub: 20mm OD × 25mm tall cylinder, 8mm center bore (press-fits onto spiral body shaft), 3 arm pivot boss pairs at 120° spacing
- Arm pivot bosses: 6mm OD × 5mm protruding, bored 1.5mm for pivot pin, at 15mm radius from center
- Arms: 3× 30mm long × 4mm wide × 2mm thick PLA links, pivoting outward from boss
- Flyweight mount: each arm has a 5mm OD × 4mm deep cavity at outer end to press a 5mm diameter × 3mm steel weight ball
- Spring seat: 4mm OD spring seat boss at pivot point (spring inner presses here, outer presses against hub face)
- Push rod attach tab: 2mm × 3mm tab on the outer face of each arm at 5mm from pivot, with 1.5mm hole for push rod pin
- Top plate: 20mm OD × 2mm disc with 3 push rod guide slots (2mm wide, 10mm long radially) — constrains push rods to radial travel
- Pre-tension screw: M2 × 5mm screw in each pivot boss for spring preload adjustment

## 3D Prompt (All-in-One)
Design a centrifugal flyweight governor mechanism for a small spinning device. The assembly has a central cylindrical hub (20mm diameter, 25mm tall, 8mm central bore) with three equally-spaced pivot mounts positioned at 120-degree intervals. Three hinged arms extend outward from these pivots. Each arm is a small link (30mm long) with a heavy metal weight ball seated at its outer end. Small compression springs (barely visible) at each pivot point push the arms inward (closed position). As the hub spins faster, the weighted arms are flung outward by centrifugal force against the spring resistance, pivoting upward and outward in a symmetric fan-like motion. The arms are connected to thin push rods that translate this outward motion to adjust some external mechanism. A top retaining plate with guide slots constrains the push rod travel. Material: light grey PLA with silver metal weight balls at arm tips.
