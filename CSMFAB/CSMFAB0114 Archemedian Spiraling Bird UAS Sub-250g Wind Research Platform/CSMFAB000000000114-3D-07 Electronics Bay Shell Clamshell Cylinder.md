# 3D Generation Prompt: Electronics Bay Shell — Clamshell Cylinder
**Carrington Storm Motors / Safe Pod Engineering Company | Version 1.0 | June 2026**

## Component Identity
**Name:** Electronics Bay Shell
**Function:** Protective outer shell for the electronics bay mid-section of the ASB-1. This is the expansion zone on the hub where the flight controller, GPS, LoRa radio, and battery management live. Opens clamshell-style for access. Includes isolation standoffs to keep electronics away from BFRP walls.
**Mass target:** 5g | **Material:** PETG

- Outer dimensions: 44mm OD maximum × 65mm long cylindrical section
- Inner dimensions: 38mm ID bore (houses all electronics on internal mounting points)
- Wall: 3mm
- Split: Clamshell split along a vertical line — left half and right half, hinged on one side
- Hinge: Living hinge (1mm PETG flexible hinge) on the left; clip-lock on the right (cantilever clip, 2mm wide)
- Cable ports: 4× circular cable entry ports × 6mm OD with removable snap-in grommets, at four positions (top, bottom, left, right) of one end face
- GPS window: 20mm × 20mm opening on the top surface (for GPS antenna sky view)
- Antenna slot: 2mm × 90mm lengthwise slot on right side for LoRa antenna wire exit
- Internal mounting bosses: 4× square 4×4mm pillar bosses at 38mm ID corners for vibration isolator grommet seating (FC mounts to these)
- M2 heat inserts: 4× M2 inserts in mounting bosses; 2× M2 inserts on lid edge for positive lock

## 3D Prompt (All-in-One)
Design a clamshell electronics enclosure for a small UAV computer bay. The enclosure is a hollow cylinder, 44mm outer diameter and 65mm long, split in half lengthwise to create two mirror-image half-shells (like a clam). One side has a flexible living hinge running the full length, connecting the two halves. The opposite side has a snap-clip latch. The inner bore is 38mm in diameter, smooth-walled. On each end face, four circular cable ports (6mm OD with removable grommets) allow wiring to pass in and out. The top end has a square GPS antenna window (20mm × 20mm opening). A thin lengthwise slot (2mm × 90mm) on the outer surface on one side allows the antenna wire to exit. Inside, four small square pillar bosses at the inner wall serve as mounting points for the flight controller circuit board via vibration-isolating rubber grommets. Material: semi-translucent white PETG to show internal components as silhouettes. Render open (clamshell pried apart) showing the interior mounting bosses.
