# 3D Generation Prompt: Central Hub / Body Core — Electronics Housing
**Carrington Storm Motors / Safe Pod Engineering Company | Version 1.0 | June 2026**

## Component Identity
**Name:** ASB-1 Central Hub / Body Core
**Function:** The inner structural spine and electronics housing of the spiral body. While 3D-01 is the full spiral body, this document isolates the hub as a standalone component for design iteration. The hub is effectively the "fuselage" — a hollow cylinder that runs up the center of the spiral, housing the battery and electronics, and connecting to the nose cone and tail assembly.
**Mass target:** 8g (hub shell only, without internals)
**Material:** PETG
**Outer diameter:** 30mm | **Inner diameter:** 26mm | **Height:** 200mm

## Feature Description
A hollow cylinder, 30mm outer diameter, 200mm tall, 2mm wall thickness. At 65mm to 130mm height (measured from base): the wall opens into a larger 42mm OD × 65mm cylinder (the electronics bay) which then closes back to 30mm above 130mm. This creates an hourglass-shaped cross-section when viewed from the side. The electronics bay section has a lengthwise hinge on one side (left) and a clip-lock on the other (right), creating a clamshell lid that opens to access the interior. The bottom 20mm of the hub is a socket receiver for the tail assembly (4× M2 heat inserts at 0°, 90°, 180°, 270° positions). The top 15mm is a socket that accepts the nose cone. An 8mm bore runs through the entire centerline for the governor shaft. GPS module window: a 16mm × 16mm × 1mm-deep recess on the top exterior surface of the electronics bay for GPS module antenna mounting.

## 3D Prompt (All-in-One)
Generate a hollow cylindrical drone fuselage body, 30mm outer diameter, 200mm tall, 2mm wall thickness, made of white matte PETG. At the midpoint (65mm to 130mm from base), the cylinder expands to 42mm outer diameter creating a bulging equipment bay. This equipment bay section has a full-length vertical hinge on one side and a spring-loaded clip latch on the opposite side, creating a clamshell access door. The top of the body tapers into a 25mm press-fit socket for a pointed nose cone. The base terminates in a 30mm receiver socket with four threaded M2 holes at 90-degree intervals. A precision 8mm diameter bore runs through the entire vertical center of the body. A rectangular GPS window recess (16×16×1mm) appears centered on the top of the equipment bay exterior. All surfaces smooth and printable without support except the internal bore. Export as STL for FDM 3D printing.
