## The Problem We Solve (3 minutes)

**Key points:**
1. Atmospheric wind data above 50 feet and below 400 feet (the low-altitude research gap) is poorly measured by existing instruments. Weather stations measure at one point. Weather balloons are single-use ($50 each). Research drones fight the wind rather than use it.

2. Small UAS operators (delivery companies, emergency responders, film crews) need accurate low-altitude wind data to plan safe missions. Currently, they estimate or guess.

3. A swarm of 10-20 ASB-1 units launched simultaneously builds a 3D wind map that no single instrument can match, at under $2,000 total hardware cost (vs. $50,000+ for equivalent research tower arrays).

## The Science (3 minutes)

**Key points:**
1. The Archimedes spiral is not science fiction — it was invented 2,200 years ago. We are applying 2025-2026 computational aerodynamics research (Nature Scientific Reports, arXiv:2506.10223) showing the spiral geometry is 17% more thrust-efficient and dramatically quieter than conventional propellers.

2. The 3-turn design (confirmed optimal for our size and wind range) achieves a Power Coefficient of 0.264 — comparable to the best small wind turbines, meaning it extracts a lot of energy from the wind.

3. Gyroscopic stability means it is self-steering into the wind — no complex control algorithms needed.
