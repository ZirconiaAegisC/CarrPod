## Introduction: ASB-1 Is the Beginning

The ASB-1 is the proof of concept. Everything designed in this series — the spiral geometry, the non-inductive materials, the LoRa swarm mesh, the tether safety system — scales. This volume describes the development roadmap for versions 2 through 5.

---

## ASB-1 (Current) — University Prototype

- Mass: 177g
- Material: PETG 3D print
- Actuation: SMA wire Smart Rope
- Altitude: up to 400 ft standard / 1,200 ft waiver
- Endurance: 90 min passive
- Swarm: up to 20 units with waiver
- Purpose: Mechatronics certificate project; research proof of concept

---

## ASB-2 — Enhanced Research Platform

**Target: Year 1 post-certificate**

Key upgrades:
- BFRP Markforged printed body (540 MPa vs. 52 MPa) — enables thinner walls (0.8mm), lighter body (~40g vs 60g)
- Solar CIGS patch on spiral surface (2.5g addition; indefinite passive endurance in sun)
- SAM-M10Q GPS (3g vs 8g, saves 5g)
- LiDAR distance sensor for altitude-above-terrain measurement
- Remote ID module (Dronetag Mini, 8g) for future-proofing
- Total mass target: 175g (nearly same, better capability)
- Upgrade cost: ~$50 per unit conversion

---

## ASB-3 — Extended Altitude Platform

**Target: Year 2**

Key upgrades:
- 400m extended tether reel (500m UHMWPE, 1.9g filament mass)
- Second battery bay (2× LiFePO₄ 2S 300mAh in parallel, 70g total — uses 35g of margin from reduced body mass)
- Upgraded FC with dual-antenna GPS for better accuracy at high altitude
- CO₂ / CH₄ gas sensor (electrochemical, 3g) for atmospheric chemistry research
- Maximum altitude: 1,200 ft AGL (§107.51 waiver maintained)
- Purpose: Atmospheric chemistry boundary layer profiling

---

## ASB-4 — Commercial Research Version

**Target: Year 3**

Key upgrades:
- Production BFRP injection-molded body (faster, cheaper per unit: $35 vs. $60 printed)
- Integrated Remote ID + ADS-B in receiver (full situational awareness)
- Swarm up to 50 units (new §107.35 waiver application)
- Weather-hardened electronics (IP54 rated bay, silicone sealed)
- Ultrasonic anemometer (direct wind measurement; no rotating probe; better accuracy)
- University licensing program: sell data subscriptions to weather services, aviation planners
- Target per-unit cost at volume (50 units): $120

---

## ASB-5 — Balloon-Hybrid Platform

**Target: Year 4-5**

Inspired by the TF-G250 autogyro (Czech sub-250g atmospheric profiler reaching 3.2 km via balloon):

- ASB-1 spiral body as autogyro rotor attached to a 0.3m³ helium balloon tether
- Balloon provides buoyancy to 3,200m AGL (32 km if balloon-launched per TF-G250 precedent)
- Spiral body autorotates in upper-atmosphere winds, collecting data on descent
- Single-use balloon launch ($15 helium balloon) + reusable ASB spiral sensor pod
- Compete with $50/sonde weather balloon market with reusable sensor: $50 balloon + $120 pod vs. $50 single-use radiosonde with no retrieval
- Major meteorological network opportunity
