# 3D Generation Prompt: Catch Net Tensioning Frame
**Carrington Storm Motors / Safe Pod Engineering Company | Version 1.0 | June 2026**

## Component Identity
**Name:** ASB-1 Recovery Net Frame System
**Function:** Octagonal frame that supports and tensions the polypropylene catch net for recovering descending ASB-1 units. Breaks down into portable components for field transport.
**Mass target:** ~600g (entirely ground support equipment, not on aircraft)
**Material:** 6061-T6 aluminum tubes (arms), 3D-printed BFRP hub and stake sockets

## Hub Dimensions
- Center hub ring: 80mm OD × 40mm tall, 8 arm sockets at 45° spacing, each 10mm OD × 20mm deep
- Hub body: 3D-printed BFRP, 3mm wall
- Net attachment hooks: 8× 3mm OD eyelet hooks on top rim of hub, 45° spacing

## Arm Dimensions
- 8 arms: each 400mm × 9.5mm OD × 1.5mm wall 6061-T6 aluminum tube
- One end: press-fits into hub socket (matching ID)
- Other end: 3D-printed BFRP stake socket (12mm OD × 15mm long) press-fit onto arm tip

## Stake Socket and Ground Stake
- Stake socket: 12mm OD with 6mm ID bore for AL ground stake
- Ground stake: 6mm OD × 150mm 6061-T6 rod, driven into ground; stake socket slides over stake top

## Net Geometry
- Polypropylene knotless mesh net: 15mm mesh squares, octagonal shape, 1200mm tip-to-tip span
- Net edges attach to arm tips via small SS hooks through mesh border cord
- Center of net is lowest point (funnel shape formed by downward arm angle: 15° below horizontal)

## 3D Prompt (All-in-One)
Design a portable octagonal drone safety catch net and frame system. The frame has a central hub ring (80mm diameter, 40mm tall) with eight arm sockets radiating outward at 45-degree intervals. Into these sockets, eight identical aluminum tube arms (400mm long, 10mm diameter) click into place. The arms all angle slightly downward (about 15 degrees below horizontal) creating a shallow dish or bowl shape when the net is attached. At each arm tip, a small plastic ground-stake socket accepts a pointed metal stake driven into the ground, holding the frame level and rigid. The entire frame spans 1.2 meters tip-to-tip when assembled. An octagonal polypropylene mesh net is strung between the arm tips, forming a gentle concave bowl — deepest at the center, shallower at the edges. The center of the net should be visually lowest, like a shallow trampoline aimed at the sky. Render the full assembled system with the net visible as a see-through wireframe mesh. Show the ground stakes anchoring the outer corners. Show a small drone silhouette descending toward the center of the net from above.
