## Introduction: Closing the Material Loop

Every Carrington Storm Motors product includes a Phoenix Protocol — a plan for end-of-life material recovery and recycling. The ASB-1 is a research device that will eventually be retired or damaged. This protocol ensures it does not become waste.

## Part 1: Material Recovery Plan

| Component | End-of-Life Recovery | Method | Recovery Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| PETG/BFRP spiral body | Mechanical grinding → filament extrusion feedstock | Comminution + filament recycling program | 80% material recovery |
| LiFePO₄ battery | Battery recycling program | Take-back to hobbyshop; Call2Recycle nationwide | >95% Li, Fe, P recovery |
| Electronic components | Circuit board recycling | E-waste drop-off; Cypress University e-waste program | 60-70% Au, Cu, Ag recovery |
| UHMWPE tether | Cannot be chemically recycled (cross-linked polyethylene) | Cut into fishing line segments → donate to local fishing club | Reuse |
| Nitinol SMA wire | Specialty metals recovery | Send to Dynalloy — they accept returned wire | NiTi reclaimed |
| Copper wiring | Copper recycling (high value) | Strip insulation; copper recycler | 99% Cu recovery |
| Servo and motor | Disassembly + component reclaiming | Reuse motor/servo if functional | Variable |

## Part 2: Field Leave-No-Trace Policy

All ASB-1 research operations at natural sites (cliffs, coastal areas, canyon) follow a strict Leave No Trace policy:

1. **No anchor in ecologically sensitive soil** — use rock anchors with webbing loops if no soil staking
2. **Retrieve all tether line** — no filament left in field (UHMWPE monofilament is hazardous to wildlife if ingested)
3. **Collect all deployment materials** — used breakaway couplings (monofilament loops), heat shrink scraps, zip ties
4. **If parachute deploys:** retrieve canopy and lines within 30 minutes — parachute material can entangle wildlife
5. **Battery waste:** carries off-site to proper disposal (never dispose of LiFePO₄ in field trash)
6. **Post-mission:** photograph the site before departure confirming no equipment remains
