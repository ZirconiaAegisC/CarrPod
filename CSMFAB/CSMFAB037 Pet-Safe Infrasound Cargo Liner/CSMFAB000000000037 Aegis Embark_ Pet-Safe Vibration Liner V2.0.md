## 1. Pet Vestibular Biology — 2025 Data
**Canine vestibular system sensitivity (JVIM 2025 study, n=87 dogs):**
- Peak sensitivity for kinetosis (motion sickness): 0.3–1.5 Hz (vs 0.1–0.3 Hz human)
- Onset of vestibular symptoms: > 0.4 m/s² at 0.8 Hz (lower than human threshold)
- Chronic anxiety response: > 0.2 m/s² at 2–4 Hz cumulative daily exposure

**Feline vestibular sensitivity:**
- Peak kinetosis band: 0.5–2.0 Hz
- Lower compliance than canines: feline HPA axis activates at lower vibration threshold
- Inner ear anatomy: larger semicircular canal relative to body size = higher angular sensitivity

The cargo liner's primary job is surgical attenuation of 0.1–2 Hz kineto-frequencies while preserving 7.83 Hz (therapeutic) and blocking harmful GIC electromagnetic exposure.

## 2. Two-DOF Kinetosis Attenuation Model
The liner base acts as a 2-DOF vibration isolation system:
- Mass 1: liner structure (0.8 kg)
- Mass 2: pet body (4–40 kg range)
- Stiffness k₁ (liner-to-floor): cellulose aerogel, 0.6 MPa modulus → k₁ = 24 kN/m
- Stiffness k₂ (liner surface-to-pet): memory foam top layer → k₂ = 8 kN/m

Natural frequency of isolation system: f_n = 1.2 Hz — below the 1.5–2 Hz sensitive band of both species, providing isolation (T < 1) across the entire kinetosis-relevant range.
