## TELLURIC CURRENT PROBLEM
Oil and gas pipelines span thousands of kilometers, creating long electrical conductors that are perfectly aligned for geomagnetic induction during GMD events. Telluric currents enter the pipeline through the cathodic protection system and exit through coating holidays (defects), causing accelerated corrosion at exit points. The P0-08 joint breaks the pipeline into electrically isolated segments ≤50km each, limiting telluric current magnitude to non-damaging levels (<1 A/m² at coating holidays).

## PIPELINE SEGMENTATION STRATEGY
- Install P0-08 joints at compressor stations (every 80-120km), river crossings, and jurisdictional boundaries
- Each segment independently monitored via FEATHER node with pipe-to-soil potential measurement
- Cathodic protection rectifiers remain operational within each isolated segment
- US pipeline network: ~2.6 million miles. Priority: 50,000 miles of high-consequence area pipelines. Joints needed: ~1,000 (at 50-mile intervals). Revenue: $12.5M.
