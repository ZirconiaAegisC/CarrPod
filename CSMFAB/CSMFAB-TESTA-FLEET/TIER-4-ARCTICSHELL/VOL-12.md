# ArcticShell Extreme Container VOL-12: Hyper-Detailed Fabrication Specification
## CSMFAB-TESTA-FLEET | TIER-4-ARCTICSHELL | Tardigrade-Structural-Pivot Architecture

Manufacturing process for ArcticShell Extreme Container. Panel fabrication: VARTM (Vacuum-Assisted Resin Transfer Molding). VARTM panel layup (24-ply), CNC trim, MXene spray, corner assembly with MRF thermal dampers, PCM battery integration, geothermal loop installation, heated seal wiring, triple-glazed window fitting, enthalpy recovery ventilator calibration.. Quality control: 100% dimensional inspection via CMM (±0.5mm). Dielectric hipot test at 50kV DC (<1μA leakage). Insulation resistance >100MΩ at 1kV DC. Load test: 500T vertical per ISO 1496-1 + 200T lateral. Thermal cycle: -40°C/4hr → +85°C/4hr, 10 cycles. Salt spray: ASTM B117, 2,000 hours — no corrosion (ceramic/BFRP immune). Water spray: 50kPa for 1 hour, no interior ingress. UV exposure: ASTM G154, 5,000 hours — no degradation. Production line: Station 1 (panel VARTM, 8hr cure), Station 2 (CNC trim, 1hr), Station 3 (MXene spray, 30min), Station 4 (corner assembly, 2hr), Station 5 (door/gasket install, 1hr), Station 6 (QC + serialization, 30min). Total: 13 hours per container. Throughput: 4 containers/day (single shift), 8/day (double). Annual: 1,000 (single), 2,000 (double). Workforce: 24 technicians + 4 engineers + 2 QC inspectors. Floor space: 2,400 m². Tardigrade curl integration: MRF dampers installed at corner posts during Station 4. Preload verification: torque wrench check at 450 N·m ±5%, recorded in CSMTest database with serial number link. Each damper individually characterized (force-velocity curve, 0-800 mm/s range, 0.5% accuracy).
