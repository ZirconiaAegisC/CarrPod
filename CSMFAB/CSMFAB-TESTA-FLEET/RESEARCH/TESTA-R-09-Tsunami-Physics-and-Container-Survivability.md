# TESTA-R-09: Tsunami Physics and Container Survivability
## CSMFAB-TESTA-FLEET | Deep Research | July 2026

Mathematical analysis of container survival in tsunami impact. Variables: wave height (H), velocity (v = √gH), impact pressure (P = ½ρv²Cd), container buoyancy (Fb = ρgV), anchoring force (Fa). For 170m wave: v = 40.8 m/s, P = 1.67 MPa, Fb = 265 kN (27 tonnes). AegisC container compressive strength 850 MPa — 509× safety factor on compression. Anchoring: 4× helical screws at 75 kN each = 300 kN total. Safety factor: 1.13 on buoyancy, 509 on compression. Container survives — floats tethered if anchors fail, self-rights via active ballast. Interior: 8-person capsule with 3-axis gimbal keeps occupants oriented regardless of container orientation.
