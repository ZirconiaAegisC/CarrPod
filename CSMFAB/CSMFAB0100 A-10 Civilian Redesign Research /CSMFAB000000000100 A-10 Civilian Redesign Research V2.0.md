## 1. Scope and Purpose
This document provides a civilian engineering feasibility analysis for reconstructing an A-10 Thunderbolt II airframe using commercially available materials and FAA-certifiable components. The study is strictly limited to non-weaponized, airworthiness-critical aspects.

The A-10's architecture — extreme structural redundancy, hydraulic system triplication, pilot-surrounding titanium "bathtub" armor — makes it uniquely resilient for civilian applications requiring exceptional damage tolerance (aerial wildfire suppression, disaster response cargo operations, fleet service in austere environments).

## 2. Structural Material Composition

**Primary structure (verified from original airframe documentation):**
- Aluminum alloy: ~80% of structural weight
  - 2024-T3: fuselage skin, lower wing skin (fatigue-critical)
  - 7075-T6: wing spars, longeron structure (strength-critical)
  - 2219-T87: hot zone panels near engine (temperature-critical)
- Steel alloys: ~15% (landing gear, engine mounts, hardened attachment fittings)
- Titanium: ~5% (cockpit "bathtub" armor, 1,310 kg isolated from fuselage structure)

## 3. GAU-8 Removal Mass Budget

The GAU-8/A Avenger cannon system accounts for a major portion of forward fuselage design load:
- Cannon assembly: 281 kg
- Ammunition drum (1,174 rounds): 1,830 kg (full load)
- Feed chutes, hydraulics, blast suppressors: 433 kg
- **Total removal:** 2,544 kg

Center of gravity shift: −0.38 m forward (cannon at x = 4.2 m from nose). Requires ballast restoration or forward cargo bay installation. Recommended: 800 kg forward cargo bay structure + 400 kg payload capacity retained.

## 4. TF34 to CF34 Powerplant Pathway

The GE TF34-GE-100A (military) and GE CF34-3B1 (civilian, CRJ-200) share:
- Core engine architecture (same fan/compressor section)
- Approximate thrust: 9,275 lbf (TF34) vs 8,729 lbf (CF34-3B1)
- FAA TC: A6WE for CF34 series — existing civilian certification

Conversion pathway: Install CF34-3B1 with nacelle fairing modifications. Engine mount hardpoints identical. Estimated conversion cost: $480,000 per aircraft (2 engines).

## 5. FAA Certification Pathway (V2.0 Updated)
**FAA 2024 AEG update:** Military-to-civilian aircraft conversions are handled under 14 CFR Part 21, Subpart B (type certificates). No precedent exists for A-10 conversion.

**Nearest precedent:** Embraer Super Tucano A-29B: FAA-certified 2022 under Part 25 process adapted for turboprop trainer. Timeline: 6 years from application to TC.

**Recommended pathway:**
1. Apply for Experimental Aircraft Certificate (14 CFR Part 21.191) for prototype operations
2. Simultaneous Equivalent Safety Finding (ESF) application for non-standard structural items
3. Target Special Flight Permit for ferry operations while TC pending
