6. Fabrication and Assembly Plan

The manufacturing process is designed for scalability, precision, and adherence to the "Dielectric Citadel" philosophy.

6.1 Injection Molding and Additive Manufacturing

The complex geometry of the labyrinthine unit cells requires advanced fabrication techniques.

  • Process: Fused Granular Fabrication (FGF) or high-precision Injection Molding.29
  • Feedstock: The pre-compounded pellets of Recycled Polypropylene (rPP) mixed with 40 wt% Magnetite ($Fe_3O_4$) and Iron Oxide Red pigment.
  • Tooling: For injection molding, the labyrinthine channels require complex tooling with collapsible cores or slide-action mechanisms to release the folded internal structures. The high thermal conductivity of the magnetite filler 12 aids in reducing cycle times by dissipating heat faster than pure polymer.

6.2 Assembly Protocol

  1. Unit Cell Aggregation: The 50x50mm unit cells are molded as a single, modular grid sized to the specific vehicle door cavity. The grid includes snap-fit interfaces for modularity.
  2. Fractal Integration: The 7.83 Hz Fractal Antenna is applied to the interior (cabin-facing) surface. This is achieved by printing the fractal pattern using conductive silver/carbon ink directly onto a thin PET film, which is then heat-laminated or in-mold labeled (IML) onto the metamaterial block.23
  3. Encapsulation: The entire assembly is encapsulated in a thin Aerogel-doped film (the Aegis-C skin) to provide thermal insulation and the final "Dull Cherry Red" surface finish. This seals the resonators and protects the antenna.

6.3 Fastening and Repair: The Non-Conductive Mandate

To maintain the integrity of the "Dielectric Citadel" 1, the assembly must avoid creating conductive paths.

  • Fasteners: The panel is mounted using Recyclable Non-Conductive Fasteners made of Nylon 6/6 or PEEK.30 Metal screws are strictly prohibited as they act as antennas for GICs and compromise the EMI shielding.
  • Snap-Fit Modularity: The fasteners utilize a Poka-Yoke snap-fit design 32, ensuring correct installation and allowing for easy disassembly.
  • Repair Strategy: The modular grid design allows for "cartridge" replacement. If a section of the door is impacted, the damaged metamaterial tiles can be unsnapped and replaced individually, without discarding the entire door card.

6.4 Recyclability and Circular Economy

  • Monolith Materiality: The device relies on a single thermoplastic matrix (PP).
  • Magnetic Separation: At the end of life, the part is ground down. The Magnetite filler allows the material to be easily separated from other automotive plastics using magnetic drum separators.33 The recovered magnetite-polymer blend can be re-extruded directly into new Silence-Blocks without loss of acoustic performance.
