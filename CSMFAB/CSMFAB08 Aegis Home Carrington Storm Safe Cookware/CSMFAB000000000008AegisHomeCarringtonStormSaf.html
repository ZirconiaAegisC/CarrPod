3.1 Granite: The False Idol

Granite is often cited for its hardness, but thermodynamically, it is flawed for this application.

  • Mineralogy: Granite is a "polymineralic" igneous rock, a chaotic mix of Quartz ($SiO_2$), Feldspar, and Mica.
  • The Quartz Inversion Trap: At $573^{\circ}C$, quartz undergoes a displacive transformation from $\alpha$-quartz to $\beta$-quartz, expanding its volume by ~0.45% instantly.2 While our oven temp ($250^{\circ}C$) is lower, the differential expansion between quartz ($CTE \sim 14 \times 10^{-6} K^{-1}$) and feldspar ($CTE \sim 4 \times 10^{-6} K^{-1}$) creates internal tension.
  • Thermal Shock Failure: When a hot granite slab is placed on a cold counter, or cold water is spilled on it, these internal stresses amplify. Research confirms that granite develops micro-cracks after repeated thermal cycling and can disintegrate.2 A warming tray that shatters is a liability.

3.2 Steatite (Soapstone): The Ancient Battery

Soapstone is the chosen material for the Aegis Home. It is a metamorphic rock composed primarily of Talc ($Mg_3Si_4O_{10}(OH)_2$) and Magnesite.

  • The Talc Matrix: Talc is soft and platy. It acts as a "solid lubricant" within the stone, allowing the matrix to absorb thermal expansion without brittle failure.4 It is virtually immune to thermal shock. You can take it from a $1000^{\circ}C$ kiln and throw it in snow; it will not crack.
  • Specific Heat Capacity ($c_p$): Soapstone has a specific heat of $\sim 0.98 \text{ J/g}\cdot\text{K}$, significantly higher than granite ($\sim 0.79 \text{ J/g}\cdot\text{K}$).2 It holds 20% more energy per kilogram.
  • Thermal Conductivity: At $6-12 \text{ W/m}\cdot\text{K}$, it absorbs heat rapidly in the oven.4 A granite block might take 45 minutes to "charge"; a soapstone block takes 15.
  • Emissivity: Soapstone acts as a near-perfect blackbody radiator ($\epsilon \approx 0.95$) in the Long-Wave Infrared (LWIR) spectrum. This heat penetrates food deeply and gently, keeping it hot without scorching the bottom.4

Decision: The core of CSMFAB000000000008 is Brazilian Architectural Soapstone. It is the only material that satisfies the "Ancient Hearth" durability requirements.
