## 3. GIGAFACTORY NETWORK — DETAILED FACILITY ANALYSIS

### Gigafactory 1 — Sparks, Nevada
- **Location:** Electric Avenue, Storey County, Nevada (Tahoe Reno Industrial Center)
- **Current Footprint:** ~1.9 million sq ft operational, 5.3 million sq ft at completion
- **Products:** 2170 cells, 4680 cells, battery packs, drive units, Powerwall, Megapack
- **Electrical Load:** ~180 MW base, 350 MW peak (estimated)
- **Grid Connection:** NV Energy (Berkshire Hathaway Energy subsidiary), 345 kV and 120 kV transmission lines
- **Transformer Substation:** Multiple MVA-class transformers, 345/120/34.5 kV step-down
- **Key Vulnerability:** Long east-west transmission corridor across Nevada desert; high soil resistivity in basin-range geology increases GIC coupling; single point of grid interconnection
- **Annual Battery Production:** 37 GWh (2025 estimate), expanding to 100 GWh at full buildout

### Gigafactory 3 — Shanghai, China
- **Location:** Lingang Industrial Zone, Pudong, Shanghai
- **Footprint:** ~860,000 sq ft
- **Products:** Model 3, Model Y (for Asian and European markets)
- **Electrical Load:** ~120 MW base, 220 MW peak
- **Grid Connection:** State Grid Corporation of China, 220 kV
- **Key Vulnerability:** China's State Grid has limited public GIC monitoring data; Shanghai is at geomagnetic latitude where GIC coupling is lower than higher-latitude Nevada — but China's longer north-south transmission corridors (1,000+ km) increase total line-integrated induced voltage
- **Annual Production:** ~950,000 vehicles (2025)

### Gigafactory 4 — Berlin-Brandenburg, Germany
- **Location:** Grünheide, Brandenburg, Germany
- **Footprint:** ~740,000 sq ft (Phase 1)
- **Products:** Model Y (European market)
- **Electrical Load:** ~90 MW base, 180 MW peak
- **Grid Connection:** 50Hertz Transmission GmbH (subsidiary of Elia Group), 380 kV grid
- **Key Vulnerability:** Berlin is at 52.5 degrees geomagnetic latitude — substantially higher GIC exposure than Nevada or Texas. Europe's interconnected synchronous grid (ENTSO-E) means GIC in Sweden or Norway can propagate through the continental grid. The 2003 Halloween solar storm caused transformer damage in Sweden and South Africa, and voltage disturbances across the European grid.
- **Annual Production:** ~400,000 vehicles (2025 estimate)

### Gigafactory 5 — Austin, Texas
- **Location:** 1 Tesla Road, Travis County, Texas (also houses Tesla HQ)
- **Footprint:** ~10 million sq ft at completion
- **Products:** Model Y, Cybertruck, 4680 cells, Tesla Semi planned
- **Electrical Load:** ~200 MW base, 420 MW peak (estimated)
- **Grid Connection:** ERCOT (Electric Reliability Council of Texas), 345 kV
- **Key Vulnerability:** ERCOT's isolated grid (not interconnected with Eastern or Western Interconnection) has weaker frequency response during disturbances — but also isolates Texas from GIC propagating from northern latitudes. The tradeoff is that ERCOT has fewer inertial resources and must rely on faster-responding protection. Texas's sedimentary basin geology provides moderate soil conductivity, reducing GIC coupling relative to igneous terrain.
- **Annual Production:** 4680 cells at scale; 250,000+ vehicles (2025)

### Gigafactory 6 — Monterrey, Mexico (under construction)
- **Location:** Santa Catarina, Nuevo León, Mexico
- **Geomagnetic Latitude:** ~25°N — lowest GIC exposure of any Tesla facility. Protection investment here is lower priority; focus on standard CEMEX-level construction hardening.

## 5. INSURANCE AND RISK MANAGEMENT CONTEXT

Tesla's 2025 annual report disclosed material climate-related risks including physical risks to manufacturing facilities. Tesla self-insures a significant portion of its property and business interruption exposure through its captive insurance subsidiary. The company has not publicly disclosed specific GMD/GIC coverage in its insurance program. FM Global provides property insurance for key manufacturing facilities; their 2024 GMD Risk Note rated transformer GIC damage as a "high-severity, credible scenario" requiring site-specific mitigation.

## 6. REGULATORY AND COMPLIANCE CONTEXT

**NERC Reliability Standard TPL-007-4:** Requires transmission planners to assess system performance during benchmark GMD events. Though Tesla is not a transmission operator, the utilities feeding its facilities (NV Energy, ERCOT, State Grid China, 50Hertz) are required to comply. Tesla can leverage this regulatory framework to advocate for utility-side GIC protection while implementing facility-side hardening independently.

**Germany's IT-Sicherheitsgesetz 2.0 (IT Security Act):** Classifies energy sector as critical infrastructure (KRITIS). Tesla's GF4 Berlin, as an industrial consumer of >100 MW, qualifies for KRITIS attention. German federal guidelines recommend GMD preparedness for critical infrastructure operators.

**China's 2022 National Emergency Response Plan:** Includes provisions for space weather events affecting critical infrastructure. Tesla's GF3 Shanghai operations are within scope of this directive.
