# Everspin Technologies: ACS Dossier — Executive Summary

## Company Identity

Everspin Technologies, Inc. (Nasdaq: MRAM) is the world's leading developer
and manufacturer of Magnetoresistive Random Access Memory (MRAM) products.
Headquartered in Chandler, Arizona, the company was founded in 2008 as a
spin-out from Freescale Semiconductor (now NXP). Everspin has been publicly
traded on the Nasdaq since October 2016.

The company operates a dedicated 200mm MRAM fabrication line alongside its
fabless foundry relationships, most notably with TSMC for advanced nodes.
Everspin's technology portfolio spans both Toggle MRAM and Spin-Transfer
Torque MRAM (STT-MRAM), making it the only commercial supplier offering both
architectures at scale.

## Leadership

Sanjeev Aggarwal serves as President and Chief Executive Officer, having
joined Everspin in 2019. Prior to Everspin, Aggarwal held executive roles at
Cypress Semiconductor and Spansion. Bill Cooper serves as Chief Financial
Officer, bringing deep semiconductor financial management experience from
prior tenures at public technology companies.

## Business Performance

Fiscal year 2025 marked a watershed for Everspin: the company achieved its
first full year of GAAP profitability with net income of $5.2 million on
revenue of $55.2 million, representing approximately 10% year-over-year
growth. Gross margin expanded to 51.2%, reflecting favorable product mix
shift toward higher-value STT-MRAM and high-reliability parts.

Q1 2026 continued the momentum: $14.9 million in revenue (+14% YoY) with
MRAM product sales of $14.1 million (+28% YoY). Gross margin improved
further to 52.7%. The company guided Q2 2026 at $15.5–16.5 million.

## Financial Position

Everspin ended 2025 with $44.5 million in cash and equivalents, declining
to $40.5 million at Q1 2026 close as the company invested in capacity
expansion and advanced product development programs. The balance sheet
carries no long-term debt, providing strategic flexibility.

## Strategic Framework

This dossier is organized across five analytical dimensions:

1. **Toggle MRAM Technology & Defense Applications** — The foundational
   technology that established Everspin's credibility, supported by
   significant U.S. Department of Defense contracts.

2. **STT-MRAM Product Portfolio & Data Center** — Next-generation
   architecture for high-density, high-performance persistent memory in
   enterprise and cloud data centers.

3. **Persist High-Reliability & LEO Satellite Demand** — Specialized
   industrial and embedded products addressing the growing low-Earth-orbit
   satellite constellation market.

4. **Unisys NOR Flash Replacement & $3B TAM** — The company's strategic
   initiative to displace NOR flash memory in a large addressable market.

5. **Financial Growth & $100M Revenue Target** — Mapping the trajectory
   from $55M to a stated goal of $100M annual revenue within 3–5 years.

## Customer Traction

Everspin serves over 1,405 end customers across multiple verticals. In
2025, the company logged 238 design wins, up from 178 in the prior period,
indicating accelerating market adoption and customer confidence in the
technology roadmap.

## Intellectual Property

With over 700 active patents, Everspin maintains one of the industry's
strongest MRAM intellectual property portfolios. This moat protects the
company's technology position across Toggle MRAM, STT-MRAM, and future
perpendicular MRAM architectures.

## Forward View

Everspin is positioned at the intersection of multiple secular growth
trends: persistent memory displacing volatile DRAM in edge computing,
radiation-hardened non-volatile storage for aerospace, NOR flash
replacement in embedded systems, and defense modernization programs. The
company's dual foundry-plus-captive-fab strategy provides both production
scale and process control critical for high-reliability end markets.
