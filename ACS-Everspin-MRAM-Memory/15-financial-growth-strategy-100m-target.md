# Financial Growth Strategy & $100M Revenue Target

## The $100M Milestone

Everspin management has articulated a target of reaching $100 million
in annual revenue within 3–5 years from the current baseline of
approximately $55–60 million. This target implies a compound annual
growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 14–18%, a trajectory that is
ambitious for a small-cap semiconductor company but within the range
achieved by successful technology product-cycle-driven semis.

Achieving $100M would represent a fundamental transformation of
Everspin's financial profile: sufficient scale to absorb public
company costs ($6–8M annually for audit, legal, insurance, and
board expenses), invest adequately in R&D to maintain the technology
lead, and generate meaningful free cash flow for the first time.

## Growth Vectors

The $100M target depends on contributions from four primary growth
vectors:

### 1. Data Center STT-MRAM Scaling (~$15–20M incremental)

The existing IBM FCM relationship provides a base of recurring
revenue that should grow as FCM5 ramps and the number of FlashSystem
arrays in the installed base increases. Additional data center
OEMs who adopt STT-MRAM for similar persistent memory applications
could roughly double this revenue stream.

Key catalysts:
- Design wins with additional enterprise storage OEMs
- Transition to higher-density STT-MRAM (2Gb, 4Gb) increasing revenue
  per socket
- CXL-attached memory module designs win adoption

### 2. Persist / High-Reliability Industrial Scaling (~$10–15M incremental)

The Persist EMxxLX family targets the fastest-growing embedded
memory segment: high-reliability applications that value endurance
and persistence but do not require the extreme environmental
qualification of defense and space.

Key catalysts:
- 128Mb and 256Mb qualification completion drives new design
  activity
- LEO satellite constellation production orders provide volume
  step-functions
- Factory automation and energy infrastructure customers convert
  from sampling to production orders
- Known Good Die (KGD) sales for MCM/SiP integration accelerate

### 3. Toggle MRAM Defense Revenue (~$5–10M incremental)

The $40M defense subcontract and $14.6M facility sustainment contract
provide revenue visibility at approximately $15–22M per year over
the contract periods. These figures include both product revenue and
engineering services (NRE). Additional defense programs that specify
Toggle MRAM for new platform designs could add incremental revenue
beyond the contracted base.

Key catalysts:
- Additional defense prime contractors adopt Toggle MRAM for new
  avionics, radar, and EW systems
- Toggle MRAM specified as a standard part on DoD preferred parts
  lists (reducing qualification burden for new designs)
- International defense sales to allied nations

### 4. Unisys NOR Flash Replacement (~$5–15M, back-end weighted)

Unisys represents the highest-upside, highest-uncertainty growth
vector. Near-term revenue (2026–2027) from early adopters will be
small; the revenue curve should steepen in 2028–2029 as production
design wins convert to volume orders.

Key catalysts:
- First production design wins ship in volume (target: H2 2027)
- Major MCU platform vendor (STMicro, NXP, Renesas, TI) validates
  Unisys in a reference design
- Automotive qualification (AEC-Q100) opens the largest NOR flash
  end market
- Cost reduction as TSMC volumes increase

A plausible revenue bridge over a 4-year horizon:

|----------------------------|---------------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Toggle MRAM (commercial)    | 20            | 19     | 18     | 17     | 16     |

| STT-MRAM DDR4 (data center) | 15            | 18     | 22     | 26     | 30     |

Note: Figures in $M, indicative only, not company guidance. Toggle

## Gross Margin Evolution

As the product mix shifts toward higher-margin STT-MRAM, Persist, and
Unisys, the company's blended gross margin is expected to rise from
the current 51–53% range toward the 55–58% range over the 4-year
horizon, driven by:

- Persist and Unisys products carrying gross margins above 55% due
  to their premium pricing relative to commodity NOR flash
- Manufacturing yield improvements on the 28nm STT-MRAM process as
  cumulative wafer volume increases
- Defense products commanding higher ASPs and gross margins than
  commercial equivalents

At $100M revenue and 55% gross margin, Everspin would generate $55M
in gross profit, providing substantial resources for R&D investment
and operating profit.

## Operating Expense Framework

Everspin's operating expenses have been approximately $23–25M
annually in recent periods. To support the product roadmap — Unisys
development, Persist qualification, Toggle process sustainment, and
next-generation MTJ R&D — operating expenses are likely to increase
to the $28–32M range over 3–5 years. Key drivers:

- R&D headcount to support multiple concurrent product development
  programs and foundry engineering at TSMC
- Sales and applications engineering headcount to support a growing
  customer base (1,405+ customers today, expanding with Unisys)
- Increased IP portfolio management costs as the patent portfolio
  grows beyond 700+ active patents

Even with increased operating expenses, at $100M revenue and 55%
gross margin, Everspin's operating profit would be approximately
$23–27M, representing a 23–27% operating margin — substantially
above the breakeven-to-low-single-digit margins of recent years.

## Free Cash Flow Inflection

Historically, Everspin has been approximately breakeven or modestly
cash-flow negative as the company invested in STT-MRAM development
and qualification. Achieving the $100M revenue target would position
the company for its first year of meaningful positive free cash flow.

Working capital requirements — primarily inventory of wafers and
finished goods — scale approximately linearly with revenue, and these
increases absorb some of the operating profit in the growth phase.
However, with TSMC providing manufacturing scale and the Chandler fab
requiring only modest capital expenditure for sustainment (largely
funded by the DoD facility contract), Everspin's capital intensity
is relatively low for a semiconductor manufacturer (estimated 3–5%
of revenue in capex beyond the DoD-funded portion).

## Capital Allocation Priorities

Management has indicated that as the company generates positive free
cash flow, capital allocation priorities will be:

1. Organic R&D investment to maintain MRAM technology leadership
2. Strategic partnerships (foundry relationships, licensing agreements
   for second-source manufacturing)
3. Potential M&A for complementary technologies (e.g., memory
   controller IP, radiation-hardened design techniques)
4. Shareholder returns (buybacks or dividends) are not anticipated
   in the near term given the growth investment opportunities

## Risks to the $100M Target

- **Technology adoption risk**: If Unisys fails to gain traction as
  a NOR flash replacement, the revenue bridge loses $12M from the
  Year 4 projection.
- **Customer concentration risk**: IBM represents a significant
  portion of data center STT-MRAM revenue. Loss or reduction of
  the IBM relationship would create a revenue gap.
- **Competition**: Larger memory manufacturers (Samsung, SK Hynix,
  Micron) have STT-MRAM development programs. If any of them
  commercialize a competitive product, Everspin's first-mover
  advantage could erode.
- **Foundry dependency**: Everspin's STT-MRAM production is
  concentrated at TSMC. Any supply disruption, capacity allocation
  issue, or pricing change at TSMC would directly impact Everspin's
  ability to ship product.
- **Macroeconomic risk**: A broad semiconductor downcycle could
  delay customer adoption of new memory technologies as OEMs
  prioritize cost reduction over performance improvement.
