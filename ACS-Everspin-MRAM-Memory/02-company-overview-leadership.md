# Everspin Technologies: Company Overview & Leadership

## Corporate Profile

Everspin Technologies, Inc. is a Delaware-incorporated semiconductor
company headquartered at 5670 W. Chandler Boulevard, Suite 100, Chandler,
Arizona 85226. The company's common stock trades on the Nasdaq Capital
Market under the ticker symbol "MRAM." As of Q1 2026, the company has
approximately 100 employees dedicated to design, manufacturing, sales,
and research and development of magnetoresistive memory solutions.

## Business Mission

Everspin's core mission is to develop, manufacture, and sell MRAM
products that fill the gap between volatile memory technologies (DRAM,
SRAM) and traditional non-volatile memory (NAND, NOR flash, EEPROM). MRAM
offers the persistence of flash memory with the speed and endurance of
DRAM, enabling new system architectures where data integrity during power
loss is essential.

## Chief Executive Officer: Sanjeev Aggarwal

Sanjeev Aggarwal assumed the role of CEO in September 2019, succeeding
Kevin Conley. Prior to Everspin, Aggarwal was CEO of a venture-backed
semiconductor company and previously served as Executive Vice President
of the Data Communications Division at Cypress Semiconductor, where he
managed a $400M+ business unit. His earlier tenure includes senior
leadership roles at Spansion and AMD. Aggarwal holds a B.S. in
Electronics Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT)
and an M.B.A. from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business.

Under Aggarwal's leadership, Everspin has sharpened its strategic focus
on high-value industrial, aerospace, and data center markets, executed
the transition from Toggle MRAM to STT-MRAM as the primary growth
platform, and guided the company to its first profitable fiscal year.

## Chief Financial Officer: Bill Cooper

Bill Cooper serves as Chief Financial Officer. His career spans over
25 years of financial leadership at public and private semiconductor
companies including positions at Microchip Technology and ON
Semiconductor. Cooper holds a B.S. in Accounting from Arizona State
University and is a Certified Public Accountant. His financial
discipline has been instrumental in maintaining Everspin's debt-free
balance sheet while funding R&D and manufacturing expansion.

## Board of Directors

Everspin's board comprises industry veterans with deep semiconductor
and memory technology experience. Key independent directors include
individuals with backgrounds at major memory and semiconductor firms,
providing governance oversight on technology roadmap decisions,
capital allocation, and strategic partnerships including the TSMC
foundry relationship and defense contract negotiations.

## Manufacturing Strategy

Everspin operates a hybrid manufacturing model combining captive
fabrication with foundry partnerships. The company's Chandler facility
houses its 200mm Toggle MRAM fabrication line and serves as the
engineering and process development center for all MRAM technologies.
For advanced-node STT-MRAM production, Everspin maintains a foundry
services agreement with Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company
(TSMC), leveraging TSMC's 28nm and 16nm FinFET process nodes. The
company also holds a foundry services agreement with Microchip
Technology for complementary manufacturing capabilities.

This dual approach allows Everspin to scale production while retaining
proprietary process knowledge and meeting the rigorous qualification
requirements of defense and aerospace customers who mandate
trusted-foundry sourcing.

## Chandler Facility

The Chandler, Arizona facility serves as corporate headquarters,
primary R&D center, and Toggle MRAM production line. The site includes
a Class 100 cleanroom for wafer fabrication, test and qualification
laboratories, and the engineering teams responsible for magnetic tunnel
junction (MTJ) process development. In 2025, a $14.6 million Department
of Defense contract was awarded for MRAM facility sustainment at this
location, reinforcing its role in the trusted domestic semiconductor
supply chain.

## Organizational Structure

Everspin organizes around three principal functions: Technology
Development (R&D and foundry engineering), Operations (manufacturing,
quality, supply chain), and Sales & Marketing (customer engagement,
applications engineering, business development). The technology team is
structured by product architecture — Toggle MRAM engineering, STT-MRAM
design, and advanced node development — reflecting the distinct process
and design challenges of each memory technology.

## Corporate Values

Everspin emphasizes reliability, innovation, and customer partnership.
The company's products frequently serve mission-critical applications
where failure is not an option — satellites in orbit, industrial safety
systems, defense avionics, and enterprise storage controllers. This
orientation toward high-reliability end markets shapes every aspect of
the company's engineering culture, from radiation-hardened-by-design
techniques to rigorous qualification testing protocols exceeding
industry standards.

## Publicly Available Information

Everspin files periodic reports with the U.S. Securities and Exchange
Commission including Forms 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K. Quarterly earnings
calls are publicly accessible. The investor relations section of
everspin.com provides access to presentations, press releases, and SEC
filings. The company typically reports Q4 and full-year results in
March, Q1 in May, Q2 in August, and Q3 in November of each calendar
year.
