## 2.0 Segment Revenue Decomposition

### 2.1 Performance Coatings — Record $5.5 Billion

| Sub-Segment | Estimated FY2025 Revenue | YoY Growth | Key Driver |
|-------------|-------------------------|-----------|-----------|
| Aerospace Coatings | ~$1.2–1.4B | Double-digit organic | $315M backlog; narrowbody/widebody build rates; MRO |
| Protective & Marine | ~$1.8–2.0B | Mid-to-high single-digit volume | 12 consecutive quarters volume growth; Asia Pacific marine |
| Automotive Refinish | ~$2.0–2.2B | Modest growth | Insurance-claims normalization; LINQ/MOONWALK share gains |
| Traffic Solutions | ~$0.3–0.5B | Stable | Infrastructure spending; reclassified from Industrial Q1 2026 |

Performance Coatings achieved record segment revenue in FY2025. Aerospace coatings registered double-digit organic growth, protective & marine posted its 12th consecutive quarter of volume expansion, and automotive refinish benefited from insurance-claims frequency normalization after pandemic-era driving-pattern disruptions.

### 2.2 Industrial Coatings — $6.5 Billion

| Sub-Segment | Estimated FY2025 Revenue | YoY Growth | Key Driver |
|-------------|-------------------------|-----------|-----------|
| Automotive OEM | ~$3.0–3.5B | Flat to modest | Mixed global LVP; share gains in China, India, Mexico |
| Industrial (general, coil, wood) | ~$2.0–2.3B | Low single-digit | Non-residential construction; powder-coating penetration |
| Packaging Coatings | ~$1.0–1.2B | Double-digit | BPA-to-non-BPA conversion; 20%+ two-year stacked |
| Traffic Solutions | Reclassified Q1 2026 | — | Transferred to Performance Coatings |

Packaging coatings is the standout growth engine within Industrial Coatings, with double-digit growth and a cumulative 20%+ two-year stacked trajectory. Automotive OEM revenues reflect the net effect of macroeconomic and tariff overhangs on global production volumes partially offset by emerging-market share gains and electrification-driven reformulation activity.

### 2.3 Global Architectural Coatings — $3.8 Billion

| Channel / Region | Estimated FY2025 Revenue | Trend |
|-----------------|-------------------------|-------|
| North America DIY | ~$0.9–1.1B | Soft; post-pandemic normalization |
| North America Professional | ~$1.0–1.2B | Growth; contractor channel strength |
| Latin America (Mexico, Brazil) | ~$0.6–0.8B | Strong growth, especially Mexico |
| Europe, Middle East, Africa | ~$0.5–0.7B | Mixed; macroeconomic headwinds |

Mexico architectural coatings have exhibited notable strength, supported by favorable residential and commercial construction market dynamics and distributor network expansion. The COMEX brand (acquired 2014) remains a leading architectural-coatings franchise in Mexico.

## 3.0 Geographic Revenue Mix

| Geography | Estimated FY2025 Revenue Share |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| United States & Canada | ~40–42% |
| Europe, Middle East & Africa | ~28–30% |
| Asia Pacific | ~18–20% |
| Latin America | ~10–12% |

PPG reports that approximately 60% of revenue is generated outside the United States, consistent with its position as a truly global coatings enterprise. Currency-translation effects (primarily USD strength against the euro, Chinese yuan, and certain emerging-market currencies) represent an ongoing 1–2% annual headwind to reported revenue growth.

## 4.0 Organic Growth Composition

PPG's +2% FY2025 organic growth decomposes approximately as:

| Component | Contribution |
|-----------|-------------|
| Price realization | +1–2% |
| Volume/mix | +0–1% |
| Net organic | +2% |

Pricing actions implemented across all three segments during FY2023–2024 to offset raw-material inflation have generally held. The trajectory has shifted from inflation-recovery pricing to a more normalized competitive-pricing environment, with volume/mix becoming the primary growth driver.

## 5.0 Critical Observations

1. **Performance Coatings margin premium:** The segment's technology intensity and service bundling generate structurally higher margins than Industrial or Architectural
2. **Packaging secular growth:** The BPA-to-non-BPA transition is a decade-scale conversion opportunity largely independent of macroeconomic cycles
3. **Aerospace visibility:** The $315M backlog and long-cycle OEM contracts provide multi-year revenue visibility unusual for a coatings business
4. **Architectural channel mix shift:** The professional-contractor channel is structurally outgrowing DIY, favoring PPG's owned-store and independent-dealer distribution model over big-box retail dependency
