## 1.0 Research Methodology

This memorandum synthesizes information from the following source categories:

1. **Primary Corporate Disclosures:** PPG Industries FY2025 Annual Report (Form 10-K), Q1 2026 Quarterly Report (Form 10-Q), earnings releases (FY2025 full-year and Q1 2026), investor presentations, and SEC filings (8-K, Proxy Statement)

3. **Technical Literature:** Product data sheets, application guides, qualification documentation, safety data sheets (SDS), and regulatory submissions for key product lines (AEROCRON, DESOPRIME, INNOVEL, HOBA, PSX, SIGMAGLIDE, STEELGUARD, ENVIROBASE, CERAMICLEAR, LINQ, MOONWALK)
4. **Industry Data:** Trade-association publications (American Coatings Association, NACE International, IMO, SAE International), market-research reports, and competitor financial disclosures
5. **Regulatory Sources:** European Chemicals Agency (ECHA), US Environmental Protection Agency, US Food and Drug Administration, European Food Safety Authority, International Maritime Organization

## 2.0 Key Findings

### 2.1 PPG's Competitive Position

PPG Industries is the world's second-largest coatings and specialty-materials company by revenue (behind Sherwin-Williams at ~$23 billion), with a more diversified end-market and geographic portfolio than any competitor. The enterprise participates across virtually every economically significant coatings end-use, providing portfolio resilience through economic cycles.

### 2.2 Technology Leadership Vectors

PPG's technology differentiation is most pronounced in:

- **Aerospace:** Chromate-free pretreatment and primer systems; $315M backlog indicating qualification-embedded demand
- **Protective Coatings:** PSX polysiloxane technology providing isocyanate-free durability with 30+ year field-performance data
- **Marine Coatings:** SIGMAGLIDE biocide-free fouling-release with documented 5–9% fuel savings; compliant with IMO CII/EEXI framework
- **Packaging Coatings:** INNOVEL/HOBA non-BPA platforms with multi-year qualification lead over competitors
- **Automotive Refinish:** LINQ/MOONWALK digital ecosystem (3,300+ installations) representing the industry's most mature digital service model
- **Automotive OEM:** Compact-process (3-Wet/B1:B2) technology reducing paint-shop energy consumption by 15–25%

### 2.3 Financial Strength

- $15.9 billion revenue with +2% organic growth in FY2025
- $1.9 billion operating cash flow (+$500M YoY improvement)
- $1.4 billion returned to shareholders ($790M buybacks, $630M dividends)
- 126-year uninterrupted dividend record (54 consecutive annual increases)
- Investment-grade balance sheet ($5.5 billion net debt, ~2.0–2.2× leverage)
- $2.2 billion cash at FY2025 year-end; $1.6 billion at Q1 2026
- $446 million annual R&D expenditure (2.8% of sales)

### 2.4 Secular Growth Tailwinds

The research identifies four secular growth vectors that operate largely independently of near-term macroeconomic fluctuations:

1. **Aerospace build-rate acceleration and chromate-free transition**
2. **Global non-BPA packaging-coating conversion** (decade-scale, $2+ billion addressable market)
3. **Protective & marine infrastructure investment** (energy, transportation, marine efficiency)
4. **Digital service-model transformation** (first-mover advantage in coatings-industry digitization)

### 2.5 Risk Assessment

Principal risks identified:

- **Automotive production cyclicality** (Industrial Coatings segment exposure)
- **Architectural DIY channel secular decline** (partially offset by pro-contractor growth)
- **Raw-material cost volatility** (mitigated by formula-based automotive pricing and procurement scale)
- **Competitive intensity in packaging coatings** (mitigated by qualification barriers)
- **Currency translation** (structural 1–2% annual headwind for USD-reporting entity with ~60% non-US revenue)
- **Geopolitical and tariff disruption** (mitigated by regional manufacturing footprint)

## 3.0 Research Gaps

The following areas warrant additional primary research:

1. **Detailed competitive teardown of PPG INNOVEL vs. AkzoNobel Accelshield and Sherwin-Williams valPure** — independent pack-resistance and sensory-panel data would strengthen comparative analysis
2. **Automotive OEM platform-level win/loss analysis** — vehicle-program-specific data on coating-supplier positions by OEM and assembly plant
3. **Marine coating specification share by vessel type and shipyard** — independent verification of PPG's claimed Asia Pacific share gains
4. **MOONWALK/LINQ customer-level ROI analysis** — quantified material savings, cycle-time reduction, and rework-rate improvement from independent body-shop operators
5. **Aerospace qualification-timeline modeling** — program-level analysis of chromate-free adoption trajectories by platform
6. **Architectural channel-economics benchmarking** — company-owned store vs. independent dealer vs. big-box retail profitability comparison
7. **Packaging-coating cost-in-use comparison** — BPA-based epoxy vs. INNOVEL on a total-applied-cost basis (including can-maker line-speed and waste-rate impacts)
