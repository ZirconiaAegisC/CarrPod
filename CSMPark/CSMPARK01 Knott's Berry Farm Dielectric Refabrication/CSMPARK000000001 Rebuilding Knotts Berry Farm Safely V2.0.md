## 1. The Five-Minute Mandate

Knott's Berry Farm, Buena Park, CA, sits 12 m above sea level in a basin fed by Coyote Creek. The 100-year flood elevation from FEMA FIS data: 8.5 m — almost park grade. A 500-year event: overtopping likely.

Simultaneously: the park contains 160 acres of continuously looped steel roller coaster track, copper-wound ride motors, and rigid concrete foundations. A Carrington Event GIC field of 20 V/km across the 500 m park diagonal induces:
$$V_{induced} = 20 \text{ V/km} \times 0.5 \text{ km} = 10 \text{ V}$$

In a 500 m steel coaster track loop (R ≈ 0.05 Ω):
$$I_{GIC} = 200 \text{ A} \quad P = I^2R = 200^2 \times 0.05 = 2,000 \text{ W (per ride circuit)}$$

This melts bearing grease, welds expansion joints, and destroys ride control computers within 4 minutes.

## 2. Structural Refabrication

**BFRP composite coaster track:**
- Pultrusion cross-section: 150×80 mm box section, basalt fiber + Elium® matrix
- Bending stiffness equivalent to 127×76×6 mm steel RHS at 35% the mass
- Zero ferromagnetism → zero GIC coupling → track survives any G5 storm
- YInMn Blue coating: SRI = 115, keeps track surface cool on hot days (prevents thermal expansion misalignment)

**Pneumatic/hydraulic ride propulsion:**
- Replace copper-wound induction motors with: compressed nitrogen accumulator systems for launch rides; hydraulic pump circuits using ceramic-coated hydraulic cylinders for traditional rides
- Hydraulic fluid: dielectric Polyalkylene Glycol (PAG) base — non-conductive, non-flammable
