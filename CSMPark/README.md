THE QUICK PITCH

A theme park is a small city — tens of thousands of visitors, dependent on electrically powered rides, food service, lighting, and emergency response, all in a defined footprint. Knott's Berry Farm, the subject of CSMPARK01, is the proving ground for dielectric refabrication of an entire public venue. The study models the park's electrical infrastructure and proposes a phased retrofit that would allow the park to function as a community shelter during a prolonged grid-down, powered by CarrPod-derived battery modules and a park-scale LoRa mesh. The perimeter fencing — chain-link steel, a giant GIC antenna — is reimagined as a BFRP dielectric barrier. The cost for the full conversion is two to three million dollars. For a community of fifty thousand people, that is forty to sixty dollars per person — less than the cost of a single ticket. Step onto the midway.
