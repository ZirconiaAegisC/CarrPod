HEADLINE: Carrington Storm Motors Raises £180M Series B to Turn Every EV Into a Grid-Defence Node

COVENTRY, UK — Carrington Storm Motors announced today it has closed a £180 million Series B funding round led by Breakthrough Energy Ventures and the NATO Innovation Fund, with participation from existing investors including the UK Infrastructure Bank. The round values the company at £1.2 billion.

The funding will accelerate production of the CSM-1, the company's electromagnetically hardened electric vehicle, and expand its mesh-network deployment to 47 critical grid nodes across the UK and continental Europe. CSM has also announced a partnership with National Grid ESO to integrate its vehicle telemetry into the grid's real-time stability monitoring platform.

"Infrastructure resilience isn't a feature—it's the entire value proposition," said Kairos Steele, CSM's founder and CEO. "Our investors understand that we're not competing with Tesla or BYD. We're building a new category: dual-use vehicles that serve as consumer transport 99.9% of the time and planetary defence infrastructure the other 0.1%."

The company reports pre-orders of 2,300 units from municipal fleets, data centre operators, and hospital networks.
