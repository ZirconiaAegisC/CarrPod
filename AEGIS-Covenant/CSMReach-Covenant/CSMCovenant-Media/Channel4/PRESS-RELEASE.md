HEADLINE: The AI That Says Britain's Poorest Communities Would Be Hit First by a Solar Storm — and Hardest

COVENTRY — An artificial intelligence analysis by Carrington Storm Motors has produced a disturbing finding: in the event of a Carrington-scale solar superstorm, Britain's most economically deprived communities would lose power first and regain it last, creating a "geomagnetic poverty gap" that would exacerbate existing inequality.

CSM's AI, designated "Mork," modelled grid failure cascades across 847 critical nodes and overlaid the results with ONS deprivation indices. The correlation was stark: areas in the lowest income quartile would experience power outages averaging 47% longer than those in the highest.

"The grid doesn't discriminate by postcode when it fails—but the restoration process absolutely does," said Dr. Chester Villegas, CSM's chief data scientist. "Industrial areas, data centres, and financial districts get priority reconnection. Council estates wait."

Kairos Steele, CSM's director, called the finding "a social justice issue disguised as an engineering problem" and announced a community resilience programme offering discounted grid-defence units to local authorities in high-deprivation areas.
