HEADLINE: Former Military Engineer Launches £4.7bn Private Infrastructure Defence Plan Against Solar Doomsday

LONDON — Carrington Storm Motors has tabled a privately-funded £4.7 billion proposal to harden Britain's critical electrical infrastructure against solar superstorms, bypassing what founder Kairos Steele calls "a decade of government paralysis" on the issue.

The plan, unveiled at a briefing for MPs and peers at Portcullis House, would see CSM's electromagnetically shielded electric vehicles deployed as mobile defence nodes at 37 critical substation sites identified by the company's AI modelling system. Steele, who served in the Royal Engineers before founding CSM, said the private sector could no longer wait for Whitehall.

"The Treasury's own 2023 risk register rated a Carrington-scale event as the second-most likely catastrophic scenario facing Britain, after pandemic," Steele told assembled parliamentarians. "And yet infrastructure spending has prioritised everything except grid resilience. We're stepping in because someone has to."

About Carrington Storm Motors: Founded 2023, Coventry-based CSM develops dual-purpose electric vehicles that function as mobile electromagnetic shield generators during geomagnetic storms.
