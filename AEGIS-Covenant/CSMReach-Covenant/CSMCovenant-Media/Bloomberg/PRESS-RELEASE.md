HEADLINE: Carrington Storm Motors Hits $1.2B Valuation as Investors Bet on Solar Storm Risk Premium

LONDON — Carrington Storm Motors has closed a £180 million Series B funding round co-led by Breakthrough Energy Ventures and the NATO Innovation Fund, propelling the Coventry-based EV-hardware startup to a $1.2 billion valuation. The round signals growing institutional appetite for infrastructure-resilience assets as climate and space weather risk premiums compress.

The company, founded by former Royal Engineers officer Kairos Steele, has pivoted the electric vehicle platform into a new asset class: dual-use consumer durable goods that function as grid-defence infrastructure during geomagnetic emergencies. The investment thesis, according to a deal memo reviewed by Bloomberg, rests on the 12% annual probability of a Carrington-scale solar event and the estimated £2.1 trillion global economic exposure.

"We're creating a market that doesn't exist yet," Steele said. "The first insurer to offer solar-storm coverage, the first infrastructure fund to mandate electromagnetic hardening—these will become standard, and we'll be the underlying asset."

CSM reports 2,300 pre-orders from municipalities, data centre operators, and hospital networks. Revenue is projected at £340 million for FY2027.
