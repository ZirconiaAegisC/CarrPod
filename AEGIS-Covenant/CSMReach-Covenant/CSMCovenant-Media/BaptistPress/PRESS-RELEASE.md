**BAPTIST PRESS: SOUTHERN BAPTIST DISASTER RELIEF INTEGRATES GEOMAGNETIC PREPAREDNESS**

NASHVILLE — Southern Baptist Disaster Relief, one of the largest faith-based disaster response organizations in the United States, has partnered with the AEGIS Covenant to integrate geomagnetic preparedness into its 82,000-volunteer training curriculum and its nationwide network of mobile kitchen and recovery units.

"Southern Baptists run toward disasters when everyone else runs away," said Dr. Thomas Abrams, SBDR National Director. "We have fed millions after hurricanes, tornadoes, and floods. But a Carrington Event is different — the disaster is everywhere at once, there's no unaffected region to stage from, and our own mobile kitchens depend on diesel that requires electronic pumps. The AEGIS Covenant helped us see the gap — and gave us the plan to close it."

The SBDR-Covenant partnership covers three domains: Fleet Hardening (MXene shielding retrofit for SBDR's 1,800 mobile kitchen, shower, and recovery units — protecting the electronic control modules and generator transfer switches that keep the fleet operational), Volunteer Training (a geomagnetic-specific module added to the existing SBDR "yellow cap" training curriculum, covering grid-down logistics, analog communications, and coordination with the Veteran Response Corps), and Pre-Positioning (identifying SBDR staging sites that overlap with Veteran Corps nexus points, ensuring coordinated response rather than duplicated effort).

SBDR has committed to training 5,000 volunteers in the geomagnetic module by Q3 2027 and to advocating for BILL-006 through the SBC's Ethics and Religious Liberty Commission.

"When the grid is gone, the Gospel still goes forward," said Abrams. "We need to be ready to carry it — literally — to the people who need it most."
