**LION'S ROAR: THE DHARMA OF THE GRID — BUDDHIST WISDOM FOR ELECTROMAGNETIC TIMES**

BOULDER, Colo. — The AEGIS Covenant has published a major essay in Lion's Roar by Venerable Thubten Chodron, exploring the intersection of Buddhist practice and the imperative to protect critical infrastructure from solar catastrophe.

"Buddhist practice teaches us to hold the suffering of the world in our hearts," writes Venerable Chodron. "It also teaches us to act. Tonglen — the practice of taking in suffering and sending out compassion — is not merely a meditation cushion exercise. It is a call to engage with the actual causes of suffering in the world. One of those causes, poorly understood by most, is the vulnerability of the electrical systems that 8 billion humans depend on for survival."

The essay, "Tonglen for the Grid," moves from meditative practice to engaged action, arguing that Buddhist practitioners have a unique role to play in the electromagnetic defense effort: as voices for the voiceless (the millions of non-human sentient beings who would also suffer in a grid-down world), as advocates for interdependence (articulating in Buddhist terms the interconnectedness that engineers describe in terms of grid topology), and as community anchors (Buddhist centers and sanghas functioning as resilience hubs in the same way that VFW posts and churches do under BILL-006).

"In the end, protecting the grid is tonglen," writes Chodron. "We take in the reality of the threat — the fear, the uncertainty, the magnitude of what we face. And we send out the response: the MXene films, the hardened substations, the trained veterans, the international coalition. This is the Bodhisattva path for the 21st century."

Lion's Roar has published the essay in its current issue and online.
