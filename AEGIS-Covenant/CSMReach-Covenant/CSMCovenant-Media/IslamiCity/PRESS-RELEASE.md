**ISLAMICITY: THE KHALIFAH'S DUTY — ISLAMIC ENVIRONMENTAL STEWARDSHIP MEETS ELECTROMAGNETIC DEFENSE**

WASHINGTON, D.C. — The AEGIS Covenant has published a comprehensive Islamic environmental stewardship framework for electromagnetic planetary defense, drawing on Quranic principles of khalifah (stewardship of Earth) and mizan (balance in creation) to argue that protecting the electrical grid is a religious obligation.

"Allah created the Sun as a sign of His power and a source of mercy — 'and We made the sun a blazing lamp' (Quran 78:13)," writes Dr. Fatima al-Zahra, AEGIS Islamic Outreach Coordinator and professor of environmental ethics. "The same Sun that enables life on Earth also produces coronal mass ejections that can destroy the systems we depend on. Understanding this dual nature — and acting to protect humanity from harm while honoring the Creator's design — is khalifah in its most sophisticated form."

The framework, published on IslamiCity and available in Arabic, Urdu, Bahasa Indonesia, and English, addresses the electromagnetic threat through five Quranic lenses: Tawhid (the unity of creation — the interconnectedness of natural and human-made systems), Ayat (signs — solar physics as a means of understanding the Creator's power), Mizan (balance — the disruption of Earth's electromagnetic balance by human infrastructure, and the obligation to restore resilience), Khalifah (stewardship — the specific duty to protect the vulnerable through MXene hardening and Coalition mutual aid), and Adl (justice — the disproportionate impact of grid failure on the Global South and the Islamic duty to address it).

IslamiCity has committed to hosting the full framework and to facilitating a global online discussion series with Islamic scholars, scientists, and humanitarian leaders on the intersection of faith and electromagnetic resilience.
