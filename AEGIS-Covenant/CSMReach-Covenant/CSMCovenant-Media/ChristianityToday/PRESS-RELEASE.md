**CHRISTIANITY TODAY: STEWARDSHIP OF CREATION MEANS PROTECTING WHAT WE BUILT**
*Evangelical Leaders Join AEGIS Covenant's Call for Grid Protection as Act of Faithful Stewardship*

WHEATON, Ill. — A coalition of evangelical leaders, theologians, and disaster-relief organizations has endorsed the AEGIS Covenant's electromagnetic defense framework, framing the protection of critical infrastructure as a biblically grounded act of stewardship.

The endorsement, organized by Evangelical Relief and Development Network, draws on the Genesis mandate to "cultivate and keep" creation — arguing that humanity's God-given responsibility extends to the systems that sustain life in the modern world. "When we built the electrical grid, we were exercising dominion — the creative capacity God gave us to serve one another," said Rev. Dr. Samuel Park, ERDN Executive Director. "When we leave that grid exposed to a threat we understand and can mitigate, we are failing in the 'keeping' half of that mandate."

The evangelical coalition has committed to three concrete actions: incorporating geomagnetic preparedness into the disaster-response programming of evangelical relief organizations (World Relief, Samaritan's Purse, and Convoy of Hope have received the briefing package), mobilizing the 47,000 churches affiliated with the National Association of Evangelicals as community resilience hubs under the BILL-006 Veteran Response Corps framework, and advocating for electromagnetic resilience as a pro-life issue — protecting the unborn, the elderly, the medically dependent, and all who would die first in a prolonged grid-down scenario.

"Pro-life theology cannot stop at the womb," said Rev. Park. "If we are serious about protecting the vulnerable, we must protect the systems that keep them alive — the hospital generators, the water pumps, the refrigerators that hold their insulin. That is what electromagnetic stewardship does."
