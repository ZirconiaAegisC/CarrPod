HEADLINE: Carrington Storm Motors CEO: 'We're Building the Airbag for Civilisation' — Full Interview

COVENTRY, UK — In an exclusive sit-down at the company's manufacturing facility, Carrington Storm Motors founder and CEO Kairos Steele laid out a vision that transcends the automotive sector: a distributed network of dual-use electric vehicles that can collectively stabilise continental power grids during solar superstorms.

"Every car we build is an insurance policy with a steering wheel," Steele said. "You drive it to work 364 days a year. On day 365—if that's the day a coronal mass ejection hits—it becomes a grid-defence node. Your car keeps the hospital running."

The company has raised £180 million in Series B funding, secured 2,300 pre-orders from institutional fleets, and partnered with National Grid ESO for real-time grid telemetry integration. Steele projects profitability by Q3 2028, driven primarily by fleet contracts rather than consumer sales.

When asked about competition, Steele was blunt: "Tesla builds great EVs. BYD builds affordable EVs. Nobody else builds an EV that can absorb a 50,000-volt geomagnetic surge and keep the lights on. We're not in the car business—we're in the civilisation-continuity business."

CSM's valuation now stands at $1.2 billion.
