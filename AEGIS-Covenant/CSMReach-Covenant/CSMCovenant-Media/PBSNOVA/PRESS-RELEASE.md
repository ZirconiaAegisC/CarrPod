**NOVA: THE SCIENCE OF SHIELDING A PLANET — AEGIS COVENANT OPENS THE LABORATORIES**

BOSTON — The AEGIS Covenant has invited PBS NOVA to produce a definitive science documentary on the physics of geomagnetic catastrophe and the quantum materials designed to prevent it — offering unprecedented laboratory access to the MXene research teams at work on three continents.

"NOVA is the gold standard for making hard science accessible and beautiful," said Dr. Kim, AEGIS CTO. "And this science is both. The physics of coronal mass ejections — a billion tons of plasma traveling at several million miles per hour — colliding with Earth's magnetosphere and inducing quasi-DC currents in every long conductor on the planet. The quantum mechanics of MXene — a two-dimensional transition metal carbide that, at 45 microns, achieves 99.7% electromagnetic attenuation. These are not mysteries. They are phenomena we can film, animate, and explain in a way that makes a NOVA viewer say 'I understand this.'"

The proposed NOVA episode would cover: the Sun's magnetic field dynamics and CME generation (with solar observatory data and visualizations), the magnetosphere-ionosphere coupling that produces aurora and GICs (with real-time data from the 200-station Aurora Monitoring Network), the materials science of MXene (with electron microscope cinematography from the Korean and German foundries), the engineering of grid-scale electromagnetic protection (with Substation Hardening Protocol demonstrations), and the biological dimension: what happens to pacemakers, insulin pumps, and other implantable medical devices during a G5 event.

"Every frame of this episode has a visual — an animation, a microscope image, an aurora time-lapse, a drone shot of a hardened substation," said Kim. "This is the science documentary NOVA was created to make."
