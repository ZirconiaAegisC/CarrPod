**PATAGONIA FILMS: THE PLANET WE PROTECT — ELECTROMAGNETIC STEWARDSHIP AS ENVIRONMENTAL IMPERATIVE**

VENTURA, Calif. — The AEGIS Covenant has proposed a documentary partnership with Patagonia Films, framing electromagnetic planetary defense through Patagonia's distinctive lens of environmental stewardship and intergenerational responsibility.

"Patagonia doesn't just make films about nature — it makes films about our responsibility to nature," said Dr. Vasquez. "The electromagnetic defense effort is, at its core, an act of stewardship. We inherited a 150-year-old electrical infrastructure that nobody built with the Sun in mind. We have a responsibility to the next generation to fix that before the next Carrington Event arrives. That is a Patagonia story."

The proposed documentary would focus on the human faces of the defense effort: the scientists who discovered MXene and are now racing to deploy it, the veteran volunteers of the Response Corps who have already served their country and are volunteering to serve again, and the coastal communities — in Portugal, in Florida, in the Philippines — where the Charlemagne fleet represents a new model of amphibious humanitarian delivery.

The Covenant has offered Patagonia Films unrestricted creative control, with the Covenant's role limited to providing access, factual accuracy review, and distribution support through its own network of partner organizations.

"Patagonia's audience buys the gear, reads the catalog, and watches the films because they believe we have a responsibility to the planet and to each other," said Vasquez. "This film would show them exactly what that responsibility looks like at civilization scale."
