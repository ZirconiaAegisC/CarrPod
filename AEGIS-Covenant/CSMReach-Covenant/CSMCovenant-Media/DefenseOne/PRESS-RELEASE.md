**DEFENSE MODERNIZATION MEETS THE SUN: AEGIS COVENANT PROPOSES GEOMAGNETIC RESILIENCE AS ACQUISITION MANDATE**

WASHINGTON, D.C. — The AEGIS Covenant has submitted a formal proposal to the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment, calling for mandatory electromagnetic resilience standards in all future defense acquisition programs.

The proposal, backed by technical analysis from three National Laboratories, argues that the Pentagon's current electromagnetic protection standards — primarily MIL-STD-188-125 for HEMP — are insufficient for the sustained geomagnetically induced currents produced by a G5-class solar event. High-altitude EMP standards address a nanoseconds-scale pulse; CME events produce quasi-DC currents lasting minutes to hours, requiring fundamentally different protection approaches.

"The DoD spends $800 billion a year buying platforms that could be rendered inoperable by a natural event we can see coming three days in advance," said Dr. Kim, AEGIS CTO. "That is not a technology failure. That is an acquisition failure."

The Covenant's proposal introduces the Geomagnetic Resilience Score (GRS), a 1-10 rating that would be required on all major defense acquisition programs above the ACAT II threshold. The GRS evaluates platform survivability against a G5 event across three domains: electrical system tolerance, communications continuity, and platform mobility post-event. Programs scoring below 6 would require mandatory remediation or a formal waiver from the Service Acquisition Executive.

MXene shielding — the core technology enabling GRS compliance — has been demonstrated at TRL 7 at Army Research Laboratory and is ready for program-of-record integration. The Covenant has identified 47 active acquisition programs that would benefit from immediate GRS evaluation, including the B-21 Raider, Columbia-class submarine, and Next Generation Air Dominance family of systems.
