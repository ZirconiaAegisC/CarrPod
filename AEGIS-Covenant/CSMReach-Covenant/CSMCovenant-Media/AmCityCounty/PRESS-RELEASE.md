**COUNTY GOVERNMENTS: THE UNACKNOWLEDGED FIRST RESPONDERS OF GEOMAGNETIC CATASTROPHE**

ARLINGTON, Va. — The AEGIS Covenant, in partnership with the National Association of Counties, has released the County Geomagnetic Resilience Playbook — a practical, county-level implementation guide for electromagnetic event preparedness designed specifically for county executives, commissioners, and emergency managers.

"Why counties?" said NACo Executive Director Matthew Brewer in the playbook's foreword. "Because when the federal government is dark and the state capital is overwhelmed, the county sheriff's dispatch, the county health department, and the county road crew are what remains of government. We need them ready."

The playbook, deliberately written at a non-technical level accessible to elected officials without engineering backgrounds, covers five core actions: Substation Mapping (identifying which transformers serve the county and their vulnerability profile), MXene Procurement (pre-negotiated pricing agreements for shielding film, available to counties through a NACo collective purchasing vehicle), Veteran Corps Integration (designating county VFW and American Legion posts as pre-positioned response hubs under BILL-006), Fuel Stabilization (a 90-day county fuel reserve protocol using additives that cost $0.03 per gallon), and Analog Communications (pre-positioned aviation-band radios, paper-based dispatch protocols, and runner networks using county roads departments).

"The federal government tells counties to prepare for everything," said Commissioner Sarah Lin of Montgomery County, Maryland, an early adopter. "This is the first time anyone has told us specifically what 'prepare' means for an electromagnetic event — and given us the tools to do it."

The playbook is available at aegis-covenant.org/county-playbook.
