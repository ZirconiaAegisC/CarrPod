**AIR SUPERIORITY IN THE ELECTROMAGNETIC DOMAIN: AEGIS COVENANT HARDENS CRITICAL AIRCRAFT SYSTEMS**

WRIGHT-PATTERSON AFB, Ohio — The AEGIS Covenant has completed electromagnetic vulnerability assessments on three Air Force platforms — the F-35A Lightning II, KC-46 Pegasus, and E-3 Sentry AWACS — revealing previously undocumented susceptibility to geomagnetically induced currents in avionics systems.

The findings were delivered to Air Combat Command and Air Mobility Command in a classified briefing.

"We build aircraft to dominate the electromagnetic spectrum in combat," said Lt. Col. James Richardson (Ret.), AEGIS Aerospace Division Lead. "We have not built them to survive that same spectrum when the Sun decides to become a combatant."

The Covenant's proposed remediation — MXene shielding film integrated into avionics bay liners, antenna feedline conduits, and fly-by-wire harness junction boxes — adds 8.7 pounds per aircraft and can be applied during standard depot maintenance cycles. AFRL has validated the shielding effectiveness at 99.7% attenuation across the frequency range relevant to GIC events.

The Air Force has authorized a follow-on assessment for the B-21 Raider and the Survivable Airborne Operations Center fleet, recognizing that airborne command posts must remain fully mission-capable during any electromagnetic contingency — natural or otherwise.

"Air superiority means nothing if your aircraft are grounded by a storm 93 million miles away," said Richardson.
