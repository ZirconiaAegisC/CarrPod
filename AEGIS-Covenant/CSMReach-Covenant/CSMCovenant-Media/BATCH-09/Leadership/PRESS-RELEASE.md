**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

## Beyond Oil: Protecting Nigeria's Energy Future from Above

ABUJA — Nigeria's economic future depends on a successful transition from oil-dependent revenues to a diversified, electrified economy. That transition, CSM-Covenant's AEGIS assessment argues, requires not just more generation capacity but also a grid that can withstand threats that no amount of gas turbines can solve.

A prolonged grid-down scenario in Nigeria following a GMD event would have consequences that cascade far beyond the power sector. Water treatment plants would fail in 48-72 hours. Fuel distribution would halt within a week, stranding generators without diesel. Cold chain storage for food and medicine would collapse. Telecommunications would degrade as backup batteries depleted. The interdependence of modern infrastructure means that grid failure is not an isolated event — it is a systemic collapse.

"Nigeria's development trajectory depends on reliable electricity," said Dr. Ndlovu. "The country has rightly focused on generation capacity as the binding constraint. But resilience against low-probability, high-consequence events is the next frontier — and it's one where smart investment now prevents catastrophic loss later."

CSM-Covenant is proposing a GMD resilience partnership with the Transmission Company of Nigeria and the Nigerian Electricity Regulatory Commission to integrate HYDRA hardening into the country's grid modernization program. The cost is modest, the technology is proven, and the alternative is unthinkable.
