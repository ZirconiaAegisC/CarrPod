## Dubai's Smart City Ambition and the Grid Risk Nobody's Modeling

DUBAI — Dubai's vision of a fully integrated smart city — autonomous transport, AI-driven utilities, blockchain-enabled government services — depends on an assumption so fundamental it's rarely questioned: the power will stay on. CSM-Covenant's AEGIS assessment suggests that assumption deserves scrutiny.

Dubai's grid reliability is among the world's best, with DEWA consistently achieving SAIDI scores that rival Singapore and outperform most European utilities. But reliability under normal conditions is not the same as resilience under extreme geomagnetic conditions. A Carrington-scale event would stress DEWA's transformer fleet in ways that routine contingency planning does not address.

"The smarter the city, the more catastrophic the blackout," said Dr. Al-Rashid. "A conventional city without power is paralyzed. A smart city without power is both paralyzed and blind — every sensor, every automated system, every digital service fails simultaneously. Dubai's 10X ambitions require 10X resilience."

CSM-Covenant's analysis identifies 12 priority substations in the Dubai-Sharjah-Abu Dhabi corridor where HYDRA hardening would protect the UAE's most critical urban infrastructure. The cost per substation — approximately AED 15 million — must be weighed against the cost of a single day of Dubai-wide grid failure, estimated at AED 4.5 billion in direct economic losses.
