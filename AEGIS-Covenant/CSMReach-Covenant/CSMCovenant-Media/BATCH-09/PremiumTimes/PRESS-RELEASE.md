## Nigeria's Power Sector Reform: The Resilience Gap

ABUJA — Nigeria's power sector has been the subject of successive reform efforts — privatization of generation and distribution, the Siemens presidential power initiative, and the ongoing restructuring of the Transmission Company of Nigeria. CSM-Covenant's AEGIS assessment argues that none of these reforms have addressed the electromagnetic resilience dimension that could undermine all other progress.

The assessment identifies 31 critical substations across Nigeria where geomagnetic-induced current could cause catastrophic transformer failure. The economic impact of a Carrington-scale event on Nigeria's grid would be approximately NGN 85 trillion in first-year losses — roughly 40% of the country's annual GDP.

"Nigeria's power sector reforms are necessary but incomplete," said Dr. Ndlovu. "You can privatize generation, improve distribution, and optimize transmission — but if a solar storm destroys the transformers, none of that matters. Resilience is not an alternative to reform; it is the foundation on which reform must be built."

CSM-Covenant's recommendation is straightforward: integrate GMD hardening specifications into the procurement requirements for all new transformers acquired through the Siemens initiative and other modernization programs. The incremental cost is less than 2% of the transformer purchase price.
