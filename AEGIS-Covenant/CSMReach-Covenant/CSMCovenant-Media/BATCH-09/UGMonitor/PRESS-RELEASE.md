## Uganda's Development Trajectory and the Grid Resilience Imperative

KAMPALA — Uganda's energy sector has undergone a remarkable transformation over the past two decades, with the Bujagali and Karuma hydropower projects dramatically expanding generation capacity and the national grid extending into previously unserved rural areas. CSM-Covenant's AEGIS assessment applauds this progress while identifying a resilience dimension that East African grid planners have not historically addressed.

Uganda's equatorial position provides strong natural protection against geomagnetic disturbance. However, the country's growing role as a regional electricity exporter — with interconnections to Kenya, Rwanda, Tanzania, and the Democratic Republic of Congo — creates a risk profile that extends beyond national borders. GIC originating in one country's transmission network flows freely across interconnections into neighboring grids.

"Uganda is rightly positioning itself as East Africa's electricity hub," said Dr. Ndlovu. "But a hub absorbs risk as well as opportunity. HYDRA hardening at Uganda's key interconnection substations would protect not only Uganda's grid but also contribute to regional resilience — a leadership role that aligns with Uganda's broader energy strategy."

CSM-Covenant has identified 12 priority substations in Uganda where HYDRA hardening would provide maximum national and regional benefit. The technology can be integrated into planned interconnection upgrades, minimizing incremental costs.
