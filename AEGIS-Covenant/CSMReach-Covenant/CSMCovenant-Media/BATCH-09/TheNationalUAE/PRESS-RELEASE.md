## UAE Energy Strategy 2050: The Missing Resilience Dimension

ABU DHABI — The UAE Energy Strategy 2050 is one of the world's most ambitious national energy transformation plans: 50% clean energy, 40% reduction in consumption, and a diversified generation mix spanning nuclear, solar, and conventional sources. CSM-Covenant's analysis identifies a critical dimension missing from the strategy's resilience framework: geomagnetic disturbance protection.

The Barakah nuclear plant, the Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum Solar Park, and the new HVDC interconnections with Oman and the broader GCC grid represent investments exceeding AED 200 billion. None of the transformers serving these assets have been specifically hardened against GIC.

"Strategy 2050 envisions an energy system for the next 30 years," said Dr. Al-Rashid. "Over that time horizon, the probability of a Carrington-scale geomagnetic storm is approximately 40%. A strategy that doesn't account for a 40% probability event with trillion-dirham consequences has a resilience gap that needs closing."

CSM-Covenant's recommendations include integrating GMD hardening specifications into all new transformer procurement for UAE energy projects, retrofitting HYDRA protection at the 18 most critical existing substations, and establishing a UAE Space Weather Response Centre in partnership with the Mohammed bin Rashid Space Centre.
