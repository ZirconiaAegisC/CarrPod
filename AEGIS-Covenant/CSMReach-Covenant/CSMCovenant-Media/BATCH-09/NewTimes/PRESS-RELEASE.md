## Rwanda's Grid: A Model for the Continent Needs the Final Layer of Protection

KIGALI — Rwanda's electrical grid is widely regarded as one of the best-managed in Africa, with the Rwanda Energy Group achieving reliability metrics that rival middle-income countries. CSM-Covenant's AEGIS assessment identifies Rwanda as a potential continental leader in grid resilience — but only if GMD protection is added to the country's already impressive infrastructure framework.

Rwanda's compact geography and equatorial position limit absolute GMD exposure. However, the country's strategy of becoming a regional ICT hub — with data centers, fiber optic networks, and a growing digital services sector — creates an economic dependency on grid reliability that amplifies the consequences of even a moderate geomagnetic disruption.

"Rwanda has positioned itself as Africa's technology hub," said Dr. Ndlovu. "But technology hubs need power. Kigali's data centers, the Kigali Innovation City, the smart city initiatives — all of these depend on transformers that have never been hardened against GMD. Leadership in ICT requires leadership in grid resilience."

CSM-Covenant has identified 8 priority substations in Rwanda where HYDRA hardening would provide the greatest protection. The total cost — approximately RWF 12 billion — aligns with Rwanda's demonstrated willingness to make strategic, forward-looking infrastructure investments.
