HEADLINE: Why the Fortune 500's Biggest Supply Chain Blind Spot Is 93 Million Miles Away

COVENTRY, UK — A new analysis from Carrington Storm Motors estimates that 47% of Fortune 500 companies have critical supply-chain dependencies on electrical infrastructure with zero electromagnetic hardening—creating a concentrated risk that no chief supply chain officer has publicly disclosed.

The analysis, shared exclusively with Fortune, maps the geographical overlap between Fortune 500 supplier networks and CSM's database of 847 vulnerable grid nodes across Europe and North America. The concentration is striking: 14 companies in the automotive, semiconductor, and pharmaceutical sectors alone account for nearly 40% of the identified exposure.

"Supply chain resilience has been the corporate obsession of the decade," said Kairos Steele, CSM's founder. "But every resilience framework assumes the grid is available. Our analysis shows that assumption is worth exactly what the Sun decides it's worth."

CSM is offering its fleet-based hardening solution as a supply-chain insurance play, targeting Fortune 500 procurement departments with a proposition: protect your Tier 1 suppliers' grid access, or accept a 12% annual probability of simultaneous multi-site disruption.
