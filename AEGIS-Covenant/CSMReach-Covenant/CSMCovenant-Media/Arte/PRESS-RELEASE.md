**ARTE: LE BOUCLIER PLANÉTAIRE — UN DOCUMENTAIRE SUR LA DÉFENSE ÉLECTROMAGNÉTIQUE MONDIALE**

STRASBOURG — The AEGIS Covenant has proposed a co-production documentary to ARTE, the Franco-German cultural network, exploring the intersection of science, geopolitics, and art in humanity's first coordinated planetary defense effort.

"ARTE occupies a unique space in documentary filmmaking — where science meets culture, where engineering meets philosophy," said Ambassador Jean-Pierre Moreau, Coalition Co-Chair. "This story exists precisely at that intersection. It is about a quantum material — MXene — that is simultaneously a triumph of materials science and, when filmed under an electron microscope, a work of abstract art. It is about a fleet — Charlemagne — that carries both humanitarian supplies and the weight of a continent's name. It is about 18 people from every corner of the Earth who represent, in microcosm, the civilization they are trying to protect."

The proposed ARTE documentary would be structured in three movements: "La Menace" (the Sun as both life-giver and destroyer, the physics of CMEs, the aurora as beauty and warning), "Le Matériau" (MXene at the atomic scale, cleanroom cinematography, the poetry of protecting a planet with a film thinner than a human hair), and "La Réponse" (the Coalition, the Charlemagne fleet, the veterans, the 18 Agents — humanity choosing to act rather than wait).

ARTE's co-production model, with its commitment to both French and German language versions, aligns with the Covenant's bilingual European communications strategy. Pre-cleared access to all European filming locations is available.
