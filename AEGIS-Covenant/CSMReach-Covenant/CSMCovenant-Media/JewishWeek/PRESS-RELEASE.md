**THE JEWISH WEEK: SHABBAT IN THE DARK — WHY ELECTROMAGNETIC PREPAREDNESS IS A COMMUNAL IMPERATIVE**

NEW YORK — The AEGIS Covenant has partnered with UJA-Federation of New York and the Jewish Community Relations Council to release an electromagnetic preparedness guide specifically designed for Jewish communal institutions: synagogues, day schools, JCCs, senior residences, and kosher food distribution networks.

"When the grid goes down, the Jewish community faces unique vulnerabilities," said Rebecca Goldstein, AEGIS Jewish Community Liaison. "Kosher food supply chains depend on refrigeration. Shabbat observance — which already assumes a day without technology — would become a permanent condition, not a weekly rhythm. And the elderly, who constitute a significant proportion of Jewish communal membership, are the most medically dependent on electricity for oxygen concentrators, CPAP machines, and refrigerated medications."

The guide, "Ner Tamid: Keeping the Eternal Light Burning," provides Jewish institutions with a six-step resilience pathway: facility energy audit with MXene hardening recommendations, generator fuel stabilization (pre-positioning 90-day additive supplies), kosher food supply-chain mapping (identifying cold-chain dependencies and pre-hardened storage alternatives), medical-device registry (maintaining an institutional census of members dependent on powered medical equipment), Shabbat-to-permanent transition protocol (adapting existing Shabbat infrastructure — manual-light timers, analog communication plans — for indefinite operation), and integration with the Veteran Response Corps county-level nexus network.

The guide has been piloted with 40 institutions across the New York metropolitan area, with a national rollout planned through JFNA's network of 146 federations.
