**CNN FILMS: "THE THREE DAY WARNING" — HUMANITY'S WINDOW TO SAVE ITSELF**

ATLANTA — The AEGIS Covenant has proposed a CNN Films documentary titled "The Three Day Warning," exploring the unique temporal dimension of the geomagnetic threat: unlike earthquakes or tsunamis, a coronal mass ejection gives humanity approximately 72 hours of advance notice — and what we choose to do with that warning defines everything.

"The title is the premise," said Dr. Vasquez. "We know the Sun will throw a Carrington-class CME at Earth. We know we will see it approximately three days before it arrives — DSCOVR satellite data gives us that window. The question that drives every frame of this film is: what do we do with those three days? Who makes the decisions? Who gets to decide which hospitals stay powered? Which transformers get the last available MXene film? Which neighborhoods evacuate and which shelter in place?"

The proposed documentary would follow the decision-makers across multiple timeframes: the solar physicists at NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center who will be the first to see the CME on their monitors; the White House Situation Room, where National Security Council staff will brief the President on a threat for which no existing continuity-of-government plan adequately accounts; the AEGIS Covenant's operations center, where the 18 Agents will activate the global response network they have spent years building; and the Veteran Geomagnetic Response Corps, whose 47,000 pre-registered members will simultaneously receive activation orders on their mobile devices.

"CNN Films has a tradition of urgent, consequential documentary storytelling," said Vasquez. "This is the most urgent story we know how to tell. The three days are coming. The question is whether the film will document our preparation — or our failure."
