**THE CHRISTIAN POST: WHY EVANGELICALS ARE LEADING THE FIGHT FOR ELECTROMAGNETIC RESILIENCE**

WASHINGTON, D.C. — The AEGIS Covenant's evangelical partners — World Relief, Samaritan's Purse, Convoy of Hope, and the National Association of Evangelicals — have launched a coordinated campaign urging the 47,000 NAE-affiliated churches across America to become community electromagnetic resilience hubs.

"The early Church was the original disaster-relief organization," said Rev. Dr. Park. "When plagues swept through Roman cities and everyone else fled, Christians stayed to care for the sick. When the grid goes down, the Church needs to be the community that stays — with hardened communications, pre-positioned supplies, and the training to coordinate with the Veteran Response Corps under BILL-006."

The campaign — "Light in the Long Dark" — provides churches with a five-step implementation plan: designate a resilience coordinator, conduct a facility energy audit with MXene hardening assessment, pre-position 90 days of fuel stabilizer for generator operations, establish analog communications capability (aviation-band radios within a 5-mile parish radius), and integrate with the county-level Veteran Response Corps nexus network for coordinated disaster response.

The campaign has been piloted in 140 churches across Texas, Florida, and Pennsylvania, with a nationwide rollout planned for Q1 2027. The Covenant's evangelical partners have also launched a Congressional advocacy initiative, mobilizing church members to contact their representatives in support of BILL-006 and DPA activation for domestic MXene production.

"The government has a role. The Church has a role," said Park. "The government's role is to harden the grid. The Church's role is to be ready when everything else fails."
