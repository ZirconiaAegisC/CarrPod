HEADLINE: The Electric Cars That Could Save Britain From Going Dark: Inside the Factory Building Mobile Grid Defences

COVENTRY — In a converted aircraft hangar on the outskirts of Coventry, a team of 47 engineers is building what might be the most unusual electric vehicles on the planet: cars designed not primarily for transport, but to function as mobile electromagnetic shield generators during solar superstorms.

Carrington Storm Motors, founded in 2023 by former Royal Engineers officer Kairos Steele, today opened its manufacturing facility to media for the first time, demonstrating how its hardened EV platform can stabilise local grid segments during geomagnetic disturbances.

Each vehicle contains copper-foil Faraday shielding, industrial-grade voltage regulators, and an onboard AI processor running continuous solar wind analysis. In a live demonstration, a CSM vehicle successfully absorbed a simulated geomagnetic surge that would have destroyed an unprotected transformer.

"We're not just building cars—we're building mobile lifeboats for the electrical age," Steele said. "When the next Carrington Event hits, and it will, these vehicles will be the difference between lights on and lights out for millions of people."
