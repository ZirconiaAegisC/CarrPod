**ARMY OF THE FUTURE: AEGIS COVENANT DEMONSTRATES MXENE-HARDENED COMBAT VEHICLES AT FORT BRAGG**

FORT BRAGG, N.C. — The AEGIS Covenant, in coordination with the Army Futures Command, has conducted the first live demonstration of MXene-shielded combat vehicles surviving simulated geomagnetic disturbance conditions at the Fort Bragg electromagnetic effects range.

The demonstration subjected a MXene-retrofitted Stryker and a hardened Joint Light Tactical Vehicle to induced currents equivalent to a G5-class geomagnetic event — conditions that rendered unshielded control vehicles inoperable within 12 seconds. Both MXene-protected platforms maintained full engine operation, communications suite functionality, and weapons-system readiness throughout the 90-minute test cycle.

"The Army fights in all domains, including the electromagnetic one," said COL Samantha Reyes, Army Futures Command liaison. "What AEGIS has demonstrated is that MXene shielding at the vehicle level is not a laboratory curiosity — it's a fieldable, affordable, maintainable solution to a threat we have acknowledged but never fully addressed."

The Covenant's vehicle-hardening protocol uses pre-cut MXene film appliqués applied to electronic control modules, power-distribution nodes, and antenna feedlines. The kits are designed for installation by unit-level mechanics without specialized tools, with a per-vehicle cost of $320 and an application time of under two hours.

Army Futures Command has recommended the technology for accelerated fielding under the Rapid Capabilities and Critical Technologies Office. If approved, MXene shielding could enter the operational force as a TACOM-directed modification by Q2 2027.

The demonstration also included the first public test of MXene-hardened SINCGARS radios, which maintained full frequency-hopping capability throughout the simulated event — a capability previously assumed to be lost entirely in a G5 scenario.
