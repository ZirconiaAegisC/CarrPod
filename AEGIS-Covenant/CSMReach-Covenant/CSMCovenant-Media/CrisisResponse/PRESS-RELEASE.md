**CRISIS WITHOUT PRECEDENT: AEGIS COVENANT LAUNCHES GLOBAL CRISIS RESPONSE COALITION**

GENEVA — The AEGIS Covenant, in partnership with eighteen international NGOs and four sovereign states, has launched the Global Geomagnetic Crisis Response Coalition, establishing the first multinational framework for coordinated response to a Carrington-scale solar event.

The Coalition fills a void explicitly acknowledged by the UN Office for Disaster Risk Reduction: no existing international agreement, treaty, or mutual-aid compact addresses a global electromagnetic catastrophe. The Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction contains no geomagnetic annex. NATO's Civil Emergency Planning mechanisms have no EMP/CME protocol.

"We are building the architecture that should have existed fifty years ago," said Ambassador Jean-Pierre Moreau, Coalition Co-Chair. "When the sun ejects a billion tons of plasma at Earth, it does not respect borders. Neither can our response."

The Coalition's founding agreement establishes four pillars: Shared Early Warning (integrating NOAA, ESA, and ISRO solar monitoring data into a unified alert system), Mutual Aid Mobilization (pre-cleared customs waivers for emergency MXene shipments across 22 signatory nations), the International Veteran Response Network (connecting veteran communities in 14 countries for grid-down coordination), and Critical Node Hardening (prioritized transformer protection at the 1,200 most geopolitically significant substations worldwide).

The Coalition has secured provisional commitments from Switzerland, Singapore, New Zealand, and Estonia, with active negotiations underway with Japan, South Korea, and Germany. The World Bank's Disaster Risk Financing facility has been engaged for standby credit arrangements.
