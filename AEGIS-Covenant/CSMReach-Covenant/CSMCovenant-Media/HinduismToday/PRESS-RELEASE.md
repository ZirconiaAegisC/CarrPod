**HINDUISM TODAY: AHIMSA AND THE GRID — PROTECTING ALL LIFE THROUGH ELECTROMAGNETIC RESILIENCE**

KAUAI, Hawaii — The AEGIS Covenant has published a Hindu theological framework for electromagnetic planetary defense, published by Hinduism Today and authored by Swami Sarvapriyananda of the Vedanta Society, arguing that protecting the electrical grid is a direct expression of ahimsa — the principle of non-harm that is central to Hindu ethics.

"Ahimsa is often understood as refraining from violence toward living beings," writes Swami Sarvapriyananda. "But the most advanced understanding of ahimsa includes the positive obligation to prevent harm — to build systems and structures that protect life from threats both human and natural. A collapsed electrical grid would cause the deaths of millions: the medically dependent, the food-insecure, the water-deprived, the cold-exposed. Hardening that grid is ahimsa in action."

The framework connects electromagnetic resilience to multiple Hindu philosophical concepts: Ahimsa (non-harm — protecting the vulnerable from a preventable catastrophe), Dharma (righteous duty — the obligation of nations and communities to act on the knowledge that a CME is inevitable), Loka-samgraha (welfare of the world — the Coalition's global mutual-aid framework as an expression of universal welfare), and Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam (the world is one family — the recognition that a Carrington Event makes no distinctions of nationality, religion, or caste).

Hinduism Today has published the framework in its print and digital editions, and has committed to disseminating it through its global distribution network, which reaches Hindu communities in over 60 countries. The framework has also been endorsed by the Hindu American Foundation, which will incorporate it into its advocacy platform.
