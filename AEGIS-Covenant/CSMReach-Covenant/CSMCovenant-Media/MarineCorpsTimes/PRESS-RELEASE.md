**EXPEDITIONARY POWER: AEGIS COVENANT DEMONSTRATES MXENE-HARDENED MEU DEPLOYMENT CAPABILITY**

CAMP LEJEUNE, N.C. — The AEGIS Covenant has completed a field exercise demonstrating that a Marine Expeditionary Unit, retrofitted with MXene electromagnetic shielding, can maintain full operational capability during and after a simulated G5-class geomagnetic event.

The exercise, conducted at the Marine Corps Mountain Warfare Training Center, subjected a company-sized element of hardened vehicles, communications suites, and tactical power systems to simulated geomagnetically induced current conditions while executing a notional humanitarian assistance mission. The hardened element maintained 94% mission capability throughout the 72-hour exercise; an unshielded control element ceased effective operations within 18 minutes of simulated CME arrival.

"Marines fight from the sea, and they fight from austere positions," said Sgt. Maj. William Torres (Ret.), AEGIS Expeditionary Operations Lead. "What this exercise proved is that MXene shielding turns 'austere' from a vulnerability into a capability. When the grid is down everywhere, a hardened MEU is the only functioning military unit on the continent."

The exercise validated MXene hardening across three MEU capability domains: Vehicle Mobility (AAV and JLTV electronic control modules, LAV-25 power distribution), Communications (AN/PRC-117G radios, VSAT terminals, and tactical data links all maintained connectivity via MXene-shielded feedlines and power supplies), and Expeditionary Power (MXene-hardened generators and microgrid controllers enabled the MEU to establish — and export — a stable tactical power grid capable of supporting civilian critical infrastructure).

The II Marine Expeditionary Force has recommended the hardening protocol for adoption across all deploying MEUs beginning FY2027.

"Every Marine a rifleman," said Torres. "Now, every MEU a mobile microgrid."
