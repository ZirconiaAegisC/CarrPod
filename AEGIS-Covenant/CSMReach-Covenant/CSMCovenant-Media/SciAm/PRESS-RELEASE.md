HEADLINE: The Physics of Civilisational Blackout: Why a Single Coronal Mass Ejection Could Unravel Modern Society

LONDON — In a comprehensive white paper released today, Carrington Storm Motors' research division has detailed the precise physical cascade by which a Carrington-magnitude coronal mass ejection would disable modern civilisation—and why current mitigation efforts are orders of magnitude insufficient.

The paper, "Geomagnetically Induced Current Cascades in Interconnected Power Systems," walks through the failure chain step by step: CME detection at L1 (Lagrange point 1) gives roughly 15-60 minutes of warning; the interplanetary magnetic field couples with Earth's magnetosphere; geomagnetically induced currents enter long-distance transmission lines; transformers saturate within seconds; and cascading voltage collapse propagates across interconnects at near-light speed.

"People imagine a solar storm as a slow-building weather event," said Dr. Chester Villegas, the paper's lead author. "It is not. From detection to continental-scale blackout can be under 15 minutes. That changes everything about how we must prepare."

CSM's approach—hardened vehicles as distributed defence nodes—is designed specifically for this temporal constraint.
