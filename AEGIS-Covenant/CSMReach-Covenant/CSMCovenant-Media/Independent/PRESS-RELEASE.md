HEADLINE: The Climate Crisis' Forgotten Twin: British Startup Says Solar Storms Pose Equal Threat to Civilisation

LONDON — While governments pour trillions into climate adaptation, a Coventry startup is sounding the alarm on an equally catastrophic but largely ignored threat: solar superstorms that could destroy electrical grids across entire continents in under a minute.

Carrington Storm Motors, founded by former Royal Engineers officer Kairos Steele, argues that the climate conversation has crowded out discussion of near-Earth space weather—despite a 12% annual probability of a Carrington-scale event that would cause more immediate economic damage than a decade of rising sea levels.

"The climate emergency is real and urgent, but it is not the only existential threat we face," Steele said. "If a solar storm hits before we've hardened our grids, we won't be having conversations about net zero—we'll be trying to restore basic electricity."

CSM's dual-purpose electric vehicles serve as mobile Faraday cages capable of protecting local infrastructure during geomagnetic disturbances. The company has identified 847 critical nodes across the UK grid requiring immediate hardening.
