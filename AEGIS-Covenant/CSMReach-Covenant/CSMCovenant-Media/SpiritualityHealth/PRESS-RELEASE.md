**SPIRITUALITY & HEALTH: THE WELLNESS DIMENSION OF ELECTROMAGNETIC PREPAREDNESS**

CHICAGO — The AEGIS Covenant has published a guide for the Spirituality & Health audience, exploring the psychological and spiritual dimensions of electromagnetic preparedness — and arguing that personal resilience begins with acknowledging the vulnerability of the systems we depend on.

"Anxiety about the state of the world is one of the most commonly reported sources of spiritual distress in contemporary life," writes Dr. Patricia Okonkwo in the guide's introduction. "But anxiety unprocessed becomes paralysis. Anxiety integrated becomes preparation — and preparation, in turn, becomes peace. The AEGIS Covenant's electromagnetic defense framework is, at its deepest level, an invitation to transform existential dread into purposeful action."

The guide, "Grounded: Spiritual Resilience in the Electromagnetic Age," covers four dimensions: the psychological (managing eco-anxiety and infrastructure-anxiety through informed action rather than avoidance), the spiritual (finding meaning in the work of protecting creation and protecting the vulnerable, across multiple faith traditions), the practical (personal and family preparedness steps that reduce anxiety by increasing agency), and the communal (the spiritual benefits of joining community resilience efforts — the Veteran Response Corps, church preparedness programs, or neighborhood mutual-aid networks).

The guide features contributions from a psychologist specializing in climate anxiety, a hospital chaplain who works with medically dependent patients and their families, a Catholic sister who bridges physics and faith, and a Zen teacher who offers a meditation on "sitting with the hum."

"The most important spiritual practice in the electromagnetic age," concludes Dr. Okonkwo, "is learning to hold both the magnitude of the threat and the possibility of effective response in the same heart. That is the definition of grounded hope."
