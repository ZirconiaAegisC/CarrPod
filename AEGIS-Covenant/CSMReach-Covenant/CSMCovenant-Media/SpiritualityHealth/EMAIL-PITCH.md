SUBJECT: "Grounded" — transforming electromagnetic anxiety into purposeful action, a guide for the Spirituality & Health audience

Spirituality & Health Editorial Team,

Eco-anxiety. Infrastructure-anxiety. The low-level dread that hums beneath daily life like the grid itself. What if that anxiety could be transformed — not suppressed, not medicated — but integrated into purposeful action that actually protects what we're anxious about losing?

"Grounded: Spiritual Resilience in the Electromagnetic Age" is the AEGIS Covenant's guide for the Spirituality & Health reader — the person who meditates in the morning and worries about the world at night. Four dimensions: psychological (managing infrastructure-anxiety through informed action), spiritual (finding meaning across faith traditions), practical (personal preparedness steps), and communal (joining community resilience efforts).

Contributors include a climate-anxiety psychologist, a hospital chaplain, Sr. María Fernández (the physicist-nun), and Roshi Joan Halifax with a meditation on "sitting with the hum."
