HEADLINE: As India Powers Forward, a Texas Firm Builds the Shield Against the Sun's Next Superstorm

NEW DELHI — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, the world's first commercially tested geomagnetic storm protection system — a development with profound implications for India's rapidly expanding power infrastructure.

India's national grid, operated by Power Grid Corporation of India Limited, now serves over 1.4 billion people and has added more than 200 GW of generation capacity in the past decade. As the world's third-largest electricity producer integrates ever-greater renewable capacity, the system's exposure to Carrington-level geomagnetic disturbance grows proportionally.

"India is building the grid of tomorrow today. It deserves the protection of tomorrow today," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "Our modular AEGIS alloy technology can be deployed at any substation in any voltage class — it doesn't ask what language you speak or what frequency you run."

CSM was founded in 2024 and holds 14 patents pending. The company's AI-driven early-warning module integrates with international space-weather data and is designed for tropical and subtropical deployment environments.
