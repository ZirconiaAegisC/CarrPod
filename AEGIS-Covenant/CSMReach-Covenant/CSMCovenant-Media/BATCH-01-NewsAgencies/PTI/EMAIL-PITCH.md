An American startup has completed independent Phase II testing of the world's first commercially viable protection system against Carrington-level geomagnetic storms — a development directly relevant to Power Grid Corporation of India's infrastructure serving 1.4 billion people.

Carrington Storm Motors' Project AEGIS uses proprietary surge-diverting alloys deployed at substations to absorb geomagnetically induced currents before they reach transformers. The modular hardware is voltage-class agnostic and specifically designed for tropical and subtropical deployment. With Solar Cycle 26 approaching peak intensity, and India's grid adding renewable capacity at an unprecedented rate, the availability of tested grid protection is timely.
