HEADLINE: Africa's Grid Gets a Shield: Texas Firm Validates First Commercial Solar Storm Protection

JOHANNESBURG — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, the world's first commercially tested geomagnetic storm protection system — a development with critical implications for Africa's expanding and increasingly interconnected power infrastructure.

Eskom and the Southern African Power Pool (SAPP) manage transmission networks that span multiple nations, voltage standards, and terrain types. A Carrington-level geomagnetic event would not respect these borders. The AEGIS system's modular alloy technology is designed for deployment across diverse grid topologies, from South Africa's coal-fired backbone to the emerging renewable corridors of the Great Rift Valley.

"Energy access is the foundation of African development," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "Protecting that foundation against a solar threat that scientists agree is a matter of when, not if, is not a luxury — it's a prerequisite."

CSM was founded in 2024, is headquartered in Austin, Texas, and holds 14 patents pending across materials science and predictive geomagnetic modeling.
