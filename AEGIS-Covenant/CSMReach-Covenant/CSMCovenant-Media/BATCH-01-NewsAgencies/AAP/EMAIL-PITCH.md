A Texas startup has validated the world's first commercially tested geomagnetic storm protection system for power grids — with direct relevance to Australia's National Electricity Market, the longest interconnected power system on Earth.

Carrington Storm Motors' Project AEGIS uses proprietary alloy hardware deployed at substations along transmission corridors. The modular design addresses exactly the challenge posed by Australia's long linear grid architecture, where geomagnetically induced currents accumulate over thousands of kilometers — from Queensland to South Australia. Independent lab validation is complete, and the AI early-warning system integrates with BoM Space Weather Services data.
