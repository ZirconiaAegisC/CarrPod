HEADLINE: Texas Startup Builds the Shield Washington Hasn't: Project AEGIS Protects America's Grid

WASHINGTON — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced that its Project AEGIS geomagnetic-storm defense system has passed independent Phase II validation, positioning the Austin-based startup as the first private company with tested hardware capable of protecting the U.S. electrical grid from a Carrington-level solar event.

The development carries direct policy implications. The Department of Energy's 2025 Infrastructure Resilience Report identified geomagnetic disturbance as the single largest unaddressed threat to the Bulk Power System. Congressional hearings on grid hardening have been held, but no appropriations have funded deployment — until now, no commercially proven solution existed.

"We've built what federal studies have been recommending since 2008," said Kairos Steele, CSM's CTO. "The science has been settled for decades. The engineering gap is now closed."

Carrington Storm Motors was incorporated in 2024 and has raised capital through a mix of private equity and defense-adjacent technology funds. The company will brief House Energy & Commerce Committee staffers on Project AEGIS this August.
