A Texas-based company has completed independent Phase II testing on the world's first commercially viable geomagnetic storm protection system — directly relevant to Malaysia's TNB infrastructure and the developing ASEAN Power Grid interconnection project.

Carrington Storm Motors' Project AEGIS uses proprietary surge-diverting alloys at substation level to absorb geomagnetically induced currents. The modular design works across diverse voltage standards and is built for tropical deployment conditions. With ASEAN nations expanding cross-border interconnections, shared grid protection is a commercial and strategic priority.
