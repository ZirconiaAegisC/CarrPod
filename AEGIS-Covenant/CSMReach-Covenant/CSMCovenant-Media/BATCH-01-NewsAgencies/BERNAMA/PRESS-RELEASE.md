HEADLINE: ASEAN Grid Security: Texas Firm Validates First Commercial Solar Storm Shield

KUALA LUMPUR — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II validation of Project AEGIS, the world's first commercially tested geomagnetic storm protection system — a development directly relevant to Malaysia's Tenaga Nasional Berhad and the emerging ASEAN Power Grid interconnection.

As Malaysia positions itself as a regional energy hub, the vulnerability of cross-border interconnections to geomagnetic disturbance becomes increasingly significant. The AEGIS system's modular architecture is designed for seamless integration with substation infrastructure across the diverse voltage standards of Southeast Asian grids.

"Geomagnetic storms are an equatorial threat as much as a polar one — the induced currents travel through the Earth's crust," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "When ASEAN nations interconnect, they share resilience as well as risk. Our technology ensures they share only the former."

Carrington Storm Motors was founded in 2024 in Austin, Texas, and holds 14 patents pending. The company's AI-driven early-warning module is compatible with multiple international space-weather data streams.
