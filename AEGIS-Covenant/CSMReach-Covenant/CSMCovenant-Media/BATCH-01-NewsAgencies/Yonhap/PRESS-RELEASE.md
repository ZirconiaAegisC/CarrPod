HEADLINE: Korean Peninsula Grid Gets a Solar Storm Shield: Texas Firm Validates Project AEGIS

SEOUL — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II validation of Project AEGIS, the world's first commercially tested geomagnetic storm protection system — a development directly relevant to the Korean Peninsula's advanced but geographically constrained power grid.

Korea Electric Power Corporation (KEPCO) operates one of the world's most technologically sophisticated grids, yet its peninsular topology and limited cross-border interconnections create specific vulnerabilities to geomagnetically induced currents. The AEGIS system's modular architecture is designed for seamless integration with high-voltage transformer infrastructure without requiring grid redesign.

"The Korean grid is an engineering marvel, but it sits at a geomagnetic latitude where a Carrington event would hit hard," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "Our proprietary alloy technology absorbs GICs before they reach critical nodes. This is protection designed for exactly this type of advanced grid."

CSM was founded in Austin, Texas in 2024, holds 14 patents pending, and integrates AI-driven early-warning modules compatible with KMA and international space-weather data streams.
