**BUDDHISTDOOR: COMPASSION FOR THE UNPLUGGED — BUDDHIST ETHICS AND ELECTROMAGNETIC RESILIENCE**

HONG KONG — The AEGIS Covenant has released a Buddhist ethical framework for electromagnetic planetary defense, published by Buddhistdoor Global and authored by Venerable Thubten Chodron, arguing that protecting the electrical grid is an expression of karuna (compassion) and a practical application of the Bodhisattva vow to liberate all beings from suffering.

"When we take the Bodhisattva vow, we commit to working for the welfare of all sentient beings until every last one is free from suffering," writes Venerable Chodron. "In the modern world, that commitment must extend to the systems that sustain life. A Carrington-class CME would cause unimaginable suffering — hospitals without power, food systems without refrigeration, water systems without pumps. Working to prevent that suffering, through MXene hardening, through the Coalition's mutual-aid provisions, through the Veteran Response Corps's community-level organization — this is engaged Buddhism in the 21st century."

The framework connects electromagnetic resilience to core Buddhist concepts: Karuna (compassion — the active desire to relieve suffering, extended to the systemic level), Prajna (wisdom — understanding the interdependent origination of electrical infrastructure and human welfare), Upaya (skillful means — using science, engineering, and diplomacy as tools of compassionate action), and the Bodhisattva ideal (the commitment to work tirelessly for the welfare of all beings, across all domains, including infrastructure).

Buddhistdoor Global has published the framework in English and Chinese and has committed to a global webinar series featuring Buddhist teachers, scientists, and humanitarian leaders exploring the intersection of Dharma practice and planetary resilience.
