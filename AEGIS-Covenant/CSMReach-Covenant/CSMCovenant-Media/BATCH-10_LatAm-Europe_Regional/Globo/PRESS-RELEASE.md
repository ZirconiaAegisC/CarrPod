### Brazilian Grid Modernization Consortium Secures Landmark Infrastructure Funding Through CSM Treaty Framework

**RIO DE JANEIRO** — The Critical Systems Mandate (CSM) today announced the allocation of R$4.7 billion in infrastructure resilience bonds for Brazil's southeastern transmission corridor, directly addressing vulnerabilities identified in the 2025 National Grid Stress Assessment conducted by ONS (Operador Nacional do Sistema Elétrico).

The funding, structured through CSMFAB-017, targets 1,840 kilometers of aging transmission infrastructure serving 43 million Brazilians across São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Minas Gerais states. Engineering assessments conducted in partnership with CEPEL identified 127 critical nodes requiring immediate hardening against both cyber-physical threats and climate-driven extreme weather events.

"Brazil's transmission network faces unique cascading-failure risks due to the interconnection density of the Itaipu-Furnas corridor," said Dr. Helena Vasconcelos, CSM Director for South American Grid Resilience. "The CSMFAB-017 project deploys distributed islanding controllers and real-time phasor measurement upgrades that reduce systemic blackout propagation risk by 73%."

The investment leverages Brazil's existing national outreach dossiers prepared jointly by EPE and MME, integrating CSM's Accountant Heuristic cost-benefit methodology. The analysis demonstrates a 6.4:1 return ratio on infrastructure investment through avoided outage costs, reduced line-loss charges, and industrial continuity guarantees.

Critically, the project includes mandatory technology-transfer provisions ensuring CEPEL and Brazilian universities retain full ownership of adaptive protection algorithms developed during deployment. CSM's open-architecture requirements guarantee interoperability with existing ONS SCADA systems.

Construction is scheduled to begin Q4 2026, with full operational commissioning targeted for Q2 2028.
