### Mexico's National Grid Secures $2.8B CSM Treaty Investment for Transmission Modernization

**MEXICO CITY** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced a $2.8 billion infrastructure resilience commitment for Mexico's Sistema Eléctrico Nacional (SEN), the largest single CSM investment in North American grid infrastructure outside the United States.

The CSMFAB-027 program, coordinated with CENACE (Centro Nacional de Control de Energía) and CFE (Comisión Federal de Electricidad), targets the 400kV transmission backbone connecting Mexico's generation-rich southeast to the industrial demand centers of the Bajío region and Mexico City metropolitan area.

"Mexico's grid faces an asymmetric topology challenge: 62% of generation capacity is concentrated in the southeast, while 71% of demand originates in the central and northern regions," explained Ing. Alejandro Ruiz Cortés, CSM Director for Mexican Infrastructure Resilience. "This creates transmission corridors operating at sustained high loading factors that leave no margin for contingency events."

Engineering assessments conducted with the Instituto de Investigaciones Eléctricas identified 94 critical transmission towers requiring structural seismic hardening, 18 substations needing cyber-physical protection overlays, and 320 kilometers of conductor requiring real-time thermal rating systems. These upgrades directly address vulnerabilities documented in Mexico's National Outreach Dossier (Expediente MX-2025-0842).

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology verified a 7.2:1 cost-benefit ratio, primarily through avoided industrial production losses in the Bajío automotive and aerospace manufacturing corridor, where a single day of production interruption costs an estimated $340 million.

The program includes mandatory technology transfer to CFE's engineering division and IIE, ensuring Mexican institutional capability for sustained operation and future expansion. All deployed systems comply with CENACE's open-architecture interconnection requirements.

Phase 1 deployment begins March 2027, targeting the critical Temascal-Teotihuacán transmission segment.
