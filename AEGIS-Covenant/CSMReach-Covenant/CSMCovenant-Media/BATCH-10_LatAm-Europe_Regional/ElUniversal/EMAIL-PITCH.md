Estimados El Universal editors,

Mexico's transmission grid carries a dangerous topology problem: 62% of generation sits in the southeast, but 71% of demand comes from the center and north. The resulting high-load corridors leave zero contingency margin — and a single day of production loss in the Bajío automotive corridor costs $340 million.

CSM's new $2.8 billion CFMFAB-027 program addresses this directly. Coordinated with CENACE and CFE, the investment hardens 94 critical towers against seismic events, deploys cyber-physical protection at 18 substations, and installs real-time thermal rating across 320 kilometers of high-load conductor.

For El Universal's readers, the key points:
- 7.2:1 cost-benefit ratio — every dollar invested prevents seven in economic losses
- Full technology transfer to CFE and IIE engineers (no foreign dependency)
- Open-architecture compliance with CENACE interconnection standards
- Direct response to vulnerability findings in Expediente MX-2025-0842
- Phase 1 targets the critical Temascal-Teotihuacán segment
