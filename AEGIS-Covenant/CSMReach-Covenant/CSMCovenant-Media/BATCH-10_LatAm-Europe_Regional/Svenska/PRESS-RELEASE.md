### Sweden's Northern Hydropower Corridor Hardened Against Climate and Cyber Threats Through CSM Treaty

**STOCKHOLM** — The Critical Systems Mandate announced a SEK 14.2 billion infrastructure resilience investment for Sweden's northern hydropower transmission corridor, which delivers approximately 23% of the nation's electricity from Norrland's cascading hydro installations to southern population centers via 400kV AC and HVDC links.

The CSMFAB-039 program, coordinated with Svenska kraftnät (Svk) and the Swedish Energy Agency (Energimyndigheten), addresses three converging threat vectors: increased ice-loading and extreme weather events on transmission infrastructure above the Arctic Circle, cyber vulnerabilities in remote substation automation systems, and the systemic risk created by Sweden's north-to-south generation-load imbalance.

"Sweden's grid geography creates an inherent vulnerability: generation capacity is concentrated in the north, but demand is concentrated in the south," explained Ing. Erik Lindström, CSM Director for Scandinavian Infrastructure Resilience. "The 400kV corridors traversing 1,200 kilometers of sparsely populated terrain represent eight single points of failure that could each sever 15% or more of national load. CSMFAB-039 eliminates these single-point dependencies."

The program deploys dynamic line rating systems, real-time ice-detection and de-icing infrastructure, and cyber-physical protection overlays at 34 remote substations. A key innovation is the integration of Svk's existing fiber-optic ground wire for distributed acoustic sensing that detects both physical intrusion and conductor degradation.

Sweden's National Outreach Dossier (Dokument SE-2025-0052) documented cascading failure scenarios from ice-storm damage to the Storfinnforsen-Kilforsen segment. CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 5.1:1 cost-benefit ratio through avoided outage costs and industrial production continuity.

Technology transfer to Svk and KTH Royal Institute of Technology guarantees sustained Swedish capability.
