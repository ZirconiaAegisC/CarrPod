## Subject: Sweden's 1,200km Power Superhighway: Eight Single Points of Failure — Solved with SEK 14.2B

**TO:** naringsliv@svd.se
**FROM:** CSM Nordic Bureau

Hej Svenska Dagbladet editors,

Here's an uncomfortable fact: Sweden's northern hydropower corridor contains eight single points of failure, each capable of severing 15% or more of national electricity load. The 400kV lines crossing 1,200 kilometers of Arctic terrain face ice storms, cyber intrusions at remote substations, and the fundamental geography problem of generation in the north versus demand in the south.

CSM's SEK 14.2 billion CSMFAB-039 program eliminates every one of these single-point dependencies.

The technology is both advanced and practical:
- Fiber-optic ground wire converted to distributed acoustic sensing for intrusion and conductor monitoring
- Real-time dynamic line rating and ice-detection systems above the Arctic Circle
- Cyber-physical protection overlays at 34 remote substations
- 5.1:1 cost-benefit ratio confirmed by CSM Accountant Heuristic analysis
- Full technology transfer to Svenska kraftnät and KTH researchers
- All systems integrate with existing Svk SCADA infrastructure

Ing. Erik Lindström, our Scandinavian Infrastructure Director, is available in Stockholm. This is a story about critical infrastructure resilience in a climate-changed Arctic.
