## Subject: Melting Glaciers Are Moving Austria's Power Towers — CSM's €1.3B Fix

Sehr geehrte Die Presse editors,

APG's own monitoring data reveals a startling finding: 23 transmission towers above 2,000 meters in the Austrian Alps are already experiencing measurable foundation displacement due to permafrost degradation and accelerated glacial retreat. These towers carry 60% of Austria's electricity.

CSM's €1.3 billion CSMFAB-045 program provides the engineering response.

We are deploying 340 rock-anchor reinforcements at tower foundations in permafrost-degradation zones, Doppler radar debris-flow early warning systems protecting 12 alpine substations, and adaptive turbine controls that compensate for reservoir level volatility driven by changing precipitation patterns.

This is a climate-adaptation story with concrete infrastructure implications:
- 23 towers already moving — documented in Dokument AT-2025-0034
- 47 alpine hydropower stations analyzed for climate vulnerability
- 4.2:1 cost-benefit ratio verified by CSM Accountant Heuristic analysis
- Technology transfer to TU Wien, University of Innsbruck, and APG
- All systems integrate with existing Verbund control infrastructure
- Debris-flow early warning uses Doppler radar with 90-second detection latency
