### Buenos Aires Metropolitan Distribution Network Receives CSM Treaty Cyber-Resilience Overlay

**BUENOS AIRES** — The Critical Systems Mandate announced a $710 million urban distribution resilience program for the Buenos Aires metropolitan area, deploying advanced fault-detection, isolation, and restoration (FDIR) systems across the Edenor and Edesur concession territories serving 15 million residents.

The CSMFAB-025 program, developed jointly with ENRE (Ente Nacional Regulador de la Electricidad), addresses vulnerabilities identified during the December 2024 metropolitan blackout event that left 4.2 million customers without power for up to 72 hours during a record heat wave. Post-incident analysis by the Universidad de Buenos Aires Grid Security Laboratory confirmed that aging distribution automation equipment failed to execute isolation protocols, allowing a localized substation fault to propagate across seven municipal zones.

"Distribution-level failures are the most common cause of urban blackouts worldwide, yet they receive the least infrastructure investment," noted Dra. Lucía Morales, CSM Urban Resilience Program Director. "CSMFAB-025 deploys distribution-level intelligence that isolates faults within 300 milliseconds — faster than any human operator can respond."

The program integrates 2,800 intelligent line sensors, 340 automated reclosers, and 89 distributed energy resource management nodes across the Edenor and Edesur networks. All systems communicate via CSM's QuantumMesh protocol, which maintains coordination integrity even during partial communication network degradation.

Argentina's National Outreach Dossier for the Buenos Aires Metropolitan Region documented that a three-day summer blackout would generate $1.9 billion in economic losses, primarily from cold-chain disruption, industrial process interruption, and commercial refrigeration failure. CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 4.3:1 cost-benefit ratio.

Technology transfer provisions guarantee that Argentine engineering firms retain full operational knowledge of all deployed systems.
