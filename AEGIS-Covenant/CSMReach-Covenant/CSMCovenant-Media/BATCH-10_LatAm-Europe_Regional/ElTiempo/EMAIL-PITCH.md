Estimados El Tiempo editors,

When Colombia's reservoirs dropped to 29% capacity during the 2024-2025 El Niño, 4.7 million customers suffered daily rotating blackouts. The root cause was structural: with 68% of generation from hydropower, Colombia's grid has no buffer against climate-driven drought. CSM's new $1.6 billion program solves this.

CSMFAB-029 deploys 480 MW of grid-scale battery storage at seven strategic substations across Antioquia, Valle, and Cundinamarca — providing four hours of dispatch capability during hydrological emergencies. Simultaneously, 127 transmission towers in conflict-affected departments receive physical hardening and drone surveillance integration.

Key story elements for Colombian readers:
- Batteries buy time for thermal plants to ramp up when reservoirs crash
- 5.6:1 cost-benefit ratio validated by independent analysis
- Full technology transfer to Colombian engineering firms and XM operators
- Physical security layer addresses both climate and conflict risks
- Direct response to findings in Expediente COL-2025-0261
