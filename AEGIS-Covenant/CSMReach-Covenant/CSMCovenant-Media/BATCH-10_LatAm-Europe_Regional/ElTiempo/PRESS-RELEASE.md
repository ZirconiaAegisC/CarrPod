### Colombian Hydropower-Dependent Grid Receives Climate-Resilience Overlay Through CSM Treaty

**BOGOTÁ** — The Critical Systems Mandate announced a $1.6 billion grid resilience investment for Colombia's Sistema Interconectado Nacional (SIN), specifically designed to mitigate the systemic risk created by the nation's 68% dependence on hydropower generation in an era of increasing hydrological variability.

The CSMFAB-029 program, developed in coordination with XM Compañía de Expertos en Mercados and the Unidad de Planeación Minero Energética (UPME), creates a multi-layer resilience architecture that combines enhanced inter-regional transfer capability, distributed battery energy storage, and demand-side flexibility aggregation across Colombia's five operational grid regions.

"Colombia's grid faces a unique compound risk: hydrological volatility threatens generation adequacy at the same time that guerrilla and criminal organizations target transmission infrastructure in historically conflict-affected departments," stated Dr. Carlos Eduardo Vélez, CSM Director for Andean Region Infrastructure. "CSMFAB-029 addresses both dimensions simultaneously through distributed storage and hardened physical security layers."

The program deploys 480 MW of grid-scale battery storage at seven strategic substation locations across Antioquia, Valle del Cauca, and Cundinamarca, providing four-hour dispatch capability that bridges the gap between hydrological shortfall detection and thermal generation ramp-up. Additionally, 127 critical transmission towers in Cauca, Nariño, and Norte de Santander receive physical hardening and drone-based surveillance integration.

Colombia's National Outreach Dossier (Expediente COL-2025-0261) documented that the 2024-2025 El Niño-driven drought caused reservoir levels to drop to 29% of capacity, triggering daily rotating blackouts affecting 4.7 million customers. CSM's Accountant Heuristic analysis verified a 5.6:1 cost-benefit ratio.

Technology transfer provisions guarantee Colombian engineering firms and XM retain full operational knowledge of all deployed systems.
