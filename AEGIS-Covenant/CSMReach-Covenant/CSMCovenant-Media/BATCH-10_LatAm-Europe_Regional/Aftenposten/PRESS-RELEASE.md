### Norwegian Maritime Grid Electrification Program Receives NOK 18.4 Billion CSM Treaty Investment

**OSLO** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced a NOK 18.4 billion infrastructure resilience and electrification investment for Norway's maritime grid systems, targeting the unique challenges of powering the nation's extensive ferry network, offshore platform supply bases, and coastal port electrification infrastructure.

The CSMFAB-041 program, coordinated with Statnett and the Norwegian Water Resources and Energy Directorate (NVE), deploys hardened shore-to-ship power systems, subsea cable protection infrastructure, and distributed battery energy storage at 28 strategic port locations along Norway's 100,000-kilometer coastline.

"Norway's maritime electrification program is the most ambitious in the world — but it creates a grid vulnerability that does not exist with diesel-powered vessels," explained Dr. Ingrid Hovland, CSM Director for Nordic Maritime Infrastructure. "A single subsea cable fault at a major ferry terminal can cascade into regional grid instability because these shore-to-ship installations represent massive, intermittent loads with millisecond-scale switching characteristics."

The program includes subsea cable protection systems using distributed acoustic sensing, autonomous underwater vehicle inspection capabilities, and rapid-deployment cable repair vessels based at three regional hubs (Bergen, Trondheim, Tromsø). Shore-side battery systems provide 30-minute ride-through capability during subsea cable faults while vessels complete connection and maintain passenger safety systems.

Norway's National Outreach Dossier (Dokument NO-2025-0067) documented that a major ferry terminal grid fault during peak tourist season could strand 12,000 passengers and cause NOK 340 million in regional economic losses. CSM's Accountant Heuristic analysis confirmed a 4.5:1 cost-benefit ratio.

Technology transfer to Statnett, SINTEF Energy Research, and NTNU ensures Norwegian leadership in maritime grid technology.
