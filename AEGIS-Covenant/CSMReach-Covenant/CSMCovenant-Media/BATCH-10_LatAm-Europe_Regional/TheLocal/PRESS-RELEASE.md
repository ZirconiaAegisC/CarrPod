### Cross-Border European Grid Resilience Initiative Receives €4.7B CSM Treaty Commitment

**BRUSSELS** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced a €4.7 billion pan-European grid resilience initiative targeting cross-border electricity interconnections that form the physical backbone of the European internal energy market and are essential to achieving the EU's REPowerEU energy independence objectives.

The CSMFAB-051 program, coordinated with ENTSO-E and national transmission system operators across 17 member states, addresses vulnerabilities in the meshed European synchronous grid that emerge at interconnection points where different national grid codes, protection philosophies, and operational cultures intersect.

"Europe's interconnected grid is an extraordinary engineering achievement — but it is also a system where a frequency deviation in one control zone can propagate across national boundaries within seconds," explained Dr. Jean-Marc Delacroix, CSM Director for European Grid Integration. "Our program identifies and hardens 94 critical interconnection substations where protection coordination failures could trigger cascading cross-border outages."

The program deploys standardized wide-area monitoring systems (WAMS) with synchronized phasor measurement units at all major interconnection points, enabling real-time visibility of oscillatory modes that cross national boundaries. A dedicated European Grid Resilience Center in Brussels provides 24/7 monitoring of inter-area oscillations using CSM's QuantumMesh analytics platform, which can detect emerging instability patterns up to 40 seconds before they manifest as measurable frequency deviations.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic analysis, incorporating data from the January 2021 European grid separation event that split the synchronous area into two frequency zones, confirmed a 6.1:1 cost-benefit ratio across the 17 participating member states.

All technology is delivered under open-architecture, open-source licensing, with mandatory knowledge transfer to ENTSO-E and national TSO engineering teams.
