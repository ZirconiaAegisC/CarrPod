### French Nuclear Fleet Grid Integration Receives €2.3B CSM Treaty Resilience Investment

**PARIS** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced a €2.3 billion infrastructure resilience investment for France's nuclear-dependent electrical grid, specifically targeting the stability risks that emerged during the 2022-2023 period when 32 of France's 56 nuclear reactors were simultaneously offline for maintenance and corrosion inspections.

The CSMFAB-053 program, coordinated with RTE (Réseau de Transport d'Électricité) and the Autorité de Sûreté Nucléaire (ASN), deploys advanced grid stability systems, cross-border interconnection reinforcement, and distributed energy resource aggregation platforms that provide the operational flexibility France's grid requires during periods of reduced nuclear availability.

"France's grid was architected around the assumption of always-available nuclear baseload — an assumption that 2022-2023 demonstrated is not valid in an era of aging fleet maintenance requirements and climate-change impacts on river-water cooling capacity," explained Dr. Amélie Dubois, CSM Director for French Infrastructure Resilience. "CSMFAB-053 deploys the flexibility infrastructure that enables the grid to maintain stability when 40% or more of nuclear capacity is simultaneously offline."

The program includes synchronous condenser installations at four strategic nuclear plant sites undergoing extended maintenance, 2,400 MW of distributed battery storage providing frequency containment reserve capability, and advanced river-temperature monitoring systems that provide early warning of cooling-water limitations during heat waves.

France's National Outreach Dossier (Document FR-2025-0047) documented that a repetition of 2022's nuclear availability crisis under projected 2030 demand conditions would require importing 18 GW from neighboring grids — exceeding existing interconnection capacity. CSM's Accountant Heuristic analysis confirmed a 5.8:1 cost-benefit ratio.

Technology transfer to RTE, EDF, and CentraleSupélec guarantees French institutional capability.
