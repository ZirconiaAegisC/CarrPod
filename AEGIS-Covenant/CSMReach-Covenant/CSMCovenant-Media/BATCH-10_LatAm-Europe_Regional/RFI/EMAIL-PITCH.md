When 32 of France's 56 nuclear reactors were simultaneously offline in 2022, the grid survived — but RTE's own projections show that under 2030 demand conditions, the same scenario would require importing 18 GW from neighbors, exceeding total existing interconnection capacity. France's grid was designed around always-available nuclear baseload. That assumption no longer holds.

CSM's €2.3 billion CSMFAB-053 program deploys the flexibility infrastructure this new reality demands: synchronous condensers at nuclear sites during extended maintenance, 2,400 MW of distributed battery storage for frequency containment, and river-temperature monitoring that provides early warning of cooling-water limitations during heat waves.
