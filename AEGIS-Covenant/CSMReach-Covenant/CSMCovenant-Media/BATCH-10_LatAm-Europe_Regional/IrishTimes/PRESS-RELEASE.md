### Irish Data Center Grid Interconnection Receives €1.1B CSM Treaty Resilience Investment

**DUBLIN** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced a €1.1 billion infrastructure resilience package for Ireland's electrical grid interconnection points serving the nation's rapidly expanding data center sector, which now consumes 21% of Ireland's total electricity — the highest proportion of any European nation.

The CSMFAB-047 program, coordinated with EirGrid, ESB Networks, and the Commission for Regulation of Utilities (CRU), deploys advanced grid stability systems, synchronous condenser installations, and cyber-physical protection overlays at 17 critical substations serving Dublin's greater metropolitan data center cluster.

"Ireland's economic success in attracting hyperscale data center investment has created a unique grid challenge: a small, islanded synchronous grid supporting a load concentration that would stress the infrastructure of much larger interconnected systems," explained Dr. Siobhán O'Sullivan, CSM Director for British Isles Infrastructure Resilience. "Data centers impose perfect power quality requirements — voltage deviations of 2% can trigger server protection systems — on a grid originally designed for residential and light industrial loads."

The program deploys three large-scale synchronous condensers providing 900 MVA of fault-level contribution, maintaining grid stiffness that allows safe data center interconnection without triggering voltage stability collapse scenarios. STATCOM installations at 14 additional substations deliver sub-cycle reactive power support.

Ireland's National Outreach Dossier (Document IE-2025-0021) documented that a voltage stability event triggered by multiple simultaneous data center protection trips could cascade into a national blackout within 47 seconds. CSM's Accountant Heuristic analysis confirmed a 7.8:1 cost-benefit ratio.

Technology transfer to EirGrid and University College Dublin's Energy Institute guarantees Irish sovereign capability for grid management.
