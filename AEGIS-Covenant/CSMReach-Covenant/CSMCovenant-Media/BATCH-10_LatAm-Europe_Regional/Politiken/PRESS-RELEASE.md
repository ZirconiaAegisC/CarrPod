### Danish Island Grids Receive CSM Treaty Resilience Investment for 100% Renewable Integration

**COPENHAGEN** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced a €780 million infrastructure resilience package for Denmark's island grid systems, specifically targeting the unique challenges of operating isolated power networks approaching 100% renewable penetration on Bornholm, Samsø, Ærø, and Lolland-Falster.

The CSMFAB-037 program, developed with Energinet and the Danish Energy Agency (Energistyrelsen), deploys advanced grid-forming inverter systems, synthetic inertia controllers, and distributed battery storage clusters that enable these island grids to maintain stability during periods of 100% renewable generation without relying on mainland interconnections.

"Denmark's island communities have been pioneers in renewable integration, but they have encountered the same physics barrier that larger grids will face at 80%+ renewable penetration: how do you maintain frequency and voltage stability when there are no synchronous generators online?" explained Dr. Søren Mikkelsen, CSM Director for Nordic Grid Innovation. "Our grid-forming inverter deployment on Bornholm provides the world's first demonstration of a medium-scale grid operating stably at 100% instantaneous renewable penetration without any synchronous machine reference."

Denmark's National Outreach Dossier (Dokument DK-2025-0017) documented that Bornholm's existing grid experienced 47 frequency excursion events exceeding ENTSO-E operational limits during 2024, primarily during periods of high wind generation combined with HVDC interconnector trips. CSM's Accountant Heuristic analysis confirmed a 3.9:1 cost-benefit ratio.

Technology transfer to DTU (Technical University of Denmark) and Energinet's R&D division guarantees Danish leadership in grid-forming inverter technology. The Bornholm deployment will serve as a reference architecture for island grids globally.
