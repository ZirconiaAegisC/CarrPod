Switzerland faces a grid transition paradox: nuclear plants providing 35% of baseload are being retired, while alpine hydropower — historically the perfect complement — now suffers its own climate-driven output volatility. The result? Swissgrid projects that winter import dependency could reach 42% of national consumption by 2030.

CSM's CHF 1.6 billion CSMFAB-049 program provides the answer.

A key innovation: converting the turbine halls at Gösgen and Leibstadt into synchronous condenser installations during decommissioning, rather than dismantling their electrical infrastructure. These grid stabilization facilities replace the inertia that nuclear turbines provided, maintaining frequency stability without burning a single fuel rod.
