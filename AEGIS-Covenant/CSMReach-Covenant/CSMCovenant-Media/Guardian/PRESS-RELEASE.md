HEADLINE: The Great Grid Undressing: How a Coventry Startup Exposed the UK's Catastrophic Solar Storm Blind Spot

LONDON — A freedom-of-information investigation by Carrington Storm Motors has revealed that 73% of Britain's electricity substations lack any form of electromagnetic pulse protection, leaving the country perilously exposed to a solar superstorm that statisticians at the firm say is overdue by roughly 80 years.

CSM's analysis, verified by independent researchers at Imperial College London, shows that a Carrington-magnitude coronal mass ejection would destroy approximately 350 high-voltage transformers across the UK within 90 seconds of impact—components that typically require 12-18 months to manufacture and replace.

"Government has spent billions on cybersecurity while ignoring the one threat that could physically melt the grid," said Kairos Steele, CSM's director. "A solar storm doesn't need a password. It doesn't care about firewalls. It just needs magnetic field lines—and we've given it a perfect path."

The company's electric vehicle platform doubles as a mobile Faraday cage, with each unit containing regulators capable of stabilising local grid segments during geomagnetic disturbances.
