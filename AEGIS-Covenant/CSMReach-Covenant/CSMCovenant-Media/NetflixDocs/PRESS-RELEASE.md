**STREAMING THE SUN: AEGIS COVENANT PITCHES THE FIRST GLOBAL GEOMAGNETIC DOCUSERIES**

LOS ANGELES — The AEGIS Covenant has released a comprehensive docuseries production bible, "Aegis," detailing an 8-episode streaming series following the 18 Agents of the Covenant as they race to implement planetary-scale electromagnetic defenses before the next solar maximum.

The series bible, available to qualified streaming platforms and production companies, outlines a narrative structure that weaves together multiple genres: scientific thriller (the MXene research teams pushing quantum materials from lab to factory floor), geopolitical drama (the 22-nation diplomatic effort to establish the Global Geomagnetic Crisis Response Coalition), military logistics (the Charlemagne fleet preparing for amphibious humanitarian delivery), and personal character arcs (each of the 18 Agents has a distinct origin story, motivation, and conflict).

"This is not a science documentary," said the bible's co-author, an Emmy-nominated series producer who has joined the Covenant's media advisory board. "This is 'The Martian' meets 'The West Wing' meets 'Planet Earth' — with actual stakes. Every episode ends on a question the viewer needs the next episode to answer."

The series has already attracted informal interest from three major streaming platforms. Filming locations span 14 countries, including MXene foundries in South Korea and Germany, the Charlemagne fleet's home port in Setúbal, Portugal, the Aurora Monitoring Network observatory in Tromsø, Norway, and the Vatican, where the Covenant's religious NGO dossier was presented to the Pontifical Academy of Sciences.

The production bible also includes detailed VFX specifications for visualizing the 45-micron MXene films — transparent, flexible, and capable of shielding entire substations — and for rendering scientifically accurate CME arrival sequences.
