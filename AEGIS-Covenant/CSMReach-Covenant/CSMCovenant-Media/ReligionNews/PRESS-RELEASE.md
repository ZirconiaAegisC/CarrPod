**RELIGION NEWS SERVICE: INTERFAITH COALITION UNITES BEHIND ELECTROMAGNETIC PLANETARY DEFENSE**

WASHINGTON, D.C. — In an unprecedented display of interfaith cooperation, the AEGIS Covenant has assembled a multifaith coalition spanning Catholicism, Protestantism, Judaism, Islam, Hinduism, and Buddhism — all united behind the theological imperative to protect the vulnerable from electromagnetic catastrophe.

The Interfaith Declaration on Geomagnetic Stewardship, signed by religious leaders from six traditions, draws on each faith's distinctive theological language while converging on a single practical conclusion: hardening the electrical grid is a moral obligation that transcends doctrinal difference.

"When you strip away the theology, every tradition arrives at the same place: protect the vulnerable," said Rabbi David Stern, a signatory from the Reform movement. "For Jews, it's pikuach nefesh — the preservation of life overrides nearly every other commandment. If hardening a transformer saves lives — and it does — there is no religious argument against doing it."

The Declaration's signatories include: Cardinal Peter Kodwo Appiah Turkson (Catholic), Rev. Dr. Samuel Park (Evangelical), Rabbi David Stern (Reform Judaism), Imam Abdullah Hassan (Sunni Islam), Swami Sarvapriyananda (Vedanta Society), and Venerable Thubten Chodron (Tibetan Buddhist). Each contributed a theological reflection from their tradition's perspective: Catholic stewardship, Evangelical dominion-keeping, Jewish pikuach nefesh, Islamic zakat and protection of the ummah, Hindu ahimsa, and Buddhist compassion.

The Declaration will be formally presented at the Parliament of the World's Religions in October.
