## Protecting the Global Grid: A View from Beijing

BEIJING — As the world's largest manufacturer of power transformers and a leader in ultra-high-voltage transmission technology, China has both a unique vulnerability to geomagnetic disturbance and a unique capacity to contribute to global solutions.

CSM-Covenant's technical assessment of China's grid infrastructure reveals that while the country's transformer manufacturing capability is world-class, the installed base of equipment in service lacks systematic GMD hardening. The gap between China's manufacturing capacity and its domestic protection status represents both a risk and an opportunity.

"China produces roughly 40% of the world's large power transformers," said Dr. Wei Zhang. "That manufacturing expertise could be leveraged to integrate GMD protection into new production — hardening transformers at the point of manufacture rather than retrofitting them in the field. That would reduce costs by an estimated 60% and accelerate global deployment by a factor of three."

CSM-Covenant is proposing a technology transfer partnership with Chinese manufacturers to incorporate HYDRA-compatible neutral grounding design into new transformer production. The partnership would benefit China's domestic grid while positioning Chinese manufacturers at the forefront of the emerging global market for GMD-resistant transformer technology.
