## India's Grid: An Investigative Look at an Unquantified National Risk

MUMBAI — The Indian Express has a proud tradition of investigative journalism that holds institutions accountable and exposes vulnerabilities before they become catastrophes. CSM-Covenant's AEGIS assessment of India's power grid presents precisely such an opportunity: a previously unquantified national risk that demands public scrutiny and policy action.

The investigation reveals that:

- No Indian transformer currently in service has been specifically hardened against geomagnetic-induced currents.
- The Power Grid Corporation of India has not published a comprehensive GMD risk assessment.
- India's transformer manufacturing capacity — while world-class — would be overwhelmed by the demand surge following a multi-site GMD failure.
- Critical substations in seismic zones face compound risk: earthquake damage followed by GMD-induced failure during the recovery period.

"We're not pointing fingers," said Dr. Patel. "India's grid operators have done extraordinary work expanding access and improving reliability. GMD hardening is simply the next frontier — and one that hasn't received the attention it deserves because the threat is invisible, slow-moving, and statistically unusual. But invisible doesn't mean imaginary."

CSM-Covenant is making its full AEGIS India dataset available to journalists, including substation-level vulnerability scores, transmission corridor risk maps, and economic impact projections at the state level.
