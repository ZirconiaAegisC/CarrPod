## Science and Security: India's Grid Needs a Geomagnetic Audit

CHENNAI — The Hindu's readership expects rigorous, evidence-based reporting on issues that matter to India's future. CSM-Covenant's AEGIS threat assessment for the Indian subcontinent delivers exactly that: a peer-reviewed, data-driven analysis of the geomagnetic vulnerability facing the world's largest synchronous grid.

The analysis incorporates data from the Indian Institute of Geomagnetism, ISRO's solar observation program, and the Power Grid Corporation's own substation specifications. It models three scenarios: a moderate GMD event (100-year return period), a severe event (Carrington-scale), and an extreme event (theoretical maximum based on stellar analogs).

The results for the severe scenario are sobering: 130+ large power transformers at risk of catastrophic failure; cascading blackouts affecting an estimated 800 million people; and an economic impact exceeding $800 billion in the first year alone. The extreme scenario — while far less probable — would effectively collapse the Indian grid for a decade.

"India has world-class scientific institutions," said Dr. Patel. "ISRO, IIG, IIT — the expertise exists. What's missing is the translational link between that scientific knowledge and infrastructure policy. CSM-Covenant was created to forge that link."

The report recommends a National Geomagnetic Resilience Mission, modeled on India's successful space and nuclear programs, to coordinate GMD hardening across the country's critical substations.
