## Hong Kong's Power Grid: The Vulnerability Behind the Vertical City

HONG KONG — Hong Kong's skyline depends on two power companies — CLP Power and Hongkong Electric — operating a grid that must serve one of the most densely populated urban environments on Earth. That grid, CSM-Covenant's analysis reveals, carries a geomagnetic risk profile that has not been publicly assessed or addressed.

Hong Kong's position at relatively low geomagnetic latitude provides partial protection against the most severe GMD effects. However, the city's grid is connected to the China Southern Power Grid through the Daya Bay nuclear plant transmission corridor, creating a pathway for geomagnetic currents originating across the broader East Asian continental landmass.

"The interconnection that ensures Hong Kong's energy security — the link to the China Southern Power Grid — is the same interconnection that creates a conduit for geomagnetic risk," said Dr. Wei Zhang. "Physics doesn't respect borders, and neither do induced currents."

CSM-Covenant's AEGIS model identifies three critical substations in the Hong Kong SAR where HYDRA hardening would significantly reduce the cascading failure risk. The cost is approximately HKD 180 million — less than one-tenth of one percent of the Hong Kong government's annual infrastructure budget.
