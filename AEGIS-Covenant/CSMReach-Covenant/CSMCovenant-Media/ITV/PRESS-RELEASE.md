HEADLINE: Coventry Engineer Who Served in Iraq and Afghanistan Returns Home to Fight an Invisible Enemy: The Sun

COVENTRY — Kairos Steele spent 14 years in the Royal Engineers, keeping power running through combat zones from Basra to Helmand. Now he is fighting a different kind of threat from a converted hangar in the West Midlands: solar superstorms capable of plunging civilisation into darkness.

"The irony isn't lost on me," Steele said at CSM's headquarters today. "I spent my career protecting electrical infrastructure from human threats. Now I'm protecting it from a star 93 million miles away—and frankly, the star scares me more."

Carrington Storm Motors, named after the 1859 solar event that set Victorian telegraph stations ablaze, has developed a fleet of electromagnetically hardened electric vehicles. Each one serves as a mobile grid-defence unit, capable of absorbing and dissipating the geomagnetic surges that would destroy unprotected transformers.

Steele, whose family still lives in Coventry, said his motivation was personal: "I've seen what happens when the lights go out. In a combat zone, it's dangerous. In a modern city of 400,000 people, it's catastrophic."
