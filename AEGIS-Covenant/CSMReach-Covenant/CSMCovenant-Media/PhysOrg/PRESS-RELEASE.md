HEADLINE: Novel Silicon-Carbide MOSFET Array Achieves 50kA Geomagnetic Surge Absorption in Compact EV Form Factor

COVENTRY, UK — Researchers at Carrington Storm Motors have demonstrated a power electronics breakthrough: a three-phase silicon-carbide MOSFET array capable of absorbing geomagnetically induced currents up to 50,000 amperes, packaged within a standard electric vehicle skateboard platform.

The array, described in a technical paper submitted to the IEEE, uses a novel switching topology that allows the system to transition from normal vehicle operation to "shield mode" in under 400 milliseconds—fast enough to respond to the steepest CME shock fronts observed in modern solar cycles.

"What makes this topology novel is its dual-mode operation," explained Dr. Chester Villegas, the paper's lead author. "During normal driving, the MOSFET array functions as a standard traction inverter. During a geomagnetic event, it reconfigures into a sink for ground-induced currents, shunting excess energy through a copper-nickel thermal dissipation mesh."

The team validated the system's performance using simulated CME waveforms derived from the 1989 Hydro-Québec event, the 2003 Halloween storms, and the 2012 near-miss. In all cases, the array maintained voltage stability within 0.5% of nominal grid parameters.
