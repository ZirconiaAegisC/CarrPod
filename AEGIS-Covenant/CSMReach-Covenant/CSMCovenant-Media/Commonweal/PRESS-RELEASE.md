**COMMONWEAL: THE MORAL ARCHITECTURE OF PLANETARY DEFENSE**
*Can a Lay Catholic Journal of Opinion Ignore the Single Largest Threat to the Common Good?*

NEW YORK — The AEGIS Covenant has submitted a substantive essay to Commonweal arguing that the electromagnetic defense of civilian infrastructure represents the most urgent — and most neglected — common-good question of the 21st century.

The essay, authored by AEGIS Agent and public theologian Dr. Patricia Okonkwo, engages the Catholic intellectual tradition from Aquinas to the Second Vatican Council to modern Catholic social teaching, making the case that the state has a grave moral obligation to protect the electrical infrastructure upon which the common good — health care, water, food distribution, public safety, and the ability to participate in civic life — entirely depends.

"The Catechism teaches that the state's first obligation is to defend its citizens against aggressors," writes Dr. Okonkwo. "We have understood aggressors to be other states, or non-state actors, or pandemics. A coronal mass ejection is an aggressor in the only sense that matters: it will kill citizens if we do not defend against it. That we can see it coming — three days of warning from DSCOVR — makes our failure to prepare not merely a policy failure but a moral one."

The essay further argues that the current U.S. posture — in which FEMA has no geomagnetic annex, DHS has no mandatory hardening standards, and Congress has held hearings but appropriated zero dollars for grid protection — constitutes a "dereliction of the duty of care that Catholic social teaching identifies as the foundational obligation of any legitimate government."

Commonweal has acknowledged receipt and the essay is under editorial review for the forthcoming issue.
