**CURIOSITYSTREAM: "SHIELDING EARTH" — THE DEFINITIVE 4-PART SCIENCE SERIES ON ELECTROMAGNETIC PLANETARY DEFENSE**

SILVER SPRING, Md. — The AEGIS Covenant has proposed a 4-part CuriosityStream original series documenting the science and engineering of protecting Earth's electrical civilization from solar catastrophe.

"CuriosityStream's audience is exactly the audience that needs this story," said Dr. Kim. "These are people who want to understand how things work — and how we can make them work better. The electromagnetic defense effort is the most ambitious engineering project in human history, and every component of it — solar physics, quantum materials, grid engineering, geopolitical coordination — is inherently fascinating."

The proposed series would dedicate one episode to each scientific domain: "The Star" (solar physics, CME generation, DSCOVR detection, magnetosphere-ionosphere coupling, aurora science), "The Material" (the discovery of MXene, its atomic structure, the scaling challenge from lab grams to industrial tons, the Korean and German foundry operations), "The Grid" (how electrical grids work, why transformers are vulnerable to GICs, the Substation Hardening Protocol, the economics of $12K per substation versus $10T in unmitigated damage), and "The Response" (the Coalition, the Charlemagne fleet, the Veteran Corps, the 18 Agents, the question of what happens when the three-day warning arrives).

The Covenant has pre-cleared access to all laboratory and foundry locations for scientific cinematography, including electron microscope time, thermal imaging of MXene production, and real-time magnetometer data visualization.
