HEADLINE: UK Startup Proposes New ESG Metric: Solar Storm Resilience Index for Institutional Investors

LONDON — Carrington Storm Motors has launched a solar storm resilience index designed for institutional investors, rating publicly traded companies on their exposure to geomagnetic disturbance risk. The index, developed by CSM's former Barclays risk analytics team, evaluates firms across three dimensions: geographical concentration of critical assets, supply-chain dependency on unprotected grid segments, and existence of electromagnetic hardening protocols.

Early screening of the FTSE 100 and S&P 500 reveals that no company in either index currently discloses geomagnetic risk in its annual report, despite the UK Treasury's National Risk Register rating a Carrington-scale event as the second most probable catastrophic scenario after pandemic.

"ESG frameworks have evolved to cover carbon, water, governance—everything except the one risk that could physically destroy a company's operating capacity in under 60 seconds," said CSM's chief financial officer. "Our index fills that gap."

The index is being offered to pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, and insurance-linked securities desks. CSM reports preliminary interest from three Nordic pension funds collectively managing £240 billion in assets.
