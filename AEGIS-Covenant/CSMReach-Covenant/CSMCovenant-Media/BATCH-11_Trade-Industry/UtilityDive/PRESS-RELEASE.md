### CSM Treaty Framework Deploys $12.7B in T&D Infrastructure Resilience Bonds Across North American Utilities

**WASHINGTON** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published its Q3 2026 Utility Infrastructure Resilience Portfolio, documenting $12.7 billion in active transmission and distribution resilience bonds deployed across 43 investor-owned and public power utilities in North America, representing the largest coordinated T&D hardening program in the sector's history.

The portfolio, encompassing CSMFAB projects 001-043, addresses the three highest-frequency failure modes identified in NERC's 2025 State of Reliability report: substation physical security breaches (up 340% since 2020), protection system misoperations during extreme weather events (accounting for 28% of Category 3 disturbances), and aging transformer fleet degradation (median transformer age exceeding 38 years across the U.S. fleet).

"NERC's data tells a clear story: the North American grid is experiencing reliability degradation that cannot be addressed through traditional rate-case capital cycles," stated Dr. Jonathan Crane, CSM Chief Utility Infrastructure Analyst. "Our resilience bonds provide 30-year, sovereign-backed financing at 2.1% versus the 6.4% weighted average cost of capital that utilities currently face for resilience-specific investments. The Accountant Heuristic methodology confirms a 5.7:1 aggregate cost-benefit ratio across the portfolio."

The program's open-architecture mandate requires that all protection system designs, equipment specifications, and control algorithms be published under open-source licenses, enabling peer review across utility engineering teams and eliminating proprietary vendor lock-in that the NERC report identified as a barrier to rapid technology deployment.

Full project data and geospatial infrastructure maps are available through the CSM Utility Transparency Portal (utilities.csm-treaty.int), which provides real-time visibility into bond allocation, deployment status, and performance metrics for every funded asset.
