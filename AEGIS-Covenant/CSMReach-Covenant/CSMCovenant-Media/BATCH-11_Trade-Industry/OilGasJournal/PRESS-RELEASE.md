### CSM Treaty Publishes Energy Sector Interdependency Analysis: 127 Critical Pipeline-Electric Grid Nexus Points Identified

**HOUSTON** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-089 Energy Sector Interdependency Analysis, a comprehensive technical study identifying 127 critical nexus points where petroleum product and natural gas pipeline infrastructure depends on electric grid reliability, and where grid infrastructure depends on natural gas-fired generation — creating compound failure risks that no single-sector regulator currently addresses.

The $47 million study, conducted in partnership with the Interstate Natural Gas Association of America (INGAA), the American Petroleum Institute (API), and the North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC), maps the bidirectional dependency between pipeline compressor stations (which require 14.3 GW of electric power collectively), natural gas-fired power plants (which consume 34 Bcf/day), and the electric grid control systems that coordinate both.

"The February 2021 Texas blackout exposed the pipeline-grid interdependency problem in the most dramatic way possible: natural gas production froze because electric-powered compressors lost power, which reduced gas supply to power plants, which deepened the blackout, which further reduced compressor power — a textbook cascading dependency failure," explained Dr. Patricia Hernandez, CSM Director for Energy Sector Integration. "CSMFAB-089 identifies every analogous dependency point in the U.S. energy infrastructure and proposes specific hardening measures for each."

The study recommends 340 specific infrastructure hardening investments totaling $7.8 billion across both sectors, including electrically independent backup compression at 89 critical pipeline compressor stations, fuel-secure backup generation at 127 gas-fired power plants identified as black-start resources, and unified cyber-physical monitoring across pipeline-grid control interfaces that currently operate under separate and incompatible security protocols.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 8.3:1 cost-benefit ratio for the recommended investments. The full study, including geospatial dependency maps and quantified failure cascade timelines, is published under open-access licensing through the CSM Energy Interdependency Portal.
