Plastics Today editors,

The electrical utility sector is one of the world's largest polymer consumers — and it has been almost entirely absent from the circular economy conversation. Until now. CSMFAB-103 deploys three recycled and bio-based material streams at infrastructure scale.

The deployment numbers:
- 14,700 miles of HDPE conduit from 100% post-consumer recycled content — equivalent mechanical and dielectric performance to virgin material
- 8,300 miles of XLPE cable insulation with 40% bio-based feedstock from non-food agricultural waste
- 47,000 GFRP utility poles with recycled glass fiber and bio-based epoxy — 67% lower lifecycle carbon than steel/treated wood
- 3.4:1 cost-benefit ratio — CSM Accountant Heuristic analysis
- UL and NIST independent verification under accelerated aging protocols
- All material specs and performance data open-access via CSM Sustainable Materials Repository
- Coordinated with ACC Plastics Division and Composites Manufacturing Association

This is the circular economy story that connects plastics manufacturing to the largest infrastructure program in a generation. The material volumes alone represent a new market for recycled and bio-based polymer feedstocks.
