The Gulf Coast's refining and petrochemical infrastructure processes 48% of U.S. capacity — representing $1.9 trillion in insured asset value — but shares a hidden vulnerability: 127 substations serve multiple operators' processing units across different facilities. A coordinated attack or hurricane-induced failure at these shared nodes creates a risk that no individual operator can mitigate alone.

CSMFAB-091 ($3.4B) deploys protection at this shared infrastructure layer:
- Physical hardening of 127 substations serving 47 refineries and 23 petrochemical complexes
- IEC 62443-compliant ICS security monitoring at 340 process-critical motor control centers
- Redundant 138kV and 230kV transmission feeds to facilities currently on single radial connections
- 11.7:1 cost-benefit ratio — CSM Accountant Heuristic analysis
- Coordinated with AFPM, DOE CESER, and individual operator engineering teams
- Technology transfer to AFPM members under open-architecture licensing
- Hurricane and storm-surge resilience design basis validated against NOAA SLOSH modeling

This is the infrastructure layer between the grid and the process units — the electrical backbone that every operator depends on but none can individually harden. CSMFAB-091 closes this shared-vulnerability gap.
