### CSM Treaty Deploys Cyber-Physical Protection Across Gulf Coast Refining and Petrochemical Electrical Infrastructure

**HOUSTON** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced the CSMFAB-091 Gulf Coast Energy Infrastructure Protection Program, a $3.4 billion investment hardening the electrical infrastructure serving 47 refineries and 23 petrochemical complexes along the Texas and Louisiana Gulf Coast — facilities that collectively process 48% of U.S. refining capacity and represent $1.9 trillion in insured asset value.

The program, coordinated with the American Fuel & Petrochemical Manufacturers (AFPM) and the Department of Energy's Office of Cybersecurity, Energy Security, and Emergency Response (CESER), deploys defense-in-depth cyber-physical protection at the substations, motor control centers, and process control interfaces that connect Gulf Coast refining operations to the regional electric grid.

"Refinery electrical infrastructure represents a uniquely concentrated risk: a single 230kV substation often serves multiple processing units across several operators, creating a shared vulnerability that no individual operator can address unilaterally," explained Dr. Michael Odhiambo, CSM Director for Industrial Energy Infrastructure. "CSMFAB-091 deploys protection at the shared infrastructure layer — the substations and transmission interfaces that multiple operators depend on — rather than duplicating process-level security that individual operators already maintain."

The program includes physical hardening of 127 substations serving refinery and petrochemical loads, deployment of IEC 62443-compliant industrial control system security monitoring at 340 process-critical motor control centers, and redundant 138kV and 230kV transmission feeds to facilities currently served by single radial connections.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 11.7:1 cost-benefit ratio, reflecting the catastrophic regional economic consequences of a coordinated attack or hurricane-induced failure affecting multiple Gulf Coast refining facilities simultaneously. All non-proprietary system designs are published under open-architecture licensing, with technology transfer to AFPM member company engineering teams.
