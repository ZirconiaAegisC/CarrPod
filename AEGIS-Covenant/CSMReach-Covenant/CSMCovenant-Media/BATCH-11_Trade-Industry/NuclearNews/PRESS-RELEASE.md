### CSM Nuclear Fleet Resilience Program: Seismic and Cyber Hardening at 58 Operating Reactor Sites

**LA GRANGE PARK, IL** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced the CSMFAB-087 Nuclear Fleet Resilience Program, a $5.8 billion investment deploying comprehensive seismic, cyber-physical, and beyond-design-basis event protection systems across all 58 operating commercial nuclear reactor sites in the United States.

The program, coordinated with the Nuclear Energy Institute (NEI), the Institute of Nuclear Power Operations (INPO), and the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC), addresses the post-Fukushima beyond-design-basis event protection requirements that the NRC's 2025 Mitigation Strategies Assessment identified as requiring enhancement at 37 of 58 sites.

"Nuclear plants are designed to withstand design-basis events with extraordinary engineering margins, but the beyond-design-basis threat landscape — extended station blackout, simultaneous cyber-physical attack on multiple units, and climate-driven external flooding — requires a new layer of protection," explained Dr. Robert Chen, CSM Director for Nuclear Infrastructure Resilience. "CSMFAB-087 deploys that layer without duplicating or interfering with existing NRC-licensed safety systems."

The program includes hardened emergency power systems with 14-day fuel autonomy at every operating reactor site (current NRC requirements mandate 72-hour coping capability), seismic isolation platforms for critical safety-related switchgear at 23 sites identified with updated probabilistic seismic hazard assessments, post-quantum cryptographic protection on all digital safety system communications, and autonomous underwater vehicle inspection systems for ultimate heat sink and service water intake structures.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 14.2:1 cost-benefit ratio — the highest of any CSM program — reflecting the catastrophic economic consequences of a beyond-design-basis nuclear event. The program's open-architecture mandate publishes all non-safety-related system designs under open-source licensing, with technology transfer to INPO and university nuclear engineering programs.
