ACSR conductor technology dates to the 1920s. Portland cement concrete to the 1820s. Infrastructure materials have been fundamentally unchanged for a century — while materials science has produced breakthrough after breakthrough that never left the laboratory. CSMFAB-099 changes this at scale.

Three technologies now deployed across 47 infrastructure projects:
- Graphene-enhanced aluminum composite conductors: 34% higher ampacity than ACSR at equivalent weight, enabling reconductoring on existing structures without new tower construction (2,300 circuit-miles deployed)
- Self-healing concrete with bacterial spore crack remediation: 62% maintenance cost reduction over 30-year lifecycle (340 substation foundations and transformer pads)
- Nano-structured ceramic transformer insulation: 18% thermal conductivity improvement, 40% partial discharge reduction (87 new transformers)
