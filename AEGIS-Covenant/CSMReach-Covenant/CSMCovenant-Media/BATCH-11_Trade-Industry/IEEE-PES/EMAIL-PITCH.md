The gap between academic grid-forming inverter research and the specifications transmission planners need for interconnection studies has been the bottleneck in synthetic inertia deployment. CSM's newly published SIRA v2.1 reference architecture closes that gap — with 17.4 GW of operational field data to back every parameter.

Technical contents of SIRA v2.1, submitted for IEEE PES Power System Stability Controls Subcommittee review:
- Grid-forming inverter inner-loop current control and outer-loop voltage/frequency regulation parameters, validated against IEEE 1547-2018 and IEEE 2800-2022
- Synchronous condenser H-selection methodology, excitation tuning for weak grids, generator protection coordination logic
- Black-start and islanded operation procedures validated through CSMFAB field tests at ERCOT, PJM, ENTSO-E, and NEM installations
- Post-quantum cryptographic authentication architecture for all control communications
- Complete PSCAD and PowerWorld simulation models released as open-source under CC BY 4.0

17.4 GW of PMU data from frequency events across four synchronous zones provides empirical validation of every control parameter. This is the reference architecture the industry has been waiting for.
