### CSM Open-Architecture Protection Platform Achieves 99.997% Coordination Accuracy Across Multi-Vendor Relay Fleets

**DENVER** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced operational validation of its QuantumMesh Protection Coordination Platform (CSMFAB-061), demonstrating 99.997% protection coordination accuracy across multi-vendor relay fleets spanning 340 substations and representing equipment from seven major protection relay manufacturers.

The $890 million program addresses the protection system miscoordination problem that NERC's 2025 State of Reliability report identified as the root cause of 28% of Category 3 disturbances — events that cascade beyond the initially faulted element because protection systems fail to isolate faults within designed clearing times.

"The North American protection relay fleet is a multi-vendor, multi-vintage patchwork where coordination studies are typically updated every five to seven years, while system topology changes occur continuously," explained Dr. Rebecca Achebe, CSM Chief Protection Systems Engineer. "CSMFAB-061 deploys a real-time coordination verification platform that continuously validates relay settings against actual system conditions, detecting coordination gaps within 30 seconds of a topology change."

The QuantumMesh platform ingests real-time DNP3 and IEC 61850 data streams from protection relays, computes coordination margins for every protection zone, and alerts engineering teams to coordination gaps before a fault exposes them. The 99.997% coordination accuracy metric was validated through 18 months of parallel operation with conventional coordination study methodologies at the 340-substation deployment fleet.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 12.4:1 cost-benefit ratio — the highest benefit ratio of any CSM infrastructure program — driven by avoided Category 3 disturbance costs, each of which averages $180 million in direct economic impact. The platform's open-architecture design publishes all coordination algorithms under open-source licensing, with technology transfer to 14 utility protection engineering departments.
