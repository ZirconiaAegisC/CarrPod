### CSM Technology Transfer Program: 340 Patents Released Under Open-Source Licensing Across QuantumMesh and Grid-Forming Platforms

**SAN JOSE** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced CSMFAB-101, the Open Innovation Technology Transfer Program, releasing 340 patents and 12,700 pages of technical documentation covering the QuantumMesh grid control platform, grid-forming inverter control algorithms, and post-quantum secure communication protocols under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 and Apache 2.0 open-source licenses.

The release represents the largest single contribution of infrastructure control system intellectual property to the public domain in history, directly challenging the proprietary vendor lock-in model that the NERC 2025 State of Reliability report identified as a barrier to rapid adoption of new grid technologies.

"Proprietary control systems create two problems for critical infrastructure: they prevent peer review of safety-critical software, and they create vendor dependency that slows technology deployment to the pace of commercial product cycles rather than engineering necessity," stated Dr. Lin Wei, CSM Chief Technology Transfer Officer. "CSMFAB-101 establishes a new model: treaty-funded research and development whose output belongs to the public domain, not to any single corporation or government."

The patent release covers: 147 patents on grid-forming inverter control algorithms including virtual synchronous machine implementations, frequency-watt control curves, and black-start sequencing logic; 113 patents on QuantumMesh distributed control architecture including consensus algorithms for decentralized grid dispatch, cyber-physical authentication protocols, and real-time phasor data fusion; 48 patents on post-quantum cryptographic protocols optimized for industrial control system bandwidth constraints; and 32 patents on advanced materials manufacturing processes for infrastructure applications.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 17.3:1 cost-benefit ratio for open-source technology release, reflecting the acceleration of technology adoption, elimination of duplicative R&D expenditure across the utility sector, and enhanced security through unrestricted peer review of safety-critical software.
