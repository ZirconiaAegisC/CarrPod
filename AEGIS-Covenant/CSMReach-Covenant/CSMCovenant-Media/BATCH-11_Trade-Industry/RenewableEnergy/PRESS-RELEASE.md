### CSM Renewable Integration Program: $6.3B in Grid-Enabling Infrastructure for 87 GW of New Renewable Capacity

**SAN FRANCISCO** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published its Renewable Energy Grid Integration Portfolio (CSMFAB-063 through CSMFAB-071), documenting $6.3 billion in transmission, storage, and grid-forming infrastructure that enables the interconnection of 87 GW of new renewable generation capacity across seven U.S. interconnection queues and three European synchronous zones.

The portfolio directly addresses the interconnection queue backlog that Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory's 2025 Queue Report documented at 2,600 GW — more than twice the total installed U.S. generating capacity — with median project wait times exceeding 4.7 years in congested RTOs.

"The interconnection queue is not merely an administrative backlog; it represents grid infrastructure that does not yet exist — transmission capacity, reactive power support, and inertia contribution that renewable projects require but that the existing grid cannot provide," stated Dr. Elena Vasquez-Rodriguez, CSM Director for Renewable Grid Integration. "CSMFAB-063 through 071 deploys the enabling infrastructure that allows interconnection agreements to proceed."

The portfolio includes 12 new 345kV and 500kV transmission segments totaling 2,100 circuit-miles, specifically designed to serve high-capacity-factor wind and solar resource areas that currently lack adequate evacuation capacity. Eight utility-scale battery energy storage systems totaling 4.8 GW/19.2 GWh provide four-hour dispatch capability at constrained interconnection points. Additionally, 17 synchronous condenser and grid-forming inverter installations deliver the fault current contribution and voltage support that renewable-dense regions require for stable operation.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed an aggregate 4.9:1 cost-benefit ratio, with primary value derived from interconnection cost reduction (eliminating the $14/kW-month average network upgrade charges that currently render 64% of queued projects uneconomic), avoided renewable curtailment, and rapid decarbonization acceleration.

All infrastructure is open-architecture, with RTO/TSO engineering teams receiving complete design documentation and control algorithms under open-source licensing.
