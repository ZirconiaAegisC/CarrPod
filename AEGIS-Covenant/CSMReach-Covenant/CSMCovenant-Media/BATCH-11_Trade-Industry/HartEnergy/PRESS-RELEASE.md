### CSM Treaty Funds Methane Monitoring and Leak Detection Grid Integration Across Permian and Bakken Basins

**MIDLAND** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced the CSMFAB-093 Production Basin Methane Infrastructure Program, a $1.7 billion investment deploying integrated methane detection, quantification, and electrical grid hardening across 340 production sites in the Permian and Bakken basins — addressing the dual challenge of emissions reduction and the electrical reliability required for electrified production operations.

The program, coordinated with the Environmental Partnership and the Department of Energy's methane mitigation research program, deploys continuous emissions monitoring systems (CEMS) at 340 sites, combined with electrical infrastructure hardening that ensures monitoring systems and electrified production equipment maintain operation during grid disturbances that historically triggered both production interruptions and undetected emissions events.

"The Permian Basin's electrification of production operations — replacing diesel and natural gas-fired pumps and compressors with electric alternatives — reduces emissions at the point of equipment but creates a new dependency on a grid that was not designed for production-critical load," explained Dr. Sarah Thompson-Wells, CSM Director for Energy Production Infrastructure. "CSMFAB-093 ensures that the environmental benefits of electrification are not undermined by the reliability limitations of the grid supporting it."

The program deploys distributed battery backup at 340 production sites, providing eight-hour ride-through capability for methane monitoring systems and critical production equipment during grid disturbances. Additionally, the program funds 340 miles of new 69kV and 138kV transmission to production areas currently served by lower-voltage distribution infrastructure inadequate for electrified operations.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 4.1:1 cost-benefit ratio, with value derived from avoided methane emissions (estimated at 2.8 million metric tons CO2-equivalent annually), reduced production downtime, and avoided flaring during grid disturbance events. All monitoring data is published through the CSM Methane Transparency Portal under open-access licensing.
