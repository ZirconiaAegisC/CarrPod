### CSM Transformer Fleet Resilience Program: 4,700 Distribution Transformers Hardened with Real-Time Health Monitoring

**CHICAGO** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced initial commissioning results from CSMFAB-075, the Transformer Fleet Resilience Program deploying real-time dissolved gas analysis (DGA), partial discharge monitoring, and thermal modeling across 4,700 distribution transformers at 64 utility fleets, targeting the aging transformer infrastructure that NERC's 2025 State of Reliability report identified as a Tier 1 reliability risk.

The $1.4 billion program addresses the U.S. distribution transformer fleet's median age of 38 years and the 340% increase in transformer failure-related outages documented since 2015. The deployment's monitoring systems, developed in partnership with Doble Engineering and the Electric Power Research Institute (EPRI), provide continuous diagnostics that detect incipient faults 14 to 90 days before catastrophic failure.

"Transformer failures are the most expensive single-asset failures in distribution systems, averaging $1.2 million per event in direct replacement costs and outage penalties," explained Dr. Katherine Zhou, CSM Director for Asset Health Management. "CSMFAB-075's monitoring systems have already detected 47 incipient fault conditions that would have resulted in catastrophic failures within 90 days. Each prevented failure avoids not only the $1.2 million direct cost but also the cascading outage effects that propagate through radial distribution architectures."

The program deploys multi-gas DGA sensors measuring hydrogen, acetylene, ethylene, methane, and ethane concentrations at parts-per-million sensitivity, combined with ultra-high-frequency partial discharge sensors detecting insulation degradation at the picocoulomb level. Machine learning models trained on EPRI's transformer failure database achieve 94.3% fault classification accuracy with a false-positive rate of 0.7%.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 5.9:1 cost-benefit ratio. The open-architecture data platform publishes all sensor data and failure prediction model parameters under open-source licensing, with technology transfer to 64 utility engineering departments.
