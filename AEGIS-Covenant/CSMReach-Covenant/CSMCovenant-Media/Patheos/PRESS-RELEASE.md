**PATHEOS: THE SUN, THE SACRED, AND THE GRID — ELECTROMAGNETIC THEOLOGY FOR A DIGITAL AGE**

DENVER — The AEGIS Covenant has published a multi-author theological reflection series on the Patheos platform, exploring the spiritual dimensions of electromagnetic planetary defense across multiple faith traditions and inviting interfaith dialogue on a question that touches every believer: what does it mean to protect creation from a star that both gives and threatens life?

The series, "Sacred Currents," features nine essays by theologians and faith leaders from Catholic, Protestant, Jewish, Muslim, Hindu, and Buddhist traditions, each engaging the geomagnetic threat through their tradition's distinctive spiritual vocabulary. The series is hosted across Patheos's faith channels, with live comment sections moderated by the authors.

"The Sun is a central religious symbol in virtually every tradition," writes Sr. Fernández in the opening essay. "It represents divine illumination, life-giving warmth, and the regularity of creation. To acknowledge that the Sun is also capable of destroying what we have built is not to diminish its sacred character — it is to confront the mystery at the heart of all religious experience: that which sustains us can also undo us."

Subsequent essays address: "Pikuach Nefesh and the Kilowatt-Hour" (Rabbi Stern on the Jewish obligation to protect life through infrastructure), "Zakat and the Electromagnetic Poor" (Imam Hassan on Islamic charity as grid resilience), "The Ahimsa of Shielding" (Swami Sarvapriyananda on non-harm extended to systems), and "Compassion for the Unplugged" (Venerable Thubten Chodron on Buddhist ethics in a grid-down world).

The full series is available at patheos.com/sacred-currents.
