**VATICAN RECEIVES AEGIS COVENANT DOSSIER: PROTECTING CREATION FROM THE SUN**

VATICAN CITY — The AEGIS Covenant has formally presented its comprehensive geomagnetic preparedness dossier to the Pontifical Academy of Sciences, marking a historic intersection of faith and science in the effort to protect the most vulnerable from electromagnetic catastrophe.

The dossier, delivered by Sr. Maria Teresa Fernández, FMA — a Salesian sister, physicist, and AEGIS Agent — frames electromagnetic planetary defense as an expression of the Catholic Church's teaching on the stewardship of creation. "Laudato Si' calls us to protect our common home," said Sr. Fernández. "A Carrington-class coronal mass ejection would destroy the electrical systems that sustain the poorest and most vulnerable first — the hospitals without backup generators, the water pumps in drought-stricken regions, the cold-chain vaccines in sub-Saharan Africa. Protecting creation means protecting the grid."

The dossier includes a theological reflection co-authored by theologians from the Pontifical Gregorian University and the Catholic University of America, drawing on the encyclicals Laudato Si' and Fratelli Tutti to argue that electromagnetic resilience is a work of mercy — feeding the hungry, giving drink to the thirsty, caring for the sick — extended to the scale of planetary infrastructure.

The Pontifical Academy of Sciences has accepted the dossier for review and has recommended it for discussion at the upcoming Plenary Session on Scientific Responses to Existential Risk. The Covenant has also briefed Caritas Internationalis, which has begun incorporating geomagnetic preparedness into its disaster-risk-reduction programming across 162 countries.

"The Church is the largest non-governmental provider of healthcare and humanitarian services on Earth," said Sr. Fernández. "If the grid goes down, those services stop. Protecting the grid is protecting the mission."
