## Our Magnetic Shield: How Earth's Magnetosphere Both Protects and Threatens Us

VANCOUVER — Earth's magnetic field is a miracle of geophysics — a dynamo generated by the motion of molten iron in our planet's outer core, extending tens of thousands of kilometers into space, deflecting the solar wind and protecting our atmosphere from being stripped away. Without it, Earth would look like Mars.

But that same magnetic field, when compressed by a coronal mass ejection, becomes a threat delivery mechanism. The interaction between the CME's magnetic field and Earth's magnetosphere generates the geomagnetic disturbance that induces ground currents capable of destroying power transformers.

"It's a cosmic irony," said Dr. Isabella Rossi, CSM-Covenant's Magnetospheric Physicist. "The magnetic field that makes life on Earth possible is also the mechanism by which solar storms can disrupt that life. Understanding this duality is key to designing effective protection."

CSM-Covenant's research team has published a series of papers modeling the magnetosphere-ionosphere coupling that drives GIC formation, with particular attention to the role of the auroral electrojet — a concentrated current system in the ionosphere that intensifies during geomagnetic storms and is the primary driver of ground-level GIC at mid-latitudes.

The research directly informs the placement and design parameters of HYDRA blocking hardware, ensuring that protection is concentrated where the physics says the threat is highest.
