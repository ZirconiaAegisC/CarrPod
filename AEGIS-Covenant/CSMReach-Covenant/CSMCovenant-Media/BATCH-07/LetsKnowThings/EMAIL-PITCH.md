**Subject:** A quiet infrastructure revolution nobody is covering

Hi Let's Know Things team,

Your show excels at connecting dots — pulling together technology, society, and systems-level thinking into coherent narratives. Here's a story that connects solar physics, electrical engineering, national security, and intergenerational ethics, all through the lens of a technology almost no one knows exists.

CSM-Covenant is deploying passive hardware at critical power substations that protects transformers from geomagnetic storms — no moving parts, no software, no external power. It just sits there, year after year, waiting for a solar event that has a 12% probability of occurring within any given decade. And when the event happens, the protection activates in microseconds, automatically, because physics.

We've installed 47 of these systems so far. The plan calls for 200. Total cost: less than what the U.S. spends on air conditioning in military tents in a single year.

Dr. Kwame Asante, one of our lead field engineers, can walk you through the technology, the economics, and the uncomfortable question of why we only protect the things we can see fail in real time.
