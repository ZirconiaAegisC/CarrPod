## Planetary Defense Beyond Asteroids: Why the Sun Is the Nearer Threat

PASADENA, CA — The Planetary Society has done extraordinary work raising awareness of asteroid impact risk. But there is another planetary defense challenge that operates on a much shorter timescale and with far higher probability: solar coronal mass ejections.

A CME of sufficient magnitude — directed at Earth with a southward-oriented magnetic field — will induce ground currents capable of destroying the backbone of modern civilization. The last event of this scale occurred in 1859, before the electrical grid existed. The next one is a matter of when, not if.

"Planetary defense isn't just about rocks from space," said Dr. Helena Kovacs, CSM-Covenant's Director of Space Weather Research. "It's about plasma from our own star. We have the technology to detect CMEs, model their propagation, and harden our infrastructure against their effects. What we've lacked until now is the organizational will to deploy those technologies at scale."

CSM-Covenant's AEGIS early warning system integrates data from SOHO, SDO, Parker Solar Probe, and the newly commissioned Vigil mission at L5 to provide up to 36 hours of advance notice for Earth-directed CMEs. Combined with the HYDRA hardening protocol, it creates a layered planetary defense system against the solar threat.
