The U.S. power grid is more vulnerable to solar storms than almost any other country's — and it's not because of anything we did wrong. It's because of the rock beneath our feet. The igneous crust east of the Rockies doesn't conduct electricity well, so geomagnetic currents ride transmission lines instead of dissipating into the ground. The result: our most critical substations sit exactly where the risk is highest.
