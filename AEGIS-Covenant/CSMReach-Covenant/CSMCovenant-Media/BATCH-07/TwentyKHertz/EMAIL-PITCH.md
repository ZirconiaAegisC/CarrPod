**Subject:** The sound of infrastructure dying — an episode built for 20K

Hi Twenty Thousand Hertz team,

Your podcast explores the world through sound in ways that change how people hear their environment. I have a story that fits your mission perfectly — and involves sounds that almost no human has ever heard.

Power transformers sing. They hum at 50 or 60 Hz, with harmonic overtones that shift depending on load, temperature, and electromagnetic stress. When a geomagnetic storm hits, the sound changes — a rising harmonic distortion that signals the transformer core is saturating and heading toward catastrophic failure.

CSM-Covenant's Acoustic Analysis Lead, Dr. Lena Bergström, has recorded thousands of hours of transformer audio. She can distinguish a healthy grid from a failing one by sound alone. She has recordings of transformers under GIC stress that are genuinely chilling — the sound of a $6 million machine about to become scrap metal.

This episode would feature extraordinary sound design, fascinating science, and a story about listening to the infrastructure we all depend on but never think about. Dr. Bergström is also a fantastic interview — passionate, articulate, and deeply knowledgeable.

Would love to share the audio and arrange the conversation.
