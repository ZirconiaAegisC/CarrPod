HEADLINE: CSM Proposes Solar-Storm-Linked Catastrophe Bond for Pension Fund Portfolios Seeking Uncorrelated Returns

LONDON — Carrington Storm Motors has initiated discussions with two European insurance-linked securities desks about structuring what would be the world's first solar-storm-triggered catastrophe bond, according to documents reviewed by Pensions & Investments. The proposed instrument would offer pension funds an uncorrelated return stream whose coupon is inversely linked to solar storm probability.

The structure, as outlined in preliminary term sheets, would see CSM contribute its vehicle fleet as the underlying resilience asset, with bondholders receiving a premium funded by fleet-contract revenues and government resilience payments. In the event of a qualifying geomagnetic storm, a portion of the principal would convert to in-kind grid-defence services rather than cash—a novel structure that CSM's bankers describe as "resilience-for-yield."

"Pension funds are desperate for uncorrelated assets," said Kairos Steele, CSM's founder. "Solar storm probability has zero correlation with equity markets, bond markets, real estate, or commodities. It is the purest diversification play available—because the sun does not care about your portfolio construction."

The company is targeting an initial issuance of £500 million, pending regulatory approval from the FCA and the PRA.
