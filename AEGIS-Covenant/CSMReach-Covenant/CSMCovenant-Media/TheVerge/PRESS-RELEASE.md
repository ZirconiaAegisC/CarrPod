HEADLINE: The Most Over-Engineered EV on Earth: Carrington Storm Motors' Apocalypse-Ready Electric Car

COVENTRY, UK — There are electric vehicles designed for range, others for luxury, and some for sheer speed. Carrington Storm Motors has built one for the end of the world—or at least, the end of the electrical grid as we know it.

The CSM-1, unveiled today at the company's Coventry facility, looks like a conventional crossover EV. But beneath its matte-black exterior lies something no other automaker has attempted: a complete electromagnetic hardening system that transforms the vehicle into a mobile grid-defence node during solar superstorms.

"We benchmarked every production EV on the market for EMP resilience," said Kairos Steele, CSM's founder. "The best-performing vehicle failed our test at 12% of the surge threshold of a Carrington-level event. Ours survived at 140%."

The user experience is deliberately uneventful: drivers receive a push notification when a geomagnetic storm is detected, the vehicle automatically enters "shield mode," and—the company claims—the lights stay on in whatever neighbourhood the car happens to be parked in. The CSM-1 starts at £58,000, with fleet pricing for municipalities.
