**JTA: PIKUACH NEFESH AND THE GRID — JEWISH ORGANIZATIONS ENDORSE ELECTROMAGNETIC PLANETARY DEFENSE**

NEW YORK — A coalition of major Jewish organizations — including the Jewish Federations of North America, the Orthodox Union, the Rabbinical Assembly (Conservative), and the Union for Reform Judaism — has endorsed the AEGIS Covenant's electromagnetic defense framework, grounding their support in the Jewish legal principle of pikuach nefesh: the obligation to preserve human life overrides nearly every other commandment.

"There is no more fundamental Jewish value than the preservation of life," said Rabbi David Stern, who convened the coalition. "Pikuach nefesh is why we are permitted to break Shabbat to save a life. It is why we build hospitals and fund medical research. And it is why we must harden the electrical transformers that power the ventilators, the dialysis machines, and the refrigerators that store insulin for millions of Jews and non-Jews alike. If a $12,000 MXene retrofit saves one life — and it will save thousands — not doing it is a violation of Jewish law."

The coalition has committed to three actions: advocating for BILL-006 and DPA activation through JFNA's Washington office, incorporating geomagnetic preparedness into Jewish community security and disaster-planning frameworks (building on the existing infrastructure developed for synagogue security), and educating the Jewish community through sermons, adult education curricula, and social media campaigns organized around the theme "Pikuach Nefesh in the 21st Century."

The coalition has also endorsed the Interfaith Declaration on Geomagnetic Stewardship and will participate in its presentation at the Parliament of the World's Religions.
