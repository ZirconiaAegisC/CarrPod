HEADLINE: Coventry EV Startup Warns FTSE 100 Directors: Your Supply Chains Have a Solar Storm Problem

LONDON — Carrington Storm Motors has issued an open letter to FTSE 100 board chairs warning that current supply-chain resilience frameworks fail to account for geomagnetic storm disruption, despite a 12% annual probability of a Carrington-scale event occurring.

The company's analysis, distributed to corporate governance committees this morning, identifies 14 FTSE 100 firms whose operations would be "critically impaired" within 72 hours of a major solar storm, owing to just-in-time logistics dependencies on unprotected electrical infrastructure.

"Corporate Britain has spent millions on ESG compliance and cybersecurity audits while ignoring the one threat vector that requires no malevolent actor—just physics," said Kairos Steele, CSM's director. "A solar storm does not discriminate between a server farm in Slough and a substation in Solihull. Both go dark simultaneously."

CSM's CFO, a former Barclays risk officer, has developed a solar storm resilience index that the company is offering to institutional investors as an ESG screening tool.
