**MUSLIM JOURNAL: ZAKAT AND THE GRID — ISLAMIC CHARITY AS ELECTROMAGNETIC JUSTICE**

CHICAGO — The AEGIS Covenant, in partnership with Islamic Relief USA and the Zakat Foundation of America, has released a theological framework for understanding electromagnetic planetary defense as an expression of zakat — the Islamic obligation of charitable giving — and the protection of the ummah.

"Zakat is not merely giving money," writes Imam Abdullah Hassan in the framework's opening reflection. "Zakat is the redistribution of resources to protect the vulnerable, to ensure that no member of the ummah — and by extension, no human being — is left without the means of survival. When we harden an electrical transformer that powers a hospital in a low-income community, when we pre-position fuel stabilizer at a mosque serving as a community refuge, when we train Muslim veterans to coordinate with the Veteran Response Corps — we are fulfilling zakat in the most literal sense."

The framework identifies specific alignments between Islamic principles and the Covenant's electromagnetic defense architecture: Zakat (charitable obligation — the Coalition's mutual-aid provisions for developing nations), Ummah (community protection — the mosque-as-resilience-hub model), Amanah (stewardship — humanity's responsibility as khalifah to protect the Earth and its systems), and Ilm (knowledge — the pursuit of scientific understanding as a religious duty, with MXene research as an expression of this principle).

Islamic Relief USA has committed to integrating geomagnetic preparedness into its global humanitarian programming, with initial pilot projects in Bangladesh, Somalia, and Pakistan — countries where electrical infrastructure is already fragile and where a CME would compound existing humanitarian crises.
