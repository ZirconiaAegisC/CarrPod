**WATERBEAR NETWORK: PROTECTING THE BLUE PLANET — A CLIMATE-ADJACENT DOCUMENTARY ON ELECTROMAGNETIC RESILIENCE**

LONDON — The AEGIS Covenant has proposed a documentary partnership with WaterBear Network, the impact-driven streaming platform, framing electromagnetic planetary defense as an essential component of climate adaptation and global resilience.

"WaterBear's audience understands that protecting the planet means protecting the systems that sustain human life," said Dr. Vasquez. "A collapsed electrical grid is not just a convenience problem. It's a water problem — municipal pumps stop. It's a food problem — refrigeration and distribution chains break. It's a medical problem — cold-chain pharmaceuticals spoil. It's a climate problem — nuclear plants without grid power lose cooling. Electromagnetic resilience is climate resilience."

The proposed documentary would connect the dots between seemingly disparate crises, showing how a Carrington-class CME would compound and accelerate every existing environmental vulnerability. It would also showcase the solutions — MXene shielding, the Charlemagne fleet's amphibious humanitarian capability, the Veteran Response Corps' community-level organization — through WaterBear's signature lens of hope, action, and impact.

The Covenant has committed to providing WaterBear with educational materials, action guides, and call-to-action resources aligned with WaterBear's mission to turn viewers into participants. "We don't just want people to watch this film," said Vasquez. "We want them to ask their local representatives why their substation hasn't been hardened. WaterBear makes that possible."
