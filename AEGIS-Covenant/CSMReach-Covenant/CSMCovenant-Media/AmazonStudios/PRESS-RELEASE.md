**AMAZON STUDIOS: "GRIDFALL" — A DOCUMENTARY EVENT FOR THE GLOBAL STREAMING AUDIENCE**

SEATTLE — The AEGIS Covenant has proposed "Gridfall," a documentary event film for Amazon Studios exploring the single point of failure in modern civilization — and the global effort to fix it before the Sun does.

"Amazon's global reach — 200 million Prime subscribers in every country on Earth — makes it the only platform capable of delivering this story at the scale the story demands," said Dr. Vasquez. "This is not a niche documentary for science enthusiasts. This is an event film for everyone who has ever flipped a light switch, charged a phone, or depended on a hospital — which is to say, everyone."

"Gridfall" would be structured as a 120-minute theatrical-quality documentary following the 72-hour countdown framework that the Covenant uses in its operational planning: T-minus 72 hours (CME detected by DSCOVR, NOAA confirms, the warning goes out), T-minus 48 hours (the Coalition activates, the Charlemagne fleet deploys, the Veteran Corps receives activation orders), T-minus 12 hours (MXene foundries go to maximum production, hospitals implement cold-chain protocols, the President receives the final briefing), and T-minus 0 (CME arrival — what happens, what works, what doesn't — and what the world looks like on the other side).

The Covenant is offering Amazon Studios full production access, first-look rights on archival material, and commitment from all principal characters. The film would be designed for simultaneous global release across all Amazon Prime territories.

"The most important documentary of this decade is the one that tells humanity it can protect itself," said Vasquez. "That's 'Gridfall.'"
