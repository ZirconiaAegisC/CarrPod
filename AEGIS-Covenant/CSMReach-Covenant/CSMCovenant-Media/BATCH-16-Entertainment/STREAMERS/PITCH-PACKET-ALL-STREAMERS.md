## NETFLIX — "The Eighteen" (Limited Series, 10 Episodes)
**Pitch:** *Stranger Things* meets *Chernobyl* meets *The West Wing*. Each of the 10 episodes follows a different Agent of Aegis in the 72 hours before, during, and after the Carrington Event. Episode 1: Director Kairos Steele at Command, making the call. Episode 2: Agent Nyx Calder in the Phantom MK-1 at a Minnesota substation. Episode 3: Captain Meridia Vaun bringing the Tourmaline into Norfolk on its hexapedal legs. Episode 4: Dr. Theron Nash in the lab, watching MXene films do exactly what the math said they would do. Episode 5: Agent Solara Vance coordinating the LoRa mesh network from a hardened bunker as the aurora paints the sky overhead. And so on. The final episode converges all eighteen agents at AEGIS Command as the lights — the first lights restored on the continent — flicker back on.

**Why Netflix:** Global audience. 10 episodes = one weekend binge. The 18 Agents = 18 distinct visual worlds. Every episode has a different color palette (the YInMn Blue of MXene, the ZrB₂-SiC ceramic white of the Charlemagne hull, the amber glow of emergency backup power).

**Casting Note:** The 18 Agents were designed as cognitive delivery vehicles for technical specifications. That means they arrive pre-written, pre-characterized, with complete dossiers (CSMGen0001 through CSMGen0018) ready for the writers' room.

## HBO/MAX — "SiblingFrequency" (Ongoing Series, 4 Seasons Planned)
**Pitch:** The podcast that CSM already produces — *SiblingFrequency* — is the foundation for an HBO audio-drama-to-series adaptation. Four episodes exist. Four seasons mapped. Season 1: The Signal (the warning). Season 2: The Cascade (the infrastructure failure, episode by episode, system by system). Season 3: The Response (the Agents, the fleet, the robots, the mesh network — competence under pressure). Season 4: The Rebuilding (five years later — what did we learn?).

**Why HBO:** Prestige. The podcast already exists. The IP is original. The tone — calm, measured, competent, no panic — is exactly HBO's brand of sophisticated disaster narrative (*Chernobyl*, *The Last of Us*). The showrunner's bible is already written (the 6,000+ files of the CSM repository).

## APPLE TV+ — "The Citadel" (Prestige Drama, 3 Seasons)
**Pitch:** A slow-burn, visually sumptuous drama about the decade BEFORE the Carrington Event. The engineers who are trying to convince the world to prepare. The politicians who don't want to hear it. The insurance companies doing the math and quietly repositioning their portfolios. The families who hear about "geomagnetic disturbance" on the news and wonder if they should buy a generator. No storm hits in Season 1. No storm hits in Season 2. The storm hits in the finale of Season 3. The tension is the gap between what science knows and what society does.

**Why Apple TV+:** This is *For All Mankind* territory — alternate history, science-as-character, slow build to emotional devastation.
**Cast Note:** Apple has relationships with the talent who would elevate this material. The role of Kairos Steele — calm, gravitational, a leader who has seen the models and emerged with a plan — is a career-defining role for any actor in their 40s-60s.

## AMAZON STUDIOS — "The Charlemagne Fleet" (Action-Spectacle Series)
**Pitch:** *The Expanse* meets *Mad Max: Fury Road*. Post-storm, the Charlemagne Fleet is the only functioning transportation network on the Eastern Seaboard. Five amphibious walking vessels, each with a distinct mission and crew. The Tourmaline does search and rescue. The Obsidian does rapid response. The Beryl does family evacuation. The Amethyst does tactical security. The Rhodonite does mass transit. Each episode follows a different ship's mission. The season arc: the fleet is running out of CMBC battery charge, and the only charging station on the coast is behind a collapsed bridge that the engineers have to figure out how to cross.

**Why Amazon:** *The Expanse* fans. The fleet spec is 77 volumes of actual engineering detail. This is the hardest sci-fi series ever made — because it's not fiction.

## HULU — "The Accountant" (Limited Series, 8 Episodes)
**Pitch:** The insurance industry has $7 trillion in assets under management. A Carrington event generates $2-6 trillion in claims in the first 30 days. The math doesn't work. This series follows the people who know the math doesn't work — the actuaries, the underwriters, the reinsurance traders at Lloyd's, the catastrophe bond structurers — as they try to sound the alarm in a world that doesn't want to hear that the most boring profession on Earth is sitting on the most explosive secret in finance. Think *The Big Short* but for geomagnetic risk. The Accountant Heuristic — every proposal must be costed, every risk probability-weighted, every assumption transparent — becomes the dramatic structure of the series itself.

## DISNEY+ — "AEGIS: Young Agents" (Animated Series, Family)
**Pitch:** A family-friendly animated series about a group of teenagers training at the AEGIS Academy — learning to pilot mini-Phantoms, operate LoRa mesh networks, and understand the science of the Dielectric Citadel. Educational but never didactic. Each episode teaches a real scientific concept (magnetohydrodynamic coupling, complex permittivity, transformer saturation) through adventure storytelling. The Williams Heuristic animated: complex truth survives translation into a 22-minute episode a 10-year-old can understand while eating a sandwich.

## PEACOCK — "Grid Down: 1989" (Limited Series, 6 Episodes)
**Pitch:** The true story of the Hydro-Québec blackout — March 13, 1989, when a G5 geomagnetic storm dropped the entire Quebec grid in 90 seconds. Six million people lost power. It was the wake-up call. This series dramatizes the event and the aftermath: the engineers who figured out what happened, the utility executives who realized this wasn't a one-off, and the slow, frustrating political process that followed in which some people listened and most people didn't. A prequel to every other AEGIS story. The event that started Carrington Storm Motors.

---

## PARAMOUNT+ — "Phantom: Origins" (Limited Series, 5 Episodes)
**Pitch:** The origin of the Phantom MK-1 robot. Told as a tech-development thriller — the engineering team (led by Engineer Orion Cross) racing to build a robot that can operate inside an electromagnetic hurricane before the next solar maximum. Every actuator, every Faraday cage layer, every joint-angle limit is a dramatic beat. The robot comes alive, literally, in Episode 3. By Episode 5, Nyx Calder walks it into a simulated G5 field and the Phantom doesn't even blink. Think *Ford v Ferrari* for robotics.
