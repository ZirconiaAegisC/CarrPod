# FILM STUDIOS — STORY TREATMENT PACKETS

---

## DISNEY — "The Light Keepers"
**Pitch:** A Pixar-quality animated feature about a family living through the year the lights went out — told from the perspective of the family's child, who doesn't understand why the refrigerator stopped humming but discovers that her parents, her neighbors, and a mysterious woman named Captain Meridia Vaun have been preparing for this her whole life. The Charlemagne fleet walks out of the ocean at sunrise. The child sees an amphibious walking ship for the first time. "Mom, the boats have legs." Think *Wall-E* meets *Moana* — post-technological wonder, resilience, community.

**Franchise Potential:** Theme park attraction. The Charlemagne fleet as a Disney ride. Aegis Home as an Epcot exhibit. The Dielectric Citadel as Tomorrowland's next anchor.

**Key Characters:** Captain Meridia Vaun (hero captain, steady, maternal), The Child (6-8, our eyes), Mork (comic relief, the AI who explains the science at 400 words per minute), Chester (the calm one, Mork's foil).

---

## WARNER BROS. — "AEGIS: First Light"
**Pitch:** A Christopher Nolan-esque thriller about the 72 hours between NOAA's G5 warning and the storm's arrival. Intercutting between Director Kairos Steele at AEGIS Command coordinating a global response, Agent Nyx Calder in a Phantom MK-1 robot navigating a substation to install a last-minute GIC-blocking capacitor, and a family in Minnesota (highest GIC risk in America) trying to prepare their home. The tension is not "will they survive" — it's "will the preparations hold." Think *Interstellar* meets *Contagion* — procedural, scientific, emotionally devastating when the math works and transcendent when it does.

**Franchise Potential:** Trilogy. Film 1: The Warning. Film 2: The Storm. Film 3: The Rebuilding. HBO Max companion series following the 18 Agents.

**Key Characters:** Kairos Steele (gravitational lead), Nyx Calder (substation action), Dr. Theron Nash (materials scientist, the one who invented the MXene film at 45 microns), the Minnesota family (audience surrogate).

## UNIVERSAL — "The Charlemagne Protocol"
**Pitch:** A disaster-action-spectacle film about the Charlemagne-Class Fleet's first real deployment. When a G5 storm hits and every port on the Eastern Seaboard loses power, the fleet — five amphibious vessels with hexapedal walking mechanisms — leaves its hardened docks and walks onto the beaches of Norfolk, New York, Miami, and Houston carrying emergency power, medical supplies, and the only functioning communications grid on the continent. Think *Jurassic Park* wonder-meets-spectacle, but the dinosaurs are 500-ton walking ships and the heroes are the engineers who built them.

**Franchise Potential:** The fleet is the franchise. Five ships = five films. Each ship (Tourmaline, Obsidian, Beryl, Amethyst, Rhodonite) gets its own mission movie. Universal's answer to the MCU.

**Key Characters:** Captain Meridia Vaun (Tourmaline), Commander Rook Draven (Beryl, tactical SUV), Engineer Orion Cross (the one who fixes things mid-mission), Operative Zephyr Arden (Amethyst, fleet coordination).

## PARAMOUNT — "Phantom: The Nyx Calder Missions"
**Pitch:** A grounded, gritty, *Mission: Impossible*-style action series about Agent Nyx Calder — a former special operator who now pilots the Phantom MK-1, a GIC-hardened robot designed to operate inside electromagnetic hurricanes. Each mission: enter a substation during a Carrington-level simulated event, install or repair critical protection equipment, get out before the induced current fries everything. Calder doesn't speak much. The Phantom does the talking — a 900kg hexapedal robot with actuator torque curves that make hydraulic excavators jealous and Faraday-cage attenuation that laughs at G5 fields. Think *Edge of Tomorrow* meets *The Martian* — procedural, tool-focused, competence pornography.

**Franchise Potential:** Ongoing series. New mission per season. Expand to other Phantom operators.

**Key Character:** Nyx Calder (the operator, stoic, hyper-competent, the robot is their partner, not their tool).

## SONY/COLUMBIA — "The Dielectric Citadel"
**Pitch:** A *Spider-Verse*-style animated film about a future Earth that DIDN'T prepare for the Carrington Event — and the generation that grows up in the five-year dark age that follows. Told in a distinctive visual style that shifts from vibrant pre-storm color to the YInMn Blue palette (the color CSM chose for its MXene shielding based on complex permittivity calculations at geomagnetic frequencies) as the story darkens. Our protagonist is a teenager who discovers her grandmother's old AEGIS emergency kit and realizes that the preparation existed — it just wasn't deployed in time. She decides to finish the job. Think *Spider-Verse* meets *Children of Men* — visually groundbreaking, emotionally devastating, ultimately hopeful.

**Franchise Potential:** Standalone masterpiece. Awards magnet. Defines "what-if" genre.

---

## LIONSGATE — "The Baker Street Adjuster"
**Pitch:** A limited series about an insurance claims adjuster in the aftermath of the Carrington Event. No superheroes. No robots. Just a woman with a clipboard, a solar-powered calculator, and the authority to decide which claims get paid and which don't — which businesses survive and which don't — which communities rebuild and which don't. She follows the money, and the money leads to uncomfortable truths: who knew the storm was coming, who prepared, who didn't, and who made a killing on the chaos. Think *Erin Brockovich* meets *The Big Short* — the disaster through the lens of the people who price it.

**Franchise Potential:** Prestige limited series. 6-8 episodes. Awards play.

**Key Character:** The Adjuster (original character, inspired by the CSMinsurance dossier series and the Accountant Heuristic).
