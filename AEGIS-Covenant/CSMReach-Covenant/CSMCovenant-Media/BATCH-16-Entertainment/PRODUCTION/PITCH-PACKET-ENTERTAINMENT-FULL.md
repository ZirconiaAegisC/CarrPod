# PRODUCTION COMPANIES, AGENCIES, DIRECTORS & CREATORS — TARGETED PITCHES

---

## PRODUCTION COMPANIES

**A24** — *"The Color of the Storm"*: An arthouse drama about the physicist who chose the color YInMn Blue for the MXene shielding not because it looked good, but because the complex permittivity at geomagnetic frequencies was optimal. A story about beauty emerging from mathematics. The aesthetic A24 was born to produce.

**Legendary Entertainment** — *"AEGIS"*: The franchise. Legendary's model (Godzilla, Kong, Dune) is world-building at scale. AEGIS is a world. The 18 Agents. The 5 ships. The 116 products. The 200 countries. Legendary builds the universe; CSM provides the source material (6,000+ files of it).

**Blumhouse** — *"The Cascade"*: A horror-adjacent thriller about the moment the grid fails. What happens in the first 24 hours when every transformer on the continent melts simultaneously? Not a monster movie — a systems-failure horror movie. The monster is the absence of electricity. Jason Blum's micro-budget, high-concept model applied to infrastructure horror.

**Skydance** — *"Charlemagne"*: The action franchise. Skydance's animation and VFX capabilities (they have their own studio) are built for the Charlemagne fleet's hexapedal walking sequences. An amphibious 500-ton vessel walking onto a beach needs the best visual effects on Earth. Skydance can deliver.

**Plan B (Brad Pitt)** — *"The Adjuster"*: The prestige drama about the insurance adjuster in the aftermath. Plan B's track record with socially conscious, character-driven prestige (*Moonlight*, *12 Years a Slave*, *The Big Short*) is the exact sensibility needed.

**Bad Robot (J.J. Abrams)** — *"First Light"*: The mystery-box approach to the Carrington Event. What if we DON'T explain the storm in the first episode? What if the audience discovers it along with the characters? Abrams' gift for withholding revelation makes the science into suspense.

**Participant Media** — *"The Prepared"*: The activist documentary. Participant's mission (entertainment that inspires social change) IS the AEGIS mission (protect civilization through informed preparation). A documentary about the engineers, the diplomats, and the communities preparing for the Carrington Event — and the ones who aren't.

**The Pitch to Agencies:** This is not one film. This is a universe. Your entire roster can live here.

## DIRECTORS — PROJECT ALIGNMENT

**Denis Villeneuve** — *"The Dielectric Citadel"*: The man who made *Arrival* and *Dune* was born to direct the story of humanity preparing for a threat it cannot see but can mathematically predict. The scale. The patience. The reverence for science. The emotional devastation of watching the math come true.

**Christopher Nolan** — *"AEGIS: First Light"*: The 72-hour countdown structure. Cross-cutting between multiple timelines, multiple characters, multiple scales (from quantum-level MXene shielding to continental geomagnetic field models). Practical effects for the Charlemagne fleet. No CGI unless absolutely necessary. The Nolan treatment.

**James Cameron** — *"The Charlemagne Fleet"*: The man who built a 1:1 scale Titanic set and invented underwater mocap for *Avatar: The Way of Water* is the only director who would insist on building a real hexapedal walking vehicle for the Charlemagne fleet. And Cameron would. And it would work.

**Ridley Scott** — *"The Cascade"*: Scott's ability to render system failure — the *Alien* ship, the *Blade Runner* city, the *Martian* habitat — applied to the electrical grid. Every transformer failure is a set piece. Every cascading system collapse is a Ridley Scott sequence waiting to be shot.

**Roland Emmerich** — *"Grid Down"*: The master of disaster (*The Day After Tomorrow*, *2012*) working with a real disaster for the first time. No aliens. No made-up physics. Just the Sun, the grid, and the math. Let Emmerich do what he does best — spectacle with scale — but ground it in the hardest science he's ever worked with.

**Adam McKay** — *"The Accountant"*: *The Big Short* for geomagnetic risk. McKay's gift for explaining complex systems through character, humor, and fourth-wall breaks is the only way to make a series about insurance actuaries into must-see television. The Accountant Heuristic becomes the film's narrative device.

## SHOWRUNNERS & CREATORS

**Eric Kripke** (*The Boys*, *Supernatural*) — The superhero deconstruction of disaster preparedness. Who are the real "heroes" when the grid fails? The engineers. The linemen. The robot operators. Not the people in capes.

**Ronald D. Moore** (*Battlestar Galactica*, *For All Mankind*) — Moore's gift for "what if" grounded in character makes him the ideal showrunner for any AEGIS series. *BSG* proved he can make a show about the end of civilization that is really about who we become in the crisis.

**Damon Lindelof** (*Watchmen*, *The Leftovers*) — The mystery-box Carrington Event. What if the storm isn't just a storm? What if the preparation reveals something about the preparers? Lindelof's theological-scientific synthesis is the exact register for a story about the Sun as both physics problem and existential reckoning.

**Vince Gilligan** (*Breaking Bad*, *Better Call Saul*) — *"The Transformer"*: A character study of a utility engineer in the years before the storm. He knows what's coming. His utility knows. They've done the math. They've written the memos. The memos are sitting in a file cabinet because hardening costs money and the PUC won't approve the rate increase. What does a competent person do when the system prevents them from being competent? Gilligan territory.

**Noah Hawley** (*Fargo*, *Legion*) — The surrealist Carrington Event. The aurora as character. The electromagnetic field as antagonist. Hawley's visual language applied to the science of GIC saturation — transformers literally singing as their cores saturate, humming at frequencies they were never designed to carry.

**Graham Yost** (*Justified*, *Silo*) — The bunker drama. AEGIS Command, deep underground, as the storm hits above. The 18 Agents sealed in their hardened facility, running the global response through a LoRa mesh network. Yost's gift for confined-space tension (*Silo*) transferred to the most important 72 hours in human history.

**Lisa Joy & Jonathan Nolan** (*Westworld*, *The Peripheral*) — The "before" story. The decade of preparation. The engineers, the diplomats, the insurance people, the families — all the parallel tracks of human activity leading up to a single moment when NOAA issues the G5 warning. *Westworld*'s multiple-timeline structure applied to the preparation timeline.

## GAMING STUDIOS

**Epic Games (Fortnite)** — *"AEGIS: The Storm"*: A limited-time Fortnite event. The G5 storm is coming. Players must gather resources, build hardened shelters, protect substations, and coordinate with their squad to keep the lights on. Educational. Intense. Reaches 250 million players in one event. The Williams Heuristic, gamified.

**Naughty Dog** (*The Last of Us*) — *"The Long Dark"*: A narrative-driven game about a parent and child navigating a post-GMD world. Five years after the storm. No zombies. No monsters. Just the quiet apocalypse of infrastructure failure. Naughty Dog's emotional storytelling applied to the most realistic apocalypse ever depicted.

**Ubisoft** (*The Division*) — *"AEGIS: Agent Protocol"*: An open-world action game set during the 72-hour warning window. Players are AEGIS agents deployed to different cities to harden substations, evacuate hospitals, and maintain order. Procedural missions. Real physics. The Charlemagne fleet as a mobile base.

**CD Projekt Red** (*Cyberpunk 2077*, *The Witcher*) — *"2086"*: A cyberpunk-noir set in a world that DID prepare for the Carrington Event — and in doing so, created a society where infrastructure is so hardened, so resilient, and so controlled by the entities that built it that individual freedom has been traded for collective survival. The dystopian cost of the Dielectric Citadel.

**Bungie** (*Destiny*) — *"AEGIS: Guardians"*: A live-service multiplayer game where players are guardians of the last powered grid on Earth. The solar storm has come and gone. The grid is running on hardened backup. Your fireteam must defend it, expand it, and reconnect the world — one substation at a time. Bungie's "hopeful post-apocalypse" tone (*Destiny*) applied to infrastructure restoration.

## COMICS & GRAPHIC NOVELS

**Marvel Comics** — *"AEGIS"*: An original Marvel series about the 18 Agents. Each Agent is a Marvel hero in the tradition of Iron Man (technology as superpower, competence as character arc). Kairos Steele as the Nick Fury of AEGIS. The Charlemagne Fleet as the Helicarrier — but it WALKS.

**DC Comics** — *"Blackout"*: A DC Black Label limited series. A world where the heroes failed — not because they weren't powerful enough, but because the threat was the Sun and no amount of punching solves a magnetic field. The story of ordinary people in a superhero universe dealing with an infrastructure problem that Superman can't punch. DC's answer to *Watchmen* for the climate-adjacent era.

**Image Comics** — *"Citadel"*: Creator-owned. The full creative freedom to explore any corner of the AEGIS universe. The 18 Agents. The ships. The robots. The insurance adjusters. The diplomats. Image is the right home for the definitive graphic novel treatment — no editorial constraints, full creative control, 100% IP ownership for the creators.

**Dark Horse Comics** — *"Phantom"*: A tech-noir series about Nyx Calder and the Phantom MK-1. Each issue is a mission. The art focuses on the robot — every panel a technical diagram come to life. The *Aliens* / *Predator* publisher doing for robotics what those franchises did for creature design.

**BOOM! Studios** — *"SiblingFrequency"*: A YA graphic novel adaptation of the CSM radio show. The story of the Carrington Event told through the eyes of teenagers who grew up listening to the broadcasts. BOOM!'s strength in YA graphic storytelling (*Lumberjanes*, *Giant Days*) applied to the most scientifically accurate disaster story ever told.
