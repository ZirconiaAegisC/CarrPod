**NATIONAL DEFENSE IMPERATIVE: AEGIS COVENANT WARNS CONGRESS OF GEOMAGNETIC THREAT TO STRATEGIC DETERRENCE**

ARLINGTON, Va. — In testimony submitted to the House Armed Services Committee, the AEGIS Covenant has warned that a Carrington-class geomagnetic event could degrade U.S. strategic deterrence capabilities within seconds of CME arrival, calling for immediate MXene hardening of all strategic command, control, and communications (C3) infrastructure.

"The unthinkable scenario is a CME arriving during a crisis with a near-peer competitor," said Brig. Gen. (Ret.) Chen. "The electromagnetic pulse from the sun doesn't distinguish between civilian transformers and military command nodes. It degrades everything simultaneously."

The Covenant's recommendations include: MXene hardening of all C3 facilities under U.S. Strategic Command authority, pre-positioning of shielded mobile command posts at 14 bases worldwide, integration of NOAA space weather alerts into the nuclear decision-making timeline, and mandatory electromagnetic resilience standards for all new military construction under the FY2027 NDAA.

The HASC Strategic Forces Subcommittee has scheduled a closed hearing for August.
