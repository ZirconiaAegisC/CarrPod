**GOVTECH FOR THE SUN: AEGIS COVENANT OPEN-SOURCES GEOMAGNETIC EARLY WARNING STACK**

SACRAMENTO — The AEGIS Covenant has released an open-source software stack enabling state and local governments to integrate NOAA solar weather data into existing emergency management dashboards, providing actionable geomagnetic disturbance warning at the county level.

The Helios Early Warning Integration Platform (HEWIP) bridges a critical gap: while NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center generates excellent solar event data, the information flows into a federal pipeline that currently takes 4-8 hours to reach state emergency operations centers — and may never reach county-level emergency managers at all.

HEWIP ingests real-time DSCOVR satellite data, ACE solar wind measurements, and ground-based magnetometer readings, then overlays the data onto county-level infrastructure maps showing which substations, hospitals, and emergency facilities face the highest geomagnetically induced current risk. The platform generates automated alerts with specific hardening recommendations based on the projected Kp index and local grid topology.

"We built this because the technology gap between federal monitoring and local response is measured in hours — and in a CME event, hours are everything," said Dr. Alex Kim, AEGIS Chief Technology Officer. "A county emergency manager in Nebraska should have the same warning as a federal operations center in Boulder."

HEWIP has been deployed in pilot with emergency management agencies in Colorado, Texas, and Florida. The full source code, documentation, and deployment playbook are available under Apache 2.0 license at github.com/aegis-covenant/hewip.
