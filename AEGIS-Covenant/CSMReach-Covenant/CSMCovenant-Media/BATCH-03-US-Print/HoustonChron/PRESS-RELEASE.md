HEADLINE: CenterPoint, the Houston Grid, and a Texas Neighbor Who Engineered the Solar Shield

HOUSTON — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS from its headquarters just 165 miles west in Austin, delivering a story with natural Houston relevance, ERCOT urgency, and the kind of energy-infrastructure authority the Houston Chronicle owns in its coverage.

Houston is not just an energy capital — it is the energy capital. CenterPoint Energy's service territory covers the nation's fourth-largest city and its critical industrial backbone, including the Houston Ship Channel and the petrochemical complex that powers a significant fraction of the global economy. A geomagnetic storm capable of damaging CenterPoint's substation transformers would not merely darken homes — it would ripple through global supply chains.

"The Houston region powers more than Texas — it powers the world. Protecting that infrastructure is not just a local priority. It's a global one," said Kairos Steele, CSM's CTO. "We built AEGIS in Austin. It's been independently validated. Houston's energy leadership should be asking: are we next?"

CSM was founded in 2024, holds 14 patents pending, and offers CTO Kairos Steele for in-person interviews at the Chronicle's Houston newsroom.
