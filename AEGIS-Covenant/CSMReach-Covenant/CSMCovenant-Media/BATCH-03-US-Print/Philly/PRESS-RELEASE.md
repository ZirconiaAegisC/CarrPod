HEADLINE: PECO, PJM, and the Grid Shield the Mid-Atlantic Never Knew It Needed

PHILADELPHIA — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, a development that raises urgent accountability questions for PECO, PPL, and the PJM Interconnection — the kind of utility-accountability journalism the Philadelphia Inquirer has practiced with distinction for decades.

The numbers that matter for Philadelphia readers: PECO serves 1.6 million electric customers. PJM coordinates the movement of electricity across 13 states and the District of Columbia. NOAA estimates a 28% probability of a major solar flare impact within two years. And as of this month, a Texas company has independently validated the first commercially tested hardware that can protect all of it. The question the Inquirer can ask — and no one else in the market will — is: what are our utilities doing about it?

"Utility accountability is the cornerstone of resilient infrastructure," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "PECO's ratepayers deserve to know whether the substation protecting their block has been hardened against a threat that science has priced at $2.6 trillion in national damage."

CSM was founded in Austin, Texas in 2024 and holds 14 patents pending. CTO interviews, lab documentation, and a utility-question briefing package are available.
