HEADLINE: DTE Energy, the Motor City's Grid, and the Solar Shield Detroit's Industrial Comeback Needs

DETROIT — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, a development with powerful resonance for Detroit's ongoing revitalization narrative — the kind of infrastructure-preparedness story the Detroit Free Press has woven into its coverage of the city's resurgence.

Detroit's industrial renaissance — from EV battery plants to advanced manufacturing hubs — depends on reliable power. DTE Energy serves 2.2 million electric customers across Southeast Michigan. The region's grid, built on an aging industrial backbone now being modernized for 21st-century demands, has zero protection against the one threat that could set that modernization back by decades: a Carrington-level geomagnetic storm.

"Detroit rebuilt itself once. It shouldn't have to do it again because of a solar storm we knew was coming and had the technology to protect against," said Kairos Steele, CSM's CTO. "DTE's modernization investments are significant. Adding geomagnetic hardening to that modernization is the kind of forward-thinking infrastructure decision Detroit deserves."

CSM was founded in Austin in 2024, holds 14 patents pending, and offers CTO interviews, lab documentation, and Michigan-specific grid vulnerability analysis.
