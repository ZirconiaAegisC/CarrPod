HEADLINE: Oncor's Texas Grid, the Sun's Wrath, and a Neighbor Who Built the Answer

DALLAS — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS from its headquarters in Austin, Texas — a hometown innovation story with direct implications for Oncor, ERCOT, and every Texan who lived through the grid anxieties of recent years.

The Dallas Morning News has chronicled Texas' unique grid independence and its vulnerabilities with authority. Here is a new chapter: a Texas company, founded in the state capital, has built and independently validated the first commercially tested hardware that protects the grid from the largest single natural threat to electrical infrastructure — a Carrington-level geomagnetic storm. ERCOT, which famously operates as an electrical island, would have no neighboring grids to lean on if its own transformers were damaged by solar-induced currents.

"Texas built its own grid. It's fitting that a Texas company built the shield for it," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "We're not waiting for a federal mandate. We're not waiting for a crisis. We built the solution, we tested it, and it's ready — right here in the Lone Star State."

CSM maintains engineering operations in Austin and holds 14 patents pending. CTO interviews, local-angle B-roll, and Texas-specific grid vulnerability data are available.
