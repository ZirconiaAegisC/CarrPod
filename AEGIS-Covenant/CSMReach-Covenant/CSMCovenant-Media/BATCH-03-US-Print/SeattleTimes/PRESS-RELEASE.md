HEADLINE: Puget Sound Energy, Seattle City Light, and the Solar Shield the Pacific Northwest's Grid Needs

SEATTLE — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, a development with particular relevance for the Pacific Northwest's hydro-powered grid, its long north-south transmission corridors, and the utilities that serve one of America's most technology-dependent populations — all core coverage areas for the Seattle Times.

The Pacific Northwest's grid is a marvel of renewable engineering, dominated by hydroelectric generation and long-distance transmission lines that carry power from the Columbia River basin to population centers from Seattle to Portland. Those same long transmission corridors are precisely the infrastructure most susceptible to geomagnetically induced currents during a Carrington-level event.

"Seattle is home to some of the most technologically sophisticated consumers in the world. Those consumers deserve a grid that's been hardened against a threat they've probably never heard of," said Kairos Steele, CSM's CTO. "We've validated the alloy science. We've tested the hardware. Now the conversation needs to happen between Puget Sound Energy, Seattle City Light, and the people they serve."

CSM was founded in Austin in 2024 with 14 patents pending. CTO availability, lab documentation, and PNW-specific grid analysis are available.
