HEADLINE: Chicago's Grid, the Sun's Next Superstorm, and the Startup That Built a Shield

CHICAGO — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, a development with direct relevance to ComEd's 4 million customers, the PJM Interconnection, and the broader Midwest transmission corridor that the Chicago Tribune has covered with distinction for generations.

The Midwest is not merely a consumer of electricity — it is the transmission crossroads of the continent. High-voltage lines radiating from Chicago connect the Great Plains wind corridor to Eastern population centers, creating long, geomagnetically sensitive pathways that amplify the very currents a Carrington event would induce. The AEGIS modular alloy technology, now independently validated, is designed precisely for deployment along such extended transmission corridors.

"Chicago is the electrical crossroads of North America. That makes it both indispensable and disproportionately exposed," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "We've now tested and validated hardware that can protect those crossroads. The question for Illinois regulators and ComEd leadership is: what's the timeline?"

CSM was founded in Austin, Texas in 2024 and holds 14 patents pending. CTO interviews, lab documentation, and technical imagery are available.
