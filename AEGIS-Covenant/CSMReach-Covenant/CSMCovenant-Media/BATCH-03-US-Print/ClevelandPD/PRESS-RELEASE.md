HEADLINE: FirstEnergy, the Rust Belt Grid, and the Solar Shield Cleveland's Industrial Infrastructure Deserves

CLEVELAND — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, a development that speaks directly to the infrastructure renewal conversation underway across Northeast Ohio — the kind of industrial-accountability journalism the Plain Dealer has anchored in Cleveland for nearly two centuries.

FirstEnergy's Ohio service territory, including the Cleveland metropolitan area, encompasses some of the most heavily industrialized electrical infrastructure in the nation. The transformers feeding Cleveland's manufacturing base, its healthcare corridor, and its growing tech sector are the same transformers that would be first to fail during a Carrington-level geomagnetic event. NOAA's 28% probability of a major X-flare impact within two years makes this a workforce, economic-development, and public-safety question — not an academic exercise.

"Cleveland built America. Protecting the grid that powers its next chapter is not optional — it's a fiduciary responsibility to the people and businesses that depend on it," said Kairos Steele, CSM's CTO. "The alloy science is validated. The hardware is tested. FirstEnergy's leadership now has a choice about whether to act before the sun does."

CSM was founded in Austin in 2024, holds 14 patents pending, and offers CTO interviews, lab documentation, and Ohio-specific analysis.
