HEADLINE: Eversource, National Grid, and the Solar Shield New England's Grid Has Never Had

BOSTON — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, a development with immediate implications for Massachusetts' aging electrical infrastructure, the ISO New England grid, and the region's ambitious clean-energy transition — all enduring editorial priorities of the Boston Globe.

New England's grid faces a distinctive convergence of vulnerabilities: an aging transformer fleet, constrained natural-gas pipeline capacity in winter, and transmission corridors that run through geologically conductive terrain that amplifies geomagnetically induced currents. The AEGIS modular alloy platform, now validated by three independent laboratories, directly addresses each of these exposure vectors.

"New England's energy resilience conversation has focused on fuel security and winter reliability — both critical," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "But a Carrington event doesn't care about our fuel mix. It cares about physics. And the physics says our long transmission corridors act as antennas for solar-induced currents. We've now built and tested the technology that breaks that circuit."

CSM was founded in Austin in 2024 with 14 patents pending. CTO availability, lab validation reports, and New England-specific infrastructure analysis are available for Globe editorial review.
