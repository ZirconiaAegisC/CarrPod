HEADLINE: Leaked: The 47-Page Pitch Deck That Convinced NATO's Innovation Fund to Bet on Solar Storm Defence

LONDON — Business Insider has obtained Carrington Storm Motors' Series B pitch deck, a 47-page document that makes one of the most unusual cases for investment in recent venture capital history: that the sun, with a 12% annual probability, will trigger a £2.1 trillion global catastrophe, and that buying hardened electric vehicles is the most cost-effective hedge.

The deck, presented to Breakthrough Energy Ventures and the NATO Innovation Fund in early 2026, opens with a slide titled "The Threat No One Is Pricing" and proceeds through actuarial modelling of geomagnetic risk, technical specifications of the CSM-1's hardening architecture, and a financial model projecting profitability by Q3 2028.

A slide marked "CONFIDENTIAL — COMPETITIVE MOAT" details the company's proprietary silicon-carbide MOSFET topology and the 2.3 million-scenario AI model that underpins its grid-node targeting algorithm. Another slide, "Total Addressable Market," estimates the global grid-hardening market at £47 billion—roughly ten times the total capitalisation of all current EV startups combined.

CSM declined to comment on the leaked deck but confirmed the £180 million raise.
