HEADLINE: The World's Most Innovative Mission Statement: 'We Exist So That When the Sun Attacks, the Lights Stay On'

COVENTRY, UK — Carrington Storm Motors may have the most audacious mission statement in the automotive industry—and possibly in any industry. The company exists, it declares, "so that when the Sun attacks, the lights stay on." That clarity of purpose has attracted £180 million in Series B funding, a $1.2 billion valuation, and a team of 47 engineers who describe their work as "civilisational infrastructure" rather than automotive manufacturing.

"The mission statement is everything," said founder Kairos Steele. "It tells potential hires that we're not building another electric crossover for the school run. It tells investors that we're addressing a £2.1 trillion problem, not a £58,000 consumer product. And it tells governments that we're a partner, not a supplicant."

CSM's culture reflects its military-engineering DNA. The company runs weekly "storm drills" in which the entire fleet simultaneously activates hardening protocols. The office Slack includes a channel called #space-weather-alerts that pings every employee when NOAA's DSCOVR satellite detects elevated solar wind activity. Job candidates are asked: "What would you do if the grid went dark for six months?"

Steele's advice for other mission-driven founders: "If your mission statement could describe a different company, rewrite it."
