SUBJECT: "The Hum Beneath the Silence" — Roshi Joan Halifax on why protecting the grid is a spiritual act

Tricycle Editorial Team,

Every zendo has a light switch. Every retreat depends on refrigeration, water pumps, and communications. The silence of meditation is made possible by the hum of the grid. Roshi Joan Halifax has written an essay for Tricycle arguing that to be truly mindful is to hold this fact in awareness — and to act on it.

"The Hum Beneath the Silence" explores Zen interdependence (engi) through the lens of electromagnetic vulnerability. The grid is pratityasamutpada made physical: each transformer connected to every other, each hospital dependent on a distant substation, each meditation hall sustained by the same network that powers the ICU down the street.
