**TRICYCLE: THE BUDDHIST REVIEW: MINDFULNESS OF THE GRID — MEDITATION AND INFRASTRUCTURE IN THE AGE OF CME**

NEW YORK — The AEGIS Covenant has published a reflective essay in Tricycle: The Buddhist Review, authored by Zen teacher and AEGIS mindfulness advisor Roshi Joan Halifax, exploring the relationship between meditative awareness and the infrastructure that makes contemplative life possible.

"Every zendo has a light switch," writes Roshi Halifax. "Every meditation retreat depends on refrigeration, water pumps, and communications networks. The silence we cultivate on the cushion is made possible by the hum of the grid. To be mindful of that fact — to hold in awareness the interdependence of spiritual practice and electrical infrastructure — is to understand that protecting the grid is a spiritual act."

The essay, "The Hum Beneath the Silence," traces the Zen concept of interdependence (engi) through the lens of electromagnetic vulnerability, arguing that the Buddhist understanding of interconnectedness finds its most urgent contemporary expression in the grid topology that links every hospital, every water treatment plant, every food distribution center — and every meditation hall — in a web of mutual dependence.

Roshi Halifax further argues that the Buddhist community has a particular responsibility to engage with the electromagnetic defense effort because contemplatives are trained to hold uncomfortable truths. "We sit with impermanence every morning," she writes. "We can sit with the knowledge that the Sun could collapse the grid within our lifetime — and from that sitting, find the clarity to act."

Tricycle has published the essay and will host a live online discussion with Roshi Halifax and Venerable Thubten Chodron.
