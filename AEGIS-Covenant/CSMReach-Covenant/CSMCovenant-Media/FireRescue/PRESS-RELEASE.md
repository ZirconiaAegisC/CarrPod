**RESCUE IN THE DARK: AEGIS COVENANT TRAINS FIRST GEOMAGNETIC RESCUE CADRE**

PITTSBURGH — Twenty-four firefighters from six states completed the first AEGIS Covenant Geomagnetic Rescue Operations (GRO) certification course this week, becoming the inaugural cadre qualified to conduct search-and-rescue operations in a grid-down, communication-denied electromagnetic event environment.

The 80-hour course, conducted at the Allegheny County Fire Academy, covered electromagnetic-effects triage (identifying and bypassing EMP-damaged vehicle systems), manual-extrication techniques without hydraulic power, analog navigation and patient tracking, and the deployment of MXene-shielded portable repeaters for intra-team communication when all cellular and radio infrastructure is disabled.

"Everything we train for assumes 911 works, the Jaws of Life have power, and mutual aid is a radio call away," said Captain Maria Santos, Pittsburgh Bureau of Fire, a course graduate. "This course asked us to train for the opposite. It was the hardest three weeks of my career, and the most necessary."

The GRO certification is the first of its kind in the United States. FEMA's National Fire Academy has reviewed the curriculum, and discussions are underway to incorporate GRO modules into the standard Incident Command System electromagnetic-annex training pipeline.

The Covenant has committed to training 1,200 GRO-certified rescue technicians across all 50 states by Q4 2027, with priority placement in departments that also serve as Veteran Response Corps nexus points under BILL-006. Each graduate receives a MXene-hardened equipment kit including portable repeaters, manual extrication tools, and analog navigation sets.

"When the grid is gone, the rescues still happen. They just happen differently," said Santos.
