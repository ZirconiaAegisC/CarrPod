HEADLINE: Nordic Pension Funds Evaluate Solar Storm Resilience as New Alternative Asset Class

LONDON — Three Nordic pension funds collectively managing £240 billion in assets are evaluating Carrington Storm Motors' solar storm resilience index as a screening tool for portfolio risk management, according to people familiar with the discussions. The move signals growing institutional recognition that geomagnetic risk represents a material, unhedged exposure in traditional equity and fixed-income portfolios.

CSM's index, developed by a team of former Barclays risk analysts, rates publicly traded companies on their exposure to solar storm disruption. Early screening results show that no major index constituent currently discloses geomagnetic risk—a disclosure gap that the pension funds view as actionable.

"Pension funds have a fiduciary duty to consider all material risks," said CSM's founder Kairos Steele. "For a fund with a 30-year liability horizon, a 12% annual probability of a £2.1 trillion event is not a tail risk—it's a certainty over a long enough time horizon."

The company is also in discussions with two European insurance-linked securities desks about structuring a catastrophe bond whose trigger would be tied to solar storm metrics rather than the traditional natural catastrophe perils of wind, flood, and earthquake.
