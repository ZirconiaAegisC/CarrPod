**ABOUT ISLAM: PROTECTING THE UMMAH FROM THE SKY — A PRACTICAL GUIDE FOR MUSLIM COMMUNITIES**

WASHINGTON, D.C. — The AEGIS Covenant and About Islam have partnered to release a community-level electromagnetic preparedness guide specifically designed for Muslim families and masjids, framed through the Islamic principle of protecting the ummah — the global community of believers.

"When we speak of protecting the ummah, we usually mean protecting Muslims from persecution, from poverty, from ignorance," writes Imam Hassan in the guide's introduction. "But the ummah is also protected when we protect the electrical infrastructure that powers the hospitals where Muslim children are born, the water pumps that provide wudu water, the refrigerators that preserve halal food, and the communication networks that connect Muslims across the world during Ramadan."

The guide, "Hifz al-Ummah: Protecting the Community in an Electromagnetic Age," provides practical steps for Muslim families and institutions: home preparedness (MXene-hardened solar chargers for essential medical devices, 90-day fuel stabilizer for generators, water purification alternatives when electric pumps fail), masjid resilience (designating the masjid as a community refuge with hardened communications, pre-positioned supplies, and coordination with the county-level Veteran Response Corps), and advocacy (contacting elected officials regarding BILL-006, DPA activation, and international mutual-aid provisions for Muslim-majority nations).

The guide is available for free download in English, Arabic, Urdu, French, and Malay at aboutislam.net. About Islam has also launched a video series featuring Muslim scientists, imams, and community leaders discussing the intersection of faith and electromagnetic resilience.
