HEADLINE: Solar Storm Warning: The Weather Story That Isn't Weather — What Your Viewers Need to Know

ATLANTA — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, offering The Weather Channel a unique opportunity to extend its space-weather coverage from forecast to solution — telling viewers not just what's coming, but what's been built to protect them.

Space weather is weather. NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center operates 24/7, issuing watches and warnings for geomagnetic storms just as the National Hurricane Center tracks tropical systems. The difference is scale. A Carrington-level event would not darken a coastline — it would darken a continent. And until this month, no commercially viable protection existed for the grid infrastructure that delivers power to every Weather Channel viewer's home.

"Your audience trusts you to tell them what's coming. We want them to know something else: someone built the shield," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "Geomagnetic storms are as forecastable as hurricanes. The difference is that until now, no one had engineered the storm shutters."

CSM was founded in 2024, holds 14 patents pending, and offers B-roll, animated storm-visualization content, and CTO interview availability.
