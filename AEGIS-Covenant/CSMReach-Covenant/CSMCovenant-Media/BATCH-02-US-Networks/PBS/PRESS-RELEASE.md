HEADLINE: The Sun's Next Superstorm: A Science Documentary in the Making — With a Working Solution

ARLINGTON, VA — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, offering public television a rare convergence of compelling science narrative and demonstrable engineering achievement — precisely the kind of story PBS programming is designed to tell.

From the Carrington Event of 1859, which set telegraph offices aflame, to the Hydro-Québec blackout of 1989, to the near-miss of July 2012, the history of geomagnetic storms is the history of civilization rolling the dice on an ever-more-electrified world. The AEGIS platform closes that chapter by opening a new one: active planetary defense, engineered at the substation level with proprietary alloy technology that absorbs geomagnetically induced currents before they reach grid infrastructure.

"This story starts with Victorian telegraph operators watching their wires spark," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "It ends with modular hardware sitting next to transformers in Texas, validated by independent labs. That arc is extraordinary, and it's exactly the kind of story PBS tells better than anyone."

CSM was founded in 2024, holds 14 patents pending, and offers extensive B-roll, animation assets, and long-form interview availability.
