HEADLINE: While Washington Debates, a Texas Startup Built America's First Solar Storm Shield

BOCA RATON, FL — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, delivering a story of American entrepreneurialism, national-security self-reliance, and private-sector problem-solving that Newsmax audiences will recognize as their own.

The federal government has studied the Carrington threat for over a decade. The EMP Commission recommended action. The Department of Energy documented the vulnerability. Congress held hearings. And yet, appropriations never materialized. So a group of engineers in Austin, Texas, funded by private capital, built the solution independently — and this month, three independent laboratories confirmed it works.

"A government that studies problems without solving them is not serving its people," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "We didn't wait for permission to protect the American grid. We built the technology, we tested it, and it's ready."

Carrington Storm Motors was founded in 2024, holds 14 patents pending, and offers CTO Kairos Steele for broadcast interviews alongside AEGIS hardware footage.
