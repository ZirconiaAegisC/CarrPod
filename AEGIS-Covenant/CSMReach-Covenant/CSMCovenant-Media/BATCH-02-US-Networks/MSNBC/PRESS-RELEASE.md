HEADLINE: Eleven Years of Congressional Hearings. Zero Dollars Appropriated. One Startup Built the Solution Anyway.

NEW YORK — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, a story that lands squarely at the intersection of policy failure, private-sector innovation, and infrastructure justice — the editorial territory MSNBC's audience engages with most deeply.

Since the 2015 EMP Commission report, Congress has held over a dozen hearings on grid vulnerability to geomagnetic disturbance. The National Academy of Sciences has priced a Carrington-level event at $2.6 trillion. The Department of Energy, in its 2025 Infrastructure Resilience Report, identified geomagnetic disturbance as the single largest unaddressed risk to the Bulk Power System. And yet, total federal appropriations for grid hardening against solar threats remain at zero.

"Democracies are supposed to protect their citizens from foreseeable catastrophic risk," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "When government fails to act on a threat it has studied for over a decade, private enterprise has a responsibility to fill the gap."

CSM was founded in Austin in 2024 with private funding. The company holds 14 patents pending and offers CTO availability, hardware B-roll, and lab validation documentation for broadcast.
