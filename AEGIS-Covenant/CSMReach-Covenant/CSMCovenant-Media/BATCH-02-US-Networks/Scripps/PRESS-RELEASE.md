HEADLINE: Local Newsrooms, Meet the Biggest Infrastructure Story of the Decade

CINCINNATI — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, delivering a story engine that every Scripps local newsroom can drive into their community with immediate, tangible relevance.

The local angle writes itself. Your station's DMA has a utility — municipal, cooperative, or investor-owned. That utility operates substations. Those substations currently have zero defense against a solar event that NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center says has a 28% probability of direct impact within two years. The National Academy of Sciences prices the damage at $2.6 trillion nationally. Your viewers will want to know: is our local grid protected?

"News directors don't need to become electrical engineers to cover this story," said Kairos Steele, CSM's CTO. "They need to ask their local utility one question on camera. We provide the context, the science, and the hardware footage that makes the answer matter."

CSM, founded in Austin in 2024, holds 14 patents pending and offers localization-ready visual assets, CTO interview slots, and utility-question guides for Scripps journalists.
