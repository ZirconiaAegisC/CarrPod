HEADLINE: Congressional Testimony Material: The Grid Vulnerability Congress Has Discussed — And a Private-Sector Solution Now Validated

WASHINGTON — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, providing precisely the kind of policy-relevant, technically substantive subject matter that CSPAN's public-affairs audience values — a private-sector answer to a documented congressional concern.

Since 2015, the House Energy & Commerce Committee and the Senate Energy & Natural Resources Committee have convened multiple hearings on electromagnetic pulse and geomagnetic disturbance threats to the Bulk Power System. The Department of Energy's 2025 Infrastructure Resilience Report identified geomagnetic disturbance as the nation's largest unaddressed grid vulnerability. Yet no federal appropriations have funded deployment of protective hardware.

"The congressional record is clear and consistent on this threat," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "Our job was not to add another white paper to that record. It was to build and test the hardware that ends the discussion. Phase II is complete. Independent labs have confirmed the results. The engineering case is closed."

CSM will brief House Energy & Commerce Committee staffers in August 2026. The company holds 14 patents pending and offers technical documentation and CTO availability for public-affairs programming.
