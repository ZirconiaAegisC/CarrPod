HEADLINE: A $2.6 Trillion Unpriced Risk: The Startup That Built a Hedge Against the Sun

NEW YORK — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS — a development that rewrites the risk calculus for every utility, every insurer, every asset manager with infrastructure exposure, and every Bloomberg Terminal subscriber watching the energy sector.

The numbers are stark. A Carrington-level geomagnetic storm would cause an estimated $2.6 trillion in U.S. economic damage, according to the National Academy of Sciences. NOAA places the probability of a major X-flare impact at 28% within the current solar cycle. That's a higher annualized risk than many events the re-insurance market prices as standard. And yet, until CSM completed Phase II validation, no commercially tested hardware existed to mitigate that risk.

"Markets price what they can measure. We've made geomagnetic resilience measurable," said Kairos Steele, CSM's Chief Technology Officer. "For the first time, a utility CFO can put a hard number against grid protection — and a hard technology behind it."

CSM was founded in Austin, Texas in 2024, raised private capital, and holds 14 patents pending. CTO Kairos Steele is available for Bloomberg studio interviews.
