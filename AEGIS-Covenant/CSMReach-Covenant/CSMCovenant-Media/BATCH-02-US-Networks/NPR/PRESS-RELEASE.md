HEADLINE: What Happens When the Sun Throws a Tantrum? One Company Has the Audio-Ready Answer

WASHINGTON — Carrington Storm Motors (CSM) today announced the successful Phase II independent validation of Project AEGIS, a story tailor-made for NPR's audio-first, intellectually curious audience — rich with sound, explanation, and consequence.

Consider the audio possibilities: the crackle of a high-voltage transformer test, the hum of a substation at midnight, the voice of a Texas engineer explaining how proprietary alloys — developed in a small Austin lab — can absorb the energy of a star. This is radio at its best: a story you can close your eyes and see.

"The Carrington threat is easy to explain over dinner," said Kairos Steele, CSM's CTO. "The sun throws a tantrum, magnetic fields hit Earth, the grid becomes a giant antenna, transformers fry, and anything that plugs into a wall stops working — for months. We've now built and tested the device that stops that chain reaction. It's not theoretical. It's sitting in a warehouse in Texas, and it works."

CSM was founded in 2024, holds 14 patents pending, and offers CTO Kairos Steele for extended audio interviews with natural sound and technical B-roll for digital platforms.
