**THE PUBLIC EXECUTIVE'S GUIDE TO LEADING THROUGH THE UNTHINKABLE**

SACRAMENTO — The AEGIS Covenant has published "Leading Through the Long Dark," a public-sector executive leadership guide for elected officials, city managers, and agency directors facing the governance challenges of a protracted grid-down electromagnetic event.

The guide, written with input from three former governors, two city managers, and a retired FEMA Regional Administrator, addresses the leadership dimensions that technology-focused preparedness documents ignore: public communication when there are no communication platforms, maintaining civil order when the social contract is strained, managing the psychological toll on public employees who are themselves disaster victims, and the moral triage decisions that no public executive has been trained to make.

"Technical experts tell you about hardening substations," said Dr. Patricia Okonkwo, AEGIS Public Governance Fellow and former city manager of Rochester. "Nobody tells a mayor what to say when 70,000 constituents are standing outside City Hall in the dark, asking when the lights come back on, and the honest answer is 'we don't know.' This guide does."

The guide's centerpiece is the "Executive Triad" framework: Message (what you say and how you say it when there's no Twitter, no email, no press conference broadcast), Mechanism (how to actually deliver services — food, water, medical care, security — using pre-positioned analog systems and veteran auxiliary personnel under BILL-006), and Meaning (maintaining public trust and social cohesion when the normal instruments of governance are unavailable).

The guide is being distributed to members of ICMA, the U.S. Conference of Mayors, and the National League of Cities.
