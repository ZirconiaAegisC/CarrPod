**EMERGENCY MEDICINE MEETS THE CARRINGTON EVENT: AEGIS COVENANT RELEASES EMS CONTINUITY PROTOCOL**

BOSTON — The AEGIS Covenant, in collaboration with the National Association of EMS Physicians, has released the Geomagnetic EMS Continuity Protocol — the first clinical-operational framework for maintaining emergency medical services during a prolonged electromagnetic disruption.

The protocol addresses a nightmare scenario for EMS directors: a Carrington-class CME simultaneously disabling ambulance electronic control modules, hospital backup generators with unshielded transfer switches, medication refrigeration systems, and every digital patient-care reporting platform. "We plan for mass-casualty incidents," said Dr. Sarah Okonkwo, AEGIS Medical Director. "We have never planned for a mass-casualty continent."

The protocol establishes four operational tiers based on event severity and duration. Tier 1 (1-7 days) activates pre-positioned MXene-shielded portable ventilators, infusion pumps, and defibrillators cached at all 14,000 Veteran Response Corps nexus points. Tier 2 (1-4 weeks) shifts to paper-based triage, manual medication tracking, and the deployment of non-electronic patient evacuation using veteran-crewed transport. Tier 3 (1-12 months) institutes field-hospital protocols derived from military combat support hospital doctrine, adapted for civilian population centers.

Critically, the protocol identifies 23 medications whose cold-chain integrity determines survival for millions of Americans — insulin, certain antibiotics, refrigerated vaccines, and antirejection drugs — and maps pre-hardened storage locations within one ground-transport hour of 92% of the U.S. population.

The protocol is available for review by credentialed EMS agencies at aegis-covenant.org/ems-protocol.
