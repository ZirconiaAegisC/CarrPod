**LAW ENFORCEMENT IN THE LONG DARK: AEGIS COVENANT RELEASES POLICE CONTINUITY DOCTRINE**

WASHINGTON, D.C. — The AEGIS Covenant has published its Law Enforcement Geomagnetic Continuity Doctrine, providing the first comprehensive operational framework for maintaining public safety during an extended grid-down electromagnetic event.

The doctrine confronts hard truths: modern policing depends on systems that would vanish in a CME. Computer-aided dispatch, NCIC databases, body-worn cameras, electronic control weapons, fleet GPS — all dependent on the grid and all susceptible to geomagnetically induced currents. The doctrine estimates that a G5-class event would reduce effective patrol capacity to approximately 12% of normal within 48 hours.

"Policing doesn't stop because the power fails," said Sheriff Marcus Webb (Ret.), AEGIS Law Enforcement Liaison. "But it has to change fundamentally, and we need to train for that change now, not during the event."

The doctrine introduces three operational shifts: Vehicle Hardening (MXene-shielded ECM retrofit kits enabling fleet operation, priced at $210 per patrol unit), Analog Dispatch (pre-positioned paper-based call tracking, handheld aviation-band radios with hardened chargers, and veteran-staffed auxiliary dispatch centers at VFW posts under BILL-006), and Community Policing Without Comms (neighborhood liaison networks, manual evidence handling, and interagency runner protocols for jurisdictions sharing a border but no functioning phones).

The International Association of Chiefs of Police and the National Sheriffs' Association have received the briefing package. Five county sheriff's departments have signed on for pilot implementation beginning Q1 2027.

"We don't get to tell the public we're closed," said Webb. "This doctrine makes sure we never have to."
