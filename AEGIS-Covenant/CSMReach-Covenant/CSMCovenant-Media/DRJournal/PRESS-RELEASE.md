**DISASTER RECOVERY 2.0: AEGIS COVENANT RELEASES 36-MONTH GRID-DOWN RECOVERY BLUEPRINT**

NEW YORK — The AEGIS Covenant has published the first comprehensive post-geomagnetic event recovery blueprint, a 412-page document detailing the phased restoration of critical infrastructure following a continent-wide grid collapse.

The Disaster Recovery Journal Framework addresses a stark reality: current FEMA doctrine assumes functional communications, drivable roads, and operable fuel distribution — none of which would survive a Carrington-class event. The Covenant's model instead plans for a 36-month primary recovery window, segmented into six distinct phases from "Initial Survival" through "Grid Resynchronization."

"The disaster recovery community has never had to contemplate a scenario where all 50 states are the disaster zone simultaneously," said Dr. Vasquez. "This framework provides the sequencing logic that has been absent from every tabletop exercise conducted to date."

Phase One — "Sustain Life" — focuses on the first 72 hours: activating the Veteran Geomagnetic Response Corps under BILL-006, establishing 14,000 community nexus points at VFW and American Legion posts nationwide, and distributing pre-positioned fuel stabilizer sufficient for 90 days of generator operation at critical care facilities.

Phase Three — "Restore the Backbone" — introduces the MXene Rapid Deployment Protocol: mobile foundries transported by C-130 to 47 pre-identified staging bases, each capable of producing 300 linear meters of shielding film per day. "We designed this to work when nothing else does," said Brig. Gen. (Ret.) Chen. "No internet. No phones. Just people, checklists, and pre-positioned material."

The full blueprint is available at aegis-covenant.org/recovery-framework.
