**VETERANS: AMERICA'S PRE-POSITIONED GEOMAGNETIC RESPONSE FORCE**

FORT BRAGG, N.C. — The AEGIS Covenant has launched the nationwide recruitment campaign for the Veteran Geomagnetic Response Corps, established under the bipartisan BILL-006, calling on America's 18 million veterans to serve as the nation's pre-positioned grid-down response force.

"Our veterans have already raised their right hand and sworn an oath to protect this country," said Brig. Gen. (Ret.) Marcus Chen, Corps Coordinator and 32-year Army veteran. "This is a new mission for a new threat — and nobody is better prepared to execute it than the men and women who have already served in austere, no-comms, no-resupply environments."

The Corps will organize veterans at the county level through existing infrastructure: VFW posts, American Legion halls, and Reserve/Guard readiness centers. Each county-level unit will receive MXene-hardened communications equipment, fuel-stabilization supplies sufficient for 90-day generator operations at critical facilities, medical cold-chain preservation kits, and pre-positioned water purification systems.

Training — conducted through a combination of in-person weekend modules and a mobile application — covers grid-down triage, analog communications relay, fuel stabilization and distribution, community security coordination with local law enforcement, and the operation of portable MXene film application equipment for emergency substation hardening.

The Corps has already received 47,000 pre-registrations during the soft-launch phase. Full activation is contingent on BILL-006's passage, which has cleared the House Veterans' Affairs Committee with bipartisan support. The Senate companion bill, S-2804, is scheduled for committee markup in September.

"Every veteran who joins is saying the same thing," said Chen. "I served once. I'll serve again. This time, the mission is saving the grid."
