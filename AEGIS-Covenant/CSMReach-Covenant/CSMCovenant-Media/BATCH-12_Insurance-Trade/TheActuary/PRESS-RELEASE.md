### CSM Publishes Actuarial Standard of Practice for Infrastructure Catastrophe Risk Modeling Under BILL-005 Framework

**WASHINGTON** — The Critical Systems Mandate, in partnership with the Casualty Actuarial Society (CAS) and the Society of Actuaries (SOA), today published the CSMFAB-ACTUARY-001 Actuarial Standard of Practice (ASOP) for Infrastructure Catastrophe Risk Modeling, providing the first standardized actuarial methodology for pricing, reserving, and capital modeling of sovereign-backed infrastructure catastrophe reinsurance.

The ASOP, developed through a two-year collaborative process with 340 participating actuaries from 127 insurers and reinsurers, establishes the methodological framework for: parametric trigger probability estimation using extreme value theory and copula-based dependence modeling for compound perils; basis risk quantification between parametric triggers and actual infrastructure loss experience; best-estimate loss distribution generation incorporating CSM's 20 insurance dossiers and 47 modeled catastrophe scenarios; and regulatory capital requirement calculation under the BILL-005 sovereign reinsurance framework.

"Infrastructure catastrophe risk actuarial work has been limited by a fundamental data problem: the most extreme events, by definition, have not occurred in the historical record, making traditional frequency-severity modeling unreliable," explained Dr. Hannah Park-Morrison, CSM Chief Actuary and Fellow of the Casualty Actuarial Society. "The BILL-005 ASOP addresses this through physics-based catastrophe modeling integrated with extreme value statistical methods, creating loss distributions that are grounded in engineering vulnerability assessments rather than extrapolation from inadequate historical data."

The ASOP's key methodological innovations include: Bayesian hierarchical models combining historical claims data with physics-based CAT model output; vine copula dependence structures for modeling correlated losses across multiple infrastructure sectors and geographic regions; and explicit treatment of model uncertainty through ensemble approaches that weight multiple CAT model outputs based on hindcast validation against historical events.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 4.6:1 cost-benefit ratio for ASOP adoption, reflecting improved pricing accuracy, reduced reserve variability, and enhanced regulatory confidence in insurer solvency under the BILL-005 framework. The ASOP is published under open-access licensing through the CAS and SOA.
