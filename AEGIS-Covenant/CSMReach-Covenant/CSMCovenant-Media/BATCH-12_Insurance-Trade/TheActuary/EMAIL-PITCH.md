The Actuary editors,

The fundamental challenge of infrastructure catastrophe actuarial work: the most extreme events have not occurred in the historical record, making traditional frequency-severity modeling unreliable. When your largest loss data point is a $70B hurricane, how do you price a $340B Houston Ship Channel scenario?

CSMFAB-ACTUARY-001, the new BILL-005 ASOP developed with CAS and SOA by 340 actuaries from 127 insurers, provides the methodology:
- Bayesian hierarchical models combining historical claims data with physics-based CAT model output
- Vine copula dependence structures for correlated losses across infrastructure sectors and geographic regions
- Parametric trigger probability estimation using extreme value theory
- Basis risk quantification between parametric triggers and actual loss experience
- Ensemble CAT model weighting based on hindcast validation
- 4.6:1 cost-benefit ratio — CSM Accountant Heuristic analysis
- 47 modeled catastrophe scenarios, 20 CSM insurance dossiers as engineering input
- Explicit treatment of model uncertainty (no pretending that models are perfect)

The ASOP shifts infrastructure CAT modeling from extrapolation of inadequate history to physics-based risk assessment with actuarial rigor. Published open-access through CAS and SOA.

Dr. Hannah Park-Morrison, FCAS, our Chief Actuary, can discuss the Bayesian hierarchical methodology and copula dependence structures with specific loss distribution examples.
