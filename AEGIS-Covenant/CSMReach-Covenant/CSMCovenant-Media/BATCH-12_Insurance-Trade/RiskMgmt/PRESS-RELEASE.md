### CSM Publishes Infrastructure Risk Management Framework: BILL-005 Integration for Corporate and Municipal Risk Managers

**NEW YORK** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-RMF-001 Infrastructure Risk Management Framework, a comprehensive guide enabling corporate risk managers, municipal chief risk officers, and infrastructure asset owners to integrate BILL-005 sovereign-backed insurance products into their organizational risk management strategies.

The framework, developed with the Risk and Insurance Management Society (RIMS), the Public Risk Management Association (PRIMA), and the Disaster Recovery Institute (DRI), provides risk managers with the analytical tools, decision frameworks, and implementation roadmaps for incorporating CSM parametric CAT bonds, sovereign reinsurance protection, and the 20 CSM insurance dossiers into enterprise risk management programs.

"Corporate and municipal risk managers have been dealing with critical infrastructure risk through the same blunt instruments for decades: self-insurance, high deductibles, or catastrophic exclusions that leave their organizations exposed to precisely the losses that risk management is supposed to address," stated Dr. Mark Harrington-West, CSM Director for Enterprise Risk Management Integration. "CSMFAB-RMF-001 provides the framework for risk managers to replace exclusion with sovereign-backed coverage, replacing unmanaged tail risk with priced-and-transferred risk."

The framework includes: infrastructure asset risk-register templates aligned with CSM's 20 insurance dossiers, enabling organizations to map their specific assets to dossier vulnerability assessments; cost-benefit analysis tools incorporating CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology for evaluating BILL-005 coverage versus self-insurance or traditional coverage alternatives; parametric product selection guidelines covering trigger mechanism design, basis risk tolerance, and coverage limit optimization; and business continuity integration protocols linking parametric claims payment timelines to infrastructure restoration milestones.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 3.7:1 cost-benefit ratio for framework adoption. The complete framework, including all templates and analytical tools, is published under open-access licensing through RIMS.
