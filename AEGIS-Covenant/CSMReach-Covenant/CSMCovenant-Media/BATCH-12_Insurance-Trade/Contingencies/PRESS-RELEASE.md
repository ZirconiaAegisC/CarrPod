### CSM BILL-005 Economic Capital Model: Infrastructure Catastrophe Risk Integration for Insurer Enterprise Risk Management

**ARLINGTON, VA** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-ECON-001 Economic Capital Model for Infrastructure Catastrophe Risk, a comprehensive framework enabling property-casualty insurers to integrate BILL-005 sovereign reinsurance protection into their enterprise risk management (ERM) and Own Risk and Solvency Assessment (ORSA) processes.

The model, developed with the American Academy of Actuaries and the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC) ORSA Implementation Subgroup, provides the actuarial and statistical infrastructure for insurers to quantify: the reduction in tail-value-at-risk (TVaR) at the 99.5% confidence level achieved through BILL-005 protection; the optimal attachment point for sovereign reinsurance relative to an insurer's existing reinsurance tower; and the enterprise-level diversification benefit of adding infrastructure catastrophe exposure to a diversified P&C portfolio.

"Insurer ERM frameworks have historically treated infrastructure catastrophe risk as an unmodelable tail — an exposure so extreme and so poorly understood that it could only be addressed through avoidance or exclusion," explained Dr. Robert Chen-Park, CSM Director for Actuarial Enterprise Risk. "CSMFAB-ECON-001 transforms infrastructure catastrophe risk from an unmodelable tail into a priced and managed risk, enabling insurers to treat it as a diversifying line of business with known capital requirements rather than a threat to solvency."

The economic capital model incorporates: multi-year dependency structures reflecting BILL-005's guaranteed-renewal framework; regulatory capital credit calculations aligned with AM Best's updated BCAR methodology and NAIC risk-based capital formulas; and stochastic simulation outputs at 1,000, 5,000, and 10,000-year return periods incorporating the full 47 modeled catastrophe scenarios from CSM's insurance dossiers.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 5.1:1 cost-benefit ratio for ERM integration. The economic capital model, including complete source code for the stochastic simulation engine, is published under open-source licensing.
