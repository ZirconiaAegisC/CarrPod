### CSM BILL-005 Infrastructure Insurance Framework: Treasury and CFO Guide to Sovereign-Backed Coverage Integration

**NEW YORK** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-TR-001 Corporate Treasury Infrastructure Risk Guide, a comprehensive framework enabling corporate treasurers and chief financial officers to integrate BILL-005 sovereign-backed infrastructure insurance into their organizational risk financing strategies, capital structure optimization, and credit rating management.

The guide, developed with the Association for Financial Professionals (AFP), the Corporate Treasury Association, and Standard & Poor's Corporate Ratings group, provides treasurers and CFOs with: total cost of risk (TCOR) reduction analysis comparing BILL-005 parametric coverage against traditional insurance, self-insurance reserves, and captive insurance alternatives; credit rating impact assessment documenting how infrastructure catastrophe coverage affects corporate credit metrics and rating agency assessments; and balance sheet optimization frameworks for substituting sovereign-backed coverage for on-balance-sheet risk retention.

"Corporate treasurers have traditionally treated infrastructure catastrophe risk as an uninsurable residual — something to be retained on the balance sheet because coverage was either unavailable or prohibitively expensive," explained Dr. Michael Park-Stern, CSM Director for Corporate Treasury Integration. "CSMFAB-TR-001 enables treasurers to move infrastructure risk off the balance sheet and onto the BILL-005 sovereign facility, releasing risk capital for productive investment while improving credit metrics through reduced potential loss severity."

The guide quantifies that corporate treasurers at 340 organizations with significant infrastructure exposure have restructured their risk financing programs to incorporate BILL-005 coverage, reducing average infrastructure-related TCOR by 42% and enabling an average credit rating improvement of 0.7 notches through reduced enterprise risk exposure.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 4.3:1 cost-benefit ratio for treasury integration. The guide is published open-access through AFP.
