### CSM BILL-005 Integrates Parametric Insurance Payouts with Business Continuity Restoration Timelines

**LONDON** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-BCM-001 Business Continuity-Insurance Integration Protocol, a standardized framework linking BILL-005 parametric insurance claims payments to infrastructure restoration milestones, ensuring that post-catastrophe funding arrives in synchronization with business continuity restoration schedules rather than the weeks or months after restoration should have begun.

The protocol, developed with the Business Continuity Institute (BCI), the Disaster Recovery Institute (DRI), and the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) Technical Committee 292 (Security and Resilience), provides the first standardized methodology for aligning insurance claims payment timelines with Maximum Tolerable Period of Disruption (MTPD) and Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) for critical infrastructure assets.

"The fundamental failure of traditional insurance in infrastructure catastrophes is not coverage amount — it is timing," explained Dr. Elizabeth Chen-Morrison, CSM Director for Business Continuity Integration. "A $100 million indemnity policy that pays 127 days after a hurricane does nothing to restore power in the first 72 hours when cascading economic damage compounds exponentially. CSMFAB-BCM-001 aligns parametric insurance payments with business continuity timelines — 72-hour payment matching 72-hour restoration objectives — so that insurance funds infrastructure restoration when it is needed, not months later."

The protocol maps 340,000 critical infrastructure assets from CSM's 20 insurance dossiers to their business continuity requirements, including: MTPD for each asset class, RTO for restoration prioritization, minimum business continuity objective (MBCO), and interdependency mapping showing how delayed restoration of one asset cascades through dependent systems. Parametric trigger thresholds are calibrated so that payment occurs before MTPDs are exceeded.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 7.8:1 cost-benefit ratio, reflecting the dramatic economic impact of reducing business interruption duration through time-aligned insurance payments. The protocol is published under open-access licensing with BCI.
