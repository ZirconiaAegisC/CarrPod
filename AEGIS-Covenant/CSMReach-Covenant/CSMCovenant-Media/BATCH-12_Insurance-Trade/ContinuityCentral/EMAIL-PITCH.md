Infrastructure continuity planning has a structural flaw: every sector plans in isolation. The electric utility has its plan. The water authority has its plan. The telecom provider has its plan. But failure chains do not respect organizational boundaries — and when they cascade, the speed of systemic disruption far exceeds any single sector's recovery capability.

CSMFAB-CC-001 models 127 compound failure chains at the asset level, quantifying the exact sequence and timeline of cascading infrastructure failure:
- Grid → Water: substation failure → pumping station power loss → pressure drop → boil-water advisory → hospital shutdown
- Grid → Telecom → Water SCADA: power loss → cell tower battery depletion → SCADA communication loss → water system loss of control
- Cross-sector priority restoration sequencing to prevent cascading re-failure during recovery
- Mandatory annual compound-failure exercises involving all interdependent sectors per geographic region
- 5.8:1 cost-benefit ratio — CSM Accountant Heuristic analysis
- Developed with TISP, All-Hazards Consortium, and WEF Infrastructure Resilience Initiative
- Open-access protocol with downloadable failure chain models

Continuity in one sector is meaningless if dependent sectors fail. CSMFAB-CC-001 provides the cross-sector framework that connects isolated plans into a coherent resilience architecture.
