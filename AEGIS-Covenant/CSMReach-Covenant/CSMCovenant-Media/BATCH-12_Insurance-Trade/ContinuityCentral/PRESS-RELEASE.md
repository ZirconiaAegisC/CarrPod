### CSM Publishes Global Infrastructure Interdependency Continuity Protocol: 127 Compound Failure Chains Modeled

**LONDON** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-CC-001 Global Infrastructure Interdependency Continuity Protocol, a comprehensive framework modeling 127 compound failure chains across electrical grid, water, telecommunications, transportation, and financial infrastructure sectors, and specifying the cross-sector continuity requirements that prevent localized failures from cascading into systemic disruptions.

The protocol, developed with the Infrastructure Security Partnership (TISP), the All-Hazards Consortium, and the World Economic Forum's Infrastructure Resilience Initiative, is the first analysis to model multi-sector infrastructure failure chains at the asset level, quantifying the precise sequence and timeline through which a substation failure cascades through water pumping, telecommunications relay, traffic control, and financial transaction processing systems.

"Infrastructure continuity planning has historically been siloed by sector — the electric utility has its continuity plan, the water authority has its plan, the telecommunications provider has its plan — but failure chains do not respect organizational boundaries," explained Dr. Patricia Nakamura-Wells, CSM Director for Cross-Sector Resilience. "A substation failure that interrupts power to a water pumping station that causes pressure loss that triggers a boil-water advisory that prevents hospital operations — that is not three separate incidents; it is one compound failure chain. CSMFAB-CC-001 models all 127 such chains identified in U.S. critical infrastructure."

The protocol specifies cross-sector continuity requirements including: minimum backup power duration standards for water and telecommunications assets dependent on grid power; priority service restoration protocols that sequence sector restoration to prevent cascading re-failure (e.g., restoring telecommunications before water SCADA that depends on it); and cross-sector exercising requirements mandating annual compound-failure exercises involving all interdependent sectors within a geographic region.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 5.8:1 cost-benefit ratio. The protocol is published under open-access licensing.
