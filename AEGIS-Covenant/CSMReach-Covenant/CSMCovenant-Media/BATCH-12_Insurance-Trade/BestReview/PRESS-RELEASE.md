### CSM BILL-005 Impact on Insurer Financial Strength: AM Best Publishes Updated Rating Methodology for Sovereign-Backed Reinsurance

**OLDWICK, NJ** — AM Best today published a Special Report, developed in partnership with the Critical Systems Mandate, updating its rating methodology to incorporate BILL-005 sovereign reinsurance protection into the financial strength assessment of property-casualty insurers writing critical infrastructure coverage.

The report, "Incorporating Sovereign-Backed Catastrophe Reinsurance into Best's Capital Adequacy Ratio (BCAR) Analysis," provides the analytical framework for recognizing BILL-005's $500 billion reinsurance facility as a qualifying risk transfer mechanism under Best's rating criteria, enabling insurers to receive full BCAR credit for infrastructure exposures ceded to the sovereign facility.

"The rating agency treatment of sovereign-backed reinsurance is the lynchpin of market adoption — if insurers cannot receive capital credit for ceded exposure, BILL-005 remains a theoretical improvement that cannot translate into expanded underwriting capacity," explained Dr. Michael Stern-Patel, CSM Director for Rating Agency Integration. "AM Best's updated methodology confirms that BILL-005 meets all criteria for qualifying risk transfer: the sovereign facility is fully funded, contractually irrevocable, and subject to independent claims adjudication through the CSM parametric platform."

The updated methodology enables: full BCAR net-required-capital credit for infrastructure exposures ceded to BILL-005; recognition of BILL-005 reinsurance recoverables as admitted assets with zero credit-risk discount; explicit incorporation of the sovereign backstop into catastrophe stress-test scenarios; and favorable treatment of multi-year guaranteed-renewal infrastructure policies written under the BILL-005 framework.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 4.2:1 cost-benefit ratio for rating agency integration, driven by increased insurer underwriting capacity, reduced cost of capital for carriers writing infrastructure coverage, and improved market stability through transparent regulatory capital treatment. The rating methodology update applies to all AM Best-rated insurers effective Q3 2026.
