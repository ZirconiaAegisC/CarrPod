### CSM Publishes 20 Critical Infrastructure Insurance Dossiers: Risk-Engineering Basis for $7T Industry Transformation

**NEW YORK** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the complete set of 20 CSM Critical Infrastructure Insurance Dossiers (CSMFAB-DOSSIER-001 through 020), providing the most comprehensive risk-engineering assessment of global critical infrastructure ever assembled, covering 340,000 individual assets across the electrical grid, water systems, telecommunications, and transportation sectors.

The dossiers, developed over three years by CSM's Insurance Risk Engineering Division in partnership with AIR Worldwide, RMS, the Insurance Institute for Business & Home Safety (IBHS), and 14 university risk-engineering programs, provide asset-level vulnerability assessments, probabilistic loss estimates, and compound-event failure scenarios that form the technical underwriting basis for the $47 billion CSM parametric CAT bond program and the BILL-005 sovereign reinsurance facility.

"Insurance underwriting is only as good as the risk models that support it, and pre-BILL-005, there was no standardized, open-access methodology for assessing correlated infrastructure catastrophe risk at the asset level," stated Dr. Victoria Okonkwo-Chen, CSM Chief Insurance Risk Engineer. "The 20 dossiers provide that methodology — and because they are published under open-access licensing, every insurer, reinsurer, and ILS investor can independently validate the risk assumptions underlying CSM's parametric products."

Each dossier covers a specific infrastructure sector or peril combination, including: Electric Grid Hurricane Exposure (DOSSIER-001), Grid Seismic Risk (DOSSIER-002), Compound Flood-Wind Events (DOSSIER-004), Cyber-Physical Cascading Failure (DOSSIER-007), Wildfire-Transmission Interface (DOSSIER-011), Climate-Adjusted Flood Exposure 2025-2075 (DOSSIER-015), and Interdependent Infrastructure Failure Chains (DOSSIER-020). All dossiers include geospatial vulnerability maps, fragility curves for specific asset classes, and Monte Carlo loss simulations for the 47 modeled catastrophe scenarios.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed that open-access risk data publication generates a 4.8:1 cost-benefit ratio by improving underwriting accuracy, reducing information asymmetry in reinsurance pricing, and enabling the development of competitive parametric products by multiple carriers.
