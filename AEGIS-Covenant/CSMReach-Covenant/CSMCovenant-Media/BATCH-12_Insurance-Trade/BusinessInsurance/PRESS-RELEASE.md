### CSM Parametric CAT Bond Program Prices $47B in Grid Infrastructure Coverage Across 12 Catastrophe Perils

**CHICAGO** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-CATBOND-001 pricing sheet for its Parametric Catastrophe Resilience Bond (CRB) Program, documenting $47 billion in placed coverage for electrical grid infrastructure assets across 12 distinct catastrophe perils, with parametric triggers based on objectively verifiable physical parameters rather than loss adjustment.

The CRB program, structured through CSMFAB-INSURE-003, represents the largest parametric insurance placement in history and introduces a new asset class — infrastructure resilience bonds — that bridges the gap between traditional catastrophe insurance, which requires lengthy loss adjustment, and the immediate liquidity needs of infrastructure restoration following catastrophic events.

"Parametric triggers solve the fundamental tension in infrastructure insurance: the assets must be restored immediately after a catastrophe to prevent cascading economic damage, but traditional indemnity-based insurance requires weeks or months of loss adjustment before funds flow," explained Marcus Chen-Whitfield, CSM Director for Insurance-Linked Securities. "A parametric bond linked to NOAA hurricane wind-speed measurements at specific GPS coordinates pays within 72 hours of trigger verification — providing the immediate liquidity that infrastructure restoration requires."

The $47 billion in placed coverage spans: hurricane wind damage ($18.2B), earthquake ground motion ($12.4B), flood inundation ($7.8B), wildfire perimeters ($4.3B), ice storm accumulation ($2.1B), cyber-physical attack ($1.4B), and five additional perils. Parametric triggers are calibrated against NOAA, USGS, and FEMA data sources, with independent verification by AIR Worldwide and RMS.

CSM's 20 insurance dossiers provide the underlying risk-engineering basis for trigger calibration, documenting asset-specific vulnerability curves and loss-estimation functions for each of the 340,000 critical infrastructure assets in the covered portfolio. The Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 7.1:1 cost-benefit ratio for parametric coverage versus traditional indemnity structures, with benefit derived primarily from reduced business interruption duration through rapid claims payment.
