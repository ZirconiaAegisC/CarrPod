### CSM BILL-005 Expands Property-Casualty Infrastructure Coverage Capacity: 340 Previously Uninsurable Locations Now Underwritten

**NEW YORK** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-PC360-001 Market Capacity Impact Analysis, documenting how BILL-005's sovereign reinsurance facility has expanded property-casualty insurance capacity for critical infrastructure assets, enabling coverage in 340 locations that were previously uninsurable due to correlated catastrophe exposure exceeding private reinsurance capacity.

The analysis, produced in partnership with the American Property Casualty Insurance Association (APCIA) and the Casualty Actuarial Society (CAS), quantifies the pre-BILL-005 infrastructure coverage gap: an estimated $490 billion in critical infrastructure asset value in high-risk locations where coverage was either unavailable at any price or available only with catastrophe exclusions that rendered the coverage commercially meaningless for infrastructure loan covenants and bond rating requirements.

"Property-casualty insurers have been caught between two irreconcilable demands: infrastructure lenders require full catastrophe coverage as a condition of financing, but reinsurance capacity for correlated peak exposures simply does not exist at the scale required," stated Dr. Jonathan Pierce-Whitfield, CSM Director for P&C Market Integration. "BILL-005 breaks this deadlock by providing the sovereign reinsurance layer that absorbs peak tail risk, enabling primary carriers to provide coverage that was previously impossible."

The analysis documents that carrier partners have underwritten $127 billion in new infrastructure coverage in the first 18 months following BILL-005 ratification, with an average premium reduction of 34% compared to the pre-BILL-005 limited-capacity market. Coverage has been extended to 340 locations in hurricane-exposed coastal zones, earthquake-prone regions, and wildfire-interface areas that were previously uninsurable.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 5.7:1 cost-benefit ratio for P&C market expansion, reflecting premium growth for carriers, coverage availability for infrastructure developers, and reduced economic disruption from uninsured catastrophe losses. The expansion analysis and underwriting guidelines are published under open-access licensing.
