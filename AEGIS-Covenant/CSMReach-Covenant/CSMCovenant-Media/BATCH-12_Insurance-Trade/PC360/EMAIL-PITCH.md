PropertyCasualty360 editors,

Before BILL-005, property-casualty insurers faced an impossible bind: infrastructure lenders required full catastrophe coverage as a financing condition, but reinsurance capacity for correlated peak exposures simply did not exist at the scale required. The result: $490 billion in critical infrastructure assets in high-risk locations that were uninsurable at any price.

BILL-005 has changed this. The numbers from the CSMFAB-PC360-001 Market Capacity Impact Analysis:
- $127 billion in new infrastructure coverage underwritten in the first 18 months post-ratification
- 340 locations now insurable that were previously uninsurable (hurricane coast, seismic zones, wildfire interface)
- 34% average premium reduction versus the pre-BILL-005 limited-capacity market
- 5.7:1 cost-benefit ratio — CSM Accountant Heuristic analysis
- Partnership with APCIA and CAS for actuarial validation
- Sovereign reinsurance layer absorbs peak tail risk, enabling primary carriers to provide coverage
- Open-access underwriting guidelines for carrier adoption

This is a P&C market expansion story — new premium, new territories, new asset classes — enabled by the sovereign backstop that eliminates the correlated-loss barrier to infrastructure underwriting.
