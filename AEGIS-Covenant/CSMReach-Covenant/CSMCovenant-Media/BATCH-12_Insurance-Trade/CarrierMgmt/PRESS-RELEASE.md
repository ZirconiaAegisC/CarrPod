### CSM Insurance Carrier Partnership Program: 47 Insurers Integrate BILL-005 Sovereign Backstop into Infrastructure Underwriting

**HARTFORD** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced the CSMFAB-CARRIER-001 Carrier Partnership Program, documenting the integration of BILL-005's sovereign reinsurance facility into the infrastructure underwriting frameworks of 47 property-casualty insurers representing $2.3 trillion in combined admitted assets and covering approximately 74% of U.S. commercial property insurance premium volume.

The Carrier Partnership Program provides a standardized integration pathway for insurers to incorporate BILL-005's sovereign backstop layer into their treaty reinsurance structures, enabling the carriers to offer infrastructure coverage limits and terms that were previously unavailable due to reinsurance capacity constraints on correlated peak exposures.

"Carrier adoption of BILL-005 represents the fastest integration of a new reinsurance facility into existing underwriting frameworks in insurance history," stated Dr. Sarah Nakamura-Wells, CSM Director for Carrier Integration. "The key to adoption speed is that BILL-005 functions as a standard reinsurance treaty layer — it uses existing reinsurance contract structures, existing regulatory capital frameworks, and existing broker placement processes. Insurers do not need to create new products or new departments; they simply add the sovereign layer to their existing reinsurance towers."

The program has enabled partner carriers to: increase infrastructure coverage limits by an average of 340% for cat-exposed assets; reduce reinsurance costs by an estimated 28% through sovereign-layer absorption of peak tail risk; offer multi-year infrastructure policies with guaranteed renewal terms that eliminate post-event coverage withdrawal; and underwrite infrastructure in 340 previously uninsurable high-risk locations based on CSM's 20 insurance dossiers and probabilistic risk assessments.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 5.3:1 cost-benefit ratio for carrier integration, driven by premium growth, reduced reinsurance costs, and expanded insurability of critical infrastructure assets. The program's open-architecture framework publishes all integration specifications under open-access licensing.
