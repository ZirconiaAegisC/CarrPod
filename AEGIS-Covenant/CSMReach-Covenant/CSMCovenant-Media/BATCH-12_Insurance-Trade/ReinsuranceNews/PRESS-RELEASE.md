### CSM Treaty Restructures Global Reinsurance Architecture: Sovereign Backstop Layer Absorbs Peak Infrastructure Cat Risks

**LONDON** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the Reinsurance Market Impact Assessment (CSMFAB-RMA-001), analyzing how BILL-005's $500 billion sovereign reinsurance facility restructures the global reinsurance market for infrastructure catastrophe risk and creates capacity for coverage that the traditional reinsurance market has been unable to provide at sustainable pricing.

The assessment, reviewed by Lloyd's, the International Underwriting Association (IUA), and Guy Carpenter, quantifies the protection gap that BILL-005 fills: an estimated $1.7 trillion in uninsured or underinsured critical infrastructure catastrophe exposure globally, concentrated in regions where traditional reinsurance capacity is either unavailable or priced at levels that render infrastructure investment uneconomic.

"The reinsurance industry has been telling governments for years that peak infrastructure catastrophe risks exceed private market capacity — Hurricane Sandy was a $70 billion event, and the industry handled it. But a $340 billion Houston Ship Channel event, or a $220 billion Los Angeles-San Andreas scenario, would overwhelm the global reinsurance market and trigger cascading solvency events," explained James Harrington-West, CSM Director for Reinsurance Architecture and former Lloyd's syndicate underwriter. "BILL-005 creates a sovereign layer that sits above traditional reinsurance — absorbing the peak risks that the private market cannot prudently retain — while leaving the existing reinsurance market structure intact for all risks below the sovereign attachment point."

The assessment quantifies: $870 billion in existing infrastructure exposure that the traditional reinsurance market can efficiently absorb; $340 billion in peak exposure that exceeds private market capacity and transfers to the BILL-005 sovereign facility; and $490 billion in currently uninsured exposure that becomes insurable because the sovereign backstop eliminates the correlated-loss scenario that made coverage unavailable at any price.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed an 8.6:1 cost-benefit ratio for the reinsurance restructuring, reflecting insurance market stability, maintained coverage availability, and reduced post-event economic disruption.
