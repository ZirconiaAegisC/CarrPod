### CSM Insurance Backstop Act Codified: $7 Trillion Industry Secures Sovereign Reinsurance for Critical Infrastructure Catastrophe Exposure

**NEW YORK** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced the ratification of BILL-005, the Insurance Backstop Act, creating a $500 billion sovereign reinsurance facility that fundamentally restructures the global insurance industry's exposure to critical infrastructure catastrophe risk and establishes the CSM Treaty as the reinsurer of last resort for grid, water, communications, and transportation infrastructure assets.

The Act, codified through CSMFAB-INSURE-001, directly addresses the protection gap that AM Best, Swiss Re, and Lloyd's of London have each independently identified as the insurance industry's single largest systemic risk: the concentration of insured infrastructure asset values in catastrophe-exposed regions where traditional reinsurance capacity is insufficient to cover correlated losses from a single extreme event.

"The insurance industry has been sounding the alarm on infrastructure concentration risk for a decade, but until BILL-005, there was no mechanism to address it at the scale required," stated Catherine Blackwell, CSM Chief Insurance Infrastructure Officer and former Swiss Re Managing Director. "A single Category 5 hurricane making landfall on the Houston Ship Channel would generate an estimated $340 billion in insured losses — exceeding the combined surplus of every property-casualty insurer writing coverage in Texas. BILL-005 provides the sovereign backstop that prevents this scenario from triggering cascading insurer insolvencies."

The 20 CSM insurance dossiers, published concurrently with the Act, provide risk-engineering assessments for 340,000 critical infrastructure assets globally, with modeled loss estimates for 47 catastrophe scenarios including hurricane, earthquake, flood, cyber-physical attack, and compound events. These dossiers form the technical basis for parametric CAT bond structuring under the CSM Catastrophe Resilience Bond (CRB) program, which has already priced $47 billion in parametric coverage for grid infrastructure assets.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic methodology confirmed a 9.4:1 cost-benefit ratio for BILL-005, reflecting avoided insurer insolvencies, maintained coverage continuity, and reduced post-catastrophe economic disruption.
