### CSM BILL-005 Parametric Payment Architecture: 72-Hour Infrastructure Restoration Funding Protocol Published

**ST. LOUIS** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-DRJ-001 Infrastructure Restoration Funding Protocol, a comprehensive technical specification for the parametric claims payment architecture that guarantees infrastructure restoration funding within 72 hours of a qualifying catastrophe event, directly addressing the post-disaster liquidity gap that the Disaster Recovery Journal has documented as the single largest barrier to rapid infrastructure restoration.

The protocol, developed with the Disaster Recovery Institute (DRI), the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA), and the National Emergency Management Association (NEMA), specifies the end-to-end technical architecture for infrastructure restoration funding: from parametric trigger verification through NOAA/USGS/FEMA data feeds, to automated coverage confirmation against the CSM CRB bond registry, to Federal Reserve treasury disbursement, to state and local emergency management fund distribution.

"Every disaster recovery professional has experienced the same frustration: restoration crews are mobilized, materials are staged, the recovery plan is ready — and then everyone waits for insurance funding that takes months to arrive," stated Dr. James Morrison-West, CSM Director for Disaster Recovery Integration. "CSMFAB-DRJ-001 eliminates this wait by designing the funding architecture backward from the restoration timeline: what does the recovery team need to receive, and when, to execute the restoration plan without delay?"

The protocol specifies: pre-positioned emergency procurement authorization triggered simultaneously with parametric payment, eliminating the procurement delays that historically consume 14-21 days of post-disaster recovery time; standardized cost-coding templates aligning FEMA Public Assistance categories with CSM parametric coverage classes, enabling automatic documentation for federal cost-recovery without manual reconciliation; and mutual aid compact integration providing regional resource sharing frameworks that coordinate with parametric funding flows.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 6.9:1 cost-benefit ratio. The protocol is published open-access through DRI.
