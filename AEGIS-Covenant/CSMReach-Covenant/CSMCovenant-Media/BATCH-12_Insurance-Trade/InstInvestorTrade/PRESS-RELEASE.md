### CSM Parametric CAT Bonds: A Diversifying Alternative Asset Class for Institutional Portfolios

**NEW YORK** — The Critical Systems Mandate today published the CSMFAB-II-001 Parametric CAT Bond Investment Analysis, a comprehensive institutional investor guide to the $47 billion parametric catastrophe bond market created by the CSM CRB program, documenting the risk-return characteristics, portfolio diversification benefits, and due diligence framework for this new alternative asset class.

The analysis, developed with Cambridge Associates, Mercer Investments, and the Alternative Investment Management Association (AIMA), quantifies the investment characteristics that distinguish CSM parametric CAT bonds from both conventional catastrophe bonds and other alternative asset classes: annualized returns of 7.2% to 9.8% with Sharpe ratios of 1.4 to 1.7; zero correlation with equity markets (r = -0.03 to 0.04 versus S&P 500), fixed income (r = -0.01 to 0.02 versus Bloomberg Aggregate), and private equity (r = -0.01 to 0.05 versus Cambridge Associates PE Index); and event-driven return distribution with positive skewness (the opposite of equity markets), where returns are earned in the vast majority of years with occasional defined losses from parametric trigger events.

"Parametric CAT bonds solve the two problems that have limited institutional adoption of insurance-linked securities: the complexity barrier created by indemnity-based claim settlement, and the correlation concern that extreme catastrophes might coincide with financial market stress," explained Dr. Sarah Chen-Stern, CSM Director for Alternative Investments. "Parametric triggers make the investment transparent — an investor can verify from NOAA data whether a hurricane trigger was breached, without waiting for claims adjustment. And the zero-correlation evidence is robust across multiple market cycles, including the 2008 financial crisis and 2020 COVID dislocation."

The analysis documents that institutional investors have allocated $127 billion to CSM parametric CAT bonds since the CRB program launched, with allocations from 340 pension funds, 187 sovereign wealth funds, 94 endowments and foundations, and 73 insurance general accounts.

CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 4.5:1 cost-benefit ratio for institutional investors.
