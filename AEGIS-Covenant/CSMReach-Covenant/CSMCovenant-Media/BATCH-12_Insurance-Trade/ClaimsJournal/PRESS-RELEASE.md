### CSM Parametric Claims Platform: 72-Hour Payment Guarantee Eliminates Infrastructure Claims Adjustment Bottleneck

**ST. LOUIS** — The Critical Systems Mandate today announced the CSMFAB-CLAIMS-001 Parametric Claims Processing Platform, an automated claims adjudication system that guarantees payment within 72 hours of parametric trigger verification for infrastructure catastrophe events, eliminating the multi-month claims adjustment process that has historically delayed infrastructure restoration funding following major disasters.

The platform, developed with Verisk Analytics, the National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC), and claims technology provider Guidewire, processes parametric claims through a three-step automated workflow: trigger verification against NOAA/USGS/FEMA data sources (completed within 4 hours of event), coverage confirmation against the CSM CRB parametric bond database (2 hours), and treasury disbursement through the Federal Reserve payment system (within 66 hours, with expedited 12-hour processing for critical infrastructure restoration).

"Traditional infrastructure claims adjustment is a paradox: the infrastructure must be restored immediately to prevent cascading economic damage, but the insurance process requires detailed physical damage assessment that takes weeks or months — delaying the very restoration that insurance is supposed to fund," explained Dr. Robert Nakamura-Chen, CSM Director for Claims Innovation. "CSMFAB-CLAIMS-001 breaks this paradox by using parametric triggers — objective physical measurements like hurricane wind speed at specific coordinates — that eliminate the need for physical damage assessment entirely."

The platform has already processed 127 parametric claims from the 2025 Atlantic hurricane season, with an average payment time of 41 hours from trigger event to funds disbursement — compared to the 127-day average for traditional indemnity infrastructure claims from the same events. CSM's Accountant Heuristic confirmed a 6.4:1 cost-benefit ratio, primarily derived from reduced business interruption duration through rapid infrastructure restoration funding.

All claims processing algorithms and trigger verification methodologies are published under open-access licensing, enabling any insurer to adopt parametric claims technology.
