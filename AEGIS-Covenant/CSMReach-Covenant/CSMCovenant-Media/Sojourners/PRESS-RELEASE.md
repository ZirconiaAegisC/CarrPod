**SOJOURNERS: THE JUSTICE OF ELECTRONS — WHY GRID PROTECTION IS A SOCIAL JUSTICE IMPERATIVE**

WASHINGTON, D.C. — The AEGIS Covenant has partnered with Sojourners to release a reflection on electromagnetic resilience as a matter of racial and economic justice, arguing that the communities that would suffer most in a Carrington-class event are the same communities that have always been last in line for infrastructure investment.

"When the transformers fail, they don't fail equally," said Rev. Dr. William Chen, AEGIS Faith Community Liaison and AME pastor. "The substations serving predominantly Black and brown neighborhoods are, on average, older, less maintained, and further from replacement inventory than those serving affluent suburbs. A CME doesn't discriminate, but our infrastructure does — and a grid-down event would be the largest racial-justice disaster in American history."

The Sojourners reflection maps the overlap between electromagnetic vulnerability and existing environmental justice indicators: the 287 EHV transformers on the Eastern Interconnection that are most vulnerable to geomagnetically induced currents are disproportionately located near or serving low-income communities and communities of color. The Covenant's Substation Hardening Protocol specifically prioritizes these transformers for first-wave MXene retrofitting.

"A just transition to a resilient grid means the most vulnerable get protected first — not last, not when the budget allows, not as a pilot program," said Rev. Chen. "This is what Isaiah meant by 'repairer of the breach.' The breach in our grid is also a breach in our society. We repair both, or we repair neither."

Sojourners has committed to incorporating geomagnetic justice into its advocacy platform and to mobilizing its network of 300,000 supporters for BILL-006 advocacy.
