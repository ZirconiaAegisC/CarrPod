## 1. Material System Overview

All structural composite components use: **Basalt Fiber + Elium® 175 thermoplastic resin**

This combination was selected over glass/epoxy, carbon/epoxy, or glass/vinyl ester for:
- **GIC immunity**: ρ > 10¹² Ω·m (vs carbon fiber ρ ~ 10³ S/m → conducting)
- **Recyclability**: Elium® thermal solvolysis at 350°C → 95% fiber + 100% monomer
- **Cost**: Basalt rock → fiber at ~$1.80–2.50/kg in-house vs $4–14/kg commercial
- **Temperature**: Service Tg = 110°C (sufficient for all non-AAJ/CMBC applications)
- **Strength**: Composite tensile at 60% FVF = 1,100 MPa (exceeds steel at same mass)

## 3. VARTM Process (standard for all panels)

**Vacuum-Assisted Resin Transfer Molding — key parameters:**
1. Dry basalt preform placed in single-sided mold
2. Vacuum bag sealed: target vacuum −97 kPa ± 2 kPa
3. Elium® 175 (viscosity 100–300 cP at 23°C): 1.2 L/m² for 3 mm panel
4. Flow-front velocity: 50–80 mm/min (adjust inlet distance for uniform fill)
5. Gel time at 23°C: 45–90 min with standard TBPEH initiator at 0.5 wt%
6. Cure: Ambient 45 min + post-cure 60°C/2 h
7. Demold: minimum 2 h after post-cure, panel cooled to <50°C

**NDT acceptance criteria (F2-047):**
- Ultrasonic C-scan: no delamination >6 mm diameter
- Fiber volume fraction (LOI per ASTM D2584): 55–65%
- Coupon tensile test (ASTM D3039): ≥1,050 MPa
