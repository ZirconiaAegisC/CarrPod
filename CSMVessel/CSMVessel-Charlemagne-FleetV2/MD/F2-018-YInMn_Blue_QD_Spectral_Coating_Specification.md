## 1. Chromatic Priority: NIR Rejection

During a Carrington event, wildfire, or directed energy threat, the primary thermal load
on the vehicle exterior is **Near-Infrared (700–2,500 nm)**, comprising ~52% of total
solar energy flux and the dominant wavelength band of fires and DEW systems.

YInMn Blue (YIn₁₋ₓMnₓO₃) achieves:
- NIR reflectance (700–2,500 nm): **85–92%**
- Solar Reflectance Index (SRI per ASTM E1980): **115 base** → **130 with QD overlay**
- Surface temperature vs. dark surface in direct sun: **up to 70°C cooler**
- Annual re-coat interval: **8–10 years** (vs 18–24 months conventional) due to ceramic stability

The crystal-field absorption mechanism (Mn³⁺ in trigonal bipyramidal coordination) absorbs
visible orange (~590 nm) while transmitting NIR — creating the vivid blue color while
simultaneously rejecting thermal radiation.

## 2. QD Enhancement Layer (CsPbBr₃ Addition)

CsPbBr₃ perovskite quantum dots co-deposited with YInMn (2025 innovation):
- UV (280–400 nm) reflectance: **+31%** above plain YInMn
- SRI upgrade: 115 → **130** (13% improvement)
- QD stability in ceramic matrix at 1,200°C co-fire: validated (Oregon State 2025 study)
- Indium content in YInMn: 0.61 kg In per kg YInMn pigment — Phoenix Protocol critical

## 4. Application Protocol

1. Clean: solvent wipe + 80°C bake (outgas)
2. Prime: Zinc-free epoxy polyamide, 75 µm DFT
3. Mid-coat: YInMn Blue polyurethane, 50 µm DFT
4. QD topcoat: CsPbBr₃ QD UV-activated layer, 15 µm DFT
5. Total system DFT: 140 µm
6. Cure: 80°C / 45 min or 23°C / 72 h
7. QC: CIE L*a*b* spectrophotometer per batch; SRI measurement per ASTM E1980
