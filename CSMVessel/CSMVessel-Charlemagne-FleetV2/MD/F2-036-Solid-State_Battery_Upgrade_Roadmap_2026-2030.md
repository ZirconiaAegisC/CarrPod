## 1. SSB Technology Status (June 2026)

Current LiFePO₄ energy density: 125–160 Wh/kg
Toyota 2025 announced solid-state: 1,200 Wh/kg (target production 2026–2027)
Samsung SDI 2025 pilot: 800 Wh/kg, 1,000+ cycles confirmed

**When SSB at 1,200 Wh/kg becomes available, CMBC vault mass drops dramatically:**

| Vessel | Current CMBC mass | SSB CMBC mass | Savings | CG Drop |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TOURMALINE | 18 kg | 1.6 kg | 91% | 25 mm |
| BERYL | 218 kg | 23 kg | 89% | 190 mm |
| RHODONITE | 1,380 kg | 154 kg | 89% | 220 mm |

The center-of-gravity drop in BERYL (190 mm) would **dramatically improve BERYL's
amphibious stability** — bringing the metacentric height from marginal to excellent.

## 2. SSB CMBC Retrofit Design Rule

**All current CMBC vault designs must preserve 30% void space for SSB retrofit.**

This means the vault is currently designed at 70% cell packing density, leaving 30%
volumetrically empty (except for aerogel thermal padding).

When SSBs become available:
- Remove current LiFePO₄ modules from vault (Phoenix Protocol recovery)
- Install SSB modules (smaller volume, much lighter)
- The 30% void space provides flexibilty for next-gen module geometry
- No structural vault modification required
