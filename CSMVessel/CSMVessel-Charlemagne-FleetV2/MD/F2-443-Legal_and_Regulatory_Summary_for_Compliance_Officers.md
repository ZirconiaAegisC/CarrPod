## Novel Vehicle Regulatory Challenge

All five Charlemagne vessels are 'novel' in the regulatory sense. File NHTSA petition at program
Month 1. Attach test data from prototype validation as supporting evidence.

Key legal risks:

1. **ALON Windshield (FMVSS 205):** Glazing standard doesn't contemplate ceramic.
   Strategy: File equivalency data showing ALON meets or exceeds FMVSS 205 performance.
   The 3× harder than steel data from F2-007 is primary evidence.

2. **Hexapedal Drivetrain (FMVSS):** No FMVSS addresses 6-wheel walk-capable vehicles.
   Strategy: Request Special Purpose Vehicle classification (precedent: all-terrain vehicles, LSVs).

3. **TOURMALINE Product Liability:** Child vehicle with 48V battery and 25 km/h speed is
   above typical toy thresholds. ASTM F963 + UL 2272 dual compliance is required.
   Insurance: minimum $10M per occurrence product liability. Partner: specialty toy manufacturer insurer.

4. **GIC Immunity Marketing Claims:** Use "designed to minimize electromagnetic coupling"
   NOT "immune to all geomagnetic events." The distinction matters legally — the former
   is an engineering specification, the latter is an unverifiable absolute claim.

5. **Amphibious Operations:** When RHODONITE operates in any waterway, USCG 33 CFR 183 applies.
   For international waterway operations: SOLAS 2025 Chapter II-1. ABS or DNV GL
   marine classification strongly recommended for institutional buyers.
