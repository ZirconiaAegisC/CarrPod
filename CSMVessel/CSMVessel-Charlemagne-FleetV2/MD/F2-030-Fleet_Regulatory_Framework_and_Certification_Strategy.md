## 1. Novel Vehicle Category Challenge

The Charlemagne Class presents regulatory challenges because no existing category covers:
- 6-wheel walk-capable vehicles
- Transparent ceramic (ALON) "windshield" (not covered by FMVSS 205)
- Amphibious land + water dual-domain operation
- Child-scale to transit-scale vehicles in one platform family

**Strategy: Proactive Special Purpose Vehicle petition to NHTSA, filed at Month 1.**

## 2. Applicable Standards by Vessel Type

### TOURMALINE (child toy vehicle)
- ASTM F963-17: Standard Consumer Safety (toy safety, Section 4.22 ride-on)
- CPSC 16 CFR 1501: Hazardous toy parts
- UL 2272: Personal electric mobility device safety (motorized toy adaptation)
- IEEE P2945 (self-certification): GIC hardening of electronic systems
- FCC Part 15: LoRa radio module authorization (PN-CC-001 already FCC-certified)

### BERYL and AMETHYST (passenger vehicles)
- FMVSS 208: Occupant crash protection (adapted for novel architecture)
- FMVSS 301: Fuel/energy system integrity (battery vault equivalent test)
- ISO 26262: Road vehicles functional safety, ASIL-B target
- UNECE R100: Electric vehicle battery safety
- IEEE P2945: GIC immunity (self-certification with test data)
- SAE J2954: Wireless charging interoperability (AMETHYST ONLY)

### RHODONITE (transit vehicle)
- FMVSS (full suite): All applicable FMVSS for transit vehicles
- ADA: Accessibility requirements (American Disabilities Act)
- SOLAS 2025: If operating within inland waterway jurisdiction
- ABS/DNV GL marine classification: Recommended for full amphibious deployment
- FTA: Federal Transit Administration safety requirements
