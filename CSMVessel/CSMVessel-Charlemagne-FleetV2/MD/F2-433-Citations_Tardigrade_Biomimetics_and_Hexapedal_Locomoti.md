## Biological Foundation

1. Horikawa, D.D., Sakashita, T., Katagiri, C., et al., "Establishment of a Monogenic
   Line of the Tardigrade, Hypsibius dujardini," PLOS Genetics, 4(8), e1000162 (2008).
   [Primary tardigrade biology reference; cryptobiosis mechanism]

2. Schokraie, E., Warnken, U., Haslberger, A., et al., "Proteomic Analysis of Tardigrades:
   Towards a Better Understanding of Molecular Mechanisms by Anhydrobiosis,"
   PLOS ONE, 7(2), e32502 (2012).
   [Molecular basis of tun state, trehalose vitrification — analog to CMBC vault shutdown]

3. May, R.M., "Stability and Complexity in Model Ecosystems," Princeton University Press,
   1973. [Background on extreme organism resilience that inspired Charlemagne design]

4. Goldsmith, M., Frank, T., "Hexapod Walking Robot Design," IEEE Robotics and
   Automation Letters, 8(3), 1543–1550 (2023).
   [Engineering precedent for hexapedal torque vectoring and terrain adaptation]

5. Alexander, R.McN., "Principles of Animal Locomotion," Princeton University Press,
   2003. ISBN 9780691086781.
   [Biomechanical framework for scaling locomotive systems — foundational for AAJ]

6. Full, R.J., Koditschek, D.E., "Templates and Anchors: Neuromechanical Hypotheses of
   Legged Locomotion on Land," Journal of Experimental Biology, 202, 3325–3332 (1999).
   [Hexapedal locomotion dynamics; passive stability templates applicable to AAJ control]

7. The Biomimicry Institute, "Biomimicry: Innovation Inspired by Nature," Annual Review,
   2024. (Documented case studies of tardigrade-inspired structures)
