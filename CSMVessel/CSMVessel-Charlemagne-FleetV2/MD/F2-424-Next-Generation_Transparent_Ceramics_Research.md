## 1. MgAl₂O₄ (Transparent Spinel) Assessment

**Crystal structure:** Cubic spinel, Fd3m space group
**Fabrication route:** Hot isostatic pressing (HIP) at 1,600°C / 200 MPa (lower than ALON 2,150°C)
**Energy saving vs. ALON:** 25% lower processing temperature → significant energy/cost reduction

**Property comparison:**

| Property | ALON | MgAl₂O₄ Spinel | Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Knoop hardness | 1,800 | 1,600 | ALON (+11%) |
| E modulus (GPa) | 334 | 280 | ALON (+16%) |
| Compressive strength (MPa) | 2,680 | 2,100 | ALON (+27%) |
| Optical range (nm) | 200–5,000 | 200–5,000 | Equal |
| Electrical resistivity | >10¹² Ω·m | >10¹² Ω·m | Equal (both GIC immune) |
| Projected cost/kg | $800–1,200 | $480–600 | Spinel (40–50% savings) |
| Suppliers | Surmet (only) | Kyocera, CeramTec (2027) | Spinel (multi-source) |

**Assessment:** Spinel is mechanically inferior to ALON but its multi-source availability 
and lower cost make it highly strategically attractive. For TOURMALINE and OBSIDIAN
domes (smaller, lower structural demand), spinel may be appropriate in Year 3+.
For AMETHYST and RHODONITE (structural requirement higher), ALON remains preferred.

**Required testing before adoption:**
NIJ Standard 0108.01 ballistic test at dome thickness used by vessel
(TOURMALINE: 8 mm, BERYL: 8 mm, AMETHYST: 12 mm)
