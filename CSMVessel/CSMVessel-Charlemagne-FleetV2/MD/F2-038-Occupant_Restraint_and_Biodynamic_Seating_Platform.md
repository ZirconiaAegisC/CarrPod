## 1. Restraint Philosophy

The Charlemagne fleet restraint system is designed to work WITH the Lattice-Lock floor
and AAJ suspension — not against them.

A conventional restraint system (rigid ISOFIX, solid bolts to steel floor) would short-circuit
the Lattice-Lock isolation by creating a rigid mechanical path from chassis to occupant.
Instead, the CMBC mounting system uses:
- BFRP/Elium® seat frame (isolates from chassis vibration)
- MRF-140CG magnetorheological fluid adaptive bladder (variable stiffness 0.3–12 MPa)
- KNbO₃–BaTiO₃ Schumann PEMF integrated at lumbar position
- Crash-mode: MRF solidifies to maximum stiffness in <0.5 ms on accelerometer trigger

## 2. Harness Material Specifications

| Component | Material | Strength | Conductivity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Main webbing | UHMWPE Dyneema SK99, 50 mm wide | 4,250 MPa fiber | ρ > 10¹⁴ Ω·m |
| Buckle body | PEEK CF40 injection molded | 220 MPa tensile | ρ > 10¹⁰ Ω·m |
| D-ring | ZrO₂ 3Y-TZP ceramic | 1,300 MPa | ρ > 10¹² Ω·m |
| Pretensioner | KNbO₃–BaTiO₃ mortar (no metal ignition) | N/A | Zero metallic conductor |

**Critical:** Standard ISOFIX uses steel anchor rods (conductors). The Charlemagne
uses ZrO₂ ceramic anchor inserts and PEEK CF40 latch mechanisms.
ECE R44 crash compliance (child seats): both PEEK CF40 buckle and ZrO₂ D-ring exceed
minimum 13.5 kN pullout force required.

## 3. Adaptive Comfort: MRF Bladder

The seat base contains a LORD MRF-140CG magnetorheological fluid bladder:
- Off-state stiffness (0 field): 0.3 MPa — extremely soft, conforms to occupant
- On-state stiffness (250 kA/m): 12 MPa — firm support for highway driving
- Response time: <1 ms (electromagnetic → fluid stiffening)
- Applied: SmCo permanent magnet in PEEK housing adjustable by secondary coil

During normal driving: stiffness adjusted by seat pressure pattern to optimize ergonomics
During crash: MRF instantly stiffens to maximum, multiplying energy absorption capacity
