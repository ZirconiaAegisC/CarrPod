## 1. Purpose

The Phoenix Protocol serves two simultaneous functions:
1. **End-of-life material recovery** — all Charlemagne fleet vehicles are fully recyclable
2. **In-house material synthesis** — recovering critical minerals and reducing supply-chain dependency

## 2. ZrB₂ In-House SHS Synthesis

**Self-propagating High-temperature Synthesis (SHS):**

Chemical reaction: Zr(powder) + 2B(powder) → ZrB₂ (exothermic, ΔH = −323 kJ/mol)

Equipment:
- Induction furnace ($85,000) or resistance-heated argon vessel ($15,000)
- Ball mill for powder blending ($22,000)
- Safety: enclosed argon atmosphere (exothermal ignition)

Process:
1. Mix Zr metal powder (99.5%, D50 <45 µm) + crystalline boron (95%, D50 <100 µm) in 1:2.05 molar ratio (5% B excess)
2. Cold-press pellets at 100 MPa
3. Place in argon vessel; ignite at one end with W wire (resistance heating)
4. Self-propagating reaction completes in seconds; T reaches 2,200°C locally
5. Allow to cool under argon; product = ZrB₂ with residual B
6. Ball-mill to <3 µm; XRD confirm ZrB₂ phase

**Cost comparison:**
- Commercial ZrB₂ powder: $280–420/kg
- In-house SHS from Zr + B: $45 (Zr metal) + $30 (B powder) + $20 (energy/labor) = **~$95–110/kg**
- Savings vs. commercial: **65–75%**

## 3. Indium Recovery from YInMn Blue (End-of-Life)

**From retired Charlemagne vehicles, YInMn Blue coating recovery:**

1. Acid leach: H₂SO₄ (1M) + H₂O₂ at 80°C → In³⁺, Y³⁺, Mn²⁺ in solution
2. Selective extraction: [P₈₈₈₈][Cl] ionic liquid → In³⁺ extracted from Mn²⁺, Y³⁺
   Recovery: 99.4% In extraction efficiency (Green Chemistry 27/4, 2025)
3. Strip from ionic liquid: 0.1M HCl → In³⁺ back to aqueous
4. Electrodeposition: DC plating at −0.85 V vs. SHE → metallic In (99.7% purity)
5. Re-use: In metal for new YInMn Blue pigment synthesis

**Value recovered per vehicle BERYL:**
- YInMn Blue coating: ~4 kg per vehicle
- In content (per kg YInMn): 0.61 kg In per kg YInMn
- In recovered: 4 × 0.61 × 0.994 = **2.42 kg In per vehicle**
- At $320/kg In market price (2026): **$775 In recovery value per BERYL**

## 4. LiFePO₄ End-of-Life Processing

At 80% capacity end-of-life (after ~5,000 cycles), CMBC modules:
1. Discharge to 0% SOC (safety)
2. Disassemble modules from vault (BFRP frame recyclable via Elium® solvolysis)
3. Route to licensed battery recycler (Redwood Materials, Li-Cycle)
4. Recovery: >95% Li, >98% Fe, >95% P
5. Recovered material → new LiFePO₄ precursor synthesis

The fact that LiFePO₄ contains no cobalt, nickel, or manganese makes recovery economics
significantly better than NMC batteries.
