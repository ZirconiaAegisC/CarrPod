# F2-428 — In-House Manufacturing Scale-Up and Synthesis Roadmap
**Series:** CSMVessel-Charlemagne-FleetV2 · **Author:** Carrington Storm Motors · **Rev:** 1.0 · **Date:** June 2026
---


## 1. Year-by-Year Build-Out Plan

### Year 1 (Current): Outsource Critical Materials
- ALON: Surmet Corporation (14–20 week lead)
- ZrB₂ powder: Treibacher/American Elements (8–12 week lead)
- LiFePO₄ cells: CATL + Gotion dual-source
- KNbO₃–BaTiO₃: TDK standard catalog
- Basalt fiber: Mafic USA

**Capital deployed Year 1:** $1.8M (VARTM molds, motor prep station, assembly jigs, cleanroom)

### Year 2: First In-House Synthesis
**ZrB₂ SHS program:**
Equipment: $85,000 induction furnace + $22,000 ball mill
Savings: $280–420/kg commercial → $95–110/kg in-house = ~65% reduction
Annual ZrB₂ demand at 1,000 BERYL/yr: ~18,000 kg
Annual savings: 18,000 × ($280 − $102) = **$3,204,000/year savings**
**Investment payback: SHS program pays for itself in 12 days of full production.**

**KNbO₃–BaTiO₃ synthesis:**
Equipment: tube furnace ($12,000), tape caster ($25,000), poling bath ($18,000)
Savings: $180–250/kg commercial → $28–40/kg in-house = ~83% reduction

### Year 3: ALON Hot-Press
Investment: $1.2M–2.5M
Expected savings: $800–1,200/kg Surmet → $180–280/kg in-house = ~78% reduction
Required precondition: >500 BERYL/yr production (volume justifies equipment)
Risk: ALON hot-press operation requires 2,150°C, N₂ atmosphere, 24–48h cycles
Highly specialized — may maintain Surmet partnership for complex geometries

### Year 4: Basalt Fiber Furnace
Investment: $350,000 (Pt-Rh bushing furnace + winding machinery)
Savings: $5–14/kg commercial → $1.80–2.50/kg in-house = ~80% reduction
Feedstock: Columbia River Basalt (Oregon/Washington) — essentially free domestic rock
Annual fiber demand at 3,000 vessels/yr: ~135,000 kg
Annual savings: 135,000 × ($8 − $2.20) = **$783,000/year savings**

## 2. In-House MXene Synthesis (Year 2, Priority)

Ti₃AlC₂ MAX Phase → LiF/HCl selective etching → delamination → Ti₃C₂Tₓ colloidal
Equipment: $75,000 total (etching reactor + sonication + filtration)
Savings: $1,500–2,500/kg commercial → $55–75/kg in-house = 96–97% reduction
Annual MXene demand at 1,000 vessels: ~120 kg
Annual savings: 120 × ($1,800 − $65) = **$208,200/year savings**
Payback: 4.4 months

## 3. Priority Order

1. ZrB₂ SHS — highest annual savings, simplest equipment, fastest payback
2. MXene synthesis — highest % savings, moderate complexity
3. KNbO₃–BaTiO₃ synthesis — significant savings, requires more specialized equipment
4. ALON hot-press — major savings but large capital, complex operation
5. Basalt fiber furnace — long-term strategic independence
