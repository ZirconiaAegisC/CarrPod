**CSMVessel0000000014 RHODONITE** — 3/4-Bus Transit Charlemagne Class

**For transit authorities, campus operators, and disaster response agencies:**

RHODONITE is the first transit vehicle that continues operating when the regional power grid fails during a major solar event. Traditional battery electric buses have GIC-vulnerable aluminum chassis, copper motor windings, and electronic control units that fail within minutes of a G5 geomagnetic storm. RHODONITE's dielectric ceramic architecture eliminates this vulnerability entirely.

**Transit specifications:**
- 24-30 seated passengers (ADA accessible)
- 185 kWh, 200 km range loaded
- 10× 35 kW hub motors
- Maximum speed: 100 km/h
- 9.6m length (legal for standard bus lanes, 3/4 of standard 12m bus)
- Full amphibious capability (critical for bus rapid transit across flood-prone corridors)

**Fleet economics:**
- Purchase: $285,000-$380,000
- vs. Proterra transit bus: $600,000-$850,000 → **RHODONITE is 50-55% of Proterra cost**
- LiFePO₄ lifecycle: 6,000 cycles → 200 km/cycle = 1,200,000 km before replacement
- Maintenance advantage: No steel corrosion, ceramic bearings never rust

**Named after Rhodonite** — manganese silicate (MnSiO₃), the mineral containing manganese — the same element in YInMn Blue pigment that coats every RHODONITE exterior surface
