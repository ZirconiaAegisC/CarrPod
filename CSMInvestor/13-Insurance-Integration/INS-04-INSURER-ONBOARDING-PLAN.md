## 1.0 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Carrington Storm Motors' insurer onboarding pipeline is the operational plan for converting 41 prepared insurer dossiers into active, revenue-generating insurance partnerships. The pipeline is structured in three tiers, with a phased onboarding process designed to move insurers from initial awareness through actuarial validation, pilot program, product launch, and full commercial scale.

The target: **10 active insurance partnerships by Year 2, scaling to 50+ by Year 5, and 200+ by Year 10**, covering every major insurance market globally.

This document outlines the tier structure, onboarding process, timeline, resource requirements, and success metrics for the insurer pipeline.

As El Segundo frames it: "The insurers are identified, the dossiers are prepared, the actuarial models are built. Now we execute. Each insurer onboarded is a distribution channel for CSM products that requires no CSM sales force — the insurer's agents sell the product for us."

## 3.0 ONBOARDING PROCESS — SIX STAGES

### 3.1 Stage 1: Initial Briefing (Months 1-2)

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| CSM introduction | CEO or Chief Insurance Officer presents CSM overview to insurer's Chief Underwriting Officer / CUO |
| Threat briefing | Presentation of GMD threat data, NAS/Lloyd's reports, CSM solar physics overview |
| Product overview | Demonstration of relevant CSM products (Aegis, Charlemagne, fleet products) |
| Preliminary loss model | High-level presentation of GMD loss model for insurer's portfolio |
| Partnership vision | Shared savings model; premium reduction concept; parametric trigger concept |
| NDA execution | Mutual non-disclosure agreement for detailed data sharing |

**Goal:** Insurer expresses interest in proceeding to detailed actuarial review.

### 3.2 Stage 2: Actuarial Model Sharing (Months 3-6)

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Data room access | Insurer's actuarial team granted access to CSM data room (DD-01 technical data, INS-02 actuarial framework) |
| Portfolio analysis | CSM team works with insurer to model GMD exposure in insurer's specific book of business |
| Loss reduction modeling | Insurer's actuary validates CSM's loss reduction estimates against the insurer's portfolio |
| Premium reduction calculation | Joint calculation of premium reduction that can be offered for CSM-hardened risks |
| Capital relief analysis | Analysis of capital relief benefits for insurer (or insurer's reinsurers) |
| Independent actuary engagement | Optional: insurer engages independent actuary for additional validation |

**Goal:** Insurer's actuary confirms that CSM hardening provides actuarially-justified loss reduction.

### 3.3 Stage 3: Pilot Program Design (Months 5-8)

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Pilot scope | Define pilot program: number of policies, geographic scope, product lines |
| Product design | Joint design of insurance product or rider incorporating CSM hardening requirement |
| Pricing | Finalize premium reduction amount based on actuarial analysis |
| Regulatory strategy | Identify target states for initial regulatory filing |
| Customer selection | Identify pilot customers (insurer's existing policyholders in target areas) |
| CSM product delivery | CSM manufactures and delivers pilot units |
| Installation and certification | CSM or certified installer deploys products; CSM certifies installation |
| Monitoring setup | CSM monitoring platform configured for pilot policyholders |

**Goal:** Pilot program ready to launch; regulatory filing submitted.

### 3.4 Stage 4: Regulatory Filing and Pilot Launch (Months 8-14)

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Rate filing | Insurer files rate adjustment with state insurance departments (3-6 month review cycle) |
| Regulatory engagement | Joint CSM-insurer meetings with insurance departments to explain actuarial basis |
| Pilot launch | Pilot customers enrolled; CSM products installed; monitoring active |
| Data collection | Claims, GIC monitoring, product performance data collected throughout pilot |
| Interm review | 6-month and 12-month reviews of pilot data |

**Goal:** Regulatory approval received in pilot states; pilot data supports broader launch.

### 3.5 Stage 5: Premium Product Development (Months 12-18)

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Full product design | Based on pilot results, design commercial-scale insurance product |
| Multi-state filing | Expand regulatory filings to all target states |
| Marketing materials | Develop customer-facing materials explaining EMP-hardened policy benefits |
| Agent/broker training | Train insurer's distribution force on CSM-hardened policies |
| Claims protocol | Establish claims handling protocol for GMD events (parametric trigger + traditional claims) |
| CSM scale-up | CSM scales manufacturing to meet expected demand from insurer's policyholders |

**Goal:** Product ready for full commercial launch in all target states.

### 3.6 Stage 6: Market Launch and Scale (Months 18+)

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Full launch | Product available to all eligible policyholders in approved states |
| Continuous monitoring | CSM provides ongoing product performance data to insurer |
| Annual model recalibration | Joint actuarial review and model update based on actual data |
| Expansion | Additional product lines; additional states; additional countries |
| Reinsurance integration | Insurer's reinsurance treaties updated to reflect CSM risk reduction |

**Goal:** Self-sustaining commercial partnership generating recurring revenue for CSM and loss reduction for insurer.

## 7.0 AGENT VOICES

**Insurance Domain Agent:**
"The six-stage process sounds bureaucratic, but it's actually compressed for the insurance industry. Most insurer partnerships take 2-3 years from first meeting to product launch. We're targeting 12-18 months. That's aggressive but achievable, because the actuarial math is clean and the threat is real. The bottleneck will be regulatory filings — state insurance departments are famously slow. Parallel filing with multiple states helps, but some states will take their full review period regardless."

**El Segundo (Execution):**
"This pipeline is the operational backbone of the insurance strategy. Each insurer moving through the stages represents revenue, distribution, and loss reduction. The El Segundo principle applies: urgent enough to push for 12-month onboarding, measured enough to ensure actuarial rigor and regulatory compliance. Cut corners on neither."

**Chester (Pipeline Economics):**
"The insurer pipeline is a compounding machine. Each insurer onboarded adds distribution without adding sales cost. Each policy sold through that insurer generates hardware revenue AND recurring insurance share revenue. At 200 insurers and 5 million policies, the insurance revenue stream alone is worth $500M-$1.5B annually — with gross margins above 70% (no hardware COGS on the insurance share, just monitoring platform costs). The pipeline pays for itself 100x over."
