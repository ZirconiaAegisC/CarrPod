## 2.0 PHASE 2: INSURANCE INDUSTRY INTEGRATION (YEAR 2–3)
### "The Customer That Funds the Retrofits"

**Revenue Target: $45M → $180M**

**Rationale:** The insurance industry is simultaneously the most vulnerable stakeholder to GMD risk and the most powerful vector for funding mitigation. When insurers:

1. Recognize GMD as a $2–$6T unmodeled exposure
2. Price that exposure into premiums (or exclude it)
3. Offer premium reductions for hardened properties
4. Fund retrofits through premium-financing mechanisms

...the result is a self-funding cycle: property owners pay for hardening through insurance savings, insurers reduce their catastrophic exposure, and CSM captures a share of the premium savings as recurring revenue.

### 2.1 Insurance Engagement Sequence

**Step 1: Actuarial Validation (Months 1–6)**
- Engage 2–3 leading reinsurance brokers (Aon, Guy Carpenter, Willis Towers Watson) to validate CSM's GMD loss models
- Run the CSM actuarial math through third-party catastrophe modelers (RMS, AIR Worldwide)
- Publish a joint white paper: "Pricing Geomagnetic Disturbance Risk in Property/Casualty Portfolios"

**Step 2: Pilot Underwriter Partnerships (Months 6–12)**
- Activate relationships from the 41 insurer-specific analyses
- Initial partners: Lloyd's syndicates, Swiss Re, Munich Re, Berkshire Hathaway (all pre-analyzed)
- Offer: CSM provides GMD exposure assessment for the insurer's portfolio; insurer agrees to offer 15–20% premium reduction on hardened properties
- Pilot: 500–1,000 properties in high-GIC-risk regions

**Step 3: Premium-Funded Retrofit Program (Months 12–24)**
- Structure: Property owner pays zero upfront; retrofit is funded by a 10-year premium surcharge that is less than the premium reduction received (net savings to property owner from day one)
- CSM receives: Retrofit contract revenue + 5–10% of ongoing premium savings as recurring annual fee
- Scale: 5,000 properties by end of Year 2

**Step 4: Regulatory Adoption (Months 18–36)**
- Work with NAIC Climate and Resiliency Task Force to include GMD in insurer solvency calculations
- Advocate for state-level mandates requiring GMD exposure disclosure in insurer rate filings
- Goal: By Year 3, GMD hardening is a standard underwriting factor, not an optional add-on

## 3.0 PHASE 3: CONSUMER / RESIDENTIAL AEGIS HOME (YEAR 3–4)
### "The Customer Who Lives in the House"

**Revenue Target: $180M → $500M**

**Rationale:** Consumer adoption follows insurance integration. Once homeowners see:
1. A 15–20% reduction in their insurance premium for Aegis Home certification
2. Government tax credits for EM-hardened homes (advocated through Phase 1 policy work)
3. Resale value premium for certified homes (appraisal industry engagement)
4. Growing public awareness of GMD risk (SiblingFrequency, media coverage)

...the consumer channel becomes self-sustaining.

### 3.1 Aegis Home Go-to-Market

**Channel 1: National Homebuilders**
- Partner with top 10 US homebuilders (Lennar, D.R. Horton, Pulte, etc.) to offer Aegis Home as a premium upgrade package
- Builder incentive: Higher margin on premium package + differentiation in competitive markets
- Target: 5% of new construction in high-GIC-risk states by Year 5

**Channel 2: Aegis Home Certified Builder Program**
- Training and certification for independent builders
- Annual certification fee: $5,000/builder
- Marketing support: CSM provides co-branded materials, website listing, and lead generation
- Target: 500 certified builders by Year 5

**Channel 3: Insurance-Preferred Contractor Networks**
- Aegis-certified builders are added to insurer preferred contractor lists
- Insurance agents recommend Aegis-certified builders to policyholders seeking premium reductions
- Creates a virtuous cycle: insurer → agent → builder → homeowner → insurer

**Channel 4: Direct-to-Consumer (DIY)**
- Aegis Home Certification Kit (H20): $500 self-assessment toolkit
- Online store for individual Aegis Home components
- Installation guides, video tutorials, community forum
- Target: DIY enthusiasts and existing home retrofitters
