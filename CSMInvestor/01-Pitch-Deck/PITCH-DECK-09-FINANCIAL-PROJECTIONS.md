**Margin expansion drivers:**
1. Learning curve effects in MXene synthesis (current lab cost ~$100/g → Year 5 target ~$5/g at production scale)
2. BFRP raw material volume discounts (basalt fiber is commodity; polymer matrix bulk pricing)
3. Labor productivity gains (standardized VARTM processes, automation of repetitive assembly)
4. Licensing revenue mix shift (near-zero marginal cost, increasing as % of total revenue)
5. Insurance recurring revenue accumulation (each new hardened property adds permanent annual revenue with no incremental cost)

## 7.0 KEY ASSUMPTIONS

| Assumption | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| MXene production cost learning rate | 15% per doubling of cumulative volume | Consistent with specialty chemical industry benchmarks |
| Aegis Home adoption rate (new construction) | 1% → 5% of high-risk region new builds | Conservative; solar panels achieved similar adoption curves |
| Government contract win rate | 40% on bids | Typical for established small business with unique technical capability |
| Insurance premium reduction rate | 15% on hardened properties | Benchmarked against fire sprinkler, hurricane shutter discounts |
| International licensing fee | 5–8% of licensee revenue | Industry standard for technology licensing in manufacturing |
| Charlemagne-Class vessel sales | 5 → 25 units/year | Conservative; market for amphibious emergency vessels is untested |
| Gross margin progression | 55% → 75% over 5 years | Benchmarked against Tesla (automotive), composites manufacturers |
