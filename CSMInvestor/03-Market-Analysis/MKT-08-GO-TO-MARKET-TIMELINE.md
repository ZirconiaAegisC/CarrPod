# MKT-08: Go-to-Market Timeline

## Project AEGIS / Carrington Storm Motors
### Phased Market Entry Plan — Milestones, Gates, Resources, Success Metrics
### Document Version: 1.0 | Date: July 2026 | Classification: Investor Confidential

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

CSM's go-to-market strategy is organized into five phases spanning six years, from pre-launch preparation through international expansion. Each phase has defined milestones, gates (conditions that must be met before proceeding), resource requirements, and success metrics. The timeline is aggressive but achievable, reflecting the urgency of the EMP/CME threat and the first-mover advantage available to the first company that brings a comprehensive dielectric protection solution to market.

Total resource requirements across all phases: approximately $350-500 million, funded through a combination of equity investment (Series A, Series B, Series C), non-dilutive government grants and contracts, and revenue from early customer deployments. The Company reaches cash-flow breakeven in Phase 2 (approximately month 28-32) and operating profitability by Phase 3.

## PHASE 0: PRE-LAUNCH (Months 0-6)

### 0.1 Phase Objectives

Establish the foundation for market entry: regulatory positioning, insurance carrier partnerships, government procurement readiness, manufacturing capacity planning, and initial team assembly.

### 0.2 Key Activities

**Regulatory & Standards**
- Submit comments to FERC on anticipated GMD NOPR
- Join NERC GMD Task Force as participating member
- Initiate IEEE Standards Working Group for dielectric EMP protection performance testing
- File for CISA CIPAC membership (Critical Infrastructure Partnership Advisory Council)
- Engage with NAIC Catastrophe Risk Subgroup on space weather risk commentary
- Begin engagement with IACS classification societies on maritime dielectric certification

**Insurance Partnerships**
- Complete EMP/CME risk model for commercial property (deliverable to insurers)
- Engage 6 Tier 1 strategic carrier prospects (Swiss Re, AXA XL, Chubb, Munich Re, Lloyd's, Berkshire)
- Develop parametric product structure for Swiss Re/Munich Re input
- Retain insurance regulatory counsel (NAIC, Solvency II, Lloyd's regulatory)
- Present at RIMS 2027 conference

**Government Procurement Readiness**
- Obtain DUNS number and register in SAM.gov
- Apply for CAGE code
- Initiate FCL (Facility Clearance) sponsorship through DOD customer
- Prepare SBIR Phase I proposals for DTRA, DARPA, and DOE
- Brief relevant congressional committees (Energy & Commerce, Armed Services, Homeland Security)

**Manufacturing & Supply Chain**
- Finalize dielectric panel manufacturing process specification
- Identify and qualify polymer and ceramic filler suppliers
- Site selection for Phase 1 manufacturing facility (5 shortlisted locations)
- Begin tooling design for panel co-extrusion line
- Establish quality control and testing protocols

**Team Assembly**
- Recruit CEO/COO/CFO (if not already in place)
- Recruit VP Government Relations (retired flag officer or SES-level)
- Recruit VP Insurance Strategy (former carrier executive)
- Recruit VP Engineering/Manufacturing (composites manufacturing experience)
- Recruit Director of Regulatory Affairs (former FERC/NERC staff)

**Intellectual Property**
- File international PCT applications for four patent families
- File US non-provisional applications
- Conduct freedom-to-operate analysis for key markets

### 0.3 Phase 0 Gates (Must Pass Before P01)

1. At least 2 of 6 Tier 1 insurance carriers have signed MOUs for joint product development
2. At least 1 National Laboratory CRADA executed (INL, Sandia, or both)
3. At least 2 SBIR Phase I proposals submitted to target agencies
4. Manufacturing facility site selected and lease/purchase option secured
5. Core executive team hired (CEO, COO, CFO, VP Government, VP Insurance)
6. PCT patent applications filed for all four patent families
7. Minimum of $15M in committed funding for Phase 1 activities

### 0.4 Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |

## PHASE 1: FIRST GOVERNMENT CONTRACTS, INFRASTRUCTURE PILOTS (Months 6-18)

### 1.1 Phase Objectives

Secure initial government contracts (SBIR Phase I/II, pilot installations), execute first 3-5 utility/infrastructure pilot projects, establish insurance carrier partnership agreements, begin manufacturing line construction, and complete EMP certification testing at National Laboratories.

### 1.2 Key Activities

**Government Contracts**
- Complete SBIR Phase I awards (DTRA, DARPA, DOE) — execute feasibility studies
- Submit SBIR Phase II proposals for prototype development
- Pursue initial sole-source justifications for DOD dielectric panel evaluation units
- Brief DOD installation commands (Army Corps of Engineers, NAVFAC, AFCEC) on CSM technology
- Submit unsolicited proposals to DTRA and STRATCOM for C4I facility protection
- Apply for DOE GRIP (Grid Resilience Innovation Partnerships) grant for utility pilot co-funding

**Utility & Infrastructure Pilots**
- Deploy 3-5 pilot substation installations (cooperative or municipal utilities, faster procurement)
- Conduct EMP testing of pilot installations at Sandia or INL
- Publish test results in peer-reviewed journal (IEEE Transactions on EMC)
- Develop case studies for utility industry publication

**Insurance Integration**
- Finalize premium-linked product structure with lead carrier partner(s)
- Launch pilot program: 20-50 commercial properties receive CSM hardening + premium reduction
- Begin parametric product regulatory approval process (lead state: Delaware or Vermont)
- Develop insurer training program for underwriters and loss control engineers

**Manufacturing**
- Break ground on Phase 1 manufacturing facility (25,000-50,000 sq ft)
- Order and install panel co-extrusion line (lead time: 8-12 months)
- Begin pilot production of dielectric panels for test installations
- Establish supplier quality agreements with polymer and ceramic filler vendors

**Team Scaling**
- Recruit manufacturing operations team
- Recruit field installation supervisors
- Recruit sales team for utility and government segments
- Team size: 60-85

### 1.3 Phase 1 Gates

1. At least 1 SBIR Phase II award received
2. At least 1 DOD pilot installation contracted (any value)
3. At least 2 utility pilot installations completed with EMP test validation
4. At least 1 insurance carrier partnership agreement signed (committing to premium-linked product offering)
5. Manufacturing facility construction underway (completion within 6 months)
6. EMP certification testing completed at National Laboratory — data supports MIL-STD-188-125 equivalence or superiority

## SECTION 3: KEY RISKS TO TIMELINE

1. **Regulatory delay risk**: If FERC GMD NOPR is delayed beyond 2028, utility adoption shifts right by 2-3 years. Impact: Phase 2-4 utility revenue reduced by 40-60%.

2. **Insurance carrier inertia risk**: If major carriers decline to include EMP/CME in policy wordings, the insurance distribution channel underperforms. Impact: Phase 2-4 insurance-linked revenue reduced by 50-70%.

3. **Manufacturing ramp risk**: If co-extrusion line lead times extend (currently 8-12 months; supply-constrained market for composite manufacturing equipment), production capacity limits revenue in Phases 2-3.

4. **Talent acquisition risk**: The specialized combination of materials science, EMP/EMC engineering, and insurance/reinsurance expertise requires recruiting from thin labor pools. Key positions may take 6-12 months to fill.

5. **Macroeconomic risk**: A recession in Phases 1-2 would delay discretionary customer spending (residential, maritime) and potentially constrain insurance carrier appetite for new product launches.

6. **Geopolitical risk**: Deterioration in US-China, US-EU, or US-Middle East relations could block international expansion and disrupt supply chains for raw materials.

7. **Technology validation risk**: If EMP testing at National Laboratories reveals performance below modeled expectations, product redesign could delay market entry by 6-18 months.
