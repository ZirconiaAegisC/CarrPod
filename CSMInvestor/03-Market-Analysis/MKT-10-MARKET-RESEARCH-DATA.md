## SECTION 9: METHODOLOGY NOTES

### 9.1 Bottom-Up vs. Top-Down Sizing

CSM's market sizing (MKT-01) uses bottom-up methodology wherever possible — counting substations, vessels, housing units, and insurance premiums and applying cost-per-unit assumptions derived from primary sources (EPRI, RSMeans, industry interviews). Top-down estimates (e.g., "3% of global insurance premiums") are cross-checked against bottom-up figures and used primarily for validation.

### 9.2 Data Limitations and Cautions

1. **EMP/CME event probability**: Long recurrence intervals (150+ years for Carrington-class events) mean the empirical record is sparse. Probability estimates from Riley (2012) — 12% per decade — are based on solar physics modeling, not a large statistical sample.

2. **Adoption curve projections**: Technology adoption for non-existent product categories must be estimated from analogs. CSM's estimates are conservative vs. cybersecurity and other regulatory-driven technologies.

3. **Government spending projections**: Subject to political change. Continuation of IIJA and IRA funding levels is assumed but not guaranteed.

4. **Insurance market response**: No precedent exists for a safety technology that addresses a peril as large as Carrington-class EMP/CME. Insurer behavior is inferred from their response to earthquake, flood, and terrorism risk, but EMP/CME may trigger different behavior due to its global, correlated nature.

5. **Maritime classification society timelines**: IACS member societies are conservative, technical organizations. Timeline estimates assume standard rulemaking processes (2-4 years from initiation to rule publication). These could be accelerated by a near-miss event.
