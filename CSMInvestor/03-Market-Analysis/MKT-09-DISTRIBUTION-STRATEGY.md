## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

CSM's distribution strategy is designed around the unique nature of EMP/CME protection products: they are not consumer goods sold through retail, nor are they conventional industrial equipment sold through distributors. They are safety-critical, regulation-driven, insurance-linked infrastructure products that require technical specification, certified installation, and ongoing monitoring. The distribution model must reflect these characteristics.

CSM employs a hybrid distribution model with four primary channels: (1) direct sales for government and large infrastructure operators; (2) insurance carrier distribution — embedded in insurance policies as a condition of coverage or premium reduction; (3) certified installer network for Aegis Home residential products; and (4) strategic partnerships with grid equipment companies, defense contractors, shipyards, and engineering firms. Each channel is optimized for its target customer segment.

The distribution strategy is capital-light: CSM does not build retail locations, does not maintain large field sales forces, and leverages partners' existing customer relationships, distribution infrastructure, and sales forces. This allows the Company to scale revenue faster than headcount and maintain gross margins above 55%.

## SECTION 1: CHANNEL DESIGN PRINCIPLES

### 1.1 Why Conventional Channels Don't Work

EMP/CME protection products cannot be sold through conventional industrial distribution for several reasons:

1. **Technical specification required**: EMP protection is not a commodity SKU that can be picked from a catalog. Each installation requires site-specific engineering assessment, system design, and integration with existing infrastructure.

2. **Trust and certification**: Customers are buying protection against a catastrophic threat they may never experience. They require third-party certification, insurance backing, and ongoing monitoring — none of which a conventional distributor provides.

3. **Regulatory compliance**: Many installations are driven by regulatory mandates (NERC, CER Directive, classification society) that require specific documentation and certification that a distributor channel cannot support.

4. **Installation complexity**: EMP protection systems must be installed by trained, certified technicians. A conventional distributor model would create quality control and liability risk.

5. **Insurance integration**: The premium-linked model requires deep integration with insurance carrier underwriting, policy issuance, and claims systems — capabilities no distributor possesses.

### 2.4 Government Sales Specialization

Government sales require specialized capabilities not needed in commercial channels:

- **Security clearances**: Account executives handling classified contracts require Secret or Top Secret clearance.
- **FAR/DFARS expertise**: Proposal managers must understand Federal Acquisition Regulation and Defense FAR Supplement requirements.
- **Cost accounting compliance**: Government contracts require compliant cost accounting systems (FAR Part 31).
- **Past performance**: Government buyers require evidence of prior successful contract performance.
- **Small business subcontracting plans**: Large contracts require plans for small business participation.
- **Government relations**: Not sales per se, but essential for informing requirements development.

## SECTION 3: INSURANCE CARRIER DISTRIBUTION — EMBEDDED IN POLICY

### 3.1 Channel Description

Insurance carriers serve as CSM's primary distribution channel for commercial property and, eventually, residential products. The model works as follows:

1. CSM partners with an insurance carrier to develop a "Resilience Credit" program.
2. The carrier identifies policyholders with significant EMP/CME exposure (large commercial properties, high-value homes, marine risks).
3. At policy renewal, the carrier offers the policyholder a choice:
   - **Option A**: Continue current coverage, but with an EMP/CME exclusion (or sublimit) added to the policy.
   - **Option B**: Install CSM's hardening system, receive a premium reduction of 10-30%, and maintain full EMP/CME coverage.
4. If the policyholder selects Option B, the carrier schedules CSM's certified installer to perform the installation.
5. CSM receives payment from the carrier (who may finance this cost and recover it through premium payments over time).

### 3.2 Why This Model Works

**For the policyholder**: Positive economic return (premium savings pay for system in 5-12 years); continued insurance coverage for EMP/CME; peace of mind.

**For the insurer**: Reduced aggregate portfolio exposure to EMP/CME; higher return on capital (lower SCR requirement); competitive differentiation; new premium pool (parametric products).

**For CSM**: Zero customer acquisition cost (carrier's underwriting process identifies prospects); carrier's trusted relationship transfers to CSM; carrier's distribution force (agents, brokers, underwriters) sells CSM's product as part of policy placement; carrier finances the installation (CSM gets paid upfront).

## SECTION 4: CERTIFIED INSTALLER NETWORK — AEGIS HOME

### 4.1 Channel Description

For residential products (Aegis Home), CSM builds a certified installer network — licensed electricians who complete CSM's training and certification program and are authorized to install Aegis Home systems.

### 4.2 Installer Network Model

| Program Element | Description |
|---|---|
| Certification requirements | Licensed master electrician; completion of CSM's 40-hour training program; passing certification exam; annual recertification; minimum 2 installations/year to maintain certification |
| Training program | 5-day intensive course covering dielectric panel installation, system integration, testing, and customer education. Delivered at CSM training centers or regional locations. |
| Territories | Exclusive or semi-exclusive territories within metropolitan areas (based on population density and expected installation volume) |
| Installer compensation | Installer purchases CSM system at wholesale price (35-45% below retail); charges customer for installation labor separately. Typical installer margin: 25-35% on combined hardware + labor. |
| Quality assurance | CSM conducts random post-installation inspections; all installations require CSM remote certification testing before system is activated. |
| Lead generation | CSM provides leads from insurance carrier referrals, website inquiries, and builder partnerships. Installers also generate their own leads. |
| Brand standards | All installers use CSM-branded vehicles, uniforms, and installation materials. Co-branded marketing materials provided. |

### 4.3 Installer Network Economics

| Metric | Home-Basic | Home-Standard | Home-Premium | Home-Estate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Retail price (installed) | $7,000 | $11,250 | $20,000 | $47,500 |
| CSM wholesale price to installer | $4,995 | $8,495 | $14,995 | $29,995 |
| CSM gross margin | 45-52% | 48-55% | 50-58% | 52-62% |
| Installer labor + overhead | $1,500-$2,000 | $2,000-$2,750 | $3,000-$5,000 | $8,000-$17,500 |
| Installer margin | 20-30% | 18-26% | 22-28% | 25-35% |

### 4.4 Builder Certification Program

Complementing the installer network, CSM's builder certification program targets luxury home builders:

| Program Element | Description |
|---|---|
| Builder requirements | Minimum 20 homes/year at $800K+ price point; commitment to offer Aegis Home in all model homes |
| Builder benefits | "Aegis Home Certified Builder" designation; co-marketing with insurance carrier partners; listing on CSM website as certified builder; lead generation from insurance carrier referrals; training on EMP/CME risk for sales teams |
| Integration | CSM-certified electrician performs installation during construction electrical rough-in; builder's electrician coordinates |
| Model home support | CSM subsidizes Aegis Home system in builder's model home (50% discount) in exchange for marketing access |
