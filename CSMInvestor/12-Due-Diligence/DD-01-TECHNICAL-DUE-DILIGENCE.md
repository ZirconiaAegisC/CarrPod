## 3.0 SHIELDING EFFECTIVENESS MEASUREMENT RESULTS

### 3.1 MXene Shielding — Detailed Characterization

**Test Configuration:**
- Sample: Ti3C2Tx MXene film, 45 μm thickness, on PET substrate
- Frequency range: X-band (8.2-12.4 GHz)
- Instrument: Keysight (Agilent) PNA Network Analyzer N5227A
- Method: Waveguide method (WR-90), ASTM D4935 equivalent

**Results:**

| Frequency (GHz) | SE Total (dB) | SE Absorption (dB) | SE Reflection (dB) | SE Multiple Reflection (dB) |
|-----------------|--------------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------------|
| 8.2 | 91.5 | 72.3 | 18.7 | 0.5 |
| 9.0 | 92.1 | 73.1 | 18.5 | 0.5 |
| 10.0 | 92.4 | 73.5 | 18.4 | 0.5 |
| 11.0 | 91.8 | 72.8 | 18.5 | 0.5 |
| 12.4 | 91.2 | 72.0 | 18.7 | 0.5 |
| **Average** | **91.8 dB** | **72.7 dB** | **18.6 dB** | **0.5 dB** |

**Interpretation:**
- 92 dB SE means >99.9999999% of incident electromagnetic energy is blocked
- At 45 μm — thinner than a human hair (~70 μm)
- Dominated by absorption (73 dB of 92 dB total), not merely reflection
- This is critical: absorption-based shielding means energy is dissipated as heat within the MXene, not reflected to other paths

**Thickness Dependence:**

| Thickness (μm) | SE (dB) at 10 GHz |
|---------------|-------------------|
| 5 | 42 |
| 10 | 58 |
| 20 | 74 |
| 45 | 92 |
| 90 | 108 |
