Carrington Storm Motors is a pre-revenue research and development company. As such, its "historical financials" are minimal — the company has not yet begun significant capital deployment. This document provides investors with:

CSM's financial posture can be summarized as: **clean slate, full transparency, built-for-scale from day one.**

As the Accountant Heuristic demands: "If the financial foundation has cracks, the company will eventually collapse, regardless of how good the technology is. Build the foundation right."

## 3.0 R&D COST ACCOUNTING

### 3.1 R&D Expense Categorization

CSM will track R&D expenses according to the following taxonomy:

| Category | Examples | Tracking Method |
|----------|----------|----------------|
| Personnel (R&D) | Materials scientists, engineers, lab technicians | Timesheets allocated to projects |
| Materials and Supplies | MAX phase precursors, etchants, polymer resins, basalt fiber, CNTs, ceramic powders, geopolymer precursors | Purchase orders coded to R&D projects |
| Laboratory Equipment | TEM, SEM, XRD, network analyzer, TEM cell, mechanical testing equipment | Capitalized as fixed assets; depreciated over useful life |
| External Testing | Independent lab validation, university partnerships | Invoices coded to R&D projects |
| Patent Costs | Patent filing, prosecution, maintenance | Capitalized as intangible assets (see Section 4) |
| Facilities (R&D portion) | Lab space rent, utilities, maintenance | Allocated by square footage |
| Software (R&D) | Simulation software (COMSOL, ANSYS HFSS), data analysis tools | Expensed or capitalized per software policy |
| Travel (R&D) | Conference presentations, collaborative research meetings | Expense reports coded to R&D |

### 3.2 Capitalization vs. Expensing (ASC 730 / IAS 38)

Under U.S. GAAP (ASC 730), all R&D costs are expensed as incurred (with limited exceptions for software development). Under IFRS (IAS 38), development costs meeting certain criteria can be capitalized. CSM will follow U.S. GAAP (expense all R&D) for simplicity and consistency with U.S. venture capital norms. If CSM expands internationally and adopts IFRS for subsidiary reporting, the IAS 38 capitalization option will be evaluated.

**Exceptions (Capitalized):**
- Patent costs (legal fees for patent filing and prosecution) — capitalized as intangible assets
- Laboratory equipment — capitalized as fixed assets, depreciated over useful life (5-10 years)
- Internally-developed software for internal use (ASC 350-40) — capitalized if qualifying criteria met

### 3.3 R&D Tax Credit Tracking

CSM will track R&D expenses in a manner compliant with IRS Section 41 (R&D Tax Credit) requirements:
- Qualified research activities identified and documented contemporaneously
- Wages, supplies, and contract research expenses segregated for credit calculation
- State R&D tax credits tracked separately (many states offer credits complementary to federal)

## 4.0 IP VALUATION METHODOLOGY

### 4.1 IP Portfolio Overview

CSM's intellectual property portfolio consists of:
- **12 CSMQuantum materials science volumes** (proprietary formulations, processing methods, characterization data)
- **72 fleet specification volumes** (product architectures, designs, integration specifications)
- **6,302 total documents** (know-how, trade secrets, analytical frameworks, methodologies)
- **Pending patent applications** (to be filed in Year 1)
- **Pending trademark registrations** (CSM, AEGIS, CHARLEMAGNE, SIBLINGFREQUENCY, DIELECTRIC CITADEL)

### 4.2 Valuation Approaches

**Cost Approach (Replacement Cost):**
- Estimated cost to recreate 6,302 documents: $50-150M
  - 18 domain experts × 3-5 years × $300K-500K/year fully loaded = ~$27-45M in personnel cost
  - Plus research materials, equipment, testing, external expertise: $20-100M
  - However, replacement cost undervalues the innovation: recreating the documents would not necessarily produce the same insights (the "discovery path" is not replicable)

**Market Approach (Comparable Transactions):**
- Comparable IP acquisitions:
  - Trade secret portfolios: typically 3-10x annual cost of creation
  - Materials science patent portfolios: $1-10M per patent family (pre-revenue)
  - Defense technology IP: 2-5x R&D expenditure
- Estimated CSM IP value (market approach): $100-500M

**Income Approach (Discounted Cash Flow):**
- Projected revenue from products using CSM IP: $500M-$5B at maturity
- Royalty rate (hypothetical licensing): 3-8% of revenue
- Discount rate: 15-25% (early-stage technology risk)
- Estimated CSM IP value (income approach): $200M-$2B

**Blended Valuation:** $200M-$1B (conservative); $500M-$2B (with validation data)

### 4.3 Financial Reporting Treatment

For financial reporting purposes (business combinations, impairment testing), CSM's IP will be valued by an independent valuation firm using the income approach (relief-from-royalty method). This valuation will be:
- Tested for impairment annually (ASC 350) or when triggering events occur
- Amortized over the estimated useful life of the IP (typically 10-15 years for materials technology, reflecting both patent life and technology obsolescence)

## 5.0 REVENUE RECOGNITION POLICY (PLANNED)

### 5.1 Revenue Streams and Recognition Method

| Revenue Stream | Recognition Method (ASC 606) | Recognition Timing |
|---------------|------------------------------|--------------------|
| Product Sales (Aegis Home, Aegis Business) | Revenue recognized upon transfer of control (shipping terms FOB destination or installation complete) | Point in time |
| Product Sales (Charlemagne — utility systems) | Revenue recognized over time if customized for specific utility; at point in time if standard configuration | Over time or point in time (contract-specific) |
| Installation Services | Revenue recognized as services are performed (input method: costs incurred) | Over time |
| Monitoring Subscriptions | Revenue recognized ratably over subscription period | Over time (monthly) |
| Maintenance Contracts | Revenue recognized ratably over contract period | Over time |
| Insurance Premium Share | Revenue recognized as earned (when underlying insurance premiums are recognized by insurer partner) | Over time |
| Government Contracts | Per contract terms; typically cost-plus-fixed-fee or firm-fixed-price | Contract-specific (ASC 606) |
| Licensing Revenue | Revenue recognized based on license type (right-to-use vs. right-to-access) | Point in time or over time |
| SiblingFrequency (free) | No revenue — educational program | N/A |

### 5.2 Multi-Element Arrangements

Many CSM customer contracts will include multiple performance obligations (product + installation + maintenance + monitoring). CSM will:

1. Identify all performance obligations in the contract
2. Determine the standalone selling price of each obligation
3. Allocate the transaction price based on relative standalone selling prices
4. Recognize revenue as each performance obligation is satisfied

**Example:** A utility contracts for Charlemagne hardening at 5 substations ($5M total):
- Equipment (transformers, GIC blockers): $3M (60%) — recognized at delivery
- Installation: $1.5M (30%) — recognized over installation period
- First year monitoring + maintenance: $500K (10%) — recognized ratably over 12 months
