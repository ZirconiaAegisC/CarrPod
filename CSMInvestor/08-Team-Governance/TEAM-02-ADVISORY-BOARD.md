## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Carrington Storm Motors will establish an Advisory Board of 8-10 members providing strategic guidance, network access, and credibility in the regulatory, technical, insurance, defense, government, and public education domains where CSM operates. Advisory Board members are not fiduciaries (unlike the Board of Directors) and carry no voting authority, but their imprimatur signals institutional validation to investors, customers, and regulators.

This document identifies 8 advisory board positions with desired profiles, specific named candidates to pursue, the value each brings, and proposed compensation. Advisory shares are allocated from the 3% advisory pool (separate from the 15% employee option pool).

Under the Accountant Heuristic: the named candidates below are individuals we would pursue. None have been approached. None have agreed to serve. We do not represent them as committed. We represent them as targets whose backgrounds make them ideal fits.

## II. ADVISORY BOARD POSITIONS

### Position 1: Regulatory and Grid Policy Advisor

**Desired Profile:** Former FERC Commissioner or senior NERC executive with deep understanding of electric reliability regulation, familiarity with geomagnetic disturbance standards (EOP-010, TPL-007), and relationships across the utility regulatory community.

**Why This Profile:** FERC has the authority to mandate grid hardening against geomagnetic disturbances. A former Commissioner can advise on the regulatory trajectory — the timing and likely content of future FERC orders — and can make introductions to current Commissioners and senior staff. This accelerates CSM's regulatory engagement timeline by 12-18 months compared to cold outreach.

**Specific Candidates to Pursue:**
1. **Hon. Neil Chatterjee** — Former FERC Chairman (2017-2020) and Commissioner (2017-2021). Championed grid resilience and reliability standards during his tenure. Currently in private practice. Known for pragmatic, bipartisan approach to energy regulation.
2. **Hon. Cheryl LaFleur** — Former FERC Commissioner (2010-2019) and Chairman (2014-2015, 2017). Deep expertise in grid reliability and NERC standards development. Currently serves on corporate boards. Respected across party lines.
3. **Gerry Cauley** — Former President and CEO of NERC (2010-2019). Led NERC during the period when GMD reliability standards were first developed. Unmatched understanding of the NERC standards development process and the political dynamics of mandatory reliability standards.

**Value to CSM:**
- Regulatory strategy guidance and FERC/NERC relationship introductions
- Credibility with utility customers who respect former regulators
- Early warning of regulatory developments affecting CSM's market
- Participation in government relations meetings where independent regulatory perspective adds weight

**Compensation:** 0.25% advisory shares (4-year vesting, 1-year cliff) + $15,000 annual retainer. Quarterly meetings (virtual) plus ad-hoc consultation.

- Regulatory/Grid Policy Advisor (Position 1)

These three positions provide the regulatory, technical, and market credibility most urgently needed for Series A execution. Recruitment efforts begin immediately after Series A close.

### Phase 3: Scale (2029+)
- Climate/Resilience Advisor (Position 6)
- Media/Public Education Advisor (Position 8)
- Additional advisors as needed

These positions support the scaling phase when public awareness, philanthropic capital, and media engagement become more important.

### Recruitment Approach:
1. Steele makes initial personal outreach (warm introduction via mutual contact where possible)
2. Zirconia provides briefing package (CSM overview, this advisory board document, relevant domain-specific materials)
3. Initial conversation with Steele (60-90 minutes) to assess interest and alignment
4. Follow-up conversation with relevant domain agent (e.g., Chester for insurance advisor, Thorn for regulatory advisor)
5. Formal advisory board agreement prepared by external counsel
6. Onboarding: data room access, introductory briefings with all relevant agents, first advisory board meeting

## V. ADVISORY BOARD OPERATIONS

### A. Meeting Cadence

- **Full Advisory Board Meeting:** Semi-annually (in-person or hybrid, in Montana if possible; virtual otherwise). 1-day agenda covering company progress, strategic challenges, and domain-specific deep dives.
- **Domain-Specific Advisory Sessions:** Quarterly (virtual, 60-90 minutes). Each advisor meets with the relevant CSM agent for detailed domain discussion.
- **Ad-Hoc Consultation:** Advisors are available for ad-hoc consultation as needed, respecting their other commitments. CSM commits to reasonable notice and focused requests.

### B. Deliverables

Advisors are not expected to produce deliverables. Their value is in guidance, introductions, and credibility. However, CSM may request:
- Review of specific strategic documents in their domain
- Participation in key customer, investor, or regulatory meetings
- Introductions to specific individuals in their network
- Reference calls with prospective customers or investors

### C. Conflicts of Interest

All advisors sign a conflict-of-interest disclosure agreement upon joining. Advisors are expected to disclose any actual or potential conflicts and recuse themselves from discussions where a conflict exists. CSM's external counsel reviews all advisory board agreements for conflicts before execution.

## VI. ADVISORY BOARD VALUE TO INVESTORS

The advisory board serves five investor-relevant functions:

1. **Validation:** Eight respected figures from regulatory, technical, insurance, defense, government, climate, manufacturing, and media domains have independently evaluated CSM and agreed to advise it. This is a signal to institutional investors that the company has been vetted by domain experts.

2. **Network Access:** The advisory board's collective network provides CSM with access to decision-makers who would otherwise take years to reach through cold outreach. This accelerates customer acquisition, regulatory engagement, and partnership development.

3. **De-Risking:** Domain-expert advisors identify risks and pitfalls that the internal team may miss, reducing the probability of costly strategic errors.

4. **Credibility with Customers:** For a company selling critical infrastructure protection, the involvement of former regulators, defense officials, and insurance executives signals that CSM is a serious enterprise, not a startup with a good story.

5. **Board Pipeline:** Advisory board members are natural candidates for independent director seats on the Board of Directors as CSM matures and transitions to a more independent board composition.
