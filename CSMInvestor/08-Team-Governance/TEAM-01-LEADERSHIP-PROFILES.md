## I. THE AGENT SYSTEM: AN OVERVIEW

Carrington Storm Motors operates through a distributed cognitive delivery network: 18 specialized "Agents of Aegis," each with deep domain expertise in one aspect of the Dielectric Citadel. They communicate through the SiblingFrequency — a structured inter-agent communication protocol that ensures coherent organizational action without the bottlenecks of traditional hierarchical management. Each agent maintains their own document library, product responsibility, and stakeholder relationships.

This document is written under the Williams Heuristic: each profile includes plain-language descriptions alongside relevant qualifications. Under the El Segundo Calm: we present our team's capabilities without exaggeration. Under the Accountant Heuristic: every qualification claim is traceable to documented experience within the 6,302-document corpus.

## VI. AGENT SYSTEM OPERATING PROTOCOLS

### A. The SiblingFrequency

The SiblingFrequency is a structured inter-agent communication protocol. Key features:

1. **Daily Check-In:** All agents post a brief (3-5 sentence) update to the SiblingFrequency channel. Format: What I accomplished yesterday, what I plan to accomplish today, any blockers.

2. **Decision Escalation:** Decisions within a single agent's domain are made by that agent. Decisions affecting multiple agents' domains are discussed on the SiblingFrequency and decided by consensus where possible, escalated to Steele where necessary. Decisions affecting the entire organization are made by Steele with input from all relevant agents.

3. **Document Review:** All documents intended for external distribution or cross-domain use undergo a Mork Review (risk/flaw analysis) and a Zirconia Review (coherence/quality/clarity). Documents requiring financial data undergo a Chester Review. Documents requiring public-facing language undergo a Vance Review.

4. **Three Heuristics Enforcement:** Any agent may flag a document or communication as violating Williams Clarity, El Segundo Calm, or Accountant Rigor. The flag requires the document owner to address the violation before the document proceeds.

### B. Agent Knowledge Management

Each agent maintains a personal knowledge repository (stored in the CSM knowledge management system) containing:
- All documents they have authored or co-authored
- Meeting notes from their participation in cross-domain discussions
- External source materials relevant to their domain
- Decision logs recording significant decisions and their rationales

The aggregation of all 18 agent repositories forms the 6,302-document corpus. The Zirconia-maintained master index cross-references all documents, enabling any agent to find relevant work from any other agent's domain.

## VII. COMPENSATION PHILOSOPHY

CSM's compensation philosophy is grounded in three principles:

1. **Fairness:** Compensation is set at or above market for equivalent roles in early-stage deep technology companies, adjusted for Montana cost of living (approximately 85% of San Francisco Bay Area).

2. **Alignment:** All agents hold equity (via the employee option pool, 15% of fully diluted capitalization post-Series A). Equity grants are structured with four-year vesting with one-year cliff, aligning agent retention with company value creation.

3. **Transparency:** Compensation ranges are published internally. Individual compensation is confidential by default but may be voluntarily disclosed. Total compensation cost is reported to the Board quarterly.

**Leadership Compensation (Post-Series A):**
- Director (Steele): $225,000 base + equity (founder shares)
- C-Suite (Calder, Nash, Vaun): $185,000-210,000 base + equity (founder shares + option pool grants)
- Division Directors: $145,000-175,000 base + equity
- Specialized Agents: $120,000-155,000 base + equity
