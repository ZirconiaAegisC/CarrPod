## Executive Summary: Ownership Architecture

Carrington Storm Motors is structured to align founder, employee, and investor incentives through a conventional venture-backed capitalization model. The company is incorporated as a Delaware C-Corporation. The cap table is built on standard NVCA-model documentation with customary investor protections and a 15% employee option pool.

**Williams Heuristic:** Who owns what, who gets paid first in a sale, and what happens to everyone's stake when we raise more money. These are the three questions every cap table must answer. The answers here are: founders own the majority until dilution, investors get their money back before anyone else in a downside scenario, and everyone gets diluted proportionally in future rounds unless they have pro-rata rights.

**Cap Table Math — Verification:**

1. Seed SAFE conversion: $500,000 / $6.00 per share = 83,333 shares (Series Seed Preferred)
2. Series A investment: $25,000,000 / $7.50 per share = 3,333,333 shares (Series A Preferred)
3. Total issued shares (excluding option pool): 4,250,000 + 3,000,000 + 1,500,000 + 500,000 + 83,333 + 3,333,333 = 12,666,666 shares
4. Option pool: 15% of fully diluted → Pool / Total = 0.15 → Pool = 0.15 × Total → Total = Issued / (1 - 0.15) = 12,666,666 / 0.85 = 14,901,960
5. Option pool shares: 14,901,960 - 12,666,666 = 2,235,294
6. Fully diluted shares: 14,901,960

*(Minor rounding differences may exist; final share counts subject to legal counsel verification at closing.)*

## Downside Protection Analysis

### TABLE 6.12: Waterfall Analysis — Moderate Downside Exit ($200M)

| Stakeholder | Liquidation Preference | Preference Amount | Remaining Proceeds | Common Distribution | Total Payout |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Series A Investors | 1x non-participating | $25.0M | $175.0M | — | *$25.0M* |
| Series B Investors | 1x non-participating | $100.0M | $75.0M | — | $100.0M |
| Common (Founders, Employees, Seed) | None | — | $75.0M | $75.0M | $75.0M |
| **Total** | | **$125.0M** | **$75.0M** | **$75.0M** | **$200.0M** |

*Series A investors have a choice: take $25M liquidation preference OR convert to Common and participate pro-rata. At $200M exit, converting to Common yields 22.4% × $200M = $44.8M > $25M preference. Investors always choose the higher of the two. Actual payout to Series A at $200M exit: $44.8M (1.8x return).

**Breakeven Analysis — When Liquidation Preference Matters:**
- Series A: Below ~$112M exit, preference ($25M) exceeds conversion value. Below $112M, Series A takes preference.
- Series B: Below ~$508M exit, preference ($100M) exceeds conversion value. Below $508M, Series B takes preference.

**Accountant Heuristic:** Non-participating preferred with 1x liquidation preference is the most founder-friendly standard investor term. It means investors get their money back first (fair — they put in cash, founders put in sweat), but they don't "double dip" by getting both their preference AND a share of remaining proceeds. "Participating" preferred would give investors their $25M back AND 22.4% of the remaining $175M — that's $25M + $39.2M = $64.2M vs. $44.8M with non-participating. The difference is why founders negotiate hard for non-participating terms.
