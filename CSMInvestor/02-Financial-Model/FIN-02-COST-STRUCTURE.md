## Executive Summary: The Cost Thesis

Carrington Storm Motors' cost structure is designed around three principles: (1) asset-light manufacturing in Years 1-3, transitioning to vertically integrated production in Years 4-5, (2) R&D intensity that front-loads innovation before competitors enter, and (3) a headcount model that scales revenue 25x (from $11.5M to $1.2B) while scaling employees 36x (from 25 to 900) — improving revenue-per-employee from $639K to $1.78M.

**Williams Heuristic:** We stockpile knowledge cheaply (6,302 documents cost far less than building factories), then convert that knowledge into premium-priced products. The margin structure reflects this: low initial margins as we build manufacturing capability, expanding margins as volume and the learning curve compound.

## Cost Optimization Levers (Already Modeled)

1. **Learning Curve:** 15% cost reduction per doubling of cumulative production volume for ACS and AHM; 10% for PMR and CCF (more engineering-heavy).
2. **Vertical Integration:** Y1-Y2 rely on contract manufacturing for AHM enclosures and Phantom chassis. By Y3-Y4, insourcing these components improves margin 3-5 points.
3. **Bulk Material Procurement:** By Y5, raw material purchasing power reduces input costs 25-40% vs. Y1 R&D-quantity pricing.
4. **Automation:** Each automated composite layup line replaces 8-12 manual technicians. Capital cost amortizes over 7 years at ~$500K/year/line, vs. $800K-1.2M/year in labor saved. Net savings per line: $300-700K/year.
5. **Geographic Labor Arbitrage:** Y4+ manufacturing expansion considers lower-cost US regions (Appalachia, Rust Belt repurposed factories) and allied-nation production (UK, Australia) with defense offset benefits.
