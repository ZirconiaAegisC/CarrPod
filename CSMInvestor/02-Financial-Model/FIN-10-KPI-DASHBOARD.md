## Executive Summary: Measuring What Matters

Carrington Storm Motors' KPI framework is built on the principle that a defense-industrial-technology company must track three categories of metrics: (1) Financial KPIs — is the business economically viable and getting stronger? (2) Operational KPIs — are we building and delivering what we promised? (3) Strategic KPIs — are we capturing the market position that generates long-term value? Each category has 5-7 leading and lagging indicators, tracked at appropriate cadences.

**Williams Heuristic:** If you can't measure it, you can't manage it. If you measure everything, you manage nothing. The KPIs below are the vital few — the metrics that, if they're green, mean the company is on track, and if they're red, mean something fundamental is wrong. Everything else is noise.

### KPI 1.1: Revenue Growth Rate

**Definition:** (Current Period Revenue / Prior Year Period Revenue) - 1

**Target:** >100% YoY through Year 5. Any quarter below 50% sequential growth (QoQ annualized) triggers a growth review.

**Why It Matters:** Revenue growth is the primary validator of product-market fit. In Years 1-3, growth >200% demonstrates that the market is adopting EMP hardening at scale. A deceleration below 100% before Year 4 would indicate that the addressable market is smaller than modeled, regulatory catalysts aren't materializing, or competitors are taking share.

**Reporting Cadence:** Monthly. Board deck includes trailing 3-month and trailing 12-month growth rates.

### KPI 1.2: Gross Margin

**Definition:** (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue

**Target by Product Line:**
| Line | Y1 Floor | Y3 Target | Y5 Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| ACS | 10% | 33% | 56% |
| AHM | 28% | 45% | 60% |
| CCF | 3% | 15% | 18% |
| PMR | 28% | 45% | 58% |

**Why It Matters:** Gross margin measures manufacturing efficiency and pricing power. If gross margin is below target, either: (a) manufacturing costs are higher than modeled (learning curve isn't materializing), or (b) pricing is weaker than expected (competitive pressure or poor negotiation). Management must diagnose which and respond.

**Corrective Action:** Margin below 80% of target for 2 consecutive quarters → manufacturing cost review or pricing strategy adjustment.

### KPI 1.3: Cash Runway

**Definition:** (Cash Balance + Available Credit Lines) / (Average Monthly Net Cash Burn)

**Target:** Never below 6 months. Yellow flag at 9 months. Red flag at 6 months (fundraising must begin immediately).

**Why It Matters:** Running out of cash is the #1 cause of startup death. This is the single most important financial metric in Years 1-3. After Series B (Year 3), cash becomes abundant and this metric transitions to a monitoring role rather than a survival metric.

### KPI 1.4: Burn Multiple

**Definition:** (Beginning Cash - Ending Cash) / (Ending Revenue Run-Rate - Beginning Revenue Run-Rate)

**Target (post-breakeven):** <1.0x (meaning each dollar of cash consumed generates >$1 of incremental annualized revenue). <0.5x is excellent.

**Interpretation:**
- Burn multiple >2.0x: inefficient growth — spending too much for each dollar of incremental revenue. Operational review required.
- Burn multiple 1.0-2.0x: acceptable for high-growth industrial company.
- Burn multiple <1.0x: capital-efficient growth. Company can self-fund an increasing share of growth.
- Burn multiple <0.0x: company is generating cash, not burning it. (Achieved post-breakeven.)

### KPI 2.1: Manufacturing Yield

**Definition:** (Units passing final QC / Units started in production) × 100. Measured per production line.

**Target:** >90% by end of Year 1 for ACS (semi-automated line). >95% by Year 3 (fully automated). >97% by Year 5.

**Why It Matters:** Yield directly impacts COGS. A 90% yield means 10% of material and labor is wasted. At 10,000 units/year, a 1% yield improvement saves ~$300K in materials and avoids rework costs. Yield is also a proxy for manufacturing process maturity — high yield means the process is dialed in.

### KPI 2.2: On-Time Delivery Rate

**Definition:** (Orders delivered by committed date / Total orders) × 100.

**Target:** >90% by Year 2; >95% by Year 4.

**Why It Matters:** Government and utility customers have low tolerance for late delivery. A pattern of late deliveries damages credibility with procurement officers, reduces reorder rates, and can trigger contract penalties. This is particularly critical for Charlemagne vessels, where late delivery of a $7M vessel damages the relationship with the entire service branch.

### KPI 2.3: Customer Concentration (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index approach)

**Definition:** Sum of squared revenue shares of top 10 customers. Scored 0-10,000 (10,000 = single customer).

**Target:** <2,000 by Year 3; <1,000 by Year 5.

**Why It Matters:** Customer concentration is the silent killer of defense contractors. Losing one big contract shouldn't threaten the company. This KPI ensures revenue diversification is happening on schedule.

### KPI 3.1: Government Contract Win Rate

**Definition:** (Government contracts won / Government contracts bid) × 100.

**Target:** >40% win rate by Year 2 (as incumbency and references build). >60% by Year 4 (as CSM becomes the default choice for EMP hardening).

**Why It Matters:** Government BD is expensive ($85K+ per bid in Year 1). A low win rate means the BD team is chasing the wrong opportunities or the value proposition isn't resonating. A high win rate means the company is bidding selectively and winning consistently — the most capital-efficient government BD approach.

### KPI 3.2: Insurance Carrier Partnership Velocity

**Definition:** New insurance carrier partnerships signed per quarter. Also: policies-in-force growth rate.

**Target:** 1-2 new carriers per quarter in Year 2-3; 3-4 per quarter in Year 4-5.

**Why It Matters:** Insurance partnerships are the highest-margin revenue stream (85% gross margin) and the most scalable distribution channel for Aegis Home. Each carrier represents a potential 5,000-50,000 policies over 5 years. Partnership velocity directly predicts IPP and AHM-insurance-channel revenue 12-18 months forward.

### KPI 3.3: International Footprint

**Definition:** Number of countries with either: (a) an active customer, (b) a certified installer, or (c) a signed distribution agreement.

**Target:** 3 countries by Year 2 (US, UK, Japan); 10 by Year 3 (add Australia, South Korea, Germany, France, Canada, Norway, Singapore); 50 by Year 5.

**Why It Matters:** The 200-country diplomatic groundwork is a strategic asset that must convert to commercial operations. This KPI measures conversion velocity. A country counts as "active" when revenue is generated, not when an LOI is signed.

### KPI 3.4: Intellectual Property Velocity

**Definition:** (New patent applications filed per quarter) + (New research documents added to corpus per month).

**Target:** 3-5 patent applications per quarter; 100+ new research documents per month.

**Why It Matters:** The research corpus is CSM's primary competitive moat. If the rate of new document creation slows, the moat is filling in. This KPI ensures R&D investment is producing durable, defensible output, not just consuming budget.

## SECTION 5: Board Deck Template Outline

Every quarterly board meeting, the CEO presents a standardized deck. The outline below ensures consistency and comparability across quarters.

### Board Deck Structure (90 minutes):

1. **CEO Letter (5 min)** — 1-page narrative: what went well, what didn't, what keeps me up at night.
2. **KPI Dashboard (10 min)** — Traffic-light summary of all KPIs vs. plan. Green/yellow/red for each. Focus discussion on reds.
3. **Financial Review (20 min)** — CFO presents: P&L vs. budget, cash flow, balance sheet, revenue by product line, margin bridge.
4. **Product & Manufacturing Update (15 min)** — COO presents: production volumes, yields, capacity expansion, new product development milestones.
5. **Commercial Update (15 min)** — CRO presents: pipeline, wins/losses, customer count, channel development, competitive intelligence.
6. **Strategic Initiatives (10 min)** — CEO presents: regulatory progress, international expansion, insurance partnerships, M&A landscape.
7. **People & Culture (5 min)** — CPO/CEO: headcount vs. plan, key hires, attrition, engagement survey results.
8. **Risks & Mitigations (5 min)** — CRO/CEO: top 5 risks, probability and impact, mitigation status.
9. **Forward Look (5 min)** — CEO: next quarter's top 3 priorities, any decisions needed from board.
10. **Closed Session (30 min)** — Board only (no management).

## SECTION 8: KPI Maturation Over Time

The metrics that matter evolve as the company matures. The table below shows which KPIs are primary at each stage.

### TABLE 10.5: KPI Priority by Company Stage

| Stage | Years | Primary KPIs | Secondary KPIs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **R&D / Pre-Revenue** | Pre-A | Research documents, patents filed, prototype performance, team quality | Cash burn, partnership LOIs |
| **Early Commercial** | Y1-Y2 | Revenue growth, cash runway, customer count, first deliveries | Gross margin, yield, win rate |
| **Scaling** | Y3-Y4 | Revenue, gross margin, EBITDA, unit volume, on-time delivery | NPS, international footprint, insurance policies |
| **Mature Growth** | Y5+ | EBITDA margin, FCF, ROIC, market share, revenue/employee | Patent velocity, brand awareness, employee engagement |

**El Segundo Calm:** The KPIs that matter in Year 1 (survival) are not the KPIs that matter in Year 5 (excellence). A company that's still obsessing over cash runway in Year 5 has failed to build a sustainable business. A company that's obsessing over NPS in Year 1 is optimizing the wrong thing (you don't have enough customers for NPS to be statistically meaningful). Match the metric to the maturity. This is the KPI equivalent of "right tool for the right job."

## SECTION 9: Red Flag Triggers

Certain KPI thresholds automatically trigger executive review (not panic, but structured diagnosis):

| Trigger | Condition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Cash < 6 months runway | Cash / Avg. Monthly Burn < 6 | CEO calls emergency board meeting within 48 hours |
| Revenue < 70% of quarterly plan | Any quarter | CFO + CRO joint diagnostic within 1 week; board notified |
| Gross margin < 80% of quarterly plan for 2 consecutive quarters | Any product line | COO + CFO manufacturing cost review; pricing strategy review |
| Key customer loss | >$1M annual revenue customer lost | CEO + CRO post-mortem within 2 weeks; board notified |
| Manufacturing yield < 85% for 1 month | Any production line | COO stops line for root cause analysis within 24 hours |
| Patent application velocity < 2/quarter for 2 quarters | After Year 2 | CTO + CEO IP strategy review; assess R&D productivity |
| Employee attrition > 15% annualized | Any quarter | CPO + CEO culture and comp review; stay interviews with key personnel |
