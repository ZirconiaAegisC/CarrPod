THE QUICK PITCH

A permanent magnet is frozen physics — a crystalline arrangement that holds a magnetic field without current. During a geomagnetic storm, the external field superimposes on that frozen field, shifting the operating point of every motor, generator, and actuator that depends on magnetic precision. CSMMag contains two studies: CSMMagEco2026, identifying new U.S. magnet companies and mapping supply chain vulnerabilities, and CSMMagTech 2026, characterizing rare-earth-free magnet materials — ferrites, alnicos, MnBi alloys — that maintain coercivity stability during geomagnetic disturbances. The Charlemagne fleet's walking leg actuators use samarium-cobalt magnets specified from this research. Step into the magnetics lab.

THE CONVERSATION

CSMMag extends that history to include geomagnetic storm resilience. Mork, the trade-off.

MORK: NdFeB magnets: energy product 35 to 50 MGOe, vulnerable to demagnetization during storms. Ferrite: 3 to 5 MGOe, immune. The leg actuator motors on the Charlemagne fleet use samarium-cobalt — an eight percent cost premium for guaranteed storm survival. The MnBi research: energy product 8 to 12 MGOe, no rare earths, promising frontier. And the supply chain study identifies twelve new U.S. magnet manufacturers establishing domestic production capacity.

CHESTER: The domestic supply chain is small — five to eight thousand metric tons projected by 2030 — but it is growing. During a Carrington event, a small domestic supply chain that works is worth more than a large foreign supply chain that does not. Right on.
