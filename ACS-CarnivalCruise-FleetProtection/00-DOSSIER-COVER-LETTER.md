## Subject: Fleet Hull Excellence Program — Transforming Hull Performance Across 94 Ships

Dear Mr. Weinstein, Mr. Bernstein, Mr. Ljoen, and Carnival Maritime Colleagues,

Carnival Corporation is already the cruise industry's recognized leader in advanced hull coating deployment. The 16 AQUATERRAS-equipped ships in the Carnival Cruise Line fleet have demonstrated consistently what the research literature confirms: advanced silicone foul-release coatings deliver an 8–10% reduction in hull-related fuel consumption, maintain performance through extended stationary periods, and last twice as long as conventional antifouling systems.

What made sense as a pilot program for 17% of the fleet now demands application as a corporation-wide standard for all 94 ships.

The arithmetic is unambiguous. At $1.8 billion in annual fuel expenditure, every percentage point of improvement across the fleet saves $18 million per year. An 8% fleet-wide improvement — the demonstrated average from AQUATERRAS-coated vessels benchmarked against their SPC-coated sister ships — represents $144 million in annual savings. This single operational change generates more net financial benefit than the entire incremental cost of fleet-wide coating deployment within the program's first full year of implementation.

The regulatory environment adds urgency. CII ratings took effect in 2023, with A-rated ships conferring commercial advantage and D- and E-rated ships triggering mandatory corrective action plans. EU ETS inclusion of maritime transport from 2024 means that every tonne of fuel burned on a voyage touching an EU port now carries a carbon cost in addition to the fuel cost. FuelEU Maritime's tightening GHG intensity targets from 2025 through 2050 make hull efficiency a compliance necessity, not merely a cost optimization.

Carnival's competitive advantage is its head start. While competitors are evaluating coating technologies that Carnival has operated successfully for seven years, the corporation already possesses the operational data, supplier relationships, yard partnerships, and technical expertise to execute fleet-wide deployment far more rapidly and cost-effectively than any competitor. However, this advantage erodes with each passing drydock cycle in which a Carnival ship leaves the yard with conventional antifouling.

The enclosed dossier provides the complete technical, financial, and operational framework for transforming this demonstrated capability into a systematic, corporation-wide program. It includes:

- **A detailed implementation framework** across five phases through 2030 and beyond
- **A comprehensive fleet prioritization matrix** covering every vessel, every brand, every route
- **A background research memorandum** synthesizing over 70 industry and technical sources
- **Full financial modeling** including program costs, projected savings, and NPV analysis
- **Complete references and glossary** for technical clarity and auditability

We respectfully request your review of this dossier and a decision on program authorization at the earliest opportunity, with the goal of initiating Phase 0 (Fleet Audit and Baseline) before the end of Q3 2026.
