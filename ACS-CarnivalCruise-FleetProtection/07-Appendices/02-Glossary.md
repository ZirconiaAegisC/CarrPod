# Glossary of Maritime and Hull Protection Terminology

**Document Number:** ACS-CCL-FP-GLO-001
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 15 July 2026
**Classification:** Confidential — Carnival Corporation Internal
**Author:** Advanced Coatings Strategy Group

---

## A

**AFS Convention:** International Convention on the Control of Harmful Anti-Fouling Systems on Ships (IMO, 2001). Bans organotin compounds (TBT) in antifouling coatings and establishes a mechanism for future restrictions on harmful biocides.

**AHR (Average Hull Roughness):** The mean peak-to-valley height of a hull surface, measured in microns (µm). Lower AHR correlates with reduced frictional resistance. New silicone foul-release coatings typically achieve 40–65 µm; aged SPC coatings can exceed 250 µm.

**ALBD (Average Lower Berth Density):** A cruise industry metric measuring capacity utilization. While not directly a hull term, it appears in revenue projections for drydock scheduling and opportunity cost calculations.

**Antifouling:** Any coating, device, or method designed to prevent the attachment and growth of marine organisms on submerged surfaces. Categories include biocide-based, biocide-free foul-release, and ultrasonic/electrolytic systems.

**AQUATERRAS:** Nippon Paint Marine's proprietary silicone-hydrogel foul-release hull coating, featuring a water-trapping hydrogel layer for reduced skin friction and enhanced fouling resistance.

---

## B

**BHD (Bare Hull Drag):** The hydrodynamic resistance of a hull independent of appendages, propeller, and sea state. Hull coating selection directly influences BHD.

**Biocide:** A chemical substance that kills or inhibits the growth of living organisms. In marine coatings, cuprous oxide (Cu₂O) is the most common biocide. Booster biocides include zinc pyrithione, copper pyrithione, Sea-Nine 211 (DCOIT), and Irgarol 1051.

**Biofouling:** The accumulation of microorganisms, algae, plants, and animals on submerged surfaces. Categories: microfouling (slime, bacteria) and macrofouling (barnacles, tubeworms, mussels, algae).

**Boottop:** The area of the hull between the light and load waterlines, subject to alternating wetting and drying cycles. Requires specialized coating products resistant to UV exposure, abrasion from fenders, and intermittent immersion.

---

## C

**CII (Carbon Intensity Indicator):** An IMO-mandated rating system (A through E) that measures a vessel's CO₂ emissions per transport work (grams CO₂ per deadweight-nautical mile for cargo; grams CO₂ per gross ton-nautical mile for passenger ships). Hull condition directly affects CII rating.

**Class (Classification Society):** Organizations that establish and maintain technical standards for ship construction and operation. Relevant societies for Carnival Corporation: Lloyd's Register (LR), DNV, RINA, Bureau Veritas (BV).

**CMP (Chugoku Marine Paints):** Japanese marine coatings manufacturer, producer of SEAFLO NEO CF and other advanced antifouling products.

**Cu₂O (Cuprous Oxide):** The primary biocide in conventional antifouling paints. Copper ions released at the coating-water interface inhibit fouling organism settlement.

---

## D

**DFT (Dry Film Thickness):** The thickness of a cured coating layer, measured in microns (µm). Specified in coating product data sheets. Typical total DFT for an advanced foul-release system: 250–350 µm (primer + tie-coat + topcoat).

**DNV (Det Norske Veritas):** Norwegian-based international classification society. Certifies coating systems and conducts independent performance verification of hull coatings. DNV-RP-B101 covers corrosion protection and coating selection.

**Drydock:** A facility where a ship is removed from the water for inspection, maintenance, and repair. Cruise ships undergo mandatory drydockings every 5 years (twice every 5 years under the 2.5/5-year inspection cycle).

---

## E

**EEDI (Energy Efficiency Design Index):** IMO-mandated design-phase metric for new ships, measuring CO₂ emissions per transport work. Advanced coatings contribute to EEDI compliance.

**EEXI (Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index):** IMO-mandated metric for existing ships (in force 2023), measuring design energy efficiency. Similar to EEDI but applied retrospectively.

**EPD (Environmental Product Declaration):** A standardized document providing quantified environmental data for a product. Increasingly required by cruise operators for coating product selection.

**ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance):** A corporate reporting framework. Hull coating selection affects ESG scores through fuel consumption reduction (Environmental), crew and passenger health via reduced biocide exposure (Social), and transparent procurement and performance reporting (Governance).

**EU ETS (European Union Emissions Trading System):** Cap-and-trade system extended to maritime transport from 2024. Cruise operators must purchase EU Allowances (EUAs) to cover CO₂ emissions on voyages touching EU ports. Hull efficiency improvements directly reduce EUA costs.

---

## F

**Foul-Release Coating:** A biocide-free coating that prevents fouling through a low-surface-energy, non-stick surface rather than killing organisms. Organisms that attach are released by hydrodynamic shear when the vessel reaches sufficient speed (typically 12–15 knots).

**FRC (Foul-Release Coating):** See Foul-Release Coating.

**FuelEU Maritime:** European Union regulation (in force 2025) setting well-to-wake GHG intensity reduction targets for maritime fuel. Hull efficiency contributes to compliance by reducing total fuel consumption.

---

## G

**GIT Coatings:** Graphite Innovation & Technologies Inc., a Canadian company commercializing graphene-enhanced foul-release hull coatings. Based in Dartmouth, Nova Scotia.

**Graphene Coating:** A marine coating containing graphene platelets (single-layer carbon atoms in a hexagonal lattice) to improve mechanical durability, reduce skin friction, and enhance foul-release properties.

---

## H

**HCI (Hull Condition Index):** A standardized scoring system used internally by Carnival Corporation to rate hull condition. Scale 1.0 (pristine) to 5.0 (critical). Components include: fouling severity, coating degradation, roughness measurements, and corrosion indicators.

**Hempaguard:** Hempel A/S brand name for its silicone-hydrogel foul-release coating product line. Hempaguard X7 (2013) and Hempaguard Max (2019) are the primary cruise-ship-relevant products.

**Hempel:** Danish-headquartered global coatings manufacturer, founded 1915. One of the world's largest marine coatings suppliers, with over 4,000 vessels using Hempaguard products.

**HullPIC:** Annual Hull Performance and Insight Conference, the premier industry conference for hull performance monitoring, coating performance evaluation, and biofouling management. Typically held in March.

**Hydrogel Coating:** A coating that incorporates a water-trapping layer at the surface, creating a micro-scale water boundary layer between the hull surface and seawater. Key technology in AQUATERRAS and Hempaguard products.

---

## I

**ICCP (Impressed Current Cathodic Protection):** An active corrosion protection system using a low-voltage DC current to prevent galvanic corrosion of the hull. Coatings and ICCP systems work in combination — a higher-quality coating reduces the ICCP current demand.

**IMO (International Maritime Organization):** The United Nations specialized agency responsible for international shipping safety and environmental regulation. Based in London.

**IMO Biofouling Guidelines:** IMO guidelines for controlling and managing ships' biofouling to minimize invasive species transfer. Original version 2011; revised 2023 (MEPC.378(80)).

**ISO 19030:** International standard for measurement of changes in hull and propeller performance. Parts 1–3 provide the framework for Carnival's hull performance monitoring methodology.

---

## K

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator):** A quantifiable performance measure used to evaluate success. In the context of the Fleet Hull Excellence Program, KPIs include fuel savings percentage, HCI score, and coating application compliance rate.

---

## L

**LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas):** A marine fuel used by several Carnival Corporation ships (Mardi Gras, Celebration, Jubilee, AIDAnova, Costa Smeralda, Costa Toscana). LNG-fueled ships have specific hull performance characteristics due to fuel density and engine configuration differences from conventional HFO/MGO vessels.

**LR (Lloyd's Register):** British-based international classification society. Major certifier of marine coatings, including GIT Coatings' graphene product (2023).

---

## M

**MEPC (Marine Environment Protection Committee):** The IMO committee responsible for environmental regulations, including the AFS Convention, GHG strategy, CII, and biofouling guidelines.

**MCR (Maximum Continuous Rating):** The maximum power output a marine engine can sustain continuously. Hull resistance is measured as a percentage of MCR required to achieve a given speed.

**MGO (Marine Gas Oil):** Low-sulfur distillate fuel used in Emission Control Areas (ECAs) and by ships equipped with exhaust gas cleaning systems (scrubbers) when operating outside ECAs.

---

## N

**NACE (National Association of Corrosion Engineers):** US-based professional organization for corrosion control. Now known as AMPP (Association for Materials Protection and Performance). NACE/SSPC joint surface preparation standards define blast cleaning levels for coating application.

**NPV (Net Present Value):** A financial metric used to evaluate the profitability of an investment by discounting future cash flows to present value. Applied in this dossier to calculate the return on hull coating investment.

---

## P

**PCD (Passenger Cruise Day):** A cruise industry metric for passenger capacity multiplied by days in operation. Used for revenue and cost allocation modeling.

**PBCF (Propeller Boss Cap Fins):** An energy-saving device attached to the propeller hub that reduces hub vortex drag. Often combined with advanced hull coatings for additive efficiency improvement.

**PPG:** US-headquartered global coatings manufacturer (Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania). Producer of Sigmaglide 2390 foul-release coating and the electrostatic application system deployed at Fincantieri shipyards.

---

## R

**ROI (Return on Investment):** A financial metric measuring the gain or loss generated on an investment relative to its cost. The Fleet Hull Excellence Program targets an ROI exceeding 400% over the 5-year program period.

**ROV (Remotely Operated Vehicle):** An underwater drone equipped with cameras and sensors, used for hull inspection without requiring divers. Increasingly replacing diver-based hull surveys for routine condition monitoring.

---

## S

**SEEMP (Ship Energy Efficiency Management Plan):** An IMO-mandated document for each vessel, describing the ship's energy efficiency management approach. Part III requires CII reporting.

**Sigmaglide:** PPG's silicone foul-release coating product line. Sigmaglide 2390 is the formulation most relevant to cruise ship application. Distinctive for compatibility with PPG's electrostatic application system.

**SPC (Self-Polishing Copolymer):** An antifouling coating that erodes at a controlled rate through the reaction of the copolymer binder with seawater, exposing fresh biocide at the surface. The dominant antifouling technology in the post-TBT era.

**SSPC (Society for Protective Coatings):** US-based professional organization focused on protective coatings. SSPC-SP standards define surface preparation requirements (e.g., SSPC-SP 10: Near-White Metal Blast Cleaning).

---

## T

**TBT (Tributyltin):** An organotin compound used as a biocide in marine antifouling paints from the 1970s until its global ban under the IMO AFS Convention (effective 2008). Caused severe environmental damage, including imposex in marine gastropods.

**TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit):** A container shipping capacity measure. Not directly a cruise term but appears in coating manufacturer reference data comparing fuel savings across vessel types.

---

## U

**Ultrasonic Antifouling:** A fouling prevention method using high-frequency sound waves generated by transducers mounted inside the hull to create a vibrating surface that discourages organism settlement. Used primarily on sea chests, box coolers, and strainer plates.

---

## V

**VOC (Volatile Organic Compound):** Organic chemicals that evaporate from coatings during application and curing. Regulatory limits on VOC content in marine coatings are tightening, favoring water-borne and high-solids coating formulations.

---

## W

**WFT (Wet Film Thickness):** The thickness of a coating at the time of application, before solvent evaporation or curing. WFT × Solids Volume Fraction = DFT.

**Wet-Dry Cycle Zone:** The area between light ballast and loaded conditions, also referred to as the boottop. Requires coatings with enhanced UV and mechanical durability.
