# Supply Chain & Operations — Electric Hydrogen

## Supply Chain Strategy
Electric Hydrogen's supply chain is designed to support the Devens gigafactory's
1.2 GW/year production target while managing critical material risks inherent to
PEM electrolyzer manufacturing.

## Critical Materials

### Iridium
Iridium is the single most supply-constrained material in the PEM electrolyzer
supply chain. Annual global production is approximately 7-8 tonnes, primarily as
a byproduct of platinum mining in South Africa. A single 100MW electrolyzer system
using traditional iridium loading (~1.5 mg/cm²) would consume roughly 400 kg —
nearly 5% of annual global production.

EH2's mitigation strategies:
1. **Low-loading MEAs**: Proprietary catalyst designs targeting <0.3 mg/cm² iridium
   loading, reducing per-system demand by ~80%.
2. **Recycling program**: Closed-loop recovery of iridium from end-of-life stacks
   and manufacturing scrap.
3. **Long-term supply agreements**: Contracts with major PGM (platinum group metals)
   producers to secure allocation.
4. **Thrifting technology**: Ongoing R&D to further reduce or potentially eliminate
   iridium through advanced catalyst architectures.

### Platinum
Platinum is less constrained than iridium but still requires active management.
Global production of ~180 tonnes/year provides more headroom, but demand from
automotive catalysts and fuel cells creates competition.

### Titanium
Titanium is used extensively for porous transport layers (PTLs) and bipolar plates
on the anode side due to its corrosion resistance in the oxidizing environment.
While titanium is abundant, processing into the specialized forms required for
electrolyzers (sintered powder PTLs, machined bipolar plates with flow fields)
represents a manufacturing bottleneck.

### PFSA Membranes
Perfluorosulfonic acid membranes (primarily Nafion™ from Chemours) are a
proprietary product with limited global manufacturing capacity. EH2's strategy
includes:
1. Multi-supplier qualification to avoid single-source dependency
2. Thin membrane development to reduce material consumption per MW
3. Potential backward integration into membrane production at sufficient scale

## Manufacturing Supply Chain

### Tier 1: In-House (Devens Gigafactory)
- Catalyst synthesis (iridium, platinum)
- MEA fabrication (coating, lamination)
- Stack assembly
- System integration and testing

### Tier 2: Strategic Suppliers (Long-Term Agreements)
- PFSA membranes
- Titanium PTLs and bipolar plates
- Power electronics components (IGBT modules, transformers)
- Specialty stainless steels (cathode side)
- Gaskets and seals (FFKM, EPDM)

### Tier 3: Commodity Suppliers
- Structural steel
- Piping and fittings
- Instrumentation and controls
- Electrical switchgear
- Standard fasteners and hardware

## Logistics and Deployment

### Factory Output Logistics
Finished 100MW systems consist of multiple containerized modules shipped via
standard intermodal logistics (truck/rail). The Devens facility has rail access,
enabling cost-effective transport to project sites across North America.

### Site Deployment Process
1. Site preparation (foundations, utilities) — concurrent with manufacturing
2. Module delivery and placement (2-4 weeks per 100MW block)
3. Interconnection (electrical, water, gas offtake) — 4-8 weeks
4. Commissioning and ramp-up — 4-8 weeks
5. Performance testing and acceptance — 2-4 weeks

Total site deployment: ~3-6 months per 100MW block, vs. 12-18 months for
traditional custom-engineered electrolyzer installations.

## Quality Management
- ISO 9001 certification targeted for Devens facility
- Statistical process control (SPC) at all critical manufacturing steps
- 100% electrochemical testing of every stack before system integration
- Traceability from raw material lot to finished system
- Field performance data fed back into manufacturing quality systems

## Operational Support

### Service Model
- Remote monitoring and diagnostics for all deployed systems
- Predictive maintenance algorithms using operating data analytics
- Regional service hubs for rapid response
- Spare parts inventory management optimized across the fleet

### Performance Guarantees
- Stack degradation rate guarantees (<3 µV/hr average over warranty period)
- System availability guarantees (>97% uptime excluding planned maintenance)
- Efficiency degradation curves with financial remedies for underperformance

## Risk Management

### Supply Chain Risks
- **Iridium price volatility**: Hedged through long-term contracts and thrifting
- **Single-source components**: Active qualification of alternative suppliers
- **Geopolitical disruption**: Domestic supply chain prioritized where feasible
- **Logistics bottlenecks**: Multiple transport modes qualified

### Operational Risks
- **Technology risk**: Extensive accelerated life testing before field deployment
- **Quality escapes**: Multi-stage testing catches defects at lowest-cost point
- **Workforce risk**: Cross-training and documented standard operating procedures
