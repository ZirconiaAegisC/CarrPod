# DOE Hydrogen Shot — $1/kg Green Hydrogen by 2031

## Overview
The U.S. Department of Energy's "Hydrogen Shot" is the first of the DOE's Energy
Earthshots™ initiatives, launched in June 2021. Its goal is to reduce the cost
of clean hydrogen to $1 per kilogram within one decade ("1 1 1"), representing
an approximately 80% reduction from the 2021 baseline cost of ~$5/kg for green
hydrogen from electrolysis.

## Energy Earthshots Framework
The Hydrogen Shot is part of a broader DOE initiative modeled on the
successful SunShot program, which drove the 75%+ cost reduction in solar PV
over the past decade:

| Earthshot           | Target                          | Deadline |
|---------------------|---------------------------------|---------|
| Hydrogen Shot       | $1/kg clean hydrogen            | 2031    |
| Long Duration Storage Shot | $0.05/kWh LCOS for 10+ hours | 2030    |
| Carbon Negative Shot| Gigaton-scale CO₂ removal at <$100/tonne | 2032    |
| Enhanced Geothermal Shot | $45/MWh enhanced geothermal | 2035    |
| Floating Offshore Wind Shot | $45/MWh floating offshore wind | 2035    |
| Industrial Heat Shot | 85% GHG reduction for industrial heat | 2035    |
| Clean Fuels & Products Shot | Cost-competitive clean fuels | 2035    |

## $1/kg Cost Target Breakdown
Achieving $1/kg clean hydrogen requires simultaneous improvements across the
entire value chain:

### Electrolyzer Cost Reduction
| Component               | 2021 Baseline | 2031 Target | Reduction |
|-------------------------|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Stack Cost              | $400-500/kW   | $80-100/kW  | 75-80%    |
| Balance of Plant        | $300-400/kW   | $120-160/kW | 55-60%    |
| Total Installed System  | $1,000-1,400/kW | $250-350/kW | 70-75%   |
| Stack Efficiency        | 55-60 kWh/kg  | 48-52 kWh/kg | 10-15% improvement |

### Renewable Electricity Cost
| Source               | 2021 LCOE ($/MWh) | 2031 Target ($/MWh) |
|----------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Utility Solar PV     | $25-35             | $15-20              |
| Onshore Wind         | $25-40             | $15-25              |

At 50 kWh/kg system efficiency, $20/MWh power translates to $1.00/kg — already
achieving the $1/kg target for power cost alone. Additional cost reductions from
electrolyzer capex, efficiency improvements, and system integration are additive.

## DOE's Strategic Approach
The Hydrogen Shot strategy includes multiple coordinated programs:

### Research, Development & Demonstration (RD&D)
- **H2@Scale**: DOE-wide initiative coordinating R&D across the hydrogen value
  chain from production through end use.
- **Hydrogen and Fuel Cell Technologies Office (HFTO)**: Primary funding
  vehicle for electrolyzer, fuel cell, storage, and infrastructure R&D.
- **National Labs**: NREL, INL, LANL, and others performing foundational
  research on catalysts, membranes, manufacturing processes, and systems
  integration.

### Manufacturing & Supply Chain
- **Advanced Manufacturing Office**: Support for domestic electrolyzer and
  fuel cell manufacturing scale-up.
- **Critical Materials Institute**: Research on reducing or replacing
  critical materials (iridium, platinum) in electrolyzers and fuel cells.

### Deployment & Market Creation
- **Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs (H2Hubs)**: $7 billion allocated under the
  Bipartisan Infrastructure Law for 7 regional hydrogen hubs that co-locate
  production, distribution, and end use to demonstrate commercial viability.

- **IRA Section 45V**: $3/kg production tax credit for clean hydrogen,
  creating a 10-year deployment incentive window (through 2033 for projects
  beginning construction by 2033).

- **DOE Loan Programs Office**: Title 17 Clean Energy Financing provides
  loan guarantees for innovative clean energy projects, including hydrogen.

## Relevance to Electric Hydrogen
Electric Hydrogen is directly aligned with the Hydrogen Shot objectives:

1. **Electrolyzer cost reduction**: The Devens gigafactory manufacturing
   approach directly targets the capex reduction pathway through economies
   of scale, automation, and standardization.

2. **Technology efficiency**: EH2's PEM technology targets the 50-55 kWh/kg
   system efficiency range, contributing to the efficiency pathway.

3. **Domestic manufacturing**: The Devens facility qualifies for IRA
   manufacturing incentives and supports DOE's goal of building a domestic
   clean energy supply chain.

4. **Project deployment**: The Texas 300MW facility with AES contributes to
   the commercial-scale demonstration targets of the Hydrogen Shot.
