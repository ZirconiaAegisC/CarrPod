# Financial Strategy & Fundraising — Electric Hydrogen

## Funding History
Electric Hydrogen has raised over $800 million across multiple funding rounds,
making it one of the most capitalized private companies in the clean hydrogen
sector.

| Round       | Date          | Amount       | Lead / Key Investors                                         |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Seed/Early  | 2020-2021     | ~$50M        | Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Prelude Ventures, Energy Impact Partners |
| Series B    | June 2022     | $198M        | Fifth Wall Climate, S2G Ventures, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries |
| Series C    | October 2023  | $380M        | BP, United Airlines, Microsoft, Temasek, Fortescue, Trinity Capital |
| Additional  | 2024-2025    | ~$200M+      | Various (debt + equity)                                      |
| **Total**   |               | **~$800M+**  |                                                             |

## Investor Profile and Strategic Rationale

### Strategic Corporates
The Series C round was notable for the concentration of strategic corporate
investors rather than traditional venture capital:

- **BP**: Access to technology for green hydrogen ambitions; supply chain
  security for electrolyzer procurement.
- **United Airlines**: Interest in sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) produced
  from green hydrogen; decarbonization pathway for aviation.
- **Microsoft**: Data center backup power and broader decarbonization
  commitment; Climate Innovation Fund.
- **Fortescue (Fortescue Future Industries)**: Iron ore mining giant
  pursuing green hydrogen for green iron/steel production (H₂-DRI).
- **Temasek**: Singapore sovereign wealth fund; long-term infrastructure
  and energy transition thesis.
- **Trinity Capital**: Equipment financing specialist; potential for
  project finance and equipment leasing structures.

### Financial Investors
- **Breakthrough Energy Ventures (BEV)**: Bill Gates-founded climate tech
  fund; patient capital with 20+ year investment horizons.
- **Prelude Ventures**: Early-stage climate tech investor.
- **Energy Impact Partners**: Utility-backed (Southern Company, National
  Grid, etc.) climate fund with strategic utility relationships.
- **Fifth Wall**: Real estate and infrastructure technology focus; CO2
  impact fund.

## Use of Funds
The $800M+ raised to date has been allocated across:

1. **Devens Gigafactory**: Capital expenditure for factory buildout,
   equipment, and manufacturing ramp (~40-50% of total funding).

2. **Technology Development**: Ongoing R&D for MEA improvement, system
   optimization, and next-generation electrolyzer technology (~15-20%).

3. **Project Execution**: Working capital and performance guarantees for
   initial commercial projects (Texas 300MW with AES) (~20-25%).

4. **Corporate Operations**: Headcount growth, facilities (Natick MA,
   San Jose CA), business development (~10-15%).

## Valuation and Capital Efficiency
- **Post-Series C Valuation**: $1 billion+ (unicorn status)
- **Capital Efficiency**: ~$800M raised to reach first commercial delivery
  and manufacturing at 1.2 GW/year — relatively capital-efficient for
  a hardware/manufacturing company targeting utility-scale energy
  infrastructure.

Key benchmark: Tesla raised approximately $2 billion in equity before
achieving positive operating cash flow with the Model S. Hardware
manufacturing businesses are capital-intensive by nature.

## Path to Profitability

### Revenue Model
Electric Hydrogen's revenue model includes:
1. **Equipment sales**: Electrolyzer systems sold to project developers
   and owners (primary near-term revenue).
2. **Service and maintenance**: Long-term service agreements (LTSAs) for
   deployed systems, providing recurring revenue at 10-15% of equipment
   price annually.
3. **Technology licensing**: Potential future licensing of MEA and stack
   technology to international partners (e.g., for non-US markets where
   IRA advantages don't apply).
4. **Project development participation**: Potential equity participation
   in projects using EH2 technology, capturing development margins and
   operational cash flows.

### Unit Economics
| Metric                  | Early Stage (2025-28) | Mature (2028+)      |
|-------------------------|-----------------------|---------------------|
| System Price ($/kW)     | $700-900              | $450-600            |
| Gross Margin            | 15-25%                | 30-40%              |
| Annual Revenue per GW   | $700-900M             | $450-600M           |
| Gross Profit per GW     | $100-225M             | $135-240M           |

At the Devens factory's 1.2 GW/year capacity, mature-state revenue of
$540-720M with gross profit of $160-290M creates a path to significant
profitability once manufacturing scales and cost targets are achieved.

### Break-Even Timeline
Based on manufacturing ramp, project delivery schedules, and market
development:
- **Revenue generation begins**: 2025-2026 (Texas project delivery)
- **Positive gross margin**: 2026-2027 (as manufacturing yield improves)
- **Operating cash flow positive**: ~2028-2030 (dependent on order book
  and manufacturing scale)

## Future Capital Needs
With gigafactory capex largely funded, future capital requirements will
be driven by:

1. **Project performance guarantees**: As project pipeline grows, EH2 may
   need to post guarantees or provide vendor financing — capital-efficient
   structures (e.g., insurance wraps, bank guarantees) are preferred.

2. **Manufacturing expansion**: If demand exceeds Devens' 1.2 GW capacity,
   a second manufacturing facility (or Devens expansion) would require
   additional capital.

3. **International expansion**: Non-US manufacturing (e.g., Europe, Asia)
   may be funded through local partnerships or licensing rather than direct
   capex.

4. **Working capital**: As order book grows, working capital for inventory
   and receivables becomes significant — equipment financing and supply
   chain financing can minimize equity dilution.

## Exit Pathways
Given the stage and capital profile, potential exit pathways include:

1. **IPO (most likely)**: Public listing on NASDAQ or NYSE, likely 2026-2029
   timeframe, allowing earlier investors to achieve liquidity. Comparable
   companies: Plug Power (PLUG, market cap range $2-10B), ITM Power (ITM.L,
   £300M-1.5B). Key IPO catalysts: $500M+ revenue run rate, positive gross
   margins, clear pathway to profitability.

2. **Strategic Acquisition**: Acquisition by a major industrial (Siemens,
   GE Vernova, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries) or energy company (BP, Shell)
   seeking to internalize electrolyzer technology. Less likely given the
   strategic corporate investors on the cap table, who invested specifically
   to secure technology access rather than acquire it.

3. **Private / Stay Independent**: Given strategic investor support and
   the scale of the opportunity, EH2 could remain private longer than
   typical VC-backed companies, building toward a later, larger IPO.
