# AES Partnership — Strategic Collaboration

## Partnership Overview
AES Corporation (NYSE: AES), a Fortune 500 global energy company with operations in
15 countries, is a foundational strategic partner for Electric Hydrogen. The AES
partnership anchors EH2's first commercial-scale deployment and validates the
company's technology with a major, creditworthy offtaker and project developer.

AES is one of the world's largest renewable energy developers and operators, with
a stated goal of achieving net-zero carbon emissions from electricity sales by 2040.
The company views green hydrogen as a critical pathway for decarbonizing sectors
that cannot be directly electrified.

## First Commercial Project: Texas 300MW Facility
The centerpiece of the AES-EH2 partnership is a 300MW green hydrogen production
facility in Texas, comprising three 100MW Electric Hydrogen electrolyzer systems.

### Project Details
| Parameter              | Value                              |
|------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Location               | Texas (specific site undisclosed)  |
| Total Capacity         | 300 MW (3 × 100MW EH2 systems)     |
| Hydrogen Output        | ~150 tonnes/day                    |
| Renewable Power Source | Grid-connected with renewable PPA  |
| Offtake               | Industrial customers, potential ammonia |
| Target COD             | 2026-2027 (estimated)              |
| Developer/Owner        | AES (or AES-led consortium)        |

### Strategic Significance
1. **First-of-kind deployment**: The Texas project is the first commercial
   installation of EH2's 100MW platform, serving as a reference plant for
   future customers.
2. **IRA economics validation**: The project demonstrates the economic viability
   of green hydrogen under the IRA's $3/kg production tax credit (Section 45V).
3. **Bankability milestone**: Successful financing of a 300MW project establishes
   a precedent for future project finance transactions.
4. **Operational data**: Real-world performance data from the Texas facility will
   validate EH2's efficiency, durability, and reliability claims.

### Project Structure
AES brings to the partnership:
- **Project development expertise**: Site selection, permitting, interconnection,
  and community engagement.
- **Renewable power procurement**: Access to AES's extensive renewable generation
  portfolio for green power supply.
- **Balance sheet strength**: Investment-grade credit for project financing.
- **EPC management**: Construction and commissioning oversight.
- **Operations experience**: Long-term plant operations and maintenance.

Electric Hydrogen provides:
- **Electrolyzer systems**: Three 100MW PEM systems manufactured at Devens.
- **Technology guarantee**: Performance warranties on efficiency and degradation.
- **Commissioning support**: Technical expertise during startup and ramp.
- **Long-term service**: Maintenance, monitoring, and technology upgrades.

## Beyond Texas: Future Pipeline
The AES partnership extends beyond the initial Texas project:

- **Joint development pipeline**: AES and EH2 are evaluating additional green
  hydrogen production sites in the United States, with a focus on regions with
  strong renewable resources and industrial hydrogen demand.
- **International expansion**: AES's global footprint (Latin America, Asia) provides
  pathways for international deployment of EH2 technology.
- **Integrated energy parks**: Potential co-location of electrolysis with AES's
  renewable generation and battery storage assets, creating integrated clean
  energy hubs.

## Strategic Rationale

### For AES
- **Technology differentiation**: Access to the largest commercial PEM electrolyzer
  platform provides competitive advantage vs. developers using smaller or less
  proven technology.
- **First-mover advantage**: Early deployment of large-scale green hydrogen
  positions AES ahead of competitors in an emerging market.
- **IRA monetization**: The partnership enables AES to capture value from the
  full IRA incentive stack (renewable PTC/ITC + hydrogen PTC + potential
  manufacturing credits).

### For Electric Hydrogen
- **Reference customer**: A major, credible partner validates the technology for
  other potential customers.
- **Revenue visibility**: The Texas project provides contracted revenue that
  supports manufacturing ramp at Devens.
- **Operational learning**: Real-world deployment accelerates technology maturation
  and fleet learning.
- **Market access**: AES's project pipeline provides a channel to market beyond
  what EH2 could achieve independently as an equipment supplier.

## Risk Considerations
- **Execution risk**: First-of-kind deployment carries technical and schedule risk.
- **IRA dependency**: Project economics depend on IRA tax credits remaining in
  place.
- **Power costs**: Green hydrogen economics are highly sensitive to electricity
  prices, which represents the largest operating cost input.
- **Offtake risk**: Securing long-term hydrogen purchase agreements at prices
  that support project returns.
