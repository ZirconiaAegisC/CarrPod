## 2. DETAILED MONTH-BY-MONTH PROGRAM

### Months 1-3: Assessment and Modeling
- **Month 1:** Kickoff meeting at GF5 Texas. Team deployment to GF1 Nevada. Soil resistivity survey (Wenner 4-pin method, 50m and 100m electrode spacing). Neutral current monitoring system installation — 6 months of baseline data collection initiated. Regulatory filing with NV Energy for NBC interconnection approval.
- **Month 2:** Team deployment to GF4 Berlin. European grid GIC modeling using ENTSO-E network data. Coordination with 50Hertz transmission operator. Regulatory filing with Bundesnetzagentur. Simultaneous remote modeling of GF3 Shanghai using available Chinese grid data.
- **Month 3:** Team deployment to GF5 Texas. ERCOT GIC modeling. Deliver Phase 1 pilot detailed design package. Consolidated risk assessment report delivered to Tesla executive team. Go/no-go decision for Phase 1.
- **Milestone:** Phase 0 completion review. Pilot design approved. Phase 1 purchase orders issued.

### Months 4-8: Pilot Deployment (GF1 Nevada)
- **Month 4:** NBC fabrication and factory acceptance testing at CSM facility. GF1 site preparation — concrete pad, conduit runs, ground grid connection. Transformer outage scheduled with NV Energy for Month 5.
- **Month 5:** 12-hour GF1 primary transformer outage. NBC installation, bypass thyristor commissioning, Rogowski coil calibration. Transformer re-energization with NBC active. GIC current injection testing using Phantom Robot (simulated DC injection, validated blocking >95%).
- **Month 6:** CSM-MX-2400 active harmonic filter installation at Cell Formation Building 1 DC bus. Factory acceptance testing — harmonic injection testing with programmable load bank.
- **Month 7:** Formation line PCU commissioning. Full system integration test — simulated GIC event using CSM's portable GIC injection test set. Validation: power quality maintained within 0.5% THD during simulated half-cycle saturation condition.
- **Month 8:** 30-day operational verification. DGA monitoring baseline established. Phantom Robot monitoring data review. Phase 1 closeout report. Phase 2 authorization.
- **Milestone:** GF1 Cell Formation Bldg 1 fully protected and validated. Operational data collected. Phase 2 go-ahead.

### Months 9-18: Substation Hardening (All Facilities)
- **Months 9-11:** GF1 Nevada remaining primary transformers (2 additional NBCs). Conduit and cable tray installation for factory-wide distribution protection (Phase 3 preparation). Power distribution modeling for factory-wide harmonic filter placement.
- **Months 12-14:** GF5 Texas substation hardening. Coordination with ERCOT for planned outages. Three transformer NBCs installed. ERCOT interconnection studies validated against Phase 0 modeling.
- **Months 15-16:** GF4 Berlin substation hardening. Coordination with 50Hertz and Bundesnetzagentur. Two transformer NBCs. European grid compliance documentation.
- **Months 17-18:** GF3 Shanghai substation hardening. Coordination with State Grid Shanghai Municipal Electric Power Company. CSM in-country engineering partner engaged. Two transformer NBCs.
- **Milestone:** All gigafactory primary substations GIC-protected. Factory-wide Phase 3 design complete.

### Months 19-30: Factory-Wide Manufacturing Protection
- **Months 19-24:** GF1 Nevada factory-wide roll-out. All formation lines receive PCU (estimated 8-12 lines depending on expansion stage). All robotic cell clusters receive GIC-rated PDUs (estimated 40-60 clusters). Electrode coating lines receive dedicated power conditioning.
- **Months 25-27:** GF5 Texas factory-wide roll-out. 4680 cell formation lines. Cybertruck assembly robotic protection. Semi line preparation.
- **Months 28-29:** GF4 Berlin manufacturing protection. Model Y assembly line hardening.
- **Month 30:** GF3 Shanghai manufacturing protection. High-volume Model 3/Y line protection prioritized.
- **Milestone:** All Tesla manufacturing lines GIC-protected.

### Months 18-24 (Parallel Track): Megapack Black-Start Integration
- **Months 18-20:** CSM-MBSC-2000 controller development and factory testing. Communication protocol validation with Tesla Megapack site controller. Grid-forming mode transition testing.
- **Months 21-22:** GF1 Nevada Megapack installation (existing ~250 MW) black-start integration. Islanding test under simulated GIC conditions. Re-synchronization testing.
- **Months 23-24:** GF5 Texas Megapack black-start integration. GF4 Berlin and GF3 Shanghai remote commissioning.
- **Milestone:** All gigafactories capable of islanded operation within 50ms of GIC detection.

### Months 24-60: Supercharger Network Hardening
- **Months 24-30:** V4 Supercharger stall hardening kit manufacturing ramp. CSM-SC-1500 modules produced at CSM's MXene fabrication facility. Target production rate: 500 kits/month by Month 30.
- **Months 24-36 (Priority Phase):** Interstate corridor deployment. I-80 corridor (Nevada-California), I-35 corridor (Texas), I-95 corridor (East Coast), I-10 corridor (Southern route). Deployment teams of 4 technicians, 2 stalls per team per day.
- **Months 36-60 (Full Deployment):** Remaining Supercharger stations deployed in priority order: Interstate corridors → urban superchargers → destination chargers. Production rate ramps to 2,000 kits/month.
- **Milestone (Month 36):** 5,000 priority stalls hardened. Interstate corridors operational during GMD events.
- **Milestone (Month 60):** Full Supercharger network GIC-hardened.

## 4. KEY RISKS AND MITIGATIONS

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Transformer outage scheduling conflicts | Medium | Schedule delay | Coordination 90+ days ahead; flexible outage windows |
| NBC supply chain delay | Low | Schedule delay | CSM maintains 4-unit buffer stock |
| Regulatory approval delay (EU, China) | Medium | Schedule delay | Early engagement; local regulatory partners |
| Tesla production schedule conflicts | High | Installation access | Schedule around planned maintenance outages |
| GIC event during construction | Low-Medium | Partial protection | Only protected transformers in service; rapid NBC deployment |
| MXene material production capacity | Low | Supply constraint | CSM MXene fab capacity adequate for full program |
