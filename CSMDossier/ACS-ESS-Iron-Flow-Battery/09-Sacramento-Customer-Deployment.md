# Sacramento Municipal Utility District Deployment

## Customer Profile

The Sacramento Municipal Utility District (SMUD) is the sixth-largest community-owned electric utility in the United States, serving approximately 1.5 million residents across a 900-square-mile service territory that includes Sacramento County and small portions of Placer and Yolo Counties. SMUD operates as a not-for-profit public utility, governed by an elected Board of Directors.

SMUD has established one of the most aggressive decarbonization targets in the utility sector: its 2030 Zero Carbon Plan commits to achieving 100% clean electricity by 2030, a full 15 years ahead of California's statewide mandate under SB 100. This accelerated timeline creates an urgent need for long-duration energy storage to integrate the substantial solar generation within SMUD's portfolio and across the CAISO balancing area.

## Project Background

In 2022, SMUD and ESS Inc announced a partnership to deploy iron flow battery systems to support SMUD's grid decarbonization goals. The project was structured as a phased deployment:

### Phase 1: Technology Validation
- Deployment of multiple Energy Warehouse units (75 kW / 500 kWh each) at SMUD's test facility
- 12-month operational data collection period
- Performance evaluated against SMUD's requirements: round-trip efficiency, availability, response time, and capacity retention over cycling
- Successful completion of Phase 1 validated the technology for larger-scale deployment

### Phase 2: Multi-MW Energy Center
- SMUD contracted with ESS for an Energy Center configuration in the multi-megawatt range
- Target applications include:
  - Solar generation shifting from midday to evening peak (the "duck curve" challenge)
  - Distribution feeder peak load management to defer transformer and conductor upgrades
  - Resource adequacy contribution to meet CPUC resource adequacy requirements
  - Ancillary services participation in CAISO markets

## Strategic Significance

The SMUD deployment is strategically significant for ESS for several reasons:

### 1. Reference Customer Validation
As a respected, technologically sophisticated public utility, SMUD's decision to deploy iron flow batteries at multi-MW scale provides a powerful reference for other utilities considering LDES procurement. SMUD is known for rigorous technology evaluation — the utility has previously piloted and deployed flow batteries, lithium-ion BESS, and flywheel storage.

### 2. California Market Entry
California represents the largest single US market for LDES, driven by:
- CPUC procurement orders requiring 1 GW of LDES by 2026
- The state's SB 100 mandate for 100% clean electricity by 2045
- CAISO's extreme duck curve, which creates 13+ GW of 3-hour ramping need every evening
- High retail and wholesale electricity prices that improve storage project economics

A successful SMUD deployment opens the door to procurements at Pacific Gas and Electric (PG&E), Southern California Edison (SCE), San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E), and the community choice aggregators (CCAs) that serve much of California's load.

### 3. CAISO Market Participation
SMUD, while a balancing authority within its own territory, operates within the CAISO wholesale market. The ESS Energy Center's demonstrated ability to participate in CAISO day-ahead and real-time markets, provide ancillary services, and meet CAISO's stringent telemetry and dispatch requirements is directly transferable to other CAISO-connected projects.

## Operational Outcomes

Performance data from the SMUD deployment has not been publicly released in full, but ESS has reported:

- System availability exceeding 98% during the initial operating period
- Capacity retention consistent with zero-degradation design expectations
- Successful participation in CAISO markets, including provision of regulation and spinning reserve
- Positive feedback from SMUD operations staff regarding system reliability and maintenance requirements

## Contract Structure

While specific contract terms are confidential, industry analysts expect the SMUD contract to include:

- **Turnkey EPC:** ESS provides engineering, procurement, and construction services, either directly or through a qualified EPC partner
- **Performance Guarantees:** Contractual commitments for minimum round-trip efficiency, availability, and capacity retention over a multi-year period
- **Long-Term Service Agreement (LTSA):** 10–20 year operations and maintenance agreement covering routine maintenance, electrolyte management, and remote monitoring
- **IRA Benefit Sharing:** Contract provisions addressing the allocation of ITC and 45X credits between the parties

## Replicability

The SMUD deployment model — phased validation followed by utility-scale procurement — is replicable across the approximately 2,000 public power utilities and 900 electric cooperatives in the United States. These non-IOU utilities often have greater flexibility in technology procurement, access to low-cost financing through tax-exempt bonds and USDA RUS loans, and direct accountability to local governing boards that prioritize cost-effective decarbonization.

ESS has explicitly identified public power and cooperative utilities as a primary near-term market segment, arguing that these entities are more likely to be early adopters of non-lithium LDES technologies than the investor-owned utilities that face more complex regulatory approval processes and shareholder return requirements.
