# DOE Grant & Financing

## Department of Energy OCED Grant

### Program Context

In 2023, the US Department of Energy's Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations (OCED) — established by the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL, also known as the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, Public Law 117-58) — awarded approximately $50 million in federal funding to ESS Inc to support the deployment of iron flow battery technology at utility scale. The grant was made under the OCED's Long-Duration Energy Storage Demonstrations program, which was appropriated $505 million in total by the BIL.

### Grant Structure

The OCED grant follows DOE's standard cooperative agreement structure:

- **Total project value:** Approximately $100+ million (federal share: ~$50 million; non-federal cost share: ~$50+ million)
- **Federal cost share:** Typically 50% for demonstration-stage projects under OCED guidelines
- **Performance period:** 3–5 years, covering design, construction, commissioning, and an initial operational and data collection period
- **Milestone-based disbursement:** Funds are drawn down as the project achieves pre-defined technical, financial, and operational milestones
- **Data sharing requirements:** The cooperative agreement requires public dissemination of operational data, performance metrics, and lessons learned to accelerate broader LDES market adoption

### Use of Funds

The DOE grant supports specific activities aligned with scaling ESS technology from commercial pilot to utility-scale deployment:

1. **Energy Center Engineering:** Detailed design and engineering for multi-MW, multi-MWh Energy Center configurations, including stack scaling, electrolyte tank farm design, balance-of-plant optimization, and grid interconnection engineering
2. **Manufacturing Process Development:** Capital investment in automated production equipment, process control systems, and quality assurance infrastructure at the Wilsonville, Oregon facility
3. **Supply Chain Qualification:** Qualification and testing of alternative suppliers for critical components to improve supply chain resilience and reduce costs
4. **Field Deployment:** Construction and commissioning of one or more utility-scale Energy Center projects at host sites to be determined in coordination with DOE
5. **Operational Data Collection:** Instrumentation and telemetry for comprehensive performance monitoring, including round-trip efficiency, capacity retention, availability, and O&M cost tracking

### Strategic Significance

The DOE grant is significant for ESS beyond the direct financial benefit:

- **Federal Validation:** Receipt of a competitive DOE grant — awarded through a rigorous merit review process including technical, financial, and market viability assessments — provides third-party validation of ESS's technology claims and commercial readiness
- **De-Risking for Utility Customers:** Utility procurement decisions are inherently risk-averse. The DOE's financial participation in deployment projects reduces perceived technology risk and may accelerate utility adoption
- **Non-Dilutive Capital:** The grant funding is non-dilutive to existing shareholders, in contrast to equity financing that would dilute existing stakes
- **Political Alignment:** The project directly supports the Biden-Harris Administration's goals for domestic clean energy manufacturing and grid decarbonization, creating political capital that may facilitate other federal opportunities (DOD, GSA, USDA)

## Australian Super Fund Financing

### Transaction Overview

In 2023, ESS Inc secured a financing commitment exceeding $50 million from an Australian superannuation (pension) fund. Australian super funds are among the world's largest institutional investors, collectively managing over AUD $3.5 trillion (approximately USD $2.3 trillion) in retirement assets. These funds have increasingly allocated capital to infrastructure and clean energy investments, seeking long-duration, inflation-linked returns that match their multi-decade liability profiles.

### Financing Structure

The Australian super fund financing was structured as a convertible note or preferred equity instrument, typical of growth-stage clean energy companies:

- **Instrument:** Convertible debt or structured equity with conversion features tied to ESS's common stock price or future financing rounds
- **Use of proceeds:** General corporate purposes, including working capital to support manufacturing scale-up, product development, and sales pipeline expansion
- **Strategic rationale:** Diversifies ESS's capital sources beyond US equity markets; Australian super funds have demonstrated appetite for long-duration infrastructure investments and may participate in project-level financing for future Energy Center deployments

### Australian Market Relevance

Australia represents a strategically important market for LDES technologies:

- **High renewable penetration:** South Australia already exceeds 70% renewable generation, and the National Electricity Market (NEM) routinely experiences negative wholesale prices during midday solar peaks
- **Grid isolation:** Australia's electricity grid is physically isolated (no international interconnections), making grid stability and energy storage critically important
- **LDES procurement:** The Australian Renewable Energy Agency (ARENA) has allocated AUD $176 million for LDES demonstration projects; state governments (NSW, Victoria, Queensland) have established long-duration storage targets within their renewable energy zones (REZs)
- **Commodity alignment:** Australia is the world's largest iron ore producer, creating natural alignment with iron flow battery manufacturing

## Other Financing Sources

### SPAC Merger Proceeds

ESS went public in October 2021 through a merger with ACON S2 Acquisition Corp, a special purpose acquisition company. The transaction generated approximately $465 million in gross proceeds, including:

- $250 million from the SPAC trust account
- $215 million from a private investment in public equity (PIPE), anchored by institutional investors including Breakthrough Energy Ventures, SoftBank, and SB Energy

The SPAC proceeds funded ESS's transition from pilot-scale manufacturing to commercial production, including the buildout of the Wilsonville automated production line.

### Additional Equity and Debt

Since going public, ESS has accessed additional capital through:

- **At-the-Market (ATM) equity programs:** Periodic sales of common stock into the open market to fund working capital needs
- **Project finance debt:** Non-recourse or limited-recourse debt secured by specific project assets and revenue contracts, as the Energy Center pipeline matures to financial close
- **Strategic investments:** Equity investments from strategic partners (including Honeywell, though specific terms have not been publicly disclosed)
- **Government grants beyond DOE:** State-level economic development incentives from Oregon (Enterprise Zone, Strategic Reserve Fund) supporting manufacturing facility expansion

### Cash Runway Analysis

As a pre-profitability company with significant capital expenditure requirements for manufacturing scale-up, ESS's cash runway is a critical financial metric for investors:

- **Quarterly cash consumption (operating):** Approximately $20–25 million per quarter, dominated by R&D, SG&A, and manufacturing overhead
- **Capital expenditure (capex):** Variable, ranging from $5–15 million per quarter depending on production line expansion phases
- **Revenue contribution:** Currently limited (~$2–4 million per quarter), insufficient to cover operating costs
- **Cash and equivalents (latest filing):** Typically $100–150 million, providing a runway of 4–6 quarters absent additional capital raises or DOE grant drawdowns

The path to cash flow breakeven depends on:
1. Manufacturing automation achieving target production volumes (500+ MWh/year) and gross margins
2. Energy Center contract wins converting the pipeline of utility RFPs into firm orders with advance payments
3. IRA 45X credits providing direct cash infusion (via transferability sales to financial institutions)
4. Continued availability of grant and strategic financing to bridge the gap to profitability
