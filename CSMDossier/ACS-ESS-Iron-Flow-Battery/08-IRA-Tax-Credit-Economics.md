# IRA Section 45X Tax Credit Economics

## The Inflation Reduction Act and Energy Storage

The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (IRA, Public Law 117-169) represents the most significant federal policy intervention in clean energy in US history. Among its many provisions, Section 45X introduced the Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit, which provides a direct per-unit tax credit for the domestic production of battery cells and modules. This credit is fundamentally different from the Investment Tax Credit (ITC) and Production Tax Credit (PTC) that apply to clean energy generation and storage projects.

## Section 45X Battery Cell Credit

The 45X credit for battery cells is structured as follows:

- **Credit amount:** $35 per kilowatt-hour of battery cell capacity produced
- **Qualifying production:** Battery cells manufactured in the United States or its territories
- **Duration:** Full credit value through 2029, followed by a phase-down: 75% in 2030, 50% in 2031, 25% in 2032, and 0% after 2032
- **Eligible entities:** The manufacturer of the battery cells, not the project developer or system integrator

For ESS Inc, this means that each 500 kWh Energy Warehouse — which contains battery cell capacity of 500 kWh — would generate a 45X credit of $35/kWh × 500 kWh = $17,500 per unit. At ESS's targeted annual production capacity of 2 GWh, the 45X credits would total $70 million annually.

## 45X vs. ITC for ESS Products

The IRA provides two distinct federal incentives relevant to ESS deployments:

### Section 48 ITC (Project-Level)
- 30% base credit for standalone energy storage projects
- Bonus adder of +10% for domestic content (steel, iron, manufactured products)
- Bonus adder of +10% for projects located in "energy communities" (brownfield sites, areas with closed coal mines or retired coal plants, areas with high fossil fuel employment)
- Bonus adder of +10% for projects located in low-income communities or on Indian lands (limited allocation)
- Maximum potential ITC: 60% of project eligible costs

### Section 45X (Manufacturing-Level)
- Direct payment to the cell manufacturer at $35/kWh
- Not linked to project location or community characteristics
- Reduces the effective manufacturing cost, which should flow through to lower system pricing over time

## Stacking Analysis

Importantly, 45X and ITC can be stacked for the same project:

1. ESS Inc claims the 45X credit on battery cells manufactured in Wilsonville, reducing its effective cost of goods sold
2. The project developer (utility, IPP, or C&I customer) claims the 48 ITC on the installed system cost
3. This stacking effectively provides a double subsidy, with the federal government subsidizing both the manufacturing input (45X) and the deployment output (ITC)

## Economic Impact on ESS System Pricing

The interaction of 45X and ITC can dramatically reduce the effective cost of iron flow battery systems to end customers:

### Illustrative Economics (per kW basis, 8-hour system)

| Component | Without IRA | With 45X Only | With ITC Only (30%) | With Both |
|-----------|------------|---------------|--------------------|-----------|
| System installed cost ($/kW) | $2,000* | $1,720* | $1,400 | $1,120 |
| Effective customer cost ($/kW) | $2,000 | $1,720 | $1,400 | $1,120 |

*Assumes 45X credit is passed through as a price reduction from ESS to the end customer. In practice, the pass-through rate depends on competitive dynamics and negotiation.

### Illustrative LCOS Impact ($/MWh, 8-hour system, 25-year life)

| Scenario | Without IRA | With Both 45X + ITC |
|----------|------------|---------------------|
| Capital recovery | $65 | $35 |
| Fixed O&M | $8 | $8 |
| Variable O&M | $3 | $3 |
| Charging cost (at $25/MWh avg) | $33 | $33 |
| **Total LCOS** | **$109** | **$79** |

At $79/MWh LCOS, the iron flow battery becomes competitive with new gas peaker plants in many US markets, particularly when carbon pricing or renewable portfolio standards constrain new fossil generation.

## Domestic Content Requirements

The IRA includes strong domestic content provisions that favor ESS technology:

- Section 48 ITC domestic content bonus requires that 100% of structural steel and iron, and 40%+ (escalating to 55% by 2027) of manufactured products, be US-produced
- ESS's all-iron electrolyte is sourced from domestic iron chloride suppliers (US is the world's 4th largest iron ore producer)
- Tank fabrication, stack assembly, and system integration are performed in Wilsonville, Oregon
- Power conversion systems may require non-US components (IGBT semiconductors, certain capacitor types), but overall domestic content is estimated at 80–90%

In contrast, lithium-ion battery systems face challenges meeting domestic content requirements because:
- Cathode active materials (NMC, LFP) are predominantly manufactured in China, South Korea, and Japan
- Graphite anode material is >80% sourced from China
- Lithium extraction and processing is concentrated in Australia (mining), Chile/Argentina (brine), and China (refining)

This domestic content advantage positions ESS to offer projects that qualify for the +10% ITC bonus adder, while competing lithium-ion projects may not.

## Transferability Provisions

The IRA introduced transferability of tax credits, allowing manufacturers and developers to sell credits to third parties with federal tax liability. This is particularly important for ESS Inc, which as a pre-profitability company has limited tax liability to apply the 45X credits against. Under Section 6418, ESS can sell its 45X credits to financial institutions or other corporations, receiving cash (typically 85–92 cents per dollar of credit value) that can be used to fund operations and growth.

## International Competitiveness

The IRA's domestic manufacturing focus has prompted responses from other jurisdictions:

- **European Union:** Green Deal Industrial Plan and Net-Zero Industry Act (NZIA) include battery manufacturing targets and potential local content requirements
- **Canada:** Clean Technology Manufacturing Investment Tax Credit (30% refundable) introduced in Budget 2023
- **Australia:** National Battery Strategy announced 2024, includes production incentives under evaluation

For ESS Inc, the IRA creates a near-term competitive moat within the US market while the company scales manufacturing and drives down costs through automation and volume. The challenge will be maintaining competitiveness as other jurisdictions introduce their own incentives for alternative flow battery technologies.
