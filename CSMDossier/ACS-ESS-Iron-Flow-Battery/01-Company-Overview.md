# ESS Inc — Company Overview

## Corporate Identity

ESS Inc (NYSE: GWH) is a long-duration energy storage company headquartered in Wilsonville, Oregon. Founded in 2011 by Dr. Craig Evans and Julia Song, the company has pioneered iron flow battery technology for grid-scale applications. ESS went public via SPAC merger with ACON S2 Acquisition Corp in October 2021, trading on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol "GWH."

## Leadership

**Eric Dresselhuys** serves as Chief Executive Officer, appointed in 2022. Dresselhuys previously co-founded Silver Spring Networks and held leadership roles at SunEdison and other energy technology companies. His expertise spans utility-scale energy infrastructure, smart grid deployments, and clean technology commercialization.

**Dr. Craig Evans** remains active as Founder and Chief Technology Officer. Evans holds a PhD in materials science and engineering and has spent his career focused on electrochemical storage systems. Under his technical direction, ESS has built a patent portfolio covering iron flow battery chemistry, cell architecture, and manufacturing processes.

## Corporate Mission

ESS Inc positions its technology as the solution for grid-scale long-duration energy storage (LDES), defined as systems capable of discharging for 4–12+ hours continuously. The company's thesis rests on a fundamental claim: as renewable energy penetration increases beyond 30–40%, short-duration lithium-ion batteries become insufficient to balance multi-day and seasonal intermittency. ESS argues that iron flow batteries — using abundant, non-toxic, domestically sourced materials — represent the lowest-cost, safest, and most sustainable path to deep decarbonization.

## Sustainability & ESG Profile

ESS's technology inherently aligns with environmental, social, and governance criteria. The iron flow battery contains no conflict minerals, produces no hazardous waste, and is fully recyclable at end of life. The electrolyte — iron dissolved in saltwater — can be neutralized and discharged to standard wastewater treatment. Electrode materials are carbon-based and can be incinerated or landfilled as non-hazardous waste. The steel, plastic, and copper components follow standard recycling streams. This end-of-life simplicity contrasts sharply with lithium-ion batteries, which require specialized recycling to recover cobalt, nickel, and lithium, and pose fire risks in the waste stream.

## Technology Differentiation

The core intellectual property revolves around an all-iron redox flow battery chemistry. Unlike conventional flow batteries that use vanadium or zinc-bromine electrolytes, ESS technology uses iron dissolved in a simple saltwater solution. Both the positive (ferrous/ferric) and negative (ferrous/metallic iron) half-reactions involve the same base element: iron. This confers unique manufacturing and supply chain advantages, as iron is among the most abundant metals on Earth.

## Public Company Status

As a publicly traded company on the NYSE, ESS files quarterly (10-Q) and annual (10-K) reports with the SEC. The company has consistently reported net losses as it invests in scaling manufacturing capacity ahead of revenue growth. Analysts covering ESS include investment banks focused on clean energy and industrials. The stock has experienced significant volatility since its SPAC debut, reflecting broader market sentiment shifts toward pre-revenue clean energy technology companies and the challenges of scaling novel manufacturing processes against capital expenditure requirements that outstrip near-term revenue generation. The company's market capitalization has ranged from approximately $200 million to over $1 billion since listing.

## Strategic Positioning

ESS occupies a distinct niche within the energy storage sector. While lithium-ion manufacturers (Tesla, CATL, BYD) dominate the 1–4 hour duration market, ESS targets exclusively the long-duration segment. The company's closest competitors are other flow battery manufacturers, notably Invinity Energy Systems (vanadium redox flow) and Redflow (zinc-bromine flow). ESS differentiates by using only iron — an element that faces no supply constraints, geopolitical dependencies, or price volatility associated with vanadium, cobalt, or lithium.

## Regulatory Tailwinds

The Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 introduced Section 45X, providing a production tax credit of $35 per kilowatt-hour of battery cell capacity manufactured in the United States. As a domestic manufacturer with a fully US-based supply chain, ESS is positioned to benefit directly from this incentive structure, potentially reducing effective system costs by 30–40%.

## Product Portfolio at a Glance

ESS markets two primary product lines serving distinct market segments:

- **Energy Warehouse:** A self-contained 75 kW / 500 kWh system packaged in a standard 40-foot shipping container, targeting commercial and industrial behind-the-meter applications, microgrids, and small utility distribution projects. Each unit provides approximately 6–7 hours of continuous discharge at rated power.
- **Energy Center:** A configurable utility-scale platform in the multi-MW to multi-hundred-MW range, assembled from standardized power blocks (stacks) and energy blocks (electrolyte tanks). The Energy Center targets front-of-meter applications including solar shifting, resource adequacy, and wholesale market participation, with durations from 4 to 12+ hours.

Both products share a common core technology — the all-iron flow battery stack and electrolyte chemistry — differentiated by the scaling of tanks (for energy capacity) and the aggregation of stacks and power conversion systems (for power capacity).
