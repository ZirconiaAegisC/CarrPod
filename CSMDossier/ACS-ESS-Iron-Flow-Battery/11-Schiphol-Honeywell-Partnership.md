# Schiphol Airport & Honeywell Partnership

## Schiphol Airport Deployment

### Airport Profile

Amsterdam Airport Schiphol (AMS/EHAM) is the third-busiest airport in Europe by passenger traffic and one of the world's major international aviation hubs, serving approximately 72 million passengers annually (pre-pandemic). Schiphol is operated by Royal Schiphol Group, which has committed to achieving net-zero emissions from its own operations by 2030 and aims to make the aviation sector more sustainable through the "Smart and Sustainable" plan.

### Project Description

ESS Inc, in partnership with the Royal Schiphol Group, deployed iron flow battery technology at Schiphol Airport to support the airport's electrification and sustainability goals:

- **System:** Energy Warehouse (75 kW / 500 kWh) iron flow battery
- **Location:** Schiphol Airport grounds, Amsterdam, Netherlands
- **Purpose:** Pilot demonstration of long-duration energy storage for airport ground operations electrification
- **Timeline:** Announced 2023; operational deployment completed 2024

### Airport-Specific Applications

The Schiphol deployment validates iron flow battery technology across several airport-specific use cases:

1. **Ground Support Equipment (GSE) Electrification:**
   - Airports globally are transitioning from diesel-powered ground support equipment (baggage tractors, belt loaders, pushback tugs, aircraft ground power units) to electric alternatives
   - This electrification creates significant new electrical loads with high simultaneity (concentrated charging during aircraft turnaround periods)
   - Iron flow batteries can buffer the charging demand, shaving peak loads on airport electrical infrastructure and avoiding costly distribution upgrades

2. **Fixed Electrical Ground Power (FEGP):**
   - Aircraft at gates require 90 kVA of 400 Hz electrical power for cabin systems during turnarounds
   - Historically provided by onboard auxiliary power units (APUs) burning jet fuel; airports are transitioning to gate-based FEGP systems
   - Energy storage at gates can provide ride-through during utility interruptions and enable time-shifting of electricity consumption

3. **Airport Critical Loads:**
   - Air traffic control systems, runway lighting, security screening, and terminal life safety systems require uninterruptible power
   - The iron flow battery's 6+ hour duration provides substantive backup capability compared to the 15–30 minutes typical of lead-acid UPS systems currently deployed

4. **Solar Integration:**
   - Airports have extensive flat roof areas (terminals, hangars, parking structures) and open land (airfield buffer zones) suitable for solar PV
   - Schiphol has installed 20+ MW of on-site solar generation; the ESS system demonstrates how LDES can shift this solar generation to match airport electrical loads

### European Market Significance

The Schiphol deployment is strategically important as ESS's first European installation:

- **European LDES Policy:** The European Union's REPowerEU plan and Electricity Market Design reform both emphasize the critical role of LDES in achieving energy independence and grid stability
- **EU Taxonomy Alignment:** Iron flow batteries align with the EU Taxonomy for Sustainable Activities, potentially qualifying projects for favorable green financing
- **Reference for Other European Airports:** Aéroports de Paris (ADP), Fraport (Frankfurt), Heathrow, and other major European airports are monitoring the Schiphol pilot as they develop their own electrification strategies

## Honeywell Partnership

### Partnership Structure

In September 2023, ESS Inc and Honeywell International (NASDAQ: HON) announced a strategic partnership to advance the commercialization of iron flow battery technology. Honeywell is a Fortune 100 diversified technology and manufacturing conglomerate with approximately $36 billion in annual revenue and deep expertise in industrial automation, process control, building technologies, and energy systems.

### Scope of Collaboration

The Honeywell partnership encompasses several workstreams:

1. **Battery Management System (BMS):**
   - Honeywell is integrating its Experion Process Knowledge System (PKS) as the advanced battery management and plant control platform for the Energy Center product line
   - Experion PKS is a mature, industrial-grade distributed control system (DCS) deployed at thousands of process plants, refineries, and power generation facilities globally
   - Integration provides ESS with a field-proven, cybersecurity-hardened control platform that meets utility and industrial customer requirements for reliability, redundancy, and OT security

2. **Sensors and Instrumentation:**
   - Honeywell's portfolio of industrial sensors (temperature, pressure, flow, level, pH, and gas detection) are being qualified for use in ESS systems
   - Co-development of application-specific sensor packages optimized for the iron chloride electrolyte environment

3. **Global Sales and Distribution:**
   - Honeywell's global sales force and channel partner network provide market access in regions where ESS has limited direct presence
   - Joint go-to-market for industrial customers in sectors including mining, oil & gas, chemicals, and data centers
   - Honeywell's existing relationships with utilities and EPC contractors facilitate Energy Center project development

4. **Supply Chain and Manufacturing:**
   - Honeywell's global procurement organization supports ESS in qualifying alternative suppliers for balance-of-system components
   - Potential for Honeywell to manufacture certain sub-components at its own facilities, providing production scale-up flexibility

### Strategic Rationale

For ESS Inc, the Honeywell partnership addresses several critical scaling challenges:

- **Brand credibility:** Honeywell's century-plus reputation in industrial controls provides assurance to risk-averse utility and industrial customers evaluating a novel storage technology from a relatively small manufacturer
- **Global reach:** Honeywell operates in 70+ countries; ESS has direct sales presence in only the US and, more recently, Europe
- **Product maturity:** The transition from ESS's internally developed BMS to Honeywell's industrial-grade DCS platform is a critical step in productizing the Energy Center for utility-scale applications that demand proven control reliability
- **Supply chain resilience:** Dual-sourcing of components through Honeywell's supply chain reduces single-point-of-failure risks in ESS's currently concentrated supply base

For Honeywell, the partnership provides:

- Entry into the fast-growing LDES market, which Honeywell has identified as a strategic growth vertical
- A differentiated, non-lithium storage technology to offer alongside Honeywell's broader energy transition portfolio (carbon capture, hydrogen, sustainable aviation fuels)
- A domestic manufacturing partner qualifying for IRA 45X credits, enhancing Honeywell's own IRA strategy
- Alignment with Honeywell's corporate commitment to achieve carbon neutrality by 2035

### Market Impact

The ESS-Honeywell partnership has been positively received by industry analysts, who note that partnerships between technology innovators (ESS) and established industrial incumbents (Honeywell) are likely to characterize the LDES market's maturation. The partnership model mirrors successful scaling strategies in solar PV (First Solar's captive manufacturing) and wind energy (Vestas, Siemens Gamesa's supply chain ecosystems).
