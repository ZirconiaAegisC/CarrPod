
# CORPORATE PROFILE: WALMART INC.
## ACS-WALMART-DISTRIBUTION-RESILIENCE

**CLASSIFICATION: COMMERCIAL-IN-CONFIDENCE**

## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

**Legal Name:** Walmart Inc.
**Headquarters:** Bentonville, Arkansas
**Ticker:** WMT (NYSE)
**Revenue (2025):** ~$680B (world's largest company by revenue)
**U.S. Stores:** 4,700+ including 3,500+ Supercenters
**Distribution Centers:** 150+ DCs in U.S., each 1-1.5M sq ft, 40-60% refrigerated
**Fleet:** 6,100+ trucks, 63,000+ trailers
**Weekly U.S. Customer Count:** ~140 million
**Employees:** ~1.6M U.S., ~2.1M globally

## 3. DISTRIBUTION NETWORK ANALYSIS

- **Grocery DCs:** Temperature-controlled (ambient, refrigerated, frozen). 24/7 operation. Ammonia refrigeration systems drawing 5-15 MW. $20-40M in perishable inventory at any time. Workforce: 800-1,500 per DC.
- **Regional DCs:** General merchandise. Less refrigeration, more conveyor and sortation systems. Still require backup power for facilities and logistics IT.
- **Fulfillment Centers:** E-commerce fulfillment. Highly automated (robotics, conveyor). IT systems critical to picking and shipping.

## 4. WALMART AS CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE

Walmart's supply chain is de facto national critical infrastructure:
- **Food:** Walmart sells ~25% of U.S. groceries. During a grid emergency, Walmart is the food distribution mechanism for 140 million weekly shoppers.
- **Pharmacy:** Walmart operates 4,600+ pharmacies. Critical medicines reach patients through Walmart distribution.
- **Fuel:** Walmart operates 1,500+ fuel stations. Emergency vehicle fuel supply.
- **Emergency Response:** Walmart has coordinated with FEMA during past natural disasters. The company's hurricane response playbook demonstrates capability for rapid distribution during emergencies — but this capability depends on functional DC power infrastructure.
