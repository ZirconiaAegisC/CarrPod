# Commercial Orders Breakdown

## Order Book Summary

As of the latest public disclosures, Heart Aerospace holds the following
combined orders for the ES-30:

| Category | Quantity |
|----------|----------|
| Firm orders | 250 aircraft |
| Options | 120 aircraft |
| Letters of intent (LOI) | 191 aircraft |
| **Total commitments** | **561 aircraft** |

The order book spans North America and Europe, with major commitments
from three anchor airline customers: United Airlines, Mesa Air Group,
and Air Canada. Additionally, Rockton (a Swedish lessor) holds a
smaller firm order with options.

## United Airlines — 100 Firm + 50 Options

United Airlines is the largest individual customer for the ES-30.
United's commitment, announced in conjunction with its broader
investment in Heart Aerospace, comprises:

- **100 firm orders** with pre-delivery payment deposits.
- **50 purchase options** exercisable at United's discretion.

United's interest in the ES-30 is grounded in its hub-and-spoke
network, where regional aircraft feed traffic from small and medium-
sized cities to major hubs. The ES-30's 200-kilometer all-electric
range and 400-kilometer hybrid range make it suitable for routes
from United's Denver, Chicago, and Houston hubs to communities within
a 200–400 kilometer radius that currently lack air service or are
served by less-efficient aircraft.

United publicly stated that the ES-30 could operate from its Denver
International Airport hub to destinations in Colorado, Wyoming,
Nebraska, and Kansas, restoring service to communities that lost
air connectivity after the retirement of 19-to-30-seat aircraft.

## Mesa Air Group — 100 Firm + 50 Options

Mesa Air Group, a Phoenix-based regional airline operating under
contract for United Express, placed a matching order of 100 firm
plus 50 options. Mesa's combined 200-aircraft commitment (including
options) represents the largest single-airline ES-30 position by unit
count.

Mesa operates a fleet of CRJ-900 and Embraer E175 regional jets under
capacity-purchase agreements with United and, previously, American
Airlines. The ES-30 provides Mesa with a lower-trip-cost aircraft for
thinner routes where 70-to-76-seat regional jets are too large to be
profitable. Mesa's CEO has described the ES-30 as a tool to "right-size"
capacity on routes where current aircraft are economically suboptimal.

Mesa's early commitment signaled confidence that the hybrid-electric
propulsion concept was mature enough for commercial operations, and the
depth of the order (100 firm, compared to typical launch orders of
25–50 aircraft for new types) was noted by industry analysts as a
strong endorsement.

## Air Canada — 30 Firm

Air Canada confirmed 30 firm orders for the ES-30, with an additional
$5 million equity investment in Heart Aerospace as part of its
participation in the company's funding rounds. Air Canada's order
targets domestic and transborder routes from its Toronto, Montreal,
and Vancouver hubs. Specific route candidates identified by the airline
include:

- Toronto Island (Billy Bishop) to nearby Ontario and Quebec communities.
- Montreal to Quebec City, Sherbrooke, and the Eastern Townships.
- Vancouver to Vancouver Island and the Gulf Islands.
- Routes currently served under Air Canada's regional capacity-purchase
  agreements with Jazz Aviation.

Air Canada's publicly stated goal of net-zero emissions by 2050 makes
the ES-30's 90% emissions reduction on all-electric flights strategically
aligned with the carrier's sustainability commitments.

## Rockton — 20 Firm + 20 Options

Rockton, a Swedish aircraft leasing company, placed a smaller but
strategically significant order of 20 firm ES-30s plus 20 options.
Rockton's involvement brings the aircraft-leasing community into the
ES-30 program, an important step for any new commercial aircraft type.

Leasing companies typically order early in a program to secure delivery
positions that they can then lease to airlines. Rockton's participation
suggests that the leasing industry views the ES-30 as having residual
value and remarketing potential, critical factors for asset-backed
financing and airline operating-lease structures.

## Letters of Intent

The 191 letters of intent represent non-binding expressions of interest
from additional airlines, lessors, and potential operators. While LOIs
do not carry the same contractual weight as firm orders, they indicate
broad market interest and are used by Heart Aerospace to plan production
rates, negotiate supplier contracts, and raise additional funding.

The LOI group is reported to include European regional airlines, Nordic
carriers (reflecting Heart Aerospace's Swedish origins), and potential
operators in island nations and archipelagos where short-range electric
aviation is particularly attractive given high fuel-import costs and
short stage lengths.

## Delivery Timeline

Deliveries are expected to begin following type certification in 2031.
The initial production rate is expected to be modest—perhaps 12–24
aircraft per year—as the Gothenburg pilot plant transitions to series
production. If the order book continues to grow, Heart Aerospace would
need to establish a higher-rate final assembly line, potentially at a
second site, to deliver against a 500+ aircraft backlog within a
commercially reasonable timeframe.
