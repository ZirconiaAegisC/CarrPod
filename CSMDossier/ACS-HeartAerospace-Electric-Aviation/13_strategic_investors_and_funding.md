# Strategic Investors & Funding

## Total Capital Raised

Heart Aerospace has raised approximately $145 million in total funding
since its founding in 2019. The funding has come from a mix of venture
capital, strategic corporate investors, government grants, and airline
partners—an unusually diverse investor base that reflects the capital-
intensive nature of aircraft development and the broad coalition of
stakeholders required to bring a new aircraft type to market.

## Series B — February 2024: $107 Million

The Series B round, announced in February 2024, raised $107 million—
the largest single fundraising event for a European electric aviation
company at the time. The round was co-led by new and existing investors
and valued the company at a significant step-up from its earlier rounds.

## Key Investor Profiles

### Breakthrough Energy Ventures (BEV)

BEV, founded by Bill Gates in 2015, invests in technologies capable of
reducing greenhouse gas emissions by at least 500 million tons per year.
BEV's investment signals confidence that electric regional aviation can
achieve globally meaningful emissions reductions. BEV's portfolio includes
ZeroAvia, battery-technology companies, and other transportation
decarbonization investments.

### Saab

Saab's participation connects Heart Aerospace to Sweden's aerospace
industrial base. Saab produced the 340 and 2000 regional aircraft in
Linkoping until 1999, retaining institutional knowledge of regional
aircraft certification, manufacturing, and support. The investment creates
potential for manufacturing collaboration and supply-chain access.

### United Airlines Ventures

United's corporate venture arm invested alongside its 100-aircraft firm
order, aligning financial interests with the ES-30 program's success.
United has also invested in ZeroAvia and Archer Aviation as part of a
multi-technology sustainability portfolio.

### Other Investors

- **EQT Ventures:** Major Swedish VC providing Nordic investment community
  validation and operational scaling expertise.
- **Y Combinator:** Early-stage accelerator; Heart Aerospace is a YC
  graduate.
- **Lowercarbon Capital:** Climate-tech focused fund.
- **Norrsken Foundation:** Swedish impact-investment foundation.

## Government Funding

**Vinnova** (Swedish innovation agency) has provided R&D grants—non-dilutive
capital validated through technical review and often funding collaborative
university projects that build aerospace engineering talent in Gothenburg.

**FAA FAST Grant ($4.1 million):** Competitive award under the Fueling
Aviation's Sustainable Transition program, supporting U.S.-based flight
testing at Plattsburgh and FAA certification activities. The award to
Heart Aerospace (now headquartered in Los Angeles) indicates FAA
recognition of the ES-30's potential for U.S. aviation sustainability.

## Funding Outlook

With ~$145 million raised, Heart Aerospace is funded through the X1
taxi-test phase and into the X2 flight-test program. Total capital required
to certify a 30-seat regional aircraft is estimated at $500 million to
$1 billion, suggesting further equity rounds, debt financing, or strategic
partnerships will be necessary in the 2027–2031 timeframe.
