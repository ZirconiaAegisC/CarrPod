# Manufacturing Strategy & Gothenburg Plant

## Gothenburg Pilot Manufacturing Plant

Heart Aerospace's manufacturing operations are centered on a pilot
production facility in Gothenburg, Sweden. The plant serves multiple
functions: engineering development, X1 and X2 prototype construction,
production-process validation, and initial series production of the
ES-30. The Gothenburg site was strategically chosen for its proximity to:

- **Aerospace workforce:** The Gothenburg region has a pool of
  aerospace engineers, technicians, and manufacturing professionals
  from Sweden's aerospace industry, including former Saab employees
  who worked on the Saab 340 and Saab 2000 regional aircraft programs.

- **Supply chain:** Sweden hosts a network of aerospace suppliers
  certified to AS9100 and EASA Part 21G production standards,
  including precision machining, composites fabrication, avionics
  integration, and systems engineering firms.

- **Research institutions:** Chalmers University of Technology in
  Gothenburg conducts research in electric power systems, battery
  technology, and lightweight structures, providing a pipeline of
  talent and collaborative research opportunities.

- **Logistics:** The Port of Gothenburg is Scandinavia's largest port,
  facilitating international shipping of major aerostructure components
  and receiving of globally sourced subsystems.

## Production Philosophy

Heart Aerospace follows a lean production model adapted from automotive
and aerospace practice. The company develops its own propulsion system
rather than purchasing off-the-shelf, giving control over motor design,
battery module engineering, power electronics, and safety-critical
propulsion software (DO-178C). Non-propulsion systems—landing gear,
avionics, environmental control, cabin interiors—are sourced from
established aerospace tier-1 suppliers.

### Assembly Sequence

1. **Fuselage assembly:** Aluminum barrels joined, windows/doors installed.
2. **Wing assembly:** Spar box with integral battery bays; ribs, stringers,
   skins, high-lift devices.
3. **Empennage assembly:** Vertical and horizontal stabilizers built and
   mated.
4. **Major join:** Wing-to-fuselage, empennage attachment, landing gear.
5. **Propulsion installation:** Motors hung on wing nacelles, wiring
   harnesses routed, turbogenerators installed in aft fuselage.
6. **Battery installation:** Modules installed in belly fairing and wing
   bays with thermal plumbing and BMS wiring.
7. **Cabin installation:** Interiors, seats, galley, lavatory.
8. **Systems testing:** Power-on, flight control checks, landing gear
   swings, pressurization leak checks, avionics integration.
9. **Ground runs:** Electric motor full-power runs, turbogenerator starts,
   battery charge/discharge cycles.
10. **Production flight test:** 2–4 flights per aircraft before delivery.

## Production Rate

The Gothenburg pilot plant targets 12–24 aircraft per year—one to two per
month, comparable to early production of ATR 42 and Embraer ERJ programs.
The site layout accommodates additional assembly stations for rate scaling.
If demand exceeds Gothenburg capacity, a second final assembly line would
likely be established in the United States given the order book composition
and LA headquarters.

## Workforce and Supply Chain

Workforce draws from former Saab aerospace employees with regional-aircraft
experience, Swedish technical university and vocational graduates, and
international aerospace talent. Supply-chain strategy emphasizes geographic
diversification (European and North American sources), dual sourcing for
critical components, strategic buffer stock, and propulsion vertical
integration as a buffer against third-party supplier delays.

## Sustainability in Manufacturing

Gothenburg plant operations use Sweden's low-carbon electricity grid.
Initiatives include aluminum and composite recycling, battery lifecycle
management with second-life stationary storage applications, and
sustainable aviation fuel for production flight tests.
