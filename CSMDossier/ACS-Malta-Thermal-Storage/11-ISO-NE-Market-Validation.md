# ISO-NE Evaluation & Market Validation

## ISO New England Overview

ISO New England (ISO-NE) is the independent system operator (ISO) and regional transmission organization (RTO) responsible for ensuring the reliable operation of the bulk electric power system and administering wholesale electricity markets across the six New England states: Connecticut, Maine, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, Rhode Island, and Vermont. ISO-NE serves a peak demand of approximately 25,000 MW (winter-peaking) and a population of approximately 15 million people.

## New England's Unique Grid Challenges

New England's electricity grid faces distinct challenges that make long-duration energy storage particularly valuable:

### 1. Natural Gas Dependency and Pipeline Constraints
Natural gas accounts for approximately 50–55% of New England's electricity generation, the highest percentage of any US region. However, the region has limited natural gas pipeline capacity, constrained by:
- Physical bottlenecks on the Algonquin Gas Transmission and Tennessee Gas Pipeline systems
- Regulatory and political opposition to new pipeline construction
- Competition between heating and power generation for gas pipeline capacity during winter cold snaps

During extreme winter weather (e.g., the Bomb Cyclone of December 2017–January 2018), natural gas prices in New England spiked to over $100/MMBtu (vs. a typical $3–5/MMBtu), and some generators were unable to procure gas at any price, forcing ISO-NE to implement emergency operating procedures. Long-duration storage can mitigate this fuel security risk by reducing reliance on natural-gas-fired generation during multi-day cold snaps.

### 2. Limited Pumped Storage Hydro Potential
Unlike regions with abundant pumped storage hydro (PSH) resources (e.g., the Southeast with TVA's Raccoon Mountain, the West with BPA's Grand Coulee and numerous California PSH plants), New England has very limited PSH potential due to:
- Topographic constraints (limited elevation differential and suitable reservoir sites)
- Environmental constraints (potential impacts on rivers, fisheries, and protected lands)
- The region's only major PSH facility is the Northfield Mountain station in Massachusetts (1,080 MW), commissioned in 1972

This geographic limitation makes siting-flexible LDES technologies like PHES particularly valuable in New England.

### 3. Offshore Wind Integration
New England states have committed to aggressive offshore wind procurement targets:
- Massachusetts: 5,600 MW by 2027 (expanded from original 1,600 MW target)
- Rhode Island: 600–1,000 MW
- Connecticut: 2,000 MW by 2030

Offshore wind generation is characterized by multi-day variability (storm systems that increase wind speeds, followed by calm periods). This variability occurs on timescales of 12–72+ hours, which exceeds the economic duration range of lithium-ion batteries (1–4 hours) and aligns well with PHES's target duration range.

## ISO-NE Long-Duration Storage Evaluation

### Context
In 2021–2022, ISO-NE undertook a formal evaluation of long-duration energy storage technologies as part of its long-term system planning process. The evaluation was motivated by:
- Massachusetts' 2021 climate law (An Act Creating a Next-Generation Roadmap for Massachusetts Climate Policy), which requires the state to achieve net-zero emissions by 2050
- ISO-NE's 2021 "Future Grid Reliability Study," which analyzed resource adequacy under deep decarbonization scenarios and identified the need for multi-day energy storage resources
- The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission's (FERC) Order 841 (2018) and subsequent technical conferences on energy storage integration, which directed RTOs/ISOs to accommodate storage resources in their market designs

### Malta's Participation
Malta Inc participated in ISO-NE's evaluation process, submitting detailed technical and economic information about the PHES technology. The evaluation addressed multiple stakeholder questions:

1. **Resource Adequacy Contribution:**
   - ISO-NE evaluated whether PHES could qualify as a capacity resource in the Forward Capacity Market (FCM), which requires resources to demonstrate the ability to deliver power during system stress conditions
   - The evaluation assessed PHES's ability to sustain output for the required duration during winter peak conditions (the ISO-NE peak is winter, not summer, unlike most other US regions)
   - PHES's 10+ hour discharge capability at rated power was evaluated as potentially exceeding the minimum duration requirements for full capacity accreditation

2. **Operational Characteristics:**
   - ISO-NE modeled PHES's charge/discharge behavior, including round-trip efficiency (assumed conservatively at 55–60%), ramp rate, minimum stable output, and self-discharge characteristics
   - The evaluation found that PHES is not suitable for fast frequency response (which requires sub-second response) but is well-suited for energy arbitrage, resource adequacy, and daily/multi-day shifting

3. **Market Participation Model:**
   - ISO-NE assessed how PHES would participate in day-ahead and real-time energy markets, the Forward Capacity Market (FCM), and the regulation market
   - The evaluation considered whether existing market rules (e.g., FERC Order 841 compliance) adequately accommodate PHES's specific characteristics, including state-of-charge management for long-duration assets, multi-day optimization horizons, and thermal constraints

4. **Cost and Revenue Modeling:**
   - ISO-NE's modeling evaluated the projected levelized cost of storage for PHES and estimated net revenues from participation in ISO-NE markets
   - Under base-case assumptions, standalone merchant revenue (energy arbitrage + capacity market) was insufficient to justify PHES investment; however, with the IRA 30% ITC and in combination with state-sponsored clean energy procurements, the economic case strengthened considerably

### Outcomes and Significance

The ISO-NE evaluation's primary value to Malta was not a procurement commitment but independent validation:

- **Third-party technical credibility:** ISO-NE's technical staff — who are responsible for ensuring the reliability of a grid serving 15 million people — did not identify any fundamental technical barriers to PHES integration
- **Market design roadmap:** The evaluation identified specific market rule modifications or clarifications that ISO-NE and stakeholders would need to implement to fully accommodate long-duration storage, providing Malta and other LDES developers with a clear regulatory engagement roadmap
- **Investor and utility confidence:** The ISO-NE evaluation provides a credible, independent reference point for other RTOs/ISOs, utilities, and project finance lenders evaluating PHES technology

## Market Validation Beyond ISO-NE

While the ISO-NE evaluation is the most publicized, Malta has also engaged with other market operators:

### CAISO (California ISO)
CAISO operates the grid for most of California and faces the most extreme renewable integration challenges in the United States:
- The "duck curve" creates 13+ GW of 3-hour ramping need every evening as solar generation declines and demand increases
- CPUC has ordered the procurement of 1 GW of LDES by 2026 and up to 4 GW by 2030
- CAISO has developed specific market participation models for energy storage resources, including state-of-charge optimization and non-generator resource (NGR) modeling

### NYISO (New York ISO)
NYISO's market includes New York State's Climate Leadership and Community Protection Act (CLCPA) mandate for 6 GW of energy storage by 2030, with specific provisions for long-duration storage. NYISO has conducted its own LDES evaluation as part of the "Grid of the Future" planning process and the Public Service Commission's "Zero Emission Credit" proceeding.

### SPP (Southwest Power Pool)
SPP operates in the central US and has experienced rapid wind generation growth (wind is frequently 50%+ of instantaneous generation). SPP's integrated transmission planning process has identified the need for storage resources to manage wind curtailment and transmission congestion, with long-duration storage being evaluated as a potential non-wires alternative to transmission investment.

## Path to Commercial Offtake

Malta's market validation strategy is focused on converting these RTO/ISO evaluations into commercial offtake agreements:

1. **Utility bilateral contracts:** Long-term power purchase agreements (PPAs) or energy storage service agreements with individual load-serving entities, similar to the utility-scale solar PPA model that drove solar deployment in the 2010s
2. **State-sponsored procurements:** Competitive solicitations under state LDES mandates (California, New York, Massachusetts, Oregon, Virginia), where Malta can submit PHES project proposals
3. **Merchant revenue with financial hedging:** For projects in organized markets, a combination of capacity market revenues (providing a base revenue floor) and energy arbitrage revenues (variable, but can be hedged through financial transmission rights or virtual power purchase agreements)
4. **DOE-supported demonstration projects:** Leveraging federal grant funding (OCED LDES demonstrations) to partially finance first commercial projects that provide operational data for subsequent fully-commercial projects
