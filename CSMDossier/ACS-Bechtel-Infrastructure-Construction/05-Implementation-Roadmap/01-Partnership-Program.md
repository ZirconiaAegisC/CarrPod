
# IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP
## ACS-BECHTEL-INFRASTRUCTURE-CONSTRUCTION
### AEGIS Standards Partnership Program

**CLASSIFICATION: COMMERCIAL-IN-CONFIDENCE**

**Phase 1 — Joint Specification (Months 1-6, ACS cost: $2.5M):**
- Month 1-2: ACS materials team embedded at Bechtel Reston HQ. Joint development of BFRP rebar specification, geopolymer concrete mix design, and dielectric bearing pad standard.
- Month 3-4: Material testing at Bechtel Materials Testing Laboratory. ASTM/AASHTO qualification testing for BFRP rebar. Compressive strength, resistivity, and durability testing for geopolymer concrete.
- Month 5-6: AEGIS certification framework development. Pilot project selection from Bechtel's active project portfolio. Target: a major bridge or power plant with $500M-1B total value, $50-75M hardening scope.

**Phase 2 — Pilot Project (Months 7-24, ACS cost: $8.5M):**
- Month 7-12: Pilot project design integration. BFRP rebar substitution in bridge deck design. Geopolymer concrete specification for piers/abutments. Dielectric bearing pad integration.
- Month 13-24: Construction phase. ACS QA/QC team on-site. MXene material supply and logistics. AEGIS certification audit.

**Phase 3 — Commercial Scale (Year 2+, ACS licensing revenue model):**
- AEGIS standards integrated into Bechtel standard specifications
- Bechtel project managers trained on AEGIS certification requirements
- ACS provides MXene material supply chain at commercial scale
- Joint marketing: "AEGIS-Ready Infrastructure — Built by Bechtel"

**Key Milestones:**
- Month 6: Specifications complete, pilot selected
- Month 24: First AEGIS-certified project completed
- Year 3: Standards integrated into Bechtel standard specs
- Year 5: $17B+ annual market for AEGIS-certified construction
