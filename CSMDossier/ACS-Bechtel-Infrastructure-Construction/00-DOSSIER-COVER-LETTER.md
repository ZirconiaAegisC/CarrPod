## II. KEY PROPOSED STANDARDS

1. **BFRP Rebar Specification:** Basalt fiber reinforced polymer (BFRP) rebar as a replacement for steel rebar in all new bridge decks, tunnel linings, and marine structures. BFRP is dielectric (non-conductive) — it cannot carry GIC. CFM-proprietary MXene coating enhances BFRP-to-concrete bonding and adds EMI shielding. Cost premium: 8-15% over steel rebar. Lifecycle benefit: corrosion-free service life (>100 years vs. 40-75 for steel).

2. **Geopolymer Concrete Specification:** Low-carbon geopolymer concrete (fly-ash/ slag-based) with CSM-MX-2400 MXene admixture for enhanced electrical resistivity and EMI shielding. Replaces Portland cement concrete in foundations, bridge piers, and containment structures. Dielectric advantage: electrical resistivity 100-1,000x higher than standard concrete, eliminating concrete as a GIC conduction path.

3. **Dielectric Bearing Pads:** All bridge bearings, expansion joints, and structural isolation pads specified with dielectric elastomeric materials incorporating MXene filler. Prevents GIC from traversing between bridge deck and substructure.

4. **AEGIS-Ready Certification Program:** Tiered certification for infrastructure projects — AEGIS Silver (electrical infrastructure hardening only), AEGIS Gold (structural + electrical hardening), AEGIS Platinum (full-site GIC immunity including protonic backup communications).
