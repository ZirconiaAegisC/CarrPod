# CRUCIS U.S. MANUFACTURING: CAPABILITIES & INFRASTRUCTURE

## OPERATIONAL OVERVIEW

Crucis Companies represents Palladyne AI's precision manufacturing division, comprising two
operational subsidiaries: Warnke Manufacturing and MKR Manufacturing. Both entities maintain
facilities in the United States with ITAR registration, AS9100D aerospace quality management
certification, and multi-decade operating histories serving Tier 1 defense prime contractors.

## WARNKE MANUFACTURING: CAPABILITIES MATRIX

Warnke's manufacturing floor encompasses a comprehensive suite of precision machining
capabilities optimized for aerospace and defense production volumes (low-to-mid hundreds of
units annually, with surge capacity for program spikes):

### CNC Machining
- 5-axis simultaneous CNC machining centers capable of producing complex airframe and
  propulsion components from aluminum, titanium, Inconel, and stainless steel alloys
- Tolerances routinely held to +/- 0.0005 inches on critical feature dimensions
- In-house CMM (Coordinate Measuring Machine) capability for first-article inspection and
  statistical process control

### Fabrication
- CNC press brake, laser cutting, and waterjet capability for sheet metal components
- Welding certifications including AWS D17.1 (aerospace fusion welding) for aluminum,
  stainless steel, and titanium alloys
- NADCAP-accredited special processes including penetrant inspection and chemical processing

### Assembly & Integration
- Clean room assembly capability for contamination-sensitive components
- Torque-controlled fastening with digital traceability
- Functional testing of mechanical assemblies prior to shipment

### Quality Management
- AS9100D certified quality management system
- First Article Inspection (FAI) per AS9102 standard
- Full material traceability from mill certification through finished part shipment
- Government Contract Quality Assurance (GCQA) compliant for DoD direct procurement

## MKR MANUFACTURING: PROGRAM CONCENTRATION

MKR's operations are distinguished by their concentration in specific weapon system programs
with multi-year procurement backlogs:

### Tomahawk Block V
Tomahawk is the U.S. Navy's primary long-range precision-strike cruise missile, deployed from
surface ships and submarines. The Block V upgrade program — encompassing the Maritime Strike
Tomahawk (MST) variant for anti-ship missions and the Block Va with enhanced navigation and
communication — represents a multi-billion dollar, 10+ year procurement commitment. MKR
manufactures precision-machined components for the navigation section, control actuation
system, and airframe structural elements.

### Harpoon Block II+
The Harpoon anti-ship missile, in continuous production since 1977, remains the most widely
deployed Western anti-ship missile. The Block II+ upgrade adds GPS guidance, improved
seekers, and networked targeting capability. MKR produces propulsion system components,
control surface actuators, and warhead section structural elements.

### Ground-Based Missile Defense
MKR supplies components for interceptor missiles deployed as part of the Ground-based
Midcourse Defense (GMD) system, including machined elements for the Exoatmospheric Kill
Vehicle (EKV) and booster separation systems. These components require the highest-level
material certifications and inspection protocols in MKR's portfolio.

## MANUFACTURING CAPACITY AND SCALABILITY

A key analytical question for Crucis is the extent of available manufacturing capacity to
support Palladyne's internal requirements (Gremlin-X airframes, DECA hardware enclosures,
avionics housings) without degrading service to external customers. Palladyne has not
publicly disclosed the capacity utilization rate of Crucis facilities.

The dual-use nature of Crucis's capabilities — serving both external defense prime customers
and Palladyne's internal hardware production — creates both opportunity and tension. Internal
demand could cannibalize revenue if it displaces higher-margin external work; alternatively,
it could improve facility utilization if the facilities were previously operating below
optimal capacity.

## STRATEGIC VALUE IN THE DEFENSE INDUSTRIAL BASE

The DoD's 2022 National Defense Industrial Strategy explicitly identified precision machining
and casting as a critical supply chain vulnerability, noting that the U.S. has lost
approximately 40% of its precision machining capacity since 2000. By acquiring and maintaining
Crucis, Palladyne positions itself as a beneficiary of the onshoring trend and DoD initiatives
including:

- Defense Production Act Title III investments in domestic manufacturing capacity
- Industrial Base Analysis and Sustainment (IBAS) program funding
- Multi-year procurement authority that primes use to commit to long-term supplier contracts

Crucis's ability to serve multiple prime contractors across multiple programs also provides an
insulation effect: a slowdown in one program (e.g., F-35 production rate adjustments) can be
offset by demand from another (e.g., Tomahawk replenishment).
