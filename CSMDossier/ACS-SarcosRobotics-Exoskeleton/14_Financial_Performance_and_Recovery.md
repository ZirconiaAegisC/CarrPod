# FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE & RECOVERY ANALYSIS

## HISTORICAL FINANCIAL TRAJECTORY

Palladyne AI's financial history reflects the transition from a pre-revenue industrial
robotics startup (Sarcos Robotics) to a revenue-generating defense technology integrator.
The company went public through a SPAC merger in 2021-2022, a path that provided capital
for the legacy exoskeleton business but also embedded the stock with the structural
challenges common to SPAC-era public listings: elevated redemption rates, warrant
overhangs, and a shareholder base not necessarily aligned with the company's subsequent
strategic transformation.

### FY2025 Performance

Total FY2025 revenue of approximately $5.2 million reflected the early-stage integration of
the GuideTech and Crucis acquisitions, which closed in November 2025, meaning their
contribution to FY2025 revenue was limited to approximately one month of combined operations.
The pre-acquisition revenue base — primarily Palladyne IQ software licenses and residual
Guardian-related income — was minimal.

Gross margins for FY2025 were not meaningfully disclosed by segment, but the overall
financial profile reflects a company still sub-scale relative to its public-company cost
structure. Operating expenses, including R&D for SwarmOS and Palladyne Pilot development,
SG&A for corporate infrastructure, and integration costs related to the three acquisitions,
substantially exceeded gross profit.

### Q2 2026 Preliminary Results

In advance of full Q2 2026 reporting, Palladyne disclosed preliminary revenue of
approximately $5.8 million, representing year-over-year growth of approximately 480%. This
growth rate, while striking on a percentage basis, must be contextualized against the low
base in Q2 2025 (approximately $1.0 million) when the company had not yet closed the
acquisitions.

The Q2 2026 preliminary revenue suggests that the combined entity is approaching a quarterly
revenue run rate consistent with the low end of the FY2026 guidance range of $24-27 million
($5.8 million quarterly times four = $23.2 million). Achieving the guidance midpoint would
require sequential growth in Q3 and Q4, driven by:

- Increased deliveries of Gremlin-X and IAI-licensed loitering munitions against the backlog
- Growing Crucis manufacturing output as production schedules accelerate
- Potential additional USAF or other DoD contract revenue recognition

## BALANCE SHEET POSITION

As of the most recent reporting period, Palladyne's balance sheet reflects:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Cash and Equivalents | ~$44 million |
| Total Assets | ~$95.7 million |
| Goodwill and Intangible Assets | Undisclosed (significant, reflecting acquisitions) |
| Debt | Limited (company has not disclosed material debt) |
| Working Capital | Adequate for near-term operational needs |

The $44 million cash position provides a runway of approximately 18-24 months at current
operating expense levels, assuming no material improvement in operating cash flow. This
runway is critical given the company's pre-profitability status and the need to fund:

- Continued investment in SwarmOS and Palladyne Pilot development
- Working capital for loitering munition production ramp
- Potential earnout payments related to the GuideTech acquisition
- General corporate expenses of a public reporting company

## CASH CONSUMPTION AND PATH TO BREAKEVEN

Palladyne has not publicly disclosed a specific timeline to EBITDA breakeven or cash flow
breakeven. The company's cash consumption rate depends on several variables:

1. **Revenue Growth Rate** — The FY2026 guidance of $24-27 million, if achieved, would
   represent a significant step toward operating leverage, but is unlikely to achieve
   full absorption of corporate overhead.

2. **Gross Margin Profile** — The blended gross margin across Palladyne Defense hardware
   (lower margin, manufacturing-intensive), Crucis precision manufacturing (moderate
   margin, stable), and Palladyne IQ software (higher margin, low volume) will determine
   how much incremental revenue contributes to overhead absorption.

3. **R&D Investment** — The company continues to invest in SwarmOS, Palladyne Pilot, and
   DECA, with R&D expenses likely representing a significant portion of operating costs.
   Decisions about capitalizing versus expensing R&D (per ASC 730 and ASC 985) affect
   reported profitability but not cash consumption.

## DILUTION RISK

With a market capitalization of approximately $130-150 million (based on ~20-22 million
shares outstanding at ~$6.72), the company has limited equity-raising capacity without
significant dilution. A secondary offering at current levels would be dilutive and
potentially difficult to execute given the 21.5% short interest, which indicates
substantial skepticism about the company's prospects among institutional investors.

The company has access to a shelf registration statement from its SPAC-era filings,
providing the mechanism for future equity raises if needed, though management has not
signaled near-term financing intentions.
