# e-Zinc — Technology Roadmap & Commercialization Strategy

## Commercialization Pathway

e-Zinc's commercialization strategy follows a deliberate, staged progression
designed to de-risk the technology and build customer confidence before
committing to high-volume manufacturing:

| Phase | Timeline | Activities | Key Milestones |
|-------|----------|------------|---------------|
| Lab validation | 2012-2018 | Fundamental electrochemistry, electrode design | Proof of reversibility, patent filings |
| Prototype | 2019-2022 | Scaled single cells, system architecture | >1,000 cycles demonstrated in lab |
| Pilot | 2023-2024 | Ontario grid-connected demonstration | Real-world performance validation |
| Pre-commercial | 2024-2026 | California deployment, product design freeze | Third-party validated performance data |
| Early commercial | 2026-2028 | First commercial orders, manufacturing partnership | Initial revenue, reference projects |
| Scale | 2028-2031 | Dedicated manufacturing facility, volume production | <$100/kWh cost target achieved |
| Mature | 2031+ | Multiple manufacturing sites, global deployment | >1 GWh/year production capacity |

## Technology Roadmap

### Generation 1 (Current — Pilot/Pre-Commercial)

- 3D zinc electrode architecture with demonstrated >1,000 cycle life
- Bifunctional air cathode with non-precious metal catalysts
- Containerized system integration
- Manual/semi-automated cell assembly

### Generation 2 (2027-2029 — Early Commercial)

- Improved electrode cycle life: >3,000 cycles validated
- Higher round-trip efficiency: >75% target
- Optimized air cathode with reduced overpotential
- Semi-automated manufacturing line
- Standardized product configurations for utility procurement

### Generation 3 (2030+ — Volume Production)

- Advanced electrode architecture: >5,000 cycle life, >20 year calendar life
- Next-generation bifunctional catalysts (perovskite, spinel, or other advanced materials)
- Fully automated manufacturing with in-line quality control
- Integrated DC-DC conversion to reduce power electronics cost
- Multi-MW standardized building blocks for utility-scale deployment

## Manufacturing Strategy

e-Zinc's manufacturing strategy seeks to minimize capital requirements during
the early commercialization phase while positioning for eventual high-volume
production:

1. **Contract manufacturing (Phase 1)** — initial commercial production through
   partnership with an established battery or industrial manufacturer, reducing
   upfront capital requirements and leveraging existing quality systems and
   supply chains

2. **Hybrid model (Phase 2)** — in-house production of proprietary components
   (electrodes, cells) with outsourced balance-of-plant manufacturing
   (containers, power electronics, thermal management)

3. **Dedicated facility (Phase 3)** — vertically integrated manufacturing at
   a purpose-built facility, justified once commercial order volume provides
   sufficient confidence in capacity utilization

## Target Markets by Phase

### Near-Term (2025-2028)

- **California utilities** — driven by CPUC long-duration storage procurement
  mandates, the most attractive early market
- **Canadian utilities** — leveraging the Ontario demonstration and federal
  clean energy programs
- **Island and remote grids** — high electricity costs and diesel dependence
  create strong economics for long-duration storage paired with renewables

### Medium-Term (2028-2031)

- **U.S. utility-scale** — broader adoption as IRA tax credits and state
  renewable portfolio standards drive demand
- **Commercial & industrial** — behind-the-meter applications for load shifting
  and resilience at large energy consumers
- **European markets** — EU renewable energy targets and carbon pricing create
  favorable economics

### Long-Term (2031+)

- **Global utility-scale** — mature product competing in the global long-duration
  storage market
- **Microgrids and remote communities** — displacing diesel generation in off-grid
  and weak-grid applications worldwide
- **Strategic/military** — resilience applications for military bases and critical
  national infrastructure

## Strategic Partnerships

Key partnership categories that will accelerate commercialization:

- **Utility partnerships** — demonstration projects and offtake agreements that
  provide reference customers and revenue visibility
- **Manufacturing partners** — contract manufacturers with established quality
  systems and supply chains
- **Renewable developers** — co-located storage with new wind and solar projects
- **Government agencies** — DOE, CEC, NRCan for grant funding, technical
  validation, and procurement preference

## Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|---------|-----------|
| Technology underperformance | High | Extensive testing, third-party validation, conservative specifications |
| Manufacturing scale-up failure | High | Contract manufacturing initially, gradual vertical integration |
| Competitive displacement | Medium | Focus on duration segments where zinc-air has structural cost advantage |
| Supply chain disruption | Low | Commodity materials with diverse supply, limited single-source dependencies |
| Policy/regulatory changes | Medium | Geographic diversification, participation in multiple regulatory regimes |
| Capital availability | Medium | Staged commercialization, non-dilutive government funding, strategic partnerships |
