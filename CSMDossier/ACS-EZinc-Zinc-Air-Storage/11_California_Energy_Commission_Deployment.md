# e-Zinc — California Energy Commission Deployment

## California Market Opportunity

California represents the most attractive near-term market for long-duration
energy storage in North America, and arguably globally. The state's energy
policy framework creates multiple drivers for storage deployment:

- **SB 100 (2018)** — 100% carbon-free electricity by 2045, with interim
  targets of 60% by 2030
- **CPUC storage procurement** — the California Public Utilities Commission
  has ordered utilities to procure significant quantities of long-duration
  storage (8+ hours) as part of integrated resource planning
- **High renewable penetration** — California already generates over 30% of
  its electricity from solar and wind, creating the "duck curve" challenge
  that storage is uniquely positioned to address
- **Retiring gas generation** — the state is actively retiring natural gas
  peaking plants and once-through-cooling thermal generation, creating
  resource adequacy gaps that storage must fill
- **Extreme weather resilience** — wildfire-related public safety power
  shutoffs and heat waves create demand for multi-day backup power

## California Energy Commission Grant

e-Zinc received a grant from the **California Energy Commission** to deploy
a grid-scale demonstration project in California. The CEC's Electric Program
Investment Charge program funds clean energy technologies that align with
California's policy goals and that are approaching commercial readiness.

## Deployment Objectives

The California deployment advances beyond the Ontario pilot in several key
dimensions:

1. **Larger scale** — increased power and energy capacity, moving from pilot
   to pre-commercial system size
2. **Commercial operating profile** — dispatching against real California grid
   conditions, including the solar ramp and evening peak
3. **Regulatory qualification** — generating performance data suitable for
   CPUC resource adequacy qualification, a prerequisite for utility procurement
4. **Technology iteration** — incorporating design improvements identified
   during the Ontario pilot

## California-Specific Value Propositions

The California deployment is designed to demonstrate value across multiple
California-specific use cases:

### Solar Integration & Duck Curve Management

California's solar generation routinely drives midday wholesale electricity
prices to zero or negative values, followed by a steep evening ramp as solar
output declines and demand increases. Long-duration storage can:

- Absorb midday solar generation that would otherwise be curtailed
- Discharge during the evening peak (4 PM to 9 PM)
- Extend discharge through the night to the next day's solar ramp

### Wildfire Resilience & Public Safety Power Shutoffs

During high-fire-risk conditions, California utilities may proactively
de-energize transmission and distribution lines to prevent wildfire ignitions.
These public safety power shutoffs can last 24-72 hours or longer. Long-
duration storage at critical facilities (hospitals, emergency operations
centers, community resource centers) can provide multi-day backup power
without the fuel supply constraints of diesel generators.

### Resource Adequacy

California's resource adequacy framework requires load-serving entities to
demonstrate sufficient capacity to meet peak demand plus a planning reserve
margin. Storage systems must be capable of discharging for a defined duration
(typically 4 hours for current requirements, with longer durations under
consideration) to qualify. e-Zinc's system, with 12-100+ hour capability,
exceeds all current and anticipated resource adequacy duration requirements.

## Regulatory Pathway

The California deployment includes a deliberate focus on regulatory qualification:

- **CPUC Rule 21 interconnection** — compliance with California's distributed
  generation interconnection standards
- **Resource adequacy qualification** — demonstrating capability to meet CPUC
  requirements for capacity accreditation
- **CAISO market participation** — if applicable, qualifying for participation
  in the California Independent System Operator's energy and ancillary services
  markets
