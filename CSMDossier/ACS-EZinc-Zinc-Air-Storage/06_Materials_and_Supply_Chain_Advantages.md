# e-Zinc — Materials & Supply Chain Advantages

## Zinc: Abundant and Inexpensive

Zinc is the **fourth most mined metal on Earth**, after iron, aluminum, and
copper. Global zinc production exceeds 13 million tonnes annually, with major
producers including China, Peru, Australia, India, and the United States. This
abundance translates directly into low and stable pricing:

| Metal | Approximate Price ($/kg) | Primary Battery Application | Supply Risk |
|-------|------------------------|---------------------------|-------------|
| Zinc | ~$3 | e-Zinc zinc-air | Low — diverse global production |
| Lithium (carbonate equivalent) | ~$15-70 | Li-ion cathode | Moderate — concentrated processing |
| Cobalt | ~$30-35 | Li-ion cathode (NMC, NCA) | High — DRC dependence |
| Nickel | ~$15-20 | Li-ion cathode | Moderate |
| Vanadium | ~$25-40 | Vanadium redox flow | High — limited production |
| Iron | ~$0.10 | Iron-air (Form Energy) | Low — most abundant metal |

At $3/kg, the zinc content of a 100 kWh storage system represents approximately
$150-300 of material cost — a small fraction of the total system cost and vastly
less than the equivalent lithium, cobalt, or vanadium requirement for competing
technologies at similar energy capacity.

## No Critical Minerals Dependency

A defining characteristic of e-Zinc's supply chain is the complete absence of
materials classified as "critical minerals" by the U.S. Department of Energy or
the European Union:

- **No lithium** — avoiding the concentrated supply chain (Australia, Chile,
  China) and projected demand-supply deficits as electric vehicle production
  scales
- **No cobalt** — bypassing the Democratic Republic of Congo's dominant market
  position and associated human rights concerns
- **No rare earth elements** — free from the supply chain concentration (China
  dominance in processing) and geopolitical risk that characterize rare earth
  markets
- **No graphite** — eliminating dependence on natural or synthetic graphite supply
  chains

## Supply Chain Resilience

e-Zinc's materials strategy delivers inherent supply chain resilience through
several mechanisms:

1. **Commodity pricing** — with the exception of specialized catalyst materials,
   all major inputs are traded on established commodity markets with transparent
   pricing and futures hedging capability

2. **Multi-source availability** — every significant material input is available
   from multiple suppliers across multiple continents, eliminating single-source
   dependency

3. **Recyclability** — zinc is one of the most recycled metals globally, with
   established recycling infrastructure and economics. End-of-life battery zinc
   can re-enter the commodity zinc market or be reused directly in new batteries

4. **Geopolitical de-risking** — unlike lithium-ion supply chains (dominated
   by China in processing) or rare earth supply chains (dominated by China in
   both mining and processing), zinc supply is geographically diversified

## Manufacturing Scalability

The materials choices enable manufacturing scalability that would be difficult
or impossible for technologies dependent on constrained materials:

- **No coating-drying-solvent recovery bottlenecks** — unlike lithium-ion
  electrode manufacturing, which requires energy-intensive NMP solvent recovery
  systems, e-Zinc's electrode fabrication processes operate at or near ambient
  conditions
- **Ambient assembly environment** — unlike lithium-ion cells that require
  expensive dry-room environments for electrolyte filling, e-Zinc's aqueous
  electrolyte can be handled in conventional manufacturing environments
- **No formation cycling bottleneck** — lithium-ion cells require days of
  initial formation cycling to build the solid-electrolyte interphase; e-Zinc
  cells require substantially shorter or no formation process

## Environmental & Regulatory Advantages

The materials platform provides several advantages in environmental and regulatory
compliance:

- **No toxic or flammable solvents** — aqueous electrolyte eliminates the NMP
  and organic carbonate solvents used in lithium-ion manufacturing
- **No PFAS** — unlike some flow battery membranes and lithium-ion binders,
  e-Zinc's system can be formulated without perfluorinated compounds
- **Inherently non-flammable** — the aqueous system cannot experience thermal
  runaway, simplifying permitting for grid-scale installations
- **Recyclable at end of life** — the primary materials (zinc, steel, copper)
  all have established recycling streams
