# e-Zinc — Company Overview & History

## Founding Origins

e-Zinc was founded in **2012** as a spinout from **TeTechS Inc.**, a technology
consulting and research firm based in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. The company's
founding was catalyzed by the recognition that zinc — the fourth most mined metal
on Earth, with an established global supply chain and well-understood electrochemistry
— could serve as the basis for a new class of rechargeable battery purpose-built
for long-duration energy storage. The initial years were devoted to fundamental
research on zinc-air electrochemical reversibility, a problem that had eluded
practical solution for decades.

## Evolution from Research to Commercialization

| Period | Focus | Key Outcomes |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 2012-2016 | Fundamental R&D | Electrode architecture patents, cycle life proof-of-concept |
| 2017-2020 | Prototype development | Lab-scale cells demonstrating >1,000 cycles, system architecture definition |
| 2021-2023 | Pilot scale | Ontario grid demonstration, California Energy Commission grant |
| 2024-Present | Commercial preparation | Manufacturing partnerships, project pipeline development |

## Headquartered in Toronto, Ontario

e-Zinc's Toronto headquarters places the company within Canada's largest clean
technology cluster, providing access to:

- **University of Toronto** — one of the world's leading electrochemical research
  institutions, with deep expertise in metal-air battery science
- **Ontario's advanced manufacturing ecosystem** — automotive and industrial
  manufacturing supply chains that can support battery production scale-up
- **Clean energy policy environment** — Ontario's electricity grid, with its
  significant nuclear and hydroelectric baseload, provides an ideal testing
  ground for long-duration energy storage technologies that can shift renewable
  energy across multi-day periods

## Mission & Vision

e-Zinc's mission is to enable a fully renewable electricity grid by providing
cost-effective, safe, and sustainable long-duration energy storage. The company's
vision positions zinc-air batteries as the dominant technology for applications
requiring 12 to 100+ hours of discharge duration — a market segment that
lithium-ion batteries cannot economically serve and that emerging competitors
are only beginning to address.

## Capitalization & Investment

e-Zinc has raised over **$30 million** in venture capital and grant funding.
Key investors include:

- **Anzu Partners** — a venture capital firm investing in breakthrough industrial
  technologies with a focus on clean energy and advanced materials
- **BDC Capital** — the Business Development Bank of Canada's venture arm,
  providing patient capital for Canadian technology companies
- **Toyota Ventures** — the corporate venture arm of Toyota Motor Corporation,
  reflecting automotive industry interest in long-duration storage for vehicle-
  to-grid integration and manufacturing electrification
- **California Energy Commission** — grant funding for grid-scale demonstration
  projects in California, validating the technology's value proposition in a
  market with aggressive renewable portfolio standards

## Strategic Context

e-Zinc operates in the rapidly emerging long-duration energy storage market,
which the U.S. Department of Energy defines as storage systems capable of
discharging for 10 hours or more. The DOE's Long Duration Storage Shot targets
90% cost reduction by 2030, aiming for a levelized cost of storage of $0.05/kWh.
Grid modeling by NREL and other institutions consistently identifies long-duration
storage as the critical missing technology for achieving very high renewable
electricity penetration levels — exactly the mission e-Zinc was founded to address.
