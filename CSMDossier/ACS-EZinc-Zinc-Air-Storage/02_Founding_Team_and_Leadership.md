# e-Zinc — Founding Team & Leadership

## CEO: James Larsen

**James Larsen** serves as Chief Executive Officer of e-Zinc, leading the company's
transition from research-stage technology development to commercial product delivery.
Larsen brings experience in clean technology commercialization and has guided the
company through multiple financing rounds, the Ontario grid demonstration project,
and the California market entry strategy. Under his leadership, e-Zinc has
established strategic partnerships with utilities, project developers, and
government agencies in both Canada and the United States.

## Leadership Capabilities

The e-Zinc executive team combines expertise in:

- **Electrochemical engineering** — deep knowledge of metal-air battery chemistry,
  electrode design, and system integration, drawing from both academic research
  and industrial battery development experience
- **Power systems engineering** — understanding of grid interconnection
  requirements, power conversion systems, and utility-scale energy storage project
  development
- **Manufacturing** — experience scaling materials processing and assembly
  operations from laboratory to pilot to commercial production volumes
- **Corporate development** — capability in venture financing, government grant
  programs, strategic partnerships, and intellectual property management

## Scientific Advisory Foundation

e-Zinc maintains close ties with the electrochemical research community at the
University of Toronto and other Canadian research institutions. The company's
technology is built on fundamental advances in zinc electrode reversibility —
specifically, the engineering of electrode architectures that prevent dendrite
formation and shape change, the two primary failure mechanisms that have
historically prevented practical rechargeable zinc batteries.

## Company Culture

e-Zinc's organizational culture balances scientific rigor — inherited from its
research-spinout origins — with the commercial urgency required to succeed in
the competitive energy storage market. The company emphasizes:

- **Evidence-based decision-making** — technical claims must be supported by
  experimental data, with an emphasis on third-party validated performance metrics
- **Safety-first design** — the inherent safety advantages of zinc-air chemistry
  (no thermal runaway, no flammable electrolytes) are treated as a core product
  attribute, not an afterthought
- **Cost discipline** — every design decision is evaluated against the target
  of <$100/kWh installed cost, reflecting the reality that long-duration storage
  economics depend fundamentally on capital cost per unit of energy capacity
