# TerraPower: Technology Portfolio — TWR, MCFR, and Future Concepts

## Beyond Natrium: TerraPower's Technology Pipeline

While the Natrium sodium-cooled fast reactor with integrated energy storage
is TerraPower's lead commercial product and the focus of the ARDP
demonstration project, the company maintains an active research and
development portfolio spanning multiple advanced reactor concepts. This
diversified technology pipeline reflects TerraPower's origins as a nuclear
innovation laboratory and its strategy of pursuing multiple technological
pathways to address different market segments and deployment scenarios.

## Traveling Wave Reactor (TWR)

The Traveling Wave Reactor (TWR) was TerraPower's founding concept,
announced in 2008 and the focus of the company's first decade of
development. The TWR is a sodium-cooled fast reactor designed to
operate on depleted uranium — a fertile material that constitutes
more than 90% of the mass of natural uranium after the fissile U-235
fraction has been separated through enrichment.

The TWR concept envisions a standing nuclear fission wave that propagates
slowly through a fuel column:
- An initial "seed" region of enriched uranium or plutonium initiates
  the chain reaction and produces excess neutrons.
- The excess neutrons are absorbed by a surrounding "blanket" of
  depleted uranium (primarily U-238), converting it to plutonium-239
  through neutron capture and subsequent beta decay.
- The newly bred plutonium sustains the fission reaction as the wave
  propagates through the fuel column.
- Over the core life (estimated at 40+ years without refueling), the
  reactor consumes its own bred fuel and achieves very high burnup
  of the initial fuel loading.

The TWR concept offers several theoretical advantages: (a) dramatically
reduced fuel cycle cost and uranium resource consumption, (b) utilization
of depleted uranium inventories (the U.S. DOE holds over 700,000 metric
tons of depleted UF6 at Paducah and Portsmouth), (c) elimination of
refueling outages over the plant life, and (d) intrinsic proliferation
resistance (no enrichment or reprocessing required after the initial
core loading).

**TWR Status:** TerraPower deprioritized the TWR concept as its lead
commercial product in favor of Natrium in 2019. This strategic decision
reflected the challenges of licensing a first-of-a-kind TWR (which would
have required demonstrating the traveling wave phenomenon at full scale
to the NRC) relative to the Natrium sodium fast reactor, which can rely
on established fast reactor physics without the novel wave propagation
phenomenon. The TWR remains an active R&D concept within TerraPower but
is not currently targeted for commercial deployment.

## Molten Chloride Fast Reactor (MCFR)

The Molten Chloride Fast Reactor (MCFR) is TerraPower's liquid-fueled
fast reactor concept, in which the fissile material (uranium and/or
plutonium chlorides) is dissolved in a circulating molten chloride
salt coolant that serves as both fuel and coolant. This liquid fuel
concept differs fundamentally from the solid-fueled Natrium and TWR
designs:

**Key MCFR Characteristics:**
- Liquid fuel enables continuous or batch removal of fission products
  without fuel fabrication, potentially enabling higher fuel burnup
  and reduced waste volume.
- Molten chloride salts operate at higher temperatures than sodium
  (600-800 degrees C), enabling higher thermal efficiency and process
  heat applications.
- The liquid fuel form eliminates the fuel fabrication step, which is
  a major cost and complexity driver for solid-fueled fast reactors.

**MCFR Development Status:** TerraPower is developing the MCFR in
partnership with Southern Company, with funding from the DOE's ARDP
Risk Reduction pathway. The MCFR is at a substantially earlier stage
of development than Natrium, with key technology challenges including:
chloride salt chemistry control and corrosion management, materials of
construction for extended operation in molten chloride salts, and
development of online fuel processing and fission product removal
systems.

The MCFR concept targets a different market segment than Natrium:
higher-temperature industrial process heat applications (hydrogen
production, petrochemical processing, steelmaking) where the higher
operating temperature of the MCFR provides a competitive advantage
over the sodium-cooled Natrium design. The MCFR could also serve as a
transmutation platform for consuming LWR spent fuel transuranics,
addressing the long-term waste management liability.

## Technology Portfolio Strategy

TerraPower's multi-technology portfolio strategy reflects several
strategic considerations:

1. **Regulatory Pathway Diversification:** Different reactor concepts
   face different regulatory timelines. The Natrium sodium fast reactor,
   with established global operational heritage, offers a nearer-term
   regulatory path than the more novel MCFR.

2. **Market Segmentation:** Natrium targets utility-scale grid-connected
   generation with energy storage; MCFR targets industrial process heat.

3. **Technology Risk Hedging:** If any single concept encounters a
   fundamental technical or regulatory obstacle, the portfolio
   provides alternative pathways to commercial deployment.

## Intellectual Property

TerraPower's intellectual property portfolio spans reactor physics,
fuel design, materials science, energy storage integration, and
advanced modeling and simulation. The company holds patents (or has
patent applications pending) covering the TWR wave propagation
concept, the Natrium storage-coupled dispatch architecture, metallic
fuel compositions and fabrication methods, and molten chloride salt
chemistry and materials. The company's IP position is a key asset
in its capital-raising strategy and partnership negotiations.
