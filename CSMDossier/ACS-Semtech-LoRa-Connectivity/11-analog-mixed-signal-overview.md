# 11 — Analog Mixed Signal & Wireless: Segment Overview

## Segment Definition

The Analog Mixed Signal & Wireless segment encompasses Semtech's portfolio of protection diodes and transient voltage suppression (TVS) devices, wireless charging solutions, video transport products, and LoRa-enabled radio transceivers and chipsets. This segment represents the historical core of Semtech's business and generates the largest absolute revenue contribution among the three segments.

## Product Categories

### Protection & TVS Devices

Semtech's protection products safeguard sensitive electronic circuits from electrostatic discharge (ESD), electrical overstress (EOS), lightning-induced surges, and other transient voltage events. These products are essential design elements in virtually every electronic device, from smartphones and laptops to data center switches and automotive electronics.

The protection portfolio includes:
- Low-capacitance ESD protection diodes for high-speed data interfaces (USB, HDMI, Thunderbolt, Ethernet)
- Surge protection devices for power lines, antenna ports, and industrial interfaces
- Filtered protection arrays combining ESD suppression with EMI/RFI filtering
- Automotive-qualified protection devices meeting AEC-Q100 reliability standards

### LoRa Radio Transceivers & Chipsets

Semtech's LoRa radio products are the foundational silicon for the global LoRaWAN ecosystem. The portfolio spans discrete transceivers requiring an external microcontroller for protocol stack execution, to fully integrated system-on-chip solutions combining a LoRa radio front-end with an embedded ARM microcontroller and flash memory. These products are sold to module manufacturers, gateway vendors, and directly to high-volume IoT device makers.

### Wireless Charging

Semtech offers wireless power receiver and transmitter ICs supporting the Qi and AirFuel inductive and resonant charging standards. These products enable cord-free charging for smartphones, wearables, hearables, medical devices, and industrial handheld equipment. Key technical attributes include high power conversion efficiency, foreign object detection, and multi-coil transmitter support for spatial freedom.

### Video Transport

Semtech's video products provide reliable transmission of high-resolution video over long distances using standard copper cabling. Applications include professional audio-visual installations, digital signage, medical imaging, and broadcast infrastructure. The technology enables uncompressed 4K and 8K video extension over category cable, eliminating the cost and complexity of fiber optic installations in many deployment scenarios.

## Competitive Positioning

### Protection Devices
Semtech holds a strong position in high-performance protection diodes, particularly for high-speed interfaces where ultra-low capacitance is required to avoid signal integrity degradation. The company competes with Nexperia, STMicroelectronics, Littelfuse, and ON Semiconductor. Key competitive factors include capacitance, clamping voltage, surge handling capability, package size, and reliability.

### LoRa Radios
Semtech is the dominant supplier of LoRa-enabled silicon, with an estimated market share exceeding 70% of LoRa chipset revenue. Competitors include STMicroelectronics (which licenses LoRa IP from Semtech), Microchip, and emerging Chinese alternatives. The primary competitive moat is Semtech's control of core LoRa IP and its deep experience in optimizing radio performance for the challenging requirements of LPWAN applications.

### Wireless Charging
Semtech competes with NXP, Renesas, Texas Instruments, STMicroelectronics, and specialized wireless power IC vendors. Differentiation centers on end-to-end efficiency, thermal performance, compliance with evolving Qi and AirFuel specifications, and support for high-power fast-charging use cases.

### Video Transport
Semtech targets professional and broadcast video extension applications, competing with specialized vendors including Valens Semiconductor and Analog Devices. The key technical attribute is reliable, visually lossless video transmission over 100+ meters of standard Ethernet cable.
