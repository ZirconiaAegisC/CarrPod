# 05 — Signal Integrity & Data Center Interconnects: Segment Overview

## Segment Definition

The Signal Integrity segment designs and markets high-performance analog and mixed-signal integrated circuits that ensure reliable, high-speed transmission of data across optical fiber and copper physical media. These products are critical building blocks in data center interconnects, enterprise networking equipment, telecom infrastructure, and high-performance computing systems.

## Technology Foundation

Signal integrity refers to the quality of an electrical or optical signal as it traverses a physical medium. As data rates increase, signal degradation from attenuation, reflection, crosstalk, and jitter becomes more severe. Semtech's products actively compensate for these impairments, enabling reliable operation at data rates from 25 Gbps to beyond 200 Gbps per lane. Key technology blocks include:

- Transimpedance amplifiers (TIAs) — Convert weak photodetector current into usable voltage signals at the receiver end of an optical link.
- Laser drivers — Provide precisely modulated current to drive laser diodes at the transmitter end of an optical link.
- Clock and data recovery (CDR) circuits — Extract embedded clock signals from high-speed serial data streams and retime data to remove accumulated jitter.
- Re-drivers and retimers — Condition degraded electrical signals in copper interconnects, extending reach and improving bit error rates.

## Addressable Markets

The Signal Integrity segment serves three primary end markets:

### Hyperscale Data Centers
The largest and fastest-growing opportunity. Hyperscale cloud providers and AI infrastructure operators require massive optical interconnect bandwidth as GPU clusters and AI training fabrics scale to tens of thousands of nodes. Every generation of Ethernet speed (400G → 800G → 1.6T → 3.2T) expands the total addressable market for optical interconnect ICs, as each speed transition requires higher-performance signal conditioning.

### Enterprise and Telecom Networking
Service provider and enterprise networks deploying 400G and 800G coherent optical links for metro, long-haul, and data center interconnect applications. These systems use coherent digital signal processors paired with high-bandwidth linear drivers and TIAs, a market segment where Semtech maintains a strong position.

### High-Performance Computing
On-board and chip-to-chip interconnects within servers, AI accelerators, and GPU clusters. Copper-based interconnects using Semtech's CopperEdge re-drivers and retimers enable high-bandwidth, low-power connections over short distances at a fraction of the cost and power of optical alternatives.

## Competitive Positioning

Semtech holds a leading market position in optical interconnect ICs for data center applications. Its FiberEdge product family competes with offerings from Broadcom, Marvell, and MACOM, differentiated by:
- Proven field reliability with hyperscale customer qualifications
- Broad portfolio spanning TIA, laser driver, and CDR functions
- Early engagement on next-generation data rate standards (1.6T and 3.2T)
- Flexible manufacturing via multiple foundry partners

In the copper interconnect market, Semtech's CopperEdge family competes with products from Astera Labs, Renesas, and Texas Instruments, differentiated by signal reach performance and power efficiency.

## Manufacturing Technology and Process Nodes

Signal Integrity products are fabricated on specialized mixed-signal process technologies at leading foundries. Optical interconnect ICs leverage silicon germanium (SiGe) BiCMOS processes that deliver the high-frequency performance required for 100+ Gbps PAM4 operation while integrating dense CMOS digital logic for configuration, calibration, and monitoring. CopperEdge products are fabricated on advanced CMOS nodes with proprietary equalization and signal conditioning IP.

As data rates increase to 212 Gbps per lane and beyond, process technology requirements become more demanding. Semtech works closely with foundry partners to access next-generation SiGe and advanced CMOS process nodes, and is evaluating sub-5nm CMOS processes for future 3.2T product generations. This foundry collaboration model is a key competitive capability, as the pool of foundries capable of delivering the required combination of high-frequency analog performance and high-volume manufacturing reliability is limited.

## Design-In Pipeline and Revenue Visibility

The Signal Integrity business benefits from a multi-year design-in pipeline that provides strong forward revenue visibility. Optical module manufacturers and system OEMs typically select their interconnect IC suppliers 12-18 months before production ramp, and once qualified, a supplier's position is highly defensible due to the cost and risk of re-qualification.

As of mid-FY2027, Semtech has secured design wins across multiple 1.6T optical module platforms at all major hyperscale customers, and is actively engaged in architecture definition for 3.2T modules expected to begin sampling in calendar 2027. The total addressable design-in pipeline, including booked and actively pursued opportunities, spans approximately $500-600 million in annualized revenue potential across the 800G, 1.6T, and 3.2T product generations.
