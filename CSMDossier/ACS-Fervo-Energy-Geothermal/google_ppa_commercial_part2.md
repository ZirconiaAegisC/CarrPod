## Corporate Offtake: Beyond Google

The Google PPA establishes a commercial precedent, but Fervo must secure additional offtake agreements to underwrite the full 400 MW Cape Station development and future projects. The universe of potential corporate offtakers for geothermal power is concentrated among technology companies with 24/7 carbon-free energy commitments and large, flat electricity load profiles.

### The Addressable Corporate Offtake Market

The technology sector's electricity consumption is concentrated in data centers, which have load characteristics that align well with geothermal generation: high capacity factors (90-95% utilization), flat load shapes (minimal diurnal variation), and locations that are increasingly expanding beyond traditional data center hubs into regions — including the western United States — where geothermal resources are available.

Companies with stated 24/7 carbon-free energy commitments include Google (2030 target), Microsoft (2030 target for 100/100/0 — 100% of electricity consumption matched with 100% carbon-free energy, producing zero carbon emissions), and Iron Mountain (data center operator, 2040 target). Amazon and Meta have announced net-zero commitments but have not explicitly committed to 24/7 hourly matching of their electricity consumption with carbon-free generation.

The total electricity consumption of these companies is enormous — Google alone consumed approximately 18 terawatt-hours in 2023 — and is growing at double-digit annual rates. If each company directed even 5-10% of its procurement to geothermal, the resulting demand would support multiple gigawatts of EGS development.

### The "Firm Carbon-Free" Product Category

A new category of electricity product is emerging in corporate procurement: "firm carbon-free energy" — electricity that is carbon-free (produced without greenhouse gas emissions) and firm (available when needed, not dependent on weather). Geothermal, nuclear, and hydropower qualify; wind and solar, without storage, do not.

The value of firm carbon-free energy relative to intermittent wind and solar is reflected in the price premium that corporate buyers are willing to pay. A 2023 analysis by the Clean Energy Buyers Institute estimated that a portfolio achieving 100% hourly carbon-free matching — requiring a combination of intermittent renewables, firm carbon-free generation, and energy storage — would have a levelized cost approximately 20-40% higher than a portfolio achieving only annual net-zero matching (renewable energy purchased on an annual basis equal to consumption but not matched hourly).

This premium represents the additional revenue opportunity available to geothermal developers relative to wind and solar developers. A geothermal developer that can deliver firm carbon-free power at a cost below the premium that corporate buyers are willing to pay captures economic rent from the mismatch between the demand for 24/7 carbon-free energy and the supply of technologies that can deliver it.

### Competitive Dynamics in Corporate Offtake

Fervo is not the only supplier of firm carbon-free energy competing for corporate offtake agreements. The competitive landscape includes:

- **Existing nuclear plants:** Several corporate buyers, including Microsoft, have contracted with existing nuclear plants for firm carbon-free power. Nuclear provides baseload generation with characteristics similar to geothermal but is geographically constrained to existing plant locations and faces public acceptance challenges in some markets.

- **Advanced nuclear:** Next-generation nuclear technologies, including small modular reactors, are targeting the corporate offtake market. Advanced nuclear developers (NuScale, TerraPower, X-energy) are pursuing commercialization timelines that overlap with Fervo's, and the two technologies compete for the same "firm carbon-free" product category.

- **Long-duration energy storage:** Technologies that can store renewable energy for 8-100 hours — including iron-air batteries (Form Energy), flow batteries, and compressed air storage — enable wind and solar to approximate the firmness characteristics of baseload generation. The cost trajectory of long-duration storage is a competitive threat to geothermal if storage costs decline to the point where wind-plus-storage or solar-plus-storage can deliver 24/7 carbon-free matching at a lower cost than geothermal.

- **Hydrogen:** Green hydrogen produced from electrolysis powered by renewable electricity can be stored and used to generate electricity during periods of low wind and solar output, providing another pathway to firm carbon-free power. The round-trip efficiency of hydrogen (electricity to hydrogen to electricity) is low — approximately 30-40% — meaning that hydrogen-firmed renewable power is likely to be more expensive than geothermal for the foreseeable future, but technological improvement and cost reduction in electrolysis could narrow the gap over time.

### Offtake Diversification Strategy

Fervo's offtake strategy should balance the premium pricing available from corporate buyers seeking 24/7 carbon-free matching against the credit quality and contract tenor available from utility offtakers. Utility PPAs, particularly with investment-grade utilities in regulated markets, provide greater credit certainty and potentially longer contract terms (20-25 years versus 10-15 years typical for corporate PPAs) but may offer lower pricing.

A diversified offtake portfolio — combining utility PPAs for the baseload portion of generation with corporate PPAs for the premium-valued "firm carbon-free" tranche — would optimize both revenue certainty and revenue maximization. The feasibility of such a split-of-take structure depends on the metering and settlement arrangements at the project's interconnection point and the willingness of the offtakers to accept the associated operational complexity.
