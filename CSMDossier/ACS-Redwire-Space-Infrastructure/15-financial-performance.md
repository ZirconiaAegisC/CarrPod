# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 15 — Financial Performance, Metrics, and Capital Structure

### 15.1 Revenue Trajectory

Redwire has demonstrated accelerating revenue growth across recent reporting
periods. Fiscal year 2025 revenue of $335.4 million represents 10.3 percent
growth over fiscal year 2024. This growth rate accelerated sharply in the
second half of 2025, with fourth-quarter revenue of $108.8 million — 56.4
percent higher than the fourth quarter of 2024 — reflecting initial
contributions from the Edge Autonomy acquisition and organic growth across both
reporting segments.

The first quarter of 2026 continued this growth trajectory with revenue of
$97.0 million, a 57.9 percent increase over the first quarter of 2025. The
sequential decline from fourth-quarter 2025 reflects normal seasonality
patterns in government contracting, where fourth calendar quarters typically
benefit from year-end budget execution activity.

### 15.2 Segment Revenue Composition

For fiscal year 2025, the Space segment contributed $209.8 million in revenue,
representing 62.5 percent of the total. The Defense Technology segment
contributed $125.6 million, representing 37.5 percent. On a pro forma basis
including a full year of Edge Autonomy contribution, the Defense Technology
segment is expected to approach parity with the Space segment in fiscal year
2026.

### 15.3 Gross Margin Expansion

Gross margin expansion represents one of the most significant positive
developments in Redwire's financial profile. The company reported a gross
margin of 26.6 percent in the first quarter of 2026, compared to 14.7 percent
in the first quarter of 2025. This 11.9 percentage point improvement reflects
several structural drivers:

**Fixed-Cost Absorption:** As revenue scales, fixed manufacturing overhead,
facility costs, and engineering support expenses are spread across a larger
revenue base, improving incremental margins.

**Program Mix Shift:** The Edge Autonomy acquisition and organic growth in
higher-margin product lines have improved the company's aggregate margin
profile relative to the lower-margin cost-type development contracts that
dominated the revenue mix in earlier periods.

**Execution Improvement:** Redwire has demonstrated improved program execution
on fixed-price contracts, reducing the cost overruns and estimate-at-completion
adjustments that compressed margins in prior periods.

**Vertical Integration:** The company's strategy of manufacturing subsystems
in-house rather than purchasing from external suppliers captures additional
margin that would otherwise accrue to third-party vendors.

### 15.4 Backlog and Book-to-Bill

Redwire's contracted backlog reached a record $498.1 million as of March 31,
2026, representing 21 percent sequential growth from the prior quarter. This
backlog includes both funded contract value — for which appropriations have
been enacted — and unfunded contract value attributable to the unexercised
option periods of multi-year contracts.

The book-to-bill ratio of 1.92 indicates that new orders exceeded recognized
revenue by nearly a factor of two during the quarter. A sustained
book-to-bill ratio above 1.0 is a leading indicator of future revenue growth,
as backlog converts to revenue in subsequent periods. The 1.92 ratio
represents Redwire's strongest quarterly bookings performance and validates
the market demand for the company's spacecraft platforms, defense products,
and space infrastructure offerings.

### 15.5 Capital Structure and Liquidity

Redwire's capital structure was recapitalized through a debt restructuring in
2025 that reduced annual interest expense by approximately $17 million
relative to the prior debt structure. This restructuring improved free cash
flow conversion and reduced the company's cost of debt capital, providing
additional financial flexibility for organic investment and future acquisition
activity.

Record liquidity of $175.2 million at the end of the most recent quarter
provides a substantial buffer against program execution risks and positions
the company to fund working capital needs associated with revenue growth
without requiring additional equity or debt capital. The company's liquidity
position is further supported by an undrawn revolving credit facility that
provides additional borrowing capacity for strategic needs.

### 15.6 Fiscal Year 2026 Guidance

Management has issued fiscal year 2026 revenue guidance of $450 million to
$500 million, representing 34 to 49 percent growth over fiscal year 2025. At
the midpoint of $475 million, this guidance implies continued double-digit
organic growth augmented by a full year of Edge Autonomy contribution.

The company has not provided explicit margin or earnings guidance for fiscal
year 2026, but the first-quarter gross margin performance of 26.6 percent
suggests that margins are tracking toward the higher end of the range implied
by the company's medium-term financial targets.

### 15.7 Capital Allocation Priorities

Management has articulated capital allocation priorities that prioritize
organic investment in manufacturing capacity and technology development,
funded by operating cash flow, followed by debt reduction to further improve
the company's interest expense profile and financial flexibility. Strategic
bolt-on acquisitions are considered opportunistically when they provide
specific technology or market access benefits, but large-scale M&A is not a
near-term priority following the Edge Autonomy transaction.
