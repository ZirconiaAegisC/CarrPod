# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 06 — Thresher: Rapid-Response LEO Platform

### 6.1 Mission Concept

The Thresher platform is Redwire's rapid-response low-Earth-orbit spacecraft
bus, designed to address the U.S. Department of Defense and intelligence
community requirement for tactically responsive space launch and operations.
Thresher enables the deployment of operationally relevant payloads on timelines
measured in weeks rather than the years typical of traditional space
acquisition programs.

### 6.2 Responsive Space Architecture

Responsive space operations demand a fundamentally different approach to
spacecraft design and manufacturing than traditional satellite programs.
Thresher addresses these requirements through standardization of interfaces,
pre-positioning of long-lead components, and a manufacturing process optimized
for speed rather than minimum unit cost.

The spacecraft bus is assembled from a modular kit comprising pre-qualified
subsystems that can be configured for specific mission profiles through
software-defined interfaces rather than hardware customization. Payload
integration follows a standardized mechanical, electrical, and data interface
specification that allows payload providers to develop and test their systems
independently and integrate them with the spacecraft bus in days rather than
months.

### 6.3 Manufacturing Timeline

Redwire has demonstrated the ability to produce Thresher spacecraft on
timelines consistent with Department of Defense responsive launch requirements.
The manufacturing process leverages the company's vertical integration to
eliminate supplier lead-time dependencies for critical subsystems, including
solar arrays, structures, and avionics produced in-house. Pre-integration
testing of subsystems in parallel with bus assembly further compresses the
end-to-end timeline.

### 6.4 Launch Vehicle Compatibility

Thresher is designed for compatibility with the range of small and medium
launch vehicles qualified for U.S. national security missions, including
Firefly Aerospace's Alpha, Northrop Grumman's Pegasus and Minotaur series,
and the emerging class of responsive launch providers. The spacecraft's
mechanical interface is configurable for multiple payload adapters, and its
mass properties fall within the capacity of small-class launchers capable of
rapid call-up from both established ranges and austere launch locations.

### 6.5 Payload Accommodation

The standard Thresher configuration provides a payload volume of approximately
0.5 cubic meters with a mass allocation of 80 kilograms and an orbit-average
power budget of 200 watts. The payload deck supports multiple mounting
configurations including nadir-facing apertures for optical payloads,
zenith-facing antennas for communications missions, and body-mounted sensors
for signals intelligence and electronic warfare applications.

### 6.6 Mission Profiles

Thresher addresses several operational mission profiles:

- Reconstitution of degraded or lost space capabilities following hostile
  action or system failures.
- Surge deployment of additional sensor capacity during periods of elevated
  geopolitical tension.
- Rapid technology demonstration and operational prototyping, enabling new
  capabilities to transition from laboratory to orbit on compressed timelines.
- Augmentation of existing constellations with additional capacity for
  high-priority tasking.

### 6.7 Relationship to the Andromeda IDIQ

Thresher is offered through the $1.8 billion Andromeda indefinite-delivery
indefinite-quantity contract, which provides U.S. government program offices
with a pre-negotiated contracting vehicle for rapid procurement. The Andromeda
framework eliminates the typical 12-to-18-month competitive source selection
cycle, enabling task orders to be issued within weeks of requirement
identification.

### 6.8 Integration with Defense Technology Segment

Thresher's rapid-response capability is complemented by Redwire's Defense
Technology segment, which provides the ground segment, mission planning, and
data exploitation capabilities necessary for time-sensitive space operations.
The combination of rapid spacecraft production with existing ground
infrastructure creates an end-to-end responsive space capability spanning
tasking to data delivery.
