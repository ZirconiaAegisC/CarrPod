# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 05 — Hammerhead: LEO Platform with PROBA Heritage

### 5.1 Platform Pedigree

The Hammerhead platform represents Redwire's flagship low-Earth-orbit
spacecraft bus, drawing on over 50 years of institutional experience traced
through the PROBA (Project for On-Board Autonomy) satellite series developed
for the European Space Agency. Redwire's European operations served as prime
contractor for multiple PROBA missions including PROBA-1 (2001), PROBA-2
(2009), and PROBA-V (2013), accumulating decades of cumulative on-orbit
operations across Earth observation, solar physics, and vegetation monitoring
missions.

### 5.2 Bus Architecture

Hammerhead employs a modular architecture with a central avionics bay, a
separable payload deck, and externally mounted power and thermal subsystems.
The standard bus configuration supports payloads up to 150 kilograms with an
average orbit-average power allocation of 300 watts. Optional configuration
enhancements extend payload mass capacity to 250 kilograms and power to 600
watts through larger solar array deployment and additional battery capacity.

The structural design uses aluminum honeycomb panels with embedded heat pipes,
a configuration flight-proven across multiple PROBA missions. The structure
provides multiple launch vehicle interfaces including compatibility with the
Vega-C, Falcon 9 rideshare, and Ariane 6 auxiliary payload adapters.

### 5.3 Avionics and Flight Software

Hammerhead's avionics suite is built around a redundant onboard computer
running Redwire's in-house flight software, which has been refined through
successive PROBA missions. The software architecture supports autonomous
mission planning, fault detection and recovery, and on-orbit reconfiguration
without ground intervention — capabilities that trace directly to the autonomy
requirements of the original PROBA program.

The guidance, navigation, and control subsystem utilizes a star tracker plus
inertial measurement unit configuration with GPS for absolute position
determination. Pointing accuracy of better than 10 arcseconds and pointing
stability of 1 arcsecond over 10 seconds have been demonstrated on-orbit,
satisfying the requirements of high-resolution optical payloads.

### 5.4 Communications Architecture

The standard communications suite includes an S-band command and telemetry link
for housekeeping and an X-band high-rate downlink for payload data. Data rates
up to 300 megabits per second are achievable with the standard X-band
transmitter and a body-fixed high-gain antenna. An optional Ka-band upgrade
increases downlink capacity to 1 gigabit per second for missions with
exceptionally high data volume requirements.

### 5.5 Mission Heritage Summary

The PROBA mission series provides Hammerhead with one of the most extensive
on-orbit heritage baselines of any small satellite platform:

- PROBA-1 (launched 2001): Earth observation and technology demonstration,
  operational for over 20 years demonstrating exceptional longevity.
- PROBA-2 (launched 2009): Solar physics observatory with four instrument
  payloads, operating continuously for over 15 years.
- PROBA-V (launched 2013): Vegetation monitoring mission providing global
  coverage every two days, bridging the gap between SPOT-VEGETATION and
  Sentinel-3.

### 5.6 Current and Planned Missions

Beyond the PROBA series, Hammerhead has been selected for multiple European
and commercial missions including Earth observation, technology demonstration,
and scientific payloads. The platform is actively marketed through the
Andromeda IDIQ contract vehicle for U.S. government customers seeking a
flight-proven LEO bus with European heritage.

### 5.7 Competitive Positioning

Hammerhead competes in the mid-range small satellite bus market against
platforms including Airbus Defence and Space's AstroBus, OHB's SmallGEO
Lite derivative, and Terran Orbital's larger bus variants. Hammerhead
differentiates on heritage depth — few platforms of its size class can claim
over 50 cumulative years of on-orbit operations across multiple mission types.
