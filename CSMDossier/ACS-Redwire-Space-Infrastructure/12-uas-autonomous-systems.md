# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 12 — Stalker Block 30 and Penguin Unmanned Aerial Systems

### 12.1 Stalker Block 30

The Stalker Block 30 is a small, silent, long-endurance unmanned aerial system
designed for tactical reconnaissance and target acquisition. The system
operates at low acoustic and visual signatures, enabling covert surveillance
of high-value targets without alerting the subject of observation. Stalker has
been deployed extensively with U.S. Special Operations Command, the U.S. Army,
and allied special operations forces, accumulating a substantial operational
flight history in diverse environmental conditions.

### 12.2 Stalker Technical Specifications

The Stalker air vehicle is hand-launched by a single operator and recovered
through a deep-stall landing that requires no prepared surface, runway, or
recovery net. The air vehicle's electric propulsion system provides a silent
loiter capability with endurance exceeding three hours at standoff ranges
sufficient to avoid detection by the target. The modular payload bay
accommodates interchangeable electro-optical, infrared, and signals
intelligence payloads that can be swapped in the field without specialized
tools.

The ground control station comprises a ruggedized tablet computer running
Redwire's proprietary mission planning and execution software, a hand
controller for manual flight when required, and a compact beyond-line-of-sight
communications terminal. The complete Stalker system, including two air
vehicles, payloads, ground control station, and support equipment, packs into
two transport cases that can be carried by a two-person team.

### 12.3 Stalker Operational Doctrine

Stalker is employed for missions including route reconnaissance, overwatch of
dismounted patrols, target acquisition for precision fires, battle damage
assessment, and persistent surveillance of points of interest. The system's
low signature permits it to operate at altitudes and ranges where larger
tactical UAS would be detected and engaged by adversary air defense systems.
Stalker's hand-launch capability eliminates the logistical burden of
pneumatic launchers or prepared runways, enabling employment from austere
locations including small boats, vehicles, and confined urban terrain.

### 12.4 Penguin UAS

The Penguin unmanned aerial system is a larger, longer-endurance platform
designed for operational and tactical-level intelligence, surveillance, and
reconnaissance missions. Unlike Stalker's hand-launched configuration, Penguin
operates from prepared surfaces or pneumatic launchers and provides endurance
measured in tens of hours rather than single-digit hours, enabling persistent
coverage of larger areas of interest.

### 12.5 Penguin Technical Specifications

Penguin accommodates a significantly larger payload than Stalker, supporting
high-resolution electro-optical and infrared sensors with laser designation
capability, wide-area surveillance payloads, and signals intelligence
collection systems. The platform's gasoline engine provides endurance of 20
or more hours at cruise speed, enabling missions that would require multiple
Stalker sorties or a larger, more expensive platform such as the MQ-9 Reaper.

The Penguin ground control segment supports beyond-line-of-sight operation
through satellite communications relay, enabling control of the aircraft from
a geographically separated operations center. This capability is particularly
valuable for maritime surveillance missions where the aircraft operates at
ranges that exceed line-of-sight data link range from the launch vessel.

### 12.6 Penguin Operational Applications

Penguin addresses mission profiles that fall between the tactical focus of
Stalker and the strategic capabilities of high-altitude long-endurance
platforms. These include maritime domain awareness and anti-piracy patrols,
border surveillance, infrastructure protection, disaster response assessment,
and support to conventional military operations requiring persistent
surveillance of named areas of interest.

### 12.7 International Market

Both Stalker and Penguin are actively marketed to allied defense ministries,
with particular emphasis on NATO member states pursuing interoperability with
U.S. forces and on Indo-Pacific partner nations seeking affordable
intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance capabilities for maritime
domain awareness. International sales are subject to export control
regulations administered by the State Department's Directorate of Defense
Trade Controls and the Commerce Department's Bureau of Industry and Security.

### 12.8 Sustainment and Modernization

Redwire holds active sustainment contracts for both Stalker and Penguin,
providing a recurring revenue stream from depot-level maintenance, repair,
and overhaul services. The company also invests in capability modernization
including improved sensors, enhanced autonomy for multi-vehicle operations,
and integration with broader command and control networks. These modernization
investments maintain the platforms' operational relevance against evolving
threat environments.
