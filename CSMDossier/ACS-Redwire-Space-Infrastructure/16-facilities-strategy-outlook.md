# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 16 — Facilities, Workforce, and Strategic Outlook

### 16.1 Global Facility Footprint

Redwire operates across 28 locations in the United States and Europe, a
geographic distribution that reflects the company's acquisition-driven growth
and its strategy of maintaining proximity to key customers, talent pools, and
supply chain nodes. The facility network encompasses spacecraft manufacturing
and integration clean rooms, solar array production lines, sensor and optical
fabrication centers, UAS manufacturing and flight test facilities, and
corporate and program management offices.

### 16.2 Key Manufacturing Sites

**Jacksonville, Florida (Corporate Headquarters):** Executive leadership,
finance, human resources, investor relations, and corporate development
functions are centered at the Jacksonville headquarters. The facility also
houses program management personnel supporting multiple Space segment programs.

**Ann Arbor, Michigan:** The 85,000 square-foot Ann Arbor facility produces
advanced fuel cell systems for spacecraft, lunar surface operations, and
defense applications requiring silent, efficient power generation.

**European Operations:** Redwire's European facilities, concentrated in
Belgium and Luxembourg, house the company's small satellite manufacturing
center of excellence, the PROBA heritage engineering team, and the optical
systems and star tracker production lines. These facilities are strategically
positioned to serve ESA, European Union, and commercial European customers.

**Defense Manufacturing Sites:** Edge Autonomy's pre-acquisition
manufacturing facilities have been integrated into Redwire's Defense
Technology segment operations, providing UAS production, sensor integration,
and flight test capabilities in proximity to key U.S. Department of Defense
customers.

### 16.3 Workforce Profile

Redwire employs approximately 1,400 personnel, with the workforce distributed
across engineering, manufacturing, program management, business development,
and corporate functions. The employee base includes cleared personnel
supporting classified national security space and defense programs, as well
as a substantial European workforce operating under the labor regulations of
multiple European Union member states.

The company's workforce reflects the technical intensity of its business, with
a high proportion of employees holding advanced degrees in aerospace
engineering, electrical engineering, mechanical engineering, physics, and
related disciplines. Redwire's ability to attract and retain technical talent
is a critical success factor in an industry characterized by intense
competition for qualified engineers and scientists.

### 16.4 Growth Strategy Framework

Redwire's growth strategy is organized around five pillars:

**Platform Proliferation:** The company seeks to expand the installed base of
its five spacecraft platforms across U.S. government, allied government, and
commercial customers, converting platform adoption into recurring revenue from
follow-on missions, constellation deployments, and sustainment contracts.

**Vertical Integration Depth:** Continued investment in in-house manufacturing
of subsystems including solar arrays, avionics, sensors, and structures
captures a greater share of spacecraft value and improves margin performance
on fixed-price contracts.

**Defense Segment Expansion:** Building on the Edge Autonomy acquisition,
Redwire aims to grow its Defense Technology segment through organic expansion
of UAS production, international sales to allied defense ministries, and
development of next-generation autonomous systems aligned with Department of
Defense modernization priorities.

**Commercial Space Market Development:** As the commercial space market matures
— driven by decreasing launch costs, the emergence of commercial space
stations, and growing private-sector investment in space-based services —
Redwire positions its platforms, payloads, and manufacturing services to
capture a share of this expanding addressable market.

**International Market Expansion:** Redwire's European operations provide a
platform for growth in markets outside the United States, including ESA
member states, European Union space programs, and allied defense ministries
in the Indo-Pacific and Middle East regions.

### 16.5 Key Growth Catalysts

Several identifiable catalysts have the potential to accelerate Redwire's
growth:

**DARPA Otter Success:** A successful on-orbit demonstration of air-breathing
electric propulsion would unlock a new VLEO mission class and position Redwire
as the incumbent for operational VLEO constellations.

**Andromeda IDIQ Task Order Flow:** Acceleration in task order awards under the
$1.8 billion Andromeda contract would provide revenue visibility and scale
enabling further manufacturing capacity investment.

**Commercial Space Station Transition:** As the ISS approaches its planned 2030
retirement, commercial space station operators will require payloads and
infrastructure that Redwire's microgravity portfolio is positioned to supply.

**International Defense Demand:** Geopolitical tension is driving demand for
tactical UAS and autonomous systems from allied defense ministries, expanding
the addressable market for Redwire's Defense Technology segment.

**Quantum Communications:** Successful demonstration of the ESA QKD satellite
could position Redwire favorably for operational quantum communications
constellations as institutions invest in quantum-resistant infrastructure.

### 16.6 Risk Factors

Redwire's outlook is subject to risks including dependence on government
budget cycles, execution risk on fixed-price development contracts, supply
chain disruption, competitive pressure from established primes and new
entrants, and integration risk from the Edge Autonomy acquisition.

The company's ability to convert its record backlog into revenue and margin
performance consistent with recent trends is the primary determinant of
near-term performance. Sustained book-to-bill above 1.0, gross margin above
25 percent, and successful execution on Otter and Andromeda are the key
metrics for assessing Redwire's strategic execution.
