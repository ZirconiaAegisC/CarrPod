# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 14 — Sensors, Payloads, and Optical Systems

### 14.1 Capability Overview

Redwire's sensors, payloads, and optical systems portfolio spans the
electromagnetic spectrum from ultraviolet through long-wave infrared and
includes radio-frequency collection and electronic warfare payloads. These
systems are integrated onto Redwire's spacecraft platforms, installed aboard
the company's UAS platforms, and sold as standalone products to third-party
platform integrators.

### 14.2 Electro-Optical and Infrared Sensors

Redwire develops and manufactures electro-optical and infrared sensor payloads
for both space-based and airborne applications. Space-based sensors include
high-resolution visible imagers for Earth observation, multispectral sensors
for agricultural and environmental monitoring, and infrared sensors for
thermal characterization and missile warning applications.

Airborne EO/IR sensors are integrated onto the Stalker and Penguin UAS
platforms and are also sold as standalone gimbal-mounted sensor packages for
integration onto third-party manned and unmanned aircraft. These sensors
provide day and night imaging, target tracking, and laser designation
capabilities in packages sized for tactical UAS weight and power constraints.

### 14.3 Space-Qualified Optical Systems

Redwire's optical systems capability includes precision telescopes, star
trackers, and optical communications terminals designed for the space
environment. The company's star tracker product line, developed through its
European operations, provides attitude determination for spacecraft ranging
from CubeSats to large geostationary platforms. These star trackers have
accumulated substantial flight heritage across multiple customers and mission
types.

Optical communications terminals represent an emerging product area for
Redwire, leveraging the company's precision optics expertise to develop
laser communication systems that provide higher data rates than radio-frequency
links while operating in a spectrum regime that is more resistant to
interception and jamming.

### 14.4 ESA Quantum Key Distribution Satellite

Redwire, in partnership with Honeywell and under contract to the European
Space Agency, is developing a quantum key distribution (QKD) satellite that
will demonstrate space-to-ground distribution of cryptographic keys using the
principles of quantum mechanics. QKD provides theoretically unbreakable
encryption because any attempt to intercept the quantum-encoded key
necessarily disturbs the quantum state and is detectable by the legitimate
communicating parties.

The QKD satellite program positions Redwire within the strategic quantum
communications market, which is expected to grow as nation-states invest in
quantum-resistant cryptography and as financial institutions, governments,
and critical infrastructure operators seek communications security that
cannot be compromised by advances in quantum computing. The Honeywell
partnership combines Redwire's spacecraft platform and optical payload
expertise with Honeywell's quantum technology and systems integration
capabilities.

### 14.5 Signals Intelligence and Electronic Warfare

Redwire's Defense Technology segment develops signals intelligence collection
systems and electronic warfare payloads for both airborne and space-based
platforms. These systems detect, identify, and geolocate radio-frequency
emitters including communications systems, radars, and electronic warfare
systems, providing situational awareness and targeting information to
military commanders.

The company's electronic warfare payloads provide electronic attack capability,
disrupting adversary communications and radar systems through targeted
radio-frequency jamming. These payloads leverage Redwire's expertise in
wideband radio-frequency design, digital signal processing, and autonomous
operation to deliver capability in packages compatible with tactical UAS
platforms.

### 14.6 DARPA Otter Sensor Integration

The DARPA Otter program includes development of an integrated sensor package
optimized for the VLEO operational environment. At VLEO altitudes, optical
sensors benefit from reduced range to target — approximately half the range
of traditional LEO altitudes — which translates to a four-fold improvement in
collected signal for passive optical systems. The Otter sensor package
leverages Redwire's space-qualified optical design expertise to capitalize
on this VLEO advantage.

### 14.7 Manufacturing and Testing

Redwire operates dedicated sensor and optical manufacturing facilities with
clean rooms rated to ISO Class 5 through Class 7, vibration and thermal
vacuum test chambers for space qualification, and optical calibration
equipment for precision alignment and characterization. These facilities
support both internal program demands and external customer contracts for
build-to-print and build-to-specification sensor manufacturing.
