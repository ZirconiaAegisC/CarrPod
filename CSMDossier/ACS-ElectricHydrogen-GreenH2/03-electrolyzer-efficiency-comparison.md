# Electrolyzer Technology Comparison: PEM vs Alkaline vs SOEC vs AEM

## Technology Landscape
Four primary electrolyzer technologies compete in the green hydrogen market:

1. **Alkaline Electrolysis (AEL)**: Mature, lowest capex, poor dynamic response
2. **Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM)**: Fast response, compact, higher capex
3. **Solid Oxide Electrolysis (SOEC)**: Highest efficiency, requires heat input
4. **Anion Exchange Membrane (AEM)**: Emerging, combines benefits of AEL and PEM

## Detailed Comparison

### Efficiency
| Technology | System Efficiency (kWh/kg H₂) | Stack Efficiency (kWh/kg H₂) |
|------------|-------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Alkaline   | 50-55                         | 47-53                        |
| PEM        | 52-57                         | 50-55                        |
| SOEC       | 37-42 (with heat)             | 35-40                        |
| AEM        | 52-58                         | 48-55                        |

Note: Lower kWh/kg indicates higher efficiency. SOEC efficiency includes thermal input;
purely electrical efficiency is ~39-42 kWh/kg.

Electric Hydrogen's PEM systems target the lower end of the PEM range (52-54 kWh/kg
system-level), approaching but not exceeding the theoretical minimum of ~39.4 kWh/kg
(based on the higher heating value of hydrogen).

### Capital Cost
| Technology | Installed Cost ($/kW) | Maturity          |
|------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Alkaline   | $500-800             | Commercial        |
| PEM        | $700-1,400           | Early Commercial  |
| SOEC       | $1,000-2,000+        | Demonstration     |
| AEM        | $600-1,000 (est.)    | Pilot/Demo        |

EH2 targets $500-700/kW at scale through gigafactory manufacturing economics, positioning
PEM cost-competitively with alkaline at volume.

### Dynamic Response
This is PEM's strongest advantage for renewable-coupled applications:

| Technology | Ramp Rate        | Cold Start    | Min Load |
|------------|------------------|---------------|----------|
| Alkaline   | 1-5% / second    | 30-60 minutes | 15-40%   |
| PEM        | 10-100% / second | <5 minutes    | 0-10%    |
| SOEC       | Slow (thermal)   | Hours         | 30-50%   |
| AEM        | 5-10% / second   | <10 minutes   | 5-15%    |

EH2's sub-30-second ramp from 10% to 100% load enables direct coupling with solar and
wind generation, eliminating the need for intermediate battery storage.

### Operating Pressure
Higher pressure reduces downstream compression costs:

| Technology | Typical Operating Pressure |
|------------|----------------------------|
| Alkaline   | Atmospheric (1-10 bar)     |
| PEM        | 30-50 bar (differential)   |
| SOEC       | Atmospheric                |
| AEM        | 1-30 bar (developing)      |

EH2's 30 bar differential-pressure operation eliminates one stage of mechanical
compression for most downstream applications.

### Footprint
| Technology | Relative Footprint per MW |
|------------|---------------------------|
| Alkaline   | 3-5x (baseline)           |
| PEM        | 1x (most compact)         |
| SOEC       | 2-3x                       |
| AEM        | 1.2-1.5x (target)         |

PEM's compact footprint reduces site preparation costs and enables containerized
deployment — a key advantage for project developers managing land constraints.

## Competitor Positioning by Technology

### PEM Competitors
- **ITM Power (UK)**: 20MW modules, strong in European markets
- **Plug Power (US)**: 5MW stacks (GenDrive), integrated with liquefaction
- **Cummins (US)**: Acquired Hydrogenics, 20MW+ capability
- **Nel Hydrogen (Norway)**: Historically alkaline, entering PEM via R&D

### Alkaline Competitors
- **Nel Hydrogen (Norway)**: Market leader in alkaline, atmospheric pressure
- **Thyssenkrupp Nucera (Germany)**: 20MW alkaline modules
- **Sunfire (Germany)**: Dual alkaline + SOEC strategy
- **Hysata (Australia)**: Capillary-fed alkaline, breakthrough efficiency claims

### Emerging Competitors
- **Verdagy (US)**: Advanced alkaline with dynamic capability claims
- **Ohmium (US)**: PEM, smaller scale (2MW units), modular approach
