# BP Partnership — Strategic Investment & Hydrogen Strategy

## Partnership Overview
BP plc (NYSE: BP), one of the world's largest integrated energy companies,
participated in Electric Hydrogen's $380 million Series C funding round in
October 2023. BP's investment, made through its bp ventures arm, represents
the oil and gas supermajor's strategic positioning in the emerging green
hydrogen economy.

## BP's Hydrogen Strategy
BP has articulated one of the most ambitious hydrogen strategies among the oil
and gas supermajors. The company's hydrogen ambitions include:

### Production Targets
- Target of 0.5-0.7 million tonnes per annum (mtpa) of hydrogen production by
  2030, with a long-term goal of 10% market share in core hydrogen markets.
- Portfolio includes both blue hydrogen (natural gas reforming with carbon capture)
  and green hydrogen (renewable-powered electrolysis), with a stated transition
  toward green hydrogen over time.

### Key Hydrogen Projects
- **H2Teesside (UK)**: 1.2 GW blue hydrogen facility in northeast England (with
  Equinor), targeting startup by 2030.
- **HyGreen Teesside (UK)**: 500 MW green hydrogen facility complementing H2Teesside.
- **H2 Kwinana (Australia)**: Large-scale green hydrogen and ammonia production
  hub in Western Australia.
- **Castellón (Spain)**: Green hydrogen at BP's Castellón refinery, replacing gray
  hydrogen with electrolysis by 2030.
- **Lingen (Germany)**: Green hydrogen production at BP's Lingen refinery, with
  offshore wind power.

### Capital Allocation
BP plans to invest $4-6 billion annually in its "transition growth engines"
(including hydrogen, renewables, EV charging, and bioenergy) by 2025,
representing approximately 30% of total capital expenditure.

## BP's Investment Rationale for EH2

### Technology Access
Investing in Electric Hydrogen gives BP direct exposure to what it views as
the leading large-scale PEM electrolyzer technology. Key attractions include:

1. **Scale**: 100MW systems match the project sizes BP is developing rather than
   requiring aggregation of many smaller electrolyzer units.
2. **Manufacturing approach**: EH2's gigafactory model aligns with BP's preference
   for scalable, repeatable technology solutions.
3. **US manufacturing**: IRA incentives make US-manufactured electrolyzers
   economically attractive for US projects.

### Strategic Hedge
BP's investment in EH2 hedges against multiple technology pathways:

- **Electrolyzer supply security**: As BP scales its hydrogen projects, access
  to reliable electrolyzer supply becomes critical. An equity stake in a
  manufacturer provides supply chain assurance.
- **Technology optionality**: By investing across the hydrogen value chain (both
  blue and green, multiple regions), BP maintains flexibility to adapt to
  changing policy environments and cost curves.
- **Competitive intelligence**: Direct engagement with an electrolyzer
  manufacturer provides insight into technology trajectories, cost curves,
  and manufacturing challenges.

### Financial Return
Beyond strategic value, BP ventures makes investments with financial return
expectations. EH2's $1B+ valuation post-Series C represents a venture-scale
investment that could appreciate significantly if the green hydrogen market
develops as projected.

## BP vs. Other Oil & Gas Hydrogen Strategies

| Company    | Hydrogen Focus         | Green H₂ Target             | Electrolyzer Strategy |
|------------|------------------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------|
| BP         | Blue + Green           | 0.5-0.7 mtpa by 2030        | Invest (EH2), buy     |
| Shell      | Green primarily        | Major projects in EU/China   | Invest, partner       |
| TotalEnergies | Green primarily    | 0.5 mtpa by 2030             | Partner with electrolyzer OEMs |
| ExxonMobil | Blue hydrogen + CCS    | Baytown, TX CCS hub          | Limited electrolyzer engagement |
| Chevron    | Blue + Green           | Smaller scale                | Venture investments   |

BP's approach is notable for its explicit commitment to green hydrogen at scale
and its direct investment in an electrolyzer manufacturer — a more integrated
strategy than most peers.

## Potential EH2-BP Project Collaborations

### US Gulf Coast
BP has significant operations along the US Gulf Coast, including refineries in
Texas and the Whiting refinery in Indiana. These facilities currently consume
gray hydrogen produced from natural gas reforming. Replacing gray hydrogen with
green hydrogen from EH2 electrolyzers would:
- Reduce refinery Scope 1 emissions
- Qualify for IRA Section 45V production tax credits
- Leverage Texas renewable resources (wind, solar) for low-cost green power

### International Opportunities
BP's global project pipeline could incorporate EH2 technology in regions where
the company is developing green hydrogen production:
- **Australia**: Abundant solar resources, existing BP infrastructure, proximity
  to Asian hydrogen markets.
- **Middle East**: BP's historical relationships in the region could facilitate
  green hydrogen projects leveraging exceptional solar resources.
- **Europe**: Policy support through EU hydrogen strategy and national subsidy
  programs.

## Risks and Considerations
- **BP capital discipline**: BP has recently moderated some transition-related
  spending amid shareholder pressure for near-term returns, potentially
  affecting hydrogen project timelines.
- **Competing technologies**: BP's parallel investments in blue hydrogen
  reflect a strategy that does not exclusively bet on green hydrogen.
- **Scale mismatch**: BP's major project sizes (multi-GW) exceed what a single
  electrolyzer supplier can currently provide, requiring multi-vendor strategy.
