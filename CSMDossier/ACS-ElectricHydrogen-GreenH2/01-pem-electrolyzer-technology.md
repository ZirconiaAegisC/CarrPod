# 100MW PEM Electrolyzer Technology — Electric Hydrogen

## Executive Summary
Electric Hydrogen (EH2) designs and manufactures 100MW proton exchange membrane (PEM)
electrolyzer systems, representing the largest single-stack electrolyzer capacity currently
available in the global market. The company's technology platform targets green hydrogen
production at costs competitive with fossil-derived gray hydrogen (<$2/kg).

## Technology Architecture

### Stack Design
The 100MW system is built from modular PEM stacks assembled into standardized blocks.
Each individual stack operates at high current density (>2 A/cm²) with a proprietary
membrane electrode assembly (MEA) that reduces precious metal loading while maintaining
durability targets exceeding 60,000 operating hours.

### Balance of Plant
The system integrates power electronics, water purification, gas separation, and thermal
management subsystems into a containerized footprint. DC power conditioning is handled
through custom rectifier designs optimized for direct coupling with renewable generation
sources — particularly large-scale solar PV and onshore wind installations.

## Technology Differentiation

### Proprietary MEA
The MEA is the core intellectual property. Electric Hydrogen has developed catalyst-coated
membranes with reduced iridium loading — a critical advantage given iridium supply
constraints. The company targets iridium loading below 0.3 mg/cm², substantially lower
than legacy PEM designs.

### High Current Density Operation
Operating at elevated current densities enables higher hydrogen output per unit of active
cell area, reducing capital costs. The trade-off between efficiency and capital cost is
managed through system-level optimization.

### Dynamic Response
The system's sub-30-second ramp capability makes it uniquely suited for pairing with
intermittent renewable generation. Unlike alkaline electrolyzers that require stable
baseload operation, EH2's PEM stacks can follow renewable generation profiles directly,
minimizing or eliminating the need for battery buffering.

### Modular Architecture
Rather than designing custom systems for each project, Electric Hydrogen employs
standardized 100MW building blocks that can be deployed individually or combined into
larger installations (e.g., 300MW at the Texas site). This approach reduces engineering
costs, accelerates deployment timelines, and enables manufacturing economies of scale.

## Comparison to Alternatives
- **Alkaline Electrolysis**: Lower capital cost but poor dynamic response, larger footprint,
  and lower operating pressure (typically atmospheric).
- **Solid Oxide Electrolysis (SOEC)**: Higher theoretical efficiency but requires high-
  temperature heat input (700-900°C), limiting deployment to sites with waste heat.
- **Anion Exchange Membrane (AEM)**: Promising but immature technology with limited
  commercial availability at scale.

Electric Hydrogen's PEM approach balances efficiency, dynamic capability, and
manufacturability for the utility-scale green hydrogen market.

## Current Status
The first commercial-scale deployment is underway at a 300MW green hydrogen production
facility in Texas. Manufacturing at the Devens, MA gigafactory is ramping to support
this and subsequent projects.
