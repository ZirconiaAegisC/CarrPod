# Amazon Partnership — Green Hydrogen for Decarbonization

## Partnership Overview
Amazon (NASDAQ: AMZN) is a strategic investor and customer of Electric Hydrogen,
aligning with Amazon's Climate Pledge commitment to achieve net-zero carbon
emissions by 2040. Amazon's participation in EH2's Series C funding round
(October 2023) signaled the technology company's serious interest in green
hydrogen as a decarbonization tool for its operations.

## Amazon's Decarbonization Challenge
Amazon's carbon footprint spans multiple hard-to-abate sectors:

| Emissions Source            | Challenge                              |
|------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Logistics / Trucking         | Long-haul trucking is difficult to electrify with batteries alone |
| Aviation (Amazon Air)        | Requires sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) or hydrogen |
| Data Centers                 | Backup power traditionally diesel generators |
| Warehouses (Fulfillment)     | Material handling equipment (forklifts) transitioning from lead-acid batteries |
| Maritime (Ocean Freight)     | International shipping emissions from consumer goods transport |

Green hydrogen offers pathways to decarbonize many of these activities, either
through direct use (fuel cells for trucks, material handling) or as a feedstock
for synthetic fuels (e-fuel for aviation, green ammonia for maritime).

## Amazon's Hydrogen Activities
Amazon is pursuing hydrogen across multiple fronts:

1. **Fulfillment Center Forklifts**: Amazon operates one of the largest fleets of
   hydrogen fuel cell forklifts, with over 15,000 units deployed across more than
   70 fulfillment centers. Plug Power is the primary supplier.

2. **Hydrogen Hubs**: Amazon has announced plans to produce hydrogen at fulfillment
   centers using on-site electrolysis, reducing transportation costs for delivered
   hydrogen.

3. **Climate Pledge Fund**: Amazon's $2 billion venture fund has invested in
   multiple hydrogen technology companies, including Electric Hydrogen.

4. **Hydrogen Fuel Cell Trucks**: Amazon is evaluating hydrogen fuel cell trucks
   for long-haul logistics through partnerships with manufacturers.

## EH2-Amazon Relationship

### Investment
Amazon participated in Electric Hydrogen's $380 million Series C round (October
2023) alongside BP, United Airlines, Microsoft, Temasek, Fortescue, and Trinity
Capital. The investment came through Amazon's Climate Pledge Fund and represents
a strategic bet on large-scale green hydrogen production.

### Strategic Alignment
The partnership is based on complementary interests:

- **Amazon needs cheap green hydrogen**: To achieve its Climate Pledge, Amazon
  requires affordable, clean hydrogen for its operations. EH2's target of <$2/kg
  green hydrogen aligns with this need.

- **EH2 needs anchor customers**: Amazon's massive hydrogen demand potential
  (forklifts, trucks, data centers, aviation) provides a substantial offtake
  opportunity.

- **Shared technology-first culture**: Both companies approach problems from a
  systems engineering and scale perspective, creating cultural alignment.

### Potential Offtake Pathways
1. **Material Handling**: The existing 15,000+ fuel cell forklift fleet represents
   current hydrogen demand, with potential for significant growth.

2. **Heavy-Duty Trucking**: As hydrogen fuel cell trucks become commercially
   available (from manufacturers like Nikola, Hyundai, Toyota), Amazon's
   logistics fleet could become a major hydrogen consumer.

3. **Data Center Backup Power**: Hydrogen fuel cells could replace diesel
   generators for backup power at AWS data centers, providing zero-emission
   reliability.

4. **Sustainable Aviation Fuel**: Long-term, green hydrogen could serve as
   feedstock for synthetic jet fuel to decarbonize Amazon Air operations.

## Comparison: Microsoft's Hydrogen Strategy
Microsoft (also an EH2 Series C investor) is pursuing hydrogen primarily for
data center backup power, having successfully tested a hydrogen fuel cell
system as a diesel generator replacement. Both tech giants' hydrogen interest
stems from the recognition that 100% renewable electricity alone cannot
decarbonize all operations.

## Market Signal
Amazon and Microsoft's investments in Electric Hydrogen send a strong market
signal to other potential customers:

- **Validation**: Two of the world's largest and most sophisticated corporate
  energy buyers are betting on EH2's technology.
- **Demand aggregation**: Corporate commitments from Amazon, Microsoft, and
  others help underwrite project economics and attract project financing.
- **Ecosystem development**: The tech sector's engagement accelerates the
  broader hydrogen supply chain, including transportation, storage, and fueling
  infrastructure.

## Risks and Considerations
- **Timing mismatch**: Amazon's near-term hydrogen demand may not align with
  EH2's project deployment timelines.
- **Competing technologies**: Battery-electric trucks and material handling
  equipment continue to improve, potentially reducing hydrogen demand in some
  applications.
- **Delivery infrastructure**: Green hydrogen production must be matched with
  distribution and fueling infrastructure, which remains underdeveloped.
