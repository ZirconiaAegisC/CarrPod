## 3. Corporate Structure & Operating Segments

### 3.1 Connectivity & Platforms (Residential)

- **Xfinity Broadband:** 28.7 million residential high-speed internet customers; nation's largest broadband provider. Network architecture: Hybrid Fiber-Coaxial (HFC) with DOCSIS 3.1 deployed ubiquitously; DOCSIS 4.0 in active rollout.
- **Xfinity Video:** Legacy linear video service (~13M subscribers, declining). Transitioning to IP-based Xfinity Stream platform.
- **Xfinity Voice:** Managed VoIP telephony (~10M lines).
- **Xfinity Home:** Residential security and home automation; integrated with broadband and mobile ecosystem.

### 3.2 Wireless (Xfinity Mobile / Comcast Business Mobile)

- MVNO operating on Verizon's network with WiFi-first offload architecture.
- 9.7 million total lines; 435K net adds in Q1 2026 (record).
- 16% penetration of residential broadband base.
- Device portfolio: Apple, Samsung, Google flagship devices plus Xfinity-branded gateways.
- CBRS spectrum holdings: ~830 licenses, primarily 3.5 GHz PAL in top markets.

### 3.3 Business Services

- ~$10 billion annual revenue (FY2025).
- Serving SMB, mid-market, and enterprise segments.
- Portfolio: Business Internet (DOCSIS + fiber), SD-WAN, managed security, 4G/5G backup, hosted voice, and unified communications.
- Masergy acquisition (2021) expanded enterprise managed services capability.

### 3.4 Content & Experiences (NBCUniversal)

- **Television:** NBC broadcast network (100th anniversary), Telemundo (Spanish-language), cable networks (USA Network, SYFY, Bravo, E!, MSNBC, CNBC).
- **Streaming:** Peacock — 46M paid subscribers, $2B+ quarterly revenue. FAST channels, live sports rights (Olympics through 2036, NFL Sunday Night Football, Premier League, WWE).
- **Studios:** Universal Pictures, Illumination, DreamWorks Animation, Focus Features. Tentpole IP: Jurassic World, Minions/Despicable Me, Fast & Furious, Super Mario Bros., How to Train Your Dragon. Upcoming: Nolan-directed feature, Spielberg project, Taylor Sheridan partnership (multi-year content agreement).
- **International:** Sky Group (UK/Europe) — 23M+ customer relationships across broadband, video, and mobile.

### 3.5 Theme Parks & Experiential

- **Universal Destinations & Experiences:** Universal Orlando Resort (including Epic Universe — opened May 2025), Universal Studios Hollywood, Universal Studios Japan, Universal Beijing Resort, Universal Studios Singapore.
- Epic Universe represents the largest capital investment in theme park history for Comcast; early attendance and revenue data indicate strong initial ROI trajectory.

## 5. Regulatory & Policy Environment

- **FCC:** Broadband consumer labels, net neutrality, spectrum allocation, pole-attachment rules.
- **DOJ Antitrust:** Vertical integration scrutiny; past TWC merger abandonment (2015) shaped subsequent M&A discipline.
- **Spectrum:** CBRS 3.5 GHz PAL holdings; 600 MHz and AWS-3 spectrum via auction participation. Mid-band spectrum access remains a policy advocacy priority.
- **Data Privacy:** CCPA/CPRA (California), GDPR (EU via Sky), and evolving federal privacy framework.
