# GraviStore Technology Overview

## Core Principle

GraviStore is an underground gravity energy storage system developed by Gravitricity Ltd
(Edinburgh, Scotland, founded 2011). The technology stores electrical energy as gravitational
potential energy by raising heavy weights in disused mine shafts and releases it by lowering
those weights under controlled descent, driving a generator.

The fundamental physics are governed by E = mgh: the energy stored is proportional to the mass
of the weight, the gravitational constant, and the vertical distance the weight can travel.
A 1,000-metre shaft with a 500-tonne weight stores approximately 1.36 MWh of usable energy.

## System Architecture

The GraviStore system consists of five primary subsystems:

1. Weight Assembly: Modular steel-and-concrete composite weights, typically 500 to 5,000
   tonnes, designed to operate within existing shaft diameters of 3 to 7 metres.

2. Hoist System: A single or dual drum winch arrangement using wire ropes rated for the full
   suspended load. The hoist is bidirectional, enabling regenerative braking during descent
   to convert gravitational potential energy back into electrical power.

3. Power Conversion System: Grid-connected power electronics including a four-quadrant drive
   that interfaces the hoist motor-generator with the grid at medium voltage. The system
   operates in both rectification (charging/raising) and inversion (discharging/lowering)
   modes.

4. Shaft Interface: Guide rails, arrestor mechanisms, and lateral restraint systems installed
   within the existing shaft lining. These prevent pendulum motion and ensure stable vertical
   travel even under seismic loading conditions.

5. Control System: Programmable logic controllers governing acceleration, deceleration, and
   emergency stop sequences. The system can transition from zero to full rated power in less
   than one second.

## Competitive Advantages

Unlike lithium-ion battery energy storage systems (BESS), GraviStore experiences zero
capacity degradation over its operating life. The 50-year design life compares favourably
against 10–15 years for utility-scale BESS. The 50,000-cycle endurance exceeds the typical
5,000–10,000 cycles of lithium-ion systems by a factor of five to ten.

The technology provides inertia response and frequency regulation services without the
electrochemical supply chain dependencies—cobalt, lithium, nickel, and graphite—that
constrain battery storage deployment at scale.

## Patent Portfolio

Gravitricity held 10 granted patents covering:
- Multi-weight shaft configurations for energy management flexibility
- Wire rope tension equalisation across multiple suspension points
- Shaft guide and arrestor systems for disused mine infrastructure
- Modular weight fabrication and assembly methods
- Hybrid gravity-hydrogen storage integration (H2FlexiStore concept)

## Development Status

A 250 kW demonstrator was successfully commissioned at the Port of Leith, Edinburgh, raising
and lowering two 25-tonne weights in a purpose-built lattice tower. The demonstrator
validated the core hoist control algorithms, power electronics interface, and mechanical
stability of the suspended weight system. No full-scale commercial deployment was achieved
before the company entered voluntary liquidation on 1 October 2025.
