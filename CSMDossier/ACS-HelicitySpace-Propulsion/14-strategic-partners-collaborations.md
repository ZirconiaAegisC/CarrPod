# 14 — Strategic Partners & Collaborations

## Classification: Proprietary — ACS Internal Use Only

---

## 1. Partnership Architecture

Helicity Space has constructed a multi-institutional collaboration network
spanning government laboratories, aerospace corporations, universities, and
non-profit research organizations. This network serves three functions: (1)
access to specialized experimental and computational capabilities, (2)
validation of the underlying plasma physics by independent third parties,
and (3) positioning for future government procurement and technology transition
programs.

---

## 2. Lockheed Martin Ventures

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Entity | Lockheed Martin Ventures (corporate venture capital arm of Lockheed Martin) |
| Investment Date | April 2024 |
| Investment Type | Strategic equity investment |
| Significance | First major defense prime contractor to invest in a fusion propulsion startup |

Lockheed Martin's interest in Helicity Space is consistent with the company's
longstanding involvement in advanced space propulsion. Lockheed Martin Space
operates the Orion crew vehicle, is the prime contractor for numerous
interplanetary spacecraft (Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter, Juno, OSIRIS-REx, Lucy),
and has an internal compact fusion reactor research program (the "Skunk Works
fusion" project, publicly disclosed 2013–2019). The venture investment signals
strategic alignment with the view that fusion propulsion is a long-term enabler
for national security space and deep-space logistics.

## 3. Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL)

| Collaboration Area | Detail |
|---|---|
| Institution | Los Alamos National Laboratory |
| Program | LA-COMPASS 3D MHD Simulation |
| Mechanism | Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA) or equivalent |
| Output | Predictive MHD modeling of Helicity Drive plasma dynamics |

LANL provides the core computational plasma physics capability. The LA-COMPASS
code has been developed over multiple decades for weapons science, fusion energy,
and space plasma research. Application to the Helicity Drive represents a
technology transfer pathway consistent with LANL's statutory mission to support
U.S. industrial competitiveness in advanced technology.

### DOE INFUSE Program

The Innovation Network for Fusion Energy (INFUSE) program, administered by the
Department of Energy's Office of Fusion Energy Sciences, pairs private fusion
companies with DOE national laboratory capabilities. Helicity Space's
participation in INFUSE provides access to LANL computational and diagnostic
resources that would otherwise be beyond the financial reach of an early-stage
startup.

### Limitless Space Institute (LSI) I2 Support

The Limitless Space Institute, a non-profit organization focused on
interstellar propulsion research, provides I2 (Interstellar Initiative) support
to Helicity Space. LSI's involvement signals the broader interstellar
exploration community's recognition of the Helicity Drive as a candidate
propulsion technology for missions beyond the solar system.

## 6. Corporate and Venture Partners

### Airbus Ventures

Airbus Ventures, the venture capital arm of Airbus SE, participated in the
December 2023 seed round. Airbus is Europe's largest aerospace corporation
and the prime contractor for the Ariane launch vehicle family. The investment
aligns with Airbus's long-term interest in advanced space transportation.

### Voyager Space

Voyager Space operates the International Space Station National Laboratory
and is developing the Starlab commercial space station. Its investment in
Helicity Space reflects interest in in-space propulsion for orbital logistics
and deep-space missions.
