# Financial Performance & Key Metrics

## Revenue Trajectory

Everspin's revenue growth reflects the transition from a Toggle MRAM-
centric business to a diversified multi-product company with STT-MRAM
and high-reliability products contributing an increasing share.

Fiscal Year 2024 revenue was approximately $50.2 million. Fiscal Year
2025 delivered $55.2 million, a 10% increase year-over-year, driven by
a 28% jump in product-level MRAM sales despite headwinds in licensing
and other revenue. Licensing revenue, which consists primarily of IP
royalties from foundry partners and technology transfer agreements,
typically fluctuates quarter to quarter and is not considered core
operating income by management.

Q1 2026 revenue reached $14.9 million, up 14% from $13.1 million in
Q1 2025. Within this total, MRAM product revenue (excluding licensing)
was $14.1 million, a 28% year-over-year increase. This product revenue
growth reflects strong demand for high-density STT-MRAM and Persist
family products in industrial, data center, and aerospace applications.

Q2 2026 revenue guidance of $15.5–16.5 million implies sequential
growth of 4–11% and year-over-year growth of approximately 10–17%.

## Gross Margin Progression

Gross margin has been a key indicator of product mix improvement. The
company's Toggle MRAM products carry moderate margins, while STT-MRAM
and high-reliability, extended-temperature, and radiation-tolerant
products command premium pricing and higher margins.

FY 2024: ~48% gross margin
FY 2025: 51.2% gross margin (+320 bps YoY)
Q1 2026: 52.7% gross margin (+150 bps QoQ)

Management expects gross margin to sustain above 50% as the product
mix continues to shift toward higher-margin STT-MRAM and specialized
high-reliability products. The target range over the medium term is
52–55%, with potential upside from yield improvements on the 28nm node.

## Profitability Milestone

After years of investment in STT-MRAM development and foundry
qualification, Everspin achieved its first GAAP-profitable year in
FY 2025, reporting net income of $5.2 million. This compared to a net
loss of approximately $1.7 million in FY 2024. The inflection to
profitability was driven by revenue growth, gross margin expansion,
and disciplined operating expense management. Operating expenses
(R&D + SG&A) were approximately $23 million in FY 2025, reflecting
the dual investment in current product support and next-generation
STT-MRAM development on advanced nodes.

## Balance Sheet Strength

Everspin maintains a conservative balance sheet with zero long-term
debt. Key balance sheet metrics:

- Cash and equivalents at December 31, 2025: $44.5 million
- Cash and equivalents at March 31, 2026 (Q1 2026 end): $40.5 million
- No outstanding bank debt or convertible notes
- Working capital (current assets less current liabilities): positive
  throughout the trailing eight quarters

The $4.0 million sequential decline in cash during Q1 2026 was
attributable to working capital investments, inventory build for new
product ramps, and capital expenditures related to facility and
equipment upgrades at the Chandler fab.

## Revenue Composition by End Market

Everspin does not disclose granular end-market revenue splits, but
management commentary points to the following approximate distribution:

- Data center and enterprise storage: ~30%
- Industrial and embedded systems: ~35%
- Aerospace and defense: ~20%
- Automotive and other: ~15%

The defense and aerospace share has been growing, supported by the
$40 million Toggle MRAM defense subcontract and the $14.6 million
DoD facility sustainment contract. The industrial share is being
driven by Persist product adoption in factory automation and
infrastructure applications.

## Operating Expenses

Everspin targets operating expenses of approximately $5.5–6.0 million
per quarter. The primary spending categories are:

- Research and Development: ~60% of operating expenses, funding MTJ
  process development, STT-MRAM circuit design, foundry qualification,
  and next-generation cell development for sub-28nm nodes.
- Sales, General and Administrative: ~40%, covering sales force,
  applications engineering support, public company compliance, and
  IP portfolio management.

## Revenue Concentration

The company's customer base is diversified across 1,405+ end customers.
No single end customer accounted for more than 10% of FY 2025 revenue,
though distribution partners collectively represent a meaningful
channel. The company sells through a combination of direct sales,
manufacturer representatives, and distribution partnerships including
Arrow Electronics and Avnet.

## Forward Revenue Target

Management has articulated a target of reaching $100 million in annual
revenue within 3–5 years, which from the current ~$55–60 million
run-rate implies a compound annual growth rate of approximately
14–18%. The growth drivers include scaling STT-MRAM in data center,
ramping Persist EMxxLX into industrial and satellite markets, and the
eventual launch of the Unisys NOR flash replacement family.

## Investment Risks

Key financial risk factors disclosed in SEC filings include: technology
adoption risk (MRAM's displacement of entrenched memory technologies),
customer concentration risk from a limited number of large distributors,
manufacturing concentration risk (dependence on TSMC for advanced-node
STT-MRAM supply), foreign exchange risk, and competition from larger
memory manufacturers with substantially greater financial resources
including Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron.
