# Patent Portfolio, Design Wins & Competitive Position

## Intellectual Property Portfolio

Everspin's intellectual property position is one of its most durable
competitive advantages. The company holds over 700 active patents
covering fundamental and applied aspects of MRAM technology:

### Patent Categories

1. **MTJ Materials and Structures** (~30% of portfolio): Patents
   covering magnetic tunnel junction layer compositions, interface
   engineering for perpendicular magnetic anisotropy (PMA), synthetic
   antiferromagnet (SAF) structures for the pinned layer, and
   diffusion barrier layers that protect the MgO tunnel barrier
   during BEOL thermal processing.

2. **MTJ Fabrication Methods** (~25%): Process patents covering
   MTJ pillar etching techniques, encapsulation and sidewall
   passivation methods, magnetic annealing protocols, and
   integration schemes that allow MRAM to be embedded within
   CMOS logic BEOL flows without degrading transistor performance.

3. **Cell and Array Architectures** (~20%): Patents on 1T-1MTJ
   cell layout, read and write circuit topologies (sense amplifiers,
   write drivers, reference cell schemes), array redundancy and
   repair methods, and error correction architectures specific to
   MRAM's error characteristics.

4. **Toggle MRAM-Specific** (~10%): Patents covering the Toggle
   write mechanism, digit line and bit line architectures,
   thermally-assisted switching techniques, and radiation hardening
   methods for field-switched MRAM cells.

5. **System-Level Applications** (~10%): Patents on MRAM
   integration in specific system contexts: persistent memory
   controllers, cache-coherent MRAM interfaces, security
   applications (physical unclonable functions, tamper detection),
   and power-fail-safe data management.

6. **Next-Generation Technologies** (~5%): Forward-looking patents
   on spin-orbit torque (SOT) MRAM, voltage-controlled magnetic
   anisotropy (VCMA) switching, multi-level cell (MLC) MRAM, and
   3D MRAM array stacking.

### Patent Geography

The portfolio is global, with filings in all major semiconductor
manufacturing and consumption jurisdictions:

- United States (USPTO): Primary filing jurisdiction; largest
  concentration of patents
- Taiwan: Essential for foundry partnership enforcement (TSMC)
- China: Growing importance as Chinese memory companies develop
  MRAM programs
- Japan, South Korea: Home to major memory and electronics
  manufacturers
- European Patent Office: Coverage for automotive and industrial
  customers

### Defensive and Offensive Value

The patent portfolio serves multiple strategic purposes:

- **Freedom to operate**: The broad prior art position makes it
  difficult for competitors to patent fundamental MRAM structures
  that would block Everspin's product roadmap.
- **Licensing revenue**: While not a primary revenue source,
  Everspin has licensed portions of its patent portfolio to
  foundry partners and, historically, to other semiconductor
  companies exploring MRAM technology.
- **Barrier to entry**: A new entrant seeking to commercialize an
  MRAM product competitive with Everspin's would need to navigate
  a dense patent landscape, increasing time-to-market and legal
  risk.
- **M&A asset**: In any acquisition scenario, the patent portfolio
  would represent a significant portion of Everspin's enterprise
  value, likely comparable to or exceeding the value of the
  physical manufacturing assets.

## Design Win Momentum

Design wins are the leading indicator of future revenue in the
semiconductor industry. A design win occurs when a customer
selects Everspin's memory for a specific product design and
completes the engineering validation, committing to use the part
in production. The design win pipeline is a critical metric for
evaluating Everspin's growth trajectory.

### Design Win Trend

| Year  | Design Wins | YoY Change |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| 2023  | ~140        | —          |
| 2024  | 178         | +27%       |
| 2025  | 238         | +34%       |

The acceleration in design wins — from +27% growth in 2024 to +34%
in 2025 — suggests increasing customer confidence in MRAM technology
and growing market pull for non-volatile memory alternatives.

### Design Win Composition

While Everspin does not provide detailed breakdowns of design win
composition, management commentary suggests the following
distribution by product line in 2025:

- Persist EMxxLX (industrial and embedded): ~45% of design wins
- Toggle MRAM (defense and specialty industrial): ~25%
- STT-MRAM DDR4 (data center): ~15%
- Unisys (NOR flash replacement, early engagement): ~10%
- Other (custom, licensing-related): ~5%

The concentration in Persist is consistent with the company's
strategic emphasis on high-reliability industrial and aerospace
applications, which generate a large number of individual design
wins (each industrial product typically uses a small quantity of
memory, so many design wins are required to build meaningful
revenue).

### Design Win Conversion

Not all design wins convert to production revenue. The semiconductor
industry attrition rate depends on the end market:
- Industrial: ~70–80% conversion, long tail (designs often run
  5–10 years)
- Defense: ~90% conversion, very long tail (designs run 10–30 years)
- Data center: ~50–60% conversion, concentrated (few designs, high
  volume each)
- Unisys/NOR replacement: Unknown, early stage

## Competitive Positioning

### Direct Competitors

No company competes directly with Everspin across its full product
portfolio, but several compete in specific segments:

**NOR Flash Suppliers** (Winbond, Macronix, GigaDevice, Micron,
Infineon): Compete with Unisys in the NOR flash replacement market.
They have incumbency advantages (established supply chains, customer
relationships, volume pricing) but are tied to a technology with
inherent limitations that MRAM overcomes.

**FRAM Suppliers** (Infineon/Cypress): Compete with Persist in
industrial and embedded sockets below 16Mb density. FRAM has higher
write endurance but is density-limited and has a weaker process
node scaling roadmap.

**Battery-Backed SRAM Suppliers** (various): Compete with Toggle
MRAM in industrial and defense sockets. BB-SRAM is a legacy
technology with a shrinking supplier base; MRAM is the natural
replacement.

### Indirect Competitors and Potential Entrants

**Samsung Electronics**: Samsung has an active STT-MRAM R&D program,
has demonstrated 1Gb STT-MRAM test chips at 28nm, and has announced
intentions to commercialize embedded MRAM (eMRAM) for its foundry
customers at the 28nm and 14nm nodes. Samsung's entry into the
standalone MRAM market would be the most significant competitive
threat Everspin could face, given Samsung's manufacturing scale,
financial resources, and customer relationships. However, Samsung
has been focused on eMRAM for its foundry business rather than
standalone MRAM products, and has not announced commercial
standalone MRAM availability as of mid-2026.

**SK Hynix**: SK Hynix has research activity in STT-MRAM and SOT-MRAM
but has not announced commercial MRAM products. Its focus remains
primarily on DRAM and NAND flash.

**Micron Technology**: Micron discontinued its own MRAM development
program in the early 2010s and has since focused on 3D XPoint
(discontinued), DRAM, and NAND. Micron currently competes with
Everspin only as a NOR flash supplier. Micron could re-enter MRAM
through internal development or acquisition.

**Intel**: Intel's Optane (3D XPoint) persistent memory was
discontinued in 2022. Intel has MRAM research activity but no
commercial products. The closure of Optane eliminated the primary
alternative persistent memory technology for data center.

### Competitive Moats

Several factors protect Everspin's competitive position:

1. **Manufacturing complexity**: MRAM fabrication is substantially
   more difficult than standard CMOS or flash memory fabrication.
   The MTJ deposition, etch, and encapsulation steps require
   specialized equipment and deep process expertise that cannot be
   replicated quickly.

2. **Intellectual property**: The 700+ patent portfolio creates a
   dense thicket that competitors must navigate.

3. **Qualification lead time**: Industrial, aerospace, and data
   center customers require 12–36 months to qualify a new memory
   component for production. Once Everspin is designed in and
   qualified, switching to a competitor requires the customer to
   re-qualify the entire memory subsystem — a significant barrier.

4. **Trusted foundry status**: Everspin's Chandler fab is a domestic
   trusted foundry for defense applications. No foreign competitor
   can supply the U.S. defense MRAM market without establishing
   equivalent trusted manufacturing capability in the U.S. — a
   process that would take years.

5. **Ecosystem maturity**: Everspin has reference designs, software
   drivers, application notes, and field application engineering
   support built up over 15+ years of commercial MRAM production.
   A new entrant would need to replicate this ecosystem.

## Patent Portfolio Risk Factors

- **Patent expiration**: The earliest Everspin patents (filed
  circa 2008) will begin expiring in the late 2020s. The company
  must continue generating new IP to maintain the portfolio's
  defensive value.

- **Litigation risk**: As MRAM becomes commercially significant,
  the risk of patent challenges (inter partes review, district
  court litigation) from competitors or non-practicing entities
  increases.

- **Geographic gaps**: Patent enforcement in China has historically
  been less predictable than in the U.S. or Europe, potentially
  limiting Everspin's ability to prevent Chinese memory companies
  from developing MRAM products that compete in global markets
  outside China.

## Summary of Competitive Position

Everspin occupies the unusual position of being both the incumbent
(in MRAM technology) and the challenger (in the broader non-volatile
memory market). It is the only company with:
- 15+ years of volume MRAM production
- A diversified product portfolio spanning defense, data center,
  industrial, and embedded applications
- A strong IP portfolio and a trusted domestic manufacturing
  capability
- A credible path to displacing a portion of the $3B NOR flash
  market

The competitive risk is not from another pure-play MRAM company
(no such company exists at commercial scale) but from entry by a
large memory manufacturer with the resources to overcome the
manufacturing and IP barriers. Everspin's strategy to build
unassailable customer relationships through deep qualification
and co-engineering — as demonstrated with the IBM FCM partnership
and the DoD defense subcontract — is designed to create switching
costs that deter even well-resourced entrants.
