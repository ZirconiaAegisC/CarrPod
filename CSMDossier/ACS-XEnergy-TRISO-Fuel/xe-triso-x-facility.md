# TRISO-X Fuel Fabrication Facility — Oak Ridge, Tennessee

## Facility Overview

The TRISO-X Fuel Fabrication Facility is X-energy's first-of-a-kind
commercial-scale HALEU TRISO fuel production facility, located in Oak Ridge,
Tennessee. TRISO-X LLC is a wholly-owned subsidiary of X-energy established
to manufacture and supply TRISO fuel for Xe-100 reactors and other advanced
reactor designs. The facility represents the first commercial-scale TRISO
fuel fabrication plant in the United States.

## Location and Site Selection

The facility is sited within the Horizon Center Industrial Park in Oak Ridge,
Tennessee, adjacent to the U.S. Department of Energy's Oak Ridge National
Laboratory (ORNL) and the Y-12 National Security Complex. Site selection
factors included:

- Proximity to ORNL's nuclear fuel cycle research expertise and facilities
- Access to a skilled nuclear workforce in the Oak Ridge region
- Existing DOE infrastructure for nuclear material handling and security
- Tennessee's supportive regulatory and economic development environment
- Proximity to HALEU enrichment capabilities (Centrus Piketon, OH)

## Production Capacity

**Design Capacity:** Initial production of approximately 700,000 TRISO fuel
pebbles per year, sufficient to fuel approximately four Xe-100 reactor
modules annually. The facility is designed with expansion capability for
multi-reactor fleet deployment.

**Phase 1:** Pilot-scale production line for qualification fuel and initial
commercial fuel batches.

**Phase 2:** Full commercial production lines with automated pebble
manufacturing, quality assurance, and packaging systems.

## Manufacturing Process Flow

The TRISO-X facility incorporates all five steps of TRISO particle
fabrication, pebble pressing, carbonization, purification, and quality
control under one roof:

1. **Kernel Receipt and Inspection:** HALEU UCO kernels received from
   enrichment and kernel fabrication subcontractors. Incoming QC verifies
   kernel dimensions, enrichment, impurities, and sphericity.

2. **Fluidized-Bed Coating:** Multiple FB-CVD coating furnaces deposit
   buffer, IPyC, SiC, and OPyC layers sequentially. Each coating furnace
   is computer-controlled with in-situ monitoring of temperature, gas
   flow rates, and deposition rates.

3. **Particle Quality Control:** Automated sampling systems extract
   coated particles from each batch for burn-leach testing, dimensional
   analysis, and SiC density measurement. Batches failing QC criteria are
   rejected and recycled.

4. **Pebble Pressing:** Qualified TRISO particles are mixed with graphite
   matrix powder and pressed into 50 mm inner fuel zone preforms. A 5 mm
   graphite shell is overmolded to form the final 60 mm pebble using
   isostatic pressing technology.

5. **Carbonization and Purification:** Green pebbles are heat-treated in
   controlled-atmosphere furnaces: carbonization at ~800°C to pyrolyze
   the phenolic binder, followed by high-temperature vacuum purification
   at ~1800-2000°C to remove metallic impurities.

6. **Final Quality Assurance:** Each pebble undergoes dimensional
   inspection, visual surface examination, gamma spectroscopy for
   enrichment verification, and crush-strength testing on a statistical
   sampling basis.

## NRC Licensing Status

TRISO-X submitted a license application to the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory
Commission (NRC) under 10 CFR Part 70 (Special Nuclear Material) for
possession and processing of HALEU material. The NRC accepted the
application for detailed technical review. The licensing process includes:

- Integrated Safety Analysis (ISA) of chemical and nuclear hazards
- Physical security plan for Category I special nuclear material
- Environmental assessment under NEPA
- Emergency preparedness and response plans
- Decommissioning funding plan

NRC review is expected to take approximately 24-36 months from application
acceptance to license issuance. Construction will not begin until the NRC
license is approved.

## Capital Investment and Job Creation

**Estimated Total Project Cost:** Approximately $300-400 million
**DOE Support:** The facility benefits from DOE cost-share funding under
the Advanced Reactor Demonstration Program (ARDP) and potentially from
the HALEU Availability Program authorized by the Inflation Reduction Act.
**Direct Employment:** Approximately 400 permanent positions at full
production capacity, including nuclear engineers, materials scientists,
radiochemists, nuclear safety specialists, and skilled manufacturing
technicians.

## HALEU Supply Chain Integration

The TRISO-X facility is a critical link in the emerging domestic HALEU
supply chain. HALEU (enriched to between 5% and 19.75% U-235) feedstock
will be sourced from:

- **Primary Source:** Centrus Energy's American Centrifuge Plant in
  Piketon, Ohio (first U.S. HALEU production facility, operational 2024)
- **Secondary Source:** DOE downblending of surplus highly-enriched
  uranium (HEU) to HALEU levels
- **Long-term Source:** Additional domestic enrichment capacity funded
  through the DOE HALEU Availability Program

X-energy's TRISO-X facility is uniquely positioned as the first commercial
customer for domestic HALEU, providing a guaranteed off-take that supports
the business case for domestic enrichment capacity expansion.
