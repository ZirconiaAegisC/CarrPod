# Chesterfield County, Virginia — ARC Site Analysis

## Site Announcement

In December 2024, Commonwealth Fusion Systems announced the selection of the James River Industrial Center in Chesterfield County, Virginia as the site for ARC-1, the world's first grid-connected commercial fusion power plant. The announcement followed a multi-year site selection process evaluating over 100 locations across the United States.

## Why Chesterfield County?

### 1. Grid Infrastructure

- **PJM Interconnection** — Chesterfield is within the PJM Regional Transmission Organization, the largest wholesale electricity market in the U.S. (serving 65 million people across 13 states and DC).
- **500 kV transmission** — Dominion Energy's Chesterfield–Cunningham 500 kV line runs within 2 miles of the site.
- **Interconnection queue** — CFS filed an interconnection request with PJM in early 2025; typical study timeline is 2-4 years for large generators.

### 2. Workforce & Talent Pipeline

- **Dominion Energy** — Virginia's largest utility, headquartered in Richmond, employs thousands of nuclear, transmission, and power plant engineers.
- **Newport News Shipbuilding** — Nuclear-qualified workforce (naval reactor construction) within 80 miles.
- **Virginia universities** — Virginia Tech, UVA, VCU, and William & Mary produce engineering graduates with relevant disciplines.
- **Community college system** — Virginia's Nuclear Education and Workforce Development program trains technicians and operators.

### 3. Regulatory & Political Support

- **Governor Glenn Youngkin** (Republican, term 2022–2026) has been a vocal supporter, framing ARC as part of Virginia's "all-of-the-above" energy strategy.
- **Virginia Clean Economy Act (VCEA)** — Mandates 100% carbon-free electricity by 2045 for Dominion Energy and 2050 for Appalachian Power. Fusion qualifies as carbon-free generation.
- **ADVANCE Act of 2024** — Federal legislation directing the NRC to establish a technology-inclusive regulatory framework for advanced nuclear and fusion reactors, streamlining the licensing pathway.
- **Chesterfield County Board of Supervisors** — Approved land-use permits and offered local tax incentives.

### 4. Site Characteristics

The James River Industrial Center offers:

- Pre-zoned heavy industrial land (no rezoning required).
- Existing road and rail access (CSX rail line adjacent).
- Barge-accessible deepwater frontage on the James River for heavy component delivery (TF magnets, vacuum vessel sectors).
- Adequate water supply for cooling (~20,000–40,000 gallons per minute for once-through or recirculating cooling).
- Proximity to Richmond for construction workforce housing and logistics.

## Environmental & Community Considerations

### Water Use

ARC requires cooling water for its steam turbine condenser. Options under study:

- **Once-through cooling** — Withdraw from and return to the James River (requires CWA Section 316(b) compliance for fish protection).
- **Closed-cycle cooling** — Cooling towers with makeup water from the James (lower withdrawal, higher consumption due to evaporation).
- **Hybrid** — Wet/dry cooling to balance water use and efficiency.

### Environmental Review

ARC will require a federal Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) under NEPA, coordinated by the NRC or DOE. Virginia DEQ will conduct a parallel state-level review. Key environmental topics:

- Thermal discharge to James River (if once-through cooling).
- Visual impact (the ARC building envelope is approximately 80–100 ft tall).
- Construction period traffic and noise.
- Radiological off-site dose limits (anticipated to be at or near zero during normal operation).

### Public Engagement

CFS has committed to:

- Quarterly community information sessions in Chesterfield.
- A Community Advisory Board with local stakeholders.
- Publicly accessible environmental monitoring data.
- STEM education partnerships with Chesterfield County Public Schools.

## Strategic Significance

Chesterfield County represents the first fusion plant siting decision in history. The location combines favorable grid access, a nuclear-experienced workforce, supportive state policy, and accessible water and transportation infrastructure. If ARC-1 succeeds here, Virginia could become the global hub for commercial fusion deployment — analogous to how Texas and Louisiana became hubs for the petrochemical industry in the 20th century.
