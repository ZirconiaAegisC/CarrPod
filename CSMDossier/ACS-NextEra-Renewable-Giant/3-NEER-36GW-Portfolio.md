# NextEra Energy — NEER 36GW Portfolio

## NextEra Energy Resources: The World's Largest Clean Energy Operator

NextEra Energy Resources (NEER) is the competitive energy subsidiary that has 
propelled NextEra Energy to its position as the premier renewable energy 
developer and operator globally. With approximately 36 gigawatts of net 
generating capacity, NEER's portfolio dwarfs that of any other independent 
power producer and even the dedicated renewables arms of other major utilities.

## Portfolio Composition
The NEER generation fleet is overwhelmingly comprised of wind and solar 
photovoltaic facilities interconnected across the United States and Canada. 
Wind energy represents the largest single technology segment, with facilities 
concentrated in the Great Plains, Texas, and the Midwest. Solar generation has 
been the fastest-growing component in recent years, with utility-scale 
photovoltaic installations increasingly deployed across the Sunbelt, Southwest, 
and Southeast.

The portfolio also includes a smaller but strategically significant nuclear 
component through ownership interests in certain merchant nuclear facilities. 
Battery energy storage systems represent a rapidly emerging asset class within 
the NEER portfolio, reflecting the increasing importance of dispatchability in 
an intermittent-heavy renewable fleet.

## Development Capability
NEER possesses what is widely regarded as the industry's most capable renewable 
energy development platform. A multidisciplinary team covering land 
acquisition, permitting, environmental assessment, meteorology, transmission 
studies, interconnection analysis, engineering procurement and construction 
management, and project finance executes the development pipeline with a rhythm 
that competitors have struggled to match.

Proprietary data analytics platforms ingest decades of meteorological and 
operational data to optimize turbine and panel placement, predict generation 
profiles, and manage degradation curves. This analytical capability translates 
directly into superior project economics and more accurate forecasts for 
offtakers and financial counterparties.

## Commercial Structure
The preponderance of NEER's generation output is sold under long-term power 
purchase agreements (PPAs) with creditworthy offtakers, including 
investor-owned utilities, municipal utilities, electric cooperatives, and 
increasingly, corporate and industrial customers. These contracts typically 
extend 15 to 25 years and provide fixed or escalating price structures that 
insulate NEER from merchant power market volatility.

The commercial origination team has been particularly successful in securing 
corporate PPAs, serving the growing demand from technology companies, data 
center operators, and industrial firms seeking to meet renewable energy 
commitments. This customer diversification has strengthened the portfolio's 
credit profile and reduced reliance on traditional utility offtakers.

## Geographic Diversification
NEER operates facilities in more than 30 U.S. states and several Canadian 
provinces. This geographic diversification mitigates exposure to localized 
weather patterns, regulatory changes, or transmission constraints in any single 
region. The portfolio's dispersion also provides natural hedging against 
resource variability, as wind and solar production patterns are imperfectly 
correlated across the vast operational footprint.

## Operational Excellence
Centralized remote operations centers monitor and control generation assets in 
real time, employing advanced analytics to optimize performance, schedule 
maintenance, and diagnose equipment issues before they escalate. The operations 
and maintenance organization has compiled one of the industry's most 
comprehensive databases of component performance data, enabling predictive 
maintenance models that reduce downtime and extend asset life.

## Project Pipeline
Beyond the 36 gigawatts of operating capacity, NEER maintains a development 
pipeline measured in tens of additional gigawatts. This pipeline encompasses 
projects at every stage of development: early-stage site evaluation, mid-stage 
permitting and interconnection, advanced-stage construction, and near-term 
commissioning. The pipeline's depth provides exceptional visibility into future 
growth and earnings contribution.

## Competitive Procurement
NEER's procurement scale yields meaningful cost advantages in equipment 
acquisition. As one of the world's largest buyers of wind turbines and solar 
modules, the company commands pricing, warranty terms, and delivery priority 
that smaller developers cannot match. These procurement advantages compound 
with the company's low cost of capital to produce levelized costs of energy 
that are among the industry's most competitive.
