## 1. THE STRATEGIC PROBLEM

Maersk operates 700+ container vessels that at any given moment have 200-400 ships underway across every major ocean route. Modern container ships are floating electrical systems: bridge electronics, engine room automation, cargo refrigeration (reefers), ballast control, fuel management, and crew safety systems all depend on clean electrical power. The ship's steel hull, moving through conductive seawater under a disturbed geomagnetic field, becomes a collector for geomagnetically induced currents. These currents enter the ship's electrical ground system and propagate into every connected electronic device.

**When ports go dark:** During a major GMD event, port operations cease. Container cranes (electrically driven, 5-25 MW each) stop. Terminal operating systems fail. Refrigerated container stacks lose power. Ships in port cannot offload. Ships at sea cannot communicate with ports to determine where to go. The global supply chain — 90% of which moves by sea — freezes.

**The AIS/GPS Collapse:** Automatic Identification System transponders and GPS receivers depend on satellite radio signals in the 1.5-1.6 GHz band. During a GMD event, ionospheric scintillation disrupts these signals. Ships disappear from tracking systems. Collision avoidance becomes visual-only — which, for a 400-meter vessel with a 3 km stopping distance, is effectively no collision avoidance at all.

## 2. THE ACS SOLUTION

**Hull Potential Management System (HPMS):** Adapted from CSM's Charlemagne Fleet (CSM-CF-700) amphibious vessel technology. Measures hull-to-seawater GIC potential and dynamically injects compensating current to maintain safe voltage envelope. Cost per Triple-E class vessel: $1.2M.

**Bridge Electronics Hardening:** GIC-rated isolation transformers, MXene-based power conditioning, and shielded equipment enclosures. Maintains navigation capability through GMD event. Cost per vessel: $380,000.

**Protonic Fleet Communication:** CSM-PC-5000 nodes on each vessel for GIC-immune ship-to-ship and ship-to-shore communication when satellite links fail. Range: 5,000 km surface wave. Cost: $40,000 per vessel + $1.03M per shore node.

**Reefer Power Protection:** Dedicated power conditioning for refrigerated container electrical buses. Prevents loss of temperature-sensitive cargo during GMD events. Cost: $25,000 per reefer bus (protects 50-100 containers).

**Crew Safety Systems:** GIC-hardened fire detection, bilge alarm, emergency lighting, EPIRB, and lifeboat systems. Cost per vessel: $95,000.

## 3. WILLIAMS HEURISTIC — PLAIN LANGUAGE

A container ship is a giant steel box floating in saltwater. Saltwater conducts electricity. During a solar storm, the Earth's magnetic field wiggles, and when a magnetic field wiggles near a conductor (the ship's steel hull in conductive seawater), it generates electrical currents. These currents flow into the ship's wiring and can fry the navigation computers. Our technology measures these currents and cancels them out before they cause problems, and provides a backup communication system that doesn't use radio waves (which the storm also disrupts). The ship keeps navigating, stays in contact, and gets its cargo safely to port.
