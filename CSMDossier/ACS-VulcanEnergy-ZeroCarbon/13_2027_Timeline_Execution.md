## 2025-2026 Pre-FID Activities

### Definitive Feasibility Study
- Front-End Engineering and Design (FEED) completion.
- Fixed-price quotations from major equipment vendors (ORC turbines, sorption columns, EDBM stacks, crystallizers).
- Well field reservoir model validated with additional 3D seismic and production history from Landau geothermal plant.
- Independent LCA update to support EU Taxonomy and customer carbon reporting.
- Banks' Independent Engineer review initiated.

### Permitting
- Extraction permit (Bewilligung under BBergG): Submission of Operating Plan; public consultation; authority review.
- Operational permit (BImSchG): Emissions inventory; noise impact assessment; chemical safety report.
- Water permit (WHG): Brine extraction and reinjection plan; groundwater monitoring network design.
- Strategic Project status provides 24-month (extraction) and 12-month (processing) permitting deadlines.

### Financing
- EIB and KfW due diligence processes (6-12 months).
- Offtake pre-payment agreements with Stellantis, Renault, VW, LGES, Umicore.
- Grant applications: EU Innovation Fund, InvestEU, German Federal Funding programmes.
- Commercial bank syndication (ING, BNP Paribas, Société Générale, UniCredit targeted).

## 2026-2027 Construction Sequence

### Q1-Q2 2026: Site Preparation & Early Works
- Final site grading and civil works at Landau industrial park.
- Construction laydown areas and temporary facilities.
- Long-lead equipment procurement:
  - ORC turbine generator sets (12-18 months delivery).
  - Sorption columns (10-14 months delivery).
  - EDBM membrane stacks (8-12 months delivery).
  - Vacuum crystallizers (10-14 months delivery).

### Q2-Q4 2026: Well Field Drilling
- Rig mobilization; 4-6 well doublets drilled initially (expandable to 8-12).
- Drilling duration: 40-60 days per well (2,500-3,500 m depth).
- Well testing: flow rate, temperature, brine chemistry confirmation.
- Geothermal plant construction in parallel with drilling campaign.

### Q3 2026 – Q2 2027: Plant Construction
- Geothermal ORC plant: turbine hall, heat exchangers, cooling systems.
- DLE plant: sorption column installation, piping, instrumentation, DCS.
- LiOH refinery: EDBM hall, MVR evaporation, crystallization, drying, packaging.
- Interconnecting pipe racks and utilities.
- Electrical substation and grid connection.

### Q2-Q3 2027: Pre-Commissioning
- Hydrostatic testing of all vessels and piping.
- Instrument loop checks and calibration.
- Motor solo runs and equipment rotation checks.
- Water commissioning: DLE columns tested with water to verify flow dynamics.
- Geothermal plant: ORC turbine mechanical run with nitrogen, then brine heat.

### Q3-Q4 2027: Commissioning & First Lithium
- Brine introduction to DLE columns; sorbent conditioning.
- First lithium eluate production; LiCl concentration verified.
- EDBM commissioning: LiOH production from purified LiCl brine.
- First LiOH·H2O crystals produced (critical milestone).
- Product sampling and qualification:
  - In-house QA/QC lab analysis.
  - External ISO 17025-accredited lab verification.
  - Customer qualification samples to Stellantis, Renault, VW, LGES, Umicore.

## Timeline Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Permitting delays (German regulatory complexity) | Medium | High | Strategic Project status; engaged permitting team; political support |
| Well field drilling delays (rig availability, weather) | Low-Med | Medium | Contracting multi-rig campaigns; Q2-Q4 drilling window avoids winter |
| Equipment delivery delays (global supply chain) | Medium | Medium | Early procurement; dual-source qualification for critical equipment |
| DLE scale-up challenges (novel technology) | Medium-High | High | Extensive pilot testing; conservative ramp-up; vendor performance guarantees |
| EDBM scale-up (first commercial LiOH EDBM) | Medium | Medium | Pilot EDBM operational; vendor guarantees; fallback to conventional LiOH production |
| Construction cost overrun | Medium | Medium | EPCM with incentivized contracts; contingency allowances; modularization |
| Lithium price at time of production (revenue risk) | Medium | High | Floor pricing in offtake agreements; geothermal electricity revenue provides cash floor |
| Funding gap (capital shortfall) | Medium | High | Multi-source financing; contingency reserve; staged scope if required |

## Ramp-Up Curve (Projected)

| Period | Output (% of nameplate) | LiOH (tons) | Key Activities |
|--------|------------------------|-------------|----------------|
| H2 2027 | 0-15% | 0-1,800 | Commissioning; first production; qualification |
| H1 2028 | 15-50% | 1,800-6,000 | Process optimization; sorbent cycling stabilization |
| H2 2028 | 50-80% | 6,000-9,600 | Steady improvement; workforce proficiency |
| 2029 | 80-100% | 19,200-24,000 | Approaching nameplate; reliability optimization |

The 18-24 month ramp-up from first lithium to approaching nameplate reflects the first-of-kind nature of commercial-scale geothermal DLE. Each unit operation must be individually stabilized and integrated before the overall plant approaches its design throughput. Conservative assumptions in the ramp-up curve are appropriate given Vulcan's novel technology risk.

## Key Success Factors for 2027

1. **Permitting approval in 2025**: Without extraction and operational permits, FID and construction cannot proceed.
2. **Financial close in 2026**: Securing the full €1.3-1.8B financing package, likely requiring DFI anchor commitment (EIB/KfW) to unlock commercial debt.
3. **Technology vendor performance**: First-of-kind commercial DLE and EDBM must meet recovery, purity, and uptime guarantees.
4. **Offtake contract continuity**: Maintaining strong customer relationships through project execution; managing expectations if ramp-up is slower than projected.
5. **Lithium market recovery**: Sustained LiOH price above $20,000/t to support project economics and maintain investor confidence through the capital-intensive construction phase.

The 2027 first-production target, while ambitious for a first-of-kind integrated geothermal-DLE facility, is achievable if the critical path items — permitting, financing, and equipment delivery — progress according to plan in 2025-2026. Vulcan's Strategic Project designation and strong offtake portfolio provide tailwinds, but the company's novel technology and substantial capital requirement represent execution risks that warrant close monitoring.
