## 2. Automotive Qualification Process

### The Qualification Ladder
Automotive qualification of a new anode material follows a structured, multi-year process:

**Phase 1: Material Characterization (6-12 months)**
- Supplier provides 1-10 kg samples
- XRD (silicon crystallinity), SEM/TEM (morphology), XPS (surface chemistry), BET (surface area)
- Electrochemical performance: half-cell cycling (capacity, FCE, rate), full-cell coin cell (initial cycling)
- Safety screening: DSC/TGA (thermal stability), ARC (accelerating rate calorimetry) for thermal runaway
- Supply chain audit: Manufacturing process review, quality systems (IATF 16949 readiness), business continuity

**Phase 2: Electrode-Level Optimization (6-12 months)**
- Supplier provides 10-100 kg samples
- Electrode formulation optimization: binder type/ratio, conductive additive, loading
- Coating process development: slurry rheology, coating window, drying profile
- Cell assembly at pilot scale (1-10 Ah pouch or cylindrical cells)
- Formation protocol optimization for SCC55 (first-cycle SEI formation may require different voltage/current profile than graphite)

**Phase 3: Cell-Level Validation (12-18 months)**
- A-Sample: 100-1,000 cells; initial performance screening, short-term cycling (<200 cycles)
- B-Sample: 1,000-10,000 cells; full performance characterization, safety testing (nail penetration, overcharge, crush, thermal stability)
- C-Sample: 10,000-50,000 cells; production-representative cells, statistical performance distribution, calendar aging initiation

**Phase 4: Production Qualification (6-12 months)**
- Production trial: Run SCC55 on production coating line (may require line modifications)
- Process capability: Demonstrate Cpk > 1.33 for all critical cell parameters
- Vehicle integration: Pack-level testing (thermal, vibration, crash), vehicle durability testing
- PPAP (Production Part Approval Process): Full documentation package submitted to OEM

**Total: 2.5-4 years** from initial material submission to production approval.

### Automotive Quality Requirements
| Parameter | Consumer Electronics | Automotive (EV) |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Cycle life target | 500-800 cycles | 1,000-1,500 cycles |
| Calendar life | 3-5 years | 10-15 years |
| Operating temperature | 0-45°C | -30 to +60°C |
| Failure rate (ppm) | <1,000 | <1-10 |
| Traceability | Lot level | Individual cell level |
| PPAP documentation | Not required | Required |

The jump from consumer electronics to automotive qualification represents a significant quality system upgrade for any materials supplier.

## 4. Powertrain Trends Affecting SCC55 Adoption

### Cell-to-Pack (CTP) and Cell-to-Chassis (CTC)
- CTP/CTC architectures integrate cells directly into the battery pack or vehicle structure without intermediate modules
- This increases pack-level energy density but places greater mechanical demands on individual cells
- SCC55's lower expansion vs. silicon-dominant composites may be advantageous in CTP designs where cells are structurally constrained

### Solid-State Battery Timeline
- Solid-state batteries eliminate the liquid electrolyte and conventional SEI formation challenges
- However, solid electrolytes introduce new challenges with silicon volume expansion — solid electrolytes cannot flow to accommodate expansion the way liquid electrolytes can
- If solid-state adoption occurs faster than expected (2028-2030), silicon anode materials with minimal expansion (like SCC55) may be preferred over high-silicon-content alternatives
- Group14's IP portfolio includes solid-state battery compatibility claims

### LFP vs. NMC Mix Shift
- LFP cathode adoption is growing in mainstream EVs due to lower cost (Tesla Model 3 RWD, Ford Mustang Mach-E Standard Range, VW ID series)
- LFP cells primarily use graphite anodes; the energy density premium of silicon is less compelling when the cathode is the limiting factor
- SCC55's addressable market is primarily high-nickel NMC cells for premium/long-range EVs
- This market segment is ~30-40% of total EV cell production by volume but higher by revenue

## 5. North American IRA-Driven Demand

### IRA Section 30D (Consumer EV Tax Credit)
- $7,500 consumer tax credit requires:
  - **Critical minerals**: 50%+ of battery critical minerals extracted/processed in U.S. or FTA countries (2024 threshold: 50%, rising to 80% by 2027)
  - **Battery components**: 60%+ of battery components manufactured/assembled in North America (2024 threshold: 60%, rising to 100% by 2029)
- Moses Lake SCC55 qualifies for both requirements, providing a competitive advantage over Chinese anode materials
- This advantage diminishes as competitors (Sila, Amprius) also establish U.S. manufacturing

### IRA Section 45X (Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit)
- 10% of production cost tax credit for electrode active materials manufactured in the U.S.
- For SCC55 at $20/kg manufacturing cost, this represents ~$2/kg subsidy
- Improves SCC55 cost competitiveness vs. imported graphite and Chinese silicon anode materials
- Sunset: phases out starting 2030; creates urgency to maximize production during credit window
