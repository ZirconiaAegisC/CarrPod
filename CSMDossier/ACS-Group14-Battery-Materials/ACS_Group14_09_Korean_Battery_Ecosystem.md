# ACS Group14 | Korean Battery Ecosystem & SCC55 Adoption Pathway

## 1. The Korean Battery Triad

### Industry Structure
South Korea's battery industry operates as a vertically integrated ecosystem anchored by three major cell manufacturers (LG Energy Solution, SK On, Samsung SDI) supported by a dense network of materials suppliers, equipment manufacturers, and research institutions. This ecosystem concentration is unmatched elsewhere and provides unique adoption dynamics for novel anode materials like SCC55.

### LG Energy Solution (LGES)
- **2023 Revenue**: ~$26B (KRW 33.7 trillion)
- **Global Manufacturing**: Poland (largest European battery plant), USA (Michigan, Ohio, Arizona, Georgia JV with GM, Honda, Hyundai), Korea (Ochang), China (Nanjing), Indonesia
- **Technology Roadmap**: Transitioning from NMC712/NMC811 to NMC9-series and NCMA cathodes; anode transition from graphite-only to graphite-SiOₓ blends to silicon-carbon composites
- **Silicon Anode Interest**: Evaluating silicon-carbon composites for high-energy-density cells targeting >350 Wh/kg for premium EV applications
- **SCC55 Relevance**: LGES's production scale (300+ GWh planned by 2025) means even 5% silicon anode adoption represents thousands of tons of material demand

### SK On
- **2023 Revenue**: ~$10B
- **Global Manufacturing**: USA (Georgia, Tennessee, Kentucky JV with Ford), Hungary, Korea (Seosan), China
- **Strategic Connection**: SK Group parent owns both SK On (battery manufacturing) and SK Materials (Group14 JV partner), creating the most direct line-of-sight for SCC55 adoption
- **NCM9 Focus**: SK On's NCM9 (90% nickel cathode) cells are specifically designed for high energy density; silicon anode integration is a natural complement to maximize energy density gains from nickel-rich cathodes
- **Ford Partnership**: Ford's BlueOval SK JV in Tennessee and Kentucky represents a direct pathway for SCC55 in North American EV production

### Samsung SDI
- **2023 Revenue**: ~$17B (including electronics materials)
- **Global Manufacturing**: Hungary (expanding to 60 GWh), Korea (Ulsan, Cheonan), Malaysia, planned US plant (Indiana JV with Stellantis)
- **Gen 5 Platform**: Current premium cells use ~5% SiOₓ in anode; Gen 6 platform (2025-2026) targets higher silicon content
- **BMW Relationship**: BMW i-series is Samsung SDI's marquee customer; BMW's Neue Klasse platform (2025+) may accelerate silicon anode adoption
- **Conservative Approach**: Samsung SDI has been more conservative than LGES or SK On in adopting new anode materials; qualification timelines may be longer

## 2. Qualification Pathway Through the SK Connection

### The SK On → SK Materials → Group14 Advantage
The SK corporate structure creates a uniquely efficient qualification pathway:

1. **SK Materials JV** produces SCC55 under license in Korea
2. **SK On** evaluates JV-produced SCC55 as a "group-affiliate" supplier, which receives priority in the qualification queue
3. **SK On's preference** for group-affiliate suppliers is reinforced by Korean chaebol business culture and SK Group's strategic direction
4. **Successful qualification at SK On** creates reference data that accelerates qualification at LGES and Samsung SDI

### Ford as the Bridge to Production
SK On's BlueOval SK joint venture with Ford provides the clearest near-term production pathway:
- **BlueOval SK plants**: Combined 129 GWh annual capacity in Tennessee and Kentucky
- **Vehicle platforms**: Ford F-150 Lightning, Mustang Mach-E, and upcoming T3 electric truck platform
- **IRA compliance**: U.S.-manufactured cells with U.S.-manufactured anode materials (Moses Lake) maximize IRA consumer tax credit eligibility
- **Timeline alignment**: BAM-2 ramp-up (2026-2028) aligns with BlueOval SK production ramp-up

## 3. Korean Materials Supply Chain for SCC55

### Carbon Precursor Sources
- **SK Innovation**: Petrochemical arm of SK Group; potential polymer precursor supplier
- **LG Chem**: Korea's largest chemical company; polymer and carbon materials expertise
- **Lotte Chemical**: Major petrochemical producer with carbon materials capabilities
- **Kolon Industries**: Specialty chemical company with carbon fiber and advanced materials expertise

### Silane Supply
- Korea currently imports silane primarily from Japanese suppliers (Mitsubishi Materials/Osaka Titanium, Kanto Denka Kogyo)
- SK Materials, as a specialty gas company, has the technical capability to develop domestic silane production
- Co-location of silane production with the SCC55 JV would reduce costs and supply chain risk
- Korean government support for domestic specialty gas production (part of materials sovereignty initiatives) could subsidize silane capacity development

### Equipment and Engineering
- Korean battery equipment ecosystem (PNT, CIS, Wonik) can supply manufacturing equipment for the JV facility
- Korean engineering firms (Samsung Engineering, SK E&C) have experience building specialty chemical plants
- Local equipment supply reduces capital costs and accelerates construction compared to importing equipment from the U.S.

## 4. Regulatory and Policy Environment

### K-REACH (Korea's REACH Regulation)
- All chemical substances manufactured or imported in quantities >1 ton/year must be registered with the Ministry of Environment
- SCC55, as a new chemical substance, requires full registration including toxicological and ecotoxicological data
- Registration timeline: 1-2 years for new substances
- SK Materials' existing K-REACH compliance infrastructure accelerates this process

### Korean Battery Industry Policy
- **K-Battery Development Strategy** (2021): Government targeting 40% global battery market share by 2030
- **R&D subsidies**: KRW 100B+ ($75M+) for next-generation battery materials R&D
- **Tax incentives**: Up to 40% tax credit for battery materials facility investments in designated zones
- **Materials sovereignty**: Government programs to reduce dependence on Chinese battery materials; silicon anode materials qualify as strategic materials

### U.S.-Korea Battery Supply Chain Alignment
- U.S.-Korea Free Trade Agreement (KORUS FTA) ensures tariff-free trade of battery materials between the two countries
- IRA "foreign entity of concern" provisions exclude China but include Korea, making Korean-sourced SCC55 IRA-compliant for U.S. EV tax credits
- U.S.-ROK leaders' joint statements (April 2023, April 2024) specifically highlight battery supply chain cooperation
