# ACS-Macys-Retail-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 03 — Store Portfolio & Real Estate

### 3.1 Store Fleet Composition

As of 2025, Macy's, Inc. operates 665 retail locations across the United
States. The portfolio comprises 432 Macy's department stores, 61
Bloomingdale's locations including outlet stores, and 170 Bluemercury
specialty beauty stores. The company also operates two additional retail
concepts: Bloomingdale's The Outlet and Macy's Backstage (off-price), which
are located both as store-in-store concepts within existing locations and as
standalone units.

### 3.2 Macy's Store Portfolio

Macy's locations span a wide range of formats, from large-format flagship
stores exceeding one million square feet — including the Herald Square
flagship in New York City, Union Square in San Francisco, and State Street in
Chicago — to smaller-format mall-based locations of approximately 100,000 to
200,000 square feet. The company's store base is heavily concentrated in
enclosed regional malls, with approximately 80 percent of Macy's locations
situated in mall properties. This concentration exposes the company to the
ongoing secular decline in mall foot traffic that has characterized the U.S.
retail landscape.

### 3.3 Real Estate Portfolio Value

Macy's real estate portfolio represents a significant source of embedded value.
The company owns approximately 290 of its retail locations, including several
flagship properties in prime urban locations with substantial redevelopment
potential. The company's owned real estate portfolio has been estimated by
independent analysts to carry a market value significantly exceeding its
carrying value on the balance sheet. Macy's has pursued a variety of
monetization strategies, including outright sale, partial sale with leaseback,
and redevelopment of excess land at existing store sites.

### 3.4 Reimagine 200 Stores Initiative

The Reimagine 200 Stores program, a cornerstone of the Bold New Chapter
strategy, targets capital investment in approximately 200 high-priority Macy's
locations through 2028. At each store, investment includes upgraded interior
design and visual merchandising, improved fitting rooms with enhanced lighting
and service features, expanded and refreshed beauty and cosmetics departments,
digital integration including interactive screens, self-checkout, and mobile
point-of-sale capabilities, and category-specific enhancements in jewelry,
shoes, and designer handbags. Early data from stores receiving Reimagine
treatment indicate improved comparable sales trends and higher customer
satisfaction scores relative to the rest of the chain.

### 3.5 Store Closure Program

The Bold New Chapter strategy calls for the closure of approximately 150
Macy's locations by 2026. Closed stores are generally older locations in Class
C and D malls with declining traffic, stores with significant deferred
maintenance requirements, locations in markets where Macy's operates multiple
stores in close proximity, and units where the lease expiration horizon makes
continued operation uneconomic. The store closure program is expected to
generate annualized operating income improvement of approximately $300 million
after accounting for sales transfer to nearby stores and digital channels.

### 3.6 Market Optimization

For markets where Macy's closes a location, the company focuses on retaining
customer relationships through nearby Macy's stores within the same market
where available, expanded digital fulfillment including same-day delivery in
select markets, Macy's Backstage off-price concepts, and the company's private
label credit card and loyalty programs that maintain customer engagement
independent of the physical store footprint.

### 3.7 Small-Format and Off-Mall Expansion

Complementing the store rationalization program, Macy's is testing small-format
concepts in the 30,000 to 50,000 square-foot range located in open-air shopping
centers and lifestyle developments. These smaller stores offer a curated
assortment of apparel, beauty, and home goods with a focus on digital
fulfillment and local-market relevance. The small-format strategy, if
successful, would provide a vehicle for Macy's to serve markets where a
full-line department store is not viable and expand the company's presence in
the faster-growing off-mall retail segment.
