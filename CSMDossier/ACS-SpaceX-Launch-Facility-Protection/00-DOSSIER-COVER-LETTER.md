## I. EXECUTIVE TRANSMITTAL

Mr. Musk, Ms. Shotwell:

ACS presents this dossier proposing dielectric hardening of SpaceX launch facilities — Cape Canaveral Space Force Station (SLC-40), Kennedy Space Center (LC-39A), Vandenberg Space Force Base (SLC-4E), and Boca Chica/Starbase, Texas — against geomagnetically induced current events. Rocket launch systems are among the most electrically intensive and electronically sensitive industrial operations on Earth. A GMD event occurring during a launch window — particularly during the critical T-0 to T+300 second phase when the vehicle is under powered flight and dependent on ground-based telemetry and flight termination system (FTS) — could be catastrophic.

**The Launch Vulnerabilities:**

- **Launch Pad Electrical Systems:** A Falcon Heavy launch pad draws up to 15 MW during pre-launch operations (cryogenic propellant loading, vehicle power-up, ground support equipment). All of this power flows through transformers and power distribution equipment that share the same GIC vulnerability as any other industrial facility — but with the additional complication that pad electrical infrastructure is exposed (not inside a building) with long cable runs that act as GIC antennas.

- **Mission Control / Firing Room:** The Launch Control Center at KSC and Mission Control in Hawthorne, CA depend on clean power for flight computer telemetry, FTS, range safety displays, and vehicle health monitoring. A GIC-induced power quality disruption during critical countdown phases could force a scrub or — worse — create ambiguous telemetry that masks a genuine vehicle anomaly.

- **Starlink Ground Station Network:** SpaceX operates 100+ Starlink gateway ground stations worldwide, each connecting the orbital constellation to terrestrial internet backbones. These ground stations are typically located in rural/remote areas with long, unprotected power feeds — ideal GIC collection antennas. A GMD event that damages ground station power electronics would disconnect the Starlink constellation from the terrestrial internet, degrading service for 2+ million subscribers.

- **Boca Chica / Starbase:** Located at 26°N geomagnetic latitude with proximity to the Gulf of Mexico (conductive seawater enhances GIC coupling through the coastal effect). The Starbase production and launch facility is SpaceX's most exposed complex, with Starship/Super Heavy launch infrastructure operating at unprecedented electrical scale (estimated 30-50 MW peak demand during full-stack operations).

**The "Space Access Must Survive" Argument:** During a national-scale grid emergency, space-based assets become even more critical — GPS for navigation, Starlink for communications, Earth observation for disaster assessment. If launch facilities are disabled, the nation loses the ability to replace, augment, or reconfigure these space assets. SpaceX is the sole provider of U.S. crew launch capability (Crew Dragon) and the dominant provider of national security launch (Falcon 9/Falcon Heavy for NSSL Phase 2). The nation depends on SpaceX's launch infrastructure continuing to function — GMD event or not.

## II. PROPOSED SOLUTION

1. **Launch Pad Electrical Hardening:** NBC installation at pad substations. GIC-rated power distribution to all pad subsystems — cryo loading, vehicle power, GSE, telemetry. Shielded cable trays with MXene coating for long outdoor cable runs.

2. **Mission Control Protection:** NBC at LCC/KSC and Hawthorne facility substations. MXene-based power conditioning for flight computer racks, telemetry processing, and FTS controllers. Protonic backup communication link between launch site and mission control.

3. **Starlink Ground Station Hardening:** Compact NBC and MXene-based power conditioning for each ground station's power input. Prioritized by latitude (>40°N stations first — highest GIC exposure). Solar + battery backup with GIC-hardened inverter to maintain ground station operation during grid outages.

4. **FTS and Range Safety Protection:** Dedicated GIC-hardened power supply for Flight Termination System receivers and command destruct transmitters. Ensures range safety capability is maintained during GMD events — a non-negotiable requirement for any launch.

5. **Protonic Backup Communications:** CSM-PC-5000 network linking Hawthorne, KSC, Vandenberg, and Starbase. GIC-immune coordination for launch operations that must continue regardless of terrestrial communication status.
