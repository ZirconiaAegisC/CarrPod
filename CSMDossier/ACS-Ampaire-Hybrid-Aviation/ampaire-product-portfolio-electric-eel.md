# Ampaire Product Portfolio — Electric EEL Technology Demonstrator

## Program Origins

The Ampaire Electric EEL was a technology demonstrator aircraft that preceded
the Eco Caravan program. Based on a Cessna 337 Skymaster — a unique push-pull
twin-engine aircraft with one engine in the nose (tractor) and one in the
rear fuselage (pusher) — the Electric EEL replaced the rear piston engine with
an electric motor while retaining the front piston engine. This configuration
provided a low-risk platform for validating hybrid-electric propulsion
concepts in flight while maintaining the safety redundancy of a twin-engine
aircraft.

## Technical Configuration

### Hybrid Architecture

The Electric EEL employed a simpler parallel hybrid architecture than the
series-parallel configuration of the Eco Caravan:

- **Front engine**: Standard Continental IO-360 piston engine, unchanged from
  the baseline aircraft, providing 210 hp
- **Rear motor**: 160 kW (215 hp) electric motor driving the rear propeller,
  powered by a 50 kWh lithium-ion battery pack installed in the cabin area
- **Power management**: Manual power control by the pilot — the front and rear
  powerplants were independently controlled, allowing the pilot to experiment
  with power split strategies

### Flight Test Program

The Electric EEL first flew in June 2019 and accumulated several hundred
flight hours through 2021, generating data on:

- Electric motor performance and thermal behavior in flight
- Battery performance under real-world power profiles including takeoff,
  climb, cruise, and descent
- Interaction between the electric and piston propulsion systems
- Pilot workload and interface requirements for hybrid-electric operation
- Noise measurements confirming significant noise reduction during
  electric-only rear propulsion

### Key Learnings

The Electric EEL program generated critical insights that directly informed
the Eco Caravan design:

1. **Electric motor reliability**: The electric propulsion system demonstrated
   perfect dispatch reliability during the test program, with no in-flight
   failures or unscheduled maintenance events — validating the fundamental
   reliability advantage of electric propulsion over piston engines
2. **Thermal management**: Battery thermal management proved more challenging
   than anticipated for missions with multiple takeoffs (touch-and-go
   training flights). This experience drove the inclusion of phase-change
   thermal buffering in the Eco Caravan's battery design.
3. **Pilot interface**: Pilots reported that the independent power controls
   for the two propulsion systems created excessive workload. This feedback
   led to the single-lever power control concept implemented in the Eco
   Caravan's power management system.
4. **Noise signature**: Acoustic measurements confirmed a 15 to 20 dBA
   reduction in flyover noise during rear-electric cruise compared with
   the baseline twin-piston configuration, validating the community noise
   benefit proposition.
5. **Regulatory engagement**: The Electric EEL's experimental airworthiness
   certificate process established Ampaire's relationship with the FAA's
   emerging electric propulsion specialists, creating a collaborative
   foundation for the subsequent Eco Caravan certification program.

## Strategic Role

The Electric EEL served as more than a technology testbed — it was a
strategic asset that:

- Demonstrated Ampaire's technical capability to investors, partners, and
  customers through tangible flight operations
- Generated media coverage and industry attention that elevated Ampaire's
  profile in the emerging electric aviation sector
- Provided flight data to support the FAA's development of certification
  standards for hybrid-electric propulsion
- Offered a platform for early customer and investor demonstration flights
  that built confidence in hybrid-electric technology

## Program Sunset

With the Eco Caravan program advancing toward certification and the Electric
EEL having fulfilled its technology demonstration mission, Ampaire retired
the Electric EEL from active flight testing in 2022. The aircraft's data
and operational experience are preserved in Ampaire's engineering knowledge
base, and key lessons learned are documented in the company's design standards
for future programs.

## Legacy and Future Demonstrators

The Electric EEL established Ampaire's approach to technology development
through rapid prototyping and flight demonstration — a methodology that
contrasts with the extensive ground-testing-before-flight approach of
larger aerospace companies. This approach, enabled by the relatively low
cost of modifying existing certified aircraft rather than building clean-sheet
prototypes, allows Ampaire to iterate quickly and incorporate flight test
learnings into production designs on compressed timelines.

Ampaire has indicated that future technology demonstrators may explore:

- Higher battery energy densities as next-generation cells become available
- Hydrogen fuel cell range extension as an alternative to combustion engine
  range extenders for zero-emission operation
- Autonomous flight capabilities for cargo operations using the hybrid
  platform as a transition technology to fully electric unmanned aircraft

These demonstrator programs would follow the Electric EEL's template:
modify an existing certified airframe with new propulsion technology, fly
extensively to generate data, and feed learnings into production programs.
