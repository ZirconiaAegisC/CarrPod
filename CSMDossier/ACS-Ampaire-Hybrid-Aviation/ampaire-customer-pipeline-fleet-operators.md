# Ampaire Customer Pipeline — Fleet Operators and Operating Economics

## Operator Value Proposition

The Eco Caravan's value proposition to fleet operators rests on three pillars:
direct operating cost reduction, operational flexibility enhancement, and
regulatory compliance positioning. Each pillar contributes to a total cost
of ownership reduction that drives the conversion investment payback period
to 2 to 4 years for typical regional airline utilization.

## Direct Operating Cost Reduction

### Fuel Cost Savings

Fuel represents 30 to 45 percent of direct operating cost for a conventionally
powered Grand Caravan. The Eco Caravan's 50 to 70 percent fuel consumption
reduction — varying by mission length and power management strategy — reduces
this to approximately 10 to 20 percent of direct operating cost.

For a representative operator flying 800 hours annually on 150 nautical mile
average stage lengths:

- Baseline fuel consumption: 40 to 50 gallons per hour at 4 to 6 USD per
  gallon = 160 to 300 USD per flight hour
- Eco Caravan fuel consumption: 15 to 25 gallons per hour = 60 to 150 USD
  per flight hour
- Annual fuel savings: 80000 to 120000 USD per aircraft at mid-range fuel
  prices

At Hawaii or European fuel prices (6 to 10 USD per gallon), annual savings
increase to 120000 to 200000 USD per aircraft — sufficient to recover the
conversion cost in under 3 years from fuel savings alone.

### Maintenance Cost Reduction

The shift from turbine to electric primary propulsion reduces maintenance
cost in several categories:

- **Engine reserves**: The PT6A requires hot section inspection at 3500 to
  4000 hours (150000 to 200000 USD) and overhaul at 6000 to 8000 hours
  (300000 to 400000 USD). The electric motor has no equivalent periodic
  overhaul requirement, with a design life exceeding 20000 hours before
  bearing replacement.
- **Routine maintenance**: The electric motor requires minimal routine
  maintenance — periodic bearing lubrication and insulation testing —
  compared with the PT6A's daily inspections, oil changes, filter replacements,
  and compressor washes.
- **Unscheduled maintenance**: Turbine engines experience unscheduled
  maintenance events (compressor damage from foreign objects, fuel control
  anomalies, hot start incidents) at a rate of approximately one event per
  2000 to 3000 flight hours. The electric motor's simpler design and fewer
  rotating components are expected to reduce unscheduled maintenance by 50
  to 70 percent.

The range extender engine — an automotive-derived piston engine — has lower
overhaul cost than the PT6A turbine (15000 to 25000 USD versus 300000 to
400000 USD) but higher routine maintenance requirements. Operators familiar
with piston engine maintenance, which is simpler and more widely understood
than turbine maintenance, may find this an acceptable trade-off.

### Battery Replacement Reserve

Battery replacement represents a new operating cost category not present in
conventionally powered aircraft:

- Estimated battery pack life: 3000 to 5000 full-equivalent cycles or 5 to
  8 years at typical regional airline utilization (600 to 1000 hours annually)
- Replacement cost: 50000 to 75000 USD per full pack replacement at projected
  future battery prices (200 to 300 USD per kWh at pack level)
- Annual battery reserve accrual: 6000 to 15000 USD per aircraft

Battery replacement cost partially offsets maintenance savings but does not
materially change the overall operating cost advantage of the hybrid
configuration.

## Operational Flexibility

### Electric-Only Operations

The ability to conduct certain flight phases and, on the shortest routes,
entire flights on electric power provides operational flexibility:

- **Noise-sensitive airports**: Airports with noise curfews or community
  noise restrictions may permit electric-only departures outside of normal
  operating hours, enabling schedule flexibility not available to turbine
  aircraft
- **Engine-out procedure simplification**: With dual power sources capable
  of independently sustaining flight, engine failure procedures are
  simplified — the pilot has 30 to 60 minutes of battery endurance to
  reach a suitable airport rather than executing an immediate forced landing
- **Hot-and-high performance**: The electric motor's power output is
  independent of density altitude, unlike turbine engines that lose
  significant power at high altitude and high temperature. This improves
  takeoff performance at high-elevation airports and in hot weather
  conditions.

### Multi-Mission Flexibility

The hybrid powertrain's variable power source allocation enables mission
optimization not possible with a single power source:

- **Maximum range**: Operate primarily on the range extender with battery
  used only for takeoff boost and reserve — maximizes range at the expense
  of fuel savings
- **Maximum fuel savings**: Operate on battery power for as much of the
  mission as possible, using the range extender only when battery capacity
  is insufficient — maximizes fuel savings on shorter routes
- **Maximum payload**: Use electric boost for takeoff to enable higher
  payload at weight-limited airports without the weight penalty of a larger
  turbine engine — improves revenue payload on short routes from challenging
  airports

The power management system automatically optimizes for the operator's
chosen priority (minimum fuel, minimum emissions, minimum cost, or maximum
range) without requiring pilot intervention beyond mode selection.

## Regulatory Compliance Positioning

### Current Regulations

The Eco Caravan's emissions profile positions operators favorably for current
and anticipated environmental regulations:

- **CORSIA (Carbon Offsetting and Reduction Scheme for International Aviation)**:
  The Eco Caravan is below the CORSIA applicability threshold (aircraft below
  5700 kg MTOW are exempt), but operators of larger aircraft in the future
  would benefit from the hybrid powertrain's reduced emissions for CORSIA
  compliance
- **EU Emissions Trading Scheme (ETS)**: Intra-European flights are subject
  to ETS carbon allowance requirements. Reduced fuel consumption directly
  reduces ETS compliance cost — at current carbon prices of 60 to 90 EUR per
  tonne, the Eco Caravan's annual carbon savings of 400 to 600 tonnes per
  aircraft represent 24000 to 54000 EUR in avoided carbon costs annually.
- **National mandates**: Several European countries have announced domestic
  flight emissions reduction targets that may create mandates or incentives
  favoring hybrid-electric aircraft

### Future Regulatory Trajectory

Aviation's regulatory environment is trending toward stricter emissions
requirements:

- **Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) mandates**: The European Union's
  ReFuelEU Aviation regulation requires increasing SAF blend percentages,
  which will increase fuel costs for conventional aircraft. The Eco Caravan's
  lower fuel consumption provides a hedge against SAF-driven fuel cost
  increases.
- **Potential carbon taxes**: Several jurisdictions are considering aviation
  carbon taxes or ticket taxes based on emissions. The Eco Caravan's low
  emissions per passenger-kilometer would minimize such costs.
- **Airport access restrictions**: Some European airports have announced
  plans to restrict or surcharge high-emission aircraft. The Eco Caravan's
  low-noise and low-emission electric operations may qualify for preferential
  access or reduced charges.

## Fleet Transition Strategy

For operators with multiple Caravans, Ampaire recommends a phased fleet
transition:

1. **Pilot conversion (1 to 2 aircraft)**: Convert one or two aircraft to
   gain operational experience, validate fuel savings, and train pilots and
   mechanics on the new technology
2. **Route optimization**: Analyze actual operating data from the pilot
   aircraft to identify which routes benefit most from hybrid conversion and
   sequence subsequent conversions accordingly
3. **Phased fleet conversion**: Convert additional aircraft at a rate of 2
   to 4 per year, aligning with scheduled engine overhaul events to avoid
   the opportunity cost of removing aircraft from service outside of
   scheduled downtime
4. **Full fleet transition**: Over 5 to 8 years, convert the majority of the
   Caravan fleet to Eco Caravan configuration, retiring the last conventionally
   powered aircraft as conversion economics and environmental mandates make
   continued turbine operation uneconomical

This phased approach minimizes operational disruption, manages capital
expenditure, and allows the operator to build institutional knowledge of
hybrid-electric operations before committing to full fleet conversion.
