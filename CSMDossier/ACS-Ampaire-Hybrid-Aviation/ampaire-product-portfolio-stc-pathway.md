# Ampaire Product Portfolio — Supplemental Type Certificate Pathway

## The STC Strategy

Ampaire's decision to pursue certification through the Supplemental Type
Certificate (STC) pathway — modifying an existing certified aircraft rather
than certifying an entirely new aircraft type — is a deliberate strategic
choice that accelerates time-to-market and reduces certification cost by an
order of magnitude. The STC approach leverages the existing Type Certificate
of the Cessna 208B Grand Caravan, adding Ampaire's hybrid-electric powertrain
as an approved modification.

## STC Versus Type Certificate

### Type Certificate (New Aircraft)

Certifying a new aircraft type under FAA Part 23 requires:

- **Duration**: Typically 5 to 10 years from program launch to certification
- **Cost**: 100 to 500 million USD for a regional aircraft, excluding
  production tooling and manufacturing certification
- **Scope**: Complete demonstration of compliance with every applicable
  airworthiness standard — structures, flight characteristics, systems,
  propulsion, and equipment
- **Risk**: High technical and schedule risk, as unanticipated compliance
  issues in any area can delay the entire program

### Supplemental Type Certificate (Modification)

The STC pathway for modifying the Grand Caravan requires:

- **Duration**: 2 to 4 years from program launch to certification, depending
  on the scope of the modification and FAA engagement
- **Cost**: 20 to 50 million USD estimated for the Eco Caravan STC program
- **Scope**: Demonstration that the modification does not adversely affect
  the aircraft's compliance with applicable airworthiness standards. Only
  the areas affected by the modification — primarily propulsion and associated
  systems — require full re-demonstration of compliance.
- **Risk**: Lower than a new Type Certificate because the baseline airframe's
  certification is already established; risk is concentrated in the new
  propulsion system integration

The STC pathway reduces Ampaire's certification cost by approximately 80 to
90 percent compared with a new Type Certificate while reducing time-to-revenue
by 3 to 6 years.

## Certification Basis

### Applicable Regulations

The Eco Caravan STC certification basis includes:

- **FAR Part 23 Amendment 64** (the amendment level applicable to late-model
  Grand Caravans) for the baseline aircraft systems that are unchanged
- **Applicable recent amendments and policy** for the novel hybrid-electric
  propulsion system, as agreed with the FAA through the issue paper process
- **FAA policy statements and advisory circulars** addressing electric and
  hybrid-electric propulsion, published as the agency develops its regulatory
  framework for this emerging technology

### Issue Papers

For novel aspects of the hybrid-electric propulsion system that are not
explicitly addressed by existing regulations, Ampaire and the FAA develop
issue papers that:

- Define the specific safety concern or regulatory gap
- Propose a means of compliance, typically based on equivalent level of
  safety analysis or adaptation of standards from related domains (automotive
  battery standards, industrial power electronics standards)
- Document FAA acceptance of the proposed approach

Key issue papers for the Eco Caravan program address:

- **Battery safety**: Thermal runaway containment and propagation prevention,
  adapted from the FAA's lithium battery safety guidance developed through
  the Boeing 787 battery certification experience
- **High-voltage electrical system**: Arc fault protection, insulation
  coordination, and electromagnetic effects, adapted from automotive and
  industrial standards (SAE, IEC, ISO)
- **Hybrid propulsion control**: Software development assurance levels and
  system safety assessment methodology for the power management system
- **Range extender engine**: Certification basis for an automotive-derived
  engine in an aviation application, including the engine's adaptation for
  aviation operating environment and duty cycle

### Means of Compliance

Ampaire demonstrates compliance through a combination of:

- **Analysis**: System safety assessments, failure modes and effects analyses,
  structural analyses, electromagnetic compatibility analyses
- **Ground testing**: Powertrain endurance testing, battery abuse testing,
  environmental qualification testing, software verification testing
- **Flight testing**: Performance, handling qualities, systems functionality,
  and reliability demonstration across the approved flight envelope
- **Similarity**: Leveraging data from the baseline Grand Caravan Type
  Certificate for unchanged systems and structure

## Certification Timeline

Ampaire's publicly stated timeline targets STC issuance in 2024 to 2025. The
program has completed:

- Project specific certification plan acceptance
- Ground testing of the powertrain
- Initial flight testing with the test aircraft
- Submittal of compliance data for non-controversial areas

Remaining milestones include:

- Completion of flight test program (estimated 200 to 300 flight test hours)
- Conformity inspection of production-representative aircraft and systems
- Submittal and FAA approval of all compliance data
- STC issuance

## Post-STC: Production Certificate and Continued Airworthiness

Following STC issuance, Ampaire will pursue:

- **Production Certificate (PC)**: FAA approval of Ampaire's production
  quality system for performing Eco Caravan conversions. The PC enables
  Ampaire to issue airworthiness certificates for converted aircraft without
  individual FAA conformity inspection.
- **Instructions for Continued Airworthiness (ICA)**: Maintenance manual
  supplements, airworthiness limitations, and continued airworthiness
  instructions for the hybrid-electric propulsion system, developed in
  accordance with FAA Order 8110.54
- **Service network authorization**: Training and authorization program for
  third-party maintenance facilities to perform line and base maintenance
  on Eco Caravan aircraft, ensuring operators have access to qualified
  maintenance support

## International Validation

Ampaire intends to pursue validation of the FAA STC by foreign civil aviation
authorities to enable Eco Caravan operations outside the United States:

- **EASA (European Union)**: Validation through the EU-US bilateral aviation
  safety agreement, with EASA acceptance of the FAA STC as the basis for a
  European supplemental type certificate
- **Transport Canada**: Validation through the bilateral agreement between
  the US and Canada
- **ANAC (Brazil)**: Important for the large Caravan fleet operating in the
  Amazon region
- **Other authorities**: As required by customer demand in specific markets

International validation typically adds 12 to 24 months to the timeline for
each authority after FAA STC issuance, depending on the authority's
familiarity with hybrid-electric propulsion technology and the degree of
technical review they require.

## Competitive Significance

The STC pathway is a critical competitive differentiator for Ampaire. While
competitors pursuing clean-sheet electric aircraft face a decade-long
development and certification timeline before generating revenue, Ampaire's
STC approach enables revenue generation within 2 to 4 years of program launch.
This time-to-market advantage provides Ampaire with a first-mover position
in the regional hybrid-electric aviation market and the opportunity to
establish customer relationships, operational experience, and brand
recognition before clean-sheet competitors enter the market.
