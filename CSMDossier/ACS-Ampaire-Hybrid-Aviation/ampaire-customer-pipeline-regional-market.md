# Ampaire Customer Pipeline — Regional Market Analysis

## Addressable Market Definition

Ampaire's addressable market is defined by the intersection of Grand Caravan
operators and route networks where hybrid-electric propulsion provides the
strongest economic and operational advantage. This market segments naturally
by geography, with each region presenting distinct value drivers for hybrid
conversion.

## North American Market

### Fleet Size

The United States and Canada account for approximately 1200 to 1500 Grand
Caravans in active service, representing the largest concentration of the
type globally. Key operating segments include:

- **FAR Part 135 commuter airlines**: Approximately 200 to 300 aircraft
  operated by regional carriers on scheduled passenger services — the
  highest-priority segment for Ampaire due to high utilization and fuel
  consumption
- **Cargo feeders**: Approximately 150 to 200 aircraft operated by FedEx
  Feeder and similar contract carriers
- **Charter and air taxi**: Approximately 300 to 400 aircraft operated on
  on-demand charter, tourism, and corporate shuttle services
- **Owner-operators**: Approximately 400 to 500 aircraft operated by
  individuals and businesses for private transportation
- **Special mission**: Approximately 100 to 150 aircraft operated for
  government, law enforcement, and research missions

### Route Economics

The North American regional airline market is characterized by:

- **Hub-and-spoke feeder routes**: Caravans feeding passengers from small
  communities to hub airports, typically 50 to 200 nautical mile stage
  lengths
- **Essential Air Service (EAS)**: Federally subsidized routes to small
  communities that would otherwise lose air service — fuel cost reduction
  directly reduces subsidy requirements
- **Charter and tourism**: Seasonal operations to recreational destinations
  with high operating tempo during peak seasons

### Regulatory Environment

The US regulatory environment is generally supportive of hybrid-electric
aviation technology:

- **FAA engagement**: The FAA has established a dedicated electric propulsion
  team and issued policy guidance supporting certification of electric and
  hybrid propulsion systems
- **State incentives**: Several states — including California, Washington,
  and Oregon — offer incentives for electric aviation infrastructure and
  operations
- **Federal funding**: The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act and
  Inflation Reduction Act include provisions supporting sustainable aviation
  technology development and deployment

### Competitive Dynamics

The North American regional airline market faces several structural pressures
that favor hybrid-electric conversion:

- **Pilot shortage**: The industry-wide pilot shortage has forced regional
  airlines to reduce service on marginally profitable routes. The Eco Caravan's
  single-pilot operation and simplified power management may partially
  mitigate crew availability constraints.
- **Fuel price volatility**: Operators with thin margins are exposed to fuel
  price fluctuations. The Eco Caravan's 50 to 70 percent fuel consumption
  reduction provides a hedge against future fuel price increases.
- **Environmental pressure**: Investor, customer, and regulatory focus on
  aviation emissions is intensifying. Caravan operators without a
  decarbonization pathway risk losing contracts with environmentally
  conscious corporate clients and facing future emissions-related costs.

## European Market

### Market Structure

The European Grand Caravan fleet is smaller than the North American fleet
(approximately 200 to 300 aircraft) but operates under conditions that
strongly favor hybrid-electric conversion:

- **High fuel prices**: European aviation fuel prices are 50 to 100 percent
  higher than US prices due to taxation, amplifying the economic benefit of
  fuel consumption reduction
- **Environmental regulation**: The European Union's emissions trading
  scheme and proposed kerosene tax create regulatory cost pressure that the
  Eco Caravan's reduced emissions mitigate
- **Short stage lengths**: European regional routes average 100 to 200
  nautical miles, within the Eco Caravan's electric and hybrid range where
  fuel savings are maximum
- **Noise restrictions**: Many European airports operate under noise quotas
  or curfews that the Eco Caravan's reduced noise signature helps operators
  comply with

### Certification Pathway

The European market requires EASA validation of the FAA STC, which Ampaire
is pursuing through the EU-US bilateral aviation safety agreement. The
validation timeline — estimated at 12 to 24 months after FAA STC issuance —
represents the primary timing constraint for European market entry.

## Asia-Pacific Market

### Market Significance

The Asia-Pacific region represents Ampaire's largest growth opportunity
beyond North America:

- **Fleet size**: Approximately 400 to 600 Caravans operating in Southeast
  Asia, the Pacific Islands, Australia, and New Zealand
- **Mission profile**: Island-hopping routes in Indonesia, the Philippines,
  and the Pacific Islands are ideally suited to the Eco Caravan — short stage
  lengths, frequent takeoff and landing cycles, and high fuel logistics costs
  for remote island airports
- **Infrastructure constraints**: Many island airports lack reliable fuel
  supply infrastructure. The Eco Caravan's reduced fuel consumption and
  ability to operate partial missions on battery power reduce dependence on
  remote fuel logistics.

### Market Development Strategy

Ampaire's Asia-Pacific market development strategy focuses on:

1. **Anchor customers**: Securing conversion commitments from one or two
   major regional operators to establish market presence and generate
   operational reference data
2. **Demonstration tours**: Conducting demonstration flights in key markets
   (Indonesia, Philippines, Australia) to build operator awareness and
   interest
3. **Conversion center establishment**: Partnering with an established MRO
   in the region to provide local conversion capability, reducing the cost
   and logistics burden of sending aircraft to the US for conversion
4. **Regulatory engagement**: Working with civil aviation authorities in key
   markets to validate the FAA STC or establish separate certification bases

## Latin American Market

### Brazil

Brazil represents the largest Latin American market for Caravan conversions,
with approximately 100 to 150 Caravans operating in the Amazon region and
other remote areas. The Amazon operating environment — short flights between
remote communities, limited ground infrastructure, high fuel logistics costs —
creates a compelling case for hybrid-electric conversion. ANAC, Brazil's
civil aviation authority, has a history of accepting FAA STCs with limited
additional technical review, which would facilitate market entry.

### Caribbean

Caribbean island-hopping routes — typically 25 to 100 nautical miles between
islands — represent the ideal mission profile for the Eco Caravan. The
combination of short stage lengths, high fuel importation costs, and
environmental sensitivity of island tourism economies creates strong demand
drivers. The Caribbean fleet of approximately 50 to 100 Caravans is served
primarily by US-registered operators, simplifying the certification pathway.

## African Market

### Operating Environment

Africa's Caravan fleet — approximately 200 to 300 aircraft — operates in
some of the world's most challenging aviation environments:

- **Remote operations**: Safari lodges, mining camps, and humanitarian
  operations far from fuel infrastructure
- **Unimproved runways**: Gravel and dirt strips that test aircraft durability
- **High utilization**: Aircraft often serve as the sole transportation link
  for remote communities, generating high annual utilization
- **Fuel logistics**: Fuel must often be transported long distances by truck
  or even aircraft, dramatically increasing effective fuel cost

The Eco Caravan's reduced fuel consumption directly addresses the fuel
logistics challenge, while the electric motor's simplified maintenance
compared with a turbine engine may improve dispatch reliability in
environments with limited maintenance support.

### Market Entry Timing

The African market is likely to develop later in Ampaire's growth trajectory
due to:

- Certification complexity across multiple national aviation authorities
- Limited financing infrastructure for aircraft acquisition and conversion
- Need for the Eco Caravan to accumulate operational reliability data before
  deployment in environments with minimal maintenance support

## Market Penetration Forecast

Ampaire's internal market penetration scenario (not publicly disclosed but
informed by the addressable market analysis) likely projects:

- **Year 1 to 3 post-STC**: 15 to 30 conversions annually, primarily North
  American launch customers
- **Year 3 to 5**: 50 to 80 conversions annually as international markets
  open, the conversion center network expands, and the operational track
  record supports broader customer adoption
- **Year 5 to 10**: 100-plus conversions annually as the technology becomes
  the standard for Caravan operators, the STC pathway is adapted to
  additional aircraft types, and regulatory pressure on aviation emissions
  creates a compliance-driven demand for hybrid conversion
