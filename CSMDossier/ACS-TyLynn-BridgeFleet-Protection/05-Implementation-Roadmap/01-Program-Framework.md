# Bridge Fleet Protection Program: Implementation Framework

**Prepared by:** T.Y. Lin International — ACS Infrastructure

## Executive Summary

This Implementation Framework establishes a structured, phased approach to deploying comprehensive corrosion protection systems across the ten toll bridges of the San Francisco Bay Area. The program is designed to align with existing Bay Area Toll Authority (BATA) capital improvement cycles, leverage planned maintenance outages, and integrate with California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) inspection and regulatory requirements. The framework is anchored in the economic analysis presented in preceding dossier sections, which demonstrates that Thermal Spray Zinc (TSZ) duplex coating systems and Impressed Current Cathodic Protection (ICCP) deliver superior life-cycle cost outcomes compared to traditional paint-only approaches — a conclusion that is materially reinforced by Caltrans' 2025 lead exposure regulations, which have significantly increased the direct and compliance costs of conventional lead-based paint systems.

## Phase 0: Foundation and Data Aggregation (Months 1–3)

### Objective
Establish the program baseline by consolidating all available condition data, developing the prioritization model, and securing multi-agency alignment.

### Key Activities

1. **Condition Data Aggregation.** Compile and cross-reference all available structural condition data from Caltrans Bridge Inspection Records (BIR), NBIS inspection reports, BATA Capital Improvement Program (CIP) documents, and individual bridge files. This includes fracture-critical member inspections, coating condition surveys, concrete chloride content test results, and cathodic protection system assessments where applicable.

2. **Prioritization Matrix Development.** Construct a multi-factor prioritization model incorporating: NBIS condition ratings; corrosion rate trends; remaining service life projections under current maintenance regimes; proximity to programmed maintenance outages (to minimize traffic disruption costs); structural redundancy characteristics; and estimated user delay costs per day of lane closure. The model will produce a weighted priority score for each of the ten toll bridge structures and their major sub-components.

3. **Stakeholder Alignment Workshop.** Convene a structured alignment workshop with Caltrans Division of Maintenance, Caltrans Division of Engineering Services (DES), BATA, the Metropolitan Transportation Commission (MTC), and the Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) California Division. The workshop will present the consolidated condition assessment, validate the prioritization methodology, and establish the governance framework for multi-agency coordination throughout program execution.

4. **Regulatory Framework Analysis.** Complete a detailed assessment of Caltrans' 2025 lead exposure regulations (Title 8, Section 1532.1 amendments) and their impact on conventional bridge painting operations. Document the compliance cost differential between traditional paint systems and TSZ duplex alternatives. Prepare a regulatory impact memorandum for inclusion in the final program charter.

5. **Funding Pathway Identification.** Map all eligible funding sources including: BATA Toll Bridge Rehabilitation Reserves; FHWA Bridge Formula Program (BFP) allocations under the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA); Bridge Investment Program (BIP) Large Bridge Project grants; California SB 1 State Transportation Improvement Program (STIP) funds; and potential public-private partnership (P3) structures under Caltrans' P3 authority.

### Deliverables
- Consolidated Bridge Fleet Condition Database
- Multi-Factor Prioritization Model and Weighted Scoring Report
- Stakeholder Alignment Workshop Report and Memorandum of Understanding
- Lead Regulation Impact Memorandum
- Funding Strategy and Phasing Plan

### Estimated Budget
$450,000 – $650,000 (primarily engineering and facilitation labor, data acquisition)

### Responsible Parties
- **Lead:** T.Y. Lin International (Program Management Office)
- **Support:** ACS Infrastructure (commercial/financial modeling)
- **Participating:** Caltrans DES, BATA, MTC, FHWA

### Risk Factors
- Data gaps in historical coating performance records may require supplemental field surveys, extending the timeline
- Multi-agency alignment may be delayed by divergent maintenance priorities across jurisdictions
- FHWA grant application cycles may not align with program phasing

## Phase 1: Pilot Demonstration Project (Months 3–12)

### Objective
Execute a single-bridge pilot project to validate TSZ duplex and/or ICCP application methodology, establish unit costs, document constructability lessons, and provide defensible cost-performance data for the fleet-wide business case.

### Recommended Pilot Structure: Carquinez Bridge (Eastbound)

The Carquinez Eastbound Bridge presents the optimal pilot candidate for the following reasons:

- **Condition:** Rated Fair with increasing corrosion noted in the substructure. The superstructure paint system is programmed for conventional repainting in FY27/28, providing a near-term opportunity to substitute TSZ duplex for conventional paint.
- **Programmed Work Alignment:** Deck overlay is scheduled for FY24/25 and seismic transmission units require repair (leaking). The convergence of multiple maintenance needs creates an efficient single-mobilization window.
- **Structural Typology:** As a steel truss structure, Carquinez Eastbound provides an ideal demonstration platform for TSZ duplex on complex geometries — lessons directly transferable to the Richmond-San Rafael Bridge and the San Francisco-Oakland West Span.
- **Traffic Impact:** The Carquinez Bridge carries lower Average Daily Traffic (ADT) than the Bay Bridge corridor, reducing user delay costs during any unanticipated construction complications.

### Key Activities

1. **Detailed Condition Survey.** Perform comprehensive coating adhesion testing, steel thickness measurements, and environmental exposure classification (ISO 12944 C3/C4) on all representative structural elements.

2. **Surface Preparation Specification.** Develop and validate surface preparation protocols (SSPC-SP 10 Near-White Blast Cleaning minimum, with SSPC-SP 5 White Metal Blast for critical faying surfaces) tailored to the bridge's existing coating system composition and environmental exposure.

3. **TSZ Duplex Application.** Apply thermal spray zinc (99.9% minimum purity, per ISO 2063) to a minimum of 5,000 square meters of representative steel surface area, including truss diagonals, chord members, floor beams, and connection details. Seal with an approved epoxy intermediate and polyurethane topcoat per the duplex specification.

4. **ICCP Installation (Substructure).** Design and install a limited impressed current cathodic protection system on one representative reinforced concrete pier showing evidence of chloride-induced corrosion. Instrument with embedded reference electrodes for long-term performance monitoring.

5. **Performance Monitoring Baseline.** Install corrosion rate sensors, coating disbondment coupons, and environmental exposure monitors. Establish the baseline data set for long-term performance comparison against conventional paint systems.

6. **Cost Tracking and Documentation.** Implement a rigorous cost-tracking protocol capturing all direct and indirect costs: surface preparation, material, application labor, containment, traffic control, waste disposal, and regulatory compliance. Normalize costs to dollars per square meter per year of design life.

### Deliverables
- Pilot Project Execution Report with detailed cost data
- Constructability Assessment and Lessons Learned Document
- Performance Monitoring Baseline Report
- Updated Life-Cycle Cost Analysis with field-validated unit costs
- Go/No-Go Recommendation for Fleet-Wide Rollout

### Estimated Budget
$4.2 million – $6.8 million (including traffic control, containment, materials, labor, instrumentation)

### Responsible Parties
- **Lead:** T.Y. Lin International (Engineering, Specification, Construction Oversight)
- **Contractor:** To be selected via Caltrans-qualified bidding process
- **Coating Supplier:** To be qualified per Caltrans Materials Engineering and Testing Services (METS) approved products list
- **Monitoring:** T.Y. Lin International (instrumentation and data analysis)

### Risk Factors
- Weather windows in the Carquinez Strait may constrain blasting and application schedules; contingency scheduling required
- Lead abatement requirements for existing coating removal will add cost — this cost is the same for conventional repainting, so the marginal economic comparison remains valid
- Contractor unfamiliarity with TSZ application may result in a learning curve; a pre-qualification demonstration will mitigate this risk

## Phase 2: High-Priority Fleet Rollout (Months 12–36)

### Objective
Expand the protection program to the highest-priority structures identified in the Phase 0 prioritization matrix, sequencing work to align with BATA CIP cycles and minimizing cumulative traffic disruption.

### Priority Structures

| Priority | Structure | Primary Intervention | Estimated Cost |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|----------------|
| 1 | San Mateo-Hayward Bridge | ICCP for substructure (Phase 2, remaining 537 piers); TSZ duplex for superstructure steel elements | $125M – $145M |
| 2 | Richmond-San Rafael Bridge | TSZ duplex for remaining steel (upper deck truss, approach structures); zone ICCP for select piers | $58M – $72M |
| 3 | San Francisco-Oakland Bay Bridge (West Span) | TSZ duplex for floor system and tower steel; ICCP for anchorage housings and pier walls | $95M – $130M |

### Key Activities

1. **San Mateo-Hayward Bridge — Substructure ICCP Phase 2.** Complete the installation of impressed current cathodic protection on the remaining 537 piers not addressed in Phase 1. This follows the successful Phase 1 installation on 321 piers ($45M programmed). The Phase 2 scope includes anode installation, wiring, transformer-rectifier units, embedded reference electrodes, and remote monitoring system integration.

2. **San Mateo-Hayward Bridge — Superstructure Protection.** Apply TSZ duplex coating to all exposed structural steel on the high-rise section, including spandrel beams, pier caps, and bearing assemblies. The tower painting ($14M, recently completed) provides a baseline for coating performance comparison on adjacent elements.

3. **Richmond-San Rafael Bridge — Remaining Steel Protection.** Following completion of the $68M steel painting (June 2024), apply TSZ duplex to the upper deck truss and approach spans. The recently issued Design Deviation Order (DDO) for deteriorated sections indicates urgency for intervention. Integrate with the programmed deck repair and joint seal replacement (FY25/26) to minimize separate mobilizations.

4. **San Francisco-Oakland West Span — Floor System and Tower Protection.** Execute the floor system coating replacement with TSZ duplex, directly addressing the cost impact of Caltrans' lead regulations on the currently programmed FY24/25 paint work. The main cable inspection ($28M, programmed) and fender replacement ($118M, IIJA-funded) provide additional access and mobilization windows.

5. **Fleet-Wide ICCP Integration.** Develop standardized ICCP design templates, monitoring protocols, and maintenance procedures for reinforced concrete substructures across the fleet. Design for remote monitoring capability to minimize ongoing inspection labor costs.

### Deliverables
- Structure-specific design and specification packages for each bridge
- Construction completion reports for each intervention
- Updated condition assessment and risk register
- Fleet-wide ICCP monitoring system integration report

### Estimated Budget
$278M – $347M (aggregate across three priority structures, including design, construction, traffic control, and monitoring)

### Responsible Parties
- **Lead:** T.Y. Lin International (engineering design, construction support, monitoring)
- **Contractors:** Multiple Caltrans-qualified bridge contractors, competitively bid
- **Program Oversight:** BATA, Caltrans DES, FHWA

### Risk Factors
- Simultaneous closures on multiple bridges could create regional traffic impacts; a corridor-level traffic management plan will be required
- Escalating steel and zinc commodity prices could impact TSZ material costs
- Federal funding disbursement timing (IIJA/BIP) may not align with construction readiness dates

## Phase 3: Fleet-Wide Deployment (Months 36–60)

### Objective
Achieve comprehensive corrosion protection coverage across the entire Bay Area toll bridge fleet, fully integrated with BATA's rolling 10-year Capital Improvement Program.

### Scope

| Structure | Intervention | Estimated Cost |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Benicia-Martinez Bridge (NB) | Preventive TSZ duplex on select steel elements; ICCP installation on approach piers | $28M – $38M |
| Benicia-Martinez Bridge (SB) | Substructure ICCP; deck/joint rehabilitation integration with corrosion protection | $22M – $30M |
| Carquinez Bridge (WB) | TSZ duplex full superstructure; piggyback on EB pilot lessons learned | $18M – $25M |
| Dumbarton Bridge | TSZ duplex for steel superstructure elements; integrated with $84M programmed paint and $52M concrete repair | $65M – $80M |
| Antioch Bridge | Preventive ICCP on substructure; selective TSZ duplex on steel elements | $12M – $18M |
| San Francisco-Oakland Bay Bridge (East Span) | Preventive ICCP on substructure elements; condition-based selective coating | $8M – $14M |

### Key Activities

1. **Systematic Condition Re-Assessment.** Prior to construction on each structure, conduct an updated condition assessment incorporating monitoring data from Phase 1 and 2 installations. Adjust intervention scope based on observed corrosion rates and coating performance trends.

2. **Integration with BATA CIP Cycles.** For each structure, schedule protection work to coincide with the next programmed CIP maintenance window. This minimizes standalone traffic control costs and leverages planned engineering and contractor mobilization.

3. **Preventive Protection Protocol Deployment.** For structures in Good condition (Antioch, Benicia-Martinez NB, SF-Oakland East Span), implement a preventive protection protocol rather than waiting for deterioration to reach the Fair/Poor threshold. This protocol includes: application of penetrating corrosion inhibitors to concrete elements; installation of galvanic anode systems at critical splash-zone locations; and selective TSZ duplex on connection details and other corrosion-vulnerable elements.

4. **BATA CIP Integration.** Formalize a Bridge Fleet Protection Program line item within BATA's Capital Improvement Program, ensuring dedicated, predictable funding across the 10-year planning horizon rather than ad-hoc project-by-project allocations.

5. **Training and Knowledge Transfer.** Develop and deliver a comprehensive training program for Caltrans maintenance personnel on TSZ duplex inspection, ICCP system maintenance, and performance monitoring data interpretation.

### Deliverables
- Fleet-wide Protection Completion Certificates for each structure
- BATA CIP Integration Document with dedicated program line item
- Caltrans Maintenance Personnel Training Program Materials
- Fleet Condition and Protection Status Dashboard

### Estimated Budget
$153M – $205M (aggregate fleet-wide, excluding Phase 2 structures)

### Responsible Parties
- **Lead:** T.Y. Lin International (program management, engineering)
- **Owner's Representative:** BATA
- **Construction:** Multiple pre-qualified contractors
- **Funding Partners:** FHWA, Caltrans, BATA

### Risk Factors
- BATA CIP budget constraints may require scope phasing adjustments
- Workforce availability for specialized TSZ application may constrain concurrent execution across multiple bridges
- Changes in FHWA or Caltrans leadership could shift funding priorities

## Phase 4: Sustained Monitoring and Adaptive Management (Ongoing)

### Objective
Establish a permanent monitoring, maintenance, and adaptive management framework that ensures the design-life performance of all installed protection systems and enables data-driven decisions for future interventions.

### Key Activities

1. **Remote Monitoring Network.** Operate and maintain the fleet-wide remote monitoring network for all ICCP systems, using embedded reference electrodes and transformer-rectifier unit telemetry. Implement automated alert thresholds for system performance deviations.

2. **Biennial Coating Condition Surveys.** Conduct comprehensive coating condition surveys on all TSZ duplex installations coinciding with each structure's NBIS inspection cycle. Use ASTM D610 rust grade assessments and ISO 4628 coating degradation ratings to track performance over time.

3. **Adaptive Management Protocol.** Establish a formal adaptive management decision framework: when monitoring data indicates accelerated corrosion rates or coating degradation exceeding design assumptions, trigger a structured review process that evaluates corrective action options, updates the life-cycle cost model, and adjusts future intervention schedules accordingly.

4. **BATA Reporting Integration.** Deliver an annual Bridge Fleet Protection Status Report to BATA, integrated with the existing Toll Bridge Program Annual Report. The report will include: protection system performance metrics; remaining service life projections; updated life-cycle cost comparisons against conventional alternatives; and recommendations for CIP adjustments.

5. **Technology Watch.** Maintain an active technology watch function that evaluates emerging corrosion protection technologies — including advanced coating formulations, autonomous inspection systems, and next-generation cathodic protection approaches — for potential integration into the program as they reach Technology Readiness Level 7 or higher.

6. **Research Publications.** Prepare and submit case study papers to peer-reviewed journals and industry conferences (TRB Annual Meeting, AMPP Annual Conference, AASHTO Subcommittee on Bridges and Structures) documenting the program's cost and performance outcomes. This contributes to the national body of knowledge and supports FHWA technology deployment initiatives.

### Deliverables
- Annual Fleet Protection Status Report (ongoing)
- Adaptive Management Review Records (as triggered)
- Technology Watch Summary (annual)
- Peer-Reviewed Publications (as opportunities arise)

### Estimated Budget
$2.5M – $3.5M per year (monitoring, reporting, adaptive management, technology watch)

### Responsible Parties
- **Lead:** T.Y. Lin International (monitoring, analysis, reporting)
- **Owner:** BATA (program funding and governance)
- **Technical Advisory:** Caltrans DES, FHWA Resource Center

### Risk Factors
- Long-term budget continuity for monitoring may be deprioritized relative to new capital projects
- Technology obsolescence in monitoring instrumentation may require periodic refresh investments
- Institutional knowledge retention as personnel transition over the program's multi-decade horizon

## Regulatory and Economic Context

### Caltrans Lead Exposure Regulations (2025)

In 2025, Caltrans adopted amendments to Title 8, Section 1532.1 (Lead in Construction), significantly tightening permissible exposure limits, medical surveillance requirements, and engineering controls for operations involving lead-containing coatings. Given that virtually all existing bridge steel paint in the Bay Area fleet contains lead-based primers, these regulations have a direct and substantial cost impact on conventional repainting operations:

- **Full containment requirements** (Class 1A or 1B containments per SSPC Guide 6) for all lead paint removal, adding $12–$18 per square foot to surface preparation costs
- **Continuous air monitoring** during all abrasive blasting operations
- **Medical surveillance programs** for all workers with potential lead exposure
- **Hazardous waste disposal** of lead-containing abrasive blast media and paint debris as California-regulated hazardous waste

These regulatory requirements apply equally to the surface preparation required for TSZ duplex application. However, the critical economic distinction is that a TSZ duplex system eliminates the *next* two to three repainting cycles over a 50-to-75-year service life. The lead compliance cost, therefore, is incurred once — not repeatedly every 15–20 years as would be the case with a conventional paint-only strategy. This is the regulatory-economic mechanism by which the TSZ duplex approach transforms from a higher-first-cost alternative to the lower-life-cycle-cost solution.

## Governance and Decision Gates

Each phase transition is gated by a formal decision review involving BATA, Caltrans, and FHWA representatives:

| Gate | Review Point | Decision Criteria |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Gate 0→1 | Phase 0 completion | Prioritization model validated; stakeholder alignment achieved; funding pathway confirmed; pilot bridge selected |
| Gate 1→2 | Phase 1 completion | Pilot cost and performance data support fleet-wide business case; construction methodology validated; regulatory compliance confirmed |
| Gate 2→3 | Phase 2 completion | Fleet-wide deployment economics confirmed; BATA CIP integration established; contractor base qualified |
| Gate 3→4 | Phase 3 completion | Monitoring network operational; adaptive management framework functional; sustained funding secured |
