# 01 — Calcium Silicate Chemistry

## Conventional Cement Chemistry

Ordinary Portland Cement is manufactured by heating a controlled mixture of limestone
and clay minerals in a rotary kiln at ~1,450°C. The critical reaction is calcination
of calcium carbonate:

    CaCO3(s) → CaO(s) + CO2(g)     ΔH = +178 kJ/mol

For every metric ton of clinker produced, approximately 0.51 metric tons of CO2 are
released from calcination alone. Additional CO2 comes from fuel combustion to sustain
kiln temperature, bringing total process emissions to ~0.8–0.9 tCO2 per ton of cement.

Worldwide cement production exceeds 4.1 billion metric tons annually, making this
decarbonation reaction the single largest chemical source of anthropogenic CO2.

## Brimstone's Feedstock Chemistry

Brimstone substitutes calcium silicate minerals for limestone. The key mineral
assemblages are found in **mafic and ultramafic rocks** (basalt, gabbro, peridotite,
wollastonite skarns). Representative minerals include:

| Mineral | Formula | CaO wt% | SiO2 wt% | CO2 on heating |
|---------|---------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Wollastonite | CaSiO3 | 48.3 | 51.7 | None |
| Anorthite | CaAl2Si2O8 | 20.2 | 43.2 | None |
| Diopside | CaMgSi2O6 | 25.9 | 55.5 | None |
| Larnite (β-C2S) | Ca2SiO4 | 65.1 | 34.9 | None |

None of these minerals contain carbonate. When heated, they dissociate or restructure
into reactive phases without evolving CO2:

    CaSiO3(s) + heat → reactive CaO·SiO2 phases
    CaMgSi2O6(s) + heat → CaO + MgO + 2 SiO2

## Clinker Phase Formation

Despite the different starting minerals, Brimstone's process produces the same four
principal clinker phases as conventional OPC:

1. **Alite (C3S):** Ca3SiO5 — 50–70% of clinker; primary strength contributor
2. **Belite (C2S):** Ca2SiO4 — 15–30%; late-age strength
3. **Aluminate (C3A):** Ca3Al2O6 — 5–10%; early setting
4. **Ferrite (C4AF):** Ca4Al2Fe2O10 — 5–15%; flux, color

The phase distribution is controlled by adjusting the raw meal composition through
addition of supplementary alumina and iron sources (bauxite, iron ore) — the same
corrective materials used in conventional cement plants.

## Mineral Processing Considerations

Calcium silicate rocks require different comminution (crushing and grinding) energy
compared to limestone. Basalt is generally harder (Mohs 6–7 vs. limestone 3–4);
however, wollastonite and certain skarn deposits are significantly softer. Brimstone
targets ore bodies where grinding work index is within acceptable economic bounds.

The company has developed proprietary beneficiation and blending protocols to achieve
the precise CaO:SiO2:Al2O3:Fe2O3 ratios required by the Bogue equations for target
clinker composition.

## Thermodynamic Equivalence

Thermodynamic modeling using FactSage and Calphad databases confirms that the free
energy of formation for the clinker phases from silicate precursors is comparable
to — and in some cases more favorable than — formation from carbonate precursors.
The absence of the endothermic calcination step (178 kJ/mol CaCO3) also reduces the
theoretical heat requirement, though practical kiln design must still deliver
clinkering temperatures.

## Key Differentiator

Brimstone is not producing an alternative cement chemistry (like geopolymers,
calcium sulfoaluminate, or magnesium oxychloride). It produces **chemically identical
OPC clinker** from a fundamentally different — and intrinsically zero-process-CO2 —
mineral feedstock. This is the basis for ASTM C150 equivalency.
