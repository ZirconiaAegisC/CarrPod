# 14 — Industry Partnerships & Alliances

## Strategic Partnership Philosophy

Cement is a relationship-driven industry. Purchase decisions involve multiple
stakeholders: ready-mix producers, general contractors, structural engineers,
architects, building owners, and government procurement officers. Brimstone's
partnership strategy is to build credibility across this entire chain in parallel.

## Research & Academic Partnerships

### Caltech (California Institute of Technology)
Brimstone is a Caltech spinout. The foundational research was conducted in the
laboratories of the Division of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering. Ongoing
collaborations include:

- Clinker phase equilibria studies at non-standard raw meal compositions
- Carbonation kinetics of MgO-rich industrial residues
- Life cycle assessment methodology development
- Graduate student pipeline for recruitment

### MIT Concrete Sustainability Hub (CSHub)
Joint research on:
- Cradle-to-grave LCA for silicate-derived OPC
- Comparative environmental product declarations (EPDs)
- Resiliency and durability of carbon-negative concrete in infrastructure

### UC Davis / UC Berkeley
- Pavement LCA with Caltrans
- Alkali-silica reaction (ASR) testing of basalt-derived cement with reactive
  aggregates
- Supplementary cementitious material interaction studies

## Industry Consortiums

### NEU (formerly NRMCA) — National Ready Mixed Concrete Association
NRMCA member companies operate ~5,500 ready-mix plants in the US. Brimstone's
NRMCA membership provides direct access to potential offtake partners and
participation in:
- Concrete Sustainability Council
- Build with Strength coalition
- Annual ConcreteWorks conference and trade show

### PCA (Portland Cement Association)
The PCA represents US cement manufacturers. Brimstone's PCA membership signals
that the company operates within the established industry framework (ASTM C150
OPC) rather than positioning as a disruptive outsider. PCA advocacy for carbon
border adjustments and Buy Clean policy benefits Brimstone.

### RMI (Rocky Mountain Institute) — Industrial Decarbonization
RMI's industrial decarbonization initiatives have featured Brimstone in case
studies, policy briefs, and convenings that connect cement decarbonization
companies with policymakers, buyers, and investors.

### World Economic Forum — First Movers Coalition
The First Movers Coalition (FMC) includes 90+ companies (Alphabet, Apple,
Aker BP, etc.) that have made advance purchase commitments for near-zero
cement and concrete. Brimstone is positioned to fulfill FMC offtake pledges
that other low-carbon cement technologies cannot match on carbon negativity.

## Government & Standards Partnerships

### US Department of Energy (DOE)
- Industrial Demonstrations Program: $189M co-funding
- Loan Programs Office: relationship for debt financing of subsequent plants
- ARPA-E: potential for process intensification R&D (low-temperature clinkering
  via mineralizers, electrified kilns)

### General Services Administration (GSA)
- Low-embodied carbon material procurement pilot
- Brimstone cement qualified for GSA projects under Interim IRA requirements

### ASTM International
- Brimstone technical staff serve on ASTM C01 (Cement) and C09 (Concrete) committees
- Contribution to development of carbon-negative cement specification categories
  and LCA-PCR standards

## Construction & Engineering Partnerships

### Fluor / Bechtel / KBR (EPC Firms)
Front-end engineering design (FEED) and EPCM (engineering, procurement, construction
management) contracts for plant construction. Brimstone has engaged with major EPC
firms with cement plant experience (Polysius/thyssenkrupp, FLSmidth, KHD Humboldt
Wedag) for kiln line supply.

### AECOM / WSP (Design & Engineering)
Partnering with major AEC firms to integrate carbon-negative cement into building
specifications from the design phase — critical because embodied carbon decisions
are locked in during schematic design, not during procurement.

## Offtake & Co-Development Agreements

### Amazon
Amazon Climate Pledge Fund investment implies preferential access to Amazon's
massive concrete demand for data centers and logistics facilities (~1.5M+ cubic
yards of concrete annually across North America).

### Tech Company Buyers (Anticipated)
- Google: committed to 24/7 carbon-free energy; expanding embodied carbon focus
- Microsoft: carbon-negative by 2030 pledge; concrete is Scope 3 bottleneck
- Meta: net-zero value chain by 2030; data center construction is primary Scope 3
  source

These tech buyers are competing on sustainability commitments and are willing to
pay green premiums to secure scarce negative-carbon materials.

## International Technology Licensing

For markets outside North America, Brimstone's go-to-market leverages licensing
agreements with incumbent cement producers. This capital-light model:

- In-region cement partner provides quarry, kiln, and market access
- Brimstone provides process IP, raw meal formulation, QA/QC protocols
- Royalty: 3–7% of revenue or $5–15/ton of cement
- Initial target regions: EU (CBAM advantage), India (basalt abundance), Middle East
  (peridotite/serpentinite resources, sovereign wealth capital)
