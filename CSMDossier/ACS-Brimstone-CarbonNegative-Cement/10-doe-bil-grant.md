# 10 — DOE Grant & Bipartisan Infrastructure Law

## Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL) — Overview

The Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA), signed November 2021, allocated
approximately $12 billion for carbon capture, direct air capture, and industrial
decarbonization programs, administered primarily through the DOE Office of Clean
Energy Demonstrations (OCED).

## The $189 Million Award

In March 2023, the Department of Energy announced Brimstone as a recipient of up to
**$189 million** in federal funding under the **Industrial Demonstrations Program**
(IDP), part of the BIL's $6.3 billion allocation for advanced industrial facilities.

Key terms:

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Total federal share | Up to $189,000,000 |
| Cost-share requirement | Minimum 50% non-federal cost share |
| Total project cost | >$378,000,000 |
| Program | Industrial Demonstrations Program (IDP) |
| Administering office | DOE OCED |
| Award structure | Milestone-based, phased disbursement |
| Performance period | ~4–6 years (construction + operational validation) |

## Use of Funds

The DOE grant is structured to fund the first commercial-scale demonstration plant
and covers:

1. **FEED (Front-End Engineering Design):** ~$15–25M
2. **Detailed engineering and procurement:** ~$40–60M
3. **Construction (civil, structural, mechanical, electrical):** ~$200–280M
4. **Commissioning and startup:** ~$15–25M
5. **Operational validation (first 2 years):** ~$20–40M
6. **Monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV):** ~$5–10M

## Policy Context — Why Cement?

The DOE's selection of Brimstone reflects cement's strategic importance in industrial
decarbonization:

- Cement is the **single largest industrial CO2 source** after energy generation
- Cement plants are **long-lived assets** (30–50 year operational life; plants being
  built today lock in emissions for decades)
- There are **no commercially proven zero-carbon alternatives to OPC** at scale
- Cement demand is projected to **grow 12–23% by 2050** (driven by developing
  economies and urbanization)

The DOE explicitly evaluates industrial demonstrations on:
- GHG emissions reduction potential (quantity and percentage)
- Technical feasibility and readiness level (TRL ≥ 6 at proposal)
- Scalability and replicability
- Community benefits and environmental justice
- Financial viability and cost-share commitment

## IRA Section 48C — Complementary Incentive

In addition to the BIL grant, Brimstone qualifies for the IRA's **48C Qualifying
Advanced Energy Project Credit**, an investment tax credit of up to 30% of qualified
capital expenditures. The 48C credit and the BIL grant are stackable (with appropriate
basis adjustment), effectively reducing Brimstone's equity requirement by:

    30% of CAPEX via 48C + $189M grant = >50% of project cost from public sources

The remaining portion is covered by private equity and project finance debt.

## DOE Loan Programs Office (LPO)

Beyond the IDP grant, Brimstone has access to DOE LPO programs:

| Program | Available | Terms |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Title 17 — Innovative Energy | Up to 80% of project cost | Treasury-rate loans |
| Tribal Energy | For projects on tribal lands | Loan guarantees |
| CIFIA (CO2 transport infrastructure) | N/A to Brimstone | — |

An LPO Title 17 loan could provide ~$250–400M in additional low-cost debt for
subsequent plants, reducing the weighted average cost of capital.

## International Analogues

- **EU Innovation Fund:** €40 billion (2020–2030) for industrial decarbonization;
  potential for EU-based Brimstone plants
- **UK Industrial Decarbonisation Challenge:** £170M for cement decarbonization
- **Canada Net Zero Accelerator:** C$8B fund; cement eligible
- **India, China:** Emerging industrial decarbonization funds under discussion at COP

## Community Benefits Plan

The DOE requires a Community Benefits Plan (CBP) addressing:
1. Community and labor engagement
2. Quality job creation (prevailing wage, project labor agreements)
3. Diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility (DEIA)
4. Justice40 Initiative: 40% of benefits to disadvantaged communities

Brimstone's CBP commits to union-labor construction, workforce training partnerships
with community colleges, and plant siting in communities affected by legacy industrial
pollution.
