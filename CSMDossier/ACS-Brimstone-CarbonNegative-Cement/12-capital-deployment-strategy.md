# 12 — Capital Deployment Strategy & Unit Economics

## First Plant Economics

Brimstone's first commercial plant targets **250,000–500,000 metric tons of cement
per year** — a mid-scale plant by US standards (average US cement plant: ~800,000 t/yr).

### CAPEX Estimate

| Component | Cost ($M) | Basis |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Quarry development | 20–40 | Greenfield basalt quarry |
| Raw material handling & grinding | 40–60 | HPGR + ball mill circuit |
| Preheater/precalciner tower | 35–55 | Modified 5-stage cyclone |
| Rotary kiln (Ø4.5–5.0m × 70–80m) | 50–75 | Refractory, drive, shell |
| Clinker cooler | 15–25 | Grate cooler |
| Finish grinding (cement mill) | 25–40 | Vertical roller mill |
| MgO carbonation system | 15–25 | Engineered carbonation cells |
| Storage, packing, loadout | 15–25 | Silos, bagging, truck/rail |
| Balance of plant (utilities, roads) | 30–45 | Power, water, site prep |
| Engineering & project management | 20–35 | FEED + EPCM |
| Contingency (15–20%) | 40–65 | |
| **Total CAPEX** | **~305–490** | |

## Revenue Stack

| Revenue Source | $/t Cement | Note |
|---------------|------------|------|
| Base cement price (Type I/II, bulk) | $120–160 | US average portland cement price |
| Green premium (20–30% above base) | +$25–50 | Verified carbon-negative |
| Carbon credit revenue ($50–100/t CO2) | +$5–20 | Voluntary market, ~100–200 kg net negative |
| IRA 45Q credit ($60–180/t CO2) | +$6–36 | Depending on pathway qualification |
| **Total Revenue per ton** | **$155–266** | |

### Gross Margin Sensitivity

| Scenario | Revenue/t | OPEX/t | Gross Margin | Margin % |
|----------|----------|--------|-------------|----------|
| Conservative (no green premium) | $120–160 | $76–118 | $42–44 | 28–35% |
| Base case (moderate premium) | $155–200 | $76–118 | $77–82 | 39–51% |
| Optimistic (full premium + 45Q) | $210–266 | $76–118 | $132–148 | 55–63% |

At 500,000 t/yr, base case annual gross profit of **$38–41 million** supports debt
service and reinvestment. The carbon-negative differentiation commands pricing power
in markets with regulatory carbon costs (California, EU).

## Scaling Economics

Cement plants exhibit significant scale economies:

| Plant Capacity (t/yr) | CAPEX $/t | OPEX $/t | Notes |
|----------------------|-----------|----------|-------|
| 250,000 | ~1,200–1,600 | ~90–118 | Sub-scale, demonstration |
| 500,000 | ~900–1,200 | ~76–95 | Minimum efficient scale |
| 1,500,000 | ~600–800 | ~60–75 | World-scale plant |
| 3,000,000 | ~450–600 | ~50–65 | Largest single-line plants |

Brimstone's strategy follows a **crawl-walk-run** trajectory: prove the process at
demonstration scale, optimize, then deploy at full scale.

## Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)

For plant 2+ (post-demonstration, with DOE LPO debt at Treasury rates):

- Total CAPEX: ~$600M
- DOE LPO debt (70% at 3.5%): $420M
- Equity: ~$180M (including 48C tax credit recapture)
- Annual EBITDA (at 1.5M t/yr, base case): ~$115–155M
- Interest expense: ~$15M/yr
- Unlevered ROIC: ~19–26%
- Levered ROE: ~30–50%

These returns attract infrastructure and private equity capital for the build-out
phase, enabling self-funding expansion after plant 3–4.

## Exit Pathways (Long-Term)

1. **Strategic acquisition:** Holcim, Heidelberg, Cemex — facing existential
   decarbonization pressure — acquire Brimstone for process IP.
2. **IPO:** Once 2–3 plants are operational with demonstrated unit economics.
3. **Licensing model:** Royalty on global cement production using Brimstone IP.
4. **Remain independent:** Vertical integration into ready-mix and aggregate.
