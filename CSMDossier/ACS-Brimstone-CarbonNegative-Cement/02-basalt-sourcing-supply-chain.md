# 02 — Basalt Sourcing & Supply Chain

## Feedstock Geology

Brimstone's process requires calcium- and magnesium-bearing silicate rocks. The
primary targeted lithologies are:

1. **Basalt** — fine-grained mafic volcanic rock, 45–52% SiO2, 8–12% CaO, 5–12% MgO
2. **Gabbro / Diabase** — intrusive equivalents, coarser grained, same bulk chemistry
3. **Wollastonite skarns** — metasomatic CaSiO3 deposits, >45% CaO
4. **Peridotite / Serpentinite** — ultramafic rocks, exceptionally high MgO (30–45%)

## Global Abundance

Basalt is the most abundant volcanic rock on Earth's surface, covering approximately
70% of the ocean floor and forming vast continental flood basalt provinces:

- **Columbia River Basalt Group (USA):** ~210,000 km³, Pacific Northwest
- **Deccan Traps (India):** ~500,000 km³
- **Siberian Traps (Russia):** ~1,000,000–4,000,000 km³
- **Paraná-Etendeka (Brazil/Namibia):** >1,000,000 km³
- **Cascades / Sierra Nevada (USA):** Active and accessible basalt formations

Unlike limestone, which forms in specific shallow marine depositional environments,
basalt is ubiquitous along tectonic plate boundaries, continental rifts, and hot-spot
volcanic provinces. The geological resource base is effectively inexhaustible.

## Quarry Operations

Brimstone's quarry model mirrors conventional aggregate mining:

1. **Overburden removal:** Topsoil and weathered cap rock stripped
2. **Drill-and-blast:** Benched quarry faces, ANFO-based fragmentation
3. **Load-and-haul:** Standard mining trucks to primary crusher
4. **Crushing circuit:** Jaw crusher → cone crusher → high-pressure grinding rolls (HPGR)
5. **Classification:** Screening and air separation to target particle size distribution
6. **Blending:** Controlled mixing of multiple quarry faces to achieve raw meal chemistry

## Baseline vs. Limestone Mining

| Parameter | Limestone Quarry | Basalt Quarry |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Average CaO content | 48–52% | 8–12% |
| Required rock mass (per t clinker) | ~1.7–2.0 t | ~6–8 t |
| Blasting energy | Moderate | Moderate–high (harder rock) |
| Grinding energy (Bond Work Index) | 8–12 kWh/t | 14–20 kWh/t |
| Beneficiation required | Minimal | Moderate–significant |
| CO2 from mining | ~5–10 kg/t clinker | ~15–25 kg/t clinker |

The higher rock mass requirement is offset by basalt's lower cost per ton ($3–8/t
vs. $6–15/t for high-grade limestone) and widespread geographic availability that
reduces haul distances.

## Logistics & Transportation

Cement plants are inherently local in their raw material radius (typically <15 km for
limestone). Brimstone targets sites where basalt quarries can be co-located with the
kiln, minimizing transport emissions. Rail-adjacent sites offer extended sourcing
radii for areas where basalt and limestone formations are interbedded.

## Supply Chain Resilience

- **No single-source dependency:** Basalt formations are regionally extensive
- **No limestone depletion risk:** Independence from carbonate quarries
- **Mine permitting:** Aggregate mining permits are generally faster than limestone
  permits; basalt quarries for construction aggregate are already common
- **Co-product sales:** Crushed basalt fractions not suitable for cement can be sold
  as construction aggregate, road base, or railway ballast

## Magnesium Byproduct Handling

The MgO-rich stream separated during beneficiation requires dedicated processing.
Brimstone's process concentrates MgO phases and exposes them to atmospheric CO2 in
engineered carbonation cells. The resulting MgCO3 is a stable mineral that can be
landfilled, used as soil amendment, or incorporated into low-grade fill applications.
