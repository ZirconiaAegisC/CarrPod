# ACS OneD Battery Sciences | Financial Position & Funding History

### Key Investors

**GM Ventures**
- General Motors' corporate venture capital arm
- Strategic investment aligned with GM's SINANODE partnership
- Investment rationale: equity exposure to a critical battery technology supplier; board observation rights; visibility into technology roadmap
- Significance: More than financial — signals deep strategic alignment between GM and OneD

**Koch Strategic Platforms**
- Venture and growth equity arm of Koch Industries
- Focus on energy, manufacturing, and industrial technology
- Investment rationale: Exposure to battery manufacturing process innovation; potential synergies with Koch's industrial gas and chemical businesses

**Volta Energy Technologies**
- Energy storage-focused venture capital firm
- Deep battery industry expertise and network
- Investment rationale: Core battery technology investment; silicon anode thesis
- Significance: Provides battery industry credibility and technical due diligence validation

## 2. Revenue Model and Unit Economics

### Revenue Streams

**Development and Evaluation Agreements**
- Fees charged to automakers and cell manufacturers for SINANODE evaluation programs
- Covers OneD's engineering support costs during evaluation phase
- Typical range: $500K-$2M per year per partner
- Current active agreements: 3-5 partners (including GM)

**Technology License Fees**
- Upfront license fee upon signing commercial SINANODE license
- Estimated range: $10-30M per licensee
- One-time, non-recurring revenue
- Triggers upon commercial agreement execution, not production start

**Per-kWh Royalties**
- Ongoing royalty on each kWh of cell production using SINANODE process
- Estimated royalty rate: $3-5/kWh in early commercial phase
- Rate likely declines over time with volume and competitive pressure
- Paid quarterly based on licensee production volumes (audited)

**Technical Support and Services**
- Annual support fees for ongoing process engineering, troubleshooting, and optimization
- Estimated: $1-3M/year per commercial licensee
- Covers OneD's cost of maintaining on-site and remote support capability

### Unit Economics per Licensee
For a representative 20 GWh/year licensee:

| Revenue Element | Annual Revenue | Margin |
|----------------|---------------|--------|
| Royalties (20 GWh × $4/kWh avg) | $80M | >90% |
| Technical support | $2M | 50% |
| **Total annual revenue per licensee** | $82M | ~88% |

## 3. Financial Projections

### Revenue Scenarios

**Near-Term (2024-2027): Pre-Commercial**
- Development fees: $3-8M/year
- Milestone payments (GM): $2-5M/year (lumpy, tied to qualification phase completion)
- License fees (first non-GM licensees): $10-30M (one-time)
- Total cumulative: $20-50M

**Medium-Term (2027-2030): Early Commercial**
- GM royalties (assuming 10-30 GWh production by 2030): $30-120M/year
- Additional licensee royalties (1-2 additional by 2030): $20-80M/year
- Technical support: $3-10M/year
- Total annual revenue by 2030: $50-210M

**Long-Term (2030+): Scaled Deployment**
- Multiple licensees across automotive and energy storage
- Potential revenue of $200-800M/year at 100-400 GWh aggregate deployed capacity
- Royalty rates may decline with volume, partially offset by market growth

### Path to Profitability
OneD's low operating cost structure enables profitability at relatively modest deployment:
- **Breakeven volume**: ~5-10 GWh of aggregate licensee production
- **Timeline to breakeven**: 2027-2028 (assuming first GM commercial production 2026-2027)
- **Profitability trajectory**: High incremental margins on royalty revenue drive rapid profit growth post-breakeven

## 4. Valuation Considerations

### Comparable Company Analysis

|---------|----------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------|
| Group14 | $3B+ (last private round) | ~$10-30M (early commercial) | 100-300x | Material manufacturing |

Public market comparables suggest valuations of 15-40x revenue for silicon anode companies. Private company valuations (Group14, Sila) reflect strategic premiums and higher growth expectations. OneD's valuation is not publicly disclosed but is likely in the $200-500M range based on capital raised and dilution norms.

### Value Drivers
Key factors that would increase OneD's valuation:
1. GM commercial production announcement with specific volume commitments
2. Second and third major automaker license agreements
3. Deployment in non-GM factories validating technology portability
4. IRA-related incentives for domestic silicon anode manufacturing process deployment
5. Patent portfolio strength demonstrated through successful defense or licensing to material competitors

### Exit Scenarios
**IPO**: OneD's capital-light, high-margin licensing model is well-suited to public market valuation. Revenue visibility from licensee production forecasts provides predictable financials. Timeline: 2027-2029, post-GM commercial deployment.

**Strategic Acquisition**: Major industrial gas companies (Linde, Air Liquide, Air Products) or battery materials companies could acquire OneD to integrate SINANODE into a broader battery supply chain offering. The silane supply chain connection makes industrial gas companies natural strategic acquirers.

**Remain Private / Profitable**: The licensing model can generate substantial cash flow without external capital. OneD could remain private indefinitely as a profitable technology licensing company, similar to ARM's licensing model in semiconductors.
