## Technology Platform

### SINANODE Architecture
OneD's SINANODE process grows silicon nanowires directly within the internal pore structure of standard graphite particles. Unlike competing approaches that require manufacturing separate silicon anode materials in dedicated facilities, SINANODE adds silicon during the existing anode slurry mixing step at battery cell factories. The silicon nanowires nucleate and grow from a silane precursor, forming conductive pathways through the graphite pore network.

### Key Technical Advantages
1. **Zero Equipment Change**: Functions within existing graphite anode manufacturing lines — no capital expenditure for new factory equipment
2. **No Separate Supply Chain**: Silicon added at slurry mixing step, eliminating need for external silicon anode material suppliers and logistics
3. **Scalable Integration**: Works with any graphite supplier; cell manufacturers retain existing graphite supply relationships
4. **Pore-Level Nanowire Growth**: Silicon confined within graphite pores minimizes volume expansion stress and preserves SEI stability
5. **200+ Patents**: Extensive IP portfolio covering nanowire growth methods, precursor chemistry, and integration processes

### Competitive Moat
OneD's process-based approach creates fundamentally different competitive dynamics from Sila Nanotechnologies or Group14. Rather than selling silicon anode material, OneD licenses the SINANODE process technology. This platform model scales through partnerships rather than capital-intensive factory construction, with GM serving as the anchor customer and validation partner.

## Process Integration

### The Drop-In Advantage
The SINANODE process integrates into the existing anode manufacturing sequence at the slurry mixing stage. A standard graphite anode line mixes graphite powder with binder and solvent to form a slurry, which is then coated onto copper foil. OneD's process introduces a silane-based precursor during slurry mixing that nucleates silicon nanowires within the graphite pores.

### Manufacturing Impact
- No new factory construction required
- No retooling of existing coating, calendaring, or slitting equipment
- Process compatible with both aqueous (water-based) and organic solvent slurry chemistries
- Slurry viscosity and coating behavior engineered to match existing production parameters
