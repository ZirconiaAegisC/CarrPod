# ACS OneD Battery Sciences | Commercial Rollout & Automotive Pipeline

## 1. Licensing Business Model

### Platform Licensing Approach
OneD's commercial model is fundamentally different from every other silicon anode company. Rather than manufacturing and selling a physical product (silicon anode material), OneD licenses its SINANODE process technology to battery cell manufacturers. This model has several defining characteristics:

1. **Revenue Model**: Upfront license fees, milestone payments, and per-kWh royalties on cells produced using SINANODE
2. **Capital Light**: OneD does not build manufacturing facilities; capital investment is borne by licensees who install silane systems on existing equipment
3. **Scalability**: Revenue scales with licensee production volume without requiring OneD to build production capacity
4. **Customer Relationships**: Deep integration with licensees' manufacturing operations creates switching costs and stickiness

### Comparison to Competitor Business Models
| Company | Business Model | Capital Requirement | Revenue per GWh | Margin Structure |
|---------|---------------|--------------------|-----------------|------------------|
| OneD | Process licensing | Low ($20-40M to support licensees) | $3-5M/GWh (royalty) | High (software-like margins on royalties) |
| Group14 | Material manufacturing | Very high ($600M+ for BAM-2) | $10-20M/GWh (material sale) | Moderate (chemical manufacturing margins) |
| Sila | Material manufacturing | Very high ($600M+ for Moses Lake) | $15-25M/GWh (material sale) | Moderate (chemical manufacturing margins) |

### Economics of the Licensing Model
For a 50 GWh/year battery factory (typical for a large cell manufacturer):
- **OneD revenue**: $150-250M/year in royalties
- **OneD costs**: $5-10M/year in technical support, process engineering, and account management
- **OneD gross margin**: >90% on incremental license revenue
- **Licensee cost**: $3-5/kWh in royalty + $2-3/kWh in silane cost = $5-8/kWh total incremental cost
- **Licensee value**: $20-30/kWh in cell cost reduction (energy density improvement of 20-30% reduces $/kWh by equivalent percentage)

## 2. Automotive Customer Pipeline

### Tier Structure
OneD's customer pipeline is organized into a tiered engagement model:

**Tier 1: Anchor Partner (GM)**
- Deepest integration: joint development, pilot co-location, shared IP on process optimization
- First commercial deployment target
- Full qualification program across multiple cell formats and chemistries
- Revenue: Not yet at commercial royalty stage; development milestone payments active

**Tier 2: Advanced Evaluation Partners (2-3 undisclosed automakers)**
- Pilot-scale SINANODE evaluation on customer's graphite grades and cell designs
- Electrochemical validation complete; pilot-scale coating trials underway or planned
- Commercial negotiations in process
- Revenue: Evaluation agreement payments; not yet under commercial license

**Tier 3: Early Engagement (5-8 automakers)**
- Initial material samples provided (SINANODE-processed graphite for evaluation)
- Coin cell and small pouch cell testing
- Technical discussions and commercial framework exploration
- Revenue: Minimal; sample supply agreements

**Tier 4: Monitoring (10+ automakers)**
- Aware of SINANODE technology through industry channels
- Following GM's progress as validation signal
- Not yet engaged in formal evaluation
- Revenue: None

### Qualification Timeline Realities
Automotive battery qualification is a multi-year process. Realistic timelines from initial engagement to commercial production:

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Initial evaluation | 6-12 months | Coin cells, single-layer pouch cells, material characterization |
| Pilot qualification | 12-18 months | Pilot-scale electrode coating, multi-layer pouch cells, safety testing |
| Production pilot | 12-18 months | Production-representative equipment, full-size cells, pack testing |
| Vehicle validation | 12-24 months | Mule vehicles, pre-production builds, durability testing |
| Commercial launch | — | Process lock, supplier qualification, production ramp |

**Total minimum timeline from first engagement to commercial production: 4-6 years.** This timeline explains why OneD's commercial revenue is still pre-revenue despite the 2022 GM announcement — automotive qualification is slow by design.

## 3. Revenue Projections and Milestones

### Near-Term Revenue Drivers (2024-2027)
- **Development agreements**: $2-5M/year from evaluation partners
- **GM milestone payments**: Triggered by qualification phase completion (estimated $5-15M total across all phases)
- **Initial licensing fees**: Upfront payments from first non-GM licensees ($10-30M per licensee)

### Medium-Term Revenue (2027-2030)
- **GM commercial royalties**: Assumes first GM SINANODE production in 2027-2028
- **Second wave licensees**: Additional automakers entering production 2028-2030
- **Royalty revenue**: Projected $50-200M/year by 2030 depending on licensee count and production volumes

### Breakeven Analysis
OneD's operating model is inherently low-cost compared to manufacturing companies:
- **Annual operating costs**: Estimated $15-25M (R&D, IP management, technical support, G&A)
- **Breakeven revenue**: Achievable with 2-3 commercial licensees at modest production volumes
- **Path to profitability**: Shorter than material manufacturers, which require massive capital deployment before revenue generation

## 4. Geographic Strategy

### China Market Considerations
China represents both the largest opportunity and the greatest challenge:
- **Market size**: China produces >70% of global lithium-ion batteries; addressable market is enormous
- **IP risk**: Chinese patent enforcement has improved but remains a concern for process technology licensing
- **Approach**: OneD is likely pursuing a China strategy involving local partnerships, joint ventures, or structured licensing with enhanced IP protection mechanisms

### Western Market Focus
OneD's near-term strategy focuses on Western markets where:
- Automotive OEMs value supply chain security and regional battery production
- IP enforcement is stronger
- IRA and European battery regulation create incentives for domestic cell manufacturing
- GM's anchor relationship provides the strongest validation signal

## 5. Key Commercial Risks

### Execution Risks
1. **GM Dependency**: Revenue concentration risk; if GM delays or cancels SINANODE deployment, OneD loses both its anchor customer and its primary validation signal
2. **Qualification Timeline**: Automotive qualification taking longer than projected could delay revenue by years
3. **Silane Supply Chain**: Large-scale SINANODE deployment requires significant silane volume expansion; silane supply constraints could bottleneck adoption

### Competitive Risks
1. **Material Cost Reduction**: If silicon anode material prices (Group14, Sila) decline faster than expected, the economic advantage of SINANODE's process licensing model narrows
2. **Solid-State Batteries**: If solid-state batteries achieve commercial-scale production sooner than expected, the liquid-electrolyte lithium-ion anode market (where SINANODE operates) could face disruption
3. **Competitor Process Innovation**: A competitor developing a similar process-integration approach could challenge OneD's IP position

### Market Risks
1. **EV Adoption Slowdown**: Slower-than-projected EV adoption reduces the addressable market for battery technology licensing
2. **Alternative Chemistries**: Sodium-ion, lithium-sulfur, or other alternative battery chemistries gaining share would reduce demand for silicon-enhanced lithium-ion anodes
