# Chelan County Site and Infrastructure — Helion Energy

## Malaga Site Location
Helion Energy's Orion commercial fusion plant is situated in Malaga, Washington, an unincorporated community in Chelan County. The site lies along the western bank of the Columbia River, approximately 10 miles southeast of Wenatchee, the county seat. GPS coordinates place the industrial parcel within the broader Wenatchee Valley industrial corridor.

## Chelan County Public Utility District Interconnection
The Chelan County Public Utility District (PUD) is the owner and operator of the regional electrical transmission and distribution system. Chelan PUD is a consumer-owned utility established in 1936, serving approximately 50,000 customers across Chelan County. The PUD is notable for generating approximately 90% of its electricity from hydroelectric dams on the Columbia River.

### Interconnection Agreement
Helion and Chelan County PUD have entered into an interconnection agreement that defines:
- Technical requirements for connecting the Orion plant to the PUD's transmission system
- Protection and control schemes to ensure grid stability during pulsed operation
- Cost allocation for any transmission system upgrades required
- Operating protocols, including power factor requirements and voltage regulation
- Curtailment provisions and outage coordination procedures

### Grid Integration Considerations
Helion's pulsed fusion reactor presents unique grid integration challenges compared to conventional generators:
- Each pulse cycle involves rapid energy extraction from and return to the electrical system
- The capacitor banks draw high currents during charging cycles
- Power quality (harmonics, voltage flicker) must be managed to avoid disturbing other customers
- The plant may require power electronics-based grid interface (inverters, STATCOM devices) to smooth power delivery

### Hydroelectric Synergy
Chelan County's abundant hydroelectric resources offer potential synergies with Helion's operations:
- The pulsed charging cycles of Orion's capacitor banks could be timed to coincide with periods of excess hydro generation
- The PUD's existing expertise in managing large rotating machinery and electrical infrastructure is relevant to fusion plant operations
- The proximity of hydro generation simplifies the process of supplying startup and auxiliary power to the Orion facility

## Transportation and Logistics
The Malaga site benefits from existing transportation infrastructure:
- State Route 28 provides highway access connecting to Wenatchee and Interstate 90
- The Columbia River barge system offers potential for heavy equipment transport
- The BNSF Railway mainline runs through the Wenatchee Valley, providing rail access
- Pangborn Memorial Airport in East Wenatchee supports corporate and light cargo aviation

## Seismic and Geotechnical Considerations
The Columbia River Basin in central Washington is in a region of moderate seismic hazard. Site-specific geotechnical investigations have been conducted to characterize:
- Soil bearing capacity for heavy equipment foundations
- Liquefaction potential under seismic loading
- Groundwater conditions affecting foundation design
- Slope stability for cut-and-fill operations

## Local Economic and Workforce Context
Chelan County has a population of approximately 80,000 and an economy traditionally based on agriculture (tree fruit), hydroelectric generation, and tourism. The county's labor force includes skilled tradespeople, electricians, and technicians drawn from the regional utility and industrial base. Wenatchee Valley College offers technical training programs relevant to plant operations. The Columbia Basin's cost of living is moderate compared to coastal Washington cities, supporting competitive wage structures.

## Community Relations
Helion has engaged with the Malaga and greater Chelan County community through:
- Public information meetings on the Orion project
- Coordination with Chelan County planning and development departments
- Workforce development partnerships with local educational institutions
- Outreach to the Confederated Tribes of the Colville Reservation and other stakeholders
- Participation in regional economic development initiatives

## Long-Term Site Potential
The Malaga site is sized to accommodate potential expansion beyond the initial 50 MW Orion plant. Helion has indicated that the modular nature of its FRC reactor design allows for deploying multiple reactor units at a single site. Future expansions could increase total site capacity to hundreds of megawatts, leveraging the existing interconnection and site infrastructure.
