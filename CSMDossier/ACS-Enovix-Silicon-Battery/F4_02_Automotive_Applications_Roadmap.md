# F4-02: Automotive Market Roadmap — 2028-2035

## The Automotive Prize

The global automotive lithium-ion battery market was approximately $120 billion
in 2025, growing at a 15-20% CAGR driven by EV adoption mandates in the EU,
China, and the United States. For Enovix, automotive represents the long-term
value creation opportunity — but one that requires patience, capital, and
significant technical evolution of the Gen2 platform.

## Why Automotive Is a Different Beast

### Qualification Requirements

Automotive battery qualification is orders of magnitude more demanding than
consumer electronics:

| Parameter | Smartphone Requirement | Automotive Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Cycle life | 500-800 cycles | 1,000-3,000 cycles |
| Calendar life | 2-3 years | 10-15 years |
| Temperature range | 0-45°C | -30 to +60°C (operating) |
| Safety standard | UL 1642 | UN R100, GB 38031, AEC-Q |
| Production volume | 1-50M cells/yr/model | 100M-1B cells/yr/platform |
| Cost sensitivity | $8-12/cell acceptable | $/kWh is primary KPI |
| Warranty | 1 year | 8 years / 100,000 miles |

### Cost Curve Requirements

The automotive battery industry's holy grail is $100/kWh at the pack level.
In 2025, LFP packs from CATL and BYD achieved ~$80-100/kWh; NMC packs were at
$110-140/kWh. Enovix's current cell-level cost of ~$150/kWh (EX1-6M at $6/cell
for ~40 Wh) is uncompetitive. To enter automotive, cost must come down via:

1. Scale: Fab-2 scale brings COGS to ~$4.50/cell (~$110/kWh at cell level)
2. Chemistry: Lower-cobalt cathode formulations (Gen3)
3. Form factor: Larger automotive cells (50-100 Ah) amortize packaging costs
4. Manufacturing maturity: Yield improvements from years of Gen2 production

Target cell cost for automotive entry: $75-90/kWh at the cell level, enabling
pack-level cost of $100-120/kWh with a premium justified by energy density.

## Automotive Product Roadmap

### Phase 1: Gen3 Development (2025-2028)

Fremont R&D focused on adapting the constrained silicon platform for automotive:

- **Large-format cell design:** Scaling from smartphone-sized cells (~5 Ah)
  to automotive-sized cells (50-100 Ah). The constraint system must be re-
  engineered because force distribution across a larger cross-section is more
  complex; non-uniform constraint leads to non-uniform aging.

- **Materials evolution:**
  - Cathode: Transition from NMC 811 to NMC 955 or cobalt-free options
  - Electrolyte: LiFSI-dominant salt systems with dual additives (FEC + LiDFOB)
    for high-temperature stability
  - Separator: Ceramic-coated PE with >200°C shutdown temperature

- **Durability targets:** >2,000 cycles to 80% SOH, >12 year calendar life,
  -30°C cold crank capability

### Phase 2: Automotive Sampling & Qualification (2028-2030)

- Provide A-samples to 2-3 automotive OEMs for evaluation
- Complete AEC-Q qualification (requires ~18 months of testing)
- Build dedicated automotive pilot line at Fremont or Fab-2 (1-5 MWh/year
  for sample production)

### Phase 3: First Automotive Platform Win (2030-2032)

- Target: Premium EV segment ($80K+ vehicles) where energy density premium
  is valued — high-performance sedans, luxury SUVs, electric supercars
- Potential initial customers: Porsche, Mercedes-AMG, Lucid, Rimac — OEMs
  competing on range and performance rather than cost
- Volume: 5,000-50,000 vehicles/year initially, scaling as platform is
  adopted across vehicle lines

### Phase 4: Mainstream Automotive (2032-2035)

- As costs decline and manufacturing scales, expand to mid-market EVs
- Potential partnership or licensing deal with a major cell manufacturer
  (LG, Samsung SDI, Panasonic) for volume production
- Fab-3 or joint venture at 5-20 GWh/year scale for automotive

## Competitive Landscape in Automotive Silicon Anodes

### Direct Competitors

- **Sila Nanotechnologies:** Partnered with Mercedes-Benz (EQG concept) and
  Panasonic. Titan Silicon anode is a silicon-graphite composite (not 100%
  silicon). Mercedes EQG with Sila anodes expected in limited production
  2026-2027.

- **Group14 Technologies:** SCC55 silicon-carbon powder for anode blending.
  Partnerships with SK Materials and Porsche. Factory in Moses Lake, WA.
  Targeting automotive via SK On's cell production.

- **Amprius:** Silicon nanowire anodes. Highest demonstrated energy density
  (500 Wh/kg at cell level) but at extreme cost premium. Focused on drones,
  eVTOL, and defense — not consumer automotive.

### Enovix's Differentiator

Enovix is alone in pursuing 100% silicon with a mechanical constraint approach.
If the constraint system proves durable at automotive scale, the energy density
advantage is structural (not incremental) and cannot be matched by silicon-
graphite blends. The counterargument: silicon-graphite composites have lower
technical risk and can leverage existing manufacturing infrastructure — an
argument that resonates with risk-averse automotive supply chain managers.
