# APPENDIX — GLOSSARY
**AEGIS Protocol:** Operational framework for infrastructure protection activities, named for the mythological shield of Zeus.
**Carrington Event:** September 1859 geomagnetic storm — the most intense on record. Would devastate modern electrical infrastructure if repeated.
**Coronal Mass Ejection (CME):** Massive release of plasma and magnetic field from the Sun's corona. Earth-directed CMEs cause geomagnetic storms.
**Geomagnetically Induced Current (GIC):** Quasi-DC current induced in long conductors during geomagnetic disturbances.
**Half-Cycle Saturation:** Transformer condition during GIC events causing harmonics, reactive power consumption, and heating.
**MXene (Ti3C2Tx):** Two-dimensional transition metal carbide with exceptional capacitance density, EMI shielding, and electrical conductivity.
**Neutral-Blocking Capacitor (NBC):** Installed in transformer neutral-to-ground to block quasi-DC GIC while passing AC.
**Phantom Robot (CSM-PR-900):** Autonomous inspection and monitoring platform for electrical infrastructure.
**Protonic Communications (CSM-PC-5000):** GIC-immune backup communication using proton-conducting ceramic transceivers.
**Williams Heuristic:** Technical claims paired with plain-language explanations. 
**El Segundo Calm:** Steady, professional, non-alarmist presentation standard.
**Accountant Heuristic:** Every protection measure carries cost, expected loss prevented, and ROI.
