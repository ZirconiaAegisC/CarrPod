# IRA Section 45X Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit

## Provision Overview

Section 45X of the Internal Revenue Code, enacted as part of the
Inflation Reduction Act of 2022, provides a production tax credit
for domestically manufactured clean energy components. The credit
applies to each eligible component produced and sold by the
taxpayer within the United States.

## Battery Module Credit

For battery modules, Section 45X provides a credit equal to
$45 per kilowatt-hour of capacity. The calculation is applied to
the nameplate DC energy capacity of the module at the point of
sale to an unrelated party.

The credit structure is:

```
Credit Value = Module Energy Capacity (kWh) x $45.00/kWh
```

For a Z3 module rated at approximately 14.4 kWh, the 45X credit
generates roughly $648 per module. At scale production of 1.25
GWh annually, the credit could provide approximately $56 million
in annual benefit to Eos.

## Key Provisions

The credit was finalized through IRS guidance released in late
2023. Key elements include:

1. **Domestic Content** — Components must be manufactured in the
   United States. Assembly alone is insufficient; the regulation
   requires substantive manufacturing transformation within US
   borders.

2. **Direct Pay and Transferability** — Taxpayers may elect to
   receive the credit as a direct payment from the Treasury
   (refundable credit) for the first five years of production, or
   may transfer the credit to an unrelated third party through a
   registered sale. This mechanism is critical for companies that
   are not yet profitable and lack sufficient tax liability to
   offset the credit.

3. **No Expiration** — Unlike the ITC and PTC for generation
   assets, the 45X credit does not have a scheduled phase-down
   or sunset. It remains in effect until US greenhouse gas
   emissions from electricity generation fall below 25% of 2022
   levels — projected to be the 2032–2035 timeframe.

4. **Critical Minerals Exclusion** — The credit carries no
   requirement for critical mineral sourcing from Free Trade
   Agreement countries, which applies to the EV consumer credit
   under Section 30D. This distinction benefits Eos because its
   zinc supply chain is already fully domestic.

## Eos Qualification

Eos qualifies for the full $45/kWh credit because:

- The Z3 module is manufactured at the Turtle Creek, PA facility.
- All substantive manufacturing steps (electrode fabrication,
  cell assembly, module integration) occur in the United States.
- Raw materials (zinc, steel, plastics, carbon) are sourced
  domestically, though this is not a 45X requirement.
- Eos' cost structure means the credit represents a material
  fraction of unit production cost.

## Financial Impact

At Eos' stated target production cost of approximately $100-120
per kWh (as the company scales), a $45/kWh credit reduces the
net manufacturing cost to $55-75 per kWh before margin. This
positions the Z3 module competitively against lithium-ion systems
at $100-150/kWh on an installed basis, particularly when
factoring in the longer asset life and lower balance-of-plant
costs.

The direct pay option is significant because Eos is not currently
profitable and would otherwise be unable to monetize the credit
through tax liability reduction. Transferability through the
registered credit market provides an additional monetization
pathway.

## Strategic Context

Section 45X is one leg of a broader federal policy framework
supporting Eos' business model. Combined with DOE loan support
(DOE Loans) and utility procurement preferences for domestically
manufactured storage, the company benefits from multi-layered
policy tailwinds that are unlikely to be reversed prior to the
emissions threshold being met under current law.
