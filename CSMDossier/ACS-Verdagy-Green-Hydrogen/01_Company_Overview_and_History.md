# Verdagy — Company Overview & History

## Founding & Origins

Verdagy was founded in **2021** in Moss Landing, California, a coastal town near Monterey
situated at the heart of California's agricultural and marine research corridor. The company
emerged from decades of foundational research in alkaline water electrolysis, building upon
scientific and engineering advances that stretch back to the earliest industrial hydrogen
production methods of the twentieth century. While the legal entity is young, its intellectual
heritage is deep: the core team brought together veterans of the electrolysis industry,
electrochemical engineering, and large-scale manufacturing who had spent careers advancing
alkaline technology at predecessor firms and research institutions.

## Corporate Headquarters

Moss Landing provides Verdagy with strategic proximity to California's clean-energy policy
ecosystem, the Monterey Bay research community, and the broader West Coast renewable energy
market. The headquarters houses executive leadership, core R&D laboratories, and the
company's pilot-scale manufacturing line. The location also offers access to the Port of
Moss Landing and the Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary research infrastructure, creating
natural synergies with marine and atmospheric science communities studying hydrogen's role
in decarbonization.

## Mission & Vision

Verdagy's stated mission is to decarbonize hard-to-abate industrial sectors — steelmaking,
ammonia production, refining, and heavy transportation — by delivering green hydrogen at
fossil-fuel-competitive prices. The company's vision positions it as the leading
manufacturer of advanced alkaline electrolyzers capable of producing hydrogen at scale for
less than $2 per kilogram, the threshold established by the U.S. Department of Energy's
Hydrogen Shot initiative. Verdagy explicitly targets the elimination of precious metals
and fluorinated polymers from electrolyzer supply chains, addressing both cost volatility
and environmental concerns associated with competing proton exchange membrane technologies.

## Investment & Capitalization

Since inception, Verdagy has raised over **$70 million** in venture and strategic capital.
Its investor syndicate includes some of the most prominent names in climate technology:

- **Breakthrough Energy Ventures** — the Bill Gates-founded fund that backs high-impact
  climate solutions capable of gigaton-scale emissions reduction
- **Temasek** — Singapore's sovereign wealth fund with a growing decarbonization portfolio
- **Bidra Innovation Ventures** — a fund focused on breakthrough technologies in energy
  and sustainability
- **Shell Ventures** — the corporate venture arm of Shell plc, providing both capital and
  strategic alignment with one of the world's largest hydrogen consumers

In addition to private capital, Verdagy secured a **$40 million grant** from the U.S.
Department of Energy's Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations, placing it among a select
group of electrolyzer manufacturers receiving federal support under the Bipartisan
Infrastructure Law's clean hydrogen provisions.

## Strategic Context

Verdagy operates at the intersection of two megatrends: the global push for industrial
decarbonization and the urgent need to scale clean hydrogen production infrastructure.
The International Energy Agency projects that hydrogen demand could grow from approximately
94 million tonnes per year to over 200 million tonnes by 2050 under net-zero scenarios,
with green hydrogen — produced via electrolysis powered by renewable electricity — comprising
the vast majority of that growth. Verdagy's technology platform is purpose-built to serve
this expanding market with a capital-cost advantage that its management believes will prove
decisive as electrolyzer manufacturing transitions from niche to commodity scale.
