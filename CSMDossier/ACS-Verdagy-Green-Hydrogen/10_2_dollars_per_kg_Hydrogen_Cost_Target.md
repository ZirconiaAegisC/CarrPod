# Verdagy — The $2/kg Hydrogen Cost Target

## Strategic Significance of the $2/kg Threshold

The $2 per kilogram price point represents a critical competitive threshold in the
hydrogen market. At this level, green hydrogen becomes cost-competitive with gray
hydrogen produced from natural gas in major industrial markets, even before accounting
for carbon pricing or regulatory mandates. Crossing this threshold transforms green
hydrogen from a premium environmental product into an economically rational choice
for industrial consumers — a transition essential to achieving the multi-gigaton
emissions reductions that hydrogen can enable.

## Gray Hydrogen Baseline

Gray hydrogen, produced via steam methane reforming of natural gas without carbon
capture, currently dominates global hydrogen production at approximately 70 million
tonnes annually. Its cost is primarily driven by natural gas prices:

| Natural Gas Price ($/MMBtu) | Gray Hydrogen Cost ($/kg) |
|----------------------------|--------------------------|
| $2.50 (U.S. benchmark) | ~$1.00-1.50 |
| $6.00 (European benchmark) | ~$2.00-2.50 |
| $10.00 (Asian benchmark) | ~$3.00-3.50 |

At U.S. natural gas prices, the $2/kg target initially appears to leave green hydrogen
at a cost disadvantage. However, this gap is bridged by several factors: the IRA 45V
production tax credit, corporate renewable energy procurement at below-wholesale prices,
and increasing carbon pricing or emissions regulations in key markets.

## The DOE Hydrogen Shot Alignment

The Department of Energy's Hydrogen Shot, announced in June 2021, set an ambitious
target: reduce the cost of clean hydrogen to $1 per kilogram within one decade
(the "1 1 1" initiative). Verdagy positions its $2/kg target as an intermediate
milestone toward this goal, achievable with technology and manufacturing improvements
already underway rather than requiring fundamental scientific breakthroughs.

## Cost Stack Decomposition at $2/kg

For green hydrogen to reach $2/kg, each cost element must be optimized:

| Cost Element | Contribution ($/kg) | % of Total | Key Lever |
|-------------|-------------------|------------|-----------|
| Electricity | $1.20 | 60% | Renewable PPA pricing, capacity factor |
| Capital recovery | $0.50 | 25% | Manufacturing scale, material cost reduction |
| Fixed O&M | $0.15 | 8% | Automation, reliability engineering |
| Water & consumables | $0.05 | 2% | Water recycling, KOH replenishment |
| Stack replacement | $0.10 | 5% | Extended stack lifetime, refurbishment |

## Regional Path to $2/kg

Not all markets can achieve $2/kg simultaneously. The path is fastest in regions with:

- **Low-cost renewable electricity** — U.S. Great Plains (wind), U.S. Southwest (solar),
  Middle East (solar), Australia (solar+wind), Chile (solar+wind)
- **Existing industrial hydrogen demand** — U.S. Gulf Coast refining and ammonia,
  European chemical clusters, Asian steelmaking
- **Supportive policy frameworks** — U.S. IRA 45V, EU Renewable Energy Directive,
  Japan's Basic Hydrogen Strategy

## Competitive Cost Positioning

At the claimed $2/kg cost point, Verdagy's technology delivers hydrogen at a cost
that undercuts PEM electrolysis by 20-40% (owing to the lower capital cost) and
approaches parity with gray hydrogen in high-gas-price regions. When the IRA 45V
credit of up to $3/kg is layered on, green hydrogen can achieve a negative effective
cost in optimal configurations — a scenario that would fundamentally reorder the
economics of industrial hydrogen consumption.

## Verification & Third-Party Analysis

Independent cost analyses of alkaline vs. PEM electrolysis generally support the
direction of Verdagy's claims — alkaline systems have historically maintained lower
capital costs — though the magnitude of the claimed 80% advantage requires validation
as the company transitions from pilot to commercial-scale production. Key variables
that will determine actual cost outcomes include manufacturing yield rates, supply
chain maturity for the large-format components, and the realized efficiency and
durability of the eDynamic system in continuous commercial operation.
