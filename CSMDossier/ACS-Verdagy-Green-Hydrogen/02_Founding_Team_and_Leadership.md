# Verdagy — Founding Team & Leadership

## CEO: Marty Neese

**Marty Neese** serves as Chief Executive Officer of Verdagy, bringing over three decades
of leadership experience in the energy technology, semiconductor, and advanced manufacturing
sectors. Prior to joining Verdagy, Neese held senior executive positions at several notable
clean energy and technology companies, where he developed deep expertise in scaling capital-
intensive manufacturing operations from pilot lines to gigawatt-scale production facilities.

Neese's background spans the critical disciplines required to commercialize electrolyzer
technology at scale: electrochemical engineering, supply chain development, factory
automation, and project finance. His leadership philosophy emphasizes operational
excellence, cost discipline, and the systematic elimination of manufacturing bottlenecks
that have historically constrained electrolyzer production capacity.

## Executive Leadership Philosophy

The Verdagy leadership team is structured around a core principle: that green hydrogen
will only achieve mass adoption if electrolyzer capital costs fall by an order of magnitude
from current PEM benchmarks. This conviction informs every strategic decision — from
material selection (rejecting iridium and platinum) to factory layout (designing for
automated, high-throughput assembly) to geographic footprint (locating near end-use
industrial clusters).

## Advisory Network

Verdagy maintains an active advisory board and technical advisory panel that includes
former executives from the chlor-alkali industry, academic electrochemists specializing
in alkaline water splitting, and experienced project developers from the renewable energy
sector. This network provides the company with strategic guidance on technology roadmap
decisions, manufacturing partnerships, and market entry timing.

## Organizational Culture

The company's culture emphasizes practical engineering over theoretical elegance — a
reflection of its roots in the chlor-alkali industry, where alkaline electrolysis has
been deployed at industrial scale for over a century. Verdagy's teams prize reliability,
manufacturability, and cost reduction above incremental performance gains that would
require exotic materials or complex manufacturing processes. This cultural orientation
is a deliberate competitive strategy: by constraining material choices to abundant,
low-cost inputs, the company forces its engineering teams to innovate within parameters
that are inherently scalable.

## Talent Strategy

Verdagy recruits from a deliberately diverse set of industries beyond clean energy,
including semiconductor capital equipment, automotive manufacturing, and industrial gas
production. This cross-pollination of manufacturing disciplines is intended to accelerate
the company's progression from prototype to high-volume production, bypassing the
scale-up challenges that have plagued earlier generations of electrolyzer startups.
