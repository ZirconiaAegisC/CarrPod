# Verdagy — 10GW Manufacturing Vision & Roadmap

## The 10GW Target

Verdagy's strategic roadmap targets **10 gigawatts** of annual electrolyzer
manufacturing capacity. To contextualize this ambition: 10 GW of electrolyzer
capacity can produce approximately 1.8 million tonnes of green hydrogen annually
(at 90% capacity factor and 55 kWh/kg system efficiency), equivalent to replacing
roughly 2.5% of current global gray hydrogen production. Reaching this scale would
position Verdagy among the top three electrolyzer manufacturers globally by volume.

## Phased Scale-Up Plan

| Phase | Annual Capacity | Timeline | Key Activities |
|-------|----------------|----------|----------------|
| Pilot | ~100 MW | 2024-2025 | Process validation, supply chain qualification |
| Early commercial | ~500 MW | 2025-2027 | First automated line, Texas facility, customer deliveries |
| Scale | ~2 GW | 2027-2029 | Multi-line factory, expanded supply chain, global sales |
| Mature | ~5 GW | 2029-2031 | Second factory site, international manufacturing |
| Full scale | ~10 GW | 2031-2033 | Multi-continent manufacturing footprint |

## Manufacturing Facility Requirements

A 10 GW electrolyzer factory would represent one of the largest electrochemical
device manufacturing facilities ever built. Key requirements include:

- **Factory footprint** — approximately 500,000-1,000,000 square feet, depending
  on vertical integration strategy
- **Capital investment** — estimated $500 million to $1 billion for a greenfield
  facility, excluding working capital
- **Workforce** — 1,000-2,500 direct manufacturing employees at full capacity
- **Supply chain throughput** — thousands of tonnes per year of stainless steel,
  nickel, polymers, and electronic components
- **Energy requirement** — substantial electricity and natural gas for manufacturing
  processes (forming, coating, heat treating)

## Technology Evolution Path

| Generation | Timeline | Improvements |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Gen 1 (current) | 2024-2026 | 3 m² cell, >100 mA/cm², <$500/kW target |
| Gen 2 | 2027-2029 | Larger cell area, higher current density, improved diaphragm |
| Gen 3 | 2030+ | Advanced electrode catalysts, optimized stack design, integrated BOP |

## Market Demand Projections

Global electrolyzer demand projections from major forecasters:

| Source | 2030 Demand (GW/year) | 2040 Demand (GW/year) |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------|
| IEA (NZE Scenario) | ~60-80 | ~200-300 |
| Bloomberg NEF | ~40-60 | ~150-250 |
| Hydrogen Council | ~60-90 | ~200-400 |

Even at 10 GW/year, Verdagy would capture only a modest share of projected 2030
demand, suggesting substantial room for multiple competitors at scale.

## Financing the Scale-Up

Reaching 10 GW of manufacturing capacity will require significant capital beyond
the $70M+ already raised. Potential capital sources include:

- **Series C/D venture rounds** — $100-300M from climate-tech and industrial VCs
- **DOE Loan Programs Office** — Title 17 Clean Energy Financing for manufacturing
- **Strategic corporate investment** — joint ventures with industrial partners
- **Public markets** — potential IPO once commercial-scale operations are
  established and revenue trajectory is demonstrated
- **Project finance** — non-recourse debt for specific manufacturing facilities

## Risks to Achieving 10GW

1. **Market demand risk** — hydrogen demand may grow slower than projected if
   carbon pricing is delayed, renewable electricity costs plateau, or competing
   decarbonization pathways (direct electrification, biofuels) capture share
2. **Technology risk** — unanticipated durability or performance issues at scale
   could undermine the economic case and customer confidence
3. **Competitive risk** — Chinese manufacturers may drive prices below levels that
   support Verdagy's cost structure, particularly in export markets
4. **Execution risk** — scaling from pilot to 10 GW manufacturing is unprecedented
   in the electrolyzer industry and requires organizational capabilities that
   the company is still developing
5. **Policy risk** — changes to IRA provisions, DOE funding, or trade policy
   could materially alter the economic viability of U.S.-based manufacturing

## End-Market Diversification

To de-risk the demand side, Verdagy targets multiple end-use markets:

| Market | Hydrogen Demand Driver | Addressable Scale |
|--------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Ammonia production | Fertilizer, maritime fuel | Very large (100+ Mt/year) |
| Refining | Desulfurization, upgrading | Large (40+ Mt/year) |
| Steelmaking | Direct reduced iron | Growing (pilot to commercial) |
| Methanol/synfuels | E-fuels for aviation, shipping | Emerging (large potential) |
| Heavy transport | Fuel cell trucks, buses, trains | Growing |
| Power generation | Turbine co-firing, energy storage | Emerging |
