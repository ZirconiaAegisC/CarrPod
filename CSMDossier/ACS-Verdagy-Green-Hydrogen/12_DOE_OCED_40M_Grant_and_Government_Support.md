# Verdagy — DOE OCED $40M Grant & Government Support

## The Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations

The U.S. Department of Energy's **Office of Clean Energy Demonstrations** was
established under the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law of 2021 with a mandate to
fund large-scale demonstrations of clean energy technologies that are technically
proven but not yet commercially deployed at scale. The OCED's hydrogen portfolio
includes multiple electrolyzer manufacturers, hydrogen hubs, and end-use
demonstration projects designed to accelerate the transition from pilot to
commercial operation.

## Verdagy's $40M Grant Structure

Verdagy's $40 million OCED grant is structured as a cost-share agreement, with
the federal funds covering a portion of the total project cost for the company's
first commercial-scale manufacturing facility and deployment. Key elements of
the grant structure:

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Total grant value | $40 million |
| Cost-share ratio | Typically 50:50 (federal:private) |
| Performance period | Multi-year, tied to project milestones |
| Disbursement mechanism | Reimbursement against incurred costs |
| Reporting requirements | Quarterly technical and financial reports |
| Milestone structure | Engineering completion, construction, commissioning, operations |

## Strategic Significance of DOE Support

Federal support provides several benefits beyond the raw dollar amount:

1. **Validation signal** — DOE selection through competitive review signals
   technology credibility to private investors and commercial customers

2. **Non-dilutive capital** — reduces the equity financing required to reach
   commercial scale, preserving shareholder value

3. **Technical resources** — access to DOE's national laboratory network
   (NREL, INL, PNNL) for independent performance validation and technical
   collaboration

4. **Policy alignment** — positions the company favorably for future federal
   procurement and additional grant programs

## The Bipartisan Infrastructure Law Hydrogen Framework

The BIL allocated $9.5 billion for clean hydrogen initiatives, including:

- **$8 billion for Regional Clean Hydrogen Hubs** (H2Hubs) — at least four
  hubs demonstrating integrated hydrogen production, processing, delivery,
  storage, and end-use
- **$1 billion for Clean Hydrogen Electrolysis Program** — cost reduction for
  electrolyzers
- **$500 million for Clean Hydrogen Manufacturing and Recycling** — domestic
  manufacturing and supply chain development

Verdagy's OCED grant falls under the broader electrolysis cost reduction and
manufacturing objectives of this framework.

## Inflation Reduction Act Provisions

The IRA provides additional support mechanisms that improve the economics of
Verdagy's technology and its customers' projects:

- **45V Clean Hydrogen Production Tax Credit** — up to $3.00/kg for hydrogen
  with lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions below 0.45 kg CO₂e per kg H₂, phased
  down for higher emissions tiers
- **48C Advanced Energy Project Credit** — up to 30% investment tax credit for
  manufacturing facilities producing clean energy equipment, including electrolyzers
- **45X Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit** — production tax credit for
  domestically manufactured clean energy components

## Compliance & Reporting

As a recipient of federal funds, Verdagy must comply with:

- **Davis-Bacon Act** — prevailing wage requirements for construction
- **Build America, Buy America** — domestic content requirements for steel,
  iron, and manufactured products
- **National Environmental Policy Act** — environmental review of funded projects
- **2 CFR Part 200** — uniform administrative requirements for federal awards

These compliance obligations impose administrative overhead but also create
competitive barriers that favor established, well-capitalized developers over
less-resourced competitors.
