# Verdagy — eDynamic Advanced Alkaline Electrolysis Technology

## Technology Overview

Verdagy's core technology platform, branded **eDynamic**, represents an evolutionary leap
in alkaline water electrolysis. Unlike proton exchange membrane electrolysis, which relies
on acidic conditions, precious metal catalysts, and fluorinated polymer membranes, alkaline
electrolysis operates in a basic (high-pH) aqueous environment that permits the use of
abundant, low-cost materials throughout the cell stack.

## Electrochemical Fundamentals

In alkaline water electrolysis, an electric current is passed through an aqueous potassium
hydroxide electrolyte solution between two electrodes separated by a porous diaphragm:

- **Cathode (reduction):** 2H₂O + 2e⁻ → H₂ + 2OH⁻
- **Anode (oxidation):** 2OH⁻ → ½O₂ + H₂O + 2e⁻
- **Overall:** H₂O → H₂ + ½O₂

The diaphragm prevents mixing of the hydrogen and oxygen gas products while allowing
hydroxide ion transport to maintain charge balance. Verdagy's innovation lies not in
the fundamental chemistry — which has been understood since the 19th century — but in
the engineering of cells, stacks, and modules that achieve unprecedented current
densities and active areas at competitive capital costs.

## The 3 m² Cell

The single most distinctive feature of the eDynamic platform is its **3 square meter**
cell active area — the largest in the commercial electrolyzer industry. Larger cells
reduce the number of individual cell frames, seals, and interconnects required per unit
of hydrogen production capacity, directly lowering both material costs and assembly
labor. This architectural decision reflects Verdagy's manufacturing-first design
philosophy: every dimension was chosen to minimize total system cost rather than to
maximize headline efficiency metrics.

## Current Density Breakthrough

Operating at current densities exceeding **100 milliamps per square centimeter**
represents a fundamental advance over conventional alkaline electrolyzers, which
typically operate in the 20-40 mA/cm² range. Higher current density means more hydrogen
production per unit of cell area, which translates directly into lower capital cost
per kilogram of hydrogen produced. Achieving this while maintaining acceptable
efficiency and degradation rates requires sophisticated electrode design, optimized
electrolyte flow distribution, and precise control of gas bubble management — areas
where Verdagy's team has accumulated significant proprietary expertise.

## Balance-of-Plant Integration

The eDynamic system is designed as a fully integrated module that includes not just
the electrolyzer stacks but also the power conditioning, electrolyte management, gas
separation, and thermal control subsystems. This turnkey approach simplifies site
installation, reduces engineering complexity for project developers, and enables
Verdagy to guarantee system-level performance rather than merely stack-level
specifications — a critical differentiator in an industry where integration risk
has historically been a barrier to deployment.
