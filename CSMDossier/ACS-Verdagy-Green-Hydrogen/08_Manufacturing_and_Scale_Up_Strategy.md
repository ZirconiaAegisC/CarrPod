# Verdagy — Manufacturing & Scale-Up Strategy

## Manufacturing Philosophy

Verdagy's approach to manufacturing is rooted in the conviction that electrolyzer
production must transition from a project-based, semi-custom model — characteristic
of the chlor-alkali industry from which alkaline electrolysis descends — to a
highly automated, continuous-flow manufacturing model inspired by automotive and
semiconductor capital equipment industries. This transition is essential to achieving
the order-of-magnitude cost reductions required for green hydrogen to compete with
fossil-derived gray hydrogen.

## Pilot Manufacturing Line

The Moss Landing facility houses Verdagy's pilot manufacturing line, which serves
multiple functions:

- **Process validation** — demonstrating that the cell and stack designs can be
  produced consistently at commercial quality levels
- **Cost modeling** — generating empirical data on labor, material, and overhead
  costs to validate the company's cost reduction roadmap
- **Supply chain qualification** — evaluating and qualifying multiple suppliers for
  each bill-of-materials component to ensure competitive pricing and supply security
- **Customer demonstration** — providing prospective customers with visibility into
  manufacturing quality and capacity

## Automation Strategy

Key manufacturing steps targeted for automation include:

| Process | Automation Approach |
|---------|-------------------|
| Electrode coating | Robotic thermal spray or electrodeposition lines |
| Diaphragm fabrication | Continuous roll-to-roll processing |
| Cell stacking | Automated pick-and-place with vision-guided alignment |
| Welding & sealing | Robotic laser welding stations |
| Quality inspection | In-line machine vision and automated electrical testing |
| Module assembly | Guided vehicle systems on assembly floor |

## Supply Chain Development

Verdagy is actively developing a supply chain that can support its target of 10 GW
of annual manufacturing capacity. Critical supply chain elements include:

- **Nickel-based electrodes** — partnerships with industrial electroplating and
  thermal spray companies capable of scaling to high-volume production
- **Stainless steel forming** — qualification of multiple metal fabricators with
  large-format press and laser cutting capability
- **Polymer diaphragm** — collaboration with specialty film manufacturers to develop
  roll-to-roll production of the proprietary separator material
- **Power electronics** — partnerships with established industrial rectifier
  manufacturers for the high-current DC power supplies

## Factory Design Principles

The next-generation factory — designed for the 10 GW target — incorporates several
key principles:

1. **Linear flow** — materials enter one end of the factory, finished modules exit
   the other, with minimal backtracking or cross-traffic
2. **Cellular manufacturing** — self-contained work cells for each major subassembly,
   enabling parallel production and fault isolation
3. **Digital twin** — a virtual replica of the factory enables simulation of
   production scenarios, bottleneck identification, and layout optimization before
   physical construction
4. **Quality at source** — inspection integrated into each manufacturing step rather
   than concentrated at end-of-line, reducing rework and scrap

## Workforce Development

Manufacturing at gigawatt scale will require a workforce of hundreds to thousands of
technicians, engineers, and operators. Verdagy's location strategy — including the
Texas deployment site — considers access to skilled manufacturing labor, proximity to
technical training institutions, and the availability of industrial infrastructure
as primary site selection criteria.
