# Verdagy — 3 m² Cell Architecture & Performance

## The Significance of Cell Size

In electrolyzer manufacturing, cell active area is a primary determinant of system
capital cost. Each cell requires its own frame, seals, electrodes, diaphragm, and
bipolar plate — components whose cost scales with cell count rather than total area.
By maximizing the active area per cell, Verdagy minimizes the number of individual
components required to achieve a given production capacity. The company's 3 square
meter cell is the largest single cell active area commercially available in the
electrolyzer industry, exceeding the typical 1-2 m² cells offered by incumbent
alkaline electrolyzer manufacturers.

## Cell Architecture

The Verdagy cell architecture comprises the following layers, arranged in a repeating
stack configuration:

1. **Bipolar plate** — separates adjacent cells, conducts electricity, and distributes
   electrolyte flow. Fabricated from stainless steel with proprietary surface treatments
2. **Cathode electrode** — high-surface-area nickel-based catalyst on a conductive
   substrate, optimized for hydrogen evolution reaction kinetics
3. **Diaphragm (separator)** — porous, non-PFAS polymer membrane that allows hydroxide
   ion transport while preventing gas mixing
4. **Anode electrode** — nickel or nickel-alloy catalyst optimized for oxygen evolution
   reaction in alkaline conditions
5. **Cell frame** — structural component that houses the active layers, manages
   electrolyte distribution, and collects gas products

## Fluid Distribution Engineering

Distributing electrolyte uniformly across a 3 m² cell area without creating dead zones
or excessive pressure drop is a non-trivial engineering challenge. Verdagy has developed
proprietary flow field designs — informed by extensive computational fluid dynamics
simulation — that ensure consistent electrolyte delivery to every portion of the active
area. Uniform distribution is essential for maintaining even current density, preventing
localized hot spots, and ensuring consistent gas product removal that would otherwise
shadow electrode surfaces and reduce effective area.

## Thermal Management

Electrolysis is exothermic: the overpotential losses described in the preceding chapter
manifest as heat that must be removed to maintain optimal operating temperature. In a
3 m² cell operating at >100 mA/cm², the absolute heat generation is substantial.
Verdagy's thermal management approach integrates active cooling channels within the
bipolar plate design, using the circulating electrolyte as the primary heat transfer
medium. This minimizes the number of additional components while maintaining tight
temperature control across the entire cell surface.

## Manufacturing Implications

The large cell format creates both opportunities and challenges for manufacturing.
On one hand, fewer cells per megawatt means fewer assembly steps, fewer quality
control points, and less material handling. On the other, handling 3 m² components
requires specialized automation equipment not commonly available in the electrolyzer
supply chain. Verdagy has invested in custom manufacturing systems — including robotic
electrode coating lines, automated cell stacking stations, and in-line quality
inspection — that are designed specifically for the large-format cell architecture.
