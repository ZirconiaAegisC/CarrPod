# Verdagy — 100MW Texas Commercial Deployment

## Project Overview

Verdagy's first commercial-scale deployment is a **100MW+ green hydrogen production
facility** located in Texas. This project represents the company's transition from
pilot-scale demonstration to commercial operation and serves as the reference plant
for subsequent customer deployments. The project is structured as five 20MW eDynamic
modules operating in parallel, with shared balance-of-plant infrastructure.

## Texas as a Strategic Location

Texas offers a uniquely favorable environment for green hydrogen production:

- **Abundant renewable electricity** — Texas leads the United States in wind power
  generation, with over 40 GW of installed wind capacity and some of the lowest
  wholesale electricity prices in the country, particularly in the ERCOT West zone
  where nighttime wind power frequently trades at or near zero

- **Existing hydrogen infrastructure** — the Texas Gulf Coast is home to the world's
  largest concentration of hydrogen pipelines, storage caverns, and industrial
  hydrogen consumers (refineries, ammonia plants, petrochemical facilities)

- **Industrial offtake** — the Gulf Coast industrial corridor consumes millions of
  tonnes of hydrogen annually, providing immediate offtake opportunities without
  requiring new distribution infrastructure

- **Business environment** — Texas's regulatory framework, workforce availability, and
  industrial ecosystem are well suited to large-scale energy infrastructure projects

## Facility Layout

The facility integrates the following major subsystems:

1. **Electrolyzer field** — five 20MW eDynamic modules, each in a standardized
   containerized configuration, arranged for efficient power distribution and
   hydrogen collection

2. **Power substation** — medium-voltage connection to the ERCOT grid with
   transformers sized for full plant capacity, plus interconnection to the
   dedicated renewable generation source

3. **Water treatment** — reverse osmosis system producing deionized water for
   the electrolysis process, with water recycling to minimize consumption

4. **Hydrogen processing** — compression, drying, and optional purification to
   meet pipeline or industrial purity specifications

5. **Hydrogen storage** — above-ground pressurized storage sufficient to buffer
   between production rate and offtake schedule

6. **Control center** — centralized monitoring and control of all plant systems

## DOE OCED Grant Integration

The $40 million grant from the Department of Energy's Office of Clean Energy
Demonstrations provides non-dilutive capital that reduces project risk and improves
project economics. The grant is structured with milestones tied to engineering
completion, construction progress, and operational performance metrics, creating
alignment between Verdagy's commercial objectives and the DOE's mission of
demonstrating clean hydrogen technologies at commercially relevant scale.

## Risk Management

Key project risks and mitigation strategies include:

- **Technology performance risk** — mitigated by factory acceptance testing and
  phased module commissioning that validates performance before full investment
- **Renewable power price risk** — managed through long-term power purchase
  agreements and geographic diversification of renewable sources
- **Offtake risk** — addressed through long-term hydrogen purchase agreements
  with industrial consumers, potentially including minimum take-or-pay provisions
- **Construction risk** — managed through fixed-price engineering, procurement,
  and construction contracts with established industrial contractors
