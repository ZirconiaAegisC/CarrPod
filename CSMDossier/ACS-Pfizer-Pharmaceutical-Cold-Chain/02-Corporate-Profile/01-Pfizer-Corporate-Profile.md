
# CORPORATE PROFILE: PFIZER INC.

## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

**Legal Name:** Pfizer Inc.
**Headquarters:** New York, NY (HQ); major manufacturing in Kalamazoo, MI; Puurs, Belgium; McPherson, KS; Rocky Mount, NC; Andover, MA; Groton, CT; Sandwich, UK; Singapore; China
**Ticker:** PFE (NYSE)
**Revenue (2025):** ~$55B
**Employees:** ~88,000

## 2. EXECUTIVE LEADERSHIP

**Dr. Albert Bourla, DVM, Ph.D. — Chairman and CEO.** Appointed CEO 2019. Led Pfizer through the COVID-19 pandemic and the development/manufacturing of the mRNA vaccine (Comirnaty) in partnership with BioNTech. Doctor of veterinary medicine with Ph.D. in biotechnology. Publicly committed to pharmaceutical access and supply chain resilience.

**David Denton — EVP and CFO.** Previously CFO of Lowe's and CVS Health. Deep understanding of retail pharmacy supply chains and cold chain logistics.

**Mike McDermott — EVP, Chief Global Supply Officer.** 30+ year Pfizer veteran. Oversees 35+ manufacturing sites and global distribution. Primary operational stakeholder for facility hardening.

**Lidia Fonseca — EVP, Chief Digital and Technology Officer.** Oversees Pfizer's digital infrastructure including manufacturing IT/OT systems, cold chain monitoring, and quality systems.

## 3. MANUFACTURING FOOTPRINT

- **Kalamazoo, MI:** Largest Pfizer sterile injectables facility. Products include Prevnar 13, Ibrance, Xeljanz. ~5,000 employees.
- **Puurs, Belgium:** mRNA vaccine manufacturing. High containment facility. ~4,000 employees.
- **McPherson, KS:** Sterile injectables and hospital products.
- **Rocky Mount, NC:** Sterile injectables. Damaged by tornado in 2023 — providing recent case study in manufacturing interruption.
- **Andover, MA:** Biologics and vaccine development/manufacturing.
- **Groton, CT:** R&D center with pilot manufacturing.
- **Sandwich, UK:** Former R&D center (now Discovery Park).

## 4. COLD CHAIN REQUIREMENTS

| Product Category | Storage Temperature | Stability Window Without Power |
|-----------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| mRNA vaccines (original) | -70°C | 12-24 hours at ambient |
| mRNA vaccines (current) | -20°C | 24-48 hours |
| Most vaccines | 2-8°C | 4-12 hours |
| Biologics (monoclonal antibodies) | 2-8°C | 2-8 hours |
| Lyophilized products | 2-25°C | 24-72 hours |

**Williams Heuristic:** "When the electricity goes out at a Pfizer cold storage warehouse, the clock starts. Within hours, hundreds of millions of dollars of life-saving medicine begins to degrade. Within 72 hours, it's all unrecoverable. Our job is to stop the clock."
