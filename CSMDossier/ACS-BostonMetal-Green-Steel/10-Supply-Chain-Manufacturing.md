# Boston Metal — Supply Chain and Manufacturing Strategy

## Overview

The transition from pilot-scale operations in Woburn, Massachusetts to commercial-scale deployment requires Boston Metal to establish robust supply chains for critical materials and to develop manufacturing capabilities for proprietary components, particularly the inert anodes that are central to MOE cell performance.

## Anode Manufacturing

### Material Sourcing

The inert anode is Boston Metal's most critical proprietary component. While the exact composition is a trade secret, published literature and patent filings indicate the anode is based on chromium-iron alloys with specific alloying additions that form protective oxide surface layers during high-temperature operation in the molten oxide electrolyte.

Key raw material requirements include:
- **Chromium metal or ferrochrome**: High-purity chromium is the base metal for the anode alloy. Global chromium production is dominated by South Africa and Kazakhstan, with Turkey, India, and Finland also contributing. Chromium supply chains are well-established for the stainless steel and superalloy industries.
- **Alloying elements**: Specific additions (proprietary) to improve oxidation resistance, electrical conductivity, and mechanical stability at operating temperature.
- **Ceramic precursors**: For protective coating applications on anode surfaces.

### Manufacturing Process

Anode manufacturing involves powder metallurgy and sintering processes:
1. **Powder preparation**: Metal powders are mixed, milled, and blended to achieve target composition and particle size distribution.
2. **Compaction**: Powder mixtures are compacted into anode preforms using cold isostatic pressing or uniaxial pressing.
3. **Sintering**: Compacted preforms are sintered at high temperature in controlled atmospheres to achieve the desired density, grain structure, and mechanical properties.
4. **Machining**: Sintered anodes are machined to final dimensions and surface finish.
5. **Quality control**: Each anode undergoes dimensional inspection, density measurement, electrical conductivity testing, and non-destructive evaluation before deployment.

### Manufacturing Scale-Up

For the Brazil demonstration plant's Phase 2 (6–10 cells), approximately 30–60 anodes will be required, depending on cell configuration. For a full-scale commercial plant producing 2 million tonnes of iron per year (approximately 250–400 cells), annual anode production of 750–1,200 units would be required.

Boston Metal faces a build-versus-buy decision for anode manufacturing:
- **In-house manufacturing**: Provides the greatest IP protection and quality control but requires capital investment, specialized facilities, and operational expertise outside Boston Metal's core competency
- **Contract manufacturing**: Leverages existing industrial capacity in powder metallurgy and advanced ceramics but requires disclosure of proprietary manufacturing specifications and creates dependency on external suppliers
- **Hybrid approach**: In-house manufacturing of critical sub-components with contract manufacturing for standardized elements — the most likely pathway based on industry precedents in aluminum smelting anode production

## Electrolyte Supply Chain

The electrolyte system is based on abundant industrial minerals:
- **Iron oxide**: Sourced from iron ore deposits — effectively unlimited and geographically diverse
- **Silica (SiO₂)**: Industrial-grade silica sand, globally abundant and low-cost
- **Lime (CaO)**: Produced from limestone, available globally
- **Magnesia (MgO)**: Produced from magnesite or seawater extraction, established global supply
- **Alumina (Al₂O₃)**: The same material used in aluminum smelting, globally traded commodity

None of these materials faces significant supply constraints, and all benefit from established global logistics infrastructure.

## Cell Fabrication

MOE cells are large, high-temperature vessels requiring specialized fabrication:
- **Steel shell**: Fabricated from structural steel plate; well within the capabilities of established heavy fabrication shops
- **Refractory lining**: High-temperature insulation and refractory materials (similar to those used in blast furnaces and aluminum reduction cells)
- **Electrode assemblies**: Custom-fabricated components requiring precision machining and assembly
- **Electrical systems**: High-current DC power supplies (rectifier transformers), busbar systems, and instrumentation; similar to equipment used in aluminum smelting and electric arc furnaces

Cell fabrication is expected to be performed by established heavy equipment manufacturers under contract, with Boston Metal providing design specifications, proprietary components (anodes), and quality oversight.

## Power Supply Infrastructure

Each commercial MOE cell requires a DC power supply delivering 50–100 kA at voltages in the range of 1.5–3.0 V. For a multi-cell plant:
- **Rectifier transformers**: Step down from grid voltage and convert AC to DC
- **Busbar systems**: Aluminum or copper conductors distributing current to individual cells
- **Power conditioning**: Harmonic filtering and power factor correction to meet grid interconnection requirements
- **Backup power**: Minimum requirements (cells can be idled during grid interruptions, unlike blast furnaces which must be kept hot)

The power supply system is similar to that used in aluminum smelters and chlor-alkali plants, and benefits from established equipment suppliers and engineering practices.

## Logistics and Infrastructure

### Inbound Logistics
- Iron ore delivery by rail or barge (dependent on site location)
- Electrolyte material delivery by truck
- Anode delivery from manufacturing facility

### Outbound Logistics
- Liquid iron transfer to downstream steelmaking (if co-located with a steel mill)
- Pig casting for sale as merchant pig iron
- Oxygen collection and distribution (if merchant oxygen market exists nearby)

### Site Infrastructure Requirements
- Electrical substation with connection to high-voltage transmission grid
- Water supply for cooling systems
- Compressed air and inert gas systems
- Laboratory and quality control facilities
- Maintenance workshop

## Workforce Requirements

A commercial MOE plant requires a workforce with skills spanning:
- High-temperature electrochemistry
- Industrial electrical systems and power electronics
- Process control and automation
- Metallurgy and materials testing
- Industrial maintenance (mechanical, electrical, instrumentation)
- Environmental, health, and safety management

Boston Metal has developed training programs at its Woburn facility for operators and technical staff, which will serve as the foundation for workforce development at licensed plants.
