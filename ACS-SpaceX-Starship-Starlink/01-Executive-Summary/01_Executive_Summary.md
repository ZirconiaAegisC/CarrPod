## 1.1 Purpose and Scope

This dossier presents a unified assessment of Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (SpaceX) as of mid-2026, with a specific focus on the interplay between the Starship launch platform, the Starlink satellite internet constellation, and the five operational frameworks driving the enterprise. The document is structured to support strategic decision-making regarding capital allocation, partnership structuring, and competitive positioning in the post-IPO era.

The five frameworks examined are: (1) Starship and Launch Services, (2) Starlink Constellation and Global Connectivity, (3) Direct-to-Cell and Mobile Connectivity, (4) Orbital AI Compute and Terafab Fabrication, and (5) Human Spaceflight and Government Programs.

## 1.2 Key Findings

### 1.2.1 Financial Position

SpaceX generated **$18.7 billion in total revenue during FY2025**, representing a 33% year-over-year increase. Adjusted EBITDA reached **$6.6 billion** (35.3% margin), though the enterprise recorded a **net loss of $4.9 billion** attributable to sustained Starship research and development expenditures and investments in the xAI compute initiative. **Q1 2026 revenue** was $4.7 billion, indicating a continued growth trajectory at an annualized run rate exceeding $19 billion.

On June 15, 2026, SpaceX completed the largest IPO in market history, raising **$85.7 billion** at a **$2.3 trillion valuation**, with shares priced at **$150**. The enterprise carried **$29.1 billion in debt** against **$15.9 billion in cash**, with **$13.2 billion in deferred revenue** — predominantly Starlink prepaid subscriptions.

### 1.2.2 Starlink Dominance

Starlink contributed **$11.4 billion in FY2025 revenue (61% of total)** with **$4.4 billion in operating profit** (38.6% margin). The subscriber base doubled year-over-year to **10.3 million**, with ARPU holding at **$66/month**. As of February 2026, Starlink surpassed **10 million active subscribers** across **164 countries**. The constellation reached **10,747 operational satellites** in orbit out of **12,496 launched**, and the service represents the largest satellite internet provider by both subscriber count and orbital assets.

### 1.2.3 Starship Development and Milestones

The Starship program, with total cumulative costs exceeding **$15 billion** and **$3 billion in R&D during FY2025 alone** ($930M in Q1 2026), has completed **12 integrated test flights**. The V3 variant is scheduled for the **first orbital payload deployment in H2 2026**, specifically targeting Starlink satellite delivery. Starship is designed for **100 metric tons to LEO in fully reusable configuration**. **Flight 13**, set for July 16, 2026 — one day after this dossier date — marks the inaugural live Starlink deployment from a Starship vehicle, a pivotal operational milestone.

### 1.2.4 Falcon Fleet: Sustained Operational Velocity

The Falcon 9 achieved its **sixth consecutive annual launch record** with **165 launches in FY2025**. The program surpassed **500 total Falcon 9 flights**, demonstrated **29 booster reuses on a single airframe**, and crossed the **600th flight-proven booster** threshold. Falcon reusability has generated cumulative savings in the billions and underwrites the capital-intensive transition to Starship. Crew Dragon and Cargo Dragon continue ISS service rotations under the NASA Commercial Crew and CRS-2 contracts.

### 1.2.5 Direct-to-Cell and Next-Generation Starlink

Starlink Direct-to-Cell reached **7.4 million monthly unique devices** in early 2026, extending connectivity to unmodified LTE handsets without terrestrial infrastructure. V3 Starlink satellites deliver **1 Tbps throughput per spacecraft**, with **60 satellites deployable per Starship launch** — a step-change in orbital deployment economics. The V2 Mobile roadmap targets **5G-quality broadband** from space by 2027.

### 1.2.6 Orbital AI Compute and Terafab

SpaceX, in coordination with Tesla, has disclosed plans for the **Terafab chip fabrication facility** at a projected cost of **$119 billion**, designed to produce processors for a planned constellation of **over 1 million orbital AI compute satellites**. The AI segment generated **$3.2 billion in FY2025 revenue**, reflecting early commercial demand for space-based inference capacity.

### 1.2.7 Government and Defense Programs

The **Golden Dome** initiative, a $2 billion Pentagon contract, encompasses 600 satellites for missile warning and defense architecture. **NASA's Artemis Human Landing System (HLS)** contract, valued at approximately $4 billion, positions Starship as the lunar lander for the Artemis III and subsequent missions. These programs anchor the government revenue pillar and provide long-duration, cost-plus-adjacent stability.
