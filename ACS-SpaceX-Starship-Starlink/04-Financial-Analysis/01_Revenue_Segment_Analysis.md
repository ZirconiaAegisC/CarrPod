### 8.2.4 Key Drivers and Sensitivity

- **Subscriber growth:** Each 1M additional subscribers adds ~$790M annualized revenue at current ARPU
- **ARPU stabilization:** If ARPU declines to $55 on average, subscriber base must exceed 25M for Starlink to reach $16.5B segment revenue (FY2028 target)
- **V3 capacity deployment:** Each Starship launch carrying 60 V3 satellites (60 Tbps aggregate) is equivalent to ~800,000 incremental subscriber capacity at current average bandwidth consumption
- **Hardware cost reduction:** Terminal manufacturing cost approaching $400 creates the inflection point for mass adoption in price-sensitive markets

Falcon 9 achieved 165 launches in FY2025 (including 16 non-revenue test/demo flights not included in revenue table). At this cadence, SpaceX launches at a rate of approximately one mission every 2.2 days, with pad turnaround times as low as 3.6 days at SLC-40.

### 8.4.2 Growth Trajectory

The AI segment is in an early, high-growth phase. Revenue increased from $0.7B in FY2023 to $3.2B in FY2025 — a 113% CAGR. The segment benefits from structural tailwinds: global GPU supply constraints, escalating energy costs for terrestrial data centers, and increasing demand for sovereign AI infrastructure (nations seeking compute capabilities independent of U.S. cloud hyperscalers).

Q1 2026 annualized revenue of $4.0B suggests continued momentum, though the segment remains unprofitable on a standalone basis due to heavy R&D investment in Terafab and orbital compute node development.

Starlink is profitable; Starship and AI Compute are investment-phase loss centers.
