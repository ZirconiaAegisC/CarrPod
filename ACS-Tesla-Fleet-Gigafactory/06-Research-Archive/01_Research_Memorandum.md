## 3. Analytical Methodology

### 3.1 Vehicle Production & Delivery Modeling

**Method:** Production volumes by factory are derived from publicly disclosed total delivery figures, Tesla's occasional disclosure of production milestones by factory (e.g., "Gigafactory Shanghai has reached an annualized production rate of X units"), and third-party tracking of vehicle identification number (VIN) registration data, export/import customs data, and factory drone overflight imagery analysis.

**Assumptions:**
- Quarterly production totals as disclosed by Tesla are accepted as accurate.
- Model mix (Model S/X vs. Model 3/Y vs. Cybertruck) is estimated from disclosed total delivery figures combined with third-party registration data in major markets. Tesla does not disclose delivery figures by individual model, introducing estimation uncertainty.
- Factory capacity utilization is estimated from disclosed production milestones and third-party production tracking, with the acknowledgment that Tesla's disclosed "installed annual capacity" figures may reflect theoretical maximum throughput under ideal conditions rather than sustainable operating rates.

### 3.2 Revenue & Financial Modeling

**Method:** Revenue by segment is sourced directly from Tesla's quarterly and annual financial disclosures. Where segment-level detail is insufficient for the analytical requirements of this dossier (e.g., automotive revenue disaggregation by model; energy revenue disaggregation by product), estimates are constructed using assumptions clearly documented in the relevant analysis sections.

**Assumptions:**
- Vehicle average selling price (ASP) is estimated from automotive revenue (excluding regulatory credits and leasing) divided by deliveries, adjusted for estimated leasing mix (which spreads revenue recognition over the lease term rather than recognizing the full vehicle price at delivery).
- Energy storage revenue per MWh is estimated from energy segment revenue divided by estimated storage deployments (in GWh), with the acknowledgment that solar revenue is included in energy segment revenue and is not separately disclosed in sufficient detail for precise extraction.
- Margin estimates by segment (beyond the consolidated automotive and energy gross margins occasionally disclosed or inferred from management commentary) are constructed from a combination of disclosed data, third-party teardown cost estimates, and assumptions about fixed cost allocation. These estimates carry greater uncertainty than the top-line revenue data.

### 3.3 Competitive Analysis Methodology

**Method:** Competitive positioning assessments are based on: (a) publicly disclosed production, delivery, and financial data from competing manufacturers; (b) third-party industry analyses (BloombergNEF, IEA, EV Volumes); (c) technical teardown and benchmarking data from firms such as Munro & Associates; (d) patent filings and technical publications; and (e) qualitative assessment of manufacturing capability, supply chain integration, and technology platform maturity based on observable indicators (factory construction progress, hiring patterns, supplier relationships).

**Limitations:** Competitive assessments are inherently limited by the asymmetry of information — Tesla's operations are observable in greater detail than those of some competitors (particularly privately held companies and those operating primarily in China, where financial and operational disclosures are less comprehensive than those required of U.S.-listed companies). Competitive assessments should be revisited as new information becomes available.

### 3.4 Forward-Looking Projections

**Method:** Forward-looking projections (revenue growth, margin trajectory, production volume, product launch timing) are constructed from: (a) extrapolation of historical trends with explicit assumptions about continuation, acceleration, or deceleration; (b) Tesla management's publicly stated targets and timelines, discounted by an assessment of historical accuracy (Tesla has a well-documented pattern of aspirational timelines that are achieved later than initially stated); (c) industry-level forecasts from BloombergNEF, IEA, and other authoritative sources, adjusted for Tesla-specific factors.

**Uncertainty Acknowledgment:** All forward-looking projections are subject to material uncertainty. Key sources of projection uncertainty include: the trajectory of global EV demand, which is influenced by macroeconomic conditions, government policy (subsidies, mandates, tariffs), and competitive dynamics; the pace of technological progress in autonomous driving and humanoid robotics, which is unpredictable on multi-year timescales; and Tesla's own execution capability, which has historically been variable — exceptionally fast in some domains (factory construction, production ramp) and slower than projected in others (FSD capability, 4680 cell manufacturing yield).

## 5. Data Limitations & Caveats

1. **Tesla disclosure limitations:** Tesla provides less granular segment reporting than many industrial peers. Model-level delivery data, geographic revenue breakdown, energy segment product-level revenue, and segment-level gross margins are not disclosed in standard financial reporting. The analytical conclusions in this dossier are therefore subject to estimation uncertainty in areas where disclosure is insufficient.

2. **Rapidly evolving factual landscape:** Tesla's operational metrics, product development status, and competitive environment evolve on a quarterly (and sometimes monthly) basis. Data and analysis presented in this dossier reflect information available as of July 2026 and should be updated as new information (particularly quarterly earnings releases, regulatory filings, and material corporate announcements) becomes available.

3. **Competitive intelligence completeness:** Analysis of competitor capabilities, particularly for privately held companies and China-based competitors, is based on publicly available information that may be incomplete. Competitor product development and manufacturing capability may be more advanced than publicly observable indicators suggest.

4. **Forward-looking statement risk:** Projections, estimates, and forward-looking statements contained in this dossier are inherently uncertain. Actual outcomes may differ materially from those projected due to changes in market conditions, competitive dynamics, technology development timelines, regulatory environments, and macroeconomic factors.
