# 07.2 — Glossary of Terms & Acronyms

## ACS-Tesla-Fleet-Gigafactory Strategic Dossier

**Section:** Appendices — Glossary
**Classification:** Confidential
**Date:** July 2026

---

## A

**ADAS** — Advanced Driver-Assistance System. Vehicle safety systems that assist the driver through warnings, automated braking, lane-keeping, or adaptive cruise control. FSD is an advanced ADAS system under the supervised deployment classification.

**AFIR** — Alternative Fuels Infrastructure Regulation. European Union regulation (EU 2023/1804) mandating minimum density and capability requirements for publicly accessible electric vehicle charging infrastructure along the Trans-European Transport Network (TEN-T).

**AI4 / Hardware 4** — Fourth-generation Full Self-Driving computer designed by Tesla. Provides approximately 3–5x the neural network inference compute of the prior HW3 generation, with upgraded camera sensor interfaces and power efficiency.

**AI5** — Fifth-generation FSD computer currently under development at Tesla. Expected to provide a further step-change increase in inference compute to support unsupervised autonomous driving and potentially serve as the compute platform for Optimus robots.

**ARP** — See Average Selling Price (ASP).

**ASC 606** — Accounting Standards Codification Topic 606: Revenue from Contracts with Customers. The U.S. GAAP standard governing revenue recognition, relevant to Tesla's accounting for FSD deferred revenue.

**ASP** — Average Selling Price. The average transaction price at which vehicles are sold, calculated as automotive sales revenue (excluding regulatory credits and leasing) divided by the number of vehicles delivered in a period.

**Autobidder** — Tesla's proprietary algorithmic energy trading and asset optimization platform. Autonomously bids battery energy storage assets into wholesale electricity markets to maximize revenue.

**Autopilot** — Tesla's standard driver-assistance system included with all new vehicle purchases. Provides traffic-aware cruise control and lane-centering. Distinct from Full Self-Driving (FSD), which is an optional upgrade.

## B

**BEV** — Battery Electric Vehicle. A vehicle propelled solely by one or more electric motors using energy stored in rechargeable battery packs, with no internal combustion engine.

**BESS** — Battery Energy Storage System. A system that stores electrical energy in batteries for later use, typically deployed at utility scale (Megapack) or residential scale (Powerwall).

**BMS** — Battery Management System. The electronic system that monitors and controls battery pack state (state of charge, temperature, cell voltage balance) to ensure safe and efficient operation.

**Body-in-White (BIW)** — The welded or joined metal structure of a vehicle body before the installation of closures (doors, hood, trunk lid), paint, trim, or running gear.

## C

**Capex** — Capital Expenditure. Investment in property, plant, equipment, and other long-lived assets. Tesla's capex primarily funds gigafactory construction, production line equipment, Supercharger station deployment, and AI training infrastructure.

**CARB** — California Air Resources Board. The California state agency responsible for air quality and vehicle emissions regulation, including the Zero-Emission Vehicle (ZEV) credit program.

**CCS (Combined Charging System)** — A standardized DC fast-charging connector used by most non-Tesla electric vehicles in North America (CCS1) and Europe (CCS2).

**Cortex 2** — Tesla's current-generation in-house AI training supercomputer, deployed within a dedicated facility. Provides the computational infrastructure for FSD neural network training and Optimus robot policy learning. Successor to the original Dojo deployment, Cortex 1.

**Cybercab** — Tesla's purpose-built dedicated robotaxi vehicle. Designed without steering wheel or pedals. Utilizes unboxed assembly process. Volume production targeting H2 2026.

**Cybertruck** — Tesla's electric pickup truck featuring a stainless steel exoskeleton body. Exclusively produced at Gigafactory Texas.

## D

**DBE** — Dry Battery Electrode. A manufacturing process that eliminates the use of liquid solvents in battery electrode coating. DBE reduces cell manufacturing capital expenditure (eliminating drying ovens and solvent recovery), factory footprint, and energy consumption. The scale-up of the DBE process for 4680 cells is Tesla's primary near-term manufacturing challenge.

**Deferred Revenue** — Cash received from customers for products or features not yet delivered, recorded as a liability on the balance sheet and recognized as revenue when the promised goods or services are delivered. Tesla carries a multi-billion-dollar FSD deferred revenue balance.

**Dojo** — Tesla's in-house developed AI training supercomputer system, utilizing the custom-designed D1 training tile and proprietary network fabric. Optimized for the video-processing workloads characteristic of FSD neural network training. The current deployment is Cortex 2.

**DPO** — Days Payable Outstanding. A measure of how long a company takes to pay its suppliers, calculated as accounts payable divided by cost of goods sold per day.

**Drive Unit** — Tesla's integrated electric motor, inverter, and single-speed reduction gearbox assembly that propels the vehicle.

## E

**End-to-End Neural Network** — A machine learning architecture in which raw sensor data (camera images) is processed through a single unified neural network that directly outputs vehicle control commands (steering, acceleration, braking), without intermediate human-designed processing modules or hand-coded control rules.

**EPA** — U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. Responsible for vehicle emissions and fuel economy standards, and for certifying the rated range of electric vehicles.

**ERCOT** — Electric Reliability Council of Texas. The independent system operator that manages the flow of electric power to the majority of the Texas grid, which operates as an electrical island with limited interconnection to the broader U.S. grid.

## F

**FCF** — Free Cash Flow. Operating cash flow minus capital expenditures. Represents the cash generated by the business that is available for discretionary allocation (debt reduction, share repurchases, dividends, acquisitions, or accumulation of cash reserves).

**FSD (Full Self-Driving)** — Tesla's suite of advanced driver-assistance and autonomous driving features. Available as a one-time purchase or monthly subscription. Current version is FSD (Supervised), requiring an attentive driver ready to take over at any moment. The long-term goal is unsupervised FSD, enabling true autonomous operation without driver attention.

**FSD Computer** — Tesla's in-house-designed system-on-chip (SoC) optimized for neural network inference for autonomous driving perception, planning, and control. Successive generations include HW3, AI4 (HW4), and the in-development AI5.

## G

**Gigacasting / Giga Press** — Tesla's term for large-format high-pressure aluminum die casting machines, manufactured by IDRA Group (Italy) and other suppliers, that produce single-piece vehicle structural components (front underbody, rear underbody) replacing assemblies of dozens or hundreds of individual stamped and welded parts.

**Gigafactory** — Tesla's term for its large-scale manufacturing facilities. The five operational vehicle and battery gigafactories are located in Fremont (California), Sparks (Nevada), Buffalo (New York), Shanghai (China), and Grünheide (Germany), with a sixth in Austin (Texas). Megafactories produce Megapack stationary storage.

**GWh** — Gigawatt-hour. A unit of energy equal to one billion watt-hours. Used to measure battery production capacity (cell factories rated in GWh of annual output) and battery energy storage deployment (Megapack and Powerwall installations rated in GWh of installed capacity).

## H

**Hardware 3 (HW3)** — See AI3.

**Hardware 4 (HW4)** — See AI4.

**Hornsdale Power Reserve** — The "Tesla Big Battery" — a 150 MW / 193.5 MWh grid-scale BESS installation in South Australia, commissioned in 2017. One of Tesla's earliest and most prominent Megapack (actually Powerpack) deployments, demonstrating the technical and economic viability of grid-scale battery storage for frequency regulation and grid stabilization.

## I

**ICE** — Internal Combustion Engine. A heat engine in which the combustion of fuel occurs with an oxidizer (usually air) in a combustion chamber. Used to contrast traditional vehicles with electric vehicles.

**IRA** — Inflation Reduction Act of 2022. U.S. federal legislation that includes significant tax incentives for electric vehicle purchases (Section 30D consumer credit) and domestic battery manufacturing (Section 45X advanced manufacturing production credit). Materially benefits Tesla's U.S. manufacturing operations.

## K

**kWh** — Kilowatt-hour. A unit of energy equal to one thousand watt-hours. Used to measure vehicle battery pack capacity (e.g., Model Y Long Range: ~81 kWh) and residential energy consumption.

## L

**LCE** — Lithium Carbonate Equivalent. A standardized measure used to express lithium quantities across different lithium chemical forms (carbonate, hydroxide, spodumene concentrate) on a common basis.

**LFP** — Lithium Iron Phosphate. A lithium-ion battery cathode chemistry using iron and phosphate rather than nickel, manganese, and cobalt. LFP cells offer lower cost per kWh and superior cycle life and thermal stability, at the cost of lower energy density (kWh per kg). Used in Tesla's standard-range vehicles and increasingly in stationary storage applications.

## M

**Magic Dock** — Tesla's integrated CCS adapter built into select V4 Supercharger cabinets, enabling non-Tesla vehicles with CCS connectors to charge without requiring the driver to possess a separate adapter.

**Megacharger** — Tesla's high-power DC charging system for the Tesla Semi, delivering 1+ MW of charging power.

**Megafactory** — Tesla's dedicated factory for Megapack production. Located in Lathrop, California and Shanghai, China, with a third under construction in Houston, Texas.

**Megapack** — Tesla's utility-scale battery energy storage system (BESS). Each unit integrates battery modules, inverter, thermal management, and AC output in a single pre-commissioned enclosure. Current generation: ~3.9 MWh per unit. Megapack 3: in development, targeting >5.2 MWh per unit.

**MSM** — Model S / Model X. Tesla's premium sedan (Model S) and SUV (Model X) vehicle platforms, produced at the Fremont Factory. These vehicles represent Tesla's oldest vehicle platforms (Model S launched 2012, Model X launched 2015) and a declining share of total deliveries.

**MWh** — Megawatt-hour. A unit of energy equal to one million watt-hours. Used to measure utility-scale battery storage installations.

## N

**NACS** — North American Charging Standard. Tesla's proprietary EV charging connector design, opened to other manufacturers in 2022 and subsequently adopted by the majority of automakers serving the North American market. Standardized by SAE International as J3400.

**NCA** — Nickel Cobalt Aluminum. A lithium-ion battery cathode chemistry using nickel, cobalt, and aluminum oxides. Historically used in Tesla's long-range vehicles (though being progressively supplemented or replaced by higher-nickel NMC variants and LFP for standard range).

**NMC** — Nickel Manganese Cobalt. A lithium-ion battery cathode chemistry using nickel, manganese, and cobalt oxides. Various NMC formulations (NMC 811, NMC 955, etc.) with differing nickel content are the dominant cathode chemistry in the EV industry and are used in some Tesla vehicle configurations.

**NHTSA** — National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. The U.S. federal agency responsible for vehicle safety standards, defect investigations, and autonomous vehicle oversight.

**NUMMI** — New United Motor Manufacturing, Inc. A former joint-venture automotive assembly plant in Fremont, California, operated by General Motors and Toyota from 1984 to 2010. The facility was purchased by Tesla in 2010 and is now the Tesla Fremont Factory.

## O

**OEM** — Original Equipment Manufacturer. In the automotive context, a company that manufactures vehicles under its own brand. Used to distinguish automakers from their suppliers.

**Optimus** — Tesla's humanoid robot program. Gen 3 hardware is current as of mid-2026. Production lines under construction at Fremont (1M units/year) and Texas (10M units/year). Designed for general-purpose task execution in manufacturing, logistics, and service environments.

**OTA** — Over-the-Air. The capability to update vehicle software wirelessly through the vehicle's cellular data connection, without requiring a service center visit.

## P

**PHEV** — Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle. A vehicle with both an internal combustion engine and an electric motor with a rechargeable battery that can be charged from an external power source.

**Powerwall** — Tesla's residential battery energy storage system. Powerwall 3 is the current generation: 13.5 kWh usable capacity, 11.5 kW continuous power, integrated solar inverter.

**Production-Delivery Gap** — The difference between vehicles produced and vehicles delivered in a given period. A positive gap (production > deliveries) indicates inventory build; a negative gap (deliveries > production) indicates inventory drawdown. Tesla aims for production and deliveries to be approximately equal over time.

## R

**Robotaxi** — An autonomous vehicle providing ride-hailing transportation services without a human driver. Tesla's robotaxi strategy encompasses both the purpose-built Cybercab vehicle and the potential deployment of personally owned Teslas into a shared autonomous fleet.

**ROIC** — Return on Invested Capital. A measure of the efficiency with which a company allocates capital to profitable investments. Calculated as net operating profit after tax divided by invested capital (debt + equity).

**RPN** — Risk Priority Number. A risk assessment metric calculated as the product of likelihood and impact scores, used to prioritize risks for management attention.

## S

**Semi** — Tesla's Class 8 battery-electric semi-truck. Pilot production in Nevada; volume production targeting H2 2026 at Gigafactory Texas.

**SiC** — Silicon Carbide. A wide-bandgap semiconductor material used in power electronics (inverters, chargers) that offers higher efficiency and higher temperature tolerance than conventional silicon semiconductors. Tesla was among the first automakers to deploy SiC power modules at volume scale in the Model 3 inverter.

**Spodumene** — A lithium-bearing mineral (lithium aluminum inosilicate, LiAl(SiO3)2) that is the primary hard-rock source of lithium. Tesla's Corpus Christi lithium refinery processes spodumene concentrate into battery-grade lithium hydroxide.

**Structural Battery Pack** — Tesla's approach of bonding 4680 cells into a rigid honeycomb structure using structural adhesive and integrating the completed pack as a stressed member of the vehicle body structure, eliminating a separate battery enclosure.

**Supercharger** — Tesla's proprietary DC fast-charging network. Current generation includes V3 (250 kW per stall) and V4 (up to 350 kW, with 1,000V architecture support) cabinets.

## T

**Telematics** — The integrated use of telecommunications and informatics to collect and transmit vehicle operational data. Tesla's telematics platform enables remote diagnostics, OTA updates, fleet management, and insurance Safety Score generation.

**TOPS** — Tera-Operations Per Second. A measure of neural network inference compute performance, equal to one trillion operations per second (predominantly multiply-accumulate operations at reduced precision: INT8, FP16, or FP8).

## U

**Unboxed Assembly** — Tesla's next-generation vehicle assembly methodology in which major subassemblies are assembled in parallel work cells and then mated in a final marriage station, in contrast to the century-old linear moving assembly line. Reduces factory footprint, eliminates sequential dependencies, and improves assembly throughput.

## V

**VIN** — Vehicle Identification Number. A unique 17-character code assigned to each vehicle manufactured, encoding manufacturer, model, model year, and manufacturing plant information. Tesla VIN data is tracked by third-party analysts to estimate production volumes by factory and model.

**VPP** — Virtual Power Plant. An aggregated network of distributed energy resources (such as residential Powerwall batteries) that can be centrally dispatched to provide grid services akin to a conventional power plant. Tesla operates the world's largest VPP fleet with 89,000+ VPP events executed as of Q1 2026.

## Z

**ZEV** — Zero-Emission Vehicle. A vehicle that produces no tailpipe emissions. ZEV credits are generated by the sale of qualifying vehicles in California and CARB-adopting states and can be sold to other automakers that do not meet ZEV program requirements. Tesla is a significant seller of ZEV credits.
