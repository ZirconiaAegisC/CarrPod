## 1. Biological Receiver for the Detected Signal

V1.0's core method — ultrasonic Doppler vibrometry detecting left-ventricular-wall vibration spectral signatures associated with coronary artery disease (CAD) — is a diagnostic technique that measures the *mechanical consequence* of turbulent, disturbed coronary flow. V2.0 adds the biological receiver side: the 2025 comprehensive shear-stress mechanosensor review confirms **Piezo1** as a central mechanosensor in disturbed-flow-exposed endothelium, with oscillatory shear stress (OSS) driving Piezo1-mediated calcium influx and downstream atherosclerotic endothelial inflammation (Piezo1–Ca²⁺/CaM/CaMKII–FAK/Src–YAP axis). The turbulence V1.0's vibrometry detects acoustically is, at the cellular level, actively signaling disease progression through this pathway — reinforcing that the vibrometry signal is not merely an epiphenomenal artifact of stenosis geometry but co-located with an active pathological signaling process.

## 2. Diagnostic Trend Confirmation

The broader field of non-invasive, acoustic/imaging-based CAD diagnostics (of which ultrasonic Doppler vibrometry is one method, alongside acoustic CAD-score systems and AI-enhanced coronary CT angiography) continues to mature through 2025–2026, with AI-driven automated plaque characterization and stenosis quantification increasingly integrated into imaging workflows. This confirms V1.0's framing — that hemodynamic-function-based diagnostics offer meaningful advantages over purely anatomical "luminology" — remains the field's operative consensus.
