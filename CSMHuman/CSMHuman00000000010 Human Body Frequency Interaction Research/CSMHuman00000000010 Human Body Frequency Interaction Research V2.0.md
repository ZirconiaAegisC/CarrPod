## 1. Molecular Update: Piezo1 as the Missing Transduction Link

V1.0 anchored its analysis on the classical seated-body resonance data (PMID 9593207) at the macroscopic (whole-body mass-spring-damper) scale, noting cellular mechanotransduction only in general terms. V2.0 closes that gap with a specific, load-bearing molecular mechanism: the **Piezo1 mechanosensitive cation channel**.

- **Vibration-induced vascular signaling (Nature Communications Biology, Jan 2025):** direct vibration exposure drives Piezo1-mediated upregulation of angiogenic chemokines (CCL2, CCL5, CXCL1, CXCL2, CXCL10) in vascular smooth muscle via the NF-κB/p65 pathway — providing a specific molecular cascade for the "biodynamic strain" V1.0 described only in mechanical terms.
- **Endothelial exocytosis under repetitive load (bioRxiv, Sept 2025, Barts/Queen Mary University London):** Piezo1 opening under low-shear repetitive mechanical stimulation triggers Weibel-Palade body exocytosis and von Willebrand Factor secretion, coupling mechanical vibration directly to acute hemostatic/inflammatory signaling — a frequency-dependent effect, since Piezo1 is a demonstrated frequency filter (inactivation kinetics vary with stimulus frequency, waveform, and duration).
- **Endothelial dysfunction disease review (*Life Sciences*, July 2026):** Piezo1 is now positioned as a therapeutic target across atherosclerosis, hypertension, heart failure, myocardial infarction, pulmonary arterial hypertension, ARDS, and diabetes mellitus — all conditions in which chronic vibration exposure is a recognized aggravating factor.

## 2. Standards Update: ISO 5349-3:2025

V1.0 referenced hand-transmitted vibration exposure against the ISO 5349-1 general framework. **ISO 5349-3:2025** (published December 2025) extends this specifically to **shock** vibration — discrete, high-amplitude impulsive events distinct from continuous sinusoidal exposure — introducing the **Vibration Peak Magnitude (VPM)** metric for machinery emission declarations across the 8 Hz–1000 Hz octave range. A companion 2025 arXiv study (Mootz & Akhavian) validates a wearable-sensor transfer-function method that reduces ISO-compliant HAVS monitoring cost, using hammer-drilling trials to demonstrate accurate palm-to-upper-arm damping correction.

**V2.0 relevance:** the biodynamic resonance data in this document (which addresses continuous sinusoidal exposure) must now be read alongside ISO 5349-3's shock/impulse framework for any Aegis Embark product exposed to both regimes (e.g., off-road Aegis Embark cabin mounts experiencing both continuous road vibration and discrete pothole shock).
