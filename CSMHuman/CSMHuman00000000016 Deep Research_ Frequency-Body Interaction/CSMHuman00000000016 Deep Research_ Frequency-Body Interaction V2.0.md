## 1. Epidermal Piezo1: A New Mechanism for the Integumentary Layer

V1.0's treatment of the skin as a dielectric, dispersive medium (relevant to both the helical sweat duct electrodynamics hypothesis and general high-frequency EMF coupling) is now complemented by direct mechanistic evidence at the cellular level. A 2025 *Nature Communications* study demonstrates that **epidermal Piezo1** mediates skin's mechanosensing response to physical stretch: knockdown of Piezo1 in epidermal stem cells measurably reduces stretch-induced tissue expansion and associated immune activation pathways (via S100A8/S100A9 calcium-signaling markers). While this is a *mechanical* (not electromagnetic) stretch-sensing mechanism, it establishes that the integumentary layer V1.0 treated primarily as a passive dielectric boundary is, in fact, an active mechanotransductive tissue in its own right — relevant to any Aegis Embark or Aegis-C wearable/seating interface that maintains sustained skin contact under vibration.
