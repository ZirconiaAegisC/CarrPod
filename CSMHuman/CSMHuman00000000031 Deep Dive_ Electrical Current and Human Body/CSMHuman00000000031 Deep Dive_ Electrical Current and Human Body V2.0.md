## 1. Defibrillation Strategy Update: DSED and Vector-Change

V1.0's safety-threshold analysis (current magnitude vs. physiological effect, from sensation threshold through ventricular fibrillation risk to sustained-contraction defibrillation) remains the correct baseline framework. V2.0 adds the current state of clinical defibrillation strategy for refractory ventricular fibrillation (VF) — cases where standard single-shock defibrillation fails:

- **DOSE-VF trial (NEJM, 2022; still the reference RCT as of 2026):** Double Sequential External Defibrillation (DSED — two rapid sequential shocks from separate defibrillators in different pad planes) and Vector-Change (VC — switching pad placement to alter current vector through the myocardium) both show improved return-of-spontaneous-circulation (ROSC) and survival compared to standard defibrillation in refractory VF.
- **2025 systematic review:** identifies persistent practical barriers (need for dual defibrillators, coordination delay, lack of standardized protocol) and methodological barriers (small sample sizes, heterogeneous RVF definitions) limiting widespread DSED/VC adoption, despite the positive trial signal.
- **AHA ACLS Electrical Cardioversion Algorithm (May 2025 update):** reconfirms synchronized cardioversion energy levels (atrial fibrillation 200J, atrial flutter 200J, narrow-complex tachycardia 100J, monomorphic VT 100J) with polymorphic VT treated as an unsynchronized high-energy shock — consistent with, not a change to, prior guidance, but formally reconfirmed current.

## 3. Applied Cross-Reference: Solar Cycle 25 GIC Relevance

Geomagnetically induced currents (GIC) during G4–G5 events (Solar Cycle 25 observed SSN ~137 peak) can, in principle, drive stray current through ungrounded or improperly isolated conductive structures in contact with occupants. V2.0 flags this document's safety-threshold table as the correct reference standard against which Aegis-C/Aegis Embark electrical-isolation design (dielectric interior surfaces, non-conductive door hardware, isolated grounding architecture) should continue to be verified, even though GIC exposure pathways are addressed in dedicated CSM electrical-safety documentation rather than in this document itself.
