## 1. NIOSH 83-110 Legacy: Confirmed as Foundational, Now Molecularly Grounded

V1.0 traces the formal crystallization of hand-transmitted vibration occupational pathology to NIOSH Publication 83-110. V2.0 retains this historical framing in full — it remains the correct account of the field's origin — and adds the missing molecular layer: the epidemiological dose-response relationships NIOSH 83-110 first quantified are now understood to be substantially mediated by Piezo1 mechanosensitive channel activation, which functions as a genuine frequency filter (transduction efficiency depends on stimulus frequency, waveform, and duration) rather than a simple linear dose accumulator. This does not change the NIOSH numerical framework but explains, at the cellular level, *why* frequency weighting (rather than raw acceleration) has always been the correct exposure metric.

## 2. Standards Update Since NIOSH 83-110

| Era | Standard | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| 1983 (V1.0 baseline) | NIOSH 83-110 | Founding occupational classification of hand-transmitted vibration hazard |
| 2001–2001 | ISO 5349-1/-2 | General requirements and workplace measurement guidance (continuous exposure) |
| **Sept 2025 (new in V2.0)** | Consolidated HAV general guidance (GG-7-25) | Training/management guidance covering EAV/ELV, equipment, health surveillance |
| **Dec 2025 (new in V2.0)** | **ISO 5349-3:2025** | Shock/impulse vibration measurement (VPM metric, 8–1000 Hz) |
