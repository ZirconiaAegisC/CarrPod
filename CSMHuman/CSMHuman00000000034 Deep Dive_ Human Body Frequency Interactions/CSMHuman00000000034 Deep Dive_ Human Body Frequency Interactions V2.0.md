## 1. Full-Spectrum Dosimetry Status: Confirmed Current

V1.0 spans the full spectrum from 50/60 Hz power-grid frequencies to prospective 6G sub-terahertz communications. V2.0 reconfirms that **no regulatory supersession** has occurred at either end of this spectrum through 2026: the ICNIRP 2010 low-frequency guideline (1 Hz–100 kHz) and the ICNIRP 2020 high-frequency guideline (100 kHz–300 GHz) both remain current, and no finalized 6G-specific biological exposure guideline exists, meaning the document's forward-looking 6G discussion remains the most current available framework rather than being superseded.

## 2. New Cross-Boundary Case Study: AM-EAS Systems

The 2026 BioEM conference finding that AM-EAS retail security systems, operating at 58 kHz — squarely within V1.0's addressed spectrum, at the boundary between the low-frequency and intermediate-frequency ICNIRP regimes — can exceed general-public exposure limits by up to 22.5× for an adult female under worst-case geometry, is added as a concrete illustration directly within this document's own frequency scope (unlike several sibling documents in this cluster where the case study sits at the edge of their focus). RF-EAS systems (8.2 MHz) were confirmed compliant under the same study conditions, illustrating the frequency-specificity of the exceedance.
