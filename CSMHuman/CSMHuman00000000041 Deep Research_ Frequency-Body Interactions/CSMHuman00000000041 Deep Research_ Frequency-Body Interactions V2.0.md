## 1. Distinguishing Confirmed Mechanical Non-Thermal Effects from Contested EM Non-Thermal Effects

V1.0's central thesis challenges the 20th-century "thermal-only" safety paradigm across acoustic, mechanical, and electromagnetic transduction. V2.0 sharpens this thesis with an important 2025–2026 distinction: **mechanical non-thermal transduction is now well-confirmed** (Piezo1/Piezo2 channels transduce mechanical stimuli into electrical/biochemical signals via a purely mechanical gating mechanism, entirely independent of any thermal effect, with frequency-dependent efficiency), whereas **electromagnetic non-thermal bioeffects remain scientifically contested** (no 2025–2026 study has been published resolving whether non-thermal EM mechanisms — as opposed to shallow, non-uniform heating — produce adverse effects at ICNIRP-compliant exposure levels). V2.0 recommends this distinction be maintained explicitly: the document's mechanical-domain argument is on considerably firmer evidentiary ground than its electromagnetic-domain argument, and conflating the two risks overstating the EM case by association with the well-established mechanical case.
