## 1. Molecular Confirmation of the Systemic Shift

V1.0's central historiographic argument is that the Nagoya Journal of Medical Science (Vol. 57) research triggered a paradigm shift away from a "localized mechanical battering" model of Vibration-Induced White Finger (VWF) toward a systemic neurovascular dysregulation model. V2.0 provides direct molecular support for this shift: Piezo1 activation drives **NF-κB/p65-mediated chemokine signaling** (CCL2, CCL5, CXCL1, CXCL2, CXCL10) — an inflammatory cascade with systemic, not purely localized, signaling character, consistent with autonomic dysregulation extending beyond the immediate zone of mechanical contact. This directly reinforces, rather than contradicts, the systemic model V1.0 argues for.

| V1.0 Historiographic Claim | V2.0 Molecular Support |
|---|---|
| VWF is not purely localized mechanical battering | Piezo1-driven inflammatory chemokine signaling is a systemic, receptor/pathway-mediated cascade, not a purely local mechanical injury response |
| Autonomic dysregulation underlies chronic vascular symptoms | NF-κB/p65 pathway activation is consistent with sustained sympathetic/inflammatory crosstalk |
