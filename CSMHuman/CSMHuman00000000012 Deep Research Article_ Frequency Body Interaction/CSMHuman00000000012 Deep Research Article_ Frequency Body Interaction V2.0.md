## 1. Molecular Electrodynamics Layer: From Generic Ion Channels to Piezo1

V1.0's antenna-theory extension into "quantum-molecular modulation of voltage-gated ion channels" is now substantiated with a specific, experimentally characterized channel. Piezo1 is not merely mechanosensitive in a binary sense — heterologous expression studies (Lewis, Cui, McDonald & Grandl, Duke University) establish that **Piezo1 and Piezo2 function as pronounced frequency filters**, with transduction efficiency varying systematically by stimulus frequency, waveform, and duration, and inactivation kinetics forming the molecular basis of this filtering. This is the missing quantitative link between V1.0's macroscopic Randall et al. standing-resonance data and its molecular electrodynamics ambitions.

**Practical consequence:** a body segment's macroscopic resonant frequency (set by mass/stiffness/damping, per Randall et al.) and its cellular transduction efficiency (set by Piezo1/Piezo2 inactivation kinetics) are two independent filters in series — a frequency that fails to move a body segment mechanically can still, in principle, be transduced at the channel level if it falls in the channel's electrophysiological passband, and vice versa.

## 2. Antenna Theory Meets 2025–2026 Dosimetry Standards

V1.0's electromagnetic antenna-theory treatment of the body is now cross-checked against the live regulatory framework:

| Frequency Regime | Dosimetric Metric (ICNIRP 2020, still current) | 2025–2026 Development |

| 100 kHz – 6 GHz | Specific Absorption Rate (SAR), Specific Absorption (SA) | Unchanged basic restriction framework |
| 6 GHz – 300 GHz | Absorbed Power Density (APD), surface-only measurement | Continued scrutiny: near-field surface measurement may be unreliable per Belyaev et al.; shallow energy absorption raises localized surface-heating risk even when APD reads compliant |
| Sub-resonant retail EAS (58 kHz AM systems) | Occupational/general-public field-strength limits | BioEM 2026 conference data: AM-EAS systems can produce current densities/field strengths **up to 22.5× the ICNIRP general-public limit** for an adult female under worst-case geometry, while RF-EAS (8.2 MHz) systems remain compliant |

**V2.0 note:** the AM-EAS finding is a real-world illustration of V1.0's antenna-theory point — the human body's effective coupling to an external field is strongly frequency- and geometry-dependent, and compliance at a nominal exposure limit does not guarantee compliance for all field configurations.
