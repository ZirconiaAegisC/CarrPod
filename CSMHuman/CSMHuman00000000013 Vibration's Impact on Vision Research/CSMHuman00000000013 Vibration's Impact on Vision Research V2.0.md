## 1. Beyond Eyeball Resonance: Neuroinflammatory and Synaptic Mechanisms

V1.0 modeled WBV's visual impact primarily through ocular biomechanics — the trabecular meshwork and eyeball behaving as a coupled mass-spring system with a resonance-dependent transmissibility. A 2025 narrative review (*Neurobiology*, March 2025) adds a second, non-mechanical pathway: sustained WBV exposure is associated with **neuroinflammation, oxidative stress, and altered synaptic plasticity/neurotransmitter signaling**, producing cognitive effects (attention, processing speed) that compound the purely optical degradation V1.0 quantified.

**Key frequency/duration dependency (unchanged from the literature, now explicitly dual-pathway in V2.0):**
- Short-term, low-frequency WBV: may transiently *improve* neuromuscular coordination and learning/memory markers (positive rehabilitation-adjacent effect)
- Long-term/high-intensity WBV: chronic nerve-tissue stress reduces conduction efficiency, producing cognitive decline, mental fatigue, and reduced attention — independent of, and additive to, the mechanical visual-acuity decrement.
