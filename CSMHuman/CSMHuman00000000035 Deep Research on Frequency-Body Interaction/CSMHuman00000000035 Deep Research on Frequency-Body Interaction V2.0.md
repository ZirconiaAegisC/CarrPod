## 1. Molecular Mechanism for the Noël et al. (2022) Stenosis Pathway

V1.0 anchors its multiscale analysis on Noël et al. (2022), which elucidates arterial stenosis pathophysiology induced by hand-transmitted vibration (HTV) at 125 Hz, integrated with Humphrey's mechanobiology framework at the microscopic scale. V2.0 adds the specific molecular link connecting the macroscopic 125 Hz HTV exposure to the microscopic mechanobiology: **Piezo1** activation under vibration exposure drives NF-κB/p65-mediated chemokine upregulation (CCL2, CCL5, CXCL1, CXCL2, CXCL10) in vascular smooth muscle — a plausible mechanistic contributor to the chronic stenotic remodeling Noël et al. describe at 125 Hz specifically, since Piezo1/Piezo2 channels are confirmed frequency filters whose transduction efficiency depends on the precise stimulus frequency.
