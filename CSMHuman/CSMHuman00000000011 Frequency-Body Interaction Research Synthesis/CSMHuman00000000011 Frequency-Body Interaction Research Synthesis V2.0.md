## 1. Standards Update: The ISO 5349 Family Expands

V1.0's bifurcation into Hand-Arm Vibration (HAV, ISO 5349) and Whole-Body Vibration (WBV, ISO 2631) exposure modes remains structurally valid, but the HAV standard itself has expanded. **ISO 5349-3:2025** adds a dedicated shock/impulse measurement part (Vibration Peak Magnitude, 8 Hz–1000 Hz octave bands), addressing discrete high-amplitude events (e.g., hammer drilling, impact wrenches) that the continuous-exposure RMS methodology in ISO 5349-1 systematically under-weights.

**Field deployment advance (2025):** a wearable multi-accelerometer transfer-function method (Mootz & Akhavian, arXiv:2509.16536) demonstrates that palm-to-upper-arm damping can be corrected computationally, enabling ISO-comparable HAVS exposure monitoring without handle-mounted instrumentation — directly relevant to retrofitting exposure monitoring onto the CDC/NIOSH worker population V1.0 references without requiring new tool hardware.

## 2. Unifying Mechanism: Piezo1 Across Both Exposure Modes

V1.0 treated HAV and WBV as parallel but mechanistically separate literatures (per CDC/NIOSH/ISO convention). V2.0 identifies a shared molecular substrate: the **Piezo1 mechanosensitive channel**, confirmed (Nature Communications Biology, Jan 2025) to transduce vibration exposure into NF-κB/p65-mediated chemokine signaling (CCL2, CCL5, CXCL1/2/10) in vascular smooth muscle — a pathway equally applicable to the digital arteries affected in HAV-induced Vibration White Finger and the lumbar/whole-body vasculature affected in WBV exposure.

| Exposure Mode | V1.0 Standard | V2.0 Shared Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Hand-Arm (HAV) | ISO 5349-1/-2 | Piezo1 → NF-κB/p65 → chemokine upregulation in digital artery smooth muscle |
| Whole-Body (WBV) | ISO 2631-1 | Same Piezo1 pathway in lumbar/paraspinal and systemic vasculature |

This unification means exposure-reduction engineering validated for one mode (e.g., isolation mounts reducing Piezo1 activation duration) is mechanistically transferable to the other.

## 3. Applied Design Implication: Dual-Exposure Aegis Platforms

Aegis Embark occupant hardware and Aegis-C construction/field equipment expose users to **both** HAV (hand tools, steering/control interfaces) and WBV (seat/cabin) simultaneously. V2.0 formalizes a combined exposure budget recommendation: since both pathways converge on Piezo1 activation duration rather than amplitude alone, cumulative daily exposure across both modes should be tracked against a unified time-based, not just mode-separated, threshold — an extension beyond the strict separation in the CDC/NIOSH source material V1.0 relied upon.
