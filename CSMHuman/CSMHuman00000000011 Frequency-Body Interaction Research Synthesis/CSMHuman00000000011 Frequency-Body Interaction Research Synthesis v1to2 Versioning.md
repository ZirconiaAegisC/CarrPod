# CSMHuman00000000011 Frequency-Body Interaction Research Synthesis
## V1.0 to V2.0 Versioning Delta
**Type:** Scientific Change Log | **Date:** June 2026 | **Applies to document:** `CSMHuman00000000011 Frequency-Body Interaction Research Synthesis V2.0`

---

## Revision Necessity — Three Drivers

1. **Standards expansion** — ISO 5349-3:2025 adds shock/impulse vibration measurement not available at V1.0 drafting.
2. **Mechanistic unification discovered** — Piezo1 research (2025) shows HAV and WBV, treated as separate literatures in V1.0, converge on one molecular pathway.
3. **Field monitoring method matured** — wearable-sensor ISO-comparable monitoring (2025) reduces the cost barrier V1.0 implicitly assumed (handle-mounted accelerometers only).

---

## V1.0 vs V2.0 — Specific Parameter Changes

| Parameter | V1.0 | V2.0 | Reason | Primary Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **HAV shock measurement** | ISO 5349-1/-2 (continuous RMS only) | ISO 5349-3:2025 added (VPM, 8–1000 Hz shock bands) | New standard covers discrete impact events under-weighted by RMS methods | ISO 5349-3:2025 |
| **Field monitoring cost** | Implicit reliance on handle-mounted accelerometers | Wearable transfer-function method validated to ISO benchmark | Removes hardware barrier to exposure monitoring | arXiv:2509.16536 (Sept 2025) |
| **HAV/WBV mechanistic relationship** | Treated as parallel, separate literatures | Unified via Piezo1 → NF-κB/p65 → chemokine pathway, applicable to both | Direct experimental confirmation of shared molecular transduction mechanism | *Nature Communications Biology* 42003-025-07524-y (Jan 2025) |
| **Exposure budgeting** | Mode-separated (HAV vs WBV limits tracked independently) | Unified time-based exposure budget recommended for dual-exposure platforms | Mechanistic unification implies shared activation-duration risk driver | Internal Aegis Embark/Aegis-C design basis |
