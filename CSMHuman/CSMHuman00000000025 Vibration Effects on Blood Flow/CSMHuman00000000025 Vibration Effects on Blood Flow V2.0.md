## 1. Mechanosensor Layer Added to the CFD Model

V1.0's patient-specific computational fluid dynamics (CFD) modeling quantifies how lateral vibration alters hemodynamic parameters (velocity profiles, wall shear stress) in forearm/hand arterial geometries. V2.0 adds the biological receiver side of this physics: a comprehensive 2025 review (*Circulation Research*, AHA) synthesizes the full range of mechanosensors and flow-sensitive effectors that convert hemodynamic shear stress into cellular response, incorporating single-cell sequencing, spatial omics, and multiomics approaches unavailable at V1.0 drafting. Within this framework, **Piezo1** is confirmed as a primary vascular smooth muscle mechanosensor directly activated by vibration-modulated shear/strain — the CFD-predicted wall shear stress changes in V1.0's model now have an identified downstream molecular receptor.

## 2. Systemic Cross-Reference

A 2025 study on whole-body vibration (Smovey-device exercise) in elderly women shows measurable changes in red blood cell count, hemoglobin, platelet count, and the aggregation/elongation indices used to characterize blood rheology — indicating that the localized forearm/hand hemodynamic effects V1.0 models are part of a broader, systemic hemorheological response to vibration exposure, not an isolated regional phenomenon.
