## 1. Direct Experimental Confirmation: Echinocyte Formation Under Vibration

V1.0's theoretical treatment of vibration-induced erythrocyte trauma (impulse, shear, and frequency-dependent membrane stress) is directly confirmed by a controlled laboratory study: vortex-mixer vibration at defined frequencies (750 rpm, 1500 rpm) and durations (5 min, 10 min) produces measurable RBC deformation, including **echinocyte formation** (spiculated membrane morphology characteristic of membrane stress) after 10 minutes of exposure, along with a duration-dependent decrease in RBC size and altered IgG cytokine expression at higher frequency/shorter container volume combinations.

| Exposure Parameter | Effect Observed |
|---|---|
| 10 min duration (either frequency) | Echinocytes present in all groups; RBC size significantly smaller than 5-min groups |
| 5 min duration, higher frequency or smaller container | Decreased RBC size; elevated IgG cytokine expression |
| 5 min duration, lower frequency, larger container | Comparatively less deformation |

**V2.0 conclusion:** this is the clearest available direct laboratory demonstration that the impulse/shear trauma mechanism V1.0 describes theoretically produces measurable, frequency- and duration-dependent morphological and immunological changes in real erythrocytes.
