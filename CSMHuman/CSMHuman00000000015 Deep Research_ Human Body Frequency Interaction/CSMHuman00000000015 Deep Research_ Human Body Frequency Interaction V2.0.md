## 1. Dosimetry Standards: Confirmed Current, With a New Exceedance Case

V1.0's radiofrequency dosimetry section is built on the ICNIRP guideline framework. V2.0 confirms **ICNIRP 2020 remains the governing standard** through 2026 (100 kHz–300 GHz), using Specific Absorption Rate (SAR) below 6 GHz and Absorbed Power Density (APD) above 6 GHz. No superseding guideline has been issued. However, a 2026 BioEM conference dataset provides a concrete exceedance case directly relevant to this document's impedance-matching theory: **AM-EAS retail security systems (58 kHz)** can induce current densities and field strengths **up to 22.5× the ICNIRP general-public exposure limit** for an adult female under worst-case body geometry, while RF-EAS systems (8.2 MHz) remain compliant under the same conditions. This demonstrates that impedance-matching between an external field and body geometry — the theoretical core of V1.0's resonance/impedance treatment — produces real, quantifiable, non-uniform risk even under a "one-size" regulatory limit.
