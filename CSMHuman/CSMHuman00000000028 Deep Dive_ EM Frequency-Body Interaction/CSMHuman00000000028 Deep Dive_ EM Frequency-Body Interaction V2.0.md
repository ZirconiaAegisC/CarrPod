V1.0's regulatory analysis is anchored on the human body as a composite volume conductor with frequency-dependent dielectric tissue properties, assessed against the ICNIRP guideline framework. ## 2. New Compliance-Gap Case Study: AM-EAS Systems

V2.0 adds a concrete, quantified regulatory compliance-gap case directly relevant to this document's dosimetric/regulatory framing: 2026 BioEM conference data shows AM-EAS retail security systems (58 kHz) can produce field strengths/current densities **up to 22.5× the ICNIRP general-public exposure limit** for an adult female under worst-case body geometry, while adjacent RF-EAS systems (8.2 MHz) remain compliant. This is precisely the kind of tissue-heterogeneity-driven, geometry-dependent regulatory gap V1.0's dielectric-medium framework predicts should exist, and V2.0 treats it as the document's first fully-worked real-world example.

## 3. Tissue Heterogeneity Note: Skin as Active Dielectric Tissue
