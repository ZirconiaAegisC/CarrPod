## 1. The Thermal-Only Paradigm: Still Unresolved

V1.0's central argument — that the 20th-century "thermal-only" safety paradigm may be insufficient for millimeter-wave (MMW) and sub-terahertz (sub-THz) exposure — is reconfirmed as an open scientific question in 2026, not resolved in either direction. No 2025–2026 in vitro or in vivo study has been published demonstrating a validated non-thermal adverse bioeffect mechanism specific to 5G MMW protocols with or without Brillouin precursor pulse formation, and ICNIRP has not revised its 2020 guidance. V2.0's contribution is confirming this remains current, live scientific uncertainty rather than allowing V1.0's framing to go stale or be mistaken for a resolved debate.

---

## 2. Absorbed Power Density: Continued Measurement-Reliability Scrutiny

The ICNIRP >6 GHz basic restriction relies on Absorbed Power Density (APD), a **surface** measurement, on the premise that above ~6 GHz, energy absorption occurs predominantly within the skin. This premise continues to draw scrutiny: near-field APD measurements may be unreliable per ongoing critique (Belyaev et al.), and shallow absorption depth means high internal SAR-equivalent heating can, in principle, coexist with an acceptable surface APD reading. This is unchanged from V1.0's own concern but is reaffirmed here as still unresolved rather than superseded.

## 3. Illustrative Real-World Case: AM-EAS Exceedance

While operating at a far lower frequency (58 kHz) than this document's MMW/sub-THz focus, the 2026 BioEM conference finding that AM-EAS retail security systems can exceed ICNIRP general-public limits by up to 22.5× for an adult female is included as an illustrative case that regulatory-limit compliance is geometry- and population-dependent even in well-studied frequency regimes — reinforcing the document's caution that MMW/sub-THz limits, based on comparatively less mature dosimetric data, warrant continued conservatism.
