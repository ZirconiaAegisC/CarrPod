## 1. Experimental Confirmation at the Erythrocyte Scale

V1.0's soft-matter cavitation framework (formation, oscillation, and collapse of vapor/gas-filled structures under mechanical stress) is directly evidenced at the cellular scale by a 2024 vortex-mixer study: controlled vibration exposure produces echinocyte formation (membrane spiculation, a soft-matter deformation response) and RBC size reduction in a frequency- and duration-dependent manner, providing an accessible experimental proxy for the cavitation-adjacent soft-matter dynamics this document treats theoretically.

## 2. Applied Cavitation-Adjacent Mechanism: HIFU Micro-Streaming

A 2025 two-stage biofilm-disruption protocol provides a directly applicable, deliberately-engineered example of the cavitation physics this document addresses: **Stage 1** uses focused 1 MHz ultrasound (0.3 W/cm², 5 minutes) to create cavitation-driven micro-streaming within a biofilm matrix, opening nanopores in the exopolysaccharide layer; **Stage 2** delivers low-frequency (180–220 Hz) acoustic vibration within 30 minutes of the HIFU pre-treatment, penetrating the now-permeable biofilm approximately 10× more efficiently than acoustic treatment alone. This two-stage cavitation-then-low-frequency-penetration sequence is a clean applied demonstration of soft-matter cavitation dynamics deliberately engineered for therapeutic effect, complementing V1.0's largely hazard-oriented framing with a beneficial-use case.
