## 1. Molecular Pathway for AVF Failure

V1.0's thesis is that AVF failure pathogenesis requires looking beyond the low-frequency domain (cardiac cycle, <2 Hz) into higher-frequency hemodynamic and electrical events. V2.0 strengthens this with a specific molecular pathway directly implicated in AVF-relevant disturbed flow: **Piezo1-mediated mechanotransduction** under oscillatory shear stress (OSS) activates the Ca²⁺/CaM/CaMKII–FAK/Src–YAP signaling axis, driving endothelial inflammation and, in the atherosclerosis literature, plaque progression — a pathway directly analogous to (and cited in the vascular biology literature as relevant to) the neointimal hyperplasia that is the proximate cause of most AVF failures. GsMTx4 (a Piezo1 inhibitor) delays plaque progression and mitigates endothelial inflammation in disturbed-flow models, establishing Piezo1 as a validated pharmacological target in disturbed-flow vascular pathology generally.
