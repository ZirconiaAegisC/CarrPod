                 ONE WORKED CASE STUDY, ONE MOONSHOT, SAME ENVELOPE
                 CSMEval13-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Present one near-term-credible case study and one explicitly speculative
research program, both relevant to aerospace/defense advanced-concepts
review.

NEAR-TERM PILE — A-10 DIELECTRIC THUNDERBOLT
A fully worked legacy-airframe redesign study in non-inductive BFRP/ceramic
composite materials, developed for GIC immunity. This is a materials-
substitution feasibility study on an existing, well-understood airframe —
the kind of thing a sustainment program office could actually evaluate.

MOONSHOT PILE — SMART ROPE / ARCHIMEDES SCREW LAUNCH-ASSIST RESEARCH
A segmented piezoelectric actuator and Archimedes-screw-based launch-assist
concept (two design iterations), plus underwater/orbital kinetic launch and
airborne autorotation physics research. We are not claiming flight-readiness
here — we are claiming this is exactly the kind of moonshot materials/
actuation research advanced-concepts divisions maintain internal review
processes to evaluate, whatever its ultimate technical maturity turns out to
be.

WHY WE'RE LABELING THE PILES OURSELVES
Nothing wastes an advanced-concepts reviewer's time faster than a submitter
who can't tell the difference between "ready for a program office" and
"ready for a whiteboard." We'd rather you trust our labeling than discover
the mislabeling yourselves.

This briefing has no ticking clock in it, and I want to be honest that this
is a deliberate choice, not a missing ingredient. Advanced-concepts review
moves at the pace advanced-concepts review moves, and pretending otherwise
would be a worse pitch, not a better one.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 WHY PATIENCE IS THE CORRECT PACE, NOT A CONSOLATION PRIZE
                 CSMEval13-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Explain why aerospace/defense advanced-concepts engagement should be paced
as an annual-conference relationship rather than a single urgent pitch.

KEY FACTS
• Aerospace primes operate at the intersection of airframe EM hardening,
  satellite/space-vehicle GIC-adjacent charging effects, and novel launch-
  assist concepts — a genuinely broad intersection that takes real review
  time to map against any one company's specific programs.
• Advanced-concepts / X-divisions exist precisely to absorb this kind of
  patient, exploratory review without needing an artificial deadline to
  justify their attention.
• The A-10 case study's materials research (basalt-fiber composites, UHT
  ceramics) has genuine dual-use relevance across sustainment programs and
  moonshot programs alike, which is worth letting a reviewer discover at
  their own pace rather than rushing.

WHY WE'RE NOT MANUFACTURING URGENCY HERE
Every other sector in this outreach series has a real clock — a regulatory
review, a storm season, a grant cycle. This sector doesn't, and manufacturing
a fake one would undercut the credibility of every other briefing in this
entire series. So: no clock. Just good material, offered on your timeline.

Since this is a patient-timeline sector by design, the right plan isn't one
meeting — it's a standing annual touchpoint we keep showing up to.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 ANNUAL RE-ENGAGEMENT POINTS
                 CSMEval13-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the recurring annual venues best suited to a long-horizon
advanced-concepts relationship with aerospace/defense primes.

PRIMARY VENUE — AIAA SCITECH FORUM
Technical paper submission pathway; the natural home for both the A-10
materials case study and the Smart Rope/Archimedes-screw research program,
presented separately given their different maturity levels.

SECONDARY VENUE — SPACE SYMPOSIUM (COLORADO SPRINGS)
Major cross-industry space event; useful for the launch-assist/moonshot
research specifically, reaching a broader mix of primes, agencies, and
advanced-concepts groups in one place.

INDUSTRY NETWORKING — NDIA
Broader defense-industrial-base networking; useful as a parallel track
alongside the more technical AIAA/Space Symposium venues.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. AIAA SciTech paper submission (technical credibility, both pieces).
2. Space Symposium exhibitor/technical-session presence (broader reach).
3. Direct advanced-concepts division follow-up with whichever primes show
   interest at either event — the annual cycle repeats regardless, so a
   missed year isn't a missed opportunity, just a later one.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Same two venues, every year, until the right advanced-concepts team is in the room. — Office of External Affairs & Strategic Partnerships, Carrington Storm Motors | Safe Pod Engineering Company
