## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Military platforms and installations are dual-hazard exposed: **directed-energy/EMP weapons effects** and **naturally-occurring Carrington-class GIC/HEMP-adjacent effects** produce overlapping engineering requirements, meaning hardening research developed for one threat has direct dual-use value for the other. The CSM corpus already contains a fully worked example of this dual-use logic: a civilian A-10 Thunderbolt II airframe redesigned in non-inductive BFRP/ceramic composite for GIC immunity, and a humanoid robot (Phantom MK-1) EM-hardened under an explicit "Project AEGIS" framework with a companion anti-capture/self-destruct chip system. Military research offices (particularly DARPA, AFRL, and service-specific rapid-capabilities offices) actively fund exactly this class of dual-use resilience research.

## 2. Agency & Organization Types in This Sector

- **Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA)** — dual-use materials/EMP-hardening research funding
- **Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL)** — directed-energy and EMP-hardening portfolios
- **Naval Surface Warfare Center (NSWC)** divisions — shipboard EMP/GIC hardening (directly parallels CSMVessel research)
- **Army Corps of Engineers, Engineer Research and Development Center (ERDC)**
- **Defense Threat Reduction Agency (DTRA)** — EMP/HEMP effects testing and standards (MIL-STD-188-125)
- **U.S. Space Force** — space-weather effects on space-based assets
- **Service-specific Rapid Capabilities Offices** and **Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR/STTR)** program offices
