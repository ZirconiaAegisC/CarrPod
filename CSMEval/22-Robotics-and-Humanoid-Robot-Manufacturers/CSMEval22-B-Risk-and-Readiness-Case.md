Design decisions made during a scaling phase have a way of becoming
permanent by accident — not because anyone decided "this is final," but
because retooling a production line is expensive enough that "good enough
for now" quietly becomes "the standard." That window is open right now for
humanoid robotics. It won't stay open long.

                 THE WINDOW BEFORE PRODUCTION VOLUME LOCKS EVERYTHING IN
                 CSMEval22-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why the humanoid/general-purpose robotics industry's current
scaling phase represents an unusually good moment to influence hardware-
design standards.

KEY FACTS
• The humanoid and general-purpose robotics industry is scaling faster than
  its electromagnetic-hardening engineering discipline — most current
  platforms use conventional aluminum/copper construction optimized for
  weight and cost, not survivability in a high-GIC or directed-energy
  environment.
• Two complete, independent hardening case studies already exist (Project
  AEGIS on a named legacy platform, and a Mars-colonization-framed protocol
  on a named commercial platform) — proof the retrofit methodology is
  transferable, not theoretical.
• The anti-capture/self-destruct chip architecture addresses a security
  concern increasingly relevant as humanoid robots move into industrial,
  defense-adjacent, and public-facing deployments — a concern that scales
  with deployment volume.
• Once production volumes lock in a construction approach, retooling cost
  rises sharply — the same dynamic seen across other hardware industries
  during their own scaling phases.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• EM-hardening and anti-capture security retrofitted after production
  volumes lock in will cost meaningfully more than the same features
  designed in during the current scaling phase.
• A manufacturer that waits until a public incident (capture, EM-related
  failure) forces the conversation loses the first-mover positioning
  available right now.
