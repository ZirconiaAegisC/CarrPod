Most current humanoid platforms are built from conventional aluminum and
copper because that's what's optimized for weight and cost — nobody sat down
and specifically decided "and also let's make sure this survives a high-GIC
environment." That's not a criticism. It's just an opening nobody's closed
yet, and we've been closing it on paper for two case studies already.

WHY THE SECURITY PIECE MATTERS AS MUCH AS THE HARDENING PIECE

deployments, "what happens if one is lost or captured" is becoming as
relevant a design question as "what happens if the grid goes down." The
self-destruct chip architecture addresses the first question directly.

SUBJECT:       The Window Before Production Volume Locks Everything In
REF:           CSMEval22-B
================================================================================

Design decisions made during a scaling phase have a way of becoming
permanent by accident — not because anyone decided "this is final," but
because retooling a production line is expensive enough that "good enough
for now" quietly becomes "the standard." That window is open right now for
humanoid robotics. It won't stay open long.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 THE WINDOW BEFORE PRODUCTION VOLUME LOCKS EVERYTHING IN
                 CSMEval22-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why the humanoid/general-purpose robotics industry's current
scaling phase represents an unusually good moment to influence hardware-
design standards.

KEY FACTS
• The humanoid and general-purpose robotics industry is scaling faster than
  its electromagnetic-hardening engineering discipline — most current
  platforms use conventional aluminum/copper construction optimized for
  weight and cost, not survivability in a high-GIC or directed-energy
  environment.
• Two complete, independent hardening case studies already exist (Project
  AEGIS on a named legacy platform, and a Mars-colonization-framed protocol
  on a named commercial platform) — proof the retrofit methodology is
  transferable, not theoretical.
• The anti-capture/self-destruct chip architecture addresses a security
  concern increasingly relevant as humanoid robots move into industrial,
  defense-adjacent, and public-facing deployments — a concern that scales
  with deployment volume.
• Once production volumes lock in a construction approach, retooling cost
  rises sharply — the same dynamic seen across other hardware industries
  during their own scaling phases.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• EM-hardening and anti-capture security retrofitted after production
  volumes lock in will cost meaningfully more than the same features
  designed in during the current scaling phase.
• A manufacturer that waits until a public incident (capture, EM-related
  failure) forces the conversation loses the first-mover positioning
  available right now.

to invent a new venue, just show up to the right ones with the right two

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 THE TECHNICAL VENUES THIS INDUSTRY ALREADY TRUSTS
                 CSMEval22-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the technical conferences and associations best suited to
introducing EM-hardening and anti-capture research to robotics
manufacturers.

PRIMARY VENUES — IEEE ROBOTICS AND AUTOMATION SOCIETY (RAS)
ICRA and IROS, RAS's two flagship annual conferences, are the natural home
for a technical paper on the Phantom MK-1 hardening methodology and
comparative survivability testing data.

HUMANOID-SPECIFIC VENUE — HUMANOIDS CONFERENCE
IEEE-RAS-sponsored, specifically focused on humanoid platforms — the most
directly relevant single venue for both hardening case studies given their
shared humanoid-platform focus.

INDUSTRY CHANNEL — ASSOCIATION FOR ADVANCING AUTOMATION (A3)
Broader industrial/warehouse robotics reach, relevant for manufacturers
outside the humanoid-specific segment.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Humanoids Conference (narrowest, highest-fit audience).
2. ICRA/IROS technical paper submission (broadest robotics research
   audience, strongest peer credibility).
3. A3 engagement for industrial/warehouse robotics manufacturers whose
   platforms aren't strictly humanoid but share the same hardening
   relevance.
