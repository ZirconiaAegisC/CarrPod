**Scope note:** Roles and institutional channels only — no individually

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Engage

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| VP of Hardware Engineering / Chief Robotics Officer | Owns platform design decisions |
| Director of Materials & Structures Engineering | Owns materials-substitution evaluation |
| Head of Security Architecture | Owns anti-capture chip relevance |
| Head of Advanced Manufacturing / Supply Chain | Owns rare-earth-free magnet sourcing |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**IEEE Robotics and Automation Society (RAS)** — engage via IEEE RAS's
public paper-submission channels for ICRA and IROS; ieee-ras.org.

**Association for Advancing Automation (A3)** — engage via A3's public
membership/conference channel; automate.org.

**Humanoids Conference** — IEEE-RAS-sponsored; engage via its public
paper-submission channel.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| ICRA (IEEE Intl. Conference on Robotics and Automation) | Annual | Flagship robotics research conference |
| IROS (IEEE/RSJ Intl. Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems) | Annual | Companion flagship conference |
| Humanoids Conference | Annual | Humanoid-platform-specific |
| A3 events | Annual + ongoing | Industrial/warehouse robotics focus |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Technical paper submission (ICRA/IROS/Humanoids) is a stronger channel here
than direct corporate outreach — this industry's engineering culture
weights peer-reviewed technical presentation heavily, and a paper reaches
multiple manufacturers' engineering staff simultaneously.
