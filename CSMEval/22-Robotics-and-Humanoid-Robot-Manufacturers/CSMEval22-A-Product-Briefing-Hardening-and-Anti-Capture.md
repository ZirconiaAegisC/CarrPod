Most current humanoid platforms are built from conventional aluminum and
copper because that's what's optimized for weight and cost — nobody sat down
and specifically decided "and also let's make sure this survives a high-GIC
environment." That's not a criticism. It's just an opening nobody's closed
yet, and we've been closing it on paper for two case studies already.

WHY THE SECURITY PIECE MATTERS AS MUCH AS THE HARDENING PIECE
As humanoid robots move into industrial, defense-adjacent, and public-facing
deployments, "what happens if one is lost or captured" is becoming as
relevant a design question as "what happens if the grid goes down." The
self-destruct chip architecture addresses the first question directly.
