# CSMEval32 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Disaster-Preparedness, Survivalist & Off-Grid Living Community
### Ref: CSMEval32-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles, publications, and event types only — no individually
named content creators, forum moderators, or ministry coordinators are
listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Engage

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| Off-grid/homesteading publication editors and content creators | Own the dominant distribution channel |
| Preparedness-community forum and podcast hosts | Own continuous audience reach |
| Local emergency-preparedness ministry/community-group coordinators | Own faith-based/community distribution layer |
| Tiny-house and self-build community network organizers | Own building-materials-specific reach |

## 2. Institutional / Program Contact Channels

**FEMA Ready.gov** — public-education program; usable as a credibility
cross-reference, not a direct outreach target; ready.gov.

**Regional preparedness expos and homesteading fairs** — engage via each
event's own public exhibitor channel (varies by event/region).

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue Type | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Regional preparedness expos | Varies, regional circuit | Physical-presence credibility |
| Off-grid living publications | Continuous | Ongoing content-relationship channel |
| YouTube/podcast creator networks | Continuous | Dominant modern distribution channel |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Treat content-creator outreach as a relationship, not a single pitch —
this community's distribution model rewards ongoing presence over one-time
placement.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named creators, moderators, or ministry coordinators.
Identify specific publications/creators/expos and their current public
contact process at time of actual outreach.
