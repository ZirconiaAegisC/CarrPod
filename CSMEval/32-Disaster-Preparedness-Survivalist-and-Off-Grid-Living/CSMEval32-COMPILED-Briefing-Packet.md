Most sectors in this outreach series need a storm, a review window, or a
conference date to create a moment of attention. This community's own
content habits — forums, YouTube channels, podcasts — create that moment
continuously, on their own schedule, which changes the whole distribution
strategy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 DISTRIBUTION THAT DOESN'T NEED A NEWS CYCLE TO WORK
                 CSMEval32-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why this community's content-consumption habits make it an
unusually efficient, continuous distribution channel rather than an
event-driven one.

KEY FACTS
• This community is the direct consumer-market analog of every
  institutional sector in this outreach series, combined into a single
  household — grid independence, water/power self-sufficiency, and reduced
  GPS dependency all show up here as existing purchasing behavior.
• Off-grid living publications, forums, and YouTube/podcast creator
  networks are the dominant modern distribution channel for this
  community — content flows continuously, not around a single scheduled
  event the way a trade conference does.
• Interest spikes predictably after any publicized grid-outage or
  solar-storm news cycle — a bonus amplifier on top of the continuous
  baseline distribution, not a requirement for it.
• Faith-based and community-organization disaster-preparedness ministries
  represent an additional, often-overlooked distribution layer with its own
  independent gathering and communication structure.

WHY THIS CHANGES THE OUTREACH APPROACH
Most of this series is built around timing a pitch to a specific moment.
This community rewards an ongoing content-creator relationship more than a
single well-timed pitch — worth treating as a different kind of
relationship from the start.

Three different kinds of doors here — a physical expo circuit, a content-
creator relationship, and a credibility anchor borrowed from an existing
federal program. Worth using all three.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 EXPOS, EDITORS, AND READY.GOV
                 CSMEval32-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the expo, publication, and credibility-anchor channels best suited
to reaching the disaster-preparedness and off-grid living community.

PHYSICAL CHANNEL — PREPAREDNESS EXPOS AND HOMESTEADING FAIRS
A regional circuit of events; engage via each event's public exhibitor
channel.

CONTENT-CREATOR CHANNEL — OFF-GRID PUBLICATIONS AND YOUTUBE/PODCAST NETWORKS
The dominant modern distribution channel for this community; engage
individual publications and creators directly, treating this as an ongoing
relationship rather than a single guest-appearance pitch.

CREDIBILITY-ANCHOR CHANNEL — FEMA'S READY.GOV
Not a direct outreach target, but a useful cross-reference anchor —
positioning CSM research as complementary to, not competing with, an
established federal public-education program lends credibility with an
audience that can be skeptical of purely commercial preparedness messaging.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Content-creator outreach first (fastest, most continuous channel, lowest
   cost per contact).
2. Regional expo circuit in parallel (physical-presence credibility).
3. Ready.gov cross-referencing woven into both of the above, not pursued as
   its own separate outreach track.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Three doors, none of them requiring a single big moment to walk through. — Office of External Affairs & Strategic Partnerships, Carrington Storm Motors | Safe Pod Engineering Company
