================================================================================

Farm equipment "dancing" off-course during the May 2024 Gannon Storm made for
a strange, slightly funny news cycle — GPS-guided tractors briefly
forgetting geometry. It stopped being funny the moment you remember that
lane-keeping, adaptive cruise, and turn-by-turn navigation in passenger
vehicles run on exactly the same GNSS dependency.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 YOUR FARM-EQUIPMENT PROBLEM IS COMING TO THE SEDAN AISLE
                 CSMEval12-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why vehicle-level GIC/EMI vulnerability and GNSS-dependent driver-
assistance systems represent a nearer-term concern than typical NVH/ADAS
roadmap planning currently assumes.

KEY FACTS
• Modern vehicles, especially EVs, are dense, GPS-dependent, electronically
  controlled platforms — a profile shared with the precision-agriculture
  equipment that visibly malfunctioned during the May 2024 Gannon Storm.
• The same GNSS-dependent systems used for lane-keeping, adaptive cruise, and
  navigation in passenger vehicles are not inherently immune to the effect
  documented in agricultural equipment.
• Occupants are simultaneously exposed to vehicle-level EM vulnerability and
  occupant-level vibration/biodynamic hazard — a dual research domain the
  CSMHuman corpus documents in detail (HAVS-adjacent pedal/steering/seat
  vibration).
• This sector has the most fully-realized, ready-to-license CSM product
  catalog of any sector on this list — thirteen distinct interior components
  already exist as complete design briefs, not concepts.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• ADAS/navigation roadmaps built without GNSS-degradation contingency carry
  forward the same failure mode already observed in a real 2024 event.
• Interior NVH programs that don't account for the biodynamic research base
  in CSMHuman may be solving a narrower version of the vibration-exposure
  problem than the data actually supports.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 SUPPLIER DAY IS THE WHOLE STRATEGY
                 CSMEval12-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the channels best suited to introducing the thirteen-component Aegis
Embark line to OEM and supplier engineering/innovation teams.

PRIMARY CHANNEL — OEM SUPPLIER DAYS / INNOVATION SCOUTING
Many OEMs run dedicated open-innovation teams specifically to review exactly
this kind of external component catalog — the most direct channel, though
access typically requires an existing supplier relationship or a scouting
program's public submission portal.

TECHNICAL CHANNEL — SAE INTERNATIONAL WCX
SAE's World Congress Experience is the automotive engineering standards
body's flagship annual event — a natural venue for a technical presentation
of the biodynamic research base underlying the component line.

CONSUMER/HMI CHANNEL — CES
Increasingly automotive-HMI-focused; relevant for the navigation-redundancy
and cabin-EMI components specifically.

SAFETY-ADJACENT CHANNEL — NHTSA
Relevant specifically for HMI/safety-adjacent research (e.g., Camera-Monitor-
System design research already cross-referenced in the CSMHuman corpus).

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Identify OEM(s) with public open-innovation/scouting submission portals.
2. SAE WCX technical session in parallel (peer credibility).
3. CES for the HMI/navigation-specific components once initial OEM interest
   is established.
