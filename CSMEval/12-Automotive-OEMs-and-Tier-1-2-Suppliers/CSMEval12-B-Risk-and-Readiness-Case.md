                 YOUR FARM-EQUIPMENT PROBLEM IS COMING TO THE SEDAN AISLE
                 CSMEval12-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why vehicle-level GIC/EMI vulnerability and GNSS-dependent driver-
assistance systems represent a nearer-term concern than typical NVH/ADAS
roadmap planning currently assumes.

KEY FACTS
• Modern vehicles, especially EVs, are dense, GPS-dependent, electronically
  controlled platforms — a profile shared with the precision-agriculture
  equipment that visibly malfunctioned during the May 2024 Gannon Storm.
• The same GNSS-dependent systems used for lane-keeping, adaptive cruise, and
  navigation in passenger vehicles are not inherently immune to the effect
  documented in agricultural equipment.
• Occupants are simultaneously exposed to vehicle-level EM vulnerability and
  occupant-level vibration/biodynamic hazard — a dual research domain the
  CSMHuman corpus documents in detail (HAVS-adjacent pedal/steering/seat
  vibration).
• This sector has the most fully-realized, ready-to-license CSM product
  catalog of any sector on this list — thirteen distinct interior components
  already exist as complete design briefs, not concepts.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• ADAS/navigation roadmaps built without GNSS-degradation contingency carry
  forward the same failure mode already observed in a real 2024 event.
• Interior NVH programs that don't account for the biodynamic research base
  in CSMHuman may be solving a narrower version of the vibration-exposure
  problem than the data actually supports.
