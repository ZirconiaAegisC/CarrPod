                 TWO VENUES YOU'RE ALREADY RUNNING
                 CSMEval03-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the two lowest-friction, already-scheduled venues for beginning a
technical exchange between CSM's applied engineering research and NOAA/NASA
space-weather science.

VENUE 1 — NOAA SPACE WEATHER WORKSHOP (ANNUAL, BOULDER, CO)
The primary cross-sector space-weather conference bringing together
forecasters, utilities, and researchers in one room. We'd propose presenting
applied case studies (grid, bridge, and vessel resilience work) and offering
our forensic thermal-anomaly datasets as a citizen/industry-science
contribution to impact-verification efforts — for review, not endorsement.

VENUE 2 — AGU FALL MEETING, SPACE WEATHER SECTION
Secondary entry point via standard abstract submission — the appropriate
channel for putting applied-engineering findings in front of the academic
heliophysics community for scrutiny.

WHAT WE'RE ACTUALLY ASKING FOR
Not funding. Not endorsement. A technical review — the kind that either
strengthens our design-basis citations or corrects them before they reach a
wider audience. If a correction is warranted, we'd rather have it now, in a
workshop room, than later, in public.

SUGGESTED FIRST STEP
An abstract submission to whichever venue's deadline comes first, reviewed
informally by SWPC's public affairs/outreach coordinator beforehand if that's
a workable norm on your end.
