# CSMEval03 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## NOAA & NASA Space Weather and Heliophysics Programs
### Ref: CSMEval03-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles, associations, and published institutional contact
channels only — no individually named federal scientists are listed. Verify
current role-holders and each organization's preferred inbound process before
any actual outreach.

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Engage

| Role | Where Found | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Public Affairs & Outreach Coordinator | NOAA SWPC | First-line technical/public engagement contact |
| Program Scientist, Applied Sciences liaison | NASA Heliophysics Division | Bridges pure science and applied-use inquiries |
| Co-chair | Solar Cycle Prediction Panel | Owns forecast-methodology credibility |
| Geophysicist, geoelectric hazard mapping team | USGS Geomagnetism Program | Produces the hazard maps the CSM corpus cites |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC)** — Boulder, CO. Engage via
NOAA's public affairs contact process for SWPC (routed through NOAA
Communications), not an individual forecaster's direct line.

**NASA Heliophysics Division** — engage via NASA's Science Mission Directorate
public engagement / applied sciences program channel.

**USGS Geomagnetism Program** — engage via USGS's public science-inquiry
channel; the program publishes its geoelectric hazard maps openly, which is
itself the intended public-engagement channel.

**International Space Environment Services (ISES)** — international
forecasting coordination consortium; engage via member-agency channels
(NOAA SWPC is the U.S. member agency).

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| NOAA Space Weather Workshop | Annual, Boulder, CO | Primary cross-sector venue; abstract submission is the entry point |
| AGU Fall Meeting, Space Weather section | Annual | Standard academic abstract submission process |
| Solar Cycle Prediction Panel proceedings | Per-cycle | Published findings, not a direct-contact channel |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

This is the one sector where the "channel" and the "content" are almost the
same thing: abstract submission to a peer-reviewed or juried venue is both the
distribution mechanism and the credibility mechanism. There is no faster,
"better" channel that skips that step, and trying to find one would undercut
the entire point of this sector's engagement.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named SWPC forecasters, NASA program scientists, or USGS
geophysicists. Identify current role-holders through each agency's public
staff directory or conference program committee listing at time of actual
outreach.
