SUBJECT:       Grid-Dependent and Independently Exposed, at the Same Time
REF:           CSMEval29-B
================================================================================

Water treatment depends on the grid to run — pumping, process control, and
disinfection systems all need continuous power. It's also, independently,
exposed to GIC through its own buried metallic pipe networks and pump-
station electrical systems. That's two exposures layered on top of each
other, and most water utilities have formally assessed neither.

PURPOSE
Establish the dual exposure (grid-dependency plus independent GIC exposure)
facing water infrastructure, and why early adoption ahead of any future
standard carries real advantage.

KEY FACTS
• Water treatment and distribution is a grid-dependent critical service —
  pumping, treatment-process control, and disinfection systems all require
  continuous power.
• Independently, buried metallic pipe networks and pump-station electrical
  systems represent a GIC exposure pathway in their own right, distinct from
  the grid-dependency risk.
• Unlike electric utilities, water utilities are not currently subject to a
  NERC-style mandatory GMD planning standard — meaning most water systems
  have never formally assessed this risk at all.
• This represents a genuine "greenfield" awareness opportunity rather than a
  standards-update conversation, unlike most other infrastructure sectors in
  this outreach series.

WHY EARLY ADOPTION HAS REAL, DURABLE ADVANTAGE
• A utility that adopts the Aegis-Hydro framework now positions itself ahead
  of any future standard, rather than playing catch-up once one exists — the
  same first-mover logic that benefited early NERC TPL-007 compliant
  utilities relative to laggards.
• Early engagement also means input into what a future standard might
  eventually look like, rather than simply reacting to one once written.
