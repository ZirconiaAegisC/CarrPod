We use the word "greenfield" carefully in this business, because it usually
means someone's exaggerating how open a market really is. Here, it's just
accurate: there is currently no NERC-style mandatory GMD planning standard
for water systems. Nobody's required to have thought about this yet, which
means nobody's behind — but someone gets to be first.

PURPOSE
Summarize Project Aegis-Hydro and supporting CSM research relevant to
water/wastewater treatment and distribution infrastructure.

1. PROJECT AEGIS-HYDRO — water system resilience framework addressing
   pipe/reservoir/pump-station GIC exposure and proposing a dielectric
   retrofit pathway (CSMProWaterTransport).
2. CONDUCTIVE-SAFE PIPE DESIGN — non-conductive water-supply piping
   (CSMFAB012).
3. INFLATABLE CERAMIC WATER HEATER PRECEDENT — non-electric auxiliary-
   system precedent relevant to treatment/utility backup systems
   (CSMFAB06).

WHY "GREENFIELD" IS ACCURATE HERE SPECIFICALLY
Electric utilities operate under mandatory NERC TPL-007/EOP-010 compliance
(CSMEval02/10). Water utilities operate under no equivalent GMD standard
today. That's not a criticism of water utilities — GIC research simply
hasn't reached this sector's standard-setting bodies yet, which is exactly
what makes early engagement valuable rather than merely interesting.
