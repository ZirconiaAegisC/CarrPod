We use the word "greenfield" carefully in this business, because it usually
means someone's exaggerating how open a market really is. Here, it's just
accurate: there is currently no NERC-style mandatory GMD planning standard
for water systems. Nobody's required to have thought about this yet, which
means nobody's behind — but someone gets to be first.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 A GREENFIELD OPPORTUNITY, SAID WITHOUT EXAGGERATION
                 CSMEval29-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize Project Aegis-Hydro and supporting CSM research relevant to
water/wastewater treatment and distribution infrastructure.

1. PROJECT AEGIS-HYDRO — water system resilience framework addressing
   pipe/reservoir/pump-station GIC exposure and proposing a dielectric
   retrofit pathway (CSMProWaterTransport).
2. CONDUCTIVE-SAFE PIPE DESIGN — non-conductive water-supply piping
   (CSMFAB012).
3. INFLATABLE CERAMIC WATER HEATER PRECEDENT — non-electric auxiliary-
   system precedent relevant to treatment/utility backup systems
   (CSMFAB06).

WHY "GREENFIELD" IS ACCURATE HERE SPECIFICALLY
Electric utilities operate under mandatory NERC TPL-007/EOP-010 compliance
(CSMEval02/10). Water utilities operate under no equivalent GMD standard
today. That's not a criticism of water utilities — GIC research simply
hasn't reached this sector's standard-setting bodies yet, which is exactly
what makes early engagement valuable rather than merely interesting.

SUBJECT:       Grid-Dependent and Independently Exposed, at the Same Time
REF:           CSMEval29-B
================================================================================

Water treatment depends on the grid to run — pumping, process control, and
disinfection systems all need continuous power. It's also, independently,
exposed to GIC through its own buried metallic pipe networks and pump-
station electrical systems. That's two exposures layered on top of each
other, and most water utilities have formally assessed neither.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 GRID-DEPENDENT AND INDEPENDENTLY EXPOSED, AT THE SAME TIME
                 CSMEval29-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish the dual exposure (grid-dependency plus independent GIC exposure)
facing water infrastructure, and why early adoption ahead of any future
standard carries real advantage.

KEY FACTS
• Water treatment and distribution is a grid-dependent critical service —
  pumping, treatment-process control, and disinfection systems all require
  continuous power.
• Independently, buried metallic pipe networks and pump-station electrical
  systems represent a GIC exposure pathway in their own right, distinct from
  the grid-dependency risk.
• Unlike electric utilities, water utilities are not currently subject to a
  NERC-style mandatory GMD planning standard — meaning most water systems
  have never formally assessed this risk at all.
• This represents a genuine "greenfield" awareness opportunity rather than a
  standards-update conversation, unlike most other infrastructure sectors in
  this outreach series.

WHY EARLY ADOPTION HAS REAL, DURABLE ADVANTAGE
• A utility that adopts the Aegis-Hydro framework now positions itself ahead
  of any future standard, rather than playing catch-up once one exists — the
  same first-mover logic that benefited early NERC TPL-007 compliant
  utilities relative to laggards.
• Early engagement also means input into what a future standard might
  eventually look like, rather than simply reacting to one once written.

Being early only matters if the right audience sees you being early — so
let's make sure the introduction happens somewhere that carries technical
weight.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 AWWA ACE IS WHERE THE FIRST MOVER GETS NOTICED
                 CSMEval29-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the channels best suited to introducing the Aegis-Hydro framework
to a water utility willing to be an early adopter.

PRIMARY VENUE — AWWA ACE (AMERICAN WATER WORKS ASSOCIATION ANNUAL CONFERENCE)
The primary technical-session venue for this sector; the right place for a
first-adopter case study to get technical-community visibility.

SECONDARY CHANNELS
• Water Environment Federation (WEF) — broader water/wastewater technical
  audience.
• Association of Metropolitan Water Agencies (AMWA) — larger metropolitan
  utility audience specifically, useful given metro utilities' typically
  larger capital budgets.
• EPA Water Infrastructure and Cyber Resilience Division — engage via EPA's
  public stakeholder-engagement channel; relevant given this division's
  broader resilience mandate, even though GMD specifically isn't yet a named
  focus area.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. AWWA ACE technical-session presence (primary venue, first-adopter
   visibility).
2. AMWA outreach in parallel (targeting utilities with the capital capacity
   to be a credible first adopter).
3. WEF and EPA engagement as broader/longer-horizon awareness-building
   tracks.
