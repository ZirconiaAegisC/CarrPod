# CSMEval29 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Water Utilities & Water Infrastructure Operators
### Ref: CSMEval29-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles and institutional channels only — no individually
named water-utility staff are listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Act

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| Director of Water Treatment Operations | Owns treatment-process risk decisions |
| Chief Engineer, Water Distribution Systems | Owns pipe-network/pump-station specifications |
| SCADA/Controls Systems Manager | Owns controls-system resilience |
| Emergency Preparedness Coordinator (utility-level) | Owns broader continuity planning |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**American Water Works Association (AWWA)** — engage via AWWA's public ACE
conference technical-session channel; awwa.org.

**Water Environment Federation (WEF)** — engage via its public conference
channel; wef.org.

**Association of Metropolitan Water Agencies (AMWA)** — engage via its
public industry-affairs channel; amwa.net.

**EPA Water Infrastructure and Cyber Resilience Division** — engage via
EPA's public stakeholder-engagement channel; epa.gov.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| AWWA ACE | Annual | Primary technical-session venue |
| WEF conferences | Annual + regional | Broader water/wastewater audience |
| AMWA events | Annual + ongoing | Larger metropolitan utility audience |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Target AMWA-member (larger metropolitan) utilities first for a first-adopter
case study — larger capital budgets make an early greenfield investment more
feasible than at a smaller utility.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named water-utility engineers or coordinators. Identify
current role-holders through each utility's public directory at time of
actual outreach.
