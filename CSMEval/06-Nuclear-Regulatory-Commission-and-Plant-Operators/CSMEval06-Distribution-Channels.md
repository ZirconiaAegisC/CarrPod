# CSMEval06 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Nuclear Regulatory Commission & Nuclear Power Plant Operators
### Ref: CSMEval06-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles and formal institutional channels only — no
individually named NRC staff or plant personnel are listed. This is the
most safety/security-sensitive sector in the series; any actual outreach
should go through each organization's formal public-engagement or industry-
affairs channel, never direct-to-individual contact.

---

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**Nuclear Energy Institute (NEI)** — Washington, D.C.-based industry trade
association; engage via NEI's public industry-affairs/media channel (nei.org).

**NRC Regulatory Information Conference (RIC)** — annual; engage via the
conference's public technical-session submission process, which is explicitly
open to industry and public technical input, not a closed regulator-only
event.

**EPRI Nuclear Sector** — engage via EPRI's public membership-office channel;
epri.com.

**American Nuclear Society (ANS)** — professional/technical society; engage
via ANS's annual meeting technical-paper submission process; ans.org.

| ANS Annual Meeting | Annual | Broader technical/academic audience |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Given the safety-critical nature of this sector, the RIC's public
technical-session submission process is the correct primary channel — it is
specifically designed to let industry and outside technical contributors
reach NRC staff and plant operators in a structured, appropriate setting,
rather than through unsolicited direct contact.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named NRC engineers, plant design managers, or INPO advisors.
This is the sector where the "no scraped PII" principle matters most —
identify current role-holders exclusively through each organization's own
formal channels at time of actual outreach.
