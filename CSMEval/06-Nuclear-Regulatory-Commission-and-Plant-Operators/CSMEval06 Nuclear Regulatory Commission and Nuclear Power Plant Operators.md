## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Nuclear plants are the single infrastructure class with the **lowest acceptable tolerance for GIC-related electrical anomaly** of any sector on this list, because a loss of offsite power event initiates emergency diesel generator dependency and, in worst-case cascades, core-cooling risk. This is not theoretical: NERC's own historical GMD-impact record documents **transformer damage or failure at four separate U.S. nuclear plants** during recorded geomagnetic storms — Maine Yankee (twice: March 1989 and April 1991), Salem 1 and Salem 2 (March and September 1989), Seabrook (a vibration-related event, November 1998), and a downrating to 55% power at Hope Creek (July 2000). This is a documented, repeating failure pattern spanning three solar cycles, and Solar Cycle 25's confirmed SSN ~137 peak places the fleet squarely back in the exposure window.

## 2. Agency & Organization Types in This Sector

- **U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC)** — Office of Nuclear Reactor Regulation, Division of Engineering
- **Nuclear Energy Institute (NEI)** — industry trade association and policy coordination body
- **Individual plant operators** — merchant and utility-owned nuclear generating stations (both single-unit and multi-unit sites)
- **Institute of Nuclear Power Operations (INPO)** — industry self-regulatory/best-practice body
- **EPRI Nuclear Sector** — parallel to EPRI's grid-side GMD/SUNBURST program, with a nuclear-specific electrical-systems research track
