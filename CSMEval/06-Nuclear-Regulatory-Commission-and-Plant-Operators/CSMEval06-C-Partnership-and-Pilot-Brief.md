                 PRESENTING THE RECORD AND THE RESPONSE TOGETHER
                 CSMEval06-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the primary and secondary venues for presenting the historical GMD
incident record (Briefing B) alongside the hardening response (Briefing A) to
the nuclear regulatory and operator community.

PRIMARY VENUE — NRC REGULATORY INFORMATION CONFERENCE (RIC)
The annual, primary NRC/industry technical-exchange venue. This is the venue
where a documented historical failure pattern and a corresponding hardware
response belong in the same room, in front of both regulators and operators
simultaneously.

SECONDARY VENUES
• Nuclear Energy Institute (NEI) — industry trade association; engage via
  NEI's regulatory-affairs working groups, particularly any group already
  focused on infrastructure resilience.
• EPRI Nuclear Sector — parallel to EPRI's grid-side GMD/SUNBURST program,
  with a nuclear-specific electrical-systems research track; a natural
  technical-validation partner given EPRI's existing GIC monitoring role on
  the grid side.
• American Nuclear Society (ANS) Annual Meeting — broader technical/academic
  audience, useful for peer credibility alongside the more operator-focused
  RIC and NEI channels.
• Institute of Nuclear Power Operations (INPO) — industry self-regulatory
  body; engage via INPO's technical-advisor channel for electrical/grid
  interface topics.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. EPRI Nuclear Sector (technical validation first — this strengthens
   everything that follows).
2. NRC RIC (regulatory + operator audience together, the highest-value single
   venue).
3. NEI and INPO (industry-adoption and best-practice channels, in parallel).
