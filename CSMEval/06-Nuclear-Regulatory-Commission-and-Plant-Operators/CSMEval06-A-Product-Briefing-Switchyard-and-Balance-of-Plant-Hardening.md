                 CSMEval06-A | June 2026 | v1.0

PURPOSE
Summarize three CSM hardening components targeting the specific offsite/
onsite transformer and auxiliary-system GIC exposure documented in the
historical U.S. nuclear plant GMD incident record.

1. SWITCHYARD PERIMETER HARDENING
   Composite (non-metallic) monopoles and dielectric perimeter fencing
   (CSMFAB0105, CSMFAB0104) applied to switchyard structures — addressing
   the structure-level GIC path independent of transformer-level mitigation
   already covered by plant-specific GIC studies.

2. NON-CONDUCTIVE AUXILIARY WIRING AND MOTOR DESIGN
   GIC-safe wiring and piezoelectric/pneumatic motor design concepts
   (CSMFAB013, CSMFAB020) for auxiliary systems — targeting balance-of-plant
   equipment that sits outside primary safety-system design-basis
   requirements but still represents an availability and equipment-protection
   concern.

3. CHROMATIC FIELD MONITOR
   Passive, low-power GIC/EMF area monitor (CSMFAB021) for switchyard and
   site-perimeter deployment — a lower-cost complement to full magnetometer-
   grade instrumentation, intended for denser deployment across a plant
   fleet.

WHAT THIS IS NOT
This is not a safety-system redesign proposal, and nothing here is presented
as a substitute for any NRC-required design-basis analysis. This is
balance-of-plant and switchyard hardware positioned to reduce the specific
transformer/auxiliary-equipment failure mode already documented in NERC's own
historical GMD-impact record.
