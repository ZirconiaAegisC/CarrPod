## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

The humanoid and general-purpose robotics industry is scaling faster than its electromagnetic-hardening engineering discipline — most current humanoid platforms use conventional aluminum/copper construction optimized for weight and cost, not survivability in a high-GIC or directed-energy environment. The CSM corpus contains two complete, independent hardening case studies (a named legacy humanoid platform redesign under "Project AEGIS," and a Mars-colonization-framed hardening protocol for a named commercial humanoid robot) plus a genuinely distinctive companion concept — a self-destruct/anti-capture chip architecture — that addresses a security concern (adversarial reverse-engineering of forward-deployed or field-lost robots) increasingly relevant as humanoid robots move into industrial, defense-adjacent, and public-facing deployments.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector
