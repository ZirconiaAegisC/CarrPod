## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Emergency management agencies plan for hurricanes, earthquakes, and wildfires with mature doctrine — but a Carrington-class geomagnetic storm is a **simultaneous, continent-scale, multi-week power/communications/GPS/fuel-distribution failure with no fixed geographic epicenter**, a hazard profile current federal and state emergency operations plans are only beginning to incorporate. The May 2024 "Gannon Storm" (G5/Kp9, the strongest since 2003) demonstrated real-world GPS/GNSS outages lasting up to two days across the U.S. Midwest and $500M+ in downstream damage — with no loss of life or civil disorder only because the event's grid impact was mild. A Carrington-scale event layered on top of a **9/10 Kp storm arriving with 18 hours or less of warning** is the planning scenario this research corpus was built to address, and Solar Cycle 25's confirmed SSN ~137 peak (vs. ~115 forecast) means the statistical exposure window is open now, not in some future cycle.

## 2. Agency & Organization Types in This Sector

- **Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)** — National Response Coordination Center, Regional Offices, National Continuity Programs
- **Department of Homeland Security / Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)** — National Risk Management Center (space weather is an explicitly designated CISA national-critical-function risk)
- **State Emergency Management Agencies (state EMAs)** — e.g., Cal OES, Texas Division of Emergency Management, New York State DHSES
- **County/City Offices of Emergency Management** — direct implementers of evacuation and shelter-in-place protocols
- **Emergency Management Assistance Compact (EMAC)** administrators — interstate mutual-aid coordination
- **National Guard Bureau** (state-level activation for grid-down civil support)
- **American Red Cross** and other congressionally-chartered disaster-response NGOs operating alongside government EM structures

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

Position the CSM corpus not as a product catalog but as a **planning-assumption update**: "Your current continuity-of-operations plans likely assume the 2019 pre-Cycle-25 SSN ~115 forecast. Observed peak activity is now SSN ~137. Here is a fully worked evacuation-protocol case study (Los Angeles County) and a physical hardware inventory (drone evacuation, non-conductive shelter pods) that can be adapted to your jurisdiction's hazard mitigation plan update cycle."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**CRITICAL / IMMEDIATE.** Emergency management five-year Hazard Mitigation Plan (HMP) update cycles (required under 44 CFR §201 for FEMA mitigation-grant eligibility) are a natural, recurring entry point — agencies are contractually required to revisit hazard assumptions on a fixed schedule, making this sector uniquely receptive to new hazard data at predictable intervals.
