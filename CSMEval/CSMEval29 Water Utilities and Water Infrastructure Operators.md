# CSMEval29 — Water Utilities & Water Infrastructure Operators
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 2: Private Industry | Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH

---

## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Water treatment and distribution is a grid-dependent critical service (pumping, treatment-process control, and disinfection systems all require continuous power) that is also, independently, a GIC exposure pathway in its own right through buried metallic pipe networks and pump-station electrical systems. Unlike electric utilities, water utilities are not currently subject to a NERC-style mandatory GMD planning standard, meaning most water systems have never formally assessed this risk at all — this sector represents a genuine "greenfield" awareness opportunity rather than a standards-update conversation.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **Municipal and regional water utilities**
- **Investor-owned water utilities**
- **Water/wastewater treatment plant operators and engineering firms**
- **Reservoir and dam management authorities**
- **Water infrastructure equipment manufacturers** (pumps, valves, SCADA systems)

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

"Unlike electric utilities, water systems have no mandatory GMD planning standard today — this represents a genuine first-mover opportunity for a utility willing to be an early adopter of Project Aegis-Hydro's dielectric retrofit framework, positioned for AWWA ACE technical-session review."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**MEDIUM-HIGH.** No regulatory deadline exists yet, which is itself the argument: early engagement now positions an adopting utility ahead of any future standard, rather than playing catch-up later.
