# CSMEval31 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Amateur Radio & Emergency Communications Community
### Ref: CSMEval31-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles, clubs, and institutional channels only — no
individually named radio operators or volunteers are listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Engage

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| ARRL Section Emergency Coordinator | Owns section-level emergency-comms coordination |
| Local ARES/RACES District Emergency Coordinator | Owns district-level activation/training |
| Club Emergency Communications Officer | Owns local-club-level coordination |
| ARRL Technical Advisor / QST contributor | Owns technical-content review |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**American Radio Relay League (ARRL)** — engage via ARRL's public
technical-content submission and convention-exhibitor channels; arrl.org.

**ARES/RACES** — regional and national coordination structures; engage via
each district's public training-coordinator contact, not an individual
volunteer's personal contact.

**QST magazine** — ARRL's member publication; engage via ARRL's public
article-submission process.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Hamvention (Dayton) | Annual, May | Largest U.S. amateur radio gathering |
| QST magazine | Monthly | Ongoing technical-article channel |
| ARES/RACES district training sessions | Ongoing, district-scheduled | Operational-audience channel |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Split the hobbyist/technical audience (Hamvention, QST) from the
operational/activation-ready audience (ARES/RACES training) — both are
valuable, but they respond to different framing and different venues.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named ARRL officers, ARES/RACES coordinators, or club
officials. Identify current role-holders through ARRL's public directory or
each district's coordination structure at time of actual outreach.
