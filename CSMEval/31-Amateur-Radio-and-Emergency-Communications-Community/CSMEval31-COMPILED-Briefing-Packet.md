Every other briefing in this series spends a paragraph explaining what
ionospheric disruption does to communications. Not this one. You've been
living with R-scale radio blackouts as routine operational knowledge longer
than most of us have been writing about it. So let's skip to the interesting
part.

                 THE ONE AUDIENCE THAT DOESN'T NEED THE IONOSPHERE EXPLAINED
                 CSMEval31-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize CSM research most relevant to the amateur radio and volunteer
emergency-communications community.

1. "THE PROTONIC FRONTIER" — proton-based, GIC-immune communication and
   logic architecture research (CSMProtonics01) — early-stage, but exactly
   the kind of long-horizon idea this community's technical culture is
   built to evaluate honestly.
2. STELLAR-AEGIS EVACUATION PROTOCOL — grid-down evacuation-coordination
   framework (CSMProEvac2026), directly relevant to ARES/RACES activation
   scenarios.
3. WAY-FINDER HAPTIC/LORA NAVIGATION CONCEPT — post-GPS navigation for
   field operators (CSMFAB038).
4. NON-CONDUCTIVE FIELD EQUIPMENT/SHELTER DESIGN — Aegis Home
   building/tool suite, relevant to field radio operations (CSMFAB010-021).

WHY WE'RE LEADING WITH THE SPECULATIVE RESEARCH, NOT HIDING IT
Most audiences get the near-term hardware first and the speculative research
later, if at all. This community gets both up front, because ham radio
operators are, in practice, the most technically sophisticated general-
public audience for exactly this kind of material — you'll tell us plainly
if the protonic communication idea holds up or doesn't, and that's more
valuable to us than polite enthusiasm.

Most technically sophisticated audiences in this outreach series are also,
professionally, somewhat removed from the actual moment a disaster happens
— they research it, regulate it, or insure it. This community does something
different: when normal infrastructure fails, ARES/RACES operators are
activated, in person, that same day.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 TECHNICALLY SOPHISTICATED AND OPERATIONALLY ON-CALL, AT ONCE
                 CSMEval31-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why this community combines technical sophistication with direct
operational relevance more than any other audience in this outreach series.

KEY FACTS
• HF propagation is directly affected by the same ionospheric disturbances
  that degrade GPS — amateur radio operators track this as routine
  operational knowledge via NOAA's R-scale, not as novel information.
• ARES (Amateur Radio Emergency Service) and RACES (Radio Amateur Civil
  Emergency Service) are the volunteer emergency-communications backbone
  that activates when normal infrastructure fails — the exact scenario the
  CSM corpus is built around.
• Direct overlap exists with county-level emergency management structures
  already addressed in CSMEval01 and CSMEval08 — many ARES/RACES volunteers
  are embedded with county EOC teams.
• This is, in practice, the most technically sophisticated and most
  naturally receptive general-public audience for CSM research of any group
  in this outreach series.

WHY THIS MATTERS TO THE REST OF THIS OUTREACH SERIES
A community that both understands the physics and shows up during the
actual event is a uniquely valuable feedback channel for every hardware and
protocol concept elsewhere in this corpus — not just a distribution target.

                 DAYTON IN MAY, QST ALL YEAR
                 CSMEval31-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the publication and gathering channels best suited to reaching the
amateur radio and emergency-communications community.

PRIMARY EVENT — HAMVENTION (DAYTON)
The largest annual U.S. amateur radio gathering; engage via its public
exhibitor/presentation channel.

PUBLICATION CHANNEL — QST MAGAZINE
ARRL's member publication; a natural technical-article distribution
channel for the protonic-communications research specifically, presented
at whatever technical rigor QST's editorial standards require.

OPERATIONAL CHANNEL — ARES/RACES DISTRICT TRAINING
Presenting the evacuation-coordination framework as a training-session topic
reaches the operational, activation-ready segment of this community
directly, distinct from the hobbyist/technical segment reached via
Hamvention and QST.

REGULATORY CONTEXT (NOT AN OUTREACH TARGET)
The FCC Amateur Radio Service's rules and licensing framework provides
regulatory context for this community but is not itself an outreach channel.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. QST magazine article submission (ongoing, not tied to a single date).
2. Hamvention presentation/exhibit (May, annual).
3. ARES/RACES district training-session topic proposal, run independently
   of the hobbyist-facing channels above.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One magazine, one big weekend in May, and a training-session slot whenever a district wants one. — Office of External Affairs & Strategic Partnerships, Carrington Storm Motors | Safe Pod Engineering Company
