PURPOSE
Summarize CSM research most relevant to the amateur radio and volunteer
emergency-communications community.

1. "THE PROTONIC FRONTIER" — proton-based, GIC-immune communication and
   logic architecture research (CSMProtonics01) — early-stage, but exactly
   the kind of long-horizon idea this community's technical culture is
   built to evaluate honestly.
2. STELLAR-AEGIS EVACUATION PROTOCOL — grid-down evacuation-coordination
   framework (CSMProEvac2026), directly relevant to ARES/RACES activation
   scenarios.
3. WAY-FINDER HAPTIC/LORA NAVIGATION CONCEPT — post-GPS navigation for
   field operators (CSMFAB038).
4. NON-CONDUCTIVE FIELD EQUIPMENT/SHELTER DESIGN — Aegis Home
   building/tool suite, relevant to field radio operations (CSMFAB010-021).

WHY WE'RE LEADING WITH THE SPECULATIVE RESEARCH, NOT HIDING IT
Most audiences get the near-term hardware first and the speculative research
later, if at all. This community gets both up front, because ham radio
operators are, in practice, the most technically sophisticated general-
public audience for exactly this kind of material — you'll tell us plainly
if the protonic communication idea holds up or doesn't, and that's more
valuable to us than polite enthusiasm.
