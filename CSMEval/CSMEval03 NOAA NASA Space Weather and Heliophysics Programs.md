## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

This is the one sector on this list that is not a *customer* for CSM hardening products so much as a **primary data source and validation partner** — nearly every CSM document's design basis (Solar Cycle 25 SSN ~137 observed peak, the 1859 Carrington Event benchmark, the March 1989 Quebec blackout waveform used in NERC's own benchmark GMD event) is downstream of NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC) and NASA heliophysics data. Closing the loop — sharing the CSM corpus's applied engineering conclusions back with the agencies producing the underlying forecasts — creates a feedback channel that strengthens both the forecasting mission and the credibility of the CSM hardening research.

## 2. Agency & Organization Types in This Sector

- **NOAA Space Weather Prediction Center (SWPC)** — operational forecasting, G-scale/R-scale/S-scale alerts, Solar Cycle Progression reporting
- **NASA Heliophysics Division** — Solar Dynamics Observatory, ACE/DSCOVR satellite programs, Solar Shield transformer-vulnerability project
- **International Space Environment Services (ISES)** — international forecasting coordination consortium
- **Solar Cycle Prediction Panel** (joint NOAA/NASA/ISES/academic panel)
- **U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) Geomagnetism Program** — produces the geoelectric hazard maps referenced throughout the CSM corpus
- University-affiliated heliophysics research centers (e.g., high-altitude observatories, space weather labs)
