— it's not about hazard urgency, it's about validation urgency.

PURPOSE
Establish why this community's fabrication and prototyping speed makes it
uniquely valuable for early validation of experimental CSM concepts, ahead
of formal industrial partnership.

KEY FACTS
• Makers, EV conversion hobbyists, and DIY/3D-printing communities already
  have the additive-manufacturing, electronics-prototyping, and open-
  source-hardware skill set the CSM corpus's more experimental concepts
  are naturally suited to.
• This is the fastest, lowest-friction distribution channel for early
  feedback on whether a speculative design actually works, before investing
  in formal industrial partnership.
• Failure here is cheap and fast — a failed weekend build costs a filament
  spool and a Saturday, not a delayed product-development milestone.
• University mechatronics and robotics clubs represent a specific,
  well-organized subset of this community with institutional resources
  (faculty advisors, lab access, capstone-project structures) beyond a
  typical individual maker's garage setup.

WHY THIS ISN'T ABOUT HAZARD URGENCY
Unlike most of this outreach series, the case here isn't "act now because
of a storm" — it's "act now because early, cheap failure is more valuable
than late, expensive failure." A different kind of urgency, but a real one.
