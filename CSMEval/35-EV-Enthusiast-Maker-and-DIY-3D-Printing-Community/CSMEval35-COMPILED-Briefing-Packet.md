────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 THREE OPEN BUILD CHALLENGES
                 CSMEval35-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Present three CSM concepts as open build challenges suited to maker,
DIY-printing, and university-club fabrication skill sets.

1. "LATTICE-LOCK" METAMATERIAL FLOOR MAT — a negative-stiffness metamaterial
   lattice designed for DLS/SLS-adjacent fabrication, meaning a prosumer
   3D printer is genuinely in range for a first attempt (CSMFAB025).
2. SMART ROPE ACTUATOR — a piezoelectric segmented actuator design, now in
   its second iteration, suited to electronics-prototyping skill sets
   (CSMFAB022, 040).
3. ARCHIMEDEAN SPIRALING BIRD UAS (ASB-1) — a sub-250g research drone
   platform, sized specifically for university mechatronics/robotics club
   capstone projects (CSMFAB0114).

BONUS MATERIAL FOR THE RIGHT SUB-COMMUNITY
Airborne Archimedes screw physics research (CSMFAB0109) is relevant to
amateur rocketry and high-power model rocketry clubs specifically, and the
Aegis Embark vehicle-interior line (CSMFAB024-038) is directly relevant to
EV conversion hobbyist builds.

WHY WE'RE ASKING FOR PROTOTYPES, NOT JUST FEEDBACK
A working prototype — even a rough one — tells us something a review
comment can't: whether the idea survives contact with an actual
fabrication process. That's worth more to us than polite encouragement.

A formal industrial partnership takes months to greenlight a first
prototype. This community can sometimes build one over a weekend. That
speed differential is the entire risk-and-readiness case for this briefing
— it's not about hazard urgency, it's about validation urgency.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 FASTER THAN A FORMAL TIMELINE, CHEAPER THAN A FAILED ONE
                 CSMEval35-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why this community's fabrication and prototyping speed makes it
uniquely valuable for early validation of experimental CSM concepts, ahead
of formal industrial partnership.

KEY FACTS
• Makers, EV conversion hobbyists, and DIY/3D-printing communities already
  have the additive-manufacturing, electronics-prototyping, and open-
  source-hardware skill set the CSM corpus's more experimental concepts
  are naturally suited to.
• This is the fastest, lowest-friction distribution channel for early
  feedback on whether a speculative design actually works, before investing
  in formal industrial partnership.
• Failure here is cheap and fast — a failed weekend build costs a filament
  spool and a Saturday, not a delayed product-development milestone.
• University mechatronics and robotics clubs represent a specific,
  well-organized subset of this community with institutional resources
  (faculty advisors, lab access, capstone-project structures) beyond a
  typical individual maker's garage setup.

WHY THIS ISN'T ABOUT HAZARD URGENCY
Unlike most of this outreach series, the case here isn't "act now because
of a storm" — it's "act now because early, cheap failure is more valuable
than late, expensive failure." A different kind of urgency, but a real one.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 MAKER FAIRE, CAMPUS RECRUITING SEASON, AND ROCKETRY CLUBS
                 CSMEval35-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the events, academic channels, and specialty-club associations
best suited to distributing the three open build challenges.

PRIMARY VENUE — MAKER FAIRE
Regional and flagship events; engage via each event's public exhibitor
channel — the broadest single venue for the Lattice-Lock and Smart Rope
challenges specifically.

ACADEMIC CHANNEL — UNIVERSITY ROBOTICS/MECHATRONICS CAPSTONE PROGRAMS
The start of each academic year is the natural recruiting window for the
ASB-1 sub-250g research-drone challenge; engage via faculty advisors and
capstone-project coordinators.

SPECIALTY CLUB CHANNELS
• Tripoli Rocketry Association and National Association of Rocketry (NAR)
  — engage for the airborne Archimedes-screw physics research specifically.
• EAA (Experimental Aircraft Association)-adjacent DIY aerospace
  communities — a secondary channel for the same research thread.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Maker Faire circuit (broadest reach, most flexible scheduling).
2. University capstone-program outreach, timed to the start of each
   academic year specifically.
3. Tripoli/NAR and EAA-adjacent community engagement for the rocketry-
   specific research thread, run independently of the broader maker
   outreach.

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE HERE
Not a purchase order or a licensing deal — a build log, a forum thread, or a
capstone-project writeup showing whether the concept actually worked. That's
the whole return this briefing is asking for.
