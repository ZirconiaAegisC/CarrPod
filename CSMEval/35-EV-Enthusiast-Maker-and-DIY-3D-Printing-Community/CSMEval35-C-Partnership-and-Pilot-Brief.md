PURPOSE
Identify the events, academic channels, and specialty-club associations
best suited to distributing the three open build challenges.

PRIMARY VENUE — MAKER FAIRE
Regional and flagship events; engage via each event's public exhibitor
channel — the broadest single venue for the Lattice-Lock and Smart Rope
challenges specifically.

ACADEMIC CHANNEL — UNIVERSITY ROBOTICS/MECHATRONICS CAPSTONE PROGRAMS
The start of each academic year is the natural recruiting window for the
ASB-1 sub-250g research-drone challenge; engage via faculty advisors and
capstone-project coordinators.

SPECIALTY CLUB CHANNELS
• Tripoli Rocketry Association and National Association of Rocketry (NAR)
  — engage for the airborne Archimedes-screw physics research specifically.
• EAA (Experimental Aircraft Association)-adjacent DIY aerospace
  communities — a secondary channel for the same research thread.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Maker Faire circuit (broadest reach, most flexible scheduling).
2. University capstone-program outreach, timed to the start of each
   academic year specifically.
3. Tripoli/NAR and EAA-adjacent community engagement for the rocketry-
   specific research thread, run independently of the broader maker
   outreach.

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE HERE
Not a purchase order or a licensing deal — a build log, a forum thread, or a
capstone-project writeup showing whether the concept actually worked. That's
the whole return this briefing is asking for.
