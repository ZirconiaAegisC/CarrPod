## Purpose of This Folder

The CSM research corpus (CSMFAB, CSMHuman, CSMMech, CSMMetal, CSMVessel, CSMPro, CSMPark, CSMProtonics, CSMTest, CSMMag, CSMBiz, and the V3 Compendiums) constitutes a comprehensive, cross-disciplinary body of engineering, materials-science, biomedical, and infrastructure-resilience research built around one central risk: **a Carrington-class coronal mass ejection (CME) striking Earth during Solar Cycle 25**, and the geomagnetically induced currents (GIC), vibration/biodynamic hazards, and electromagnetic-interference conditions that follow.

This folder (`CSMEval`) is a **distribution and business-development target directory**: for every sector of society that has a genuine, time-sensitive need for this research, there is one dedicated dossier file. Each file identifies:
1. **Why the sector cannot wait** — the specific, real-world risk exposure and timing driver
2. **The company/organization types** that make up the sector
3. **The specific CSM documents, products, and folders** most relevant to that sector's pain points
4. **The job titles, departments, and organizational functions** most likely to act on this information
5. **The real trade associations, regulators, standards bodies, and conferences** that are the fastest distribution channels into the sector
6. **A suggested outreach angle** and **priority/timing rationale**

## Why Now: The Timing Case Common to All 35 Dossiers

- **Solar Cycle 25 is at/past its observed peak** — NOAA SWPC's Solar Cycle 25 Progression Report (Q1 2026) confirms an observed sunspot number peak of **SSN ~137**, exceeding the pre-cycle forecast of ~115 — a ~19% upward revision that raises the probability of a Carrington-class event materially above the design assumptions most infrastructure operators are still using.
- **The May 2024 "Gannon Storm"** was the most powerful geomagnetic storm in over 20 years (G5/Kp9), and while the bulk power grid held (thanks to NERC's TPL-007/EOP-010 protocols and NOAA early warning), it still caused an estimated **$500–565 million in U.S. agricultural losses** from GPS/GNSS disruption to precision farm equipment, and induced observable transformer, harmonic-filter, and voltage-support-equipment stress across North America.
- **Lloyd's of London** (2025 systemic risk scenario) modeled a hypothetical severe space-weather event at **$2.4 trillion in global economic losses over five years**, with a separate North American grid-specific Carrington-level scenario estimated at **$0.6–2.6 trillion**, hinging on transformer replacement lead times of up to 12 months.
- **NERC Standard TPL-007** (Transmission System Planned Performance for GMD Events) and **EOP-010** (Geomagnetic Disturbance Operations) are the current mandatory compliance framework for the North American bulk power system — but compliance is an assessment/planning obligation, not a guarantee of hardened physical infrastructure. This is precisely the gap the CSM "Aegis"/"Dielectric Citadel" product lines and CSMHuman biodynamic-safety research are designed to close.
- **Insurance brokers were placed on active alert in January 2026** following a G4 storm watch, with explicit guidance to stress-test client exposure ahead of "the big one" — meaning the underwriting and risk-transfer community is currently, actively receptive to hardening data.
