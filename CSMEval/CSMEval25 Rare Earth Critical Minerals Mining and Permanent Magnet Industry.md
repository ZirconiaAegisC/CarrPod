## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

This is one of the few sectors on this list where the CSM research aligns with an already-fast-moving, well-funded, real-world industry trend rather than needing to create urgency from scratch. Real rare-earth-free permanent magnet manufacturing is scaling right now: Niron Magnetics broke ground on its Sartell, Minnesota facility in September 2025 (targeting 1,500 tons/year of iron-nitride magnets by 2026, backed by a $52.2 million DOE/Treasury Section 48C tax credit), and MP Materials announced a $1.25 billion rare-earth magnet manufacturing campus in Northlake, Texas in February 2026. China still produces roughly 90% of global rare-earth permanent magnets, making domestic supply-chain independence a live national-security and industrial-policy issue the CSM corpus's magnet research and Aegis-Forge recycling concept both speak to directly.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **Rare-earth-free magnet manufacturers** (e.g., iron-nitride-based producers)

- **Materials recycling and e-waste reclamation companies**
- **Magnet end-users across robotics, EV motors, wind turbines, and data center cooling/power electronics** (cross-reference CSMEval12, 16, 22)
