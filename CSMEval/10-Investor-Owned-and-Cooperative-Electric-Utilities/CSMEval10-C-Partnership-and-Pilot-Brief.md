Investor-owned, cooperative, municipal, merchant-transmission — four very
different capital-approval processes, one hardware conversation. Let's match
the channel to the structure.

                 MATCHED TO YOUR OWNERSHIP STRUCTURE
                 CSMEval10-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Match outreach channel to utility ownership type, since capital-approval
processes differ meaningfully across investor-owned, cooperative, and
municipal structures.

INVESTOR-OWNED UTILITIES (IOUs)
Capital expenditure requires state PUC rate-case cost-recovery approval —
engage via EEI's annual convention and be prepared to speak to rate-case
framing directly, not just engineering merit.

GENERATION & TRANSMISSION / DISTRIBUTION COOPERATIVES
Member-owned, board-governed — engage via NRECA's TechAdvantage conference,
which is specifically engineering/technology-focused rather than the broader
annual meeting.

MUNICIPAL UTILITIES
Publicly owned, typically city-council-adjacent budget approval — engage via
APPA's annual conference.

CROSS-CUTTING TECHNICAL CHANNEL
IEEE PES General Meeting and DistribuTECH International reach engineering
staff across all three ownership types in one venue — useful for the
technical-credibility layer regardless of which ownership type you're
ultimately speaking to.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. DistribuTECH (broadest technical audience, all ownership types).
2. Ownership-specific association conference matched to the utility type.
3. Direct capital-planning conversation once the venue-level introduction is made.
