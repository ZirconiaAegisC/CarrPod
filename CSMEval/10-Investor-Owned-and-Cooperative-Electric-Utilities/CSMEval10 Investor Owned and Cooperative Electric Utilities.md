## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Utilities are the direct, individual implementers of the NERC TPL-007/EOP-010 GMD compliance regime described in CSMEval02 — but compliance assessment and physical hardening are two different budget lines, and physical hardening (as opposed to modeling/planning) is where the CSM "Aegis" product suite has the most to offer. Lloyd's of London's North American grid scenario places the cost of a true Carrington-level event at **$0.6–2.6 trillion**, hinging directly on **transformer replacement lead times of up to 12 months** — meaning any hardening option that reduces the *probability* of transformer damage (rather than just planning around its consequences) has an outsized value proposition to a utility's capital planning process.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **Investor-Owned Utilities (IOUs)** — the largest capital-budget holders, subject to state PUC rate-case cost-recovery review
- **Generation & Transmission (G&T) Cooperatives** and **Distribution Cooperatives** — member-owned, NRECA-affiliated
- **Municipal utilities** — publicly owned, APPA-affiliated
- **Transmission-only companies** — merchant transmission developers
- **Independent Power Producers (IPPs)** with owned substation/interconnection assets
