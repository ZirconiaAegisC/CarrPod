# CSMEval01 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Federal & State Emergency Management Agencies
### Ref: CSMEval01-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** This guide identifies *roles, departments, associations, and
published institutional contact channels* relevant to distributing the CSMEval01
briefing packet. It intentionally does **not** name or list specific individuals'
personal contact information. Any named person below is listed only because an
organization has itself publicly designated that person as its point of contact
for the exact category of inquiry described (e.g., conference sponsorship/press) —
the same category of information that organization publishes for any vendor,
exhibitor, or journalist who would reach out. Before any actual outreach, verify
current role-holders and confirm the organization's preferred contact process.

## 4. Suggested Channel-Matching (Not a Contact List — a Routing Guide)

- **For HMP/hazard-data content (Briefing B):** State Hazard Mitigation Officer,
  reached through the state EMA's general public inquiry line — most states
  publish this on their EMA website's "Mitigation" or "Planning" page.
- **For hardware/pilot content (Briefings A & C):** EMAC coordinator and county
  EOC managers, most efficiently reached at IAEM/NEMA conference exhibit halls
  rather than cold email — this sector's own professional culture favors
  in-person/conference-first engagement over unsolicited email, per the
  associations' own exhibitor programs existing specifically for this purpose.
- **For policy/continuity content:** DHS CISA stakeholder engagement channel
  and FEMA's National Continuity Programs public engagement contact.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named FEMA regional staff, state hazard mitigation officers, or
county EOC managers are listed here, even though these role titles are the
correct functional target. Those names change with elections, appointments, and
reorganizations, and compiling them from personal/professional profiles for a
cold-outreach list was out of scope for this guide by design. When ready to
conduct actual outreach, source current role-holders through the organization's
own published staff directory or general inquiry line at the time of send.
