PURPOSE
Quantify why a Carrington-class geomagnetic event during current Solar Cycle 25
conditions falls outside the assumptions embedded in most existing five-year
Hazard Mitigation Plans (HMPs), and why the update cycle already required under
44 CFR §201 is the natural point to close that gap.

KEY FACTS
• Solar Cycle 25 observed peak: SSN ~137 (NOAA SWPC, Q1 2026) vs. ~115 pre-cycle
  forecast — a ~19% upward revision in storm-producing solar activity.
• May 2024 "Gannon Storm" (G5/Kp9): strongest geomagnetic storm since 2003.
  GPS/GNSS outages up to two days across the U.S. Midwest; $500M+ in estimated
  U.S. agricultural losses from precision-equipment disruption. Grid impact was
  mild — this was not a worst-case event, and it still cost half a billion dollars.
• Modeled evacuation-window compression (LA County case study, CSMProEvac2026
  V2.0): traffic signal controllers fail at T+15 min; cellular base-station UPS
  depleted at T+45 min; GPS degraded by T+2 hr; major bridge deck closures by
  T+8 hr from joint seizure. Effective evacuation window: T+0 to T+45 minutes.
• Warning-time improvement available: Schumann Resonance frequency-shift
  monitoring can extend detection lead time from the current 15–45 minutes
  (standard alert systems) to 4–6 hours pre-impact — a ~6-8x improvement in
  the single variable that matters most to an evacuation order.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• HMPs updated on the standard five-year cycle without revised solar-cycle data
  carry forward a hazard assumption that is now measurably out of date.
• Continuity-of-Operations (COOP) plans built around 15-45 minute warning
  windows may not accommodate mobility-impaired population evacuation at all.
• FEMA BRIC grant cycles reward documented, quantified hazard updates — an
  unrevised HMP is a missed, not merely delayed, funding opportunity.
