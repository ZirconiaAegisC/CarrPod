PURPOSE
Identify three existing programmatic pathways — requiring no new authority or
process — through which a state or local emergency management agency could
pilot CSM hazard data or hardware.

PATHWAY 1 — HAZARD MITIGATION PLAN (HMP) UPDATE CYCLE
  What it is: the five-year hazard-assumption revision already required under
  44 CFR §201 for FEMA mitigation-grant eligibility.
  What we offer: updated Solar Cycle 25 hazard data and the LA County
  evacuation-timing case study (CSMProEvac2026) as a drop-in reference for the
  space-weather hazard section of your next HMP revision.
  Owner: State Hazard Mitigation Officer.

PATHWAY 2 — FEMA BRIC (BUILDING RESILIENT INFRASTRUCTURE AND COMMUNITIES) GRANT
  What it is: existing federal grant program funding hazard-mitigation capital
  projects, including facility hardening.
  What we offer: the Safe Refuge Center MXene-shielding retrofit specification
  (documented at $85,000 per 30,000 sq ft facility in the LA County case study)
  falls within typical BRIC facility-hardening project scopes.
  Owner: Grants/Mitigation Division.

PATHWAY 3 — EMAC MUTUAL-AID DEPOT PRE-POSITIONING
  What it is: the existing interstate Emergency Management Assistance Compact
  equipment-staging framework.
  What we offer: Aegis-Ascension Drone Backpack units are sized for the same
  depot-and-deploy model already used for other specialized mutual-aid
  equipment — no new staging doctrine required, only a new item on the shelf.
  Owner: EMAC state coordinator / regional logistics.

SUGGESTED FIRST CONVERSATION
Not a purchase order. A one-hour technical call between your hazard mitigation
planning lead and our engineering team, benchmarked against your jurisdiction's
own next HMP revision deadline — so the timing serves your calendar, not ours.
