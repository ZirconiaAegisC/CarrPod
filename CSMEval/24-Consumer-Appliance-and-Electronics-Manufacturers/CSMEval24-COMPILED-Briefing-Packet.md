Every other sector in this outreach series requires some explaining before
the pitch even starts — what's a monopole, what's cathodic protection, what's
a switchyard. Not this one. Everybody already owns a refrigerator, a
microwave, and an oven, and everybody already understands, immediately,
what "still works when the power's been out a while" would mean to them.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 A KITCHEN NOBODY HAS TO EXPLAIN
                 CSMEval24-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize the CSM non-electric/minimally-conductive kitchen and climate
appliance category redesign.

1. "STELLAR-CHILL" ELECTROCALORIC REFRIGERATOR — compressor-free
   refrigeration (CSMFAB04).
2. "SAFE-WAVE" GAN SOLID-STATE MICROWAVE — magnetron-free microwave
   generation (CSMFAB05).
3. "STONE-BAKE" OVEN / "ANCIENT HEARTH" CERAMIC COOKTOP — non-electric/
   minimal-electronics cooking (CSMFAB07, CSMFAB02, CSMFAB08).
4. INFLATABLE CHEMICALLY-BONDED PHOSPHATE CERAMIC WATER HEATER — non-
   electric water heating (CSMFAB06).
5. CONDUCTIVE-PROOF TOOL DESIGN — non-conductive small-appliance/tool
   design precedent (CSMFAB015).

WHY THIS READS AS A PRODUCT LINE EXTENSION, NOT A REPLACEMENT
Nobody's being asked to discontinue a conventional refrigerator line. This
is positioned as a resilience/emergency-preparedness product line extension
— a distinct category alongside the standard lineup, for the growing
off-grid and emergency-preparedness consumer segment.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 THE EASIEST "YES" IN THIS ENTIRE OUTREACH SERIES
                 CSMEval24-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why the consumer appliance/electronics sector represents the
lowest-friction entry point in the entire CSMEval outreach series.

KEY FACTS
• Home appliances are the most consumer-visible, easiest-to-understand entry
  point into the entire CSM resilience thesis — no specialized vocabulary
  required to explain why a non-electric water heater matters during an
  extended outage.
• The hardest part of most consumer-product development — reimagining an
  entire appliance category around new operating principles — is already
  done: electrocaloric cooling, solid-state microwave generation, and
  geothermal/air-fry-style cooking are all already-specified concepts, not
  open questions.
• A growing off-grid and emergency-preparedness appliance brand niche
  already exists in the market (cross-reference CSMEval32), meaning this
  product category has a real, if still developing, commercial precedent.
• Positioning as a product-line extension rather than a platform replacement
  removes the biggest objection a conventional appliance manufacturer would
  otherwise raise.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED (FROM A COMPETITIVE STANDPOINT)
• A manufacturer that doesn't enter this category risks ceding it entirely
  to newer, resilience-focused entrants as the off-grid/emergency-
  preparedness niche continues to grow.

New appliance categories launch at specific shows, on a predictable annual
calendar — so let's use that calendar instead of fighting it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 TIMED TO THE SHOWS THAT ALREADY LAUNCH NEW CATEGORIES
                 CSMEval24-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the trade shows and associations best suited to launching a
resilience/emergency-preparedness appliance product line extension.

PRIMARY VENUES
• CES (Consumer Technology Association) — the broadest new-product-category
  launch venue; strong fit for the more electronics-forward items (Safe-Wave
  microwave, Conductive-Proof tool design).
• International Housewares Association (IHA) Annual Show — the more
  kitchen/housewares-specific venue, strong fit for Stellar-Chill,
  Stone-Bake, and Ancient Hearth.

ASSOCIATION CHANNEL
Association of Home Appliance Manufacturers (AHAM) — engage via AHAM's
public industry-affairs channel for broader manufacturer reach beyond the
two show calendars above.

TIMING NOTE
The emergency-preparedness product angle benefits from proximity to any
widely-reported grid-outage news event — worth holding launch timing loosely
flexible around the CES/IHA calendar to catch a relevant news cycle when
possible.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. IHA (kitchen-specific fit) and CES (broader electronics fit), run in the
   same show season where possible.
2. AHAM engagement in parallel for manufacturer-association credibility.
3. Public-facing launch timing flexed toward any relevant grid-outage news
   cycle.
