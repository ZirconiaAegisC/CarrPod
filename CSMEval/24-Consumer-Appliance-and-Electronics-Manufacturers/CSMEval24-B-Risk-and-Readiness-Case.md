PURPOSE
Establish why the consumer appliance/electronics sector represents the
lowest-friction entry point in the entire CSMEval outreach series.

KEY FACTS
• Home appliances are the most consumer-visible, easiest-to-understand entry
  point into the entire CSM resilience thesis — no specialized vocabulary
  required to explain why a non-electric water heater matters during an
  extended outage.
• The hardest part of most consumer-product development — reimagining an
  entire appliance category around new operating principles — is already
  done: electrocaloric cooling, solid-state microwave generation, and
  geothermal/air-fry-style cooking are all already-specified concepts, not
  open questions.
• A growing off-grid and emergency-preparedness appliance brand niche
  already exists in the market (cross-reference CSMEval32), meaning this
  product category has a real, if still developing, commercial precedent.
• Positioning as a product-line extension rather than a platform replacement
  removes the biggest objection a conventional appliance manufacturer would
  otherwise raise.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED (FROM A COMPETITIVE STANDPOINT)
• A manufacturer that doesn't enter this category risks ceding it entirely
  to newer, resilience-focused entrants as the off-grid/emergency-
  preparedness niche continues to grow.
