# CSMEval24 — Consumer Appliance & Electronics Manufacturers
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 2: Private Industry | Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH

---

## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Home appliances are the most consumer-visible, easiest-to-understand entry point into the entire CSM resilience thesis — a refrigerator, microwave, oven, and water heater are things every household already owns and understands, unlike a transmission monopole or a GIC vulnerability assessment. The CSM corpus has already done the hardest part of consumer-product development for this sector: reimagining the entire kitchen/climate appliance category around non-electric or minimally-conductive operating principles (electrocaloric cooling instead of compressors, solid-state GaN microwave generation instead of magnetrons, geothermal/air-fry ovens instead of resistive heating elements).

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **Major home appliance manufacturers** (refrigeration, cooking, water heating)
- **Consumer electronics manufacturers** (broader EMI-shielding relevance)
- **Off-grid and emergency-preparedness appliance brands** (a growing niche category — see also CSMEval32)
- **Appliance component suppliers** (compressors, heating elements, control electronics)

## 5. Key Associations, Regulators & Events to Engage

- **Association of Home Appliance Manufacturers (AHAM)**
- **Consumer Technology Association (CTA)** — CES

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

"A complete non-electric/minimally-conductive kitchen appliance category redesign already exists — refrigeration, cooking, and water heating — positioned as a resilience/emergency-preparedness product line extension rather than a full platform replacement, suitable for CES or IHA new-product review."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**MEDIUM-HIGH.** Best timed to CES and IHA's annual show cycle; the emergency-preparedness product angle benefits from proximity to any widely-reported grid-outage news event.
