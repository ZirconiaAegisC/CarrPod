                 CSMEval30-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the facilities-engineering and clinical channels needed to reach
both halves of this sector's audience.

PRIMARY VENUE — ASHE ANNUAL CONFERENCE
American Society for Health Care Engineering; the primary facilities/
NFPA 99/Joint Commission-compliance-focused venue — the right first stop
for the extended-outage backup-power case.

CLINICAL CHANNELS (CROSS-REFERENCE CSMEval26)
• American Heart Association (AHA) Scientific Sessions and Heart Rhythm
  Society (HRS) — for the defibrillation-strategy and clinical-practice
  audience specifically.
• American College of Emergency Physicians (ACEP) — for emergency
  department clinical leadership.

REGULATORY/STANDARDS CHANNELS
• Joint Commission — accreditation standards relevant to emergency power.
• NFPA 99 Health Care Facilities Code committee — the correct venue for any
  proposal touching the backup-power standard itself.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. ASHE Annual Conference (facilities engineering, primary venue).
2. Direct biomedical-engineering department outreach at individual health
   systems, in parallel.
3. AHA/HRS/ACEP engagement for the clinical half of the audience, following
   the same pattern established in CSMEval26.
