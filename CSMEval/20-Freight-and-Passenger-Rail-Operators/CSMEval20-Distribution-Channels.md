# CSMEval20 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Freight & Passenger Rail Operators
### Ref: CSMEval20-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles and institutional channels only — no individually
named railroad or transit-authority staff are listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Act

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| Chief Signal & Communications Engineer | Owns signaling capital decisions |
| VP of Network Operations / Engineering | Owns broader capital planning |
| Director of Positive Train Control Systems | Owns PTC-specific investment |
| Rolling Stock Engineering Director | Owns crew/vehicle-side decisions |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**Association of American Railroads (AAR)** — Washington, D.C.-based; engage
via AAR's public technical-standards channel; aar.org.

**American Public Transportation Association (APTA)** — engage via APTA's
Rail Conference channel; apta.com. (See also CSMEval07 for the federal-side
cross-reference — a single conference presence can serve both dossiers.)

**Railway Association of Canada** — engage via its public industry-affairs
channel; railcan.ca.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| AAR technical committees | Ongoing | Freight-specific standards channel |
| APTA Rail Conference | Annual | Passenger/transit capital-planning audience |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Split by ownership type first (freight vs. passenger/transit) — the two
audiences rarely share a capital-approval process even when they share
track.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named railroad or transit-authority engineers. Identify
current role-holders through each organization's public directory at time
of actual outreach.
