**Scope note:** Roles and formal institutional channels only — no individually

below (SBIR/STTR portal, program-office technical-exchange requests), not
direct/personal contact with individual personnel.

## 2. Formal Institutional Channels (the Correct Way In)

**DoD SBIR/STTR Program** — the primary, formal, rolling mechanism for a small
research organization to submit a technology-readiness package. This is a
structured government portal process, not a cold-email process — outreach
should point recipients to a specific open topic, not attempt to bypass the
portal.

**DARPA / AFRL / NSWC / DTRA** — each maintains a public engagement or
"contact us" channel for unsolicited technical inquiries, distinct from
formal proposal submission; use these for an initial "is this relevant to
your portfolio" inquiry, not a full pitch.

## 4. Procurement-Integrity Note

Defense outreach is governed by specific rules around unsolicited
communications with government personnel and acquisition integrity. Any
actual outreach in this sector should go through the formal SBIR/STTR portal
or a program office's published public-engagement channel — not personal
email addresses of individual service members or civilian staff, regardless
of how those addresses might be found.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named DARPA/AFRL/NSWC/DTRA personnel, and no direct-to-person
contact strategy of any kind. This sector's correct channel is procedural,
not personal, by design.
