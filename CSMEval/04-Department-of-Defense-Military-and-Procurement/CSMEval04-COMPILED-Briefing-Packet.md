────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 DUAL-USE EM-HARDENING RESEARCH: TWO WORKED EXAMPLES
                 CSMEval04-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize two CSM dual-use engineering research examples with direct
relevance to EMP/GIC-hardening portfolios already funded across DARPA, AFRL,
NSWC, and DTRA.

1. A-10 DIELECTRIC THUNDERBOLT (CIVILIAN/MILITARY REDESIGN)
   A legacy A-10 Thunderbolt II airframe redesign study in non-inductive
   BFRP/ceramic composite materials, developed for GIC immunity. The
   underlying materials research (basalt-fiber composites, UHT ceramics)
   is the same class of research applicable to EMP-hardening of legacy
   airframes generally — a naturally occurring-hazard study with a directed-
   energy-hazard dual-use path.

2. PHANTOM MK-1 AEGIS ROBOT + ANTI-CAPTURE CHIP SYSTEM
   An EM-hardened humanoid ground robot developed under an explicit
   "Project AEGIS" framework, paired with a companion anti-capture/
   self-destruct chip architecture for forward-deployed robotics —
   addressing both survivability (EM hardening) and security (anti-
   reverse-engineering) in a single research thread.

WHY WE'RE SHOWING YOU THIS AND NOT A UTILITY COMPANY
Naturally-occurring GIC and directed-energy/HEMP effects overlap enough in
their engineering requirements that materials research developed for one has
real value for the other — this is precisely the kind of dual-use logic
SBIR/STTR solicitations are designed to surface.

Most of this outreach series is built around a hazard case — a storm, a
deadline, a regulation. This one is built around a funding-mechanism case
instead, because the honest truth is that "the sun might do something bad
someday" isn't, by itself, a defense-procurement argument. What is a
defense-procurement argument: research that solves two problems (a natural
hazard and a directed-energy hazard) with one materials program, at a moment
when budget offices are actively rewarded for finding exactly that kind of
efficiency.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 WHY DUAL-USE FRAMING SPECIFICALLY MATTERS HERE
                 CSMEval04-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why dual-use (natural-hazard / directed-energy-hazard) framing, not
urgency framing, is the correct case for this sector, and why that framing has
institutional receptivity right now.

KEY FACTS
• Naturally-occurring Carrington-class GIC and directed-energy/HEMP weapons
  effects produce overlapping engineering requirements — materials hardened
  against one class of effect frequently have direct relevance to the other.
• Legacy platforms (aircraft, ground vehicles, shipboard systems) represent
  large existing EM-vulnerability surfaces that are expensive to redesign from
  scratch but are natural candidates for materials-substitution retrofits.
• DARPA, AFRL, and service Rapid Capabilities Offices maintain standing
  interest in dual-use materials research precisely because it stretches a
  single R&D dollar across two threat categories.
• SBIR/STTR is a rolling, standing mechanism — there is no single hard
  deadline to miss, which lowers the cost of engaging early.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED (FROM OUR SIDE, NOT YOURS)
• Dual-use research presented with civilian/naturally-occurring framing alone
  risks being screened out by reviewers looking specifically for
  directed-energy relevance — hence leading with the dual-use case explicitly
  in this briefing rather than assuming it's obvious.

Defense procurement doesn't have one door — it has a lot of doors, most of
them clearly labeled, which is honestly more courtesy than most industries
extend a newcomer. Here's which ones fit a materials-research package like
this one.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 ROLLING MECHANISMS, NOT A SINGLE DOOR
                 CSMEval04-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
List the specific funding mechanisms, program offices, and trade events best
positioned to receive a dual-use EM-hardening materials research package.

FUNDING MECHANISMS
• SBIR/STTR (DoD-wide, rolling topic releases) — the standard first-contact
  mechanism for a small research organization; Phase I is explicitly designed
  for exactly this kind of early-stage technology-readiness package.
• DARPA Defense Sciences Office / Materials Program — program-manager
  technical exchange is the appropriate first contact, not a formal proposal.
• AFRL Directed Energy Directorate, EMP Hardening Branch — relevant given the
  A-10 redesign's materials overlap with EMP-hardening research.

TRADE / TECHNICAL EVENTS
• National Defense Industrial Association (NDIA) — broad defense-industrial
  base networking and technical-symposium venue.
• AFCEA (Armed Forces Communications and Electronics Association) —
  particularly relevant given CSM's Protonic Communication and robotics
  research threads.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Identify the current SBIR/STTR solicitation cycle with a materials or
   EM-hardening topic area open.
2. Submit a Phase I proposal referencing the dual-use case in Briefing B.
3. In parallel, pursue an informal DARPA/AFRL program-manager technical
   exchange — these conversations often happen before, not instead of, a
   formal proposal.
4. Use NDIA/AFCEA events for the networking layer that makes step 3 possible.
