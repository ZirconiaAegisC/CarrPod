## 1. Why This Community Cannot Wait

Citizen space-weather observation is a genuinely active contributor to real forecasting and impact-verification work — amateur aurora photographers and sunspot observers were part of the public record-keeping that helped characterize the May 2024 Gannon Storm's severity in real time, and dedicated citizen-science communities (e.g., long-running solar-cycle observation trackers) maintain some of the most detailed publicly-available sunspot/flare tracking available outside official channels. This community is a natural amplifier for Solar Cycle 25 SSN ~137 messaging and a potential direct research-collaboration partner, not just a passive audience.

## 2. Organization & Group Types in This Community

- **Local astronomy clubs** (thousands of chartered clubs, often affiliated with regional or national umbrella organizations)
- **Citizen-science solar observation communities** (dedicated solar-cycle tracking forums and data-sharing networks)
- **Aurora chasing and photography communities** (rapidly growing given recent high-visibility aurora events)
- **Amateur satellite-tracking and space-situational-awareness hobbyists** (overlap with drone/UAS-adjacent tracking interest)
