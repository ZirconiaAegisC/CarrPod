                 CSMEval33-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why this community functions as a genuine research amplifier
rather than a passive audience, and why that distinction matters for how
this outreach should be framed.

KEY FACTS
• Citizen space-weather observation is an active contributor to real
  forecasting and impact-verification work, not merely an interested
  hobbyist activity.
• Dedicated citizen-science communities maintain some of the most detailed
  publicly-available sunspot/flare tracking outside official channels — a
  genuine data resource, not just an engaged readership.
• Amateur satellite-tracking and space-situational-awareness hobbyists
  overlap with drone/UAS-adjacent tracking interest (cross-reference
  CSMEval23), extending this community's relevance beyond pure astronomy.
• This community is a natural amplifier for Solar Cycle 25 SSN ~137
  messaging specifically, given its existing tracking of the same
  underlying data.

WHY THE COLLABORATION FRAME MATTERS
Treating this community purely as a distribution channel would waste its
most valuable asset — independent, high-quality observational data that
could meaningfully strengthen the corpus's own forensic and design-basis
claims if an actual collaborative relationship develops.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 STAR PARTIES AS STANDING INFRASTRUCTURE
                 CSMEval33-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the umbrella organizations, citizen-science partnerships, and
recurring gatherings best suited to building an actual research
collaboration with this community.

UMBRELLA ORGANIZATION — ASTRONOMICAL LEAGUE
The U.S. umbrella organization for local astronomy clubs; engage via its
public outreach-coordinator channel to reach chartered clubs broadly.

COLLABORATION CHANNEL — NASA/NOAA CITIZEN-SCIENCE PARTNERSHIP PROGRAMS
Existing aurora and space-weather citizen-observation initiatives; engaging
alongside an established program lends credibility and avoids duplicating
infrastructure this community already trusts.

RECURRING GATHERINGS — REGIONAL STAR PARTIES AND ASTRONOMY FESTIVALS
A low-cost, standing, nationwide distribution and relationship-building
channel available continuously throughout the year, not tied to a single
flagship event.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Astronomical League outreach-coordinator contact (broadest reach into
   chartered clubs).
2. NASA/NOAA citizen-science partnership program engagement (credibility
   and existing-infrastructure alignment).
3. Regional star party presence, timed opportunistically around any visible
   aurora event or NOAA G-storm alert for maximum natural interest.
