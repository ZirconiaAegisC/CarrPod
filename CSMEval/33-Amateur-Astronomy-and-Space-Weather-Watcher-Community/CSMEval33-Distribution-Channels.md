# CSMEval33 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Amateur Astronomy & Space-Weather-Watcher Community
### Ref: CSMEval33-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles, clubs, and programs only — no individually named
club officers or forum moderators are listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Engage

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| Local astronomy club program directors/outreach coordinators | Own club-level engagement |
| Citizen-science solar observation network moderators/administrators | Own data-sharing network relationships |
| Aurora-chasing community group administrators | Own rapidly-growing sub-community reach |
| Regional astronomical league officers | Own regional coordination |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**Astronomical League** — U.S. umbrella organization for local astronomy
clubs; engage via its public outreach-coordinator channel; astroleague.org.

**NASA/NOAA citizen-science partnership programs** — engage via each
agency's public citizen-science program page (e.g., aurora/space-weather
observation initiatives).

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue Type | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Regional star parties and astronomy festivals | Continuous, regional circuit | Low-cost, recurring, year-round |
| Astronomical League chapter events | Ongoing | Chartered-club-level reach |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Time outreach opportunistically around visible aurora events or NOAA G-storm
alerts for maximum natural interest, but don't rely on those events as the
only entry point — this community's baseline attention is high year-round.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named club officers, network moderators, or program
administrators. Identify current role-holders through the Astronomical
League's public directory or each network's own public contact process at
time of actual outreach.
