SUBJECT:       Hours Versus Months, Said Plainly
REF:           CSMEval16-B
================================================================================

Most backup-power design assumes an outage measured in hours. A true
Carrington event's grid impact, per the Lloyd's of London scenario already
circulating in the insurance sector (see CSMEval11), hinges on transformer
replacement lead times of up to twelve months. Those are not the same
planning problem, and treating them as the same planning problem is where
this briefing wants to spend its four minutes.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 HOURS VERSUS MONTHS, SAID PLAINLY
                 CSMEval16-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish the mismatch between typical data-center backup-power design
assumptions and true Carrington-event grid-outage timescales.

KEY FACTS
• Most current backup-power design assumes short utility outages (hours),
  consistent with typical grid-reliability events.
• A true Carrington-class event's grid impact (transformer damage,
  multi-day-to-multi-week regional outages) is precisely the failure mode
  data-center site-hardening design is supposed to survive, but at a
  duration well beyond hours-scale planning.
• Every other sector in this outreach series increasingly depends on data
  centers being up — GPS/GNSS correction services, insurance underwriting
  systems, and utility SCADA/EMS platforms all run on cloud/colocation
  infrastructure.
• Rare-earth permanent magnet supply is explicitly identified by magnet
  manufacturers themselves as an "AI Infrastructure & Data Centers"
  application category, tying critical-minerals supply-chain risk directly
  to this sector.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• A facility designed to hours-scale backup assumptions has no meaningful
  answer to a months-scale grid event beyond eventual fuel/generator
  exhaustion.
• Every downstream sector depending on this one's uptime inherits the same
  exposure, magnified by however many other systems depend on it.

The single best moment to influence a data center's hardening specification
is before the concrete is poured — which means the channels below matter more
for their timing than for their size.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 CATCHING THE BUILDOUT WHILE IT'S STILL ON THE DRAFTING TABLE
                 CSMEval16-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the standards bodies and conferences best positioned to reach
site-hardening decision-makers during the current data-center buildout.

STANDARDS/CERTIFICATION CHANNEL — UPTIME INSTITUTE
Data center resilience standards and certification body; engage via their
public standards-development and Tier certification review channels — the
most credibility-bearing single channel in this sector.

CONFERENCE CHANNELS
• Data Center Dynamics (DCD) conference series — broad industry reach.
• 7x24 Exchange International — critical facilities reliability association,
  specifically focused on uptime/reliability engineering.
• AFCOM — data center management professional association.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Uptime Institute engagement first (standards credibility carries into
   every other conversation).
2. DCD conference presence (broadest audience, new-build decision-makers).
3. 7x24 Exchange and AFCOM in parallel (reliability-engineering and
   management audiences respectively).

WHY SPEED MATTERS MORE THAN USUAL HERE
Once a facility is built, GIC-shielding and non-conductive-envelope
retrofits become dramatically more expensive and disruptive. This is one of
the few sectors on this list where "we'll get to it next year" has a real,
quantifiable cost difference attached.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Before the concrete, not after — that's the whole timing argument. — Office of External Affairs & Strategic Partnerships, Carrington Storm Motors | Safe Pod Engineering Company
