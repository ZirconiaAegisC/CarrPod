The single best moment to influence a data center's hardening specification
is before the concrete is poured — which means the channels below matter more
for their timing than for their size.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 CATCHING THE BUILDOUT WHILE IT'S STILL ON THE DRAFTING TABLE
                 CSMEval16-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the standards bodies and conferences best positioned to reach
site-hardening decision-makers during the current data-center buildout.

STANDARDS/CERTIFICATION CHANNEL — UPTIME INSTITUTE
Data center resilience standards and certification body; engage via their
public standards-development and Tier certification review channels — the
most credibility-bearing single channel in this sector.

CONFERENCE CHANNELS
• Data Center Dynamics (DCD) conference series — broad industry reach.
• 7x24 Exchange International — critical facilities reliability association,
  specifically focused on uptime/reliability engineering.
• AFCOM — data center management professional association.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Uptime Institute engagement first (standards credibility carries into
   every other conversation).
2. DCD conference presence (broadest audience, new-build decision-makers).
3. 7x24 Exchange and AFCOM in parallel (reliability-engineering and
   management audiences respectively).

WHY SPEED MATTERS MORE THAN USUAL HERE
Once a facility is built, GIC-shielding and non-conductive-envelope
retrofits become dramatically more expensive and disruptive. This is one of
the few sectors on this list where "we'll get to it next year" has a real,
quantifiable cost difference attached.
