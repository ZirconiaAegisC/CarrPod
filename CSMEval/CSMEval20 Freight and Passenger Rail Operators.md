# CSMEval20 — Freight & Passenger Rail Operators
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 2: Private Industry | Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH

---

## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

This dossier is the private-sector companion to CSMEval07 (the federal/Amtrak regulatory angle): the actual capital-budget decisions for catenary, signaling, and rolling-stock hardening are made by the freight and commuter rail operating companies themselves, not by FRA. Continuous-welded rail is a long-conductor GIC pathway with the same physics as a transmission line, and modern Positive Train Control (PTC) systems add a GNSS/timing dependency that mirrors the same GPS-disruption risk documented in the May 2024 Gannon Storm's effect on farm equipment.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **Class I freight railroads** (the large national/continental carriers)
- **Short line and regional freight railroads**
- **Commuter rail authorities** (diesel and electrified)
- **Urban light rail / metro operators** (third-rail and catenary systems)
- **Rolling stock manufacturers**
