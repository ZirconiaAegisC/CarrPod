# CSMEval08 — Municipal Emergency Management, Public Works & Parks/Attraction-Safety Departments
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 1: Government & Public Sector | Priority: MEDIUM

---

## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

City- and county-level government sits at the intersection of every hazard on this list — grid, water, transportation, and public-assembly-facility safety all land on municipal desks during an actual event, even though the causal hazard (a solar storm) originates far above any local jurisdiction's control. Municipalities that own or regulate high-density public-assembly infrastructure (amusement parks, fairgrounds, civic arenas) have an additional dual hazard the CSM corpus explicitly addresses: combined GIC exposure and flood/structural risk at large mechanically complex sites. The corpus contains a fully worked example — a named regional amusement park — that is directly reusable as a template for any comparable municipal or quasi-municipal attraction.

## 2. Agency & Organization Types in This Sector

- **City/County Offices of Emergency Management** (local tier beneath state EMAs — see CSMEval01)
- **Municipal Public Works Departments** — water, sanitation, and local grid-interface infrastructure
- **City/County Parks and Recreation Departments** — where municipalities own or co-regulate amusement/attraction infrastructure
- **Local Building & Safety / Fire Marshal offices** — permitting authority over ride/attraction electrical and structural systems
- **Special districts** (water districts, joint powers authorities) with municipal-adjacent infrastructure responsibility

## 4. Target Contacts — Roles & Departments to Reach

- City/County Emergency Manager
- Director of Public Works
- Parks and Recreation Director / Attractions Safety Officer
- Chief Building Official / Fire Marshal (ride and electrical permitting)
- City Engineer

## 5. Key Associations, Regulators & Events to Engage

- **National League of Cities (NLC)** and **National Association of Counties (NACo)**
- **International Association of Amusement Parks and Attractions (IAAPA)** (also see CSMEval15)
- **American Public Works Association (APWA)** annual congress
- State associations of city/county officials (state-level chapters)

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

"We've already completed a full Carrington-and-flood dual-hazard resilience redesign for a major regional amusement park — this is directly adaptable as a template briefing for any municipality with comparable public-assembly infrastructure under its permitting authority."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**MEDIUM.** Best distributed through NLC/NACo annual conferences and APWA's congress; less urgent than state/federal tiers but valuable as a broad, low-friction distribution layer given the sheer number of municipal entities.
