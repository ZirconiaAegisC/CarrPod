# CSMEval30 — Healthcare Systems & Hospital Facilities Management
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 2: Private Industry | Priority: CRITICAL

---

## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Hospitals are one of the few facility types where a power/communications outage converts almost immediately into a direct, individual safety-of-life risk — ventilators, dialysis, surgical suites, and cardiac monitoring/defibrillation equipment all depend on continuous power, and current hospital backup-generator design (per Joint Commission/NFPA 99 requirements) is generally sized for outages measured in hours to a few days, not the extended, transformer-replacement-timescale regional outages a true Carrington event implies (see CSMEval11's Lloyd's scenario data). This sector also directly intersects with the CSMHuman research corpus's clinical content: cardiac defibrillation strategy (DSED/VC), ultrasonic diagnostic vibrometry, and vibration-exposure biomechanics are all active hospital biomedical-engineering and clinical-practice topics.
