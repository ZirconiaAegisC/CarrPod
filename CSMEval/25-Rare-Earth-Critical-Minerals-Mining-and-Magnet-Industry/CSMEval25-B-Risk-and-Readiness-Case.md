PURPOSE
Establish why this sector's national-security and industrial-policy framing
creates an unusually well-funded and well-timed moment for CSM's magnet and
critical-materials research contribution.

KEY FACTS
• China still produces roughly 90% of global rare-earth permanent magnets —
  a concentration level that makes domestic supply-chain independence a live
  national-security and industrial-policy issue, not just a business
  strategy question.
• Niron Magnetics' Sartell, MN facility (groundbreaking September 2025) is
  backed by a $52.2 million DOE/Treasury Section 48C tax credit — direct
  evidence of active federal policy support for exactly this kind of
  domestic capacity.
• MP Materials' $1.25 billion Northlake, TX campus (announced February
  2026) demonstrates private capital is moving at scale alongside the
  federal policy support.
• Magnet end-users across robotics (CSMEval22), automotive/EV (CSMEval12),
  and data centers (CSMEval16) all have a direct stake in this supply
  chain's resilience — this sector's success has ripple effects across at
  least three other dossiers in this series.

WHY THIS MATTERS BEYOND THE MAGNET INDUSTRY ITSELF
A recycling/reclamation process (Aegis-Forge) and a supply-chain landscape
map aren't just useful to magnet producers — they're useful to every
downstream sector depending on magnet availability, several of which are
also targets in this outreach series.
