PURPOSE
Summarize CSM magnet and critical-materials research as a complementary
contribution to an already-active domestic supply-chain scaling effort.

1. NEW US MAGNET COMPANIES IDENTIFIED — domestic magnet supply-chain
   landscape mapping (CSMMagEco2026).
2. RARE-EARTH-FREE MAGNET TECHNOLOGY SURVEY — technology-landscape research
   (CSMMagTech 2026).
3. AEGIS-FORGE THERMAL CASCADE INDUCTIVE MATERIAL SEPARATOR — critical-
   materials recycling/reclamation process (CSMFAB0108).
4. MATERIALS-SCIENCE SUPPLY-CHAIN RISK ASSESSMENT — critical minerals and
   rare-earth supply chain section of the 6-part Materials Study.

WHAT'S ACTUALLY HAPPENING RIGHT NOW IN THIS INDUSTRY
Niron Magnetics broke ground on its Sartell, Minnesota facility in September
2025 (targeting 1,500 tons/year of iron-nitride magnets by 2026, backed by a
$52.2 million DOE/Treasury Section 48C tax credit). MP Materials announced a
$1.25 billion rare-earth magnet manufacturing campus in Northlake, Texas in
February 2026. China still produces roughly 90% of global rare-earth
permanent magnets.

WHY WE'RE OFFERING RESEARCH, NOT A PRODUCT
This corpus doesn't compete with either announced facility — it's a
complementary research contribution (landscape mapping, recycling process,
supply-chain risk data) offered alongside an already-fast-moving industry
trend.

                 CSMEval25-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why this sector's national-security and industrial-policy framing
creates an unusually well-funded and well-timed moment for CSM's magnet and
critical-materials research contribution.

KEY FACTS
• China still produces roughly 90% of global rare-earth permanent magnets —
  a concentration level that makes domestic supply-chain independence a live
  national-security and industrial-policy issue, not just a business
  strategy question.
• Niron Magnetics' Sartell, MN facility (groundbreaking September 2025) is
  backed by a $52.2 million DOE/Treasury Section 48C tax credit — direct
  evidence of active federal policy support for exactly this kind of
  domestic capacity.
• MP Materials' $1.25 billion Northlake, TX campus (announced February
  2026) demonstrates private capital is moving at scale alongside the
  federal policy support.
• Magnet end-users across robotics (CSMEval22), automotive/EV (CSMEval12),
  and data centers (CSMEval16) all have a direct stake in this supply
  chain's resilience — this sector's success has ripple effects across at
  least three other dossiers in this series.

WHY THIS MATTERS BEYOND THE MAGNET INDUSTRY ITSELF
A recycling/reclamation process (Aegis-Forge) and a supply-chain landscape
map aren't just useful to magnet producers — they're useful to every
downstream sector depending on magnet availability, several of which are
also targets in this outreach series.
