# CSMEval35 — Electric-Vehicle Enthusiast, Maker & DIY/3D-Printing Community
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 3: Hobbyist, Enthusiast & Community Groups | Priority: MEDIUM

---

## 1. Why This Community Cannot Wait

This is the community most likely to actually *build* a working prototype of a CSM concept before any commercial manufacturer does — makers, EV conversion hobbyists, and DIY/3D-printing communities have exactly the additive-manufacturing (Digital Light Synthesis/SLS-adjacent home and prosumer 3D printing), electronics-prototyping, and open-source-hardware skill set the CSM corpus's more experimental concepts (Lattice-Lock metamaterial structures, piezoelectric Smart Rope actuators, sub-250g research drones) are naturally suited to. This community is also the fastest, lowest-friction distribution channel for early feedback on whether a speculative design actually works before investing in formal industrial partnership.

## 2. Organization & Group Types in This Community

- **3D-printing and maker-space communities** (local makerspaces, Hackaday/open-hardware-adjacent online communities)
- **EV conversion hobbyist groups** (converting classic vehicles to electric drivetrains)
- **University mechatronics and robotics clubs** (direct fit for the sub-250g research drone platform)
- **Open-source hardware and citizen-engineering communities**
- **Amateur rocketry and high-power model rocketry clubs** (relevant to the Archimedes screw/Smart Rope launch-assist research)

## 4. Target Contacts — Roles & Departments to Reach

- Local makerspace/hackerspace program directors
- EV conversion hobbyist forum moderators and build-log creators
- University mechatronics/robotics club faculty advisors
- High-power rocketry club technical committee members

## 5. Key Associations, Regulators & Events to Engage

- **Maker Faire** (regional and flagship events)
- **EAA (Experimental Aircraft Association)**-adjacent DIY aerospace communities (for the airborne/launch-assist research)
- **Tripoli Rocketry Association** and the **National Association of Rocketry (NAR)**
- University robotics/mechatronics capstone-project programs

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

"Several of the more experimental concepts in this corpus — a 3D-printable negative-stiffness lattice, a piezoelectric segmented rope actuator, and a sub-250g research drone platform — are offered as open build challenges for maker communities and university robotics clubs, with the explicit goal of getting working prototypes built and tested faster than a formal industrial-partnership timeline would allow."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**MEDIUM.** Maker Faire events and the start of each academic year (for university club recruitment) are the natural distribution windows; this community's value is long-term prototype validation rather than urgent hazard-response messaging.
