# CSMEval05 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## State DOTs, Bridge Authorities & Highway Agencies
### Ref: CSMEval05-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles, associations, and institutional channels only — no
individually named DOT staff or consultancy principals are listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Act

| Role | Where Found | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Chief Bridge Engineer / Director of Structures | State DOT | Owns bridge asset-management decisions |
| Technical Subcommittee Chair | AASHTO Committee on Bridges and Structures | Owns cross-state technical standards discussion |
| Principal/Associate, Structural Resilience Practice | Engineering consultancies (e.g., firms like T.Y. Lin International) | Advises multiple DOT/authority clients at once |
| Resilience Program Lead | FHWA Office of Bridges and Structures | Federal-level program/grant channel |
| Chief Engineer | Regional bridge authority | Owns toll-bridge/authority-specific decisions |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**AASHTO (American Association of State Highway and Transportation
Officials)** — engage via AASHTO's Committee on Bridges and Structures
technical-committee process; Washington, D.C.-based; aashto.org.

**ASCE (American Society of Civil Engineers) Structural Engineering
Institute (SEI)** — engage via SEI's technical-committee and conference
paper-submission channels; asce.org.

**FHWA Office of Bridges and Structures** — engage via FHWA's public
technical-assistance and Bridge Preservation/Resilience program channels;
fhwa.dot.gov.

**International Bridge Conference (IBC)** — annual technical conference,
Pittsburgh-founded, now run by the Engineers' Society of Western
Pennsylvania — engage via the conference's paper/exhibit submission channel.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| AASHTO Annual Meeting | Annual | Committee on Bridges and Structures session |
| ASCE/SEI Structures Congress | Annual | Peer-reviewed technical presentation |
| International Bridge Conference | Annual | Bridge-engineering-specific technical/trade venue |
| FHWA Bridge Preservation program cycles | Rolling | Federal resilience-funding channel |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Engineering consultancies (structural resilience practices at major firms)
are a useful force-multiplier channel — a single firm relationship can surface
the methodology to multiple DOT/authority clients. Engage via the firm's own
business-development contact process, not an individual engineer's direct
line.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named DOT bridge engineers or consultancy principals — even
though the existing CSM corpus already references one firm by name as an
audit recipient, this guide intentionally does not extend that into a
personal-contact list for unrelated firms.
