## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Long-span steel bridges are, electromagnetically, enormous unintentional antennas: continuous steel girders, cables, and rebar form low-resistance paths that concentrate telluric/GIC current exactly the way transmission lines do — a risk category bridge engineers have historically not modeled at all, because GMD/GIC analysis has been a power-systems discipline, not a structural one. This corpus already contains **city-specific, fully worked resilience audits** for the Bay Area, London, Pittsburgh, and Seattle, plus a global infrastructure resilience audit addressed directly to a named international structural engineering firm — meaning the outreach packet for this sector does not need to be built from scratch; it exists and is ready to route to the equivalent department in any additional jurisdiction.

- **American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials (AASHTO)**
