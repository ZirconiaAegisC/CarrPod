Every national lab already has a formal front door for exactly this kind of
conversation. We just need to knock on it correctly, which turns out to be
the easy part.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 THE TECHNOLOGY TRANSFER OFFICE IS THE DOOR
                 CSMEval09-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the formal technology-transfer pathways and technical conferences
best suited to initiating a national-lab validation relationship.

PRIMARY PATHWAY — LAB TECHNOLOGY TRANSFER OFFICES
Sandia, NREL, Lawrence Livermore, Oak Ridge, and university-affiliated FFRDCs
each maintain a formal CRADA/licensing technology-transfer office — the
correct, designed-for-this-purpose entry point for proposing an independent
validation or co-development relationship.

MATERIALS-SPECIFIC PATHWAY — DOE CRITICAL MATERIALS INSTITUTE
Direct mission overlap with the rare-earth-free magnet and supply-chain
research; engage via CMI's annual review process.

CONFERENCE PATHWAYS
• Materials Research Society (MRS) Spring/Fall meetings — broad
  materials-science peer audience.
• American Ceramic Society (ACerS) Annual Meeting — UHT ceramics-specific
  peer audience.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Identify the one or two labs whose existing portfolio most closely matches
   a specific CSM materials claim (e.g., Sandia for EM effects/pulsed power,
   NREL for critical materials).
2. Approach that lab's Technology Transfer office directly with a specific,
   narrow validation request — not the full corpus at once.
3. Use MRS/ACerS conference presentations to build the broader peer-review
   record in parallel.
