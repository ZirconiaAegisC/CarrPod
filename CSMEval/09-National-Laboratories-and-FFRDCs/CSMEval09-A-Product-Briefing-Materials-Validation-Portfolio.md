Here's a genuinely refreshing kind of outreach email: one where the entire ask
is "please try to find out if we're wrong." Independent validation isn't a
threat to a materials-science claim — it's the only thing that turns a claim
into a fact. Four claims below, offered in exactly that spirit.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 FOUR MATERIALS CLAIMS, OFFERED FOR INDEPENDENT SCRUTINY
                 CSMEval09-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize four CSM materials-science research threads suited to national
laboratory technical validation and potential CRADA-style partnership.

1. ULTRA-HIGH-TEMPERATURE CERAMIC / COMPOSITE CLAIMS
   A 6-part Materials Study documenting UHT ceramic and composite material
   properties used throughout the CSM hardware corpus — a natural fit for
   independent lab-scale validation.

2. RARE-EARTH-FREE MAGNET TECHNOLOGY
   Magnet technology research aimed at reducing rare-earth dependency —
   directly overlapping with DOE Critical Materials Institute's existing
   portfolio and mission.

3. CRITICAL-MINERALS SUPPLY CHAIN RESEARCH
   Research identifying new/emerging US magnet-material producers — relevant
   to critical-minerals supply-chain independence work already underway at
   several national labs.

4. AEGIS-FORGE THERMAL CASCADE INDUCTIVE MATERIAL SEPARATOR
   A materials recycling/reclamation process (critical-minerals recovery via
   thermal/inductive separation) — a process-validation candidate for labs
   with materials-recycling research portfolios.

WHY THIS BRIEFING READS DIFFERENTLY FROM THE REST OF THE SERIES
Every other sector in this outreach series gets asked to consider adopting
something. This sector is asked to consider testing something — and to tell
us, plainly, where it doesn't hold up. That's a more valuable relationship to
us than a sale would be.

NEXT STEP

what gives the rest of the outreach effort its credibility.
