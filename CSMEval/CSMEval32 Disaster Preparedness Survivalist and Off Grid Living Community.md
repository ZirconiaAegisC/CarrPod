# CSMEval32 — Disaster-Preparedness, Survivalist & Off-Grid Living Community
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 3: Hobbyist, Enthusiast & Community Groups | Priority: HIGH

## 1. Why This Community Cannot Wait

This community is the direct consumer-market analog of every institutional sector on this list combined into a single household: off-grid living practitioners already prioritize non-electric appliances, independent water/power systems, and reduced dependency on grid/GPS infrastructure — meaning the entire Aegis Home product philosophy (electrocaloric refrigeration, GaN solid-state microwave, geothermal ovens, non-electric water heating) maps almost one-to-one onto this community's existing values and purchasing behavior, without requiring the "why should I care about Carrington events" education step other consumer audiences need.

## 2. Organization & Group Types in This Community

- **Disaster-preparedness ("prepper") online communities and forums**
- **Off-grid living and homesteading associations**
- **Survivalist training and skills-course organizations**
- **Tiny-house and self-sufficient-living builder networks** (overlapping interest in non-conductive/independent building systems)
- **Faith-based and community-organization disaster-preparedness ministries** (many churches and community groups run organized preparedness programs)

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

"Every value this community already holds — independence from the grid, non-electric appliances, self-sufficient building systems — is exactly what the Aegis Home product philosophy was built around, just with a specific, well-documented hazard (Carrington-class GIC) as the design driver rather than general self-sufficiency."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**HIGH.** This community's content-consumption habits (YouTube, podcasts, forums) mean distribution can happen continuously rather than around a single event; interest spikes predictably after any publicized grid-outage or solar-storm news cycle.
