## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

This sector is, right now, the most actively receptive audience on this entire list. **Lloyd's of London published a systemic risk scenario in March 2025** modeling a hypothetical severe solar storm at **$2.4 trillion in global economic losses over five years** (expected direct insurance loss ~$17 billion), and as recently as **January 2026**, the trade press explicitly reported insurers and brokers being placed on alert following a G4 storm watch, with guidance to "stress-test clients' reliance on power, satellites, and timing-sensitive supply chains" ahead of "the big one." This is not a sector that needs to be convinced the risk exists — it is a sector actively building underwriting models and looking for hardening/mitigation data that can inform pricing, exclusions, and client risk-engineering services.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **Lloyd's of London syndicates** — the specialist marketplace insuring nearly a third of global space risk
- **Global reinsurers** (e.g., the major Bermuda/European reinsurance groups) modeling systemic/catastrophic scenarios
- **Specialty insurers** writing energy, satellite, aviation, marine, and business-interruption lines
- **Insurance brokers** advising corporate clients on space-weather exposure
- **Catastrophe modeling firms** (the analytical vendors that build the loss models insurers price against)
- **Risk engineering / loss control consultancies** retained by large insureds
