## 1. Why This Community Cannot Wait

Amateur ("ham") radio operators are the one community on this entire list that already treats space weather as routine operational knowledge — HF propagation is directly affected by the same ionospheric disturbances that degrade GPS, and radio blackouts (NOAA's R-scale) are a standard part of amateur-radio situational awareness. This community is also the backbone of volunteer emergency communications (ARES/RACES) that activates when normal infrastructure fails — precisely the scenario the CSM corpus is built around. Ham radio operators are, in practice, the most technically sophisticated and most naturally receptive general-public audience for this material of any group on this list.

## 2. Organization & Club Types in This Community

- **National amateur radio societies** — e.g., the American Radio Relay League (ARRL) in the U.S.
- **Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES)** and **Radio Amateur Civil Emergency Service (RACES)** local groups — the operational emergency-communications arm
- **Local and regional ham radio clubs** (thousands of chartered clubs nationally)
- **Emergency Operations Center (EOC) volunteer radio teams** embedded with county EM agencies (direct overlap with CSMEval01/08)
- **Skywarn and weather-spotter networks** with overlapping volunteer membership

## 4. Target Contacts — Roles & Departments to Reach

- ARRL Section Emergency Coordinator (per state/section)
- Local ARES/RACES District Emergency Coordinator
- Club Emergency Communications Officer (per local club)
- ARRL Technical Advisor / Space Weather column contributors (ARRL publishes ongoing propagation/space-weather content)

## 5. Key Associations, Regulators & Events to Engage

- **American Radio Relay League (ARRL)** — national convention (Hamvention/Dayton is the largest annual U.S. gathering)
- **ARES/RACES** regional and national coordination structures
- **QST magazine** (ARRL's member publication — a natural technical-article distribution channel)
- **FCC Amateur Radio Service** rules and licensing framework (regulatory context, not an outreach target)

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

"This community already understands ionospheric disruption and GPS degradation better than almost any other public audience — we're offering our GIC-immune communications research and evacuation-coordination framework as a technical-article submission to QST or a presentation topic for ARES/RACES district training sessions."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**HIGH.** Hamvention (annual, May) and ARRL's ongoing publication cycle are the best distribution channels; this community's baseline interest is high year-round given its existing space-weather awareness culture.
