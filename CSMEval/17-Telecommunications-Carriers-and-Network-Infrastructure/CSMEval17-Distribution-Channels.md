# CSMEval17 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Telecommunications Carriers & Network Infrastructure Providers
### Ref: CSMEval17-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles and institutional channels only — no individually named
carrier staff or FCC officials are listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Act

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| VP of Network Engineering / Network Resilience | Owns hardening specification |
| Director, Network Innovation Labs / Advanced Technology Group | Owns long-horizon research intake |
| Chief Network Officer | Owns overall resilience strategy |
| Public Safety Communications Program Manager | Owns FirstNet-adjacent resilience |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**FCC Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau** — engage via the FCC's
public stakeholder-engagement and rulemaking-comment channels; fcc.gov.

**CTIA** — wireless industry trade association; engage via CTIA's public
policy/technical channel; ctia.org.

**NTCA – The Rural Broadband Association** — engage via NTCA's public
membership/conference channel; ntca.org.

**Alliance for Telecommunications Industry Solutions (ATIS)** — standards
body; engage via ATIS's public standards-committee process; atis.org.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| ATIS standards committees | Ongoing | Correct venue for hardening standards proposals |
| CTIA events | Annual + ongoing | Broad wireless-carrier reach |
| NTCA events | Annual + ongoing | Rural/regional carrier reach |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Split outreach deliberately: the near-term hardening pitch belongs with
standards bodies and trade associations; the protonic-communications
research belongs with individual carriers' network-innovation labs, which
don't have a single shared association-level channel.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named carrier engineers or FCC staff. Identify current
role-holders through each organization's public directory at time of actual
outreach.
