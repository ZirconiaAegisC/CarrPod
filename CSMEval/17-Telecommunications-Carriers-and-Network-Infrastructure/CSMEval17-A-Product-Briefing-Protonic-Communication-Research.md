Your networks are the thing everyone needs restored first after a storm, and
also one of the things a storm damages most directly — buried copper,
aerial lines, and HF radio propagation are all exposed to the same physics
that takes down the grid. That's the near-term briefing. The far-term one is
stranger, and we think that's a compliment to it.

PURPOSE
Summarize near-term hardening options and one long-horizon exploratory
research thread relevant to telecom network resilience.

NEAR-TERM
1. NON-CONDUCTIVE CENTRAL OFFICE/SWITCHING FACILITY MATERIALS — Aegis-C
   composite shielding and non-conductive building suite (CSMFAB01,
   CSMFAB010).
2. POST-GPS TIMING/SYNCHRONIZATION RESILIENCE — the Way-Finder LoRa/haptic
   navigation concept, relevant to GNSS-dependent network timing
   (CSMFAB038).

LONG-HORIZON RESEARCH THREAD
3. "THE PROTONIC FRONTIER" — a proton (H⁺)-based communication and logic
   architecture, explicitly framed as a GIC-immune alternative to
   electron/copper-based systems. We're not claiming commercial maturity.
   We're claiming this is exactly the kind of exploratory research a
   network-innovation lab tracks regardless of near-term deployability.

WHY WE'RE NOT OVERSELLING THE PROTONIC RESEARCH
It would be easy to wrap "proton-based communication" in more confidence
than the research currently supports. We'd rather undersell it accurately
than oversell it and lose credibility on the near-term hardening pitch that
sits right next to it.
