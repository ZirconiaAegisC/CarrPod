Every other sector in this series gets to be one thing at a time — victim or
responder, hazard-exposed or hazard-relevant. Telecom doesn't get that
simplicity, and the risk case here is really just naming that plainly.

                 EVERYONE CALLS YOU FIRST
                 CSMEval17-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why telecom's dual role as hazard victim and emergency-response
enabler warrants a distinct, higher-priority hardening case than a typical
infrastructure sector.

KEY FACTS
• Long buried/aerial copper and fiber runs with copper armor/repeaters are
  GIC-exposed by the same long-conductor physics affecting transmission
  lines, bridges, and pipelines.
• HF radio propagation is directly disrupted by the same ionospheric effects
  that degrade GPS — a second, independent telecom-specific exposure
  pathway.
• Communications are the first thing emergency management, utilities, and
  the public need restored after any major event — meaning a telecom
  network's own recovery timeline sets the pace for every other sector's
  recovery, not just its own.
• Public-safety networks (FirstNet-adjacent systems) carry this dependency
  in its most acute form — a network outage there isn't just an
  inconvenience, it's a first-responder capability gap.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• A telecom outage compounds every other sector's recovery timeline in this
  outreach series — grid crews, water utilities, and emergency managers all
  depend on working communications to coordinate their own response.
• Near-term hardening gaps here have second-order effects nowhere else on
  this list carries as directly.
