also one of the things a storm damages most directly — buried copper,

Summarize near-term hardening options and one long-horizon exploratory
research thread relevant to telecom network resilience.

NEAR-TERM
1. NON-CONDUCTIVE CENTRAL OFFICE/SWITCHING FACILITY MATERIALS — Aegis-C
   composite shielding and non-conductive building suite (CSMFAB01,
   CSMFAB010).
2. POST-GPS TIMING/SYNCHRONIZATION RESILIENCE — the Way-Finder LoRa/haptic

   (CSMFAB038).

LONG-HORIZON RESEARCH THREAD
3. "THE PROTONIC FRONTIER" — a proton (H⁺)-based communication and logic
   architecture, explicitly framed as a GIC-immune alternative to
   electron/copper-based systems. We're not claiming commercial maturity.

than the research currently supports. We'd rather undersell it accurately
than oversell it and lose credibility on the near-term hardening pitch that
sits right next to it.

Every other sector in this series gets to be one thing at a time — victim or
responder, hazard-exposed or hazard-relevant. Telecom doesn't get that
simplicity, and the risk case here is really just naming that plainly.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 EVERYONE CALLS YOU FIRST
                 CSMEval17-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why telecom's dual role as hazard victim and emergency-response
enabler warrants a distinct, higher-priority hardening case than a typical
infrastructure sector.

KEY FACTS
• Long buried/aerial copper and fiber runs with copper armor/repeaters are
  GIC-exposed by the same long-conductor physics affecting transmission
  lines, bridges, and pipelines.
• HF radio propagation is directly disrupted by the same ionospheric effects
  that degrade GPS — a second, independent telecom-specific exposure
  pathway.
• Communications are the first thing emergency management, utilities, and
  the public need restored after any major event — meaning a telecom
  network's own recovery timeline sets the pace for every other sector's
  recovery, not just its own.
• Public-safety networks (FirstNet-adjacent systems) carry this dependency
  in its most acute form — a network outage there isn't just an
  inconvenience, it's a first-responder capability gap.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• A telecom outage compounds every other sector's recovery timeline in this
  outreach series — grid crews, water utilities, and emergency managers all
  depend on working communications to coordinate their own response.
• Near-term hardening gaps here have second-order effects nowhere else on
  this list carries as directly.

Near-term hardening and long-horizon research need different rooms — let's

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 TWO SPEEDS, TWO CHANNELS
                 CSMEval17-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Match near-term hardening and long-horizon protonic-communications research
to the channels best suited to each.

NEAR-TERM HARDENING CHANNEL
• CTIA (wireless industry trade association) — engage via CTIA's public
  policy/technical-committee channel.
• NTCA – The Rural Broadband Association — relevant for rural/regional
  carrier infrastructure specifically.
• Alliance for Telecommunications Industry Solutions (ATIS) — standards-
  development body, the correct venue for any hardening standard proposal.

LONG-HORIZON RESEARCH CHANNEL
• Carrier Network Innovation Labs / Advanced Technology Groups — direct
  engagement, positioned as a 12-24 month relationship rather than a single
  pitch, consistent with the protonic-communications research's own maturity
  level.

REGULATORY/PUBLIC-SAFETY CHANNEL
• FCC Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau — relevant specifically for
  FirstNet-adjacent public-safety communications resilience.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. ATIS standards engagement for near-term hardening (fastest path to
   technical adoption).
2. CTIA/NTCA conference presence for broader carrier reach.
3. Network Innovation Lab outreach for the protonic research thread, paced
   as its own longer relationship, not bundled into the near-term pitch.
