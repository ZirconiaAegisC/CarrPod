Near-term hardening and long-horizon research need different rooms — let's
send each to the right one.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 TWO SPEEDS, TWO CHANNELS
                 CSMEval17-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Match near-term hardening and long-horizon protonic-communications research
to the channels best suited to each.

NEAR-TERM HARDENING CHANNEL
• CTIA (wireless industry trade association) — engage via CTIA's public
  policy/technical-committee channel.
• NTCA – The Rural Broadband Association — relevant for rural/regional
  carrier infrastructure specifically.
• Alliance for Telecommunications Industry Solutions (ATIS) — standards-
  development body, the correct venue for any hardening standard proposal.

LONG-HORIZON RESEARCH CHANNEL
• Carrier Network Innovation Labs / Advanced Technology Groups — direct
  engagement, positioned as a 12-24 month relationship rather than a single
  pitch, consistent with the protonic-communications research's own maturity
  level.

REGULATORY/PUBLIC-SAFETY CHANNEL
• FCC Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau — relevant specifically for
  FirstNet-adjacent public-safety communications resilience.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. ATIS standards engagement for near-term hardening (fastest path to
   technical adoption).
2. CTIA/NTCA conference presence for broader carrier reach.
3. Network Innovation Lab outreach for the protonic research thread, paced
   as its own longer relationship, not bundled into the near-term pitch.
