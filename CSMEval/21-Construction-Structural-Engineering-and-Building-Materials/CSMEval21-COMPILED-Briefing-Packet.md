Every hardening idea in this entire outreach series eventually needs someone
to actually specify, procure, and pour it. That's this sector. The good news:
we're not asking you to invent new chemistry. Basalt rebar and geopolymer
binders both already have real, if niche, commercial supply chains.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 MATERIALS ADJACENT TO WHAT YOU ALREADY SELL
                 CSMEval21-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize CSM non-conductive building materials, with emphasis on their
proximity to existing commercial supply chains rather than novel chemistry.

1. BFRP REBAR — Basalt-Fiber-Reinforced-Polymer rebar fabrication plan
   (CSMFAB09), positioned against ACI 440.11-25 compliance already
   referenced within the CSM corpus.
2. AEGIS GEOPOLYMER CONCRETE — ZrB₂ nano-whisker-doped dielectric concrete
   formulation, addressing pumpability alongside dielectric performance
   (CSMFAB0107).
3. NON-METALLIC STRUCTURAL MONOPOLE/TOWER PRECEDENT — composite power-line
   monopole (CSMFAB0105), a real-load structural proof point.
4. FULL NON-CONDUCTIVE BUILDING SYSTEMS CATALOG — piping, wiring, hardware,
   and ventilation suite (CSMFAB010-020), plus aerogel thermal-break
   insulation (CSMFAB03).

WHY "ADJACENT TO EXISTING SUPPLY CHAINS" IS THE WHOLE PITCH
Materials that require inventing an entire new manufacturing base take a
decade to reach a real project. Materials adjacent to an existing supply
chain can reach a real project in a much shorter window — which is exactly
the argument this sector, more than any other on this list, can actually
evaluate and act on.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 EVERY OTHER SECTOR ON THIS LIST IS ACTUALLY WAITING ON YOU
                 CSMEval21-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why this sector has the shortest realistic path from research to
real-project specification of any sector in the outreach series, and why
that makes it disproportionately high-leverage.

KEY FACTS
• BFRP rebar and geopolymer concrete binders both have existing, if niche,
  commercial supply chains — not blue-sky chemistry requiring new
  manufacturing infrastructure.
• The CSM corpus already references ACI 440.11-25 compliance internally for
  BFRP rebar, meaning the standards-alignment work has already started, not
  just the materials research.
• Structural engineering consultancies (the corpus already names T.Y. Lin
  International as a resilience-audit recipient, see CSMEval05) are a
  natural force-multiplier channel — one firm relationship surfaces
  materials options to multiple clients at once.
• Concrete and rebar manufacturers face a genuine strategic choice here:
  treat BFRP as a threat to conventional steel rebar, or as a
  diversification opportunity into a new product category.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• Every hardening idea across the other 34 dossiers in this series is
  ultimately gated by this sector's willingness to specify and build with
  non-conductive materials — a bottleneck this sector may not realize it
  represents.
• Incumbent-material manufacturers who treat BFRP purely as competitive
  threat risk ceding the diversification opportunity to a newer entrant.

This sector's real gatekeepers are standards committees more than any
single conference — so the channel list below leads with committees on
purpose.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 COMMITTEE WORK, NOT JUST TRADE SHOWS
                 CSMEval21-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the standards committees and trade shows best positioned to move
BFRP rebar and geopolymer concrete from research to real-project
specification.

PRIMARY CHANNEL — ACI COMMITTEE 440 (FRP REINFORCEMENT)
Directly relevant to BFRP rebar adoption pathways; the CSM corpus already
references ACI 440.11-25 compliance internally, making this the most
natural first committee engagement.

SECONDARY CHANNELS
• International Code Council (ICC) — building-code adoption pathway,
  necessary for any material to move from "permitted in some jurisdictions"
  to broadly specifiable.
• American Institute of Steel Construction (AISC) — the incumbent-material
  counterpoint/dialogue partner; worth engaging directly rather than
  avoiding, since steel/BFRP coexistence (not replacement) is the more
  realistic near-term outcome.

TRADE SHOW CHANNELS
• World of Concrete — concrete-industry-specific audience.
• Greenbuild — sustainability-framed audience, relevant given geopolymer
  concrete's lower-carbon profile as a secondary selling point.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. ACI Committee 440 engagement (fastest path to a technical, standards-
   literate audience).
2. World of Concrete / Greenbuild trade show presence (broader
   contractor/specifier reach).
3. ICC code-adoption engagement as a longer, parallel track.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Multi-year adoption timeline, started today instead of next year — that's the entire ask. — Office of External Affairs & Strategic Partnerships, Carrington Storm Motors | Safe Pod Engineering Company
