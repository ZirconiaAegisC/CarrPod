# CSMEval21 — Construction, Structural Engineering & Building Materials Industry
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 2: Private Industry | Priority: HIGH

---

## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Every hardening product in the CSM corpus — from bridges to nuclear switchyards to amusement parks — ultimately depends on this sector actually being able to specify, procure, and build with the non-conductive materials the research proposes. Basalt-Fiber-Reinforced-Polymer (BFRP) rebar and dielectric geopolymer concrete are not speculative chemistry; they are adjacent to materials already in limited commercial use (basalt rebar and geopolymer binders both have existing, if niche, commercial supply chains), meaning this sector has the shortest realistic path from "interesting research" to "spec'd on a real project" of any sector on this list.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **General contractors and construction management firms** (especially those specializing in critical infrastructure)
- **Structural engineering consultancies** — the corpus already names T.Y. Lin International as a resilience-audit recipient (see CSMEval05)
- **Concrete and rebar manufacturers** (conventional steel rebar producers who may see BFRP as either threat or diversification opportunity)
- **Composite materials manufacturers** (basalt fiber, GFRP, geopolymer binder producers)
- **Building code and standards organizations**

## 4. Target Contacts — Roles & Departments to Reach

- Principal / Director of Structural Resilience Practice (engineering consultancies)
- VP of Preconstruction / Materials Innovation (general contractors)
- Product Development Director (composite/rebar manufacturers)
- Building code committee member (ICC, ACI committees)

## 5. Key Associations, Regulators & Events to Engage

- **American Concrete Institute (ACI)** — Committee 440 (FRP reinforcement) is directly relevant to BFRP rebar adoption pathways; note the CSM corpus already references ACI 440.11-25 compliance internally
- **International Code Council (ICC)**
- **American Institute of Steel Construction (AISC)** (as the incumbent-material counterpoint/dialogue partner)
- **World of Concrete** and **Greenbuild** trade shows

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

"BFRP rebar and dielectric geopolymer concrete are adjacent to materials with existing commercial supply chains, not blue-sky chemistry — this corpus offers a GIC-resilience specification rationale that ACI Committee 440 members and structural engineering firms can evaluate against current FRP reinforcement standards work."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**HIGH.** ACI committee cycles and World of Concrete/Greenbuild trade shows are the natural entry points; this sector's adoption timeline is realistically multi-year, so early positioning matters more than speed.
