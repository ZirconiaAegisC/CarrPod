Read back through this outreach series' other 34 dossiers and a pattern
shows up: bridges, nuclear switchyards, amusement parks, data centers, water
utilities — nearly every hardening idea eventually resolves into "someone
needs to build this with non-conductive materials." That someone is this
sector.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 EVERY OTHER SECTOR ON THIS LIST IS ACTUALLY WAITING ON YOU
                 CSMEval21-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why this sector has the shortest realistic path from research to
real-project specification of any sector in the outreach series, and why
that makes it disproportionately high-leverage.

KEY FACTS
• BFRP rebar and geopolymer concrete binders both have existing, if niche,
  commercial supply chains — not blue-sky chemistry requiring new
  manufacturing infrastructure.
• The CSM corpus already references ACI 440.11-25 compliance internally for
  BFRP rebar, meaning the standards-alignment work has already started, not
  just the materials research.
• Structural engineering consultancies (the corpus already names T.Y. Lin
  International as a resilience-audit recipient, see CSMEval05) are a
  natural force-multiplier channel — one firm relationship surfaces
  materials options to multiple clients at once.
• Concrete and rebar manufacturers face a genuine strategic choice here:
  treat BFRP as a threat to conventional steel rebar, or as a
  diversification opportunity into a new product category.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• Every hardening idea across the other 34 dossiers in this series is
  ultimately gated by this sector's willingness to specify and build with
  non-conductive materials — a bottleneck this sector may not realize it
  represents.
• Incumbent-material manufacturers who treat BFRP purely as competitive
  threat risk ceding the diversification opportunity to a newer entrant.

NEXT STEP
Briefing C identifies ACI, ICC, and the World of Concrete/Greenbuild trade
shows as the channels most likely to move this from research to spec sheet.
