This sector's real gatekeepers are standards committees more than any
single conference — so the channel list below leads with committees on
purpose.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 COMMITTEE WORK, NOT JUST TRADE SHOWS
                 CSMEval21-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the standards committees and trade shows best positioned to move
BFRP rebar and geopolymer concrete from research to real-project
specification.

PRIMARY CHANNEL — ACI COMMITTEE 440 (FRP REINFORCEMENT)
Directly relevant to BFRP rebar adoption pathways; the CSM corpus already
references ACI 440.11-25 compliance internally, making this the most
natural first committee engagement.

SECONDARY CHANNELS
• International Code Council (ICC) — building-code adoption pathway,
  necessary for any material to move from "permitted in some jurisdictions"
  to broadly specifiable.
• American Institute of Steel Construction (AISC) — the incumbent-material
  counterpoint/dialogue partner; worth engaging directly rather than
  avoiding, since steel/BFRP coexistence (not replacement) is the more
  realistic near-term outcome.

TRADE SHOW CHANNELS
• World of Concrete — concrete-industry-specific audience.
• Greenbuild — sustainability-framed audience, relevant given geopolymer
  concrete's lower-carbon profile as a secondary selling point.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. ACI Committee 440 engagement (fastest path to a technical, standards-
   literate audience).
2. World of Concrete / Greenbuild trade show presence (broader
   contractor/specifier reach).
3. ICC code-adoption engagement as a longer, parallel track.
