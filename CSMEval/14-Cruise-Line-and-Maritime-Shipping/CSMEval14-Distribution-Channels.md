## 1. Roles Most Likely to Act

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| VP of Newbuild & Innovation (cruise line) | Owns design specification for new vessels |
| Chief Naval Architect / VP of Fleet Engineering | Owns technical retrofit feasibility |
| Director of Marine Operations Safety & Resilience | Owns operational risk framing |
| Classification society Technical Committee lead, hull materials | Owns standards approval pathway |
| Shipyard Chief Engineer, new-build programs | Owns construction-phase implementation |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**Cruise Lines International Association (CLIA)** — engage via CLIA's public
conference/exhibitor channel; cruising.org.

**Society of Naval Architects and Marine Engineers (SNAME)** — engage via
SNAME's public technical-paper submission channel; sname.org.

**DNV, ABS, Lloyd's Register** — each classification society maintains a
public technical-forum/rules-development process for proposed materials and
design standards; engage each society's own public channel directly.

**International Maritime Organization (IMO)** — engage via IMO's public
member-state and NGO-consultative-status channels for longer-horizon
standards engagement; imo.org.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| CLIA events | Annual/ongoing | Cruise-industry operator audience |
| SNAME technical meetings | Annual | Naval architecture/marine engineering peer audience |
| Classification society technical forums | Ongoing | Standards-track, longer horizon |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Time operator outreach to announced newbuild programs when possible — this
is the single highest-leverage moment to influence design specifications,
and it's public information (newbuild announcements are press events by
design).

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named cruise-line executives, naval architects, or
classification-society reviewers. Identify current role-holders through each
organization's public leadership page at time of actual outreach.
