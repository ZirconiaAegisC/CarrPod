A cruise ship is, in miniature, a self-sufficient coastal city — its own
power, water, medical care, food storage, and several thousand people who
cannot simply walk home. It's also a steel hull sitting in seawater, which is
close to the ideal geometry for concentrating GIC current. We already built
the full resilience retrofit for one of the largest fleets in the world.
Here's what's in it.

                 CSMEval14-A | June 2026 | v1.0

Summarize "Project Noah's Aegis" and the broader CSMVessel research corpus,

   strategy, Carnival Corporation anchor case study.

unquestioned default. Whether or not every vessel needs a full amphibious
redesign, the underlying materials case deserves a look.

A land-based facility that loses power still has a "outside" to evacuate to.
A vessel mid-voyage doesn't have that option in the same way — which is
exactly why this sector's risk case reads differently than any onshore
facility's.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 EVERYTHING A COASTAL CITY NEEDS, WITH NOWHERE TO WALK TO
                 CSMEval14-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why maritime GIC exposure combines infrastructure-scale
consequence (independent power/water/medical systems, thousands of people)
with a uniquely limited evacuation option compared to onshore facilities.

KEY FACTS
• A large cruise vessel independently generates its own power, produces its
  own water, and maintains onboard medical facilities and food storage — a
  self-sufficient infrastructure profile comparable to a small coastal city.
• A steel hull in seawater is an efficient GIC current sink — the same
  physics that makes bridges and pipelines GIC-exposed, applied to a vessel
  that, unlike a bridge, is also carrying its full population at the moment
  of exposure.
• Classification societies (DNV, ABS, Lloyd's Register) are the technical-
  standards gatekeepers for any hull/systems redesign — meaning any
  hardening pathway must eventually clear a classification review, not just
  an operator's internal engineering sign-off.
• The CSM maritime research corpus is, by volume, one of the largest and
  most mature product lines in the entire repository, built around a real
  named anchor case study rather than a hypothetical vessel.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• Mid-voyage grid-down conditions remove the "evacuate to shore
  infrastructure" option available to most onshore facilities facing the
  same hazard.
• Classification-society review timelines mean hardening pathways not
  already in a standards conversation face a longer runway than onshore
  retrofits.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 TWO TRACKS: OPERATORS NOW, CLASSIFICATION SOCIETIES IN PARALLEL
                 CSMEval14-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the operator-facing and standards-facing channels needed to move
the fleet-wide retrofit methodology from one anchor case study to broader
industry adoption.

TRACK 1 — OPERATOR ENGAGEMENT
• Cruise Lines International Association (CLIA) — the primary cruise-
  industry trade association; engage via CLIA's public conference/exhibitor
  channel, timed to newbuild program announcements when possible.
• Direct VP of Newbuild & Innovation outreach at operators with announced
  newbuild programs — the highest-leverage single moment to influence
  design specifications before steel is cut.

TRACK 2 — CLASSIFICATION SOCIETY / STANDARDS ENGAGEMENT (LONGER HORIZON)
• DNV, ABS, Lloyd's Register — each maintains public technical forums for
  proposed materials and design standards; engage each society's technical
  committee process directly, in parallel with operator conversations, since
  this track moves on a longer clock regardless of operator interest.
• Society of Naval Architects and Marine Engineers (SNAME) — technical peer
  venue bridging operators, shipyards, and classification societies.

LONGER-HORIZON TRACK — IMO
International Maritime Organization standards engagement is the longest-
horizon track here — worth beginning now precisely because it's slow, not
instead of the faster tracks.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. CLIA + operator newbuild-program outreach (fastest potential traction).
2. SNAME technical session (peer credibility bridging all stakeholders).
3. Classification-society and IMO engagement, running in parallel throughout,
   not sequenced after the others.
