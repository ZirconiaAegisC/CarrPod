## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

A cruise ship is, in miniature, everything a coastal city needs to survive a grid-down event — independent power generation, water production, medical facilities, food storage, and thousands of people who cannot simply walk home — except it is also a steel-hulled vessel sitting in seawater, which is precisely the geometry that makes a hull an efficient GIC current sink. The CSM corpus's maritime research is, by volume, one of the largest and most mature product lines in the entire repository, built explicitly around a named real-world company (Carnival Corporation) as the anchor case study, plus an entirely separate biomimetic-vehicle fleet concept (the Charlemagne Class) exploring amphibious GIC-immune designs from first principles.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **Cruise line operators** — the corpus's own anchor example is Carnival Corporation; the same framework extends to any major cruise brand
- **Classification societies** (e.g., DNV, ABS, Lloyd's Register) — the technical-standards gatekeepers for any hull/systems redesign
- **Shipyards and naval architecture firms**
- **Marine electrical systems integrators**
- **Ferry operators and other passenger-vessel operators** with similar hull/systems exposure at smaller scale
