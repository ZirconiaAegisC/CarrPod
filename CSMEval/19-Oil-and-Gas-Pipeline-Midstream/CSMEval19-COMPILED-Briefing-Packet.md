SUBJECT:       Cathodic Protection Wasn't Built for This Century's Storm
REF:           CSMEval19-A
================================================================================

Cathodic protection systems were designed to fight ordinary soil corrosion,
patiently, over decades. Nobody designed them to fight a sudden telluric
current spike from a geomagnetic storm — and pipe-to-soil potential doesn't
politely wait for the corrosion engineer's normal maintenance cycle when
that happens.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 CATHODIC PROTECTION WASN'T BUILT FOR THIS CENTURY'S STORM
                 CSMEval19-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize CSM hardening options addressing GIC-driven cathodic-protection
disruption and accelerated corrosion in pipeline infrastructure.

1. AEGIS FUEL INFRASTRUCTURE HARDENING PROPOSAL — a dedicated framework
   addressing GIC-driven cathodic-protection disruption and pipe-to-soil
   potential swings at joints and valve stations.
2. AEGIS GFRP PIPELINE SYSTEM — a glass-fiber-reinforced-polymer pipe
   alternative removing the steel-pipe GIC pathway at the material level
   (CSMMetal0106 / CSMFAB0106).
3. NON-CONDUCTIVE VALVE/FITTING AND FACILITY HARDWARE — dielectric building
   materials suite applicable to pump and valve stations (CSMFAB010-016).

WHY FUEL CONTINUITY IS A FORCE MULTIPLIER, NOT JUST A SECTOR CONCERN
Every emergency-response sector on this list — generators, emergency
vehicles, evacuation transport — depends on fuel distribution continuing to
function. Hardening this sector doesn't just protect your own assets; it
protects the response capacity of every sector reading the rest of this
outreach series.

SUBJECT:       A Well-Documented Risk That Rarely Gets the Microphone
REF:           CSMEval19-B
================================================================================

GIC's effect on pipelines isn't a novel claim we're introducing to the
conversation — it's a well-documented exposure pathway that just doesn't get
the same public attention as grid or bridge GIC risk. That's less about the
physics and more about which sectors have historically had a louder
microphone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 A WELL-DOCUMENTED RISK THAT RARELY GETS THE MICROPHONE
                 CSMEval19-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish GIC-driven cathodic-protection disruption as a well-documented,
if under-discussed, pipeline integrity risk, and its force-multiplier
relevance to broader disaster response.

KEY FACTS
• Long steel pipelines are a well-documented GIC exposure pathway distinct
  from the power-grid case — induced currents accelerate cathodic-
  protection-system disruption and pipe-to-soil potential swings.
• This contributes to accelerated corrosion at exactly the joints and valve
  stations operators depend on for leak prevention — a safety and
  environmental concern, not just an asset-integrity one.
• Fuel distribution is a first-order dependency for every other emergency-
  response sector in this outreach series (generators, emergency vehicles,
  evacuation transport).
• NACE International (now AMPP) has decades of corrosion-engineering
  expertise directly applicable to GIC/cathodic-protection interaction —
  meaning this isn't a hazard category without an existing professional
  home, just one that hasn't yet connected space-weather data to its own
  standard practice.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• Under-discussed doesn't mean low-consequence — accelerated corrosion at
  joints and valve stations is a leak-prevention concern with real safety
  and environmental stakes.
• A sector whose own hazard doesn't get much public airtime risks being
  systematically under-resourced relative to its actual downstream
  importance to disaster response broadly.

The nice thing about this sector: corrosion engineers already have exactly
the technical vocabulary needed to evaluate this risk. We're not introducing
a new discipline — we're introducing a new input to a discipline that
already exists.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 CORROSION ENGINEERS ALREADY HAVE THE RIGHT VOCABULARY
                 CSMEval19-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the standards bodies and conferences best suited to introducing
GIC/cathodic-protection interaction data to pipeline corrosion-engineering
and integrity professionals.

PRIMARY CHANNEL — AMPP (FORMERLY NACE INTERNATIONAL)
Corrosion engineering professional association; directly relevant given its
existing cathodic-protection expertise — engage via AMPP's Annual Conference
technical-session submission channel.

INDUSTRY CHANNELS
• American Petroleum Institute (API) — standards and annual conferences;
  broader midstream-industry reach.
• Pipeline Research Council International (PRCI) — research-consortium
  channel, well suited to a technical-validation conversation.
• Association of Oil Pipe Lines (AOPL) — policy/advocacy-focused industry
  association.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. AMPP Annual Conference (correct technical audience, existing
   vocabulary/expertise).
2. PRCI research-consortium engagement in parallel (validation pathway).
3. API standards-committee cycle for broader industry standard-setting
   relevance.
