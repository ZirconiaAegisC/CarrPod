## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**AMPP (Association for Materials Protection and Performance, formerly NACE
International)** — engage via AMPP's public Annual Conference technical-
session channel; ampp.org.

**American Petroleum Institute (API)** — engage via API's public standards-
committee and conference channels; api.org.

**Pipeline Research Council International (PRCI)** — engage via PRCI's
public research-membership channel; prci.org.

**Association of Oil Pipe Lines (AOPL)** — engage via AOPL's public
industry-affairs channel; aopl.org.

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

Lead with AMPP specifically — this sector's corrosion engineers already have
the technical framework to evaluate a GIC/cathodic-protection claim quickly,
making it the fastest path to a substantive technical conversation.
