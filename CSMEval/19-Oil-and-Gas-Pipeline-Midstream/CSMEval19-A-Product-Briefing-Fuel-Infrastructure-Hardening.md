REF:           CSMEval19-A

Cathodic protection systems were designed to fight ordinary soil corrosion,
patiently, over decades. Nobody designed them to fight a sudden telluric
current spike from a geomagnetic storm — and pipe-to-soil potential doesn't
politely wait for the corrosion engineer's normal maintenance cycle when
that happens.

PURPOSE
Summarize CSM hardening options addressing GIC-driven cathodic-protection
disruption and accelerated corrosion in pipeline infrastructure.

1. AEGIS FUEL INFRASTRUCTURE HARDENING PROPOSAL — a dedicated framework
   addressing GIC-driven cathodic-protection disruption and pipe-to-soil
   potential swings at joints and valve stations.
2. AEGIS GFRP PIPELINE SYSTEM — a glass-fiber-reinforced-polymer pipe
   alternative removing the steel-pipe GIC pathway at the material level
   (CSMMetal0106 / CSMFAB0106).
3. NON-CONDUCTIVE VALVE/FITTING AND FACILITY HARDWARE — dielectric building
   materials suite applicable to pump and valve stations (CSMFAB010-016).
