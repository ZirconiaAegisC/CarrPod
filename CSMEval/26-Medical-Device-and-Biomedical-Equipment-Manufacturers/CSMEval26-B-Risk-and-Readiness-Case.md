Most of this outreach series is timed to a solar cycle, a storm season, or a
regulatory review window. This sector's interest doesn't work that way, and
pretending otherwise would misrepresent both the science and the audience.

                 EVERGREEN, NOT STORM-CYCLE
                 CSMEval26-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Explain why medical device manufacturers' interest in this research corpus
is evergreen rather than tied to a specific solar-cycle timing driver, and
why that changes how this briefing should be read.

KEY FACTS
• This sector is unique in this outreach series: rather than needing
  introduction to underlying science, medical device manufacturers are the
  direct commercial audience for a large share of the CSMHuman research
  corpus's own citations.
• The DSED/VC defibrillation evidence base and ultrasonic vibrometry
  diagnostic literature are both active, evolving clinical topics
  independent of any space-weather framing — the CSMHuman corpus happens to
  track them as part of its broader research scope.
• Regulatory Affairs relevance (FDA 510(k)/PMA pathway) means any literature
  synthesis offered here needs to be held to a higher evidentiary standard
  than a general-audience briefing — worth naming plainly rather than
  glossing over.

WHY WE'RE NOT MANUFACTURING URGENCY HERE, EITHER
Like the aerospace/defense advanced-concepts sector (CSMEval13), this
briefing has no artificial clock. Clinical literature synthesis is valuable
whenever it's current and accurate — not more valuable during a storm
season, less valuable outside of one.
