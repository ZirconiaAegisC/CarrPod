Most of this outreach series is timed to a solar cycle, a storm season, or a
regulatory review window. This sector's interest doesn't work that way, and
pretending otherwise would misrepresent both the science and the audience.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 EVERGREEN, NOT STORM-CYCLE
                 CSMEval26-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Explain why medical device manufacturers' interest in this research corpus
is evergreen rather than tied to a specific solar-cycle timing driver, and
why that changes how this briefing should be read.

KEY FACTS
• This sector is unique in this outreach series: rather than needing
  introduction to underlying science, medical device manufacturers are the
  direct commercial audience for a large share of the CSMHuman research
  corpus's own citations.
• The DSED/VC defibrillation evidence base and ultrasonic vibrometry
  diagnostic literature are both active, evolving clinical topics
  independent of any space-weather framing — the CSMHuman corpus happens to
  track them as part of its broader research scope.
• Regulatory Affairs relevance (FDA 510(k)/PMA pathway) means any literature
  synthesis offered here needs to be held to a higher evidentiary standard
  than a general-audience briefing — worth naming plainly rather than
  glossing over.

WHY WE'RE NOT MANUFACTURING URGENCY HERE, EITHER
Like the aerospace/defense advanced-concepts sector (CSMEval13), this
briefing has no artificial clock. Clinical literature synthesis is valuable
whenever it's current and accurate — not more valuable during a storm
season, less valuable outside of one.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No storm needed for this one to matter. Good data matters on its own schedule. — Office of External Affairs & Strategic Partnerships, Carrington Storm Motors | Safe Pod Engineering Company

This sector already has its own well-established annual technical calendar
— we're just asking to be a cross-reference at the right stop on it.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 THE CLINICAL CALENDAR, NOT OUR CALENDAR
                 CSMEval26-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the clinical and industry-association venues best suited to
cross-referencing the CSMHuman literature synthesis against practicing
clinical affairs and R&D teams.

PRIMARY VENUE — AHA SCIENTIFIC SESSIONS
American Heart Association's annual meeting; the primary distribution event
for the DSED/VC defibrillation-strategy literature specifically.

SECONDARY VENUES
• Heart Rhythm Society (HRS) Annual Meeting — cardiac rhythm management
  audience specifically.
• AdvaMed — medical device industry trade association; broader
  manufacturer-side reach beyond the clinical societies.
• AAMI (Association for the Advancement of Medical Instrumentation) —
  relevant for the instrumentation/diagnostic-equipment angle specifically.
• American College of Emergency Physicians (ACEP) — relevant for the
  clinical-practice side of defibrillation-strategy literature.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. AHA Scientific Sessions (broadest, highest-credibility single venue).
2. HRS Annual Meeting (cardiac-rhythm-specific technical audience).
3. AdvaMed and AAMI in parallel (manufacturer/instrumentation-side reach).
