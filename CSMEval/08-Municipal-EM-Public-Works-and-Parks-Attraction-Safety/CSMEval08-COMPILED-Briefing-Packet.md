────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 PROJECT AEGIS-K, AND WHAT IT TEACHES EVERY OTHER MUNICIPALITY
                 CSMEval08-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize Project Aegis-K and related CSM research directly transferable to
municipal public-assembly infrastructure, evacuation planning, and water/
building-materials continuity.

1. PROJECT AEGIS-K (KNOTT'S BERRY FARM DIELECTRIC/AMPHIBIOUS REFABRICATION)
   A dual-hazard (GIC + flood) resilience redesign for a major regional
   amusement park — dense mechanical/electrical infrastructure, high public
   occupancy, and a documented, reusable template for comparable municipal or
   quasi-municipal attractions.

2. STELLAR-AEGIS EVACUATION PROTOCOL (LOS ANGELES COUNTY)
   County-level evacuation protocol template, already built and ready to
   adapt — the same case study referenced in the emergency-management sector
   briefing series, relevant here at the county/city implementation level.

3. AEGIS-HYDRO WATER SYSTEM RESILIENCE
   Municipal water-system continuity research, relevant to Public Works
   departments responsible for water/sanitation infrastructure.

4. NON-CONDUCTIVE MUNICIPAL BUILDING MATERIALS
   BFRP rebar, geopolymer concrete, and the broader non-conductive building
   suite (CSMFAB09, CSMFAB0107, CSMFAB010) — applicable to any public
   facility a municipality builds or retrofits.

WHY THE TEMPLATE MATTERS MORE THAN THE ORIGINAL PROJECT
The value to you isn't that one amusement park got hardened — it's that the
methodology used to harden it now exists in reusable form, ready for whatever
public-assembly infrastructure sits in your own jurisdiction.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 EVERYONE ELSE'S HAZARD BECOMES YOUR TUESDAY
                 CSMEval08-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Explain why municipal government sits at the intersection of every hazard in
this outreach series, and why a dual-hazard (GIC + flood/structural) template
is particularly valuable at this level.

KEY FACTS
• Municipalities that own or regulate high-density public-assembly
  infrastructure (amusement parks, fairgrounds, civic arenas) face a combined
  GIC exposure and flood/structural risk at large, mechanically complex sites
  — a dual-hazard profile most single-hazard planning doesn't address.
• The Project Aegis-K case study demonstrates this dual-hazard methodology
  already worked, at real scale, for a named regional attraction.
• Local Building & Safety / Fire Marshal offices already hold permitting
  authority over ride/attraction electrical and structural systems — meaning
  the regulatory hook to apply this methodology already exists, without new
  authority being required.
• Solar Cycle 25's confirmed SSN ~137 peak keeps every upstream hazard in this
  outreach series (grid, rail, bridges) live simultaneously — and all of them
  ultimately land on municipal desks in a real event.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• Municipal-level planning that treats "space weather" as someone else's
  federal/state responsibility misses the local implementation gap this
  briefing addresses directly.
• Public-assembly infrastructure with combined GIC and flood/structural
  exposure is a higher-consequence gap than either hazard modeled alone.

There are thousands of municipalities in this sector, which means the right
strategy isn't a thousand individual pitches — it's a small number of venues
that already gather most of them in one place, one time a year.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 REACHING A LOT OF CITIES WITHOUT A LOT OF MEETINGS
                 CSMEval08-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the broad distribution channels best suited to reaching a large
number of municipal entities efficiently, rather than a jurisdiction-by-
jurisdiction outreach campaign.

BROADEST CHANNEL — NLC / NACo ANNUAL CONFERENCES
The National League of Cities (NLC) and National Association of Counties
(NACo) each hold annual conferences reaching city/county officials broadly —
the right venue for a general-audience introduction to this research corpus,
even for municipalities without an attraction/park hazard specifically.

ATTRACTION-SPECIFIC CHANNEL — IAAPA
The International Association of Amusement Parks and Attractions is the
direct-fit channel for the Project Aegis-K template specifically (also
referenced in the amusement-park sector's own briefing series, CSMEval15) —
worth engaging once, with material useful to both audiences.

OPERATIONS CHANNEL — APWA ANNUAL CONGRESS
The American Public Works Association's annual congress reaches the water,
sanitation, and public-infrastructure staff most relevant to the Aegis-Hydro
and non-conductive building-materials research.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. IAAPA (narrowest, highest-fit audience for the flagship case study).
2. APWA (operations-focused audience for water/materials research).
3. NLC/NACo (broadest reach, general-audience introduction).
