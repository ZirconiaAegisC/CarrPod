# CSMEval09 — National Laboratories & Federally Funded R&D Centers
## Outreach & Distribution Dossier
### Prepared June 2026 | Tier 1: Government & Public Sector | Priority: HIGH

---

## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

National laboratories are the natural technical-validation and scale-up partner for nearly every materials-science claim in the CSM corpus — ultra-high-temperature ceramics, MXene EMI coatings, rare-earth-free magnetics, and inductive-separation/recycling processes are all active national-lab research portfolios in their own right. This sector differs from the others on this list: the goal is not persuasion about urgency but **technical partnership and independent validation**, which is exactly what gives the rest of the CSM outreach effort credibility with harder-to-convince audiences (insurers, regulators, OEMs).

## 2. Agency & Organization Types in This Sector

- **DOE National Laboratories** — e.g., Sandia National Laboratories (EMP/GMD effects testing heritage), National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL, materials/critical minerals), Lawrence Livermore and Oak Ridge (advanced materials, ceramics)
- **NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology)** — measurement science, EMF/dosimetry standards
- **Federally Funded Research and Development Centers (FFRDCs)** operated by universities/nonprofits under DOE, DoD, or DHS sponsorship
- **DOE Critical Materials Institute** — directly relevant to the rare-earth-free magnet and Aegis-Forge recycling research
- **University-affiliated advanced materials research centers** with DOE/NSF funding relationships

## 6. Suggested Outreach Angle

"We are seeking independent technical validation and potential CRADA-style partnership on a materials-science corpus spanning UHT ceramics, MXene EMI coatings, and rare-earth-free magnetics — all framed around Carrington-event/GIC resilience but with clear dual-use value across your existing materials and critical-minerals research portfolios."

## 7. Priority Level & Timing

**HIGH, but relationship-paced.** National lab technology-transfer relationships take longer to establish than regulatory or insurance outreach; begin now via Technology Transfer offices and major materials-science conferences to be positioned for the next CRADA or SBIR-adjacent funding cycle.
