## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

The North American bulk power system is already under a **mandatory** federal compliance regime for geomagnetic disturbance (GMD) risk: NERC Standard **TPL-007** (Transmission System Planned Performance for GMD Events) and **EOP-010** (Geomagnetic Disturbance Operations), both issued under FERC Order No. 851. During the May 2024 Gannon Storm, reliability coordinators and transmission operators executed pre-arranged GMD operating procedures while the grid oscillated between G3 and G5 conditions for three days — the system held, but isolated impacts to large power transformers, voltage-support equipment, transmission breakers, and harmonic filters were recorded, and EPRI's SUNBURST GIC monitoring network logged high current/harmonic levels. NERC has since opened a formal review of the event to improve TPL-007/EOP-010 guidance. This is a live, active regulatory-update cycle — the exact moment new hardening research has maximum institutional receptivity.

## 2. Agency & Organization Types in This Sector

- **North American Electric Reliability Corporation (NERC)** — the FERC-designated Electric Reliability Organization; owns TPL-007/EOP-010
- **Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC)** — issues NERC standard mandates via Order Nos. (e.g., Order 851)
- **U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), Office of Cybersecurity, Energy Security, and Emergency Response (CESER)**
- **State Public Utility Commissions (PUCs)** — cost-recovery approval authority for utility GMD-hardening capital expenditures
- **Regional Transmission Organizations / Independent System Operators (RTOs/ISOs)** — PJM, MISO, ERCOT, CAISO, ISO-NE, NYISO, SPP
- **Electric Power Research Institute (EPRI)** — operates the SUNBURST GIC monitoring network, industry's primary GMD research consortium
