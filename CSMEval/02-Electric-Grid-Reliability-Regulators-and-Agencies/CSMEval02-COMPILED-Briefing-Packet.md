Let's start with a confession: nobody has ever fallen in love with a monopole.
That's fine. That's not the job. The job of a transmission structure is to be
extremely, reliably, boringly good at holding a wire in the air — right up
until a coronal mass ejection asks that wire to also behave like an antenna,
at which point "boring" becomes the single most valuable property a structure
can have. (Genuinely — in this line of work, "boring" is the compliment.) Three
pieces of hardware below are boring in exactly the right way.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 PHYSICAL-LAYER, NON-CONDUCTIVE GRID HARDENING
                 CSMEval02-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize three CSM physical-hardening components positioned to complement —
not replace — existing NERC TPL-007/EOP-010 GIC modeling and thermal-impact
assessment work.

1. AEGIS POWER LINE MONOPOLE (CSMFAB0105)
   Composite (non-metallic) transmission structure. Removes the structure
   itself as a GIC current path, addressing tower/monopole vulnerability
   independent of the conductor and transformer mitigation already covered
   by TPL-007 system studies.

2. AEGIS DIELECTRIC FENCING (CSMFAB0104)
   Non-conductive substation perimeter fencing. Targets substation/perimeter
   conductive-hazard exposure — a physical-security-adjacent, electrically-
   adjacent gap that sits outside most existing GIC system models, which
   typically stop at the transformer and bus level.

3. CHROMATIC FIELD MONITOR (CSMFAB021)
   Passive EMF/GIC area detector, positioned as a lower-cost complement to
   full SUNBURST-class monitoring instrumentation — intended for broader,
   denser deployment across substation fleets where full magnetometer-grade
   sensors aren't budgeted, feeding early local anomaly signal rather than
   replacing EPRI's SUNBURST network's system-level data.

WHERE THIS FITS YOUR EXISTING COMPLIANCE WORK
None of the three above is a substitute for TPL-007 Category P1-P7 planning
event studies, EOP-010 operating procedures, or EPRI's GIC/harmonic modeling.
They are physical-hardening and low-cost-monitoring options that sit downstream
of your modeling — the kind of thing a utility implements *after* a
vulnerability assessment identifies where hardening capital should go.

Here's a sentence I want you to read twice: the grid held during the Gannon
Storm, and that is the good news, and it is also — stay with me — the single
most alarming sentence in this entire briefing. It held during a three-day
G3-to-G5 oscillation that still managed to damage transformers, voltage-support
equipment, breakers, and harmonic filters across the system. (Imagine your
smoke detector going off, the building not burning down, and everyone
concluding smoke detectors are unnecessary. That is, structurally, the risk in
treating "the grid held" as "the grid is fine.") NERC opened a formal review of
that event. That review is open right now. That is your door, propped open,
mid-storm, by the regulator itself.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 THE GANNON STORM WAS THE PRACTICE ROUND
                 CSMEval02-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Establish why NERC's active post-Gannon-Storm review, combined with the
confirmed Solar Cycle 25 peak, places grid-hardening research in a live
regulatory-update window rather than a speculative future scenario.

KEY FACTS
• NERC Standard TPL-007 (GMD Planned Performance) and EOP-010 (GMD Operations)
  are mandatory under FERC Order No. 851 — this is a compliance obligation,
  not a voluntary best practice.
• May 2024 Gannon Storm: reliability coordinators and transmission operators
  ran pre-arranged GMD operating procedures across a three-day G3-to-G5
  oscillation. The system held. Isolated impacts to large power transformers,
  voltage-support equipment, transmission breakers, and harmonic filters were
  recorded.
• EPRI's SUNBURST GIC monitoring network logged elevated current/harmonic
  levels throughout the event.
• NERC has opened a formal review of the Gannon Storm specifically to improve
  TPL-007/EOP-010 guidance — an active, in-progress regulatory update cycle.
• Solar Cycle 25 confirmed peak: SSN ~137 (NOAA SWPC, Q1 2026) vs. ~115
  pre-cycle forecast.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• A "the grid held" narrative can understate accumulating hardware stress
  across repeated sub-catastrophic events — transformers and breakers don't
  reset to zero wear after an oscillation like Gannon's.
• Physical-layer hardening (structures, fencing, monitoring density) sits
  outside the scope of TPL-007 system studies and may be underrepresented in
  post-event review submissions unless explicitly raised.
• A formal review window closes eventually — data submitted after the review
  concludes has less influence on the resulting guidance revision.

                 WHERE THIS DATA ACTUALLY WANTS TO GO
                 CSMEval02-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
List the specific working groups, associations, and technical venues best
positioned to receive physical-layer GIC-hardening data ahead of NERC's
TPL-007/EOP-010 guidance revision.

PRIMARY CHANNEL — NERC GMD TASK FORCE / PROJECT 2013-03
The standing working group most directly responsible for GMD mitigation
standard language. Best entry point for hardening data intended to influence
the post-Gannon-Storm guidance revision specifically.

TRADE ASSOCIATION CHANNELS (BY UTILITY OWNERSHIP TYPE)
• Edison Electric Institute (EEI) — investor-owned utilities
• American Public Power Association (APPA) — public/municipal utilities
• National Rural Electric Cooperative Association (NRECA) — cooperatives
Each represents a different ownership structure with different capital
approval processes (PUC-regulated vs. board-governed vs. member-governed) —
worth tailoring the pitch to which one you're speaking with.

TECHNICAL/RESEARCH CHANNEL
• EPRI GMD/SUNBURST program annual workshops — the most credible technical
  venue for hardware validation data, given EPRI's own operational role in
  post-Gannon GIC monitoring.
• IEEE Power & Energy Society (PES) GMD/GIC technical committees and annual
  General Meeting — peer-reviewed technical presentation pathway.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. EPRI GMD/SUNBURST workshop (technical credibility first)
2. NERC GMD Task Force / Project 2013-03 (regulatory relevance second)
3. EEI/APPA/NRECA (commercial adoption third, matched to utility type)
