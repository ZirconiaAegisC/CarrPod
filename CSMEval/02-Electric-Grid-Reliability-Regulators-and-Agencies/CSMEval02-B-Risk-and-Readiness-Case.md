                 THE GANNON STORM WAS THE PRACTICE ROUND
                 CSMEval02-B | June 2026 | v1.0

PURPOSE
Establish why NERC's active post-Gannon-Storm review, combined with the
confirmed Solar Cycle 25 peak, places grid-hardening research in a live
regulatory-update window rather than a speculative future scenario.

KEY FACTS
• NERC Standard TPL-007 (GMD Planned Performance) and EOP-010 (GMD Operations)
  are mandatory under FERC Order No. 851 — this is a compliance obligation,
  not a voluntary best practice.
• May 2024 Gannon Storm: reliability coordinators and transmission operators
  ran pre-arranged GMD operating procedures across a three-day G3-to-G5
  oscillation. The system held. Isolated impacts to large power transformers,
  voltage-support equipment, transmission breakers, and harmonic filters were
  recorded.
• EPRI's SUNBURST GIC monitoring network logged elevated current/harmonic
  levels throughout the event.
• NERC has opened a formal review of the Gannon Storm specifically to improve
  TPL-007/EOP-010 guidance — an active, in-progress regulatory update cycle.
• Solar Cycle 25 confirmed peak: SSN ~137 (NOAA SWPC, Q1 2026) vs. ~115
  pre-cycle forecast.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• A "the grid held" narrative can understate accumulating hardware stress
  across repeated sub-catastrophic events — transformers and breakers don't
  reset to zero wear after an oscillation like Gannon's.
• Physical-layer hardening (structures, fencing, monitoring density) sits
  outside the scope of TPL-007 system studies and may be underrepresented in
  post-event review submissions unless explicitly raised.
• A formal review window closes eventually — data submitted after the review
  concludes has less influence on the resulting guidance revision.
