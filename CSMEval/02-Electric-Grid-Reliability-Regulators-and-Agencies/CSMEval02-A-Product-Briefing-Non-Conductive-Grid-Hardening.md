Let's start with a confession: nobody has ever fallen in love with a monopole.
That's fine. That's not the job. The job of a transmission structure is to be
extremely, reliably, boringly good at holding a wire in the air — right up
until a coronal mass ejection asks that wire to also behave like an antenna,
at which point "boring" becomes the single most valuable property a structure
can have. (Genuinely — in this line of work, "boring" is the compliment.) Three
pieces of hardware below are boring in exactly the right way.

PURPOSE
Summarize three CSM physical-hardening components positioned to complement —
not replace — existing NERC TPL-007/EOP-010 GIC modeling and thermal-impact
assessment work.

1. AEGIS POWER LINE MONOPOLE (CSMFAB0105)
   Composite (non-metallic) transmission structure. Removes the structure
   itself as a GIC current path, addressing tower/monopole vulnerability
   independent of the conductor and transformer mitigation already covered
   by TPL-007 system studies.

2. AEGIS DIELECTRIC FENCING (CSMFAB0104)
   Non-conductive substation perimeter fencing. Targets substation/perimeter
   conductive-hazard exposure — a physical-security-adjacent, electrically-
   adjacent gap that sits outside most existing GIC system models, which
   typically stop at the transformer and bus level.

3. CHROMATIC FIELD MONITOR (CSMFAB021)
   Passive EMF/GIC area detector, positioned as a lower-cost complement to
   full SUNBURST-class monitoring instrumentation — intended for broader,
   denser deployment across substation fleets where full magnetometer-grade
   sensors aren't budgeted, feeding early local anomaly signal rather than
   replacing EPRI's SUNBURST network's system-level data.

WHERE THIS FITS YOUR EXISTING COMPLIANCE WORK
None of the three above is a substitute for TPL-007 Category P1-P7 planning
event studies, EOP-010 operating procedures, or EPRI's GIC/harmonic modeling.
They are physical-hardening and low-cost-monitoring options that sit downstream
of your modeling — the kind of thing a utility implements *after* a
vulnerability assessment identifies where hardening capital should go.
