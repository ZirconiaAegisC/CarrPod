ISO 5349-3:2025 is, as of this writing, a matter of months old. If your
vibration-emission declarations and exposure-assessment methodologies
haven't caught up yet, that's not a failing on your part — it's just how
new the standard actually is. We happen to have already done the reading.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 A STANDARD THAT'S BARELY OLDER THAN THIS EMAIL
                 CSMEval27-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize the CSMHuman corpus's synthesis of ISO 5349-3:2025 and related
2025-2026 vibration-exposure research.

1. ISO 5349-3:2025 SHOCK/IMPULSE VIBRATION STANDARD — published December
   2025, adding a dedicated Vibration Peak Magnitude metric to the existing
   continuous-exposure ISO 5349-1 framework. Tracked across more than a
   dozen CSMHuman documents.
2. LOW-COST WEARABLE HAVS EXPOSURE MONITORING — a 2025 wearable-sensor
   transfer-function validation method that could materially lower the cost
   of compliant exposure monitoring.
3. PIEZO1 MOLECULAR MECHANISM RESEARCH — vibration-induced vascular injury
   mechanism research, relevant to the biological rationale underlying
   exposure limits.
4. DAMPED TOOL/EQUIPMENT INTERFACE DESIGN PRECEDENT — the Aegis Embark
   vibration-mitigation product line (armrest, pedal, steering wrap;
   CSMFAB026, 029, 036), relevant as design precedent for PPE and tool-
   interface manufacturers specifically.

WHY THE TIMING HERE IS UNUSUALLY NARROW
Most of this outreach series has a multi-year or evergreen timeline. This
one doesn't — manufacturers and consultancies are actively updating
compliance documentation right now, this quarter, which makes this a
genuinely narrow window rather than a standing invitation.

SUBJECT:       Compliance Cost Is Coming Down at the Same Moment the Bar Is Going Up
REF:           CSMEval27-B
================================================================================

Two things happening at once, both worth knowing about together: the
measurement standard just got more demanding (ISO 5349-3:2025's new
shock/impulse metric), and a validated wearable-sensor method just made
compliant monitoring cheaper. That's a rare pairing — usually a stricter
standard just means a bigger compliance bill.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 COMPLIANCE COST DOWN, STANDARD UP — A RARE PAIRING
                 CSMEval27-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Explain why the combination of a new, stricter vibration standard and a
lower-cost monitoring method creates a genuinely time-sensitive outreach
window for this sector.

KEY FACTS
• ISO 5349-3:2025, published December 2025, adds a dedicated shock/impulse
  vibration measurement standard (Vibration Peak Magnitude metric) to the
  existing continuous-exposure ISO 5349-1 framework.
• Tool manufacturers' vibration-emission declarations and safety
  consultancies' exposure-assessment methodologies both need updating to
  stay current with the new standard.
• A 2025 wearable-sensor validation method exists that could materially
  lower the cost of compliant exposure monitoring — a rare case of a
  stricter standard arriving alongside a cost-reduction tool rather than
  purely a cost increase.
• The CSMHuman corpus's 2026 update cycle has already tracked this exact
  standards change across more than a dozen documents, plus the underlying
  Piezo1 molecular-mechanism research explaining the biological rationale.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• Manufacturers and consultancies that haven't updated documentation for
  ISO 5349-3:2025 are, as of this standard's December 2025 publication,
  already behind — not eventually behind.
• Missing the lower-cost monitoring method means potentially over-paying for
  compliance relative to what's now achievable.

Everywhere else in this outreach series, we've argued for patience or annual

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 MOVE FAST, BECAUSE THE STANDARD ALREADY DID
                 CSMEval27-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the fastest, most direct channels for reaching manufacturers and
consultancies during the narrow ISO 5349-3:2025 compliance-update window.

STANDARDS CHANNEL — ISO TECHNICAL COMMITTEE 108
Owns the ISO 5349 series directly; engage via ISO TC 108's public
committee-participation process — the single most authoritative channel for
anything touching the standard itself.

RESEARCH CHANNEL — NIOSH
Foundational HAVS research body (NIOSH 83-110); engage via NIOSH's public
research-partnership channel, particularly relevant for the Piezo1
molecular-mechanism research.

FASTEST-REACH CHANNEL — AIHA CONNECT
American Industrial Hygiene Association's annual conference; likely the
single fastest way to reach a large number of consultancies and
manufacturers' compliance staff in one place, right now.

INDUSTRY CHANNEL — POWER TOOL INSTITUTE
Engage via its public industry-affairs channel for manufacturer-specific
reach.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING (COMPRESSED, GIVEN THE NARROW WINDOW)
1. AIHA Connect (fastest, broadest reach — prioritize this even if it means
   deprioritizing the others initially).
2. ISO TC 108 engagement in parallel (authoritative-channel credibility).
3. NIOSH and Power Tool Institute as immediate follow-ups.

For once, we're the ones saying "let's not wait for the next annual cycle." — Office of External Affairs & Strategic Partnerships, Carrington Storm Motors | Safe Pod Engineering Company
