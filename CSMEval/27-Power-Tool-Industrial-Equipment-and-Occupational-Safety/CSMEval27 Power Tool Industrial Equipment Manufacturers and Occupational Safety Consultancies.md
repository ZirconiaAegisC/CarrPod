## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Hand-Arm Vibration Syndrome (HAVS) and Whole-Body Vibration (WBV) exposure are governed by an active, evolving standards environment: **ISO 5349-3:2025**, published December 2025, adds a dedicated shock/impulse vibration measurement standard (Vibration Peak Magnitude metric) to the existing continuous-exposure ISO 5349-1 framework — meaning tool manufacturers' vibration-emission declarations and safety consultancies' exposure-assessment methodologies both need updating right now to stay current. The CSMHuman corpus's 2026 update cycle has already tracked this exact standards change across more than a dozen documents, along with a 2025 wearable-sensor validation method that could materially lower the cost of compliant exposure monitoring.

## 2. Company Types in This Sector

- **Power tool manufacturers** (pneumatic, electric, and impact tools)
- **Industrial/heavy equipment manufacturers** (with operator vibration exposure — construction, mining, agriculture)
- **Occupational health and safety consultancies**
- **Personal protective equipment (PPE) manufacturers** (anti-vibration gloves, damped tool handles)
- **Wearable exposure-monitoring device manufacturers** (cross-reference CSMEval26)
