────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 WHERE THE BODY ACTUALLY MEETS THE RISK
                 CSMEval28-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Explain why footwear is an unusually well-suited product category to carry
step-voltage, vibration-exposure, and navigation-redundancy research to
market.

KEY FACTS
• Step-voltage hazards (relevant to switchyard, substation, and other
  GIC-exposed work environments referenced throughout this outreach series)
  are a footwear-solvable problem — the Terra-Grip outsole's >20,000V EH
  rating addresses this directly.
• Vibration-exposure dosimetry (the Bio-Metric anklet) has both consumer
  athletic and occupational-safety crossover applications, connecting this
  sector to the power-tool/industrial-safety dossier (CSMEval27) and the
  medical-device dossier (CSMEval26).
• Post-GPS navigation (Way-Finder haptic insoles) applies the same
  GNSS-disruption resilience concept found across the automotive
  (CSMEval12), drone (CSMEval23), and rail (CSMEval07/20) dossiers, but at
  the individual-person level.
• The full fifteen-product dossier already exists as a fabrication-ready
  engineering specification, not a research question.

WHY THIS SECTOR CONNECTS TO MOST OF THE REST OF THIS SERIES
Footwear is where several other dossiers' underlying research (vibration,
step-voltage, GPS-redundancy) becomes a wearable consumer or occupational
product — worth noting for a company already selling into adjacent safety
or athletic-performance categories.

approach this sector, not a lack of urgency about the product itself.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 OPPORTUNISTIC, ON PURPOSE
                 CSMEval28-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the trade show, industry network, and standards-committee channels
best suited to a licensing or co-development conversation for the Aegis
Embark footwear line.

PRIMARY VENUE — OUTDOOR RETAILER
The trade show most directly matched to this product line's outdoor/
performance framing; engage via its public exhibitor channel.

INDUSTRY NETWORK — TWO TEN FOOTWEAR FOUNDATION
An industry network channel useful for warmer, relationship-based
introductions beyond a trade-show floor.

STANDARDS CHANNEL — ASTM F13 COMMITTEE
The Committee on Safety and Traction for Footwear; directly relevant to the
Terra-Grip non-conductive, step-voltage-rated outsole specifically —
engaging here gives the safety-footwear-specific components a credible
standards-track record independent of the consumer-athletic components.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. Outdoor Retailer (primary trade-show introduction).
2. Two Ten Footwear Foundation networking in parallel (relationship-based
   follow-up).
3. ASTM F13 engagement specifically for the Terra-Grip/safety-footwear
   angle, run independently of the consumer-athletic conversation.

WHY "OPPORTUNISTIC" IS THE RIGHT WORD, NOT "LOW PRIORITY"
No hard regulatory deadline exists for this sector, which means the product
itself can be introduced whenever the right footwear-innovation-team
relationship opens up — patience here is a strategy, not a compromise.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
No deadline. Just the right room, whenever it opens. — Office of External Affairs & Strategic Partnerships, Carrington Storm Motors | Safe Pod Engineering Company
