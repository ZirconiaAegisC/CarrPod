# CSMEval15 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Amusement Park & Theme Park Operators
### Ref: CSMEval15-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles and institutional channels only — no individually named
park operators or ride-engineering staff are listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Act

| Role | Why |
|---|---|
| VP of Facilities Engineering / Park Operations | Owns site-hardening decisions |
| Director of Ride Engineering & Maintenance | Owns ride-support structural specifications |
| Risk Management / Safety Director | Owns combined-hazard risk framing |
| Chief Sustainability/Resilience Officer (larger operators) | Owns broader resilience strategy |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**International Association of Amusement Parks and Attractions (IAAPA)** —
Orlando, FL-based; engage via IAAPA's public exhibit/sponsorship channel;
iaapa.org. (See also CSMEval08 for the municipal-attraction cross-reference.)

**ASTM International, F24 Committee (Amusement Rides and Devices)** — engage
via ASTM's public committee-membership and standards-development process;
astm.org.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| IAAPA Expo | Annual, typically November | Primary global industry trade show |
| ASTM F24 Committee meetings | Ongoing | Standards-development track |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

IAAPA Expo's single-venue concentration makes it disproportionately valuable
here — one well-prepared exhibit or session reaches most of this sector's
relevant decision-makers in one week, more efficiently than any per-operator
outreach campaign.

## 5. What Was Deliberately Left Out

No individually named park executives or ride engineers. Identify current
role-holders through each operator's public leadership page at time of
actual outreach.
