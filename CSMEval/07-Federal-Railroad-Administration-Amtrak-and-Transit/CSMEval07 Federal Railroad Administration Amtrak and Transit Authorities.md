## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Continuous-welded steel rail, overhead catenary systems, and trackside signaling circuits form exactly the kind of long, continuous, low-resistance conductive network that concentrates GIC — the same physics affecting transmission lines and bridges, applied to a network that carries both freight and passengers and that depends on signaling-circuit integrity for basic collision avoidance. Unlike a bridge, a rail signaling failure has an active safety-of-life dimension in real time, not just a structural-integrity dimension after the fact. The CSM corpus already contains a dedicated rail-hardening protocol built around exactly this risk profile.

## 2. Agency & Organization Types in This Sector

- **Federal Railroad Administration (FRA)** — safety regulator for the national rail network
- **Amtrak** — national intercity passenger rail operator
- **Class I freight railroads** — the large national/continental freight carriers
- **Regional commuter rail authorities** and **urban transit authorities** (light rail/subway systems with third-rail or catenary electrification)
- **American Public Transportation Association (APTA)** member agencies

"Positive Train Control and catenary systems share the same long-conductor GIC exposure profile as transmission lines, but with a real-time safety-of-life dimension. Project Aegis-Rail offers a dielectric hardening framework purpose-built for this application, ready for FRA R&D program or APTA technical-committee review."
