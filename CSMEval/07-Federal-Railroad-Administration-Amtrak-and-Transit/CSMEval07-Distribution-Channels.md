# CSMEval07 — Distribution Channel & Role Guide
## Federal Railroad Administration, Amtrak & Transit Authorities
### Ref: CSMEval07-Channels | June 2026

---

**Scope note:** Roles and institutional channels only — no individually named
FRA, Amtrak, railroad, or transit-authority staff are listed.

---

## 1. Roles Most Likely to Act

| Role | Where Found | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Signal and Train Control Division staff | FRA Office of Railroad Safety | Owns signaling safety policy |
| Chief Engineer, Electrification & Signaling | Amtrak | Owns catenary/signaling engineering decisions |
| VP of Engineering / Chief Signal Engineer | Class I freight railroads | Owns freight signaling investment |
| Director of Rail Systems Engineering | APTA member transit agencies | Owns transit signaling/electrification |

## 2. Institutional Contact Channels

**American Public Transportation Association (APTA)** — Washington,
D.C.-based; engage via APTA's Rail Conference technical-session submission
channel; apta.com.

**Association of American Railroads (AAR)** — freight rail standards body;
Washington, D.C.-based; engage via AAR's public technical-standards
engagement channel; aar.org.

**FRA Research, Development and Technology program** — engage via FRA's
public R&D program office and rolling solicitation announcements; fra.dot.gov.

## 3. Conferences & Standing Venues

| Venue | Cadence | Note |
|---|---|---|
| APTA Rail Conference | Annual | Broadest passenger/transit rail engineering audience |
| FRA R&D solicitation cycle | Rolling | Federal research-partnership channel |
| AAR technical standards committees | Ongoing | Freight-specific standards channel |

## 4. Channel-Matching Notes

APTA's Rail Conference is the single highest-leverage venue here — it reaches
Amtrak, transit authorities, and interested Class I staff in one place, which
matters more in this sector than in most others on this list, given how
fragmented rail ownership and operation is across passenger/freight/transit
lines.
