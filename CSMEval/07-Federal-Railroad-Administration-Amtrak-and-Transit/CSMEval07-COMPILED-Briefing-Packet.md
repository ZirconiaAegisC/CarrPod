Transmission lines get all the GIC attention because they're the obvious
long conductor. Continuous-welded rail and overhead catenary are the quiet
ones — just as long, just as continuous, and carrying a much more
time-sensitive kind of consequence if a signaling circuit gets it wrong.
Project Aegis-Rail exists because "quiet" and "carries a real-time
safety-of-life dimension" is not a combination anyone should leave
unaddressed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 PROJECT AEGIS-RAIL: HARDENING THE OTHER LONG CONDUCTOR
                 CSMEval07-A | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Summarize Project Aegis-Rail and supporting CSM research relevant to
catenary/signaling GIC exposure and post-GPS train-control navigation
redundancy.

1. PROJECT AEGIS-RAIL DIELECTRIC HARDENING PROTOCOL
   A dedicated hardening framework for catenary and trackside signaling
   circuits, applying the same GIC load-case methodology used for
   transmission lines and bridges to a network that also carries a
   real-time collision-avoidance function.

2. NON-METALLIC STRUCTURAL COMPONENTS (TRACKSIDE/STATION)
   BFRP rebar and composite-monopole precedent (CSMFAB09, CSMFAB0105),
   applicable to trackside structures and station facilities on the same
   basis as bridge and highway structures.

3. WAY-FINDER HAPTIC/LORA NAVIGATION CONCEPT
   Post-GPS navigation redundancy concept (CSMFAB038) with direct relevance
   to automated/Positive Train Control (PTC) systems that depend on GPS for
   position-referencing — a GPS-degradation scenario already documented
   during the May 2024 Gannon Storm.

WHY SIGNALING IS DIFFERENT FROM STRUCTURAL RISK
A bridge that's damaged closes a route. A signaling circuit that fails
silently doesn't close anything — it just stops telling the truth about where
trains are. That distinction is why Project Aegis-Rail treats signaling
integrity as the primary design driver, not an afterthought to structural
hardening.

SUBJECT:       The Risk That Grows as the System Gets Smarter
REF:           CSMEval07-B

Here's the counterintuitive part: modernizing a rail network — more Positive
Train Control, more automated signaling, more GPS-referenced positioning —
makes the system safer under normal conditions and more exposed under GIC
conditions, simultaneously. Not a reason to stop modernizing. A reason to
harden while you do it, instead of after.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 THE RISK THAT GROWS AS THE SYSTEM GETS SMARTER
                 CSMEval07-B | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Explain why rail signaling's GIC exposure carries a real-time safety-of-life
dimension distinct from the structural-integrity risk profile affecting
bridges and highways, and why that risk grows with electronic modernization.

KEY FACTS
• Continuous-welded rail and overhead catenary systems form long,
  continuous, low-resistance conductive networks — the same underlying
  physics as transmission-line and bridge GIC exposure.
• Unlike structural damage, a signaling failure can be silent — a system that
  reports incorrect train position/occupancy rather than visibly failing.
• The May 2024 Gannon Storm produced documented GPS/GNSS degradation across
  the U.S. Midwest lasting up to two days — a direct precedent for
  GPS-referenced Positive Train Control systems losing positional accuracy
  during a geomagnetic event.
• Solar Cycle 25's confirmed SSN ~137 peak keeps this exposure live for the
  remainder of the current solar maximum period.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• PTC and automated signaling investment, without a parallel GIC-hardening
  and GPS-redundancy investment, increases electronic dependency without a
  matching increase in electromagnetic resilience.
• A real-time safety-of-life framing (versus the structural-integrity framing
  used for bridges) means the cost of being wrong here is measured
  differently than a closed route — it's measured in whether the system knows
  the truth about where trains are.

                 TWO CHANNELS, PACED TO THIS SECTOR'S OWN CLOCK
                 CSMEval07-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the primary channels for introducing Project Aegis-Rail to FRA,
Amtrak, Class I freight railroads, and transit authorities.

CHANNEL 1 — FRA RESEARCH, DEVELOPMENT AND TECHNOLOGY PROGRAM
FRA's rolling R&D solicitation cycle is the natural federal-level entry
point for a signaling-hardening framework — engage through FRA's public R&D
program office, positioning Aegis-Rail as a safety-research proposal, not a
vendor pitch.

CHANNEL 2 — APTA ANNUAL RAIL CONFERENCE
The American Public Transportation Association's annual Rail Conference
reaches Amtrak, Class I freight, and transit-authority engineering staff in
one venue — the right place for a technical-committee presentation of the
Aegis-Rail framework.

SECONDARY CHANNEL — AAR (ASSOCIATION OF AMERICAN RAILROADS)
Freight rail's standards body; relevant if Class I freight engagement becomes
a priority alongside the passenger-rail-focused APTA channel.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. APTA Rail Conference technical session (broadest first audience).
2. FRA R&D program engagement in parallel (federal research credibility).
3. AAR engagement if freight-specific interest develops.
