                 TWO CHANNELS, PACED TO THIS SECTOR'S OWN CLOCK
                 CSMEval07-C | June 2026 | v1.0
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

PURPOSE
Identify the primary channels for introducing Project Aegis-Rail to FRA,
Amtrak, Class I freight railroads, and transit authorities.

CHANNEL 1 — FRA RESEARCH, DEVELOPMENT AND TECHNOLOGY PROGRAM
FRA's rolling R&D solicitation cycle is the natural federal-level entry
point for a signaling-hardening framework — engage through FRA's public R&D
program office, positioning Aegis-Rail as a safety-research proposal, not a
vendor pitch.

CHANNEL 2 — APTA ANNUAL RAIL CONFERENCE
The American Public Transportation Association's annual Rail Conference
reaches Amtrak, Class I freight, and transit-authority engineering staff in
one venue — the right place for a technical-committee presentation of the
Aegis-Rail framework.

SECONDARY CHANNEL — AAR (ASSOCIATION OF AMERICAN RAILROADS)
Freight rail's standards body; relevant if Class I freight engagement becomes
a priority alongside the passenger-rail-focused APTA channel.

SUGGESTED SEQUENCING
1. APTA Rail Conference technical session (broadest first audience).
2. FRA R&D program engagement in parallel (federal research credibility).
3. AAR engagement if freight-specific interest develops.
