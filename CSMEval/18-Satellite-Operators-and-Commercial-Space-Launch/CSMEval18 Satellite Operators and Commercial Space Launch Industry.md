## 1. Why This Sector Cannot Wait

Satellites experience space weather directly, not as a downstream secondary effect: the May 2024 Gannon Storm expanded Earth's upper atmosphere enough to increase orbital drag on low-Earth-orbit satellites, prompting NASA's ICESat-2 mission to enter safe mode and complicating ESA space-debris tracking. Lloyd's of London already underwrites nearly a third of all global space risk, meaning this sector's insurers (see CSMEval11) are already pricing this exposure. The CSM corpus's robotics research explicitly extends into this domain with a Mars-colonization-framed hardening protocol for a named commercial humanoid robot platform, and its "Smart Rope" program represents an independent-track alternative launch-assist concept.
