## 3.0 DATA MANAGEMENT PLAN

### 3.1 Open Data Commitment

Carrington Storm Motors is committed to an open-access data policy for all federally funded research. All measurement data generated under this Phase I SBIR will be made publicly available under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License (CC BY 4.0) within 12 months of project completion, subject to the following considerations:

1. **Embargo Period:** Raw measurement data will be embargoed for 6 months following project completion to permit the PI to submit peer-reviewed publications and for CSM to file provisional patent applications covering any unanticipated inventions arising from the Phase I work.

2. **Data Repository:** Data will be deposited in a recognized open-access repository (e.g., Zenodo, Dryad, or a DHS-designated data repository) with a Digital Object Identifier (DOI) for permanent citation.

3. **Data Types Covered:** TEM cell S-parameter measurements (S11, S21) for all shielding effectiveness tests; four-point probe conductivity data for MXene films and CNT-polymer composites; impedance analyzer complex permittivity data; mechanical test data (ASTM D790 flexural, ASTM D2344 short-beam shear); environmental aging data; and cost model spreadsheets.

4. **Proprietary Data Exclusion:** Processing parameters that constitute CSM trade secrets (MXene passivation chemistry composition, CNT dispersion optimization conditions, VARTM cure cycle temperature ramp rates) are excluded from the open-data commitment and will be maintained as confidential business information under the SBIR Data Rights Clause.

### 3.2 NIST-Traceable Measurements

All measurement equipment used in this Phase I effort will be calibrated to NIST-traceable standards:

- Vector Network Analyzer: calibration using NIST-traceable calibration kits (SOLT — Short/Open/Load/Through) prior to each measurement session
- Four-Point Probe: verified against NIST Standard Reference Material (SRM) for sheet resistance
- TEM Cell: field uniformity verified per IEEE 1309-2013 calibration standard
- Force/Torque Measurements (Universal Testing Machine): load cell calibration per ASTM E4

Calibration certificates will be maintained in the project files and available for DHS review.

### 3.3 Peer-Reviewed Publication Plan

CSM targets publication of Phase I results in the following peer-reviewed venues:

1. **IEEE Transactions on Electromagnetic Compatibility:** Shielding effectiveness of MXene-BFRP composite panels — ASTM D4935-18 and IEEE 299-2006 measurement results.
2. **ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces:** Frequency-selective conductivity of CNT-polymer composites — percolation threshold analysis, DC-to-60 Hz conductivity characterization, and GIC reduction measurement results.
3. **Space Weather (AGU Journal):** Cost-benefit analysis of dielectric infrastructure hardening for GMD protection — actuarial modeling methodology, substation retrofit cost projections, and comparison to operational GMD mitigation strategies.

All publications will acknowledge DHS SBIR funding support and will be deposited in PubMed Central or equivalent open-access repository in compliance with federal public access policies.

## 4.0 FACILITIES DESCRIPTION

### 4.1 Organizational Structure

Carrington Storm Motors operates as a virtual/distributed research organization with core laboratory facilities located for access to the principal investigators and key materials science capabilities. The organizational structure is documented in CSMInvestor PITCH-DECK-10-TEAM (Section 6.0, Organizational Structure) and CSMInvestor TEAM-04-ORG-STRUCTURE.

### 4.2 Electromagnetic Testing Laboratory

**Primary EM Test Facility:**
- TEM Cell: Capable of generating electric field strengths equivalent to 20 V/km geoelectric field (Carrington-level benchmark per NERC GMD Task Force). Validated through Charlemagne hull testing (94.0% GIC reduction demonstrated) and Aegis Home whole-house scale model testing (96.7% GIC reduction demonstrated). Test configurations conform to MIL-STD-461G and IEC 61000-4-24.
- Shielded Enclosure: For IEEE 299-2006 shielding effectiveness measurements on Aegis-C panels. Dimensions sufficient for 30 cm × 30 cm test article.
- Ground Plane: For fall-of-potential ground resistance measurements (IEEE 81-2012).

**RF/Microwave Measurement Capability:**
- Vector Network Analyzer: 10 MHz–18 GHz, 2-port, S-parameter measurement. Calibrated per manufacturer specifications using NIST-traceable calibration kits.
- Coaxial Holder: ASTM D4935-18 configuration for planar material SE measurement.
- Waveguide Adapters: X-band (8.2–12.4 GHz), Ku-band (12.4–18 GHz).

### 4.3 Materials Characterization Laboratory

- X-Ray Diffractometer: Phase identification, crystallinity assessment for MAX phase precursors and MXene products. Cu Kα radiation, θ-2θ configuration.
- Scanning Electron Microscope with EDS: Surface morphology, cross-sectional imaging, elemental mapping. Resolution sufficient for MXene flake morphology and Aegis-C composite cross-section analysis.
- Four-Point Probe Station: Sheet resistance and bulk conductivity per ASTM F390.
- Impedance Analyzer: Complex permittivity (ε', ε'') from DC to 1 MHz, enabling frequency-selective conductivity characterization for CNT-polymer composites.
- Universal Testing Machine: Flexural (ASTM D790), short-beam shear (ASTM D2344), tensile (ASTM D3039, D882). Load cell capacity: 5 kN.
- TGA/DSC: Thermal stability, oxidation onset temperature, glass transition temperature, cure kinetics.
- Environmental Chamber: 85°C/85% RH capability, interior volume sufficient for multiple Aegis-C panel specimens.

### 4.4 Composite Fabrication Laboratory

- VARTM Equipment: Vacuum pump (ultimate pressure < 1 mbar), resin trap, vacuum gauges, tooling plates (aluminum, 400 mm × 400 mm), bagging consumables.
- Curing Oven: Temperature controlled to 200°C, interior dimensions for 30 cm × 30 cm panels.
- Twin-Screw Extruder: For CNT-polymer melt-mixing at controlled loading fractions (2–15 wt%).
- MXene Synthesis Wet Laboratory: Fume hood, magnetic stirrers, centrifuges, vacuum filtration apparatus. MILD etching protocol (LiF-HCl) implemented; no direct HF usage.

### 4.5 Documentation and Computing Infrastructure

- CSMQuantum Materials Science Library: 12 volumes, indexed and cross-referenced, with complete synthesis protocols, characterization data, and literature reviews.
- CSMFAB BOM-REGISTRY: JSON database (registry.json) containing 50+ product bills of materials with supplier pricing, lead times, and manufacturing process specifications.
- CSMVessel Fleet Specification: 72 volumes (MD format and 3D-Fab SCAD models) with complete manufacturing protocols.
- Research Corpus: 6,302 documents in searchable, indexed repository with cross-references between materials science, electromagnetic theory, product engineering, actuarial science, and diplomatic outreach domains.
- Computing: Standard laptop and desktop computing for data acquisition, analysis (Python, MATLAB, or equivalent), and report generation.

### 4.6 Facility Cost Sharing

All facilities and equipment described above are existing CSM assets, valued at $45,620 (6-month prorated usage) and contributed as in-kind cost share to this Phase I effort. See SBIR-02-BUDGET-JUSTIFICATION, Section 8.0 for detailed cost share valuation.
