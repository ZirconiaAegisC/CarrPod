## The Problem

Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC) production accounts for approximately 7–8% of global
anthropogenic CO2 emissions. The dominant source is process chemistry: heating limestone
(CaCO3) to ~1,450°C in a rotary kiln drives off CO2 as an unavoidable reaction product:

    CaCO3 + heat → CaO + CO2

Roughly 60% of cement's carbon footprint comes from this calcination reaction, while
the remaining ~40% comes from fuel combustion to reach kiln temperatures. Over 4 billion
metric tons of cement are produced annually, making this the single largest industrial
source of CO2 after power generation.

## The Brimstone Solution

Brimstone replaces limestone with **calcium silicate rocks** — primarily basalt and
related mafic formations. Calcium silicates (e.g., CaSiO3, Ca2SiO4) contain no
carbonate ion. When heated in a kiln, they decompose to reactive calcium oxide and
silicate phases without releasing CO2:

    CaSiO3 + heat → CaO + SiO2   (no CO2 emitted)

The resulting clinker is chemically and mineralogically identical to OPC clinker.
Brimstone's product is **ASTM C150 certified**, meaning it is a true drop-in replacement
that requires zero modification to ready-mix plants, precast facilities, or construction
practices.

## Carbon Negativity

The process generates a **magnesium oxide (MgO) byproduct** from the magnesium silicates
naturally present in basalt. This MgO reacts passively with atmospheric CO2 to form
magnesium carbonate (MgCO3), permanently sequestering carbon. Quantified cradle-to-gate,
the process absorbs more CO2 than it emits — making Brimstone cement genuinely
**carbon-negative**, not merely carbon-neutral.

Third-party life cycle assessment verified by **SCS Global Services** confirms the
net-negative carbon balance under ISO 14040/14044 standards.

## Competitive Moats

1. **Feedstock abundance:** Calcium silicate rocks (basalt) are the most abundant rock
   type in Earth's crust; supply is effectively unlimited.
2. **Identical product:** ASTM C150 OPC — no new standards, no customer retooling.
3. **Carbon-negative, not just neutral:** Competitors reduce emissions; Brimstone
   inverts the sign.
4. **Process-technology lock-in:** Patents on the silicate-to-clinker pathway.
5. **First-mover regulatory tailwinds:** Buy Clean policies, IRA 45Q credits,
   carbon border adjustments (CBAM in EU), and federal procurement preference
   for low-embodied-carbon materials.
