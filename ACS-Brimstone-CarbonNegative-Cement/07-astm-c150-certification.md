# 07 — ASTM C150 Certification

## What is ASTM C150?

ASTM C150 is the **Standard Specification for Portland Cement**, published and
maintained by ASTM International (formerly the American Society for Testing and
Materials). It is the foundational specification for ordinary Portland cement in
the United States and is recognized globally in building codes and construction
specifications.

C150 defines:
- Chemical composition limits (oxide ratios, loss on ignition, insoluble residue)
- Physical requirements (fineness, soundness, setting time, compressive strength)
- Optional performance requirements (low alkali, moderate sulfate resistance, etc.)

## Types Under ASTM C150

| Type | Description | Typical Application |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Type I | Normal | General construction |
| Type II | Moderate sulfate resistance | Marine, underground |
| Type III | High early strength | Precast, cold-weather |
| Type IV | Low heat of hydration | Mass concrete (dams) |
| Type V | High sulfate resistance | Severe sulfate soils |

Brimstone's product is certified under **Types I and II** (the most common grades,
representing >80% of the US cement market), with an active development program for
Types III and V.

## Certification Process

Achieving ASTM C150 certification is not a one-time event but an ongoing compliance
program:

1. **Raw material qualification:** Demonstrate controlled chemistry of input minerals
2. **Process validation:** Show that the manufacturing process produces consistent
   clinker phase composition
3. **Round-robin testing:** Multiple independent labs (CCRL — Cement and Concrete
   Reference Laboratory) validate the product against ASTM test methods
4. **Plant certification:** On-site audit of quality control systems
5. **Ongoing compliance:** Monthly CCRL proficiency samples, daily in-house testing
   with statistical process control (SPC)

## Significance for Market Entry

ASTM C150 certification is the single most important regulatory milestone for any
cement company. Key implications:

- **Building codes:** IBC (International Building Code) and ACI 318 reference ASTM
  C150; certified cement is automatically code-compliant
- **State DOTs:** Nearly all state Departments of Transportation specify ASTM C150
  for highway and bridge construction
- **Specifier acceptance:** Architects and engineers can specify Brimstone cement
  without any special approvals or performance testing
- **No alternative pathway required:** Unlike non-OPC cements (calcium sulfoaluminate,
  geopolymers, alkali-activated materials) that require expensive and time-consuming
  code amendments (ACI 318, ASTM C1157 acceptance with supplementary testing)

## Third-Party Validation

The certification is maintained through independent third-party testing by CCRL
and is listed in the US General Services Administration (GSA) and Buy Clean
procurement databases.
