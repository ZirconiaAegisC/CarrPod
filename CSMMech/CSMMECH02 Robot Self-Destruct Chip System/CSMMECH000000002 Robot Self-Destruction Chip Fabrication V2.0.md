## 2. Micro-Chip Tile Architecture

**YSZ pentagonal tiles (15 mm edge, 3 mm thick):**
- Material: Yttria-Stabilized Zirconia (3Y-TZP), gel-cast and under-sintered to 92% theoretical density
- Under-sintering rationale: 92% TD leaves micro-porosity → reduces fracture toughness to K_IC = 4 MPa·m^0.5 (vs 10 for fully sintered YSZ) → more complete fragmentation
- Spectral tag: 0.5 mol% Eu³⁺ dopant → characteristic 613 nm red emission under UV illumination → 1 fragment detectable in 1 million random pieces
- Total tile count per full chassis: ~4,240 tiles

## 3. Weak-Interface Sintering Assembly

Tiles are assembled into macro-structure using calcium silicate (CaSiO₃) adhesive:
- Bond preparation: CaSiO₃ slurry (20 vol% solids, 1 μm particles)
- Applied by screen printing onto tile faces, 50 μm layer
- Calcined at 600°C: bond strength 2–5 MPa (2025 ceramic journal data)
- Bulk YSZ tile: tensile strength 1,200 MPa — bond is the designed weak link
- Structural integrity: 4,240 tiles × 2 MPa × 225 mm² per face = 190 kN load capacity → survives 98% of operational loading
- Destruction threshold: triggered at 100× design load (seismic event, direct kinetic hit) OR by acoustic signal
