# 13 — TRINA SOLAR LICENSE: CHINA MARKET ACCESS & TERMS

## Agreement Structure

In April 2025, Oxford PV and Trina Solar Co., Ltd. announced an exclusive patent and technology license agreement granting Trina Solar the right to manufacture and sell perovskite-silicon tandem photovoltaic modules incorporating Oxford PV's proprietary technology in the People's Republic of China (including Hong Kong and Macau, excluding Taiwan). The agreement is structured as a multi-year, royalty-bearing license with milestone payments and minimum volume commitments.

The agreement represents a strategic synthesis of the two companies' complementary positions: Oxford PV brings foundational intellectual property, proven cell and module technology, and proprietary deposition equipment design; Trina Solar brings the world's largest silicon cell and module manufacturing footprint (approximately 90 GW of module capacity as of Q1 2025), established supply chains, access to Chinese utility-scale project development, and deep experience in high-volume photovoltaic manufacturing.

## Key Commercial Terms

While the full commercial terms remain confidential, publicly disclosed elements of the agreement include:

**Exclusivity**: Trina Solar receives exclusive rights for tandem module manufacturing and sales in mainland China for the duration of the agreement. Oxford PV retains all rights outside China and retains the right to supply tandem modules to Chinese customers from non-Chinese manufacturing sites.

**Royalty Structure**: A per-watt royalty on tandem modules manufactured and sold by Trina Solar under the license. The royalty rate is reported to be in the range of USD 0.02–0.04/W DC for the initial license period, with step-down provisions linked to cumulative volume thresholds. This royalty structure is designed to provide meaningful revenue to Oxford PV without rendering Trina Solar's tandem modules uneconomic relative to competing high-efficiency technologies.

**Milestone Payments**: A series of upfront and milestone-based payments are triggered by technology transfer completion, first production, and cumulative volume thresholds. The total milestone payments over the agreement term are estimated at USD 150–200 million.

**Minimum Volume Commitments**: Trina Solar commits to minimum annual tandem module production and sales volumes, escalating over the agreement term. Failure to meet minimum volume commitments triggers reduced exclusivity or conversion to non-exclusive terms.

**Technology Transfer Package**: Oxford PV provides a comprehensive technology transfer package including cell process specifications, perovskite precursor chemistry and sourcing information, deposition equipment specifications and vendor qualification, module integration and encapsulation specifications, and quality control protocols. The deposition equipment is supplied by Oxford PV's designated equipment manufacturing partner under a separate supply agreement.

**Improvements and Grant-Backs**: Both parties grant each other rights to improvements made to the licensed technology during the agreement term. This provision ensures that Trina Solar benefits from Oxford PV's ongoing R&D advances (for example, the Gen 4 and Gen 5 perovskite formulations) and that Oxford PV benefits from Trina Solar's manufacturing innovations.

## Strategic Rationale

The Trina Solar license serves multiple strategic objectives for Oxford PV:

### 1. China Market Access

China represents approximately 40% of global photovoltaic installations and is projected to remain the world's largest solar market through at least 2030. Direct market entry through a wholly owned Chinese subsidiary or joint venture manufacturing operation would require capital investment in excess of USD 500 million and expose Oxford PV to significant geopolitical, intellectual property, and operational risks. The licensing model provides revenue-linked, capital-light access to the Chinese market while transferring manufacturing execution risk to the world's most experienced high-volume solar manufacturer.

### 2. Revenue Diversification

The Trina Solar license provides a revenue stream — royalty and milestone payments — that is partially decoupled from Oxford PV's own manufacturing capacity and volume. This revenue stream provides financial resilience during the manufacturing ramp-up period, when Oxford PV's own production volumes are constrained by yield improvement and capacity expansion timelines. Oxford PV has stated that licensing revenue is projected to exceed direct sales revenue by 2028, reflecting the combined effect of expanding Trina Solar production volumes and the capital efficiency of the licensing model relative to owned-and-operated manufacturing.

### 3. Technology Validation

Adoption of Oxford PV's technology by the world's largest solar module manufacturer represents a powerful validation signal to the broader industry, including potential licensees in other geographies (India, the United States, the Middle East), silicon cell suppliers considering tandem cell investments, and financial investors evaluating Oxford PV's valuation and credit quality.

### 4. Supply Chain Development

Trina Solar's volume demand for perovskite precursor chemicals, TCO sputtering targets, specialty encapsulants, and edge seal materials will drive the development of a competitive, multi-supplier ecosystem for these materials, reducing cost and single-supplier risk for Oxford PV's own Brandenburg operations. The Chinese chemical industry has demonstrated rapid capability development in response to demand signals from the photovoltaic industry, as evidenced by the development of domestic polysilicon, silver paste, and EVA encapsulant supply chains.

### 5. Learning Curve Acceleration

Data from Trina Solar's high-volume manufacturing operations — including yield data, reliability test results, and field performance data across Chinese climate zones — will inform Oxford PV's own manufacturing and product development programs. The license agreement includes provisions for sharing of non-proprietary operational data and joint participation in reliability and field performance working groups.

## Competitive Implications

The Trina Solar license, while opening the Chinese market, has significant competitive implications for other Chinese solar manufacturers:

- **LONGi Green Energy**, the world's second-largest module manufacturer and a major investor in tandem R&D, is excluded from the Oxford PV IP portfolio for the Chinese market. LONGi's tandem program, which has achieved 33.9% champion cell efficiency (HBC-tandem, December 2024), must navigate around Oxford PV's granted patents or develop independently patentable tandem architectures.
- **JinkoSolar**, **JA Solar**, and **Canadian Solar** are similarly excluded. These manufacturers face a strategic decision: invest in independent tandem R&D programs with uncertain IP clearance, attempt to design around Oxford PV's patent portfolio, or accept a competitive disadvantage relative to Trina Solar in the premium high-efficiency segment.
- **Hanwha Qcells** (South Korea), which operates significant manufacturing capacity outside China and has its own tandem R&D program, may be a candidate for a non-China license agreement.

## IP Protection in China

The enforceability of patent rights in China has historically been a concern for Western technology companies. Oxford PV's approach to this challenge includes:

- **Extensive patent filing in China**: Over 120 of Oxford PV's 400+ granted patents have Chinese counterparts, providing a comprehensive IP portfolio that covers not only perovskite compositions but also device architectures, manufacturing processes, and module designs.
- **Trade secret protection**: The most critical manufacturing parameters — including precise precursor stoichiometries, deposition process conditions, and reliability-enhancing additive packages — are protected as trade secrets rather than disclosed in published patents, and are transferred to Trina Solar under confidentiality provisions.
- **Deposition equipment control**: The proprietary perovskite deposition equipment, which embodies critical process know-how in its hardware configuration, is supplied to Trina Solar under an equipment supply agreement that includes use restrictions and anti-reverse-engineering provisions.
- **Structuring**: The license agreement includes audit rights, reporting obligations, and non-compliance penalties that provide Oxford PV with monitoring and enforcement mechanisms.
