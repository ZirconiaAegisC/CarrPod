# 15 — INDUSTRY PARTNERSHIPS, COLLABORATIONS & CONSORTIA

## Partnership Ecosystem Overview

Oxford PV's partnership ecosystem spans the photovoltaic value chain from fundamental research to commercial deployment. The company's partnership strategy is designed to access complementary capabilities — silicon cell manufacturing, reliability testing, module interconnection technology, project development, and specialty application engineering — while preserving exclusive control over its core perovskite intellectual property and deposition process.

## Academic and Research Partnerships

### University of Oxford — Founding Relationship

Oxford PV was founded in 2010 as a spinout from the Clarendon Laboratory at the University of Oxford, where Professor Henry Snaith's research group first demonstrated the potential of organometal halide perovskites as photovoltaic absorbers. The company maintains a close research collaboration with the University through:

- **Joint research projects**: Co-funded doctoral and postdoctoral research positions focused on perovskite stability, novel contact materials, and advanced characterization techniques.
- **IP pipeline**: A technology transfer agreement that provides Oxford PV with an exclusive option to license perovskite-related intellectual property developed within the Snaith research group.
- **Shared facilities**: Access to specialized characterization equipment (time-resolved terahertz spectroscopy, photoemission spectroscopy, transient absorption spectroscopy) at Oxford's Department of Physics.
- **Scientific advisory**: Professor Snaith serves as Chief Scientific Officer, providing strategic scientific guidance and maintaining the connection between the company's commercial roadmap and the frontiers of academic perovskite research.

### Fraunhofer Institute for Solar Energy Systems (ISE)

The collaboration with Fraunhofer ISE, Germany's preeminent solar energy research institute, spans multiple work packages:

- **Matrix Shingle Technology**: Joint development of shingled interconnection processes for tandem cells, culminating in the June 2026 demonstration of a 25.6% efficient shingled tandem module (see Section 10).
- **Module reliability testing**: Fraunhofer ISE serves as an independent calibration and testing partner, providing NMI-traceable efficiency measurements and conducting extended reliability testing beyond the scope of TÜV qualification.
- **HoTSun Project**: A German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs and Energy (BMWi)-funded consortium project focused on the development of high-temperature-stable perovskite solar modules for operation in hot climates. Oxford PV leads the industrial work package, with Fraunhofer ISE providing characterization and modeling support.
- **Outdoor monitoring**: Fraunhofer ISE operates test stands at its Freiburg and Gran Canaria sites for Oxford PV tandem modules, providing independent outdoor performance and degradation data.

### Additional Academic Collaborations

- **Helmholtz-Zentrum Berlin (HZB)**: Collaboration on advanced perovskite characterization using synchrotron radiation techniques (grazing-incidence wide-angle X-ray scattering, X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy) at the BESSY II synchrotron facility.
- **Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e)**: Joint research on plasma-assisted atomic layer deposition of barrier layers and transparent conductive oxides for perovskite-silicon tandem devices.
- **University of Cambridge**: Collaboration on computational materials discovery, using density functional theory (DFT) and machine-learned interatomic potentials to screen perovskite compositions for stability and optoelectronic properties.

## Industrial and Commercial Partnerships

### Silicon Cell Suppliers

Oxford PV maintains strategic supply relationships with three qualified tier-1 silicon cell manufacturers. These partnerships extend beyond transactional supply to include:

- **Technical collaboration**: Joint development of silicon cell architectures optimized for tandem integration, including tuning of the silicon cell's front-surface field, emitter sheet resistance, and anti-reflection coating for compatibility with the overlying perovskite cell.
- **Volume commitments**: Multi-year supply agreements that provide Oxford PV with guaranteed cell volumes at competitive pricing while providing the cell suppliers with a premium channel for their highest-efficiency cells.
- **Data sharing**: Exchange of electrical and reliability data to support continuous improvement of the silicon-to-tandem interface.

### Equipment Manufacturers

Oxford PV has established deep technical partnerships with key photovoltaic equipment manufacturers:

- **Von Ardenne GmbH (Dresden, Germany)**: Supplier of TCO sputtering systems, adapted for tandem cell processing. The partnership includes joint development of sputtering processes optimized for the perovskite surface, which is more mechanically and chemically sensitive than the silicon surfaces for which standard solar TCO sputtering processes were developed.
- **3D-Micromac AG (Chemnitz, Germany)**: Supplier of laser scribing and edge deletion systems. The partnership focuses on optimizing laser parameters (wavelength, pulse duration, fluence) for selective ablation of the perovskite and TCO layers without damage to the underlying silicon cell.
- **Teamtechnik GmbH (Freiberg, Germany)**: Supplier of automated stringing systems, adapted for tandem cell handling requirements (lower mechanical load tolerance, sensitivity to gripper residues).

### Project Developers and EPC Contractors

- **BayWa r.e.**: A German-headquartered renewable energy developer and EPC contractor that has placed initial orders for Oxford PV tandem modules for commercial rooftop installations in Germany and the Netherlands. BayWa r.e. provides feedback on installation, commissioning, and operational performance.
- **Eneco**: A Dutch energy company that has committed to deploying Oxford PV tandem modules in utility-scale projects in the Netherlands, a land-constrained market where module efficiency translates directly to project capacity within limited site areas.
- **Lightsource bp**: A global solar developer with headquarters in London; engaged in discussions for pilot deployment of tandem modules at a UK utility-scale site.

## Government and Consortia Relationships

### German Federal Funding

Oxford PV's German subsidiary, Oxford PV Germany GmbH, has been the recipient of multiple grants from the German federal government, reflecting Germany's strategic interest in maintaining domestic photovoltaic manufacturing capability:

- **BMWi Energieforschungsprogramm**: Research funding for perovskite tandem technology development, including the HoTSun consortium project.
- **GRW (Gemeinschaftsaufgabe Verbesserung der regionalen Wirtschaftsstruktur)**: Investment grants supporting the Brandenburg manufacturing facility, recognizing the facility's contribution to economic development in the structurally disadvantaged Brandenburg region.
- **KfW (Kreditanstalt für Wiederaufbau)**: Debt financing facilities for capital equipment investment, providing below-market interest rates through the KfW's environmental and renewable energy lending programs.

### UK Government

As a UK-headquartered company, Oxford PV has benefited from:

- **Innovate UK**: Research grants and innovation loans supporting early-stage technology development at the Oxford R&D center.
- **UK Infrastructure Bank**: Engagement regarding potential debt financing for UK-based manufacturing operations, though no UK manufacturing facility has been announced.

### European Union

- **Horizon Europe**: Participation in collaborative research projects under the EU's flagship research and innovation program, focused on perovskite stability and sustainability (lead recycling, circular economy for perovskite modules).

### Industry Associations

- **SolarPower Europe**: Oxford PV is a member of the European solar industry association and participates in its Manufacturing Workstream, which advocates for EU policy support for domestic photovoltaic manufacturing.
- **European Solar Manufacturing Council (ESMC)**: Membership in the industry body representing European solar manufacturers, providing input on trade policy, supply chain resilience, and EU industrial strategy.

## Intersolar Europe 2026

Oxford PV's presence at Intersolar Europe 2026 (Munich, June 2026) served as a comprehensive showcase of the company's technology and partnership ecosystem:

- **Product Showcase**: First public display of the Gen 3 tandem module series, including the standard glass-glass variant and the bifacial variant in pre-production form.
- **Fraunhofer ISE Joint Presentation**: Unveiling of the 25.6% shingled tandem module result alongside Fraunhofer ISE researchers.
- **Trina Solar Joint Pavilion**: A dedicated section of the Oxford PV stand highlighting the Trina Solar licensing partnership, with Trina Solar representatives presenting the timeline for tandem module production at Trina Solar's Chinese manufacturing facilities.
- **HAPS Demonstration**: First public display of an ultralight tandem module targeted at high-altitude pseudo-satellite applications, generating significant interest from the aerospace and defense sectors.
- **Investor and Analyst Briefing**: A closed-door session for financial analysts and institutional investors, presenting updated technology roadmap milestones, manufacturing capacity expansion plans, and preliminary 2025 financial performance indicators.
