# 04.01 — Revenue & Delivery Trajectory

## 4.1.1 Historical Revenue Performance

| Period | Revenue ($M) | YoY Growth | Vehicles Delivered | ASP ($K, implied) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FY2021 | 27.1 | — | 1,441 | ~100 (limited Dream Edition only) |
| FY2022 | 608.2 | +2,144% | 6,900 | ~92 |
| FY2023 | 595.3 | -2% | 10,500 | ~79 |
| FY2024 | 803.0 | +35% | 10,200 | ~84 |
| FY2025 | 1,352.3 | +68% | 15,841 | ~85 |
| Q4 2025 | 522.7 | +123% YoY | 5,345 | ~98 (Gravity mix boost) |
| Q1 2026 | 282.5 | +20% YoY | 3,093 | ~91 |

**Key Observations:**

1. FY2024 revenue grew 35% on essentially flat deliveries (+0% units; 10,200 vs. 10,500), driven by ASP improvement from the Grand Touring and Sapphire mix shift.
2. FY2025 revenue growth of 68% was powered by both unit growth (+55% deliveries) and mixed ASP increase (+$1K vs. FY2024) as Gravity joined the product mix at its $118K+ Grand Touring price point.
3. Q4 2025 was the first quarter in which Gravity materially contributed to delivery volumes. Q4 revenue of $522.7M represented 39% of full-year FY2025 revenue — a quarter that saw 34% of annual deliveries.
4. Q1 2026 results were depressed by the Gravity stop-sale, which constrained deliveries to 3,093 units. Absent the stop-sale, deliveries at Q4 2025 exit rate would have trended toward 5,500–6,000, and Q1 revenue would have been in the $450–500M range.

## 4.1.4 FY2026 Guidance and Projections

### Lucid Official Guidance:

- **Production:** 25,000–27,000 units
- **Capex:** $1.2–1.4 billion
- **Liquidity Runway:** Into H2 2027 (at current burn rates, before additional capital)
- **Gross Margin:** Not guided (implied: remains sharply negative through FY2026)
- **Cash Burn:** Not explicitly guided; analyst consensus free cash flow burn of $(2.0)–$(2.5) billion for FY2026

### Revenue Bridge FY2025A → FY2026E:

| Scenario | Deliveries | ASP | Vehicle Revenue | Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Base Case (Guidance Midpoint)** | 26,000 | ~$92K | ~$2,392M | ~$2,550M (+89% YoY) |
| **Bull Case** | 30,000 | ~$95K | ~$2,850M | ~$3,050M (+126% YoY) |
| **Bear Case** | 22,000 | ~$88K | ~$1,936M | ~$2,050M (+52% YoY) |

**Base Case Assumptions:**

- Gravity: 13,000–15,000 deliveries, ASP $110K (Grand Touring mix heavy, Touring ramp later)
- Air: 11,000–13,000 deliveries, ASP $78K (Pure and Touring dominant)
- Gravity stop-sale fully resolved; Q2–Q4 2026 quarterly deliveries building to 7,000–8,000/quarter by Q4 2026 exit rate
- Regulatory credit revenue of $80–100M
- No material robotaxi vehicle sale revenue in FY2026 (engineering validation vehicles only)

## 4.1.5 Medium-Term Revenue Trajectory (2027–2030)

| Year | Deliveries | ASP ($K) | Vehicle Revenue ($B) | Other Revenue ($B) | Total Revenue ($B) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FY2026E | 26,000 | 92 | 2.4 | 0.2 | **2.6** |
| FY2027E | 45,000–55,000 | 88 | 3.9–4.8 | 0.3–0.4 | **4.2–5.2** |
| FY2028E | 90,000–110,000 | 75 | 6.7–8.2 | 0.5–0.7 | **7.2–8.9** |
| FY2029E | 150,000–180,000 | 68 | 10.2–12.2 | 0.8–1.2 | **11.0–13.4** |
| FY2030E | 220,000–280,000 | 62 | 13.6–17.4 | 1.2–2.0 | **14.8–19.4** |

**Key Ramp Assumptions:**

- Midsize platform (Cosmos + Earth) begins volume production at AMP-1 in H2 2027; reaches 50,000+ annual run rate by end of 2028
- AMP-2 CBU Phase 2 begins production in 2028; contributes 30,000–50,000 units annually by 2029
- ASP declines as Midsize platform (sub-$50K entry, blended ASP ~$55–65K) becomes the dominant volume driver
- Gravity and Air maintain premium positioning (Gravity ~25,000/yr, Air ~15,000/yr at steady state)
- Robotaxi vehicle sales and trip revenue become material beginning in 2028–2029

## 4.1.6 Risk Factors to Revenue Trajectory

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Midsize platform delay beyond 2027 | Medium | High — delays volume ramp by 12+ months | AMP-1 tooling prioritization; Midsize engineering team scaled to ~800 |
| Gravity demand softens post-initial reservation fulfillment | Low-Medium | Medium — reduces FY2026–2027 revenue by $400–800M | Touring variant at lower ASP ($94.9K) broadens addressable market; lease program expansion |
| Macro recession suppresses luxury auto demand | Medium | Medium — ASP and volume both pressured | Geographic diversification; Saudi government fleet orders provide demand floor |
| AMP-2 CBU delays (construction, supply chain, export licensing) | Medium | Medium-High — defers 50–100K annual unit capacity to 2029+ | SKD operations continue; PIF funding ensures construction timeline |
| Regulatory credit market softens (ZEV credit price decline) | Low-Medium | Low — <$100M annual revenue impact | Credit sales are incremental; not core to thesis |
| Uber robotaxi deployment delayed beyond H2 2026 | Medium | Low — near-term revenue negligible; sentiment impact | 75 engineering vehicles already delivered; technology validation proceeds independent of Uber timeline |
