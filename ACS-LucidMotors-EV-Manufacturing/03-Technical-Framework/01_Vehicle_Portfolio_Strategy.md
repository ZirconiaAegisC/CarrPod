## 3.1.2 Lucid Air (Large Sedan Platform)

**Launch:** September 2021 (Dream Edition); continuous deliveries since  
**Segment:** Full-size luxury sedan (EPA: Large Car)  
**Competes Against:** Tesla Model S, Mercedes-Benz EQS, BMW i7, Porsche Taycan

### Current Variants (2026 Model Year):

| Variant | Powertrain | EPA Range | 0–60 mph | Base MSRP (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Air Pure | RWD, single motor | 420 mi | 4.5s | $69,900 |
| Air Touring | AWD, dual motor | 406 mi | 3.4s | $79,900 |
| Air Grand Touring | AWD, dual motor | 512 mi | 3.0s | $112,400 |
| Air Grand Touring Performance | AWD, dual motor | 446 mi | 2.6s | $135,600 |
| Air Sapphire | AWD, tri-motor | 427 mi | 1.89s | $250,500 |

### Technology Highlights:

- **900V+ architecture:** Industry's highest-voltage production EV system, enabling 300 kW DC fast charging (200 miles in ~12 minutes).
- **Proprietary wound stator motor:** Lucid's in-house permanent magnet synchronous motor design achieves >10.5 kW/kg specific power — roughly double the industry average.
- **Battery pack:** 92 kWh (Pure) to 118 kWh (Grand Touring) nominal; pouch-cell format supplied by Panasonic Energy (Japan) and Samsung SDI.
- **Aerodynamics:** Coefficient of drag (Cd) of 0.197 (Air Pure) — lowest of any production vehicle globally.
- **Wunderbox onboard charger:** 19.2 kW AC bidirectional charging; vehicle-to-load (V2L) and vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) capability.

### Production & Delivery Metrics:

| Period | Air Produced | Air Delivered | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| FY2021 | 2,500 | 2,400 | Ramp commenced |
| FY2022 | 7,180 | 6,900 | Supply chain headwinds |
| FY2023 | 10,900 | 10,500 | Efficiency improvements |
| FY2024 | 11,100 | 10,200 | Gravity launch overlap |
| FY2025 | 15,500 (est.) | 13,841 (est.) | Gravity ramp begins |
| Q1 2026 | 4,200 (est.) | 2,100 (est.) | Gravity production priority |

**2026 Car and Driver 10Best Award:** The Lucid Air was named to the Car and Driver 10Best list for 2026, recognizing segment-leading range, efficiency, and driving dynamics.

### Air Market Assessment:

The full-size luxury sedan market is contracting globally (ICE and EV), with buyers migrating toward SUVs. Lucid's Air strategy has shifted accordingly: (1) reduce Pure pricing to expand addressable market, (2) position Air as a technology halo for the Gravity and Midsize platforms, (3) maintain Grand Touring and Sapphire trims as brand-defining, high-margin products. Annual Air volume is expected to plateau at 15,000–20,000 units as Gravity and Midsize become the volume drivers.

## 3.1.3 Lucid Gravity (Large SUV Platform)

**Reveal:** November 2023 (LA Auto Show)  
**Launch:** Q4 2024 (customer deliveries commenced late 2024)  
**Segment:** Full-size luxury three-row SUV  
**Competes Against:** Tesla Model X, Mercedes-Benz EQS SUV, Rivian R1S, BMW iX, Cadillac Escalade IQ

### Variants (2026 Model Year):

| Variant | Powertrain | EPA Range (est.) | 0–60 mph | Base MSRP (USD) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Gravity Touring | AWD, dual motor | 440+ mi | 3.5s | $94,900 |
| Gravity Grand Touring | AWD, dual motor | 440+ mi | 3.4s | $118,000 |
| Gravity Dream Edition | AWD, dual motor | 440+ mi | 3.3s | $149,000 (limited) |

### Key Attributes:

- **Three-row seating:** Seven-passenger configuration with fold-flat second and third rows. Total cargo volume: 112+ cubic feet.
- **Shared platform:** Common 900V+ architecture, motor technology, and battery modules with Air; ~60% parts commonality.
- **Towing capacity:** 6,000 lbs (estimated) with tow package.
- **Air suspension:** Standard adaptive air suspension with adjustable ride height (5.7-inch range) and continuous damping control.
- **2026 World Luxury Car of the Year:** Awarded by World Car Awards jury in March 2026.

### Q1 2026 Stop-Sale Incident:

The Gravity's initial delivery ramp was materially impacted by a 29-day voluntary stop-sale (February–March 2026) stemming from a seat component supplier quality issue. The stop-sale affected approximately 1,400 units in-transit and at Studios. Resolution was achieved in late March 2026. Order intake rebounded +144% in March versus February following resolution, indicating sustained demand. Lingering effects include: (1) approximately $1.47 billion in inventory on the balance sheet at Q1 2026 close due to units held during the stop-sale, (2) $200M+ in inventory impairment charges recognized in Q1 2026, and (3) a ~3,000-unit delivery headwind in Q1 2026 (actual deliveries of 3,093 versus analyst expectations of ~5,500).

### Gravity Market Assessment:

The full-size luxury SUV segment represents a fundamentally larger addressable market than the sedan segment. Global luxury SUV sales (ICE + EV) exceed 2 million units annually. Gravity's three-row configuration positions it against the largest and highest-margin sub-segment. If Lucid can achieve sustained 25,000+ annual Gravity deliveries at ASPs above $100,000, the product alone could generate $2.5B+ in annual revenue — exceeding FY2025 total company revenue.

## 3.1.4 Midsize Platform (Cosmos, Earth, Third Model)

**Announced:** September 2023 (Technology & Manufacturing Day)  
**Production Start:** 2027 (ramping) — delayed from original late 2026 target  
**Segment:** Mid-size SUV (two models initially, third model later)  
**Entry Price:** Below $50,000 (base Cosmos)  
**Competes Against:** Tesla Model Y, Cadillac Lyriq, Genesis GV70 Electrified, Polestar 3, future Rivian R2

### Platform Architecture:

- **926V electrical architecture:** Higher voltage than the Large platform; further efficiency gains.
- **Scalable battery:** 60–90 kWh pack sizes projected; targeting 300+ miles EPA range in base configuration.
- **Shared Atlas EDU components:** Common motor and inverter technologies adapted for cost-sensitive mid-size application.
- **Manufacturing location:** AMP-1 (Casa Grande) initially; AMP-2 (Saudi Arabia) for regional production long-term.
- **Annual volume target:** 150,000+ units/year at full ramp (across all midsize variants, both manufacturing campuses).

### Product Variants:

| Model | Body Style | Launch Timing | Target ASP | Volume Ambition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lucid Cosmos | Mid-size SUV (2-row) | 2027 (ramp) | $48,000–$75,000 | 75K+ units/yr |
| Lucid Earth | Mid-size SUV (variant) | 2027+ | $55,000–$85,000 | 50K+ units/yr |
| Third Model (TBD) | TBD (sedan or crossover) | 2028+ | $50,000–$80,000 | TBD |

### Strategic Importance:

The Midsize platform is the existential volume play for Lucid. At current AMP-1 fixed cost absorption rates, the Large platform alone cannot achieve gross margin breakeven without substantial volume — and the Large platform's addressable market is insufficient to deliver the 50,000+ annual units that would be needed. The Midsize platform, with its sub-$50K entry point and mass-market SUV positioning, targets the heart of the global EV market where Tesla Model Y currently dominates (1.2M+ units/year globally).

Key risks: (1) the 12–18 month delay in Midsize ramp means Lucid must bridge from Gravity-only volumes through H2 2027, (2) the sub-$50K entry price requires aggressive battery cost reduction (below $80/kWh at the pack level, from current estimated $110–125/kWh), (3) Midsize tooling capex overlaps with ongoing Gravity ramp spending, pressuring free cash flow.

## 3.1.5 Atlas Electric Drive Unit

**Announced:** June 2023  
**Status:** Production commenced; supply agreement with Aston Martin active

The Atlas drive unit is Lucid's external-facing propulsion business. It packages Lucid's proprietary motor, inverter, and transmission technologies into a compact EDU sold to third-party OEMs. The founding supply agreement, with Aston Martin Lagonda for its future EV portfolio, was announced in 2023. Terms include per-unit pricing, technical support, and access to Lucid's efficiency IP.

### Atlas Specs (Representative):

- **Motor type:** Permanent magnet synchronous, hairpin stator winding
- **Peak power:** 350–500 kW (scalable)
- **Specific power:** >9 kW/kg
- **Voltage range:** 800–926V compatible
- **Integrated inverter:** Silicon carbide (SiC) MOSFET, 900V+ rated

### Revenue Potential:

Atlas generates a modest but strategically important revenue stream. At an estimated $5,000–$8,000 per unit to external OEMs, and with Aston Martin volumes projected at 10,000–15,000 units/year at full ramp, annual Atlas external revenue could reach $50–120 million. More significant is Lucid's use of Atlas as the propulsion backbone for its own Midsize platform and robotaxi fleet — internal transfer pricing avoids margin recognition, but the unit economics benefit from shared R&D amortization.
