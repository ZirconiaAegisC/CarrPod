# 03.02 — AMP-1 Manufacturing & Arizona Operations

## 3.2.1 Facility Overview

**Location:** 1 Lucid Way, Casa Grande, Arizona 85194 (Pinal County)  
**Site Area:** 590 acres (Phase 1: ~82 acres under roof; Phase 2: ~160 acres under roof total)  
**Investment to Date:** ~$2.4 billion cumulative capital expenditure (through Q1 2026)

## 3.2.2 Manufacturing Phases

### Phase 1 (Complete: 2020–2023)

Phase 1 occupies the western portion of the Casa Grande site and houses:

- **General Assembly (GA1):** 999,000 sq. ft. Purpose-built for Lucid Air production. Single-shift operation capable of 34,000 units/year. Currently producing Air Pure, Touring, Grand Touring, and Sapphire variants on mixed-model line. First-pass yield: ~85% (Q1 2026, improving from ~78% at FY2024 exit).
- **Body Shop (BS1):** Integrated within GA1 footprint. Robotic welding and adhesive bonding for aluminum-intensive Air body-in-white. 230+ Fanuc and ABB robots. Cycle time: ~90 seconds per station.
- **Paint Shop (PS1):** Integrated within GA1. Automated base coat/clear coat system supplied by Dürr AG. Capacity: 40+ jobs per hour. Six standard colors plus Sapphire-exclusive hues.
- **Battery Pack Assembly (BP1):** In-house module and pack assembly. Pouch cells received from Panasonic Energy (Japan) and Samsung SDI; modules assembled, tested, and installed into packs on-site. Capacity: ~40,000 packs/year.
- **Electric Drive Unit Production (EDU1):** Lucid's proprietary wound-stator motor manufacturing. In-sourced stator winding, rotor assembly, and final drive unit integration. One of two Lucid motor plants globally (AMP-1 and Newark pilot).
- **Stamping (ST1):** In-house stamping for select aluminum and steel body panels. Die sets for Air doors, hood, and decklid.

### Phase 2 (Ongoing: 2023–2026)

Phase 2 occupies the eastern portion of the site and adds:

- **General Assembly (GA2):** 2.8 million sq. ft. Designed for Gravity SUV production (current) and Midsize platform (future, tooling underway). Dual-line configuration: one line for Gravity, one line reserved for Midsize. Installed capacity: 56,000 units/year (combined).
- **Body Shop (BS2):** Gravity-specific body-in-white facility. Optimized for steel-intensive Gravity structure (versus aluminum-intensive Air). Higher robotic density: ~350 robots for increased automation.
- **Stamping Expansion (ST2):** Additional press lines for Gravity body panels. Schuler servo press (2,500-ton) installed Q3 2025.
- **Battery Pack Assembly (BP2):** Second pack line for Gravity. Higher throughput (~60 packs/hour) with automated material handling.
- **Logistics Center (LC1):** 700,000 sq. ft. centralized parts receiving, sequencing, and kitting facility supporting GA1 and GA2 lines.

### Phase 3 (Planned: 2027–2028)

- **Midsize Platform Tooling:** Dedication of GA2 second line to Midsize platform production. Tooling capex estimated at $400–600 million.
- **Battery Module Expansion:** Third module line to support higher-volume Midsize production.
- **On-Site Supplier Park:** Reserved land for Tier 1 supplier co-location (seats, IP, bumpers, tires) to reduce inbound logistics cost and inventory carrying requirements. Letters of intent signed with three suppliers as of Q1 2026.

## 3.2.4 Manufacturing Efficiency & Cost Structure

### Key Cost Drivers (Q1 2026 Estimates):

| Cost Element | Per-Unit Cost (Approx.) | % of ASP (Air Pure $69.9K) |
|---|---|---|
| Battery Pack (92 kWh) | $10,000–$11,500 | 14–16% |
| Electric Drive Unit | $5,500–$7,000 | 8–10% |
| Body Structure & Stamping | $8,000–$9,500 | 11–14% |
| Interior/Trim/Infotainment | $9,000–$11,000 | 13–16% |
| Chassis & Suspension | $6,000–$7,500 | 9–11% |
| Direct Labor | $9,000–$12,000 | 13–17% |
| Manufacturing Overhead (Fixed) | $20,000–$28,000 | 29–40% |
| **Total Cost of Goods Sold** | **$67,500–$86,500** | **97–124%** |
| Logistics & Delivery | $2,500–$3,500 | 4–5% |
| Warranty & Reserves | $3,000–$5,000 | 4–7% |

### Fixed Cost Absorption:

The dominant driver of Lucid's negative gross margin is fixed manufacturing overhead — depreciation on the $2.4B AMP-1 investment, facilities costs, indirect labor, and tooling amortization. At 17,840 units produced in FY2025, fixed overhead per unit was approximately $28,000. At the FY2026 guidance midpoint (26,000 units), this would decline to approximately $19,000 per unit. At 50,000 units, it approaches $10,000 per unit. At 90,000 units (installed capacity), it would reach approximately $5,500 per unit. This absorption curve is the central premise of Lucid's margin recovery thesis.

### Variable Cost Reduction Initiatives:

1. **Battery cost:** Targeting <$100/kWh at the pack level by late 2026 through cell chemistry improvements, supplier diversification, and scale.
2. **Insourcing:** Stator winding, EDU assembly, and battery pack assembly performed in-house at AMP-1, capturing margin otherwise paid to Tier 1 suppliers.
3. **Design-to-cost:** Gravity features approximately 35% fewer unique fasteners than Air; Midsize platform targets 50% parts count reduction versus Large platform.
4. **Supplier consolidation:** Reduction from 500+ production suppliers (2023) to <350 targeted by end of 2026.

## 3.2.5 Supply Chain & Logistics

### Supplier Geography:

| Region | % of Supply Base | Key Components |
|---|---|---|
| United States | ~45% | Stamping, interiors, electronics, glass |
| Mexico | ~15% | Wire harnesses, small stampings, plastic trim |
| Europe | ~20% | Chassis components, bearings, seal systems |
| Asia (Japan, Korea, China) | ~20% | Battery cells, semiconductors, displays |

### Logistics Network:

- **Inbound:** Cross-dock consolidation center in Phoenix, AZ (~45 miles from AMP-1) for supplier milk runs. Rail-served for Asian container freight via Port of Long Beach.
- **Outbound:** Finished vehicles transported via enclosed carrier to regional delivery centers in Newark (CA), Dallas (TX), Chicago (IL), Atlanta (GA), and Newark (NJ). Average delivery-to-customer time from AMP-1: 12–18 days (U.S.).
- **Inventory:** Q1 2026 inventory balance of $1.47 billion includes approximately 2,400 units held during Gravity stop-sale, raw materials for Midsize pre-build, and strategic battery cell buffer of ~45 days.

## 3.2.6 Workforce

AMP-1's manufacturing workforce is non-unionized. Arizona is a right-to-work state. Lucid's hourly manufacturing compensation (base + benefits) is approximately $55–65/hour all-in, competitive with regional manufacturing peers. The company recruited heavily from the Phoenix-area aerospace and semiconductor manufacturing talent pools (Honeywell, Intel, Boeing). Attrition in manufacturing roles was ~12% in FY2025, within industry norms.

**Training:** Lucid operates a 12-week Manufacturing Technician Training Program in partnership with Central Arizona College and Pinal County workforce development. New hires complete classroom and on-the-job training before line assignment. Approximately 400 technicians have completed the program since 2021.
