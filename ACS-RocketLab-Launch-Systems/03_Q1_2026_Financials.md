# 03 — Q1 2026 Financial Results & Q2 2026 Guidance

## Q1 2026 Financial Highlights

Rocket Lab delivered Q1 2026 revenue of $200.3 million, representing 63.5% year-over-year growth compared to $123 million in Q1 2025. This marks the company's highest quarterly revenue on record and the first time surpassing the $200 million threshold. The strong performance was driven by sustained Electron launch cadence (approximately 5 missions), recognition of Space Systems contract milestones — particularly related to the SDA Tranche 3 Tracking Layer program — and growing contributions from the Mynaric and GEOST product lines.

Key financial metrics for Q1 2026:

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Q1 2025 | YoY Change |
|--------|---------|---------|------------|
| Revenue | $200.3M | $122.5M | +63.5% |
| GAAP Gross Margin | 38.2% | ~27% | +11.2pp |
| Operating Loss | ~($38M) | ~($50M) | Improvement |
| Net Loss | ($45M) | ~($58M) | Improvement |
| Adjusted EBITDA | Positive | ~($10M) | Inflection |
| Cash & Equivalents | $1.48B | ~$0.95B | +$530M |
| Backlog | $2.2B | ~$1.4B | +57% |

GAAP gross margin reached 38.2% in Q1 2026, a significant sequential improvement from the FY2025 average of approximately 24.4%. The margin expansion reflects several converging factors:

1. **Space Systems Mix**: Higher proportion of Space Systems revenue at structurally higher margins. Merchant component sales (solar cells, reaction wheels, separation systems) command 40–50% gross margins, while prime spacecraft contracts deliver 20–30% margins.

2. **Launch Fixed-Cost Absorption**: Increased launch cadence (annualized run rate of 20+ launches) spreads fixed pad, range, and personnel costs across more missions. The incremental cost of an additional Electron launch is significantly below the $7.5M revenue per mission.

3. **Manufacturing Efficiencies**: The SolAero solar cell production line and Rutherford engine production line benefit from learning curve effects. SolAero MOCVD reactor utilization rates improved as demand for space-grade solar cells from both Rocket Lab internal programs and external customers increased.

4. **Acquisition Contribution Margin**: Mynaric CONDOR laser terminal shipments and GEOST sensor deliveries contributed at margins above the corporate average, as these products carry higher pricing power within their specialized defense niches.

5. **Favorable Revenue Mix Within Space Systems**: Within the Space Systems division, the contribution from higher-margin component sales increased relative to lower-margin prime spacecraft contracts, as SDA Tranche 3 satellite manufacturing ramped but had not yet peaked.

Net loss improved to $45 million in Q1 2026, down from a net loss of approximately $58 million in Q1 2025. The improvement of approximately $13M YoY was driven by higher gross profit ($76.5M vs. ~$33M) partially offset by increased R&D spending on Neutron and SDA bus engineering. Interest income of approximately $18M (annualized ~$72M) on the $1.48B cash balance offset a meaningful portion of operating losses.

Adjusted EBITDA turned positive during the quarter, representing an important inflection in operating leverage. Management has indicated that Adjusted EBITDA profitability is sustainable on a go-forward basis barring abnormal expense items, with further improvement expected as Neutron R&D spending declines post-first flight and SDA production achieves steady-state throughput.

## Backlog Growth and Composition

The contract backlog reached $2.2 billion as of March 31, 2026, reflecting a 20.2% quarter-over-quarter increase from approximately $1.83 billion at year-end FY2025. The growth was fueled by new bookings including follow-on SDA satellite orders, additional MACH-TB HASTE mission task orders, classified national security spacecraft contracts, and commercial constellation manufacturing awards.

Backlog composition by customer type:
- **U.S. Government** (SDA, DoD, NASA, classified): ~55%
- **Commercial Constellation Operators**: ~30%
- **International Government & Civil**: ~10%
- **Research & University**: ~5%

By segment:
- **Space Systems**: ~70% by dollar value
- **Launch Services**: ~25%
- **Engineering Services**: ~5%

The backlog provides approximately 9–10 quarters of visible revenue, characteristic of established defense primes. The dominance of Space Systems within the backlog (70%) is notable — it reflects the structural shift in Rocket Lab's business from a launch company to a space infrastructure company.

## Balance Sheet and Liquidity

Rocket Lab held $1.48 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities as of March 31, 2026. Total liquidity, including undrawn credit facilities, exceeds $2 billion. The company carries negligible long-term debt (<$50M), consisting primarily of equipment financing and facility leases.

Key balance sheet metrics:
- **Cash & Equivalents**: $1.48B
- **Total Assets**: ~$2.8B (including ~$400M in goodwill and intangible assets from acquisitions)
- **Long-Term Debt**: <$50M
- **Net Cash Position**: >$1.4B (cash less total debt)
- **Working Capital**: >$1.5B
- **Tangible Book Value**: ~$1.8B

The cash position generates approximately $60–70M in annual interest income at prevailing rates, materially offsetting operating losses and extending runway. At the current burn rate (operating cash consumption of approximately $80–100M annually net of interest income), the cash runway extends well beyond 10 years, providing ample time to reach Neutron commercial operations and GAAP profitability.

## Q2 2026 Guidance

Management issued Q2 2026 revenue guidance of $225 million to $240 million, representing 55–65% year-over-year growth compared to Q2 2025's $145 million. The midpoint of $232.5 million implies continued sequential growth from Q1 2026.

| Metric | Q2 2026 Guidance | Q1 2026 Actual | Implied Change |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|----------------|
| Revenue | $225M – $240M | $200.3M | +12% to +20% sequential |
| Electron Launches | 5–6 planned | ~5 | Stable cadence |
| Gross Margin | ~38–40% | 38.2% | Continued expansion |

The Q2 guidance reflects: (1) a full quarter of Mynaric CONDOR laser terminal shipments following production ramp, (2) SDA Tranche 3 milestone billings from the first spacecraft entering assembly, (3) continued high-cadence Electron operations at LC-1, and (4) HASTE mission revenue recognition under the MACH-TB contract. Management has explicitly noted that Neutron launch revenue is not incorporated into FY2026 guidance, pending first flight later in the year.

Full-year FY2026 revenue, extrapolating from Q1 actual and Q2 guidance, is tracking toward $900–950M, representing 50–58% annual growth. This trajectory, if sustained, would place Rocket Lab within striking distance of $1B annual revenue in FY2027 — a threshold that typically triggers institutional investor re-evaluation of a company's maturity and investability.
