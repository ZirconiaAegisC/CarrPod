## Capital Formation Summary

Helion Energy has raised $1.5 billion in cumulative private capital across at least seven funding rounds, achieving a $15.5 billion post-money valuation in its most recent Series G (June 2026). The company is the single largest recipient of private fusion investment globally, accounting for approximately 57% of the $2.64 billion in total private fusion sector capital recorded through 2025.

## Valuation Analysis

### Valuation Progression

The $15.5 billion Series G valuation represents a compound annual growth rate in valuation that far exceeds typical venture trajectories, even in deep-tech sectors. Key valuation drivers:

1. **Technical Milestone Progression:** Valuation inflection points correlate with prototype achievements — Trenta (100M°C), Polaris D-T fusion, and Polaris temperature record.
2. **Commercial Contract Anchoring:** The Microsoft PPA (2023) provided a concrete revenue pathway, transforming Helion from a pure R&D enterprise into a pre-revenue commercial entity with a binding offtake commitment.
3. **Regulatory Clarity:** The ADVANCE Act (2024) and Washington state licensure (2026) removed existential regulatory risk, expanding the addressable market and de-risking the investment thesis.
4. **Strategic Investor Signaling:** Sam Altman's sustained capital commitment (approximately one-third ownership) and SoftBank's participation provided validation that attracted follow-on institutional capital, culminating in Thrive Capital's Series G lead.

Relative to publicly funded fusion programs:
- **ITER:** ~$22B+ estimated total cost; no private capital return mechanism.
- **NIF (National Ignition Facility):** ~$3.5B construction cost; achieved ignition December 2022 but at a per-shot cost incompatible with commercial power.
- **Helion:** $1.5B to date; commercial PPA signed; plant under construction; regulatory licenses issued.

Helion's capital efficiency — measured as private capital deployed per technical milestone achieved — is unmatched in the fusion sector.
