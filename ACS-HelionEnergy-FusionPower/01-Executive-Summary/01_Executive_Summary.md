# Executive Summary: Helion Energy Fusion Power Program

**Date:** 15 July 2026
**Classification:** Corporate Dossier — Strategic Analysis
**Subject:** Helion Energy — Polaris Prototype, Orion Commercial Plant, and Market Position

---

## Overview

Helion Energy (Everett, Washington) is the world's most advanced private fusion energy company. Founded by CEO David Kirtley, the firm has raised $1.5 billion in cumulative capital, achieving a $15.5 billion valuation following its June 2026 Series G ($465 million, led by Thrive Capital). Helion holds the first-ever fusion power purchase agreement (PPA) with Microsoft (2023), requiring delivery of 50 MW by 2028 with financial penalties for non-performance. The company is simultaneously constructing its first commercial fusion plant — Orion (Malaga, Washington, Chelan County) — which broke ground in July 2025.

Helion's approach is fundamentally distinct from all competitors: field-reversed configuration (FRC) plasma confinement, pulsed magnetic compression, and direct electricity recovery without steam turbines. Commercial fuel targets deuterium-helium-3 (D-He3), operating at 200 million °C, with deuterium-tritium (D-T) used solely for validation testing.

## Key Findings

### Technical Maturity
- **Seven generations of prototypes** culminating in Polaris (7th-generation, 60-ft, 150 million °C capable).
- Polaris achieved D-T fusion in early 2026 and broke its own temperature record in February 2026.
- Polaris features 50 MJ capacitor banks, spans 30,000 sq ft, and is the first privately funded machine to demonstrate D-T fusion.
- Tiny Merge, a 1/8-scale Polaris derivative, commenced construction in spring 2026.

### Commercial Traction
- **Microsoft PPA (2023):** World's first fusion power purchase agreement; 50 MW delivery obligation by 2028 with financial penalties.
- **Nucor development agreement:** 500 MW collaborative development deal.
- **Orion plant under construction:** 50 MW capacity, Malaga, WA; broke ground July 2025; generator building earthwork commenced spring 2026; Polaris assembly/office building complete.

### Regulatory Achievement
- **First fusion company** to receive Washington Department of Health Radioactive Materials License (RML) and Radiation Air Emissions License (RAEL) — June 2026.
- **ADVANCE Act (2024)** and Washington state laws **HB 1924/HB 1018** establish a dedicated fusion regulatory pathway, distinct from fission.
- Transmission interconnection agreement with Chelan County Public Utility District in progress.

### Financial Position
- $1.5 billion total capital raised; $15.5 billion post-money valuation.
- Sam Altman holds approximately one-third ownership; resigned from board March 2026 as OpenAI explored Helion partnership.
- Key investors: SoftBank, Lightspeed Venture Partners, Mithril Capital, Capricorn Investment Group, Bill Ford.
- $2.64 billion total private fusion sector capital (2025); Helion represents the largest single-position concentration.
- ARPA-E allocated $135 million to fusion investment in April 2026.

### Manufacturing & Supply Chain
- Helion manufactures proprietary capacitors and ultra-high-pressure ceramics in-house — a critical differentiator and technical barrier to entry.
- Supply chain vertical integration mitigates dependency on third-party advanced manufacturing capacity.

## Strategic Significance

Helion Energy occupies a unique position at the intersection of technical validation, commercial execution, and regulatory enablement. The Polaris prototype has demonstrated the core physics; the Orion plant represents the transition from experimental to commercial operations. The Microsoft PPA imposes hard delivery milestones, rendering the 2028 deadline a binary inflection point. Concurrently, Nucor's 500 MW development agreement signals industrial-scale demand validation.

The regulatory framework — established through the ADVANCE Act, HB 1924, and HB 1018 — provides Helion with a first-mover licensing advantage that competitors cannot readily replicate. The Washington DOH RML and RAEL licenses (June 2026) are precedential achievements.

Helion's direct electricity recovery via pulsed magnetic compression eliminates the steam turbine cycle that constrains all magnetic confinement and inertial confinement competitors, yielding a fundamentally simpler and potentially lower-cost power plant architecture.

## Risk Factors

- **Schedule execution risk:** The company has a documented history of missed deadlines.
- **PPA penalty exposure:** Microsoft delivery obligation carries financial penalties for non-performance by 2028.
- **D-He3 supply chain:** Commercial-scale He3 availability remains unresolved; the fuel cycle is unproven at industrial scale.
- **Investor concentration:** Sam Altman's one-third ownership and board resignation introduce governance uncertainty.
- **Manufacturing scalability:** In-house capacitor and ceramics production must scale from prototype to commercial plant volume.
