# Microsoft Power Purchase Agreement & Commercial Partnerships

**Date:** 15 July 2026
**Classification:** Corporate Dossier — Section 03.3

---

## Overview

Helion Energy has secured the world's first fusion power purchase agreement (PPA) — a 2023 contract with Microsoft Corporation obligating Helion to deliver 50 megawatts of net electrical power by 2028, with financial penalties for non-performance. This agreement, combined with a 500 MW development deal with Nucor Corporation, establishes Helion as the only fusion company with binding commercial offtake commitments.

### Strategic Context
Microsoft's agreement with Helion aligns with the company's commitment to carbon-negative operations by 2030. The PPA provides Microsoft with:
- Early-mover access to fusion-generated electricity
- A hedge against long-term renewable energy price volatility
- Demonstrated commitment to advanced clean energy technologies for regulatory and reputational positioning

For Helion, the PPA provides:
- Revenue certainty for initial commercial operations
- A creditworthy offtaker to support project financing
- Market validation that has facilitated subsequent capital raises
- A hard delivery deadline that disciplines organizational execution

### Penalty Exposure
The financial penalty provisions of the PPA are not publicly disclosed. However, the existence of penalties creates a binary risk-reward profile for Helion:
- **Scenario A (on-time delivery):** Helion becomes the first commercial fusion operator globally; PPA revenue stream validates the business model; subsequent offtake agreements priced at premium.
- **Scenario B (delayed delivery):** Financial penalties erode capital reserves; reputational damage complicates future customer acquisition; valuation impact on private and eventual public markets.

## Nucor Development Agreement

### Agreement Structure

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Counterparty | Nucor Corporation |
| Agreement Type | Development Agreement |
| Capacity Target | 500 MW |
| Status | Pre-construction development phase |

### Strategic Significance
The Nucor agreement addresses industrial-scale clean electricity demand. Steel manufacturing — Nucor's core business — is among the most energy-intensive industrial sectors and faces mounting decarbonization pressure. A 500 MW fusion power supply would:

- Displace natural gas and grid electricity in electric arc furnace operations
- Provide firm, dispatchable, carbon-free power — a profile that intermittent renewables cannot match
- Establish Helion as an industrial-scale supplier beyond the technology-sector early-adopter market

The 500 MW scale is an order of magnitude beyond the Orion 50 MW plant, implying a second-generation commercial plant design and a substantially larger manufacturing footprint.

## Transmission Interconnection

Helion's transmission interconnection agreement with Chelan County Public Utility District (PUD) is in progress as of mid-2026. This agreement is a prerequisite for delivering power from the Orion plant to Microsoft (and ultimately to the broader grid). Key considerations:

- **Chelan County PUD** operates significant hydroelectric generation and transmission assets in central Washington.
- The Columbia River hydropower system provides a stable grid backbone for interconnection.
- Interconnection queue processes at the PUD and potentially at the Bonneville Power Administration (BPA) level are regulatory prerequisites.

## Market Implications

### Fusion as a Firm Clean Power Source
The Microsoft PPA establishes a market precedent: fusion electricity is commercially bankable — at least for one of the world's largest corporations. This precedent has two follow-on effects:

1. **Competitive pressure on other fusion developers:** Commonwealth Fusion Systems, TAE Technologies, General Fusion, and Zap Energy must now demonstrate not just technical capability but commercial offtake viability.
2. **Regulatory normalization:** A binding PPA strengthens the case for fusion-specific regulatory frameworks, as it demonstrates genuine commercial demand rather than purely experimental interest.

### Pricing Implications
The PPA strike price is not publicly disclosed. However, Helion's direct electricity recovery architecture (no steam cycle capital cost) and compact plant footprint suggest a potentially competitive levelized cost of electricity (LCOE) relative to other firm clean power sources (nuclear fission, natural gas with carbon capture, geothermal).

### Scalability Signal
The jump from 50 MW (Orion/Microsoft) to 500 MW (Nucor) implies Helion's confidence in a modular, scalable plant architecture rather than a one-off demonstration. This is consistent with the FRC approach, which does not require the economies of scale that drive tokamak reactor sizing.
