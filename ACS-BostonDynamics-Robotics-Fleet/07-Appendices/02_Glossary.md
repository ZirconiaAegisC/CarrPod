# Glossary of Terms

**Classification:** ACS Internal — Confidential  
**Date:** 15 July 2026  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## A

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Actuator** | A component that converts electrical energy into mechanical motion. In robotics, typically refers to the motor + transmission + sensor assembly at each robot joint. |
| **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** | Total annualized revenue from recurring sources (software subscriptions, maintenance contracts, RaaS fees). Excludes one-time hardware sales. |
| **Atlas** | Boston Dynamics' bipedal humanoid robot platform, designed for industrial manufacturing environments. Production version unveiled at CES 2026: fully electric, 190cm, 91kg, 50kg lift capacity, IP67-rated. |

## B

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Backdrivability** | The ability of a robot joint to be moved by external forces (rather than only by its own actuator). Essential for dynamic locomotion and safe human-robot interaction. |
| **BOM (Bill of Materials)** | The complete list of components, subassemblies, and raw materials required to manufacture a single unit of a product. |

## C

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **CAM+ (Spot)** | Boston Dynamics' panoramic inspection payload for Spot. Features a 360° camera and integrated PTZ (pan-tilt-zoom) camera with 30x optical zoom. |
| **CBRNE** | Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear, and Explosive — the five categories of weapons-of-mass-destruction threats. Spot is deployed for CBRNE reconnaissance by public safety and military agencies. |
| **CFIUS (Committee on Foreign Investment in the United States)** | Interagency US government committee that reviews foreign acquisitions of US businesses for national security implications. Hyundai's 2021 acquisition of BD was subject to CFIUS review. |
| **CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System)** | Software platform for managing industrial maintenance operations (e.g., SAP PM, IBM Maximo). Orbit integrates with CMMS to automate work-order generation from robot-detected anomalies. |
| **CORE (Spot)** | Boston Dynamics' edge computing payload for Spot. Features a Velodyne LiDAR sensor and NVIDIA GPU for high-density point cloud capture and on-robot processing. |

## D

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)** | A valuation method that estimates the present value of an investment based on projected future cash flows, discounted to account for the time value of money and risk. |
| **DOF (Degrees of Freedom)** | The number of independent movements a robot can perform. Spot has 12 DOF (3 per leg); Atlas has an estimated 28 DOF across its full kinematic chain. |
| **Dynamic Locomotion** | Locomotion that depends on momentum and dynamic stability rather than static balance. BD's core technical competency, developed over three decades of legged-robot research. |

## E

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **EOD (Explosive Ordnance Disposal)** | The detection, identification, and neutralization of explosive devices. Spot is deployed by 60+ North American bomb squads for EOD reconnaissance. |
| **ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)** | Integrated software platform for managing business processes (e.g., SAP S/4HANA, Oracle Cloud ERP). Orbit integrates with ERP systems for robot utilization cost allocation and maintenance parts procurement. |
| **EV/Revenue (Enterprise Value to Revenue)** | A valuation multiple comparing a company's enterprise value to its annual revenue. Used in comparable company analysis. |

## F

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **FCF (Free Cash Flow)** | Cash generated by a company's operations after capital expenditures. A key metric for DCF valuation. |
| **FTE (Full-Time Equivalent)** | A unit measuring worker hours. 1 FTE = one full-time employee's annual working hours (~2,000 hours). |

## G

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Gemini Robotics-ER** | Google DeepMind's embodied-reasoning AI model, adapted for robotic manipulation and autonomous task execution. Integrated into BD's Orbit platform for debris detection, gauge reading, and cognitive task planning. |
| **Gross Margin** | Revenue minus cost of goods sold, divided by revenue. Measures the profitability of a company's products before operating expenses. |

## H

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Humanoid Robot** | A bipedal robot with an anthropomorphic form factor (two arms, two legs, torso, head), designed to operate in environments built for human ergonomics. |

## I

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit)** | A sensor that measures acceleration, angular velocity, and orientation. Core component of a robot's proprioceptive sensing system. |
| **IP Rating (Ingress Protection)** | International standard (IEC 60529) classifying the degree of protection against solid particles and liquid ingress. IP67 = dust-tight and immersion-resistant to 1 meter for 30 minutes. IP54 = limited dust ingress and splash-resistant. |
| **IPO (Initial Public Offering)** | The first sale of a company's shares to public investors. |

## L

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging)** | A sensing technology that uses laser pulses to measure distance and generate 3D point clouds of the environment. Used for robot navigation, mapping, and inspection data capture. |

## M

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **MES (Manufacturing Execution System)** | Software for monitoring and controlling manufacturing operations on the factory floor. Atlas task sequencing integrates with MES platforms (Siemens Opcenter, Rockwell FactoryTalk). |
| **MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures)** | Average operating time between equipment failures. A key reliability metric. |
| **MTTR (Mean Time to Repair)** | Average time required to repair a failed component and restore operation. Atlas's target is sub-15-minute actuator replacement. |

## O

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Orbit** | Boston Dynamics' enterprise fleet management and telemetry platform. Orbit 4.1 provides real-time fleet telemetry, acoustic vibration sensing, multi-robot mission orchestration, and enterprise system integration. |

## P

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Proprioceptive Sensing** | Sensing of a robot's own internal state — joint angles, motor currents, actuator temperatures, IMU data. Distinct from exteroceptive sensing (perception of the external environment). |

## R

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **RaaS (Robotics-as-a-Service)** | A commercial model in which customers pay for robotic capability on a recurring subscription or per-use basis rather than purchasing hardware outright. |
| **RMAC (Robotics Manufacturing & Automation Center)** | Hyundai Motor Group's internal robotics manufacturing and deployment division. A 2026 Atlas production allocation recipient. |

## S

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition)** | Industrial control system architecture for monitoring and controlling equipment. Orbit integrates with SCADA platforms (Ignition, Wonderware, WinCC) for robot status display in control rooms. |
| **SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)** | A computational method by which a robot builds a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously tracking its location within that map. |
| **Spot** | Boston Dynamics' quadruped (four-legged) robot platform for inspection, data collection, and remote sensing. 3,000+ units deployed across 40+ countries. |
| **Stretch** | Boston Dynamics' mobile warehouse robot for trailer/container unloading. 500 parcels/hour throughput; deployed at DHL, Maersk, H&M, Gap, and Otto Group. |
| **SWAT (Special Weapons and Tactics)** | Specialized law enforcement units. 60+ North American SWAT teams deploy Spot for barricaded-suspect reconnaissance and hazmat assessment. |

## T

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)** | The comprehensive cost of acquiring, operating, maintaining, and disposing of an asset over its useful life. TCO models in this dossier span 5-year horizons at 7% discount rate. |

## W

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital)** | The blended cost of a company's debt and equity financing, used as the discount rate in DCF analysis. |
| **WMS (Warehouse Management System)** | Software for managing warehouse and distribution center operations. Stretch integrates with major WMS platforms (Manhattan Associates, Blue Yonder, SAP EWM) for task assignment and inventory tracking. |
