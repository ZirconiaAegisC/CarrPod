## 3. IPO Scenario Analysis

### 3.1 Timing

| Roadshow narrative (Atlas + Orbit + AI) | Forming; dependent on above milestones |

### 3.2 Likely Venue

NYSE or NASDAQ. A Korean exchange (KRX) dual-listing is possible given Hyundai's ownership, but the primary listing is expected to be US-based given BD's Massachusetts headquarters, US investor familiarity with robotics platforms, and the deeper US capital markets for technology IPOs.

### 3.3 Governance Considerations

- **Hyundai Control:** At 80% ownership, Hyundai will retain majority voting control post-IPO. Public shareholders will hold a minority interest with limited governance influence. This control premium should theoretically compress the public-market multiple relative to widely held peers.
- **Intercompany Transactions:** Atlas production allocation to Hyundai RMAC and Mobis actuator supply represent related-party transactions that will require IPO prospectus disclosure and ongoing audit committee oversight. The arm's-length nature of these transactions will be scrutinized by public-market investors.
- **Dual-Class Share Structure:** Likely. Hyundai will almost certainly retain super-voting shares to preserve control post-IPO, following the model of other founder/family-controlled technology IPOs.

### 3.4 IPO Size Estimate

Assuming a 15–25% free float (typical for controlled-company technology IPOs) and a $4B–$10B enterprise value at IPO:

| Enterprise Value | Free Float | Primary + Secondary Size |
|-----------------|------------|-------------------------|
| $4B | 15% (primary only) | $600M |
| $6B | 20% (85% primary, 15% secondary) | $1.2B |
| $10B | 25% (70% primary, 30% secondary) | $2.5B |

A $1B–$2B IPO would rank among the largest robotics-sector public offerings in history.

## 4. Post-IPO Trajectory Risks

### 4.1 Public-Market Scrutiny of Unit Economics

As a private entity under Hyundai's patient-capital umbrella, BD has not been required to disclose the economics of its hardware platforms. Post-IPO, quarterly reporting will expose:

- Gross margins per platform (Spot, Stretch, Atlas).
- Customer concentration (likely a material risk given enterprise sales model).
- R&D capitalization and amortization practices.
- Stock-based compensation expense (likely to increase materially with public-company equity incentives).

The transition from private R&D-centric financial management to public-market quarterly earnings discipline represents a material organizational risk.

### 4.2 Hyundai Stake Management

Post-IPO, Hyundai faces a strategic tension: as majority owner, it benefits from BD's commercial success — but as BD's largest customer (via RMAC, Georgia Metaplant, Mobis), it also benefits from below-market transfer pricing on Atlas units and components. Public shareholders will demand arm's-length pricing and transparent intercompany governance. How Hyundai navigates this dual role will significantly impact BD's public-market multiple.

### 4.3 Market Timing

The 2027 IPO window is contingent on macroeconomic conditions (interest rates, technology-sector sentiment, IPO market receptivity). If the 2026–2027 period sees a technology-market correction or a rotation away from speculative-growth equities, BD's IPO could be delayed or repriced downward. The $20B+ speculative valuation reported in Korean media should not be treated as an anchor for offering-price expectations.
