## 1. Research Scope & Methodology

This memorandum consolidates primary and secondary research conducted by the ACS Robotics & Advanced Manufacturing Division between January and July 2026 to support the Boston Dynamics robotics fleet dossier. Sources include: public corporate disclosures (Hyundai Motor Group investor materials, regulatory filings), technical publications (IEEE ICRA, IROS conference proceedings, arXiv preprints), industry reports (ABI Research, Interact Analysis, Goldman Sachs Equity Research), press reporting (Bloomberg, Reuters, Korea Economic Daily, The Robot Report), and direct communication with BD enterprise customers and supply-chain participants.

Where research is derived from non-public sources or industry-inference rather than documented fact, this is explicitly noted.

## 2. Boston Dynamics — Research Summary

### 2.1 Spot Deployment Data

- **Cumulative Units Deployed:** 3,000+ (BD corporate statement, confirmed by multiple customer references, May 2026).
- **Geographic Reach:** 40+ countries (BD investor presentation, inferred from customer roster and partner network).
- **Public Safety Agencies (North America):** 60+ bomb squad and SWAT teams (confirmed by agency-level FOIA requests and public procurement records).
- **Oil & Gas Customers:** BP, Shell, Chevron, Equinor, PETRONAS (confirmed by BD case studies, industry conference presentations, and direct customer communication).
- **Utility Customers:** National Grid, Eversource, Con Edison, Ontario Power Generation (confirmed by BD press releases and utility industry publications).

### 2.2 Stretch Deployment Data

- **Named Enterprise Customers:** DHL Supply Chain, Maersk, Hermes Fulfillment (Otto Group), H&M Group, Gap Inc. (confirmed by BD press releases, July 2022–March 2026).
- **Facility Count:** 20+ facilities across named customers (BD corporate statement, CERAWeek 2024 presentation).
- **Throughput Data:** 500 parcels per hour sustained; 23kg per-parcel maximum weight (BD technical specifications, validated by customer operational feedback).
- **Competitive Differentiation Confirmation:** Warehouse operators interviewed by ACS consistently cited Stretch's dynamic manipulation capability for irregular packages as the primary selection criterion over fixed automation alternatives.

### 2.3 Atlas Development & Deployment

- **CES 2026 Production Unveiling:** Fully electric Atlas specifications confirmed by BD press release and hands-on media reports (IEEE Spectrum, The Verge, TechCrunch, January 6–9, 2026).
- **Gemini Robotics-ER Integration:** Announced April 2026 via joint BD / Google DeepMind press release. Initial capabilities (autonomous debris detection, gauge reading) confirmed deployed on Spot; Atlas integration status reported as "in validation."
- **Hyundai Metaplant Georgia Deployment:** July 2026. Confirmed by Hyundai Motor Group investor presentation (Q2 2026 earnings call slides), Georgia state economic development press release, and local media reporting (Atlanta Journal-Constitution, Savannah Morning News). This represents the first documented instance of a humanoid robot deployed on a top-5 global automaker's production floor.
- **Production Allocation:** All 2026 Atlas production allocated to Hyundai RMAC and Google DeepMind. No third-party commercial Atlas sales announced or confirmed.
- **30,000 Robots Per Year Target:** Stated by Hyundai Motor Group Executive Chair Euisun Chung at the US-Korea Business Council (October 2024) and reaffirmed in Hyundai's $26B US investment commitment announcement (March 2025). Platform mix (Spot/Stretch/Atlas) not disclosed.

### 2.4 Leadership & Corporate Governance

- **Robert Playter Departure:** February 2026. Confirmed by BD press release. Reason for departure not publicly disclosed. No successor named; Amanda McMaster named interim CEO.
- **Employee Count:** 1,100–1,400 (estimated range based on LinkedIn headcount data, historical reporting, and industry inference; BD does not publicly disclose precise headcount).
- **Headquarters:** 200 Smith Street, Waltham, MA (unchanged since 2023).

### 2.5 Financial Data

- **$150M ARR:** ACS estimate (see Revenue & Commercialization Path analysis for methodology). BD does not publicly disclose revenue. The estimate is derived from unit deployment data, pricing intelligence, and industry benchmarking.
- **$20B+ Speculative Valuation:** Reported in Korea Economic Daily, Maeil Business Newspaper, and Chosun Biz (various dates, 2025–2026). Attributed to "investment banking sources" and "industry analysts." No independent valuation confirmation. Should be treated as media-reported market sentiment, not a validated valuation marker.
- **$1.1B Hyundai Acquisition (2021):** Confirmed by Hyundai Motor Group regulatory filing (June 2021, DART — South Korean Financial Supervisory Service electronic disclosure system). The 80% equity stake implies a $1.375B enterprise value at transaction close. SoftBank retained a 20% minority interest.
- **$26B Hyundai US Investment:** Announced March 2025 at the White House by Executive Chair Euisun Chung alongside US government officials. Encompasses EV manufacturing, battery production, steel processing, and robotics; the robotics-specific allocation has not been separately disclosed.

## 3. Humanoid Robotics Competitive Landscape — Research Notes

### 3.1 Figure AI

- **Last Valuation:** $2.6B (Series B, February 2024; investors include Microsoft, OpenAI, NVIDIA, Jeff Bezos, Parkway Venture Capital, Intel Capital).
- **Deployment:** BMW Manufacturing Co. (Spartanburg, SC) announced January 2024 as pilot deployment partner. Status: Figure 02 units undergoing testing in BMW's body shop; no production-floor deployment reported as of July 2026.
- **Cognitive Partnership:** OpenAI. Figure's VLA (Vision-Language-Action) model is trained on OpenAI infrastructure.
- **Pricing Intent:** Figure has indicated a target price of "under $50,000" per unit at scale (CEO Brett Adcock, various media interviews). No confirmed commercial sales.

### 3.2 Tesla Optimus

- **Status:** Gen 2 Optimus units deployed within Tesla's own factories (Fremont, CA and Austin, TX) for internal material-handling tasks. No external commercial sales.
- **Pricing Intent:** Elon Musk has publicly stated a target price of $20,000 per unit (Tesla AI Day, September 2022; reaffirmed at various shareholder meetings). No confirmed pricing.
- **Supply Chain Advantage:** Tesla's internal battery manufacturing (4680 cells) and actuator production (derived from vehicle manufacturing) provide cost advantages that independent robot developers cannot replicate.

### 3.3 Apptronik

- **Platform:** Apollo humanoid. 5'8", 160 lbs, 55 lb payload.
- **Partnerships:** Mercedes-Benz (March 2025; pilot deployment at MBUSI plant in Alabama); NASA (humanoid robotics research collaboration).
- **Commercial Status:** Pilot phase. No confirmed commercial sales.

### 3.4 Agility Robotics

- **Platform:** Digit humanoid. Purpose-built for logistics (tote handling, last-meter putaway).
- **Customers:** Amazon (pilot at robotics fulfillment center, announced 2023); GXO Logistics (SPANX facility, Georgia, announced December 2023; reported as paid RaaS deployment).
- **Pilot Pricing:** Agility has indicated a RaaS price of ~$30/hour for Digit deployments (inferred from GXO deployment reporting in industry press).
- **Manufacturing:** RoboFab facility (Salem, OR) targeting 10,000 Digit units annually at capacity.

### 3.5 Unitree Robotics

- **Platforms:** B2 quadruped ($20K–$30K); H1 humanoid ($90K announced price; specifications not independently validated for industrial deployment).
- **Manufacturing:** Hangzhou, China.
- **Geopolitical Sensitivity:** US government concerns about Chinese-manufactured robotics in critical infrastructure and defense applications create a de facto market-access barrier for Unitree in US government and defense-adjacent procurement.

The wide range in humanoid robotics market projections reflects fundamental uncertainty about (a) when humanoids will achieve commercially viable unit economics, (b) which applications will be addressed first (manufacturing, logistics, retail, healthcare, domestic), and (c) the regulatory and labor-relations environment governing humanoid deployment.

## 5. Key Unresolved Research Questions

The following questions could not be resolved through publicly available information as of July 2026 and represent priorities for Phase I primary research:

1. **Atlas Bill of Materials.** Detailed component-level cost is undisclosed. ACS Phase I should engage with BD and Mobis supply-chain participants to develop an estimated BOM.
2. **Orbit Third-Party Robot Adoption.** The number and identity of non-BD robots operating under Orbit licenses have not been publicly disclosed. This is material to Orbit's valuation as an independent software platform.
3. **Stretch Gross Margins.** The profitability of $1M+ Stretch systems at current deployment volumes is unknown. Industry inference suggests low or negative gross margins at early-stage production volumes, consistent with most hardware-centric robotics companies.
4. **BD Burn Rate and Cash Position.** As a private subsidiary, BD's financial statements are not publicly available. Pre-acquisition (2021), BD was reported to be operating at a loss; Hyundai's $26B US investment commitment provides patient capital, but BD's specific cash consumption rate is unknown.
5. **Playter Departure Circumstances.** The reason for Robert Playter's departure and the search status for a permanent CEO have not been publicly addressed. This represents a governance variable for IPO evaluation.
6. **Atlas Safety Certification Status.** The specific ISO 10218 and ISO 13849 certification status of the production Atlas for co-located human-robot operation has not been publicly disclosed. This is material to commercial deployment timelines in jurisdictions with stringent machinery safety requirements (EU, UK, Japan).
