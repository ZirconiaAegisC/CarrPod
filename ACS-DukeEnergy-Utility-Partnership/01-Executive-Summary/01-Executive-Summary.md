
# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
## ACS-DUKEENERGY-UTILITY-PARTNERSHIP
### Pilot Substation Dielectric Hardening Program

**CLASSIFICATION: COMMERCIAL-IN-CONFIDENCE**

---

## 1. STRATEGIC PROBLEM

Duke Energy operates one of the largest transmission networks in the United States — 50,000+ miles of transmission lines across six states, served by 3,300+ substations. Every single one is vulnerable to GIC. During a Carrington-class event, the quasi-DC currents induced in these long transmission corridors would flow through substation transformer neutrals, causing half-cycle saturation that produces harmonics, consumes reactive power, and — in the worst case — permanently damages transformer windings.

**Geological Amplification:** Duke's service territory includes the Appalachian Mountain corridor (NC, SC, KY) where igneous and metamorphic bedrock creates high soil resistivity (1,000-3,000 ohm-m). This amplifies GIC electric fields by 5-10x compared to sedimentary basins. The Blue Ridge province through western North Carolina is among the highest GIC-risk zones in the continental U.S.

**Transformers Are the Chokepoint:** Duke Energy maintains approximately 3,300 substation transformers ranging from 10 MVA to 500 MVA. Large power transformers (LPTs) have a manufacturing lead time of 12-24 months under normal conditions, and only 5-10 spare LPTs are typically available nationally at any given time through the EEI Spare Transformer Equipment Program (STEP). A GMD event damaging 20-50 transformers simultaneously would exceed the entire national spare LPT inventory, potentially extending outages to 6-18 months.

## 2. THE ACS SOLUTION

**Neutral-Blocking Capacitor (NBC) Installation:** CSM-NBC-2400 devices installed at priority substations block quasi-DC GIC at the transformer neutral connection. Cost per substation: $1.2-3.4M depending on transformer MVA rating and number of transformers.

**Pilot Program Design:** 12 substations selected by multi-factor risk scoring:
1. Geomagnetic latitude (higher latitude = higher risk)
2. Soil resistivity at substation site
3. Transformer criticality (customer count served, replacement difficulty)
4. Transmission line orientation and length
5. Regional grid stability contribution (voltage support role)

**SCADA Integration:** Real-time GIC monitoring integrated into Duke Energy's grid control center, providing operators with:
- Live transformer neutral current display
- GIC detection alarms with severity classification
- Automated protection status for each NBC-equipped transformer
- Integration with NOAA SWPC early warning feed via CSM-EWG-2000

**Regulatory Rate-Base Recovery:** ACS provides complete PUC filing support:
- Expert testimony on GIC risk and mitigation
- Actuarial analysis from CSM's 20+ insurance dossiers
- Cost-benefit analysis demonstrating ratepayer benefit
- Precedent documentation from other critical infrastructure investments

## 3. THE "FIRST UTILITY TO HARDEN" ADVANTAGE

**Regulatory Positioning:** The first utility to deploy comprehensive GIC protection establishes the benchmark that regulators will use to evaluate every other utility's grid resilience plans. Duke gains the opportunity to define "adequate GMD protection" in its own terms.

**Insurance Market Access:** Based on CSM's insurance dossiers, a utility with documented GIC hardening can negotiate 5-12% premium reductions on transmission infrastructure coverage and gain access to Lloyd's specialty lines that are currently unavailable for unhardened grids.

**Investor Relations:** ESG and resilience metrics are increasingly driving utility valuation. Duke's GIC hardening program becomes a centerpiece of its sustainability and reliability narrative to investors.

**Mutual Aid Credit:** In regional emergency scenarios, Duke's hardened substations become anchor points for the surrounding grid. This provides negotiating leverage in regional reliability councils and mutual aid agreements.
