## I. EXECUTIVE TRANSMITTAL

Ms. Good:

The ACS Infrastructure Protection Division of Carrington Storm Motors proposes a landmark utility partnership: Duke Energy as the first major U.S. investor-owned utility to systematically harden its transmission substation network against geomagnetically induced current (GIC) events. Duke Energy operates 50,000+ miles of transmission lines, 3,300+ substations, and serves 8.2 million customers across six states (North Carolina, South Carolina, Florida, Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky). Not one of Duke Energy's thousands of transformers incorporates GIC protection.

**The "First Utility to Harden" Advantage:** The first utility to deploy comprehensive GIC protection gains a permanent competitive advantage in regulatory proceedings, insurance markets, and rate-base justification. It positions Duke Energy as the industry leader in grid resilience — a narrative that commands premium valuation from both regulators and investors. This is not merely a compliance play for NERC TPL-007-4 (which requires GMD vulnerability assessment). This is an opportunity to define the standard that every other utility will eventually be required to follow.

**The Regulatory Rate-Base Opportunity:** FERC Order 830 and NERC TPL-007-4 establish the regulatory framework for GMD mitigation. State public utility commissions (PUCs) in Duke's service territory — particularly North Carolina, which has shown progressive regulatory appetite for grid modernization — provide a pathway for rate-base recovery of hardening investments. ACS has prepared regulatory support documentation for PUC rate case filings, demonstrating that GIC hardening costs $0.08-0.15 per customer per month when amortized over the 25-year transformer life cycle.

## II. PROPOSED PARTNERSHIP

1. **Pilot Program:** NBC installation at 12 high-risk substations (selected by geomagnetic latitude, soil resistivity, transformer criticality, and customer density). Target: complete within 24 months. Cost: $18.4M (ACS/CSM). Duke Energy provides site access, outage scheduling, and operational integration.

2. **SCADA Integration:** CSM Early Warning Gateway integration with Duke Energy's grid control center (Charlotte, NC). Real-time GIC monitoring and automated transformer protection status display for system operators.

3. **Mutual Aid Framework:** During GMD events, Duke's hardened substations serve as "anchor points" for the regional grid — providing stable power reference to neighboring utilities even if their own substations experience GIC-induced voltage collapse. This creates a mutual aid credit that Duke can leverage in regional reliability negotiations.

4. **Regulatory Support Package:** ACS provides complete regulatory filing support including expert testimony, actuarial analysis from CSM's insurance industry dossiers, and cost-benefit analysis suitable for PUC rate case proceedings.

5. **"First Mover" Branding:** ACS will jointly issue a white paper with Duke Energy documenting the hardening program, establishing Duke as the industry benchmark for GMD resilience.
