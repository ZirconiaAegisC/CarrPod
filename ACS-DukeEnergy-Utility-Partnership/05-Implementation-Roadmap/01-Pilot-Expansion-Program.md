
# IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP
## ACS-DUKEENERGY-UTILITY-PARTNERSHIP
### 24-Month Pilot + 60-Month Expansion

**CLASSIFICATION: COMMERCIAL-IN-CONFIDENCE**

| Phase | Duration | Milestones |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Phase 0 — 12-site assessment | Months 1-3 | Complete GIC vulnerability scoring. Regulatory filing package prepared. |
| Phase 1 — 12 pilot NBC installations | Months 4-24 | 2 substations per quarter. SCADA integration at Month 12. |
| Phase 2 — Expansion to 200+ substations | Months 24-60 | 8-10 substations per quarter. Regional hardening centers established. |
| Phase 3 — O&M and monitoring | Months 18+ ongoing | Phantom Robot fleet deployed for continuous monitoring. |

**Month 1-3:** Kickoff in Charlotte. Soil resistivity mapping at 12 sites using Wenner 4-pin method. Transmission line GIC modeling using PSS/E and PSCAD. Regulatory filing preparation with NC Utilities Commission.

**Month 4-24:** NBC fabrication, factory testing, site installation. Transformer outage coordination with Duke Operations (planned maintenance windows). Post-installation GIC injection testing. SCADA integration at Duke Energy Control Center (Charlotte).

**Months 24-60:** Fleet expansion. Regional hardening center in Charlotte (serving Carolinas), second center in Indianapolis (Midwest territory). Automated prioritization dashboard for substation selection.

**Resource Requirements:** 12 ACS/CSM personnel (4 substation engineers, 2 SCADA engineers, 2 regulatory specialists, 2 construction managers, 2 PMO). AEGIS 18-agent team for strategic oversight.

**Regulatory Milestones:** NCUC rate case filing (Month 3). FERC 205 filing for transmission formula rate recovery (Month 4). NERC TPL-007 compliance documentation update (Month 18).
