THE CONVERSATION

A product testing laboratory. The Phantom MK-1 stands in a TEM cell. Mork is at the test console. Chester observes a CarrPod vibration test. Zirconia stands at the protocol index.

ZIRCONIA: Welcome to CSMTest. I am Zirconia, director of test and validation, and these are Mork and Chester. The tradition of standardized testing — from MIL-STD to IEEE to ASTM — provides the framework. CSMTest applies it to electromagnetic resilience. Mork, the Phantom test.

MORK: Five phases. Electromagnetic exposure in a TEM cell, swept from 0.001 Hz to 1 kHz at Carrington-calibrated field strengths. Vibration at magnetostrictive frequencies. Thermal cycling from minus 40 to plus 85 Celsius. Combined environment — all three simultaneously, finding the interactions. Degraded-mode operation — sensor disabled, joint partially seized, mesh interrupted. The Phantom that passes all five phases is certified.

CHESTER: The combined-environment test is where the truth emerges. An actuator that works under EM alone and vibration alone but overheats when both are applied. A sensor that drifts when the EM noise floor superimposes on the thermal drift. The test finds those interactions before the field does. Right on.
