# GENERAL DYNAMICS — UNDERSEA PROPULSION PARTNERSHIP
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 06

---

### PARTNERSHIP SCOPE

Beta Technologies and General Dynamics (Mission Systems division) are collaborating on the adaptation of Beta's electric propulsion technology for undersea military applications. The partnership repurposes Beta's high-power-density electric motor and power electronics expertise for submarine and unmanned undersea vehicle (UUV) propulsion systems, marking a significant diversification into the defense maritime sector beyond Beta's core electric aviation mission.

### TECHNICAL TRANSLATION

The undersea propulsion application leverages the same core technologies developed for the H500A electric engine — permanent magnet motor topology, silicon carbide (SiC) power electronics, liquid-cooled thermal management, and high-voltage DC distribution — adapted for the unique requirements of submerged operation:

| Requirement | Adaptation |
|---|---|
| Pressure tolerance | Motor housing rated for deep-submergence pressures |
| Acoustic signature | Low-noise electromagnetic design; active noise cancellation |
| Magnetic signature | Degaussing integration for naval stealth requirements |
| Corrosion resistance | Marine-grade materials and coatings; galvanic isolation |
| Shock hardening | MIL-S-901D shock qualification for naval combatants |
| Cooling | Seawater heat exchanger replacing air-cooled architecture |
| Control interface | Naval combat system integration via MIL-STD-1553 |

### APPLICATION DOMAINS

**Manned Submarine Propulsion**
- Main propulsion motor replacements for legacy DC motor systems
- Auxiliary maneuvering thruster motors
- Pump-jet propulsor electric drives

**Unmanned Undersea Vehicles (UUVs)**
- Large-displacement UUV (LDUUV) propulsion
- Extra-large UUV (XLUUV) main drive
- Seabed warfare and surveillance platform propulsion

**Special Mission Applications**
- Swimmer delivery vehicle (SDV) propulsion
- Underwater sensor platform station-keeping
- Mine countermeasure (MCM) vehicle propulsion

### STRATEGIC SIGNIFICANCE

The General Dynamics partnership provides Beta Technologies with three strategic benefits beyond its aviation core business:

1. **Revenue diversification** — Defense maritime contracts provide non-cyclical revenue streams independent of commercial aviation adoption rates and certification timelines.

2. **Technology maturation** — Military qualification requirements (MIL-S-901D shock, MIL-STD-461 EMI, MIL-STD-810 environmental) drive propulsion system robustness that feeds back into aviation reliability.

3. **Scale economies** — Increased motor production volume across aviation and maritime applications improves manufacturing utilization at the Burlington facility and strengthens the supply chain for SiC power electronics, magnets, and precision machining.

### DEFENSE CONTRACTING CONTEXT

General Dynamics Mission Systems is the prime contractor, with Beta Technologies serving as a critical propulsion subsystem supplier. Contract vehicles are expected to fall under the U.S. Navy's Program Executive Office (PEO) Submarines and PEO Unmanned and Small Combatants (USC), with initial development funded through Other Transaction Authority (OTA) agreements or Broad Agency Announcement (BAA) contracts.
