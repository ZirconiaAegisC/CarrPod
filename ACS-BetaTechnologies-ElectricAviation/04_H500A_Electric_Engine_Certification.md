# H500A ELECTRIC ENGINE — CERTIFICATION & TESTING
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 04

---

### PROPULSION STRATEGY

The H500A electric engine is Beta Technologies' proprietary propulsion system, developed entirely in-house and representing the single most critical enabling technology for the entire ALIA aircraft portfolio. Recognizing that propulsion system performance, reliability, and certification would be the pacing item for aircraft type certification, Beta initiated the H500A program as a standalone Type Certificate (TC) effort under 14 CFR Part 33, targeting TC issuance in H1 2026.

This standalone engine certification strategy — decoupling propulsion from airframe certification — enables Beta to generate propulsion system certification artifacts independently, accelerating the CX300 Type Certificate process by eliminating engine-related issue papers from the critical path for aircraft certification.

### TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Motor topology | Permanent magnet synchronous motor (PMSM) |
| Peak power | ~500+ horsepower |
| Continuous rating | Engineered for continuous climb and cruise power |
| Cooling architecture | Liquid-cooled stator housing with integrated heat exchanger |
| Winding configuration | Hairpin stator windings for high slot-fill factor |
| Power electronics | Silicon carbide (SiC) inverter, integrated motor drive |
| Redundancy | Dual-wound stator sections (single-point failure tolerance) |
| Control system | Full Authority Digital Engine Control (FADEC), DO-178C Level B |
| Weight | Optimized for installed thrust-to-weight ratio |

### TESTING PROGRAM

The H500A has accumulated over 85,000 hours of combined flight and ground testing as of H1 2026, spanning the following test campaigns:

| Test Campaign | Scope |
|---|---|
| Dynamometer endurance | Full-power continuous operation, thermal soak cycles |
| Altitude chamber | Performance characterization to 15,000 ft MSL |
| Vibration and shock | DO-160G environmental qualification |
| Electromagnetic compatibility | DO-160G Section 21, radiated and conducted emissions |
| Lightning protection | DO-160G Section 23, direct and indirect effects |
| Icing ingestion | Fan and blade ice shedding tolerance |
| Flight test | Iron bird ground rig + ALIA prototype flight envelope |
| Battery integration | Full-stack propulsion and energy storage validation |

### CERTIFICATION BASIS

The H500A is certificated under 14 CFR Part 33 (Airworthiness Standards: Aircraft Engines), marking one of the first electric propulsion systems to complete a standalone engine TC process at the FAA. Key certification elements include:

- **Part 33 Subpart B** — Block tests, endurance tests, teardown inspection
- **Part 33 Subpart C** — Rotor integrity, overspeed, containment
- **Part 33 Subpart E** — Control system software (DO-178C)
- **Part 33 Subpart F** — Electrical and electronic engine control systems
- **Special conditions** — Developed for high-voltage electrical propulsion (600V+ bus), battery-to-motor power distribution, and SiC power electronics qualification

### COMMERCIAL APPLICATION BEYOND BETA

The H500A is not solely an internal program. Under a 10-year, $1 billion motor supply agreement with Eve Air Mobility (a subsidiary of Embraer), Beta will supply H500A-derived electric propulsion units for Eve's eVTOL aircraft program. This agreement validates the H500A architecture as an industry-standard propulsion solution and creates a secondary revenue stream independent of ALIA aircraft sales.

| Agreement Element | Detail |

| Term | 10 years |
| Contract value | ~$1 billion |
| Scope | Supply of electric motors and associated controllers |
| Deliveries | Phased, aligned with Eve's eVTOL certification timeline |

### STRATEGIC SIGNIFICANCE

The H500A program positions Beta Technologies as both an aircraft OEM and a Tier 1 propulsion supplier, diversifying revenue across OEM and supplier business models. The standalone TC strategy also creates a barrier to entry for competitors requiring propulsion certification, while the SiC inverter expertise developed in-house represents defensible intellectual property.
