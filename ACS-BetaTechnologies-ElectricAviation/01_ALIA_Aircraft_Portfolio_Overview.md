# ALIA AIRCRAFT PORTFOLIO OVERVIEW
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 01

---

### STRATEGIC ARCHITECTURE

Beta Technologies has developed a dual-platform electric aircraft portfolio anchored by the ALIA airframe, a single-pilot, all-electric composite design with 50-foot wingspan optimized for aerodynamic efficiency and minimal energy consumption. The common-core architecture enables two mission-specific variants sharing approximately 85% airframe commonality, reducing development cost, accelerating certification timelines, and simplifying manufacturing scale-up.

The portfolio addresses three distinct addressable markets: (1) regional cargo and passenger transport via conventional takeoff and landing (CTOL), (2) urban and peri-urban air mobility via vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL), and (3) defense logistics via an autonomous heavy-lift cargo drone derivative.

### PLATFORM VARIANTS

**ALIA CX300 (CTOL)** — The lead certification variant. Configured for conventional runway operations, capable of carrying 6 passengers (plus pilot) or up to 200 cubic feet of cargo with 1,250 lb payload capacity. Nominal range of 215 nautical miles. Designed for regional air mobility corridors, organ transport logistics, and feeder cargo networks. Target Part 23 certification in H2 2027.

**ALIA A250 (VTOL)** — The vertical-lift variant, incorporating four forward and four aft lift rotors plus a single aft pusher propeller. Shares the CX300 fuselage, wing planform, energy storage, and avionics suite. Configured for operations from vertiports, hospital helipads, and constrained urban sites. Designed for intra-city passenger transport and emergency medical services. Certification targeted to follow CTOL approval, estimated in 2028.

**MV250 (Military Cargo Drone)** — The autonomous derivative engineered for defense logistics. Accelerated 6 months ahead of original timeline with first flight in 2026. Removes crew systems for increased payload fraction. Configured for contested logistics resupply, ship-to-shore cargo, and austere field operations.

### OPERATIONAL HERITAGE

The ALIA platform has accumulated over 135,000 nautical miles of flight test data across the three variants as of mid-2026. This includes cross-country deployments, cold-weather testing in Plattsburgh, New York, high-altitude envelope expansion, and the first-ever eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP) organ transport flights completed in July 2026 covering a demonstrated range of 253 nautical miles in a single mission profile.
