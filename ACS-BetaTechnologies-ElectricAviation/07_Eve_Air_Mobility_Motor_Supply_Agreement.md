# EVE AIR MOBILITY — $1B MOTOR SUPPLY AGREEMENT
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 07

---

### AGREEMENT STRUCTURE

Beta Technologies and Eve Air Mobility (NYSE: EVEX), a subsidiary of Embraer S.A., executed a 10-year electric motor supply agreement valued at approximately $1 billion. Under the agreement, Beta will supply H500A-derived electric propulsion units and associated motor controllers to Eve for integration into Eve's eVTOL aircraft, which targets entry into service in the 2027–2028 timeframe.

### STRATEGIC RATIONALE

**For Beta Technologies:**
- Validates the H500A as an industry-standard propulsion architecture, not merely a captive internal component
- Creates a second, independent revenue stream decoupled from ALIA aircraft delivery schedules
- Provides incremental production volume to amortize manufacturing fixed costs at Burlington
- Establishes Beta as a Tier 1 aerospace propulsion supplier with a marquee OEM customer
- Generates revenue during the pre-certification period when ALIA aircraft sales are limited

**For Eve Air Mobility:**
- Accesses flight-proven propulsion technology (H500A has 85K+ flight/ground test hours)
- Avoids the cost and schedule risk of internal motor development and Part 33 certification
- Leverages Beta's FAA relationship and propulsion certification experience
- Benefits from volume-driven unit cost reductions as Beta scales production

### SUPPLY CHAIN ARCHITECTURE

Beta's motor supply to Eve creates a multi-tier supply chain configuration:

```
Beta Technologies (Tier 1 Supplier)
  ├─ Supplies motors + controllers to Eve (eVTOL OEM)
  ├─ Supplies motors + controllers to Beta ALIA program (internal)
  └─ Burlington, VT manufacturing (shared lines)
            │
Eve Air Mobility (eVTOL OEM)
  ├─ Integrates Beta motors into Eve eVTOL airframe
  ├─ Manages final assembly at Taubaté, Brazil facility
  └─ Aircraft certification under ANAC/FAA bilateral agreement
```

### COMPETITIVE IMPLICATIONS

The Beta-Eve agreement establishes an industry precedent for electric propulsion supply relationships in the advanced air mobility (AAM) sector. It signals that propulsion specialization — where dedicated propulsion companies supply multiple airframe OEMs — may become the dominant industry structure, analogous to the relationship between engine manufacturers (GE, Pratt & Whitney, Rolls-Royce) and airframe OEMs (Boeing, Airbus, Embraer) in conventional aviation.

For Beta competitors developing both airframes and propulsion internally, the dual business model (OEM + supplier) may prove challenging to replicate, as it requires both airframe certification expertise and Tier 1 propulsion supplier quality management systems.

### RISK FACTORS

- Eve's eVTOL program schedule risk flows to Beta's motor delivery schedule and revenue recognition
- Technology export controls may constrain supply to Brazil under ITAR/EAR
- Beta's own ALIA production ramp may compete with Eve deliveries for manufacturing capacity
- Any H500A certification delays impact both Beta and Eve certification timelines
