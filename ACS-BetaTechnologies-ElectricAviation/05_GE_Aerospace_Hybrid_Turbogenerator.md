# GE AEROSPACE — HYBRID ELECTRIC TURBOGENERATOR PARTNERSHIP
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 05

---

### PARTNERSHIP OVERVIEW

Beta Technologies and GE Aerospace have entered into a strategic collaboration to develop a hybrid-electric turbogenerator system, integrating GE's gas turbine expertise with Beta's electric propulsion and high-voltage power management architecture. The partnership aims to produce a turbine-based range extender that addresses the fundamental limitation of battery-electric aircraft: constrained range dictated by energy density of lithium-ion chemistry.

### TECHNICAL CONCEPT

The hybrid turbogenerator system consists of a compact gas turbine engine (derived from GE's CT7 or T700 lineage) mechanically coupled to a high-power-density electric generator, feeding DC power into the ALIA's high-voltage DC bus architecture. The system operates in series-hybrid configuration: the turbine drives only the generator and provides no direct mechanical thrust to the aircraft. The generated electrical power supplements battery energy, extending range without requiring the turbine to operate from takeoff.

| Parameter | Target |
|---|---|
| Turbine architecture | Derived from GE CT7/T700 family |
| Generator type | High-speed permanent magnet alternator |
| Electrical output | 300-500 kW (target range) |
| Fuel type | Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) compatible |
| Operating mode | Series hybrid (generator only) |
| Integration | ALIA aft-fuselage bay (pusher configuration) |

### OPERATIONAL CONCEPT

1. **Takeoff and climb** — Battery power only (zero-emission, low-noise phases)
2. **Cruise** — Turbogenerator activates, provides sustained DC bus power
3. **Descent and landing** — Battery power (turbine secured)
4. **Range extension** — Turbogenerator enables 400-600+ nm range (vs. 215 nm battery-only)

This operational profile preserves the environmental and noise benefits of electric propulsion during terminal-area operations while dramatically expanding the mission envelope for inter-city and regional routes that exceed battery-only range.

### STRATEGIC RATIONALE

| Driver | Implication |
|---|---|
| Range limitation | Battery energy density (~260 Wh/kg current state) caps range at ~215 nm; turbogenerator extends to 400-600+ nm |
| Certification pathway | GE brings decades of FAA turbine certification data; hybrid certification via Part 33 + special conditions |
| Customer demand | Regional operators require 400+ nm routes for operational viability |
| Defense applications | Military logistics missions require range and fuel flexibility |
| SAF compatibility | Aligns with industry decarbonization targets and regulatory incentives |

### DEVELOPMENT STATUS

The partnership is in the advanced design and prototyping phase. Key milestones include:

- Conceptual design review (completed)
- Preliminary design review
- Ground test article fabrication
- Dynamometer testing at GE's Lynn, Massachusetts facility
- Flight test integration on ALIA testbed aircraft

### COMPETITIVE CONTEXT

Several eVTOL developers are pursuing hybrid-electric architectures to address range limitations. Beta's partnership with GE Aerospace — the world's largest aircraft engine manufacturer — provides a level of turbine expertise, certification data, and supply chain maturity unavailable to competitors relying on startup or in-house turbine development. The GE relationship is a significant competitive moat in the electric aviation sector.
