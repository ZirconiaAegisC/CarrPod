# eVTOL INTEGRATION PILOT PROGRAM (eIPP)
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 09

---

### PROGRAM BACKGROUND

The FAA's eVTOL Integration Pilot Program (eIPP) is a federal initiative designed to accelerate the operational integration of electric vertical takeoff and landing aircraft into the National Airspace System (NAS). Modeled on the drone Integration Pilot Program (IPP) that preceded it, the eIPP provides selected participants with regulatory flexibility, FAA operational expertise, and public visibility to conduct real-world eVTOL operations in advance of full aircraft type certification under Part 23.

Beta Technologies was selected for 7 of the 8 eIPP programs — more than any other developer — covering operations across 10+ states. This dominant selection rate reflects Beta's advanced operational maturity relative to competitors and the FAA's confidence in Beta's safety management system and operational procedures.

The 253 nm demonstrated range exceeded the nominal 215 nm specification, validating the performance margin available under real-world operational conditions and suggesting that the published specification incorporates conservative reserves.

### REGULATORY SIGNIFICANCE

The eIPP provides Beta with three critical regulatory advantages:

1. **Operational data for certification** — Real-world mission data on energy consumption, systems reliability, charging turnaround, and pilot workload informs the certification basis and continued airworthiness instructions.

2. **FAA relationship building** — Continuous operational interaction with FAA personnel builds institutional familiarity with Beta's aircraft, procedures, and safety culture, which accelerates issue paper resolution during type certification.

3. **Precedent for commercial operations** — FAA approval of eIPP missions establishes a regulatory template for Part 135 commercial operations post-certification, reducing the timeline from type certification to revenue service.

### MARKET VALIDATION

The eIPP organ transport flights serve as a public demonstration of electric aviation's most compelling near-term use case: time-critical logistics where speed and reliability are paramount and the cost tolerance is high. Organ transport represents a $5B+ annual logistics market in the United States, with current delivery methods (courier flights, charter aircraft) suffering from high cost and limited availability. Beta's demonstrated capability positions the ALIA platform as a direct substitute for these legacy transport modes.
