# MV250 MILITARY CARGO DRONE — PROGRAM ASSESSMENT
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 10

---

### PROGRAM ORIGINS

The MV250 is the military derivative of the ALIA platform, developed in response to U.S. Department of Defense requirements for autonomous, electric, heavy-lift cargo resupply in contested logistics environments. The program was accelerated six months ahead of its original development timeline in response to urgent operational needs identified by the U.S. Air Force's Agility Prime program and U.S. Army Future Vertical Lift (FVL) logistics modernization initiatives.

### CONFIGURATION DIFFERENTIATORS

The MV250 removes all crew systems (cockpit, life support, crew displays, windshield, pressurization) from the baseline ALIA CX300 airframe, reallocating weight and volume to payload capacity. The aircraft operates as a fully autonomous platform with remote supervisory control.

| Parameter | MV250 (Military) | CX300 (Civilian CTOL) |
|---|---|---|
| Crew | Unmanned (optionally piloted) | 1 pilot |
| Payload capacity | Increased (crew mass reallocated) | 1,250 lb |
| Cargo volume | Expanded (cockpit volume repurposed) | 200 cu ft |
| Autonomy | Full autonomous mission execution | Pilot-in-command |
| Communications | Military LOS/BLOS datalink (Link 16 compatible) | Civil VHF/ADS-B |
| Navigation | GPS + inertial + terrain-referenced (GPS-denied capable) | Dual GPS/SBAS |
| Survivability | Reduced acoustic and thermal signature | Civil noise standards |
| Certification | Military airworthiness release (non-FAA) | Part 23 Type Certificate |

### DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE ENGAGEMENT

Beta has participated in the U.S. Air Force's Agility Prime program, which provides non-dilutive funding, access to military test ranges, and airworthiness evaluation for electric VTOL platforms. The acceleration of the MV250 program by six months — targeting first flight in 2026 — indicates strong DoD support and the potential for a program of record following successful flight demonstration.

The MV250 aligns with the DoD's Operational Energy Strategy, which prioritizes reducing the logistics footprint of fuel supply chains (historically, fuel represents 70% of tonnage shipped to forward operating bases). An electric logistics aircraft that can be recharged from forward-deployed generators or microgrids offers a step-change reduction in the logistics burden of logistics itself.

### GENERAL DYNAMICS SYNERGY

The MV250 program benefits from Beta's broader defense relationships, particularly the General Dynamics undersea propulsion partnership. While distinct in application, these defense programs share common technology threads (SiC power electronics, permanent magnet motors, high-voltage DC architecture) and create a defense business unit scale that supports dedicated facilities, security clearances, and contracting infrastructure.

### PRODUCTION AND FIELDING

If the MV250 transitions to a Program of Record (POR), production would occur at Beta's Burlington facility on a dedicated defense production line, segregated from commercial ALIA production for ITAR compliance. Fielding would follow the standard defense acquisition pathway:

1. **Rapid prototyping** — OTA-funded demonstration (current phase)
2. **Middle-tier acquisition** — Rapid fielding pathway for urgent operational needs
3. **Program of Record** — Full-rate production decision (target ~2028-2029)
