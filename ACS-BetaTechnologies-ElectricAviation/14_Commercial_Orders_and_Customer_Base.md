Beta Technologies has assembled a $3.9 billion commercial aircraft backlog comprising 991 aircraft across 289 firm orders and 602+ options, making it one of the largest order books in the advanced air mobility sector. The backlog spans cargo, passenger, medical logistics, and defense customers across North America, Asia-Pacific, and Europe.

### CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION RISK

While the $3.9B backlog is diversified across six anchor customers and multiple mission segments, revenue concentration risk exists given that UPS and United Therapeutics together may represent 40-50% of the near-term delivery schedule. Both customers, however, are investment-grade counterparties with strategic alignment to electric aviation:

- **UPS** — Publicly stated carbon-neutral by 2050 goal; electric aviation is a direct pathway to decarbonizing its aviation segment, which represents ~20% of total UPS carbon emissions
- **United Therapeutics** — Founded by Dr. Martine Rothblatt, a pioneer in both biotechnology and electric aviation; United Therapeutics' organ manufacturing business model is predicated on rapid organ delivery enabled by electric aircraft

### CONTRACT STRUCTURE

Beta's commercial orders utilize pre-delivery payment (PDP) structures typical of commercial aerospace, with escalating deposits at contract signing, production start, and pre-delivery milestones. Non-refundable PDPs reduce the risk of order cancellations and provide working capital for production ramp-up.
