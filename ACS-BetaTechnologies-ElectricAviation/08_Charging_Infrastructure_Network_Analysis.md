# CHARGING INFRASTRUCTURE NETWORK ANALYSIS
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 08

---

### NETWORK OVERVIEW

Beta Technologies has independently developed a proprietary electric aircraft charging network spanning the continental United States, a strategic infrastructure asset that differentiates the company from all competitors in the advanced air mobility sector. No other eVTOL developer has deployed a comparable operational charging network at national scale, creating a first-mover advantage in ground infrastructure that is as critical to commercial operations as the aircraft itself.

### SITE ACQUISITION AND DEVELOPMENT

Beta employs a multi-pronged site acquisition strategy:

1. **Airport lease agreements** — Direct leases with airport authorities for GA ramp or dedicated Beta charging pads
2. **Existing FBO partnerships** — Installing Charge Cubes at Fixed Base Operator facilities for turnkey operations
3. **Customer co-location** — Chargers installed at customer operating bases (UPS hubs, hospital networks)
4. **Greenfield vertiport development** — Select sites with dedicated Beta-designed vertiport infrastructure

### COMPETITIVE MOAT ANALYSIS

| Barrier | Beta Advantage |
|---|---|
| Capital cost | ~$30-50M invested to date (from IPO proceeds); competitors have not allocated comparable capital to charging |
| Permitting lead time | 12-18 month site development pipeline; late entrants face delayed operational capability |
| Interoperability | First-to-deploy creates de facto connector standard; FAA/industry may adopt Beta connector as basis for standard |
| Customer lock-in | Operators investing in Beta charging will face switching costs to alternative aircraft |
| Network effects | Each additional site increases utility of all ALIA aircraft and vice versa |

### RENEWABLE ENERGY INTEGRATION

Beta has committed to powering its charging network with 100% renewable energy. Initial sites utilize grid-sourced renewables via power purchase agreements (PPAs) and renewable energy certificates (RECs). Select sites incorporate on-site solar photovoltaic arrays and battery energy storage systems (BESS) to reduce grid dependence and provide resilience against utility outages.
