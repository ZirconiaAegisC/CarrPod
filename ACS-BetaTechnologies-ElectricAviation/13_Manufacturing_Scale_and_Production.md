# MANUFACTURING SCALE AND PRODUCTION
## Beta Technologies Corporation — ACS Dossier File 13

---

### BURLINGTON PRODUCTION CENTER

Beta Technologies' primary manufacturing facility is located at Patrick Leahy Burlington International Airport (KBTV) in South Burlington, Vermont. The 180,000 square foot production center was purpose-built for electric aircraft manufacturing and integrates design engineering, composite fabrication, final assembly, flight test, and delivery operations on a single campus with direct runway access.

### FACILITY CAPABILITIES

| Area | Function | Capacity |
|---|---|---|
| Composite fabrication | Automated fiber placement (AFP), hand layup, cure ovens | 2 shifts; expandable to 3 |
| Subassembly | Wing assembly, fuselage join, empennage build-up | 2 production lines |
| Electrical integration | Battery pack assembly, wiring harness fabrication, power electronics integration | Clean-room environment; ESD-controlled |
| Final assembly | Aircraft integration, systems test, conforming production | 2 parallel lines |
| Paint and finish | Prep, prime, paint, markings application | Cross-draft booth |
| Flight test | Pre-delivery flight test, production flight test, certification test | Dedicated ramp, hangar, and crew |
| Delivery center | Customer acceptance, training, handover | Customer briefing rooms; simulator |

### SUPPLY CHAIN

Beta's supply chain strategy balances vertical integration of core IP (motors, power electronics, battery packs) with conventional aerospace procurement for commodity components:

| Category | Strategy | Key Suppliers |
|---|---|---|
| Electric motors (H500A) | Full vertical integration (in-house design + manufacture) | Internal |

### MANUFACTURING PROCESS INNOVATION

Beta has invested in manufacturing process technologies to support scalable electric aircraft production:

| Technology | Application | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Automated fiber placement (AFP) | Wing skins and fuselage sections | 4x throughput vs. hand layup |
| Out-of-autoclave (OoA) curing | Composite structures | Reduced capital cost; faster cycle time |
| Robotic drilling and fastening | Wing-to-fuselage join | Precision; repeatability; reduced labor |
| Automated electrical testing | Wire harness and power electronics | Reduced test time; data capture for certification |
| Digital twin manufacturing | Process simulation and quality assurance | First-pass yield improvement |

### SCALABILITY ANALYSIS

The Burlington facility is designed for expansion. The 180,000 sq ft footprint can accommodate two parallel final assembly lines at an estimated combined capacity of 150-200 aircraft per year with three-shift operations. Additional capacity can be achieved through:

1. **Brownfield expansion** — Adjacent land at KBTV available for facility expansion
2. **Greenfield second site** — Potential for a second production facility in a lower-cost location for high-volume production
3. **Contract manufacturing** — Potential for Spirit AeroSystems or similar tier-1 aerostructures supplier to produce major subassemblies
