## 1. YDIH Publication History

The Younger Dryas Impact Hypothesis (YDIH) was first proposed by Firestone, West, Kennett,
et al., in *PNAS* (2007), suggesting that a fragmented comet impacted the Laurentide Ice
Sheet ~12,800 years ago, triggering the Younger Dryas cooling event, massive wildfires,
and the extinction of North American megafauna.

For over 15 years, the YDIH was classified as "controversial" to "fringe" — but Brodsky
V2.0 correctly identifies that the **evidentiary landscape has fundamentally shifted
between 2023 and 2026.**

## 2. 72-Confirmed-YDB-Site Evidence (USC News, May 2026)

impact markers. This represents approximately **triple the number of confirmed sites

## 3. PMC Peer-Review Article (2025)

Powell, J.L. "Premature Rejection in Science: The Case of the Younger Dryas Impact
Hypothesis." *Science Progress*, 2025. — This article formally argues the YDIH has been
subject to premature rejection by the scientific establishment, using the sociological
theory of "premature discovery" (a concept from Gunther Stent, 1972) to explain the
two-decade resistance to YDIH evidence.

Powell documents: (a) hostile review processes that rejected YDIH papers on methodological
grounds while accepting contradictory papers on weaker evidence, (b) the unstated assumption
that "cosmic impact" explanations are inherently less credible than terrestrial ones, and
(c) the gradual tipping point reached when independent labs began replicating YDB findings.
