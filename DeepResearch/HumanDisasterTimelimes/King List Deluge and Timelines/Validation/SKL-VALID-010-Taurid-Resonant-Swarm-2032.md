## 1. The Taurid Resonant Swarm (TRS)

The Taurid meteor stream is a broad debris trail from Comet 2P/Encke, producing two
radiant points (Northern Taurids and Southern Taurids) active from September to
December annually. The **Taurid Resonant Swarm** is a denser concentration of debris
within the main stream that Earth encounters approximately every 2,500-3,000 years.

**Key astronomical parameters:**
- **Parent body:** Comet 2P/Encke (orbital period: 3.3 years)
- **Stream width:** ~0.3 AU
- **Peak encounter:** Earth passes through the densest portion approximately every
  2,500-3,000 years, with lesser encounters every few decades
- **Previous major encounter:** ~3200 BCE — consistent with the 3,200 BP geomagnetic
  excursion (Event 5 in the Khabarovsk timeline) and the Bronze Age Collapse, if the
  next encounter window aligns with ~5,200 years post-previous
