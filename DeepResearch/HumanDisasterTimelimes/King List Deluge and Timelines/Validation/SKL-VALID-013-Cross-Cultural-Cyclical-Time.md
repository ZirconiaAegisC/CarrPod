## 2. Common Mathematical Architecture

Of the 5 systems, **4** (SKL, Vedic, Maya, Chinese) use the number 60 or its multiples
as a foundational unit — evidence of an independent invention of sexagesimal (or
multiples-thereof) counting across Eurasia and Mesoamerica.

**The convergence on 432 (and 60) across unrelated cultures:**
- Vedic: 4,320 × 100 = 432,000
- SKL: 28,800 (8 SAR) = 432 × 66.67
- Maya: 144,000 × 3 = 432,000
- Nordic (optional but notable): 432,000 warriors at Ragnarok

The probability of this convergence occurring through chance is extremely low (see
SKL-VALID-008 for calculation), suggesting either:
1. A deep-time shared cultural origin (Proto-Indo-European, ~6,000-9,000 BP)
2. Independent astronomical derivation (all cultures observed the same sky, and
   precession-driven numbers emerge naturally from that observation)

## 3. The Astronomical Derivation Hypothesis

Brodsky V2.0 adopts position (2): the shared numbers derive from shared astronomical
observation.
- 26,000-year precession cycle ÷ 60 = 433.3 ≈ 432 (when rounded to sexagesimal value)
- 1 SAR (3,600) = 60 × 60 — pure sexagesimal
- Maya baktun (144,000 days ≈ 394.5 years) derives from Venus-Sun synodic cycles
  (Venus synodic period = 584 days; 584 × 250 = 146,000 days ≈ 1 baktun)

The common thread: all five cultures developed sophisticated astronomical observation
systems that encoded cyclical time in numerical frameworks. The frameworks were
different (base-60, base-20, base-10 scaled), but the underlying observations —
precession, planetary cycles, solar-lunar correlation — were the same.
