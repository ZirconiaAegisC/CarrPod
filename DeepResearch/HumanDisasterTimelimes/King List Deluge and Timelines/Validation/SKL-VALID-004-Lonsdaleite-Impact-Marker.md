## 1. What Is Lonsdaleite?

Lonsdaleite is a hexagonal polymorph of diamond, named after crystallographer Dame
Kathleen Lonsdale (1903-1971). Unlike cubic diamond (the common form), lonsdaleite
has a **hexagonal crystal lattice** that forms ONLY under extreme shock compression.

- **Formation threshold:** >50 GPa (gigapascals) shock pressure
- **Natural occurrence:** ONLY in meteorite impact craters and meteorite specimens
- **Terrestrial impossibility:** No known geological process on Earth's surface
  produces pressures exceeding 10 GPa outside of impact events
- **First identification:** Canyon Diablo meteorite (Arizona), 1967
