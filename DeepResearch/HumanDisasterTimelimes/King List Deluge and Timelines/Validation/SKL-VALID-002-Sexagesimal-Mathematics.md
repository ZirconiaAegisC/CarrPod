## 2. Academic Validation of Sumerian Sexagesimal Mathematics

**Neugebauer, Otto.** *The Exact Sciences in Antiquity.* Princeton University Press, 1952.
Neugebauer (1899-1990), a mathematician and historian of science, established the base-60
system's origins in Sumer. He documented:
- The base-60 system was fully developed by the Ur III period (2112-2004 BCE)
- 1 sar = 3,600 in the sexagesimal system (60 × 60)
- Multiplication/division by 60 is the core operational principle

**Friberg, Jöran.** *A Remarkable Collection of Babylonian Mathematical Texts.* Springer,
2007. Friberg details how the Sumerians used multiplicative factors (especially 6 and 10)
to convert between different numerical scales. The factor-6 conversion (sar ÷ 6 = 600
"real" years) is consistent with scholarly reconstructions of Sumerian numerical inflation.
