## 1. The Primary Source

The Weld-Blundell Prism (Ashmolean Museum, Oxford, accession AN1923.444), also known as
WB-444, is a baked clay quadrangular prism inscribed with cuneiform on all four sides.
It is the most complete surviving recension of the Sumerian King List (SKL).

- **Excavator:** Herbert Weld-Blundell, British archaeologist, 1922 expedition
- **Provenance:** Larsa, modern Tell Senkereh, Iraq (approximately 31°17'N, 45°51'E)
- **Dating:** Isin-Larsa period, approximately 1817-1800 BCE (reign of Sin-magir)
- **Language:** Sumerian with Akkadian scribal notations
- **Material:** Baked clay, 20 cm height, 9 cm width
- **Collection:** Ashmolean Museum of Art and Archaeology, University of Oxford

## 2. External Academic Verification

Jacobsen, Thorkild. *The Sumerian King List.* Assyriological Studies, No. 11. University
of Chicago Press, 1939. — The definitive academic publication. Jacobsen collated 15
different SKL manuscripts, including WB-444, and established the standard scholarly text.

Michalowski, Piotr. "History as Charter: Some Observations on the Sumerian King List."
*Journal of the American Oriental Society*, Vol. 103, No. 1 (1983), pp. 237-248. —
Established the SKL as primarily an ideological document from the Isin dynasty,
serving political purposes of legitimizing Isin's succession claims.

Glassner, Jean-Jacques. *Mesopotamian Chronicles.* Society of Biblical Literature, 2004.
— Updated translation and commentary confirming the SKL's 8 antediluvian kings and
241,200-year total.

Brodsky V2.0 correctly identifies:
- The 8 antediluvian kings by name/sar count (Alulim: 28,800; Alalgar: 36,000;
  En-men-lu-ana: 43,200; En-men-gal-ana: 28,800; Dumuzid: 36,000;
  En-sipad-zid-ana: 28,800; En-men-dur-ana: 21,000; Ubara-Tutu: 18,600)
- The 241,200-year total sum matches Jacobsen's published transliteration
- The sexagesimal (base-60) nature of the sar units: 1 sar = 3,600 years in the
