# Ampaire Customer Pipeline — Airline Partners and Launch Customers

## Customer Strategy Overview

Ampaire's customer strategy targets regional airlines operating short-haul
routes where the Eco Caravan's fuel savings generate the maximum economic
benefit. The company has secured commitments from several regional carriers
that collectively represent a launch order book sufficient to absorb the
first 2 to 3 years of production capacity. These launch customers serve dual
roles: as revenue-generating aircraft operators and as references that
validate the Eco Caravan's operational and economic performance for the
broader market.

## Mokulele Airlines

### Profile

Mokulele Airlines is a Hawaii-based regional carrier operating a fleet of
Cessna 208B Grand Caravans on inter-island routes connecting the Hawaiian
Islands. Mokulele operates more than 100 daily departures on routes typically
ranging from 25 to 150 nautical miles — precisely the mission profile where
hybrid-electric propulsion provides maximum fuel savings.

### Strategic Significance

Mokulele's significance to Ampaire extends beyond the fleet size:

- **Ideal mission profile**: Short island-hopping routes with multiple daily
  takeoffs and landings maximize the benefit of electric boost for takeoff
  and regenerative braking during descent
- **High fuel costs**: Hawaii's fuel prices are among the highest in the
  United States due to transportation costs, amplifying the economic benefit
  of 50 to 70 percent fuel consumption reduction
- **Environmental sensitivity**: Hawaii's tourism-dependent economy and
  environmental consciousness create receptivity to reduced-emission aviation
- **Visibility**: Mokulele's high-frequency operations in a geographically
  concentrated market provide Ampaire with a highly visible demonstration
  of the Eco Caravan's operational capability

### Fleet Commitment

Mokulele has announced intentions to convert a significant portion of its
Caravan fleet to the Eco Caravan configuration, representing one of
Ampaire's largest single-customer commitments. The conversion program would
unfold over 3 to 5 years as aircraft cycle through their conversion slots.

## Ravn Alaska

### Profile

Ravn Alaska is an Alaskan regional airline serving communities across Alaska
with a mixed fleet that includes Cessna 208 Caravans. Alaska's unique
operating environment — remote communities with limited or no road access,
harsh weather conditions, and high operating costs — creates both challenges
and opportunities for hybrid-electric propulsion.

### Strategic Significance

Ravn Alaska provides Ampaire with validation in extreme operating conditions:

- **Cold weather operation**: Alaska's winter conditions provide the most
  demanding test of battery performance at low temperatures and the thermal
  management system's ability to maintain battery temperature in extreme cold
- **Remote operations**: Operating from airports with limited ground
  infrastructure demonstrates the Eco Caravan's self-sufficiency and
  reliability in environments where maintenance support is limited
- **Essential air service**: Many of Ravn's routes are federally subsidized
  Essential Air Service routes where operating cost reduction directly
  reduces government subsidy requirements, creating a policy alignment
  supporting hybrid-electric adoption
- **Gravel runway operations**: Demonstrating that the electric propulsion
  system tolerates the vibration and debris environment of unpaved runways
  is essential for the large portion of the global Caravan fleet operating
  from unimproved strips

### Operational Data Value

Ravn Alaska's diverse operating conditions — temperature extremes, short and
unimproved runways, high payload utilization — generate operational data that
validates the Eco Caravan across the aircraft's full operating envelope. This
data is essential for Ampaire's sales process with other operators who
require evidence of performance in challenging conditions before committing
to conversions.

## Personal Airline Exchange

### Business Model

Personal Airline Exchange (PAX) represents a different customer model: rather
than a traditional airline, PAX is a fractional ownership and charter
operator that provides Caravan-based air service to individuals and businesses.
PAX's customers value the environmental profile of hybrid-electric aviation
as a differentiator in a market where sustainability credentials influence
purchasing decisions.

### Strategic Significance

PAX demonstrates the Eco Caravan's applicability to business models beyond
scheduled airline service:

- **Fractional ownership**: Hybrid-electric operating economics improve the
  value proposition for fractional ownership programs by reducing variable
  operating costs
- **Premium charter**: Environmentally conscious charter customers may pay
  a premium for reduced-emission flights, creating a revenue premium that
  accelerates payback on the conversion investment
- **Brand differentiation**: The Eco Caravan's distinctive noise signature
  and visible electric propulsion differentiate PAX's product in a
  competitive charter market

## Additional Customer Pipeline

Beyond the announced launch customers, Ampaire's pipeline includes:

### Regional Airlines

Approximately 50 to 80 regional airlines worldwide operate Grand Caravans,
representing the core addressable market. Key geographic concentrations:

- **United States**: Commuter airlines in Alaska, Hawaii, the Pacific
  Northwest, the Great Lakes region, and the Northeast
- **Caribbean**: Island-hopping carriers connecting Caribbean island nations
  on routes ideally suited to the Eco Caravan's range
- **Southeast Asia**: Operators in Indonesia, the Philippines, and Malaysia
  connecting island communities where fuel logistics costs amplify the
  benefit of reduced consumption
- **Africa**: Safari and charter operators in East and Southern Africa where
  the Caravan's short-field performance and the Eco Caravan's reduced
  operating cost are both valued
- **Australia and Pacific Islands**: Remote community transport and tourism
  operators

### Cargo Operators

Cargo-configured Grand Caravans operated by FedEx Feeder and other freight
operators represent a distinct market segment. Cargo operations typically
involve:

- Predictable, repeatable routes well-suited to the Eco Caravan's range
- Night operations where noise reduction is valuable for airport community
  relations
- Fuel cost as a dominant operating expense
- Higher utilization (1000 to 1500 hours annually) that accelerates fuel
  savings payback

Cargo operators may become a significant customer segment as the Eco Caravan
demonstrates reliability in high-utilization scheduled service.

### Government and Special Mission

Government operators of Grand Caravans — for aerial survey, law enforcement,
medical evacuation, and military utility missions — represent a niche but
potentially lucrative market segment. Government customers value:

- Reduced fuel logistics burden for remote operations
- Lower acoustic signature for surveillance missions
- Compliance with government emissions reduction mandates
- Domestic manufacturing content for Buy America compliance

## Sales Process

Ampaire's sales process for airline customers follows a structured engagement
path:

1. **Initial inquiry**: Customer contacts Ampaire or is contacted through
   the sales team's outreach to Caravan operators
2. **Economic analysis**: Ampaire prepares a route-specific fuel savings
   analysis based on the customer's actual operating data, demonstrating
   the payback period for conversion
3. **Demonstration flight**: Customer representatives fly on the Eco Caravan
   demonstrator, experiencing the electric propulsion's noise reduction and
   performance firsthand
4. **Letter of intent**: Customer signs a non-binding LOI indicating fleet
   conversion intentions and desired delivery timeline
5. **Definitive agreement**: Binding purchase agreement with delivery
   positions, pricing, and contract terms, executed upon or after STC
   issuance
6. **Pre-delivery preparation**: Customer identifies donor aircraft, prepares
   maintenance facilities, and trains pilots on the hybrid system's
   operational characteristics
7. **Aircraft delivery**: Converted aircraft delivered, pilot and mechanic
   training completed, and operational support initiated
