# Ampaire Manufacturing and Scale-Up — Certification and Production Timeline

## Master Schedule Overview

Ampaire's certification and production ramp follows a phased approach that
aligns regulatory milestones with production readiness and market development.
The master schedule spans from the current STC development phase through
initial full-rate production, with decision gates at each phase transition.

## Phase 1: Certification Development (Current Phase)

### Key Activities

- Completion of the Eco Caravan flight test program: approximately 200 to
  300 hours of developmental and certification flight testing
- Submission of compliance data packages to the FAA covering structural
  modifications, propulsion system performance, battery safety, and systems
  integration
- FAA review and approval of compliance data
- Conformity inspection of the certification test aircraft
- Resolution of any FAA findings or requests for additional data

### Production Activities in Parallel

During the certification phase, Ampaire conducts limited production activities
in parallel with the certification program:

- Conversion of 2 to 4 customer aircraft under experimental airworthiness
  certificates for customer evaluation and operational familiarization
- Establishment of the initial supply chain and production processes at the
  Hawthorne facility
- Development of production tooling, work instructions, and quality
  documentation
- Training of initial production technicians

### Decision Gate: STC Issuance

The critical milestone is FAA issuance of the Supplemental Type Certificate
for the Eco Caravan. STC issuance triggers:

- Authorization to perform conversions that result in standard-category
  airworthiness certification
- Ability to deliver production aircraft to customers for revenue service
- Eligibility to pursue FAA Production Certificate for the conversion process
- Initiation of international validation activities

Ampaire targets STC issuance in the 2024 to 2025 timeframe, subject to the
FAA's review timeline and any findings requiring additional data or analysis.

## Phase 2: Initial Production Ramp (STC + 0 to 18 Months)

### Production Rate

Initial production at the Hawthorne facility targets 1 to 2 aircraft per
month (12 to 24 annually), constrained by:

- Available hangar space and technician capacity at Hawthorne
- The learning curve associated with a new production process
- Supply chain maturation — initial supplier delivery schedules are conservative
  until production cadence stabilizes

### Production Certificate

Concurrent with initial production, Ampaire pursues FAA Production Certificate
approval under FAR Part 21 Subpart G. The Production Certificate establishes
that Ampaire's quality system meets FAA requirements for producing the STC-
approved modification, enabling:

- Issuance of airworthiness certificates for converted aircraft without
  individual FAA conformity inspection
- Production efficiency through delegated quality assurance authority
- Foundation for scaling production through the authorized conversion center
  network

Production Certificate issuance is targeted within 6 to 12 months of STC
issuance.

### Customer Deliveries

Initial production aircraft are allocated to:

- **Launch customers**: Mokulele Airlines, Ravn Alaska, and other carriers
  with announced commitments receive priority delivery positions
- **Demonstration aircraft**: 1 to 2 aircraft retained by Ampaire for customer
  demonstration, pilot training, and continued operational data collection
- **Market development**: Aircraft placed with operators in key geographic
  markets to build operational track record and generate reference data for
  subsequent sales

## Phase 3: Production Scale-Up (STC + 18 to 36 Months)

### Rate Increase

Production rate increases to 3 to 5 aircraft per month (36 to 60 annually)
through:

- Implementation of multi-shift operations at Hawthorne
- Activation of the first authorized conversion center partners, adding
  parallel production capacity
- Supply chain maturation and supplier rate readiness
- Workforce expansion and training pipeline maturity

### Authorized Conversion Center Network

The first wave of authorized conversion centers, typically 2 to 4 facilities,
are activated during this phase. Centers are selected based on:

- Geographic proximity to customer concentrations
- Existing Cessna Caravan maintenance experience and tooling
- Quality system capability and willingness to invest in the training and
  certification required for authorization
- Sufficient hangar capacity to dedicate to conversions without displacing
  existing maintenance business

Each authorized center contributes 1 to 2 aircraft per month of production
capacity after ramp-up.

### International Validation

Validation of the FAA STC by foreign aviation authorities proceeds during
this phase, with priority given to markets where customers have placed orders:

- EASA validation for European operations
- Transport Canada validation for Canadian operations
- ANAC validation for Brazilian operations
- Additional authorities as required for specific customer commitments

International validation enables delivery of Eco Caravans to customers
operating under non-US registries, expanding the addressable market beyond
the US fleet of Grand Caravans.

## Phase 4: Full-Rate Production (STC + 36 to 60 Months)

### Target Production Rate

Full-rate production targets 60 to 120 aircraft annually (5 to 10 per month),
representing approximately 5 to 10 percent of the global installed Grand
Caravan fleet converted annually. At this rate, the installed fleet of 2000
aircraft provides a 15 to 30 year production runway before exhausting the
conversion-eligible fleet, at which point Ampaire would have established a
recurring revenue base from aftermarket support and would be positioned to
address additional aircraft types.

### Production Network

The conversion center network expands to 6 to 10 authorized facilities
globally, with centers in:

- North America: 3 to 5 centers serving the US, Canadian, and Caribbean markets
- Europe: 1 to 2 centers serving EASA-registered operators
- Asia-Pacific: 1 to 2 centers serving operators in Southeast Asia, Australia,
  and the Pacific Islands where Caravans are widely used for island-hopping
  and remote community transport
- Latin America: 1 center serving the large Caravan fleet in Brazil and
  neighboring countries

### Aftermarket Maturity

By full-rate production, the aftermarket infrastructure for Eco Caravan
support reaches maturity:

- Spare parts inventory positioned at regional distribution centers
- Field service representatives supporting major operators
- Training programs for pilot type rating (if required) and maintenance
  technician certification established at approved training organizations
- Technical publications — maintenance manual, illustrated parts catalog,
  wiring diagram manual — maintained and updated through the continued
  airworthiness process
- Service bulletin and airworthiness directive management processes
  established and functioning

## Production Economics at Scale

At full-rate production, estimated unit economics per Eco Caravan conversion:

- **Conversion cost**: 800000 to 1.2 million USD (labor, materials, kits)
- **Donor aircraft cost**: 1.2 to 2.0 million USD (customer-provided aircraft
  cost is external to Ampaire)
- **Removed engine and propeller credit**: 250000 to 400000 USD (sale of
  removed PT6A and propeller offsets conversion cost)
- **Total customer investment**: 1.8 to 2.8 million USD net of engine credit
  (for Ampaire-provided aircraft), or 0.8 to 1.2 million USD for conversion
  of customer-provided aircraft
- **Customer payback period**: 2 to 4 years based on fuel savings of 75000
  to 150000 USD annually at 600 to 1000 flight hours per year

These economics are attractive relative to the alternative of purchasing a
new conventionally powered aircraft (4 to 5 million USD for a new Grand
Caravan EX) and offer rapid payback from fuel savings alone, before
accounting for reduced maintenance cost and potential carbon credit or
emissions compliance benefits.
