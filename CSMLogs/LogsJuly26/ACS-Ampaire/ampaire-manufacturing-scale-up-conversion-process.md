# Ampaire Manufacturing and Scale-Up — Conversion Process Engineering

## Conversion Philosophy

Ampaire's manufacturing model is fundamentally different from traditional
aircraft manufacturing. Rather than building complete aircraft from raw
materials, Ampaire converts existing Cessna 208B Grand Caravans — either
customer-owned aircraft or aircraft acquired by Ampaire for conversion and
resale. This conversion-based model dramatically reduces manufacturing
capital requirements, compresses production lead time, and leverages the
existing global supply chain for Caravan parts and maintenance.

## Conversion Workflow

### Phase 1: Aircraft Induction and Assessment (1 Week)

Upon arrival at the conversion facility, each donor aircraft undergoes:

- **Records review**: Complete audit of aircraft maintenance records, AD
  compliance, service bulletin compliance, and damage history
- **Physical inspection**: Airframe inspection per Cessna structural
  inspection manual, with particular attention to corrosion, fatigue, and
  damage that could affect the conversion
- **Engine and propeller valuation**: The removed PT6A engine and propeller
  are assessed for resale value; a typical mid-time PT6A-114A commands 250000
  to 400000 USD on the used market, partially offsetting the conversion cost
- **Conversion scope definition**: Determination of any airframe-specific
  work required beyond the standard conversion — avionics upgrades, interior
  refurbishment, corrosion repair

Aircraft that fail the induction assessment due to excessive corrosion or
structural issues are rejected for conversion, with the customer notified
of the findings.

### Phase 2: Deconstruction (2 Weeks)

The deconstruction phase removes the existing propulsion and associated
systems:

- **Engine removal**: PT6A engine, propeller, engine mount, and cowling
  removed
- **Fuel system modification**: Existing fuel system is modified to serve
  the reduced fuel capacity requirements of the hybrid configuration. Fuel
  tanks may be partially deactivated or converted to battery compartment
  space.
- **Electrical system removal**: Existing electrical generation and
  distribution system components that will be replaced by the hybrid
  electrical system are removed — generator, voltage regulator, battery,
  associated wiring
- **Instrumentation removal**: Legacy engine instruments replaced by the
  hybrid powertrain display system are removed; remaining instruments are
  retained

All removed components are cataloged, assessed for resale value, and either
sold through Ampaire's parts redistribution channel or returned to the
customer.

### Phase 3: Structural Modification (2 Weeks)

Structural modifications prepare the airframe for hybrid powertrain
installation:

- **Motor mount installation**: A new motor mount structure, designed to
  the same structural standards as the original engine mount, is installed
  on the firewall. The mount accommodates the electric motor and provides
  attachment points for the motor controller and cooling system.
- **Battery compartment installation**: Under-floor and aft fuselage
  compartments are modified with structural reinforcements, fireproof
  liners, and battery module attachment rails. The modifications are
  designed to meet the structural requirements of FAR 23.561 (emergency
  landing conditions) for the battery mass.
- **Range extender installation**: The automotive-derived range extender
  engine is installed in a modified nacelle, with structural provisions
  for the engine mount, cooling system, and fire suppression system
- **Cooling system integration**: Radiators, ducts, and cooling lines are
  installed, with careful attention to minimizing cooling drag while
  ensuring adequate heat rejection at maximum continuous power in hot-day
  conditions

All structural modifications are performed in accordance with FAA-approved
engineering data and inspected by authorized representatives.

### Phase 4: Systems Integration (3 Weeks)

Systems integration connects the hybrid powertrain components:

- **High-voltage electrical system**: Motor power cables, battery power cables,
  contactors, fuses, and insulation monitoring system installed per the
  certified design. Cable routing follows defined paths with appropriate
  separation from fuel lines, control cables, and low-voltage wiring.
- **Low-voltage control system**: CAN bus wiring, sensor harnesses, cockpit
  display and control wiring installed and tested
- **Thermal management system**: Cooling loops filled, pressure-tested, and
  verified for flow rate and heat rejection capacity
- **Power management system**: Controllers installed, software loaded, and
  system configuration verified
- **Fire protection**: Fire detection and suppression system for the battery
  compartments and range extender engine compartment installed and tested

Systems integration is the most labor-intensive phase, requiring approximately
800 to 1200 person-hours of skilled technician time per aircraft.

### Phase 5: Ground Testing (1 Week)

After systems integration, the aircraft undergoes ground testing:

- **Power-on checks**: All systems powered sequentially, verifying correct
  voltages, communication bus integrity, and sensor functionality
- **Motor run**: Electric motor operated through its power range with the
  aircraft restrained, verifying thrust output, vibration levels, and thermal
  behavior
- **Range extender run**: Combustion engine started and operated through its
  power range, verifying generator output, engine parameters, and thermal
  behavior
- **Combined operation**: Motor and range extender operated together across
  representative mission power profiles
- **Failure mode testing**: Simulated system failures to verify correct
  annunciation, automatic reconfiguration, and pilot procedures

### Phase 6: Flight Test and Delivery (1 Week)

Post-conversion flight testing verifies aircraft performance and systems
operation:

- **Production flight test**: 3 to 5 flight hours per aircraft covering all
  normal and emergency procedures, performance verification at representative
  weights and center of gravity positions, and systems functional checks
- **Squawk correction**: Any discrepancies identified during flight test are
  corrected and verified
- **Records completion**: Aircraft logbooks updated with the conversion STC
  entry, weight and balance revised, equipment list updated, and flight manual
  supplement inserted
- **Customer acceptance**: Customer or customer's representative performs
  acceptance inspection and acceptance flight
- **Delivery**: Aircraft released to customer with complete documentation
  package

Total conversion cycle time from induction to delivery: 6 to 8 weeks per
aircraft. As experience accumulates and processes mature, Ampaire targets
reducing cycle time to 4 to 6 weeks.

## Conversion Centers

### Hawthorne Facility

Ampaire's primary conversion center at Hawthorne Municipal Airport (KHHR)
in Hawthorne, California, has initial capacity for approximately 12 to 24
conversions annually in a single shift operation. The facility includes:

- 2000 square meter hangar with space for 4 to 6 aircraft simultaneously in
  various stages of conversion
- Back shop areas for component preparation, electrical harness fabrication,
  and battery module assembly
- Engineering offices co-located with the production floor for rapid
  resolution of production issues

### Partner Network

To scale production beyond the Hawthorne facility's capacity, Ampaire is
developing a network of authorized conversion centers at existing maintenance,
repair, and overhaul (MRO) facilities with Cessna Caravan experience. These
partner facilities:

- Receive training on the Eco Caravan conversion process from Ampaire
- Perform conversions under Ampaire's Production Certificate using Ampaire-
  supplied conversion kits (powertrain components, structural modification
  kits, electrical harnesses, software loads)
- Are audited by Ampaire for quality system compliance
- Enable distributed production capacity that can scale with demand without
  requiring Ampaire to invest in additional company-owned facilities

The partner network model is analogous to the relationship between aircraft
manufacturers and authorized completion centers for business jet interiors —
the OEM provides the engineering, certification, and kits; the completion
center provides the labor and facilities.

## Conversion Kit Strategy

Ampaire's conversion kit approach simplifies the partner model and enables
scalable production:

- **Powertrain kit**: Electric motor, motor controller, generator, power
  management controllers, cockpit displays — shipped as a pre-tested assembly
  from Ampaire or a qualified supplier
- **Battery kit**: Pre-assembled and tested battery modules shipped in
  accordance with hazardous materials transportation regulations
- **Structural kit**: Motor mount, battery compartment liners, cooling system
  components, fire suppression system — prefabricated and kitted per aircraft
- **Electrical kit**: Complete wiring harness set, connectors, contactors,
  fuses — fabricated by Ampaire and shipped as an installation-ready kit
- **Documentation kit**: Conversion instructions, engineering data, inspection
  requirements, and record-keeping templates

The kit-based approach ensures consistency across conversion centers while
minimizing the technical capability required at partner facilities — the
conversion becomes primarily a mechanical assembly and integration task
rather than a design and development task.
