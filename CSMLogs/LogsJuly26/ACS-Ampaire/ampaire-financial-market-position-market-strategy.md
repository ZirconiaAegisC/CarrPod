# Ampaire Financial and Market Position — Market Strategy and Growth Roadmap

## Strategic Vision

Ampaire's long-term strategic vision is to become the dominant provider of
hybrid-electric propulsion for sub-regional and regional aircraft, starting
with the Cessna Grand Caravan STC and expanding to additional aircraft types
over time. The company's strategy follows a disciplined sequence: prove the
technology and business case on a single aircraft type, establish production
capability and customer relationships, and then expand the addressable market
through additional STCs and technology licensing.

## Phase 1: Caravan Dominance (Current to STC + 3 Years)

### Strategy

Focus all resources on completing Eco Caravan certification and establishing
initial production and customer operations. The objective is to convert the
Grand Caravan's large installed base into a captive market for Ampaire's
hybrid-electric conversion, creating a monopoly position on the type.

### Key Actions

- Complete FAA STC for the Eco Caravan
- Deliver initial aircraft to launch customers Mokulele Airlines and Ravn
  Alaska, establishing operational track record
- Ramp production to 1 to 2 aircraft per month at Hawthorne
- Establish first authorized conversion centers to expand capacity
- Accumulate fleet operational data demonstrating fuel savings, reliability,
  and maintenance cost reduction

### Success Metrics

- 50-plus aircraft in revenue service generating positive operator
  testimonials
- Fleet accumulated 50000-plus flight hours with dispatch reliability
  exceeding 95 percent
- Operator-reported fuel savings consistent with 50 to 70 percent claims
- Production capacity of 24 to 48 aircraft annually established

## Phase 2: Geographic Expansion (STC + 2 to 5 Years)

### Strategy

Extend the Eco Caravan's market reach internationally through regulatory
validation and localized production capability.

### Key Actions

- Secure EASA validation of the FAA STC, opening the European market
- Establish conversion center partnerships in Europe, Asia-Pacific, and
  Latin America
- Develop regional supply chains for battery modules and powertrain
  components to reduce logistics cost and import duties
- Adapt the Eco Caravan's operational and maintenance documentation for
  international regulatory environments
- Pursue government incentives and partnerships for hybrid-electric aviation
  in target markets

### Market Prioritization

International market entry is prioritized based on:

1. **Fleet concentration**: Markets with the largest Grand Caravan fleets
   (Brazil, Indonesia, Australia) are prioritized
2. **Fuel cost economics**: Markets with high fuel costs (Europe, Pacific
   Islands) have the strongest customer payback proposition
3. **Regulatory receptivity**: Markets with existing bilateral agreements
   accepting FAA STCs are easier to enter than those requiring independent
   certification
4. **Environmental policy alignment**: Markets with carbon pricing, emissions
   mandates, or aviation decarbonization incentives provide additional demand
   drivers

## Phase 3: Platform Expansion (STC + 4 to 8 Years)

### Strategy

Extend the AMP-H570 hybrid-electric powertrain to additional aircraft types
beyond the Grand Caravan, expanding the addressable market.

### Candidate Aircraft Types

Ampaire has identified several aircraft types as candidates for hybrid-electric
conversion, prioritized by installed base size, mission profile, and
powertrain compatibility:

**Cessna 208B Grand Caravan EX**: The current-production Caravan variant with
the more powerful PT6A-140 engine. The EX's higher payload and performance
expand the routes where hybrid conversion is viable.

**Cessna 207 Skywagon**: A 7-passenger utility aircraft with similar
characteristics to the Caravan but a smaller installed base (approximately
600 aircraft). The 207's lower purchase price makes conversion economics
more challenging due to the conversion cost representing a larger percentage
of aircraft value.

**Pilatus PC-12**: A 9-passenger single-engine turboprop with a large installed
base (approximately 1800 aircraft) and higher performance than the Caravan.
The PC-12's pressurized cabin and longer range position it for longer regional
routes where the hybrid system's fuel savings proposition is somewhat weaker
but still economically attractive at high fuel prices.

**De Havilland Canada DHC-6 Twin Otter**: A 19-passenger twin turboprop widely
used for regional and utility operations. The Twin Otter's two engines create
an opportunity for a hybrid conversion that replaces one or both engines with
electric propulsion. The larger passenger capacity improves fuel savings per
available seat mile. The Twin Otter's current production by De Havilland
Canada creates potential for an OEM partnership.

**Britten-Norman Islander**: A 9-passenger twin piston aircraft with a large
installed base in island-hopping and short-haul markets — the ideal mission
profile for hybrid conversion. The Islander's piston engines create lower
conversion economics hurdles than turbine-powered aircraft.

### STC Development Economics

Each additional aircraft STC is estimated to require 20 to 40 million USD
and 24 to 36 months for development and certification, assuming the AMP-H570
powertrain is adapted rather than redesigned. The capital for additional STCs
may come from Ampaire's own cash flow from Caravan conversions, from strategic
partners (the OEM of the aircraft being converted), from government grants
supporting sustainable aviation technology, or from additional equity funding.

### Technology Licensing Model

For some aircraft types, Ampaire may pursue a licensing model rather than
performing conversions directly:

- **OEM partnership**: The aircraft manufacturer licenses Ampaire's technology
  and offers factory-new hybrid-electric aircraft. This model provides the
  widest market reach and strongest certification support but requires
  aligning with the OEM's product strategy and timeline.
- **MRO partnership**: Ampaire licenses the STC to major MRO organizations
  that perform conversions for customers in their region. This model
  accelerates geographic expansion with lower capital investment.
- **OEM-developed STC**: Ampaire provides engineering services to the aircraft
  OEM, which owns the STC and manages certification, production, and support.
  This model provides licensing revenue but lower margins than Ampaire-owned
  STCs.

## Phase 4: Technology Evolution (STC + 6 to 12 Years)

### Next-Generation Powertrain

As battery energy density improves, Ampaire's powertrain architecture can
evolve toward greater electrification:

- **Increased battery energy density**: Improvement from approximately 250
  Wh/kg (current) to 400 to 500 Wh/kg (projected 2030s) would enable all-
  electric operation on routes up to 150 to 200 nautical miles, covering the
  majority of current Caravan routes without range extender operation.
- **Motor power density improvement**: Next-generation motors using advanced
  magnetics and integrated cooling could increase power-to-weight ratio by
  30 to 50 percent, enabling higher payload or reduced empty weight.
- **Range extender evolution**: The combustion engine range extender could be
  replaced by a hydrogen fuel cell as that technology matures, providing
  zero-emission range extension.

### Autonomous Operations

Longer-term, the hybrid-electric powertrain's fly-by-wire control capability
positions Ampaire's platform for autonomous cargo operations. The electric
motor's precise digital control, combined with the redundant power sources,
provides a natural platform for autonomous flight systems. Cargo operators —
who face fewer regulatory barriers to automation than passenger carriers —
may be the initial adopters of autonomous hybrid-electric regional aircraft.

### Market Evolution Scenarios

Ampaire's strategy accounts for multiple market evolution scenarios:

**Accelerated adoption scenario**: Stringent carbon regulations, high fuel
prices, and successful technology demonstration drive rapid hybrid-electric
adoption. Under this scenario, Ampaire's production ramp accelerates, multiple
STCs are pursued in parallel, and the company achieves market leadership in
the 9 to 19 passenger regional segment within 5 to 7 years.

**Gradual adoption scenario**: Certification delays, operator conservatism,
and infrastructure constraints slow adoption. Under this scenario, Ampaire
maintains a slower production ramp, focuses on the Caravan STC as the primary
revenue source, and conserves capital for an extended timeline to profitability.

**Technology disruption scenario**: Breakthrough battery technology or
regulatory changes accelerate the transition to all-electric or hydrogen
aircraft faster than anticipated. Under this scenario, Ampaire's hybrid
architecture serves as a transition technology that captures value during
the 10 to 15 year window before all-electric regional aircraft become viable
across the full mission envelope.

### Long-Term Exit and Value Creation

Ampaire's ultimate value creation thesis rests on:

- **Installed base**: Converting a significant portion of the global 2000-plus
  Caravan fleet and establishing aftermarket relationships that persist for
  the aircraft's remaining service life
- **Technology platform**: The AMP-H570 powertrain as a proven, certified
  propulsion system adaptable to multiple aircraft types under an STC
  framework that competitors cannot easily replicate
- **Operational data**: A proprietary database of hybrid-electric aircraft
  operations — performance, fuel consumption, maintenance, reliability —
  that provides a competitive advantage in refining the technology and
  convincing customers of its viability
- **Regulatory expertise**: Deep institutional knowledge of hybrid-electric
  aircraft certification that is scarce and valuable as more aircraft types
  and propulsion systems pursue certification

These assets, combined with the company's first-mover position and capital
efficient approach, position Ampaire to create substantial enterprise value
in the emerging market for sustainable regional aviation propulsion.
