# Ampaire Manufacturing and Scale-Up — Supply Chain Development

## Supply Chain Strategy

Ampaire's supply chain strategy is built on two complementary principles:
first, leverage the existing automotive and industrial supply base for
electric powertrain components where aviation-specific development is
unnecessary; second, develop aviation-qualified suppliers for components
that require aerospace-grade quality assurance and traceability. This
dual approach minimizes supply chain development cost and lead time while
ensuring that safety-critical components meet aviation regulatory
requirements.

## Electric Powertrain Supply Chain

### Electric Motor

The electric propulsion motor is sourced from a Tier 1 automotive electric
drive supplier with existing production capability for motors in the 200 to
300 kW power class — a segment driven by demand from electric buses, medium-
duty trucks, and high-performance passenger vehicles. Ampaire works with the
supplier to:

- Adapt the motor's mechanical interfaces for aviation installation
- Qualify the motor to aviation environmental standards (DO-160)
- Establish aviation-grade quality assurance and traceability for aviation-
  destined production units
- Develop the continued airworthiness documentation required for the
  aviation aftermarket

The supplier benefits from access to a new market (aviation) with higher
margins and longer product lifecycles than automotive programs, while Ampaire
benefits from the supplier's automotive-scale production economics and
established quality systems.

### Power Electronics

The motor inverter and DC-DC converters are sourced from industrial power
electronics manufacturers with experience in traction, renewable energy, and
marine applications — all environments with reliability requirements closer
to aviation than consumer electronics. Silicon carbide power modules are
sourced from the semiconductor industry's established SiC supply chain,
primarily suppliers serving the electric vehicle market.

### Battery Cells

Battery cells are sourced from major automotive cell manufacturers — Panasonic,
Samsung SDI, or LG Energy Solution — through standard commercial supply
agreements. Ampaire does not develop proprietary cell chemistry but instead
qualifies existing automotive cells for aviation use through:

- Additional screening and acceptance testing beyond automotive standards
- Characterization of cell performance across the aviation operating
  envelope (temperature, pressure, vibration)
- Aviation-specific abuse testing to validate containment and propagation
  prevention features

This approach allows Ampaire to ride the automotive industry's massive
investment in cell manufacturing capacity and technology improvement, rather
than funding its own cell development program.

### Battery Module Assembly

Battery module assembly — integrating cells, cooling systems, BMS, and
structural enclosures — is performed in-house at Ampaire's Hawthorne facility
and at qualified contract manufacturing partners. Module assembly requires
cleanroom conditions, precision welding or bonding for cell interconnects,
and rigorous end-of-line testing of each module. As production volumes
scale, Ampaire plans to establish a dedicated battery module assembly
facility with capacity for 500 to 1000 modules annually.

## Airframe Supply Chain

### Donor Aircraft

The primary "raw material" for Eco Caravan production is the donor aircraft
itself. Ampaire sources donor Grand Caravans through multiple channels:

- **Customer-provided aircraft**: Airlines and operators provide their own
  Caravans for conversion, typically at mid-life when the aircraft is due
  for an engine overhaul — the avoided overhaul cost offsets a significant
  portion of the conversion cost
- **Ampaire-acquired aircraft**: Ampaire purchases used Caravans on the open
  market, converts them, and sells or leases the completed Eco Caravan to
  customers
- **Fleet transactions**: Multi-aircraft transactions with operators retiring
  or refreshing their Caravan fleets

The used Caravan market is well-established, with approximately 50 to 100
aircraft changing hands annually. Prices for mid-time Caravans (approximately
10000 to 15000 hours total time) range from 1.2 to 2.0 million USD depending
on age, equipment, and condition.

### Cessna-supplied Parts

For airframe parts required during conversion — structural components,
hardware, standard parts — Ampaire sources through the established Cessna
parts distribution network operated by Textron Aviation. This ensures that
replacement parts maintain the same certified configuration as the original
aircraft, avoiding the need for additional parts certification.

### Specialty Suppliers

Certain conversion-specific components are sourced from specialty aerospace
suppliers:

- **Motor mount**: Precision welded steel tube structure fabricated by an
  FAA-certified welding supplier
- **Battery compartment liners**: Stainless steel and fireproof composite
  assemblies fabricated by an aerospace sheet metal and composites supplier
- **Cooling system**: Custom radiators and ducting designed by Ampaire and
  fabricated by aerospace thermal management suppliers
- **Wiring harnesses**: Fabricated by an aerospace electrical interconnect
  supplier to Ampaire's design using qualified aerospace wire and connectors

## Quality Assurance

### Supplier Qualification

Ampaire qualifies suppliers through a structured process:

1. **Initial assessment**: Supplier quality system audit against AS9100 or
   equivalent standard
2. **First article inspection**: Complete dimensional, material, and
   functional verification of the first production article from each supplier
3. **Production process validation**: Statistical process capability
   demonstration for critical characteristics
4. **Ongoing surveillance**: Periodic audits, receiving inspection, and
   supplier performance monitoring

Non-aviation suppliers (automotive, industrial) receive additional support
from Ampaire's quality team to establish the aviation-specific documentation,
traceability, and configuration control requirements that differ from their
existing customer base.

### Incoming Inspection

All received components and materials undergo incoming inspection based on
criticality classification:

- **Class A (Safety-Critical)**: 100 percent inspection of all characteristics
  that affect safety of flight — motor mount welds, battery cell lot
  acceptance data, power electronics functional test results
- **Class B (Mission-Critical)**: Sampling inspection based on ANSI/ASQ Z1.4
  with tightened inspection for new suppliers — cooling system components,
  electrical connectors, structural fasteners
- **Class C (Non-Critical)**: Receipt verification against purchase order and
  certificate of conformance review — interior trim, standard hardware,
  consumables

### Traceability

All safety-critical and mission-critical components are serialized and
traceable through Ampaire's enterprise resource planning system from supplier
receipt through installation on a specific aircraft. This traceability
supports:

- Incident investigation: Rapid identification of the supply chain provenance
  of any component involved in a service difficulty
- Continued airworthiness: Tracking of airworthiness limitations and life-
  limited parts
- Configuration management: Ensuring that only approved part numbers are
  installed on each aircraft configuration

## Supply Chain Risk Management

Ampaire maintains a supply chain risk register tracking:

- **Single-source dependencies**: Components available from only one qualified
  supplier, with mitigation plans including safety stock, second-source
  qualification, and design-for-alternate procurement
- **Lead time risks**: Components with procurement lead times exceeding 26
  weeks, with mitigation through long-term purchase agreements and vendor-
  managed inventory
- **Geographic risks**: Suppliers in regions subject to geopolitical,
  natural disaster, or logistics disruption risk
- **Financial risks**: Suppliers with financial stability concerns that could
  affect delivery continuity

Critical sole-source components — including the electric motor, power
electronics, and battery cells — are maintained with 6 to 12 months of safety
stock to buffer against supply disruption during the production ramp phase.
As volumes increase, dual-sourcing or second-source qualification will be
implemented where economically feasible.
