## 5.1 Division Overview

Redwood Energy was formally launched in June 2025 as the company's third operational division, complementing recycling and materials production. The division repurposes degraded but serviceable electric vehicle battery packs — typically at 70–80% of original nameplate capacity — into stationary energy storage systems. This approach extracts additional economic value from batteries before they enter the recycling stream, effectively doubling the productive lifecycle of lithium-ion cells.

## 5.2 Crusoe Microgrid: Flagship Deployment

The division's inaugural commercial deployment is a 12 MW power / 63 MWh energy capacity microgrid located at Crusoe Energy Systems' data center facility. The system was constructed within four months and reached operational profitability within the same construction window. It represents the largest repurposed-EV-battery stationary storage installation in North America as of mid-2026.

The Crusoe installation validates the technical and economic viability of second-life battery storage at utility-relevant scale. The rapid construction timeline — materially faster than new-build lithium-ion storage procurement, which typically requires 12–18 months from order to commissioning — derives from the availability of pre-assembled, pre-tested battery modules that require minimal reconfiguration.

## 5.3 Repurposing Methodology

Redwood's battery repurposing workflow proceeds through five stages:

1. **State-of-Health Assessment:** Inbound EV packs are characterized through electrochemical impedance spectroscopy, capacity measurement at C/3 discharge rate, and visual inspection. Packs retaining ≥ 70% original capacity with no evidence of physical damage or cell swelling are designated for second-life deployment.
2. **Module Reconfiguration:** Packs are disassembled to the module level. Modules are re-wired into series-parallel configurations optimized for stationary storage voltage windows (typically 600–1500 VDC).
3. **Battery Management System (BMS) Integration:** A Redwood-designed BMS is deployed to manage the heterogeneous population of modules from multiple OEMs and varying states of health. The BMS employs active balancing and per-module state-of-charge estimation to maximize usable capacity across the aggregated system.
4. **Power Conversion System Integration:** Bidirectional inverters and transformers are integrated at the container or enclosure level.
5. **Commissioning & Grid Interconnection:** Systems undergo UL 9540A fire testing, IEEE 1547 interconnection compliance, and utility acceptance testing prior to energization.

## 5.4 Commercial Model

Redwood Energy operates under an "Energy-as-a-Service" contracting model whereby Redwood retains ownership of the storage asset and sells stored electricity and grid services to the host customer under a long-term power purchase agreement or services contract. This model eliminates upfront capital expenditure for the customer while providing Redwood with a recurring revenue stream and residual asset value at end-of-life, when the degraded packs are fed back into the recycling stream.

A representative project at a General Motors assembly plant, deployed at 1.5 MW / 7.2 MWh, is reported to have generated $3 million in annual energy cost savings for the host facility through peak shaving and demand charge reduction.

## 5.5 Capacity Targets & Market Sizing

Redwood Energy targets 20 GWh of deployed second-life storage capacity by 2028. The addressable market for second-life battery storage is projected at $62.7 billion by 2033, growing at a compound annual growth rate of 28.6%. The US grid is estimated to require over 600 GWh of storage capacity by 2030 to accommodate increasing intermittent renewable generation penetration; second-life batteries represent a materially lower-cost pathway relative to new-build lithium-ion storage, leveraging a feedstock stream — degraded EV packs — that would otherwise enter recycling prematurely.

## 5.6 Revenue Implications

Storage revenues derive from three principal streams: (1) demand charge reduction and peak shaving for commercial and industrial customers, (2) frequency regulation and ancillary services in wholesale electricity markets, and (3) energy arbitrage between low-cost and high-cost electricity pricing periods. At anticipated deployment scale, Redwood Energy is positioned to contribute a material and growing share of consolidated corporate revenue through the latter half of the decade.
