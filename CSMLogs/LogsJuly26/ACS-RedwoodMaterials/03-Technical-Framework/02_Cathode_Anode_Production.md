## 4.1 Strategic Rationale

Redwood's cathode and anode production capability represents the critical bridge between recycling operations and domestic cell manufacturing. Absent a domestic source of cathode active material (CAM) and anode copper foil, US-based gigafactories — representing over 500 GWh of announced cell production capacity — remain dependent on imported precursor materials, predominantly from China. Redwood targets 100 GWh of annual CAM and anode foil production capacity by the close of 2025, scaling to 500 GWh by 2030 — sufficient to supply approximately five million electric vehicles.

## 4.2 Cathode Active Material (CAM) Production

### 4.2.1 Precursor Synthesis

The CAM production sequence initiates with co-precipitation of nickel-cobalt-manganese (NCM) or nickel-cobalt-aluminum (NCA) hydroxide precursor from recycled metal sulfate feedstocks. Precursor particle morphology — including tap density, particle size distribution (D50 typically 10–14 µm), and specific surface area — is controlled through continuous stirred-tank reactor (CSTR) parameters including pH, temperature, ammonia concentration, and residence time.

Redwood's precursor synthesis capability is chemistry-agnostic, supporting NCM 523, NCM 622, NCM 811, and NCA formulations. The recycled feedstock provides an inherent purity advantage: recovered metal sulfates contain negligible concentrations of problematic impurities (iron, chromium, zinc) that complicate virgin-mined material processing.

### 4.2.2 Lithiation & Calcination

Precursor is blended with recycled lithium carbonate or lithium hydroxide in stoichiometric proportion and calcined in controlled-atmosphere roller-hearth kilns. Calcination parameters (temperature ramp rate, peak temperature, dwell time, oxygen partial pressure) are chemistry-specific and represent core process intellectual property concentrated within Redwood's engineering team.

Post-calcination CAM undergoes jet-milling to deagglomerate and achieve target particle size specification, followed by surface coating where required (e.g., aluminum oxide or boron oxide nanocoating for NCM 811 to suppress transition metal dissolution during cycling).

### 4.2.3 Quality Assurance

Produced CAM is subjected to a battery of electrochemical and physical characterization tests:
- Tap density and particle size distribution (laser diffraction)
- Specific surface area (BET nitrogen adsorption)
- X-ray diffraction (crystal structure verification, cation mixing quantification)
- Coin cell cycling (capacity, first-cycle efficiency, capacity retention over 100+ cycles)
- Inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometry (elemental purity)

## 4.3 Anode Copper Foil Production

Redwood produces electrodeposited copper foil from recycled copper recovered during the mechanical separation stage. Copper is dissolved in sulfuric acid, purified through solvent extraction, and electrodeposited onto rotating drum cathodes under controlled current density to produce foil at thicknesses between 6 and 12 µm with tensile strength and elongation specifications suitable for lithium-ion cell manufacturing.

Co-production of anode foil alongside CAM enables Redwood to offer integrated cathode-anode material supply agreements, reducing supply chain complexity for cell manufacturers.

## 4.4 Cost Structure & Competitive Advantage

Recycled CAM production economics benefit from several structural advantages relative to virgin-mined equivalents:

- **Feedstock cost elimination:** Black mass is acquired at negative or near-zero net cost (processors are paid tipping fees for inbound batteries, and manufacturers pay for scrap disposal).
- **Energy intensity reduction:** Hydrometallurgical refining of recycled black mass requires approximately 35–50% less energy than equivalent metal production from mined and beneficiated ore, per unit of metal produced.
- **Logistics compression:** Domestic recycling-to-CAM production eliminates trans-Pacific shipping of concentrates and precursors.
- **Tariff insulation:** Section 301 tariffs on Chinese CAM and precursor imports, IRA Section 45X production tax credits for domestic battery materials, and IRA Section 30D consumer EV credit domestic-content requirements create a combined effective subsidy of $35–$45 per kWh for domestically produced CAM supplied to qualifying cell manufacturers.
