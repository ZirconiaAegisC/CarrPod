## 3.1 Feedstock Acquisition & Logistics

Redwood's feedstock supply chain encompasses three primary input streams: end-of-life consumer electronics batteries (laptops, power tools, mobile devices), end-of-life electric vehicle battery packs, and manufacturing scrap from gigafactory production lines. The company has established collection partnerships with over 300 retail and municipal collection points across the United States, supplemented by direct manufacturer take-back agreements with every major automotive OEM partner.

End-of-life EV battery packs are received at regional consolidation centers for discharge verification, hazard classification, and safe-transport preparation. Packs are transported under DOT Special Permit to the Sparks, Nevada processing campus. Inbound logistics represent a material cost component; the 600-acre Charleston, South Carolina facility under construction is strategically sited to reduce freight distances to southeastern automotive assembly plants and port facilities.

Annual processing capacity exceeds 20 GWh of battery feedstock — equivalent to approximately 250,000 electric vehicle battery packs or 15,000 metric tons of lithium-ion cells. Redwood captures an estimated 70%+ of all end-of-life battery packs processed within the United States.

## 3.2 Mechanical Pre-Processing

Incoming battery packs undergo a proprietary mechanical size-reduction sequence conducted under inert atmospheric conditions to prevent thermal runaway during shredding. The process proceeds through the following stages:

1. **Discharge & Decommissioning:** Residual charge is dissipated through controlled resistive load banks. Coolant fluids are drained and segregated for treatment.
2. **Primary Shredding:** Packs are reduced to module-level fragments under nitrogen purge.
3. **Secondary Shredding:** Module fragments are further reduced to sub-50mm particulate.
4. **Classification & Separation:** A multi-stage separation train employing magnetic separation (ferrous metals), eddy-current separation (non-ferrous metals), density classification, and electrostatic separation yields four output streams: ferrous metals (steel casings), non-ferrous metals (aluminum, copper foil), anode materials (graphite, residual lithium), and cathode-rich "black mass" powder.
5. **Black Mass Refinement:** The black mass — containing lithium, nickel, cobalt, and manganese in oxide form — is milled to a controlled particle size distribution optimized for subsequent hydrometallurgical dissolution.

## 3.3 Hydrometallurgical Processing

Redwood's core competitive moat resides in its hydrometallurgical refining circuit. The black mass is processed through a sequence of leaching, solvent extraction, and precipitation stages to isolate battery-grade metal salts.

**Leaching:** Black mass is dissolved in a proprietary acid mixture under controlled temperature and pressure. Continuous-flow reactor designs, developed in-house, achieve residence times materially shorter than industry-standard batch leaching while maintaining dissolution efficiencies exceeding 98% for target metals.

**Solvent Extraction:** The pregnant leach solution undergoes sequential solvent extraction to isolate individual metal ions. Redwood's solvent extraction chemistry is designed for selective separation of nickel, cobalt, and manganese from lithium-rich raffinate streams. The process eliminates the need for intermediate precipitation and re-dissolution steps characteristic of competitor approaches.

**Lithium Recovery:** Lithium is recovered from the raffinate through a proprietary direct lithium extraction (DLE) process employing selective adsorption media. This contrasts with conventional evaporation-pond approaches, which require 12–18 months and impose geographic constraints. Redwood's DLE process achieves lithium recovery rates exceeding 95% with residence times measured in hours.

**Precipitation & Crystallization:** Isolated metal solutions are precipitated as battery-grade sulfates (nickel sulfate hexahydrate, cobalt sulfate heptahydrate, manganese sulfate monohydrate) and lithium carbonate or lithium hydroxide monohydrate, depending on cathode chemistry requirements.

## 3.4 Material Recovery Rates

| Metal | Recovery Rate | Purity Grade |
|-------|---------------|--------------|
| Lithium | ≥ 95% | Battery-grade Li₂CO₃ / LiOH·H₂O |
| Nickel | ≥ 95% | Battery-grade NiSO₄·6H₂O |
| Cobalt | ≥ 95% | Battery-grade CoSO₄·7H₂O |
| Copper | ≥ 95% | Electrorefined Cu foil |
| Manganese | ≥ 90% | Battery-grade MnSO₄·H₂O |
| Graphite | ≥ 85% | Purified flake graphite |

## 3.5 Scale & Strategic Context

The United States currently processes approximately 3% of global lithium and cobalt production — a figure Redwood's operations are designed to materially increase. The Sparks facility alone can produce sufficient lithium to supply over one million electric vehicles annually at full cathode production capacity. Global benchmark prices for battery-grade lithium carbonate have ranged from $15,000 to $80,000 per metric ton over the 2022–2026 period; recycled domestic production provides both price stability and supply security independent of Chinese refining capacity.
