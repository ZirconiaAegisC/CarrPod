## 7.1 Facility Overview

Redwood operates across two primary campus locations with a combined footprint of approximately 775 acres, positioning it as the largest dedicated battery recycling and materials manufacturing complex in the Western Hemisphere.

### 7.1.1 Sparks, Nevada — 175-Acre Campus

The Sparks campus, located within the Tahoe Reno Industrial Center in Storey County, Nevada, serves as the company's headquarters and primary processing facility. Site capabilities include:

- Mechanical shredding and black mass production lines
- Hydrometallurgical refining circuit (leaching, solvent extraction, precipitation)
- Cathode active material precursor and calcination production capacity (100 GWh annual target)
- Anode copper foil electrodeposition lines
- Research and development laboratories (analytical chemistry, electrochemistry, process engineering)
- Administrative headquarters

Proximity to the Tesla Gigafactory Nevada and the Panasonic Energy cell manufacturing facility — the highest-volume lithium-ion cell production plant in North America — provides geographic alignment with the largest single manufacturing scrap feedstock source.

### 7.1.2 Charleston, South Carolina — 600-Acre Greenfield Site

The Berkeley County, South Carolina facility represents Redwood's most significant capital expansion. A 600-acre site is under active development with phased construction targeting initial operations in 2026–2027. Strategic site selection factors include:

- Proximity to southeastern US automotive assembly plants (BMW Spartanburg SC, Mercedes-Benz Vance AL, Hyundai Motor Group Metaplant GA, Volkswagen Chattanooga TN, Volvo Ridgeville SC)
- Deep-water port access for potential international feedstock and product logistics
- Lower electricity costs relative to western US markets
- Access to a skilled chemical process and automotive manufacturing workforce
- South Carolina economic development incentives, including property tax abatements and infrastructure grants

Upon full build-out, the Charleston campus is expected to double Redwood's aggregate processing capacity.

## 7.3 Process Technology

Redwood's manufacturing infrastructure incorporates several proprietary process technologies that differentiate its operations from conventional recycling and precursor production facilities:

**Continuous-Flow Hydrometallurgy:** Conventional battery recyclers employ batch leaching — a sequential process in which discrete batches of black mass are dissolved, filtered, and transferred to downstream unit operations. Redwood has developed and deployed continuous-flow leaching reactors that maintain steady-state dissolution chemistry, achieving higher throughput per unit capital investment and more consistent product quality.

**Integrated Water Treatment:** The hydrometallurgical circuit incorporates zero-liquid-discharge water treatment, recovering process water and reagent chemicals for recirculation. Sodium sulfate byproduct is crystallized and sold as a commodity chemical, eliminating liquid waste discharge permitting requirements.

**Automated Material Handling:** Black mass, metal salt powders, and cathode precursor materials are conveyed through enclosed pneumatic and mechanical systems under controlled humidity (<1% relative humidity for lithium salts). Automated weigh-batching and blending systems ensure stoichiometric precision in precursor synthesis.

**Process Control Architecture:** A plant-wide distributed control system (DCS) provides real-time monitoring and closed-loop control of all critical process parameters. The system is designed for remote operational support, enabling centralized process engineering oversight across both Nevada and South Carolina facilities.

## 7.4 Energy Infrastructure

The Sparks campus electric load at full CAM production capacity is estimated at 150–200 MW, placing it among the highest-load industrial facilities in northern Nevada. Redwood has secured electricity supply agreements incorporating renewable energy procurement targets. The operational Crusoe microgrid (12 MW / 63 MWh) serves as a demonstration of Redwood Energy's capability to provide on-site storage and demand management for its own industrial operations.

## 7.5 Capital Expenditure & Workforce

Aggregate capital expenditure through 2026 is estimated at $2.5–$3.0 billion, inclusive of land acquisition, facility construction, process equipment, and commissioning. The DOE ATVM loan commitment of $2 billion provides non-dilutive capital specifically designated for facility expansion. The workforce of approximately 1,350 personnel reflects a deliberate emphasis on direct manufacturing labor, process engineering, and quality assurance staffing over administrative headcount.
