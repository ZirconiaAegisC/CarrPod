## 6.1 Partnership Architecture

Redwood's partnership strategy is organized around a "full lifecycle" model in which the company serves as the exclusive or primary materials steward for an automotive OEM's battery supply chain across three phases: (1) manufacturing scrap reclamation at the gigafactory, (2) end-of-life pack collection and recycling at vehicle decommissioning, and (3) second-life energy storage redeployment where battery health permits. This model creates multi-decade revenue visibility, as batteries manufactured today are expected to return to Redwood for recycling in 12–18 years.

## 6.2 General Motors — Flagship Partnership

The GM partnership, announced in 2023 and expanded progressively, represents the most comprehensive OEM lifecycle agreement in the battery industry. Key quantitative metrics:

- **28,000 metric tons** of manufacturing scrap and end-of-life battery material processed to date
- **10,000 battery packs** refurbished and redeployed into stationary storage applications
- **1.5 MW / 7.2 MWh** second-life storage system deployed at a GM assembly plant, generating **$3 million in annual savings**
- Full lifecycle management covering Ultium Cells LLC (GM-LG Energy Solution joint venture) manufacturing scrap from Lordstown OH, Spring Hill TN, and Lansing MI facilities

The GM agreement serves as the commercial template for all subsequent OEM partnerships.

## 6.4 Manufacturing Scrap Management

Manufacturing scrap — defective cells, electrode offcuts, formation rejects — typically represents 5–15% of gigafactory output during production ramp phases. For a 50 GWh facility, this equates to 2.5–7.5 GWh annually of material that must be either landfilled or recycled. Redwood provides dedicated on-site or near-site collection and processing for manufacturing partners, converting what would otherwise be a hazardous waste disposal cost into a materials recovery revenue stream.

## 6.5 End-of-Life Collection Network

Redwood has established a nationwide collection network encompassing:

- **Automotive dismantlers:** Agreements with major vehicle dismantling and salvage operators for EV battery pack extraction
- **Dealership returns:** Warranty-return and collision-damage pack collection through OEM dealer networks
- **Consumer electronics:** Partnerships with retailers (Best Buy, Amazon) and municipal waste authorities for portable battery collection
- **Micromobility:** Lyft partnership provides dedicated collection for e-bike and e-scooter batteries, a high-volume, rapidly growing segment

The network covers all 50 US states and processes batteries from over 300 collection nodes.

## 6.6 Strategic Implications

The partnership portfolio creates structural barriers to competitive entry. OEMs that have integrated Redwood into their battery supply chain planning — including material specifications, logistics routing, and IRA compliance documentation — face material switching costs to alternative recyclers. The multi-year, often exclusive, nature of these agreements provides Redwood with a contracted feedstock pipeline that competitors cannot replicate absent equivalent processing capacity, which in turn requires contracted feedstock to justify capital investment — a circular dependency that advantages the first mover.
