## 14.1 Battery Chemistry & Materials

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Black Mass** | The cathode-rich powder produced by mechanical shredding and separation of lithium-ion batteries, containing lithium, nickel, cobalt, and manganese in metal oxide form. The primary feedstock for hydrometallurgical refining. |
| **CAM (Cathode Active Material)** | The electrochemically active component of a lithium-ion battery cathode; typically a lithium metal oxide such as NCM (lithium nickel cobalt manganese oxide) or NCA (lithium nickel cobalt aluminum oxide). |
| **CSTR (Continuous Stirred-Tank Reactor)** | A reactor design in which reactants are continuously fed and product is continuously withdrawn, maintaining steady-state conditions. Used in Redwood's precursor co-precipitation process. |
| **DLE (Direct Lithium Extraction)** | A process for extracting lithium from brines or process solutions using selective adsorption media, solvent extraction, or ion exchange, avoiding the long-duration evaporation pond method. |
| **Electrolyte** | The lithium salt solution (typically LiPF₆ in organic carbonate solvents) that conducts lithium ions between cathode and anode during battery charge and discharge. |
| **Hydrometallurgy** | The use of aqueous chemistry for the recovery of metals from ores, concentrates, or recycled materials. Central to Redwood's refining process. |
| **NCM 523 / 622 / 811** | Nickel-Cobalt-Manganese cathode chemistry designations indicating the atomic ratio of the three metals (e.g., NCM 811 = 80% nickel, 10% cobalt, 10% manganese). Higher nickel content increases energy density; higher cobalt content improves stability. |
| **NCA** | Lithium Nickel Cobalt Aluminum Oxide cathode chemistry. |
| **Precursor (pCAM)** | The mixed metal hydroxide intermediate produced prior to lithiation and calcination in cathode active material production. |
| **Pyrometallurgy** | High-temperature smelting process for metal recovery. Less selective and more energy-intensive than hydrometallurgy; used by some competitors but not Redwood's primary process. |
| **Solvent Extraction (SX)** | A chemical separation process in which metal ions are selectively transferred from an aqueous solution to an organic solvent phase, enabling separation of individual metals. |

## 14.2 Process & Manufacturing

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Calcination** | Thermal treatment of precursor powder blended with lithium source at controlled temperature and atmosphere to produce cathode active material with the desired crystal structure. |
| **DCS (Distributed Control System)** | A computerized control system for a manufacturing process in which controller elements are distributed throughout the plant. Redwood's DCS provides centralized monitoring and control across unit operations. |
| **Gigafactory** | A term popularized by Tesla referring to a large-scale battery cell manufacturing facility, typically with annual production capacity measured in gigawatt-hours (GWh). |
| **GWh (Gigawatt-Hour)** | One billion watt-hours. A unit of energy capacity commonly used to measure battery production, processing, and storage. 20 GWh approximates the battery capacity of 250,000 electric vehicles. |
| **Nameplate Capacity** | The designed maximum production rate of a manufacturing facility, typically expressed as an annual volume. |
| **ZLD (Zero Liquid Discharge)** | A water treatment approach in which all wastewater is purified and recycled, leaving no liquid effluent discharge. Redwood's hydrometallurgical circuit incorporates ZLD. |

## 14.3 Financial & Commercial

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **ATVM (Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing) Loan Program** | A US Department of Energy direct loan program providing financing for manufacturing facilities producing advanced technology vehicles and qualifying components. |
| **Conditional Commitment** | A preliminary approval by the DOE Loans Program Office indicating intent to provide a loan, subject to satisfaction of conditions precedent before final loan documents are executed and funds disbursed. |
| **EaaS (Energy-as-a-Service)** | A business model in which the energy asset owner retains ownership and sells energy output and services to the customer under a long-term contract, eliminating upfront customer capital expenditure. |
| **ITC (Investment Tax Credit)** | A federal tax credit that reduces the income tax liability of the owner of qualifying energy property. Under the IRA, standalone energy storage projects qualify for a 30% ITC. |
| **Non-Dilutive Capital** | Financing that does not require the issuance of equity or ownership stakes. Examples include debt, grants, and tax credits. |
| **Offtake Agreement** | A contract between a producer and a buyer in which the buyer commits to purchase a specified volume of future production, typically at a formula-based price. |
| **PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)** | A long-term contract under which a power producer sells electricity to a customer at an agreed price, providing revenue certainty for the producer and price certainty for the customer. |
| **Section 45X (IRA)** | Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit providing a per-unit tax credit for domestically produced battery components and critical minerals. |
| **Section 30D (IRA)** | Clean Vehicle Credit providing up to $7,500 per qualifying EV, contingent on meeting domestic battery component and critical mineral content thresholds. |

## 14.4 Energy Storage

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Ancillary Services** | Grid services that support the reliable operation of the transmission system, including frequency regulation, voltage support, and operating reserves. |
| **BMS (Battery Management System)** | The electronic system that monitors and controls battery operation, including state-of-charge estimation, cell balancing, temperature management, and safety cutoff functions. |
| **Demand Charge** | A component of commercial and industrial electricity billing based on the customer's maximum power draw during a billing period. Energy storage can reduce demand charges through peak shaving. |
| **Energy Arbitrage** | The practice of charging a battery when electricity prices are low and discharging when prices are high, capturing the price spread. |
| **Peak Shaving** | The use of energy storage to reduce a facility's maximum grid power draw, thereby reducing demand charges. |
| **Second-Life Battery** | An electric vehicle battery pack that has degraded below the threshold for automotive use (typically 70–80% of original capacity) but retains sufficient capacity for less demanding stationary storage applications. |
| **SOH (State of Health)** | The ratio of a battery's current maximum capacity to its original rated capacity, expressed as a percentage. The primary metric for determining second-life suitability. |
| **UL 9540A** | A test standard for evaluating thermal runaway fire propagation in battery energy storage systems, required for permitting and insurance purposes. |

## 14.5 Regulatory & Policy

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **FTA Country** | A country with which the United States has a free trade agreement in effect. IRA Section 30D requires that an increasing percentage of battery critical minerals be extracted or processed in the US or an FTA country for a vehicle to qualify for the consumer EV tax credit. |
| **NEPA (National Environmental Policy Act)** | Federal law requiring environmental review of major federal actions, including DOE loan commitments and disbursements. |
| **Section 301 Tariffs** | Tariffs imposed under Section 301 of the Trade Act of 1974 on Chinese imports, including certain battery materials and components, following a finding of unfair trade practices. |
