## Overview

Redwood Materials represents the singular operational force restoring domestic sovereignty over the lithium-ion battery supply chain. Founded in 2017 by JB Straubel, former Tesla CTO and co-founder, the company has engineered a closed-loop ecosystem encompassing end-of-life battery collection, hydrometallurgical recycling, cathode and anode precursor production, and utility-scale second-life energy storage. No other North American entity operates at comparable scale across all four segments simultaneously.

The company processes in excess of 20 GWh of end-of-life batteries annually — equivalent to approximately 250,000 electric vehicles — and recovers over 70% of all decommissioned battery packs within the United States. Material recovery rates exceed 95% for lithium, nickel, cobalt, and copper, positioning Redwood as the definitive counterweight to Chinese dominance over critical mineral processing, which currently controls 97% of global lithium and cobalt refining capacity.

## Strategic Significance

The United States faces a structural vulnerability in its energy transition. Domestic production of lithium, cobalt, nickel, and graphite cathode precursors remains negligible relative to demand projections. Redwood addresses this gap through three integrated business lines: battery recycling and materials recovery, cathode and anode active material production (targeting 100 GWh annual capacity by end of 2025, scaling to 500 GWh by 2030), and Redwood Energy, a grid-scale storage division launched June 2025 that repurposes degraded EV batteries into stationary storage assets.

## Financial Position

Private capital raised totals $2.3 billion across multiple rounds, most recently a $425 million Series E closed January 2026 with participation from Eclipse, Nvidia NVentures, Google/Alphabet, Capricorn Investment Group, and Goldman Sachs Asset Management. A $2 billion conditional commitment from the Department of Energy's Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing Loan Program provides additional non-dilutive capital. Revenue reached $200 million in 2024. The company employs approximately 1,350 personnel following strategic restructuring actions, maintaining a reported valuation exceeding $6 billion.

## Operational Footprint

Primary operations are anchored at the 175-acre Sparks, Nevada campus, with a 600-acre greenfield facility under active development in Charleston, South Carolina. The company has forged full-lifecycle partnerships with General Motors, Panasonic, Toyota, Ford, Volkswagen, BMW, Volvo, Rivian, Lyft, and Ultium Cells. The July 2025 commissioning of a 12 MW / 63 MWh repurposed-battery microgrid — the largest of its kind in North America — demonstrated profitable deployment within four months of construction.

## Competitive Context

The abrupt collapse of Ascend Elements into bankruptcy proceedings in April 2026 has further concentrated the domestic battery recycling landscape, elevating Redwood's position as the sole vertically integrated operator at commercial scale. Strategic hires including former Tesla CFO Deepak Ahuja signal advanced preparation for public market access.
