## 2.1 Founding & Leadership

Redwood Materials was incorporated in 2017 in Carson City, Nevada by Jeffrey Brian "JB" Straubel. Straubel served as Chief Technology Officer of Tesla, Inc. from 2004 to 2019 and is widely credited as the principal architect of Tesla's battery technology roadmap, including the design of the 18650, 2170, and 4680 cell form-factor specifications. His departure from Tesla to focus full-time on Redwood represented a deliberate strategic pivot from cell design to materials-level supply chain reengineering.

## 2.2 Corporate Structure & Governance

Redwood Materials, Inc. is a Delaware C-corporation headquartered in Carson City, Nevada. The company operates through wholly owned subsidiaries holding title to its real property assets in Storey County, Nevada and Berkeley County, South Carolina. The capital structure reflects multiple preferred share classes issued across Series A through E rounds; lead institutional investors include funds managed by Goldman Sachs Asset Management, Capricorn Investment Group, and T. Rowe Price.

## 2.3 Mission & Strategic Mandate

The corporate mission — "to create a circular supply chain for lithium-ion batteries" — translates into three operational mandates:

1. **Reclamation:** Recover and process end-of-life lithium-ion batteries and manufacturing scrap at scale, exceeding 95% material recovery rates for critical metals.
2. **Remanufacture:** Produce battery-grade cathode active material (CAM) and anode copper foil from recycled feedstock, achieving cost parity with or advantage over virgin-mined equivalents.
3. **Redeployment:** Extend battery asset lifetimes through second-life stationary storage applications, displacing new-build lithium extraction demand.

## 2.5 Workforce & Culture

Headcount stands at approximately 1,350 full-time employees across Nevada and South Carolina campuses following a 10% reduction-in-force executed in April 2026. The reduction, preceded by a 5% restructuring in November 2025, reflects a deliberate resource reallocation from early-stage R&D toward commercial-scale production engineering and energy storage deployment. The engineering workforce is concentrated in chemical engineering, metallurgy, process engineering, and power systems integration disciplines.

## 2.6 Intellectual Property Portfolio

Redwood maintains a proprietary technology stack spanning mechanical size-reduction, hydrometallurgical leaching, solvent extraction, and direct precursor synthesis. Key trade-secret-protected innovations include continuous-flow leaching reactor design, mixed-hydroxide precipitation parameter optimization, and lithium-specific solvent extraction chemistry. The company does not publicly disclose its patent portfolio in detail; competitive advantage is primarily secured through process know-how, trade secrets, and operational scale rather than patent exclusivity.

## 2.7 Competitive Positioning

As of mid-2026, Redwood is the only vertically integrated battery recycling and cathode materials manufacturer operating at commercial scale in North America. The April 2026 bankruptcy of Ascend Elements, previously the second-largest domestic recycler, substantially consolidated the competitive landscape. Chinese incumbents — CATL subsidiary Brunp, GEM Co., and Huayou Cobalt — dominate global recycling and precursor production but face escalating geopolitical and tariff barriers to US market entry. European competitors (Northvolt/Revolt, Li-Cycle) operate at materially smaller scale.
