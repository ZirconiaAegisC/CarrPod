## 8.1 Revenue

Redwood Materials reported revenue of $200 million for the fiscal year 2024, representing the first publicly disclosed revenue figure. The company does not publish detailed financial statements as a privately held entity; revenue is understood to be derived from three principal lines of business:

1. **Recycled Material Sales:** Battery-grade metal sulfates (nickel, cobalt, manganese), lithium carbonate, lithium hydroxide, and copper foil sold to cell manufacturers and cathode producers.
2. **Processing & Tipping Fees:** Fees charged to OEM partners and electronics manufacturers for battery collection, logistics, and processing services.
3. **Redwood Energy Storage Revenue:** Power purchase agreement and grid services revenue from deployed second-life storage assets (growing contribution from June 2025 onward).

Revenue growth is projected to accelerate as cathode active material production reaches nameplate capacity at the Sparks facility and as the Charleston campus initiates operations. Industry consensus estimates suggest a $500 million–$1 billion revenue range by 2027, contingent on CAM production ramp and commodity pricing for nickel, cobalt, and lithium.

## 8.4 Growth Trajectory

The revenue growth thesis rests on three sequential catalysts:

**Phase 1 (2024–2026):** Scrap and EOL processing revenue. Established, recurring revenue from contracted OEM manufacturing scrap volumes. Modest margin profile due to ongoing capital investment and operational scaling.

**Phase 2 (2025–2028):** CAM and anode foil production at scale. The transition from toll-processing of black mass to the sale of finished battery materials represents a step-change in revenue per unit of input material. CAM sells at $12–$25 per kilogram, compared to black mass at $2–$6 per kilogram of contained metal value.

**Phase 3 (2026–2030):** Redwood Energy storage deployments at scale. The recurring revenue model — 10–20 year power purchase agreements with creditworthy commercial and industrial offtakers — provides revenue visibility and asset-level financing opportunities distinct from the working-capital-intensive materials business.

## 8.5 Market Context

Global demand for lithium-ion batteries is projected to exceed 5,000 GWh annually by 2030, up from approximately 700 GWh in 2024. The US Inflation Reduction Act's Section 45X production tax credit provides $35 per kWh for domestic battery cell production and 10% of production cost for electrode active materials manufactured in the United States. The combined effect of demand growth and domestic manufacturing incentives creates a demand floor for Redwood's materials output that exceeds projected domestic supply through at least 2035, per Department of Energy modeling.
