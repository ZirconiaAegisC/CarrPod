# Sublime Systems — Market Impact: The Global Cement Industry

## Cement Market Overview

The global cement market produced approximately 4.1 billion tonnes of cement in 2024, with a market value of approximately $350–400 billion at average realized prices. Cement is the second-most consumed substance on Earth after water and the essential binding agent in concrete — the world's most used construction material.

China's dominance of global cement production is a critical factor in cement decarbonization: approximately half of global cement emissions originate in China, and any technology aiming for gigatonne-scale emissions reduction must ultimately be deployable in the Chinese market.

### Emissions Intensity

The average CO₂ intensity of global cement production is approximately 600–650 kg CO₂ per tonne of cement, with a range of approximately 400–900 kg CO₂/t depending on plant efficiency, fuel mix, and clinker substitution rates. Process emissions (from limestone decomposition) account for approximately 60% of the total, with thermal emissions (from fuel combustion) accounting for the remaining 40%.

### Demand Drivers and Projections

Cement demand is projected to grow at approximately 1–2% annually through 2050, driven by:
- Urbanization in developing economies (India, Africa, Southeast Asia)
- Infrastructure investment (transportation, water, energy)
- Housing demand from growing populations
- Renovation and retrofitting of aging infrastructure in developed economies

This growth trajectory means that even if all existing cement plants were retrofitted with best-available technology, new capacity additions would be required, creating an opportunity for low-carbon technologies to capture greenfield deployment.

## Sublime's Addressable Market

### Segment 1: Premium Low-Carbon Cement

The most immediate addressable market for Sublime cement is the premium low-carbon segment, where buyers are willing to pay a price premium for verified low-embodied-carbon materials. This segment includes:

- **Green building projects**: LEED v4.1 and BREEAM certifications award credits for low-embodied-carbon materials, creating demand pull
- **Corporate sustainability commitments**: Technology companies (Microsoft, Google, Amazon), financial institutions, and consumer goods companies with net-zero commitments are specifying low-carbon concrete for their construction projects
- **Government procurement**: Federal, state, and local Buy Clean policies create guaranteed demand for low-carbon construction materials
- **Infrastructure projects with climate mandates**: Transportation and water infrastructure projects increasingly include embodied carbon limits in procurement specifications

The premium low-carbon cement market is estimated at 10–20 million tonnes annually in 2026, growing to 50–100 million tonnes by 2035 as building codes and procurement standards evolve.

### Segment 2: Carbon-Compliance Markets

As carbon pricing expands, cement produced via conventional kilns becomes increasingly expensive. In markets with carbon prices of $75–150 per tonne of CO₂ (EU ETS trajectory, potential US carbon pricing), the embedded carbon cost in conventional cement is $45–100 per tonne — creating a cost umbrella under which low-carbon alternatives can compete without needing a "green premium."

### Segment 3: Developing Economy Greenfield Deployment

The largest long-term opportunity is in greenfield cement capacity additions in developing economies. India, Africa, and Southeast Asia will require substantial new cement capacity over the coming decades. Deploying Sublime's technology for greenfield plants avoids locking in decades of high-emissions kiln-based production and may be cost-competitive where low-cost renewable electricity is available.

## Competitive Landscape

### Conventional Cement with Carbon Capture

Carbon capture on cement kilns is the incumbent industry approach to decarbonization:
- **Strengths**: Retrofit existing assets, familiar technology (amine scrubbing, calcium looping, oxyfuel), strong industry support
- **Weaknesses**: Captures only 60–90% of process emissions, high energy penalty (2–4 GJ/t CO₂), requires CO₂ transport and storage infrastructure, does not eliminate upstream emissions from quarrying and fuel supply
- **Cost**: $60–120 per tonne of CO₂ captured, equivalent to $40–80 per tonne of cement

### Alternative Binders (Geopolymers, Alkali-Activated Materials)

Geopolymer and alkali-activated binders replace Portland cement with industrial wastes (fly ash, slag) activated by alkaline solutions:
- **Strengths**: Very low embodied CO₂ (80–90% reduction possible), use of waste materials, established technology in niche applications
- **Weaknesses**: Dependent on declining supplies of coal fly ash and blast furnace slag (both are byproducts of industries that are themselves decarbonizing), variable composition of waste feedstocks, performance challenges (setting time, workability, durability)
- **Market penetration**: Limited to specialty applications; unlikely to address more than 5–10% of cement demand

### Limestone Calcined Clay Cement (LC3)

LC3 replaces a portion of clinker with calcined clay and limestone:
- **Strengths**: Uses abundant raw materials (clay, limestone), 30–40% CO₂ reduction, works within existing cement plant infrastructure, growing adoption in developing countries
- **Weaknesses**: Still requires clinker production in a kiln, so cannot achieve >40–50% emissions reduction; clay calcination adds process complexity
- **Market penetration**: Fastest-growing low-carbon cement technology; being adopted by major producers in India, Latin America, and Africa

## Regulatory Tailwinds

### EU Emissions Trading System and CBAM
As with steel, the EU's carbon pricing framework creates direct financial incentives for low-carbon cement:
- Free allowance phase-out reduces the implicit subsidy for conventional cement production
- CBAM charges on imported cement equalize carbon costs between domestic and imported products
- At €100/tCO₂, the carbon cost on conventional cement (approximately 0.6 tCO₂/t cement) is €60/t — a substantial fraction of the typical selling price of €80–120/t

### U.S. Federal and State Buy Clean
Federal and state procurement policies increasingly mandate or incentivize low-embodied-carbon construction materials, directly benefiting Sublime's cement product.

### Green Building Standards
LEED v4.1, BREEAM, and other green building standards increasingly incorporate embodied carbon criteria, creating market pull for verified low-carbon cement products.
