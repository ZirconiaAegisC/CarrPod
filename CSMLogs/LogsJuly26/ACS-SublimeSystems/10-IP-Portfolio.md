# Sublime Systems — Intellectual Property Portfolio Assessment

## Patent Strategy Overview

Sublime Systems' intellectual property strategy protects its electrochemical cement production technology through a portfolio of patents covering process chemistry, electrolyzer design, membrane configurations, electrolyte compositions, and product formulations. The portfolio is built on foundational research conducted at MIT and subsequent development by the Sublime technical team.

## Foundational MIT Patents

The core patent family was developed in Professor Yet-Ming Chiang's laboratory at MIT and is exclusively licensed to Sublime Systems:

**Electrochemical Hydroxide Systems and Methods for Cement Production** (US Patent 11,286,190 and related family members): Covers the fundamental electrochemical pH-swing process for producing calcium silicate cements. Key claims include:

- The method of generating acidity and basicity in separate electrochemical compartments to dissolve calcium-bearing minerals and precipitate calcium silicates
- Cell configurations with cation-exchange membranes for calcium ion transport
- Electrolyte compositions for efficient calcium dissolution and precipitation
- Process conditions (pH ranges, current densities, temperatures) for cement-quality calcium silicate production

This foundational patent family has priority dates in the 2019–2020 timeframe, establishing early priority in the electrochemical cement field.

## Electrolyzer Design Patents

Subsequent patent filings cover specific electrolyzer configurations optimized for cement production:

**Membrane configurations**: Patents covering two-compartment (CEM-separated), three-compartment (CEM and AEM), and bipolar membrane configurations for the pH-swing process. Each configuration has different trade-offs in terms of energy efficiency, calcium transport selectivity, and system complexity.

**Electrode architectures**: Patents covering electrode designs optimized for the specific requirements of calcium dissolution (acidic, oxidizing environment at anode) and calcium silicate precipitation (basic, hydrogen-evolving environment at cathode).

**Flow field designs**: Patents covering electrolyte flow configurations (flow-through vs. flow-by, serpentine vs. interdigitated flow fields) that optimize mass transport while minimizing pressure drop and pumping energy.

**Stack assembly**: Patents covering methods for assembling multiple electrolyzer cells into stacks, including sealing, electrical interconnection, and electrolyte distribution manifold designs.

## Process Chemistry Patents

**Electrolyte compositions**: Patents covering specific electrolyte formulations optimized for:
- Calcium ion conductivity and transport
- Membrane compatibility and lifetime
- Electrode reaction kinetics
- Calcium silicate precipitation morphology control

**Feedstock processing**: Patents covering methods for preparing calcium-bearing feedstocks, including:
- Grinding and classification to optimize dissolution kinetics
- Pre-treatment to enhance reactivity of silicate feedstocks
- Impurity management in waste-derived feedstocks

**Precipitation control**: Patents covering methods for controlling calcium silicate hydrate precipitation, including:
- pH, temperature, and residence time control for target Ca/Si ratio
- Seeding strategies for controlled particle size distribution
- Additives to modify product morphology and reactivity

## Product Formulation Patents

**Cement blending**: Patents covering formulations that blend Sublime's electrochemically produced calcium silicates with:
- Supplementary cementitious materials (fly ash, slag, calcined clay)
- Limestone (producing blended cements comparable to ASTM C595 Type IL)
- Chemical admixtures for specific performance characteristics

**Concrete mix designs**: Patents covering optimized concrete mix designs using Sublime cement, including admixture compatibility and performance in specific applications (ready-mix, precast, high-performance concrete).

## Freedom to Operate

Sublime's FTO position is strengthened by:
- Early priority dates (2019–2020) on foundational electrochemical cement patents
- MIT's extensive prior art in electrochemical materials processing
- Operation in a relatively new field where the patent landscape is not yet densely populated, unlike mature technologies such as Portland cement kilns or PEM water electrolyzers

## Competitive IP Landscape

The electrochemical cement patent landscape has grown rapidly since 2020, with filings from:
- Academic institutions (MIT, Caltech, Northwestern, ETH Zurich)
- Startups (Sublime, Brimstone, Fortera)
- Established cement companies (Holcim, Heidelberg Materials, CRH)
- Electrolyzer manufacturers exploring new applications beyond hydrogen

Sublime's early priority dates and focused portfolio provide a defensible position, but the expanding landscape means that freedom-to-operate analyses must be continuously updated as new patents are published and granted.

## Trade Secrets

Beyond the patent portfolio, Sublime protects certain aspects of its technology as trade secrets:
- Specific electrolyte compositions (patent filings describe ranges but may not disclose the optimal composition)
- Electrode catalyst formulations and manufacturing methods
- Membrane treatment and conditioning procedures
- Process control algorithms and operating protocols
- Quality control testing methods and specifications

The trade secret strategy follows the well-established practice in electrochemical industries (chlor-alkali, aluminum smelting, PEM electrolysis) of patenting core inventions while keeping operational know-how confidential.

## IP Licensing and Enforcement

The IP portfolio supports Sublime's technology licensing business model:
- Licensees receive access to the patent portfolio and technical know-how
- Licensing agreements include grant-back provisions for improvements developed by licensees
- Electrolyzer component supply (membranes, electrodes) provides a recurring revenue stream and IP enforcement mechanism
- Trade secrets are protected through confidentiality agreements, limited access to sensitive technical information, and operational security protocols
