# Global Market Expansion Strategy

## Strategic Framework

Energy Vault's global expansion strategy is organized around a hub-and-spoke model: establish an anchor reference project with a creditworthy utility or government offtaker in each target region, then scale through replication, asset acquisition, and partnership frameworks. The $1.35 billion contracted backlog is geographically diversified across six continents, with concentration risk managed through deliberate counterparty and regulatory-regime diversification.

## Market-by-Market Deployment

### South Africa and SADC Region

The Eskom partnership represents Energy Vault's most strategically significant market entry.

**Anchor Project**: EVx 2.0™ GESS — 25 MW / 100 MWh at an Eskom generation site.

**Framework Agreement**: Up to 4 GW / 4 GWh across the Southern African Development Community (SADC), encompassing South Africa, Namibia, Botswana, Zambia, and Zimbabwe.

**Structural Rationale**:

- South Africa's grid experiences chronic load-shedding, with capacity shortfalls of 4–6 GW during peak demand periods.
- The Integrated Resource Plan (IRP 2023) calls for 9.4 GW of new storage capacity by 2032.
- Eskom's transmission infrastructure provides the interconnection backbone for storage co-location at existing generation sites.
- G-VAULT's 35+ year asset life aligns with Eskom's long-duration infrastructure planning horizon.
- Local block manufacturing creates direct employment and industrial development benefits, supporting political and regulatory support.

**Phased Rollout**:

| Phase | Capacity     | Timeline         | Scope                                         |
|-------|-------------|------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| 1     | 25 MW / 100 MWh | H2 2026–2027  | Eskom anchor site; technology validation      |
| 2     | 500 MW       | 2027–2029        | Additional Eskom sites; first private offtakers |
| 3     | 4 GW         | 2029–2035        | Full SADC regional deployment                 |

### Japan

The BayWa r.e. Japan acquisition provides Energy Vault with an 850 MW renewable-and-storage development pipeline in one of the world's most attractive storage markets.

**Market Drivers**:

- Japan's feed-in-tariff (FIT) regime for solar is rolling off, creating a massive opportunity for storage retrofits behind existing PV assets.
- The Japanese government's Green Transformation (GX) policy targets 36–38% renewable penetration by 2030, requiring significant storage capacity.
- Japan's island geography creates natural capacity islands where grid stability requires fast-responding storage.
- Capacity market and ancillary service market reforms (2024–2026) are establishing transparent revenue frameworks for storage.

**Pipeline Composition** (est.):

| Technology      | Capacity       | Status                  |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| BESS (standalone) | ~500 MW       | Development / permitting|
| Solar + BESS    | ~250 MW (solar); ~100 MW (BESS) | Development |
| G-VAULT (target)| 100–200 MW     | Pre-development          |

### Australia (National Electricity Market)

Australia represents Energy Vault's most advanced deployment theater outside the United States, with two anchor projects in late-stage development and a growing pipeline.

**Stoney Creek Energy Hub**:

- Technology: G-VAULT EVx 2.0™
- Capacity: 125 MW / 1,000 MWh (8-hour duration)
- Offtake: A$310 million, 14-year Long-Term Energy Service Agreement (LTESA)
- COD Target: 2028
- Strategic Significance: First grid-scale gravity storage in the NEM; technology validation for the Australian market

**EBOR Energy Storage**:

- Technology: B-VAULT BESS
- Capacity: 100 MW / 870 MWh
- COD Target: 2027
- Market: NEM frequency control ancillary services (FCAS) + energy arbitrage

**Australian Market Fundamentals**:

- NEM has the world's highest penetration of variable renewable energy (~35% in 2024, targeting 82% by 2030).
- Coal retirements (Eraring, Liddell, Yallourn) are removing 10+ GW of dispatchable capacity by 2035.
- The Capacity Investment Scheme (CIS) underwrites revenue for new dispatchable capacity.
- The LTESA mechanism provides revenue certainty with government-backed offtaker credit.

### European Union

The EU Green Energy partnership provides a framework agreement for 1.8 GWh of B-VAULT deployment over a four-year program.

**Market Drivers**:

- EU Energy Storage Target: 200+ GW by 2030 (European Commission recommendation).
- REPowerEU mandates accelerated renewable deployment, creating storage demand.
- National capacity market mechanisms (Italy, UK, Belgium, Poland) with explicit storage eligibility.
- Grid frequency stability requirements increasing with synchronous generator retirements.

### United States (Domestic Market)

Energy Vault's US operations remain the primary near-term revenue driver, anchored by Calistoga, Cross Trails, and the development pipeline in CAISO and ERCOT.

**Key US Market Drivers**:

- IRA investment tax credit (ITC): 30% base, with potential adders for domestic content (+10%), energy community (+10%), and low-income (+10–20%), yielding 40–70% effective ITC.
- Production tax credit (PTC) option under §45Y for storage charged by renewable energy.
- State-level storage mandates: California (19.5 GW by 2035), New York (6 GW by 2030), Massachusetts, New Jersey, and others.
- FERC Order 2222 enabling aggregated distributed storage participation in wholesale markets.

## Partnership and M&A Strategy

Energy Vault's international expansion is executed through a combination of organic development and targeted acquisitions. The BayWa r.e. Japan transaction exemplifies the acquisition model: purchasing a de-risked development pipeline with local permitting, land control, and interconnection studies already in place, then layering Energy Vault's technology and structuring expertise to accelerate financial close.

The partnership model (Eskom, EU Green Energy, Crusoe) enables market entry without the capital intensity of fully-owned local subsidiaries. Energy Vault typically retains technology supply, O&M, and EMS licensing revenue, while local partners manage development, permitting, and community relations.
