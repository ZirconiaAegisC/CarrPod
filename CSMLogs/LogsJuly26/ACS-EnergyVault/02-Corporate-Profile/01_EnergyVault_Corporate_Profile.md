## Corporate Strategy

Energy Vault's corporate strategy is organized around five interconnected pillars:

1. **Asset Vault Own & Operate** — Shift capital expenditure from Energy Vault's balance sheet to a co-investment fund while retaining recurring management fees, operations and maintenance (O&M) revenue, and asset-level equity returns.

2. **G-VAULT Gravity Storage** — Proprietary long-duration energy storage (LDES) technology with multi-decade asset life and zero chemical degradation. Targeting the 8–24 hour discharge duration segment where lithium-ion economics degrade.

3. **B-VAULT Battery Systems** — Integrated BESS solutions spanning 1–8 hour durations. Leverages third-party cell supply with proprietary integration, controls, and VaultOS EMS.

4. **AI Digital Infrastructure** — Convergence of 24/7 carbon-free energy for hyperscale data centers. Partnership-based model (Crusoe Energy Systems, Peak Energy) to capture behind-the-meter and front-of-meter opportunities.

5. **Global Market Expansion** — Strategic market entry in South Africa (Eskom), Japan (BayWa r.e.), Australia (NEM), Europe (EU Green Energy), and the Middle East.

## Governance and Board

Energy Vault maintains a majority-independent Board of Directors with expertise spanning utility infrastructure, structured finance, IP law, and global energy markets. The company has adopted standard NYSE governance practices including separate Chairman and CEO roles, fully independent Audit and Compensation Committees, and annual say-on-pay advisory votes.

## Corporate Development Posture

The company maintains a disciplined M&A strategy focused on accretive pipeline acquisitions (exemplified by the BayWa r.e. Japan acquisition), technology bolt-ons that extend the VaultOS EMS platform, and structured partnership models rather than capital-intensive organic expansion. The $150 million convertible notes issuance (February 2026, 4.5% coupon, 5-year maturity, $2.50 strike) provides balance-sheet flexibility for strategic initiatives including the $75–100 million internal systems integration program.

## Intellectual Property

Energy Vault maintains a global patent portfolio exceeding 200 granted and pending patents covering gravity storage mechanical systems, composite block manufacturing, crane control software, hybrid gravity-BESS system architectures, and VaultOS EMS dispatch optimization algorithms. The IP estate is concentrated in the United States, European Union, Australia, China, Japan, and South Africa — mirroring the company's commercial deployment footprint.
