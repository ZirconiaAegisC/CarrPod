# Glossary of Terms — Energy Vault Dossier

## A

**AC-Coupled**: System architecture where battery storage connects to the AC grid via its own inverter, independent of generation assets. Contrasts with DC-coupling.

**Adjusted EBITDA**: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization, adjusted for non-recurring, non-cash, or non-operational items including stock-based compensation, asset impairments, and restructuring charges.

**Ancillary Services**: Services necessary to support the transmission of electric power from generator to consumer, maintaining reliable grid operations. Includes frequency regulation, voltage support, spinning reserves, and non-spinning reserves.

**Asset Vault**: Energy Vault's co-investment fund platform for owning and operating energy storage infrastructure assets. Structured as a series of closed-end institutional funds.

**Availability Guarantee**: Contractual commitment ensuring an energy storage asset is available for dispatch at a specified percentage of rated capacity over a defined period.

## B

**Balance of Plant (BOP)**: All supporting components and auxiliary systems of a power plant or energy storage facility, excluding the primary generating or storage equipment. Includes transformers, switchgear, civil works, site preparation, and auxiliary power systems.

**Battery Management System (BMS)**: Electronic system managing rechargeable battery cells by monitoring state, calculating secondary data, reporting data, controlling environment, and balancing cells.

**BayWa r.e.**: Renewable energy developer and service provider, subsidiary of BayWa AG. Energy Vault acquired BayWa r.e.'s Japan development pipeline (850 MW) in early 2026.

**BESS**: Battery Energy Storage System. A system using rechargeable batteries to store electrical energy for later discharge to the grid.

**Black Start**: The ability of a generation or storage resource to start up and energize a portion of the grid without relying on external electric power. A critical grid resilience capability.

**B-VAULT**: Energy Vault's integrated battery energy storage system product line, encompassing DC-block and AC-coupled architectures.

## C

**Capacity Market / Resource Adequacy (RA)**: A market mechanism ensuring sufficient generation and storage resources are available to meet peak electricity demand. Resources receive capacity payments in exchange for availability commitments.

**Capped Call Transaction**: An option strategy purchased by convertible note issuers to reduce potential dilution upon conversion, by effectively raising the conversion price for accounting and dilution purposes.

**Commercial Operation Date (COD)**: The date on which a power generation or storage facility is declared commercially operational and capable of delivering energy or services in accordance with contractual specifications.

**Composite Block**: The 30-metric-ton modular mass used in Energy Vault's G-VAULT system, fabricated from locally sourced aggregate material bound with a polymer binder. Serves as the storage medium for gravitational potential energy.

**Convertible Senior Notes**: Debt instruments that can be converted into a predetermined number of the issuer's common shares, typically at the holder's option, under specified conditions. Energy Vault issued $150M of 4.50% convertible notes in February 2026.

**Crusoe Energy Systems**: Energy Vault's partner for AI powered-shell digital infrastructure. Crusoe provides modular, containerized GPU compute solutions for behind-the-meter and co-located deployment.

## D

**DC-Block**: A self-contained, containerized battery storage unit integrating cells, modules, BMS, thermal management, and fire suppression in a single ISO-container form factor.

**DC-Coupled**: System architecture where battery storage shares a common DC bus with generation assets (e.g., solar PV), using a shared inverter.

**De-SPAC**: A transaction in which a private company becomes publicly traded by merging with a Special Purpose Acquisition Company (SPAC). Energy Vault completed its de-SPAC with Novus Capital Corp II in February 2022.

**Duration**: The length of time an energy storage system can discharge at its rated power output. Typically expressed in hours (e.g., "4-hour duration").

## E

**EBOR Energy Storage**: 100 MW / 870 MWh B-VAULT battery project in Australia's National Electricity Market, in late-stage development with a target COD of 2027.

**Energy Arbitrage**: The practice of purchasing and storing electricity during low-price periods and selling it during high-price periods.

**Energy Management System (EMS)**: Software platform controlling, optimizing, and monitoring energy storage dispatch against market signals, offtake obligations, and asset constraints. Energy Vault's EMS is branded as VaultOS™.

**EPC (Engineering, Procurement, and Construction)**: A contracting model where a single contractor is responsible for all design, procurement, and construction activities for a project.

**EVx 2.0™**: Energy Vault's second-generation commercial gravity energy storage system. A modular, building-enclosed design rated at 25 MW / 100 MWh per module, with >80% round-trip efficiency and 35+ year design life.

**Eskom**: South Africa's state-owned electricity public utility. Energy Vault's anchor customer for the EVx 2.0™ reference project (25 MW / 100 MWh Phase 1, up to 4 GW / 4 GWh across SADC).

## F

**Frequency Control Ancillary Services (FCAS)**: Grid services that maintain electrical frequency within acceptable limits (e.g., 50 Hz ±0.15 Hz in Australia). Storage assets provide FCAS through fast-responding charge/discharge modulation.

**Frequency Regulation**: A grid service that continuously adjusts power output to maintain the balance between generation and load, keeping system frequency within prescribed limits.

## G

**G-VAULT**: Energy Vault's gravity-based energy storage system product line. Utilizes the EVx 2.0™ technology to store energy as gravitational potential energy by raising and lowering composite blocks.

**GESS (Gravity Energy Storage System)**: A category of energy storage that stores electrical energy as gravitational potential energy by lifting masses, and recovers it by controlled lowering with regenerative motor-generators.

**Grid-Forming Inverter**: An inverter capable of establishing and maintaining grid voltage and frequency independently, without relying on an external grid reference. Critical for islanded operation and black-start capability.

## I

**Investment Tax Credit (ITC)**: A U.S. federal tax credit for qualifying energy property, including energy storage. Under the IRA, standalone storage qualifies for a 30% base credit with potential adders for domestic content, energy communities, and low-income siting.

**IRA (Inflation Reduction Act of 2022)**: Landmark U.S. legislation providing expanded and extended tax credits for clean energy technologies, including standalone energy storage ITC eligibility.

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return)**: A financial metric representing the annualized effective compounded return rate that sets the net present value of all cash flows to zero. Used as a target return measure for infrastructure investments.

## L

**LCOE (Levelized Cost of Energy)**: The average net present cost of electricity generation for a generating plant over its lifetime, expressed per MWh.

**LCOS (Levelized Cost of Storage)**: The average net present cost of storing and discharging electricity for a storage asset, expressed per MWh discharged.

**LDES (Long-Duration Energy Storage)**: Energy storage systems capable of discharging at rated power for 8 hours or longer. Contrasts with short-duration storage (typically 1–4 hours).

**LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate)**: A lithium-ion battery chemistry using lithium iron phosphate as the cathode material. Offers lower energy density than NMC/NCA chemistries but superior safety, cycle life, and thermal stability. The dominant chemistry for stationary BESS applications.

**Limited Partner (LP)**: An investor in a fund that provides capital but does not participate in day-to-day management. LPs in Energy Vault's Asset Vault funds are institutional infrastructure investors.

**LTESA (Long-Term Energy Service Agreement)**: An Australian government-backed contractual mechanism providing revenue certainty for energy infrastructure projects through an underwriting framework. Energy Vault's Stoney Creek project holds an A$310M, 14-year LTESA.

## M

**MW (Megawatt)**: Unit of power equal to one million watts. In the context of energy storage, typically refers to the rated discharge power capacity of the system.

**MWh (Megawatt-hour)**: Unit of energy equal to one megawatt of power sustained for one hour. Represents the total energy storage capacity of a system.

## N

**NEM (National Electricity Market)**: Australia's wholesale electricity market, interconnecting Queensland, New South Wales, Victoria, South Australia, and Tasmania. Operated by AEMO.

**NTP (Notice to Proceed)**: A formal authorization issued by a project owner to a contractor to commence work under an EPC or construction contract. Triggers the start of the construction schedule.

## O

**O&M (Operations and Maintenance)**: The activities, processes, and costs associated with operating and maintaining a power generation or storage facility over its operational lifetime.

**Offtake Agreement**: A contractual arrangement between an energy project developer/owner and a purchaser (offtaker) for the sale of energy, capacity, or services produced by the facility. Provides revenue certainty.

**OIC (Original Institutional Co-Investors)**: The anchor LP group in Energy Vault's Asset Vault Fund I, representing $300 million in committed capital.

## P

**PCS (Power Conversion System)**: The inverter and transformer system that converts DC power from batteries to AC power for grid delivery, and vice versa for charging.

**Peak Energy**: US-based sodium-ion battery manufacturer. Energy Vault's strategic partner for 1.5 GWh of sodium-ion cell supply, targeting integration into the B-VAULT platform.

**PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)**: A long-term contract between an electricity generator and a purchaser for the sale of electricity and associated attributes.

**PTC (Production Tax Credit)**: A U.S. federal per-kWh tax credit for electricity generated by qualifying renewable energy facilities. Available as an alternative to the ITC under IRA provisions.

**PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: A metric for data center energy efficiency, calculated as total facility energy divided by IT equipment energy. Lower PUE indicates higher efficiency.

## R

**Round-Trip Efficiency (RTE)**: The ratio of energy discharged from a storage system to the energy required to charge it, expressed as a percentage. Accounts for all losses in the charge-store-discharge cycle.

## S

**SADC (Southern African Development Community)**: A regional economic community of 16 member states in Southern Africa. Energy Vault's Eskom framework agreement provides for potential deployment of up to 4 GW / 4 GWh of G-VAULT storage across the SADC region.

**Sodium-Ion Battery**: An emerging battery chemistry using sodium ions as charge carriers instead of lithium. Advantages include abundant raw materials, lower cost, wider operating temperature range, and inherent safety. Energy density is lower than LFP but sufficient for stationary storage applications.

**SOSA Energy Storage**: 150 MW hybrid G-VAULT + B-VAULT project in Texas (ERCOT), in late-stage development.

**Sponsor (Fund Sponsor)**: The entity that forms, manages, and co-invests in an investment fund. Energy Vault Holdings serves as sponsor of the Asset Vault funds.

**Stoney Creek Energy Hub**: 125 MW / 1,000 MWh G-VAULT EVx 2.0™ gravity storage project in Australia, backed by an A$310M, 14-year LTESA.

## T

**Tolling Agreement**: A contract in which the offtaker pays a fixed capacity charge for the right to dispatch a storage asset, plus a variable charge for energy throughput. The offtaker assumes wholesale market price risk.

## V

**VaultOS™**: Energy Vault's proprietary cloud-based Energy Management System. Provides multi-asset optimization, automated market bidding, degradation modeling, and 24/7 carbon matching across G-VAULT, B-VAULT, and hybrid configurations.

**VESDA (Very Early Smoke Detection Apparatus)**: An aspirating smoke detection system providing early warning of fire conditions. Standard equipment in B-VAULT containers.

## W

**WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital)**: The average rate a company is expected to pay to finance its assets, weighted by the proportion of equity and debt in the capital structure. Used as a discount rate for project and investment evaluation.
