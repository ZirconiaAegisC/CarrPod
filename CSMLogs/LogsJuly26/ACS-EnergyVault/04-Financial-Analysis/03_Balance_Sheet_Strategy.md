## $150M Convertible Notes — February 2026

In February 2026, Energy Vault issued $150 million in convertible senior notes, representing the company's most significant capital markets transaction since the 2022 de-SPAC listing.

### Key Terms

| Parameter                  | Detail                                                          |
|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Principal Amount           | $150 million                                                    |
| Coupon                     | 4.50% per annum, payable semi-annually                          |
| Maturity                   | February 2031 (5-year term)                                     |
| Conversion Price           | $2.50 per share (subject to anti-dilution adjustments)          |
| Conversion Premium         | ~25–30% above reference price at pricing                        |
| Use of Proceeds            | $75–100M internal integration; remaining for general corporate  |
| Optional Redemption        | Callable after Year 3 if stock price exceeds 130% of conversion |
| Capped Call                | Purchased capped call transactions to reduce dilution           |

### Strategic Rationale

The convertible notes issuance serves multiple strategic objectives:

1. **Internal Systems Integration ($75–100M)**: Energy Vault has guided $75–100 million in investment for internal systems, process automation, and digital infrastructure to scale operations from current project volumes to the multi-GW annual deployment target. This includes ERP implementation, supply-chain management systems, VaultOS cloud infrastructure scaling, and global project management and controls platforms.

2. **Balance-Sheet Liquidity Buffer**: Post-issuance cash balance of over $100 million provides a multi-year runway for operations, reducing dependence on project-level cash flow for corporate overhead.

3. **Strategic Optionality**: Available capital for bolt-on acquisitions (e.g., BayWa Japan pipeline acquisition, potential technology tuck-ins), co-investment capacity for Asset Vault Fund II, and performance bonding / letters of credit for large-scale project bids.

4. **Cost of Capital Optimization**: At 4.50% coupon, the convertible notes represent the lowest-cost external capital available to Energy Vault at current credit profile, significantly below the cost of equity or traditional corporate debt.

### Dilution Analysis

| Scenario                                    | Fully Diluted Share Impact  |
|---------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Notes held to maturity (no conversion)      | 0 shares                     |
| Full conversion at $2.50                    | +60 million shares           |
| Capped call coverage                        | Reduces effective dilution   |

The capped call transactions offset dilution up to a specified ceiling (typically 75–100% above conversion price), meaning dilution is limited unless the stock price significantly exceeds the conversion price.

## Asset-Light Model Validation

A critical element of Energy Vault's balance-sheet strategy is the verification that the Asset Vault platform genuinely externalizes project capital expenditure. Key indicators:

1. **Project-Level Debt**: All project debt sits at the Asset Vault portfolio company level, non-recourse to Energy Vault Holdings.

2. **LP Equity Commitments**: Asset Vault Fund I's $300 million commitments represent capital that Energy Vault does not need to provide from its own balance sheet (beyond its 10–20% co-investment).

3. **Working Capital Efficiency**: Equipment supply contracts with Asset Vault portfolio companies are structured with milestone payments that align cash inflows from the portfolio company with cash outflows to equipment suppliers, minimizing working-capital drag on Energy Vault Holdings.

4. **Asset Recycling**: The capital recycling model — develop, stabilize, recapitalize / sell, reinvest — ensures that sponsor capital is returned and re-deployed, rather than being permanently tied up in operating assets.

## Financial Covenants and Credit Profile

The convertible notes impose limited financial maintenance covenants (typical for convertible instruments). Key considerations:

- No financial maintenance covenants during the first three years
- Limited incurrence-based covenants for additional indebtedness
- Change-of-control put at 100% of principal plus accrued interest
- Standard reporting covenants (SEC filings, quarterly financials)

Energy Vault does not currently carry a public credit rating. At the guided FY2026 cash balance of $150–200 million and approaching adjusted EBITDA breakeven, the company's credit profile supports continued access to the convertible and project-finance markets without reliance on dilutive equity issuance.

## Summary

Energy Vault's balance sheet has transitioned from a constrained, pre-revenue posture (FY2024: $26M cash) to a well-capitalized growth platform (FY2025: $103.4M cash; FY2026E: $150–200M). The $150 million convertible notes issuance provides patient capital at an attractive cost, while the Asset Vault platform offloads project capital expenditure to third-party investors. The primary balance-sheet priority for FY2026–2027 is the execution of the internal systems integration program and the demonstration of sustained operating cash flow generation, which would validate the asset-light model and position the company for self-funding growth beyond the current capital envelope.
