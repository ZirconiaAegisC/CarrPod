# Asset Vault Economics — Fund-Level and Sponsor-Level Analysis

## Fund I — Structural Overview

Energy Vault's Asset Vault Fund I is the foundational vehicle for the company's own-and-operate strategy. The fund structure and economics are designed to align Energy Vault's interests with limited partners (LPs) while generating durable, multi-layered revenue for the sponsor.

| Parameter                        | Detail                                                             |
|----------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Fund Size                        | $300 million (total committed capital, inclusive of co-investment) |
| Energy Vault Co-Investment       | 10–20% of committed capital (~$30–60M)                            |
| LP Composition                   | Institutional infrastructure investors (pension, insurance, SWF)   |
| Investment Period                | 3–4 years from first close                                         |
| Fund Life                        | 10 years (with two 1-year extension options)                       |
| Target Gross Portfolio IRR       | 12–15% (unlevered, pre-tax, pre-fees)                              |
| Target Net LP IRR                | 10–12% (after fees, carried interest, pre-tax)                     |

## Sponsor Economics — Revenue Layers

Energy Vault generates revenue from the Asset Vault through four distinct layers:

### Layer 1: Asset Management Fees

Fees earned for sourcing, underwriting, acquiring, and managing portfolio assets on behalf of LPs.

| Fee Component                           | Rate                            | Estimated Annual Revenue (Fund I at Scale) |
|-----------------------------------------|---------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Management Fee (commitment period)      | 1.25–1.50% of committed capital | ~$4.0–4.5M                                 |
| Management Fee (post-investment period) | 0.75–1.00% of invested capital  | ~$2.5–3.0M (as portfolio stabilizes)        |
| Acquisition / Deployment Fee            | 1–2% of transaction value       | ~$3–6M per year (deployment phase)          |
| Disposition Fee                         | 0.5–1.0% of gross proceeds      | Realized upon asset sale / recap             |

**Total Annual Management Fee Run Rate at Scale**: $6–10 million (Fund I only)

### Layer 2: Operations & Maintenance (O&M)

Long-term (10–20 year) O&M agreements with each Asset Vault portfolio company provide stable, inflation-linked cash flows.

| O&M Revenue Component       | Typical Rate                          | Revenue at 1.5 GW Deployed           |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Fixed O&M Fee               | $6–8/kW-year                          | $9–12M annually                      |
| Variable O&M Fee            | $2–4/MWh dispatched                   | $5–8M annually (at 30% capacity factor)|
| Performance Incentive       | Bonus for availability >98%           | $1–2M annually (est.)                |
| **Total O&M Revenue**       |                                       | **$15–22M annually**                 |

At scale, O&M contracts for 1.5 GW of operating assets are projected to generate $15–22 million in annualized revenue at attractive margins (25–35% gross margin, 15–20% operating margin after field service personnel and monitoring center costs).

### Layer 3: Technology Supply Margin

Energy Vault supplies G-VAULT and B-VAULT equipment to Asset Vault portfolio companies. While these are related-party transactions, they are structured at arm's-length terms with independent LP committee approval.

| Equipment Type          | Typical Project Size  | Equipment Revenue    | Gross Margin       | Gross Profit Contribution |
|-------------------------|-----------------------|----------------------|--------------------|----------------------------|
| B-VAULT BESS (100 MW)   | $25–40M (EPC excl. BOP)| $25–40M             | 20–25%             | $5–10M per project         |
| G-VAULT EVx 2.0™ (25 MW/100 MWh) | $40–60M (EPC excl. civil) | $40–60M    | 25–35%             | $10–21M per project         |

With 1.5 GW of portfolio assets under the Fund, technology supply could represent $300–600 million in total equipment revenue over the deployment phase, with gross profit contribution of $75–180 million — all realized while maintaining asset ownership economics.

### Layer 4: Asset-Level Equity Returns

Energy Vault's co-investment in Fund I entitles the company to its pro-rata share of portfolio-level free cash flow and any proceeds from asset sales or recapitalizations.

**Target Portfolio Cash Flow Profile (Fund I, at scale)**:

| Portfolio Metric                   | Estimate at 1.5 GW Deployed      |
|------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Annual Portfolio EBITDA            | $80–120M (unlevered)             |
| Less: Debt Service (50% leverage)  | ($25–40M)                        |
| Annual Distributable Cash Flow     | $40–80M (levered equity cash flow)|
| Energy Vault Sponsor Share (15%)   | $6–12M annual distribution       |
| Carried Interest (20% over 8% pref)| $5–15M upon realization          |

**Annualized Recurring EBITDA from Fund I — All Layers Combined**:

| Revenue Layer                | Annual EBITDA Contribution ($M) |
|------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Asset Management Fees        | $6–10                          |
| O&M Services                 | $5–8                           |
| Asset-Level Distributions    | $6–12                          |
| **Total Recurring EBITDA**   | **$17–30M (Fund I)**           |

At the guided target of $60 million in annual recurring EBITDA from Fund I upon full deployment, the incremental bridge is expected to come from carry realization (lump-sum, but annualized over fund life) and higher asset-level distributions as projects achieve steady-state operations.

## Fund-Level Return Attribution

The total return to LPs in Asset Vault Fund I is attributable to three components:

1. **Operating Cash Yield (60–70% of total return)**: Contracted revenue from RA agreements, energy arbitrage, and ancillary services, distributed quarterly as available cash.

2. **Capital Appreciation (20–30%)**: Increase in asset value as projects transition from development to operational status, with value crystallization upon sale or recapitalization.

3. **ITC / Tax Benefit Monetization (5–10%)**: ITC transfer proceeds or tax equity partnership flip structures, distributed to LPs as return of capital or special distribution.

## Conclusion

The Asset Vault economics create a structurally compelling model: Energy Vault earns fees at every stage of the asset lifecycle (acquisition, operations, disposition) while retaining equity upside through co-investment. The platform transforms the company's financial profile from a lumpy, project-dependent equipment manufacturer into a compounding infrastructure platform with visible, multi-year revenue streams and margin expansion potential. At full Fund I scale, the guided $60 million in annual recurring EBITDA would represent the majority of the company's current enterprise value, implying significant upside as successive fund vintages compound the recurring earnings base.
