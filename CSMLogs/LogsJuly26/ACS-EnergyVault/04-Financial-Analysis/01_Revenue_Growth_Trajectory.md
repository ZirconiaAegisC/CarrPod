The shift toward recurring revenue (Asset Management Fees, O&M, EMS licensing) is the key margin and valuation narrative. As recurring revenue grows from ~15% of the mix in FY2024 to a projected 40–55% by FY2028E, the quality and predictability of earnings should improve, supporting multiple expansion from the current development-stage valuation toward infrastructure-asset-manager comparables.

## Gross Margin Trajectory

Energy Vault achieved a critical inflection point in FY2025, reporting $48 million in gross profit and a 23.6% gross margin — the first year of positive gross margin at the consolidated level. This improvement reflects:

1. **B-VAULT Supply Chain Maturation**: Multi-sourcing of LFP cells has reduced per-unit procurement costs, while design standardization has reduced integration labor hours per MW deployed.

2. **Asset Vault Fee Contribution**: Asset management fees carry near-100% incremental margins, providing high-margin revenue that lifts the blended gross margin.

3. **O&M Margin Accretion**: O&M contracts carry 25–35% gross margins, above the equipment sales margin profile. As the installed base of operating assets grows, O&M revenue scales with minimal incremental fixed cost.

4. **Volume Leverage on Fixed Costs**: Engineering, supply-chain management, and project management overhead is being absorbed across a growing revenue base, improving operating leverage.

| Period          | Gross Margin Range  | Commentary                                                     |
|-----------------|---------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| FY2024          | Negative           | Sub-scale; fixed cost absorption not achieved                 |
| FY2025          | 23.6%              | Inflection point; BESS margin improvement                     |
| FY2026E (guide) | 15–25%             | Conservatism reflecting project mix; G-VAULT first deployments |
| FY2027E (est.)  | 20–30%             | Recurring revenue mix shift; G-VAULT margin maturation         |
| FY2028E+ (est.) | 25–35%             | Scale economics; O&M and fees dominant margin drivers          |
