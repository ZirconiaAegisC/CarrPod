# Executive Summary — Energy Vault Holdings, Inc. (NYSE: NRGV)

## Investment Thesis

Energy Vault is executing the most capital-efficient energy storage deployment model in the sector. The company has redefined its operating architecture through the **Asset Vault** platform — an own-and-operate fund structure that transforms Energy Vault from a pure technology provider into a recurring-revenue infrastructure owner, yielding durable, contracted cash flows backed by utility and C&I offtake agreements. Across its integrated portfolio of gravity, battery, and digital solutions, Energy Vault now manages a 1.1 GW operating and development pipeline, controls $1.35 billion in contracted backlog, and has delivered four consecutive quarters of accelerating revenue growth culminating in the company's first positive adjusted EBITDA quarter in Q4 2025.

## Five-Pillar Framework

### 1. Asset Vault — Own & Operate Infrastructure
A $300 million co-investment fund (OIC) targeting 1.5 GW of energy storage assets under ownership. Contracted revenue is underpinned by long-term tolling agreements and offtake contracts. The Calistoga (California) and Cross Trails (Texas) assets are now operational and generating an annualized $10 million of contracted EBITDA. The platform is structured to recycle capital upon project stabilization, compounding equity returns and scaling toward $100–150 million of annual recurring EBITDA by 2029. Fund I alone is projected to deliver approximately $60 million in annual recurring EBITDA once fully deployed.

### 2. G-VAULT — Gravity Energy Storage Systems (GESS)
The EVx 2.0™ platform delivers 25 MW / 100 MWh modular blocks with 35+ year asset life, near-zero degradation, and round-trip efficiency exceeding 80%. Deployments include the landmark Eskom 100 MWh project in South Africa — the anchor for up to 4 GW / 4 GWh across the SADC region — and the Stoney Creek 125 MW / 1 GWh project in Australia backed by a A$310 million, 14-year Long-Term Energy Service Agreement (LTESA).

### 3. B-VAULT — Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS)
Energy Vault's turnkey BESS platform delivers integrated DC-block and AC-coupled architectures spanning short-duration (1–4 hour) and long-duration (8+ hour) applications. Deployment velocity continues to accelerate, with 540 MW of managed BESS assets across the portfolio and the flagship EBOR 100 MW / 870 MWh project representing one of the largest BESS installations in the Australian NEM.

### 4. AI & Digital Infrastructure
The intersection of AI-driven data-center load growth and grid decarbonization represents a structural demand tailwind. Energy Vault's partnership with Crusoe Energy Systems for powered-shell digital infrastructure and Peak Energy's sodium-ion 1.5 GWh manufacturing commitment position the company at the center of 24/7 carbon-free energy delivery for hyperscale and colocation customers. VaultOS™ EMS provides the software orchestration layer across all assets.

### 5. Global Market Expansion
The portfolio now spans six continents. Key market accelerants include the BayWa r.e. acquisition (850 MW pipeline, Japan), the EU Green Energy partnership (1.8 GWh, 4-year program), and the Eskom SADC framework (up to 4 GW). The company's $1.35 billion backlog is regionally diversified, reducing single-market concentration risk.

## Risk Considerations

**Execution risk** on large-scale projects, particularly in emerging markets with evolving regulatory regimes. **Technology adoption risk** — gravity storage remains a relatively novel modality compared with lithium-ion incumbency. **Financing risk** — the company's growth strategy relies on continued access to project finance and co-investment capital. **Supply-chain concentration** in major structural and electromechanical components. **Currency and geopolitical risk** inherent to multi-jurisdictional infrastructure deployment.

## Conclusion

Energy Vault has built a structurally differentiated business model that marries proprietary long-duration storage technology with an asset-ownership platform generating recurring, contracted cash flows. The company's $1.35 billion backlog, accelerating MW deployment velocity, and visible path to sustained positive EBITDA create a compelling risk-adjusted profile for infrastructure and energy transition investors. A 12–36 month horizon should see the crystallization of the Asset Vault compounding model, gravity storage validation at utility scale, and material cash-flow generation from the integrated portfolio.
