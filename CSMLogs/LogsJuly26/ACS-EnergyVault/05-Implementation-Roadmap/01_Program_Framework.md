# Program Framework — Implementation Roadmap

## Overview

This document provides the integrated implementation framework for Energy Vault's five-pillar strategy over a 36-month horizon (H2 2026 through H1 2029). The framework coordinates technology deployment, project execution, capital formation, and operational scaling across all regions and business lines.

## Strategic Pillar Interdependencies

The five strategic pillars are operationally interdependent:

```
                    ┌─────────────────────────────┐
                    │     Global Market Expansion  │
                    │  (Demand creation, pipeline) │
                    └─────────────┬───────────────┘
                                  │
         ┌────────────────────────┼────────────────────────┐
         │                        │                        │
         ▼                        ▼                        ▼
┌────────────────┐    ┌────────────────┐    ┌────────────────┐
│   G-VAULT      │    │   B-VAULT      │    │  AI Digital    │
│ Gravity Storage│    │ Battery Systems│    │ Infrastructure │
└───────┬────────┘    └───────┬────────┘    └───────┬────────┘
        │                     │                     │
        └─────────────────────┼─────────────────────┘
                              │
                              ▼
                 ┌────────────────────────┐
                 │    Asset Vault          │
                 │  Own & Operate Platform │
                 │  (Capital, ownership,   │
                 │   recurring revenue)    │
                 └────────────────────────┘
```

The Asset Vault is both the financial engine and the demand aggregator. Technology products (G-VAULT, B-VAULT, VaultOS) supply into Asset Vault portfolio companies and third-party customers. Regional market expansion feeds the pipeline into both channels.

## Governance and Program Management

### Program Governance Structure

The implementation program is governed through a three-tier structure:

1. **Executive Steering Committee**: Quarterly review of program progress against KPIs, resource allocation decisions, and strategic trade-offs. Chaired by CEO. Membership: CFO, CTO, Chief Development Officer, General Counsel.

2. **Program Management Office (PMO)**: Monthly integrated schedule review, risk register maintenance, budget variance tracking, and cross-workstream dependency management. Reports to CFO.

3. **Workstream Leads**: Weekly tactical coordination within each pillar. Accountable for milestone delivery and escalation of blockers to PMO.

### Risk and Issue Management

A consolidated risk register is maintained with the following categories:

| Risk Tier | Definition                                         | Escalation Path                | Review Cadence     |
|-----------|----------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|--------------------|
| Tier 1    | Threatens FY guidance, material contract, or safety| Immediate to Steering Committee| Real-time          |
| Tier 2    | Schedule slip >90 days or budget variance >15%     | PMO → Steering Committee       | Monthly            |
| Tier 3    | Schedule slip 30–90 days or budget variance 5–15%  | Workstream Lead → PMO           | Monthly            |
| Tier 4    | Schedule slip <30 days or budget variance <5%      | Managed within workstream       | Weekly             |

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Eskom EVx 2.0™ execution** — The reference project must achieve COD on schedule and meet performance specifications. This single project validates the G-VAULT technology at utility scale and unlocks the SADC pipeline.

2. **Fund II formation** — A successful Fund II first close of $500M+ demonstrates institutional investor confidence in the Asset Vault model and provides the capital base for the next growth phase.

3. **Recurring revenue inflection** — Crossing ~40% recurring revenue mix (from management fees, O&M, and asset distributions) shifts the market's valuation framework from project-based equipment manufacturer to infrastructure platform.

4. **Cash flow self-sufficiency** — Achieving sustained positive free cash flow (ex-investment activities) by 2028 validates the asset-light model and provides independence from capital markets for ongoing operations.

5. **AI infrastructure validation** — Material revenue contribution from the Crusoe partnership and powered-shell deployments validates a third growth vertical beyond storage.

## Conclusion

The 36-month implementation roadmap provides a structured pathway from the current inflection point (first positive adjusted EBITDA quarter, $1.35B backlog, Fund I deployed) to a mature, compounding platform generating $100–150 million in annual recurring EBITDA and operating across six continents. The critical execution window is H2 2026 through 2027, during which the Eskom reference project, Fund II formation, and continued revenue growth must converge to validate the long-term investment thesis.
