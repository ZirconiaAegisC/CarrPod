## 1. JUMANJI

### 1.1 Franchise Performance

| Film | Year | Global Box Office | Budget | ROI Assessment |
|------|------|------------------|--------|---------------|
| Jumanji (original) | 1995 | $263M | $65M | Profitable |
| Jumanji: Welcome to the Jungle | 2017 | $962M | $90M | Extremely profitable |
| Jumanji: The Next Level | 2019 | $801M | $125M | Extremely profitable |
| **Franchise Total** | | **$2.03B** | **$280M** | **>7x multiplier** |

### 1.2 Strategic Assessment

Jumanji is SPE's most reliable non-Spider-Man franchise, characterized by high-concept premise (board game/video game body-swap), star-driven ensemble casting (Dwayne Johnson, Kevin Hart, Jack Black, Karen Gillan), and strong global appeal. The franchise's production economics benefit from moderate VFX requirements (relative to superhero films) and location-agnostic jungle settings.

**Key Risk:** The franchise is critically dependent on the availability and continued marketability of its core ensemble cast. Scheduling conflicts have delayed the fourth installment. Johnson's expanding slate across multiple franchises restricts windows for Jumanji production.

**Franchise Health Index: 7/10**

### 1.3 Recommendation

Prioritize greenlight of Jumanji 4 for a 2027/2028 release window. Evaluate generational refresh strategy — introduce younger ensemble cast with legacy characters in supporting/mentor roles to de-risk talent dependency.

| Film | Year | Global Box Office | Budget | ROI Assessment |
|------|------|------------------|--------|---------------|
| Bad Boys | 1995 | $141M | $19M | Profitable |
| Bad Boys II | 2003 | $273M | $130M | Modest profitability |
| Bad Boys for Life | 2020 | $426M | $90M | Strongly profitable |
| Bad Boys: Ride or Die | 2024 | $405M | $100M | Strongly profitable |
| **Franchise Total** | | **$1.25B** | **$339M** | |

### 2.2 Strategic Assessment

The Bad Boys franchise has demonstrated unusual longevity, with the third and fourth installments outperforming the first two in both nominal and inflation-adjusted terms. The Will Smith/Martin Lawrence chemistry is the franchise's primary asset. Directors Adil El Arbi and Bilall Fallah have successfully transitioned the franchise from the Michael Bay-era bombast to a more character-driven action-comedy tone.

**Key Risk:** Will Smith's post-2022 Oscars incident marketability remains a variable, though Bad Boys: Ride or Die's performance suggests audience separation of personal controversy from on-screen product.

**Franchise Health Index: 6/10**

### 3.1 Franchise Performance

| Film | Year | Global Box Office | Budget | ROI Assessment |
|------|------|------------------|--------|---------------|
| Ghostbusters (original) | 1984 | $296M | $30M | Legendary |
| Ghostbusters II | 1989 | $215M | $37M | Profitable |
| Ghostbusters (reboot) | 2016 | $229M | $144M | Loss |
| Ghostbusters: Afterlife | 2021 | $204M | $75M | Modestly profitable (COVID-impacted) |
| Ghostbusters: Frozen Empire | 2024 | $202M | $100M | Marginal profitability |
| **Franchise Total** | | **$1.15B** | | |

### 3.2 Strategic Assessment

Ghostbusters occupies a unique niche as a nostalgia-driven family franchise with strong brand recognition but limited global expansion capacity. The franchise's domestic-heavy box office split (~60% domestic) constrains total addressable market. The transition from legacy cast to a new generation in Afterlife successfully refreshed the IP but has not yet generated breakout growth.

**Franchise Health Index: 4/10**

### 3.3 Recommendation

Evaluate Ghostbusters as a streaming/premium VOD-first property rather than a theatrical tentpole. The franchise's demographic profile (family audiences with high home-viewing preference) and moderate global box office ceiling suggest a distribution strategy optimized for ancillary windows.

## 4. VENOM

### 4.1 Performance (See Also: File 05, Section 4)

| Film | Global Box Office | Budget | Rotten Tomatoes |
|------|------------------|--------|-----------------|
| Venom (2018) | $856M | $100M | 30% |
| Venom: Let There Be Carnage (2021) | $507M | $110M | 57% |

Venom is the only SSU property with demonstrated audience demand. The franchise's profitability — particularly Venom (2018) at 8.5x budget multiple — justifies continued investment. The critical-to-commercial disconnect (low RT scores, high box office) indicates an audience base that values the Tom Hardy performance/spectacle over critical reception.

### 4.2 Recommendation

Develop Venom 3 with clear MCU crossover potential. Negotiate Tom Holland Spider-Man integration with Marvel Studios. The Venom franchise's financial value would increase substantially if integrated into the MCU narrative architecture.

**Franchise Health Index: 5/10** (revised upward pending MCU integration)

## 5. UNCHARTED

### 5.1 Performance

| Film | Year | Global Box Office | Budget | ROI Assessment |
|------|------|------------------|--------|---------------|
| Uncharted | 2022 | $407M | $120M | Profitable |

### 5.2 Strategic Assessment

Uncharted's first theatrical entry — starring Tom Holland and Mark Wahlberg, directed by Ruben Fleischer — exceeded financial expectations despite mixed critical reception. The film represents SPE's most successful PlayStation IP adaptation to date in financial terms (though The Last of Us on HBO has greater cultural impact).

The Uncharted franchise has substantial sequel potential. The video game source material spans five critically acclaimed entries with a deep narrative universe. The film's ending explicitly sets up sequel material.

**Key Advantage:** Unlike Jumanji or Bad Boys, Uncharted franchise potential is not limited by actor aging or availability. The property allows for an evolving ensemble cast and multi-generational storytelling.

### 5.3 Recommendation

Fast-track Uncharted 2 for 2027 theatrical release. Develop cross-media strategy: theatrical film → PlayStation game → merchandise / theme park activations where possible.

**Franchise Health Index: 7/10**
