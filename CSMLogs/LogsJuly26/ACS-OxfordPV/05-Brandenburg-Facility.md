# 05 — BRANDENBURG FACILITY: SITE, ACQUISITION & INFRASTRUCTURE

## Site History and Acquisition

Oxford PV's manufacturing operations are centered at a former Bosch Solar facility located in Brandenburg an der Havel, approximately 70 km west of Berlin, in the state of Brandenburg, Germany. The site represents a strategic acquisition that provided Oxford PV with a substantially pre-equipped photovoltaic manufacturing plant, dramatically reducing the capital expenditure and lead time required to establish commercial-scale tandem module production.

The Brandenburg facility was originally constructed in 2008 as a state-of-the-art crystalline silicon cell and module manufacturing plant for Bosch Solar Energy AG, a subsidiary of Robert Bosch GmbH. Bosch invested approximately EUR 530 million in the facility before exiting the solar manufacturing business in 2013–2014 amid the broader European solar manufacturing downturn precipitated by Chinese overcapacity and price competition. The plant produced heterojunction (HJT) solar cells and modules during its Bosch ownership, establishing a workforce and cleanroom infrastructure directly relevant to Oxford PV's tandem technology.

Oxford PV acquired the facility in 2021 for an undisclosed sum, signing a lease-to-own agreement with the building's owner. The purchase included approximately 12,000 m² of cleanroom space (ISO Class 5–7), process utilities (deionized water, compressed dry air, nitrogen generation, process cooling water), chemical handling infrastructure, and gas distribution systems.

## Factory Layout and Material Flow

The Brandenburg facility is organized into discrete production zones following a linear process flow from incoming materials through finished goods dispatch:

**Zone A — Incoming Materials & Quality Control**: Dedicated receiving area with automated unpacking, visual inspection stations, and electrical performance testing (IV and EL) of incoming silicon cells. Temperature- and humidity-controlled storage for perovskite precursors, solvents, and encapsulant materials.

**Zone B — Surface Preparation**: Wet-chemical cleaning stations, UV-ozone treatment conveyors, and plasma treatment chambers for silicon cell surface activation. This zone operates at ISO Class 6 cleanliness with positive-pressure cascading to adjacent zones.

**Zone C — Perovskite Deposition**: The core of the manufacturing operation. Contains the proprietary vacuum co-evaporation and hybrid chemical vapor deposition tools. This zone operates at ISO Class 5 (critical process areas) with stringent particulate and molecular contamination controls. Active monitoring of oxygen (<10 ppm) and water vapor (<1 ppm) in the process atmosphere.

**Zone D — Back-End Processing**: Front TCO sputtering, ARC deposition, edge deletion (laser scribing), and electrical characterization. ISO Class 6.

**Zone E — Module Assembly**: Automated stringing, layup, lamination (multi-daylight laminators), framing, junction box attachment, and flash testing. ISO Class 7.

**Zone F — Finished Goods**: End-of-line testing (IV, EL, hipot, visual inspection), packaging, and warehousing. Controlled environment (25°C, <60% RH).

## Capital Investment

The refurbishment and retooling of the Brandenburg facility for tandem module production represents a cumulative investment of approximately EUR 120 million through 2025. The investment has been funded through a combination of equity financing (including the GBP 80 million Series E round in early 2025), German federal and state government grants under the *Energieforschungsprogramm* and *Gemeinschaftsaufgabe Verbesserung der regionalen Wirtschaftsstruktur* (GRW) programs, and secured debt facilities.

## Workforce

As of Q2 2025, the Brandenburg facility employs approximately 280 personnel across manufacturing, quality, engineering, and administrative functions. The workforce includes:

- **Production operators**: ~140 personnel working across three shifts (24/5 operation).
- **Process engineers**: ~45 personnel with expertise in vacuum deposition, wet chemistry, laser processing, and module assembly.
- **Quality and reliability**: ~35 personnel managing in-line metrology, end-of-line testing, reliability laboratory, and supplier quality.
- **Facilities and maintenance**: ~25 personnel.
- **Supply chain and logistics**: ~20 personnel.
- **Management and administration**: ~15 personnel.

Approximately 40% of the workforce were retained from the Bosch Solar operation, providing continuity of experience in HJT cell and module manufacturing. The remaining 60% were recruited from the German solar manufacturing ecosystem, the Eastern European technical workforce (particularly Poland and the Czech Republic), and Oxford PV's UK R&D center.

## Environmental and Regulatory Compliance

The Brandenburg facility operates under a *Bundes-Immissionsschutzgesetz* (BImSchG) permit issued by the Landesamt für Umwelt Brandenburg. Key environmental management systems include:

- **Lead management**: The perovskite absorber layer contains lead (approximately 0.8 g/m²), which is below the threshold for RoHS-restricted substances but requires occupational exposure controls (air monitoring, PPE, hygiene protocols) and waste stream management (solid waste classified as hazardous, recycled through a licensed lead smelter).
- **Solvent abatement**: Thermal oxidizers with 99.5% destruction efficiency for volatile organic compounds from perovskite precursor solvents.
- **Wastewater treatment**: On-site neutralization, metals precipitation, and filtration before discharge to municipal treatment.
- **ISO 14001**: Environmental management system certification, achieved Q3 2023 and maintained through annual surveillance audits.
