# IRA Production Tax Credit — Section 45V Economics

## Overview
Section 45V of the Internal Revenue Code, enacted as part of the Inflation
Reduction Act (IRA) of 2022, provides a production tax credit (PTC) for
qualified clean hydrogen produced at a qualified clean hydrogen production
facility. This credit is the most financially significant policy incentive
in the global hydrogen industry and fundamentally shapes US green hydrogen
project economics.

## Credit Structure

### Tiered Credit Levels
The credit amount is determined by the lifecycle greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions
rate of the hydrogen production process, with lower emissions qualifying for
higher credit rates:

| Emissions Rate (kg CO₂e / kg H₂) | Credit Rate ($/kg H₂) | Percentage of Full Credit |
|----------------------------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| <0.45                            | $3.00                | 100%                      |
| 0.45 – 1.5                       | $1.00                | 33.3%                     |
| 1.5 – 2.5                        | $0.75                | 25%                       |
| 2.5 – 4.0                        | $0.60                | 20%                       |
| >4.0                             | $0.00                | 0%                        |

Note: Credit rates shown are for facilities meeting prevailing wage and
apprenticeship requirements. Rates are reduced to 20% of these amounts
(⅕) if requirements are not met. This creates a strong incentive for
union-scale labor practices.

### Key Qualification Requirements
1. **Clean hydrogen**: The hydrogen must be produced through a process that
   results in lifecycle GHG emissions below 4.0 kg CO₂e per kg H₂.

2. **Qualified facility**: The production facility must be located in the
   United States or a US territory.

3. **Begin construction**: Construction must begin before January 1, 2033.
   The credit applies for 10 years from the date the facility is placed
   in service.

4. **Prevailing wage & apprenticeship**: To qualify for the full credit
   rate, projects must pay prevailing wages and employ registered
   apprentices during construction and for a specified period during
   operations. Failure to meet these requirements reduces the credit to
   20% of the full amount.

5. **Lifecycle analysis**: Emissions are determined using the GREET
   (Greenhouse gases, Regulated Emissions, and Energy use in
   Technologies) model developed by Argonne National Laboratory.

### Three Pillars (45VH2-GREET Model)
The IRS and Treasury, in proposed regulations (December 2023), introduced
three key requirements for electrolytic hydrogen to qualify for the full
credit, known as the "Three Pillars":

1. **Incrementality (Additionality)**: The electricity used must be from
   new clean generation sources that came online within 36 months of the
   hydrogen facility. This ensures hydrogen production does not simply
   consume existing clean electricity and shift other consumers to fossil
   generation.

2. **Temporal Matching**: Hydrogen production must be matched to renewable
   generation on an hourly basis starting in 2028, with annual matching
   allowed through 2027. This requirement aligns electrolyzer operation
   with actual renewable generation, ensuring true "green" hydrogen
   production.

3. **Deliverability (Geographic Correlation)**: The renewable generation
   must be located in the same region as the hydrogen facility, as
   defined by DOE's needs assessment regions. This prevents credit for
   hydrogen produced using renewable energy transmitted across
   uncongested grids.

### Grandfathering Provisions
Projects that begin construction before January 1, 2023 are grandfathered
and exempt from the incrementality requirement. This provision is
significant for projects that had early development momentum before the
IRA.

## Economic Impact on Green Hydrogen
The $3/kg credit for Tier 1 hydrogen production dramatically changes the
economics of green hydrogen in the United States:

### Illustrative Economics (2026, with 45V at $3/kg)
| Cost Component              | $/kg H₂       | % of LCOH |
|-----------------------------|---------------|-----------|
| Electricity ($25/MWh, 55 kWh/kg) | $1.38      | —         |
| Electrolyzer Capital Recovery    | $0.40      | —         |
| O&M, Water, Other                | $0.25      | —         |
| **Unsubsidized LCOH**            | **$2.03**  | —         |
| Less: IRA 45V Credit             | ($3.00)    | —         |
| **Net Cost (with 45V)**          | **~$0.00** | —         |

At $25/MWh renewable power, the 45V credit results in near-zero net hydrogen
production cost, enabling green hydrogen to competitively displace gray
hydrogen in industrial applications while generating significant developer
returns.

### Sensitivity: Power Price vs. Credit Level
| Electricity Price ($/MWh) | Unsubsidized LCOH ($/kg) | Net with $3/kg 45V ($/kg) |
|---------------------------|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| $15                       | $1.48                    | ~$0.00                    |
| $25                       | $2.03                    | ~$0.00                    |
| $35                       | $2.58                    | ~$0.00                    |
| $50                       | $3.40                    | $0.40                     |
| $70                       | $4.50                    | $1.50                     |

This sensitivity illustrates why 45V is transformative: even at $35/MWh
power, well above utility-scale solar costs in the US, the net hydrogen
cost remains near zero with the full credit.

## Direct Pay and Transferability
The IRA introduced two mechanisms that are particularly important for
startup companies and project developers:

1. **Direct Pay (Elective Pay)**: Tax-exempt entities (municipalities,
   cooperatives, tribes) can receive the credit as a direct payment
   equivalent to the tax credit amount.

2. **Transferability**: For-profit entities can sell their tax credits
   to third parties with tax liability. This is critical for startup
   companies and project developers that do not yet have sufficient tax
   liability to monetize the credits themselves. Credits are sold at a
   discount (typically 85-95 cents on the dollar) reflecting transaction
   costs and risk.

For Electric Hydrogen, transferability enables monetization of manufacturing
credits (45X, 48C) even if the company is not yet profitable, providing
cash flow during the growth phase.

## Policy Risk Assessment
| Risk Factor                   | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation                                    |
|-------------------------------|-----------|--------|----------------------------------------------|
| Full IRA repeal               | Low       | High   | Bipartisan support for manufacturing provisions |
| Modification of Three Pillars  | Medium    | Medium | Industry engagement with Treasury rulemaking   |
| 45V rate reduction            | Low-Med   | High   | Administrative vs. legislative; harder to change |
| 45V not extended beyond 2033   | Medium    | Medium | Projects constructed before deadline still get 10 years |
