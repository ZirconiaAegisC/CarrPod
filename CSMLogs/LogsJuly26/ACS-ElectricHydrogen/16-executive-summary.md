## What Electric Hydrogen Does
Electric Hydrogen designs, manufactures, and deploys the world's largest
proton exchange membrane (PEM) electrolyzer systems — standardized 100MW
units that produce green hydrogen from water and renewable electricity.
The company's mission is to make green hydrogen cost-competitive with
fossil-derived hydrogen, enabling deep decarbonization of industrial
sectors including refining, ammonia, steel, and heavy transport.

## Why It Matters
Hydrogen production currently generates ~900 million tonnes of CO₂ annually —
roughly 2% of global emissions. Nearly all hydrogen today is "gray" hydrogen
produced from natural gas without carbon capture. Green hydrogen, produced
via electrolysis powered by renewables, eliminates these emissions and
enables decarbonization of hard-to-abate sectors where direct electrification
is impractical.

Electric Hydrogen's technology directly addresses the two primary barriers
to green hydrogen adoption:
1. **Cost**: Targeting <$2/kg green hydrogen (competitive with gray H₂)
   through manufacturing scale and technology innovation.
2. **Scale**: 100MW standardized systems replace custom-engineered projects,
   enabling rapid deployment at utility scale.

## Five Key Pillars

### 1. PEM Electrolyzer Technology (Files 01-03)
100MW PEM systems with proprietary membrane electrode assembly (MEA)
technology featuring reduced precious metal loading (iridium <0.3 mg/cm²).
Efficiency of 52-57 kWh/kg at system level with sub-30-second ramp rates
enabling direct renewable coupling. Compact, high-pressure (30 bar)
operation reduces downstream compression costs versus alkaline alternatives.

### 2. Devens Gigafactory & Manufacturing (Files 04-06)
First large-scale dedicated electrolyzer factory in the U.S., with 1.2 GW/yr
annual capacity. Standardized 100MW building blocks manufactured using
automotive-style production rather than custom project engineering. Vertical
integration from catalyst synthesis through system assembly. IRA manufacturing
credits (48C, 45X) improve facility economics.

### 3. Strategic Partnerships (Files 07-09)
- **AES**: First commercial project — 300MW Texas green hydrogen facility
  (3 × 100MW EH2 systems). AES provides project development, renewable
  power, and offtake.
- **Amazon**: Strategic investor (Climate Pledge Fund) and potential offtaker
  for material handling (15,000+ fuel cell forklifts), trucking, and data
  centers.
- **BP**: Series C investor (bp ventures). Technology access for BP's
  ambitious green hydrogen strategy (0.5-0.7 mtpa by 2030). Potential
  collaboration at Gulf Coast and international sites.

### 4. Green Hydrogen Market & DOE Hydrogen Shot (Files 10-12)
Global hydrogen market: 95 mtpa today, projected 250-800 mtpa by 2050.
DOE Hydrogen Shot targets $1/kg by 2031. IRA Section 45V provides $3/kg
production tax credit through 2033, making U.S. green hydrogen highly
competitive. Electric Hydrogen competes against Nel Hydrogen, ITM Power,
Plug Power, and Cummins, differentiating on 100MW module size, U.S.
manufacturing, and proprietary MEA technology.

### 5. Financial Strategy & IRA Economics (Files 13-15)
$800M+ raised from strategic investors (BP, Amazon, Microsoft, United
Airlines, Temasek, Fortescue). IRA Section 45V at $3/kg makes green hydrogen
economically superior to gray hydrogen in the U.S. for a 10-year window.
Target LCOH path: ~$2/kg (2026) → <$1.50/kg (2030). Revenue model: equipment
sales, long-term service agreements, potential technology licensing.
Exit pathway: IPO likely 2026-2029 at $500M+ revenue run rate.

## Investment Thesis
Electric Hydrogen represents a pure-play investment in utility-scale green
hydrogen production equipment — analogous to investing in wind turbine
manufacturers (Vestas) or solar manufacturers (First Solar) at the early
stages of their respective markets. Key investment considerations:

**Strengths:**
- Largest commercial PEM electrolyzer (100MW) — scale advantage
- U.S. manufacturing with IRA incentives creates cost moat
- Strategic corporate investor base provides market access and validation
- First commercial project (AES/Texas) de-risks technology at scale
- Proprietary MEA technology addresses iridium supply constraint

**Risks:**
- First-of-kind deployment execution risk
- IRA policy dependency (45V tax credit)
- Capital-intensive manufacturing business model
- Competitive catch-up risk (ITM Power, Nel scaling PEM)
- Technology disruption risk (Hysata efficiency, alkaline improvements)

**Key Milestones to Watch:**
1. Texas 300MW project: COD, performance metrics, operational data
2. Devens factory: Production ramp to 1.2 GW/yr, unit cost trajectory
3. Order book: New customer announcements beyond AES
4. Financial: Revenue generation, gross margin trajectory
5. Policy: Final 45V regulations, IRA amendment risk
