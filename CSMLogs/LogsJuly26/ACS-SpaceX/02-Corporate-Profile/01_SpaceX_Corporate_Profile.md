## 2.1 Founding and Mission

Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (SpaceX) was founded in 2002 by Elon Musk with the stated mission of reducing space transportation costs and enabling the colonization of Mars. Headquartered in Hawthorne, California, with primary launch operations at Cape Canaveral Space Force Station (SLC-40), Kennedy Space Center (LC-39A), Vandenberg Space Force Base (SLC-4E), and the Starbase development and production complex in Boca Chica, Texas, SpaceX has evolved into the world's dominant launch provider and satellite internet operator.

## 2.2 Ownership and Governance

### 2.2.1 Pre-IPO Structure

Prior to the June 2026 initial public offering, SpaceX was privately held with approximately 76% voting control retained by Elon Musk through a multi-class share arrangement. Major pre-IPO institutional investors included Fidelity, Google, Valor Equity Partners, Founders Fund, DFJ, and Baillie Gifford, alongside sovereign wealth interests from the UAE (Mubadala) and Qatar (QIA). The last private fundraising round (Series N, December 2024) valued the company at $350 billion.

### 2.2.2 IPO and Post-Public Governance

On June 15, 2026, SpaceX debuted on NASDAQ under the ticker SPACX, raising $85.7 billion at $150 per share for a market capitalization of $2.3 trillion — the largest initial public offering in market history. The offering comprised 571.3 million Class A shares. Post-IPO, Elon Musk retains approximately 58% voting control through Class C super-voting shares, ensuring continued founder-led strategic direction.

## 2.3 Operating Segments

### 2.3.1 Starlink (Consumer and Enterprise Connectivity)

Revenue: $11.4B (FY2025). The largest operating segment by revenue and profit. Includes consumer broadband subscriptions, maritime services, aeronautical in-flight connectivity, enterprise-grade backhaul, and government/military Starshield variants. Operates across 164 countries with 10.3 million subscribers and a $66/month blended ARPU. Starlink's direct-to-consumer model bypasses terrestrial last-mile infrastructure entirely.

### 2.3.2 Space (Launch Services)

Revenue: $4.1B (FY2025). Encompasses Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy commercial, civil, and national security launch services. The Falcon 9 fleet executed 165 launches in FY2025, marking the sixth consecutive annual launch record. The segment also includes Crew Dragon and Cargo Dragon missions under NASA's Commercial Crew and CRS-2 programs.

### 2.3.3 Artificial Intelligence Compute

Revenue: $3.2B (FY2025). A nascent but rapidly scaling segment encompassing orbital AI inference capabilities, ground-segment compute services, and early customer contracts for space-based distributed computing. Closely linked to the Terafab chip fabrication initiative and the planned 1-million-satellite orbital AI constellation.

## 2.5 Workforce and Culture

SpaceX employs an estimated 18,000+ personnel globally as of mid-2026, with the largest concentrations in Hawthorne, Starbase, Cape Canaveral, Redmond, and Bastrop. The organizational culture emphasizes vertical integration, aggressive timelines, flat hierarchy, and iterative hardware development. The company maintains an estimated average employee tenure significantly lower than industry peers, consistent with its high-intensity operational tempo.

## 2.6 Competitive Position

SpaceX commands approximately 87% of global orbital launch mass to orbit, operates the world's largest commercial satellite constellation, and is the only entity currently flying a fully reusable orbital-class rocket booster. Primary competitors include United Launch Alliance (ULA), Arianespace, and Roscosmos in launch; Amazon Kuiper, Eutelsat/OneWeb, and China's GuoWang in satellite broadband; and emerging Chinese reusable launcher programs (LandSpace, Space Pioneer) in next-generation launch.

## 2.7 Regulatory and Licensing Footprint

SpaceX holds launch licenses from the FAA Office of Commercial Space Transportation, spectrum authorizations from the FCC and ITU for Starlink operations across Ku, Ka, V, and E-band frequencies, and operating licenses in 164 sovereign jurisdictions for Starlink consumer service. The company maintains NASA safety certifications for crewed flight operations and NSSL certification from the U.S. Space Force for national security payloads.
