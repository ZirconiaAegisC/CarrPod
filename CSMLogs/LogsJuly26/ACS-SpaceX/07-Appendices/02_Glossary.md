# Glossary of Terms and Acronyms

**Classification:** ACS Proprietary — Appendices
**Version:** 1.0 — July 15, 2026

---

## A

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **ACS** | Advanced Capabilities Syndicate — the commissioning entity for this dossier |
| **Adj. EBITDA** | Adjusted Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization — a non-GAAP profitability metric excluding non-recurring, non-cash, and non-operational items |
| **AI Compute** | SpaceX operating segment encompassing orbital artificial intelligence inference, distributed computing, and Terafab chip fabrication |
| **ARPU** | Average Revenue Per User — monthly revenue per subscriber, a standard telecommunications metric; Starlink ARPU is $66/month (FY2025) |
| **Artemis** | NASA's lunar exploration program, encompassing the Space Launch System (SLS), Orion crew vehicle, Gateway space station, and Human Landing System (HLS) |
| **AST SpaceMobile** | Direct-to-handset satellite competitor utilizing large phased-array satellites to provide LTE broadband to unmodified smartphones |

## B

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Boca Chica** | Location of SpaceX's Starbase development, production, and launch facility in Cameron County, Texas, on the Gulf of Mexico |
| **Booster (Super Heavy)** | The first stage of the Starship/Super Heavy launch system, powered by 33 Raptor 3 engines; designed for full reusability via tower-catch recovery |

## C

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **CCP** | Commercial Crew Program — NASA program contracting with commercial providers (SpaceX, Boeing) for ISS crew transportation services |
| **CCtCap** | Commercial Crew Transportation Capability — the specific NASA contract vehicle for operational crew rotation missions |
| **CNSA** | China National Space Administration |
| **COGS** | Cost of Goods Sold |
| **CRS-2** | Commercial Resupply Services 2 — NASA's second contract round for ISS cargo delivery, held by SpaceX (Cargo Dragon), Northrop Grumman (Cygnus), and Sierra Space (Dream Chaser) |

## D

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **D2C** | Direct-to-Cell — Starlink's service providing LTE connectivity from satellites to unmodified mobile handsets, also known as Supplemental Coverage from Space (SCS) |
| **DCF** | Discounted Cash Flow — a financial valuation method projecting future cash flows and discounting to present value |
| **DoD** | United States Department of Defense |
| **Dragon** | SpaceX's spacecraft family including Cargo Dragon (ISS resupply) and Crew Dragon (human spaceflight); the first commercially developed orbital spacecraft |
| **Draco** | SpaceX's hypergolic bipropellant thruster, using monomethylhydrazine (MMH) and nitrogen tetroxide (NTO), used for Dragon attitude control |

## E

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **EBITDA** | Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization |
| **eNodeB** | Evolved Node B — the LTE base station that handles radio communication with user devices; in Starlink D2C, the eNodeB is integrated into the satellite payload |
| **EVA** | Extravehicular Activity — activity performed by an astronaut outside a spacecraft (spacewalk) |

## F

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **F9** | Falcon 9 — SpaceX's two-stage, partially reusable medium-lift launch vehicle, powered by 9 Merlin 1D engines |
| **FH** | Falcon Heavy — SpaceX's heavy-lift variant using three Falcon 9 boosters in a side-core configuration |
| **FP8** | 8-bit floating point number format commonly used for AI inference workloads, offering higher throughput than FP16/FP32 at acceptable precision loss |

## G

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **GaAs** | Gallium Arsenide — a semiconductor material used in high-efficiency multi-junction solar cells for space applications |
| **Gateway** | (1) Starlink Gateway: Ground station connecting the satellite constellation to the terrestrial internet backbone; (2) Lunar Gateway: NASA's planned space station in lunar NRHO |
| **Gbps** | Gigabits per second — a measure of data throughput |
| **Golden Dome** | Pentagon program ($2B) for a proliferated LEO missile warning and tracking constellation, utilizing SpaceX Starshield satellites |
| **GSE** | Ground Support Equipment — infrastructure for vehicle integration, propellant loading, and launch operations |
| **GTO** | Geostationary Transfer Orbit — an elliptical orbit used as an intermediate step to reach geostationary orbit |
| **GuoWang** | China's planned 12,992-satellite LEO broadband constellation, operated by China Satellite Network Group (CNSA/CASC) |

## H

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **HBM4** | High Bandwidth Memory, 4th generation — ultra-high-bandwidth stacked DRAM used in AI accelerators |
| **HLS** | Human Landing System — NASA's lunar lander program for the Artemis missions; SpaceX's Starship HLS is the selected lander for Artemis III and IV |
| **HSF** | Human Spaceflight |

## I

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **IaaS** | Inference-as-a-Service — cloud-based AI model inference offered on a pay-per-use basis |
| **IFT** | Integrated Flight Test — SpaceX's designation for full-stack Starship/Super Heavy test flights (e.g., IFT-1 through IFT-13) |
| **IPO** | Initial Public Offering — SpaceX's June 15, 2026 public listing on NASDAQ ($85.7B raised, $2.3T valuation) |
| **ISL** | Inter-Satellite Link — optical laser communication between satellites, enabling direct space-to-space data routing without ground station intermediaries |
| **ISRU** | In-Situ Resource Utilization — the practice of harvesting and processing local resources (e.g., Martian atmosphere, lunar regolith) for life support and propellant production |
| **ISS** | International Space Station |
| **ITU** | International Telecommunication Union — the United Nations specialized agency for information and communication technologies, responsible for global spectrum coordination and orbital slot allocation |

## K

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **KSC** | Kennedy Space Center — NASA's primary launch site on Merritt Island, Florida; home to LC-39A (SpaceX-leased) and LC-39B (NASA SLS) |
| **Ku-band / Ka-band** | Microwave frequency bands used by Starlink: Ku-band (12–18 GHz) for consumer downlink/uplink; Ka-band (26.5–40 GHz) for gateway backhaul |

## L

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **LCH4** | Liquid Methane — Starship/Raptor engine fuel, chosen for its high specific impulse, clean burning characteristics, and potential for ISRU production on Mars |
| **LEO** | Low Earth Orbit — the orbital regime below approximately 2,000 km altitude; Starlink operates primarily at 530–570 km |
| **LOX** | Liquid Oxygen — oxidizer for Starship/Raptor engines; constitutes approximately 78% of propellant mass |
| **LTE** | Long-Term Evolution — the 4G mobile communication standard |
| **LTE-M / NB-IoT** | Low-power wide-area network variants of LTE optimized for Internet of Things (IoT) applications; key to Starlink D2C's ability to reach low-power devices |

## M

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Merlin** | SpaceX's gas-generator-cycle kerolox (RP-1/LOX) engine, powering the Falcon 9 and Falcon Heavy; nine Merlin 1D engines on the Falcon 9 first stage |
| **MNO** | Mobile Network Operator — a telecommunications company that owns and operates cellular network infrastructure |
| **MSS** | Mobile Satellite Service — a radio communication service between mobile earth stations and space stations |

## N

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **NASA** | National Aeronautics and Space Administration |
| **NRHO** | Near-Rectilinear Halo Orbit — the highly elliptical lunar orbit selected for NASA's Lunar Gateway and the Orion-HLS rendezvous point |
| **NSSL** | National Security Space Launch — the U.S. Space Force program for procuring launch services for classified and national security payloads |
| **NTN** | Non-Terrestrial Network — 3GPP designation for satellite-based components of 5G and future mobile networks |

## O

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **OLM** | Orbital Launch Mount — the launch table and integration structure at Starbase used for Starship/Super Heavy launches |

## P

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Pbps** | Petabits per second — 1,000 terabits per second; the projected total capacity of a fully deployed V3 Starlink constellation |
| **PFLOPS** | PetaFLOPS — 10^15 floating-point operations per second; a measure of supercomputing performance |
| **PLMN** | Public Land Mobile Network — a mobile network identified by its Mobile Country Code (MCC) and Mobile Network Code (MNC) |
| **PWSA** | Proliferated Warfighter Space Architecture — the U.S. Space Development Agency's LEO constellation for military communications, tracking, and targeting |

## R

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Raptor** | SpaceX's full-flow staged-combustion methalox engine family; Raptor 3 is the current production variant with ~230 tons of sea-level thrust |
| **RP-1** | Rocket Propellant-1 — a highly refined form of kerosene used as fuel in the Falcon 9's Merlin engines |

## S

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **S-1** | SEC registration statement for initial public offering; SpaceX filed in May 2026 |
| **S/Ku/Ka/V/E-band** | Standard letter designations for microwave frequency bands per IEEE 521-2019; E-band (71–76/81–86 GHz) provides very high bandwidth for gateway backhaul |
| **SAR** | Synthetic Aperture Radar — a form of radar imaging capable of high-resolution ground observation through cloud cover and darkness |
| **SBIRS** | Space-Based Infrared System — the U.S. legacy missile warning satellite constellation being superseded by the Next-Gen OPIR and proliferated LEO (Golden Dome) architectures |
| **SCS** | Supplemental Coverage from Space — the FCC regulatory framework authorizing satellite operators to use terrestrial mobile spectrum for direct-to-handset connectivity |
| **SG&A** | Selling, General, and Administrative expenses |
| **SIGINT** | Signals Intelligence |
| **SLC** | Space Launch Complex — launch pad designation (e.g., SLC-40 at Cape Canaveral, SLC-4E at Vandenberg) |
| **SLS** | Space Launch System — NASA's heavy-lift launch vehicle for the Artemis program |
| **SOFR** | Secured Overnight Financing Rate — the reference interest rate benchmark used in SpaceX's floating-rate debt instruments |
| **SSO** | Sun-Synchronous Orbit — a near-polar orbit where the satellite passes over any given point at the same local solar time each day |
| **Starbase** | SpaceX's Starship/Super Heavy development, manufacturing, and launch complex in Boca Chica, Texas |
| **Starshield** | SpaceX's classified government/military satellite program, derived from the Starlink bus, offering enhanced encryption, dedicated beam steering, and optional sensor payloads |
| **SuperDraco** | SpaceX's high-thrust hypergolic abort engine on Crew Dragon; 8 SuperDracos provide crew escape capability during launch |

## T

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Tbps** | Terabits per second — 1,000 gigabits per second; V3 Starlink satellites deliver 1 Tbps each |
| **Terafab** | Joint SpaceX-Tesla semiconductor fabrication facility; planned $119B investment to produce custom AI accelerators for the orbital AI compute constellation |
| **TLE** | Two-Line Element set — a standardized data format encoding orbital parameters for satellite tracking |

## U

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **ULA** | United Launch Alliance — a Boeing/Lockheed Martin joint venture operating Atlas V and Vulcan Centaur launch vehicles |

## V

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **VLEO** | Very Low Earth Orbit — the orbital regime below approximately 450 km, where atmospheric drag significantly limits satellite lifetime without active propulsion; targeted for orbital AI compute satellites to minimize latency |
| **V1.5 / V2 Mini / V2 Mobile / V3** | Starlink satellite generation designations: V1.5 (current operational majority, ~306 kg), V2 Mini (active deployment, ~800 kg), V2 Mobile (D2C-optimized, ~1,500 kg, 2027), V3 (Starship-era, ~2,000 kg, 1 Tbps) |
| **VWAP** | Volume-Weighted Average Price — the average price of a security weighted by trading volume over a specified period (used for 30-day IPO stabilization tracking) |

## W

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **WACC** | Weighted Average Cost of Capital — the blended cost of debt and equity financing used as a discount rate in discounted cash flow (DCF) valuation |

---

*Terms defined as they specifically apply within the context of this dossier. For general aerospace and telecommunications definitions, consult the CCSDS, 3GPP, and IEEE standards bodies.*
