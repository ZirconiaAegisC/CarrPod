## 11.3 Medium-Term Horizon (FY2028–FY2030)

### 11.3.1 Starship

- **Operational cadence:** Weekly launches from multiple pads (Starbase, LC-39A, SLC-37)
- **Full-stack reusability:** Both booster and upper stage recovered and rapidly reused (<48-hour turnaround demonstrated)
- **Commercial manifest:** 30+ external customer launches/year at <$20M per launch
- **Crew certification:** NASA human-rating for Starship for Artemis and LEO crew transfer
- **Point-to-point cargo:** Initial DoD rapid-response logistics demonstrations

### 11.3.2 Starlink

- **V3 constellation:** 12,000 V3 satellites operational; total capacity >12 Pbps
- **Subscriber base:** 50M globally
- **D2C V2 Mobile:** 5G NR NTN operational; 50M+ monthly unique devices connected
- **Enterprise services:** Aviation (1,000+ aircraft), maritime (5,000+ vessels), military (allied forces)
- **ARPU:** Stabilized at $55–60/month blended; enterprise revenue ~20% of segment

### 11.3.3 AI Compute

- **Orbital fleet:** 2,000 operational AI compute satellites
- **Commercial revenue:** $20B+ annualized (inference-as-a-service and enterprise)
- **xAI Grok:** Exclusive inference capacity for Grok-4/Grok-5 model families
- **Terafab Phase 2:** Volume chip production online; satellite manufacturing rate of 50+/day

### 11.3.4 Human Spaceflight

- **Mars cargo mission:** Uncrewed Starship Mars transit in 2028 window (opportunistic)
- **Lunar surface base (Artemis Base Camp):** SpaceX as logistics provider for habitation and cargo
- **Private astronaut missions:** 2–4 per year via Starship (DearMoon replacement, Polaris III on Starship)

## 11.4 Long-Term Horizon (FY2031–FY2035)

### 11.4.1 Mature State Projection

By 2035, SpaceX is positioned to operate:

- **Launch:** 500+ Starship launches annually; Falcon 9 retired; marginal cost <$5M per launch
- **Starlink:** 100M+ subscribers; $60B+ annual revenue; operating margin >50%
- **AI Compute:** 100,000 orbital AI satellites; dominant position in global inference market; $150B+ annual revenue
- **Government:** Sustained $5B+/year revenue from NASA, DoD, allied nations; Mars mission contracts
- **Terafab:** 1M+ chip-scale packages produced per year; commercial chip sales to third parties

### 11.4.2 Mars Colony Initiation

SpaceX's long-term objective — a self-sustaining city on Mars — enters initial execution phase with:
- Regular cargo missions (2028, 2031, 2033 windows)
- First crewed Mars mission in 2031–2033 window
- ISRU propellant production demonstrated by 2033
- Initial surface infrastructure: power (solar/nuclear), habitation, greenhouse
