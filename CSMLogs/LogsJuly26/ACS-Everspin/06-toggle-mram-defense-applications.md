# Toggle MRAM: Defense Applications & Government Contracts

## Strategic Importance to U.S. Defense

Toggle MRAM occupies a unique position in the U.S. defense electronics
supply chain as the only domestic, commercially proven, non-volatile
memory technology qualified for radiation-hardened applications. The
Department of Defense has designated Everspin's Toggle MRAM process as
a strategic trusted foundry capability, recognizing that the magnetic
tunnel junction fabrication expertise concentrated at the Chandler,
Arizona facility represents a national security asset not available
from offshore semiconductor suppliers.

The technology's inherent characteristics align with defense system
requirements: radiation tolerance (both total dose and single-event
effects), operation across full military temperature range, data
retention without power for two decades, and unlimited write endurance
for continuous data logging in flight recorders, mission computers, and
weapon system controllers.

## $40 Million Defense Subcontract

In 2025, Everspin was awarded a $40 million subcontract by a major
U.S. defense prime contractor for Toggle MRAM process technology
development and advanced engineering services. The subcontract spans
2.5 years and covers:

1. **Process sustainment and enhancement**: Maintaining and improving
   the Chandler fab's MTJ fabrication capability to meet evolving
   defense system requirements.

2. **Radiation-hardened design development**: Engineering radiation-
   hardened-by-design (RHBD) techniques for Toggle MRAM cells and
   peripheral circuits, targeting improved total ionizing dose (TID)
   tolerance beyond 300 krad(Si) and reduced single-event transient
   (SET) sensitivity.

3. **Technology insertion support**: Assisting the prime contractor
   with the integration of Toggle MRAM into next-generation defense
   platforms, including avionics upgrades, radar processing systems,
   and secure communications equipment.

4. **Supply chain assurance**: Maintaining qualified domestic
   manufacturing capacity to guarantee multi-decade supply of
   radiation-tolerant non-volatile memory for defense systems with
   20–30 year operational lifetimes.

This subcontract represents the largest single program award in
Everspin's corporate history and validates the strategic value of
the company's Toggle MRAM technology to national security.

## $14.6 Million DoD Facility Sustainment Contract

Complementing the development subcontract, Everspin was separately
awarded a $14.6 million contract from the Department of Defense for
MRAM facility sustainment at the Chandler site. This contract funds
the operational costs of maintaining the trusted 200mm MRAM
fabrication line, including:

- Equipment maintenance and calibration for MTJ deposition tools
  (ion beam deposition, RF sputtering, magnetic annealing furnaces)
- Cleanroom infrastructure (particle monitoring, chemical management,
  HVAC and humidity control)
- Technical personnel sustaining engineering
- Process monitoring and statistical process control systems
- Cybersecurity compliance for defense contractor information systems

The facility sustainment contract ensures that the trusted domestic
MRAM production capability remains available and qualified for
defense programs independent of commercial demand cycles.

## Defense System Applications

Toggle MRAM has been designed into numerous defense platforms across
all branches of the U.S. military. Representative applications include:

- **Avionics mission computers**: Replacing battery-backed SRAM to
  eliminate maintenance burden and ensure data integrity through
  power cycles.

- **Radar digital signal processors**: Storing calibration coefficients,
  beam-forming tables, and waveform parameters with instant access and
  non-volatility.

- **Electronic warfare (EW) systems**: Storing threat libraries and
  jamming profiles with the ability to update in the field without
  block-erase delays.

- **Guided munitions and missiles**: Guidance and navigation data
  storage surviving gun-launch shock (tested to >30,000g) and
  radiation environments.

- **Satellite bus and payload controllers**: Radiation-tolerant
  configuration storage, telemetry buffering, and fault log recording.

- **Secure communications (COMSEC) equipment**: Cryptographic key
  storage with the property that keys are instantly erased by
  overwriting — true zeroization without residual magnetic imprint.

## Microchip Foundry Services Agreement

Everspin holds a foundry services agreement with Microchip Technology
Inc. (also headquartered in Chandler, Arizona) that provides
complementary manufacturing capabilities. Microchip's involvement
extends the trusted foundry ecosystem supporting defense MRAM supply,
offering both process support and potential second-source manufacturing
for defense programs requiring assured access to multiple domestic
foundries.

Microchip also serves as a distribution channel for Everspin products
into the broader embedded systems market, including defense
subcontractors and industrial clients who already use Microchip
microcontrollers and FPGAs. This relationship streamlines MRAM
integration for customers who can source both their processor and
their non-volatile memory through a single supplier.

## Qualification and Certification

Defense customers require extensive qualification data before
approving a memory component for system integration. Everspin's
Toggle MRAM products have been characterized across:

- MIL-STD-883 (Test Method Standard for Microcircuits)
- MIL-PRF-38535 (Performance Specification for Integrated Circuits)
- MIL-STD-750 (Test Methods for Semiconductor Devices)
- NASA EEE-INST-002 (Instructions for EEE Parts Selection)
- Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) Qualified Products List (QPL)

Radiation test reports are available to qualified defense customers
documenting TID tolerance, heavy-ion single-event latchup immunity,
and proton and neutron upset cross-sections. These reports form the
basis for radiation hardness assurance (RHA) classifications assigned
to each product variant.

## Strategic Advantages for Investors

From an investment perspective, Everspin's defense business provides:

- **Revenue visibility**: Multi-year contracts with firm delivery
  schedules provide predictable baseline revenue.

- **Margin support**: Defense-grade parts command significant price
  premiums over commercial equivalents, typically 3–10×.

- **Barriers to entry**: Qualified trusted foundry status,
  MIL-PRF-38535 QPL listing, and system-level qualification create
  high switching costs that protect the installed base.

- **Secular growth tailwind**: U.S. defense modernization programs
  increasingly require radiation-tolerant, maintenance-free memory
  solutions as systems become more complex and deployed in contested
  electromagnetic environments.
