## 2. TECHNOLOGY TREND LANDSCAPE

### 2.1 Solid Rocket Motor Demand Forecast

The U.S. SRM industrial base faces a structural demand-supply gap. Pre-Ukraine (2021), annual SRM demand was estimated at 60-80 units across all services. Post-Ukraine (2026), demand is projected at 200-250 units annually, driven by:

- **Munitions Replenishment:** GMLRS rounds expended in Ukraine at a rate exceeding 5,000/year. Production capacity pre-2022 was ~7,000/year. Target: 14,000/year by FY2027.
- **INDOPACOM Posture:** The Pacific Deterrence Initiative requires prepositioned munitions stocks across Japan, Guam, Philippines, and Australia.
- **Hypersonic Proliferation:** LRHW, CPS, HACM, and allied hypersonic programs (GCAP, AUKUS) require dedicated SRM stages.
- **Missile Defense:** Next-Generation Interceptor (NGI), THAAD ER, and PAC-3 MSE enhancements demand high-performance SRMs.

### 2.2 Electromagnetic Spectrum Operations (EMSO)

The DoD's 2023 Electromagnetic Spectrum Superiority Strategy elevated EMSO to an independent warfighting domain. Key developments:

- **Joint Electromagnetic Spectrum Operations (JEMSO):** Formalizing EW as a joint function
- **Cognitive EW:** AI/ML-driven emitter identification and adaptive jamming; L3Harris is an early leader via NGJ-LB cognitive capability and Palantir AIP integration
- **Distributed EW:** Networked jamming pods on attritable UAS and MALD-N decoys; L3Harris developing miniaturized GaN-based EW payloads
- **Allied Interoperability:** AUKUS Pillar II EW workstream; NATO EW Task Force standardization

### 2.3 Proliferated LEO (pLEO) Space Architecture

| Attribute | Legacy (GEO/MEO) | Proliferated LEO (SDA PWSA) |
|---|---|---|
| Satellites per Constellation | 4-8 | 100-500+ |
| Unit Cost | $500M-$2B | $15M-$40M |
| Development Cycle | 10-15 years | 2-4 years (Tranche model) |
| Resilience | Low (single-point failure) | High (mesh network; attrition-tolerant) |
| L3Harris Posture | Payload subcontractor | Prime: satellite bus + payload + integration |

The shift to pLEO architecture benefits L3Harris disproportionately because (a) it rewards manufacturing agility over exquisite engineering, (b) satellite buses are standardized and produced at volume, and (c) the 2-year Tranche model requires rapid integration skills that L3Harris has demonstrated across SDA Tranche 0, 1, and 2.

## 4. POLICY & REGULATORY CONTEXT

### 4.1 Defense Industrial Base Executive Orders

- **EO 14017 (America's Supply Chains):** Identified SRMs and specialty chemicals as critical supply chain vulnerabilities.
- **DPA Title III Investments:** $2.5B+ allocated to munitions industrial base expansion. L3Harris has been a direct recipient ($215M for SRM expansion).
- **DoD National Defense Industrial Strategy (NDIS) 2023:** Prioritizes resilient supply chains, workforce development, and flexible acquisition. Benefits merchant suppliers like L3Harris MSL.

### 4.2 Export Control & ITAR

- L3Harris is a major ITAR registrant; technology controlled under USML Categories IV (launch vehicles/missiles), XI (military electronics), and XV (spacecraft).
- AUKUS export control reforms (ITAR exemption for UK/Australia, expected 2027) would significantly reduce friction for Falcon IV and Link 16 sales to AUKUS partners.

## 5. RESEARCH CONCLUSIONS

1. **Structural Demand Growth:** The convergence of great-power competition, munitions restocking, and pLEO proliferation creates a 5+ year demand super-cycle for L3Harris's core capabilities.
2. **Oligopolistic Positioning:** L3Harris is the sole independent merchant SRM supplier, one of two NGJ primes, and a top-3 SDA satellite prime. Barriers to entry are exceptionally high across all three positions.
3. **Digital Differentiation:** The Palantir/Anduril/Amazon partnership ecosystem provides a competitive advantage that legacy primes cannot easily replicate without significant cultural and architectural transformation.
4. **IPO Catalyst:** The MSL IPO is a near-term (6-9 month) value-unlocking event that should re-rate the residual L3Harris entity as a higher-multiple sensors/communications/space franchise.
5. **Risk Balance:** Primary risks (budget dependency, SRM execution, fixed-price exposure) are well-understood and actively managed. The risk/reward skew is favorable.
