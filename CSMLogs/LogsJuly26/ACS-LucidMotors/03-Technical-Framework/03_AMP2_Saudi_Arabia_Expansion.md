# 03.03 — AMP-2 Saudi Arabia Global Expansion

## 3.3.1 Strategic Rationale

Lucid's manufacturing presence in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia represents the most ambitious geographic diversification of any Western EV manufacturer. The strategic logic operates on four levels:

1. **Sovereign Demand Anchor:** The Public Investment Fund (PIF), as Lucid's ~60% shareholder, has committed to purchase up to 100,000 Lucid vehicles over a 10-year period for government and quasi-government fleets. This provides a guaranteed demand floor that de-risks AMP-2's ramp.
2. **Regional Export Hub:** Saudi Arabia's geographic position — equidistant between European, African, and South Asian markets — and its membership in the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) free trade zone make AMP-2 a logical export base for right-hand-drive and left-hand-drive markets underserved by AMP-1.
3. **Cost Arbitrage:** Saudi Arabia offers industrial electricity at ~$0.048/kWh (versus ~$0.075/kWh in Arizona), competitive labor costs in manufacturing roles, zero corporate income tax for qualified manufacturing investments in King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC) for up to 20 years, and government-provided infrastructure (land, utilities, port access) under the PIF joint venture structure.
4. **Supply Chain Diversification:** A second manufacturing continent hedges against U.S.-specific risks including trade policy, logistics disruptions, and single-site dependency. Battery cell supply for AMP-2 is expected to source from regional and Chinese suppliers, diversifying away from Panasonic/Samsung SDI concentration at AMP-1.

---

## 3.3.2 Joint Venture Structure

**Entity:** Lucid Saudi Arabia Manufacturing Company (operating entity)  
**Ownership:** Lucid Group (majority, ~67%) / Public Investment Fund (minority, ~33%)  
**Governance:** Separate board with Lucid and PIF appointees; operational control retained by Lucid  
**Location:** King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC), Rabigh Governorate, Makkah Province, Saudi Arabia  
**Site Area:** ~1.35 million square meters (~335 acres) allocated  
**Investment Framework:**

- PIF provides land, infrastructure (power, water, roads, port connectivity), and incentives at no cost to the JV.
- Lucid contributes technology, manufacturing IP, equipment, and operational management.
- Capital expenditures for Phase 2 (150K CBU capacity) are jointly funded; PIF's share reduces Lucid's direct cash outlay.
- Lucid has committed to local workforce development with a target of 20% Saudi national employment at Phase 1 ramp, rising to 50%+ by 2030 under the Kingdom's Vision 2030 Saudization targets.

## 3.3.3 Phased Manufacturing Buildout

### Phase 1 — SKD Assembly (Operational: 2024–Present)

**Status:** Inaugurated September 2024; first vehicles delivered Q3 2025  
**Capacity:** 5,000 vehicles/year  
**Process:** Semi-knocked-down (SKD) assembly — vehicle kits (painted body shells, powertrains, interiors) shipped from AMP-1 Casa Grande to KAEC; final assembly, testing, and delivery performed at AMP-2.  
**Workforce:** ~250 employees (Phase 1)  
**Models:** Lucid Air (Touring and Grand Touring variants only)  
**Output (FY2025):** ~800 units assembled; used primarily for government fleet deliveries, Studio demonstrators, and regional homologation.

**SKD Logistics:** Kits are containerized at AMP-1, transported via Union Pacific rail to the Port of Long Beach, shipped via container vessel to King Abdullah Port (adjacent to KAEC, ~5 km from AMP-2), and trucked to the assembly line. End-to-end transit time: 28–35 days per container. Duty treatment: components enter under GCC harmonized tariff code exemptions for qualified industrial investments.

### Phase 2 — CBU Manufacturing (Under Construction: 2025–2027+)

**Status:** Ground broken Q1 2025; building shell construction underway as of Q2 2026  
**Target Completion:** 2027 (initial production); full ramp 2028–2029  
**Capacity:** 150,000 vehicles/year at full buildout  
**Scope:** Complete-build-unit (CBU) manufacturing — stamping, body shop, paint shop, general assembly, and battery pack assembly on site. This is a full replica of AMP-1's Phase 2, adapted for Saudi conditions.

**Phase 2 Facility Specifications:**

| Facility | Planned Area | Equipment |
|---|---|---|
| Stamping Shop | ~200,000 sq. ft. | Schuler press line (3,000-ton), coil-fed blanking |
| Body Shop | ~450,000 sq. ft. | 400+ robots, mixed-model (Air + Gravity + Midsize) |
| Paint Shop | ~300,000 sq. ft. | Dürr EcoRP painting robots, e-coat, clear coat |
| General Assembly | ~900,000 sq. ft. | Dual-line: Air/Gravity + Midsize; 70 JPH target |
| Battery Pack Assembly | ~150,000 sq. ft. | Cell-to-pack line; regional cell supply |
| Logistics Center | ~350,000 sq. ft. | Automated storage/retrieval; supplier park adjacent |

**Product Allocation (Phase 2):**

- Line 1: Lucid Air and Lucid Gravity for MENA and European export markets.
- Line 2: Midsize platform (Cosmos and Earth) for regional and Asian export markets.
- The 150K capacity split is planned as: ~50K Large platform, ~100K Midsize platform.

## 3.3.4 Regional Market Access

### Middle East & North Africa (MENA):

- GCC markets (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman) represent 50M+ population with high GDP per capita (~$30,000–$80,000) and government-led EV adoption incentives.
- Saudi Arabia alone targets 30% EV new car sales by 2030 under Vision 2030 and the Saudi Green Initiative. With a total addressable market of ~600,000 new vehicles/year, this implies ~180,000 EV units/year domestically by 2030.
- Lucid's first-mover advantage in Saudi premium EV manufacturing, combined with government fleet procurement commitments, positions it to capture 30–50% of the Saudi premium EV market.

### European Export:

- AMP-2's KAEC location, with direct container port access at King Abdullah Port, enables competitive shipping times to Southern European ports (Marseille, Barcelona, Genoa, Piraeus) — approximately 7–10 days transit.
- Lucid's existing European Studio network (12 locations) provides the retail infrastructure.
- AMP-2 production for Europe avoids the 10% U.S.-EU tariff on passenger vehicles and benefits from GCC free trade arrangements with select European nations.

### Asian Export:

- KAEC to India (Mumbai): ~5 days transit. Right-hand-drive capability can be engineered into the AMP-2 line.
- KAEC to Southeast Asia (Singapore, Jakarta): ~10–12 days transit.

## 3.3.5 Economic Impact & Government Relations

### Saudi Vision 2030 Alignment:

Lucid AMP-2 is a flagship project under Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 economic diversification program. The project contributes to:

- **Industrial development:** First full-scale automotive manufacturing facility in the Kingdom's history.
- **Job creation:** Projected 4,000–5,000 direct jobs and 15,000+ indirect jobs at full Phase 2 ramp.
- **Technology transfer:** Lucid has agreements in place for knowledge transfer to Saudi engineers and technicians, including training rotations at AMP-1 in Arizona.
- **Non-oil GDP:** Automotive manufacturing is a targeted non-oil GDP growth sector under the National Industrial Development and Logistics Program (NIDLP).

### Risk Considerations:

1. **Geopolitical:** Regional stability in the Middle East; U.S.-Saudi bilateral relations directly affect technology transfer licensing and export controls.
2. **Regulatory:** U.S. Department of Commerce export controls on advanced EV powertrain technology could constrain the scope of technology that can be relocated to Saudi Arabia.
3. **Currency:** Saudi riyal is pegged to the U.S. dollar at SAR 3.75 = $1.00, providing currency stability for dollar-denominated Lucid financials.
4. **Workforce Availability:** The Saudi automotive manufacturing workforce is nascent; heavy reliance on expatriate labor in early years may affect unit labor cost projections and Saudization compliance.
5. **Capital Commitment:** The PIF's commitment to Phase 2 funding is subject to the PIF's overall investment portfolio priorities, which compete with other Vision 2030 giga-projects (NEOM, Red Sea Project, Qiddiya, etc.).

## 3.3.6 Competitive Dynamics in MENA

| Manufacturer | Saudi Presence | EV Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Lucid | AMP-2 (only SA-based EV OEM) | Full CBU manufacturing by 2027 |
| Tesla | No local manufacturing | Export from Fremont/Berlin; limited SA presence |
| BYD | Exploring SA assembly | CKD assembly discussions with SA government |
| Ceer (PIF/Tech JV) | Planned 2025+ | Saudi national EV brand; mid-market positioning |
| Hyundai | No EV manufacturing | Gasoline vehicle assembly; exploring EV |
| VinFast | Announced interest | No commitment |

Ceer, a Saudi national EV brand launched by PIF in partnership with Hon Hai (Foxconn), represents a potential competitive dynamic. Ceer targets the mid-market segment (~$35,000–$55,000 ASP), which positions it below Lucid's premium portfolio. However, both Lucid and Ceer ultimately serve the same sovereign shareholder (PIF), and a complementary rather than directly competitive product strategy appears to be the intended outcome. Lucid = premium/luxury; Ceer = mass-market.

## 3.3.7 Financial Implications for Lucid Consolidated

- AMP-2 Phase 1 SKD revenues flow through Lucid's consolidated financials with modest margin contribution (SKD kits recognized as intercompany transfer).
- Phase 2 capex of approximately $2.0–2.5 billion (total) will be funded roughly 50/50 between Lucid and PIF. Lucid's $1.0–1.25B share must be absorbed within its consolidated capex envelope (FY2026 guidance: $1.2–1.4B total company capex, which includes early Phase 2 spending).
- The DDTL facility ($500M, April 2026) is explicitly available to fund AMP-2 capital expenditures.
- Full Phase 2 ramp at 150,000 units/year would roughly double Lucid's global manufacturing capacity from ~90K (AMP-1) to ~240K (combined), creating the production infrastructure necessary for a positive FCF thesis.
