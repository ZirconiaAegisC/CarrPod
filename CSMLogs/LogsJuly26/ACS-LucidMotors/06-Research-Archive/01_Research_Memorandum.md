## 6.1 Scope and Methodology

This research memorandum documents the analytical framework, data sources, assumptions, and scenario modeling underpinning the ACS-LucidMotors-EV-Manufacturing dossier (dated July 2026). The analysis encompasses Lucid Group's vehicle portfolio, manufacturing operations on two continents, autonomous mobility strategy, software platform, financial position, capital structure, and implementation roadmap.

### Analytical Methodology:

1. **Bottom-Up Production Modeling:** Quarterly production and delivery volumes were reconstructed from Lucid's quarterly earnings releases (10-Q, 8-K), investor presentations, and management commentary on earnings calls. Production capacity was modeled against installed manufacturing footprint (AMP-1 90K/year, AMP-2 5K SKD + 150K CBU) with utilization rate assumptions informed by industry benchmarks.

2. **Cost Structure Analysis:** Unit-level cost breakdowns were derived from Lucid's reported cost of goods sold (COGS), inventory balances, headcount disclosures, supplier relationships (Panasonic, Samsung SDI, Aston Martin), and industry cost benchmarks for battery cells, electric drive units, stamping, and vehicle assembly.

3. **Margin Pathway Projection:** Gross margin and free cash flow breakeven projections employed a fixed/variable cost separation model with sensitivity to production volume as the primary independent variable. Volume scenarios were derived from product launch timelines and capacity expansion schedules.

4. **Capital Structure Analysis:** Dilution scenarios were built from Lucid's reported capital structure — shares outstanding, convertible notes (2026, 2028), preferred stock (April 2026 Series A), and committed facilities (DDTL, PIF term loan) — with PIF ownership modeled under varying future capital formation assumptions.

5. **Comparative Analysis:** Industry benchmarks were drawn from Tesla (10-K, 10-Q), Rivian (10-K, 10-Q), and broader automotive manufacturing data (IHS Markit, Bloomberg NEF, McKinsey Center for Future Mobility). Where Lucid-specific data was unavailable, estimates are labeled as such and were informed by the closest available peer.

## 6.2 Key Assumptions

### Macroeconomic:
- Brent crude oil price: $70–85/bbl average FY2026–FY2029 (supports Saudi fiscal capacity and PIF investment appetite)
- U.S. Federal Reserve policy rate: 3.50–4.00% through FY2027; gradual normalization thereafter
- U.S. GDP growth: 1.8–2.3% real GDP growth FY2026–FY2029 (no recession assumed in base case)
- Inflation (CPI): 2.3–2.8% (moderating from 2024–2025 levels)
- U.S. dollar index (DXY): 100–110 range (strong dollar assumption)

### Industry:
- Global EV penetration: 18% of new car sales in 2025, rising to 25% by 2028, 35% by 2030 (Bloomberg NEF Electric Vehicle Outlook 2025 baseline)
- U.S. EV market: 9% penetration in 2025 (1.4M units), rising to 15% by 2028 (2.5M units)
- Premium/luxury EV segment ($70K+ ASP): ~350,000 units/year globally (2025), growing to ~600,000 units by 2028
- Battery cell cost: $95–110/kWh (pack level, NMC) at end of 2025, declining to $75–$90 by 2028, $55–$70 by 2030
- U.S. Inflation Reduction Act (IRA): Section 30D consumer tax credit ($7,500) and Section 45X advanced manufacturing production credit remain in effect through the forecast period. No material repeal or modification assumed.

### Lucid-Specific:
- Air production plateaus at 15,000–18,000 units/year (sedan market structural decline)
- Gravity captures 5–8% of global full-size luxury SUV segment by 2028
- Midsize platform (Cosmos + Earth) captures 2–4% of global mid-size premium electric SUV segment by 2029
- PIF continues to provide capital as needed through FY2029 (primary financing assumption)
- Uber robotaxi partnership deploys on schedule (late 2026 commercial launch, scaling through 2028)
- AMP-2 Phase 2 CBU construction proceeds on schedule (no material geopolitical or regulatory delays)
- No acquisitions, divestitures, or material corporate restructurings

### Scenario Modeling:

Three scenarios were constructed for the FY2026–FY2029 period:

**Base Case (60% probability weighting):**
- FY2026 production: 26,000 units (guidance midpoint)
- Gravity ramp sustained; Midsize platform enters volume production H2 2027
- AMP-2 Phase 2 CBU production begins 2028
- PIF provides capital as needed; 2026 Convert refinanced via PIF-led exchange
- FY2029 production: ~165,000 units; FCF breakeven achieved

**Bull Case (20% probability weighting):**
- Gravity demand exceeds expectations; FY2026 production 30,000 units
- Midsize platform pulled forward to mid-2027; AMP-2 CBU begins late 2027
- Uber robotaxi demand accelerates; additional AV partnership announced
- Margin improvement faster than base case (gross margin positive by mid-2027)
- FY2029 production: ~210,000 units; FCF positive by mid-2028

**Bear Case (20% probability weighting):**
- Gravity demand softens; FY2026 production 22,000 units
- Midsize platform delayed to 2028; AMP-2 CBU delayed to 2029
- Cash burn forces dilutive equity raise at distressed valuation
- PIF assumes >75% ownership through repeated structured capital injections
- FY2029 production: ~100,000 units; FCF remains negative through forecast period
- Equity value for public (non-PIF) shareholders approaches zero

## 6.4 Limitations and Caveats

1. **Forward-Looking Statements:** This dossier contains forward-looking projections, including revenue, production, margin, and free cash flow estimates. These are inherently uncertain and subject to material revision based on execution, market conditions, regulatory changes, and capital availability. Actual results may differ materially.

2. **Lucid Data Limitations:** Lucid does not disclose detailed unit economics (bill of materials, per-unit cost breakdown, gross margin by product line, DreamDrive Pro take rates by model). Estimates marked "est." or "estimated" represent the research team's best approximations based on available data and industry benchmarks. These estimates may be inaccurate.

3. **PIF Intent Inferred, Not Stated:** The analysis of PIF's strategic calculus (Section 04.03) is based on publicly available information about PIF's investment history, Vision 2030 objectives, and portfolio strategy. PIF has not publicly stated its specific intentions regarding future Lucid funding. Statements about PIF's continued support are analytical assumptions, not guarantees.

4. **Autonomous Vehicle Regulatory Uncertainty:** The regulatory pathway for commercial robotaxi deployment — particularly the NHTSA FMVSS exemption process and CPUC deployment permit — involves uncertain timelines and approval criteria. Delays in regulatory milestones could materially alter the robotaxi revenue projections.

5. **No Investment Recommendation:** This dossier is prepared for internal research and strategic analysis purposes. It does not constitute investment advice, a recommendation to buy or sell securities, or a valuation opinion. The dossier does not include a target price, discounted cash flow valuation, or relative valuation analysis.

6. **Conflict of Interest:** As of the date of this memorandum, Advanced Climate Solutions and its affiliates do not hold a material position (long or short) in LCID common stock, preferred stock, or derivatives. This may change without notice.

7. **Currency:** All financial figures are in United States dollars (USD) unless otherwise noted.

8. **Date of Analysis:** All data, projections, and analysis are as of July 15, 2026, unless otherwise dated in the text.
