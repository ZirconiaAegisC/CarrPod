## 4.3.2 April 2026 Capital Raise: Detailed Terms

On April 10, 2026, Lucid announced a $1.05 billion liquidity package anchored by the Public Investment Fund. The transaction closed in two tranches:

### Tranche 1: PIF Series A Convertible Preferred Stock ($550M)

- **Instrument:** Series A Convertible Preferred Stock, newly authorized class
- **Dividend:** 6.0% per annum, payable-in-kind (PIK) — accrued dividends increase the liquidation preference; no cash dividend obligation
- **Conversion Price:** $2.87 per share of LCID common stock (represents a 15% premium to the 30-day VWAP at pricing)
- **Conversion Rights:** Holder (PIF) may convert at any time; mandatory conversion if LCID common stock trades above 200% of conversion price for 20 of 30 consecutive trading days
- **Liquidation Preference:** Senior to common equity; pari passu with any future preferred classes; $550M plus accrued PIK dividends
- **Voting Rights:** Votes with common stock on an as-converted basis
- **Redemption:** Callable by Lucid after 3 years if common stock price exceeds 250% of conversion price

### Tranche 2: PIF Common Equity ($300M)

- **Instrument:** Common stock, LCID
- **Pricing:** $2.50/share (approximate; 120 million shares)
- **Lockup:** 180 days from closing

### Tranche 3: Uber Common Equity ($200M)

- **Instrument:** Common stock, LCID
- **Pricing:** $2.50/share (80 million shares)
- **Lockup:** 360 days from closing (extended lockup relative to PIF common, reflecting strategic partnership duration)
- **Commercial Agreement:** Tied to the robotaxi fleet partnership (see Section 03.04)

### Tranche 4: Delayed-Draw Term Loan ($500M)

- **Lender:** Public Investment Fund
- **Amount:** $500 million committed, undrawn at close
- **Availability Period:** Through December 31, 2028
- **Interest Rate:** SOFR + 250 basis points (floating, paid in cash quarterly)
- **Use of Proceeds:** AMP-2 Phase 2 capital expenditures; general corporate purposes
- **Security:** Unsecured; ranks pari passu with existing senior debt
- **Covenants:** Minimum liquidity covenant of $750M; negative pledge on material IP; cross-default with other PIF facilities

**2026 Convertible Note Refinancing:**

The $1.75 billion 2026 convertible notes mature in December 2026. At the current LCID share price (~$2.30–2.80), the notes are trading well below their $12.00 conversion price, making conversion unlikely. Lucid must either:

1. **Refinance:** Issue new convertible or straight debt. Challenging given negative free cash flow and high leverage.
2. **PIF Backstop:** The PIF has historically provided capital on demand. A PIF-led refinancing — either a new convertible note, an extension, or a straight debt instrument — is the most probable outcome.
3. **Equity Raise:** Dilutive common equity issuance to repay the notes. Possible but shareholder-dilutive at current depressed share price.
4. **Exchange Offer:** Offer noteholders new securities (longer-dated convert, equity + cash, etc.) in exchange for the 2026 notes.

The 2026 note maturity is the single most important near-term capital markets event for Lucid and will likely be addressed in H2 2026.

## 4.3.5 PIF's Strategic Calculus

The Public Investment Fund's cumulative investment in Lucid exceeds $10 billion (across PIPE, private placements, common equity, preferred stock, and debt facilities). At LCID's current market capitalization (~$6–7 billion), PIF's position carries a substantial unrealized loss. PIF's continued capital commitment can be understood through the following strategic lens:

### Why PIF Continues to Fund:

1. **Vision 2030 Flagship:** Lucid is the anchor tenant of Saudi Arabia's automotive manufacturing sector. Abandoning the investment would signal failure of a high-profile Vision 2030 industrial development initiative.
2. **AMP-2 Is Sunk:** PIF has committed billions to the KAEC manufacturing campus, including land, infrastructure, and joint venture equity. Walking away would strand those assets with no alternative occupant.
3. **Technology Access:** Lucid's EV technology (900V+ architecture, wound-stator motors, battery pack engineering) represents a multi-decade intellectual property base that PIF cannot readily replicate through alternative investments. Ceer (the Saudi national EV brand) is a complementary mass-market play but lacks Lucid's premium technology.
4. **Low Cost of Capital:** PIF's cost of funding is effectively the opportunity cost of Saudi Arabia's foreign reserves and oil revenue. At a sovereign level, the financial loss on Lucid is immaterial relative to the Kingdom's ~$700 billion in foreign reserves and the strategic value of industrial diversification.
5. **Downside Protection:** PIF's preferred stock structure (6% PIK, senior to common) provides a degree of downside protection that common equity holders lack. In a liquidation or restructuring scenario, PIF would recover its preferred capital ahead of common shareholders.
6. **Control Premium:** At 60%+ ownership (fully diluted), PIF effectively controls Lucid's strategic direction. The value of controlling the only premium EV manufacturer with U.S. and Saudi dual-manufacturing capability may exceed the mark-to-market value of PIF's common equity position.

### PIF Risk Factors:

1. **Oil Price Sensitivity:** Sustained low oil prices (<$60/bbl Brent) would pressure Saudi fiscal balances and could reduce PIF's appetite for continued Lucid funding.
2. **Vision 2030 Prioritization:** The Kingdom is simultaneously funding multiple giga-projects (NEOM, Red Sea, Qiddiya, Diriyah Gate, etc.). Budgetary prioritization could shift capital away from automotive and toward real estate and tourism.
3. **Political Leadership Change:** PIF's strategy is ultimately directed by the Crown Prince and PIF Governor. Any change in leadership could alter the calculus.
4. **Diversification Imperative Fading:** If Saudi Arabia successfully diversifies its economy through other means (tourism, mining, entertainment, sports), the strategic imperative to maintain a money-losing automotive investment diminishes.

## 4.3.6 Uber's Strategic Rationale

Uber's $200 million equity investment and 35,000-vehicle robotaxi commitment can be assessed from several angles:

1. **Supplier Diversification:** Uber's autonomous mobility strategy currently relies heavily on Waymo (Phoenix, Austin) and Motional (Las Vegas). Lucid provides a third vehicle supplier, reducing Uber's dependency risk.
2. **Purpose-Built Vehicles:** Unlike Tesla Model Ys retrofitted with autonomous sensors (Uber's current AV fleet), Lucid can deliver vehicles purpose-engineered for autonomous ride-hailing from the production line — no aftermarket sensor integration required. This reduces per-vehicle deployment cost and time.
3. **Downside Protection for Lucid:** Uber's equity commitment locks in a commercial partner for Lucid's autonomous vehicle production. This creates a minimum viable demand pool for the robotaxi vehicle variant, de-risking the capital investment in autonomous vehicle engineering.

**Total incremental capital required FY2026–FY2029: approximately $8–10 billion.**

PIF is expected to provide the majority of this funding, either through direct equity investments, convertible preferred instruments, debt facilities, or a combination. The key question is the **form** of future PIF capital:

- **Equity at current depressed prices** would be highly dilutive to existing common shareholders. PIF's control position means this dilution primarily affects public float holders.
- **Preferred stock / structured instruments** (like the April 2026 Series A) preserve common equity upside for existing holders while providing PIF with seniority and yield.
- **Asset-level financing** (e.g., a PIF-funded leasing entity that owns AMP-2 and leases it to Lucid) would remove capex from Lucid's balance sheet while preserving operational control.
