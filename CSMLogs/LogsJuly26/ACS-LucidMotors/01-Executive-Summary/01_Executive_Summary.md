### 1.0 Strategic Positioning

Lucid Group occupies a singular position in the global electric vehicle landscape. The company has achieved what no other post-Tesla startup has replicated: vertical integration of proprietary electric drive unit technology, a dual-model luxury portfolio commanding segment-leading efficiency metrics, two operational manufacturing campuses on separate continents, and a capital structure anchored by the sovereign wealth of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. The appointment of Silvio Napoli as CEO in May 2026 signals a transition from founder-led engineering culture to scaled industrial execution, with explicit focus on manufacturing throughput, cost discipline, and commercialization of the autonomous mobility business.

### 1.2 Manufacturing Footprint

**AMP-1 (Casa Grande, Arizona):** 90,000 units/year installed capacity on 590 acres. Phase 1 complete at 34,000 units; Phase 2 general assembly, body shop, and stamping operational. Produces Lucid Air sedan (three variants: Pure, Touring, Grand Touring) and Lucid Gravity SUV (launched Q4 2024). Currently undergoing expansion to accommodate Midsize platform tooling with $1.2–1.4B planned FY2026 capex.

**AMP-2 (King Abdullah Economic City, Saudi Arabia):** Joint venture with Public Investment Fund (PIF). Phase 1 operational at 5,000 units/year semi-knocked-down (SKD) assembly. Phase 2 under construction targeting 150,000 units/year complete-build-unit (CBU) capacity. First Saudi-assembled Lucid Air units delivered Q3 2025. Strategic mandate: sovereign wealth anchor demand, regional export hub for MENA and European markets.

### 1.4 Autonomous Mobility & Robotaxi

Lucid's autonomous strategy rests on three pillars: (1) Atlas electric drive unit as a licensable propulsion platform for autonomous vehicle fleets, (2) a strategic partnership with Nuro Inc. for autonomous delivery vehicle technology, and (3) an Uber Technologies collaboration encompassing a minimum 35,000-vehicle robotaxi commitment with $500M total investment ($200M Uber direct + Gravity and Midsize platform vehicles). The California Department of Motor Vehicles granted Lucid a driverless testing permit in Q2 2026. Seventy-five engineering vehicles have been delivered to fleet partners for validation. Commercial robotaxi service is anticipated to commence in late 2026.
