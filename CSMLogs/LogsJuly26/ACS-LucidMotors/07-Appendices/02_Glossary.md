# 07.02 — Glossary of Terms

## A

**ABS:** Anti-lock Braking System. Electronic safety system preventing wheel lockup during braking.

**ADAS:** Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems. Electronic systems that assist drivers with driving and parking functions. Lucid's implementation is DreamDrive (standard) and DreamDrive Pro (enhanced).

**AMP-1:** Advanced Manufacturing Plant 1. Lucid's primary manufacturing facility located in Casa Grande, Arizona. Installed capacity of 90,000 vehicles per year.

**AMP-2:** Advanced Manufacturing Plant 2. Lucid's second manufacturing facility, a joint venture with the Public Investment Fund, located in King Abdullah Economic City (KAEC), Saudi Arabia. Phase 1 SKD (5,000 units/year) operational; Phase 2 CBU (150,000 units/year) under construction.

**ASP:** Average Selling Price. Total vehicle revenue divided by total vehicles delivered in a given period.

**Atlas Drive Unit:** Lucid's proprietary electric drive unit (motor, inverter, transmission) marketed to third-party OEMs and used internally. Supply agreement with Aston Martin signed 2023.

## B

**Bill of Materials (BOM):** The complete list of raw materials, components, and sub-assemblies required to manufacture a vehicle, along with their quantities and costs.

**Body Shop:** Manufacturing facility where the vehicle's structural body (body-in-white) is assembled from stamped metal panels through welding, riveting, and adhesive bonding.

**Body-in-White (BiW):** The welded sheet metal structure of a vehicle body before painting and before the addition of moving parts (doors, hood, decklid), trim, chassis, or powertrain.

**Brent Crude:** Global benchmark price for crude oil, reflecting oil produced in the North Sea. Relevant to Saudi Arabia's fiscal capacity and PIF's investment capability.

## C

**CBU:** Completely Built-Up. A fully manufactured vehicle, assembled from stamped body panels through to a finished, drivable unit. Contrasts with SKD and CKD assembly.

**CCS (Combined Charging System):** A standardized electric vehicle charging connector that integrates AC (Level 1/2) and DC (Level 3 fast charging) in a single port. Used by Lucid and most non-Tesla EVs in North America and Europe.

**Cd (Coefficient of Drag):** A dimensionless measure of aerodynamic efficiency. Lower values indicate less air resistance. Lucid Air Pure holds the production vehicle record at Cd 0.197.

**CEO:** Chief Executive Officer. The highest-ranking executive, responsible for overall company strategy and operations. Silvio Napoli has held this position since May 2026.

**CFO:** Chief Financial Officer. Executive responsible for financial management, capital markets, treasury, and investor relations. Gagan Dhingra holds this position.

**COGS:** Cost of Goods Sold. The direct costs attributable to the production and delivery of vehicles, including materials, labor, manufacturing overhead, and logistics.

**CPUC:** California Public Utilities Commission. Regulatory body responsible for permitting commercial autonomous vehicle passenger service in California.

**CKD:** Completely Knocked Down. A vehicle manufacturing method in which all parts and components are shipped unassembled, and the vehicle is fully assembled at the destination facility. Contrasts with CBU and SKD.

## D

**D2C (Direct-to-Consumer):** A retail model in which the manufacturer sells vehicles directly to customers through company-owned stores and online platforms, rather than through independent franchise dealers. Lucid uses this model in North America.

**DC Fast Charging:** High-power electric vehicle charging using direct current, typically delivering 50–350 kW. Lucid's 900V+ architecture supports up to 300 kW charging, adding ~200 miles of range in approximately 12 minutes.

**DDTL:** Delayed-Draw Term Loan. A committed loan facility that the borrower (Lucid) can draw upon over a specified availability period. The PIF provided a $500 million DDTL in April 2026, available through December 2028.

**DreamDrive:** Lucid's standard advanced driver-assistance system (ADAS), included on all vehicles. Provides adaptive cruise control, lane-keeping assist, blind-spot monitoring, and automatic emergency braking.

**DreamDrive Pro:** Lucid's enhanced ADAS, available as a $9,000–$10,000 option or ~$150/month subscription. Adds Highway Assist (hands-free driving on mapped highways), Automated Lane Change, and a hardware suite (Mobileye EyeQ6, surround cameras, radar) capable of supporting future Level 3 autonomy.

## E

**EDU:** Electric Drive Unit. The combined assembly of electric motor, inverter, and single-speed transmission that propels an electric vehicle. Lucid's proprietary EDU design achieves specific power of >10.5 kW/kg.

**EPA Range:** The estimated driving range for an electric vehicle on a full charge, as tested and certified by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency using standardized test cycles. Lucid Air Grand Touring holds the longest EPA range rating of any production EV at 512 miles.

**EV:** Electric Vehicle. A vehicle propelled by one or more electric motors, using energy stored in rechargeable battery packs.

## F

**FCF:** Free Cash Flow. Operating cash flow minus capital expenditures. A measure of a company's ability to generate cash after maintaining or expanding its asset base. Lucid's FCF is currently deeply negative.

**FMVSS:** Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards. U.S. regulations administered by NHTSA that specify minimum safety performance requirements for motor vehicles. Purpose-built autonomous vehicles without steering wheels or pedals require FMVSS exemptions.

**FOV:** Field of View. The angular extent of what a camera or sensor can observe, typically expressed in degrees.

## G

**GA (General Assembly):** The manufacturing line where painted vehicle bodies are fitted with the powertrain, interior, exterior trim, glass, and all finishing components to produce a completed vehicle.

**GCC:** Gulf Cooperation Council. A regional political and economic union comprising Saudi Arabia, United Arab Emirates, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, and Oman. The GCC functions as a free trade zone, facilitating AMP-2 vehicle exports within the region.

**Gross Margin:** Revenue minus Cost of Goods Sold (COGS), divided by Revenue, expressed as a percentage. A measure of per-unit profitability before operating expenses. In Q1 2026, Lucid's gross margin was approximately -110%.

## H

**HMI:** Human-Machine Interface. The means by which a driver or passenger interacts with the vehicle's systems — screen displays, touchscreens, voice control, physical controls, and haptic feedback.

## I

**IP:** Instrument Panel. The dashboard assembly in a vehicle, including the structure, displays, controls, air vents, and trim surfaces.

**IRA (Inflation Reduction Act):** U.S. legislation enacted in 2022 that includes Section 30D ($7,500 consumer EV tax credit) and Section 45X (advanced manufacturing production credit for battery cells and modules produced in the U.S.). Lucid benefits from both provisions.

## J

**JPH:** Jobs Per Hour. A measure of a manufacturing line's throughput rate, representing the number of completed units produced per hour. Lucid's GA1 line runs at approximately 20 JPH; GA2 is designed for 30 JPH.

## K

**KAEC:** King Abdullah Economic City. A planned urban development and special economic zone on Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast, located in Rabigh Governorate, Makkah Province. Home to AMP-2 and King Abdullah Port.

**kWh:** Kilowatt-hour. A unit of energy equal to one kilowatt of power sustained for one hour. Used to measure battery pack capacity (e.g., Lucid Air Pure: 92 kWh pack).

**kW (Kilowatt):** A unit of electric power equal to 1,000 watts. Used in reference to charging power (e.g., 300 kW DC fast charging) and motor output (e.g., Atlas EDU: up to 500 kW peak).

## L

**LFP:** Lithium Iron Phosphate. A lithium-ion battery chemistry using iron phosphate as the cathode material. Lower energy density than NMC but lower cost, longer cycle life, and improved thermal stability. Lucid is evaluating LFP for the Midsize platform to achieve the sub-$50K entry price.

**Liquidation Preference:** The amount that preferred stockholders are entitled to receive in a liquidation, merger, or sale before any distribution is made to common stockholders. The PIF's Series A Preferred Stock carries a $550 million liquidation preference plus accrued dividends.

**LCID:** The Nasdaq Global Select Market stock ticker symbol for Lucid Group, Inc.

## M

**MENA:** Middle East and North Africa. A geographic region encompassing the Arabian Peninsula, Levant, and North African states. A target market for AMP-2 vehicle exports.

**Midsize Platform:** Lucid's forthcoming vehicle platform targeting the mid-size SUV segment. Two models announced: Lucid Cosmos (two-row SUV) and Lucid Earth (SUV variant), with a third model to follow. Entry price below $50,000. Production ramping in 2027.

**MPGe:** Miles Per Gallon Equivalent. A measure of fuel efficiency applied to electric vehicles, quantifying the distance traveled on the energy equivalent of one gallon of gasoline. Lucid Air Pure achieves 146 MPGe (EPA combined).

## N

**NHTSA:** National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. The U.S. federal agency responsible for vehicle safety standards (FMVSS), recalls, and fuel economy regulations.

**NIDLP:** National Industrial Development and Logistics Program. A Saudi Vision 2030 program aimed at transforming the Kingdom into a leading industrial and logistics hub. Lucid AMP-2 is a flagship project under this program.

**NMC:** Nickel Manganese Cobalt. A lithium-ion battery cathode chemistry offering high energy density and power output. Used in Lucid's current battery packs (supplied by Panasonic Energy and Samsung SDI). More expensive than LFP but higher energy density.

## O

**OEM:** Original Equipment Manufacturer. A company that produces vehicles (e.g., Lucid, Tesla, Ford). In the context of the Atlas drive unit, external OEM refers to companies purchasing Lucid's EDU for their own vehicles (e.g., Aston Martin).

**OTA:** Over-the-Air. A method of delivering software updates to vehicles wirelessly via cellular or Wi-Fi connection, without requiring a visit to a service center. Lucid delivers major UX releases approximately every 6–8 weeks via OTA.

**OpEx:** Operating Expenses. The ongoing costs of running a business beyond direct manufacturing costs, including research and development, selling, general, and administrative expenses.

## P

**PIF:** Public Investment Fund. The sovereign wealth fund of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. Lucid's largest shareholder (~60% ownership, fully diluted). The PIF has committed over $10 billion to Lucid since 2018.

**PIK (Payment-in-Kind):** A form of dividend or interest payment in which the obligation is settled by issuing additional securities rather than paying cash. The PIF's Series A Preferred Stock carries a 6.0% PIK dividend.

**PIPE:** Private Investment in Public Equity. A transaction in which a public company issues shares to accredited investors in a private placement. PIF participated in a $915 million PIPE concurrent with Lucid's 2021 SPAC merger.

## Q

**10-K:** Annual report filed with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) containing comprehensive financial statements, business description, risk factors, and management discussion and analysis.

**10-Q:** Quarterly report filed with the SEC containing unaudited financial statements and management discussion for the most recent quarter.

**8-K:** Current report filed with the SEC to announce material events (acquisitions, executive changes, capital raises, earnings results) between 10-Q and 10-K filings.

## R

**R&D:** Research and Development. Expenses incurred in the discovery, design, and engineering of new products, technologies, and manufacturing processes. Lucid's R&D spend was approximately $1.1 billion in FY2025.

**Regulatory Credits:** Tradable credits generated by manufacturers of zero-emission vehicles (ZEV credits) or vehicles exceeding fuel economy standards (CAFE/GHG credits). Lucid sells credits to other OEMs who require them for regulatory compliance. FY2025 credit revenue: approximately $55 million.

## S

**SAE:** SAE International (formerly Society of Automotive Engineers). A global standards organization that defines the Levels of Driving Automation (Level 0 through Level 5).

**SAE Levels:**
- **Level 2:** Partial automation. Vehicle controls both steering and acceleration/deceleration; driver must monitor the driving environment at all times. (Lucid DreamDrive Pro: Highway Assist)
- **Level 3:** Conditional automation. Vehicle handles all aspects of driving under specific conditions; driver must be ready to take control when requested. (Lucid: Highway Pilot, planned 2027)
- **Level 4:** High automation. Vehicle handles all aspects of driving within a defined operational design domain; no driver attention required. (Lucid robotaxi target)

**SEC:** U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission. Federal agency responsible for enforcing securities laws and regulating securities markets.

**Series A Preferred Stock:** Lucid's newly authorized class of convertible preferred stock issued to PIF in April 2026. Carries a 6.0% PIK dividend and converts to common stock at $2.87 per share.

**SG&A:** Selling, General, and Administrative expenses. Operating costs related to marketing, sales, corporate overhead, and administrative functions.

**SiC (Silicon Carbide):** A semiconductor material used in power electronics (inverters) that enables higher voltage operation, lower switching losses, and greater efficiency compared to traditional silicon. Used in Lucid's 900V+ inverters.

**SKD:** Semi-Knocked Down. A vehicle manufacturing method in which major sub-assemblies (painted body, powertrain, interior) are shipped in partially assembled form, with final assembly, testing, and finishing completed at the destination. AMP-2 Phase 1 uses SKD assembly at 5,000 units/year.

**SOFR:** Secured Overnight Financing Rate. A broad measure of the cost of borrowing cash overnight collateralized by U.S. Treasury securities. The reference rate for Lucid's PIF term loan and DDTL facility. Replaced LIBOR in 2023.

**SPAC:** Special Purpose Acquisition Company. A publicly traded shell company formed to acquire a private company, enabling the private company to become publicly traded without a traditional IPO process. Lucid merged with Churchill Capital Corp IV (CCIV) in July 2021.

**Studio:** Lucid's term for its company-owned retail locations where customers can view vehicles, take test drives, and place orders. Distinct from traditional dealerships. 45 locations in North America, 12 in Europe, 5 in the Middle East.

## T

**TSB:** Technical Service Bulletin. A communication from the manufacturer to service centers describing a known issue and the prescribed repair procedure. Distinct from a safety recall, TSBs are typically not communicated to owners unless they report the issue.

## U

**UX:** User Experience. In an automotive context, the overall experience of interacting with the vehicle's digital interfaces — displays, controls, infotainment, and software features. Lucid's platform is branded Lucid UX.

## V

**V2G:** Vehicle-to-Grid. Technology enabling bidirectional flow of electricity between an EV's battery and the electric grid, allowing the vehicle to serve as distributed energy storage.

**V2H:** Vehicle-to-Home. Technology enabling an EV's battery to provide backup power to a home during grid outages. Lucid's Wunderbox onboard charger supports V2H capability.

**Vision 2030:** The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia's strategic framework for economic diversification, social reform, and reduced dependence on oil. Automotive manufacturing (including Lucid AMP-2) is a targeted growth sector under Vision 2030.

## W

**Warranty Accrual:** An accounting estimate set aside to cover future warranty claims on vehicles sold. Recorded as an expense in the period of sale and adjusted as claims are experienced.

**Wunderbox:** Lucid's proprietary bidirectional onboard charger. Supports 19.2 kW AC charging, 300 kW DC fast charging, vehicle-to-load (V2L), and vehicle-to-home (V2H) functionality.

## Z

**ZEV:** Zero-Emission Vehicle. A vehicle that produces no tailpipe emissions (i.e., a battery electric vehicle or hydrogen fuel cell vehicle). ZEV mandates in California and other CARB states require manufacturers to earn ZEV credits proportional to their sales volume.
