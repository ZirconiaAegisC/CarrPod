## Founding and Heritage

T.Y. Lin International was founded in 1954 by Professor Tung-Yen Lin, widely recognized as the "Father of Prestressed Concrete" for his pioneering contributions to the theory and practice of prestressed concrete design. Professor Lin's seminal work fundamentally transformed structural engineering in the 20th century, enabling spans and structural efficiencies previously thought unattainable.

Professor Lin earned his master's degree in civil engineering from the University of California, Berkeley, in 1933. He joined the UC Berkeley faculty in 1946 and served as a professor of civil engineering until his retirement from active teaching in 1976. During his three decades at Berkeley, he educated generations of structural engineers who would go on to design many of the world's most significant bridges and buildings.

In 1986, President Ronald Reagan awarded Professor Lin the National Medal of Science — the United States' highest honor for scientific achievement — in recognition of his contributions to structural engineering, particularly prestressed concrete theory and practice. He remains one of the very few civil engineers to have received this distinction.

Professor Lin's philosophy — that engineering should serve human needs through elegance, efficiency, and economy — continues to animate the firm's approach to structural design. His conviction that long-span bridges are not merely transportation infrastructure but expressions of a society's highest technical and aesthetic aspirations informs every project the firm undertakes.

## Corporate Structure and Global Reach

T.Y. Lin International is headquartered in San Francisco, California — a location that has positioned the firm at the center of one of the world's most seismically active and bridge-intensive regions. The firm employs more than 3,000 engineers, technicians, and support professionals across offices in the Americas, Asia, and the Middle East.

Annual revenue is estimated at approximately $470 million, with some industry analyses placing the figure above $950 million depending on the scope of consolidated reporting. The firm is a core operating company within the Sidara collaborative (formerly known as the Dar Group), a global network of specialized design and engineering firms with combined annual revenues exceeding $3.15 billion. This collaborative structure provides T.Y. Lin International with access to complementary expertise in architecture, planning, project management, and sustainability consulting, while preserving the firm's distinct engineering identity and client relationships.

## Core Service Areas

**Bridge Design and Engineering.** The firm's defining practice, encompassing all phases of bridge development from conceptual design through construction engineering and inspection. Capabilities span the full range of bridge typologies: segmental concrete box girders, cable-stayed bridges, suspension bridges, arch bridges, extradosed bridges, and conventional steel and concrete girder structures.

**Seismic Engineering.** A particular strength rooted in the Bay Area's seismic environment. T.Y. Lin International has developed advanced capabilities in performance-based seismic design, seismic retrofit of existing structures, nonlinear time-history analysis, and seismic isolation and energy dissipation technologies. The firm's seismic expertise is embedded in multiple landmark retrofits and new-design projects that have advanced the state of practice for earthquake-resilient bridges.

**Structural Engineering.** Beyond bridges, the firm provides comprehensive structural engineering services for transportation facilities, transit stations, waterfront structures, and specialty buildings. The firm's structural practice draws on the same deep analytical capabilities and material expertise that distinguish its bridge work.

**Construction Engineering.** T.Y. Lin International provides construction engineering services including temporary works design, erection engineering, staged construction analysis, and value engineering. The firm's ability to bridge the design-construction interface is a significant asset in complex projects where construction methodology critically influences structural performance.

**Inspection, Rehabilitation, and Asset Management.** The firm conducts NBIS bridge inspections, fracture-critical member inspections, underwater inspections, and comprehensive condition assessments. Rehabilitation design services address structural strengthening, corrosion protection, deck replacement, bearing and joint replacement, and seismic retrofit. The firm's asset management practice supports owners in developing long-term capital improvement strategies informed by condition data and life-cycle cost analysis.

**Bridge Information Modeling (BrIM).** T.Y. Lin International has invested in the development of Bridge Information Modeling capabilities, creating digital twins of bridge structures that serve as repositories for design, construction, inspection, and maintenance data throughout the asset life cycle. BrIM supports the data-driven decision-making that is central to the Bridge Fleet Protection Program.

## Landmark Projects

**San Francisco-Oakland Bay Bridge East Span (Engineer of Record).** T.Y. Lin International, in a joint venture with Moffatt & Nichol, served as Engineer of Record for the $6.5 billion replacement of the eastern span of the Bay Bridge — the largest public works project in California history and one of the most significant bridge projects completed in the United States in the past half-century. The project's centerpiece is the Self-Anchored Suspension (SAS) span, the world's longest bridge of its type, featuring a single 525-foot tower and a single main cable. The structure was designed to a 150-year service life with a 1,500-year return period seismic event. The East Span's completion in 2013 demonstrated the firm's capacity to deliver a project of extraordinary complexity under intense public scrutiny, in one of the most seismically demanding environments on earth.

**Hoover Dam Bypass (Mike O'Callaghan–Pat Tillman Memorial Bridge).** T.Y. Lin International served as the design lead for the bridge portion of this project, which carries U.S. Route 93 across the Colorado River 1,500 feet downstream of Hoover Dam. The 1,060-foot concrete arch main span is the longest concrete arch span in the Western Hemisphere. The project required solutions to extreme site constraints, including 900-foot-deep canyon geometry, ambient temperatures exceeding 48°C (118°F), and complex wind loading patterns through the canyon.

**Benicia-Martinez Bridge (Northbound).** T.Y. Lin International designed the 8,904-foot northbound Benicia-Martinez Bridge, which carries I-680 across the Carquinez Strait. Completed in 2007, the structure was the first Caltrans segmental concrete bridge constructed in two decades and was designed to the agency's lifeline seismic performance standards. The bridge features a 659-foot main span and five lanes of traffic, replacing the previous two-lane configuration that had served as the Carquinez Strait crossing since 1962.

**Golden Gate Bridge Seismic Retrofit.** T.Y. Lin International has contributed to multiple phases of the ongoing seismic retrofit of the Golden Gate Bridge, the most iconic suspension bridge in the United States. The firm's work has addressed the complex challenge of strengthening a historically significant structure to meet contemporary seismic performance standards without altering its defining aesthetic and historic character.

**Port Mann Bridge (British Columbia, Canada).** T.Y. Lin International served as Owner's Engineer for this cable-stayed bridge carrying Highway 1 across the Fraser River east of Vancouver. The 850-meter main span bridge, completed in 2012, is one of the widest long-span bridges in the world, carrying 10 lanes of traffic. The firm's role encompassed design review, construction engineering support, and quality assurance.

**Champlain Bridge (Montreal, Canada).** T.Y. Lin International contributed to this landmark cable-stayed replacement bridge, which opened in 2019. The 3.4-kilometer structure carries the A-10, A-15, and A-20 autoroutes across the St. Lawrence River. The project was delivered under an accelerated design-build-finance-operate-maintain procurement and incorporates a 125-year design life — among the longest explicit design life requirements for any major bridge structure.

**Taiwan High Speed Rail — 25 Elevated Bridges.** T.Y. Lin International designed 25 elevated bridge structures for the Taiwan High Speed Rail project, which connects Taipei in the north to Kaohsiung in the south at operating speeds of 300 km/h (186 mph). The bridges were designed for seismic resilience in Taiwan's highly active tectonic environment, demonstrating the firm's capacity to deliver high-volume, standardized bridge designs with consistent quality under aggressive schedule constraints.

**Moscone Convention Center (San Francisco, California).** Beyond bridges, T.Y. Lin International served as structural engineer for the expansion of San Francisco's Moscone Convention Center, a major civic facility spanning three city blocks. The project demonstrates the firm's capacity to deliver structural engineering at the intersection of architectural ambition and seismic performance requirements.

## Strategic Position for the Bridge Fleet Protection Program

T.Y. Lin International occupies a position that is uniquely suited to the delivery of the Bay Area Bridge Fleet Protection Program. This position is grounded in three fundamental attributes:

**First, institutional knowledge of the fleet.** T.Y. Lin International served as Engineer of Record for the SF-Oakland Bay Bridge East Span and as designer of the Benicia-Martinez Northbound Bridge. The firm has contributed to the seismic retrofit of the Golden Gate Bridge and has performed inspection and rehabilitation engineering on multiple other fleet structures. This institutional knowledge encompasses not merely the as-built condition of these structures, but the design intent, the structural behavior, the material specifications, the construction quality records, and the decades of inspection and monitoring data that together constitute the essential baseline for a sophisticated asset protection program. The firm designed these bridges. The firm understands every bolt, cable, and connection.

**Second, unmatched corrosion protection and bridge preservation expertise.** The firm's decades of experience in bridge inspection, rehabilitation design, and construction engineering have built a deep technical capability in the assessment and remediation of structural deterioration. The firm has executed coating condition assessments, cathodic protection designs, and comprehensive rehabilitation programs for steel and concrete bridges in marine environments across the United States and internationally. This corrosion protection expertise is supported by advanced analytical capabilities in life-cycle cost modeling, finite element analysis of corrosion-damaged structures, and performance monitoring system design.

**Third, a trusted relationship with the owner agencies.** T.Y. Lin International's multi-decade relationship with Caltrans, BATA, MTC, and the FHWA California Division provides the foundation of trust and technical credibility essential for a program of this scale and duration. The firm has demonstrated its capacity to work within Caltrans' design and construction standards, BATA's capital programming processes, and the FHWA's federal-aid project delivery requirements. This institutional relationship reduces program risk by ensuring that technical recommendations are grounded in an understanding of the owner's constraints, priorities, and decision-making processes.

The Bridge Fleet Protection Program represents the next chapter in T.Y. Lin International's stewardship of the Bay Area's toll bridge infrastructure. The same engineering judgment, technical rigor, and commitment to long-term performance that delivered the East Span — designed to serve the region for 150 years — can now be applied to protecting the entire fleet for the century ahead.

## Bay Area Bridge Fleet Experience

T.Y. Lin International's engagement with the Bay Area toll bridge fleet extends across more than three decades, encompassing design, inspection, rehabilitation, and seismic retrofit of structures representing the full range of bridge typologies present in the fleet. This sustained engagement has produced an institutional knowledge base unmatched by any other engineering firm.

**SF-Oakland Bay Bridge East Span (1998–2013).** The firm's role as Engineer of Record encompassed all phases of design and construction for the $6.5 billion replacement structure, including development of the structural system, seismic performance criteria, material specifications, construction engineering, and quality assurance. The East Span's 150-year design life required explicit consideration of corrosion protection strategies — including stainless steel reinforcing in critical locations, high-performance concrete mix designs, and comprehensive coating system specifications — that are directly relevant to the protection strategies proposed for the broader fleet.

**SF-Oakland Bay Bridge West Span — Ongoing.** The firm has provided inspection and engineering services for the West Span for decades, including participation in the seismic retrofit program (completed 2004) and ongoing inspection, condition assessment, and rehabilitation design. This continuity of engagement provides an understanding of the West Span's structural behavior, deterioration mechanisms, and maintenance history that cannot be replicated through document review alone.

**Benicia-Martinez Bridge Northbound (1997–2007).** As designer of the northbound Benicia-Martinez Bridge, the firm possesses the complete design record, including structural calculations, material specifications, geotechnical data, and construction-phase documentation. This knowledge base directly supports the efficient development of the preventive protection strategy recommended for this structure.

**Golden Gate Bridge (1990s–present).** The firm's participation in multiple phases of the Golden Gate Bridge seismic retrofit has provided direct experience with the unique engineering challenges of protecting and strengthening a historic suspension bridge in a marine environment — experience directly applicable to the SF-Oakland West Span, a suspension bridge of similar vintage and exposure conditions.

**Fleet-Wide Inspection and Rehabilitation.** T.Y. Lin International has performed NBIS routine inspections, fracture-critical member inspections, and underwater inspections on multiple fleet structures. The firm has designed and overseen rehabilitation projects addressing coating replacement, concrete repair, bearing replacement, joint replacement, and seismic retrofit. This fleet-wide inspection and rehabilitation experience provides a comprehensive, comparative understanding of the deterioration mechanisms affecting each structure and the relative effectiveness of different maintenance and protection strategies.

## Commitment to Sustainability

T.Y. Lin International recognizes that infrastructure decisions made today will shape the environmental footprint of transportation systems for decades to come. The firm's sustainability practice integrates life-cycle assessment (LCA) methodologies into the design decision-making process, enabling quantitative comparison of the environmental impacts — including embodied carbon, global warming potential, and resource depletion — associated with alternative structural systems and material choices.

The Bridge Fleet Protection Program's emphasis on long-life protection systems is fundamentally aligned with sustainability principles. The extension of structural service life through durable corrosion protection reduces the consumption of materials, energy, and financial resources that would otherwise be required for repeated rehabilitation cycles. The substitution of TSZ duplex for conventional paint systems eliminates the environmental burden associated with the production, application, removal, and disposal of multiple paint cycles over the life of each structure. The program's sustainability benefits — quantified through the life-cycle assessment documented in companion dossier volumes — reinforce its alignment with Caltrans' climate action objectives and BATA's commitment to sustainable infrastructure investment.
