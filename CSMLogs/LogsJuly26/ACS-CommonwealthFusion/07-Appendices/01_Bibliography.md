## 1. Peer-Reviewed Publications — ARC Physics Basis

The following five publications constitute the core peer-reviewed physics basis for the ARC-class compact high-field tokamak design:

1. Sorbom, B.N., et al. "ARC: A compact, high-field, fusion nuclear science facility and demonstration power plant with demountable magnets." *Fusion Engineering and Design*, Vol. 100, pp. 378–405, 2015. DOI: 10.1016/j.fusengdes.2015.07.008

2. Kuang, A.Q., et al. "Conceptual design study for heat exhaust management in the ARC fusion pilot plant." *Fusion Engineering and Design*, Vol. 137, pp. 221–242, 2018. DOI: 10.1016/j.fusengdes.2018.09.011

3. Whyte, D.G., et al. "Smaller & sooner: exploiting high magnetic fields from new superconductors for a more attractive fusion energy development path." *Journal of Fusion Energy*, Vol. 35, pp. 41–53, 2016. DOI: 10.1007/s10894-015-0050-1

4. Rodriguez-Fernandez, P., et al. "Overview of the SPARC physics basis towards the exploration of burning-plasma regimes in high-field, compact tokamaks." *Plasma Physics and Controlled Fusion*, Vol. 64, 054003, 2022. DOI: 10.1088/1361-6587/ac56a1

5. Creely, A.J., et al. "Overview of the SPARC tokamak." *Journal of Plasma Physics*, Vol. 86, 865860502, 2020. DOI: 10.1017/S0022377820001257
