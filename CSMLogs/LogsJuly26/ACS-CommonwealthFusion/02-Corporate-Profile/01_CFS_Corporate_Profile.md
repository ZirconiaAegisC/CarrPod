## 2. Founding & Institutional Heritage

CFS emerged from decades of fusion research at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, specifically the Plasma Science and Fusion Center. The founding thesis—that high-temperature superconducting magnets could enable commercially viable tokamaks at a fraction of the physical scale of ITER-class machines—was validated through MIT's design studies and published in peer-reviewed literature. Five foundational papers define the ARC-class physics basis, providing the theoretical and empirical grounding for the compact high-field approach.

The company was incorporated with dual ambitions: to demonstrate net-energy fusion via a compact tokamak (SPARC) on an accelerated timeline, and to establish a domestic HTS magnet manufacturing capability with applications beyond fusion energy.

## 3. Leadership

Bob Mumgaard serves as Chief Executive Officer. The leadership team comprises fusion physicists, magnet engineers, manufacturing specialists, and energy-sector executives drawn from MIT, national laboratories, and the private energy industry. The governance structure includes representation from major investors including Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Temasek, and strategic corporate partners.

## 4. Technology Platform

CFS's proprietary advantage resides in its rare-earth barium copper oxide (REBCO) high-temperature superconducting magnet technology. Each toroidal field magnet generates 20 tesla—more than double the field strength of niobium-tin or niobium-titanium low-temperature superconducting magnets used in predecessor tokamaks. This field intensity enables:

- Plasma confinement at dramatically reduced machine volume
- Avoidance of the ITER-scale construction paradigm
- Design compactness that reduces capital cost and construction duration
- Cross-sector applicability in wind energy, medical imaging, and transportation

## 5. Strategic Partnerships

### 5.1 Nvidia Corporation
Deployment of the Omniverse digital twin platform for full-physics simulation of SPARC and ARC reactors. Enables predictive maintenance, design iteration, and operational scenario modelling without physical prototyping.

### 5.2 Siemens AG
Integration of Siemens industrial manufacturing software for HTS magnet production scale-up and plant-wide automation architecture.

### 5.3 Google LLC
Binding power purchase agreement for 50% of ARC commercial output—the first corporate fusion PPA in history. Also provides cloud infrastructure and computational resources.

### 5.4 Dominion Energy
Lessor of the James River Industrial Center site in Chesterfield County, Virginia, for the ARC commercial plant. Facilitates grid interconnection studies and transmission infrastructure planning.

## 8. Physical Infrastructure

The Devens campus encompasses magnet fabrication, tokamak assembly, cryogenic systems, power supply infrastructure, and administrative operations. The magnet manufacturing line represents a first-of-kind industrial capability for HTS magnet production at scale. The facility has produced 18 toroidal field magnets, each weighing 24 tons, with consistent performance characteristics validated through cryogenic testing.

## 9. Intellectual Property

CFS maintains a portfolio encompassing HTS magnet design and fabrication methods, tokamak configuration geometry, plasma control algorithms, and ARC plant architecture. IP strategy leverages both patent protection and trade-secret maintenance for magnet manufacturing processes. The five peer-reviewed ARC physics basis papers serve as public-domain validation of the underlying scientific approach while proprietary engineering implementations remain protected.
