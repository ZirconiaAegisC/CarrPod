# HTS Magnet Technology & Manufacturing

**Dossier Reference:** ACS-CFS-FE-2026-TECH-002
**Subject:** REBCO High-Temperature Superconducting Magnet Systems
**Status:** 18 TF Magnets Manufactured; Full Installation Summer 2026

---

## 1. Technology Overview

Commonwealth Fusion Systems' core technological differentiation resides in its rare-earth barium copper oxide (REBCO) high-temperature superconducting magnet technology. This capability enables the compact high-field tokamak architecture that distinguishes CFS from both government-led fusion programs and competing private fusion ventures.

The critical insight is that fusion power density scales with the fourth power of magnetic field strength (P ∝ B^4). By developing HTS magnets capable of sustained 20-tesla operation, CFS achieves fusion-relevant plasma confinement in a machine volume approximately 1/40th that of ITER, which operates at approximately 5.3 tesla using niobium-tin low-temperature superconductors.

## 2. REBCO Conductor Properties

REBCO (rare-earth barium copper oxide, typically YBa₂Cu₃O₇₋ₓ or GdBa₂Cu₃O₇₋ₓ) is a ceramic high-temperature superconductor with critical temperature (Tc) exceeding 90 K—well above the boiling point of liquid nitrogen (77 K). This contrasts with niobium-titanium (Tc ≈ 9 K) and niobium-tin (Tc ≈ 18 K) used in predecessor superconducting magnets.

### 2.1 Key Parameters

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Critical temperature | > 90 K |
| Operational temperature | ~20 K (-253 °C) |
| Operational field | 20 tesla |
| Conductor current | > 30,000 amperes |
| Conductor form | Multi-filamentary tape |
| Tape width | ~12 mm |
| Tape thickness | < 0.1 mm |
| Substrate | Hastelloy or equivalent high-strength alloy |
| Buffer layers | Multi-layer oxide deposition for crystallographic alignment |

### 2.2 Operating Margin

REBCO is operated at approximately 20 K—well below its critical temperature—to maintain a substantial engineering current density (Je) margin under the extreme Lorentz forces present at 20 tesla. The temperature margin provides resilience against thermal disturbances and AC losses that could otherwise trigger a quench.

## 3. Magnet Design

### 3.1 Toroidal Field Coil Configuration

Each of the 18 toroidal field magnets is a monolithic, non-insulated winding of REBCO tape conductor. Key design features include:

- **Non-insulated winding:** Enables current sharing between adjacent turns, providing intrinsic quench protection through current redistribution. A localised hot spot causes current to bypass the affected region, distributing energy across the coil rather than concentrating it.
- **Structural reinforcement:** The winding pack is encased in a high-strength structural shell—typically stainless steel or Inconel—to resist the enormous out-of-plane Lorentz forces generated at 20 tesla.
- **Impregnation:** Epoxy or equivalent impregnation provides mechanical integrity and thermal conduction between turns.
- **Cryogenic interface:** Conduction-cooled via thermal connection to the liquid helium cryogenic circuit, maintaining the winding pack at approximately 20 K.

### 3.2 Magnet Specifications (Per Coil)

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Peak field | 20 tesla |
| Mass | ~24 tons |
| Coil geometry | D-shaped (modified Princeton D) |
| Winding type | Non-insulated REBCO |
| Operating temperature | ~20 K (-253 °C) |
| Operating current | > 30 kA |
| Stored energy | ~GJ range (total for 18-coil set) |
| Cooling method | Conduction-cooled, liquid helium circuit |

## 4. Manufacturing Process

CFS has established the first industrial-scale HTS magnet manufacturing line at its Devens, Massachusetts facility. The production of 18 full-scale TF magnets represents a manufacturing milestone without precedent in the superconducting magnet industry.

### 4.1 Production Stages

1. **REBCO tape procurement and qualification:** Incoming tape is tested for critical current uniformity, mechanical integrity, and dimensional consistency across kilometre-scale lengths.
2. **Winding:** Precision winding of the D-shaped coil form under controlled tension and alignment. Turn-to-turn registration is critical for electromagnetic performance and mechanical integrity.
3. **Joint fabrication:** Low-resistance superconducting joints between tape segments enable practical coil dimensions beyond single tape-length constraints.
4. **Impregnation and curing:** Vacuum-pressure impregnation with epoxy system, followed by controlled thermal cure cycle.
5. **Structural casing assembly:** Integration of the winding pack into the structural shell with precision alignment.
6. **Cryogenic testing:** Full-current testing at operational temperature in a dedicated test cryostat to validate electromagnetic and thermal performance.
7. **Installation and commissioning:** Transport and integration into the SPARC tokamak assembly.

### 4.2 Quality Assurance

Each magnet undergoes comprehensive acceptance testing prior to installation:

- Critical current measurement at operational temperature
- Insulation resistance and high-potential (hi-pot) testing
- Dimensional metrology against design specifications
- Structural integrity verification
- Quench detection system functional testing

## 5. Quench Protection

HTS magnets present unique quench protection challenges due to their higher temperature margin and slower normal-zone propagation compared to LTS magnets. CFS employs multiple protection strategies:

- **Non-insulated winding:** Current-sharing behaviour distributes energy during a quench, reducing peak hot-spot temperature.
- **Active quench detection:** Voltage-based detection algorithms with fast-response triggering.
- **Energy extraction:** Rapid discharge of stored magnetic energy into external dump resistors.
- **Distributed temperature sensing:** Fibre-optic or voltage-tap temperature monitoring at multiple locations within each coil.

## 6. Adjacent Market Applications

The HTS magnet manufacturing capability developed for SPARC and ARC has significant commercial applicability beyond fusion energy, representing a second revenue pathway independent of fusion breakeven:

### 6.1 Wind Turbine Generators
Direct-drive HTS generators promise higher power density, reduced tower-head mass, and elimination of rare-earth permanent magnets for multi-megawatt offshore wind turbines.

### 6.2 Magnetic Resonance Imaging
HTS magnets could enable higher-field MRI systems (11.7 T and above) with improved spatial resolution and reduced helium consumption compared to LTS-based systems.

### 6.3 Electric Propulsion
HTS motors and generators for marine, aviation, and heavy transport applications benefit from high power density and efficiency advantages.

### 6.4 Scientific Instrumentation
High-field magnets for materials science, particle physics detectors, and nuclear magnetic resonance spectroscopy.

## 7. Supply Chain Considerations

REBCO tape production is concentrated among a limited number of global suppliers, creating supply chain concentration risk. CFS's manufacturing strategy includes qualification of multiple tape vendors and investment in production capacity expansion. The rare-earth elements in REBCO—yttrium, gadolinium, barium—are subject to geopolitical supply dynamics, though the quantities required per magnet are modest relative to other rare-earth applications.
