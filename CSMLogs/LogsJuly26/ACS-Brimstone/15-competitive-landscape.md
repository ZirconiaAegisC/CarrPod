# 15 — Competitive Landscape & Technology Comparison

## Low-Carbon Cement Taxonomy

The decarbonization of cement and concrete follows multiple technology pathways,
each with distinct trade-offs:

1. **CCS on conventional kilns** — capture CO2 from limestone calcination
2. **Alternative feedstocks** — replace limestone with non-carbonate minerals
3. **Alternative clinker chemistries** — belite-rich, CSA, alkali-activated
4. **SCM substitution** — replace clinker with fly ash, slag, calcined clay, etc.
5. **CO2 curing / mineralization** — inject CO2 into concrete during curing
6. **Electrification / alternative fuels** — reduce combustion CO2 (not process CO2)

Brimstone sits in pathway 2 (alternative feedstock) and is the only company to
achieve carbon-negative ASTM C150 OPC from silicate minerals at scale.

## Direct Competitor Analysis

### CarbonCure Technologies (Halifax, NS)
- **Technology:** Injects captured CO2 into fresh concrete; CO2 reacts with Ca2+
  to form nano-CaCO3, permanently mineralized
- **Carbon reduction:** 4–6% reduction per cubic yard (not per-ton cement)
- **Limitation:** Does not address the ~60% of cement emissions from calcination.
  CarbonCure works at the concrete plant, not the cement plant.
- **Status:** Deployed at 800+ concrete plants; acquired by Compass Datacenters (2024)
- **Brimstone advantage:** Addresses the root cause (process CO2) rather than
  downstream concrete. Carbon-negative vs. marginal reduction.

### Fortera (San Jose, CA)
- **Technology:** Low-temperature aqueous process to produce reactive calcium
  carbonate cement from limestone, capturing process CO2 in the product
- **Carbon reduction:** ~60% vs. OPC
- **Limitation:** Requires new ASTM specification; product is not OPC. Produces
  re-carbonatable CaCO3, not calcium silicate cement.
- **Status:** ~$100M+ raised; first commercial plant in Redding, CA (2024)
- **Brimstone advantage:** ASTM C150 (no new standard needed), carbon-negative
  (not just lower), simpler dry process vs. aqueous.

### Sublime Systems (Somerville, MA)
- **Technology:** Electrochemical cement production at ambient temperature — uses
  electrolysis rather than a kiln to decalcify calcium silicate minerals
- **Carbon reduction:** Zero process CO2; carbon intensity depends on electricity
  grid mix
- **Limitation:** Electrochemical process is fundamentally different from standard
  kiln line; new manufacturing paradigm with unknown CAPEX/scale economics.
  Product must still achieve ASTM C150 equivalence.
- **Status:** ~$140M+ raised (including $87M DOE BIL grant); pilot plant operating
- **Brimstone advantage:** Uses standard kiln infrastructure (lower technology risk,
  proven equipment supply chain, known CAPEX benchmarks).

### Solidia Technologies (Piscataway, NJ)
- **Technology:** CO2-cured concrete using a proprietary cement chemistry
  (wollastonite-based, low-lime)
- **Carbon reduction:** ~70% vs. OPC
- **Limitation:** Requires CO2 curing chambers at precast plants — only applicable
  to precast concrete, not ready-mix. Not ASTM C150.
- **Status:** Commercial deployments at select precast plants; limited ready-mix
  applicability
- **Brimstone advantage:** Applicable to all concrete segments (ready-mix, precast,
  prestressed, masonry) — the full addressable market.

### Heidelberg Materials / Fortera JV
- Heidelberg is pursuing CCS at its Edmonton, AB, plant (1M t/yr CO2 capture
  by 2026) and at Brevik, Norway (world's first full-scale CCS cement plant,
  400,000 t/yr CO2 captured from 2025)
- **Carbon reduction:** ~50–90% (net-zero, not negative)
- **Limitation:** CCS adds $40–80/t OPEX and $200–400M CAPEX per plant; does not
  eliminate the fundamental calcination chemistry
- **Brimstone advantage:** Structural solution (eliminate CO2 at source) vs.
  incremental solution (capture CO2 after creation). At scale, silicate process
  is lower cost than limestone + CCS.

### LC3 (Limestone Calcined Clay Cement)
- **Technology:** Replace 50% of clinker with calcined clay and limestone; reduces
  clinker factor
- **Carbon reduction:** 30–40% vs. OPC
- **Limitation:** Requires widespread calcined clay supply chain; still emits process
  CO2 from the clinker fraction; not carbon-negative
- **Status:** Being adopted in India, Latin America, Africa; slower in US/EU
- **Brimstone advantage:** Complementary rather than competitive — LC3 still needs
  clinker; Brimstone can supply the carbon-negative clinker component for LC3 blends,
  creating a hyper-decarbonized product.

## Barriers to Entry

1. **ASTM C150 certification:** Requires years of testing and CCRL validation
2. **Capital intensity:** $400M+ for first plant; >$500M raised by Brimstone
3. **Process patents:** Silicate-to-OPC clinker pathway is patent-protected
4. **Feedstock access:** Basalt quarry rights secured
5. **Buyer switching cost:** Once a specifier writes "Brimstone Type I/II" into
   project documents, substitution requires change-order justification
6. **Carbon credit registration:** Verified carbon-negative status creates a
   moat against claims of "greener" but not verifiably negative competitors
