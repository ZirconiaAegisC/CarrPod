## Investor Profiles

### Breakthrough Energy Ventures (BEV)
Founded by Bill Gates in 2015. BEV is the climate technology fund with the highest
risk tolerance and longest time horizon (>20 years). BEV's investment thesis
specifically targets the five "Grand Challenges" — cement/manufacturing among them.
BEV's backing provides:

- Technical due diligence credibility (access to BEV's science fellow network)
- Policy advocacy in DC (BEV's policy team engages directly with DOE, EPA, Congress)
- Portfolio synergies with other BEV cement and concrete companies

### DCVC (Data Collective)
Deep-tech venture capital with $3B+ under management. DCVC's thesis centers on
"Deep Tech" — companies built on fundamental scientific breakthroughs. Their
investment signals validation of the underlying chemistry and process engineering.

### Fifth Wall
The largest venture capital firm focused on real estate and the built environment.
Fifth Wall's LPs include major real estate developers, homebuilders, and construction
companies, creating a direct channel to cement and concrete customers.

### Amazon Climate Pledge Fund
Amazon's $2 billion venture investment program. Amazon's interest is strategic:
the company is one of the world's largest builders (data centers, warehouses, and
logistics centers consuming millions of cubic yards of concrete annually). An
Amazon investment implies a pathway to preferential offtake.

## Capital Deployment Strategy

### Phase I: Demonstration Plant (~$400M)
- BIL grant: $189M (~47%)
- Equity (Series C + earlier): $150–200M (~38–50%)
- 48C tax credit (refundable): ~$30–50M (~10%)
- Green bank debt: $0 (minimizes leverage during technology risk phase)

### Phase II: Plant 2 & 3 (~$600M each)
- DOE LPO Title 17 loan: Up to 80% of CAPEX
- 48C tax credits (re-authorized): ~20–30%
- Equity from cash flow and new rounds: ~20–30%
- Green bonds / sustainable debt: TBD

### Phase III: Global Licensing + JVs
- Technology licensing fees from international cement partners
- Joint ventures with sovereign wealth funds in Middle East, Asia
- Royalty model with in-region basalt quarry operators

## Valuation Framework

Cement is a $350 billion+ global market growing at 4–6% CAGR. A company that captures
even 1% market share at $120–180/ton revenue and 25–35% gross margin (bolstered by
carbon credits and green premiums) generates $400–700M in annual gross profit.
Valuation comps:

- **Holcim:** ~$50B market cap, ~1.5x revenue
- **Heidelberg Materials:** ~$20B market cap, ~0.9x revenue
- **CarbonCure (private, concrete CO2 injection):** $800M+ valuation (2022)
- **Fortera (low-CO2 cement, private):** $350M+ valuation (2023)

Brimstone's valuation reflects a premium for carbon-negative differentiation,
ASTM C150 compliance, process patent protection, and the >$500M capital base that
creates a significant moat against undercapitalized competitors.
