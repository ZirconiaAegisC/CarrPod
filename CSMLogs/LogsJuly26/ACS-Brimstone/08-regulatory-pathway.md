# 08 — Regulatory Pathway & Policy Tailwinds

## Federal Procurement: Buy Clean & GSA

The **Buy Clean Initiative**, launched by Executive Order 14057 (December 2021),
directs federal agencies to prioritize procurement of construction materials with
substantially lower embodied carbon. The General Services Administration (GSA)
manages the federal building portfolio (~370 million square feet) and has issued
**Interim IRA Low-Embodied Carbon Material Requirements** that set GWP limits for:

- Concrete (per cubic yard, by strength class)
- Cement (per metric ton)
- Steel (per short ton)
- Asphalt (per ton)

Brimstone's carbon-negative cement exceeds the most stringent GSA tier (Top 20%
lowest GWP) and qualifies for federal procurement preference across all agencies
(DoD, GSA, VA, USACE).

## Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) — Section 45Q

The IRA expanded and enhanced the 45Q tax credit for carbon sequestration:

| Credit Type | 2022 | Post-IRA (2023–26) | Post-IRA (2027+) |
|-------------|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Industrial CCS ($/t CO2) | $50 | $85 | $85 |
| Direct Air Capture ($/t CO2) | $50 | $180 | $180 |
| Utilization ($/t CO2) | $35 | $60 | $60 |

Brimstone's MgO carbonation may qualify under the **utilization pathway** if the
MgCO3 product finds commercial use (e.g., aggregate, fill), or under the **direct
air capture pathway** if the carbonation is characterized as passive DAC. The
differential between DAC ($180/t) and utilization ($60/t) represents a significant
revenue optimization opportunity.

## IRA Section 48C — Advanced Energy Project Credit

The $10 billion **48C Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit** provides an
investment tax credit of up to 30% for manufacturing facilities that reduce
greenhouse gas emissions. Brimstone's first commercial plant is positioned to
claim 48C for:

- Kiln equipment and precalciner
- CO2 mineralization (MgO carbonation system)
- Renewable energy integration

## EPA: Low-Embodied Carbon Labeling

The EPA's **Interim Determination on Low-Embodied Carbon Construction Materials**
(November 2023) establishes thresholds for "substantially lower" embodied carbon:

- Cement: GWP at least 20% below the PCA industry average
- Concrete: GWP at least 20% below the NRMCA regional benchmark

Brimstone cement is eligible for the EPA's new LEC (Low-Embodied Carbon) label,
which feeds into GSA procurement decisions and AEC specifications.

## State-Level Momentum

| State | Policy | Impact |
|-------|--------|--------|
| California | SB 596 (cement net-zero by 2045); Buy Clean California Act | Minimum GWP limits on state projects |
| New York | Low-Embodied Carbon Concrete Leadership Act (LECCLA) | Procurement preference |
| Colorado | HB 1303 (Buy Clean Colorado) | GWP disclosure & limits |
| Oregon, Washington | Buy Clean legislation in progress | Pacific NW preference |
| New Jersey, Maryland | Low-carbon concrete bills enacted | 2024–25 implementation |

California's **SB 596** requires the cement industry to achieve net-zero by 2045,
creating a direct regulatory pull for carbon-negative products in the largest US
state economy.

## EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)

The EU CBAM, phased in 2023–2034, imposes a carbon price on imported cement equal
to the EU-ETS allowance price. By 2034, imported cement with a GWP of ~850 kg/t
faces a border charge of €70–100+/t. Brimstone's carbon-negative product would
incur **zero CBAM liability**, creating a structural export advantage into the
European market.
