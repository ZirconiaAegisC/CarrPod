# 03 — Kiln Process Innovation

## Standard Cement Kiln Line

A conventional dry-process cement plant consists of:

1. **Preheater tower (4–6 stages):** Cyclone suspension preheater; raw meal enters
   at top, travels counter-current to exhaust gases; reaches ~800–900°C
2. **Precalciner:** Secondary burner vessel between preheater and kiln; ~60% of fuel
   is fired here; calcination (CaCO3 → CaO + CO2) occurs at ~900°C
3. **Rotary kiln:** Inclined rotating steel cylinder, refractory-lined; material
   reaches clinkering temperature (~1,450°C); residence time 20–40 minutes
4. **Clinker cooler:** Grate or rotary cooler; recovers heat to preheater and
   secondary/tertiary air ducts
5. **Fuel system:** Typically coal/petcoke, increasingly supplemented with alternative
   fuels (tires, biomass, RDF)

## Process Modifications for Silicate Feedstock

Brimstone's process uses the **same basic kiln configuration** — a critical advantage
for capital efficiency and permitting. The key differences:

### Precalciner Operation

Since calcium silicates do not undergo calcination (no CO2 to drive off), the
precalciner's primary function shifts from calcination to:

- Dehydration of any hydrous silicate phases (serpentine, chlorite)
- Pre-reaction of CaO with SiO2 to form preliminary belite (C2S) at ~1,100–1,200°C
- Oxidation of any reduced iron phases

The absence of the endothermic calcination step (ΔH = +178 kJ/mol) **reduces the
precalciner fuel demand** by approximately 40–50%.

### Kiln Thermal Profile

The rotary kiln still requires clinkering temperatures (~1,450°C) for alite (C3S)
formation, but the heat load is redistributed:

| Zone | Conventional | Brimstone Silicate |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Precalciner fuel | 60% of total | 35–45% of total |
| Kiln burner fuel | 40% of total | 55–65% of total |
| Overall thermal energy | 3.0–3.4 GJ/t clinker | 2.5–2.9 GJ/t clinker |

The net thermal energy reduction (10–20%) translates to lower fuel costs and a smaller
CO2 footprint from combustion.

### Refractory Considerations

Silicate melts at clinkering temperatures are chemically similar to standard clinker
liquid phase. No special refractory requirements beyond those of conventional cement
kilns (magnesia-spinel, dolomite, or high-alumina brick in the burning zone).

### Clinker Cooler

The clinker produced is mineralogically identical to OPC clinker and behaves identically
in the cooler. Heat recovery to secondary and tertiary air follows standard design
parameters.

## Emissions Profile

| Emission Source | Conventional OPC | Brimstone Process |
|----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Process CO2 (calcination) | ~510 kg/t clinker | **0 kg/t clinker** |
| Combustion CO2 (fuel) | ~290 kg/t clinker | ~230–260 kg/t clinker |
| Electricity (grinding, fans) | ~40 kg CO2e/t | ~45–50 kg CO2e/t |
| Mining & transport | ~10 kg CO2e/t | ~20 kg CO2e/t |
| MgO carbonation (credit) | 0 | **−300 to −450 kg CO2/t** |
| **Net CO2 (cradle-to-gate)** | **~850 kg/t** | **~−50 to −150 kg/t** |

## Fuel Flexibility

Brimstone's kiln can accept the same alternative fuels as conventional plants:
- Waste-derived fuels (tires, RDF, biomass)
- Green hydrogen (for net-zero combustion CO2)
- Electrification potential (kiln burner replacement with plasma or resistive heating
  for the clinkering zone, which represents ~55–65% of thermal load)

## Retrofit Potential

Because the kiln configuration is standard, existing cement plants could theoretically
retrofit to silicate feedstock, though the economic case favors greenfield plants
co-located with basalt quarries. A retrofit would require:
- Modified precalciner burner configuration
- Adjusted raw meal blending and storage
- Additional MgO handling and carbonation infrastructure
