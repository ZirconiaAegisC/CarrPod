# 04 — Carbon-Negative Mechanism

## Defining Carbon-Negative Cement

Carbon-negative means that the cradle-to-gate (or cradle-to-grave) life cycle assessment
shows a **net removal of CO2 from the atmosphere** — not merely a reduction in emissions.
Brimstone achieves this through two mechanisms operating in tandem:

1. **Elimination of process CO2:** Using calcium silicate instead of limestone
2. **Passive carbonation of magnesium byproduct:** MgO sequesters atmospheric CO2

## Mechanism 1: Zero Process CO2

The standard calcination reaction that defines cement's dominant carbon footprint:

    CaCO3 → CaO + CO2 (↑)

is eliminated by design. The replacement chemistry:

    CaSiO3 → CaO + SiO2

releases zero CO2. This eliminates approximately 510 kg of CO2 per metric ton of
clinker — roughly 60% of conventional cement's cradle-to-gate emissions.

## Mechanism 2: Magnesium Byproduct Carbonation

Basalt typically contains 5–12 wt% MgO in minerals such as:

- **Forsterite:** Mg2SiO4
- **Diopside:** CaMgSi2O6
- **Enstatite:** MgSiO3

During the kiln process, these magnesium silicates decompose, producing reactive
magnesium oxide (MgO) as a byproduct. When exposed to the atmosphere, MgO undergoes
passive carbonation:

    MgO(s) + CO2(g) → MgCO3(s)     ΔG° = −49 kJ/mol (spontaneous at ambient)

The carbonation reaction is:
- **Spontaneous** at ambient temperature and CO2 partial pressure
- **Exothermic** (releases ~1.18 GJ per ton of CO2 sequestered)
- **Permanent:** MgCO3 (magnesite) is a geologically stable mineral

## Carbonation Engineering

Brimstone uses **engineered carbonation cells** to accelerate the natural reaction:

1. Crushed MgO-rich slag/byproduct is spread in thin layers (<1 m depth)
2. Ambient air is drawn through the beds using low-power fans
3. Moisture is maintained at optimal levels (~10–15% water content)
4. Residence time: 3–12 months depending on particle size and ambient conditions
5. Carbonation monitoring via thermogravimetric analysis (TGA) and CO2 mass balance

## Quantification

For a basalt feedstock containing 10 wt% MgO:

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Basalt processed per t clinker | ~7 metric tons |
| MgO in feedstock | ~700 kg |
| MgO recoverable as reactive byproduct | ~500–600 kg |
| CO2 sequestration capacity (stoichiometric) | ~550–660 kg CO2 |
| Realized sequestration (80% conversion) | ~440–530 kg CO2 |
| Process CO2 avoided (vs. limestone) | ~510 kg CO2 |
| **Net CO2 balance (cradle-to-gate)** | **~−50 to −150 kg CO2/t clinker** |

## Permanence

Mineral carbonation is the most durable form of carbon sequestration known. MgCO3
is thermodynamically stable over geological timescales — unlike biomass, DAC with
geologic storage, or ocean alkalinity enhancement, there is zero risk of re-release.
The IPCC classifies mineral carbonation as permanent sequestration with >10,000-year
residence time.

## Verification

Third-party verification by **SCS Global Services** under ISO 14064-3 validates the
carbon accounting. The carbon-negative claim is auditable and registered, enabling
participation in voluntary and compliance carbon markets.
