## 1. Delivery Volume Trajectory

### 1.1 Historical Production and Delivery Data

| Period | Production (Units) | Deliveries (Units) | Production/Delivery Ratio |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| FY2023 | 57,232 | 50,122 | 87.6% |
| FY2024 | ~37,400 (est.) | ~37,400 (est.) | ~100% |
| FY2025 | 42,284 | 42,284 | 100% |
| Q1 2026 | 10,236 | 10,365 | 101.3% |
| Q2 2026 | 12,613 | 12,194 | 96.7% |

The production-to-delivery ratio has converged to approximately 100% since FY2024, indicating effective demand absorption of production output and limited inventory build. The Q2 2026 production of 12,613 units exceeded consensus guidance of 9,000–11,000 units, and the company responded by raising its full-year FY2026 delivery outlook to 65,000–70,000 units from prior guidance.

### 1.2 Quarterly Volume Growth

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Q2 2026 | QoQ Change |
|--------|---------|---------|------------|
| Production | 10,236 | 12,613 | +23.2% |
| Deliveries | 10,365 | 12,194 | +17.6% |

The QoQ production acceleration is primarily attributable to R2 line startup contribution, improved R1 line throughput, and resolution of select supply chain bottlenecks. At the Q2 exit rate, the company is producing approximately 4,200 units per month, which must further accelerate to achieve the mid-point of the 65,000–70,000 guidance range (requiring an average of ~5,400–5,800 units/month for the full year, or ~8,500+ units/month for H2 2026 to compensate for the slower H1 start).

### 1.3 Product Mix Evolution

The introduction of R2 production in Q2 2026 initiates a shift in product mix that will accelerate through FY2027–2028:

- **R1 Platform (R1T, R1S):** Expected to maintain relatively stable volume as the premium flagship line, constrained by segment size more than production capacity
- **EDV (Commercial Vans):** Demand-gated by Amazon's phased deployment schedule under the 100,000-unit framework agreement
- **R2 Platform:** Projected to become the dominant volume contributor by FY2027, with the Normal line reaching steady-state and Georgia tooling in progress

| Q1 2026 | $1.38B | +11% YoY | R1 and R2 initial contribution |

### 2.2 Average Selling Price (ASP) Dynamics

Rivian's ASP has historically been elevated by the premium positioning of R1 vehicles, with R1T and R1S configurations commonly exceeding $80,000–$100,000 at point of sale. The introduction of R2 at a ~$45,000 starting price will exert downward pressure on blended ASP as R2 mix increases. However, this ASP decline is intentional and positive: lower ASP vehicles at higher volume and with improved unit margins (due to simpler design and manufacturing) are expected to contribute more aggregate gross profit than the higher-ASP, lower-volume R1 line alone.

### 2.3 Revenue per Vehicle Delivered

| Metric | FY2025 | Q1 2026 |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Revenue | $5.4B | $1.38B |
| Deliveries | 42,284 | 10,365 |
| Implied Revenue/Unit | ~$127,700 | ~$133,100 |

The Q1 2026 implied revenue per delivery of approximately $133,100 reflects the premium R1-weighted product mix. This metric will decline as R2 volumes scale, with a target blended ASP in the FY2027–2028 timeframe estimated at $65,000–$75,000 depending on R1/R2/EDV mix and option take rates.

## 3. Revenue Composition

### 3.1 Vehicle Sales Revenue

Vehicle sales constitute the dominant revenue stream (>95% of total), comprising:
- Direct consumer vehicle sales (R1T, R1S, R2)
- Commercial fleet sales (EDV to Amazon)
- Vehicle trade-in and pre-owned sales (small but growing contribution)
- Delivery and destination charges

### 3.2 Ancillary Revenue Streams

While currently small relative to vehicle sales, Rivian is cultivating several ancillary revenue categories:
- **Software and Services:** Revenue from connected vehicle subscriptions, over-the-air feature upgrades (e.g., enhanced autonomy features), and in-vehicle commerce
- **Accessories and Merchandise:** Rooftop tents, camp kitchens, crossbars, gear tunnel accessories, and branded merchandise
- **Charging Revenue:** Future revenue from non-Rivian vehicle access to the RAN charging network
- **Technology Licensing:** Revenue from the Volkswagen Group joint venture for shared electrical architecture and software platform licensing — potentially a high-margin, recurring revenue stream
- **Regulatory Credits:** Revenue from the sale of zero-emission vehicle (ZEV) credits and greenhouse gas (GHG) credits to other OEMs

### 3.3 Revenue Concentration Risk

The Amazon EDV contract and the Uber robotaxi agreement represent two significant single-customer revenue concentrations. Any material change to these commercial relationships — whether through renegotiation, delayed deployment, or termination — would have a disproportionate impact on the forward revenue trajectory.

## 6. Key Growth Drivers and Risks

### 6.1 Growth Drivers
- R2 volume ramp at Normal (FY2026–2027) and Georgia (2029+)
- EDV deliveries to Amazon under the phased framework agreement
- Uber robotaxi fleet deployment (10,000 firm + 40,000 option)
- Volkswagen JV licensing revenue
- International market entry (Canada operational; European expansion under evaluation)
- Software and services attach-rate growth

### 6.2 Growth Risks
- R2 demand at scale (50,000+ annual units) unproven in a competitive mid-size EV segment
- Amazon EDV order pacing is at Amazon's discretion; near-term volume may not track to the 10,000/year headline rate
- Uber robotaxi deployment timeline is contingent on technology readiness and regulatory approval — revenue contribution likely post-2028
- Macroeconomic headwinds (interest rates, consumer credit availability) may suppress EV purchase intent
- Potential reduction or elimination of the $7,500 federal EV tax credit would increase effective customer cost and reduce demand
