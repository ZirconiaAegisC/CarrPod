# Glossary of Terms

**Document Identifier:** ACS-RVN-APDX-002
**Date:** July 2026

---

## A

**ACM3 (Autonomy Compute Module 3):** Rivian's third-generation central compute platform for autonomous driving, designed to process sensor data and execute driving policy algorithms. Part of the RAP1 hardware platform.

**ADAS (Advanced Driver-Assistance System):** Electronic systems that assist drivers in driving and parking functions. Includes features such as adaptive cruise control, lane-keeping assist, and automated emergency braking.

**ASP (Average Selling Price):** The average price at which a product is sold, calculated as total revenue from product sales divided by the number of units sold.

**ATVM (Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing) Loan Program:** A U.S. Department of Energy direct loan program established under the Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007 to support the manufacturing of eligible advanced technology vehicles and components in the United States.

## B

**Bill of Materials (BOM):** A comprehensive inventory of the raw materials, sub-assemblies, intermediate assemblies, sub-components, parts, and quantities of each needed to manufacture a product.

**Body Shop:** The section of an automotive assembly plant where the vehicle's body structure (body-in-white) is assembled from stamped metal components through welding, bonding, and riveting processes.

## C

**Capex (Capital Expenditure):** Funds used by a company to acquire, upgrade, or maintain physical assets such as property, industrial buildings, and equipment.

**CCS (Combined Charging System):** A standardized connector system for electric vehicle DC fast charging, combining AC and DC charging capabilities in a single connector.

**COGS (Cost of Goods Sold):** The direct costs attributable to the production of goods sold by a company, including materials, direct labor, and manufacturing overhead.

**Convertible Note/Debt:** A type of debt security that can be converted into a predetermined number of the issuer's equity shares, typically at the discretion of the noteholder.

## D

**D2C (Direct-to-Consumer):** A sales model wherein the manufacturer sells products directly to end consumers, bypassing traditional intermediary channels such as franchised dealerships.

**Deliveries:** The number of vehicles transferred to end customers during a reporting period. In Rivian's reporting, deliveries generally exclude vehicles used for internal purposes (company fleet, test vehicles, marketing).

**DOE (Department of Energy):** The U.S. federal government department responsible for energy policy, including the Loan Programs Office that administers the ATVM loan program.

**Drive Unit:** The assembly comprising an electric motor, inverter, gearbox, and associated components that delivers torque to the vehicle's wheels. Rivian produces single-motor, dual-motor, and quad-motor configurations.

## E

**EBITDA (Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization):** A measure of a company's operating performance. Adjusted EBITDA excludes certain non-recurring or non-cash items.

**ECU (Electronic Control Unit):** An embedded system in automotive electronics that controls one or more electrical systems or subsystems in a vehicle.

**EDV (Electric Delivery Van):** Rivian's purpose-built commercial electric van platform, produced for Amazon under a 100,000-unit framework agreement. Available in EDV 500 and EDV 700 configurations.

**Enduro Motor:** Rivian's in-house designed and manufactured electric drive unit, introduced in 2023, available in dual-motor and single-motor configurations.

## F

**FMVSS (Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards):** U.S. federal regulations specifying design, construction, performance, and durability requirements for motor vehicles and regulated safety-related components.

**Free Cash Flow:** The cash generated by a company's operations after accounting for capital expenditures. A measure of financial performance and flexibility.

## G

**Georgia Facility:** Rivian's second manufacturing plant, located in Morgan and Walton Counties, Georgia. Phase 1 targets 300,000 units of annual production capacity; vertical construction began in spring 2026 with vehicle production targeted for late 2028.

**Gross Margin:** Gross profit as a percentage of revenue. Gross profit equals revenue minus cost of goods sold.

**Gross Profit:** Revenue minus cost of goods sold. Represents the profit generated from core manufacturing and sales activities before operating expenses.

## I

**ISO 26262:** An international standard for functional safety of electrical and electronic systems in production road vehicles. Defines Automotive Safety Integrity Levels (ASIL) from A (least stringent) to D (most stringent).

## J

**JPO (Jobs Per Hour):** A measure of automotive assembly line throughput, indicating the number of vehicles produced per hour of line operation.

## L

**LDM (Language Driver Model):** Rivian's proprietary autonomous driving policy architecture that leverages large language model-derived neural network approaches for trajectory planning and driving decision-making, with integrated explainability features.

**LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging):** A remote sensing method that uses pulsed laser light to measure distances to objects and create high-resolution 3D representations of the environment. Core sensor modality in Rivian's autonomous driving hardware suite.

## M

**Mobile Service:** Rivian's fleet of service vehicles capable of performing maintenance, diagnostics, and repairs at customer locations rather than requiring a service center visit.

## N

**NACS (North American Charging Standard):** The EV charging connector standard originally developed by Tesla and adopted by Rivian and other manufacturers. Formerly known as the Tesla charging connector.

**NHTSA (National Highway Traffic Safety Administration):** The U.S. federal agency responsible for vehicle safety standards, including regulations governing automated driving systems.

**Normal Facility:** Rivian's primary manufacturing plant in Normal, Illinois, acquired from Mitsubishi in 2017. Currently produces R1T, R1S, EDV, and R2 vehicles.

## O

**OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness):** A manufacturing metric that measures the percentage of truly productive manufacturing time, accounting for availability, performance, and quality.

**OTA (Over-the-Air):** The wireless delivery of software updates, feature upgrades, and configuration changes to vehicles without requiring a physical service visit.

## P

**Parc:** The total population of a given vehicle model or brand in operation (cumulative deliveries minus vehicles retired from service).

**Production/Production Volume:** The number of vehicles manufactured during a reporting period, regardless of whether they have been delivered to customers.

## R

**R1 Platform:** Rivian's first-generation vehicle platform, underpinning the R1T pickup truck and R1S sport utility vehicle. Features a skateboard architecture with a structural battery pack and integrated drive units.

**R1T:** Rivian's five-passenger electric pickup truck, the company's first production consumer vehicle.

**R1S:** Rivian's seven-passenger electric sport utility vehicle, the second consumer vehicle on the R1 platform.

**R2 Platform (R2):** Rivian's second-generation vehicle platform, the basis for a five-passenger mid-size electric SUV targeting a ~$45,000 starting price. First saleable production achieved April 2026.

**R3 Platform:** Rivian's announced future compact crossover platform, positioned below the R2 in terms of size and price.

**RAN (Rivian Adventure Network):** Rivian's proprietary DC fast-charging network, sited along travel corridors and near outdoor recreation destinations.

**RAP1 (Rivian Autonomy Platform 1):** The integrated hardware suite (sensors, compute, redundancy systems) for Rivian's autonomous driving capability, deployed in conjunction with the LDM software stack.

## S

**SAE (Society of Automotive Engineers):** The international standards development organization responsible for the J3016 standard defining levels of driving automation (Level 0 through Level 5).

**SAE Level 2:** Partial driving automation; the vehicle can control both steering and acceleration/deceleration simultaneously, but the driver must remain engaged and monitor the driving environment at all times.

**SAE Level 4:** High driving automation; the vehicle can perform all driving functions under certain conditions without human intervention. The driver is not required to monitor the driving environment in the vehicle's operational design domain.

**Skateboard Platform:** An EV architecture where the battery pack, electric motors, and core structural components are integrated into a flat, low-profile chassis, upon which various body styles can be mounted.

## T

**Tier-1/Tier-2/Tier-3 Supplier:** Automotive supply chain classification: Tier-1 suppliers deliver directly to the OEM; Tier-2 suppliers deliver to Tier-1 suppliers; Tier-3 suppliers deliver raw materials or basic components to Tier-2 suppliers.

## U

**Universal Hands-Free:** Rivian's SAE Level 2 advanced driver-assistance system featuring hands-free operation on 3.5 million miles of mapped roadways across the United States and Canada.

## V

**V2H (Vehicle-to-Home):** Bidirectional charging capability allowing an electric vehicle to supply power back to a home electrical system.

**VIN (Vehicle Identification Number):** A unique 17-character identifier assigned to every motor vehicle at the time of manufacture.

## W

**Well-to-Wheel Efficiency:** A measure of energy efficiency accounting for the entire energy lifecycle, from primary energy extraction through vehicle propulsion.

## Z

**ZEV (Zero-Emission Vehicle) Credits:** Tradeable regulatory credits earned by manufacturers for producing and selling zero-emission vehicles in states that have adopted California's ZEV program. Credits can be sold to other manufacturers to meet regulatory requirements.

**Zonal Electrical Architecture:** An automotive electrical and electronic system architecture where control functions are organized by physical zones within the vehicle rather than by function, reducing wiring harness mass and complexity.
