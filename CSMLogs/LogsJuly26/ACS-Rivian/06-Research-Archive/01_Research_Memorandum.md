## 3. Analytical Methodology

### 3.1 Financial Analysis

Financial data is derived from Rivian's quarterly earnings releases and SEC filings. Forward-looking estimates represent management guidance where available; where management has not provided specific guidance, estimates are derived from consensus analyst estimates, historical trend extrapolation, and independent modeling. All financial figures are stated on a GAAP basis unless explicitly noted as "adjusted" (non-GAAP). Key assumptions underlying financial projections are documented in the relevant dossier sections.

### 3.2 Production and Capacity Analysis

Production and delivery data are sourced directly from Rivian's quarterly operational updates. Capacity estimates for the Normal facility represent management commentary and independent analysis; Georgia capacity figures are based on Rivian's public statements and DOE ATVM loan documentation. Production ramp trajectories are modeled using industry-standard S-curve and experience-curve methodologies, adjusted for Rivian-specific ramp data from the R1 and R2 launches.

### 3.3 Competitive Analysis

Competitive positioning is assessed using publicly available information from competitor SEC filings, press releases, product specification pages, third-party vehicle reviews, and industry analyst reports. Market share and segment data are sourced from S&P Global Mobility, BloombergNEF, and Automotive News. Pricing comparisons are based on manufacturer-suggested retail prices (MSRP) as published on company websites as of mid-2026.

### 3.4 Technology Assessment

The assessment of Rivian's autonomous driving technology (RAP1/ACM3 hardware, LDM software architecture, Universal Hands-Free) is based on Rivian's public technical disclosures, patent filings, executive presentations, and product demonstrations. Comparative technology assessments are based on publicly available information from competitor disclosures and independent technical analyses.

## 4. Key Assumptions and Limitations

### 4.1 Forward-Looking Assumptions

This dossier incorporates forward-looking statements and projections. Key assumptions include:
- Continuation of current US federal EV tax credit ($7,500) through the projection period
- No material adverse changes to US trade policy affecting battery materials, semiconductors, or vehicle components sourced from China or other nations
- Sustained consumer demand for electric vehicles consistent with industry forecasts
- Successful execution of Rivian's announced production ramp, product launch, and partnership plans
- Availability of capital on terms consistent with historical access patterns
- No material adverse regulatory changes affecting autonomous vehicle deployment

### 4.2 Methodological Limitations

- The dossier relies substantially on company-provided information and forward-looking statements, which are inherently subject to revision and may differ materially from actual outcomes
- Financial projections beyond FY2026 are extrapolative and subject to wide confidence intervals
- Competitive and market analysis is based on publicly available information, which may not reflect non-public competitive developments
- Technology assessments are based on public disclosures and may overstate or understate actual technical capability and maturity
- The dossier reflects information available as of July 2026; subsequent developments may materially alter the analysis
