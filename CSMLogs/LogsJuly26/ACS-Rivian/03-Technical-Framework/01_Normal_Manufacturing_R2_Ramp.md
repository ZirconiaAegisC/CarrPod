# Normal, Illinois Manufacturing & R2 Production Ramp

**Document Identifier:** ACS-RVN-TECH-001
**Date:** July 2026

---

## 1. Facility Overview

The Normal, Illinois manufacturing plant is Rivian's principal operational production facility. Acquired from Mitsubishi Motors North America in January 2017 for approximately $16 million, the site occupies roughly 3.3 million square feet of enclosed manufacturing space on approximately 500 acres in McLean County, Illinois. The facility was redesigned and retooled from 2017 through 2020 to accommodate Rivian's skateboard platform architecture, integrating stamping, body shop, paint operations, general assembly, battery pack production, and electric drive unit manufacturing within a single contiguous campus.

## 2. Current Production Lines

### 2.1 R1 Line — R1T and R1S

The R1 production line manufactures the company's flagship R1T pickup truck and R1S sport utility vehicle. Both vehicles share a common skateboard platform comprising a structural battery pack enclosure, integrated thermal management system, and front and rear drive unit assemblies. The line operates with both Enduro dual-motor and quad-motor drivetrain configurations, utilizing Rivian's in-house motor and inverter designs introduced in 2023. Current production cadence for FY2025 reached 42,284 total units produced and delivered across all platforms.

### 2.2 EDV Line — Commercial Delivery Vans

The EDV line produces the EDV 500 and EDV 700 commercial electric delivery vans under the standing agreement with Amazon. These vehicles utilize a purpose-built architecture distinct from the R1 skateboard, optimized for last-mile delivery duty cycles with high-roof cargo configurations, roll-up rear doors, and integrated fleet telematics. Production is demand-gated against Amazon's phased delivery schedule under the 100,000-unit framework agreement.

### 2.3 R2 Line — Mid-Size Platform

The R2 production line represents the newest addition to the Normal facility. The R2 is a five-passenger mid-size electric SUV built on a next-generation platform architecture that reduces part count, simplifies manufacturing complexity, and targets a materially lower bill-of-materials cost relative to the R1 platform. The R2 line achieved first saleable production in April 2026, with initial employee deliveries commencing the same month. External customer deliveries are imminent as of mid-2026.

## 3. R2 Production Ramp Analysis

### 3.1 Ramp Cadence

The R2 production ramp represents the single most critical manufacturing initiative at Rivian during FY2026–2027. The ramp is structured in three phases:

**Phase I — Pilot and Early Production (Q1–Q2 2026)**
- Line commissioning and validation completed in Q1 2026
- First saleable customer-intent vehicles produced in April 2026
- Employee delivery program initiated as a controlled validation fleet
- Production rate: low single-digit units per day; emphasis on process stability, quality gate compliance, and supply chain fill-rate verification

**Phase II — Rate Climb (Q3–Q4 2026)**
- Progressive increase in line speed, shift structure, and throughput
- Target: exit Q4 2026 at a weekly run rate sufficient to support a meaningful portion of the 65,000–70,000-unit FY2026 total delivery guidance
- Critical path dependencies: battery cell supply from Rivian's qualified suppliers, production tooling throughput validation, and tier-2 supplier ramp synchronization

**Phase III — Steady-State Production (FY2027)**
- Target: sustained high-volume output on the R2 line
- Two-shift or three-shift operation as dictated by order backlog and market demand
- Continuous improvement programs targeting yield, cycle time reduction, and direct labor per unit

### 3.2 Supply Chain Architecture

The R2 platform introduces a simplified supply chain architecture compared to the R1. Key design differences include:
- Reduced wiring harness complexity through domain-based zonal electrical architecture
- Fewer unique stamped body-side components
- Simplified interior trim and seating system with reduced SKU count
- Streamlined battery pack module design leveraging lessons from R1 and EDV programs
- Joint electrical architecture co-developed with Volkswagen Group

### 3.3 Manufacturing Efficiency Targets

The R2 production system targets a per-unit manufacturing cost significantly below the R1 baseline. Key levers include:
- Higher commonization of components across the R2 platform
- Reduced direct labor hours per vehicle through design-for-manufacturing optimization
- Lower capital intensity per unit of installed capacity vs. the R1 line
- Co-location benefits from shared Normal facility infrastructure (paint, stamping, logistics)

## 4. Workforce and Operational Structure

The Normal facility employs an estimated 8,000+ workers across manufacturing, engineering, supply chain, quality, and administrative functions. The workforce model combines direct Rivian employees with contracted support services for logistics, facilities maintenance, and select sub-assembly operations. The ramp of R2 production has driven incremental hiring in manufacturing operations, quality engineering, supplier quality, and production control functions throughout FY2026.

## 6. Risk Factors

- **Single-site concentration:** Until Georgia reaches production, nearly all Rivian volume depends on Normal uninterrupted operations
- **Multi-line complexity:** Managing four distinct vehicle lines (R1T, R1S, EDV, R2) within a single facility increases operational complexity relative to dedicated-line factories
- **R2 ramp execution:** The R2 ramp trajectory will determine whether Rivian achieves the FY2026 delivery guidance and establishes credibility in the mid-size segment
- **Supply chain scaling:** Tier-2 and tier-3 supplier readiness for R2 volume ramp remains a key execution risk, particularly for novel components with limited alternative sources
- **Labor market:** The Bloomington-Normal labor market is finite; aggressive hiring for R2 may stress local workforce availability and drive wage inflation
