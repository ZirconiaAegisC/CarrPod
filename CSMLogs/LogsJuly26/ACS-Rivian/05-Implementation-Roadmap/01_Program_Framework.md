## 1. Program Scope and Objectives

The ACS-Rivian-EV-Infrastructure dossier establishes a multi-dimensional program framework for evaluating, monitoring, and decision-support regarding Rivian Automotive's operational and strategic trajectory. The program scope encompasses manufacturing scale-up, product platform deployment, infrastructure build-out, autonomous fleet commercialization, and financial sustainability. This document defines the implementation roadmap, milestone architecture, and governance framework.

## 2. Strategic Pillars

The program is organized around five strategic pillars, each with defined objectives, milestones, and risk monitoring parameters:

### Pillar I: Manufacturing Scale-Up
**Objective:** Achieve sustained production capacity of 200,000+ annual units across Normal and Georgia facilities by FY2030.
- Normal IL: Optimize multi-line production (R1T, R1S, EDV, R2); achieve R2 steady-state by late FY2026
- Georgia: Complete Phase 1 construction and commissioning; first saleable vehicles by late 2028; ramp to 300,000-unit annual capacity by FY2030

### Pillar II: R2 Platform Success
**Objective:** Establish R2 as a top-tier competitor in the mid-size EV crossover segment with sustained annual volume exceeding 100,000 units.
- First saleable production: Achieved April 2026
- External customer deliveries: Imminent (Q3 2026)
- Volume ramp: Achieve Normal steady-state output
- Georgia transition: Seamless production transfer to Georgia without customer delivery interruption
- Market acceptance: Achieve segment share targets consistent with 100,000+ annual unit demand

### Pillar III: Autonomous Fleet Commercialization
**Objective:** Deploy and operationalize the Uber robotaxi fleet, achieving commercial driverless operations in initial markets.
- RAP1/ACM3 hardware validation: Complete by late 2026
- LDM software safety case: Regulatory submission timeline TBD
- Initial fleet deployment (safety driver phase): 2027–2028 (projected)
- Commercial driverless operations: 2028–2030 (projected, market-dependent)
- Fleet scale: 10,000 firm units; evaluate 40,000-unit option exercise by 2030

### Pillar IV: Financial Sustainability
**Objective:** Achieve positive vehicle gross profit, operating income breakeven, and positive free cash flow on a sustained basis.
- Positive vehicle gross profit: Late FY2026 / FY2027
- Quarterly operating income breakeven: FY2028 (projected)
- Annual operating income positive: FY2029–2030 (projected)
- Positive free cash flow: FY2030+ (projected)

### Pillar V: Infrastructure and Ecosystem
**Objective:** Scale service, retail, and charging infrastructure in proportion to the growing vehicle parc and operational requirements.
- Service centers: Scale from 97 to 150+ by FY2028
- Mobile service fleet: Scale from ~700 to 1,200+ by FY2028
- Retail spaces: Scale from 36 to 60+ by FY2028, with R2-market-appropriate locations
- RAN charging stations: Scale with delivery volumes; open to non-Rivian vehicles for incremental revenue
- Fleet depot charging: Develop dedicated infrastructure for robotaxi operations

## 4. Program Governance

| Review Type | Frequency | Scope |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| Weekly Operations Review | Weekly | Production metrics, delivery throughput, critical supply chain status |

### 4.2 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

**Manufacturing:**
- Units produced (by model line, by week/month/quarter)
- Units delivered (by model line)
- Production/delivery ratio
- Plant utilization rate
- First-pass yield (quality gate pass rate)
- Line speed (jobs per hour)

**Financial:**
- Revenue ($M)
- Gross profit ($M) and gross margin (%)
- Adjusted EBITDA ($M)
- Capital expenditures ($M)
- Cash and equivalents ($M)
- Revenue per vehicle delivered
- Cost of goods sold per vehicle produced

**R2 Platform:**
- R2 production ramp rate (units/week)
- R2 order and reservation backlog
- R2 customer delivery satisfaction scores
- R2 warranty claims per 1,000 vehicles

**Autonomous:**
- Universal Hands-Free miles driven
- Universal Hands-Free utilization rate
- LDM disengagement rate (testing environment)
- Uber fleet deployment progress (vehicles deployed)
- Regulatory approval status (by jurisdiction)

**Infrastructure:**
- Service center count and capacity utilization
- Mobile service fleet count and utilization
- Retail space count and foot traffic
- RAN station count and utilization
- Customer satisfaction (Net Promoter Score)

## 5. Risk Management Framework

### 5.1 Risk Categories and Mitigation

| Risk Category | Top Risks | Mitigation Strategy |
|--------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Production Execution | R2 ramp slower than planned; Normal multi-line complexity reduces throughput | Phased ramp with quality gates; line-specific OEE targets; buffer inventory for critical components |
| Supply Chain | Tier-2/3 supplier capacity constraints for R2 volume; single-source components | Dual-sourcing qualification; strategic inventory of long-lead items; supplier development engineering support |
| Financial | Cash consumption exceeds plan; capital market access constrained | DOE loan draw acceleration; expense contingency triggers; scenario planning for delayed capital inflows |
| Technology | LDM autonomy stack maturity insufficient for regulatory approval; sensor supply chain disruption | Parallel development tracks; incremental deployment approach (geofenced → expanded); sensor vendor diversification |
| Market | R2 demand below volume targets; competitive price compression | Pricing flexibility (R2 configurable options); marketing investment; lease and financing programs |
| Regulatory | State-level direct-sales restrictions; autonomous vehicle deployment regulation changes | Legal advocacy and state-by-state compliance strategy; federal AV framework engagement |
| Geopolitical | Trade policy affecting battery materials or components; China-Taiwan semiconductor supply risk | Supply chain geographic diversification; strategic inventory of critical electronics |

### 5.2 Contingency Triggers

Defined triggers will prompt escalation and contingency plan activation:
- R2 production ramp deviates >20% from plan for two consecutive months
- Cash balance projected to fall below $2.0 billion within 12 months
- Georgia construction milestone delays exceeding 6 months
- Key supplier force majeure or bankruptcy
- Major regulatory action restricting autonomous deployment
- Quarterly gross margin reversal below 0%
