## 1. Corporate Overview

Rivian Automotive, Inc. (NASDAQ: RIVN) is an American electric vehicle manufacturer and automotive technology company headquartered in Irvine, California. Founded in 2009 by Robert "RJ" Scaringe, the company designs, develops, and manufactures category-defining electric vehicles and accessories, and sells them directly to customers in the consumer and commercial markets. Rivian's mission is to preserve the natural world and create a future of zero-emission transportation and energy independence.

The company is governed as a Delaware corporation with a single-class common stock structure and a dual-class structure that grants the founder enhanced voting control through Class B shares.

## 3. Product Portfolio

### 3.1 R1 Platform (Current Generation)

- **R1T** — Five-passenger electric pickup truck; up to 400+ miles range; 0–60 mph in 3.0 seconds; 11,000 lbs towing capacity
- **R1S** — Seven-passenger electric SUV; up to 400+ miles range; three-row configuration; 7,700 lbs towing capacity
- Both R1 vehicles utilize Rivian's proprietary skateboard platform architecture, featuring quad-motor and dual-motor drivetrain options, air suspension, and an integrated battery pack serving as a structural component

### 3.2 R2 Platform (2026 Launch)

- **R2** — Five-passenger mid-size electric SUV; target starting price approximately $45,000; designed for high-volume manufacturing with simplified architecture and reduced component count
- First saleable production achieved April 2026
- Employee deliveries commenced April 2026; external customer deliveries imminent
- Represents the company's entry into the high-volume mid-size crossover/SUV segment, competing with the Tesla Model Y, Ford Mustang Mach-E, Hyundai Ioniq 5, and Kia EV6

### 3.3 EDV (Electric Delivery Van)

- **EDV 500 / EDV 700** — Commercial electric delivery vans developed in collaboration with Amazon
- Purpose-built for last-mile delivery, featuring optimized cargo volume, driver ergonomics, and fleet management integration
- Amazon's initial order of 100,000 units by 2030 remains the anchor commercial contract

### 3.4 R3 Platform (Future)

- Announced as a compact crossover platform targeting an even lower price point than R2
- Further details pending formal product roadmap updates

## 4. Manufacturing Footprint

### 4.1 Normal, Illinois Facility

Acquired from Mitsubishi in 2017 for $16 million, the Normal plant spans approximately 3.3 million square feet on 500 acres. The facility underwent extensive retooling and modernization to accommodate Rivian's skateboard platform production. Current production includes R1T, R1S, EDV commercial vans, and R2. The plant features integrated stamping, body, paint, and general assembly operations alongside battery pack and drive unit production.

### 4.2 Georgia Facility (Under Construction)

Located east of Atlanta in Morgan and Walton Counties, the Georgia site encompasses approximately 1,800 acres. Phase 1 targets 300,000 units of annual capacity (increased 50% from the initial 200,000-unit plan). Vertical construction began in spring 2026 with vehicle production expected in late 2028. The facility is supported by a $4.5 billion DOE Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) loan.

## 6. Corporate Positioning and Competitive Landscape

Rivian occupies a distinctive position in the EV industry as a vertically integrated manufacturer with its own vehicle platforms, software stack, and direct-to-consumer distribution model. The company competes across multiple segments:

- **Adventure/Lifestyle EV Trucks and SUVs** — Against Tesla Cybertruck, Ford F-150 Lightning, GMC Hummer EV, and upcoming entries from Chevrolet and Ram
- **Mid-Size EV Crossovers (R2)** — Against the Tesla Model Y, Ford Mustang Mach-E, Hyundai Ioniq 5, Kia EV6, and Volkswagen ID.4
- **Commercial EV Vans** — Against Ford E-Transit, BrightDrop Zevo, and Mercedes-Benz eSprinter
- **Autonomous Mobility** — Against Waymo, Cruise, Zoox, and Tesla's planned robotaxi service

Rivian's differentiation stems from its brand identity as an outdoor and adventure-oriented manufacturer, its vertically integrated software and electronics architecture, and its multi-platform product strategy spanning consumer, commercial, and autonomous mobility markets.
