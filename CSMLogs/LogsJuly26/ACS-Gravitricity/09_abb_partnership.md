# ABB Partnership

## Partnership Formation

In December 2023, Gravitricity announced a formal partnership with ABB, the Swiss-Swedish
multinational technology company and one of the world's leading suppliers of mine hoist
systems. The partnership was structured as a memorandum of understanding covering the
supply of hoist technology for GraviStore gravity storage installations, with particular
focus on the Pyhäsalmi Mine project in Finland.

The partnership was a significant validation of Gravitricity's technology, as ABB is the
dominant global supplier of mine hoist systems with an installed base of over 1,000 hoists
worldwide and a market share exceeding 50% in the mining hoist segment. ABB's involvement
signalled to potential investors and project partners that the GraviStore hoist
requirements could be met with proven, off-the-shelf technology rather than bespoke
development.

## ABB Hoist Technology

ABB's mine hoist product line includes:

- Hydraulic disc brake systems: Fail-safe, spring-applied braking with programmable
  deceleration profiles. ABB's brake systems are certified to EN 1806 and comply with
  the braking requirements of mining regulations worldwide, including the demanding
  Swedish and Finnish standards that apply to the Pyhäsalmi site.

- ACS6000 medium-voltage drives: ABB's medium-voltage AC drive platform, capable of
  controlling synchronous and induction motors from 3 to 36 MW. The ACS6000 uses
  integrated gate-commutated thyristor (IGCT) power semiconductors with active front-end
  technology, providing four-quadrant operation and regenerative braking capability—the
  same functionality required for GraviStore's bidirectional energy flow.

- AMZ synchronous motors: ABB's low-speed, high-torque synchronous motors designed for
  direct drum coupling, eliminating the need for a gearbox between the motor and the
  hoist drum. Direct drive reduces mechanical losses, maintenance requirements, and
  capital cost.

- ABB Ability Mine Hoist Monitor: A digital condition monitoring system that collects
  data from sensors on the hoist mechanical, electrical, and braking systems and applies
  predictive analytics to schedule maintenance before failures occur. For a GraviStore
  installation targeting 50+ years of operation with 50,000+ cycles, predictive
  maintenance is essential for achieving the target availability of 97%.

## Scope of the Partnership

The MOU covered the following areas:

1. Hoist system supply for the Pyhäsalmi project: ABB would provide the complete
   electromechanical hoist package—motor, drive, brakes, controls, and condition
   monitoring—for the 2 MW GraviStore installation. The equipment would be based on
   ABB's standard mine hoist product range, configured for the specific duty cycle and
   response time requirements of gravity energy storage.

2. Technology roadmap collaboration: ABB and Gravitricity would jointly develop a
   standardised hoist specification for future GraviStore installations across a range
   of power ratings (1–10 MW) and shaft configurations. The objective was to create a
   modular, repeatable design that could be deployed across multiple sites with minimal
   site-specific engineering.

3. Service and maintenance: ABB would provide long-term service agreements for GraviStore
   installations, leveraging its global service network in mining regions to support
   hoist maintenance, spare parts supply, and remote monitoring.

4. Joint marketing: ABB and Gravitricity would collaborate on marketing to mining
   companies seeking to repurpose disused shafts for energy storage, combining ABB's
   credibility in the mining sector with Gravitricity's proprietary gravity storage
   technology.

## Strategic Significance

The ABB partnership addressed one of the key investor concerns about Gravitricity: whether
the hoist technology for gravity energy storage existed at commercial readiness. By
partnering with ABB, Gravitricity could credibly claim that the hoist—the single most
critical and expensive subsystem—was available from a Tier 1 supplier with a global track
record.

For ABB, the partnership represented an entry into the emerging gravity energy storage
market, which, if successful, could become a meaningful new revenue stream for the mine
hoist division. ABB's hoist business is otherwise tied to the cyclical mining industry,
and energy storage offered a counter-cyclical diversification opportunity.

## Post-Liquidation Impact

The ABB MOU was a non-binding commercial arrangement and would not survive Gravitricity's
liquidation in its current form. However, the technical work completed under the
partnership—including the preliminary hoist specification for the Pyhäsalmi project and
the concept-level standardised design—remains valuable. Any acquirer of Gravitricity's
IP and project rights could approach ABB to re-establish the partnership on
substantially similar terms, given ABB's strategic interest in the gravity storage
market.

ABB's involvement also provides a partial technical due diligence reference for
prospective IP acquirers. The fact that ABB's engineering team reviewed the GraviStore
hoist duty cycle, confirmed the suitability of off-the-shelf equipment, and signed an
MOU provides independent validation that the core electromechanical requirements are
achievable.
