# IP Sale and Asset Acquisition

## Sale Process

Hilco Global, a specialist in the valuation and disposal of intellectual property and
intangible assets, was appointed as the sales agent for Gravitricity's IP portfolio
following the company's entry into voluntary liquidation on 1 October 2025.

The sale package comprises:

1. Patent Portfolio: 10 granted patents covering:
   - Multi-weight mine shaft gravity energy storage (core GraviStore patent)
   - Wire rope tension equalisation system
   - Shaft guide and arrestor mechanisms for retrofitting disused mine shafts
   - Modular weight fabrication and assembly methods enabling weight construction within
     shaft diameter constraints
   - Hybrid gravity-hydrogen storage configuration (co-located GraviStore and H2FlexiStore
     in a single shaft)
   - Control algorithms for grid frequency response using gravity storage
   - Emergency descent and energy dissipation system (failsafe for loss of grid connection)
   - Lined rock shaft hydrogen storage (H2FlexiStore core patent)
   - Pressure monitoring and leak detection system for lined rock shaft gas storage
   - Shaft-sealing system for high-pressure gas containment

   Patent jurisdictions include the UK, European Patent Office (EPO), United States,
   China, Australia, Canada, South Africa, and India—selected to cover both existing
   mining regions and future growth markets.

2. Trademarks: "GraviStore" and "H2FlexiStore" registered in the UK and EUIPO.

3. Technical Documentation and Know-How:
   - Port of Leith demonstrator: design drawings, test data, control system source code
     (if not subject to third-party licensing), commissioning reports
   - Pyhäsalmi Mine FEED study: 60% complete engineering package, including shaft survey
     data, geotechnical analysis, hoist specification (with ABB input), grid connection
     study (Fingrid), and environmental scoping report
   - H2FlexiStore SIF study: geotechnical modelling data from Edinburgh University,
     hydrogen compatibility testing data, techno-economic model (Guidehouse), regulatory
     pathway report (Energy Reform), life-cycle assessment (Premzero)
   - Business development: expressions of interest, non-disclosure agreements, and
     memoranda of understanding with potential project partners, shaft owners, and
     mining companies (subject to confidentiality obligations)

4. Commercial Agreements (to the extent assignable):
   - ABB MOU for hoist technology supply
   - Callio Pyhäjärvi MOU for Pyhäsalmi Mine project
   - SGN consortium agreement for Ofgem SIF project (post-completion; IP rights may be
     governed by consortium agreement terms)

## Bid Deadline and Process

The deadline for indicative bids was set for 25 February 2026. Hilco's marketing materials
positioned the IP portfolio as a "turnkey opportunity" for an acquirer seeking to enter
the gravity energy storage market with a proven technology concept, an advanced project
pipeline, and independent technology validation from Imperial College London.

## Potential Acquirers

The following categories of acquirer are most likely to find value in the Gravitricity IP:

### Mining Companies

Mining companies with portfolios of disused shafts represent the most logical acquirers,
as they control the critical site resource and can develop GraviStore installations as
a diversification strategy or a post-closure land use solution. Companies such as
Anglo American, Glencore, Rio Tinto, and BHP have established closure and rehabilitation
obligations for their legacy mine sites, and GraviStore offers a potential revenue-
generating post-mining land use.

The South African gold mining sector, where shafts exceeding 2,000 metres depth are
common, is particularly attractive for ultra-deep, high-energy-capacity installations.
Companies such as Harmony Gold, Gold Fields, and Sibanye-Stillwater control significant
portfolios of deep shafts.

### Energy Storage Developers

Independent energy storage developers and infrastructure funds—such as Gore Street
Capital, Gresham House, Harmony Energy, or Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners—could
acquire the IP to add gravity storage to their technology portfolios, which are
currently dominated by BESS. The long asset life and absence of degradation risk offer
portfolio diversification benefits.

### Industrial Technology Companies

Companies with existing positions in the hoist and winding equipment market—ABB,
Siemens, FLSmidth, or Ingeteam—could acquire the IP to offer a complete gravity
storage solution to their mining and utility customers, integrating their hoist
equipment with Gravitricity's patented system configuration and control algorithms.

### Infrastructure and Pension Funds

Long-duration, low-degradation infrastructure assets with 50+ year lives align well
with the liability-matching requirements of pension funds and infrastructure investors.
An infrastructure fund could acquire the IP, fund the Pyhäsalmi first-of-a-kind
project, and build a portfolio of GraviStore assets generating regulated or contracted
revenues. This model would require a strategic partner with mining engineering
capability to manage the technical aspects of project development.

## Valuation Considerations

The value of the IP portfolio is highly uncertain and depends on the acquirer's
assessment of:

- The probability of achieving a commercial-scale reference project (Pyhäsalmi)
- The size of the addressable market for gravity storage in disused mine shafts
- The time and capital required to reach commercial deployment (likely 2–3 years
  minimum from acquisition)
- The competitive landscape: whether alternative gravity storage concepts emerge
  (e.g., Energy Vault's above-ground crane-based system, which has progressed to
  commercial deployment in China, albeit with different technical characteristics)

Given the liquidation context, the IP is likely to sell for a fraction of
Gravitricity's cumulative investment (estimated at £10–£15 million over the company's
14-year life). Comparable IP fire sales in the energy technology sector suggest a
range of £50,000–£500,000, depending on the number and motivation of bidders.
