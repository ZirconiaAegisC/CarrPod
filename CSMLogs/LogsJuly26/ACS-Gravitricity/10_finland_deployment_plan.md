# Finland Deployment Plan

## Strategic Rationale for Finland

Gravitricity's selection of Finland as the location for its first full-scale commercial
deployment was driven by a confluence of geological, regulatory, market, and partnership
factors that made Pyhäsalmi the most viable near-term project among the company's global
pipeline of candidate sites.

Finland offered three structural advantages over the UK, where Gravitricity was
headquartered:

1. Mine repurposing policy: Finland has an explicit national policy of supporting the
   economic repurposing of disused mines, administered through Business Finland and the
   regional development agencies. The Pyhäjärvi municipality and Callio Pyhäjärvi had
   already secured political and financial support for mine repurposing before
   Gravitricity's engagement. In contrast, the UK lacks a coordinated policy framework
   for mine shaft repurposing for energy storage.

2. Grid connection process: Fingrid's connection process for storage assets is more
   streamlined than the UK's, with pre-defined technical requirements and connection
   charges that are published and stable. The UK's connection queue, by contrast, has
   been characterised by long lead times and speculative applications that congest the
   queue.

3. Local stakeholder alignment: The Pyhäjärvi municipality, Callio Pyhäjärvi, and the
   regional council were uniformly supportive. The project was seen as a high-profile
   demonstration of post-mining economic transition, which aligned with the political
   priorities of the Centre Party, the dominant political force in northern Ostrobothnia.

## Deployment Timeline

The project timeline, as envisioned in Gravitricity's 2024 business plan, was as follows:

H2 2024 — Front-End Engineering Design (FEED) completion
- Final hoist specification with ABB
- Detailed shaft survey (laser scanning of full shaft depth)
- Geotechnical investigation (borehole drilling from surface alongside shaft collar)
- Grid connection agreement finalised with Fingrid
- Environmental Impact Assessment scoping report submitted to ELY Centre

H1 2025 — Final Investment Decision and Permitting
- Environmental permit application submitted (Finnish Environmental Protection Act)
- Building permit application submitted to Pyhäjärvi municipality
- Project financing closed (target: €12–€15 million total capital requirement)
- Long-lead equipment orders placed (hoist drum, motor, drive)

H2 2025 — Site Mobilisation and Construction
- Shaft preparation: cleaning, lining repair, guide rail installation
- Headframe modification for hoist drum and sheave installation
- Surface construction: hoist house, power conversion building, control room
- Weight fabrication: steel frame assembly and concrete ballast casting

H1 2026 — Commissioning
- Subsystem testing: hoist no-load, brake testing, drive commissioning
- Weight installation: lowering of weight assembly into shaft
- Integrated system testing: charge-discharge cycles at increasing power levels
- Grid compliance testing: frequency response, voltage control, power quality
- Commercial operation commencement

## Financing Plan

Gravitricity's financing plan for the Pyhäsalmi project combined three sources:

1. Equity: £5–£7 million from the £40 million fundraise targeted in 2023. The Pyhäsalmi
   project was intended to be the primary use of proceeds from the equity round.

2. Business Finland Energy Aid: A Finnish government investment grant for innovative
   energy projects, potentially covering 30–40% of eligible capital costs (approximately
   €3–€5 million). Business Finland had expressed preliminary interest in supporting the
   project as part of its "Smart Energy" programme.

3. EU Innovation Fund or Horizon Europe: European Union funding for first-of-a-kind clean
   energy demonstration projects. The Innovation Fund, in particular, targets projects
   with the potential for significant greenhouse gas emission reductions and
   replicability. Gravitricity had engaged consultants to prepare an Innovation Fund
   application for the 2024 call.

## Status at Liquidation

The failure of the 2023–2024 equity fundraise was fatal to the Pyhäsalmi project
timeline. Without the equity component, the Business Finland and EU funding applications
could not proceed, as both require evidence of secured co-financing. The FEED study
was not completed to 100% (approximately 60% at liquidation), and the environmental
permit application had not been submitted.

The project remains in a state of suspended animation: the physical site and the local
partnership (Callio Pyhäjärvi) remain available, the ABB MOU provides a basis for
equipment supply discussions, and the engineering work completed to date reduces the
development timeline for any new project sponsor. The estimated time to commercial
operation from a restart decision is approximately 18–24 months, compared to 24–30
months for a greenfield project starting from scratch.
