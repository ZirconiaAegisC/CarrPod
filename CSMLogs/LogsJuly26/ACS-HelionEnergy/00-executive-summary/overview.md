# Helion Energy: Executive Overview

## Corporate Identity
- **Legal Name:** Helion Energy, Inc.
- **Headquarters:** Everett, Washington, United States
- **Founded:** 2013
- **CEO & Co-Founder:** David Kirtley
- **Employees:** ~375 (as of mid-2025)
- **Website:** helionenergy.com

## Mission & Core Thesis
Helion Energy is building a commercial fusion power platform based on the Field-Reversed Configuration (FRC) approach. Unlike mainstream tokamak or laser-inertial-confinement fusion, Helion pursues a pulsed, magneto-inertial scheme using deuterium-helium-3 (D-He3) fuel. The key technical differentiator is direct electricity recovery: the expanding plasma pushes back against the magnetic field, inducing current in the compression coils, bypassing the steam turbine cycle entirely. This architecture eliminates the need for large cooling infrastructure, heat exchangers, and steam generators, enabling a compact, transportable power plant form factor.

## Fusion Approach
- **Confinement Method:** Field-Reversed Configuration (FRC)
- **Heating Method:** Magneto-Inertial Fusion (MIF) via electromagnetic compression
- **Fuel Cycle:** D-He3 (Deuterium + Helium-3) with D-D side reactions
- **Energy Conversion:** Direct inductive energy recovery into compression coils (>95% theoretical recovery efficiency)
- **Pulse Rate:** 1 Hz (one pulse per second at commercial scale)
- **Target Ion Temperature:** 200 million °C (commercial); 150 million °C demonstrated (Polaris, Feb 2026)
- **Commercial Target Gain:** Q_eng >> 1 (engineering gain accounting for energy recovery)

## Key Milestones
- **2013:** Company founded by David Kirtley, John Slough, Chris Pihl, and George Votroubek.
- **2014-2019:** Five prototype generations (IPA, IPA-C, Venti, Grand, Trenta) built and tested.
- **2021:** Trenta achieves ion temperatures of 100 million °C (9 keV).
- **2023:** Microsoft signs first-ever fusion power purchase agreement with Helion.
- **July 2025:** Orion site groundbreaking in Malaga, Washington.
- **February 2026:** Polaris achieves 150 million °C, becoming the first private fusion device to demonstrate D-T fusion conditions.
- **June 2026:** Helion becomes first fusion company to receive Washington State Department of Health Radioactive Materials License (RML) and Radioactive Air Emissions License (RAEL).
- **June 2026:** Series G funding round of $465 million led by Thrive Capital, raising total to $1.5 billion at $15.5 billion valuation.

## Competitive Landscape Position
Helion is distinguished by its capital efficiency relative to other fusion companies. The FRC approach's smaller physical footprint allows faster iteration cycles; Helion has built and tested seven full-scale prototype generations, each incorporating learnings from its predecessor. The direct electricity recovery architecture, if proven at commercial scale, would give Helion a significant cost advantage over steam-cycle fusion designs (e.g., Commonwealth Fusion Systems SPARC/ARC, General Fusion MTF).

## Intellectual Property
Helion's IP portfolio covers FRC formation, electromagnetic compression, direct energy recovery, capacitor design, and fusion fuel handling. Key patent families:
- Pulsed FRC formation and acceleration methods
- Magneto-inertial compression coil geometries
- Inductive energy recovery circuit topologies
- Tritium management and He-3 breeding systems
- Pulsed capacitor manufacturing techniques

The company selectively patents some technologies while maintaining others as trade secrets, particularly around capacitor manufacturing processes and Formation Section design tolerances.

## Enterprise Status
As of mid-2026, Helion is in the transition phase from research-and-demonstration to commercial construction. Polaris serves as the technology proving ground for Orion components. The company maintains strict vertical integration of critical subsystems: capacitor banks, Formation Section modules, divertor assemblies, and compression coils are all designed and manufactured in-house. Helion has not filed for an IPO; it remains privately capitalized with long-duration venture backing.

## Key Risks
1. **He-3 Fuel Supply:** D-He3 fusion requires He-3, which is rare on Earth. Helion's D-D side-reaction pathway generates He-3 in-situ, but net breeding ratios remain to be publicly demonstrated.
2. **Tritium Management:** D-D reactions produce tritium as a byproduct, requiring handling, inventory tracking, and regulatory compliance.
3. **Pulse Repetition:** Sustaining 1 Hz operation at commercial gain levels with capacitor bank recharge and thermal cycling has not been publicly demonstrated.
4. **First-of-Kind Execution:** Orion is the world's first commercial fusion plant; construction, commissioning, and licensing risks are significant.
5. **Microsoft PPA Penalties:** The power purchase agreement includes financial penalties if 50 MW delivery is not achieved by the 2028 target.
