# Orion Site Development: Malaga, Washington

## Site Selection Rationale
Helion selected a ~30-acre parcel in Malaga, Washington (Chelan County) for the Orion commercial fusion plant after evaluating multiple candidate sites across the Pacific Northwest. Malaga is approximately 7 miles southeast of Wenatchee and 140 miles east of Seattle.

### Key Site Selection Factors
1. **Grid Access:** Direct interconnection with Chelan County Public Utility District (PUD), which operates a robust hydroelectric-based grid with existing substation infrastructure.
2. **Proximity to HQ:** ~150 miles from Helion's Everett headquarters, enabling close engineering oversight during construction and commissioning.
3. **Industrial Zoning:** Site is zoned for industrial use with compatible neighboring land uses.
4. **Geotechnical Stability:** Basalt bedrock foundation suitable for heavy industrial equipment mounting.
5. **Water Access:** Columbia River proximity for cooling water if needed (though minimal cooling is required).
6. **Workforce Availability:** Chelan and Douglas counties have an existing skilled industrial workforce from the hydroelectric and agricultural processing industries.
7. **Regulatory Environment:** Washington State's proactive fusion regulatory framework (HB 1924, HB 1018) provides a clear, streamlined permitting pathway.
8. **Land Economics:** Agricultural/industrial land costs in Malaga are significantly lower than in the Puget Sound region.

## Site Infrastructure

### Grid Interconnection
Chelan County PUD is the interconnecting utility:
- **Substation:** Existing Malaga substation or new dedicated interconnection point
- **Voltage:** 115 kV or 230 kV transmission (exact voltage not publicly confirmed)
- **Interconnection Agreement:** Executed between Helion and Chelan County PUD
- **Grid Study:** Interconnection system impact study completed or in progress
- **Power Delivery:** Orion's 50 MW output represents ~5-10% of Chelan County PUD's peak load

### Transportation & Logistics
- **Heavy Haul Route:** State Route 28 and county roads rated for heavy equipment transport
- **Rail Access:** BNSF Railway mainline passes within 5 miles of the site
- **Port Access:** Components shipped via Port of Seattle or Port of Everett, then trucked to site
- **On-Site Roads:** Designed for 50-ton crane and heavy transporter access

### Utilities
- **Electricity (Construction):** Temporary construction power from Chelan County PUD
- **Electricity (Operation):** Orion is a net power producer; parasitic load estimated at 5-10 MW
- **Water:** Municipal water connection from Malaga water district; minimal process water requirement
- **Sanitary Sewer:** Septic system or municipal connection
- **Natural Gas:** Not required for plant operation (no boiler, no steam)
- **Telecom:** Fiber or fixed wireless for SCADA and remote monitoring

### Environmental Considerations
- **SEPA Review:** Completed with DNS (Determination of Non-Significance)
- **NEPA Review:** Environmental Assessment (EA) in progress under DOE or NRC oversight
- **Air Emissions:** Radioactive Air Emissions License (RAEL) granted by WA DOH (June 2026)
- **Water Discharge:** No thermal discharge (no steam condenser); minimal industrial wastewater
- **Noise:** Capacitor bank discharge produces audible noise; sound mitigation measures planned
- **Visual Impact:** Low-profile industrial facility; no cooling towers or tall stacks required
- **Ecological Survey:** Standard pre-construction biological and cultural resource surveys conducted

### Local Economic Impact
- **Construction Jobs:** ~200-300 construction jobs during peak
- **Permanent Jobs:** ~50-75 operations and maintenance positions
- **Property Tax Revenue:** Significant increase to Chelan County tax base
- **Indirect Economic Activity:** Local services, housing, and supply chain development in the Wenatchee Valley

## Construction Partners
Helion has not publicly disclosed its general contractor or major subcontractors for the Orion facility. Given the specialized nature of fusion construction, the project likely involves:
- A national-scale industrial general contractor (e.g., Bechtel, Fluor, Kiewit) or a regional heavy industrial builder
- Specialized subcontractors for vacuum systems, cryogenics, and high-voltage electrical systems
- Direct Helion engineering oversight for fusion-specific subsystems (compression coils, capacitor banks, formation sections)

## Schedule Risks
- **Permitting Delays:** NEPA EA process timelines are uncertain; litigation risk from environmental groups is non-zero but low given fusion's minimal environmental footprint.
- **Supply Chain:** Long-lead items (specialty capacitors, magnetic coil conductors, power electronics) have extended manufacturing timelines.
- **First-of-a-Kind Construction:** No established construction standards for fusion plants; learning curve costs and delays expected.
- **Workforce Availability:** Skilled trades (electricians, pipefitters, instrumentation techs) are in high demand across the Pacific Northwest.
- **Weather:** Central Washington winters are relatively mild, but seasonal construction constraints exist (November-February).
