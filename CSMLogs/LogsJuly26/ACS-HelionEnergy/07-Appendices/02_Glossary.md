# Glossary of Terms

**Date:** 15 July 2026
**Classification:** Corporate Dossier — Section 07.2

---

## A

**ADVANCE Act (2024):** Accelerating Deployment of Versatile, Advanced Nuclear for Clean Energy Act. U.S. federal legislation establishing a distinct regulatory pathway for fusion energy, separate from nuclear fission.

**Agreement State:** A U.S. state that has entered into an agreement with the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) to assume regulatory authority over certain categories of radioactive materials, including byproduct materials. Washington is an Agreement State.

**Aneutronic Fusion:** Fusion reactions that produce primarily charged particles (protons, alpha particles) rather than neutrons. D-He3 and p-B11 are aneutronic fuels. Aneutronic reactions enable direct electromagnetic energy conversion and minimize neutron activation of reactor components.

**ARPA-E:** Advanced Research Projects Agency–Energy. A U.S. Department of Energy agency that funds high-risk, high-reward energy technology research. Allocated $135 million to fusion in April 2026.

---

## B

**Balance of Plant (BOP):** All supporting systems and infrastructure of a power plant outside the primary energy generation system — including grid interconnection, cooling, control systems, and auxiliary power.

**Beta (Plasma Beta):** The ratio of plasma pressure to magnetic field pressure. FRC plasmas operate at high beta (approaching unity), enabling compact confinement geometries.

---

## C

**Capacitor Bank:** An array of high-energy-density capacitors used to store and rapidly discharge electrical energy. Helion's Polaris prototype uses a 50 MJ capacitor bank to drive magnetic compression pulses.

**Chelan County PUD:** Chelan County Public Utility District. The public utility providing electricity transmission and distribution infrastructure in Chelan County, Washington. Party to Helion's transmission interconnection agreement.

**Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS):** A Massachusetts-based fusion company developing a high-temperature superconducting (HTS) tokamak (SPARC). Helion's primary competitor by valuation (~$10B). Capital raised: $2B+.

---

## D

**D-D (Deuterium-Deuterium):** A fusion reaction between two deuterium nuclei. Produces neutrons (at 2.45 MeV) as a side reaction in D-He3 plasmas.

**D-He3 (Deuterium-Helium-3):** Helion's target commercial fusion fuel. Reaction: D + He3 → p (14.7 MeV) + He4 (3.6 MeV). Aneutronic — produces charged particles, not neutrons.

**D-T (Deuterium-Tritium):** The most readily ignited fusion fuel. Reaction: D + T → n (14.1 MeV) + He4 (3.5 MeV). Produces high-energy neutrons; used by Helion for testing and validation.

**Direct Electricity Recovery:** Conversion of fusion energy directly to electrical current via electromagnetic induction from expanding plasma against magnetic fields. Eliminates the steam turbine cycle.

**DOE:** U.S. Department of Energy.

**DOH:** Washington State Department of Health. Regulatory authority for Helion's RML and RAEL licenses.

---

## F

**Field-Reversed Configuration (FRC):** A compact toroidal plasma configuration in which the internal magnetic field reverses direction, forming a self-organized, high-beta plasma structure. The foundation of Helion's fusion architecture.

**First Wall:** The innermost surface of a fusion reactor vacuum vessel, directly exposed to plasma and radiation.

**Fusion Power Purchase Agreement (PPA):** A long-term contract for the sale of electricity generated by a fusion power plant. Helion holds the world's first fusion PPA (Microsoft, 2023).

---

## G

**General Fusion:** A Canadian-British fusion company developing magnetized target fusion (MTF) with mechanical piston compression. Demonstration plant under construction at UKAEA Culham.

---

## H

**HB 1924 (2023):** Washington State House Bill establishing the statutory foundation for fusion energy regulation. Authorized Washington DOH to regulate fusion under existing radioactive materials authority.

**HB 1018 (2025):** Washington State House Bill refining fusion licensing standards and establishing DOH review timelines.

**HTS (High-Temperature Superconductor):** Superconducting materials operating at relatively high temperatures (e.g., 20 K), enabling stronger magnetic fields. Used by CFS, not by Helion.

---

## I

**ICF (Inertial Confinement Fusion):** Fusion approach in which fuel capsules are compressed by lasers or other drivers to achieve fusion conditions. Example: National Ignition Facility (NIF).

**ITER:** International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor — the world's largest tokamak, under construction in Cadarache, France. Estimated cost: $22B+. Not a commercial project.

---

## L

**LCOE (Levelized Cost of Electricity):** The per-unit cost of electricity (typically $/MWh) accounting for capital, operating, and fuel costs over a plant's lifetime, discounted to present value.

---

## M

**MTF (Magnetized Target Fusion):** A hybrid approach combining magnetic plasma confinement with mechanical or electromagnetic compression. Used by General Fusion.

**Mithril Capital:** Venture capital firm; early and multi-round investor in Helion.

---

## N

**NIF (National Ignition Facility):** Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory laser ICF facility. Achieved ignition (Q > 1) in December 2022. Not designed for commercial power generation.

**NRC (Nuclear Regulatory Commission):** U.S. federal agency responsible for regulating civilian use of nuclear materials. Under the ADVANCE Act, developing a fusion-specific regulatory framework.

**Nucor Corporation:** Major U.S. steel manufacturer. Signed a 500 MW fusion development agreement with Helion.

---

## O

**Orion:** Helion's first commercial fusion power plant, 50 MW capacity, under construction in Malaga, Washington (Chelan County). Broke ground July 2025. Target delivery: 2028.

---

## P

**p-B11 (Proton-Boron-11):** An aneutronic fusion fuel targeted by TAE Technologies. Requires approximately 3 billion °C — significantly higher than D-He3.

**Plasma:** The fourth state of matter; an ionized gas in which electrons are separated from atomic nuclei. Fusion reactions occur in plasma at extreme temperatures (100M–200M°C for magnetic fusion).

**Polaris:** Helion's 7th-generation prototype. First privately funded device to demonstrate D-T fusion (2026). 60 ft length, 150M°C target temperature, 50 MJ capacitor banks, 30,000 sq ft facility.

**PPA (Power Purchase Agreement):** See Fusion Power Purchase Agreement.

**(Q) Engineering Q:** The ratio of net electrical power output to total electrical power input for a fusion plant. Must substantially exceed unity for commercial viability.

---

## R

**RAEL (Radiation Air Emissions License):** Washington DOH license authorizing controlled airborne emissions of radioactive materials. Issued to Helion in June 2026 — a precedential first for fusion.

**RML (Radioactive Materials License):** Washington DOH license authorizing possession and handling of radioactive materials. Issued to Helion in June 2026 — a precedential first for fusion.

---

## S

**SoftBank Group:** Japanese multinational investment holding company; participated in Helion Series E and subsequent rounds.

**SPARC:** Commonwealth Fusion Systems' net-energy demonstration tokamak, under construction in Devens, Massachusetts.

**Steam Turbine Cycle:** The conventional method of converting thermal energy to electricity — water is heated to steam, which drives a turbine connected to a generator. Helion's direct electricity recovery eliminates this cycle.

**Stellarator:** A magnetic confinement fusion device using complex three-dimensional magnetic field coils to confine plasma. Example: Wendelstein 7-X (Germany).

---

## T

**TAE Technologies:** California-based fusion company using beam-driven FRC technology. Target fuel: p-B11. Norman (5th generation) and Copernicus (6th generation) prototypes.

**Thrive Capital:** Venture capital firm; lead investor in Helion Series G ($465 million, June 2026).

**Tiny Merge:** Helion's 1/8-scale Polaris derivative, designed for rapid, low-cost FRC parameter optimization. Construction began spring 2026.

**Tokamak:** A toroidal magnetic confinement device using a combination of toroidal and poloidal magnetic fields. The most extensively researched fusion configuration. Examples: ITER, JET, SPARC.

**Trenta:** Helion's 6th-generation prototype. Achieved 100 million °C plasma temperature.

**Tritium:** A radioactive isotope of hydrogen (half-life 12.3 years). Used with deuterium for D-T fusion reactions. Tritium decays to He3, providing a potential He3 production pathway.

---

## Z

**Zap Energy:** A fusion company (Everett, Washington) developing sheared-flow-stabilized Z-pinch technology. Co-located with Helion in Everett.

**Z-Pinch:** A plasma confinement approach in which an electric current passing through the plasma generates a self-constricting magnetic field. Zap Energy uses sheared-flow stabilization to suppress Z-pinch instabilities.
