## Key Investor Profiles

### Sam Altman (Largest Individual Investor)
- **Role:** Co-founder and former CEO of OpenAI; former President of Y Combinator
- **Helion Relationship:** Investor since 2014; board member until March 2026
- **Total Invested:** >$375 million
- **Investment Philosophy:** Views fusion as the most important technology for enabling abundant AI compute. Has stated that the marginal cost of energy must approach zero for AI to achieve its full potential.
- **Conflicts of Interest:** Dual role at OpenAI (exploring fusion partnerships) and Helion (~33% owner) raised governance concerns. Resigned from Helion board in March 2026, partially addressing these.
- **Risk Tolerance:** Extremely high. Altman has invested a substantial portion of his personal wealth in a pre-revenue, high-risk technology company.

### SoftBank Vision Fund 2
- **Fund Size:** ~$56 billion (Vision Fund 2)
- **Helion Investment:** Led Series F ($425M, 2024); participated in Series G ($465M, 2026)
- **Energy Investments:** Typically focused on AI-adjacent technologies; Helion is SoftBank's largest fusion investment
- **Investment Thesis:** Fusion power for AI data centers aligns with SoftBank's broader thesis of AI-driven infrastructure demand
- **Exit Horizon:** 5-10 years; SoftBank is a long-duration investor

### Thrive Capital
- **Founded:** 2009 by Josh Kushner
- **Notable Investments:** OpenAI, Stripe, Instagram, GitHub, Spotify, Slack
- **Helion Investment:** Led Series G ($465M, June 2026) — Thrive's first major energy investment
- **Investment Thesis:** Thrive's proximity to OpenAI (major investor in OpenAI's $6.6B round, October 2024) suggests a thesis linking Helion's fusion power to AI data center energy demand
- **Significance:** A crossover technology investor leading a fusion round signals fusion's transition from "energy tech" to "infrastructure tech" in the venture capital taxonomy

### Lightspeed Venture Partners
- **Helion Relationship:** Multi-round participant (Series E, F, G)
- **Energy Investments:** Active in climate tech and advanced energy
- **Notable Partners:** The Lightspeed team includes partners with deep technology and science backgrounds

### Bill Ford
- **Role:** Executive Chairman, Ford Motor Company
- **Helion Relationship:** Personal investment (not a Ford corporate investment)
- **Rationale:** Industrial applications of fusion; potential future integration with electric vehicle manufacturing and charging infrastructure

### Dustin Moskovitz (via Good Ventures / Open Philanthropy)
- **Role:** Facebook co-founder; philanthropist focused on global catastrophic risk reduction
- **Helion Relationship:** Multi-round participant
- **Rationale:** Fusion as a solution to climate change (catastrophic risk); aligns with Open Philanthropy's mission

## Venture Debt and Non-Dilutive Funding
Helion has not publicly disclosed any venture debt facilities. The company's funding has been entirely equity-based. However, Helion is eligible for several forms of non-dilutive funding:
- **DOE Milestone-Based Fusion Program:** Up to $50 million over 5 years
- **ARPA-E Fusion Supply Chain Program:** Competitive grants ($135M total program)
- **DOE Loan Programs Office:** Potential loan guarantees for Orion or follow-on plants
- **Washington State Incentives:** Potential state tax incentives for clean energy manufacturing and deployment

## Exit Scenarios and Return Analysis

### Scenario 1: Successful IPO (2029-2031)
- **Trigger:** Orion achieves commercial operation in 2028-2029; first PPAs are delivering power
- **Valuation at IPO:** $30-50 billion (based on pipeline of follow-on PPAs and manufacturing scalability)
- **Investor Returns:** Series G investors (entered at $15.5B) would see 2-3x; Series E investors (entered at ~$3-4B) would see 10-15x
- **Probability:** 20-30% (estimated)

### Scenario 2: Strategic Acquisition (2028-2032)
- **Trigger:** A major energy or technology company acquires Helion for its technology and PPA pipeline
- **Acquisition Price:** $25-40 billion (premium to last private round)
- **Likely Acquirers:** Microsoft, a major utility (NextEra, Duke), an oil major (Shell, BP, Chevron), or a technology company (Alphabet, Amazon)
- **Probability:** 15-25% (estimated)

### Scenario 3: Project Finance Model (2030+)
- **Trigger:** Orion is operating commercially; subsequent plants are financed through project debt
- **Valuation Growth:** Slower; Helion remains private with periodic secondary sales
- **Investor Returns:** Long-duration returns through dividends or secondary sales; lower IRR but lower risk
- **Probability:** 25-35% (estimated)

### Scenario 4: Technology Failure or Significant Delay
- **Trigger:** Polaris or Orion encounters a fundamental physics or engineering barrier; 2028 PPA deadline is missed by 5+ years
- **Outcome:** Down round or recapitalization; significant dilution for existing shareholders
- **Probability:** 15-25% (estimated)

## Governance Considerations
- **Board Composition:** Not publicly disclosed. Prior to March 2026, Sam Altman held a board seat. Current board likely includes David Kirtley (CEO), representatives from major investors (SoftBank, Thrive Capital), and independent directors.
- **Founder Control:** David Kirtley remains CEO and has guided the company since founding in 2013. Founder equity combined with Altman's stake may give insiders effective control of board decisions.
- **Share Class Structure:** Not publicly disclosed; may include multiple share classes with differential voting rights (common in venture-backed technology companies).
- **IPO Readiness:** With $1.5B raised and ~375 employees, Helion has the scale for a public offering. Governance structures (audit committee, compensation committee, independent board majority) would need to be formalized pre-IPO.

## Investment Risks (From Limited Partner Perspective)
1. **Concentration Risk:** Sam Altman's ~33% stake means a single individual's financial circumstances or personal decisions could impact the company.
2. **Valuation Risk:** $15.5 billion for a pre-revenue company with unproven technology at commercial scale is aggressive by any metric.
3. **Liquidity Risk:** Venture capital investments in fusion have no clear exit precedent; the timeline to liquidity is uncertain.
4. **Regulatory Risk:** Federal fusion regulation is still in development and could impose costs or delays.
5. **Competition Risk:** Multiple well-funded fusion companies (CFS, TAE, Zap, General Fusion, Tokamak Energy) are pursuing different technical approaches; the winning approach(es) are not yet determined.
