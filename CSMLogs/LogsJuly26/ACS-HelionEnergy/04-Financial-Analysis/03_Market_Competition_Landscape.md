## Fusion Sector Overview

The global private fusion industry has attracted $2.64 billion in cumulative capital as of 2025, with the United States hosting the largest concentration of funded fusion companies. The sector spans multiple technical approaches — magnetic confinement (tokamak, stellarator, FRC, Z-pinch), inertial confinement (laser, projectile), and hybrid magneto-inertial designs. Helion Energy is the highest-valued private fusion company globally ($15.5 billion) and the only one with a binding commercial PPA and a commercial plant under construction.

## Competitive Landscape: Head-to-Head

### Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS)

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Headquarters | Devens, Massachusetts |
| Technology | Tokamak with high-temperature superconducting (HTS) magnets |
| Key Prototype | SPARC (under construction) |
| Follow-On Plant | ARC (design phase) |
| Capital Raised | $2B+ |
| Estimated Valuation | ~$10B |
| Key Investors | Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Eni, Temasek, Bill Gates |
| Commercial Traction | No PPA; targeting net-energy demonstration then commercial deployment |
| Regulatory Status | Pre-licensure |

**CFS vs. Helion:**
- **Technology risk:** CFS leverages the tokamak — the most-studied and best-characterized fusion configuration — reducing physics uncertainty at the cost of plant complexity (HTS magnets, tritium breeding blanket, steam turbine).
- **Plant economics:** Helion's direct electricity recovery eliminates ~30% of conventional plant capex (steam cycle), potentially giving a structural cost advantage.
- **Commercial readiness:** Helion has a signed PPA and a plant under construction; CFS is building a net-energy demonstration machine (SPARC) with commercial plant design (ARC) at a conceptual stage.
- **Fuel cycle:** Helion targets D-He3 (aneutronic); CFS targets D-T (neutronic, requires tritium breeding). D-T is technically validated but generates activation and waste; D-He3 avoids these but depends on an unproven He3 supply chain.

### TAE Technologies

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Headquarters | Foothill Ranch, California |
| Technology | Beam-driven FRC (field-reversed configuration sustained by neutral beam injection) |
| Key Prototype | Norman (5th generation); Copernicus (6th generation, under construction) |
| Capital Raised | $1.2B+ |
| Estimated Valuation | $3B–$5B |
| Key Investors | Google, Venrock, NEA, Vulcan Capital |
| Commercial Traction | No PPA; medical isotope spin-off (TAE Life Sciences) |
| Fuel | Hydrogen-boron (p-B11) — aneutronic, but requires ~3 billion °C |

**TAE vs. Helion:**
- **Both use FRC geometry**, but TAE sustains FRCs with neutral beam injection (steady-state approach) while Helion uses pulsed magnetic compression.
- **TAE's fuel (p-B11) is even more aneutronic** than D-He3 but requires temperatures two orders of magnitude higher — a fundamentally harder physics problem.
- **TAE's Norman prototype** has demonstrated FRC stability at relevant conditions but not fusion-relevant temperatures.
- **TAE's isotope spin-off** provides an interim revenue stream that Helion lacks.

### General Fusion

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Headquarters | Vancouver, British Columbia / Oxfordshire, UK |
| Technology | Magnetized target fusion (MTF) — mechanical compression of plasma via steam-driven pistons |
| Key Prototype | Lawson Machine 26 (LM26, Burnaby, BC); Demonstration plant (UKAEA Culham, UK, under construction) |
| Capital Raised | $400M+ |
| Key Investors | Jeff Bezos, Shopify CEO, Temasek, BDC |
| Commercial Traction | UKAEA partnership; no PPA |

**General Fusion vs. Helion:**
- **Mechanical compression via pistons** is a fundamentally different approach from magnetic compression; the piston mechanism introduces moving mechanical parts in a fusion environment.
- **UK demonstration plant** leverages UK regulatory framework (fusion not regulated as nuclear), providing a parallel regulatory pathway.
- **Lower valuation and capital raised** reflects perceived technology risk and earlier development stage.

### Zap Energy

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Headquarters | Everett, Washington (same city as Helion) |
| Technology | Sheared-flow-stabilized Z-pinch |
| Key Prototype | FuZE-Q (under construction) |
| Capital Raised | $200M+ |
| Key Investors | Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Chevron Technology Ventures |
| Commercial Traction | No PPA |

**Zap vs. Helion:**
- **Z-pinch approach** is even more compact than FRC and does not require external magnetic coils for confinement — the plasma's self-generated magnetic field provides confinement.
- **Both headquartered in Everett, WA** — competing for the same local talent pool.
- **Earlier stage:** Zap has not yet demonstrated fusion-relevant temperatures or neutron yields.

## Market Size & Addressable Demand

### Global Electricity Market
- Global electricity generation: ~30,000 TWh annually (2025)
- Projected 2050 demand: 45,000–60,000 TWh (electrification of transport, industry, heating)
- Firm clean power capacity gap (2050): Estimated 5,000–15,000 GW of dispatchable carbon-free generation needed

### Fusion Addressable Market Segments

| Segment | Addressable Capacity (GW) | Key Drivers |
|---------|--------------------------|-------------|
| Data center / technology sector | 50–200 | 24/7 carbon-free power commitments (Microsoft, Google, Amazon) |
| Industrial (steel, chemicals, cement) | 200–1,000 | Process heat + electricity; decarbonization mandates |
| Utility-scale baseload replacement | 2,000–5,000 | Coal and natural gas plant retirements |
| Hydrogen / synthetic fuel production | 500–2,000 | Electrolysis and thermochemical processes |
| Distributed / remote power | 50–200 | Island grids, mining operations, military bases |

### Helion's Near-Term Addressable Market

The technology sector — specifically hyperscale data center operators with 24/7 carbon-free energy commitments — represents Helion's most immediate and accessible market:

| Operator | 24/7 Carbon-Free Commitment | Potential Fusion Offtake |
|----------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| Microsoft | Carbon-negative by 2030 | 50 MW (signed PPA) |
| Google | 24/7 CFE by 2030 | Potential PPA candidate |
| Amazon | Net-zero by 2040 | Potential PPA candidate |
| Meta | Net-zero by 2030 | Potential PPA candidate |
| Apple | Carbon-neutral by 2030 | Potential PPA candidate |

The Nucor 500 MW agreement extends the addressable market into industrial decarbonization — a sector where electrification alone cannot fully displace fossil fuel process heat requirements.

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Accelerate Orion to 2028 delivery:** The Microsoft PPA deadline is the single most consequential corporate milestone. Schedule compression investments are warranted even at premium cost.
2. **Secure He3 supply chain visibility:** The D-He3 fuel cycle is central to the long-term commercial thesis. A He3 sourcing strategy — even a preliminary one — should be developed and disclosed to investors and partners.
3. **Lock in second and third PPAs:** With technology sector demand for 24/7 carbon-free power accelerating, Helion should convert expressions of interest into binding offtake agreements before competitors enter the market.
4. **Expand manufacturing capacity:** Capacitor and ceramics production capacity will be the binding constraint on plant deployment rate. Investment in production scale-up should precede, not follow, commercial order flow.
5. **Maintain regulatory leadership:** Active participation in NRC rulemaking under the ADVANCE Act will ensure that the federal framework reflects Helion's precedential Washington State experience.
