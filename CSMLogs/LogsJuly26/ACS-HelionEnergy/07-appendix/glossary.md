# Helion Energy: Glossary of Terms & Acronyms

## Fusion Physics & Technology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Field-Reversed Configuration (FRC)** | A compact toroidal plasma configuration where magnetic field lines close entirely within the plasma, with no toroidal field coils linking the plasma. Characterized by extremely high beta (β > 0.8). |
| **Magneto-Inertial Fusion (MIF)** | A fusion approach that combines magnetic confinement (to insulate the plasma) with inertial compression (to heat it to fusion conditions). Pulsed electromagnetic compression is the MIF method Helion uses. |
| **Deuterium (D)** | A stable isotope of hydrogen with one proton and one neutron. Abundant in seawater (~1 in 6,420 hydrogen atoms). Primary fusion fuel for all mainstream fusion concepts. |
| **Helium-3 (He-3)** | A light, non-radioactive isotope of helium with two protons and one neutron. Extremely rare on Earth (~0.0001% of helium). Used in D-He3 fusion, which produces primarily charged particles (protons and He-4) rather than neutrons. |
| **Deuterium-Tritium (D-T)** | The fusion reaction with the lowest ignition temperature (~100M°C). Produces a 14.1 MeV neutron and a 3.5 MeV alpha particle. The primary fuel for tokamaks (ITER, SPARC) and laser ICF (NIF). |
| **Deuterium-Helium-3 (D-He3)** | An aneutronic fusion reaction producing a proton (14.7 MeV) and He-4 (3.6 MeV). Requires higher temperatures (~200M°C) but produces far fewer neutrons than D-T. Helion's primary fuel cycle. |
| **Deuterium-Deuterium (D-D)** | A fusion reaction between two deuterium nuclei. Two branches: D-D → He3 + n (50%) and D-D → T + p (50%). Occurs as a side reaction in Helion's D-He3 fuel cycle. Produces 2.45 MeV neutrons and tritium as products. |
| **Tritium (T)** | A radioactive isotope of hydrogen with one proton and two neutrons. Half-life: 12.3 years. Beta emitter (mean energy: 5.7 keV). Produced by D-D fusion and neutron capture in lithium. Used in D-T fusion and can decay into He-3. |
| **Plasma Beta (β)** | The ratio of plasma pressure to magnetic pressure (β = p_plasma / (B²/2μ₀)). FRCs achieve β > 0.8; tokamaks operate at β ~ 0.05. Higher beta means more efficient use of magnetic field energy. |
| **Q (Fusion Gain)** | The ratio of fusion power output to heating power input. Q = 1 is "breakeven." Helion targets Q_eng (engineering gain, including energy recovery) >> 1. Q_sci (scientific gain) ignores recirculating power. |
| **Ion Temperature** | The kinetic temperature of ions in a plasma, typically measured in electron-volts (eV) or degrees Celsius. 1 keV ≈ 11.6 million °C. Polaris: 150M°C (~13 keV). Target commercial: 200M°C (~17 keV). |
| **Confinement Time (τ_E)** | The characteristic time for energy to be lost from the plasma. Must satisfy the Lawson criterion (n·τ_E·T > threshold) for net energy gain. In FRCs, confinement is on the order of milliseconds. |
| **Formation Section (FS)** | In Helion's architecture, the pair of quartz tubes where FRC plasmoids are formed via theta-pinch discharge and then accelerated axially toward the central compression section. |
| **Divertor** | The region of a magnetic confinement device where open field lines channel exhaust (fusion ash, impurities) out of the plasma. In FRCs, the divertor is at the ends of the linear device. |
| **Direct Energy Recovery** | Helion's method of converting fusion energy to electricity without a steam cycle. The expanding plasma pushes against the compression magnetic field, inducing current in the coils via Faraday's law. |
| **Capacitor Bank** | The energy storage system that powers the pulsed compression. Polaris: 50 MJ bank. Orion: 100+ MJ bank. Composed of thousands of custom-manufactured high-energy-density capacitors. |
| **Theta Pinch** | A plasma formation technique where a rapidly rising azimuthal magnetic field (produced by a coil wound around a cylindrical tube) ionizes and compresses gas into a plasma column. Used in Helion's Formation Sections. |
| **Neutron Activation** | The process by which structural materials become radioactive due to neutron bombardment. Less severe in D-He3 fusion (2.45 MeV neutrons) than D-T fusion (14.1 MeV neutrons). |
| **Plasma-Facing Component (PFC)** | Materials and structures that directly contact or are exposed to the fusion plasma. Must withstand high heat fluxes, particle bombardment, and erosion. Examples: tungsten tiles, beryllium coatings, liquid metal surfaces. |

## Helion-Specific Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Polaris** | Helion's 7th-generation prototype. 60-foot device. Achieved 150M°C in February 2026 — first private D-T fusion demonstration. Currently testing subsystems for Orion. |
| **Orion** | Helion's first commercial fusion plant. 50 MW net electrical output. Under construction in Malaga, WA since July 2025. Target: commercial operation by 2028 (Microsoft PPA deadline). |
| **Tiny Merge** | A 1/8-scale version of Polaris used for risk-reduction testing. Enables faster iteration on Formation Section dynamics, merging physics, and component lifetime testing. |
| **Trenta** | Helion's 5th-generation prototype (2021). Achieved 100M°C ion temperatures, validating the FRC compression approach at scale. Now retired. |
| **FRC Merge** | The collision and merging of two counter-propagating FRC plasmoids in the central chamber. Converts translational kinetic energy to thermal energy; key heating mechanism before compression. |

## Regulatory & Licensing

| Acronym | Full Name | Description |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **RML** | Radioactive Materials License | WA DOH license authorizing possession, handling, and storage of radioactive materials (tritium, activated components). First fusion RML issued to Helion, June 2026. |
| **RAEL** | Radioactive Air Emissions License | WA DOH license authorizing trace radioactive air emissions. First fusion RAEL issued to Helion, June 2026. |
| **NRC** | Nuclear Regulatory Commission | U.S. federal agency responsible for regulating commercial nuclear materials and facilities. Developing fusion-specific regulatory framework per ADVANCE Act. |
| **DOE** | Department of Energy | U.S. federal department responsible for energy policy, nuclear safety, and fusion R&D. Operates national laboratories (PPPL, LANL, ORNL, LLNL). |
| **ARPA-E** | Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy | A DOE agency that funds high-risk, high-reward energy technology development. Active in fusion supply chain and technology programs. |
| **NEPA** | National Environmental Policy Act | U.S. law requiring environmental review of federal actions. Orion undergoing NEPA Environmental Assessment (EA). |
| **SEPA** | State Environmental Policy Act | Washington State environmental review law. Orion SEPA review completed with Determination of Non-Significance (DNS). |
| **ADVANCE Act** | Accelerating Deployment of Versatile, Advanced Nuclear for Clean Energy Act | 2024 federal law directing the NRC to develop a fusion-specific regulatory framework. |
| **ALARA** | As Low As Reasonably Achievable | Regulatory principle requiring radiation doses to be minimized considering economic and social factors. |
| **FES** | Fusion Energy Sciences | The DOE Office of Science program that funds fusion research and operates fusion facilities at national laboratories. |
| **LPO** | Loan Programs Office | DOE office with $40B+ in loan authority for innovative clean energy projects. Potential source of debt financing for commercial fusion plants. |

## Commercial & Financial

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **PPA** | Power Purchase Agreement — a long-term contract between an electricity generator (Helion) and a buyer (Microsoft) specifying the price, quantity, and delivery terms for electricity. |
| **Constellation Energy** | Largest competitive energy supplier in the U.S. (32 GW). Serves as power marketer for Helion's Microsoft PPA, managing scheduling, transmission, and regulatory compliance. |
| **Nucor (NYSE: NUE)** | Largest U.S. steel producer. Signed a 500 MW fusion development agreement with Helion in 2023. |
| **SoftBank Vision Fund 2** | $56B technology investment fund. Led Helion's Series F ($425M, 2024). |
| **Thrive Capital** | Venture capital firm founded by Josh Kushner. Led Helion's Series G ($465M, June 2026). Major investor in OpenAI. |
| **Series G** | Helion's most recent funding round: $465M at $15.5B valuation (June 2026). |
