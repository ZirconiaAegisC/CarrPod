# Helion Energy: Chronological Timeline

## 2013
- Helion Energy, Inc. founded by David Kirtley, John Slough, Chris Pihl, and George Votroubek
- Spinout from University of Washington plasma physics research
- Initial seed funding from founders and angel investors

## 2014
- Series A funding (~$3-5M) led by Mithril Capital
- Sam Altman makes first investment in Helion
- Capricorn Investment Group participates as early investor
- Construction of IPA (1st prototype) for FRC formation proof-of-concept

## 2015
- ARPA-E ALPHA Program funding award for FRC formation and compression
- IPA-C (2nd prototype) construction and testing — compression demonstration
- Sam Altman increases investment position

## 2016
- Series B funding round (~$10-15M)
- Venti (3rd prototype) construction — scaling validation

## 2017
- Venti operations and FRC formation reliability testing
- Grand (4th prototype) design and initial construction

## 2018
- Series C funding round (~$25-35M)
- Grand prototype operational — integrated system testing
- Expansion of Everett, WA manufacturing and testing facility

## 2019
- Trenta (5th prototype) design and construction begins
- ARPA-E BETHE Program funding for FRC stability and compression scaling
- Team growth to ~100 employees

## 2020
- Series D funding round (~$40-50M); Sam Altman leads with $50M+ personal investment
- Trenta construction completed
- Initial Trenta experimental campaigns

## 2021
- Series E funding round: $500 million (Sam Altman invests $375M of this round)
- Dustin Moskovitz (Good Ventures) participates
- Trenta achieves ion temperatures of 100 million °C (9 keV) — major milestone
- Polaris (6th/7th prototype) design begins
- Expansion of Everett facility for capacitor and coil manufacturing
- Team grows to ~200 employees

## 2022
- Polaris construction begins at Everett facility
- Trenta continues experimental campaigns (pulse repetition, diagnostics)
- Orion site search and preliminary siting analysis
- WA HB 1018 introduced in Washington Legislature

## 2023
- May: **Microsoft PPA signed** — world's first fusion power purchase agreement (50 MW by 2028)
- Nucor 500 MW development agreement announced
- WA HB 1018 passed — directs WA DOH to study fusion regulatory framework
- DOE Milestone-Based Fusion Development Program selects Helion as one of eight participants
- Polaris assembly continues
- Orion site selection: Malaga, WA (Chelan County)

## 2024
- WA HB 1924 enacted — establishes fusion-specific regulatory framework
- July 9: **ADVANCE Act signed into law** — directs NRC to develop fusion regulatory framework
- Series F funding round: $425 million led by SoftBank Vision Fund 2
- Lightspeed Venture Partners participates
- Post-money valuation estimated at $8-10 billion
- Polaris nearing completion
- Orion site acquisition finalized
- NEPA Environmental Assessment initiated

## 2025
- **July: Orion groundbreaking** — construction begins at Malaga, WA site
- Polaris initial commissioning and system integration tests
- SEPA Determination of Non-Significance issued for Orion
- WA DOH RML and RAEL applications submitted
- Public comment period for Orion licenses
- Team grows to ~375 employees
- Tiny Merge (1/8-scale Polaris) construction begins

## 2026
- **February: Polaris achieves 150 million °C** — first private fusion device to reach D-T fusion conditions
- February: First demonstration of D-T fusion in a privately built device
- **March: Sam Altman resigns from Helion Board of Directors**
- **April: ARPA-E announces $135M fusion supply chain program** — Helion expected to participate
- **June: WA DOH issues RML and RAEL** — Helion becomes first fusion company to receive state radioactive materials and air emissions licenses
- **June: Series G funding round: $465 million** led by Thrive Capital
- Post-money valuation: **$15.5 billion**
- Total raised to date: **$1.5 billion**
- Orion construction continues (steel erection, building envelope)
- Polaris experimental campaigns continue at 150M°C

## 2027 (Projected)
- Orion mechanical/electrical rough-in completion
- Fusion core installation at Orion
- Capacitor bank assembly at Orion
- Polaris continues component validation for Orion
- NEPA Environmental Assessment completion
- NRC proposed fusion rule published for comment
- Late 2027: Orion first plasma (target)

## 2028 (Projected)
- Orion commissioning phase
- Orion first commercial power delivery to Microsoft (target per PPA)
- NRC fusion rule finalized
- Potential follow-on plant announcement (Nucor or other)
- Potential IPO exploration (if Orion is operating commercially)

## 2029-2030 (Projected)
- Orion commercial operation established
- Second plant design and site selection
- Manufacturing facility scale-up
- IPO or strategic transaction considered

## 2030-2035 (Projected)
- Multi-plant fleet deployment
- Gigawatt-scale fusion capacity
- He-3 supply chain at industrial scale
- Fusion cost curve establishment (learning rate, economies of scale)

---

*Sources: Helion Energy public announcements, press releases, SEC filings, Washington State legislative records, U.S. DOE and NRC public documents, and credible news reporting. Dates marked "projected" are based on Helion's public statements and reasonable extrapolation; actual timelines may differ.*
