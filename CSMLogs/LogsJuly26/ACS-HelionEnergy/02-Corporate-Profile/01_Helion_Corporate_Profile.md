## Founding & Mission

Helion Energy was founded in 2013 by David Kirtley, a plasma physicist and aerospace engineer whose prior work included research on field-reversed configuration (FRC) plasma confinement at the University of Washington and at MSNW LLC, a fusion research spinoff. Kirtley's thesis was that magnetic compression of FRC plasmas, combined with direct electromagnetic induction for electricity recovery, could produce a commercially viable fusion power plant that bypasses the steam turbine cycle entirely.

The company's founding mission is to deliver commercially competitive fusion electricity using deuterium-helium-3 (D-He3) fuel, leveraging pulsed magnetic compression and direct energy conversion. Unlike mainstream approaches (tokamaks, stellarators, laser inertial confinement), Helion's architecture inherently produces electricity without intermediate thermal conversion.

## Intellectual Property & Competitive Moat

Helion's competitive position rests on four pillars:

1. **FRC plasma physics expertise:** Seven generations of increasingly capable prototypes (culminating in Polaris) represent approximately a decade of proprietary experimental data.
2. **Direct electricity recovery:** Patented pulsed magnetic compression and electromagnetic induction architecture eliminates the need for steam turbines, heat exchangers, and cooling towers.
3. **In-house advanced manufacturing:** Proprietary capacitor fabrication and ultra-high-pressure ceramic component production create barriers to replication.
4. **Regulatory first-mover position:** First fusion company to secure Washington DOH RML and RAEL licenses (June 2026); the ADVANCE Act and Washington state laws HB 1924/HB 1018 codify a fusion-specific regulatory pathway.

## Historical Track Record

Helion has a documented pattern of ambitious timeline projections and schedule adjustments, characteristic of first-of-kind energy technology development. Despite repeated deadline revisions, the company has consistently delivered record-breaking technical milestones:

- **Trenta (6th generation):** Achieved 100 million °C plasma temperatures.
- **Polaris (7th generation):** First private D-T fusion demonstrated; broke own temperature record in February 2026 (target 150 million °C).
- **Tiny Merge (1/8 scale Polaris):** Construction initiated spring 2026 for rapid iteration.
- **Orion groundbreaking:** July 2025 — transitioning from experimental to commercial construction.
- **Licensure:** June 2026 — first fusion RML and RAEL licenses issued by Washington DOH.

The 2028 Microsoft delivery deadline represents the most consequential schedule commitment in the company's history.
