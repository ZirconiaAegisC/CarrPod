# Strategic Partnerships & Corporate Relationships

## Nucor Corporation
In 2023, Helion Energy and Nucor Corporation (NYSE: NUE), the largest steel producer and recycler in the United States, announced a development agreement to explore the deployment of a 500 MW fusion power plant at a Nucor steel manufacturing facility. This agreement represents the largest single fusion power development deal ever announced.

### Key Terms
- **Capacity:** 500 MW (10x Orion's rated output)
- **Structure:** Joint development agreement (not a binding PPA)
- **Timeline:** Not publicly specified; likely 2030s
- **Significance:** Steel manufacturing requires firm, 24/7 high-temperature process heat and electricity; fusion is one of the few zero-carbon technologies capable of meeting this demand profile.

### Strategic Rationale
- **For Nucor:** Access to firm clean power for electric arc furnaces (EAFs), which currently consume ~500-600 kWh per ton of steel produced. A 500 MW fusion plant could power ~20-25% of a large EAF mill's electricity demand, reducing exposure to electricity price volatility and carbon pricing.
- **For Helion:** A pathway to gigawatt-scale deployments with a creditworthy industrial offtaker; steel industry decarbonization is a premium market for fusion in the 2030-2050 timeframe.

## OpenAI & Sam Altman Relationship
Sam Altman, co-founder and former CEO of OpenAI, is Helion's largest individual investor (~33% ownership stake, over $375 million invested). The Altman-Helion relationship is unusual in its depth and duration:
- Altman was an early-stage investor (2014-2015 timeframe)
- He has invested personally across multiple rounds, not through a fund
- He served on Helion's board of directors until early 2026
- He has publicly characterized Helion as one of the most important companies in the world

### OpenAI-Helion Exploration (2025-2026)
In 2025, OpenAI and Helion explored a potential partnership involving Helion supplying power for OpenAI's AI training compute infrastructure. OpenAI's compute requirements are projected to grow significantly, and the company has expressed interest in securing dedicated, firm, carbon-free power. However:
- In March 2026, Sam Altman resigned from Helion's board of directors
- The partnership exploration was not publicly formalized into a binding agreement
- OpenAI is concurrently exploring other energy partnerships (nuclear, geothermal, large-scale solar + storage)

### Governance Implications
Altman's dual role (Helion investor and OpenAI CEO at the time) raised potential conflict-of-interest questions:
- Could a Helion-OpenAI power deal be structured at above-market rates, enriching Altman as a Helion shareholder?
- Altman's resignation from the Helion board in March 2026 partially addressed these concerns, though his ~33% equity stake remains.

## ARPA-E & Department of Energy
Helion has received U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) and ARPA-E (Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy) support:

### ARPA-E Fusion Programs
- **ALPHA Program (2015-2018):** Helion received ARPA-E funding under the Accelerating Low-Cost Plasma Heating and Assembly (ALPHA) program, focused on advancing FRC formation and compression technologies.
- **BETHE Program (2019-2022):** Funding under the Breakthroughs Enabling Thermonuclear-Fusion Energy (BETHE) program for FRC stability and compression scaling.

### Milestone-Based Fusion Development Program (2023-Present)
In 2023, the DOE announced a milestone-based fusion development program authorized under the Energy Act of 2020. Helion was selected as one of eight fusion companies to receive funding and technical support. Key aspects:
- **Funding:** Up to $50 million per company over 5 years (subject to congressional appropriations)
- **Structure:** Milestone-based payments tied to demonstrated technical progress
- **Technical Support:** Access to DOE national laboratory capabilities (PPPL, ORNL, LANL, LLNL)
- **Regulatory Assistance:** DOE support for NRC licensing pathway development

### Fusion Supply Chain Initiative (April 2026)
In April 2026, ARPA-E announced $135 million in funding for fusion supply chain development. Helion is expected to participate in this program, targeting:
- Domestic capacitor manufacturing and supply chain
- Tritium-compatible materials supply chain
- Fusion-specific power electronics and switchgear
- Specialty magnets and coil manufacturing

## Academic & National Laboratory Collaborations
- **Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory (PPPL):** Collaborative research on FRC stability, diagnostic development
- **Los Alamos National Laboratory (LANL):** Plasma simulation and modeling support
- **University of Washington (UW):** Proximity-based collaboration; UW has a strong plasma physics group with FRC expertise
- **MIT Plasma Science and Fusion Center (PSFC):** Not a formal collaborator, but Helion's approach is sometimes compared/contrasted with Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS), an MIT spinout

## Washington State Government
The State of Washington has been notably supportive of Helion's development:
- **Governor Jay Inslee:** Has publicly supported fusion as a clean energy technology and signed fusion-friendly legislation
- **WA Dept. of Commerce:** Provided economic development support for the Orion siting process
- **WA Dept. of Ecology:** Streamlined SEPA review for fusion facilities
- **WA Dept. of Health:** Issued first fusion RML and RAEL licenses (June 2026)
- **WA Legislature:** Passed HB 1924 and HB 1018 establishing fusion-specific regulatory framework

## Future Partnership Opportunities
1. **Additional Tech Companies:** Google, Amazon, and Meta have ambitious 24/7 carbon-free energy goals and growing data center loads. A successful Helion PPAs with Microsoft could catalyze additional tech-sector offtake agreements.
2. **Industrial Heat Applications:** Fusion's ability to produce high-temperature heat could serve ammonia production, cement manufacturing, direct air capture, or hydrogen production (via high-temperature electrolysis or thermochemical cycles).
3. **International Expansion:** The UK, Japan, and South Korea have active fusion regulatory development programs and could be initial export markets for Helion's technology.
4. **Defense Applications:** The U.S. Department of Defense has expressed interest in compact fusion for mobile power generation (expeditionary bases, directed energy weapons). Helion's FRC approach is inherently compact and potentially transportable.
