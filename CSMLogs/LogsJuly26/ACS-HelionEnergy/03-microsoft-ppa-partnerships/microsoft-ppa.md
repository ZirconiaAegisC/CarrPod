# Microsoft Power Purchase Agreement: First Fusion PPA in History

## Agreement Overview
In May 2023, Helion Energy and Microsoft Corporation signed the world's first power purchase agreement (PPA) for fusion-generated electricity. The agreement commits Helion to deliver 50 MW of net electrical power to Microsoft's grid-connected operations by 2028, with financial penalties for non-delivery. Constellation Energy, one of the largest competitive energy suppliers in the United States, serves as the power marketer, managing scheduling, transmission, and delivery to Microsoft's load centers.

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Signatories | Helion Energy, Inc. (seller), Microsoft Corporation (buyer), Constellation Energy (marketer) |
| Signing Date | May 10, 2023 |
| Contracted Capacity | 50 MW (net, to grid) |
| Delivery Deadline | 2028 |
| Penalty Structure | Financial penalties for non-delivery (specifics not publicly disclosed) |
| Pricing | Not publicly disclosed |
| Contract Duration | Not publicly disclosed (likely 10-20 year term) |
| Delivery Location | Grid-delivered (not behind-the-meter at a Microsoft data center) |
| Source Plant | Orion (Malaga, WA) |

## Strategic Significance

### For Microsoft
Microsoft's climate commitments include becoming carbon negative by 2030 and removing all historical carbon emissions by 2050. Fusion power aligns with Microsoft's strategy to secure firm, dispatchable, carbon-free electricity for its growing data center fleet. AI workloads, in particular, are driving unprecedented electricity demand growth. Microsoft's data center electricity consumption was estimated at 20-25 TWh annually in 2023 and is projected to grow substantially. The Helion PPA provides:
- A hedge against long-term electricity price volatility
- A first-mover advantage in securing fusion power capacity
- A public demonstration of commitment to advanced clean energy technologies
- Alignment with Microsoft's broader climate investment strategy (the company's $1 billion Climate Innovation Fund)

### For Helion
The Microsoft PPA provides Helion with:
- **Revenue Certainty:** A guaranteed offtake agreement upon commercial operation, de-risking project financing
- **Market Validation:** Implicit endorsement from a Fortune 50 corporation with rigorous procurement standards
- **Investor Confidence:** A tangible commercial pathway that Helion's investors can underwrite
- **Schedule Discipline:** The 2028 deadline with financial penalties forces organizational focus
- **Public Credibility:** Demonstrated ability to secure a commercial contract at a pre-revenue stage

### For the Fusion Industry
The Microsoft PPA is a watershed moment for the fusion sector:
- It establishes a precedent that fusion power can be commercially contracted before technical demonstration
- It signals corporate demand for fusion electricity as a long-term power purchase strategy
- It validates the commercial fusion timeline (late 2020s) that multiple fusion companies are targeting
- It provides a template for future fusion PPAs (structure, penalty clauses, marketer role)

## Constellation Energy's Role
Constellation Energy, headquartered in Baltimore, Maryland, operates 32 GW of generating capacity and is the largest competitive energy supplier in the U.S. Constellation's role in the Helion-Microsoft PPA includes:
- **Power Marketing:** Selling Orion's output into the wholesale electricity market
- **Scheduling & Dispatch:** Managing the hourly scheduling and dispatch of Orion's output to match Microsoft's load profile
- **Transmission Arrangements:** Contracting for transmission capacity from Chelan County PUD to Microsoft's load centers
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Ensuring the power transactions comply with FERC, NERC, and state regulatory requirements
- **Risk Management:** Managing the financial and operational risk of a first-of-a-kind generation resource

Constellation's involvement is critical because Helion is not an experienced power generator; Constellation provides the market interface, regulatory expertise, and credit support necessary for a bankable PPA.

## Penalty Structure and Delivery Risk
The PPA includes financial penalties if Helion fails to deliver 50 MW by the 2028 deadline. While exact penalty amounts are not publicly disclosed, the existence of penalties creates:
- **Financial Risk for Helion:** Non-delivery would trigger cash payments to Microsoft, straining Helion's balance sheet
- **Reputational Risk:** A failed first-of-a-kind PPA would damage Helion's credibility with future customers and investors
- **Investor Impact:** Helion's investors would likely need to cover penalty payments through additional capital raises

The penalty structure is believed to include:
- Annual penalties for each year of delay past 2028
- Potential termination rights for Microsoft after a maximum delay period
- Force majeure provisions for events beyond Helion's reasonable control

## Grid Integration Considerations
Delivering 50 MW from Malaga, WA to Microsoft's load centers (primarily in the Western U.S.) involves:
- **Transmission:** Chelan County PUD → Bonneville Power Administration (BPA) transmission system → Microsoft load
- **Congestion Risk:** The BPA transmission system has limited east-west capacity during peak hours
- **Scheduling Challenges:** Orion's pulsed operation must be buffered to provide firm, grid-synchronous power
- **Resource Adequacy:** Orion's capacity must be accredited by the Western Electricity Coordinating Council (WECC)

## Comparison: Helion vs. Microsoft's Other Clean Energy Deals
| Deal | Capacity | Technology | Year | Status |
|------|----------|-----------|------|--------|
| Helion PPA | 50 MW | Fusion | 2023 | Pre-construction |
| Three Mile Island Restart | 835 MW | Nuclear (PWR) | 2024 | In development |
| Brookfield Renewables | 10.5 GW | Wind/Solar | 2024 | Framework agreement |
| Various PPAs | 20+ GW | Wind/Solar/Hydro | 2012-2025 | Operating |

Microsoft's Helion PPA is unique in being a pre-commercial technology bet rather than a conventional renewable or nuclear offtake agreement. It represents a small fraction of Microsoft's total clean energy portfolio but carries outsized strategic significance.
