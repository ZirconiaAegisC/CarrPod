# M&A STRATEGY: CRUCIS COMPANIES ACQUISITION (NOVEMBER 2025)

## TRANSACTION STRUCTURE

Concurrent with the GuideTech acquisition in November 2025, Palladyne AI acquired the Crucis
Companies, a holding entity comprising two precision manufacturing operations: Warnke
Manufacturing and MKR Manufacturing. Both subsidiaries have deep, decades-long relationships
with major U.S. defense primes and the Department of Defense supply chain. The combined
consideration for the Crucis acquisition was not separately disclosed from the GuideTech
transaction in available public filings, with Palladyne reporting aggregate acquisition costs
across all three entities.

## WARNKE MANUFACTURING

Warnke is a precision machining and fabrication operation specializing in tight-tolerance
components for aerospace and defense applications. Its customer base includes Lockheed Martin,
Northrop Grumman, and Boeing, with components flowing into platforms including:

- F-16 Fighting Falcon — structural and actuation components
- F-35 Lightning II — airframe and systems components across all three variants
- B-52 Commercial Engine Replacement Program (CERP) — engine mounting and ancillary components
- Various missile systems — guidance section housings, control surface components

Warnke's manufacturing certifications include AS9100D (aerospace quality management) and
ITAR registration, providing an immediate compliance framework for Palladyne's hardware
production requirements.

## MKR MANUFACTURING

MKR complements Warnke with capabilities in complex assemblies and sub-system integration.
Key programs include:

- Tomahawk cruise missile — precision-machined components for the Block V upgrade program
- Harpoon anti-ship missile — structural and propulsion components
- Ground-based missile defense — interceptor component manufacturing

The Tomahawk and Harpoon programs are particularly significant given the surge in demand for
both systems. The U.S. Navy's multi-year procurement of Tomahawk Block V missiles and the
increased international demand for Harpoon — partly driven by replenishment requirements
following transfers to Ukraine and Taiwan — position MKR's order book favorably.

## STRATEGIC IMPORTANCE

The Crucis acquisition serves three strategic purposes:

1. **Vertical Integration** — By owning precision manufacturing capacity, Palladyne can
   theoretically reduce cost and shorten lead times for components used in its own
   autonomous systems, particularly Gremlin-X airframes and avionics housings.

2. **Revenue Diversification** — Crucis provides a stable, recurring revenue base from
   established defense programs with multi-year procurement visibility, offsetting the
   lumpy, program-dependent revenue profile of the autonomous systems business.

3. **Supply Chain Positioning** — As the DoD prioritizes onshoring and supply chain
   resilience through initiatives like the Defense Production Act Title III and the
   CHIPS Act defense provisions, owning qualified, ITAR-compliant manufacturing
   facilities positioned Palladyne to capture incremental demand from prime contractors
   seeking to diversify their own supply chains.

## DEFENSE PRIME RELATIONSHIPS

Crucis's multi-decade relationships with Lockheed Martin, Raytheon (now RTX), and Boeing
represent intangible assets of significant value. These relationships provide:

- Direct line-of-sight into future program requirements and procurement rhythms
- Qualification status that would take new entrants years to achieve
- Potential channel for introducing Palladyne's autonomous systems into established
  weapons system upgrade programs

The integration of Crucis into Palladyne's organizational structure maintains operational
autonomy for the manufacturing subsidiaries, preserving their existing management teams and
quality systems while adding corporate-level financial and strategic oversight.
