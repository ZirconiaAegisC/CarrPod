# COMMERCIAL APPLICATIONS & MARKET STRATEGY

## DUAL-USE STRATEGY FRAMEWORK

Palladyne AI's commercial strategy rests on a dual-use framework in which technologies
developed for defense applications are adapted for commercial markets, and vice versa. This
approach has been a staple of defense technology companies for decades (notably employed by
Palantir, Anduril, and SpaceX) but requires disciplined product management to prevent the
company from simultaneously under-serving both markets.

## COMMERCIAL PATHWAYS FOR DEFENSE TECHNOLOGIES

### SwarmOS Commercial Applications

While SwarmOS is primarily a defense product, Palladyne has signaled commercial applications
including:

- **Infrastructure Inspection** — Coordinated multi-drone inspection of power lines,
  pipelines, bridges, and wind turbines. The commercial drone inspection market is
  projected to exceed $8 billion by 2030.

- **Precision Agriculture** — Swarm-coordinated crop monitoring, targeted spraying, and
  yield optimization using multiple UAVs operating collaboratively.

- **Emergency Response** — Multi-drone search and rescue, wildfire monitoring, and disaster
  assessment using coordinated aerial swarms.

These commercial applications represent potential revenue streams that are independent of
defense procurement cycles but face competition from well-funded commercial drone companies
including Skydio, DJI (geopolitically constrained), and Brinc Drones.

### DECA Commercial Licensing

The DECA edge computing architecture could be adapted for commercial IoT, autonomous vehicles,
and smart infrastructure applications. However, Palladyne has not announced specific
commercial DECA deployments, and the architecture's defense orientation (anti-jam
communications, military environmental qualification) may make it cost-uncompetitive for
price-sensitive commercial markets.

### Palladyne IQ: The Primary Commercial Vehicle

As described in the preceding dossier entry, Palladyne IQ is the company's primary commercial
product. Its commercial strategy targets three tiers of customers:

1. **Tier 1 Manufacturers** — Automotive OEMs, aerospace primes, and large-scale logistics
   operators with existing robot fleets seeking to add autonomy without replacing hardware.
   These customers represent high-value, long-sales-cycle opportunities.

2. **Mid-Market Manufacturers** — Companies with 500-5,000 employees that have under-
   automated production lines. Palladyne IQ's retrofit capability addresses the capital
   constraint these companies face: they cannot afford complete greenfield automation but
   can justify incremental software investment.

3. **System Integrators** — Palladyne has indicated a channel strategy through automation
   system integrators who incorporate IQ into turnkey solutions for end customers. This
   approach leverages integrators' existing customer relationships and reduces Palladyne's
   direct sales and support burden.

## MARKET SIZING AND GROWTH FORECASTS

The industrial robot autonomy software market is projected at approximately $3.5 billion
in 2026, growing at a CAGR of 12-14% through 2030. Palladyne's share of this market is
negligible at present, representing both a risk (lack of commercial traction) and an
opportunity (large addressable market).

## GEOPOLITICAL DEMAND DRIVERS

Palladyne's commercial strategy benefits from several exogenous demand drivers:

- **Reshoring of Manufacturing** — U.S. manufacturing construction spending has doubled since
  2021, driven by CHIPS Act, Inflation Reduction Act, and broader supply chain
  diversification away from China. This creates domestic demand for automation solutions.

- **Labor Scarcity** — U.S. manufacturing faces a structural labor shortage, with
  approximately 800,000 open manufacturing positions as of 2026. Automation adoption is
  increasingly driven by labor availability rather than pure ROI calculations.

- **Defense Industrial Base Expansion** — The DoD's push for expanded domestic weapons
  production (155mm shells, Javelin missiles, GMLRS rockets, loitering munitions) creates
  demand for manufacturing automation that can accelerate production ramp rates.

## COMMERCIAL STRATEGY RISKS

1. **Dilution of Focus** — Pursuing both defense and commercial markets simultaneously risks
   under-resourcing both. Companies of Palladyne's size ($5-27 million revenue) rarely have
   the organizational bandwidth to excel in two distinct go-to-market strategies.

2. **Channel Conflict** — Selling Palladyne IQ through system integrators while also
   pursuing direct enterprise sales creates potential channel conflict.

3. **Commercial ROI Uncertainty** — Unlike defense contracts, which are funded by
   appropriated budgets and have a defined procurement process, commercial sales require
   demonstrable ROI within customer investment horizons. Palladyne has not published
   third-party validated ROI data for IQ deployments.

4. **Brand Perception** — The company's identity transformation from industrial robotics to
   defense AI may create confusion among commercial customers, who may not associate
   Palladyne with industrial automation solutions.
