# RISK FACTORS, SHORT INTEREST & MARKET POSITION

## STOCK PRICE DECLINE AND VALUATION CONTEXT

Palladyne AI shares have declined approximately 90% from the all-time high of $66.72 to the
current trading level of approximately $6.72. This decline reflects a confluence of factors:

- **Pre-Revenue SPAC Valuation** — The all-time high was achieved during the post-SPAC period
  when the company (then Sarcos) was valued primarily on the exoskeleton narrative and TAM
  projections rather than demonstrated revenue.

- **Strategic Pivot Discount** — The market has applied a discount to companies that abandon
  their original business plan, reflecting uncertainty about whether the new strategy will
  succeed and skepticism about management's capital allocation judgment.

- **Dilution from Acquisitions** — The GuideTech and Crucis acquisitions, while funded
  primarily with cash, may have included equity components that diluted existing shareholders.

- **Public Market Small-Cap Dynamics** — Micro-cap and small-cap stocks (PDYN's market cap is
  approximately $130-150 million) have been disproportionately affected by the shift toward
  passive investing and reduced sell-side analyst coverage.

## SHORT INTEREST ANALYSIS

Palladyne's short interest of approximately 21.5% of float is among the highest in the small-
cap defense sector. High short interest reflects several bear arguments:

1. **SPAC Hangover Thesis** — A significant cohort of short sellers targets post-SPAC
   companies as a structural short, arguing that the SPAC process systematically overvalues
   the target relative to a traditional IPO and that fundamental deterioration follows.

2. **Integration Risk** — Integrating three acquired companies simultaneously while pivoting
   the core business is a multi-dimensional execution challenge. Short sellers may be betting
   that integration costs, culture clashes, and management bandwidth constraints will prevent
   the company from achieving its revenue guidance.

3. **Cash Burn and Dilution Risk** — As discussed in the financial analysis, the company
   consumes cash and may eventually require additional equity financing, which would dilute
   existing shareholders at depressed valuation levels.

4. **Competitive Position Skepticism** — Short sellers may believe that Palladyne's
   competitive position in both loitering munitions (versus AeroVironment, Anduril, Shield
   AI) and industrial autonomy software (versus Intrinsic, Symbotic) is insufficient to
   generate sustainable returns.

### Short Squeeze Potential

High short interest creates the potential for a short squeeze if the company delivers
positive catalysts that force short sellers to cover. Potential squeeze triggers include:

- Quarterly revenue exceeding guidance, demonstrating execution capability
- A significant new DoD contract award, particularly a program of record
- Announcement of a strategic partnership with a Tier 1 defense prime
- Insider buying by management or directors signaling confidence

However, short squeeze potential should not be conflated with investment merit. Many highly
shorted companies remain shorted for valid fundamental reasons, and the short interest may
reflect accurate skepticism rather than a mispricing opportunity.

## RISK FACTOR INVENTORY

### Execution Risks
- Failure to convert $24 million backlog into recognized revenue within expected timelines
- Production ramp challenges for Gremlin-X and IAI-licensed loitering munitions
- Integration friction between GuideTech, Crucis, and legacy Palladyne operations
- Management bandwidth constraints across three distinct business lines

### Market Risks
- Government budget continuing resolutions delaying new contract awards
- DoD program of record competitions lost to incumbent contractors
- Commercial market adoption of Palladyne IQ slower than anticipated
- Loitering munition market consolidation reducing the number of independent procurement
  opportunities

### Competitive Risks
- AeroVironment's established Switchblade franchise and Army relationships
- Anduril's scale, venture funding, and political access
- Shield AI's combat-proven Hivemind autonomy platform
- Intrinsic (Alphabet) and Symbotic's resources in industrial autonomy

### Financial Risks
- Cash consumption exceeding available runway before reaching breakeven
- Dilution from future equity raises at depressed valuations
- Earnout obligations from GuideTech acquisition consuming cash
- Warranty or performance obligations on defense hardware deliveries

### Regulatory and Compliance Risks
- ITAR and export control compliance for loitering munition sales
- Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC 2.0) requirements for DoD contracts
- Cost Accounting Standards compliance for government contracts
- Potential CFIUS review of foreign partnerships (IAI)

## MITIGANTS AND OFFSETS

- $44 million cash position provides near-term liquidity buffer
- Crucis manufacturing provides a stable revenue base independent of new contract awards
- IAI partnership brings combat-proven systems that reduce technical development risk
- Defense megatrends (autonomy, loitering munitions, onshoring) provide structural tailwinds
- Small size allows for rapid decision-making versus prime contractor bureaucracies

## INVESTMENT THESIS SUMMARY

The long thesis for Palladyne AI rests on the proposition that the company can execute a
successful transformation from pre-revenue exoskeleton developer to revenue-generating
defense technology integrator, achieving the scale and profitability necessary to justify
a valuation materially above current levels. The principal catalysts are backlog conversion,
program-of-record wins for loitering munitions, and continued growth in the defense vertical.

The short thesis rests on the proposition that the transformation is an acquisition-driven
assemblage of disparate assets lacking a unifying competitive advantage, that management
lacks the operational experience to integrate three companies while developing new products,
and that the company will require dilutive financing before achieving sustainable
profitability.

The resolution of these competing theses will be determined by the company's execution over
the next 6-12 months, with particular focus on Q3 and Q4 FY2026 revenue delivery against
guidance, new contract announcements, and the velocity of backlog conversion.
