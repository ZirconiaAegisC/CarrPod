# USAF CONTRACT & GOVERNMENT PROGRAMS

## $13.8 MILLION U.S. AIR FORCE CONTRACT

Palladyne AI has disclosed a $13.8 million contract with the United States Air Force, though
specific program details remain limited in public disclosures as of July 2026. The contract's
existence was confirmed in company presentations and regulatory filings. Several
characteristics can be inferred:

### Contract Type Assessment

Given Palladyne's stage of development and the typical USAF acquisition taxonomy, the
contract likely falls under one of the following categories:

- **Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase III** — Sole-source, non-competitive
  award for commercialization of technology previously developed under SBIR Phase I/II.
  SBIR Phase III contracts can scale to significant dollar values and are a common pathway
  for non-traditional defense contractors to secure USAF funding.

- **Other Transaction Authority (OTA)** — OTAs enable non-traditional defense contractors
  to bypass the Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) and Defense Federal Acquisition
  Regulation Supplement (DFARS) requirements, accelerating contract award timelines from
  years to months. The USAF has been an aggressive user of OTA authority for autonomous
  systems programs.

- **AFWERX STRATFI/TACFI** — The AFWERX Strategic Financing (STRATFI) and Tactical
  Financing (TACFI) programs provide funding to transition technologies from prototype
  to production. STRATFI awards range from $15-50 million with matching private capital,
  while TACFI awards typically range from $3-15 million.

### Probable Scope

The contract most likely relates to one or more of Palladyne's autonomous systems
capabilities:

1. SwarmOS integration and testing with USAF unmanned systems
2. Palladyne Pilot adaptation for USAF C2 requirements
3. Loitering munition evaluation (Gremlin-X or IAI-derivative for USAF tactical needs)
4. DECA architecture evaluation for edge computing on USAF UAS platforms

## GOVERNMENT CONTRACTING ENVIRONMENT

Palladyne operates within a defense procurement environment characterized by several
favorable structural trends for non-traditional entrants:

### Replicator Initiative

Deputy Secretary of Defense Kathleen Hicks launched the Replicator initiative in August 2023
with the explicit goal of fielding thousands of attritable autonomous systems across multiple
domains by August 2025 (since extended). Replicator emphasizes rapid, non-traditional
acquisition pathways and favors companies that can deliver production-scale hardware quickly.
Palladyne's combined software-manufacturing proposition aligns with Replicator's emphasis on
production at scale.

### DIU (Defense Innovation Unit)

The Defense Innovation Unit serves as a bridge between commercial technology companies and
DoD acquisition programs. DIU's Other Transaction authority enables prototype contracts that
can transition to production without full FAR/DFARS compliance. Palladyne has referenced
engagement with DIU in investor presentations though specific contract awards through DIU
have not been publicly confirmed.

### Congressional Support for Loitering Munitions

The FY2025 NDAA specifically authorized:
- $500 million for the Army's LASSO (Low Altitude Stalking and Strike Ordnance) program
- Additional funding for Marine Corps Organic Precision Fires (OPF) programs
- Expanded authority for loitering munition procurement through FMS channels

These program lines represent the addressable market for Palladyne's Gremlin-X and IAI-
licensed platforms.

## CONTRACTING RISK FACTORS

1. **Program of Record Hurdle** — The gap between a technology demonstration contract and a
   Program of Record with multi-year procurement authority is substantial. Many defense
   technology startups have secured initial USAF/USSOCOM contracts without successfully
   transitioning to a production program.

2. **Incumbent Advantages** — AeroVironment (Switchblade) and other established contractors
   have existing production lines, established qualification status, and relationships
   with program offices. Palladyne enters these competitions as a challenger.

3. **Budget Uncertainty** — While the topline NDAA numbers are supportive, continuing
   resolutions (CRs) and potential sequestration remain risks to new-start programs,
   which are more vulnerable to cuts than established programs of record.

4. **Compliance Burden** — Even under streamlined acquisition pathways, defense contracting
   requires compliance with cybersecurity requirements (CMMC 2.0), cost accounting
   standards, and intellectual property data rights assertions that impose overhead on
   small companies.
