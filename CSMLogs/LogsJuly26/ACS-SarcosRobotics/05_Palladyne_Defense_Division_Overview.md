# PALLADYNE DEFENSE DIVISION OVERVIEW

## DIVISION MANDATE

Palladyne Defense, formally launched following the GuideTech and Crucis acquisitions in late
2025, represents Palladyne AI's primary growth vector. The division consolidates all
defense-oriented autonomous systems, loitering munitions, and avionics activities under a
single operational entity reporting through CEO Benjamin Wolff.

## ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

Palladyne Defense comprises four interconnected capability areas:

1. **Loitering Munitions** — Encompasses both internally-developed systems (GuideTech
   Gremlin-X) and licensed platforms through the IAI strategic partnership (HARPY, HAROP,
   Mini HARPY). This segment addresses the rapidly expanding demand for precision-strike
   loitering munitions across U.S. and allied forces.

2. **SwarmOS** — Autonomous swarming software platform enabling coordinated multi-vehicle
   operations across heterogeneous unmanned systems. Provides the central unifying
   technology layer for Palladyne Defense.

3. **Avionics & DECA Architecture** — Miniaturized flight control, guidance, and
   communication systems derived from GuideTech's engineering portfolio. The DECA
   (Distributed Edge Computing Architecture) framework provides a modular hardware and
   software stack for autonomous system integration.

4. **Palladyne Pilot** — Multi-domain autonomous platform for integration and coordination
   of unmanned systems across air, ground, and maritime domains. Designed for military
   command-and-control applications.

## COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

Palladyne Defense occupies the mid-tier defense technology integrator space, competing
primarily against:

- **AeroVironment** (Switchblade series, established loitering munition incumbent)
- **Anduril Industries** (broader autonomous systems portfolio, significant venture funding)
- **Shield AI** (autonomous piloting, Hivemind platform)
- **UVision** (Hero loitering munition family, partnered with several U.S. primes)

Palladyne's differentiation lies in its combined hardware-manufacturing-software proposition.
Unlike pure software companies, Palladyne owns precision manufacturing capacity (Crucis).
Unlike pure hardware companies, it offers an autonomous software stack (SwarmOS). Whether
this vertical integration thesis translates into competitive advantage remains unproven.

## ADDRESSABLE MARKET

The loitering munition market alone is projected to exceed $3 billion annually by 2028, with
compound annual growth exceeding 15%. Additive markets for autonomous swarm software, edge
computing for defense, and precision manufacturing position Palladyne Defense against a total
addressable market exceeding $10 billion. Palladyne's challenge is converting this TAM into
captured revenue, a process that requires converting the $24 million backlog into delivered
systems and securing follow-on production contracts.
