# Financial Position and Strategic Outlook

## Liquidity and Runway

QuantumScape ended FY2025 with $970.8M in cash, equivalents, and marketable securities.
Q1 2026: $904.7M, reflecting quarterly operating cash consumption. Management guidance:
this liquidity funds operations through 2029, extending beyond critical milestones of
Eagle Line startup, customer qualification, and initial licensing agreements.

The extended runway reduces the risk of needing capital markets access before generating
material licensing revenue — a strategic asset providing flexibility and negotiating
leverage in the current challenging environment for pre-revenue technology companies.
The company carries no long-term debt, eliminating interest expense and refinancing risk.

## FY2026 Financial Guidance

**Adjusted EBITDA**: $(250)M to $(275)M loss (excludes stock-based compensation and
certain non-cash/non-recurring items). Reflects operating expenses for R&D, engineering,
Eagle Line, and corporate functions, partially offset by PowerCo JV payments, royalty
prepayments, and JDA engineering fees — not yet approaching breakeven.

**Capital Expenditures**: $40–60M, reflecting Eagle Line completion, R&D equipment, and
IT. Modest relative to liquidity position and reflective of the capital-light strategy —
this annual capex is less than the quarterly capex of a major battery manufacturer
operating a single gigafactory.

## Operating Expense Structure

- **R&D**: Largest category — materials research, cell design, process development,
  testing and validation. Expected to continue at elevated levels for the "beyond QSE-5"
  roadmap.
- **Manufacturing Operations**: Eagle Line direct materials, labor, maintenance, quality.
  Increases with ramp but partially offset by customer sampling and engineering revenue.
- **SG&A**: Corporate overhead — executive, finance, legal, HR, business development.
  Controlled as appropriate for pre-revenue stage.
- **Stock-Based Compensation**: Significant non-cash expense for Bay Area talent
  competition; excluded from Adjusted EBITDA.

## Revenue Trajectory

**Phase 1 (2025–2026)**: Modest milestone-based payments from PowerCo JV and JDA
engineering fees. FY2025 revenue of $19.5M is representative.

**Phase 2 (2027–2028)**: Growing revenue from expanded JV payments, technology access
fees from additional licensees, increased engineering services. Revenue remains lumpy
due to milestone-based structure but grows in magnitude.

**Phase 3 (2029+)**: Royalty revenue commences as licensee production facilities begin
commercial operations. Royalties are recurring and scale with production volumes.
Transition to positive operating cash flow expected during this phase, timing dependent
on licensee ramp pace.

## Key Financial Risks

### Dilution Risk
If the timeline to royalty revenue extends beyond 2029 or unexpected costs arise,
additional capital may be needed through equity or debt, diluting shareholders.
Management's 2029 runway guidance addresses but does not guarantee against this.

### Customer Concentration
PowerCo/VW is expected to represent the dominant share of near-term revenue. If the
relationship deteriorates — technology issues, VW strategy change, or commercial
disputes — the revenue impact would be material. JDA relationships with two additional
OEMs mitigate but are at an earlier stage.

### Technology Risk
Residual risk remains in scaling Eagle Line to target yields, achieving automotive
warranty-level cycle life and reliability, and maintaining performance during transition
from QuantumScape's controlled environment to licensee factories at commercial scale.

### Competitive Risk
If a competitor (Toyota, Samsung SDI, CATL, or a startup) commercializes a competitive
solid-state technology before QuantumScape's licensees achieve meaningful production,
the licensing proposition may be diminished. Conversely, competitor setbacks highlight
commercialization difficulty and strengthen QuantumScape's relative position.

### Macroeconomic and Policy Risk
EV adoption is sensitive to interest rates, fuel prices, consumer confidence, government
policy (incentives, emissions regulations, tariffs), and competitive dynamics (Tesla
pricing, Chinese EV exports). The IRA's domestic battery manufacturing provisions favor
QuantumScape's US ceramic ecosystem strategy, but political changes represent policy risk.

## Strategic Milestones (2026–2029)

**2026**: Eagle Line QSE-5 cells to customers (Q2); field testing commences; additional
JDA/licensing announcements possible; beyond-QSE-5 development advances; ceramic supply
chain build-out with Murata and Corning.

**2027**: C-sample delivery; PPAP initiation with lead customer(s); license agreement
execution with additional OEM(s); Eagle Line capacity expansion as warranted.

**2028**: PPAP completion with initial customer(s); licensee facility construction or
conversion underway; revenue growth from technology access fees and engineering services;
potential first royalty revenue from initial low-volume production.

**2029**: Licensee commercial production ramp; royalty revenue scaling with volumes;
path to positive operating cash flow; expansion of licensee base and licensed capacity.

## Investment Thesis

The case rests on the proposition that QuantumScape has solved the fundamental
challenges of solid-state commercialization: a functioning ceramic separator suppressing
dendrites, a manufacturing process (Cobra) overcoming the throughput bottleneck, and a
cell design (QSE-5) delivering competitive or superior performance. The licensing model
translates this technology leadership into attractive financial returns without the
capital intensity of vertical integration.

Primary risks: execution (achieving target yields and reliability on Eagle Line and at
licensee facilities), timing (pace of licensee capacity deployment), and competition
(emergence of superior solid-state technology). The extended liquidity runway through
2029 provides a substantial buffer against timing risk and management flexibility to
navigate the uncertainties of the commercialization path.
