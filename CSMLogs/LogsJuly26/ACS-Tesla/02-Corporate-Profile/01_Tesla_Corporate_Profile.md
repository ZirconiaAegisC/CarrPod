## 1. Corporate Identity & Legal Structure

**Legal Name:** Tesla, Inc.
**Incorporation:** Delaware, United States (originally incorporated July 1, 2003 as Tesla Motors, Inc.; name changed to Tesla, Inc. effective February 1, 2017)
**Headquarters:** 1 Tesla Road, Austin, Texas 78725, United States
**Stock Ticker:** TSLA (Nasdaq GS)
**Fiscal Year:** Calendar year ending December 31
**Independent Auditor:** PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP

Tesla, Inc. operates through a consolidated corporate structure with wholly-owned subsidiaries in each major manufacturing jurisdiction. Key operating subsidiaries include Tesla Shanghai Co., Ltd. (People's Republic of China), Tesla Manufacturing Brandenburg SE (Federal Republic of Germany), and Tesla Texas (United States). The corporate reorganization completed in 2024 consolidated previously separate entity structures under a streamlined holding framework designed to optimize global tax efficiency, intellectual property (IP) domiciling, and intercompany transfer pricing compliance.

As of Q1 2026, Tesla employs approximately 125,000 full-time personnel across its global operations, representing a deliberate deceleration in headcount growth following the workforce optimization initiatives undertaken in 2024–2025. The organizational structure is functionally organized across engineering (hardware, software, manufacturing), vehicle production operations, energy operations, sales and service, and corporate functions (finance, legal, human resources, government affairs).

**Q1 2026 Services & Other Revenue:** $2.3 billion

### 2.4 Emerging Business Lines

Multiple revenue lines are in pre-revenue or early-revenue stages and are reported within the segments above but warrant separate strategic tracking:

- **Full Self-Driving (FSD) Software:** 1.28 million active subscriptions globally. Revenue recognition is partially deferred pending feature delivery milestones. Represented $1.1+ billion in recognized revenue in 2025 with significantly greater deferred revenue on the balance sheet awaiting feature-complete recognition triggers.
- **Optimus Humanoid Robot:** Pre-revenue. Development and capital expenditure phase. Gen 3 hardware in validation. Mass production lines under construction in Fremont and Texas.
- **Tesla Insurance:** Available in 12 U.S. states. Captive insurance entity underwriting real-time driving behavior-based policies. Premium volume approaching $500 million annualized run rate.
- **Supercharger Network (Third-Party Access):** Revenue from non-Tesla EV charging via NACS-adopted connectors and Magic Dock adapters. Early-stage but accelerating with major OEM NACS adoption commitments.

## 5. Key Strategic Partnerships & Supplier Relationships

Tesla's supply chain architecture is characterized by aggressive vertical integration combined with strategic partnerships in upstream raw materials:

- **Panasonic Corporation:** Joint venture operation of 18650 and 2170 cell production lines within Gigafactory Nevada. Long-term supply agreement for cells from Japan.
- **CATL (Contemporary Amperex Technology Co. Limited):** LFP cell supply for Shanghai-made standard-range vehicles. Technology licensing arrangement for LFP cathode production in Nevada.
- **LG Energy Solution:** 2170 cell supply for vehicles produced in China and, historically, for Fremont production.
- **Lithium Suppliers:** Multiple long-term offtake agreements with producers in Australia (Greenbushes, Pilgangoora), Chile (SQM, Albemarle), and the United States (Piedmont Lithium, Lithium Americas). The Corpus Christi lithium refinery will reduce but not eliminate third-party dependence.
- **Nickel Suppliers:** Agreements with BHP (Nickel West, Australia), Vale (Canada/Indonesia), and Prony Resources (New Caledonia) for Class 1 nickel suitable for high-nickel cathode chemistries.
- **NACS Adoption Partners:** Ford, General Motors, Rivian, Volvo, Polestar, Mercedes-Benz, Nissan, Honda, Toyota, Subaru, Lucid, Jaguar Land Rover, Hyundai Motor Group, BMW Group, and others have announced adoption of the North American Charging Standard (NACS) connector, providing Tesla's Supercharger network with a structural competitive moat and recurring third-party revenue stream.

## 6. Intellectual Property Portfolio

Tesla's IP strategy is unconventional relative to industry norms. The company's 2014 patent pledge commits to not initiating patent litigation against parties using Tesla's patented technology "in good faith." Despite this, Tesla maintains an active patent portfolio exceeding 3,500 active patents and pending applications globally, concentrated in:

- Battery cell chemistry, electrode processing, and pack thermal management
- Electric motor design, power electronics, and drivetrain integration
- Vehicle body structures, including large-format gigacasting aluminum alloys and tooling
- Manufacturing process automation and quality control systems
- Autonomous driving perception, planning, and control algorithms
- Supercharger power electronics and thermal management
- Optimus actuator design, kinematic control, and battery integration
- Neural network training infrastructure and data pipeline architectures

The patent pledge serves a dual strategic purpose: (1) accelerating industry transition to sustainable energy by removing IP barriers to entry, and (2) establishing Tesla's technical approaches as de facto standards (e.g., NACS connector, 48V低压 architecture, gigacasting methodology) that create ecosystem lock-in regardless of competitor adoption of specific patents.

## 7. Regulatory & Compliance Framework

Tesla operates under the regulatory jurisdiction of multiple national and sub-national authorities. Key regulatory relationships include:

- **United States:** National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) for vehicle safety standards and autonomous vehicle oversight; Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) for emissions and fuel economy standards; Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) for public company disclosure and governance; Federal Trade Commission (FTC) for consumer protection; Department of Energy (DOE) for Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing loan program compliance; state-level departments of motor vehicles for autonomous vehicle deployment permits.
- **People's Republic of China:** Ministry of Industry and Information Technology (MIIT) for manufacturing licensing; China Compulsory Certification (CCC) for product certification; Cybersecurity Administration for data security and cross-border data transfer compliance.
- **European Union:** European Commission for type approval, emissions standards, and competition law; European Data Protection Board for GDPR compliance; member-state transport authorities for individual market homologation.
- **Germany:** Kraftfahrt-Bundesamt (KBA) for vehicle type approval; Brandenburg state environmental authorities for Gigafactory Berlin water usage and emissions permits.
