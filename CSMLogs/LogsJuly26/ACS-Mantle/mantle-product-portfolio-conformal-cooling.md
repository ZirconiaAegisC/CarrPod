# Mantle Product Portfolio — Conformal Cooling Solutions

## The Cooling Challenge

Injection molding cycle time is dominated by cooling — typically 60 to 80
percent of the total cycle. Conventional mold cooling uses straight drilled
holes that cannot follow the contours of complex part geometries. This creates
hot spots, uneven cooling, part warpage, and extended cycle times. Mantle's
conformal cooling capability exploits the geometric freedom of additive
manufacturing to place cooling channels that follow the cavity surface at a
constant standoff distance, delivering uniform heat extraction.

## Design Methodology

### Thermal Simulation Inputs

Mantle's TrueShape Advanced software includes an integrated thermal simulation
module that imports Moldflow or Moldex3D analysis results. The module identifies
hot spot regions where the bulk mold temperature exceeds the target ejection
temperature. Based on these thermal maps, the software proposes conformal
cooling channel geometries that:

- Maintain 3 to 8 mm standoff from the cavity surface
- Keep channel-to-channel spacing at 2 to 3 times channel diameter
- Route around ejector pin locations and slide mechanisms
- Minimize pressure drop to stay within standard mold temperature controller
  pumping capacity of 2 to 4 bar at 20 to 40 liters per minute

### Channel Geometries

The FMP process can print channels as small as 1.5 mm diameter with a minimum
wall thickness of 2 mm between the channel and the cavity surface. Typical
production channels range from 4 to 8 mm diameter. Non-circular cross-sections
— obround, teardrop, or freeform — are achievable, allowing the channel shape
to optimize the trade-off between cooling surface area and structural integrity
of the insert.

### Baffle and Bubbler Integration

For deep cores and tall standing features, Mantle can print internal baffle
and bubbler geometries directly into the insert. Traditional toolmaking
requires drilling and plugging operations to create internal flow passages
in cores — a time-consuming process prone to coolant leaks. Printed baffles
are monolithic with the insert body, eliminating leak paths.

## Performance Validation

### Thermal Imaging Verification

Mantle validates conformal cooling performance using infrared thermal imaging
during molding trials at customer facilities. Thermocouples embedded in the
insert at multiple depths record actual metal temperatures throughout the
cycle. Results consistently show:

- Temperature variation across the cavity surface reduced from 15-25C
  (conventional cooling) to 3-7C (conformal cooling)
- Reduction in hot spot peak temperatures by 15 to 30C
- Cycle time reduction of 25 to 40 percent for typical packaging and consumer
  product applications

### Case Study: Cosmetic Packaging

A L'Oreal compact case tool running polypropylene demonstrated:

- Cycle time reduced from 22 seconds (conventional) to 14 seconds
- Scrap rate from warpage reduced from 3.2 percent to 0.4 percent
- Annual production capacity increased by 57 percent on the same press
- Payback on conformal cooling investment: under 4 months of production

### Case Study: Automotive Interior

An OtterBox phone case mold running thermoplastic polyurethane:

- Cycle time reduced from 48 seconds to 32 seconds
- Surface quality improved from SPI B-1 to SPI A-2 with same texture
- Sink mark defects eliminated on thick-to-thin transitions
- Conformal cooling allowed elimination of one post-mold fixturing station,
  saving 95000 USD annually in labor and floor space

## Design for Additive Guidelines

Mantle provides customers with design guidelines that maximize the benefit
of conformal cooling:

1. **Minimum wall thickness**: 2 mm between channel and cavity at any point
2. **Channel aspect ratio**: Channels deeper than 10 times diameter require
   support structures during printing that increase post-processing time
3. **Drainage**: All channels must slope at least 1 degree toward drain ports
   to allow complete coolant evacuation during mold changes
4. **Connection interfaces**: Channels terminate at standardized NPT or BSPP
   threaded ports on the non-cosmetic faces of the insert for connection to
   the mold temperature controller manifold
5. **Inspection access**: Include borescope access ports at channel elbows
   and terminations for periodic inspection of internal channel condition

## Economic Model

Conformal cooling adds approximately 15 to 25 percent to the insert cost
compared with a straight-cooled insert of the same dimensions. For production
volumes above 50000 cycles per year, the cycle time savings alone provide
a payback period of 2 to 6 months. When factoring in reduced scrap, improved
part quality, and extended tool life from lower peak temperatures, the
lifetime cost advantage of conformal cooling typically exceeds 200 percent
of the incremental insert cost over a production run of one million cycles.
