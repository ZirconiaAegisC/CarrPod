# Mantle Customer Pipeline — Consumer Goods Sector

## Strategic Importance

Consumer packaged goods represent Mantle's largest commercial beachhead and
highest-volume customer segment. The sector's characteristics align closely
with Mantle's value proposition: high product turnover rates requiring new
tooling every 6 to 18 months, cost sensitivity on tooling lead times, and
cosmetic finish requirements that benefit from conformal cooling quality
improvements.

## Key Customer: L'Oreal

### Relationship Overview

L'Oreal was among Mantle's earliest commercial customers and remains a
flagship reference account. The cosmetics giant operates hundreds of injection
molds globally across its portfolio of brands spanning skincare, haircare,
makeup, and fragrance. The relationship began with a single test mold for a
lipstick component and has expanded to production programs across multiple
product categories.

### Application Profile

Typical L'Oreal mold insert programs on the P-200:

- Compact cases, lipstick mechanisms, and mascara components
- Insert sizes ranging from 50 mm by 40 mm to 120 mm by 80 mm
- Materials: H13 tool steel for production inserts, P20 for prototype and
  bridge tooling
- Surface requirements: SPI A-1 to A-2 on cosmetic surfaces with complex
  textures and logos
- Production volumes: 500000 to 2 million cycles per insert set

### Value Delivered

L'Oreal reports that Mantle inserts have reduced their tooling lead time
from an average of 10 weeks to under 2 weeks for standard cosmetic packaging
components. More significantly, the conformal cooling capability has reduced
cycle times by 25 to 35 percent across multiple programs. For a high-volume
product running 2 million cycles per year, the cycle time reduction alone
saves approximately 1200 press-hours annually — equivalent to freeing up one
injection molding machine for additional production.

## Key Customer: PepsiCo

### Application Focus

PepsiCo utilizes Mantle tooling primarily for closure and dispensing system
components — bottle caps, sports closures, pump dispensers, and trigger
sprayers. These components demand high wear resistance due to abrasive
polypropylene compounds and require dimensional precision for consistent
seal performance and consumer ergonomics.

### Production Scale

PepsiCo's closure tooling programs run at extreme volumes — typically 5 to
20 million cycles annually per insert set. At these volumes, cycle time
reduction from conformal cooling generates substantial economic returns.
Mantle reports that conformal cooling on a 48-cavity closure mold reduced
cycle time from 6.8 seconds to 4.9 seconds — a 28 percent improvement that
increased annual production capacity by approximately 8 million units from
the same mold base.

### Tool Life Performance

H13 inserts produced via FMP have demonstrated equivalent or superior tool
life compared with conventionally manufactured inserts in PepsiCo's closure
applications. After 1.5 million cycles, FMP inserts showed wear patterns
consistent with wrought H13, with no premature failure modes attributable
to the additive manufacturing process. This validation at extreme production
volumes provides Mantle with critical data for customer confidence in the
technology's production readiness.

## Key Customer: OtterBox

### Relationship Overview

OtterBox, a leading manufacturer of protective cases for mobile devices,
uses Mantle tooling for its high-volume phone case production. The company's
product development cycle, driven by annual smartphone releases, demands
rapid tooling turnaround to meet launch deadlines.

### Technical Requirements

OtterBox tooling presents specific challenges well-suited to Mantle's
capabilities:

- Large insert sizes (150 mm by 80 mm typical) for phone case geometries
- Thermoplastic polyurethane (TPU) molding requiring precise thermal control
  to avoid material degradation
- Co-molding of rigid and soft materials requiring complex shutoff geometries
- Textured cosmetic surfaces requiring consistent finish across insert sets

Pre-Mantle, OtterBox relied on tooling sourced primarily from China with
8 to 12 week lead times and significant communication overhead for design
changes and quality issues. Mantle's ability to deliver inserts in under a
week and iterate rapidly on design changes has reduced OtterBox's tooling
cycle by approximately 80 percent for programs moved to the P-200.

## Broader Consumer Goods Pipeline

Beyond the anchor customers, Mantle has engagements with:

- **Personal care brands**: Razor handle molds, toothbrush component tooling,
  deodorant package molds
- **Food and beverage packaging**: Thin-wall container molds, tamper-evident
  closure tooling, single-serve coffee pod molds
- **Household products**: Storage container tooling, cleaning product
  dispensing components, appliance cosmetic panels

The consumer goods pipeline benefits from a multiplier effect: once a
company qualifies Mantle for one product category, expansion to additional
categories typically follows within 12 to 18 months as the tooling engineering
organization gains confidence in the technology.

## Sales Motion

Mantle's consumer goods sales strategy employs a land-and-expand model:

1. **Initial engagement**: A paid benchmark project producing one or two
   inserts for a representative mold geometry
2. **Pilot program**: The customer leases or purchases a single P-200 system
   and converts 10 to 20 percent of their tooling spend to Mantle inserts
3. **Expansion**: Based on pilot results, the customer adds P-200 capacity
   and converts 50 to 80 percent of compatible tooling to FMP inserts
4. **Enterprise adoption**: Multiple P-200 systems across multiple facilities,
   integrated into the customer's standard tooling procurement process

Each stage typically spans 6 to 12 months, with the full land-and-expand cycle
from initial engagement to enterprise adoption taking 18 to 36 months.
