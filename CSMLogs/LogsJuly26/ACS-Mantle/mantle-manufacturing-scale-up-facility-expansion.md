# Mantle Manufacturing and Scale-Up — Facility and Capacity Expansion

## Headquarters and Production Facility

Mantle operates from its headquarters and primary manufacturing facility in
San Francisco, California. The facility spans approximately 4000 square meters
and houses R&D, machine assembly, application engineering, and a customer
demonstration center. The San Francisco location provides access to the Bay
Area's deep talent pool in precision engineering, materials science, and
software development — critical competences for Mantle's multidisciplinary
technology stack.

## Machine Production Capacity

### Current State

As of the most recent reporting period, Mantle's San Francisco facility has
an annual production capacity of approximately 50 to 80 P-200 systems per year.
Assembly is organized in a cellular manufacturing layout with four parallel
assembly stations, each capable of producing one system every two to three
weeks. Key subassemblies — the extrusion head, CNC gantry, and motion control
system — are built in dedicated work cells that feed the final assembly stations.

### Supply Chain

Mantle sources critical components from a network of primarily domestic suppliers:

- Linear motion components (rails, bearings, ballscrews) from a Tier 1 supplier
  in the Midwest United States
- Spindle motors from a Swiss manufacturer with a North American distribution hub
- Control electronics from a US-based industrial automation supplier
- Granite machine bases from a precision granite fabricator in the Northeast
- Sheet metal enclosures from a local Bay Area fabrication shop
- FMP paste raw materials — metal powder from a US gas-atomized powder
  producer, thermoplastic binder components from chemical suppliers in the
  US and Germany

Dual-sourcing strategies are in place for all single-source components with
longer than 8-week lead times.

### Production Ramp Plan

Mantle's capacity expansion roadmap targets:

- **Year 1-2**: Optimize existing San Francisco facility to reach 100 systems
  per year through lean manufacturing implementation and second-shift operations
- **Year 2-3**: Establish a second assembly facility in the Midwest, closer to
  the concentration of tooling customers in Michigan, Ohio, and Indiana
- **Year 3-4**: Expand total capacity to 200-plus systems per year across
  both facilities

The Midwest facility would also house an application engineering center
providing local support to automotive tooling customers and serve as a
demonstration site for prospective customers in the region.

## FMP Material Production

FMP paste manufacturing is currently performed in-house at the San Francisco
facility in a dedicated materials processing area. The mixing and cartridge
filling process operates under cleanroom conditions (ISO Class 8) to prevent
contamination of the paste with foreign particles that could cause nozzle
clogs or inclusions in printed parts.

Current paste production capacity supports approximately 12000 cartridges
per year — sufficient for roughly 3000 to 4000 insert builds. As the installed
base of P-200 systems grows, Mantle plans to outsource paste production to a
contract manufacturer with existing powder handling and mixing capabilities
in the specialty chemicals sector. This transition would free internal
resources for new material development while ensuring supply scalability.

## Application Engineering and Customer Support

Mantle's application engineering team, based in San Francisco, provides:

- Pre-sale feasibility analysis of customer tooling designs
- Build orientation and support strategy recommendations
- Post-processing consultation for unusual geometries
- Remote troubleshooting during customer builds
- On-site support for initial customer production ramp

As the customer base grows, Mantle plans to establish regional application
engineering centers co-located with the Midwest assembly facility and a
potential European center in Germany or Switzerland to serve the dense
concentration of precision tooling manufacturers in central Europe.

## Workforce Development

Mantle's workforce strategy recognizes the shortage of skilled toolmakers
as both a market opportunity and a hiring challenge. The company employs
approximately 80 to 120 people across engineering, manufacturing, sales,
and administration. Key workforce initiatives include:

- **Operator training program**: A structured 2-week curriculum that trains
  individuals with basic mechanical aptitude to operate the P-200 system,
  replacing the years of apprenticeship required for traditional toolmaking
- **Partnership with trade schools**: Collaborations with manufacturing
  technology programs at community colleges to develop curriculum modules
  on additive tooling
- **Internal apprenticeship**: A 12-month program developing advanced skills
  in tooling design, materials science, and application engineering for
  early-career engineers

These programs directly support Mantle's value proposition of reducing the
skilled labor barrier in toolmaking while building a pipeline of talent
familiar with the company's technology.

## Capital Investment in Scale-Up

Mantle has deployed a significant portion of its 100 million-plus USD funding
toward scaling operations. Key capital investments include:

- CNC machining equipment for in-house production of precision components
- Materials characterization laboratory with scanning electron microscopy,
  mechanical testing, and thermal analysis capabilities
- ERP and manufacturing execution system implementation for production tracking
- Customer relationship management and field service management platforms
- Cloud infrastructure for the TrueShape software platform and machine
  monitoring data pipeline

Ongoing investment priorities focus on automation of FMP paste production,
expansion of the application engineering team, and development of the
Midwest facility.
