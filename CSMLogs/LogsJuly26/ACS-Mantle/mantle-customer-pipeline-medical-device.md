# Mantle Customer Pipeline — Medical Device Sector

## Market Characteristics

Medical device manufacturing represents a high-value, technically demanding
market segment for Mantle. Medical tooling requirements differ fundamentally
from consumer goods and automotive: lower production volumes (typically 10000
to 500000 units annually), more stringent regulatory requirements, higher
material costs for medical-grade polymers, and tolerance for higher tooling
cost in exchange for faster time-to-market and documented quality assurance.

## Regulatory Environment

### FDA and ISO Compliance

Medical device tooling must operate within a regulated quality system.
While injection molds themselves are not regulated medical devices, tooling
suppliers to medical device manufacturers must demonstrate compliance with:

- **ISO 13485**: Quality management system requirements for medical device
  manufacturers and their critical suppliers
- **21 CFR Part 820**: FDA quality system regulation for medical devices
- **Material traceability**: Full chain of custody documentation for tool
  steel materials from mill to finished insert
- **Process validation**: Documented evidence that the manufacturing process
  consistently produces inserts meeting specifications

Mantle's quality system documentation and material traceability capabilities
position the company to serve medical device customers without requiring
additional facility certifications. However, individual customers may require
customer-specific audits of Mantle's facility and processes before approving
Mantle as a qualified tooling supplier.

### Biocompatibility Considerations

Medical device tooling must not introduce substances that could affect the
biocompatibility of the molded part. Mantle's FMP process uses materials
and processing conditions that have been evaluated for extractables and
leachables. The debinding and sintering process removes essentially all
organic binder components, leaving a fully dense metal insert with no
residual organic content that could migrate into the molded part. Customers
typically perform their own extractables testing on parts molded from
Mantle inserts as part of their device qualification process.

## Application Categories

### Diagnostic Device Components

Mantle inserts are used in tooling for:

- **Microfluidic consumables**: Lab-on-a-chip cartridges, lateral flow assay
  housings, PCR reaction vessels with sub-millimeter feature precision
- **Sample collection devices**: Blood collection tube racks, specimen
  transport containers, swab handles
- **Point-of-care instrument housings**: Handheld diagnostic device enclosures,
  benchtop analyzer bezels and panels

These applications benefit from Mantle's ability to produce inserts with
very fine features — channels as narrow as 0.3 mm, ribs as thin as 0.5 mm —
that are difficult or impossible to machine conventionally.

### Surgical Instrument Components

Tooling for surgical instruments represents a growing application area:

- **Disposable instrument handles**: Scalpel handles, forceps grips, retractor
  bodies molded from autoclavable engineering thermoplastics
- **Implant trial components**: Sizing trials and surgical guides used in
  orthopedic procedures, produced in low volumes (500 to 5000 units annually)
  where rapid tooling minimizes inventory carrying cost
- **Drug delivery devices**: Inhaler components, auto-injector bodies, pen
  injector mechanisms requiring precision shutoffs for dose accuracy

### Implantable Device Packaging

Sterile barrier packaging for implantable devices requires mold inserts with
mirror-finish surfaces (SPI A-1 or better) and extremely tight flatness
tolerances to ensure hermetic seal integrity. Mantle's integrated CNC
finishing achieves the required surface quality, and the conformal cooling
capability provides the thermal uniformity needed to avoid warpage in thin-wall
packaging components.

## Customer Profile: Medical Device Manufacturers

### Relationship Models

Medical device manufacturers typically engage Mantle through one of two models:

**Direct Purchase Model**: Large medical device companies with captive toolrooms
purchase P-200 systems and produce inserts internally. This model appeals to
organizations that prioritize control over their tooling supply chain and have
sufficient mold volume to justify the capital investment. A P-200 system
pays back in 12 to 18 months for organizations producing 100 or more inserts
annually.

**Service Bureau Model**: Smaller device companies and contract manufacturers
source inserts through Mantle's network of certified job shops or through
Mantle's internal application engineering team. This model suits organizations
with intermittent tooling needs or those that prefer to avoid capital equipment
investment.

### Validation Pathway

Medical device customers follow a structured validation pathway with Mantle:

1. **Material qualification**: The customer evaluates FMP H13 and P20 against
   their approved material specifications. Mantle provides full material
   certification packages including chemistry, mechanical properties, and
   metallographic examination results.
2. **Process capability study**: A test insert with representative geometry is
   produced and measured. Process capability indices (Cpk) must meet the
   customer's minimum requirements, typically 1.33 or higher.
3. **Molding trial**: The insert is run in the customer's production molding
   environment. Parts are evaluated for dimensional conformance, surface
   quality, and any evidence of contamination or material interaction.
4. **Regulatory submission support**: Mantle provides documentation required
   for the customer's regulatory submissions, including material certifications,
   process descriptions, and quality records.
5. **Production authorization**: Upon successful completion of validation,
   Mantle inserts are approved for production use on the specific device
   program.

The validation pathway typically takes 3 to 6 months from initial engagement
to production authorization.

## Growth Trajectory

The medical device segment currently represents a smaller portion of Mantle's
revenue than consumer goods or automotive but offers higher margins and
longer customer retention. Medical device programs, once qualified, tend to
run for 5 to 10 years or longer — far exceeding the 1 to 3 year program
lifecycle in consumer goods. Mantle is investing in regulatory expertise and
medical-specific application engineering resources to accelerate growth in
this segment, targeting medical device revenue to represent 20 to 25 percent
of total revenue within 3 to 5 years.
