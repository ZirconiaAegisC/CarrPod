# F3-02: Fab-2 Malaysia — 100 MWh/Year Expansion Program

## Strategic Rationale

Fab-2 represents Enovix's capacity expansion from pilot-commercial scale
(Fab-1 at ~6 MWh/year) to meaningful commercial volume (~100 MWh/year).
This order-of-magnitude scale-up is designed to:

1. Achieve cost competitiveness through volume manufacturing economics
2. Support multiple simultaneous OEM design wins (each representing 10-50M
   units/year)
3. Establish a beachhead for eventual automotive-scale production

## Facility Specifications

### Location and Footprint

Fab-2 is being constructed on a greenfield site adjacent to Fab-1 in the
Batu Kawan Industrial Park, leveraging existing infrastructure (power,
water, logistics) and the trained workforce base. Total site area is
approximately 500,000 sq ft with a planned building footprint of 350,000
sq ft.

### Production Capacity

- Annual nameplate capacity: 100 MWh (approximately 15-20 million EX1-6M
  equivalent cells per year)
- Number of production lines: 8 lines, each mirroring the Fab-1 Gen2 design
- Line layout: 4 parallel pairs with shared formation and aging stations
  to maximize equipment utilization

### Construction Timeline

| Phase | Timeline | Milestone |
|---|---|---|
| Site preparation | Q3 2024 - Q1 2025 | Earthworks and foundation |
| Building construction | Q1 2025 - Q3 2025 | Shell completion |
| Cleanroom fit-out | Q3 2025 - Q1 2026 | ISO certification |

| Process qualification | Q3 2026 - Q4 2026 | First articles |
| Production ramp | Q1 2027 - Q4 2027 | Target: 50% utilization |

### Capital Expenditure

Total estimated CapEx for Fab-2: $180-220 million, broken down as:

- Building and facilities: $45-55M
- Cleanroom and utilities: $35-45M
- Manufacturing equipment: $80-95M (8 lines × ~$10-12M per line)
- Automation and MES (Manufacturing Execution System): $10-15M
- Contingency: $10M

Funding: Enovix has earmarked approximately $150M of its $350M+ total raised
capital for Fab-2, with the balance expected from:
- Customer prepayments or NRE (non-recurring engineering) agreements
- Equipment lease financing (sale-leaseback of manufacturing tools)
- Potential Malaysian government co-investment through MIDA programs

## Manufacturing Innovations for Fab-2

### Lessons Applied from Fab-1

Fab-2 incorporates several design improvements based on Fab-1 operational
experience:

1. **In-line metrology at every station:** Fab-1 relied on off-line QC
   sampling; Fab-2 integrates automated optical inspection (AOI) and
   electrical testing at each process step, enabling real-time SPC
   (statistical process control) that should reduce scrap rate from 12%
   to <5%.

2. **Automated material handling:** Fab-1 uses manual trolley transport
   between stations. Fab-2 deploys an overhead automated material handling
   system (AMHS) with cleanroom-compatible robots, reducing labor cost by
   ~30% and eliminating human-induced contamination and handling damage.

3. **Centralized electrolyte distribution:** A bulk electrolyte mixing and
   distribution system (vs. per-line mixing at Fab-1) improves formulation
   consistency and reduces electrolyte cost by ~20% through bulk purchasing.

4. **Formation energy recovery:** Bi-directional formation cycling equipment
   recovers discharge energy back to the facility grid, reducing net energy
   consumption by ~25% — a significant cost at 100 MWh/year scale.

### Yield and Cost Targets

| Metric | Fab-1 (Q4 2025) | Fab-2 Target (2027) |
|---|---|---|
| First-pass yield | 78% | 92% |
| OEE | 72% | 85% |
| Capacity CpK | 0.95 | 1.33 |
| Direct labor per cell | $0.65 | $0.35 |
| COGS per cell | $6.00 | $4.50 |

At $4.50/cell COGS and $8-10 ASP, gross margins approach 45-55% —
competitive with established battery manufacturers at comparable scale.

## Capacity Phasing Strategy

Enovix intends to phase Fab-2's 8 lines in three tranches:

- **Tranche 1 (Q1-Q2 2027):** 2 lines (25 MWh) — serve initial design wins
  from smartphone OEMs; de-risk process before broader rollout

- **Tranche 2 (Q3-Q4 2027):** 3 lines (37.5 MWh) — activated as additional
  customer purchase orders are received; aligns capacity with demand to
  avoid under-utilization

- **Tranche 3 (2028):** 3 lines (37.5 MWh) — capacity for consumer
  electronics diversification (laptops, wearables) and/or early automotive
  sample production

This phased approach conserves capital and reduces the risk of building
capacity ahead of demand — a common pitfall in battery manufacturing
scale-ups.
