# F5-01: FY2025 Financial Overview — Revenue, Costs & Cash Position

## Executive Summary

Fiscal Year 2025 marked Enovix's transition from a pre-revenue R&D-stage company
to a commercial entity with initial product shipments. While revenue remains
nominal (~$25M), the year was defined by operational milestones: Fab-1 first
production, first OEM shipments, and capital positioning for the Fab-2 scale-up.

## Revenue Decomposition

### Sample and NRE Revenue (~$15M, 60%)

Payments from OEMs for evaluation samples and non-recurring engineering (NRE)
to customize EX1-6M cells for specific phone designs. NRE payments typically
range from $2-5M per OEM and are recognized over the development period. These
are strategically important not for their dollar value but because they
indicate OEM commitment — an OEM that pays for NRE is serious about adoption.

### Product Revenue (~$8M, 32%)

Revenue from first commercial cell shipments beginning in Q3 2025. Volume was
modest (~2.5M units for the year) but validated the production line and
generated initial customer feedback.

### Defense Contracts (~$2M, 8%)

Grinergy / US Army contract revenue recognized in FY2025.

## Cost Structure Analysis

### Why COGS > Revenue

This is characteristic of any manufacturing startup during the initial ramp:

1. **Fixed cost absorption:** Fab-1's fixed costs (depreciation, facilities,
   indirect labor) are ~$30M/year regardless of volume. At 2.5M units, this
   represents ~$12/unit of fixed cost — more than the ASP. At 11.5M units,
   fixed cost drops to ~$2.60/unit.

2. **Low yield:** At 78% grade-A yield in Q4, 22% of produced cells are
   scrapped or downgraded, creating waste that inflates per-unit cost.

3. **Learning curve labor:** Direct labor productivity improves ~15-20% per
   doubling of cumulative volume (Wright's Law). Fab-1 is still in the steep
   portion of this curve.

4. **Material costs:** Low-volume purchasing results in 20-30% price premium
   vs. high-volume contracts. As Fab-2 volume kicks in, material costs should
   decline 15-25%.

### Path to Gross Profitability

Enovix management has guided to gross margin breakeven at approximately
50-60% of Fab-1's nameplate capacity (~5.7-6.9M units/year), which implies:

- Revenue: ~$50-60M at $8-10 ASP
- COGS: ~$50-55M (fixed cost $30M + variable cost ~$3.50/unit × 6M units)
- Timeline: H2 2026, assuming continued yield improvement and the first
  commercial design wins converting to volume orders

## Cash Runway Analysis

### Burn Rate

FY2025 operating cash burn was approximately $100-110M (net loss minus
non-cash depreciation/stock comp plus working capital investments).
Monthly burn: ~$8-9M.

### Runway Calculation

At $230M cash and ~$9M/month burn, Enovix has approximately 25-26 months
of runway without additional fundraising — sufficient through late 2027.

### Key Sensitivities

- **Revenue acceleration:** If design wins convert faster and revenue reaches
  $80-100M in 2026 (vs. $50-60M base case), cash burn drops materially and
  runway extends to 2029+.
- **Fab-2 CapEx timing:** The $180-220M Fab-2 build is the largest cash
  drain. Enovix has indicated it will phase this investment and may pursue
  equipment lease financing to reduce upfront cash outlay.
- **Additional capital raise:** Management has signaled openness to an equity
  or convertible raise in 2026-2027, particularly if the stock price recovers
  from current levels (ENVX traded ~$10-15 in 2025, down from a peak of ~$35
  in 2021).

## Balance Sheet Strength

With $215M net cash, minimal debt, and no take-or-pay supply agreements,
Enovix has one of the stronger balance sheets among pre-profit battery
startups. This provides negotiating leverage with OEMs (no desperation pricing)
and the ability to weather a slower-than-expected commercial ramp.
