# 08 — Plasma Gun & Jet Formation

## Classification: Proprietary — ACS Internal Use Only

---

## 1. System Role

The plasma guns are the primary electromechanical interface between the stored
electrical energy (capacitor banks) and the magnetized plasma that enters the
peristaltic nozzle. Each gun must perform three functions simultaneously:

1. **Ionize** the injected neutral gas into a fully ionized plasma
2. **Accelerate** the plasma to the required injection velocity (50–200 km/s)
3. **Embed** a controlled magnetic helicity signature (poloidal + toroidal field
   components) in the ejected jet

These functions must be achieved with high shot-to-shot reproducibility and with
a jitter of less than 1 microsecond between guns in a volley.

---

## 2. Coaxial Gun Design

Each gun is a coaxial (Marshall-type) plasma accelerator, a design with over five
decades of heritage in laboratory plasma physics and fusion research.

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| Inner electrode diameter | 2–5 cm |
| Outer electrode diameter | 5–15 cm |
| Electrode length | 10–30 cm |
| Electrode material | Tungsten or molybdenum (erosion-resistant) |
| Insulator | Alumina or boron nitride |
| Discharge current | 50–500 kA (peak) |
| Discharge voltage | 5–20 kV |
| Pulse width | 5–20 μs |
| Gas injection | Piezoelectric fast valve, puff duration ~100 μs |

The gun operates in the "snowplow" regime: the discharge current sheet forms behind
the gas puff, and the J × B force accelerates the plasma layer down the coaxial
barrel. As the current sheet sweeps through the neutral gas, it ionizes and entrains
additional mass, reaching full ionization before exiting the muzzle.

---

## 3. Helicity Embedding

The magnetic helicity carried by each jet is determined by the gun's internal
magnetic field configuration:

- **Poloidal field (B_p)**: generated by the axial discharge current flowing
  through the central electrode and returning through the plasma
- **Toroidal field (B_θ)**: generated by an external solenoid wrapped around the
  gun barrel, carrying a bias current before the main discharge

The ratio B_θ / B_p at the gun muzzle determines the twist of the ejected flux
tube, which in turn dictates the braiding pattern when multiple jets merge in
the nozzle inlet.

---

## 4. Jet Injection Geometry

The four guns are positioned with their muzzles at the nozzle inlet plane, aimed
along converging trajectories (typically 5°–15° off the nozzle axis). The
convergence angle is a design parameter that trades off:

| Parameter | Effect of Increased Convergence Angle |
|---|---|
| Merging speed | Faster merging, earlier plectonemic braiding |
| Turbulent mixing | Higher turbulence at the merge point |
| Axial velocity component | Reduced (cosine loss) |
| Helicity linking | Stronger mutual flux linkage |

The operational convergence angle for ECLAIR is set at approximately 10° based on
LA-COMPASS simulation results that optimize the trade between fast plectonemic
formation and minimal cosine thrust loss.

---

## 5. Timing Synchronization

Multi-gun synchronization is the critical engineering challenge for the plasma gun
array. Each gun's discharge is triggered by a dedicated spark-gap or thyratron
switch, driven by an FPGA-based timing controller. The timing requirements are:

- **Intra-volley jitter**: ≤ 1 μs between any two guns in the same volley
- **Pulse-to-pulse jitter** (for repetition-rated operation): ≤ 5 μs
- **Fuel valve timing**: must precede the discharge by 50–150 μs to allow gas
  to fill the gun breech

Jitter exceeding the specification degrades plectonemic formation by misaligning
the merging flux tubes in time.

---

## 6. Gun Array Scalability

The modular gun architecture is central to the Helicity Drive's scalability.
Key scaling considerations:

- **Electrical**: each gun has its own capacitor bank and trigger circuit. Adding
  guns increases total pulsed-power requirements linearly, not superlinearly.
- **Mechanical**: guns are mounted on a common flange at the nozzle inlet. The
  flange diameter scales as √N for N guns arranged circumferentially.
- **Magnetic**: the nozzle field at the inlet must be strong enough to guide N
  jets simultaneously, setting a minimum coil current that scales weakly with N.

The analogy to reciprocating engine cylinders is apt: adding cylinders increases
total displacement (thrust) without changing the fundamental per-cylinder
thermodynamics (plasma dynamics per gun).

---

## 7. Erosion and Lifetime

Electrode erosion is the primary lifetime-limiting mechanism for the plasma guns.
For the ECLAIR single-shot experiment, erosion is negligible. For a repetition-
rated flight engine operating at 10–100 Hz over thousands of hours, electrode
lifetime becomes a design constraint. Mitigation strategies include:

- **Refractory electrode materials** (tungsten, molybdenum)
- **Pre-ionization** to distribute the discharge footpoint and reduce local
  current density
- **Gas buffering** with a sacrificial gas layer near the electrode surface
- **Modular replacement**: guns are designed as line-replaceable units
