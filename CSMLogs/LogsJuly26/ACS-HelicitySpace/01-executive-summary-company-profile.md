## Executive Summary

Helicity Space Corporation represents a paradigm shift in space propulsion technology. The
company is developing the Helicity Drive, a magneto-inertial fusion propulsion system that
directly addresses the fundamental limitation constraining deep-space exploration: the
exhaust velocity ceiling of chemical rockets. By leveraging magnetized plasma compression
combined with magnetic reconnection heating, the Helicity Drive projects specific impulse
ranging from 3,000 to 30,000 seconds while generating usable thrust at low net fusion gains
— a decisive advantage over approaches that require a self-sustained terrestrial reactor.

The core value proposition is temporal: reducing Mars transit from months to weeks renders
crewed interplanetary missions logistically viable under existing biomedical constraints.
The technology operates across a power span from 100 kW to 1 GW, scaling by adding
identical plasma gun modules analogous to adding cylinders in a reciprocating engine.

## Intellectual Property Position

Helicity Space is building a defensive patent portfolio around three interacting plasma
physics mechanisms:

1. **Peristaltic magnetic compression** — passive coil geometry producing three-dimensional
   convergent compression at constant magnetic field energy
2. **Plectonemic magnetic confinement** — self-organized, wall-free plasma containment via
   braided magnetic flux tubes
3. **Magnetic reconnection heating** — direct ion energization through topological
   rearrangement of magnetic field lines

These three mechanisms constitute the Helicity Drive architecture. No single mechanism is
unprecedented in isolation; the company's intellectual priority lies in the controlled
co-temporal application of all three within a single propulsive device.

## Strategic Positioning

Helicity Space occupies a unique position at the intersection of three converging trends:
(1) renewed governmental appetite for fusion propulsion through NASA NIAC and DOE INFUSE,
(2) declining cost of high-field magnets and pulsed-power electronics driven by the
terrestrial fusion industry, and (3) expanding commercial interest in cislunar and
interplanetary logistics. The company is not waiting for terrestrial breakeven; its
technology produces useful propulsive output well before self-sustained fusion.
