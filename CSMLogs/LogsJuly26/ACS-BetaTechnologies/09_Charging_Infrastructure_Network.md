# Charging Infrastructure Network

## Network Overview

Beta Technologies has designed, developed, and deployed the largest purpose-built electric
aircraft charging network in the United States. The network currently comprises 123 planned
charging sites, of which 61 are active and operational as of mid-2026, spanning 26 U.S. states.
This infrastructure is a critical enabler of Beta's aircraft operations and a strategic moat
against competitors who must either build their own networks or rely on partner infrastructure.

## Technical Specifications

### Charger Design
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Maximum Power Output | 320 kW |
| Voltage Architecture | 400–800V DC (configurable) |
| Connector Standard | Combined Charging System (CCS) with aviation-specific modifications |
| Full Recharge Time (CX300) | 50 minutes (typical mission depletion) |
| Opportunity Charge (30-min Turn) | 20–30 minutes (top-up to 80%+ SOC) |
| Grid Connection | 480V three-phase AC input |
| Communication Protocol | ISO 15118 (Plug & Charge) with aviation extensions |
| Operating Temperature | -22°F to 122°F (-30°C to 50°C) |
| Weather Rating | NEMA 4X / IP65 outdoor rated |

### Site Architecture
Each charging site includes:
- One or more 320 kW DC fast charger units
- Aircraft parking pad with integrated tie-downs and ground power
- Weather-protected charger enclosure with integrated cable management
- Remote monitoring and diagnostics capability (satellite/cellular backhaul)
- Optional: battery energy storage system (BESS) for peak shaving and grid-independent operation
- Optional: solar canopy for renewable energy generation and weather protection
- Site security (fencing, lighting, camera monitoring)

## Network Deployment

### Geographic Distribution — 26 States
Beta's charging network spans 26 U.S. states, with deployment concentrated along:
- Eastern Seaboard (Northeast through Mid-Atlantic to Southeast)
- Great Lakes region
- Gulf Coast corridor
- West Coast (California, Oregon, Washington)
- Select interior states connecting coastal corridors

### Site Selection Criteria
| Criterion | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Airport Proximity | On-airport or adjacent-to-airport locations for operational access |
| Route Spacing | 100–150 nm spacing to match CX300 practical range with reserves |
| Grid Capacity | Adequate three-phase power availability at site |
| Customer Demand | Existing or projected customer operating bases |
| eIPP Route Alignment | Coverage of designated eIPP corridors |
| Weather Resilience | Sites selected for year-round operational viability |

## Operational Model

### Ownership and Operation
Beta Technologies owns and operates the charging network directly. Sites are located on
airports under long-term lease, license, or easement agreements. Beta provides:
- 24/7 remote monitoring and diagnostics
- Preventive and corrective maintenance
- Customer support and troubleshooting
- Network management software (scheduling, billing, utilization tracking)

### Customer Access
- Beta fleet customers access the network as part of aircraft purchase/support agreements
- Network interoperability with other electric aircraft (non-Beta) under evaluation
- Potential future pay-per-use model for third-party aircraft operators
- Charger reservation and scheduling through Beta's operations portal

## Strategic Importance

### Competitive Moat
The charging network represents a significant barrier to entry for competitors:
- **Capital Investment:** $50M+ invested in site development, equipment, and deployment
- **Time to Replicate:** 3–5 years minimum to permit, construct, and commission 100+ sites
- **Site Exclusivity:** Long-term airport agreements limit competitor access to prime locations
- **Operational Experience:** 61 active sites generating real-world data on utilization,
  reliability, and maintenance requirements
- **Customer Lock-In:** Operators who base their fleets around Beta's charging network face
  switching costs if they consider alternative aircraft

### Certification and Operational Integration
The charging network is an integral part of Beta's certification and operational ecosystem:
- Flight test operations use network chargers, validating real-world performance
- eIPP operations demonstrate networked charging in commercial service
- Reliability data from 61 active sites supports certification credit for operational suitability
- Customer pilot training includes charging procedures and network familiarization
