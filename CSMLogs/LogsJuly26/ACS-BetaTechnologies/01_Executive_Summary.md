# Beta Technologies — Executive Summary

## Company Identity
- **Full Name:** Beta Technologies, Inc.
- **Ticker:** NYSE: BETA
- **CEO & Founder:** Kyle Clark
- **Headquarters:** South Burlington, Vermont, United States
- **Founded:** 2017
- **IPO:** Late 2025
- **Industry Recognition:** #1 TIME World's Top GreenTech Companies 2025

## Mission
Beta Technologies designs, certifies, and manufactures electric aircraft and electric propulsion
systems for commercial, cargo, military, and medical logistics applications. The company pursues
a vertically integrated strategy spanning aircraft manufacturing, electric engine production,
charging infrastructure deployment, and pilot/maintenance training.

## Strategic Framework
The company operates across five integrated domains:

1. **ALIA Aircraft Portfolio** — Two electric aircraft platforms (CTOL CX300 and VTOL A250)
   targeting distinct mission profiles under a common type design philosophy.
2. **Electric Propulsion Systems** — Proprietary H500A electric engine plus strategic
   partnerships with GE Aerospace (hybrid turbogenerator) and Eve Air Mobility ($1B motor supply).
3. **Charging Infrastructure** — 123 charging sites (61 active) delivering 320 kW with
   50-minute recharge, deployed across 26 states.
4. **Military & Defense** — MV250 military drone accelerated development, General Dynamics
   undersea propulsion collaboration, and dual-use technology applications.
5. **Certification & Manufacturing** — FAA AC 21.17-4 accelerated pathway, Part 23 certification
   for CX300 targeted H2 2027, 180,000 sq ft production center with two lines.

## Competitive Position
Beta Technologies holds the most eIPP program participations (7 of 8) of any electric aviation
developer, operates the largest purpose-built electric aircraft charging network in the United
States, and has secured a $3.9 billion aircraft order backlog — all pre-revenue certification.
The company's dual CTOL/VTOL platform strategy, combined with military diversification and a
proprietary propulsion ecosystem, positions it uniquely across the civil, defense, and logistics
value chains.

## Risk Factors
- Pre-revenue stage with significant net losses as certification progresses
- Dependence on FAA certification timelines and regulatory frameworks
- Capital-intensive manufacturing scale-up (20 to 80+ aircraft annually)
- Emerging competition from both established aerospace OEMs and electric aviation startups
- Supply chain concentration risk for battery cells, power electronics, and composite materials
- First-mover execution risk in a nascent market with unproven long-term demand elasticity
