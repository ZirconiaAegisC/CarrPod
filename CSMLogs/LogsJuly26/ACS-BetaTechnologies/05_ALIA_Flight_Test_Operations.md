# ALIA Flight Test Operations

## Overview

Beta Technologies' flight test program represents one of the most extensive electric aircraft
test campaigns in the world. The company has accumulated over 135,000 nautical miles of
cumulative flight experience across its ALIA platform prototypes — a data set that directly
supports certification credit, operational validation, and continuous design iteration.

## Flight Test Campaign Phases

### Phase 1: Initial Airworthiness (2019–2021)
- First flight milestones for ALIA prototypes
- Ground vibration testing
- Initial hover, transition, and wing-borne flight for VTOL variants
- Aerodynamic model validation and correlation

### Phase 2: Envelope Expansion (2021–2023)
- Full flight envelope clearance
- Performance data collection (climb, cruise, descent, endurance)
- Energy management characterization (battery consumption modeling)
- Avionics and fly-by-wire integration testing
- Cross-country deployment capability demonstrations

### Phase 3: Certification Flight Testing (2024–2027)
- FAA conformity inspections
- Type Inspection Authorization (TIA) flight tests
- Systems certification testing (propulsion, electrical, avionics, structures)
- Flight loads survey and structural demonstration
- Function and reliability (F&R) testing
- Cold-weather and hot-weather environmental testing

### Phase 4: Production Validation (2026–2027)
- Production-conforming aircraft flight testing
- Customer demonstration flights
- eIPP operational validation flights
- Pilot training curriculum validation

## Safety Record

Beta Technologies has maintained a strong safety record throughout its flight test program,
with no hull losses or serious injuries. The company attributes this to:
- Conservative envelope expansion methodology
- Extensive hardware-in-the-loop (HITL) and pilot-in-the-loop (PITL) simulation
- Redundant flight-critical systems on all test articles
- Chase aircraft and telemetry monitoring for envelope expansion flights
- Rapid prototyping and iteration culture that allows design fixes before repeating tests
