# ALIA CX300 — CTOL Electric Aircraft Specifications

## Platform Overview

The ALIA CX300 is Beta Technologies' conventional takeoff and landing (CTOL) electric aircraft
designed for regional passenger, cargo, and medical logistics missions. It forms the foundation
of Beta's certification strategy, pursuing FAA Part 23 certification as the company's first
type-certificated production aircraft.

The CX300 configuration was selected as the lead certification program to establish Beta's
certification basis with the FAA before pursuing the more complex VTOL variant certification.

## Design Philosophy

The ALIA platform is a blended-wing-body design with a distinctive arched wing form that
optimizes aerodynamic efficiency while providing lift across the full flight envelope.
The aircraft uses distributed electric propulsion with multiple motors integrated into
the airframe, eliminating the mechanical complexity of conventional turbine or piston
powertrains.

### Key Design Attributes
- Blended-wing-body architecture for aerodynamic efficiency and structural integration
- Distributed electric propulsion with H500A electric engine modules
- Fixed-wing conventional takeoff and landing (CTOL) configuration
- All-composite airframe for weight optimization and corrosion resistance
- Fly-by-wire flight control system with envelope protection
- Redundant power distribution and battery management architecture
- Fixed tricycle landing gear for operational simplicity

## Mission Profiles

### Regional Passenger Transport
- Hub-to-spoke regional connectivity between secondary airports
- 150–200 nm practical operating radius with IFR reserves
- Complementary to existing airline networks, not competitive with hub-to-hub routes

### Cargo and Logistics
- Express parcel and time-critical cargo delivery
- Palletized and containerized cargo options
- Off-peak airport utilization for logistics operators

### Medical Logistics (eIPP)
- Organ transplant transport (first operational mission type)
- Blood and tissue delivery between hospital networks
- Pharmaceutical cold-chain logistics
- 253 nm demonstrated organ transport route (July 2026)

### Pilot Training
- Type rating and operational familiarization for customer pilots
- Simulator and flight training integration

## Certification Pathway

| Milestone | Target Date |
|-----------|-------------|
| Certification Basis Established | 2024–2025 |
| Conformity Inspections | 2025–2026 |
| Flight Test Campaign | 2025–2027 |
| Type Inspection Authorization (TIA) | 2026–2027 |
| Part 23 Type Certificate Issuance | H2 2027 |

The CX300 certification program leverages FAA Advisory Circular AC 21.17-4, which provides
an accelerated pathway for novel aircraft types through performance-based standards and
issue paper resolution processes tailored to electric propulsion systems.
