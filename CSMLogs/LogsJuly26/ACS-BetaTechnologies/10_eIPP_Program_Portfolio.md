# eIPP Program Portfolio — Electric Infrastructure Pilot Programs

## Program Overview

The Electric Infrastructure Pilot Program (eIPP) is an FAA initiative authorized under the
FAA Reauthorization Act to accelerate the integration of electric aircraft into the National
Airspace System. The program selects qualified electric aircraft developers to conduct
operational pilot programs demonstrating electric aircraft in revenue or revenue-like
service, generating data to inform permanent regulatory frameworks.

Beta Technologies participates in **7 of 8 eIPP programs** — more than any other electric
aviation developer — reflecting the FAA's confidence in Beta's technology maturity,
operational readiness, and safety culture.

## eIPP Program Structure

Each eIPP program is defined by:
- **Geographic Corridor:** A designated route or region for electric aircraft operations
- **Mission Type:** Cargo, passenger, medical, or other defined operational profile
- **Participating Operators:** Airlines, cargo carriers, hospital networks, or logistics providers
- **Infrastructure Requirements:** Charging sites on or near the operational route
- **Data Collection:** Operational data shared with FAA to inform permanent rulemaking
- **Duration:** Typically 2–4 years, with renewal or transition to permanent operations

## Operational Data Collection

Each eIPP program collects data for FAA analysis, including:

| Data Category | Specific Metrics |
|---------------|------------------|
| Flight Operations | Flight hours, cycles, routes flown, weather conditions |
| Energy Management | Energy consumption per flight, charge cycles, battery degradation |
| Infrastructure | Charger utilization, reliability, maintenance events |
| Safety | Occurrence reports, system anomalies, pilot reports |
| Airspace Integration | ATC interactions, route efficiency, conflict events |
| Economic | Operating costs, maintenance costs, dispatch reliability |
| Community Impact | Noise measurements, public acceptance surveys, local economic data |

## Competitive Significance

Beta's leadership in eIPP participation (7 of 8 programs) creates multiple strategic advantages:

- **Regulatory Influence:** Operational data from Beta's programs will disproportionately
  shape permanent FAA regulations for electric aviation.
- **Operational Experience:** 7 concurrent operational programs generate unmatched real-world
  operating data, informing design improvements and customer support.
- **Customer Relationships:** eIPP participation embeds Beta with key launch customers who
  are likely to remain with Beta for fleet expansion.
- **Infrastructure Validation:** Charging network deployment is co-optimized with eIPP routes,
  ensuring infrastructure is built where it generates maximum operational value.
- **First-Mover Data Advantage:** No competitor can match the breadth of operational data
  Beta generates through 7 simultaneous pilot programs.

## Transition to Permanent Operations

The eIPP programs are designed as a bridge to permanent commercial operations:
- Data from pilot programs informs FAA rulemaking for permanent electric aircraft operations
- Participating operators develop operational expertise and fleet plans during pilot phase
- Infrastructure built for eIPP operations remains as permanent charging network assets
- Beta transitions pilot program customers to commercial aircraft purchase and support agreements
