# Organ Transport Mission Profile — eIPP Medical Logistics

## Mission Overview

In July 2026, Beta Technologies is scheduled to commence the first electric aircraft organ
transport flights under the eIPP program — a milestone that marks the first revenue-oriented
commercial application of electric aviation in the United States. The inaugural organ
transport mission will cover a 253-nautical-mile route, demonstrating the ALIA CX300's
capability to serve time-critical medical logistics with zero direct emissions.

## Mission Significance

Organ transport represents a compelling early use case for electric aviation due to multiple
converging factors:

- **Time Criticality:** Organ viability windows (ischemic time) demand rapid transport;
  electric aircraft can bypass road congestion, reducing door-to-door transit times
- **Predictable Routes:** Organ transport follows established hospital pairings with
  known distances, matching the CX300's 215 nm range (with charging stops for longer routes)
- **Cost Pressure:** Healthcare systems face rising organ transport costs; electric aircraft
  offer dramatically lower direct operating costs than helicopters or fixed-wing turboprops
- **Environmental Mandate:** Hospital systems have aggressive carbon reduction commitments;
  zero-emission organ transport aligns with ESG goals
- **Regulatory Support:** eIPP framework provides operational approval pathway while FAA
  develops permanent regulations

## Mission Profile — 253 nm Route

### Flight Planning
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Route Distance | 253 nautical miles |
| Aircraft | ALIA CX300 |
| Payload | Organ transport container, medical escort (1–2 persons), support equipment |
| Cruise Altitude | 6,000–10,000 ft (weather-dependent) |
| Estimated Flight Time | 1.5–2.0 hours |
| Energy Reserve | IFR alternate + 30-minute reserve per Part 135 equivalent |
| Recharge Requirement | Intermediate charge stop or pre-positioned charged aircraft |

### Ground Operations
| Phase | Duration | Activity |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Organ Recovery | — | Surgical team recovers organ at donor hospital |
| Ground Transport | 15–45 min | Ambulance/sprinter to departure airport |
| Aircraft Loading | 5–10 min | Organ container secured, medical escort onboard |
| Preflight | 10–15 min | Aircraft systems check, route clearance, weather briefing |
| Flight | 1.5–2.0 hr | Direct flight to destination |
| Ground Transport | 15–45 min | Ambulance/sprinter to recipient hospital |
| Organ Implantation | — | Surgical team begins transplantation |

### Charging Integration
For routes exceeding 215 nm (single-charge range), Beta's charging network enables:
- Pre-positioned charged aircraft at both origin and destination airports
- Opportunity charging at intermediate network sites (50-minute full charge or 20–30 minute top-up)
- Aircraft swapping: pre-charged aircraft positioned at waypoint airports for seamless relay
- Ground power for organ container thermal management during charging stops

## Medical Logistics Requirements

### Organ Container Integration
- Temperature-controlled transport container (typically 2°C–8°C for most organs)
- Aircraft power supply for container thermal management
- Shock and vibration isolation from aircraft systems
- Real-time organ condition telemetry to transplant teams
- Redundant container power (aircraft + internal battery)

### Regulatory Compliance
- UNOS (United Network for Organ Sharing) transport protocols
- FDA human cells, tissues, and cellular products (HCT/P) regulations
- FAA operational approval under eIPP framework
- State-specific organ transport regulations
- Hospital credentialing and standard operating procedures

## Scalability and Network Effects

As the organ transport mission matures, Beta expects to:
- Expand from single routes to regional organ transport networks
- Transition from demonstration flights to contracted medical logistics services
- Integrate with UNOS organ allocation systems for automated aircraft dispatch
- Develop dedicated organ transport aircraft configurations (medical interior, container systems)
- Extend organ transport to VTOL operations when A250 is certified (enabling hospital helipad landings)

## Broader Medical Logistics Applications

Beyond organ transport, the electric aircraft medical logistics market includes:
- Blood and blood product distribution between hospital networks
- Pharmaceutical cold-chain logistics (vaccines, biologics, specialty drugs)
- Laboratory sample transport (time-critical diagnostics)
- Medical equipment and supply emergency delivery
- Disaster response medical logistics (with VTOL capability in future)

## Partnership Ecosystem

Beta's organ transport operations involve a multi-stakeholder ecosystem:
- **Organ Procurement Organizations (OPOs):** Coordinating organ recovery and allocation
- **Transplant Centers:** Recipient hospital surgical teams
- **Hospital Networks:** Logistics and sustainability leadership
- **FAA eIPP Program Office:** Operational approval and data collection
- **State Health Departments:** Regulatory approval for medical transport
- **Medical Device Manufacturers:** Organ container and monitoring equipment suppliers
- **Insurance Providers:** Coverage for electric aircraft medical transport

## Strategic Value

The organ transport mission serves Beta's broader strategy by:
- Demonstrating electric aircraft in a socially valued, high-visibility application
- Generating operational data for FAA certification and permanent rulemaking
- Building hospital network relationships that extend to broader logistics partnerships
- Providing a narrative for public acceptance of electric aviation
- Creating a recurring medical logistics revenue stream ahead of passenger service
