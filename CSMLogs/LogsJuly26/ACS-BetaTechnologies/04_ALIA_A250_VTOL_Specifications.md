# ALIA A250 — VTOL Electric Aircraft Specifications

## Platform Overview

The ALIA A250 is Beta Technologies' vertical takeoff and landing (VTOL) electric aircraft,
designed to provide helicopter-like operational flexibility with significantly lower noise,
operating cost, and emissions. The A250 builds on the same ALIA platform architecture as
the CX300 but incorporates a dedicated lift system enabling vertical operations from
helipads, rooftops, and unprepared landing zones.

The A250 follows the CX300 in Beta's certification sequencing, leveraging the common type
design philosophy to accelerate regulatory approval after the Part 23 CTOL certification
baseline is established.

## VTOL Configuration

The A250 integrates a distributed electric lift system that provides vertical thrust for
takeoff, hover, and landing phases while transitioning to wing-borne flight for cruise
efficiency. The design philosophy emphasizes safety through redundancy — the distributed
propulsion architecture allows continued safe flight and landing after any single point
failure in the propulsion system.

### Key Design Attributes
- Common ALIA blended-wing-body platform with CX300
- Distributed electric lift rotors for vertical flight phases
- Pusher propeller(s) for cruise propulsion
- Tilt or independent lift/cruise architecture (Beta proprietary design)
- All-composite airframe sharing tooling with CX300 production
- Fly-by-wire with transition automation (VTOL-to-cruise and cruise-to-VTOL)
- Battery architecture optimized for VTOL energy demands

## Mission Profiles

### Urban Air Mobility
- City-center to airport transfers
- Inter-city connector flights bypassing ground congestion
- On-demand air taxi operations
- Heliport-to-heliport networks in metropolitan regions

### Emergency Medical Services (EMS)
- Scene response for trauma and critical care transport
- Hospital-to-hospital transfer at rotorcraft speeds and altitudes
- Organ transport with vertical landing at hospital helipads

### Corporate and Executive Transport
- Private point-to-point VTOL transport
- Reduced ground time compared to traditional rotorcraft
- Access to both airport and non-airport landing sites

### Offshore and Remote Operations
- Offshore platform crew change and logistics
- Remote community access where runways are unavailable
- Disaster relief and humanitarian response

## Certification Pathway

| Milestone | Target Date |
|-----------|-------------|
| G-1 Issue Paper Resolution | 2025–2027 |
| Means of Compliance Development | 2026–2028 |
| Conformity Inspections | 2027–2028 |
| Flight Test Campaign | 2027–2028 |
| Type Certificate (Special Class or Part 27) | ~2028 |

The A250 VTOL certification presents greater regulatory complexity than the CX300 due to
the combination of vertical lift and wing-borne flight regimes, as well as the lack of
existing airworthiness standards for civilian eVTOL aircraft. Beta is working with the FAA
through the issue paper and special condition processes to establish the certification basis.
