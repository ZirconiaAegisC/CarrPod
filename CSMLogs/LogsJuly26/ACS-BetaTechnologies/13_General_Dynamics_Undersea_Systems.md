# General Dynamics — Undersea Propulsion Partnership

## Collaboration Overview

Beta Technologies and General Dynamics have established a strategic partnership to adapt
Beta's electric propulsion technology for undersea applications, specifically submarine and
unmanned underwater vehicle (UUV) propulsion systems. This partnership extends Beta's
electric propulsion technology beyond aviation into the maritime defense domain, creating
a second non-aviation revenue stream alongside the Eve Air Mobility motor supply agreement.

## Strategic Rationale

### For Beta Technologies
- **Technology Diversification:** Extends H500A-derived propulsion technology into a
  large, well-funded defense market segment
- **Revenue Diversification:** Undersea propulsion contracts are typically long-duration,
  multi-year programs with stable funding visibility
- **Scale Economics:** Additional production volume for electric motors, power electronics,
  and battery systems drives down unit costs across all applications
- **Technology Transfer:** Undersea application requirements (pressure tolerance, corrosion
  resistance, acoustic quieting) may yield innovations applicable to aviation

### For General Dynamics
- **Propulsion Modernization:** Electric propulsion offers acoustic advantages critical for
  undersea warfare (reduced detectable signature compared to mechanical drive systems)
- **Commercial Technology Leverage:** Beta's commercial aviation propulsion investment
  provides mature, tested technology at lower cost than a bespoke defense development program
- **Supply Chain Resilience:** Diversifies propulsion supply chain beyond traditional
  defense-only suppliers
- **UUV Market Entry:** Commercial electric motor technology enables longer-endurance,
  lower-cost UUV propulsion than purpose-built defense systems

## Technical Applications

### Manned Submarine Propulsion
- Electric drive motors for main propulsion and maneuvering thrusters
- Power-dense motor designs for space-constrained submarine machinery spaces
- Acoustic signature reduction through inherently quieter electric drive (no gear noise,
  smoother torque delivery than diesel or turbine mechanical drive)
- Integration with submarine electrical power systems (nuclear or diesel-electric)
- High-efficiency power conversion reducing thermal rejection requirements

### Unmanned Underwater Vehicle (UUV) Propulsion
- Propulsion motors for large-displacement UUVs (LDUUV) and extra-large UUVs (XLUUV)
- Battery-based energy storage with aviation-derived high energy density cells
- Long-endurance propulsion profiles with adaptive power management
- Low acoustic signature for clandestine UUV operations
- Pressure-tolerant motor designs for deep-depth operation without heavy pressure housings

### Seabed and Infrastructure Systems
- Electric actuators and propulsion for seabed sensor networks
- Undersea charging stations for UUV energy replenishment
- Electric pumps and systems for undersea infrastructure

## Technology Adaptation

Adapting the H500A electric propulsion system for undersea applications requires:

| Adaptation | Aviation (H500A) | Undersea Variant |
|------------|------------------|------------------|
| Cooling Medium | Air + liquid loop | Seawater heat exchanger |
| Pressure Tolerance | 0–12,000 ft atmospheric | 0–1,000+ ft hydrostatic (depth-rated) |
| Corrosion Protection | Standard aviation coatings | Marine-grade materials and coatings |
| Acoustic Requirements | Community noise standards | Ultra-quiet for undersea warfare |
| Power Density | Optimized for aircraft weight | Optimized for volume (submarine) and weight (UUV) |
| Reliability | Aviation MTBF standards | Extended mission duration reliability |
| Certification | FAA Part 33 equivalent | NAVSEA / military submarine standards |

## Defense Market Context

### U.S. Navy Submarine Programs
- **Columbia-class SSBN:** Next-generation ballistic missile submarine (electric drive)
- **Virginia-class SSN:** Attack submarine with evolving propulsion requirements
- **SSN(X):** Future attack submarine design (electric propulsion likely)
- **UUV Family of Systems:** Large and extra-large UUV programs (Orca XLUUV, Snakehead LDUUV)

### International Submarine Markets
- AUKUS (Australia-UK-US) submarine cooperation presents additional electric propulsion
  requirements for future platforms
- Allied navies modernizing submarine fleets with electric drive systems
- Global UUV market expanding for ISR, mine countermeasures, and seabed warfare

## General Dynamics' Role

General Dynamics operates the following relevant business units:
- **General Dynamics Electric Boat:** Primary designer and builder of U.S. Navy nuclear
  submarines (Columbia-class, Virginia-class)
- **General Dynamics Mission Systems:** Undersea sensor, communication, and combat systems
- **General Dynamics Land Systems:** Potential crossover for ground vehicle electric propulsion

The partnership likely involves Electric Boat as the primary customer for submarine propulsion
applications and Mission Systems for UUV integration.

## Contract Structure

Specific contract values and terms are not publicly disclosed, but the partnership is
structured around:
- Joint development agreements for undersea motor variants
- Technology licensing or supply agreements for production motors and power electronics
- Cooperative research and development agreements (CRADAs) with Navy laboratories
- Potential for classified program participation

Beta's advantage lies in its commercial aviation-derived technology — the H500A's 85,000+

competitors cannot match without equivalent investment.

## Strategic Significance

The General Dynamics partnership serves Beta's strategic objectives by:
- Establishing Beta as a defense propulsion supplier beyond aviation (naval domain)
- Creating stable, long-cycle defense revenue to offset commercial aviation market volatility
- Providing a second validation point (after Eve Air Mobility) for Beta's propulsion as a
  standalone business
- Building relationships with the U.S. Navy acquisition community, complementing USAF and
  Army relationships through the MV250 program
- Accessing classified defense technology needs that may drive next-generation propulsion
  development applicable to future aviation products
