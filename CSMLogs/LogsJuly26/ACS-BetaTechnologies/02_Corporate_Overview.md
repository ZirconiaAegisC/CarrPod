# Beta Technologies — Corporate Overview

## Founding and History

Beta Technologies was founded in 2017 by Kyle Clark, a former professional ice hockey player
turned aerospace entrepreneur. The company was conceived with a singular thesis: that electric
propulsion would transform aviation by enabling quieter, lower-cost, zero-emission aircraft
for regional transport, cargo logistics, and military applications.

The company chose South Burlington, Vermont as its headquarters — deliberately outside
traditional aerospace clusters — to build a vertically integrated engineering culture from
first principles. Early funding came through private investment rounds, with the company
maintaining a deliberately low public profile during its first seven years of R&D.

In late 2025, Beta Technologies completed its initial public offering on the New York Stock
Exchange under the ticker symbol BETA, marking the transition from private development-stage
enterprise to publicly traded electric aviation manufacturer.

## Leadership

### Kyle Clark — Chief Executive Officer & Founder
Kyle Clark leads the company's strategic vision, product development, and corporate direction.
His engineering background and unconventional path from professional athletics to aerospace
have shaped Beta's culture of applied physics rigor combined with pragmatic execution.
Clark is a licensed pilot and has personally flown Beta's prototypes, embodying the company's
test-pilot engineering ethos.

### Key Leadership Functions
- **Engineering & Design:** Integrated airframe, propulsion, avionics, and systems engineering
- **Manufacturing & Supply Chain:** Vertically integrated production, supplier partnerships
- **Certification & Regulatory Affairs:** FAA liaison, Part 23/Part 27 certification programs
- **Commercial Operations:** eIPP deployment, charging network buildout, customer delivery
- **Defense Programs:** Military variant development, DoD contracting, dual-use applications
- **Finance & Strategy:** Capital allocation, investor relations, market development
- **Corporate Development:** Partnerships (GE Aerospace, Eve Air Mobility, General Dynamics)

## Corporate Structure and Facilities

### Primary Campus — South Burlington, Vermont
- **Production Center:** 180,000 square foot manufacturing facility
- **Production Lines:** Two operational lines with capacity for 20 aircraft in 2026, scaling to
  80 in 2027
- **Engineering Center:** Design, analysis, integration, and test laboratories
- **Flight Test Operations:** On-site flight test team with dedicated test infrastructure
- **Battery Development:** In-house battery pack design, testing, and production
- **Propulsion Manufacturing:** H500A electric engine production line

### Operational Footprint — 26 States
Beta maintains operational presence across 26 U.S. states through its charging network
deployment, eIPP program participation, customer demonstration flights, and military
testing partnerships.

## Industry Recognition

In 2025, TIME magazine named Beta Technologies the **#1 World's Top GreenTech Company**,
recognizing its leadership in electric aviation, charging infrastructure deployment, and
integrated approach to decarbonizing regional air transport. This recognition preceded the
company's IPO and validated the market's assessment of Beta as a category-defining enterprise
in the sustainable aviation sector.

## Strategic Vision

Beta Technologies' long-term strategy is organized around three horizons:

1. **Near-Term (2026–2028):** Achieve FAA certification for H500A engine and ALIA CX300
   CTOL, deliver initial production aircraft, complete eIPP organ transport operations,
   and expand charging network to full operational capacity.

2. **Mid-Term (2028–2031):** Certify ALIA A250 VTOL, scale production to hundreds of
   aircraft annually, expand military programs, and achieve operating profitability through
   aircraft sales, service contracts, and charging network revenue.

3. **Long-Term (2031+):** Establish Beta as the dominant electric aviation platform across
   North America, expand internationally through regulatory harmonization, and leverage
   propulsion technology into adjacent markets (marine, heavy ground transport, grid storage).

## ESG and Sustainability Commitment

Beta Technologies' entire business model is predicated on environmental sustainability:
- Zero direct operating emissions for electric aircraft
- Noise footprint reductions of 90%+ compared to conventional rotorcraft
- Charging infrastructure powered by renewable grid integration
- Organ transport missions reducing road-based logistics emissions
- Battery lifecycle management and second-life applications under development
