# MV250 Military Drone Program

## Program Overview

The MV250 is Beta Technologies' military unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) program — an accelerated
development effort to adapt the ALIA platform for defense applications including intelligence,
surveillance, reconnaissance (ISR), cargo resupply, and contested logistics. The MV250 leverages
the core ALIA airframe, propulsion system, and avionics architecture while adding military-specific
capabilities and operational flexibility.

The program is being accelerated in response to demonstrated DoD interest in electric aviation
for logistics and ISR missions, where the low acoustic signature, reduced thermal footprint,
and lower operating costs of electric propulsion provide distinct tactical advantages.

## Mission Profiles

### Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR)
- Persistent surveillance with low acoustic and thermal signature
- Electro-optical/infrared (EO/IR) sensor payloads
- Signals intelligence (SIGINT) and electronic warfare (EW) payloads
- Long-endurance orbits at medium altitude with reduced detectability
- Optionally manned or fully autonomous operation
- Maritime domain awareness and border security applications

### Contested Logistics
- Resupply of forward operating bases in contested environments
- Autonomous cargo delivery to austere landing zones
- Medical evacuation (MEDEVAC) with lower signature than conventional rotorcraft
- Ship-to-shore logistics for naval operations
- Distributed operations supporting agile combat employment (ACE) concepts

### Training and Target Operations
- Adversary air training (low-cost threat representation)
- Target drone applications for weapons system testing
- Swarm operations research and development

## Technical Configuration

### Airframe
- Common ALIA blended-wing-body platform with structural modifications for military payloads
- Hardpoints for external stores (sensors, pods, cargo containers)
- Reinforced landing gear for unprepared surface operations
- Reduced radar cross-section features (not stealth, but lower observability than conventional)
- Modular payload bay for rapid mission reconfiguration

### Propulsion
- H500A electric propulsion system (common with CX300/A250)
- Enhanced thermal management for sustained high-power military profiles
- Option for hybrid-electric range extension (GE Aerospace turbogenerator integration)
- Battery system optimized for military duty cycles (high-power bursts, loiter)

### Avionics and Autonomy
- Military-grade navigation (GPS/INS with anti-jam capability)
- Beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS) autonomous operation
- Detect and avoid (DAA) system for airspace integration
- Secure command and control (C2) data link with encryption
- NATO Standardization Agreement (STANAG) compliance for allied interoperability
- Open architecture for third-party mission system integration

## Acceleration Factors

The MV250 program is being accelerated due to several converging factors:

1. **DoD Demand Signal:** The U.S. Department of Defense has explicitly identified electric
   aviation for logistics and ISR as a priority capability area under the Replicator and
   other rapid acquisition initiatives.
2. **Commercial Platform Maturity:** The ALIA CX300's advanced flight test status provides
   a mature baseline for military adaptation, reducing development risk and timeline.
3. **Low Signature Advantage:** Electric propulsion provides passive signature reduction
   (acoustic and thermal) that is tactically valuable and difficult to replicate with
   conventional propulsion.
4. **Operating Cost:** Electric aircraft offer dramatically lower cost per flight hour than
   conventional military ISR and logistics platforms, enabling higher utilization rates.
5. **Congressional Support:** Defense appropriations include funding for electric aviation
   technology development and procurement.

### Differentiation
The MV250's primary differentiation from incumbent platforms is the combination of:
- Very low acoustic and thermal signature (electric propulsion)
- Significantly lower operating cost per flight hour
- Dual-use platform (commercial + military production lowers unit cost)
- Optionally manned capability (flexibility between autonomous and piloted operations)
- Rapid production scalability using commercial manufacturing lines

## Defense Contracting Strategy

Beta Technologies is pursuing multiple pathways to defense revenue:
- Direct U.S. Air Force, Army, and Navy procurement of MV250 aircraft
- Defense Innovation Unit (DIU) and AFWERX rapid prototyping contracts
- Other Transaction Authority (OTA) agreements for accelerated acquisition
- Foreign Military Sales (FMS) to allied nations
- Dual-use technology licensing and cooperative research and development agreements (CRADAs)

## International Market Potential

The MV250's characteristics make it attractive for allied and partner nation militaries:
- Lower acquisition cost than legacy ISR platforms
- Reduced training and logistics burden (electric powertrain simplicity)
- Compatibility with civil airspace (FAA certification basis)
- Exportable technology (no ITAR-sensitive turbine engine technology for baseline configuration)
- NATO/coalition interoperability through STANAG-compliant systems
