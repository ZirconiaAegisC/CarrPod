# Eve Air Mobility — Strategic Motor Supply Partnership

## Partnership Overview

Beta Technologies and Eve Air Mobility (a subsidiary of Embraer S.A.) have entered into a
10-year strategic supply agreement valued at approximately $1 billion. Under this agreement,
Beta will supply H500A-based electric motors and related propulsion components for Eve's
eVTOL aircraft program, creating a recurring propulsion revenue stream for Beta that is
independent of its own aircraft delivery schedule.

## Strategic Significance

### Validation of Beta's Propulsion Technology
The Eve contract represents the first major third-party validation of Beta's electric
propulsion technology. A $1 billion commitment from an established aerospace OEM (Embraer)
signals confidence that:
- Beta's H500A engine meets or exceeds industry requirements for aviation electric propulsion
- The H500A certification pathway is credible and transferable to other airframe applications
- Beta can scale propulsion production to support both internal and external demand
- Electric propulsion is maturing from prototype to production-quality articles

### Dual Revenue Model
The partnership establishes Beta Technologies as both:
1. **Airframe OEM:** Revenue from ALIA CX300 and A250 aircraft sales
2. **Propulsion Tier-1 Supplier:** Revenue from motor sales to third-party airframe OEMs

This dual-revenue model diversifies Beta's income streams, reduces dependence on its own
certification timeline, and creates recurring aftermarket revenue (spares, overhauls,
technical support) over the life of each supplied motor.

## Production and Delivery

### Production Allocation
Beta must balance production capacity between:
- Internal demand (CX300 and A250 propulsion systems)
- External demand (Eve Air Mobility motor supply)
- Spare motor inventory for customer support

The 180,000 sq ft production center is designed with scalable production lines, and the
Eve contract likely triggered capital investment in dedicated motor production capacity.

### Delivery Schedule
Exact delivery quantities and timing are not publicly disclosed, but the 10-year contract
likely includes:
- Initial low-rate production deliveries during Eve's flight test phase
- Ramp-up to production-rate deliveries coinciding with Eve's aircraft certification
- Steady-state production at hundreds of motors per year at peak
- Aftermarket spares and replacements over the contract life

## Competitive Implications

### For Beta Technologies
- **Revenue Diversification:** Propulsion revenue reduces cash burn pressure during pre-revenue
  aircraft certification years
- **Scale Economics:** Higher motor production volume drives down unit costs for both internal
  and external applications
- **Industry Standard Positioning:** Establishes H500A as a potential industry-standard
  propulsion solution, creating path to additional third-party motor supply contracts
- **Certification Precedent:** Each H500A certification creates a reusable compliance package,
  reducing incremental certification cost per airframe application

### For Eve Air Mobility
- **Time-to-Market:** Leveraging Beta's certified motor accelerates Eve's aircraft certification
  by eliminating parallel propulsion development
- **Risk Reduction:** Adopting a certified propulsion system reduces Eve's technical and
  certification risk
- **Cost Predictability:** Fixed-price supply agreement provides cost certainty for a major
  aircraft system

### For the Electric Aviation Industry
- Establishes a supplier-OEM model analogous to the conventional aviation engine market
  (GE/Pratt/Rolls-Royce supplying airframers)
- Validates the thesis that electric propulsion can be a standalone business, not merely an
  integrated airframe subsystem
- Creates competitive pressure for other electric propulsion developers to pursue third-party
  supply agreements

## Risk Factors

- Beta's ability to scale motor production to meet both internal and external demand
- Eve Air Mobility's own certification timeline and aircraft market success
- Potential for competing motor suppliers to emerge with certified alternatives
- Warranty and reliability claims exposure over the 10-year contract life
- Currency and inflation risk on long-duration fixed-price supply agreements
