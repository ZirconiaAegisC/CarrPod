# Defense and Dual-Use Technology Strategy

## Strategic Framework

Beta Technologies' defense and dual-use strategy is built on the principle that electric
aviation technology developed for commercial markets has inherent military value, and
that military procurement can accelerate commercial technology maturation. The strategy
spans three pillars: (1) direct military aircraft sales (MV250), (2) defense propulsion
supply (General Dynamics undersea systems), and (3) dual-use technology development
funded by DoD research and development programs.

## Pillar 1: Direct Military Aircraft — MV250

### Target Platforms
- **U.S. Air Force:** Agile Combat Employment (ACE) logistics, ISR, and adversary air training
- **U.S. Army:** Future Vertical Lift (FVL) ecosystem, contested logistics, MEDEVAC
- **U.S. Navy / Marine Corps:** Ship-to-shore logistics, maritime ISR, expeditionary operations
- **U.S. Special Operations Command (SOCOM):** Low-signature infiltration and resupply
- **Allied Nations:** Export through Foreign Military Sales (FMS) and Direct Commercial Sales (DCS)

### Value Proposition to DoD
| Attribute | Military Value |
|-----------|---------------|
| Low Acoustic Signature | Reduced detection range by adversaries; improved survivability |
| Low Thermal Signature | Reduced IR missile threat; harder to detect/track |
| Low Operating Cost | Higher sortie rates within fixed operations budgets |
| Simplified Logistics | No jet fuel supply chain; electric charging infrastructure |
| Reduced Maintenance | Electric powertrain has ~90% fewer moving parts than turbine engines |
| Dual-Use Production | Commercial production lines reduce unit cost through volume |
| Optional Manning | Autonomous, remotely piloted, or onboard pilot as mission requires |

### Acquisition Pathways
- Other Transaction Authority (OTA) agreements for rapid prototyping
- Defense Innovation Unit (DIU) commercial solutions openings
- AFWERX Agility Prime (airworthiness certification support)
- Traditional Program of Record (PoR) procurement (longer timeline)
- Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) / Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR)

## Pillar 2: Defense Propulsion Supply

### General Dynamics Undersea Partnership
- Submarine electric drive motors and power electronics
- UUV propulsion systems for large and extra-large unmanned underwater vehicles
- Seabed infrastructure electric actuation and power systems
- Technology transfer from aviation H500A to undersea variants

### Potential Additional Defense Propulsion Applications
- Ground vehicle hybrid-electric propulsion (tactical and combat vehicles)
- Shipboard auxiliary power and emergency propulsion
- Directed energy weapon power systems (high-power electrical architecture)
- Expeditionary base microgrid and energy storage systems

## Pillar 3: Dual-Use Technology Development

### DoD R&D Funding Programs
| Program | Focus | Beta Applicability |
|---------|-------|-------------------|
| DARPA | Breakthrough technology | Electric propulsion, autonomy, energy storage |
| AFRL (Air Force Research Lab) | Air Force-specific R&D | Electric flight, low-observable propulsion |
| ARL (Army Research Lab) | Army-specific R&D | Contested logistics, electric VTOL |
| ONR (Office of Naval Research) | Navy-specific R&D | Electric ship propulsion, UUV energy |
| DIU (Defense Innovation Unit) | Commercial tech transition | Accelerated acquisition of Beta technology |
| OSD R&E (Research & Engineering) | Cross-service R&D | Electric aviation standards and certification |

### Technology Areas for Dual-Use Development
- **High-Energy-Density Batteries:** Military demands for longer endurance drive battery
  development that benefits commercial range/payload
- **Autonomous Operations:** Military BVLOS and autonomous cargo delivery systems inform
  commercial autonomous aircraft certification
- **Cybersecurity:** Military-grade cyber-hardening of flight control and charging systems
  enhances commercial safety and security
- **Counter-UAS:** Electric aircraft detection and defense requirements may yield commercial
  airspace integration solutions
- **Advanced Manufacturing:** DoD manufacturing technology programs support Beta's production
  scaling with advanced processes and materials

## Strategic Benefits of Defense Diversification

### Financial
- Defense contracts provide stable, long-cycle revenue that offsets commercial aviation
  market volatility
- DoD R&D funding reduces Beta's internal R&D expenditure for dual-use technologies
- Foreign Military Sales open international markets with U.S. government support
- Production volume from defense orders improves commercial manufacturing economics

### Technical
- Defense requirements push performance boundaries (speed, endurance, payload) that benefit
  commercial products
- Military testing regimes provide additional validation data for FAA certification
- Cyber-hardening for defense applications enhances commercial platform security
- Autonomous systems developed for military use inform commercial autonomy certification

### Market
- DoD adoption serves as a powerful reference customer for commercial and international buyers
- Military operations demonstrate safety and reliability in demanding environments
- Alliance interoperability requirements (NATO) create standardized interfaces benefiting
  commercial operations
- Defense relationships provide insight into emerging technology requirements before they
  become commercial market demands

## Risk Management

### Key Risks
- **Program Dependency:** Over-reliance on specific defense programs that may be canceled
  or reduced in scope
- **Classification Constraints:** Classified technology may limit commercial applications
  or create export control complications
- **Acquisition Timeline Mismatch:** DoD acquisition cycles (5–10 years) may not align with
  Beta's commercial timeline (2–3 years)
- **Reputational Risk:** Association with weapons systems may affect brand perception in
  commercial markets
- **Resource Diversion:** Defense program demands may divert engineering and management
  attention from commercial certification priorities

### Mitigation Strategies
- Maintain defense as a complementary, not primary, revenue stream (commercial aviation
  remains core business)
- Pursue multiple defense programs to reduce single-program dependency
- Structure dual-use technology development so commercial applications retain priority
- Clearly separate defense and commercial branding and communications
- Dedicated defense business unit with separate engineering, program management, and BD
  resources from commercial operations
