# H500A Electric Propulsion System

## System Overview

The H500A is Beta Technologies' proprietary electric engine — a fully integrated propulsion
module consisting of an electric motor, power electronics, thermal management, and control
software designed specifically for aviation applications. The H500A represents the first
dedicated aviation electric engine to pursue FAA certification as a standalone propulsion
article.

## Certification Status

| Milestone | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Certification Program Initiation | 2021–2022 |
| Endurance and Durability Testing | 2022–2025 |
| FAA Certification Issuance | H1 2026 |
| Cumulative Test Hours | 85,000+ hours |

The H500A certification in H1 2026 marks a watershed moment for electric aviation — it
establishes the first FAA-approved electric propulsion article and creates a template for
future electric engine certification programs across the industry.

## Technical Architecture

### Electric Motor
- Permanent magnet synchronous motor (PMSM) architecture
- Direct-drive (no gearbox), maximizing reliability and minimizing maintenance
- Continuous power rating: optimized for 130–170 kt cruise at typical mission weights
- Peak power: available for takeoff and climb with thermal monitoring
- Liquid-cooled stator with integrated thermal management system

### Power Electronics
- Silicon carbide (SiC) MOSFET-based inverter for high efficiency and power density
- Bidirectional capability for regenerative energy capture during descent
- Integrated DC-DC conversion for avionics and accessory power
- Redundant control channels with fail-operational architecture
- Integrated fault detection, isolation, and recovery (FDIR) logic

### Thermal Management
- Liquid cooling loop integrated into airframe heat exchangers
- Active thermal monitoring with multiple sensor arrays
- Derating logic for sustained high-power or high-temperature operation
- Cold-soak preheat capability for winter operations

### Control Software
- DO-178C Design Assurance Level (DAL) A/B software architecture
- Torque-based thrust control with integrated propeller speed governing
- Health monitoring and predictive maintenance algorithms
- Flight envelope protection integration
- Battery state-of-charge and state-of-health-aware power management

## Certification Basis

The H500A certification program addresses unique challenges of aviation electric propulsion:

| Challenge | Beta Approach |
|-----------|---------------|
| No Existing Standards | Developed certification basis through FAA issue paper process |
| High-Voltage Safety | Compliance with electrical system safety standards adapted for aviation |
| Battery Integration | Battery system certified separately, H500A includes power management interfaces |
| Electromagnetic Interference | DO-160 EMI/EMC testing for avionics compatibility |
| Thermal Runaway | Integrated containment and monitoring per FAA special conditions |
| Lightning Protection | Direct and indirect effects testing per AC 20-136/AC 20-158 |

## Production and Supply Chain

The H500A is manufactured at Beta's South Burlington, Vermont facility:
- In-house motor winding, magnet assembly, and rotor balancing
- In-house power electronics assembly and testing
- Proprietary control software compiled and loaded on-site
- Acceptance test procedure (ATP) for each production unit
- Traceability from raw materials through final assembly

## Reliability and Durability

The 85,000+ hour test program includes:
- Accelerated life testing (ALT) exceeding certification requirements
- Endurance runs simulating multiple aircraft lifetimes
- Thermal cycling across extreme operational temperatures
- Vibration testing covering full flight envelope spectra
- Ingress protection (IP) testing for environmental robustness
- Fail-safe and fail-operational fault injection testing

## Competitive Position

The H500A certification positions Beta Technologies as both an airframe OEM and a propulsion
system supplier — a dual-revenue model uncommon in aviation. The Eve Air Mobility contract
($1 billion over 10 years for motor supply) validates this strategy and creates a recurring
revenue stream independent of Beta's own aircraft delivery schedule.
