# Boston Metal — Policy and Regulatory Landscape

## Overview of Steel Decarbonization Policy

The policy environment for industrial decarbonization has evolved rapidly since the Paris Agreement, with an accelerating trend toward mandatory emissions reduction requirements, carbon pricing mechanisms, and green procurement standards. Boston Metal's MOE technology is positioned to benefit from multiple policy tailwinds across its target markets.

## European Union

### EU Emissions Trading System (EU ETS)

The EU ETS, the world's largest carbon market, covers the steel sector among other industrial emitters. Key provisions affecting steel decarbonization:
- Free allowance phase-out: Free emissions allowances for steel producers are being gradually reduced, reaching zero by 2034 under the current trajectory
- Carbon price trajectory: EU ETS allowance prices have ranged from €60–100 per tonne of CO₂ in 2023–2025, with projections of €100–150 per tonne by 2030
- Innovation Fund: Revenue from EU ETS allowance auctions funds the Innovation Fund, which supports demonstration of breakthrough low-carbon technologies

At a carbon price of €100 per tonne of CO₂, the cost advantage of MOE-produced steel (approximately 1.8 tonnes of CO₂ avoided per tonne of steel) over conventional blast furnace steel would be approximately €180 per tonne — more than sufficient to offset any technology cost premium.

### Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)

The EU CBAM is a carbon tariff on imported goods that equalizes the carbon cost between domestic EU production (subject to EU ETS) and imported production. For steel:
- Transitional phase began October 2023 requiring quarterly reporting of embedded emissions
- Full implementation from 2026 with financial adjustment based on embedded emissions
- CBAM certificates priced at the weekly average EU ETS auction price
- Importers must purchase CBAM certificates corresponding to the embedded emissions of imported steel, net of any carbon price paid in the country of origin

For MOE-produced steel exported from Brazil to the EU, the low embedded emissions would result in minimal CBAM charges, providing a significant cost advantage relative to conventional steel imports from countries without equivalent carbon pricing.

### Other EU Policies

- **EU Taxonomy for Sustainable Activities**: Classifies steel production with emissions below specified thresholds as environmentally sustainable, facilitating access to green financing
- **Innovation Fund**: Has allocated billions of euros for industrial decarbonization demonstration projects
- **Important Projects of Common European Interest (IPCEI)**: Provides state aid approval for breakthrough industrial technologies
- **Steel safeguard measures**: Import quotas on certain steel products that may be adjusted to favor low-emissions imports

## United States

### Inflation Reduction Act (IRA)

The IRA, enacted in 2022, includes several provisions directly relevant to Boston Metal:
- **Section 45X — Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit**: Provides tax credits for production of eligible components, potentially including electrolytic cells for iron production
- **Section 48C — Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit**: Provides investment tax credits of up to 30% for facilities manufacturing or recycling advanced energy property
- **Section 50161 — Advanced Industrial Facilities Deployment Program**: $5.8 billion for deployment of advanced industrial technologies reducing greenhouse gas emissions
- **DOE Loan Programs Office**: Expanded authority for loans and loan guarantees for innovative clean energy technologies

### Federal Buy Clean Initiative

The Biden administration's Federal Buy Clean Task Force, established in 2021, promotes procurement of low-carbon construction materials for federally funded projects. Key developments:
- General Services Administration (GSA) and Department of Transportation have issued standards for low-embodied-carbon steel, concrete, and asphalt
- Federal procurement represents approximately $650 billion annually in goods and services
- Buy Clean standards create a significant demand-side pull for green steel

### State-Level Initiatives

- **California**: Buy Clean California Act establishes maximum acceptable global warming potential for steel used in state projects, declining over time
- **New York**: Low Embodied Carbon Concrete Leadership Act; similar steel provisions under development
- **Colorado, Oregon, Washington**: Buy Clean legislation enacted or under consideration

## Brazil

### National Climate Policy

Brazil's Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) under the Paris Agreement commits to reducing greenhouse gas emissions by 37% below 2005 levels by 2025 and 43% by 2030. The industrial sector, including steel, is identified as a key area for emissions reduction.

### Industrial Decarbonization Incentives

- **RenovaBio**: Provides a framework for carbon credit generation and trading
- **REIDI**: Special tax regime for infrastructure development that may apply to industrial decarbonization investments
- **FINEP and BNDES**: Federal innovation and development agencies providing concessional financing for technology development

### State-Level Incentives

States hosting potential MOE deployment sites offer additional incentives:
- Tax holidays, infrastructure support, and expedited permitting for strategic industrial investments
- Preferential electricity tariffs for energy-intensive industries

## Carbon Markets and Voluntary Offsets

### Compliance Carbon Markets

The expansion of compliance carbon markets globally creates a direct financial incentive for steel producers to adopt low-emissions technologies:

| Jurisdiction | Current Carbon Price | Projected 2030 Price |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| EU ETS | €60–100/tCO₂ | €100–150/tCO₂ |
| UK ETS | £35–75/tCO₂ | £70–120/tCO₂ |
| California Cap-and-Trade | $30–40/tCO₂ | $50–80/tCO₂ |
| China National ETS | ¥50–80/tCO₂ | ¥100–200/tCO₂ |
| Korea ETS | ₩10,000–20,000/tCO₂ | ₩20,000–40,000/tCO₂ |

### Voluntary Carbon Markets

Green steel premiums in voluntary markets represent an additional revenue opportunity:
- Automotive OEMs paying 20–30% premiums for green steel to meet scope 3 emissions targets
- Consumer goods companies, technology companies, and construction firms making green steel procurement commitments
- Certification standards under development (ResponsibleSteel, Climate Bonds Initiative) to verify green steel claims

## Risk Factors in the Policy Environment

1. **Policy reversal risk**: Changes in government could weaken or reverse green steel procurement commitments and climate policies, particularly in the United States
2. **CBAM implementation uncertainty**: The detailed implementation rules for CBAM, including verification methodologies and treatment of indirect emissions, are still evolving
3. **Green steel definition**: There is no universally accepted definition of "green steel," creating risk of greenwashing that could undermine premium pricing for genuinely low-emissions products
4. **Trade friction**: Steel trade is politically sensitive, and green steel preferences could be challenged as trade barriers under World Trade Organization rules
