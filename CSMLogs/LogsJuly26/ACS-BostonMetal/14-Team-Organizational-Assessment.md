# Boston Metal — Team and Organizational Assessment

## Leadership

### Tadeu Carneiro — Chief Executive Officer

Tadeu Carneiro brings over forty years of experience in the global metals and mining industry to Boston Metal. His career at CBMM (Companhia Brasileira de Metalurgia e Mineração), the world's leading producer of niobium, spanned 28 years, including service as Chief Executive Officer. Under his leadership, CBMM expanded its global market presence and deepened its technology partnerships with steel producers worldwide.

Carneiro's profile is notable for combining three critical competencies for Boston Metal's commercialization:
1. **Deep metals industry credibility**: Relationships with steel producers, mining companies, and industry associations that facilitate technology adoption
2. **Brazilian market access**: Native Portuguese speaker with extensive business network in Brazil, essential for the Brazilian demonstration plant
3. **Technology commercialization experience**: Track record of transforming materials science innovations into commercial products at scale

Carneiro also serves as Chairman of the Board of Directors of a São Paulo Stock Exchange-listed mining company, further extending his network and influence in the global mining sector.

### Professor Donald Sadoway — Co-Founder and Scientific Advisor

Professor Donald Sadoway of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology is the scientific founder of Boston Metal. His research group in the Department of Materials Science and Engineering has pioneered molten oxide electrolysis over more than two decades, generating the foundational intellectual property on which Boston Metal is built.

Sadoway is one of the world's foremost authorities on molten salt and molten oxide electrochemistry. His contributions to the field include:
- Development of the liquid metal battery (Ambri, another Sadoway spinout)
- Fundamental studies of electrode kinetics in molten salts and oxides
- Application of electrochemical engineering principles to metals extraction
- Mentorship of numerous graduate students and postdoctoral researchers who have populated the technical staff of Boston Metal and other electrochemical technology companies

Sadoway's continued involvement as Scientific Advisor provides Boston Metal with ongoing access to fundamental scientific expertise and the broader MIT electrochemical research community.

## Technical Team

Boston Metal's technical organization encompasses several hundred engineers and scientists organized into functional groups:

### Cell Engineering
Responsible for the design, modeling, and engineering of MOE cells at pilot and commercial scale. Disciplines include mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, thermal engineering, and computational modeling (finite element analysis for thermal, structural, and electromagnetic simulations).

### Electrochemistry and Materials
Responsible for anode development, electrolyte chemistry, and fundamental process characterization. Disciplines include materials science, electrochemistry, physical chemistry, and high-temperature ceramics.

### Process Development
Responsible for scaling laboratory discoveries to pilot and commercial operations. Functions include pilot plant operations, process control development, and scale-up engineering.

### Manufacturing and Supply Chain
Responsible for establishing anode manufacturing capabilities, supplier qualification, and logistics for commercial deployment.

### Project Engineering
Responsible for the design, permitting, and construction of commercial demonstration and licensed plants. Functions include civil, structural, mechanical, electrical, and instrumentation engineering, environmental permitting, and project management.

## Organizational Culture

Boston Metal's culture reflects its MIT roots:
- **Scientific rigor**: Decisions are grounded in experimental data and physical understanding
- **Mission-driven**: Team members are motivated by the climate impact potential of the technology
- **Pragmatic innovation**: Balance between fundamental research aspirations and commercial timelines
- **Cross-functional collaboration**: Electrochemists, mechanical engineers, process engineers, and business development professionals working in integrated teams

## Talent Pipeline

Boston Metal benefits from a strong talent pipeline through:
- **MIT recruitment**: Access to graduating students and postdoctoral researchers in materials science, chemical engineering, and mechanical engineering
- **Aluminum industry talent**: Electrochemical and high-temperature processing expertise from the aluminum smelting industry, which shares significant technical overlap with MOE
- **Steel industry expertise**: Metallurgists, process engineers, and operations managers from the conventional steel industry who bring operational and commercial knowledge
- **Boston/Cambridge ecosystem**: The Greater Boston area's concentration of universities, research institutions, and technology companies provides a deep talent pool

## Organizational Challenges

As Boston Metal transitions from a research and development organization to a commercial technology company, several organizational challenges emerge:

### Scaling from R&D to Commercial Operations
The skills and organizational structures optimized for R&D differ from those required for commercial technology deployment. Boston Metal must build or acquire capabilities in:
- Industrial project management and construction
- Manufacturing quality systems and supply chain management
- Customer-facing functions (licensing sales, technical support, field service)
- Regulatory compliance across multiple jurisdictions

### Geographic Dispersion
With operations in Woburn, Massachusetts (pilot plant and headquarters), a demonstration plant in Brazil, and future licensed plants globally, Boston Metal must develop the organizational infrastructure to manage distributed operations while maintaining technology quality standards and IP protection.

### Talent Competition
The broader climate technology sector competes intensely for talent with electrochemical and materials science expertise. Companies including Form Energy, Ambri, Lilac Solutions, and various battery and electrolyzer manufacturers draw from the same talent pool.

### Knowledge Management
As the organization grows, preserving and transferring the deep tacit knowledge held by early team members becomes critical. Formal documentation, training programs, and knowledge management systems must supplement the informal knowledge transfer that occurs naturally in smaller organizations.
