# ACS Group14 | SK Materials Korea Joint Venture

## 1. Partnership Overview

### Strategic Context
The joint venture between Group14 Technologies and SK Materials represents a critical pillar of Group14's international expansion strategy. SK Materials, a subsidiary of SK Group (South Korea's second-largest conglomerate after Samsung), brings capital, operational expertise in specialty materials manufacturing, and access to the Korean battery ecosystem — the world's most concentrated cluster of lithium-ion battery manufacturing.

### JV Structure
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Partners | Group14 Technologies (USA) + SK Materials (South Korea) |
| Announcement | 2021 |
| Production Location | South Korea (specific site not publicly disclosed) |
| Annual Capacity Target | 10,000 metric tons of SCC55 |
| Technology | Group14 SCC55 technology licensed to the JV |
| SK Role | Manufacturing operations, local supply chain, customer relationships |
| Group14 Role | Technology transfer, process engineering support, quality oversight |
| Governance | Joint board; specific ownership split not publicly disclosed |

### Strategic Rationale for Group14
- **Capital efficiency**: SK Materials likely provides the majority of capital for the Korean facility, reducing Group14's balance sheet burden
- **Market access**: Direct entry into the Korean battery manufacturing ecosystem without navigating Korean business relationships independently
- **Supply chain localization**: Korean cell manufacturers strongly prefer locally-sourced materials for supply chain security and just-in-time logistics
- **Risk diversification**: Geographic diversification of manufacturing beyond Moses Lake reduces single-point-of-failure risk
- **Technology validation**: Partnership with a major Korean chaebol validates SCC55 technology for the most demanding battery customers globally

### Strategic Rationale for SK Materials
- **Growth beyond semiconductor materials**: SK Materials' core business is specialty gases and materials for semiconductor manufacturing; SCC55 represents diversification into the high-growth battery materials sector
- **Vertical integration opportunity**: SK Group owns SK On (battery manufacturing); a secure silicon anode supply strengthens the group's battery value chain
- **Technology access**: Rather than developing silicon anode technology independently, SK gains immediate access to Group14's proven SCC55 platform
- **First-mover advantage**: Among Korean materials companies, SK Materials is first to establish a significant silicon anode production JV with a Western technology leader

## 2. Korean Battery Manufacturing Ecosystem

### Why Korea Matters
South Korea is home to three of the world's top six lithium-ion battery manufacturers:

| Manufacturer | 2023 Global Market Share | Major Customers |
|-------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| LG Energy Solution | ~14% | GM, Ford, Stellantis, Hyundai, Tesla |
| SK On | ~6% | Ford, Hyundai, Volkswagen |
| Samsung SDI | ~5% | BMW, Stellantis, Rivian |

Collectively, Korean manufacturers represent ~25% of global lithium-ion battery production and three of the most demanding customers for anode materials.

### Korean Cell Manufacturers' Silicon Adoption Status
- **LG Energy Solution**: Currently using SiOₓ (silicon oxide) in low percentages (<5%) in premium cells; actively evaluating silicon-carbon composites for next-generation cells
- **SK On**: Evaluating silicon anode materials through its partnership ecosystem; SK Group's ownership of both SK On and SK Materials creates internal alignment for SCC55 adoption
- **Samsung SDI**: Using SiOₓ in Gen 5 cells for BMW; developing silicon-carbon composite technology independently but open to supplier qualification

### Qualification Dynamics
Korean cell manufacturers have rigorous qualification processes:
- **Phase 1**: Material characterization (6-12 months) — physical, chemical, and electrochemical properties evaluated against specifications
- **Phase 2**: Electrode-level testing (6-12 months) — slurry mixing, coating, cell assembly at pilot scale
- **Phase 3**: Cell-level validation (12-18 months) — A/B/C-sample builds with progressively larger production-representative cells
- **Phase 4**: Production qualification (6-12 months) — qualification on production equipment, stability testing, customer approval

Total qualification timeline: 2.5-4 years from initial material submission to production adoption. This explains the long lead time between SCC55 commercial availability and volume uptake.

## 3. Manufacturing and Supply Chain Integration

### Korean Facility Design
The SK JV facility is expected to mirror the BAM-2 process flow with adaptations for Korean context:
- **Silane supply**: Korea imports silane primarily from Japan and China; SK Materials may develop domestic silane production capability to secure the JV supply chain
- **Carbon precursor supply**: Korean petrochemical industry (SK Innovation, LG Chem, Lotte Chemical) can supply polymer precursors; potential for co-location at petrochemical complexes (Ulsan, Yeosu, Daesan)
- **Power costs**: Korean industrial electricity ($0.08-0.12/kWh) is higher than Moses Lake ($0.03-0.04/kWh); process optimization for energy efficiency is more critical
- **Labor**: Highly skilled chemical process workforce but higher labor costs than Moses Lake

### Quality and Specification Alignment
Korean cell manufacturers have exacting anode material specifications:
- **Particle size**: Tighter D50 tolerance (±1 µm vs. ±2 µm for Western customers)
- **Purity**: Trace metal specifications often 2-5x more stringent than standard Western requirements
- **Moisture content**: Maximum moisture specifications of <100 ppm (Korean manufacturing operates at lower dew points)
- **Batch-to-batch consistency**: Korean OEMs demand statistical process capability (Cpk > 1.33) on all critical parameters

### Logistics and Delivery
- Just-in-time delivery expectations: 24-48 hour delivery from Korean facility to Korean cell plants
- Packaging: 500 kg supersacks or 1-ton flexible intermediate bulk containers (FIBCs)
- Moisture control: Packaging must maintain <100 ppm moisture for 6 months in Korean seasonal humidity (summer RH routinely exceeds 80%)

## 4. Competitive Dynamics in Korea

### Incumbent Graphite Suppliers
Korean cell manufacturers have long-standing relationships with graphite suppliers:
- **BTR China**: Largest anode material supplier globally; dominant in Korean market
- **Shanshan China**: Strong presence with LG Energy Solution and Samsung SDI
- **POSCO Chemical (Korea)**: Domestic Korean anode material producer; natural graphite focus
- **Mitsubishi Chemical (Japan)**: Premium synthetic graphite supplier for high-performance cells

SCC55 must displace established relationships with these suppliers. The SK Group connection (SK On + SK Materials JV) provides the most natural entry point.

### Chinese Silicon Anode Competition
Chinese anode material producers are rapidly developing silicon anode capabilities:
- **BTR**: Operating silicon anode production lines in China; expanding capacity
- **Shanshan**: Silicon anode materials in qualification with multiple cell manufacturers
- **Putailai**: Investing in silicon-carbon composite R&D and pilot production

The price competition dynamic is significant: Chinese producers target $10-15/kg for silicon anode materials at scale, pressuring Group14's $15-25/kg target.

### Geopolitical Risk
The Korean JV must navigate increasing geopolitical complexity in battery supply chains:
- **IRA requirements**: For Korean manufacturers to qualify for U.S. EV tax credits, battery materials must be sourced from countries with U.S. free trade agreements (Korea qualifies) but cannot be sourced from "foreign entities of concern" (China, Russia, North Korea, Iran)
- **Korean IRA-equivalent**: Korea's K-REACH and emerging battery supply chain policies may introduce domestic content requirements
- **Technology transfer controls**: U.S. export controls on advanced manufacturing technology could affect Group14's ability to transfer certain process IP to the Korean JV
