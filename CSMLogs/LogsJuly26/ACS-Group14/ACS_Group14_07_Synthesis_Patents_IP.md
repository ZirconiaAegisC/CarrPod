# ACS Group14 | Intellectual Property Portfolio & Patent Landscape

## 1. Group14 IP Strategy Overview

Group14's patent portfolio is a core strategic asset, protecting the carbon scaffold synthesis methodology, silicon infiltration processes, and composite particle architectures that define SCC55. As of 2024, Group14 holds over 100 granted and pending patents across multiple jurisdictions.

### Portfolio Architecture
Group14's IP strategy employs a layered defense:
- **Composition of matter patents**: Cover the SCC55 material itself — carbon scaffold with specific pore architecture hosting silicon
- **Method patents**: Protect the manufacturing processes — carbon synthesis, CVI deposition, post-processing
- **Application patents**: Cell designs incorporating SCC55, electrode formulations, electrolyte compatibility
- **Defensive publications**: Prevent competitors from patenting incremental improvements

### Key Patent Families

**Carbon Scaffold Synthesis**:
- Polymer precursor formulations for hierarchically porous carbon
- Hard/soft templating methods for bimodal pore size distribution
- Stabilization and carbonization thermal profiles
- Post-synthesis activation and surface functionalization

**Silicon Incorporation (CVI Process)**:
- Chemical vapor infiltration of silicon into porous carbon
- Silane precursor delivery and distribution systems
- Temperature/pressure/concentration control for amorphous silicon deposition
- Passivation protocols to control native oxide formation

**Composite Particle Architecture**:
- Silicon-depleted outer rind on composite particles
- Silicon domain size distribution within carbon scaffold
- Dopant incorporation (N, B, P) in carbon scaffold
- Surface coatings (Al₂O₃, TiO₂, carbon coatings) on composite particles

**Electrode and Cell Design**:
- Electrode formulations optimized for SCC55
- Binder systems for silicon-carbon composite electrodes
- Electrolyte formulations with enhanced SEI stability on SCC55
- Pre-lithiation methods specific to SCC55

## 2. Competitive Patent Landscape

### Sila Nanotechnologies
- **Core patent family**: Silicon-dominant composite with porous silicon particles in a carbon matrix
- **Key differentiator**: Higher silicon content (80-95%) than Group14; different scaffold architecture
- **Patent focus**: Silicon particle engineering rather than carbon scaffold engineering
- **Filings**: 80+ patents/applications

### Amprius Technologies
- **Core patent family**: Silicon nanowire anodes grown directly on current collector
- **Key differentiator**: No carbon scaffold — silicon nanowires accommodate expansion through one-dimensional geometry
- **Patent focus**: Nanowire growth methods (CVD on metal catalysts), current collector design
- **Filings**: 60+ patents/applications

### Enovix Corporation
- **Core patent family**: 3D cell architecture with silicon anode and integrated constraint system
- **Key differentiator**: Imposes mechanical constraint on silicon expansion via cell design, not material design
- **Patent focus**: Cell architecture, electrode stacking, mechanical constraint systems
- **Filings**: 100+ patents/applications

### OneD Battery Sciences
- **Core patent family**: Silicon nanowires grown on graphite particles (SINANODE platform)
- **Key differentiator**: Hybrid approach — nanowires on commercial graphite, not novel carbon scaffold
- **Filings**: 50+ patents/applications

## 3. Freedom-to-Operate Analysis

### White Space Opportunities
Areas where the patent landscape is relatively open:
- **Alternative silicon precursors**: Most patents specify silane (SiH₄); alternative precursors (chlorosilanes, organosilanes, silicon nanoparticles) offer white space
- **Bio-derived carbon scaffolds**: Most carbon scaffold patents specify synthetic polymers; biomass-derived porous carbon (lignin, cellulose, chitosan) is less heavily patented
- **Dry electrode processing**: Applying dry electrode coating (Maxwell/Tesla-type process) to silicon-carbon composites is relatively unexplored in patents
- **Solid-state electrolyte compatibility**: How SCC55-type materials perform with solid electrolytes is an emerging field with limited patent coverage

### Risk Areas
Potential infringement risks:
- **Template removal chemistry**: Certain silica template removal methods (HF etching, specific alkaline etches) may be covered by broader semiconductor/materials patents
- **Fluidized bed CVD**: If Group14 transitions to fluidized bed reactors for BAM-2, the fluidized bed silane deposition patent landscape (dominated by polysilicon industry — REC Silicon, Wacker, Hemlock) must be navigated
- **FEC-containing electrolytes**: FEC as an SEI additive is broadly patented (Samsung SDI, LG Chem, Panasonic); Group14's electrolyte patents must claim specific SCC55 synergy

## 4. Trade Secret Strategy

### What Is Kept as Trade Secret
Not all IP is patented. Group14 likely maintains as trade secrets:
- **Precise thermal profiles**: Exact time-temperature ramps for carbonization and CVI
- **Silane concentration and flow rate parameters**: The specific CVI recipe that yields optimal silicon loading and distribution
- **Precursor formulation details**: Exact polymer composition, molecular weight, and template ratios
- **Quality control acceptance criteria**: Internal specifications that are tighter than published data
- **Equipment design details**: Custom reactor geometries, gas distribution manifolds, material handling systems

### Trade Secret Protection Measures
- Employee and visitor NDAs as condition of facility access
- Segmented information access — no single employee has complete process knowledge
- Raw material suppliers provided with blinded specifications
- Customer qualification data shared under strict confidentiality agreements

## 5. Patent Litigation Risk

### Assertion Risk
As the silicon anode market commercializes, patent assertion activity is expected to increase:
- **Non-practicing entities (NPEs)** may acquire early university patents and assert against commercial players
- **Cross-licensing** between Group14, Sila, Amprius, and Enovix may become necessary as technologies converge
- **Chinese competitors** (BTR, Shanshan) have growing patent portfolios; enforcement in China may be challenging

### Defensive Positioning
- Group14's 100+ patent portfolio provides countersuit ammunition
- Early filing dates (2015-2018 priority) on core patents give Group14 prior art advantages
- Participation in DOE and national lab programs (PNNL, Argonne) provides access to government-held patents
