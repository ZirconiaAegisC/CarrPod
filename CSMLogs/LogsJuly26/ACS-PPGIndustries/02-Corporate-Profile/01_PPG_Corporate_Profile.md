PPG Industries was established in 1883 as Pittsburgh Plate Glass Company by Captain John B. Ford and John Pitcairn. Over 143 years, the enterprise transformed from a regional flat-glass manufacturer into the world's second-largest coatings company and a dominant force in specialty materials. The corporate tagline—"We Protect and Beautify the World"—commemorates its 10th anniversary in 2026.

## 2.0 Capital History & Shareholder Philosophy

PPG holds one of the longest continuous dividend records in American industry:

- **126 years of uninterrupted dividends** (since 1899)
- **54 consecutive annual dividend increases**
- Designation as an S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrat

Shareholder return mechanisms in FY2025:
- **$790 million** in share repurchases
- **$630 million** in dividend payments
- **$1.4 billion** total capital returned
- **$1.9 billion** remaining under current share repurchase authorization

The company maintains an investment-grade credit profile. FY2025 year-end cash stood at $2.2 billion against $5.5 billion net debt, providing balance-sheet capacity for both organic capital deployment and strategic portfolio evolution.

## 4.0 Segment Architecture

PPG reports under three operating segments. Segment realignments effective from Q1 2026 shifted the Traffic Solutions business into Performance Coatings, harmonizing with the service-intensive protective and infrastructure portfolio.

### 4.1 Global Architectural Coatings ($3.8B FY2025)

Serves consumer (DIY), professional contractor, and trade channels. Products include interior and exterior architectural paints, stains, wood-care finishes, adhesives, and sealants. Key brands: PPG PAINTS, DULUX, GLIDDEN, OLYMPIC, COMEX, and RENNER. Channel strategy emphasizes owned-store networks (company-operated and independent dealer), home-center retail, and digital direct-to-pro.

### 4.2 Performance Coatings ($5.5B FY2025 — Record)

The highest-margin segment. Sub-categories:

- **Aerospace Coatings:** Transparencies, sealants, chemical-management services, and chromate-free pretreatment systems for commercial, military, and general aviation
- **Protective & Marine Coatings:** Corrosion-resistant, fire-protective, and fouling-release coatings for energy, infrastructure, marine newbuild, and dry-dock
- **Automotive Refinish:** Waterborne and high-solids repair coatings, ancillaries, and digital color-matching tools (PPG LINQ/MOONWALK)
- **Traffic Solutions:** Pavement markings, signage, and road safety materials (transferred into segment Q1 2026)

### 4.3 Industrial Coatings ($6.5B FY2025)

Application-embedded coatings for OEM manufacturing processes:

- **Automotive OEM:** Electrocoat (e-coat), primer, basecoat, clearcoat, and adhesives/sealants for light-vehicle and commercial-vehicle assembly
- **Industrial Coatings:** Coil and extrusion coatings, general industrial finishes, wood coatings for furniture and flooring, and specialty films
- **Packaging Coatings:** Interior and exterior can and closure coatings for beverage, food, and consumer packaging—including the flagship non-BPA platforms PPG INNOVEL and PPG HOBA

## 5.0 Technology Foundation

PPG's R&D organization spans approximately 20 innovation centers globally, including the Allison Park Global Coatings Innovation Center (Pennsylvania), the Burbank Aerospace Application Development Center (California), and facilities in Milan, Shanghai, and Amsterdam. Core technology platforms encompass:

| Platform | Application |
|----------|------------|
| Siloxane and polysiloxane chemistry | Protective, marine, and architectural topcoats |
| Epoxy and polyurethane systems | Aerospace primers, industrial corrosion protection |
| Non-BPA polymer dispersion | Packaging can and closure linings |
| Acrylic and alkyd emulsion technology | Architectural and industrial decorative |
| UV/EB curable coatings | Wood, plastic, and specialty substrates |
| Powder coatings (thermoset & thermoplastic) | Automotive, appliance, general industrial |
| Electrocoat (cathodic epoxy and acrylic) | Automotive and general metal finishing |

## 6.0 Sustainability Profile

- **43% of FY2025 sales** from products classified as sustainably advantaged (measured against internally validated criteria addressing energy, emissions, waste, and material health)
- **28% reduction in water intensity** at priority manufacturing sites, from a 2017 baseline
- Science-based greenhouse gas reduction targets aligned with the Paris Agreement framework
- Circular-economy initiatives including OEM paint-shop closed-loop recycling and packaging-container return programs

## 7.0 Governance & Leadership

The Board of Directors comprises 10 directors (9 independent), led by an Independent Chair. Executive leadership maintains deep continuity in coatings and specialty-chemical sectors. The Audit, Sustainability and Innovation, Nominating and Governance, and Officers-Directors Compensation Committees oversee strategic, financial, and ESG governance.
