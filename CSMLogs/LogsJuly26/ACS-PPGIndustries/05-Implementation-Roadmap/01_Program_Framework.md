# Implementation Roadmap: ACS-PPG Program Framework

**Document Control:** ACS-IR-001 | CLASSIFICATION: CONFIDENTIAL | 15 July 2026 | Version 1.0

## 1.0 Program Architecture

The ACS-PPG Industries Coatings & Materials engagement program is structured across five workstreams aligned with the technical frameworks established in this dossier. The program contemplates a phased execution spanning a 12-to-18-month horizon, with defined milestones, deliverables, and governance mechanisms.

## 2.0 Workstream Structure

### Workstream A: Aerospace Coatings & Specialty Materials

| Phase | Activities | Timeline | Deliverables |
|-------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| A1: Assessment | Technical-product portfolio review; qualification-status audit; competitive benchmarking | Months 1–3 | Aerospace coatings technology assessment report; competitive landscape map |
| A2: Qualification | Specification-gap analysis (BMS, AIMS, AMS); chromate-free transition roadmap | Months 2–6 | Qualification roadmap; regulatory-compliance timeline |
| A3: Commercial | Supply-agreement framework; chemical-management service-model evaluation; MRO channel strategy | Months 4–9 | Commercial-term sheet; service-model proposal |
| A4: Execution | Product qualification initiation; supply-chain integration; application engineering support deployment | Months 7–18 | Qualification test protocols; supply-agreement execution |

### Workstream B: Protective & Marine Coatings Infrastructure

| Phase | Activities | Timeline | Deliverables |

| B4: Execution | Specification registration; project-specific coating-system proposals; application training | Months 7–18 | Registered specifications; training-completion reports |

## 3.0 Governance Framework

### 3.1 Steering Committee

| Role | Responsibilities | Cadence |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| Executive Sponsor | Strategic alignment; resource allocation; escalation resolution | Quarterly |
| Program Director | Cross-workstream coordination; milestone tracking; risk management | Monthly |
| Technical Lead (per workstream) | Subject-matter expertise; deliverable quality; timeline adherence | Bi-weekly |
| Commercial Lead | Contractual framework; pricing; terms and conditions | Monthly |
| Legal & Compliance | Regulatory review; IP protection; antitrust compliance | As needed |

### 3.2 Reporting Cadence

| Report | Frequency | Audience | Content |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Executive Dashboard | Monthly | Steering Committee | Milestone status, budget vs. actual, risks, decisions required |
| Workstream Status | Bi-weekly | Program Director, Workstream Leads | Activity progress, issues, next-period priorities |
| Technical Review | Per phase gate | Technical Leads, Subject-Matter Experts | Phase-gate deliverables, technical findings, go/no-go recommendation |
| Quarterly Business Review | Quarterly | Executive Sponsor, Steering Committee | Program health, strategic alignment, resourcing, FY outlook |

### 3.3 Phase-Gate Criteria

Each phase transition requires:
1. Completion of all prior-phase deliverables with documented sign-off
2. Technical feasibility confirmation (where applicable)
3. Commercial-viability assessment update
4. Risk-review completion with mitigation-status confirmation
5. Resource-availability confirmation for next phase
6. Steering Committee authorization

## 5.0 Risk Register

| Risk ID | Description | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---------|------------|------------|--------|-----------|
| R01 | Aerospace qualification-cycle delay | Moderate | High — revenue recognition timeline | Early engagement with OEM qualification authorities; parallel-track testing |
| R02 | Regulatory timeline shift (EU BPA ban) | Low | Moderate — demand timing | Monitoring; flexible supply agreements |
| R03 | Competitive response from AkzoNobel, Axalta, Sherwin-Williams | Moderate | Moderate — pricing, share | Technology differentiation; service-model lock-in |
| R04 | Raw-material supply disruption | Low–Moderate | Moderate — production continuity | Multi-source qualification; safety-stock policy |
| R05 | Automotive OEM volume decline | Moderate | Moderate — Industrial Coatings revenue | Diversification across OEMs and regions |
| R06 | IP / confidentiality breach | Low | High — competitive disadvantage | Robust NDA/confidentiality framework; data-segmentation protocols |
