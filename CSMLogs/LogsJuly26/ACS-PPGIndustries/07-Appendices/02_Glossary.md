## 2.0 Coatings Chemistry & Application

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Acrylic | Polymer chemistry based on acrylate and methacrylate monomers; used in waterborne and solvent-borne architectural and industrial coatings |
| Alkyd | Polyester-modified with fatty acids; traditional architectural and industrial coating binder |
| Anodize | Electrochemical surface treatment for aluminum, creating a protective oxide layer |
| Basecoat | Pigmented color layer applied over primer in multi-layer automotive and aerospace coating systems |
| Binder | Film-forming polymer component of a coating |
| BPA | Bisphenol A — monomer used in epoxy-resin synthesis for can linings |
| Chromate-Free | Coating formulations without hexavalent chromium (Cr⁶⁺) compounds; regulatory-driven transition in aerospace and industrial applications |
| Clearcoat | Transparent, unpigmented topcoat providing gloss, UV protection, and chemical resistance |
| Coil Coating | Continuous application of coating to metal strip or coil before fabrication |
| Compact Process | Automotive paint-shop process eliminating primer bake oven (3-Wet; B1:B2); reduces energy consumption and footprint |
| Conversion Coating | Chemical pretreatment creating a surface layer for corrosion resistance and paint adhesion |
| Crosslinking | Chemical reaction forming bonds between polymer chains, typically during thermal cure |
| DFT | Dry Film Thickness — the thickness of a coating after curing, typically measured in microns (µm) or mils |
| E-Coat / Electrocoat | Electrodeposition coating process using electrical current to deposit paint on conductive substrates; primary automotive corrosion-protection layer |
| Epoxy | Thermoset polymer system based on epoxide functional groups; widely used for corrosion-resistant primers |
| Fouling-Release Coating | Non-biocidal marine hull coating with low surface energy that prevents organism adhesion |
| Intumescent Coating | Passive fire-protection coating that expands (intumesces) under heat exposure to form an insulating char layer |
| Non-BPA Coating | Can-lining coating chemistry free of bisphenol A monomer; regulatory-driven transition in metal packaging |
| Polyurethane | Polymer based on isocyanate-polyol reaction; used in high-durability topcoats |
| Polysiloxane | Inorganic-organic hybrid polymer providing exceptional UV resistance and durability without isocyanate curing |
| Powder Coating | Solvent-free coating applied as dry powder and fused/cured by heat; zero-VOC environmental advantage |
| Pretreatment | Surface preparation (cleaning, conversion coating) prior to paint application |
| Primer | First coating layer applied after pretreatment; provides corrosion protection and adhesion for subsequent layers |
| PVDF | Polyvinylidene fluoride — fluoropolymer resin used in premium architectural coil coatings for exceptional weatherability |
| Solids Content | Percentage of non-volatile material in a coating formulation (by weight or volume) |
| Solvent-Borne | Coating using organic solvents as the carrier/diluent; traditional technology, declining due to VOC regulations |
| TiO₂ | Titanium dioxide — white pigment providing opacity (hiding power) and brightness |
| Topcoat | Final coating layer providing appearance, durability, and environmental resistance |
| UV-Curable Coating | Coating cured by ultraviolet radiation; extremely fast cure speed, used in wood, plastic, and specialty applications |
| VOC | Volatile Organic Compound — regulated due to air-quality and health considerations |
| Waterborne | Coating using water as the primary carrier/diluent; environmentally preferred technology |
| WFT | Wet Film Thickness — coating thickness at application, before solvent/water evaporation |

## 4.0 Marine & Protective Coatings Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Antifouling | Coating that prevents marine organism growth on vessel hulls through biocide release |
| Ballast Tank | Ship compartment filled with seawater for stability; requires robust corrosion protection |
| CII | Carbon Intensity Indicator — IMO regulation rating vessel CO₂ emissions per ton-mile |
| Dry-Dock | Periodic vessel maintenance event when hull is accessible for coating inspection and repair |
| EEXI | Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index — IMO framework for existing vessel efficiency |
| Fouling-Release | Non-biocidal coating preventing organism attachment through low surface energy |
| IMO | International Maritime Organization — UN agency regulating shipping |
| LNG Carrier | Liquefied Natural Gas carrier; specialized vessel type |
| Newbuilding | Construction of new vessel at a shipyard |
| Polysiloxane | Inorganic polymer providing exceptional UV and weathering durability in protective topcoats |
