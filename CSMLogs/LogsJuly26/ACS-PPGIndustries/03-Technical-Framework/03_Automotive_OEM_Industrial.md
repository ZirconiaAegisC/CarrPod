## 1.0 Domain Overview

PPG's Automotive OEM and Industrial Coatings operations operate within the Industrial Coatings segment ($6.5 billion, FY2025). The business encompasses process-integrated coating solutions applied during the manufacture of light vehicles, commercial vehicles, appliances, heavy equipment, architectural metal components, wood products, and industrial goods. Automotive OEM coatings are deeply embedded in OEM manufacturing processes, characterized by long qualification cycles, co-located application support, and formula-based pricing indexed to raw-material baskets.

## 2.0 Automotive OEM Coatings

### 2.1 Value Chain Position

Automotive OEM coatings are applied at the vehicle-assembly plant within the paint shop—one of the most capital-intensive and environmentally regulated zones of automotive manufacturing. The typical coating-layer stack comprises:

| Layer | Dry Film Thickness | Function | PPG Technology |
|-------|-------------------|----------|---------------|
| Pretreatment (phosphate or zirconium) | Nanoscale | Corrosion resistance, adhesion | PPG X-BOND zirconium pretreatment |
| Electrocoat (e-coat) | 15–25 µm | Full-body corrosion protection | PPG POWERCRON, PPG ENVIRO-PRIME (tin-free) |
| Primer | 20–30 µm | Chip resistance, UV block, surface smoothing | PPG ENVIROPRIME, waterborne and compact-process primers |
| Basecoat | 12–25 µm | Color, metallic/pearlescent effects | PPG ENVIROBASE High Performance, compact-process basecoats |
| Clearcoat | 35–50 µm | Gloss, scratch resistance, UV durability, chemical resistance | PPG CERAMICLEAR (silica-nanoparticle), PPG DURACLEAR |

**Compact Process** (3-Wet / B1:B2) integration reduces the traditional primer-bake-basecoat-bake-clearcoat-bake sequence by eliminating the primer oven, delivering energy savings of 15–25% per vehicle body and reducing paint-shop footprint.

### 2.2 Electrification-Driven Reformulation

The global light-vehicle electrification transition drives new coating requirements:

| Application | Requirement | PPG Response |
|-------------|------------|-------------|
| Battery enclosures (aluminum and steel) | Dielectric protection, fire resistance, corrosion | PPG CORACHAR battery fire protection, PPG POWERCRON e-coat for battery trays |
| Battery-cell interconnect | Electrical insulation, thermal conductivity management | PPG dielectric powder and UV-curable coatings |
| Electric motor components | Thermal dissipation, electrical isolation | PPG specialty wire enamels and impregnation resins |
| Lightweight substrates (aluminum, CFRP, magnesium) | Adhesion, galvanic-corrosion prevention | PPG pretreatment systems for mixed-metal bodies |

## 3.0 Industrial Coatings

The industrial coatings sub-segment addresses diverse end-markets:

### 3.1 Coil & Extrusion Coatings

Pre-painted metal (coil coating) applies liquid or powder coatings to continuous metal strip (steel, aluminum, galvalume) before fabrication into building panels, appliance wrappers, automotive body panels, and metal furniture. PPG offers fluoropolymer (PVDF), silicone-modified polyester (SMP), plastisol, and polyester coil-coating systems under the DURANAR, CORAFLON, and TRUEFORM brands. The business is linked to non-residential and residential construction activity, appliance production, and automotive body-panel sourcing strategies.

### 3.2 General Industrial Finishes

Liquid and powder coatings applied to fabricated metal goods, heavy equipment (agricultural, construction, mining), automotive components, electrical enclosures, and consumer durables. Powder coatings (PPG ENVIROCRON) represent a structurally growing portion of the portfolio, driven by zero-VOC regulatory advantages, high transfer efficiency, and overspray reclaim capability.

### 3.3 Wood Coatings

Coatings for kitchen cabinetry, office and residential furniture, flooring, and building products (moulding, millwork). PPG's wood-coatings business is concentrated in North America, with growing presence in Southeast Asia furniture-manufacturing clusters. UV-curable and waterborne technologies are displacing traditional solvent-borne nitrocellulose and acid-catalyzed systems due to VOC regulation and productivity advantages.

## 4.0 Competitive Dynamics

| Competitor | Automotive OEM | Industrial |
|------------|---------------|-----------|
| BASF (Glasurit, R-M, Chemetall) | Major global competitor | Limited |
| Axalta Coating Systems | Major (especially N.A. and China) | Moderate (general industrial) |
| AkzoNobel | Moderate (selected OEMs) | Strong in coil and wood |
| Sherwin-Williams | Growing via Valspar acquisition | Strong in coil, general industrial |
| Nippon Paint / Kansai Paint | Strong in Asia | Regional |
| Henkel (pretreatment/adhesives) | Major pretreatment competitor | Limited |

PPG's competitive advantages in Automotive OEM include: (a) a comprehensive technology stack spanning pretreatment through clearcoat, enabling single-supplier paint-shop management, (b) compact-process expertise reducing OEM capital and energy costs, (c) formula-based raw-material indexing insulating margins from input volatility, and (d) deep co-located engineering support at hundreds of assembly plants globally.

## 5.0 Risk Factors

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Global light-vehicle production cyclicality | High | Formula-based pricing; diversified OEM mix; aftermarket |
| Tariff and trade-policy disruption | Moderate–High | Localized manufacturing in each major region |
| Electric-vehicle adoption pace uncertainty | Low–Moderate | Battery-enclosure coating growth offsets potential volumetric decline from simplified paint processes |
| Raw-material inflation (TiO₂, epoxy resins, solvents, monomers) | Moderate | Contractual pass-through mechanisms; alternative formulations |
| OEM insourcing of paint-shop operations | Low | Technical complexity and capital intensity favor external supply |

## 6.0 Engagement Relevance

Automotive OEM coatings provide a material revenue base ($3–4 billion estimated) with a technological trajectory driven by electrification, lightweighting, and sustainability mandates. The industrial coatings portfolio adds breadth and diversification, with coil, powder, and wood-coating platforms representing growth vectors in construction, durable-goods manufacturing, and furniture markets globally.
