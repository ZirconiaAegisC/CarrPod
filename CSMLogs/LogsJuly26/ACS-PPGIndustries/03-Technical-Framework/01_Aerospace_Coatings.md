## 1.0 Strategic Context

Aerospace coatings and specialty materials represent PPG's most technology-intensive and regulation-protected portfolio. The segment participates across the full aircraft lifecycle: original equipment manufacturing (OEM), maintenance, repair, and overhaul (MRO), and aftermarket. PPG supplies coatings, sealants, transparencies (windows and canopies), chemical-management services, and pretreatment systems to commercial aviation, military, and general aviation customers globally.

## 2.0 Portfolio Architecture

### 2.1 Commercial Aviation Coatings

PPG is one of two dominant suppliers (alongside AkzoNobel Aerospace Coatings) to the global commercial airframe market. The product hierarchy encompasses:

| Layer | Function | PPG Product Families |
|-------|----------|---------------------|
| Surface Pretreatment | Chromate-free conversion coating and anodize replacement | PPG AEROCRON, DESOPRIME pretreatment systems |
| Primer | Corrosion inhibition, adhesion promotion | PPG DESOPRIME CF (chromate-free), DESOPRIME HS |
| Basecoat / Topcoat | UV resistance, color, aesthetics, aerodynamic smoothness | PPG DESOTHANE HS, AEROCRON, DESOTHANE CA series |
| Clearcoat | Gloss retention, chemical resistance, cleanability | PPG CERAMIC-CLEAR aerospace, DESOTHANE HD |
| Interior Coatings | Flame-smoke-toxicity compliance, anti-microbial | PPG AEROCRON interior systems |

### 2.2 Sealants & Specialty Materials

PPG produces fuel-tank, fuselage, and aerodynamic sealants based on polysulfide, polythioether, and silicone chemistries. Key products include PRC and PRO-SEAL branded sealant families, specified across Airbus, Boeing, Embraer, COMAC, and Bombardier platforms. Sealants represent high switching-cost consumables due to OEM qualification requirements embedded in type-certificate documentation.

### 2.3 Transparencies

PPG manufactures cockpit windows, passenger-cabin windows, and canopies for commercial, regional, business, and military aircraft. The business is characterized by extremely long qualification cycles (3–7 years), sole-source positions on key platforms, and high aftermarket capture rates through mandated replacement intervals tied to airframe hours or pressurization cycles.

### 2.4 Chemical Management Services

Through the PPG AEROSPACE Managed Services program, PPG provides on-site inventory management, point-of-use dispensing, waste-stream consolidation, and process-control support at major airline and MRO facilities. This creates annual recurring revenue streams and substantial customer integration.

## 3.0 Current Market Dynamics

### 3.1 Demand Backlog

PPG reported an aerospace coatings backlog of **$315 million** as of Q1 2026, reflecting contracted but not-yet-recognized revenue tied to airframe OEM build schedules and multi-year MRO agreements. The backlog is driven by:

- Airbus A320neo-family ramp to rate 75/month
- Boeing 737 MAX rate recovery toward 50+/month (subject to ongoing FAA oversight)
- Widebody production increases (A350, 787)
- Post-pandemic MRO activity normalization (deferred maintenance unwinding)
- Military modernization programs (F-35, next-generation rotorcraft, tanker fleets)

### 3.2 Competitive Moats

**Regulatory Barriers.** All aerospace coatings and sealants require qualification under OEM material specifications (e.g., Boeing BMS, Airbus AIMS, SAE AMS standards). Qualification cycles range from 18 months to 5+ years and require multi-million-dollar testing investments. Once qualified, products are listed in type-certificate and repair-manual documentation, making substitution difficult and costly.

**Chromate-Free Transition.** The global regulatory trajectory toward hexavalent-chromium elimination drives a generational product-reformulation cycle. PPG's early investment in chromate-free pretreatment and primer technology positions the company to capture specification adoption as platforms transition. European REACH authorization deadlines for chromate-containing products create near-term urgency.

## 4.0 Growth Trajectory

PPG has reported **double-digit organic growth** in aerospace coatings for multiple consecutive quarters. Key growth drivers:

1. **Narrowbody build-rate acceleration** — both Airbus A320neo and Boeing 737 MAX families consume significant coating and sealant volumes per shipset
2. **Widebody recovery** — A350 and 787 production rates recovering, each platform representing substantially higher coating content per aircraft versus narrowbody
3. **MRO cycle normalization** — global airline capacity exceeding 2019 levels, driving consumable reorder and scheduled repaint events
4. **Next-generation platform qualifications** — positioned for COMAC C919 ramp, upcoming clean-sheet designs, and military sixth-generation programs
5. **Chromate-free adoption** — regulatory pull-through converting legacy specification positions into premium-priced next-generation product sales

## 5.0 Risk Factors

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Boeing 737 MAX production uncertainty | Moderate | Diversification across OEM platforms; MRO volume insulates |
| Supply-chain inflation (titanium dioxide, epoxy resins, specialty solvents) | Low–Moderate | Formula-based pricing agreements; hedging; backward integration |
| Commercial aerospace cyclicality | Moderate | MRO aftermarket provides counter-cyclical buffer |
| Competitive encroachment (AkzoNobel, Mankiewicz, Sherwin-Williams) | Low | Qualification moats; deep specification integration |

## 6.0 Engagement Relevance

The aerospace coatings and specialty materials portfolio constitutes a core strategic interest area within the ACS framework. PPG's technology leadership, regulatory-driven demand, and $315 million contracted backlog provide visibility into a multi-year growth arc insulated from short-cycle industrial volatility.
