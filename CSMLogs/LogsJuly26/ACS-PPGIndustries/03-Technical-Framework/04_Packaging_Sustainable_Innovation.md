## 1.0 Strategic Significance

Packaging coatings represent one of PPG's fastest-growing technology platforms—recording **double-digit organic growth** with a cumulative **20%+ two-year stacked trajectory** through FY2025. The business sits within the Industrial Coatings segment and addresses the interior and exterior coating of metal packaging containers for beverage, food, personal-care, household, and industrial products. The fundamental growth driver is a global regulatory and consumer-led transition away from bisphenol A (BPA)-based epoxy can linings toward next-generation non-BPA coating technologies—a market opportunity estimated at over $2 billion globally in the metal-packaging coatings segment.

## 2.0 Technical Architecture

### 2.1 Conventional Epoxy Systems (Legacy)

Historically, the overwhelming majority of metal food and beverage can interiors have been protected by epoxy-resin coatings synthesized from BPA and epichlorohydrin. These coatings provide exceptional corrosion resistance, adhesion, flexibility, and food-contact compliance. However, regulatory and consumer pressure regarding potential endocrine-disruption effects of free BPA migration has driven a secular shift toward BPA-free alternatives.

### 2.2 PPG INNOVEL — Next-Generation Non-BPA Platform

PPG INNOVEL is the company's flagship non-BPA coating system for aluminum and steel beverage cans, food cans, and closures. Key technical attributes:

| Property | Performance |
|----------|------------|
| BPA content | Non-detect (below 10 ppb analytical threshold) |
| Application method | Spray (interior) and roller-coat (exterior, ends) |
| Cure mechanism | Thermal crosslinking (180–210°C, 1–3 minutes typical) |
| Substrate compatibility | Aluminum (3104-H19, 5182 alloy), tinplate, electrolytic chromium-coated steel (ECCS) |
| Pack resistance | Cola, citrus, beer, isotonic beverages, canned foods (low-acid and high-acid) |
| Organoleptic neutrality | No detectable flavor or odor transfer in sensory panel testing |
| Regulatory compliance | EU Framework Regulation (EC) 1935/2004, US FDA 21 CFR 175.300, China GB 4806.10-2016 |

PPG INNOVEL is based on proprietary acrylic-epoxy hybrid dispersion chemistry. The polymer backbone achieves adhesion, flexibility, and barrier performance comparable to conventional BPA-based epoxy without the BPA monomer or BADGE (bisphenol A diglycidyl ether) structural unit.

### 2.3 PPG HOBA — High-Performance Overprint and Basecoat Alternatives

PPG HOBA extends the non-BPA platform to external can coatings—overprint varnishes, basecoats for decoration, and end/closure linings. HOBA systems maintain the abrasion resistance, pasteurization resistance, and adhesion characteristics required for high-speed filling lines (1,200–2,200 cans/minute) and retort sterilization processes (121°C, 30–90 minutes for food cans).

## 3.0 Regulatory & Market Drivers

### 3.1 European Union

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) significantly reduced the tolerable daily intake (TDI) for BPA in 2023, and the European Commission's 2024 proposal to ban BPA and other bisphenols in food-contact materials (with transition periods) creates a regulatory forcing function for conversion. Major European brand owners (beverage, food) have established 2025–2028 internal targets for BPA-free packaging portfolios, effectively mandating coating reformulation.

### 3.2 North America

The US FDA maintains that BPA is safe at current exposure levels, but the market has moved ahead of regulation. Major beverage companies and food processors have announced voluntary BPA-free commitments. State-level regulatory fragmentation (California Proposition 65 warnings, Washington State regulatory action) adds compliance complexity.

### 3.3 Asia Pacific

China's GB 9685-2016 standard includes positive-list migration limits for BPA, and regulatory activity is increasing. The region is the fastest-growing beverage-can market globally, driven by urbanization, premiumization (craft beer, ready-to-drink beverages), and sustainability preferences for infinitely recyclable aluminum packaging over single-use plastics.

## 5.0 Sustainability Alignment

Metal packaging coatings directly support circular-economy objectives:

- **Aluminum and steel cans are infinitely recyclable** without loss of material properties
- PPG non-BPA coatings are designed for compatibility with standard recycling processes (thermal de-coating during remelt)
- Coatings represent <0.5% of can weight, minimizing material intensity
- PPG targets 50% of sales from sustainably advantaged products by 2030 (43% in FY2025)
