## 1.0 Domain Overview

PPG's Protective & Marine (P&M) Coatings business operates at the intersection of corrosion science, fire protection engineering, and hydrodynamic surface design. The segment serves the global infrastructure, energy, marine, and industrial-maintenance markets with high-performance coating systems engineered for extreme service environments. P&M coatings are a material component of the Performance Coatings segment, which achieved a record $5.5 billion in FY2025 revenue.

## 2.0 Product Technology Base

### 2.1 Corrosion Protection

PPG's protective coatings portfolio addresses atmospheric, immersion, and underground corrosion across:

| Exposure Class | Coating Chemistry | Key Product Lines |
|---------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Atmospheric (C1–C5 per ISO 12944) | Epoxy, polyurethane, polysiloxane, acrylic | PPG PSX, PPG AMERCOAT, PPG SIGMASHIELD |
| Immersion (Im1–Im3) | Solvent-free epoxy, glass-flake epoxy, polyester | PPG SIGMAGUARD, PPG PHENGUARD, PPG SIGMACOVER |
| Underground / Buried | Fusion-bonded epoxy (FBE), liquid epoxy, coal-tar epoxy | PPG HIRRI, PPG ENVIROCRON |
| High-temperature service (≤650°C) | Inorganic zinc silicate, silicone-modified | PPG HI-TEMP, PPG PITT-CHAR |

**PPG PSX Polysiloxane** represents the flagship protective topcoat technology. The inorganic polysiloxane binder provides exceptional UV durability, gloss retention, and chemical resistance without isocyanate curing chemistry, simultaneously addressing worker-safety and environmental VOC concerns.

### 2.2 Fire Protection

**PPG STEELGUARD** intumescent epoxy coatings are applied to structural steel in commercial buildings, industrial facilities, offshore platforms, petrochemical plants, and transportation infrastructure. The product line includes:

- STEELGUARD 651 (solvent-based, cellulosic fire protection up to 120 minutes)
- STEELGUARD 951 (water-based, low-VOC, hydrocarbon fire scenarios)
- STEELGUARD FM 550 (thin-film, factory-applied for pre-fabricated steel)

Intumescent coatings expand 30–50× their dry-film thickness under fire exposure, forming an insulating char layer that delays the steel core reaching critical-failure temperature (typically 550°C). PPG competes directly with AkzoNobel (Interchar/Chartek), Jotun (Steelmaster), and Sherwin-Williams (FIRETEX) in this category.

### 2.3 Marine Coatings

PPG's marine coatings portfolio spans newbuilding and dry-dock maintenance for commercial shipping, naval vessels, offshore structures, and superyachts:

| Vessel Zone | Function | Product |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| Underwater hull (flat bottom and vertical sides) | Anticorrosive + antifouling or fouling release | PPG SIGMAGLIDE, SIGMA ECOFLEET, SIGMA SAILADVANCE |
| Boottop / splash zone | Abrasion resistance, cyclic immersion | PPG SIGMASHIELD, PPG SIGMACOVER |
| Topsides and superstructure | Aesthetics, UV durability | PPG PSX, PPG SIGMADUR |
| Ballast tanks | Corrosion inhibition, edge-retentive application | PPG SIGMAGUARD, PPG PHENGUARD |
| Cargo tanks | Chemical resistance, ease of cleaning | PPG SIGMA PHENGUARD, PPG NOVAGUARD |
| Decks | Slip resistance, mechanical durability | PPG SIGMADECK, PPG SIGMADUR |

**PPG SIGMAGLIDE** is the company's biocide-free fouling-release coating. Based on silicone-hydrogel polymer technology, it creates an ultra-low-friction surface that prevents organism attachment without releasing toxins into the marine environment. The product delivers documented fuel-efficiency improvements of 5–9% versus conventional antifouling systems, aligning with the International Maritime Organization's (IMO) Carbon Intensity Indicator (CII) regulations effective 2023 and the Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index (EEXI) framework.

## 3.0 Market Position & Competitive Landscape

PPG has recorded **twelve consecutive quarters of volume growth** in Protective & Marine coatings—a period spanning FY2023 through Q1 2026. This contrasts with the broader protective coatings market, which has grown at roughly GDP-linked rates of 2–4% annually, indicating material share capture.

Competitor landscape:

| Competitor | Relative Strength |
|------------|------------------|
| AkzoNobel (International Paint) | Dominant in marine; strong in protective |
| Jotun | Strong in marine (especially newbuilding) and protective |
| Hempel | Concentrated in marine and container coatings |
| Sherwin-Williams (Protective & Marine) | Strong in North American protective infrastructure |
| Chugoku Marine Paints (CMP) | Concentrated in Asian marine markets |
| Kansai Paint | Expanding marine presence |

PPG's competitive differentiation rests on polysiloxane technology leadership, fire-protection certification breadth, and Asia Pacific marine share gains—particularly in the Chinese and South Korean shipbuilding hubs, which account for over 70% of global merchant-vessel newbuilding tonnage.

### 3.1 Asia Pacific Marine Dynamics

The Asia Pacific marine coatings market is structurally concentrated around three major shipbuilding nations—China, South Korea, and Japan—with China commanding approximately 50% of global newbuilding orders. PPG's share gains in this region stem from:

- SIGMAGLIDE specification wins on LNG carrier newbuilds and container vessels, where fuel-efficiency guarantees are contractually linked
- Expansion of technical-service teams at major shipyards (Shanghai Waigaoqiao, Hyundai Heavy Industries, Samsung Heavy Industries)
- Localized R&D capabilities at the Shanghai and Busan application centers

## 4.0 Infrastructure Demand Drivers

Protective coatings demand is correlated with:

1. **Global energy capital expenditure** — upstream, midstream, and downstream oil and gas; LNG liquefaction and regasification terminals; renewable-energy infrastructure (offshore wind foundations, substations)
2. **Bridge and highway maintenance cycles** — North American infrastructure investment (IIJA), European TEN-T corridor projects, Belt and Road Initiative-linked transport corridors
3. **Industrial maintenance** — chemical processing, water and wastewater, mining, pulp and paper, power generation
4. **Data center construction** — intumescent fire protection for structural steel in hyperscale facilities

## 5.0 Sustainability & Regulatory Alignment

Protective and marine coatings directly address sustainability through:

- **Extended asset life:** Corrosion-protection systems extend structural service life, reducing material consumption and embodied carbon
- **VOC reduction:** Waterborne and ultra-high-solids formulations
- **Fuel-efficiency gains:** SIGMAGLIDE fouling-release reduces vessel fuel consumption and corresponding CO₂ emissions
- **Fire-safety compliance:** Intumescent coatings eliminate the environmental burden of passive fire-protection materials while enabling exposed architectural steel

## 6.0 Engagement Relevance

PPG's 12-quarter volume-growth streak in Protective & Marine coatings signals operational momentum independent of GDP-linked growth rates. The combination of technology differentiation (PSX polysiloxane, SIGMAGLIDE, STEELGUARD), geographic expansion in Asia Pacific marine, and secular demand from energy and infrastructure investment constitutes a durable growth vector with relevance across multiple ACS engagement scenarios.
