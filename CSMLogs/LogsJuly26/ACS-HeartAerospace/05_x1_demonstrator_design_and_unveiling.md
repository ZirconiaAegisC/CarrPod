# X1 Demonstrator: Design & Unveiling

## Program Rationale

The Heart Aerospace X1 is a full-scale technology demonstrator built to
validate the aerodynamic configuration, electric propulsion integration,
battery thermal management, and ground handling characteristics of the
ES-30 design. It is not a production-prototype aircraft and will not
carry commercial passengers; its purpose is risk reduction and data
collection across the flight envelope.

The X1 represents the first physical manifestation of the clean-sheet
ES-30 design at full scale. Prior to its construction, Heart Aerospace
conducted subscale wind-tunnel testing, computational fluid dynamics
simulations, structural coupon and component-level testing, and
propulsion system bench testing at the Gothenburg facility.

## Physical Configuration

The X1 features a 32-meter wingspan matching the planned production
aircraft dimensions. This is notably large for a 30-seat aircraft and
directly supports the aerodynamic efficiency targets for battery-electric
cruise. Key physical characteristics include:

- **Full-scale wing:** Including the production-representative high-lift
  devices, aileron configuration, and propeller nacelle placements.
- **Full-scale empennage:** T-tail configuration as planned for
  production.
- **Representative fuselage:** Cockpit and forward fuselage sections
  built to production specifications; the cabin section may be
  instrumented rather than fitted with seats.
- **Four electric motor positions:** Matching production nacelle
  locations with fully functional motor mounts and wiring harnesses.

The landing gear on the X1 is representative of the production design,
allowing taxi tests to validate steering, braking, and structural loads.

## Unveiling — September 2024, Gothenburg

Heart Aerospace unveiled the X1 demonstrator in September 2024 at its
Gothenburg, Sweden facility. The event was attended by investors,
airline partners, government officials, and international media. The
unveiling represented a milestone for European electric aviation,
demonstrating that a full-scale, clean-sheet hybrid-electric regional
aircraft had moved from the design phase to physical construction.

At the unveiling, CEO Anders Forslund stated that the X1 would begin
taxi tests and that the company was targeting first electric flight
in 2026. The aircraft was shown in a fully painted exterior scheme,
with the characteristic Heart Aerospace logo on the forward fuselage
and the long, slender high-aspect-ratio wing drawing attention from
aerospace analysts.

## Instrumentation and Data Collection

The X1 carries extensive flight-test instrumentation not present on
the production design:

- **Load sensors:** Strain gauges and load cells at critical structural
  points including wing roots, empennage attachments, landing gear
  trunnions, and motor mounts.
- **Propulsion sensors:** Motor torque, RPM, voltage, current, winding
  temperature, and inverter status continuously monitored for each of
  the four electric motors.
- **Battery monitoring:** Per-cell voltage and temperature monitoring
  across the entire battery pack. Thermal imaging of battery modules
  during charge and discharge cycles.
- **Aerodynamic sensors:** Pitot-static arrays, angle-of-attack vanes,
  sideslip sensors, and wake-survey rakes for propeller performance
  characterization.
- **Environmental sensors:** Cabin noise, vibration accelerometers,
  electromagnetic interference monitoring, and thermal mapping of the
  cabin and avionics bays.

## Manufacturing Location

The X1 was constructed at Heart Aerospace's Gothenburg pilot
manufacturing plant. This facility was established to prove out
production processes in parallel with the test program, allowing
manufacturing engineers to refine tooling, assembly sequences, and
supply-chain logistics ahead of the production ramp.

Swedish aerospace manufacturing heritage—particularly the Saab 340
and Saab 2000 regional aircraft programs—provided a skilled local
workforce and a network of tier-1 and tier-2 aerospace suppliers
already certified to EASA production organization approval standards.

## Relationship to Certification

Data from the X1 directly feeds the certification basis for the
ES-30. EASA and FAA require demonstration of performance, handling
qualities, structural integrity, and system safety through flight
test. The X1 generates the initial dataset that anchors the
certification compliance showing, particularly for:

- Aerodynamic performance and stall characteristics
- Electric propulsion system reliability and failure modes
- Battery safety during normal and emergency operations
- Ground handling and crosswind capability
- Avionics and fly-by-wire system performance
