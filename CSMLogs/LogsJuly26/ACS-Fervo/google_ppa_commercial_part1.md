## The Landmark Google Power Purchase Agreement

In 2023, Fervo Energy and Google announced a 5-megawatt power purchase agreement — the first corporate PPA for geothermal power in history. The agreement, while modest in capacity (5 MW is approximately 1% of Cape Station's target nameplate capacity), represents a commercial validation milestone whose significance extends beyond the specific megawatt-hours contracted.

### The Corporate Renewable Energy Market

Corporate procurement of renewable energy — led by technology companies including Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta — has become one of the largest demand drivers for new renewable generation in the United States. According to the Clean Energy Buyers Association, corporate buyers contracted for over 20 gigawatts of renewable energy in 2023, predominantly wind and solar.

Technology companies' electricity demand is growing rapidly, driven by data center expansion to support cloud computing and digital services. Google, in particular, has committed to operating on 24/7 carbon-free energy by 2030 — a commitment that requires matching its electricity consumption with carbon-free generation on an hourly basis, not just on an annual net basis as traditional renewable energy procurement achieves.

This 24/7 matching requirement is the key to understanding Google's interest in geothermal. Wind and solar, while cost-competitive, are intermittent: a data center that purchases enough wind energy on an annual basis to offset its consumption may still draw electricity from carbon-emitting sources during calm, dark periods when wind and solar output is low. Geothermal, by contrast, produces power continuously — 24 hours per day, 365 days per year — matching the flat load profile of a data center and enabling the hourly matching that Google's 24/7 carbon-free energy commitment requires.

### Commercial Terms

The specific pricing and terms of the Google-Fervo PPA have not been publicly disclosed. However, the structure of the agreement and the context of corporate renewable energy procurement allow reasonable inference about the commercial parameters.

The PPA is likely structured as a fixed-price contract with a term of 10-15 years, with pricing in the range of $60-90/MWh — consistent with geothermal PPAs in the western United States and reflecting the premium that corporate buyers are willing to pay for firm, dispatchable carbon-free electricity. The contract may include provisions for energy attribute certificates (renewable energy credits) that Google can use to substantiate its carbon-free energy claims.

The pricing of the Google PPA relative to wind and solar PPAs, which have been as low as $20-30/MWh in the western United States, reflects the value of geothermal's firm, dispatchable characteristics. The price premium is justified by the avoided cost of energy storage or backup generation that would be required to firm intermittent wind and solar to achieve the same level of hourly matching.

### Strategic Value Beyond Revenue

The Google PPA provides Fervo with value beyond the contracted revenue stream. The association with Google — one of the world's most prominent corporate renewable energy buyers and a company known for rigorous technical due diligence — serves as an implicit endorsement of Fervo's technology and execution capability. This endorsement is valuable in discussions with other potential off-takers, project finance lenders, and equity investors.

The PPA also provides a template for future corporate offtake agreements. If the Google contract performs as expected — reliable delivery of contracted capacity at the contracted price — Fervo can approach other corporate buyers with a reference transaction that demonstrates the bankability of EGS power. The corporate PPA market, at over 20 GW annually, is large enough that even a small share represents a substantial revenue opportunity for the EGS industry.

### Additionality and Emissions Impact

The concept of "additionality" — the requirement that renewable energy procurement cause new renewable generation to be built that would not have been built otherwise — is important to corporate buyers' sustainability claims. The Google PPA for Cape Station power is clearly additional: the 5 MW of capacity contracted by Google is part of the 400 MW Cape Station development that would not proceed at its current scale and pace without the revenue certainty provided by offtake agreements.

The emissions impact of displacing grid electricity with geothermal power depends on the carbon intensity of the grid electricity being displaced. In the CAISO market, where Cape Station power will be delivered, the marginal generation source varies by time of day: natural gas during evening peak periods, solar during midday. Displacing natural gas generation avoids approximately 0.4 metric tons of CO2 per megawatt-hour, meaning that the Google PPA (5 MW at 90% capacity factor, approximately 39,000 MWh annually) avoids roughly 15,600 metric tons of CO2 per year — equivalent to removing approximately 3,400 passenger vehicles from the road.
