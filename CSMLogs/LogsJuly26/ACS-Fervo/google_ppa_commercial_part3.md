## Project Finance and Bankability

The transition from venture-funded technology development to project-financed infrastructure construction is a critical inflection point for any energy technology company. Fervo Energy's ability to access project finance — non-recourse debt secured by project assets and cash flows rather than by the corporate balance sheet — will determine the pace and scale of Cape Station development.

### The Project Finance Structure

Project finance for power generation assets is a well-established discipline with standardized structures. The project company — a special-purpose entity that owns the Cape Station assets — enters into long-term contracts for revenue (PPA), fuel supply (not applicable for geothermal, which has zero fuel cost), operations and maintenance, and debt service. The project's cash flows, net of operating costs, are dedicated to debt service, with lenders having a first-priority claim on project revenues.

For geothermal projects, the project finance structure must address risks that are specific to subsurface resource development. The primary risk is resource risk — the possibility that the geothermal reservoir does not perform as expected, resulting in lower-than-projected power output or faster-than-expected thermal decline. This risk is managed through a combination of resource characterization (well testing before financial close), reservoir modeling (predicting long-term performance), and financial structuring (debt service coverage ratios that provide a buffer against underperformance).

### The Role of DOE Loan Guarantees

The DOE's Loan Programs Office (LPO) has the authority to provide loan guarantees for innovative energy projects under Title XVII of the Energy Policy Act. A DOE loan guarantee effectively substitutes the US government's credit for the project's credit, enabling lenders to provide debt financing at interest rates and terms that would not be available without the guarantee.

For EGS projects, which lack the operational track record that lenders require for project finance on a standalone basis, a DOE loan guarantee could be the difference between projects that are financeable and projects that are not. The LPO has expressed interest in geothermal lending and has the statutory authority to provide guarantees for up to 80% of eligible project costs.

The process of obtaining a DOE loan guarantee is rigorous, involving detailed due diligence on the project's technology, economics, environmental impacts, and community effects. The timeline from initial application to financial close is typically 18-24 months, making it essential that Fervo initiate the process well in advance of the capital deployment schedule for Cape Station.

### Tax Equity Considerations

The monetization of the Investment Tax Credit — converting the 30% credit into cash to fund project construction — requires a tax equity investor. The tax equity market in the United States is served by a limited number of large financial institutions — approximately 20-30 banks and insurance companies — that have sufficient federal tax liability to use renewable energy tax credits.

The tax equity market has historically been concentrated in wind and solar transactions, which have standardized structures that tax equity investors understand. Geothermal projects, with their longer development timelines, different risk profile, and smaller transaction volume, face a less developed tax equity market. Fervo may need to work with tax equity investors to develop geothermal-specific structures, or may need to structure Cape Station's financing to use the Production Tax Credit (a per-megawatt-hour credit over a 10-year period) rather than the Investment Tax Credit, depending on which credit is better matched to the project's tax equity investor pool.

### Commercial Validation Milestones

The progression of commercial validation for EGS follows a sequence of milestones that de-risk the technology and enable progressively larger and lower-cost capital formation:

1. **Pilot project operational (achieved):** Project Red's 3.5 MW demonstration plant has been producing power since 2023, generating operational data that validates EGS performance.

2. **Initial corporate PPA (achieved):** The Google 5 MW PPA demonstrates corporate offtake demand and establishes a commercial template.

3. **Utility-scale PPA (pending):** A larger offtake agreement — 50-100 MW with a utility or corporate buyer — would provide the revenue certainty required for project finance of the first Cape Station phase.

4. **Project finance close (pending):** Successful closing of non-recourse project debt for Cape Station Phase 1, potentially with DOE loan guarantee support, would demonstrate that EGS is bankable and open the capital markets to future EGS projects.

5. **Commercial operation (future):** The commencement of commercial operations at Cape Station Phase 1 would demonstrate that EGS can be delivered on time and on budget at utility scale, validating the technology for lenders, offtakers, and equity investors.

Each milestone reduces the risk premium that capital providers require and lowers the cost of capital for subsequent projects. The path from venture funding to infrastructure capital is well-understood for wind and solar projects, which have achieved these milestones over the past two decades. Fervo is navigating the same path for EGS, with the advantage that the path has already been mapped by predecessor technologies.

## Investor Implications

For investors in Fervo Energy, the Google PPA and the broader commercial validation process have several implications:

The PPA validates demand for EGS power at a price point that supports project economics, addressing one of the key risks that investors perceived in the EGS business model — the risk that geothermal power would not be competitive with wind and solar in the absence of carbon pricing or other policy support.

The growth of 24/7 carbon-free energy commitments among corporate buyers creates a structural demand driver for geothermal that is independent of policy support. Even if tax credits were reduced or eliminated, the premium that corporate buyers are willing to pay for firm carbon-free power could sustain EGS economics.

However, the corporate PPA market is concentrated among a small number of large buyers, creating offtake concentration risk. Fervo's dependence on a handful of technology companies for revenue — particularly if Google represents a significant portion of Cape Station's contracted offtake — introduces a risk that is different in character from the utility offtake risk that conventional geothermal developers face.
