## Program Context

COSMIC is the UK Space Agency's flagship debris remediation initiative,
forming part of the UK's National Space Strategy commitment to space
sustainability. The program seeks to develop a sovereign UK capability for
active debris removal, leveraging Astroscale's UK subsidiary based at the
Harwell Space Cluster in Oxfordshire.

The UK is particularly exposed to orbital debris risk given its insurance
market (Lloyd's of London underwrites a significant share of global satellite
policies) and its growing domestic satellite manufacturing base (SSTL,
Airbus Defence & Space UK, AAC Clyde Space, Open Cosmos).

## Mission Objectives

1. **Demonstrate sovereign UK capture capability:** Develop and fly a
   domestically-led debris removal mission.
2. **Validate novel capture technologies:** Go beyond magnetic docking to
   evaluate mechanical, adhesive, and net-based capture for targets without
   pre-installed interface plates.
3. **Build UK industrial supply chain:** Engage UK-based suppliers for
   spacecraft subsystems, GNC, propulsion, and ground segment.
4. **Establish regulatory precedent:** Work with the UK Civil Aviation
   Authority (CAA) and the UK Space Agency to develop the licensing
   framework for commercial debris removal operations, including liability
   allocation for third-party debris.

## Technical Approach

COSMIC is evaluating two capture modalities in parallel:

### Option A — Magnetic Capture (ELSA-M Heritage)
Leverages the lightweight Docking Plate and magnetic capture system proven
on ELSA-M ground testing. Lowest technical risk but requires the target
satellite to have a pre-installed DP — which legacy UK debris does not.

### Option B — Robotic Arm Capture (ADRAS-J2 Heritage)
Adapts the robotic arm developed for ADRAS-J2. Targets the Launch Vehicle
Adapter ring or other standardized structural interfaces common to most
satellite buses. Higher technical complexity but applicable to any target
regardless of preparation.

### Option C — Tentacle/Net Capture (New Development)
A deployable net or tentacle-based capture system for targets that are
tumbling too rapidly for robotic arm capture. This is the highest-risk,
highest-reward option, applicable to the broadest set of debris objects.

## Regulatory Pathway

The UK's Space Industry Act 2018 and the Outer Space Act 1986 provide the
licensing basis. A key open question is **liability for damage caused by or
to the target debris during capture operations**. The COSMIC program aims to
establish the first UK license for active debris removal, creating a
template for future commercial operations.
