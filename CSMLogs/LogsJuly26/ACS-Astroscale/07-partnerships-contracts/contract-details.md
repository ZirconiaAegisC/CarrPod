## ADRAS-J2 Contract Structure (¥13.2B)

The ¥13.2B contract with JAXA is the **largest single contract** in
Astroscale's history and the anchor of the current backlog.

### Risk Factors

1. **Schedule Risk:** ADRAS-J2 has a firm launch window in FY2027. Delays in
   CDR, supplier deliveries, or launch vehicle availability would defer
   revenue recognition.
2. **Technical Risk:** Robotic arm capture of a non-cooperative tumbling
   object has never been demonstrated. ADRAS-J flight data mitigates but
   does not eliminate this risk.
3. **Single-Customer Concentration:** JAXA contracts (ADRAS-J2 + ISSA-J1 +
   REFLEX-J) represent ~44% of total backlog. Delays or cancellations would
   have a material impact.

## ELSA-M Contract Structure (¥12.0B Cumulative)

ELSA-M is funded through a multi-party arrangement:

- **ESA contribution:** ~40% through the Space Safety Programme.
- **UKSA contribution:** ~30% through the National Space Innovation
  Programme and ESA subscription.
- **Eutelsat OneWeb contribution:** ~20% as prepaid service fees.
- **Astroscale contribution:** ~10% co-investment.

The contract includes **option pricing for follow-on missions**:
- Mission 1 (demonstration): ¥12.0B (includes non-recurring engineering).
- Missions 2–5 (operational): ~¥3.0B each (recurring cost).

## APS-R Contract Structure (¥5.5B)

US Space Force contract structured as a Cost-Plus-Fixed-Fee (CPFF)
arrangement with an Award Fee component. Key terms:

- **Period of Performance:** 36 months from award.
- **Fee Structure:** Fixed fee of 8% on allowable costs + up to 7% award
  fee based on technical performance milestones.
- **Data Rights:** US Government receives Government Purpose Rights (GPR)
  for technology developed under the contract. Astroscale retains commercial
  rights.
- **ITAR/EAR:** APS-R is subject to US export controls. Astroscale must
  maintain a Technology Control Plan (TCP) restricting access by non-US
  persons.

## Revenue Recognition Policy

Astroscale applies Japanese GAAP (JGAAP) for consolidated reporting:

- **Percentage-of-Completion Method:** Applied to ADRAS-J2, ISSA-J1, and
  ELSA-M. Revenue is recognized based on the ratio of costs incurred to
  total estimated costs. Requires reliable cost estimation and periodic
  review.
- **Completed Contract Method:** Applied to small study contracts where
  reliable estimation is not feasible.
- **Milestone Method:** Applied to COSMIC and REFLEX-J study phases.
