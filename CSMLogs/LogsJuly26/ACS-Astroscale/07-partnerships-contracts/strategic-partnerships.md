# Astroscale — Strategic Partnerships

## Government Agency Partnerships

### Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA)

Astroscale's foundational government partner. The relationship spans:

- **CRD2 Phase I (ADRAS-J):** JAXA provided ¥5.5B in funding, target
  orbital data, ground station access, and diplomatic engagement with the
  launching State of the target debris object.
- **CRD2 Phase II (ADRAS-J2):** ¥13.2B contract for debris capture and
  deorbit. JAXA serves as both customer and technical partner.
- **ISSA-J1:** ¥2.3B contract for in-situ space situational awareness.
  Demonstrates JAXA's willingness to use Astroscale for missions beyond
  debris removal.
- **REFLEX-J:** ¥1.2B study contract for on-orbit refueling technology.

The JAXA relationship is a **strategic moat**: it provides flight heritage,
technical credibility, and a reference customer that validates Astroscale
for international government contracts.

### European Space Agency (ESA) & UK Space Agency (UKSA)

- **ELSA-M:** Co-funded as a Public-Private Partnership. ESA provides
  technological oversight and contributes to development costs. UKSA funds
  the UK-based industrial component through its National Space Innovation
  Programme (NSIP).
- **COSMIC:** UKSA-funded study for UK sovereign debris removal capability.

### United States Space Force (USSF)

- **APS-R:** ~$37M contract for prototype refueling servicer development.
  Represents Astroscale's entry into the US defense market, which is
  expected to be the largest single source of revenue for on-orbit
  servicing through 2035.

## Commercial & Industrial Partnerships

### Exotrail (France)

Partnership announced 2025. Exotrail's **SpaceVan** orbital transfer
vehicle (OTV) uses electric propulsion for last-mile satellite delivery.
The partnership explores:
- Integration of Astroscale's docking plate on SpaceVan for post-mission
  removal.
- Joint commercial offering: Exotrail delivers the satellite to its
  operational orbit; Astroscale guarantees its removal at end-of-life.
- Shared technology development on electric propulsion for RPO.

### Mitsubishi Electric (MELCO)

Mitsubishi Electric is Japan's largest satellite manufacturer and a
strategic Astroscale shareholder. The relationship provides:
- Communications subsystem expertise for Astroscale servicers.
- Access to Mitsubishi Electric's satellite bus technology and supply chain.
- A channel to Japanese government satellite programs (QZSS, Himawari, IGS)
  that will eventually require servicing and refueling.

### Eutelsat OneWeb

ELSA-M's anchor commercial customer. OneWeb's commitment validates the
commercial EOL business model and provides a reference customer for the
broader megaconstellation market. The OneWeb contract structure — where
Eutelsat pays over the satellite's operational life — creates a **recurring
revenue stream** model that Astroscale aims to replicate with other
operators.
