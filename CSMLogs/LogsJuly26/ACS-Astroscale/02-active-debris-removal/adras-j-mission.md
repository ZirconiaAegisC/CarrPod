# ADRAS-J — Active Debris Removal by Astroscale — Japan

## Mission Overview

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Full Name | Active Debris Removal by Astroscale — Japan |
| Launch Date | February 18, 2024 |
| Launch Vehicle | Rocket Lab Electron |
| Launch Site | Mahia Peninsula, New Zealand (LC-1) |
| Target Object | JAXA H-IIA upper stage (approx. 3 tons, 11m length) |
| Orbit | ~600 km LEO, 98° inclination |
| Mission Duration | ~24 months (through deorbit initiation) |

## Historical Significance

ADRAS-J represents the **world's first privately-led Rendezvous and Proximity
Operations (RPO) mission to a non-cooperative piece of orbital debris**. No
prior commercial entity had demonstrated the ability to safely approach,
characterize, and maintain controlled proximity to an unprepared, tumbling
rocket body in low Earth orbit.

The mission forms Phase I of JAXA's Commercial Removal of Debris Demonstration
(CRD2) program, which pioneered a novel public-private partnership model where
JAXA procures debris removal as a commercial service rather than developing the
capability in-house.

## Mission Phases

### Phase 1 — Launch & Commissioning (Feb–Mar 2024)
- Successful injection into a lower parking orbit by Electron.
- Spacecraft bus checkout: propulsion, power, thermal, GNC, communications.
- Orbit-raising sequence using Astroscale's proprietary electric propulsion.

### Phase 2 — Far-Range Approach (Mar–Apr 2024)
- Ground-guided phasing maneuvers to close range from hundreds of km to ~50 km.
- Initial acquisition of the target using narrow-field optical cameras.
- Transition from absolute (GPS-based) to relative (camera/lidar-based)
  navigation.

### Phase 3 — Close-Range RPO (Apr–Jul 2024)
- Approach from 50 km to 15 meters from the H-IIA upper stage.
- Selfie-style imagery of ADRAS-J adjacent to the debris object.
- Full 360° fly-around observation capturing the target's tumble state,
  surface condition, and structural integrity.
- Demonstration of station-keeping and controlled circumnavigation.

### Phase 4 — Extended Observation & Deorbit (Aug 2024–Mar 2026)
- Long-duration proximity monitoring to characterize the debris environment.
- Collection of data on target rotational dynamics, material degradation,
  and potential capture points.
- Deorbit sequence initiated March 2026 using onboard propulsion.

## Key Technical Achievements

1. **Non-Cooperative RPO:** Demonstrated approach and station-keeping relative
   to an uncooperative target with no navigation aids, reflectors, or
   communication link.
2. **Autonomous Hazard Detection:** Onboard software for real-time collision
   risk assessment and autonomous retreat capability.
3. **Multi-Sensor Fusion:** Combined visible-spectrum cameras, thermal infrared,
   and scanning lidar for relative navigation in varied lighting conditions.
4. **Ground-In-The-Loop Operations:** Tokyo mission control with a ~2-second
   command latency operating the spacecraft in near-real-time during critical
   proximity phases.
