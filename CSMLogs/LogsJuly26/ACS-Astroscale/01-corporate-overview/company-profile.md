## Mission & Vision

Astroscale's stated mission is to secure long-term orbital sustainability by
providing end-to-end on-orbit servicing solutions. The company has articulated a
"Commonplace by 2030" target, under which active debris removal and satellite
life-extension services become routine, commercially viable operations rather
than experimental demonstrations.

The company operates across the full servicing lifecycle:
- **Active Debris Removal (ADR):** Capturing and deorbiting defunct spacecraft
  and rocket upper stages that pose collision risks in congested orbits.
- **End-of-Life (EOL) Services:** Planned removal of satellites at the
  conclusion of their operational lives, contracted in advance.
- **Life Extension:** Rendezvous, docking, and propulsion augmentation to
  extend the revenue-generating life of active satellites.
- **Refueling:** On-orbit propellant transfer to replenish station-keeping
  capability for geostationary and low-Earth-orbit assets.
- **In-Situ Space Situational Awareness (ISSA):** Inspection and characterization
  of resident space objects.

## Governance & Leadership

Nobu Okada founded Astroscale after a career in IT consulting and government
advisory roles. He has positioned the company as the private-sector leader in
orbital debris remediation, securing early-mover advantage through a series of
technology demonstrations beginning with the IDEAS platform and culminating in
the historic ADRAS-J mission.

The executive team draws experience from JAXA, ESA, NASA, and major aerospace
primes including Airbus, Mitsubishi Electric, and Lockheed Martin.

## Public Listing

Astroscale listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange Growth Market in June 2023 and
graduated to the Prime Market, trading under the symbol 186A.T. The listing
provided access to Japanese institutional capital and raised the company's
profile among global ESG and space-economy investors.

## Single Segment Reporting

The company reports financials under a single "On-Orbit Servicing" operating
segment, reflecting the integrated nature of its technology platform. Revenue
is primarily project-based, recognized under Japanese GAAP using the
percentage-of-completion method for long-term government and commercial
contracts.
