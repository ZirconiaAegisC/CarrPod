## Key Observations

### Revenue Growth Trajectory

Revenue has grown at **>75% CAGR over FY2023–FY2026**, driven by
progression of major programs from study phases into development and
execution. The divergence between "Revenue" (P&L recognized) and "Project
Income" (cash receipts + P&L recognized) reflects the percentage-of-
completion accounting treatment of long-term contracts.

### Path to Profitability

Astroscale has not achieved profitability and does not expect to do so
before **FY2028–FY2029**. The path to breakeven requires:

1. **Scale:** At least 2–3 operational missions per year generating
   ¥8–12B in annual revenue.
2. **Gross Margin Improvement:** Moving from negative gross margins (R&D
   costs exceed recognized revenue) to positive margins as development
   costs amortize and recurring manufacturing replaces non-recurring
   engineering.
3. **Operating Leverage:** SG&A growing slower than revenue; R&D
   expenditure declining as a percentage of revenue as the technology
   platform matures.

### Dilution Risk

The ¥18.0B convertible bond, if fully converted at the 125% premium,
would increase outstanding shares by approximately 18–22%, depending on
the share price at conversion. This represents a **moderate dilution
risk** to existing shareholders, partially mitigated by the expectation
that the share price will exceed the conversion price based on
ADRAS-J2 / ELSA-M execution.
