# Orbital Debris Removal — Global Market Analysis

## Market Overview

The on-orbit servicing and debris removal market is projected to grow from
an estimated **$3.5B in 2026 to $14–18B by 2035**, driven by the confluence
of three secular trends:

1. **Orbital Congestion:** Over 36,000 trackable debris objects (>10 cm) in
   LEO; an estimated 1 million objects 1–10 cm; and 130 million sub-cm
   particles. Each collision generates thousands of new fragments.
2. **Megaconstellation Deployment:** SpaceX Starlink (~6,000 satellites),
   Amazon Kuiper (3,200 planned), and Chinese constellations (Guowang,
   13,000 planned) will more than triple the active satellite population in
   LEO before 2030.
3. **Regulatory Pressure:** The FCC's 5-year deorbit rule, the
   Inter-Agency Space Debris Coordination Committee (IADC) guidelines, and
   the UN Committee on the Peaceful Uses of Outer Space (COPUOS) Long-Term
   Sustainability Guidelines are hardening from "best practice" to binding
   requirements.

## Market Segments

### Active Debris Removal (ADR) — ~$8B TAM by 2035

Removal of existing non-functional objects that pose collision risk. Key
customers are government space agencies (JAXA, ESA, NASA, UKSA, DLR, CNES,
ISRO) rather than commercial operators, since the polluting entity typically
no longer exists or cannot be compelled to pay.

| Customer | Program | Estimated Value |
|----------|---------|:---------------:|
| JAXA | CRD2 (ADRAS-J/J2) | ¥13.2B |
| ESA | ClearSpace-1 | €120M |
| UKSA | COSMIC | ¥2B+ |
| NASA | OSAM portfolio | $1B+ (multi-year) |
| DLR | Potential ADR | €50–100M |

### End-of-Life Services (EOL) — ~$5B TAM by 2035

Pre-planned removal of satellites at operational end. Revenue model based
on per-satellite service contracts. The megaconstellations create a
recurring revenue stream: with ~3,000 satellites reaching EOL annually by
2030, at an estimated $1–2M per removal, the annual addressable market is
$3–6B.

### Life Extension — ~$2B TAM by 2035

Dock-and-drive services for GEO communications satellites. There are ~550
active GEO satellites, each worth $100M–$500M in replacement cost. Life
extension of 3–5 years justifies a ~$30M service fee per satellite.

### Refueling — ~$2B TAM by 2035

Propellant replenishment for both LEO and GEO satellites. Higher-margin
than dock-and-drive for GEO assets that retain functional propulsion.
US Space Force demand for tactical GEO maneuver capability adds a defense
premium.

4. A publicly-traded vehicle providing transparent access to capital.
