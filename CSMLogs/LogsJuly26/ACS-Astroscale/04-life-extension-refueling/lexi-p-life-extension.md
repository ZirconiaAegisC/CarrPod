# LEXI-P & REFLEX-J — Life Extension & Refueling Programs

## LEXI-P — Life Extension In-Orbit Platform

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Full Name | Life Extension In-Orbit Platform |
| Target Market | Commercial GEO satellite operators |
| Service Model | Dock-and-drive life extension |
| Status | Commercial offering, contract negotiation phase |
| Pipeline Value | ~¥1.5B initial contract |

### Service Description

LEXI-P uses a **dock-and-drive** approach: the servicer spacecraft docks
with the client satellite's launch vehicle adapter ring or apogee kick
motor nozzle, and assumes attitude control and station-keeping duties using
its own propulsion and reaction wheels.

Key service parameters:
- Life extension of 3–7 years, depending on client propellant status and
  servicer fuel load.
- Compatible with all major GEO satellite buses: Airbus Eurostar, Boeing
  702, SSL 1300, Thales Alenia Spacebus, Mitsubishi Electric DS2000.
- No modification required to the client satellite.
- Service can be initiated while the client satellite remains operational,
  avoiding revenue interruption.

### Market Positioning

LEXI-P competes with Northrop Grumman's Mission Extension Vehicle (MEV),
which has demonstrated the dock-and-drive model with MEV-1 (docked to
Intelsat 901 in 2020) and MEV-2 (Intelsat 1002 in 2021).

Astroscale's differentiation strategy:
- **Lower cost:** Targeting ~30% below MEV pricing through smaller servicer
  design and Japanese manufacturing cost base.
- **Multi-client capability:** A single LEXI-P servicer can undock from one
  client and redock with another, unlike MEV which remains attached.
- **Upgrade path:** LEXI-P servicers are designed to be refueled by APS-R,
  creating an integrated servicing ecosystem.

## REFLEX-J — Refueling Experiment Japan

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Full Name | Refueling Experiment Japan |
| Funding | JAXA |
| Phase | Technology study |
| Contract Value | ~¥1.2B |
| Objective | Demonstrate on-orbit propellant transfer |

### Technology Focus

REFLEX-J is a **pathfinder for on-orbit refueling technology** intended to
mature the fluid-transfer systems that will be operationalized on APS-R and
future commercial refueling missions.

Key technology areas:
1. **Zero-G Fluid Dynamics:** Understanding propellant behavior in
   microgravity during transfer — slosh, ullage collapse, bubble ingestion
   risk.
2. **Hermetic Quick-Disconnect:** Development of a Japanese-sourced
   quick-disconnect coupling competitive with Orbit Fab's RAFTI.
3. **Propellant Mass Gauging:** Techniques for accurately measuring
   propellant quantity before, during, and after transfer in microgravity.
4. **Contamination Control:** Preventing propellant leakage from
   contaminating the client satellite's solar arrays, thermal radiators,
   and optical sensors.

### Strategic Rationale

REFLEX-J ensures that Japan maintains a sovereign capability in on-orbit
refueling, reducing dependence on US-developed interfaces (RAFTI). It also
positions Astroscale to offer refueling services to Japanese government
satellites (QZSS navigation, Himawari weather, IGS reconnaissance), which
may require domestic technology for national security reasons.
