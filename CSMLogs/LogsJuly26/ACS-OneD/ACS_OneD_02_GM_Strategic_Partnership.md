# ACS OneD Battery Sciences | GM Strategic Partnership & EV Qualification

## 1. Partnership Genesis

### Announcement and Structure
In September 2022, General Motors and OneD Battery Sciences announced a strategic partnership to integrate SINANODE technology into GM's Ultium electric vehicle battery platform. The partnership encompasses a joint development program, pilot production validation at GM battery manufacturing facilities, and a commercial framework for SINANODE process licensing across GM's battery operations.

### Strategic Drivers for GM
GM's decision to partner with OneD was driven by several strategic factors:

1. **Capital Efficiency**: SINANODE's drop-in process eliminates the need for GM to invest in dedicated silicon anode material factories — a multi-hundred-million-dollar capital avoidance versus building or contracting for silicon anode material supply.

2. **Supply Chain Control**: By integrating silicon addition at GM-owned battery plants rather than purchasing silicon anode material from third parties, GM retains control over a critical cell performance lever. This contrasts with competitors who depend on Sila or Group14 for silicon anode material supply.

3. **Ultium Platform Flexibility**: The SINANODE process works across all Ultium cell formats (pouch, prismatic, and cylindrical), enabling silicon enhancement across GM's entire EV portfolio without format-specific anode material qualification.

4. **Cost Trajectory**: Process licensing shifts cost from capital-intensive material procurement to an operating expense model. Long-term silicon precursor costs (silane) are projected to decline as the silane supply chain scales for semiconductor and battery industries.

5. **Vertical Integration Philosophy**: GM's battery strategy emphasizes vertical integration from cell manufacturing through vehicle production. SINANODE extends this control deeper into the anode manufacturing step.

### Strategic Drivers for OneD
- **Anchor Customer**: GM provides the scale, credibility, and validation rigor required to commercialize a battery process technology
- **Pilot Facility Access**: GM's battery manufacturing facilities provide real-world production environments for process validation — conditions impossible to replicate in a startup lab
- **Volume Commitment**: GM's planned battery production volume (hundreds of GWh annually by 2030) provides the commercial pull to scale silane supply chains and process equipment
- **Industry Validation**: A successful GM partnership creates pull from other automakers who require silicon anode capability but lack GM's vertical integration scale

## 2. Joint Development Program

### Program Phases
The GM-OneD joint development program follows a structured qualification sequence:

**Phase 1: Process Feasibility (2022-2023)**
- SINANODE process demonstrated on GM-specified graphite grades
- Electrochemical validation in coin cells and single-layer pouch cells
- Slurry mixing process parameters optimized for GM's anode manufacturing equipment

**Phase 2: Pilot Validation (2023-2024)**
- SINANODE process integrated into pilot-scale anode coating line
- Multi-layer pouch cells (5-10 Ah) manufactured and tested
- Formation protocol optimization for silicon-containing anodes
- Safety testing: nail penetration, overcharge, thermal runaway propagation

**Phase 3: Production Pilot (2024-2025)**
- SINANODE process deployed on production-representative mixing and coating equipment
- Full-size Ultium cells (>100 Ah) manufactured with silicon-enhanced anodes
- Pack-level testing including vibration, thermal cycling, and accelerated aging
- Manufacturing quality metrics and statistical process control established

**Phase 4: Commercial Readiness (2025-2026)**
- Process specification and control limits finalized
- Silane supply chain qualified for production volumes
- Cost model validated at production scale
- Vehicle integration testing (mule vehicles, pre-production builds)

## 3. Ultium Platform Integration

### Cell Format Compatibility
GM's Ultium platform supports multiple cell formats, each with specific anode manufacturing parameters. SINANODE has been tested across:

- **Large-format pouch cells** (GM-LG Energy Solution JV, Lordstown, OH): Primary target format. Large pouch cells provide maximum energy density benefit from silicon enhancement.
- **Prismatic cells** (GM-Samsung SDI JV, New Carlisle, IN): Secondary target. Prismatic format requires different slurry viscosity and coating parameters.
- **Cylindrical cells** (potential future format): SINANODE process concept compatible; slurry coating parameters adapted for cylindrical electrode winding.

### Chemistry Compatibility
SINANODE silicon-enhanced anodes have been validated with cathode chemistries across the Ultium roadmap:
- **NMC811**: Current Ultium cathode chemistry; full compatibility demonstrated
- **NMC9xx (ultra-high nickel)**: Future Ultium chemistry; stability testing underway
- **LMFP (lithium manganese iron phosphate)**: Lower-cost Ultium variant; silicon enhancement is particularly valuable for LFP-based cells where energy density is the primary limitation

### Factory Integration Planning
GM's battery manufacturing joint venture plants are designed with modular mixing and coating lines. SINANODE integration involves:
1. Installation of silane precursor delivery system adjacent to existing slurry mixers
2. Addition of gas handling and abatement systems for silane safety
3. Integration of process monitoring and control systems into existing factory MES (Manufacturing Execution System)
4. Operator training on silane handling and SINANODE process parameters

| General Motors | Process integration (SINANODE) | OneD Battery Sciences |

| Porsche | Silicon anode material (SCC55) | Group14 Technologies |
| Tesla | Internal development (silicon in 4680) | Internal |

### Strategic Assessment
GM's SINANODE approach is optimized for capital efficiency and supply chain control, at the cost of longer development timeline versus simply buying silicon anode material. The partnership commits GM to a specific technology path; if OneD's process fails to meet production requirements, GM's alternative silicon anode strategies are less mature.

## 5. Commercial Framework

### Licensing Structure
While specific financial terms are confidential, the OneD licensing model for GM is understood to include:
- Upfront technology access and development fees
- Milestone payments tied to qualification phase completion
- Per-kWh royalty on cells manufactured with SINANODE process
- Annual minimum royalty commitments post-commercialization

### Exclusivity Considerations
The partnership agreement likely includes a time-limited exclusivity period for specific cell formats or chemistries, after which OneD can license the technology to other automakers. The duration and scope of exclusivity directly impact OneD's ability to build a multi-customer licensing business.
