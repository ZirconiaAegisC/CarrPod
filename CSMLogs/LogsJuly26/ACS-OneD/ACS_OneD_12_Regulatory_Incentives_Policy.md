# ACS OneD Battery Sciences | Regulatory Environment & Policy Incentives

## 1. US Policy Landscape for Battery Manufacturing

### Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) of 2022
The IRA is the most significant US industrial policy for battery manufacturing in decades. Key provisions relevant to SINANODE:

**Section 45X: Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit**
- Provides a production tax credit (PTC) for battery cells manufactured in the United States: $35/kWh for battery cells
- Battery module production: $10/kWh
- Electrode active materials: 10% of production cost
- Critical minerals processing: 10% of production cost

**Impact on SINANODE**:
- The cell-level PTC improves the economics of US-based cell manufacturing, increasing the addressable market for SINANODE process licensing
- Cell manufacturers receiving 45X credits have additional margin to invest in silicon enhancement technology
- OneD's licensing model doesn't directly capture IRA credits, but licensees' improved economics enable faster adoption

**Section 48C: Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit**
- Investment tax credit of up to 30% for qualifying advanced energy manufacturing facilities
- Covers battery manufacturing facilities, including anode and cathode production
- Allocated through competitive application process ($10B authorized)

**Impact on SINANODE**:
- Licensees can apply 48C credits to SINANODE equipment installation (silane systems, modified mixers)
- Reduces the capital cost barrier for SINANODE deployment from $2-5M to effectively $1.4-3.5M per production line

### Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL) Battery Materials Processing Grants
- $3.1B for battery materials processing and battery manufacturing grants
- Group14 received $100M grant for BAM-2 silicon anode material facility
- These grants are primarily targeted at material manufacturing, not process technology — SINANODE's process licensing model does not directly qualify for material processing grants

### US Department of Energy Loan Programs Office (LPO)
- Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) loan program expanded under IRA
- Provides low-cost debt financing for vehicle and component manufacturing facilities
- Could finance SINANODE deployment at scale as part of broader cell manufacturing facility financing

## 2. Foreign Entity of Concern (FEOC) Regulations

### FEOC Definition and Timeline
IRA battery credits phase in restrictions on battery components and critical minerals from Foreign Entities of Concern (FEOC), defined to include China, Russia, North Korea, and Iran:

- **2024**: Battery components from FEOC disqualify vehicles from IRA consumer tax credit
- **2025**: Critical minerals from FEOC disqualify vehicles
- Enforcement mechanism: Consumer clean vehicle credit ($7,500 per vehicle) requires increasing domestic/FTA country content

### Relevance to SINANODE
SINANODE's position in the FEOC framework is favorable:
- SINANODE process technology is US-developed and owned (OneD is a US company)
- The process is deployed at US-based battery factories operated by US or allied-country companies (GM-LGES JV, GM-Samsung SDI JV)
- Silane precursor can be sourced from US producers (REC Silicon, Moses Lake) or FTA countries (South Korea, Japan)
- Graphite supply remains the critical mineral FEOC concern; SINANODE is graphite-supplier-agnostic and can utilize FTA-compliant graphite

### Competitive Advantage Under FEOC
OneD's US-based process technology has an intrinsic advantage over Chinese silicon anode material suppliers in the IRA/FEOC regulatory environment. Chinese silicon anode material companies (BTR, Shanshan, Putailai) are potentially excluded from US automotive supply chains under FEOC rules, creating a protected market window for Western silicon anode technologies.

## 3. European Regulatory Environment

### EU Battery Regulation (2023)
The EU Battery Regulation establishes requirements for batteries sold in the European market:
- Carbon footprint declaration and maximum thresholds
- Recycled content requirements for critical minerals (cobalt, lithium, nickel)
- Due diligence requirements for supply chain human rights and environmental impacts
- Digital battery passport with performance and durability information

### Relevance to SINANODE
- SINANODE's energy density improvement reduces the carbon footprint per kWh of battery capacity
- Process integration at EU-based cell factories supports local manufacturing requirements
- Silicon nanowire technology does not introduce additional critical minerals (silicon is abundant and non-toxic)

### EU Battery Manufacturing Support
- Important Projects of Common European Interest (IPCEI) for battery manufacturing provides state aid approval for national battery subsidies
- European Battery Alliance supports development of EU battery manufacturing ecosystem
- Member state-level incentives for battery factory construction (e.g., Germany, France, Hungary)

## 4. Technology Neutrality of Incentives

### Policy Risk Assessment
Battery manufacturing incentives (IRA 45X, EU state aid) are technology-neutral with respect to anode chemistry — they do not differentiate between graphite, silicon-enhanced, or other anode technologies. This neutrality means:
- SINANODE adoption is driven by commercial factors (energy density, cost) rather than policy preferences
- There is no regulatory "cliff" that creates urgency for silicon anode adoption
- Policy risk is low: existing incentives are not contingent on specific anode technology choices

### Potential Future Policy Catalysts
- **Domestic silicon anode supply chain requirements**: Future IRA amendments could require domestic silicon anode technology for full credit eligibility, benefiting SINANODE's US-based process technology
- **Performance-based standards**: Fuel economy or EV range standards that indirectly drive silicon anode adoption by incentivizing higher energy density
- **Industrial decarbonization incentives**: Expanded incentives for process technologies that reduce industrial carbon footprint (SINANODE's integration model avoids building new factories, reducing embodied carbon)

## 5. Compliance and Permitting

### Battery Factory Permitting with Silane
Adding silane handling to a battery factory requires environmental and safety permitting:
- **Air quality permits**: Silane combustion products (SiO₂ particulate) require emissions permitting; silane abatement systems designed to meet particulate emission limits
- **Hazardous materials permits**: Silane storage above threshold quantities triggers Tier II reporting under EPCRA; Process Safety Management (PSM) requirements for quantities above 10,000 lbs
- **Fire code compliance**: NFPA 318 and local fire code requirements for hazardous production materials

### Permitting Timeline
Permit modification to add silane handling to an existing battery factory is estimated at 6-12 months, which should be factored into SINANODE deployment planning. New battery factories can incorporate silane handling into the initial permit application, reducing timeline impact.

### Liability Considerations
OneD's licensing model raises questions of liability allocation between the technology licensor and the manufacturer-licensee:
- Process safety: Licensee is the operator and bears primary responsibility for safe silane handling
- Product performance: Warranty responsibility for cell performance rests with the cell manufacturer; OneD's license includes process specifications that, if followed, should produce in-specification anodes
- IP indemnification: OneD provides patent infringement indemnification to licensees, a standard provision in technology licensing agreements
