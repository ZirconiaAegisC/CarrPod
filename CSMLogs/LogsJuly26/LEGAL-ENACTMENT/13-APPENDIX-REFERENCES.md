================================================================================
                      APPENDIX — REFERENCES AND SOURCES
================================================================================

                   DOMESTIC CRITICAL MINERALS INDEPENDENCE ACT
                   HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, 119TH CONGRESS
                                2ND SESSION, 2026

================================================================================

This appendix sets forth the principal data sources, statutory
references, and factual predicates upon which the Domestic Critical
Minerals Independence Act of 2026 is based. All data cited in this
Act are drawn from the following sources as of calendar year 2025
and early 2026, except where otherwise noted.

================================================================================

                    PART I — GOVERNMENT DATA SOURCES

    A. UNITED STATES GEOLOGICAL SURVEY (USGS)

        1. Mineral Commodity Summaries 2025, U.S. Geological Survey,
           January 2025. Lithium chapter, pp. 104-105. Provides:
           (a) Global lithium mine production data by country;
           (b) United States lithium production, consumption, and
               import dependence data;
           (c) Global lithium reserves and resources estimates;
           (d) Lithium price data and market analysis.

        2. Mineral Commodity Summaries 2026, U.S. Geological Survey,
           January 2026. Updated production, consumption, and
           trade data for lithium and other critical minerals.

        3. USGS Professional Paper 1802: Critical Mineral Resources
           of the United States — Economic and Environmental Geology
           and Prospects for Future Supply, 2017.

        4. Smackover Formation Lithium Resource Assessment, USGS
           Southern States Energy Board cooperative assessment,
           2023-2025.

    B. DEPARTMENT OF ENERGY (DOE)

        1. Critical Materials Assessment, U.S. Department of Energy,
           Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy, July
           2023. Provides comprehensive assessment of supply risk
           and importance to clean energy technologies for all
           critical materials.

        2. National Blueprint for Lithium Batteries 2021-2030,
           Federal Consortium for Advanced Batteries, June 2021.
           Establishes national vision for lithium battery supply
           chain development.

        3. Lithium-Ion Battery Supply Chain for Electric Drive
           Vehicles: Technical Assessment, DOE Vehicle Technologies
           Office, 2024.

        4. Quadrennial Technology Review, Chapter 6: Innovating
           Clean Energy Technologies in Advanced Manufacturing,
           DOE, 2015 (updated 2020).

    C. DEPARTMENT OF DEFENSE (DOD)

        1. DOD Critical Minerals Strategy, Office of the Under
           Secretary of Defense for Acquisition and Sustainment,
           2023.

        2. Assessing and Strengthening the Manufacturing and
           Defense Industrial Base and Supply Chain Resiliency
           of the United States, Report to President, Executive
           Order 13806, September 2018.

        3. Defense Production Act Title III Annual Reports to
           Congress, FY2020-FY2025.

        4. Quadrennial Defense Review Reports, 2014, 2018.

    D. OTHER GOVERNMENT SOURCES

        1. International Energy Agency (IEA), Global Critical
           Minerals Outlook 2024, May 2024. Comprehensive global
           assessment of critical mineral supply chains and
           projected demand.

        2. IEA, The Role of Critical Minerals in Clean Energy
           Transitions, World Energy Outlook Special Report,
           2021 (updated 2023).

        3. Congressional Research Service, Critical Minerals and
           U.S. Public Policy, CRS Report R47982, updated
           quarterly.

        4. Government Accountability Office, Critical Minerals:
           Building a Domestic Supply Chain, GAO-24-106452,
           2024.

                      PART II — INDUSTRY AND MARKET DATA

    A. LITHIUM MARKET DATA

        1. Benchmark Mineral Intelligence, Lithium Ion Battery
           Supply Chain Database, Q4 2025.

        2. S&P Global Commodity Insights, Lithium Market Service,
           2025 Annual Review.

        3. Fastmarkets, Lithium Supply and Demand Forecasts,
           Q1 2026.

        4. Albemarle Corporation, Annual Reports and SEC Filings,
           FY2020-FY2025. Provides production data for Silver
           Peak, Nevada, and other global operations.

    B. PROJECT-SPECIFIC DATA

        1. Lithium Americas Corp., Thacker Pass Project: Feasibility
           Study and National Instrument 43-101 Technical Report,
           2023-2025. Confirms 13.7 million metric tons LCE
           measured and indicated resources.

        2. Standard Lithium Ltd., Smackover Formation Arkansas
           Projects: Preliminary Feasibility Study, 2024. Confirms
           lithium concentrations of 100-500 mg/L in Smackover
           brines.

        3. Controlled Thermal Resources Ltd., Hell's Kitchen
           Lithium and Power Project, Salton Sea, CA: Technical
           Reports and Environmental Impact Statements, 2022-2025.

        4. Berkshire Hathaway Energy Renewables, Salton Sea
           Geothermal Lithium Recovery Demonstration Project
           Status Reports, 2023-2025.

        5. ioneer Ltd., Rhyolite Ridge Lithium-Boron Project:
           Definitive Feasibility Study, 2023.

               PART III — STATUTORY AND REGULATORY REFERENCES

    A. THE INFLATION REDUCTION ACT OF 2022 (Public Law 117-169)

        1. Section 45X: Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit,
           26 U.S.C. 45X. Provides production tax credits for
           battery components manufactured in the United States.

        2. Section 30D: Clean Vehicle Credit with domestic content
           requirements for critical minerals and battery
           components.

        3. Section 48C: Qualifying Advanced Energy Project Credit,
           including support for critical material processing
           facilities.

    B. THE DEFENSE PRODUCTION ACT OF 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4501 et seq.)

        1. Title I: Priorities and Allocations Authority.

        2. Title III: Expansion of Productive Capacity and Supply,
           50 U.S.C. 4531-4533. Authority for direct purchases,
           purchase commitments, loans, and loan guarantees for
           critical materials.

        3. Title VII: General Provisions, 50 U.S.C. 4551-4568,
           including reporting requirements.

    C. FIXING AMERICA'S SURFACE TRANSPORTATION ACT (Public Law
       114-94)

        1. Title 41, FAST-41: Federal Permitting Improvement,
           42 U.S.C. 4370m. Established the Federal Infrastructure
           Permitting Dashboard and coordinated review process.

    D. OTHER PERTINENT STATUTES

        1. National Environmental Policy Act of 1969,
           42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.

        2. Clean Water Act, 33 U.S.C. 1251 et seq.

        3. Clean Air Act, 42 U.S.C. 7401 et seq.

        4. Endangered Species Act, 16 U.S.C. 1531 et seq.

        5. Mining Law of 1872, 30 U.S.C. 22 et seq.

        6. Energy Policy Act of 2005, Title XVII loan guarantee
           program, 42 U.S.C. 16511 et seq.

        7. Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007, Section
           136 ATVM program, 42 U.S.C. 17013.

        8. Energy Act of 2020, Section 7001 et seq., critical
           minerals provisions.
