    SEC. 301. DOMESTIC LITHIUM HYDROXIDE CONVERSION FACILITIES.

        (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

            (1) Lithium hydroxide is the preferred lithium chemical
                for high-nickel cathode chemistries that dominate
                next-generation electric vehicle battery designs,
                including NMC 811, NMC 955, and NCA cathodes;

            (2) The United States currently possesses less than three
                percent (3%) of global lithium hydroxide conversion
                capacity, with no commercial-scale lithium hydroxide
                conversion facility operating within the United States
                as of the date of enactment of this Act;

            (3) Conversion of lithium carbonate or lithium chloride
                intermediate products to battery-grade lithium
                hydroxide monohydrate requires specialized
                crystallizer, reactor, and purification systems
                representing capital investments of approximately
                five hundred million dollars ($500,000,000) to one
                billion dollars ($1,000,000,000) for a facility
                with annual capacity of twenty-five thousand (25,000)
                metric tons.

        (b) CONVERSION FACILITY GRANT PROGRAM. —

            (1) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Energy shall
                establish a competitive grant program to support
                the construction and commissioning of domestic
                lithium hydroxide conversion facilities.

            (2) ELIGIBLE COSTS. — Grants may fund up to thirty-five
                percent (35%) of eligible capital costs for qualifying
                conversion facilities.

            (3) MINIMUM CAPACITY. — To be eligible for funding, a
                facility shall have a planned annual production
                capacity of not less than ten thousand (10,000)
                metric tons of battery-grade lithium hydroxide
                monohydrate.

            (4) PREFERENCE. — The Secretary shall give preference in
                grant awards to facilities that:
                    (A) Are co-located with domestic lithium
                        extraction operations;
                    (B) Utilize renewable energy or other low-carbon
                        energy sources for process heat and power;
                    (C) Include water recycling and zero liquid
                        discharge systems;
                    (D) Commit to employing workers at prevailing
                        wage rates and utilizing registered
                        apprenticeship programs.

        (c) NUMBER OF FACILITIES. — The Secretary shall endeavor to
        support the construction and operation of not fewer than five
        (5) domestic lithium hydroxide conversion facilities with
        aggregate annual production capacity of not less than one
        hundred twenty-five thousand (125,000) metric tons by 2035.

    SEC. 302. CATHODE AND ANODE ACTIVE MATERIAL MANUFACTURING.

        (a) CATHODE ACTIVE MATERIAL (CAM) MANUFACTURING. —

            (1) GRANT PROGRAM. — The Secretary of Energy shall
                establish a competitive grant program to support
                the construction and expansion of domestic cathode
                active material manufacturing facilities.

            (2) TARGET CAPACITIES. — The grant program shall target
                the establishment of domestic manufacturing capacity
                for:
                    (A) Not less than three hundred thousand
                        (300,000) metric tons per year of cathode
                        active material by 2035;
                    (B) Not less than two hundred thousand (200,000)
                        metric tons per year of anode active
                        material by 2035.

            (3) CHEMISTRY COVERAGE. — The program shall support
                manufacturing capacity for multiple cathode
                chemistries including:
                    (A) Lithium nickel manganese cobalt oxide
                        (NMC) in multiple stoichiometries;
                    (B) Lithium nickel cobalt aluminum oxide (NCA);
                    (C) Lithium iron phosphate (LFP);
                    (D) Lithium manganese iron phosphate (LMFP).

        (b) ANODE ACTIVE MATERIAL (AAM) MANUFACTURING. —

            (1) The grant program shall support domestic manufacturing
                capacity for:
                    (A) Synthetic graphite anode material;
                    (B) Natural graphite purification facilities;
                    (C) Silicon-based and silicon-composite anode
                        materials;
                    (D) Lithium metal anode foil production.

            (2) The Secretary shall coordinate with the Department of
                Defense to ensure that domestic anode material
                manufacturing capacity is sufficient to meet both
                commercial and defense requirements.

        (c) PREVAILING WAGE AND APPRENTICESHIP. — As a condition of
        receiving any grant or loan guarantee under this section,
        a facility shall comply with prevailing wage requirements
        under the Davis-Bacon Act (40 U.S.C. 3141 et seq.) during
        construction and shall utilize registered apprenticeship
        programs meeting the standards of Section 45X(b) of the
        Internal Revenue Code.

    SEC. 303. LOAN GUARANTEES AND GRANT PROGRAMS.

        (a) TITLE XVII LOAN GUARANTEE PROGRAM EXPANSION. —

            (1) The Secretary of Energy is directed to expand the
                Title XVII Innovative Technology Loan Guarantee
                Program (42 U.S.C. 16511 et seq.) to include as
                eligible projects:
                    (A) Lithium mining and beneficiation facilities;
                    (B) Lithium hydroxide and lithium carbonate
                        conversion facilities;
                    (C) Cathode active material and anode active
                        material manufacturing facilities;
                    (D) Direct Lithium Extraction technology
                        deployment at commercial scale;
                    (E) Battery recycling and secondary material
                        recovery facilities.

            (2) The Secretary shall establish a dedicated Critical
                Minerals Processing loan guarantee facility within
                the Title XVII program with loan authority of not
                less than fifteen billion dollars ($15,000,000,000).

            (3) The Secretary shall establish streamlined application
                procedures for projects with total capital costs
                below five hundred million dollars ($500,000,000).

        (b) CRITICAL MINERALS PROCESSING GRANT PROGRAM. —

            (1) The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the
                Secretary of Commerce, shall establish a Critical
                Minerals Processing Grant Program providing direct
                grant assistance for:
                    (A) Feasibility studies and front-end engineering
                        design for processing facilities;
                    (B) Construction and commissioning of processing
                        facilities;
                    (C) Retrofitting of existing chemical processing
                        facilities for critical mineral production;
                    (D) Process intensification and cost reduction
                        technology deployment.

            (2) Total grant authority under this subsection shall not
                exceed ten billion dollars ($10,000,000,000) for the
                period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036.

        (c) STATE AND LOCAL INCENTIVE COORDINATION. — The Secretary
        of Commerce shall establish an interagency working group to
        coordinate Federal loan guarantees and grants with State and
        local economic development incentives to maximize the
        effectiveness of public investment in critical mineral
        processing infrastructure.

    SEC. 304. STRATEGIC PROCESSING RESERVE.

        (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is hereby established within the
        Department of Energy a Strategic Processing Reserve Program
        to maintain minimum domestic processing capacity for lithium
        and other critical battery minerals during periods of market
        disruption or predatory pricing by foreign state-owned
        enterprises.

        (b) MECHANISM. — The Program shall operate through:

            (1) Processing Capacity Contracts. — The Secretary of
                Energy may enter into contracts with domestic
                processing facilities guaranteeing a minimum
                processing volume at a floor price sufficient to
                maintain operational viability during market
                disruptions;

            (2) Toll Processing Agreements. — The Secretary may
                enter into toll processing agreements with domestic
                facilities to process strategic stockpile materials;

            (3) Standby Capacity Payments. — The Secretary may make
                periodic payments to maintain idle processing
                capacity in a state of operational readiness for
                rapid reactivation.

        (c) TRIGGER CONDITIONS. — The Secretary may activate
        Processing Capacity Contracts and Standby Capacity Payments
        upon a determination that:

            (1) Market prices for lithium chemicals have fallen
                below the domestic industry estimated sustainable
                production cost for a period of not less than three
                (3) consecutive months;

            (2) Foreign state-owned or state-directed enterprises
                are engaged in below-cost sales of lithium chemicals
                in United States or allied nation markets;

            (3) A national emergency or supply chain disruption
                threatens the availability of lithium chemicals
                for defense requirements.

        (d) FUNDING. — There is authorized to be appropriated to the
        Secretary of Energy two billion dollars ($2,000,000,000) for
        the Strategic Processing Reserve Program for the period of
        fiscal years 2027 through 2036.
