    SEC. 701. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

        (a) TOTAL AUTHORIZATION. — There is hereby authorized to be
        appropriated for the programs and activities authorized under
        this Act a total of fifty billion dollars ($50,000,000,000)
        for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036, allocated
        as follows:

            (1) TITLE I — DOMESTIC LITHIUM MINING. — Three billion
                dollars ($3,000,000,000) for permitting acceleration,
                environmental reviews, tribal consultation, and
                infrastructure support for domestic lithium mining.

            (2) TITLE II — DLE TECHNOLOGY. — Five billion dollars
                ($5,000,000,000) for Direct Lithium Extraction
                research, development, demonstration, and deployment.

            (3) TITLE III — PROCESSING INFRASTRUCTURE. — Fifteen
                billion dollars ($15,000,000,000) for loan guarantees,
                grants, and direct investment in lithium processing
                and battery material manufacturing facilities.

            (4) TITLE IV — BATTERY RECYCLING. — Four billion dollars
                ($4,000,000,000) for battery collection infrastructure,
                recycling technology grants, and recycled content
                program administration.

            (5) TITLE V — INTERNATIONAL PARTNERSHIPS. — Seven hundred
                fifty million dollars ($750,000,000) for bilateral
                and multilateral critical minerals agreements,
                technical assistance, and diplomatic engagement.

            (6) TITLE VI — RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT. — Five billion
                two hundred fifty million dollars ($5,250,000,000)
                for battery chemistry research, manufacturing process
                development, materials substitution, and national
                laboratory coordination.

            (7) TITLE VIII — PERMITTING REFORM. — Two hundred fifty
                million dollars ($250,000,000) for permitting agency
                capacity building, staff expansion, and technology
                modernization.

            (8) TITLE IX — STRATEGIC STOCKPILE. — Twelve billion
                dollars ($12,000,000,000) for the National Lithium
                Stockpile, including acquisition, storage, and
                maintenance.

            (9) TITLE X — IMPLEMENTATION AND OVERSIGHT. — Two
                hundred fifty million dollars ($250,000,000) for
                program administration, oversight, and annual
                reporting.

            (10) EMERGENCY RESERVE. — Four billion dollars
                ($4,000,000,000) held in reserve for unanticipated
                supply chain disruptions, technology breakthroughs,
                or other emergent requirements to be released only
                upon certification by the Secretary of Energy and
                approval by the Committees on Appropriations of the
                House of Representatives and the Senate.

        (b) SUPPLEMENTAL APPROPRIATIONS. — Amounts authorized under
        this Act are in addition to, and not in lieu of, amounts
        otherwise authorized or appropriated for critical mineral
        activities under other provisions of law.

        (c) OFF-BUDGET AUTHORITY. — Appropriations for strategic
        stockpile acquisition under Title IX shall be classified as
        emergency requirements for purposes of budget enforcement.

    SEC. 702. ADVANCED MANUFACTURING PRODUCTION CREDIT EXTENSION.

        (a) EXTENSION OF CREDIT. — Section 45X of the Internal Revenue
        Code of 1986 (26 U.S.C. 45X) is amended as follows:

            (1) The phase-out provision of Section 45X(b)(3)(B) is
                amended to extend the phase-out commencement date
                from December 31, 2029, to December 31, 2039;

            (2) The phase-out schedule is amended to provide for
                a seventy-five percent (75%) credit rate for calendar
                years 2030 through 2032, a fifty percent (50%) credit
                rate for calendar years 2033 through 2035, a
                twenty-five percent (25%) credit rate for calendar
                years 2036 through 2039, and a zero percent (0%)
                credit rate thereafter.

        (b) EXPANSION OF ELIGIBLE COMPONENTS. — Section 45X(c) is
        amended to include as eligible components:

            (1) Battery-grade lithium carbonate produced in the
                United States, with a credit amount of ten percent
                (10%) of the cost of production;

            (2) Battery-grade lithium hydroxide monohydrate produced
                in the United States, with a credit amount of ten
                percent (10%) of the cost of production;

            (3) Cathode active material produced in the United States,
                with a credit amount equal to ten percent (10%) of
                the cost of production, except that for cathode
                active material produced with at least fifty percent
                (50%) domestically sourced critical mineral content,
                the credit amount shall be fifteen percent (15%);

            (4) Anode active material produced in the United States,
                with a credit amount equal to the product of ten
                percent (10%) and the cost of production.

        (c) LABOR REQUIREMENTS. — The prevailing wage and
        apprenticeship requirements of Section 45X(b)(7) and (8)
        shall apply to any credit claimed under this section.

    SEC. 703. INVESTMENT TAX CREDIT FOR CRITICAL MINERAL PROCESSING.

        (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — Subpart E of part IV of subchapter A of
        chapter 1 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 is amended by
        adding at the end the following new section:

            "SEC. 48J. CRITICAL MINERAL PROCESSING INVESTMENT CREDIT.

            (a) ALLOWANCE OF CREDIT. — For purposes of section 46,
                the critical mineral processing investment credit
                for any taxable year is an amount equal to thirty
                percent (30%) of the qualified investment for such
                taxable year with respect to any qualified critical
                mineral processing facility.

            (b) QUALIFIED INVESTMENT. — The term 'qualified investment'
                means, with respect to any taxable year, the basis of
                any qualified critical mineral processing facility
                placed in service by the taxpayer during such taxable
                year.

            (c) QUALIFIED CRITICAL MINERAL PROCESSING FACILITY. —
                The term 'qualified critical mineral processing
                facility' means a facility located in the United
                States that:
                    (1) Processes lithium-bearing ores, brines,
                        clays, or recycled materials to produce
                        lithium carbonate, lithium hydroxide,
                        or lithium metal;
                    (2) Produces cathode active material or anode
                        active material for use in lithium-ion
                        batteries;
                    (3) Recycles lithium-ion batteries or battery
                        manufacturing scrap to recover lithium,
                        cobalt, nickel, manganese, or graphite.

            (d) LABOR REQUIREMENTS. — The provisions of section
                48(a)(10) shall apply to credits under this section."

        (b) EFFECTIVE DATE. — The amendment made by this section
        shall apply to property placed in service after December 31,
        2026.

    SEC. 704. DEPARTMENT OF ENERGY LOAN PROGRAM EXPANSION.

        (a) TITLE XVII AUTHORITY. — Section 1702 of the Energy Policy
        Act of 2005 (42 U.S.C. 16512) is amended:

            (1) In subsection (a), by adding at the end:
                "Critical mineral mining, processing, and recycling
                projects";

            (2) By adding at the end a new subsection providing
                that the Secretary shall establish a dedicated
                application and review track for critical mineral
                projects, with a target review period of not more
                than one hundred eighty (180) days from application
                acceptance to conditional commitment.

        (b) ATVM PROGRAM EXPANSION. — Section 136 of the Energy
        Independence and Security Act of 2007 (42 U.S.C. 17013) is
        amended to authorize the Secretary of Energy to make loans
        under the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing
        (ATVM) program for:

            (1) Domestic lithium mining and processing facilities
                that directly supply United States-based battery
                or vehicle manufacturing operations;

            (2) Construction of domestic battery cathode and anode
                active material manufacturing facilities;

            (3) Expansion of domestic battery recycling facilities.

        (c) APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be appropriated
        to the Secretary of Energy ten billion dollars
        ($10,000,000,000) for the cost of loan guarantees under the
        programs amended by this section, to remain available until
        expended.
