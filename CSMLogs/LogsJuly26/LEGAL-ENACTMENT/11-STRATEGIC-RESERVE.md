================================================================================
             TITLE IX — NATIONAL STRATEGIC LITHIUM STOCKPILE
================================================================================

    SEC. 901. ESTABLISHMENT AND PURPOSE.

        (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is hereby established in the
        Department of Energy a National Strategic Lithium Stockpile
        for the purpose of ensuring a reliable domestic supply of
        lithium for national defense, essential civilian demand, and
        emergency response requirements.

        (b) ADMINISTRATION. — The Stockpile shall be administered by
        the Secretary of Energy, acting through a newly established
        Strategic Lithium Stockpile Office, in coordination with:

            (1) The Secretary of Defense, for defense-related
                stockpile requirements;

            (2) The Secretary of Commerce, for civilian industrial
                base assessments;

            (3) The Secretary of State, for international stockpile
                coordination and allied nation access;

            (4) The Secretary of Homeland Security, for emergency
                response planning.

        (c) PURPOSE. — The Stockpile shall serve the following
        purposes:

            (1) To ensure that the Department of Defense and the
                defense industrial base have uninterrupted access
                to lithium for all military requirements during any
                national emergency or supply chain disruption;

            (2) To protect the domestic economy from supply
                disruptions, predatory pricing by foreign state-
                owned enterprises, and price volatility in
                international lithium markets;

            (3) To provide a buffer stock that enables the United
                States to meet its obligations under international
                critical minerals agreements and mutual defense
                treaties;

            (4) To serve as a market stabilization mechanism through
                strategic acquisition and release operations.

        (d) STOCKPILE COMPOSITION. — The Stockpile shall hold:

            (1) Battery-grade lithium carbonate (minimum 99.5% purity);

            (2) Battery-grade lithium hydroxide monohydrate (minimum
                56.5% lithium hydroxide content);

            (3) Such lithium metal and other lithium compounds as the
                Secretary of Energy determines necessary.

        (e) STORAGE LOCATIONS. — Stockpile materials shall be stored
        at not fewer than three (3) geographically dispersed, secure
        storage locations within the United States, with at least
        one (1) location in the eastern United States, at least one
        (1) location in the central United States, and at least one
        (1) location in the western United States.

================================================================================

    SEC. 902. STOCKPILE TARGETS AND ACQUISITION.

        (a) STOCKPILE TARGETS. —

            (1) INITIAL TARGET. — Not later than December 31, 2030,
                the Stockpile shall hold not less than the equivalent
                of a ninety-day (90-day) supply of lithium based on
                projected domestic demand for calendar year 2030.

            (2) INTERMEDIATE TARGET. — Not later than December 31,
                2033, the Stockpile shall hold not less than the
                equivalent of a one hundred twenty-day (120-day)
                supply of lithium.

            (3) FULL TARGET. — Not later than December 31, 2035,
                the Stockpile shall hold not less than the equivalent
                of a one hundred eighty-day (180-day) supply of
                lithium based on projected domestic demand for
                calendar year 2035.

            (4) DEMAND ASSESSMENT. — The Secretary of Energy, in
                consultation with the Secretary of Defense and the
                Secretary of Commerce, shall annually publish
                projected domestic lithium demand for defense,
                energy, and industrial applications for the
                subsequent five, ten, and fifteen-year periods.

        (b) ACQUISITION REQUIREMENTS. —

            (1) DOMESTIC PURCHASE PREFERENCE. — To the maximum extent
                practicable, all lithium acquired for the Stockpile
                shall be:
                    (A) Extracted from domestic mineral resources;
                    (B) Processed in domestic facilities;
                    (C) Manufactured by entities incorporated in
                        the United States, Canada, Australia, or
                        another Minerals Security Partnership
                        partner nation.

            (2) PURCHASE AUTHORITY. — The Secretary of Energy may:
                    (A) Enter into multiyear procurement contracts
                        at fixed or index-linked prices;
                    (B) Purchase lithium on spot markets during
                        periods of low prices;
                    (C) Acquire lithium through royalty-in-kind
                        arrangements for mining on Federal lands;
                    (D) Require domestic processing facilities
                        receiving Federal support under this Act
                        to sell a specified percentage of production
                        to the Stockpile at formula-based prices.

            (3) ACQUISITION LIMITATIONS. — The Secretary shall not
                purchase lithium for the Stockpile when market prices
                exceed one hundred fifty percent (150%) of the
                trailing five-year average price, adjusted for
                inflation, unless the Secretary certifies to Congress
                that such purchases are necessary for national security.

        (c) APPROPRIATIONS. — There is authorized to be appropriated
        to the Secretary of Energy for Stockpile acquisition twelve
        billion dollars ($12,000,000,000) for fiscal years 2027
        through 2036, to remain available until expended.

    SEC. 903. RELEASE CONDITIONS AND MARKET STABILIZATION.

        (a) RELEASE CONDITIONS. — The Secretary of Energy may release
        materials from the Stockpile only upon:

            (1) PRESIDENTIAL DETERMINATION. — A determination by the
                President that the release is necessary:
                    (A) For national defense purposes during a
                        declared national emergency, war, or
                        military contingency operation;
                    (B) To respond to a severe supply disruption
                        that threatens the domestic economy or
                        energy security; or
                    (C) To meet United States obligations under a
                        mutual defense treaty or international
                        critical minerals agreement.

            (2) CONGRESSIONAL NOTIFICATION. — The President shall
                notify Congress not later than fifteen (15) days
                before any release of Stockpile materials, except
                that in the case of an emergency requiring immediate
                release, notice shall be provided not later than
                three (3) days after the release.

        (b) MARKET STABILIZATION MECHANISM. —

            (1) The Secretary of Energy may conduct market
                stabilization acquisitions and releases within
                established price bands to:
                    (A) Moderate extreme price volatility in
                        domestic lithium markets;
                    (B) Support domestic processing facilities
                        facing below-cost pricing by foreign
                        state-owned enterprises;
                    (C) Ensure adequate lithium supply for
                        domestic battery manufacturing during
                        periods of international supply disruption.

            (2) Market stabilization releases shall be conducted
                through public auction or competitive bidding
                procedures designed to maximize transparency and
                minimize market disruption.

        (c) RELEASE RECEIPTS. — Proceeds from Stockpile releases shall
        be deposited in the Strategic Lithium Stockpile Acquisition
        Fund and shall be available, without further appropriation,
        for future Stockpile acquisitions.

        (d) ROTATION. — The Secretary of Energy shall establish a
        stockpile rotation program to sell materials approaching
        the end of their storage life and replace them with new
        production, provided that at all times the Stockpile
        maintains the minimum inventory levels required under
        subsection 902(a) of this Title.

    SEC. 904. DEFENSE PRODUCTION ACT TITLE III AUTHORITY.

        (a) PRESIDENTIAL DETERMINATION. — The President is directed
        to make a determination under Section 303 of the Defense
        Production Act of 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4533) that:

            (1) Lithium is a critical material essential to the
                national defense;

            (2) Domestic industrial capacity for lithium extraction,
                processing, and manufacturing is insufficient to
                meet national defense requirements, creating a
                strategic vulnerability;

            (3) Without Presidential action under Title III of the
                Defense Production Act, United States industry
                cannot reasonably be expected to provide the needed
                capacity in a timely manner.

        (b) AUTHORIZED ACTIONS. — Pursuant to the determination under
        subsection (a), the President, acting through the Secretary
        of Defense and the Secretary of Energy, may:

            (1) Make direct purchases and purchase commitments for
                lithium and lithium products for the Stockpile;

            (2) Make loans and loan guarantees to domestic lithium
                mining, processing, and recycling facilities;

            (3) Enter into purchase agreements for lithium and
                lithium products at prices determined by the
                President to be necessary to incentivize domestic
                production;

            (4) Install additional equipment and facilities at
                government-owned or privately-owned facilities to
                expand domestic lithium production capacity.

        (c) SUPPLEMENTAL AUTHORITY. — The authorities granted under
        this section are in addition to, and not in lieu of,
        authority otherwise available under the Defense Production
        Act or any other provision of law.

        (d) REPORTING. — The President shall include a detailed
        accounting of actions taken under this section in the annual
        report to Congress required under Section 722 of the Defense
        Production Act.
