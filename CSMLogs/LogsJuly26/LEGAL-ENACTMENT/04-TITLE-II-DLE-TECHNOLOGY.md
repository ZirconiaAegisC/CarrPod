                TITLE II — DIRECT LITHIUM EXTRACTION TECHNOLOGY
================================================================================

    SEC. 201. DIRECT LITHIUM EXTRACTION RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM.

        (a) ESTABLISHMENT. — The Secretary of Energy, acting through
        the Office of Fossil Energy and Carbon Management and the
        Advanced Research Projects Agency-Energy (ARPA-E), shall
        establish a Direct Lithium Extraction Research and Development
        Program.

        (b) PROGRAM OBJECTIVES. — The Program shall pursue the
        following objectives:

            (1) Reduction of direct lithium extraction (DLE) production
                costs to not more than four thousand dollars ($4,000)
                per metric ton of lithium carbonate equivalent at
                commercial scale by 2030;

            (2) Achievement of lithium recovery rates exceeding ninety
                percent (90%) from target brine resources;

            (3) Reduction of water consumption in DLE processes to
                not more than thirty (30) cubic meters of fresh water
                per metric ton of lithium carbonate equivalent
                produced;

            (4) Development of selective sorbent, solvent extraction,
                membrane, and electrochemical DLE technologies suitable
                for diverse brine chemistries found in domestic
                resources;

            (5) Validation of DLE technologies at pilot scale (minimum
                production of one hundred (100) metric tons of lithium
                carbonate equivalent per year) and demonstration scale
                (minimum production of one thousand (1,000) metric tons
                of lithium carbonate equivalent per year).

        (c) FUNDING. — There is authorized to be appropriated to the
        Secretary of Energy for the Direct Lithium Extraction Research
        and Development Program five billion dollars ($5,000,000,000)
        for the period of fiscal years 2027 through 2036, of which:

            (1) Not less than one billion five hundred million dollars
                ($1,500,000,000) shall be allocated for competitive
                grants to private sector entities, universities, and
                national laboratories for DLE technology development;

            (2) Not less than two billion dollars ($2,000,000,000)
                shall be allocated for pilot and demonstration-scale
                DLE facility construction and operation;

            (3) Not less than five hundred million dollars
                ($500,000,000) shall be allocated for brine resource
                characterization and assessment;

            (4) Remaining amounts shall be allocated for program
                administration, technical assistance, and technology
                transfer activities.

        (d) TECHNOLOGY NEUTRALITY. — The Program shall maintain
        technology neutrality, evaluating and funding all viable DLE
        technology pathways including, but not limited to, adsorption
        using aluminum-based, titanium-based, or manganese-based
        sorbents; solvent extraction using crown ethers, ionic liquids,
        or other selective extractants; membrane separation using
        nanofiltration or electrodialysis; and electrochemical
        intercalation using lithium-selective electrode materials.

    SEC. 202. ARKANSAS SMACKOVER FORMATION DEVELOPMENT INITIATIVE.

        (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

            (1) The Smackover Formation extends across approximately
                132,000 square miles in southern Arkansas, eastern
                Texas, and northern Louisiana;

            (2) Lithium concentrations in Smackover Formation brines
                range from 100 to 500 milligrams per liter, with the
                highest concentrations identified in Columbia,
                Lafayette, and Union Counties, Arkansas;

            (3) Existing brine extraction infrastructure from oil,
                gas, and bromine production operations in the region
                provides a substantial foundation for accelerated
                lithium extraction development;

            (4) The Smackover Formation represents the most technically
                and economically viable near-term domestic lithium
                brine resource, with the potential to support annual
                production of over one hundred thousand (100,000)
                metric tons of lithium carbonate equivalent.

        (b) SMACKOVER LITHIUM DEVELOPMENT OFFICE. — The Secretary of
        Energy shall establish, within the Office of Fossil Energy and
        Carbon Management, a Smackover Lithium Development Office to
        coordinate Federal support for lithium extraction from the
        Smackover Formation.

        (c) REGIONAL DEMONSTRATION FACILITY. — The Secretary of Energy
        shall provide funding for the design, construction, and
        operation of a regional Direct Lithium Extraction demonstration
        facility in southern Arkansas with a minimum production
        capacity of five thousand (5,000) metric tons of lithium
        carbonate equivalent per year, to be operational not later
        than December 31, 2030.

        (d) BROMINE CO-EXTRACTION. — The Smackover Lithium Development
        Office shall coordinate with existing bromine extraction
        operators in the region to develop integrated bromine-lithium
        co-extraction processes that maximize resource utilization and
        minimize environmental impacts.

        (e) WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT. — Not less than fifty million
        dollars ($50,000,000) of amounts authorized under Section 201
        shall be allocated for workforce development and training
        programs in the Smackover Formation region, in coordination
        with Arkansas community colleges, technical schools, and the
        University of Arkansas system.

    SEC. 203. SALTON SEA GEOTHERMAL LITHIUM RECOVERY PROJECT.

        (a) FINDINGS. — The Congress finds that:

            (1) The Salton Sea Known Geothermal Resource Area in
                Imperial County, California, contains geothermal
                brines with lithium concentrations ranging from 100
                to 300 milligrams per liter;

            (2) Existing geothermal power plants in the Salton Sea
                area process approximately fifty thousand (50,000)
                acre-feet of geothermal brine per year, representing
                a potential lithium production of over three hundred
                thousand (300,000) metric tons of lithium carbonate
                equivalent annually if DLE technology is deployed at
                all existing facilities;

            (3) Lithium recovery from geothermal brines offers the
                unique advantage of co-production with zero-carbon
                baseload electricity, substantially reducing the
                carbon intensity of lithium production.

        (b) DEMONSTRATION PROJECT FUNDING. — The Secretary of Energy
        shall provide grants and loan guarantees for not fewer than
        three (3) commercial-scale DLE demonstration projects at
        existing Salton Sea geothermal power facilities, with a target
        aggregate production capacity of not less than fifty thousand
        (50,000) metric tons of lithium carbonate equivalent per year
        by 2032.

        (c) ENVIRONMENTAL BENEFITS. — The Secretary of the Interior,
        in coordination with the State of California, shall evaluate
        the potential for lithium extraction revenue to fund dust
        suppression, habitat restoration, and public health mitigation
        measures addressing environmental challenges at the Salton Sea.

    SEC. 204. GREAT SALT LAKE MINERAL EXTRACTION AUTHORIZATION.

        (a) FEASIBILITY STUDY. — The Secretary of the Interior, in
        coordination with the Utah Geological Survey and the Utah
        Division of Water Rights, shall conduct a comprehensive
        feasibility study on the potential for sustainable lithium
        and other critical mineral extraction from the Great Salt Lake.

        (b) STUDY REQUIREMENTS. — The feasibility study shall address:

            (1) Estimates of total and recoverable lithium and other
                critical mineral resources;

            (2) Assessment of Direct Lithium Extraction technologies
                suitable for Great Salt Lake brine chemistry;

            (3) Analysis of potential impacts on Great Salt Lake water
                levels, salinity gradients, and ecosystem health;

            (4) Evaluation of potential conflicts with existing mineral
                extraction operations, including magnesium and
                potassium production;

            (5) Regulatory and legal framework analysis for mineral
                extraction from the Great Salt Lake;

            (6) Economic modeling of extraction operation viability
                under various market price scenarios.

        (c) PILOT PROJECT AUTHORIZATION. — Subject to the findings of
        the feasibility study and upon issuance of all applicable
        Federal, State, and local permits, the Secretary of the
        Interior may authorize one or more pilot-scale DLE projects at
        the Great Salt Lake, provided that such projects include
        robust environmental monitoring and adaptive management plans.

        (d) FUNDING. — There is authorized to be appropriated to the
        Secretary of the Interior twenty-five million dollars
        ($25,000,000) for the feasibility study required under this
        section, and one hundred million dollars ($100,000,000) for
        pilot project cost-sharing grants, for the period of fiscal
        years 2027 through 2031.
