                     TITLE VIII — PERMITTING REFORM
================================================================================

    SEC. 801. FAST-41 EXPANSION FOR CRITICAL MINERAL PROJECTS.

        (a) EXPANSION OF COVERED PROJECT CATEGORIES. — Section 41001
        of the Fixing America's Surface Transportation Act (42 U.S.C.
        4370m) is amended to include within the definition of
        "covered project" any project that:

            (1) Involves mining, processing, or manufacturing of
                lithium, cobalt, nickel, manganese, graphite, or
                rare earth elements;

            (2) Has a total estimated capital investment of not less
                than fifty million dollars ($50,000,000);

            (3) Is located within the United States or on the Outer
                Continental Shelf.

        (b) CRITICAL MINERALS PERMITTING COUNCIL. —

            (1) ESTABLISHMENT. — There is hereby established a
                Critical Minerals Permitting Council, chaired by
                the Secretary of the Interior and composed of the
                heads of each Federal agency with permitting
                authority over critical mineral projects.

            (2) DUTIES. — The Council shall:
                    (A) Develop a single, coordinated permitting
                        timetable for each covered critical mineral
                        project;
                    (B) Resolve interagency disputes within thirty
                        (30) days of referral;
                    (C) Publish quarterly reports on permitting
                        progress for all covered projects;
                    (D) Identify and eliminate redundant or
                        duplicative permitting requirements.

            (3) STAFF AND RESOURCES. — The Council shall be supported
                by a dedicated staff of not fewer than fifty (50)
                full-time equivalent positions, with funding
                authorized under Section 701 of this Act.

        (c) PERMITTING DASHBOARD. — The Council shall establish and
        maintain a public-facing online permitting dashboard that:

            (1) Tracks the status of each covered project through
                the permitting process;

            (2) Displays the original permitting timetable and
                any revisions or delays;

            (3) Identifies the lead agency and each cooperating
                agency for each project;

            (4) Provides contact information for public inquiries.

    SEC. 802. NEPA MODERNIZATION AND COORDINATION.

        (a) SINGLE ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW DOCUMENT. —

            (1) The lead Federal agency for a covered critical
                mineral project shall be responsible for preparing
                a single environmental review document that
                satisfies the requirements of:
                    (A) The National Environmental Policy Act of
                        1969 (42 U.S.C. 4321 et seq.);
                    (B) Any applicable State environmental review
                        statutes if the State has entered into a
                        coordination agreement under subsection (c).

            (2) Other Federal and State agencies shall cooperate
                with the lead agency by providing relevant data,
                analyses, and expertise in a timely manner.

        (b) PAGE LIMITS. —

            (1) Environmental Impact Statements prepared for covered
                critical mineral projects shall not exceed three
                hundred (300) pages, excluding appendices.

            (2) Environmental Assessments prepared for covered
                critical mineral projects shall not exceed seventy-
                five (75) pages, excluding appendices.

        (c) STATUTORY DEADLINES. —

            (1) The lead agency shall complete a draft Environmental
                Impact Statement not later than eighteen (18) months
                after the later of:
                    (A) The date of formal application acceptance; or
                    (B) The date of publication of the Notice of
                        Intent in the Federal Register.

            (2) The lead agency shall issue a final Environmental
                Impact Statement and Record of Decision not later
                than twenty-four (24) months after the date specified
                in paragraph (1).

            (3) The lead agency shall complete an Environmental
                Assessment not later than twelve (12) months after
                the date specified in paragraph (1)(A) or (B), as
                applicable.

        (d) PENALTY FOR AGENCY DELAY. — If the lead agency fails to
        meet a deadline established under this section, the agency
        shall submit to Congress within thirty (30) days a written
        explanation of the reasons for the delay and a plan for
        completing the required review. The agency head shall appear
        before the relevant committees of Congress within sixty (60)
        days to provide testimony on the delay if so requested.

    SEC. 803. STATE-FEDERAL PERMITTING ALIGNMENT.

        (a) COORDINATION AGREEMENTS. — The Secretary of the Interior
        is authorized to enter into coordination agreements with
        State governments to:

            (1) Establish concurrent Federal-State environmental
                review processes;

            (2) Delegate Federal environmental review responsibilities
                to States with equivalent or more stringent State
                environmental laws;

            (3) Share environmental data and analyses to eliminate
                duplication;

            (4) Jointly fund environmental monitoring and mitigation
                activities.

        (b) STATE PERMITTING ASSISTANCE. — The Secretary of the
        Interior shall establish a State Permitting Assistance
        Program providing:

            (1) Grants to State environmental agencies for additional
                staff and technology to process critical mineral
                project permit applications;

            (2) Technical assistance for States in developing
                efficient permitting processes;

            (3) Model State legislation and regulations for
                streamlined critical mineral project permitting.

        (c) FUNDING. — There is authorized to be appropriated to the
        Secretary of the Interior one hundred fifty million dollars
        ($150,000,000) for the State Permitting Assistance Program
        for fiscal years 2027 through 2031.

    SEC. 804. PERMITTING TIMELINE ACCOUNTABILITY.

        (a) PERMITTING TIMETABLE. —

            (1) The Critical Minerals Permitting Council shall
                establish, for each covered project, a binding
                permitting timetable specifying the date by which
                each Federal permit, license, and approval shall
                be issued.

            (2) The permitting timetable shall provide for issuance
                of all Federal permits not later than:
                    (A) Thirty (30) months from application
                        acceptance for projects requiring an
                        Environmental Impact Statement;
                    (B) Eighteen (18) months from application
                        acceptance for projects requiring an
                        Environmental Assessment.

        (b) JUDICIAL REMEDIES. —

            (1) If a Federal agency fails to issue a required permit,
                license, or approval by the deadline specified in
                the permitting timetable, the project proponent may
                bring an action in the United States District Court
                for the District of Columbia to compel agency action.

            (2) The court shall set a schedule for agency action
                and may retain jurisdiction to enforce compliance.

        (c) ANNUAL REPORT. — The Council shall submit to Congress an
        annual report on:

            (1) The number of covered critical mineral projects and
                their statuses;

            (2) Agency compliance with permitting timetables;

            (3) Average permitting durations;

            (4) Recommendations for statutory or regulatory changes
                to further improve permitting efficiency.

        (d) SUNSET. — The authorities granted under this Title shall
        terminate on the date that is ten (10) years after the date
        of enactment, unless reauthorized by Congress.
