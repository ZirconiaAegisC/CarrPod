                                   A BILL

    To establish a comprehensive domestic supply chain for lithium and
    critical minerals, to reduce dependence on foreign sources of such
    minerals, to strengthen national security, to promote economic
    competitiveness, and for other purposes.

================================================================================

    Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the
    United States of America in Congress assembled,

================================================================================

                               SHORT TITLE

    SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE; TABLE OF CONTENTS.

        (a) SHORT TITLE. — This Act may be cited as the "Domestic
        Critical Minerals Independence Act of 2026".

        (b) TABLE OF CONTENTS. — The table of contents for this Act
        is as follows:

            Sec. 1. Short title; table of contents.
            Sec. 2. Findings and declarations.
            Sec. 3. Definitions.
                TITLE I — DOMESTIC LITHIUM MINING
            Sec. 101. Expedited permitting for domestic lithium mines.
            Sec. 102. Mining royalty framework.
            Sec. 103. Environmental and reclamation standards.
            Sec. 104. Tribal consultation and community benefit agreements.
                TITLE II — DIRECT LITHIUM EXTRACTION TECHNOLOGY
            Sec. 201. Direct Lithium Extraction Research and Development Program.
            Sec. 202. Arkansas Smackover Formation Development Initiative.
            Sec. 203. Salton Sea Geothermal Lithium Recovery Project.
            Sec. 204. Great Salt Lake Mineral Extraction Authorization.
                TITLE III — PROCESSING AND MANUFACTURING INFRASTRUCTURE
            Sec. 301. Domestic Lithium Hydroxide Conversion Facilities.
            Sec. 302. Cathode and Anode Active Material Manufacturing.
            Sec. 303. Loan guarantees and grant programs.
            Sec. 304. Strategic Processing Reserve.
                TITLE IV — BATTERY RECYCLING AND CIRCULAR ECONOMY
            Sec. 401. National Battery Collection Infrastructure.
            Sec. 402. Advanced Recycling Technology Grants.
            Sec. 403. Recycled Content Mandates.
            Sec. 404. Spent Battery Export Controls.
                TITLE V — STRATEGIC INTERNATIONAL PARTNERSHIPS
            Sec. 501. Bilateral Critical Minerals Agreements.
            Sec. 502. Western Hemisphere Minerals Partnership.
            Sec. 503. African Critical Minerals Engagement.
            Sec. 504. Indo-Pacific Minerals Security Framework.
                TITLE VI — RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT
            Sec. 601. Next-Generation Battery Chemistry Research.
            Sec. 602. Advanced Manufacturing Process Development.
            Sec. 603. Critical Materials Substitution Program.
            Sec. 604. National Laboratories Coordination.
                TITLE VII — FUNDING AND TAX INCENTIVES
            Sec. 701. Authorization of appropriations.
            Sec. 702. Advanced Manufacturing Production Credit extension.
            Sec. 703. Investment Tax Credit for critical mineral processing.
            Sec. 704. Department of Energy loan program expansion.
                TITLE VIII — PERMITTING REFORM
            Sec. 801. FAST-41 expansion for critical mineral projects.
            Sec. 802. NEPA modernization and coordination.
            Sec. 803. State-federal permitting alignment.
            Sec. 804. Permitting timeline accountability.
                TITLE IX — NATIONAL STRATEGIC LITHIUM STOCKPILE
            Sec. 901. Establishment and purpose.
            Sec. 902. Stockpile targets and acquisition.
            Sec. 903. Release conditions and market stabilization.
            Sec. 904. Defense Production Act Title III authority.
                TITLE X — IMPLEMENTATION AND OVERSIGHT
            Sec. 1001. Critical Minerals Supply Chain Office.
            Sec. 1002. Annual reporting requirements.
            Sec. 1003. Inspector General oversight.
            Sec. 1004. Sunset and reauthorization provisions.
