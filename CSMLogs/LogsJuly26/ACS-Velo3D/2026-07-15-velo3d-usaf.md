# U.S. Air Force — Additive Manufacturing Programs

## USAF Additive Manufacturing Strategy

The U.S. Air Force has identified additive manufacturing as a strategic capability for maintaining aircraft readiness, reducing sustainment costs, and enabling next-generation platform performance. The Air Force's approach spans multiple commands and programs, from research and development to depot-level maintenance.

## Air Force Organizational Structure for AM

| Organization | Role |
|-------------|------|
| Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) | Materials and process research; qualification methodology |
| Air Force Life Cycle Management Center (AFLCMC) | Platform-specific component qualification and procurement |
| Air Force Sustainment Center (AFSC) | Depot maintenance at Air Logistics Complexes |
| Rapid Sustainment Office (RSO) | Accelerated adoption of sustainment technologies |
| Air Education and Training Command (AETC) | Workforce training and certification |

## Engine Sustainment Applications

Aircraft engine sustainment is the single largest cost driver in Air Force operations and maintenance budgets. Additive manufacturing addresses key engine sustainment challenges:

### Legacy Engine Support

- **TF33**: Powers B-52H Stratofortress; engine has been out of production for decades; castings and forgings no longer available
- **F108 (CFM56)**: Powers KC-135 Stratotanker; commercial engine with declining military-specific component availability
- **T56**: Powers C-130 Hercules; large installed base with aging component supply chain
- **F100/F110**: Powers F-15 and F-16; mature engines with component obsolescence issues

### Current-Generation Engine Support

- **F119**: Powers F-22 Raptor; low production volume means limited spares inventory
- **F135**: Powers F-35 Lightning II; large and growing fleet requiring sustainment infrastructure

### Component Types

- **Turbine Blades and Vanes**: High-pressure turbine components with complex internal cooling
- **Combustor Components**: Fuel nozzles, swirlers, heat shields
- **Compressor Components**: Airfoils, cases, and stator components
- **Structural Components**: Engine mounts, brackets, and accessory drive components

## Airframe & Systems Applications

### Structural Components

- **Brackets and Fittings**: Topology-optimized for weight reduction; consolidating multiple sheet metal details into single printed parts
- **Hydraulic Manifolds**: Integrated fluid routing reducing fittings, tubing, and potential leak paths
- **Landing Gear Components**: High-strength steel components for landing gear systems

### Environmental & Thermal Systems

- **Heat Exchangers**: Compact, high-efficiency designs enabled by SupportFree internal passages
- **Ducting**: Complex air distribution components for environmental control systems
- **Electronic Cooling**: Thermal management for radar, electronic warfare, and directed energy systems

## Air Logistics Complexes

The Air Force operates three Air Logistics Complexes that provide depot-level maintenance:

- **Ogden ALC (Utah)**: Landing gear, composites, F-35 sustainment
- **Oklahoma City ALC (Oklahoma)**: Engines, KC-135, B-52, E-3 AWACS sustainment
- **Warner Robins ALC (Georgia)**: C-130, F-15, avionics sustainment

Each ALC has additive manufacturing capabilities, and Velo3D systems could be deployed to any or all of these facilities for specific component applications.

## AFRL Qualification Programs

AFRL's Materials and Manufacturing Directorate has active programs for:

- **Material Allowables Generation**: Statistical databases for additively manufactured material properties
- **Process Qualification**: Methodologies for qualifying AM processes for flight-critical components
- **Non-Destructive Evaluation**: Inspection techniques for complex AM geometries
- **Digital Twin**: Simulation tools for predicting AM part quality and performance
- **Cold Spray Integration**: Combining additive manufacturing with cold spray repair technologies

## RSO Initiatives

The Rapid Sustainment Office accelerates technology transition from laboratories to operational use:

- **PACER**: Parts and Component Engineering Repository — digital catalog of approved AM parts
- **MAMLS**: Mobile Additive Manufacturing Laboratory System — deployable AM capability
- **Advanced Manufacturing Olympics**: Challenge-based acquisition for AM solutions

## Future Platforms

Next-generation Air Force platforms are being designed with additive manufacturing in mind:

- **Next Generation Air Dominance (NGAD)**: Sixth-generation fighter program expected to incorporate AM components
- **B-21 Raider**: Northrop Grumman has indicated extensive use of additive manufacturing
- **Collaborative Combat Aircraft (CCA)**: Autonomous wingman drones likely to benefit from AM for rapid production

## Strategic Context

The Air Force's additive manufacturing investments should be understood in the context of strategic competition with China. The U.S. Air Force faces a numerical disadvantage in the Western Pacific and must offset quantity with quality. Additive manufacturing enables performance-optimized components that are difficult for adversaries to replicate through conventional manufacturing.
