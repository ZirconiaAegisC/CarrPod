## Executive Summary

This document presents the fleet-wide prioritization matrix for advanced hull coating deployment across all 94 vessels in the Carnival Corporation & plc fleet. The matrix accounts for vessel age, current coating type, operational route profile, drydock schedule proximity, and projected fuel savings potential to produce a ranked, phased deployment plan. Every vessel in the corporation's nine-brand fleet is assigned a priority tier, a recommended advanced coating product, and a target application drydock.

## Priority 1: Ships Nearing Drydock 2026–2027 (~31 Ships)

Approximately 31 vessels across the corporation's brands are scheduled for drydock between mid-2026 and end-2027. These vessels represent the immediate deployment opportunity. Every vessel in this cohort should receive an advanced silicone foul-release coating (AQUATERRAS, Hempaguard X7, or PPG Sigmaglide 2390) at its upcoming drydock, unless the vessel is within 24 months of planned retirement.

## Priority 2: Older Ships (Pre-2010, ~28 Ships)

These vessels, built before 2010, represent the highest potential for fuel savings improvement through advanced coating technology. Many operate with traditional biocide-based SPC antifouling systems that have been refreshed on a standard 5-year cycle but lack the foul-release properties of modern silicone systems.

### Recommended Coating Assignment for Priority 2 Ships

For Priority 2 vessels, the recommended approach is:
1. **Immediate (within 6 months):** Conduct in-water hull survey to quantify current roughness and fouling condition.
2. **At next scheduled drydock:** Strip existing SPC to bare steel (SSPC-SP 10), apply full silicone foul-release system.
3. **Interim:** Increase hull cleaning frequency to every 30 days for ships with >12 months remaining until drydock.
4. **Post-application:** Enroll in robotic hull grooming program for ships deployed in warm-water regions.

### Newbuilding Coating Specification Requirements

1. **Surface preparation:** SSPC-SP 10 near-white metal blast with anchor profile 50–75 microns for steel substrate.
2. **Anti-corrosive primer:** Epoxy tie-coat compatible with silicone foul-release topcoat; minimum DFT 150 microns.
3. **Foul-release topcoat:** Full silicone hydrogel or silicone foul-release system; minimum DFT 150 microns.
4. **Application conditions:** Strict adherence to manufacturer-specified temperature, humidity, and dew point ranges; documented in shipbuilding quality records.
5. **Warranty:** Minimum 5-year performance warranty from coating manufacturer, with defined acceptance criteria for hull roughness and speed loss.
6. **Inspection:** Third-party coating inspector (NACE CIP Level 3 or FROSIO Level III) present for all coating application on newbuilds.

## Route-Specific Coating Selection Rationale

### Caribbean / Bahamas (Warm-Water, High Fouling)
- **Dominant brands on this route:** Carnival Cruise Line (16 ships year-round), Royal Caribbean competitor benchmark, plus seasonal deployments from Princess, HAL, Costa.
- **Fouling pressure:** Highest in the fleet due to year-round warm water (26–31°C), high nutrient loads near island ports, and frequent stationary periods at private destinations (Half Moon Cay, Princess Cays, Amber Cove, Mahogany Bay, Grand Turk).
- **Recommendation:** AQUATERRAS, based on Carnival's 16-ship operational history in this region, including COVID-19 layup periods where ships remained stationary for 60–200 days with minimal fouling accumulation compared to SPC-coated vessels at the same anchorages.
- **Secondary option:** Hempaguard X7, with 4,000+ vessel installed base including significant Caribbean-operating tonnage.

### Mediterranean (Moderate)
- **Dominant brands:** Costa Cruises (year-round), AIDA Cruises (seasonal), plus summer deployments from multiple brands.
- **Fouling pressure:** Moderate seasonal variation; peak fouling risk in August–September (24–28°C); winter water temperatures of 14–17°C reduce fouling pressure.
- **Recommendation:** Hempaguard X7 or PPG Sigmaglide 2390. The cost differential between premium AQUATERRAS and Hempaguard X7 is less justified in the Mediterranean fouling environment compared to the Caribbean.

### Alaska and Northern Europe (Cold-Water, Low Fouling)
- **Dominant brands:** Holland America Line (Alaska), Princess Cruises (Alaska), P&O Cruises UK (Northern Europe), Cunard (transatlantic and Northern Europe), AIDA (Northern Europe seasonal).
- **Fouling pressure:** Lowest in the fleet; cold water (2–16°C) naturally suppresses fouling organism growth rate.
- **Recommendation:** Hempaguard X7 or PPG Sigmaglide 2390. The primary benefit in these environments is fuel savings from reduced skin friction rather than fouling prevention. Environmental sensitivity of Alaska and Norwegian fjord ecosystems favors biocide-free foul-release coatings.
- **Special consideration:** Alaska routes through Glacier Bay National Park and Tracy Arm Fjord require coatings that comply with heightened environmental scrutiny. Biocide-free silicone foul-release coatings meet this requirement.

### World Cruises and Long-Haul (Mixed Climate Zones)
- **Vessels operating world cruises:** Queen Mary 2 (Cunard), Island Princess and Coral Princess (Princess), Zuiderdam and Zaandam (HAL), Seabourn Sojourn (Seabourn).
- **Challenge:** Vessels encounter everything from tropical to polar water temperatures on a single voyage, requiring broad-spectrum coating performance over a 100+ day itinerary.
- **Recommendation:** AQUATERRAS, based on documented performance across the full temperature range and proven resistance to fouling accumulation during multi-day sea passages where in-water cleaning is not possible.

## Monitoring and Adjustment Protocol

1. **Quarterly Review:** The prioritization matrix is reviewed quarterly by the Fleet Hull Excellence Center (FHEC) to incorporate updates from: drydock schedule changes, ship sales or retirements, coating performance data from completed applications, and changes in fuel pricing that affect ROI calculations.
2. **Priority Reclassification:** Vessels may be reclassified between priority tiers based on new data. Reclassification from Priority 2 to Priority 1 (or vice versa) requires FHEC Director approval. Reclassification of newbuilds requires Steering Committee approval.
3. **Route Changes:** If a vessel is redeployed to a significantly different route (e.g., from Alaska to Caribbean), the recommended coating assignment is re-evaluated based on the new route's fouling pressure and environmental conditions.
