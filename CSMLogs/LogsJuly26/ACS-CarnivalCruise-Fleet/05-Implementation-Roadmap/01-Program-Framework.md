## Executive Summary

This document defines the five-phase implementation framework for the Fleet Hull Excellence Program, a corporation-wide initiative to systematically deploy advanced hull coating technologies across all 94 vessels in the Carnival Corporation & plc fleet. The program leverages a decade of operational data from Carnival's AQUATERRAS pilot fleet, independent verification from Hempel's 4,000+ vessel installed base, graphene-based coating programs exceeding 600 vessels, and proven electrostatic application methods to deliver a standardized, accountable, and ROI-justified pathway to fleet-wide adoption.

At a projected annual fuel spend of $1.8 billion, every 1% improvement in hull efficiency saves approximately $18 million per year. The accelerated deployment of advanced silicone-based and graphene-enhanced foul-release coatings is expected to yield a sustained 6–10% reduction in hull-related fuel consumption, representing annual savings of $108 million to $180 million once fleet-wide adoption is achieved.

## Phase 0: Fleet Audit and Baseline (Months 1–6)

### Phase Objective
Establish a comprehensive, ship-by-ship baseline of hull condition, coating configuration, fuel performance, and drydock scheduling to enable data-driven prioritization and KPI tracking throughout the program lifecycle.

### Key Activities

**Hull Condition Baseline for All 94 Ships**
- Deploy in-water hull surveys across the entire fleet, prioritizing ships with the longest time since last drydock.
- Employ diver-conducted visual inspections with standardized photographic documentation of hull fouling condition per IMO Biofouling Guidelines categories.
- For ships within 12 months of drydock, conduct ultrasonic thickness gauging at specified locations to capture steel condition data and coating dry film thickness (DFT) measurements.
- Compile findings into a centralized Hull Condition Index (HCI) database, scored from 1.0 (pristine) to 5.0 (critical), with photographic evidence and surveyor notes.

**Coating Type Inventory**
- Catalogue every vessel's current hull coating system: product name, manufacturer, date of application, number of coats, dry film thickness, and any documented performance issues.
- Classify each vessel into one of four coating categories: Traditional SPC (Self-Polishing Copolymer) Antifouling, Advanced SPC, Silicone Foul-Release, or Graphene-Enhanced Foul-Release.
- Map coating age against vessel age and route type to identify correlation patterns between coating degradation and operational profile.

**Fuel Performance Baseline per Ship**
- Extract 36 months of fuel consumption data per vessel from Carnival's Ship Energy Efficiency Management Plan (SEEMP) and onboard performance monitoring systems.
- Normalize for weather, speed, route, and loading conditions using ISO 15016:2015 methodology.
- Calculate baseline hull contribution to fuel consumption by subtracting known non-hull variables (engine efficiency, hotel load, HVAC, itinerary variance).
- Establish fuel performance baseline in metric tons per nautical mile, per vessel, per operational season.

**Drydock Schedule Mapping Through 2035**
- Compile the complete corporation drydock schedule from existing planning documents, including scheduled duration, shipyard location, and scope of work for each vessel through 2035.
- Identify scheduling windows, yard capacity constraints, and opportunities to align coating application with other planned work.
- Map each vessel's next two drydock dates to create forward visibility for coating selection and procurement planning.

**Prioritization Matrix Development**
- Develop a weighted scoring model incorporating: drydock proximity (40%), current coating age and condition (25%), fuel consumption penalty relative to fleet average (20%), and vessel remaining service life (15%).
- Tier vessels into three priority categories based on composite score.
- Validate scoring with brand technical directors and drydock planning teams.

## Phase 1: Accelerate Existing Program (Months 6–18)

### Phase Objective
Expand the proven AQUATERRAS and Hempaguard advanced silicone foul-release coating programs to all vessels entering scheduled drydock during this period, targeting approximately 20 additional ships. Standardize coating specifications, train yard partners, and establish procurement frameworks.

### Key Activities

**Expand Advanced Coating Deployment**
- Prioritize the approximately 31 ships entering drydock between mid-2026 and end-2027 for advanced coating application.
- For warm-water and high-fouling route vessels (Caribbean, Bahamas, Mexican Riviera), specify AQUATERRAS (Nippon Paint Marine) or Hempaguard X7 (Hempel) silicone foul-release systems.
- For cold-water and low-fouling route vessels (Alaska, Northern Europe), evaluate Hempaguard X7 and PPG Sigmaglide 2390 based on route-specific performance data.
- Establish a minimum 5-year performance warranty in yard contracts for all advanced coating applications.

**Standardize Coating Specification**
- Publish the Carnival Corporation Advanced Hull Coating Specification (Document ACS-CCL-FP-SPEC-001), defining product requirements, application procedures, surface preparation standards, quality control protocols, and acceptance criteria.
- Align specification with NACE/SSPC joint standards for surface preparation (SSPC-SP 10 near-white metal blast for steel substrate, SSPC-SP 13 for surface preparation of concrete when applicable).
- Include mandatory application conditions: ambient temperature range, relative humidity limits, steel temperature above dew point (minimum +3°C), and maximum recoat intervals.
- Specify minimum DFT per coat, total system DFT, and curing requirements per manufacturer technical data sheets.

**Train Yard Partners**
- Conduct a three-day coating application certification program at major drydock facilities serving the Carnival fleet: Grand Bahama Shipyard, Fincantieri (Palermo, Monfalcone, Marghera), Navantia (Cadiz), Damen Shiprepair (Rotterdam, Brest), and Sembcorp Marine (Singapore).
- Certify a minimum of 40 applicator teams across yard partners, with annual recertification.
- Deploy a dedicated Coating Technical Superintendent to oversee all Phase 1 applications, present at each yard during coating operations.

**Procurement Framework**
- Negotiate corporation-wide volume pricing agreements with Nippon Paint Marine, Hempel, and PPG for advanced foul-release coatings.
- Target 15–22% volume discount over standard yard pricing through aggregated demand across all nine brands.
- Establish a centralized coating inventory at four regional logistics hubs (Miami, Barcelona, Singapore, Sydney) to ensure supply availability and reduce yard demurrage risk.

## Phase 2: Fleet Standardization (Months 18–36)

### Phase Objective
Complete advanced coating application on all remaining vessels at their next scheduled drydock. Deploy robotic in-water hull grooming as a complementary technology. Install ultrasonic antifouling systems on high-fouling route vessels. Integrate hull performance monitoring into the corporation's digital fleet management platform.

### Key Activities

**Complete Fleet Coating Rollout**
- Apply advanced foul-release coatings to all remaining vessels (approximately 43 ships) at their next scheduled drydock.
- Include horizontal bottom surfaces, vertical sides, boottop, and sea chests in the coating scope.
- For vessels with existing advanced coatings approaching end of design life, plan recoating as part of standard drydock scope.

**Deploy Robotic Hull Grooming**
- Procure and deploy 8 robotic hull grooming units (HullWiper or equivalent) across the fleet's major turnaround ports: Miami (2 units), Port Canaveral (1), Barcelona (1), Civitavecchia (1), Singapore (1), Sydney (1), and a mobile deployment unit (1) for seasonal repositioning.
- Establish grooming frequency protocol: every 14 days for Caribbean-based ships, every 21 days for Mediterranean, every 30 days for Alaska and Northern Europe.
- Integrate grooming data (coverage percentage, fouling severity index, propeller polish frequency) into the central hull performance database.

**Install Ultrasonic Antifouling on High-Fouling Route Ships**
- Identify 15–20 vessels operating consistently on high-biofouling routes (Southeast Asia, South Pacific, tropical South America, Caribbean year-round deployments).
- Install ultrasonic antifouling transducer arrays on sea chests, box coolers, and strainer plates, locations where traditional coatings have limited effectiveness.
- Select from commercially available systems: NRG Marine Sonihull, Ultrasonic Antifouling Ltd, or Cathelco/USEA.
- Monitor ultrasonic system effectiveness through reduced sea chest fouling and improved heat exchanger efficiency.

**Integrate Hull Performance Monitoring**
- Deploy hull performance sensors on all vessels not already equipped: shaft torque meters, speed-through-water logs, and hull roughness measurement capability.
- Integrate data feeds into Carnival's Fleet Operations Center (FOC) in Miami and Carnival Maritime in Hamburg.
- Develop automated deviation alerts: when a vessel's fuel consumption exceeds the predicted model by >3% for the same speed and environmental conditions, trigger a hull condition investigation workflow.
- Link hull performance data to the corporation's SEEMP Part III (CII) reporting and EU ETS compliance documentation.

## Phase 3: Optimization (Months 36–60)

### Phase Objective
Leverage accumulated performance data from Phases 1–2 to optimize coating selection, extend drydock intervals where coating condition permits, implement full proactive hull management, and integrate digital twin technology for predictive maintenance.

### Key Activities

**Extend Drydock Intervals**
- For vessels demonstrating coating condition scores above acceptable thresholds (HCI <2.0) at the 4.5-year mark, evaluate extending drydock intervals from the standard 5-year cycle to 6 or 7 years.
- Conduct enhanced in-water surveys at the 4-year and 5-year marks for candidate vessels, using ROV-based hull inspection to complement diver surveys.
- Present drydock extension proposals to classification societies (DNV, Lloyd's Register, RINA, Bureau Veritas) for approval on a vessel-by-vessel basis, with supporting coating condition evidence.
- Model the financial impact: each avoided drydock day saves approximately $95,000–$185,000 in lost revenue plus $40,000–$75,000 in yard costs.

**Full Proactive Hull Management**
- Transition from reactive drydock-based coating management to continuous proactive management: a 24/7 hull condition monitoring program with monthly reporting.
- Deploy ROV-based hull inspection capability at each regional hub, reducing dependence on diver availability and improving inspection consistency.
- Establish the Fleet Hull Excellence Center (FHEC) as a dedicated team within Carnival Maritime, staffed with 6 technical specialists including a coatings engineer, a naval architect, a data scientist, and three regional hull superintendents.
- Implement a condition-based maintenance (CBM) approach: coatings are refreshed based on measured condition, not calendar interval.

**Digital Twin Integration**
- Develop a digital twin of each vessel's underwater hull, incorporating: coating type, application date, coating thickness as-applied, grooming history, route profile, water temperature data, salinity data, and fuel consumption trend.
- Use machine learning models trained on fleet-wide data to predict coating degradation rates per vessel, per route, per coating type.
- Generate predictive maintenance recommendations: when to schedule touch-up coating in-water, when to plan full recoating, and which coating product is optimal for the vessel's next drydock based on its operational profile.
- Integrate digital twin outputs with drydock planning and the corporate ERP (SAP) to automatically generate work orders and procurement requisitions.

## Phase 4: Continuous Improvement (Ongoing, from Month 36)

### Phase Objective
Establish permanent processes for data-driven coating selection, vendor performance benchmarking, technology horizon scanning, and annual program review to ensure the fleet remains at the forefront of hull protection technology throughout its operational life.

### Key Activities

**Data-Driven Coating Selection per Route and Itinerary**
- Maintain a Coating Performance Database (CPD) that tracks every coating application on every vessel, correlating product selection with fuel consumption outcomes, grooming frequency, and coating lifespan.
- Develop an annual Coating Recommendation Algorithm that outputs the optimal coating specification for each vessel's next drydock based on: historical performance of coatings on that route, water temperature and salinity profile, average speed, port time percentage, and projected fuel price.
- Review and update coating specifications annually based on algorithm outputs and vendor product evolution.

**Vendor Performance Benchmarking**
- Conduct semi-annual vendor scorecards for all coating suppliers: Nippon Paint Marine, Hempel, PPG, Jotun, International Paint (AkzoNobel), Chugoku Marine Paints, and GIT Coatings.
- Scorecard dimensions: product performance vs. warranty claims (30%), technical support responsiveness (20%), price competitiveness (20%), R&D investment and technology roadmap (15%), and supply reliability (15%).
- Use scorecard results to inform procurement decisions and volume allocation across suppliers.

**Technology Horizon Scanning**
- Maintain a Technology Watch List tracking emerging hull protection technologies: air lubrication systems (ALS) hull interaction, biomimetic surfaces, photocatalytic coatings, electrolytic systems, and autonomous hull cleaning robots.
- Attend and present at key industry conferences: Hull Performance and Insight Conference (HullPIC), International Conference on Marine Coatings, SMM Hamburg, and Cruise Ship Interiors Expo.
- Publish an annual Technology Outlook Report for the Carnival Maritime leadership team with recommendations for pilot projects and technology adoption.

**Annual Program Review**
- Conduct an annual Fleet Hull Excellence Program review with brand presidents, CFO, CMO, and Carnival Maritime leadership.
- Present: fleet-wide KPI dashboard, year-over-year fuel savings, coating performance by product and route, vendor scorecards, technology watch list updates, and budget vs. actual financial performance.
- Approve the following year's coating selection recommendations, drydock schedule adjustments, and R&D budget allocation.
