# 04 — Plectonemic Magnetic Confinement

## Classification: Proprietary — ACS Internal Use Only

---

## 1. Conceptual Basis

Plectonemic magnetic confinement is the second enabling mechanism in the Helicity Drive
architecture and constitutes the primary plasma stability strategy. The term "plectonemic"
derives from the Greek plektos (twisted, braided) and nema (thread), describing the
self-organized braided topology that magnetic flux tubes adopt when injected with both
poloidal and toroidal magnetic field components — also known as magnetic helicity.

In the Helicity Drive, four plasma guns inject individual magnetized plasma jets into the
peristaltic nozzle inlet. Each jet carries a specific magnetic helicity signature. As the
jets merge at the nozzle entrance, their embedded flux tubes braid around one another
into a plectonemic structure: a multiply-linked, self-organized magnetic configuration
that is both stable against macroscopic MHD instabilities and fully detached from any
material wall.

---

## 2. Magnetic Helicity Injection

Magnetic helicity, defined as K = ∫ A · B dV (where A is the magnetic vector potential
and B is the magnetic field), is a topological invariant in ideal MHD. It quantifies the
degree of linkage and twist in a magnetic field configuration. The Helicity Drive injects
helicity through:

1. **Poloidal field component** — generated by internal plasma gun electrodes, producing
   azimuthal currents and associated axial field
2. **Toroidal field component** — generated by the peristaltic coil assembly, producing
   axial currents and associated azimuthal field
3. **Relative orientation of the four jets** — each jet enters at a distinct azimuthal
   position (0°, 90°, 180°, 270°) with a defined helicity sign, such that pairwise
   merging produces mutual linking

The total helicity injected per pulse is:

```
K_total = Σ K_i (jet i) + Σ K_ij (mutual linking of jets i and j)
```

## 3. Stability Properties

The plectonemic configuration is stable against the three primary MHD instability classes
that plague magnetic confinement devices:

### Kink Instability (m = 1)

In a simple cylindrical pinch, the m = 1 kink mode displaces the plasma column when the
axial current exceeds the Kruskal-Shafranov limit. In the plectonemic configuration, the
mutual linking of braided flux tubes provides a restoring force: displacement of one tube
stretches its magnetic links to neighboring tubes, creating a potential well that
suppresses the kink.

### Sausage Instability (m = 0)

The m = 0 mode constricts and expands the plasma column periodically, leading to pinch-off.
The peristaltic compression field — which is strongest at the throat and weaker at the
inlet — imposes an axial gradient that breaks the translational symmetry required for
sausage growth. The braided topology adds a shear-stabilization effect.

### Interchange Instability

Gravitationally and magnetically driven interchange modes require a region of unfavorable
curvature (plasma on the "outside" of a curved field line). The plectonemic topology
ensures that any given flux tube alternates between regions of favorable and unfavorable
curvature along its length, averaging to net stability. This is analogous to the average
minimum-B principle in tandem mirror devices but achieved through topology rather than
coil complexity.

## 4. Wall-Free Containment

A crucial operational advantage of plectonemic confinement is that the plasma does not
contact any material surface during the fusion burn. The magnetic topology forms a
self-contained toroidal-knot-like structure that is fully suspended within the
peristaltic nozzle. This eliminates:

- **Sputtering and erosion** of first-wall materials
- **Impurity injection** that would radiatively quench the plasma
- **Thermal loading** on nozzle surfaces during the highest-temperature phase
- **Neutron activation** of structural components (for D-T phase; D-He³ reduces this
  further)

The wall-standoff distance is maintained by the radial pressure balance:

```
p_plasma + B²/(2μ₀) = B²_nozzle/(2μ₀)
```

The plasma beta at the throat can approach unity because the confining field is generated
by the plasma's own braided currents rather than external coils alone.

## 5. Experimental Validation Strategy

The ECLAIR experiment is designed to directly observe plectonemic braiding. The four-gun
configuration is the minimal arrangement that can produce a multiply-linked topology;
future Helicity Drive variants may employ 6, 8, or more guns for higher-thrust
configurations. Key diagnostic targets include:

- **Magnetic probe arrays** at the nozzle inlet to reconstruct the 3D field topology
- **Interferometry** to measure plasma density in the braided region
- **High-speed visible imaging** (5 million fps) to capture the braiding dynamics
- **Spectroscopy** to confirm that the plasma remains free of wall-material impurities

The LA-COMPASS 3D MHD code at Los Alamos National Laboratory provides predictive
simulation capability for the plectonemic formation process.
