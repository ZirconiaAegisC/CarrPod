## 3. Lockheed Martin Ventures Strategic Investment

Lockheed Martin Ventures (LMV) made a strategic equity investment in April 2024.
The amount was not disclosed. Key points:

- **Timing**: 4 months after the seed round close, suggesting follow-on interest
  rather than a new priced round
- **Strategic significance**: LMV investments typically indicate alignment with
  a Lockheed Martin business unit's technology roadmap. The most likely aligned
  unit is Lockheed Martin Space.
- **Historical context**: LMV has invested in multiple space technology startups,
  including rocket propulsion, satellite buses, and space domain awareness. The
  Helicity Space investment is LMV's first in fusion propulsion.
- **Implications for future rounds**: LMV's involvement provides a validation
  signal to other defense primes and institutional investors, potentially
  facilitating Series A fundraising.

## 5. DOE INFUSE Program

The Innovation Network for Fusion Energy (INFUSE) program, managed by the DOE
Office of Fusion Energy Sciences (FES), awards in-kind access to national
laboratory capabilities rather than direct funding. Helicity Space's INFUSE
award provides:

- Computational time on LANL high-performance computing clusters
- Access to LA-COMPASS code and LANL plasma physics expertise
- Diagnostic development support leveraging LANL's plasma diagnostic
  infrastructure

The INFUSE program is a bridge between the private fusion industry and DOE's
national laboratory complex. Helicity Space's inclusion signals DOE recognition
of fusion propulsion as a legitimate near-term application of fusion science,
distinct from terrestrial fusion energy.

## 6. Limitless Space Institute (LSI)

The Limitless Space Institute is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization dedicated
to advancing interstellar exploration research and education. LSI's I2
(Interstellar Initiative) program provides financial and institutional support
to propulsion concepts with interstellar-range potential.

| LSI Support Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Program | I2 (Interstellar Initiative) |
| Scope | Research grant and institutional endorsement |
| Strategic value | Positions Helicity Drive in interstellar propulsion community |
| Non-dilutive | Yes (grant-based, no equity) |

LSI's support is strategically significant because it places the Helicity Drive
within the interstellar exploration conversation — a community that evaluates
propulsion concepts on multi-decade horizons consistent with Helicity Space's
technology roadmap.

## 8. Path to Series A

A Series A round would fund the transition from ECLAIR (single-shot experiment)
to a repetition-rated engineering prototype. Key milestones that would support
a Series A valuation:

1. Successful plasma jet demonstration (July 2026 — achieved)
2. Peer-reviewed publication of ECLAIR results demonstrating the three mechanisms
3. Completion and acceptance of the NASA NIAC Phase I final report
4. Demonstration of sustained plectonemic formation and reconnection heating at
   fusion-relevant temperatures
5. LOI or MOU with a spacecraft prime contractor for integration studies
