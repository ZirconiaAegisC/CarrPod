# Mantle Core Technology — Tool Steel Material Systems

## H13 Hot Work Tool Steel

### Composition and Properties

H13 is a chromium-molybdenum-vanadium hot work tool steel widely used in
high-pressure die casting and injection molding of engineering thermoplastics.
Mantle's FMP-derived H13 maintains the alloy composition of wrought H13:
approximately 0.40 percent carbon, 5.25 percent chromium, 1.35 percent
molybdenum, and 1.00 percent vanadium. After sintering and heat treatment,
FMP H13 achieves:

- Hardness: 48-52 HRC
- Tensile strength: 1520 MPa minimum
- Yield strength at 600C: 890 MPa
- Elongation at break: 8 percent
- Thermal conductivity: 24.3 W/m-K at 20C
- Coefficient of thermal expansion: 12.2 x 10^-6 per K

### Heat Treatment

Post-sintering heat treatment follows the standard H13 protocol: austenitize
at 1020C for 30 minutes, oil quench to below 70C, then double temper at
540-600C depending on target hardness. Mantle recommends vacuum heat treating
to avoid decarburization of the as-sintered surface. The resulting
microstructure shows fine tempered martensite with uniformly distributed
alloy carbides, indistinguishable from conventionally processed H13 under
metallographic examination.

### Mold Performance

Tools printed from FMP H13 have demonstrated successful production runs
exceeding 500000 cycles in polypropylene injection molding and 250000 cycles
in glass-filled nylon applications. Conformal cooling channels printed
directly into the insert body reduce cycle times by 25 to 40 percent compared
with conventionally drilled straight cooling lines. Thermal fatigue resistance
measured by Uddeholm testing protocols shows equivalent performance to wrought
H13.

## P20 Pre-Hardened Mold Steel

### Composition and Properties

P20 is a chromium-molybdenum pre-hardened mold steel used for large injection
mold bases, compression molds, and structural components. FMP P20 composition
targets 0.35 percent carbon, 1.70 percent chromium, and 0.40 percent
molybdenum. As-sintered properties:

- Hardness: 28-32 HRC (pre-hardened condition)
- Tensile strength: 980 MPa
- Elongation at break: 15 percent
- Polishability: SPI A-2 finish achievable

### Machinability Advantage

P20's primary advantage over H13 is its machinability in the pre-hardened
state. FMP P20 can be CNC finished with standard carbide tooling at cutting
speeds 2-3 times faster than H13. This makes P20 the material of choice for
mold bases and large inserts where post-machining modifications are expected
during mold tryout. Mantle's integrated CNC finishes P20 inserts to Ra 0.4
micron surface roughness without additional polishing steps for most
applications.

### Applications

FMP P20 serves as the default material for prototype molds, bridge tooling,
and low-to-medium volume production tooling up to 100000 cycles. Its lower
alloy cost compared with H13 makes it economical for larger inserts where
the full thermal performance of H13 is not required.

## Material Development Pipeline

Mantle is qualifying additional tool steel grades for the FMP platform:

- **420 Stainless Steel**: For corrosive polymer applications and medical
  device tooling requiring autoclave sterilization compatibility
- **D2 Cold Work Tool Steel**: High wear resistance for abrasive filled
  polymers and metal injection molding feedstock
- **H11 Tool Steel**: Lower carbon variant of H13 with improved toughness
  for large die casting inserts

Each new material grade requires 12 to 18 months of qualification testing
including sintering parameter optimization, mechanical property validation,
and production-scale mold trials with partner customers.

## Quality Control Protocols

Every FMP material batch undergoes incoming inspection including particle size
distribution analysis via laser diffraction, binder content determination
through thermogravimetric analysis, and rheological characterization with
capillary viscometry. Sintered test coupons accompany every build for
destructive tensile testing, hardness measurement, and metallographic
examination. Mantle maintains a material database correlating paste batch
parameters with final part properties for continuous process improvement.
