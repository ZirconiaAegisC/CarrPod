# Mantle Product Portfolio — P-200 System Configurations

## System Overview

The Mantle P-200 is offered in a single hardware configuration with optional
software and service tiers. The base system includes the extrusion subsystem,
CNC subsystem, metrology package, and TrueShape software. Customers select
material starter kits and service levels based on their production requirements.

## Base Configuration

The standard P-200 system includes:

- P-200 hybrid manufacturing machine with integrated extrusion and CNC
- TrueShape toolpath generation software with one-year license
- FMP material starter kit: 10 cartridges each of H13 and P20
- External debinding and sintering furnace system
- On-site installation and 5-day operator training program
- 12-month warranty covering parts and labor
- Remote diagnostics and over-the-air software updates

## Software Packages

### TrueShape Standard

Included with every P-200 system. Features automatic toolpath generation for
straight-cut parting lines, planar shutoff surfaces, and straight cooling
channels. Import support for STEP, IGES, and Parasolid formats. Automated
inspection reporting with deviation heat maps.

### TrueShape Advanced (Optional Annual License)

Adds capabilities for:

- 3D conformal cooling channel design with thermal simulation
- Complex curved parting line generation
- Multi-cavity family mold layout optimization
- Warpage compensation modeling based on sintering simulation
- Integration with Moldflow and Moldex3D for cycle time optimization

## Material Cartridge System

FMP materials are supplied in sealed 500cc cartridges that load into the
P-200 extrusion system through a quick-connect manifold. Each cartridge
includes an RFID tag that encodes batch number, manufacturing date, and
rheological calibration data. The P-200 reads cartridge data on insertion
and automatically adjusts extrusion parameters to compensate for batch-to-batch
variation.

Cartridge pricing:

- H13 tool steel: 850 USD per cartridge
- P20 mold steel: 620 USD per cartridge
- Development materials (420 SS, D2): 1100 to 1400 USD per cartridge

A typical mold insert measuring 100 mm by 75 mm by 50 mm consumes 3 to 5
cartridges of material, yielding a material cost of 2500 to 4200 USD per
insert — comparable to the cost of buying a forged tool steel blank plus
rough machining.

## Furnace System

The debinding and sintering furnace is a separate unit supplied by Mantle
and tuned specifically for FMP process parameters. The furnace features:

- 200 mm by 200 mm by 150 mm usable chamber volume
- Maximum temperature 1350C with uniformity within 5C
- Programmable ramp-and-soak profiles for debinding and sintering
- Nitrogen-hydrogen reducing atmosphere capability
- Solvent recovery system capturing 95 percent of binder volatiles
- Cycle data logging with traceability to individual part serial numbers

The furnace processes up to four small inserts or two large inserts per cycle,
with total cycle time between 20 and 36 hours depending on part mass and
geometry.

## Service Tiers

### Essential Support

Included with system purchase. Covers 48-hour remote response during business
hours, software updates, and access to the Mantle knowledge base.

### Production Support (Annual Contract: 18000 USD)

Adds 24-hour remote response, 48-hour on-site service in North America,
annual preventive maintenance visit, one replacement extrusion head per year,
and priority access to application engineering support.

### Enterprise Support (Annual Contract: 35000 USD)

Includes all Production Support features plus 24-hour on-site response,
dedicated application engineer assigned to the account, quarterly process
optimization reviews, and guaranteed spare parts inventory maintained at
the customer site. Designed for facilities operating three or more P-200
systems.
