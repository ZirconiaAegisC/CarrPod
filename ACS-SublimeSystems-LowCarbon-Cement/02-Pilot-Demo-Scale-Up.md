# Sublime Systems — Pilot Plant and Commercial Scale-Up

## Holyoke Pilot Facility

Sublime Systems operates its pilot plant in Holyoke, Massachusetts, a former industrial city in western Massachusetts with access to low-cost hydroelectric power from the Connecticut River. The Holyoke facility, commissioned in 2023, has a production capacity of approximately 250 tonnes of cement per year.

The Holyoke pilot serves multiple functions:
- Electrolyzer stack testing and design iteration
- Feedstock qualification across multiple calcium sources
- Product quality optimization and consistency demonstration
- Certification testing for ASTM standards
- Customer sample production for concrete testing

The facility employs approximately 50 engineers, scientists, and technicians across electrochemical engineering, materials science, chemical engineering, and process development disciplines.

### Pilot Milestones Achieved

At the Holyoke pilot facility, Sublime Systems has demonstrated:
- Continuous multi-week electrolyzer operation with stable performance
- Cement production from both limestone and calcium silicate feedstocks
- Concrete compressive strength meeting or exceeding ASTM C595 and C1157 standard specifications
- Compatibility with conventional concrete admixtures (water reducers, set retarders, air entrainment)
- Hydrogen byproduct collection and utilization

## Commercial Demonstration Plant

Sublime Systems is developing its first commercial-scale demonstration facility in partnership with CRH, the world's largest cement producer with operations across 29 countries and annual cement production exceeding 50 million tonnes.

### Facility Specifications

The commercial demonstration plant is designed for an initial capacity of approximately 25,000–50,000 tonnes of cement per year — roughly 100–200 times the capacity of the Holyoke pilot. Key equipment includes:

- **Electrolyzer stacks**: Scaled from laboratory/pilot single-cell units (approximately 0.1–1.0 m² electrode area) to commercial stacks (approximately 10–50 m² total electrode area per stack), with multiple stacks operating in parallel
- **Feedstock preparation**: Crushing, grinding, and classification systems for calcium-bearing feedstocks; similar to conventional cement raw material preparation but without the need for raw meal homogenization
- **Electrochemical reaction vessels**: Tanks and flow systems for calcium dissolution and precipitation, with continuous rather than batch operation
- **Product processing**: Filtration, washing, drying, and blending systems for the precipitated calcium silicate product
- **Hydrogen handling**: Compression, storage, and utilization systems for the hydrogen byproduct

### Scale-Up Engineering Challenges

Scaling the Sublime process from 250 to 25,000+ tonnes per year involves several engineering challenges:

**Electrolyzer Stack Scale-Up**
- Transition from laboratory-scale electrolyzer cells (tens of cm²) to commercial stacks (tens of m²)
- Maintaining uniform current distribution, electrolyte flow distribution, and pH control across larger electrode areas
- Managing heat generation as stack power increases (I²R heating)
- Ensuring membrane durability over extended operation (multi-year lifetimes targeted)

**Continuous vs. Batch Processing**
- The Holyoke pilot likely operates in batch or semi-continuous mode
- Commercial deployment requires fully continuous operation: continuous feedstock feeding, continuous calcium extraction, continuous precipitation, continuous product withdrawal
- Process control systems must maintain steady-state conditions across interconnected unit operations with varying residence times

**Feedstock Handling at Scale**
- A 25,000 t/yr plant processing limestone requires approximately 35,000–40,000 tonnes of limestone annually
- A plant using calcium silicate minerals requires similar throughput, with the specific feedstock determining grinding energy requirements and dissolution kinetics

**Water Management**
- The electrochemical process is aqueous; water management (recycling, makeup, treatment) at commercial scale is a significant engineering consideration
- Closed-loop water systems minimize water consumption and wastewater discharge

**Product Consistency**
- Maintaining consistent cement quality across varying feedstock compositions and operating conditions requires robust quality control systems and potential blending of multiple production batches

### Commercial Deployment with CRH

The partnership with CRH for the first commercial-scale deployment provides several advantages:
- **Site selection**: CRH operates hundreds of cement plants and terminals globally, providing multiple potential locations for the Sublime process integration
- **Raw material access**: CRH controls extensive limestone and aggregate reserves
- **Product distribution**: Sublime cement can be distributed through CRH's existing terminal and logistics network
- **Customer relationships**: CRH's customer base provides immediate market access for the Sublime product
- **Regulatory expertise**: CRH's experience with environmental permitting and cement industry regulation in multiple jurisdictions

## Timeline to Full Commercialization

| Phase | Year | Milestone |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Laboratory | 2020–2022 | Proof of concept, electrolyte chemistry optimization |
| Pilot | 2023–2025 | Holyoke facility operations, product certification |
| Demo Plant Design | 2024–2026 | Commercial demo plant engineering and permitting |
| Demo Plant Construction | 2026–2028 | Construction and commissioning |
| Demo Plant Operation | 2028–2030 | Ramp-up to nameplate capacity, product qualification |
| First Full-Scale Commercial | ~2030–2032 | Licensed or partnered plant 500,000+ t/yr |

## Workforce Development

The Holyoke facility serves as a training ground for operators and engineers who will staff the commercial demonstration plant. Key workforce competencies include:
- Electrochemical process operations and troubleshooting
- Electrolyzer maintenance and membrane replacement
- Process analytical chemistry and quality control
- Industrial safety (hydrogen handling, chemical handling, electrical safety)
- Continuous process optimization and statistical process control
