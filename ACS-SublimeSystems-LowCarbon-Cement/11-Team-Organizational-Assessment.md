# Sublime Systems — Team and Organizational Assessment

## Leadership

### Dr. Leah Ellis — Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer

Dr. Leah Ellis is the scientific and entrepreneurial driving force behind Sublime Systems. Her background uniquely combines electrochemical expertise with entrepreneurial vision:

- **Education**: PhD in Chemistry from Dalhousie University, where she worked with Professor Jeff Dahn, one of the world's most cited battery scientists. Her doctoral research focused on lithium-ion battery materials and electrochemical characterization.
- **Postdoctoral Research**: MIT Department of Materials Science and Engineering, working with Professor Yet-Ming Chiang. It was during this postdoctoral period that Ellis developed the electrochemical cement concept, recognizing that the same electrochemical principles used in battery materials could be applied to cement production.
- **Awards and Recognition**: Ellis has been recognized in the MIT Technology Review Innovators Under 35 list, Forbes 30 Under 30 in Energy, and as a World Economic Forum Technology Pioneer. These accolades have elevated Sublime's visibility and credibility with investors, partners, and policymakers.

Ellis's leadership style emphasizes:
- Rigorous scientific grounding in electrochemical fundamentals
- Mission-driven commitment to climate impact
- Pragmatic approach to commercialization, balancing technology perfection with market timing
- Effective communication of complex electrochemical concepts to non-technical audiences (investors, policymakers, customers)

### Professor Yet-Ming Chiang — Co-Founder and Scientific Advisor

Professor Yet-Ming Chiang is the Kyocera Professor of Ceramics in the Department of Materials Science and Engineering at MIT. His entrepreneurial track record is among the most accomplished in materials science:

| Company | Technology | Outcome |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| A123 Systems | Lithium-ion batteries | NASDAQ IPO, later acquired |
| 24M Technologies | Semi-solid battery manufacturing | Private; partnerships with Volkswagen, Kyocera |
| Form Energy | Iron-air multi-day energy storage | $800M+ funding; first factory under construction |
| Desktop Metal | Metal 3D printing | NYSE-listed via SPAC merger |
| Sublime Systems | Electrochemical cement | $200M+ funding; pilot production |

Chiang's involvement provides Sublime with:
- Technical guidance on electrochemical systems engineering
- Credibility with the MIT technology licensing ecosystem
- Access to his network of investors, strategic partners, and technical talent
- Pattern recognition from multiple successful technology commercialization journeys

## Organizational Culture

Sublime's organizational culture reflects its MIT roots and climate mission:
- **Scientific rigor**: Decisions grounded in experimental data and fundamental understanding
- **Mission intensity**: Team members motivated by the climate impact potential of decarbonizing cement
- **Cross-functional collaboration**: Electrochemists, cement chemists, and process engineers working in integrated teams
- **Entrepreneurial urgency**: Balancing scientific thoroughness with commercial timeline imperatives

## Organizational Scale-Up Challenges

As Sublime transitions from a pilot-scale technology company to a commercial enterprise, key organizational challenges include:

### Building Commercial Capabilities
The organization must develop or acquire capabilities in:
- Manufacturing engineering and supply chain management
- Sales and business development (licensing and partnerships)
- Customer technical support (concrete producers, construction firms)
- Project finance and structured transactions

### Geographic Expansion
As Sublime deploys plants with partners globally, the organization must develop:
- International project management capabilities
- Multi-jurisdictional regulatory expertise
- Cross-cultural partnership management
- Distributed quality assurance systems

### Knowledge Management
Preserving and transferring the deep tacit knowledge of early technical team members becomes critical:
- Formal documentation of operational procedures and troubleshooting
- Training programs for licensee personnel
- Knowledge management systems for lessons learned across deployment sites

### Culture Preservation Through Growth
Maintaining the mission-driven, scientifically rigorous culture as the organization grows requires intentional effort:
- Hiring for cultural alignment as well as technical capability
- Leadership communication reinforcing mission and values
- Organizational structures that preserve cross-functional collaboration at scale
