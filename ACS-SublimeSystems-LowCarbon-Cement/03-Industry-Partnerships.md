# Sublime Systems — Industry Partnerships and Strategic Alliances

## CRH Strategic Partnership

CRH plc, headquartered in Dublin, Ireland, is the world's largest cement and building materials producer by revenue, with operations in 29 countries and annual cement production exceeding 50 million tonnes. CRH's strategic investment in and partnership with Sublime Systems represents the single most consequential industry endorsement of electrochemical cement technology.

### Partnership Structure

The CRH-Sublime partnership encompasses:
- **Equity investment**: CRH participated in Sublime's funding rounds, taking a strategic equity position
- **Joint development agreement**: Collaboration on the design, engineering, and construction of the first commercial-scale Sublime cement plant
- **Offtake commitment**: CRH has committed to purchasing Sublime cement for distribution through its existing sales channels
- **Technical collaboration**: CRH provides cement industry expertise in product formulation, quality control, concrete mix design, and regulatory certification

### Strategic Rationale for CRH

CRH's engagement with Sublime reflects the growing recognition among cement industry leaders that decarbonization is both a regulatory imperative and a market opportunity:

1. **Technology optionality**: Similar to ArcelorMittal's investment in Boston Metal, CRH is hedging its decarbonization portfolio across multiple pathways — carbon capture, alternative fuels, supplementary cementitious materials, and now electrochemical cement

2. **First-mover advantage**: As the first major cement producer to commercialize electrochemical cement, CRH gains valuable operational experience, IP access, and market positioning

3. **Regulatory positioning**: With the EU Emissions Trading System reducing free allowances and CBAM creating carbon cost exposure, low-carbon cement provides a competitive advantage

4. **Customer demand**: Major construction firms and project owners are increasingly specifying low-carbon concrete, driven by green building certification (LEED, BREEAM) and corporate sustainability commitments

5. **Resource optionality**: CRH's extensive mineral reserves include calcium silicate deposits that could serve as non-carbonate feedstocks for the Sublime process, enabling true zero-CO₂ cement

## Siam Cement Group (SCG)

SCG, one of Southeast Asia's largest industrial conglomerates and Thailand's largest cement producer, invested in Sublime Systems through its corporate venture capital arm. The investment reflects several strategic considerations:

- **Southeast Asian market expansion**: SCG provides a potential pathway for Sublime technology deployment in the rapidly growing Southeast Asian cement market
- **Feedstock diversification**: Southeast Asia has abundant calcium silicate mineral resources (volcanic in origin) that could serve as zero-carbon feedstocks
- **Climate vulnerability**: Thailand and neighboring countries are among the most climate-vulnerable nations, creating strong domestic political drivers for decarbonization
- **Technology leadership**: SCG has positioned itself as a technology leader among Southeast Asian industrial conglomerates

## Lowercarbon Capital

Lowercarbon Capital, founded by Chris Sacca, is a climate-focused venture capital firm that participated in Sublime's early funding rounds. Lowercarbon's investment thesis centers on technologies with the potential for gigatonne-scale emissions reduction — a criterion that Sublime's electrochemical cement technology meets given the 3 billion tonnes of annual CO₂ emissions from cement production.

## Engine Ventures

Engine Ventures (formerly The Engine), the MIT-affiliated venture firm focused on "tough tech" — capital-intensive technologies requiring extended development timelines — was an early investor in Sublime Systems. Engine's model is specifically designed for companies like Sublime that require patient capital, specialized facilities, and access to MIT's research ecosystem.

Engine's support extends beyond capital:
- Access to MIT facilities and equipment during early development
- Talent recruitment from the MIT graduate and postdoctoral community
- Operational support for laboratory and pilot facility setup
- Introduction to follow-on investors through Engine's LP network

## Academic Origins — MIT and Beyond

### Leah Ellis and the Yet-Ming Chiang Connection

Sublime Systems was co-founded by Dr. Leah Ellis, who developed the core electrochemical cement concept during her postdoctoral research in Professor Yet-Ming Chiang's laboratory at MIT. Ellis earned her PhD from Dalhousie University in collaboration with Professor Jeff Dahn, one of the world's foremost battery scientists.

Yet-Ming Chiang is a serial entrepreneur and MIT materials science professor who has co-founded multiple successful companies including:
- A123 Systems (lithium-ion batteries, NASDAQ-listed before acquisition)
- 24M Technologies (semi-solid battery manufacturing)
- Form Energy (multi-day iron-air energy storage)
- Desktop Metal (metal 3D printing, NYSE-listed)

Chiang's track record of translating materials science innovations into commercial ventures provides Sublime with experienced guidance in navigating the transition from laboratory discovery to commercial deployment.

### Intellectual Property Foundation

The core electrochemical cement patents were filed by MIT and exclusively licensed to Sublime Systems. The foundational IP covers:
- Electrochemical pH-swing process for calcium extraction and precipitation
- Electrolyzer cell designs optimized for cement precursor production
- Specific electrolyte compositions and operating conditions
- Methods for product formulation into cementitious binders

## Customer Partnerships

### Concrete Producers and Construction Firms

Sublime has engaged with leading concrete producers and construction firms to validate its cement in commercial concrete mixes:

- Compatibility testing with industry-standard admixtures and supplementary cementitious materials
- Field demonstrations at construction project sites

### Standards and Certification

Sublime cement has achieved certification under ASTM C595 (Standard Specification for Blended Hydraulic Cements) and ASTM C1157 (Standard Performance Specification for Hydraulic Cement), two of the primary standards governing cement use in the United States. These certifications are essential for commercial acceptance, as most construction specifications reference ASTM standards.

Certification under these performance-based standards (rather than prescriptive composition standards like ASTM C150 for Portland cement) allows Sublime cement to be used as a drop-in replacement without requiring changes to building codes or construction specifications.
