# Sublime Systems — ASTM Certification and Standards Pathway

## The Importance of Cement Standards

Cement is one of the most heavily standardized industrial products in the world. Building codes, construction specifications, and engineering design standards all reference specific cement types defined by standards organizations. For any new cement technology to achieve commercial adoption, it must either (a) meet existing standard specifications or (b) drive the creation of new standards — a process that can take years or decades.

Sublime Systems has successfully navigated this challenge by achieving certification under existing, performance-based ASTM standards.

## ASTM Cement Standards Overview

The American Society for Testing and Materials (ASTM) maintains the primary cement standards used in the United States and referenced in many international building codes:

### ASTM C150 — Standard Specification for Portland Cement
- **Type**: Prescriptive composition standard
- **Requirements**: Specifies chemical composition limits (CaO, SiO₂, Al₂O₃, Fe₂O₃, MgO, SO₃, loss on ignition) and the mineralogical phases that must be present (C₃S, C₂S, C₃A, C₄AF)
- **Relevance to Sublime**: Sublime cement cannot meet ASTM C150 because it does not contain the specified clinker phases produced by high-temperature kilns

### ASTM C595 — Standard Specification for Blended Hydraulic Cements
- **Type**: Performance-based standard for blended cements
- **Requirements**: Specifies performance criteria (strength, setting time, soundness, sulfate resistance) for cements that blend Portland cement clinker with supplementary cementitious materials
- **Relevance to Sublime**: Sublime cement has achieved certification under C595 as a blended hydraulic cement, demonstrating that its electrochemical calcium silicate product functions as a cementitious binder meeting standard performance requirements

### ASTM C1157 — Standard Performance Specification for Hydraulic Cement
- **Type**: Pure performance standard (no prescriptive composition requirements)
- **Requirements**: Specifies only performance criteria — compressive strength, setting time, soundness, sulfate resistance, alkali-silica reactivity — without prescribing chemical composition or manufacturing process
- **Relevance to Sublime**: Sublime cement has achieved certification under C1157, which is the most flexible standard and the ideal pathway for novel cement chemistries

## Certification Testing Regime

Achieving ASTM certification requires extensive testing across multiple dimensions:

### Physical Properties
- **Compressive strength**: The most critical property — measured at 1, 3, 7, and 28 days using standard mortar cube tests (ASTM C109). Sublime cement must meet or exceed the strength requirements specified for the designated type (GU — General Use, HE — High Early Strength, etc.)
- **Setting time**: Measured using the Vicat needle test (ASTM C191). Must fall within specified initial and final setting time ranges to ensure adequate working time for concrete placement
- **Soundness**: Measured by autoclave expansion test (ASTM C151). Ensures the cement does not undergo destructive expansion after setting due to the presence of free CaO or MgO
- **Fineness**: Measured by Blaine air permeability test (ASTM C204) or by 45-micron wet sieving. Affects reactivity and water demand
- **Heat of hydration**: Measured for low-heat cement types to ensure thermal cracking is not a concern in mass concrete placements

### Chemical and Durability Properties
- **Sulfate resistance**: Exposure to sulfate solutions to determine resistance to sulfate attack (ASTM C1012)
- **Alkali-silica reactivity**: Testing to ensure the cement does not contribute to deleterious alkali-silica reaction when used with reactive aggregates (ASTM C1567)
- **Chloride permeability**: For applications requiring low chloride ion penetration (ASTM C1202)
- **Drying shrinkage**: Measurement of length change upon drying (ASTM C596)

### Concrete Performance
Beyond standard cement testing, Sublime has conducted extensive concrete performance testing:
- Workability and slump retention (compatibility with water-reducing admixtures)
- Air entrainment characteristics for freeze-thaw durability
- Compatibility with supplementary cementitious materials (fly ash, slag, silica fume)
- Long-term durability testing (freeze-thaw resistance, scaling resistance, carbonation)

## European Standards Pathway

For market access in Europe, Sublime cement must meet European standards (EN 197 series). The European standardization framework is more prescriptive than ASTM in some respects, but EN 197-1 does include provisions for non-Portland cement types, and EN 197-5 (published 2021) expanded the range of permitted cement constituents. Achieving CE marking through compliance with harmonized European standards is a critical step for EU market access.

## Building Code Acceptance

ASTM certification is necessary but not sufficient for commercial adoption. Building codes must also reference the relevant ASTM standard for the cement type in question:

- **International Building Code (IBC)**: References ASTM C595 and C1157, providing a pathway for Sublime cement use in US construction
- **ACI 318 (Building Code Requirements for Structural Concrete)**: References ASTM cement standards, enabling structural design using Sublime cement
- **State and local building codes**: May reference additional standards or require supplemental testing

## The Certification Advantage

Achieving ASTM certification under C595 and C1157 provides Sublime Systems with several critical advantages:

1. **Drop-in replacement**: Sublime cement can be specified and used in construction projects without requiring changes to building codes, construction specifications, or concrete production practices

2. **Reduced adoption friction**: Architects, engineers, and contractors can specify Sublime cement using familiar standards and design parameters, eliminating the education and acceptance barriers that novel, non-standard materials face

3. **Performance validation**: ASTM certification provides independent, third-party validation of Sublime cement's performance, addressing concerns about novel cement technologies' reliability and durability

4. **Procurement eligibility**: Government and institutional procurement specifications overwhelmingly reference ASTM standards, making ASTM-certified cement eligible for publicly funded projects

5. **Insurance and liability**: Standardized products are easier to insure and involve lower liability risk for designers and contractors, reducing barriers to adoption

## Ongoing Standards Development

Sublime Systems is actively engaged with ASTM committees and other standards organizations to:
- Develop new standard specifications specifically for electrochemically produced cements (which would provide even clearer market access)
- Contribute performance data to inform revisions of existing standards
- Participate in sustainability standards development (ASTM E60 committee on sustainability, development of product category rules for environmental product declarations)

The publication of long-term durability data (5-year, 10-year performance) will be critical for establishing Sublime cement as a proven, reliable material suitable for structural applications.
