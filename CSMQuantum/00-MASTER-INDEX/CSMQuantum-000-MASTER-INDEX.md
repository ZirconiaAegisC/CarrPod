## Priority Matrix — Immediate Quantum Research Needs

### TIER 1 — CRITICAL (Begin immediately)

1. **MXene Ti₃C₂Tₓ Surface Termination Optimization** — Most transformative cost impact. MXene is $2,000/kg commercial today. In-house synthesis drops to $55-75/kg. Quantum simulation could discover: (a) optimal surface termination (-O/-OH/-F ratio) for maximum SE per unit thickness, (b) V₂CTₓ or Mo₂TiC₂Tₓ alternatives with superior absorption at lower cost, (c) MAX phase precursor compositions that achieve 92+ dB SE at 10 μm instead of 45 μm — a 4.5× material reduction.

2. **ZrB₂-SiC Grain Boundary Engineering** — The composite is the structural backbone of every Aegis-C panel. Quantum DFT of grain boundary cohesion with SiC intergranular phase could identify: (a) optimal SiC vol% for maximum fracture toughness without exceeding percolation threshold, (b) ternary additions (Hf, Ta, Nb) that increase oxidation resistance while maintaining dielectric behavior, (c) grain boundary dopant segregation that suppresses grain growth during flash sintering.

### TIER 2 — HIGH (Proceed when CRITICAL papers placed)

3. **YInMn Blue Indium-Sparing Formulations** — Indium is the most supply-chain-vulnerable element in the portfolio ($150-350/kg, ~700 tons annual global production). Quantum CASSCF calculations of Mn³⁺ crystal field splitting can identify which substitution elements (Ga, Al, Fe, Sc) preserve the 590 nm absorption band while reducing In content by 50-70%. Target: Y(In₀.₃Ga₀.₄Mn₀.₃)O₃ or similar.

4. **CsPbBr₃ Perovskite QD Encapsulation Design** — Perovskite QDs degrade under humidity and UV. Quantum MD of water molecule interaction with CsPbBr₃ surfaces can identify optimal capping ligands and encapsulation architectures. Target: 10-year outdoor lifetime instead of current ~1,000 hour stability.

5. **Dielectric Interface Joint Design** — Panel joints are the dominant SE loss mechanism (12 dB penalty). Quantum NEGF transport calculations through ZrO₂/PEEK/ZrB₂-SiC interfaces can optimize contact resistance while maintaining >10¹² Ω surface resistivity. Target: joint SE penalty ≤ 6 dB.

### TIER 3 — MEDIUM (Queue for later placement)

Papers 06, 08, 09, 10, 12: Valuable but not blocking immediate manufacturing decisions.

### TIER 4 — SPECULATIVE

Paper 11: Protonic communication in yttria-stabilized zirconia represents an entirely new communication paradigm. Quantum path integral calculations of proton hopping in YSZ could validate or refute the CSMProtonics01 hypothesis. High-risk, high-reward.

## Quantum Partner Selection Criteria

When evaluating quantum computing companies for these research partnerships, we require:

1. **Qubit count and fidelity** — Minimum ~100 logical qubits for DFT-VQE tasks; ~500+ for CASSCF excited states
2. **Error correction maturity** — Surface code or equivalent fault-tolerant architecture with demonstrated breakeven threshold
3. **Quantum chemistry expertise** — Demonstrated experience with VQE, QPE, or quantum embedding methods applied to materials science
4. **Hybrid classical-quantum workflow** — Integration with existing DFT codes (VASP, Quantum ESPRESSO, Gaussian)
5. **IP terms** — Co-development agreement with CSM retaining manufacturing rights; quantum partner retains algorithm IP
6. **Timeline** — Initial results within 12-18 months; full optimization within 24-36 months
