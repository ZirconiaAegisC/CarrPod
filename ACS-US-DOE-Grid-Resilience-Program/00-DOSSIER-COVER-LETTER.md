## I. EXECUTIVE TRANSMITTAL

Secretary Granholm:

The ACS Infrastructure Protection Division of Carrington Storm Motors submits this proposal for a Department of Energy-led national grid resilience program utilizing AEGIS/CSM dielectric hardening technology. This is a government program proposal — not a commercial sale. We propose a public-private partnership structure in which DOE provides program direction, funding oversight, and regulatory coordination, while ACS/CSM provides the technology, engineering, materials, and implementation framework for systematic GIC hardening of the U.S. electric grid.

**The National Security Context:** The U.S. electric grid is the most critical infrastructure system in the nation. Without electricity, nothing else works — not water treatment, not telecommunications, not healthcare, not food distribution, not defense systems. The grid's vulnerability to GMD events is well-documented by DOE itself: the 2020 DOE Electromagnetic Pulse Resilience Action Plan, the 2023 Grid Resilience State and Tribal Formula Grants program guidance, and the 2024 DOE Office of Electricity GMD Research and Development Strategy all identify GIC transformer failure as a high-impact threat requiring federal coordination. What is missing is an implementation mechanism — a program that moves from assessment to hardening at the scale of the U.S. transmission system (200,000+ circuit-miles, 55,000+ substations).

**The $5B / 10-Year Program Vision:** ACS proposes a DOE-led program to systematically assess prioritized transformers across the U.S. transmission grid and deploy GIC protection where the risk assessment warrants. The program would be structured in three tranches:

**Tranche 1 (Years 1-3, $800M):** National GIC vulnerability assessment using CSM's methodology (adapted from EPRI GMD guidelines, validated through CSM's 6,302-document research base). Develop a standardized transformer hardening prioritization framework. Fund 50 pilot NBC installations at the highest-risk substation transformers in each NERC region. Integrate with existing DOE OE-417 emergency reporting data for real-time GMD situational awareness.

**Tranche 2 (Years 4-7, $2.4B):** Scale deployment to 800 priority substation transformers across the continental U.S. Establish regional hardening centers — centralized NBC fabrication, testing, and installation coordination facilities serving each NERC region. Integration with the Grid Resilience State and Tribal Formula Grants program to provide state-level co-funding leverage.

**Tranche 3 (Years 8-10, $1.8B):** Complete hardening of all critical transformers (>100 MVA) serving major metropolitan areas, defense installations, and critical infrastructure clusters. Establish permanent GMD monitoring and NBC maintenance program. Transfer operational responsibility to the utility industry with DOE oversight.

**Alignment with Existing Programs:** This proposal is designed to integrate with — not duplicate — existing DOE programs:
- **Grid Resilience State and Tribal Formula Grants (IIJA Section 40101):** ACS hardening projects would be eligible expenses under the "weatherization and resilience" category. This proposal provides the technical framework for states to include GIC hardening in their grant applications.
- **OE-417 Emergency Reporting:** CSM's GIC monitoring data would supplement existing DOE electric emergency incident reporting, providing real-time transformer status during GMD events.
- **NERC TPL-007-4:** This proposal implements the mitigation measures that NERC's standards identify as necessary but do not fund.

## II. PROGRAM GOVERNANCE

**DOE Role:** Program direction, funding allocation, regulatory coordination (FERC, NERC, state PUCs), interagency liaison (DHS, FEMA, DoD).

**ACS/CSM Role:** Technology provider, engineering design, NBC manufacturing and supply, installation oversight, GIC monitoring network deployment, program technical documentation.

**Industry Role:** Utility site access, transformer outage scheduling, operational integration, cost-sharing (estimated 20-40% utility contribution per substation).

## III. TECHNOLOGY FRAMEWORK

The core technology — CSM-NBC-2400 neutral-blocking capacitors — is validated by EPRI's 2024 GMD Mitigation Guidelines Update, with field testing at Idaho National Laboratory. NBCs block >95% of quasi-DC GIC current. CSM's MXene materials (Ti3C2Tx) provide compact, high-performance capacitive filtering for substation control and protection systems. CSM-PC-5000 protonic communications provide GIC-immune backup links for grid control centers during GMD events when conventional SCADA communications fail.
