
# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
## ACS-US-DOE-GRID-RESILIENCE-PROGRAM
### National GIC Hardening Framework — $5B/10-Year Program

**CLASSIFICATION: COMMERCIAL-IN-CONFIDENCE / PRIVILEGED GOVERNMENT PROPOSAL**

---

## 1. THE NATIONAL IMPERATIVE

The U.S. electric grid — 200,000+ circuit-miles of transmission, 55,000+ substations — is the single most critical infrastructure system in the nation. It is also almost entirely unprotected against geomagnetic disturbances. DOE's own assessments (2020 EMP Resilience Action Plan, 2024 OE GMD R&D Strategy) identify GIC transformer failure as a high-impact threat requiring federal coordination. What is missing is an implementation mechanism — a program that moves from assessment to hardening at national scale.

The National Research Council's 2008 landmark study estimated the GDP impact of a Carrington-class event at $1-2 trillion (2008 dollars). Adjusted for inflation and grid growth, that estimate now exceeds $2.6 trillion. A $5B national hardening program represents 0.19% of the loss it prevents.

## 2. THE PROGRAM

**Three-Tranche Structure:**

**Tranche 1 (Years 1-3, $800M):** National GIC vulnerability assessment using CSM methodology. 50 pilot NBC installations at highest-risk substation transformers. Integration with DOE OE-417 emergency reporting.

**Tranche 2 (Years 4-7, $2.4B):** Scale to 800 priority substation transformers. Regional hardening centers. Grid Resilience State and Tribal Formula Grants integration.

**Tranche 3 (Years 8-10, $1.8B):** Complete hardening of critical transformers (>100 MVA) serving major metros, defense installations, critical infrastructure clusters. Transfer to utility industry with DOE oversight.

## 3. GOVERNANCE

**DOE:** Program direction, funding, regulatory coordination, interagency liaison.
**ACS/CSM:** Technology provider, engineering, NBC supply, installation oversight, monitoring.
**Industry:** Utility site access, outage scheduling, operational integration, 20-40% cost-share.
