## 2. DETAILED YEAR-BY-YEAR PLAN

**Year 1 (Federal: $180M):**
- Q1-Q2: Program office established at DOE HQ. Interagency MOUs with NOAA, DHS, FERC, NERC. CSM technical team embedded at DOE Office of Electricity.
- Q3-Q4: National GIC hazard mapping initiated (USGS collaboration). Priority 1 transformer identification methodology finalized. Regional hardening center site selection.
- Hardware: 5 pilot NBC installations at highest-risk substations. Phantom Robot fleet procurement (20 units).

**Year 2 (Federal: $220M):**
- Q1-Q2: Geoelectric hazard mapping complete. Transformer vulnerability scoring applied to 55,000+ substations. Priority 1 list published (800-1,200 transformers).
- Q3-Q4: Regional hardening centers construction begins (7 centers). Field verification at 100 highest-scoring sites. Pilot program expands to 25 total hardened transformers.
- SCADA: OE-417 GIC monitoring module development. NOAA SWPC feed integration.

**Year 3 (Federal: $240M):**
- Q1-Q4: Pilot program reaches 50 hardened transformers. Regional centers 1-3 operational (Eastern Interconnection). State formula grant integration begins — 5 pioneer states submit GIC-focused applications. Field verification complete at 100 sites. OE-417 GIC module operational.

**Years 4-7 (Federal: $420M/year average):**
- Systematic deployment at 200 transformers/year (across 7 regional centers)
- Rate increases to 250/year by Year 7 as centers reach full operational tempo
- By Year 7: 850 total transformers hardened
- State grant co-funding at 15-20 states participating
- Utility cost-share increases from 20% to 30% as industry confidence grows

**Years 8-10 (Federal: $360M/year average):**
- Complete Priority 1 hardening (all transformers scoring >65)
- Begin Priority 2 hardening (scores 50-65)
- Operation and maintenance program established
- Transfer of operational ownership to utility industry with DOE oversight
- By Year 10: 2,050+ transformers hardened, covering all major metropolitan areas, defense installations, and critical infrastructure clusters

## 5. RISK AND MITIGATION

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Congressional appropriation delays | Medium | IIJA mandatory funding baseline. Multi-year appropriation authority requested. |
| Utility industry resistance | Low-Medium | Cost-share incentive structure. NERC compliance integration. Insurance premium benefits. |
| NBC supply chain constraint | Low | CSM MXene fab capacity expansion in program design. Regional fab centers. |
| GMD event during partial deployment | Low | Protected transformers serve as grid anchors. Partial protection better than none. |
| Political administration change | Medium | Bipartisan infrastructure resilience support. Program design spans administrations. |
