# Liquidation Timeline and Process

## Chronology of Events

The following timeline documents the key events leading to Gravitricity's voluntary
liquidation on 1 October 2025.

### 2011–2019: Formation and Development

- 2011: Gravitricity Ltd incorporated in Edinburgh, Scotland, by Martin Wright (co-founder
  and Executive Chairman). The company's initial focus was exclusively on gravity energy
  storage in disused mine shafts.
- 2012–2016: Concept development, patent filings, and early-stage fundraising from angel
  investors and innovation grants (Scottish Enterprise, UK government energy innovation
  programmes).
- 2017–2018: Crowdcube equity crowdfunding campaign, raising approximately £1.5 million
  from retail investors. The campaign emphasised the technology's potential for long-
  duration storage and the 50-year asset life.
- 2018: Imperial College London publishes comparative storage analysis, providing
  independent validation of GraviStore cost projections.
- 2019: Construction of the Port of Leith 250 kW demonstrator begins.

### 2020–2023: Demonstrator Success, Fundraising Challenges

- 2020–2021: Port of Leith demonstrator commissioned and operated. Test results are
  positive, confirming technical feasibility. However, the COVID-19 pandemic disrupts
  fundraising activity and delays commercial negotiations with potential project partners.
- 2022: Charlie Blair appointed as Managing Director. The company begins exploring
  hydrogen storage (H2FlexiStore) as a complementary technology, recognising that the
  gravity storage business alone may not achieve the scale required to attract
  institutional investment.
- Early 2023: Gravitricity launches a £40 million equity fundraising round, targeting
  institutional investors and strategic corporate partners. The use of proceeds includes
  the Pyhäsalmi Mine full-scale deployment, further technology development, and working
  capital.
- Mid 2023: The fundraising encounters difficulties. Investor feedback indicates concerns
  about: (a) the lack of a commercial-scale reference project, (b) uncertainty around
  shaft acquisition costs and permitting timelines, (c) the long development timeline
  to revenue, and (d) the perception that gravity storage is "interesting but unproven"
  compared to the rapidly scaling lithium-ion BESS industry, which was receiving
  billions in institutional capital.
- December 2023: ABB partnership announced, intended to address investor concerns about
  technology maturity. The partnership is reported positively in trade media but does not
  unlock the institutional fundraising.

### 2024: Pivot and Decline

- Q1 2024: Gravitricity pivots strategy, increasing emphasis on H2FlexiStore and the
  Ofgem SIF-funded feasibility study. The company positions itself as a "shaft-based
  energy storage platform" rather than solely a gravity storage company, hoping to
  broaden investor appeal.
- Mid 2024: The £40 million fundraising round is formally abandoned, having failed to
  secure a lead investor. The company's cash reserves are critically depleted.
- H2 2024: The Ofgem SIF feasibility study is completed, generating positive technical
  findings for H2FlexiStore but no immediate revenue.
- Late 2024: Gravitricity's board of directors commissions an insolvency practitioner
  to advise on options, including administration, a pre-pack sale, or a Creditors'
  Voluntary Liquidation (CVL).

### 2025: Liquidation

- Q1 2025: Efforts to find a buyer for the company as a going concern or for its IP
  assets do not yield an acceptable offer within the available timeframe.
- Q2 2025: The board resolves to place the company into Creditors' Voluntary Liquidation.
- August 2025: Hilco Global is appointed to market the company's IP assets (brand,
  patents, and know-how) for sale.
- 1 October 2025: Gravitricity Ltd enters voluntary liquidation. The estimated assets
  available to creditors total £7,913.
- February 2026: Deadline for bids for the IP assets via Hilco.

## Financial Position at Liquidation

The liquidator's Statement of Affairs, filed at Companies House, reported estimated
assets of £7,913. This figure is understood to consist primarily of:

- Cash at bank
- Office equipment (computers, furniture)
- IP assets (patents, trademark registrations for "GraviStore" and "H2FlexiStore")

The total deficiency as regards creditors has not been publicly disclosed, but the
extremely low asset figure relative to the £40 million fundraising target indicates
that the company's cash reserves were essentially exhausted at the point of liquidation.

## Crowdcube Investors

Retail investors who participated in the 2017–2018 Crowdcube fundraising received
notification of the liquidation with minimal information. Under UK insolvency law,
shareholders rank below all creditors in the distribution hierarchy, and the £7,913
in available assets will be consumed by the costs of the liquidation process and
preferential creditors (employees, HMRC) before any distribution to unsecured creditors,
let alone shareholders.

The Crowdcube investors are likely to receive nothing.

## Directors

- Martin Wright: Co-founder and Executive Chairman. A mechanical engineer and serial
  entrepreneur in the renewable energy sector, Wright was the public face of
  Gravitricity and the primary fundraiser.
- Charlie Blair: Managing Director, appointed 2022. Blair's background includes senior
  roles in energy infrastructure development and project finance.

Both directors remained with the company through the liquidation process.
