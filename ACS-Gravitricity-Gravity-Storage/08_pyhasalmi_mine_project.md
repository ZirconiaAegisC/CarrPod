# Pyhäsalmi Mine Project

## Site Overview

The Pyhäsalmi Mine, located in Pyhäjärvi municipality, northern Ostrobothnia, Finland, is
one of Europe's deepest base-metal mines. Originally discovered in 1958 by Outokumpu Oyj,
the mine produced copper, zinc, and pyrite (sulphur) from a volcanogenic massive sulphide
(VMS) deposit for over 60 years, reaching a final depth of 1,445 metres below surface—the
deepest mine in Europe.

The mine was operated by First Quantum Minerals from 2002, and later by Pyhäjärven
Kaivos Oy. Mining operations ceased in 2022, and the site transitioned to closure and
rehabilitation, managed by the state-owned entity Callio Pyhäjärvi, which was established
to repurpose the mine infrastructure for scientific research, data centres, and energy
storage projects.

The site offered an exceptionally attractive combination of depth, diameter, existing
infrastructure, grid connection, and a supportive local authority actively seeking
post-mining economic diversification.

## Auxiliary Shaft Specifications

The Pyhäsalmi auxiliary shaft was selected as Gravitricity's first full-scale deployment
target. Key parameters:

- Depth: 530 metres (1,738 feet)
- Diameter: 4.5 metres
- Lining: Concrete throughout the full depth, installed during original mine development
- Condition Assessment (conducted 2023): Shaft lining found to be in good structural
  condition with minor water ingress (estimated <2 litres/second), well within the
  acceptable range for GraviStore operations
- Deviation from Vertical: Less than 1 degree over the full 530-metre depth
- Surface Infrastructure: Existing headframe, winding house (available for hoist
  installation), substation, workshop, and access roads
- Grid Connection: 110 kV connection at the mine site, with sufficient capacity for the
  planned 2 MW GraviStore installation

## Planned Installation

The Pyhäsalmi installation was designed as a 2 MW / 15 MWh GraviStore system, using the
auxiliary shaft's full 530-metre depth. The design parameters:

- Weight mass: approximately 1,200 tonnes, fabricated from steel and concrete at the
  surface using the existing mine workshop facilities
- Hoist system: a single-drum electric winder with 64 mm wire rope, 3.5 m drum diameter
- Power conversion: 2 MW four-quadrant drive with 110 kV grid connection
- Cycle duration: approximately 8 hours for a full charge-discharge cycle at rated power,
  suitable for daily peak-shaving and frequency regulation services
- Guide system: roller guides attached to the concrete shaft lining at 5-metre intervals

## Economic and Market Context

Finland's electricity system has several characteristics that make gravity storage
economically attractive:

1. High renewable penetration: Approximately 45% of Finland's electricity was generated
   from renewables in 2023, dominated by hydro, wind, and biomass. The Finnish government
   targets carbon neutrality by 2035, implying continued growth in wind generation.

2. Frequency regulation market: Finland participates in the Nordic frequency containment
   reserve (FCR) and frequency restoration reserve (FRR) markets operated by Fingrid, the
   Finnish transmission system operator. GraviStore's sub-second response time makes it
   well-suited to FCR-D (frequency containment reserve for disturbances) and FFR (fast
   frequency response) services.

3. No pumped hydro potential: Finland lacks the topography for conventional pumped hydro
   storage, and interconnection capacity to Swedish and Norwegian hydro reservoirs is
   limited by transmission constraints during high-demand periods.

4. Industrial demand: The Pyhäjärvi region hosts energy-intensive industries including
   a steel mill (SSAB in Raahe, approximately 150 km away) and forestry products
   processing, providing anchor loads for a local storage facility.

## Project Status at Liquidation

At the time of Gravitricity's voluntary liquidation on 1 October 2025, the Pyhäsalmi
project was at the advanced feasibility stage. Key milestones achieved:

- Memorandum of Understanding with Callio Pyhäjärvi (signed 2023)
- Preliminary shaft survey and lining condition assessment completed
- Grid connection study completed by Fingrid, confirming available capacity
- Front-end engineering design (FEED) approximately 60% complete
- Environmental impact assessment scoping commenced
- ABB partnership agreement signed (December 2023) for hoist technology supply

The project had reached the point where construction could commence within 12–18 months
of a final investment decision, conditional on securing project finance. The estimated
capital cost was approximately €12–€15 million, which Gravitricity intended to finance
through a combination of equity, Finnish government investment grants (Business Finland
energy aid programme), and European Union innovation funding (Horizon Europe or the
Innovation Fund).

## Post-Liquidation Status

The Pyhäsalmi mine site remains under the management of Callio Pyhäjärvi, which continues
to seek tenants and projects for the repurposed mine infrastructure. The GraviStore
project is technically orphaned—the IP, engineering designs, and commercial agreements
are assets of the liquidated Gravitricity estate, but the physical site and the local
partnership remain available. Any acquirer of the Gravitricity IP could potentially
reactivate the Pyhäsalmi project with a reduced development timeline relative to a
greenfield site, given the advanced state of engineering and permitting work.
