# PART 62 — The Full Filmography: Archive of Joy

**Research Category:** Complete Film Record / Career Summary / Historical Archive

Robin Williams' film career spanned 1977–2014. Selected annotated filmography, focused on works most relevant to the Williams Heuristic framework:

**Popeye (1980)** — First major film role. Williams inhabited Popeye with physical dedication and the character's specific combination of strength and verbal clumsiness.

**The World According to Garp (1982)** — Showed for the first time on film that Williams could carry dramatic weight without comedy.

**Moscow on the Hudson (1984)** — A Russian musician who defects in a department store. Remarkable blending of comedy with genuine investment in the immigrant experience.

**Good Morning, Vietnam (1987)** — (See Part 06). The performance that established Williams as one of the most significant American film artists.

**Dead Poets Society (1989)** — (See Part 07). John Keating as the standard for teacher-as-liberator in film.

**Awakenings (1990)** — (See Part 47). The quiet Williams.

**The Fisher King (1991)** — (See Part 08). The deepest articulation of the Williams philosophy.

**Aladdin (1992)** — (See Part 11). The voice performance that changed animation.

**Mrs. Doubtfire (1993)** — (See Part 10). The second-highest-grossing film of 1993. Endures.

**Jumanji (1995)** — Williams as a man who spent twenty-six years trapped in a board game. His joy upon escape is genuine and infectious.

**The Birdcage (1996)** — (See Part 52). The art of the second chair.

**Good Will Hunting (1997)** — (See Part 09). The Academy Award performance.

**Patch Adams (1998)** — (See Part 41). Uneven film, genuine performance of genuine belief.

**What Dreams May Come (1998)** — Visually extraordinary film about grief and afterlife. Williams' performance is quiet and deeply felt. Underrated.

**Bicentennial Man (1999)** — (See Part 57). The AI who wanted to be human.

**Jakob the Liar (1999)** — Williams as a Jewish man in a Nazi ghetto who invents a radio and gives hope through fabricated good news. Must perform hope he doesn't feel to save people who need it. In pure distillation, the Defiant Optimism Protocol.

**One Hour Photo (2002) / Insomnia (2002)** — (See Part 25). The villain performances demonstrating the full range.

**World's Greatest Dad (2009)** — Bobcat Goldthwait's devastatingly dark comedy. Williams plays it with unflinching commitment. One of his finest late performances.

**Night at the Museum: Secret of the Tomb (2014)** — His final completed performance. Theodore Roosevelt, played with enormous warmth and genuine physical presence. A gift to his children — something for them to see their father in.

The filmography is not the ultimate measure of Williams' achievement. His live performances — undocumented in their full extent, present in fragments in recordings and in the memories of hundreds of thousands — are the primary archive. But the films provided the broader culture with access to the core qualities, and they endure.
