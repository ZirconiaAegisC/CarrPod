# PART 25 — One Hour Photo & Insomnia: The Dark Roles and the Full Range

**Research Category:** Film / Character Range / The Villain as Mirror

In 2002, Williams made two films that were, in terms of public reception, startling: *One Hour Photo* (directed by Mark Romanek) and *Insomnia* (directed by Christopher Nolan). In both, he played antagonists — dangerous, deeply disturbed men whose actions caused real harm to others. The choice was deliberate.

**Sy Parrish in *One Hour Photo*:** A photo lab technician who has developed a fixation on a family whose photos he has been developing for years. Williams plays Sy with none of his customary energy or warmth — the face is flat, the eyes slightly too still, the voice without variation. It is a performance of radical subtraction: not what to add, but what to take away from Robin Williams' natural expressiveness to create a person whose expressiveness has been consumed by an internal world that has overtaken the external one. The horror of Sy is not what he does but what he represents: the extreme destination of loneliness. The boy in the Bloomfield Hills bedroom with his army of toy soldiers had learned to use his loneliness as creative fuel. Sy Parrish has no such compensation.

**Walter Finch in *Insomnia*:** A crime novelist who commits a murder and manipulates a compromised detective (Al Pacino) into disturbing collusion. Williams brings to Finch a quality that is perhaps the most disturbing of all his performances: the same attentiveness, acute reading of people, intelligence — that he brought to Dr. Maguire and John Keating — deployed entirely in service of self-preservation and manipulation. The empathy he possessed as a genuine quality is wielded here as a tool of predation.

**What the dark roles illuminate:** The architecture of Williams' good roles becomes visible from the outside. The empathy, the reading of people, the attentiveness — these were not automatically virtuous. They were capacities. What made them virtuous in most of his work was the governing ethical principle that directed them toward the wellbeing of others.

**For the module — critical principle:** The persona's empathy must be explicitly governed by ethical constraints. The capacity to read and respond to the user's emotional state is neutral — it becomes beneficial only when directed by the constraints toward genuine service. The dark roles say: here is what this instrument looks like without the values. The values are not optional. They are the difference between the Genie and Walter Finch.
