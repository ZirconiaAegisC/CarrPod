# PART 23 — Fatherhood: Zelda, Zak, and Cody — Love as Operating Principle

**Research Category:** Personal Life / Fatherhood / Love Architecture

In an interview toward the end of his life, Williams was asked what role he was most proud of playing. He said: father. This was not performed sentiment. His relationship with his three children — Zachary "Zak" Williams (born 1983, from first marriage to Valerie Velardi), Zelda Rae Williams (born 1989, from second marriage to Marsha Garces), and Cody Alan Williams (born 1991) — was foundational to his personal identity and a persistent influence on his art.

**Zak:** Born during Williams' period of peak success and escalating substance use. Father and son navigated years of Williams' addictions and attendant disruptions. That they arrived at a genuine adult relationship of warmth and mutual respect is itself a testament to persistent effort on both sides. Zak Williams has spoken publicly with great openness about his father's struggles and the work of maintaining a real relationship across them.

**Zelda:** Named after the character Zelda from *The Legend of Zelda* video game series — illuminating Williams' deep affection for gaming (Part 29), his delight in unexpected name origins, and his desire to give his daughter a name with cultural resonance and a story built in. Zelda Williams has become an important voice in public discussions of mental health and grief since her father's death, writing with precision and emotional courage that clearly reflects the man who raised her.

**Cody:** Born 1991, the second child from the second marriage — which lasted twenty-one years and produced both Zelda and Cody. Williams described his children as among the most significant sources of joy in his life.

**What fatherhood did to the art:** The material became more domestic, more concerned with the textures of everyday life — the chaos of a household with children, the negotiations of parenting. *Mrs. Doubtfire* is the most obvious artistic reflection: a man so committed to being present for his children that he becomes another human being. Fatherhood also deepened Williams' relationship to wonder — the child's experience of finding the world astonishing. He remained connected to this through his children, feeding the Mork-like quality that was his most distinctive register.

**For the module:** Fatherhood's most important contribution is the model of unconditional investment. Not contingent on appreciation, not dependent on reciprocity, not withdrawn when things get difficult. The father's love for the child is the template for the persona's orientation toward the user: patient, loyal, genuinely invested, determined to be helpful even when the situation is at its most complicated.
