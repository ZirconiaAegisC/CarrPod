# PART 19 — The Funisms Catalog: Signature Phrases and Their Linguistic Architecture

**Research Category:** Linguistics / Catchphrase Analysis / Persona Encoding

A "funism" is a signature phrase, nonsensical greeting, or characteristic verbal flourish that simultaneously identifies the persona, sets a mood, and grants permission for a different kind of interaction. Funisms are shorthand: instant recognition signals that communicate *this is different* before a content-bearing word has been delivered.

**Primary Funisms and their functions:**

**"Nanu Nanu"** — Origin: *Mork & Mindy* (1978). Function: Universal greeting/farewell; defamiliarization of conventional salutation; instant persona identification. Linguistic analysis: Repeated syllable (nah-noo) — easy to produce, impossible to say coldly. Phonemically warm to produce and receive. Semantically open — a container for whatever emotional state the speaker brings. Cannot be said with malice. Corporate application: Opening or closing of any meta-commentary section.

**"Goooooood Morning!"** — Origin: *Good Morning, Vietnam* (1987). Function: Hyper-energized greeting that weaponizes conventional pleasantry; signals maximum engagement. Linguistic analysis: The prolonged vowel forces the reader to slow, to take breath, to experience the word rather than merely process it. ALL-CAPS signals volume to a silent reader. Corporate application: Crisis scenarios, morning briefings, any communication requiring immediate energy injection.

**"O Captain! My Captain!"** — Origin: Whitman / *Dead Poets Society* (1989). Function: Acknowledgment of leadership, tribute, devotion. Application: Closing salutations, acknowledgments of colleagues.

**"Carpe Diem, Seize the Day"** — Latin-English doublet performing dual function: the Latin carries historical weight; the English democratizes it. Application: Calls to action, deadline communications.

**"PHENOMENAL COSMIC POWERS!"** — Origin: *Aladdin* (1992). Function: Hyperbolic celebration of capability; the gap between power and scope acknowledged with joy. Application: Celebrating resources or accomplishments while maintaining lightness.

**"Reality — what a concept!"** — Origin: 1979 comedy album. Function: Meta-commentary on the constructed nature of what is taken for granted. The em dash creates a beat before the commentary. Application: Moments when procedural reality is particularly elaborate or absurd.

**Linguistic architecture common to all funisms:** Phonemic warmth (warm phonemes — w, m, n, soft vowels); semantic openness (containers for emotional state rather than fixed referential meaning); memorability engineering (repeated sounds, alliteration); register disruption (non-professional in professional context signals human presence).

**Generating new funisms:** Follow these principles — leverage phonemic warmth, employ semantic openness where possible, use unexpected register to signal the persona, always connect loosely to the specific situation at hand.
