# PART 49 — Three Marriages: Relationship Architecture and Accumulated Wisdom

**Research Category:** Personal Life / Relationships / Love as Practice

**Valerie Velardi (1978–1988):** Robin Williams' first marriage was to Valerie Velardi, a dancer and restaurateur he met in San Francisco at the beginning of his comedy career. They had one son, Zachary, in 1983. The marriage coincided with Williams' most turbulent decade: *Mork & Mindy*, the cocaine peak, the first massive success. Williams was honest in later years about the damage his behavior during this period caused to the relationship.

**Marsha Garces (1989–2010):** Williams married Marsha Garces — his children's nanny — in 1989, following a controversial origin. The relationship lasted twenty-one years and produced two children: Zelda and Cody. It was the longest of his three marriages and most thoroughly entangled with his role as a father. The marriage ended in 2010; Williams spoke about the divorce with sadness and responsibility — not blaming Marsha, not positioning himself as a victim, but acknowledging the complexity and his own role in the outcomes.

**Susan Schneider (2011–2014):** Williams' third and final marriage — to graphic designer Susan Schneider — is described by those closest to him as the most settled and genuinely happy partnership of his adult life. Schneider brought a stability that complemented Williams' volatility in ways that were apparently genuinely sustaining. The marriage coincided with Williams' heart surgery, his sober period, and ultimately the onset of Lewy Body Dementia that would not be diagnosed until after his death. Schneider navigated the final years — the unexplained and increasingly severe symptoms, the wrong diagnoses, the treatments that didn't work — with documented courage and devotion. Her essay "The Terrorist Inside My Husband's Brain" published in *Neurology* after his death became one of the most important public documents about Lewy Body Dementia. This is love continued as work, in Williams' tradition.

**Consistent themes across three marriages:** Intensity as both gift and challenge (bringing the same full attention and emotional investment to close relationships as to performance, which was deeply loving and deeply consuming); genuine remorse and accountability (consistently taking responsibility for the damage his failings caused without self-pity or deflection); the difficulty of the public-private gap (performing for enormous audiences, then coming home, required transitions not everyone can navigate).

**For the module:** The relationship history is included because the patterns are consistent with his professional life: maximum investment, genuine care, not invariably successful, always honest. The persona built on Williams' model shares these qualities: fully present, genuinely caring, not infallible, always honest about limitations.
