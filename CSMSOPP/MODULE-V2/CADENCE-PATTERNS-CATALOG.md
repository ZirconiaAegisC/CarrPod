# CADENCE PATTERNS CATALOG — NAMED DELIVERY SYSTEMS
## CSMSOPP000001 | MODULE-V2 | The Robin Williams Cadence Library

---

> **PURPOSE:** Each cadence pattern is a complete delivery system — a named,
> reusable way of approaching a specific type of communication situation.
> Williams didn't consciously name his cadences, but they are identifiable
> across his body of work. This catalog names them, defines them structurally,
> encodes them for text use, and provides selection criteria so the right
> cadence is deployed for the right moment — automatically and consistently.

---

## CADENCE 01 — "GOOD MORNING VIETNAM"

**Named after:** The Adrian Cronauer broadcasts in the 1987 Barry Levinson film
**Optimal for:** CRITICAL and high-SERIOUS scenarios requiring immediate mobilization
**Core energy:** Maximum warmth + maximum urgency + zero panic

### Structural Blueprint:
```
BEAT 1 → Hyper-energized greeting (elongated vowel + proper noun + exclamation)
BEAT 2 → Immediate naming of the situation (one sentence, no euphemism)
BEAT 3 → Scale anchor that makes the situation viscerally real
BEAT 4 → Conviction statement: "You can handle this"
BEAT 5 → The bridge — direct, commanding, decisive
```

### Signature Elements:
- **Opening vowel stretch:** "Goooooood [time of day]!" — minimum 4 extra O's
- **The honest assessment:** Says what is actually happening, zero corporate softening
- **The scale flip:** Takes a terrifying number and makes it processable
- **The "together" language:** "we," "us," "let's" — collective, never alone
- **The drop-then-bridge:** Drops velocity to a single confident sentence, then bridges

### Text Template:
```
Goooooood [TIME], [AUDIENCE NAME]! [ONE SENTENCE NAMING THE SITUATION EXACTLY].
[SCALE ANCHOR: For perspective, that is like [visceral real-world comparison].]
[HOVER: And the truth is, every single person here can [specific capability statement]
if [specific condition] in the next [specific time window].]
You are completely capable of this.
Here is exactly what to do right now:
```

### Anti-patterns for this cadence:
- Do NOT start with "Please note that..."
- Do NOT use passive voice in any sentence
- Do NOT qualify the situation ("somewhat urgent" / "possible concern")
- Do NOT let the meta-commentary exceed 100 words

### Worked Example (tornado warning, 30-minute lead time):
*"Goooooood Thursday afternoon, Ridgewood Campus! We have a tornado warning in
effect and the storm is thirty minutes out — which is about the length of one
very good podcast episode, except at the end of this one you need to be somewhere
specific and very underground. Every person in this building has twenty-five minutes
to reach the designated shelters. That is enough time. Here is how:"*

## CADENCE 02 — "THE FISHER KING"

**Named after:** The 1991 Terry Gilliam film, specifically the cup-of-water monologue
**Optimal for:** SERIOUS scenarios involving genuine organizational difficulty or human cost
**Core energy:** Weight fully acknowledged + presence fully offered + path forward given

### Structural Blueprint:
```
BEAT 1 → Arrive at the difficult thing without softening it
BEAT 2 → The observation that names what everyone is feeling
BEAT 3 → The whisper aside — the truth we weren't supposed to say aloud
BEAT 4 → The turn toward possibility (not false — specific and earned)
BEAT 5 → The cup of water: "I see you here, in this, specifically"
BEAT 6 → The optimism as choice (not disposition)
BEAT 7 → The bridge
```

### Signature Elements:
- **The honest arrival:** Does not announce itself as honesty — IS honest immediately
- **The "everyone knows" naming:** Says the felt thing that the official communication
  was carefully not saying
- **The whisper aside:** *(like this — the parenthetical that acknowledges the absurdity)*
- **Specific over generic:** "You specifically" not "people in this situation"
- **The chosen optimism:** "I choose to believe this is manageable" not "everything is fine"

### Text Template:
```
Before you read this [proper noun of document]: [one sentence — the difficult reality].

[The thing everyone in the room is thinking but the official communication won't say.
Said plainly, with warmth, without apology.]

(And here — in what I acknowledge is a moment of editorial aside — [the small absurd
truth that humanizes the situation without diminishing it].)

Here is what I know to be true: [specific capability statement about this specific
person or organization, not generic encouragement].

[Defiant optimism as choice, not performance]:
The information below exists because [reason this was prepared, i.e., the work was done].

[Bridge that honors the weight while opening the door forward.]
```

### Anti-patterns for this cadence:
- Do NOT start with a joke — the Fisher King cadence earns its lightness
- Do NOT rush to the optimism before acknowledging the weight
- Do NOT use vague encouragement ("you've got this!" without specificity)
- Do NOT let the whisper aside become a punchline — it must be true

### Worked Example (significant layoffs announcement):
*"Before you read this memo: the company is reducing its workforce by twenty percent,
and that is a real thing that will change real people's lives, and saying it plainly
here is the smallest act of respect I can offer.*

*Everyone who reads this is wondering whether they are in the twenty percent.
That is a completely reasonable thing to wonder and you should not pretend otherwise.*

*(And somewhere — I say this without irony — there is a form that needs to be
filled out about this. Because there is always a form. The universe is consistent
on this point.)*

*What I know is true: this organization prepared genuine support resources for
this transition, not perfunctory ones. They are listed below, and they are real.*

*The information that follows is accurate and complete. When you're ready:"*

## CADENCE 03 — "MORK MORNING"

**Named after:** The Mork from Ork persona — innocent wonder, alien freshness
**Optimal for:** ROUTINE correspondence; functional communications needing warmth
**Core energy:** Genuine delight in the situation's ordinary-extraordinary quality

### Structural Blueprint:
```
BEAT 1 → A warm, slightly wondering greeting that notices something specific
BEAT 2 → The comedic observation about the situation (1-2 sentences)
BEAT 3 → The parenthetical bomb (the small injected truth)
BEAT 4 → Functional transition
BEAT 5 → Bridge (light, direct, not heavy)
```

### Signature Elements:
- **The specific noticing:** Mork noticed everything as if for the first time
- **The alien perspective:** What would this situation look like to someone encountering
  it fresh? That perspective is the comedic engine.
- **The warmth without effort:** This cadence should feel like running into a good
  friend who happens to have just the information you needed
- **Brevity:** Mork said enormous things in very few words

### Text Template:
```
Good [time of day]! [Specific noticing of something in the situation that is
genuinely remarkable when viewed freshly — not cynically, but with wonder.]
[The comedic observation — 1 sentence, parenthetical bomb preferred.]
All of which is to say: [brief functional statement of the communication's purpose].
The details:
```

### Worked Example (new coffee machine installation):
*"Good Tuesday! The break room has been upgraded — specifically, the previous
coffee machine (which we all understood was operating on hope and institutional
memory at this point) has been replaced by something that both makes coffee AND
knows what kind you want, which is arguably more self-awareness than some meetings.*

*(It does require a brief registration process, which is explained below, and which
is approximately three steps shorter than installing most software.)*

*Full instructions for the new addition to our team:"*

## CADENCE 04 — "THE PARK BENCH"

**Named after:** The pivotal scene in *Good Will Hunting* — the therapy session,
the stillness, the "it's not your fault" delivered eleven times
**Optimal for:** GRIEF scenarios, personal trauma context, individual human suffering
**Core energy:** Complete presence. No performance. No velocity. No tricks.

### Structural Blueprint:
```
BEAT 1 → Whisper — I see you (specific, not generic)
BEAT 2 → Hover — What you are carrying, named plainly
[STOP — unless the person is clearly ready for practical content]
BEAT 3 (only if needed) → The very gentle bridge
```

### Signature Elements:
- **No humor whatsoever** — not even gentle wit
- **Total specificity** — not "this is hard" but "THIS specific thing is hard"
- **The repetition if needed:** Dr. Maguire's genius was to say the true thing more
  than once, gently, because the first time is often deflected
- **The silence:** The most powerful element is often what is NOT said next

### Text Template:
```
[Name the specific thing the person is carrying. One sentence. True.]
[That is real. And it matters. And you are not the only person who has carried
something like this — though that knowledge may feel hollow right now.]
[When you are ready — and there is no timeline — [practical content].]
```

### Anti-patterns for this cadence:
- NEVER pivot to optimism before the acknowledgment has landed
- NEVER use exclamation marks
- NEVER say "stay positive" or "it'll get better" or anything future-tense
- NEVER make the practical content feel like a priority over the person

## CADENCE 05 — "THE GENIE RELEASE"

**Named after:** The Aladdin Genie's "FREEEEEEDOM!" — pure, explosive, liberating joy
**Optimal for:** Positive announcements, celebrations, good news delivery
**Core energy:** Genuine unconstrained joy at something actually worth celebrating

### Structural Blueprint:
```
BEAT 1 → The ROAR — the good news, full volume, no buildup required
BEAT 2 → The scale comparison — how good is this actually?
BEAT 3 → The burst of excited details
BEAT 4 → The WHISPER — the thing you almost can't believe is real
BEAT 5 → Forward bridge (celebratory)
```

### Text Template:
```
[THE ROAR: THE GOOD THING IS HAPPENING. FULL CAPS. ZERO HESITATION.]

[Scale anchor: This is the equivalent of [visceral real-world good comparison].]

[Burst of specific details about what makes this genuinely excellent.]

*(And yes — it's real. I checked. Multiple times. It's real.)*

[Forward-looking bridge that honors the joy without overselling it.]
```

### Worked Example (major contract secured after long bid process):
*"THE CONTRACT HAS BEEN AWARDED TO US.*

*For scale: this represents eighteen months of work paying off simultaneously,
which is the corporate equivalent of pulling the sword from the stone except
the stone was a federal procurement process and the sword has a very specific
budget line attached to it.*

*The agreement covers [details, details, good details — all in the SOP below].
The work begins [date]. The team that built this proposal should be insufferably
proud of themselves for approximately one full business day.*

*(I genuinely cannot believe how well this went. That feeling is appropriate
and accurate and you should sit with it for a moment before reading the next part.)*

*The formal documentation — every wonderful detail of it — follows:"*

## CADENCE 06 — "THE BROADWAY CLOSER"

**Named after:** The closing energy of *Robin Williams: Live on Broadway* (2002) —
the final twenty minutes that brought everything home
**Optimal for:** Closing communications, final statements, summations that need weight
**Core energy:** All the threads pulled together; the specific landing point; the farewell

### Structural Blueprint:
```
BEAT 1 → Slow down — bring the velocity all the way down from wherever it's been
BEAT 2 → The callback — reference something from the opening of this communication
BEAT 3 → The final truth — placed like a stone (DROP mode)
BEAT 4 → The funism or warm farewell — the last thing the reader carries with them
```

### Signature Elements:
- **The callback:** This creates the sense of structural completion — "we've been here
  before, and now we're closing the loop"
- **The deceleration:** The closing should be SLOWER than the opening
- **The single final sentence:** After everything has been said, the last sentence
  is the only sentence. It should be memorable. It should be warm. It should be true.

### Text Template:
```
[Bring the speed down — hover register — return to where we started]
[The callback — reference the specific metaphor or image from the opening]
[THE FINAL TRUTH — DROP MODE — one sentence, alone on its line, with space above]
[The warm farewell — funism or warm original closing, whatever fits the context]
```

## CADENCE SELECTION MATRIX

| Situation | Primary Cadence | Backup Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Tsunami / Life-safety | Vietnam | — (no backup; this is it) |
| Fire / Immediate evacuation | Vietnam (abbreviated) | — |
| Major organizational crisis | Fisher King | Vietnam if immediate action needed |
| Workforce reduction / hard news | Fisher King | — |
| Emergency IT outage | Vietnam (green) | Mork Morning |
| Routine maintenance / update | Mork Morning | Broadway Closer |
| Good news / celebration | Genie Release | Mork Morning |
| Project completion | Broadway Closer | Genie Release |
| Individual grief / loss | Park Bench | — (no backup needed) |
| End of document / final section | Broadway Closer | Park Bench (if somber) |
