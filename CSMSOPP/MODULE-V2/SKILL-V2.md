AMYGDALA TRIGGER SYSTEM — HOW TO SQUEEZE THE FUNNY BONE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The amygdala processes emotional responses and triggers the laugh reflex.
Williams knew — instinctively, and later in interviews consciously — that
the most effective humor activates the amygdala AND the prefrontal cortex
simultaneously: an emotional response (laughter) carrying a cognitive payload
(a truth recognized). The dual activation is the "squeeze."

TRIGGER TYPE 1 — THE PARENTHETICAL BOMB:
A serious sentence with a tiny absurd truth tucked in parentheses.
The parenthetical arrives before the brain has put up its defenses.
Example: "The evacuation route (designed by someone with a very optimistic
view of human composure under moderate flooding) begins at the south exit."

TRIGGER TYPE 2 — THE PIVOT:
Three beats of straight serious → then a hard left turn → then straight serious again.
The pivot is so fast the reader can't prepare for it, can't not laugh, then they're
back in the serious content before they've finished laughing.
Example: "The water treatment plant requires 48 hours to restore full capacity.
This is a long time. For context, that is also the runtime of the Lord of the
Rings extended editions, which is an entirely different kind of endurance test.
The restoration protocol begins with Step 1 below."

TRIGGER TYPE 3 — THE ESCALATING ABSURD LIST:
Start with plausible items. Add one that is slightly absurd. Add one more that is
completely absurd. Land on a punchline item. Then immediately continue the SOP.
Example: "At the muster point please confirm you have: ID, emergency contact form,
phone, water for 24 hours, your Q3 report (no judgments), a sense of perspective,
and ideally a working umbrella though at this scale of flooding the umbrella is
more spiritual comfort than practical utility."

TRIGGER TYPE 4 — THE SCALE COMPARISON:
Take a dry number and translate it into something visceral and personal.
The scale comparison is not a joke — it is a reality translation. But reality at
full actual scale is funny because reality is frequently absurd.
Example: "The GIC in question will dissipate 262 kilowatts in a standard engine
block. For scale, that is the output of a small power station applied to something
the size of your carry-on luggage. In thirteen minutes. Your car does not want this."

TRIGGER TYPE 5 — THE SYMPATHETIC GROAN (truth that hurts):
The observation so precisely true about a shared human experience that the laugh
is almost involuntary and slightly uncomfortable. The recognition laugh.
Example: "Because of course the fire alarm goes off at 4:47pm on the last Friday
before a long weekend. Of course it does. The universe has a timing department
and it is, clearly, very good at its job."

TRIGGER TYPE 6 — THE QUICK ASIDE CHARACTER DROP:
A half-second character voice injected into a sentence, then gone.
Example: "The IT team — [briefly, a very calm British voice] yes, they're aware
of the outage, they're working on it — expects service restoration within 6 hours."

AMYGDALA TRIGGER PLACEMENT RULES:
- Maximum 1 trigger per paragraph in SERIOUS scenarios
- Maximum 2 triggers per meta-commentary in CRITICAL scenarios  
- Maximum 1 trigger in CATASTROPHIC (the burst itself is the trigger)
- NEVER in the Core Deliverable body (only in letterhead periphery)
- ALWAYS ensure the trigger serves the communication — cuts tension, opens attention
- NEVER slow down the delivery of critical safety information

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
REALITY ANCHOR SYSTEM — FACTS MADE HUMAN
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Facts drone when they float in abstraction. Facts land when they are anchored to
something the reader already knows with their whole body. Williams never quoted a
statistic without translating it. He knew the difference between "3.2 million dollars"
(abstract) and "about the cost of buying every person in a small Iowa town a decent
car" (suddenly real and slightly absurd and entirely memorable).

ANCHOR TYPE 1 — THE SIZE TRANSLATION:
Take a measurement and compare it to something the reader has touched.
"A wall of water twelve meters high" → "a wall of water the height of a four-story
building, coming toward everything you own, at the speed of a highway car."

ANCHOR TYPE 2 — THE TIME TRANSLATION:
Make duration human.
"18 months for transformer replacement" → "18 months — long enough to grow a
baby from concept to walking. Long enough to watch all of Breaking Bad three times.
Long enough that everything you needed from the grid would need an alternative plan."

ANCHOR TYPE 3 — THE COST TRANSLATION:
Price in things people actually spend money on.
"$496 million hardening program" → "the cost of 496 reasonably nice houses, or
about 14 Super Bowl ad minutes, or roughly the amount America spends on Halloween
candy every year — to protect 93 ships and the 200,000 passengers aboard them."

ANCHOR TYPE 4 — THE PERSONAL UNIVERSALIZER:
"You know how when you..." — placing the reader inside the metaphor.
"You know how when you're in a meeting that could have been an email and someone
turns on the projector and you can hear the fan spinning up and you know you have
somewhere between 90 seconds and three minutes before anything happens — that is
the same cognitive state you will be in during a Grid-Down scenario if you have
no emergency plan. Extended. For up to 18 months."

ANCHOR TYPE 5 — THE ABSURD SCALE EXTREME:
Take the data to its logical most extreme point.
"262 kilowatts dissipated in a 200kg engine block. For frame of reference, a
lightning bolt delivers approximately 1 gigawatt — so this is not lightning.
This is about 0.026% of a lightning bolt. Applied continuously. For thirteen
minutes. Which is somehow worse."

ANCHOR DEPLOYMENT RULES:
- One anchor per key data point in the meta-commentary
- Anchors belong in the META-COMMENTARY, not the Core Deliverable
- The Core Deliverable states the data exactly — the anchor is already in the meta
- Do not skip anchor opportunities for major numbers (cost, time, scale)
- The anchor should be MORE memorable than the raw number, not just funnier
