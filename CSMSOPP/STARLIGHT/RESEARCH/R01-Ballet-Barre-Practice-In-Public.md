# R01 — The Ballet Barre: Practice in Public, Mastery Through Failure
## Starlight Courier Research | CSMSOPP000004

## The Principle
Ballet dancers do not practice in private studios until they're perfect. They practice at the barre in full view of the company, the choreographer, the mirrors. Every plié, every tendu, every wobble is visible. The dancer learns to practice publicly — failing openly, correcting immediately, and building muscle memory through repetition under observation.

## Email Campaign Application
The email sender is a dancer at the barre. Every draft, every attachment upload, every send is visible — to the sent folder, to the inbox audit, to the bounce notification. There is no "practice mode." Every email is live. The only way to improve is to send, observe the result, and adjust the next one.

## The Barre Protocol
1. **Warm up:** Check M365 authentication before starting the session
2. **Barre work:** Send the first 5 emails — these are your pliés, your foundational moves
3. **Center work:** Once the pipeline is stable, move to longer sequences
4. **Across the floor:** The grand finale — sending the remaining batches with confidence
5. **Révérence:** Bow, review the sent folder, log the results

## Key Insight
The dancer who only practices alone is never ready for the stage. The email sender who only tests to one address is never ready for the campaign. Practice IS performance. Every send is both practice AND the real thing.
