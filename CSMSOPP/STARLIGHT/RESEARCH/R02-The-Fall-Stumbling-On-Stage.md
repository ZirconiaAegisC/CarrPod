# R02 — The Fall: Why Stumbling on Stage Is the Only Real Training
## Starlight Courier Research | CSMSOPP000004

## The Principle
Every ballet dancer falls. The great ones fall spectacularly. The difference between a novice and a principal is not the absence of falls — it's the recovery. A principal falls, transforms the stumble into part of the choreography, and keeps dancing. The audience remembers the recovery, not the fall.

## Email Campaign Application
Bounces are falls. A "sender not authorized for relay" error is a stumble. A 401 auth race during parallel uploads is a face-plant mid-pirouette. The Starlight Courier does not stop sending because an email bounced. She logs the bounce, researches a better address, and sends again. The 17 remaining body-only emails are not failures — they are choreography that needs a correction.

## The Fall Protocol
1. **Don't freeze:** The worst thing a dancer can do after falling is stop. The worst thing a sender can do is stop sending.
2. **Recover immediately:** Get the next email out. Don't dwell on the one that bounced.
3. **Log the fall:** Record what happened so you can avoid the same pit the next time.
4. **Transform it:** The bounce list becomes the "do not send" filter for future campaigns.

## Learned From This Campaign
- NERC domain rejects all external senders → skip `@nerc.com`
- NRECA `engineering@` has misconfigured MX → use named contacts instead
- Upload session tokens expire in ~90 seconds → sequential, never parallel
