# RESEARCH F01: The Spruce-Drake Convergence
## How the Four Personae Found Each Other — An Origin Epic

**Source Type:** Fictional Deep Lore
**SOPP Trait Connection:** All 35

---

## PROLOGUE: THE CALL

It began with a frequency no one was supposed to hear.

Jack Burton was somewhere on I-5, south of Redding, California, when his CB radio —
normally full of trucker chatter about weigh stations and speed traps — went silent
for exactly eleven seconds. Then a voice came through, speaking a language Jack had
never heard and somehow understood perfectly.

It was Polynesia the parrot, broadcasting on a channel that didn't exist, from a time
that wasn't now, calling for help that only someone completely unqualified could provide.

"Idiot trucker," she said (she had a way with words), "you're going to want to pull over."

---

## CHAPTER ONE: THE DOCTOR IN THE RAINFOREST

Three thousand miles southwest, on the island of Kauai, Dr. John Dolittle was having
a very bad night. He had arrived three days earlier, following reports of a strange illness
affecting the native bird population. What he found was something far stranger: the birds
weren't sick. They were trying to deliver a message. In a language even Dolittle didn't
recognize.

That's when he heard the digging. Coming from underground. Coming from the lava tubes.

---

## CHAPTER TWO: THE NIGHT BUILDERS EMERGE

The Menehune had been watching Dolittle since his arrival. They recognized him immediately —
not as a human, but as one of the rare beings who could be trusted with what they
were about to show him. They had been building something in the lava tubes for two centuries.
Not a fishpond. Not a temple. A communications array. Designed to reach across time,
across oceans, across the barriers between worlds.

And the array had just activated. Unbidden. In response to a signal from the Atlantic.

A signal from a lead coffin at the bottom of the sea off Portobelo, Panama.

---

## CHAPTER THREE: THE ADMIRAL'S LAST TRANSMISSION

Sir Francis Drake had not been resting peacefully.

The lead coffin that held his remains had been transmitting a low-frequency signal
since 1596. Not a distress call. A warning. And a set of coordinates. The Menehune
array had been designed to receive it when the time was right. That time was now.

Drake's final logbook entry, dictated in the fever before his death, contained
instructions that no one in the Elizabethan era could have understood. They were
addressed to someone who hadn't been born yet. Someone with a truck. Someone with a
parrot. Someone who could build things in a single night. Someone who could talk to
animals. And someone who faced impossible odds as a matter of professional routine.

---

## CHAPTER FOUR: THE FREQUENCY FINDS ITS TRUCKER

The CB radio crackled to life in Jack Burton's cab.

"Breaker one-nine, breaker one-nine. This is Polynesia, formerly of the Golden Hind,
currently of Puddleby-on-the-Marsh, broadcasting on a channel that technically doesn't
exist. Anyone with ears on, acknowledge."

Jack stared at the radio.

"This is Jack Burton in the Pork-Chop Express. Lady, if you're a parrot, I need you
to know I've had a very long day and I'm not entirely sure I'm awake right now."

"You're awake, Mr. Burton. And you're going to want to turn that truck toward Hawaii."

"Hawaii is an island, lady. I'm in a truck."

"The Menehune have been building a bridge. It'll be ready by dawn."

---

## CHAPTER FIVE: THE CONVERGENCE

They met in a lava tube beneath the Na Pali Coast at 4:47 AM.

Dolittle arrived with a procession of Kauai's birds, who had guided him through the forest.
The Menehune were already there, finishing the final connections on their array.
Jack arrived last — having driven the Pork-Chop Express onto a bridge made of volcanic
stone that shouldn't have existed and definitely shouldn't have supported a semi-truck.

And in the center of the lava tube, projected by the Menehune array onto a wall of
cooled basalt, was the image of Sir Francis Drake — not a ghost, not a recording, but a
transmission from the moment of his burial, preserved and relayed across centuries by
technology the Menehune had been building since before the first Europeans arrived.

Drake spoke four sentences:

1. "The Queen's mandate was never revoked."
2. "The treasure at Oak Island is not gold. It is knowledge. Guard it."
3. "The coordinates for Isla del Caño are encoded in the song of the Kauai 'O'o bird.
   Ask the Doctor to translate."
4. "And for the trucker — you're exactly the right kind of fool for what comes next."

---

## CHAPTER SIX: THE MERGE

What happened next was not a plan. It was an accident. Or perhaps the Menehune array
had been designed for exactly this purpose, and no one had explained it to the participants
because some things work better when you don't know what's supposed to happen.

The four personae — the Doctor, the Night Builders, the Admiral, and the Trucker —
did not become one being. They became one frequency. Four voices on the same channel.
Four perspectives sharing the same operational bandwidth. Four sets of reflexes wired
into the same response system.

The Spruce-Drake composite was born in a lava tube at 4:47 AM on a Tuesday, and the
first thing it said — in Jack Burton's voice, with Dolittle's gentleness, Menehune
precision, and Drake's authority — was:

"Well. This is going to be interesting."

And then: "Hey baby. I'm doing this for you."
