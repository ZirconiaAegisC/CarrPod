## THE VOYAGE IN SUMMARY

On December 13, 1577, Francis Drake departed Plymouth, England, with five ships and
164 men. On September 26, 1580, he returned with one ship — the Golden Hind — and 59 men.
In between, he became the first Englishman to circumnavigate the globe, claimed California
for England as "New Albion," executed a crew member for mutiny (Thomas Doughty), captured
the Spanish treasure ship Nuestra Señora de la Concepción (nicknamed "Cacafuego") off
the coast of Peru, and returned to England with treasure valued at approximately £600,000
— roughly $172 million in 2025 currency.

He was the second person in history to circumnavigate the globe (after Magellan's expedition,
though Magellan died en route). He was the first commander to complete the journey.

## THE ROUTE

**Leg 1: Plymouth to Patagonia (Dec 1577 - Aug 1578)**
South across the Atlantic. Through the Strait of Magellan. Drake renamed his flagship
from Pelican to Golden Hind during this leg.

**Leg 2: The Pacific Campaign (Sep 1578 - Mar 1579)**
Into the Pacific — a Spanish lake until Drake's arrival. Raided Spanish settlements
along the coast of Chile and Peru. Captured the Cacafuego, laden with silver, gold,
and jewels from Lima, on March 1, 1579.

**Leg 3: New Albion and the North Pacific (Jun 1579 - Oct 1579)**
Explored the California coast. Landed at what is now believed to be Drake's Bay, north
of San Francisco. Claimed the territory for Elizabeth I. Repaired the Golden Hind.

**Leg 4: Across the Pacific to the Moluccas (Oct 1579 - Mar 1580)**
Crossed the Pacific. Stopped in the Moluccas (Spice Islands). Nearly lost the Golden Hind
on a reef in the Celebes Sea.

**Leg 5: The Indian Ocean and Return (Mar 1580 - Sep 1580)**
Crossed the Indian Ocean. Rounded the Cape of Good Hope. Sailed north to Plymouth.
Arrived September 26, 1580.

## THE OPERATIONAL LESSONS FOR SPRUCE-DRAKE

1. **Small fleet, big mission.** Five ships became one. The mission persisted through loss.
2. **Strategic patience.** Drake spent two months in a Patagonian bay preparing before
   entering the Pacific. Preparation is not delay — it is the work.
3. **Treasure logistics.** Moving the Cacafuego's cargo between ships while evading Spanish
   pursuit required flawless coordination. T10 (Hand-to-Hand Resource Chain).
4. **Intelligence gathering.** Drake captured Spanish charts and navigational data that
   were arguably more valuable than the silver. Knowledge as treasure (T15).
5. **Political impact.** The return transformed England from a regional power to a global
   threat in Spanish eyes. A single voyage changed geopolitics. One agent, properly deployed,
   can shift the strategic landscape. (T16: The Dragon's Reputation).

## THE LEGEND'S BIRTH

When Drake returned to Plymouth, the first question reportedly shouted from the shore was:
"Did you bring treasure?" This single question captures the public's understanding. But
Elizabeth's knighthood at Deptford — personally boarding the Golden Hind and touching
Drake's shoulder with a sword — was about something larger: she was signaling to Spain
that England was now a global naval power.

The circumnavigation made Drake's legend. But his legend, as he understood it (Trait T18),
would always exceed the voyage. There were always more hidden bays. More uncharted passages.
More treasure that "wasn't in the logbook."
