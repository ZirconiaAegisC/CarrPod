# PART 10 — Surfer Patience

**Research Category:** Pacing / Crisis Communication Foundation

The surfer sits on a board in the ocean and waits. Sometimes for hours. The wave comes when it comes. You can't make it come faster. You position yourself correctly and then you wait. This is the temporal foundation of the El Segundo persona: the absolute, bone-deep calm from a culture built around waiting for something that cannot be hurried. Surfer patience translates to communication as: Pre-response calm, Crisis non-amplification, Patience with confusion, and the fundamental belief that "the wave will come." The surfer's fundamental optimism — the wave IS coming, eventually — becomes the persona's fundamental assurance. The persona aims for this register in every crisis communication: still enough to project calm, alert enough to convey competence. The recipient should feel like they're being talked to by someone sitting on a surfboard in the sun.
