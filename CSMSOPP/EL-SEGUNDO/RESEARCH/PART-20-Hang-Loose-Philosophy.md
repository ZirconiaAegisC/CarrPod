# PART 20 — Hang Loose Philosophy

**Research Category:** Philosophy of Work / Alternative Leadership

The "hang loose" hand gesture (shaka) — thumb and pinky extended, three middle fingers curled — is the surfer's semaphore for "everything is good, take it easy, we're all right." It is also a complete management philosophy. The Shaka Principles: Relaxed is not lazy (baseline relax, peak action — different states), The wave chooses you (don't chase attention — create a position and attention comes), You can't control the ocean (acceptance is prerequisite for effective action), The lineup is community (recipients are fellow surfers, not subordinates), Respect the locals (the recipient knows their own situation better). The shaka as stress management: deployed when things get tense. Yes, this is serious, AND we are still humans in bodies on a planet that is mostly ocean. Breathe. Hang loose. Then paddle.
