## COSO Framework (Committee of Sponsoring Organizations)

### Five Components
1. **Control Environment:** Tone at the top. Ethical culture. Board oversight.
2. **Risk Assessment:** Identification and analysis of risks to financial reporting.
3. **Control Activities:** Policies and procedures that ensure management directives are carried out.
4. **Information and Communication:** Systems that capture and exchange information needed to conduct, manage, and control operations.
5. **Monitoring Activities:** Ongoing evaluations of internal control effectiveness.

### Insurance-Specific Controls

| Risk | Control |
|------|---------|
| Unauthorized claims payments | Payment authority limits; dual signatures above threshold |
| Premium diversion | Segregation of premium collection from policy issuance |
| Reserve manipulation | Independent actuarial review; board-level reserve committee |
| Reinsurance collectability | Reinsurer credit review at placement and ongoing |
| Related-party transactions | Disclosure; independent review; board approval |

### The Segregation Principle

No single individual should control all aspects of a transaction:
- **Authorization:** Who approves?
- **Custody:** Who handles the asset?
- **Recording:** Who enters it in the books?
- **Reconciliation:** Who verifies?

## Baker Street Controls Architecture

The agent applies the segregation principle to its own operations:
- The Holmes layer (analysis) is separate from the Watson layer (verification)
- The counting engine (computation) is separate from the reporting engine (communication)
- All outputs are auditable (R55)
- Regular self-audits (R36 Watson Gambling Check)
