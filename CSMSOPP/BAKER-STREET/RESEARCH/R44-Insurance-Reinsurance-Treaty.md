## Treaty Types

### Proportional Treaties
| Type | Mechanism | Application |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Quota Share | Reinsurer takes fixed % of all risks in a portfolio | New entrants, capital relief, start-up portfolios |
| Surplus Share | Reinsurer takes % of risks exceeding retention | Property portfolios with varying values |

### Non-Proportional Treaties
| Type | Mechanism | Application |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Excess of Loss (per risk) | Reinsurer pays losses exceeding retention, per individual risk | Large commercial property accounts |
| Excess of Loss (per occurrence) | Reinsurer pays losses exceeding retention, per event | Catastrophe protection |
| Aggregate Excess of Loss | Reinsurer pays when aggregate annual losses exceed attachment point | Frequency protection; stop-loss |
| Stop Loss | Reinsurer pays when loss ratio exceeds specified percentage | Portfolio-wide protection |

### Facultative Reinsurance
Individual risk placement, not automatic. Used for risks exceeding treaty capacity, risks excluded from treaty, or when treaty capacity is fully deployed. Negotiated per risk.

### Key Structural Elements
- **Retention:** Amount the ceding company retains before reinsurance attaches
- **Limit:** Maximum reinsurer payment
- **Layer:** More than coverage in excess of a specific retention (e.g., $5M xs $5M = $5M coverage above $5M retention)
- **Reinstatement:** Number of times coverage resets after a loss (e.g., 1 reinstatement = coverage replenished once)
- **Premium:** Usually calculated as % of ceded premium (proportional) or rate applied to subject premium (non-proportional)

### Baker Street Reinsurance Analysis
For every major client, the agent maps:
1. Current treaty structure and capacity
2. Gaps between treaty limits and realistic catastrophe scenarios
3. Facultative placement needs
4. Reinsurance credit quality (reinsurer rating, collateral requirements)
