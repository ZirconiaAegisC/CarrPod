## COSO ERM Framework

Enterprise Risk Management integrates risk management into strategy-setting and performance management.

### Components
1. **Governance and Culture:** Board oversight, operating structure, desired culture
2. **Strategy and Objective-Setting:** Risk appetite, business context, strategic objectives
3. **Performance:** Risk identification, assessment, prioritization, response
4. **Review and Revision:** Monitoring, reassessment, continuous improvement
5. **Information, Communication, and Reporting:** Internal/external communication, reporting systems

### Insurance ERM Risks

| Risk Category | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| Underwriting Risk | Pricing inadequacy, adverse selection, concentration |
| Reserve Risk | Underestimation of ultimate liabilities |
| Market Risk | Investment portfolio deterioration |
| Credit Risk | Reinsurance recoverable collectability |
| Operational Risk | System failure, fraud, process breakdown |
| Liquidity Risk | Inability to meet obligations when due |
| Strategic Risk | Competitive displacement, regulatory change |
| Reputational Risk | Loss of stakeholder trust |
| Emerging Risk | New perils (cyber, climate, Carrington) with limited data |

### Risk Appetite Statement
"ABC Insurance will maintain surplus sufficient to withstand a 1-in-250-year aggregate loss event without breaching minimum regulatory capital requirements."

The Baker Street agent helps formulate and monitor risk appetite by linking exposure data (R61-R70) to capital models.
