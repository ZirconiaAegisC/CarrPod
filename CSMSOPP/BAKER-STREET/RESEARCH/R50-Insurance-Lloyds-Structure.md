## Lloyd's Architecture

### The Corporation of Lloyd's
- Not an insurance company — a marketplace and regulatory body
- Overseen by the Council of Lloyd's
- Operates under the Lloyd's Act (UK Parliament)
- Maintains the Central Fund for policyholder security

### Syndicates
- Underwriting vehicles managed by Managing Agents
- Each syndicate backed by Members (corporate or individual Names) providing capital
- Syndicates compete within Lloyd's; they are not a single risk pool
- Annual venture: syndicates are reconstituted each year

### Brokers
- Only Lloyd's-accredited brokers can place business in the market
- Brokers represent insureds (or ceding insurers for reinsurance)
- The broker presents the risk in the Underwriting Room

### The Chain of Security
1. Syndicate-level assets (premium trust funds)
2. Members' funds at Lloyd's (capital supporting underwriting)
3. Central Fund (mutual security — last resort)

### Financial Strength
- Lloyd's maintains ratings from AM Best, S&P, Fitch
- Central Fund provides an additional layer of security beyond syndicate-level capital
- Historically, no valid Lloyd's policyholder claim has gone unpaid

### The Futureset Program
Lloyd's internal research arm for systemic risk scenarios — including the March 2025 solar storm scenario ($2.4 trillion global loss). The Baker Street agent's CSMEval11 outreach materials target this exact program as the warmest introduction to the insurance sector.
