## The Charlemagne Fleet

The CSMVessel Charlemagne-Class Fleet research represents the marine hardening program for cruise ships and commercial vessels against Carrington-level EMP/GIC events.

### Key Systems at Risk
| Ship System | GIC/EMP Effect | Consequence | Insurance Line |
|-------------|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| Navigation (GPS) | Signal degradation/loss | Grounding, collision risk | Hull, P&I |
| Communications (SATCOM) | Blackout | Unable to call for assistance | P&I, crisis management |
| Engine Control | Microprocessor damage | Propulsion loss | Hull, machinery |
| Power Management | Transformer damage | Blackout at sea | Hull, BI |
| Safety Systems | Fire suppression, watertight doors | Cascading damage | Hull, P&I |
| Passenger Systems | HVAC, lighting, galley | Passenger injury/illness | P&I, medical payments |

### Charlemagne Hardening Specifications
- MXene EMI shielding for critical electronics bays
- Faraday-cage architecture for navigation and communications
- Redundant mechanical backup for critical safety systems
- GIC monitoring and automated isolation systems

### Insurance Quantification
- Base hull value: variable by vessel class
- BI exposure: $X per day out of service
- P&I exposure: passenger and crew liability
- Hardening premium credit: reduction proportional to survivability improvement
