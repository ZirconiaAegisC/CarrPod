## The Physics

### Coronal Mass Ejection (CME)
A massive expulsion of plasma and magnetic field from the Sun's corona. Travels at 1-5 million mph. Reaches Earth in 15-72 hours depending on velocity. The Carrington Event of 1859 arrived in 17.6 hours.

### Geomagnetic Storm
When the CME's magnetic field couples with Earth's magnetosphere, it induces geoelectric fields at the surface. These fields drive Geomagnetically Induced Currents (GIC) through long conductors — power lines, pipelines, undersea cables, railways.

### E3 EMP Component
Unlike the E1 (fast) and E2 (intermediate) EMP components from nuclear detonations, a CME produces only the E3 component — a slow, sustained pulse lasting minutes to hours. It couples efficiently with long transmission lines.
