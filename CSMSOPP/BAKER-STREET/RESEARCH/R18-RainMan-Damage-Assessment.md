## The Scenario

If Raymond Babbitt were handed a catastrophe exposure dataset — say, 20,000 properties in a hurricane-prone coastal zone, each with construction type, year built, wind mitigation features, and replacement cost — he would process it not as "high risk" or "low risk" but as 20,000 discrete data points, each compared against every other, instantly identifying:

1. Which properties lack wind mitigation features
2. Which construction types correlate with highest claims frequency
3. Which zip codes have anomalous loss patterns
4. Which policy limits are misaligned with replacement cost

## Baker Street Catastrophe Protocol

### Pre-Event
- Exposure concentration analysis by peril, by LOB, by geographic zone
- Reinsurance attachment point verification against modeled loss scenarios
- Policy limit adequacy check against inflation-adjusted replacement costs

### Post-Event
- FNOL processing: categorize claims by severity (toothpick method — sort all 246, not just the first 10)
- Reserve setting: each claim individually reserved based on historical matching claims
- Subrogation identification: each claim scanned for third-party liability (Holmes layer activated)
