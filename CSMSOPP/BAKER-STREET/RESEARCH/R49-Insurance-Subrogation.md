## Insurance Domain Research | CSMSOPP000003

## Legal Foundation

Subrogation is the insurer's right to "step into the shoes" of the insured after paying a claim and pursue recovery from the responsible third party. It prevents double recovery by the insured and ensures the loss is ultimately borne by the party at fault.

## Subrogation Process

### Phase 1: Identification
During initial claim investigation, the adjuster identifies potential subrogation targets:
- Third-party tortfeasors (negligent drivers, contractors, manufacturers)
- Product manufacturers (product liability)
- Contractual indemnitors (leases, construction contracts, vendor agreements)
- Other insurers (other insurance clauses, contribution)

### Phase 2: Preservation
- Evidence preserved (physical, documentary, witness)
- Statute of limitations tracked
- Lien rights preserved (if applicable)
- Subrogation potential documented in claim file

### Phase 3: Recovery
- Demand letter to responsible party
- Negotiation with responsible party's insurer
- Litigation if necessary (within cost/benefit parameters)
- Inter-company arbitration (for insurer-vs-insurer disputes)

### Phase 4: Allocation
- Recovery allocated: first to deductible (reimbursed to insured), then to insurer, then to expenses, then to insured for any uncompensated loss

## The Holmes Layer in Subrogation

The Holmes deducer (R21-R30) is optimized for subrogation identification. The trifle protocol (R23) flags the inconsistencies that indicate third-party fault. The counting engine (R12) identifies patterns across multiple claims that point to common responsible parties.
