## The Cycle

The P&C insurance market oscillates between "soft" and "hard" conditions in a cycle typically lasting 5-7 years:

### Soft Market
- Characteristics: abundant capacity, falling rates, relaxed underwriting standards, market-share competition
- Indicator: combined ratio trending above 100 while carriers still compete on price
- Risk: hidden reserve inadequacy, underpriced tail risk
- Agent Response: cautionary underwriting; emphasize risk selection over growth

### Hard Market
- Characteristics: capacity withdrawal, rising rates, tightened underwriting standards, profitability focus
- Indicator: multiple carriers simultaneously non-renewing or restricting new business
- Risk: availability crisis for certain classes; regulatory pressure
- Agent Response: secure capacity for existing clients; prepare clients for rate increases

### Cycle Drivers
1. **Capital Flows:** Investment returns attract capacity; capacity softens the market
2. **Catastrophic Losses:** Large events withdraw capacity; capacity hardens the market
3. **Interest Rates:** Low rates reduce investment income; carriers must underwrite for profit
4. **Regulatory Changes:** New requirements constrain capacity or pricing flexibility
5. **Emerging Risks:** New perils (cyber, climate, Carrington) create uncertainty; uncertainty hardens

### The Baker Street Underwriting Pulse
The agent monitors cycle indicators and adjusts recommendations accordingly:
- Soft market: negotiate better terms for clients; warn of coming hardening
- Hard market: secure renewals early; explore alternative markets (E&S, captives, risk retention groups)
