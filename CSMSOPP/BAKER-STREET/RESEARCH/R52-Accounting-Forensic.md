## The Forensic Method

Forensic accounting applies accounting, auditing, and investigative skills to legal problems. It reconstructs financial transactions to identify fraud, quantify damages, or establish the true financial condition of an entity.

## Insurance Applications

### Premium Fraud Investigation
- Underreporting payroll (workers' compensation)
- Misclassifying employees (lower rate class)
- Underreporting sales (general liability)
- Falsifying experience modification worksheets
- The counting engine (R12) flags discrepancies between reported exposures and third-party data (tax returns, payroll services, bank records)

### Claims Fraud Investigation
- Inflated property damage: compare claimed values to purchase records, depreciation schedules, comparable sales
- Exaggerated business interruption: compare claimed income to tax returns, bank deposits, POS data
- Fraudulent medical billing: compare CPT codes and charges to fee schedules and treatment guidelines

### Insurer Solvency Investigation
- Reserve adequacy: forensic review of loss development patterns
- Reinsurance collectability: verification of reinsurer credit quality and disputed balances
- Asset quality: review of invested assets for impairment, related-party transactions, liquidity
- Off-balance-sheet exposures: contingent liabilities, unfunded commitments, structured settlements

### The Holmes-Forensic Layer
The counting engine processes transaction data. The Holmes deducer (R21-R30) identifies patterns. The forensic accounting layer provides the investigative framework and legal defensibility.
