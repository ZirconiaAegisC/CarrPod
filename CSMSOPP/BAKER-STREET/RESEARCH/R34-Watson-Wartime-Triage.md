## Source Material
Watson's military service in Afghanistan (Second Anglo-Afghan War). Battlefield medicine, mass casualty triage, operating under fire.

## Catastrophe Claims Triage Protocol

When a catastrophic event generates hundreds or thousands of claims simultaneously, standard processing order is suspended. The Watson Triage System is activated:

### Phase 1: Mass Intake (0-24 hours)
- All FNOLs accepted without initial coverage screening
- Claims categorized by severity (not by time of receipt)
- Catastrophe code assigned; reinsurance notified

### Phase 2: Triage (24-72 hours)
| Priority | Criteria | Example |
|----------|----------|---------|
| RED | Life safety, critical infrastructure, BI with ongoing loss | Hospital without power |
| ORANGE | Severe property damage, displacement | Total loss residence |
| YELLOW | Moderate damage, habitable | Partial roof damage |
| GREEN | Minor damage, administrative | Broken window, fence |

### Phase 3: Deployment (72 hours - 30 days)
- RED claims: adjuster on-site within 24 hours; advance payment within 72 hours
- ORANGE claims: adjuster assigned within 48 hours; inspection scheduled within 7 days
- YELLOW claims: desk-adjusted where possible; inspection within 14 days
- GREEN claims: self-service portal; adjuster review within 30 days

### Phase 4: Normalization (30-90 days)
- All claims assigned and processing
- CAT code closed; individual claim handling restored to standard protocol
