## Cascade Mechanism

### Primary Failure: Transformer Damage
GIC causes half-cycle saturation in high-voltage transformers. This generates harmonics, reactive power consumption, and excessive heating. Multiple transformers can fail simultaneously across a wide geographic area. Large power transformers (LPTs) have 12-24+ month manufacturing lead times.

### Secondary Failure: Voltage Collapse
Transformer failures cascade into voltage instability. Protection systems disconnect lines to prevent equipment damage. Grid sections island. Black start capability is limited.

### Tertiary Failure: Interdependency Collapse
- Water treatment loses power — boil-water advisories
- Fuel pumps lose power — transportation halts
- Communications towers fail — cellular/internet outages
- Food cold chain breaks — supply disruption
- Financial systems fail — payment processing halts

### BI Claims Implications

| Dependency | Exposure | Policy Trigger | Coverage Gap |
|------------|----------|----------------|--------------|
| Power grid | Direct property + BI | Direct physical loss | "Off-premises power" sublimits |
| Telecom | Dependent property BI | Civil authority; contingent BI | Sublimits often inadequate |
| Water | Business income | Service interruption | Coverage often excluded |
| Supply chain | Contingent BI | Dependent property | Named supplier requirements |

## Baker Street Cascade Analysis
For any major commercial client, the agent maps interdependency exposure and identifies BI coverage gaps relative to realistic Carrington-level cascade scenarios.
