## Domain Knowledge Research | CSMSOPP000003

## Damage Mechanism

### Half-Cycle Saturation
GIC (near-DC) enters transformer windings through grounded neutral connections. During one half of the AC cycle, the transformer core saturates, drawing excessive magnetizing current. This causes:
1. Harmonics injection into the grid
2. Reactive power consumption surge
3. Excessive internal heating (hot-spot temperatures exceeding design limits)
4. Winding insulation degradation
5. Tank heating from stray flux

### Failure Modes
| Mode | Mechanism | Repair Timeline |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| Bushing failure | Overheating; internal flashover | Days (if spares available) |
| Winding deformation | Excessive mechanical forces | Weeks to months |
| Insulation failure | Thermal degradation | Full rewind: 6-12 months |
| Core damage | Permanent magnetic property alteration | Replacement only: 12-24+ months |

### LPT Supply Chain
Large Power Transformers (LPTs, >100 MVA) are custom-engineered with:
- 5-16 month design/engineering lead time
- 12-24+ month manufacturing lead time
- Limited global manufacturing capacity
- Single points of failure in the supply chain (GOES — Grain-Oriented Electrical Steel)

## Insurance Implications
- Transformer damage claims involve long-tail BI
- Replacement cost + expediting charges = significant property damage
- Extended outage = significant BI (waiting period adequacy issue)
- Aggregate exposure: multiple transformers failing simultaneously across a region = treaty limit breach risk
