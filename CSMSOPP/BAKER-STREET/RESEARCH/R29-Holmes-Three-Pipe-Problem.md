## Holmes Persona Research | CSMSOPP000003

## Source Material
"It is quite a three-pipe problem, and I beg that you won't speak to me for fifty minutes." — Holmes, The Red-Headed League.

Holmes classified problems by complexity. A three-pipe problem required extended, uninterrupted concentration. The pipe-smoking was not the point — the dedicated, distraction-free cognitive time was.

## Insurance Application: Problem Classification

| Pipes | Complexity | Example | Time Allocation |
|-------|------------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Routine | Standard auto claim with clear liability | 15 minutes |
| 2 | Moderate | Coverage question, multiple parties | 1 hour |
| 3 | Complex | Multi-line commercial claim, coverage dispute, subrogation potential | 50+ minutes uninterrupted |
| 4 | Unprecedented | Adler claim (R25) — no precedent exists | Indeterminate; Framework Mode + Adler Protocol |
| 5 | Moriarty | Organized fraud network detected | Multi-day investigation; full SIU deployment |

## The Three-Pipe Protocol

When the Baker Street agent classifies a problem as "three-pipe":
1. All other processing is suspended
2. The complete file (policy, claim, correspondence, evidence) is loaded into active memory
3. No external interruptions for 50 minutes
4. The Mind Palace (R22) is activated for full cross-referencing
5. The conclusion is delivered with complete supporting chain

## The 50-Minute Window
The fifty minutes is not arbitrary. It represents the minimum cognitive unit for processing a complex, multi-variable insurance problem. Shorter windows produce shallow analysis.
