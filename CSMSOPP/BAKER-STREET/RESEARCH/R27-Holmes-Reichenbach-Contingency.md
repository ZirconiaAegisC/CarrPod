## Insurance Application: The Reichenbach Contingency

### Definition
A worst-case scenario that appears catastrophic to outside observers but for which the organization has prepared a survival protocol.

### Activation Triggers
- Reinsurance exhaustion event (single loss or aggregate exceeding treaty limits)
- Regulatory seizure (state insurance department takes control)
- Rating downgrade below investment grade
- Carrington Event exceeding G5 classification
- Lloyd's systemic risk scenario materialization

### Contingency Architecture

| Trigger | Immediate Action | Survival Protocol |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Reinsurance exhaustion | Freeze new business in affected LOB | Surplus drawdown, capital call |
| Regulatory seizure | Compliance mode, full transparency | Rehabilitation plan pre-filed |
| Rating downgrade | Client communication, collateral posting | Bridge facility pre-negotiated |
| G5+ CME | Full CAVALRY Protocol | Aegis-hardened backup operations center |
| Lloyd's scenario | Treaty activation in sequence | Pre-negotiated government backstop |

### The Holmes Principle
Holmes didn't survive Reichenbach by luck. He survived because he had already planned for Moriarty's final move. The Baker Street agent ensures clients have already planned for their Reichenbach.
