## Scenario Deconstruction

### Event Parameters (Lloyd's Futureset, March 2025)
- Extreme G5-class geomagnetic storm
- Duration: multi-day grid disruption
- Geographic scope: northern hemisphere mid-latitudes (US, Canada, Europe)
- Modeled global economic loss: $2.4 trillion over five years
- Expected direct insurance loss: approximately $17 billion

### Loss Breakdown by Line

| Line | Estimated Loss | Key Exposure |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| Power Generation | $4-6B | Transformer damage; extended outage |
| Business Interruption | $3-4B | Widespread, prolonged, interdependent |
| Marine/Aviation | $2-3B | Satellite navigation loss; communications |
| Property | $2-3B | Fire following electrical failure |
| Satellite | $1-2B | Constellation degradation |
| Other | $3-4B | Multiple miscellaneous lines |

### The Protection Gap
$2.4 trillion economic loss vs. $17 billion insured loss = protection gap of ~99.3%.
Most economic losses from Carrington-level events would be uninsured because:
1. Power grid assets have minimal insurance (public utilities self-insure)
2. Extended BI claims face sublimits and exclusions (off-premises power, civil authority)
3. Satellite insurance excludes acts of nature beyond specific perils
4. Supply chain disruption is largely uninsured

### The Mitigation Opportunity
If Aegis-C and related hardening products reduce modeled loss by 60-80% for protected assets:
- The protection gap shrinks
- Underwriting becomes viable for previously uninsurable exposures
- New premium pools emerge from currently excluded risks
