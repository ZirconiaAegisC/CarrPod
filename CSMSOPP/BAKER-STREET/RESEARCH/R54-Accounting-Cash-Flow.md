## The Cash Flow Trinity

### Operating Cash Flow
Cash generated from core insurance operations:
- Premium collections
- Claim payments
- Operating expense payments
- Reinsurance recoveries
- Investment income (interest/dividends, not realized gains)

Insurance-specific anomaly: premium is collected before claims are paid — creating positive operating cash flow even when underwriting is unprofitable. This "float" masks underwriting losses and delays recognition of reserve inadequacy.

### Investing Cash Flow
- Purchase/sale of invested assets
- Capital expenditures
- Acquisitions/dispositions

### Financing Cash Flow
- Debt issuance/repayment
- Stock issuance/repurchase
- Dividend payments

## Liquidity Analysis

### Key Metrics
- **Operating Cash Flow Ratio:** Operating CF / Current Liabilities. Ability to meet obligations from operations.
- **Quick Ratio:** (Cash + Short-term Investments + Receivables) / Current Liabilities. Ability to meet obligations without liquidating long-term assets.
- **Cash Conversion Cycle:** Time from premium collection to claim payment. Insurance is unique — negative cash conversion cycle (collect before you pay).

## The Baker Street Cash Flow Monitor

The agent continuously monitors:
1. **Cash flow from operations:** Red flag if declining while combined ratio is >100 (float masking losses)
2. **Investment realization patterns:** Red flag if realizing gains to offset underwriting losses (selling the furniture to pay the rent)
3. **Reinsurance recovery timing:** Red flag if reinsurance recoverables are growing faster than collections
