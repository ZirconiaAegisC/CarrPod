## Industry Architecture

### Primary Market
| Layer | Function | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Direct Writers | Sell directly to insureds | GEICO, Progressive, USAA |
| Independent Agency | Sell through independent agents | Most commercial carriers |
| Captive Agents | Sell exclusively for one carrier | State Farm, Allstate, Farmers |
| MGAs/MGUs | Underwriting authority delegated by carriers | Program business, specialty lines |
| Wholesale Brokers | Access excess/surplus lines markets | E&S placement for hard-to-place risks |
| Retail Brokers | Represent insureds to market | Aon, Marsh, Willis, local brokers |

### Reinsurance Market
| Structure | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Treaty Reinsurance | Automatic coverage for a portfolio of policies |
| Facultative Reinsurance | Individual risk placement |
| Quota Share | Proportional sharing of premiums and losses |
| Excess of Loss | Coverage above a retention level |
| Catastrophe Bonds / ILS | Capital market alternative to traditional reinsurance |

### Lloyd's of London
Not a company — a marketplace. Syndicates (managed by managing agents) underwrite risks through brokers who access the Lloyd's platform. Council of Lloyd's (the Franchise Board) oversees. Central Fund provides mutual security.

### Regulatory Architecture
- State insurance departments (U.S.): primary regulation
- NAIC: model laws, accreditation, financial regulation standards
- Solvency II (EU): risk-based capital framework
- Bermuda Monetary Authority: leading reinsurance domicile
- Lloyd's Franchise Board: self-regulation within UK regulatory framework
