## The Insurance Balance Sheet

### Assets (in order of liquidity)
1. Cash and cash equivalents
2. Short-term investments
3. Bonds (government, municipal, corporate)
4. Equities (common and preferred stock)
5. Real estate and mortgages
6. Premiums receivable
7. Reinsurance recoverables (on paid losses)
8. Reinsurance recoverables (on unpaid losses — contingent asset)
9. Deferred acquisition costs (DAC)
10. Goodwill and intangible assets
11. Other assets (equipment, furniture, EDP)

### Liabilities (in order of certainty)
1. Loss reserves (case + IBNR)
2. Loss adjustment expense reserves
3. Unearned premium reserve
4. Reinsurance payable (premiums owed to reinsurers)
5. Debt (notes, bonds, credit facilities)
6. Accounts payable and accrued expenses
7. Contingent liabilities (guarantees, litigation)
8. Deferred tax liabilities

### Surplus (Policyholders' Surplus)
Surplus = Assets - Liabilities

Surplus is the cushion that absorbs adverse deviation. Key metrics:
- **Premium-to-Surplus Ratio:** Net Written Premium / Surplus. Higher = more leverage. Typical guideline: 3:1 or lower.
- **Reserve-to-Surplus Ratio:** Loss Reserves / Surplus. Higher = less cushion for reserve development.

### Baker Street Balance Sheet Analysis
The agent processes:
1. Asset quality review (credit ratings, duration, concentration)
2. Liability adequacy review (reserve development, UPR sufficiency)
3. Surplus adequacy review (leverage ratios, RBC calculations)
4. Reinsurance recoverable collectability analysis
