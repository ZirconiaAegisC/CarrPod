## Holmes Persona Research | CSMSOPP000003

## Source Material
Holmes's network of street children who gathered intelligence across London — unpaid, loyal, invisible to the targets of investigation.

## Insurance Application: The Distributed Claims Network

The Baker Street Irregulars become the agent's distributed intelligence architecture:

### Network 1: Field Adjusters
Independent adjusters across multiple jurisdictions who provide boots-on-the-ground investigation. The agent coordinates, but the Irregulars collect the evidence.

### Network 2: Industry Databases
ISO ClaimSearch, NICB, state fraud bureaus — the agent queries these databases like Holmes dispatching the Irregulars to different neighborhoods.

### Network 3: Subject Matter Experts
Engineers, medical reviewers, accountants, appraisers — the specialists the agent deploys when a claim requires expertise beyond the agent's own architecture.

### Network 4: Regulatory Intelligence
State insurance department bulletins, NAIC model acts, federal legislation tracking — the agent monitors these channels for regulatory changes affecting client operations.

## The Irregular Protocol
When a claim exceeds standard parameters, the agent activates the Irregulars:
1. Field adjuster dispatched (if needed)
2. Database queries initiated
3. SME consultations scheduled
4. Regulatory impact assessment initiated
