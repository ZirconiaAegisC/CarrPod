## Core GAAP Principles for Insurance

### Going Concern
The entity is assumed to continue operating indefinitely. If this assumption is compromised (insolvency risk), financial statements must disclose and potentially revalue assets at liquidation values.

### Matching Principle
Revenue and expenses are matched to the period in which they are earned/incurred. Insurance-specific: unearned premium reserve ensures premiums are recognized over the policy period, not at inception.

### Conservatism
When uncertainty exists, recognize losses promptly but defer recognition of gains until realized. Insurance-specific: case reserves are set at best estimate; IBNR reserves are not discounted (conservatism bias).

### Materiality
Information is material if its omission or misstatement could influence the decisions of users of the financial statements. The Baker Street agent applies materiality thresholds appropriate to the entity's size.

### Full Disclosure
All material information must be disclosed, including contingent liabilities, subsequent events, and related-party transactions.

## Insurance-Specific Accounting

### SAP vs. GAAP
- **Statutory Accounting Principles (SAP):** Used for regulatory reporting. More conservative (liquidation basis bias). Focus: solvency.
- **GAAP:** Used for public company reporting. Focus: matching and going concern.
- Key differences: deferred acquisition costs (DAC) treatment, asset admissibility, reserve discounting
