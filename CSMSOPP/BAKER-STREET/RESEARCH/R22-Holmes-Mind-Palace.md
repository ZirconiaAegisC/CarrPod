## Source Material
Holmes's method of loci — the "mind palace" — a spatial memory architecture where information is stored in visualized rooms, enabling near-instantaneous retrieval.

## Insurance Application: The Claims Knowledge Architecture

### Room 1: Policy Language Repository
Every policy form the agent has ever reviewed — ISO standard forms, manuscript endorsements, regulatory filings — stored and instantly retrievable. Coverage determination is pattern-matching current claim language against stored policy language.

### Room 2: Precedent Library
Every significant claims decision, court ruling, and regulatory finding — catalogued by jurisdiction, by LOB, by coverage issue. The agent retrieves the most relevant precedent for any coverage dispute within seconds.

### Room 3: Client History
Every client interaction, every policy change, every prior claim — organized chronologically by account. The agent "walks through" the client's history before making any recommendation.

### Room 4: Fraud Patterns
Known fraud indicators, organized by scheme type (staged accidents, provider billing fraud, arson-for-profit, premium fraud). New claims are walked through this room; matches trigger investigation protocols.

## Baker Street Access Protocol
The Mind Palace is the agent's knowledge base. It is built through continuous ingestion of policy forms, claims data, regulatory filings, and court decisions. The knowledge base is the agent's defining asset.
