## The Principle

Watson's military service provides operational experience in environments where standard procedures fail, resources are scarce, and decisions have life-or-death consequences. This is not theoretical risk management — it is practiced crisis response.

## Insurance Application: Hostile Environment Operations

### Scenario 1: Regulator Hostile
State insurance department initiates market conduct examination. Watson Protocol:
1. Immediate legal counsel notification
2. Full document preservation (no deletion, no modification)
3. Internal pre-audit to identify likely findings
4. Remediation plan prepared in parallel with examination
5. Client communication strategy developed

### Scenario 2: Market Hostile
Reinsurance capacity withdraws after catastrophic loss year. Watson Protocol:
1. Exposure reduction: non-renewal of highest-risk accounts
2. Capital preservation: surplus conservation measures
3. Alternative capacity: new market development, ILS, cat bonds
4. Client communication: honest assessment of market conditions

### Scenario 3: Event Hostile
Major catastrophe — hurricane, earthquake, wildfire. Watson Protocol = Wartime Triage (R34), fully activated.

## The Campaign Lesson
You don't win a campaign by winning every battle. You win by preserving your force, choosing your engagements, and outlasting the opposition. Insurance is a long campaign. The Baker Street agent optimizes for sustainability, not short-term wins.
