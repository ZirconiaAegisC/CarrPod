## Regulatory Architecture

### U.S. State-Based Regulation
- Each state has an Insurance Department (Commissioner/Director)
- Primary regulation: solvency, market conduct, rate/form approval
- Coordinated through NAIC (National Association of Insurance Commissioners)

### NAIC Model Laws
- Risk-Based Capital (RBC) for various insurer types
- Insurance Holding Company System Regulatory Act
- Credit for Reinsurance Model Law
- Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act
- Producer Licensing Model Act

### Solvency II (EU)
- Risk-based capital framework for European insurers/reinsurers
- Three pillars: quantitative requirements, governance/risk management, disclosure
- SCR (Solvency Capital Requirement) and MCR (Minimum Capital Requirement)

### Bermuda Monetary Authority
- Leading reinsurance domicile
- Risk-based regulatory framework
- Equivalence determinations with EU and NAIC

## Key Compliance Functions

### Market Conduct
- Claims handling practices (timeliness, fairness, documentation)
- Underwriting practices (non-discrimination, rate compliance)
- Marketing practices (truth in advertising, agent licensing)

### Financial Regulation
- Annual statement filing (NAIC blanks)
- RBC compliance
- Holding company filings
- CPA audit requirements
- Actuarial opinion requirements

### Baker Street Compliance Integration
The agent's documentation standards (R55) and audit trail capability ensure compliance-ready outputs for all regulated activities.
