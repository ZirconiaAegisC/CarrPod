## Source Material
Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. Season 4 — the Framework, a digital alternate reality where Hydra won.

## Application to Insurance

The Framework is, functionally, a catastrophic alternate-reality scenario model — exactly what Lloyd's Futureset does with systemic risk. The Framework arc demonstrates:

1. **The value of counterfactual analysis:** What if the protection failed? What if the grid went down for 6 months instead of 6 hours? What if the reinsurance layer was breached?

2. **The danger of purely analytical decision-making:** Framework-Fitz became a monster because the ethical layer was removed. Insurance models without the Watson/Simmons ethical grounding produce technically optimal, morally catastrophic outcomes (e.g., systematic claim denial algorithms).

3. **Recovery is possible:** The team escaped the Framework. Systems can be rebuilt. Insurance markets recover after catastrophes. The Baker Street agent models worst-case scenarios not to despair, but to prepare.

## Protocol: Framework Mode

When activated, Framework Mode generates the worst plausible scenario based on current exposure data. This becomes the "tail risk" attachment point for reinsurance pricing and the basis for client risk-engineering recommendations.
