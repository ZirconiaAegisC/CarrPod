# R66 — The Protection Gap: Insured vs. Economic Loss Differential
## Domain Knowledge Research | CSMSOPP000003

---

## The Protection Gap Defined

The protection gap is the difference between total economic losses from a disaster and the portion covered by insurance. It represents the financial burden borne by individuals, businesses, and governments.

## Protection Gap by Peril

| Peril | Typical Protection Gap | Reason |
|-------|----------------------|--------|
| Hurricane (US) | 30-50% | High insurance penetration; NFIP for flood |
| Earthquake (US) | 80-90% | Very low earthquake insurance penetration |
| Flood (Global) | 70-80% | Most flood-prone areas lack coverage |
| Carrington Event | 95-99% | No established market; exclusions widespread |
| Cyber | 50-70% | Rapidly evolving; coverage gaps in silent cyber |

## The Carrington Protection Gap

The $2.4 trillion Lloyd's scenario projects ~$17 billion insured = 0.7% penetration. Reasons:

1. **Power grid:** Public utilities self-insure; no commercial market
2. **Extended BI:** Civil authority, off-premises power, contingent BI — heavily sublimited or excluded
3. **Satellite:** Solar events often excluded; space insurance market capacity limited
4. **Supply chain:** Not directly insured; dependent on contingent BI which requires physical damage trigger
5. **Government backstop:** No TRIA-like program exists for CME/EMP events

## Baker Street Gap Analysis

For each client, the agent quantifies the protection gap:
1. Total exposure to Carrington-level event
2. Current insurance coverage (policy limits less sublimits/exclusions)
3. Gap = (1) - (2)
4. Mitigation options: Aegis-C hardening, enhanced sublimits, parametric triggers, captive formation
