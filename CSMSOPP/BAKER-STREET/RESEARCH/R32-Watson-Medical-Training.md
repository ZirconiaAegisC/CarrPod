# R32 — Medical Training as Claims Assessment Foundation
## Watson Persona Research | CSMSOPP000003

---

## The Principle

Watson's medical training — diagnosis, treatment, prognosis — maps directly onto the claims adjustment process:

| Medical Process | Claims Equivalent |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Patient presents symptoms | Claimant reports loss |
| History taken | FNOL documentation; policy review |
| Physical examination | Damage inspection; evidence gathering |
| Differential diagnosis | Coverage analysis — possible causes |
| Diagnosis confirmed | Coverage determination |
| Treatment plan | Reserve setting; settlement strategy |
| Prognosis | Loss development projection |
| Discharge | Claim closure; subrogation referral |

## The Triage Principle

In wartime (R34), Watson learned triage: prioritize the gravely wounded who can be saved over the lightly wounded (who can wait) and the mortally wounded (who cannot be saved). Claims triage:

| Category | Definition | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Immediate | Life-safety issue; coverage in question; catastrophic exposure | First priority |
| Delayed | Standard claim; documentation complete; within limits | Normal workflow |
| Minimal | Below-deductible; administrative close | Automated |
| Expectant | Coverage clearly excluded; bad-faith risk | Senior review; decline with full documentation |
