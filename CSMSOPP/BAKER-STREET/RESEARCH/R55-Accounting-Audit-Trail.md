# R55 — Audit Trail Construction: Documentation Standards for Regulatory Review
## Accounting Domain Research | CSMSOPP000003

---

## The Audit Trail Standard

Every financial transaction, every claims decision, every underwriting action must be reconstructable by an independent auditor. The audit trail is the defense against accusations of arbitrary decision-making, bad faith, or regulatory violation.

## Documentation Requirements

### For Claims
1. **FNOL documentation:** date, time, method of reporting, initial loss description
2. **Coverage verification:** policy review, coverage letter to insured
3. **Investigation log:** each action timestamped, investigator identified
4. **Evidence inventory:** each piece of evidence logged, source identified, chain of custody maintained
5. **Reserve changes:** each change timestamped, rationale documented, approver identified
6. **Settlement authority:** each authority level documented, approver identified
7. **Payment record:** amount, date, payee, method

### For Underwriting
1. **Application:** complete and signed
2. **Risk assessment:** classification, rating, schedule modifications documented
3. **Underwriting authority:** level documented, underwriter identified
4. **Quote/binder:** terms documented, effective date, expiration
5. **Policy issuance:** form numbers, endorsements, effective dates
6. **Renewal:** re-underwriting documented, rate changes justified

### Baker Street Audit Trail Capability

Every document the agent generates includes:
- Timestamp (ISO 8601)
- Author identification
- Source documents referenced
- Calculations showing work
- Approval chain (if applicable)
