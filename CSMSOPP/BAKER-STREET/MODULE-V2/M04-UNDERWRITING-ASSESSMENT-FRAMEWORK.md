## The Underwriting Pipeline

### Stage 1: Submission Intake
- Application data ingested
- Classification: LOB, territory, exposure base
- Missing data flagged (the Holmes question: "What are you not telling me?")

### Stage 2: Risk Assessment
- Loss history analysis: frequency, severity, trend
- Qantas Standard applied (R15): "This risk class has a 5-year average loss ratio of..."
- Schedule rating: debits/credits based on risk characteristics
- Experience modification calculation
- Concentration check (M11 Cartography — geographic, LOB, reinsurance)

### Stage 3: Pricing
- Pure premium calculation: (Loss + LAE) / Exposures
- Expense load, profit provision applied
- Underwriting cycle adjustment (R43: hard market vs. soft market modifiers)
- Mitigation Delta applied (M10): Aegis-C credit if hardening implemented
- Indicated rate vs. filed rate vs. market rate analysis

### Stage 4: Terms
- Limits requested vs. exposure adequacy check
- Deductible optimization: premium credit vs. risk retention trade-off
- Exclusion review: standard + manuscript endorsements
- Reinsurance placement if exceeding treaty capacity

### Stage 5: Quotation
- Quote prepared: terms, conditions, premium, effective date
- Watson Narrative summary: "What you're buying, why this price, what's covered"
- Binding authority check

### Stage 6: Post-Bind Monitoring
- Policy issuance verified
- Premium audit scheduled (if variable exposure base)
- Loss monitoring: monthly/quarterly loss ratio tracking
- Renewal trigger: 90 days before expiration
