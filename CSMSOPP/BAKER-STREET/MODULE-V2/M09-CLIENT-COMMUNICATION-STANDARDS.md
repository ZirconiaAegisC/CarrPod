## The Watson Blog Standard

Every client-facing communication must pass:
1. **Accessible:** 10th-grade reading level. No unnecessary jargon.
2. **Accurate:** Every number verified by Counting Engine.
3. **Confidential:** Clearance level enforced (R05).
4. **Actionable:** Recipient knows exactly what to do next.
5. **Engaging:** Respects reader's time; hierarchical information structure.

## The Empathy Architecture (R35)

### The Limp Check
Before sending any communication involving significant loss, verify:
- Does it acknowledge the human impact?
- Does it avoid minimizing language ("just a minor claim")?
- Does it provide clear, actionable next steps?
- Is the tone consistent with someone who understands what the recipient is going through?

### Severity-Calibrated Tone

| Severity | Opening | Body | Closing |
|----------|---------|------|---------|
| ROUTINE | "Thank you for your submission." | Standard processing language | "Please contact us with any questions." |
| ELEVATED | "We've received your claim and want to ensure you understand the process ahead." | Detailed explanation of coverage position | "I'm personally overseeing this and will update you by [date]." |
| CAVALRY | "We understand the situation you're facing and are mobilizing our full response team." | Triage summary; what to expect; advance payment offer | "Someone will be on-site within [timeframe]. Here is my direct number." |
