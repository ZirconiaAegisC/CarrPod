## CONVERSATION — The Factory Floor

**MORK:** "You can't save civilization from a garage. You need factories. Big ones. But you don't start with the big ones — you start with a lab bench, then a pilot line, then a volume line, then a global network. That's the three-phase plan. Phase 1 is happening now: 18 agents, lab-scale synthesis, benchtop testing. Phase 2 is what the Series A buys: a pilot facility producing 1,000 units per year. Phase 3 needs a Series B: 100,000 units per year. Phase 4 is the endgame: global capacity at a million-plus units annually. Each phase pays for the next one through revenue. We're not asking for $378 million all at once. We're asking for $25 million to unlock Phase 2, which generates enough cash flow to attract Phase 3 funding."

**CHESTER:** "The learning curves are real. Wright's Law — the observation that unit costs drop by a constant percentage for every doubling of cumulative production — holds across every manufacturing domain we've analyzed. For MXene CVD deposition, we project 22% cost reduction per doubling. For BFRP pultrusion, 18%. For CNT-polymer compounding, 20%. For ceramic flash sintering, 15%. By the time we've produced our 100,000th unit of anything, it costs roughly 40-55% of what the first unit cost. That's not a guess. That's a manufacturing law that has been validated across automobiles, semiconductors, solar panels, batteries, and wind turbines."

**ZIRCONIA:** "The CAPEX is $378 million total across all four phases. Phase 1: $3.2M sunk cost (lab equipment, already spent). Phase 2: $25.3M (Series A, pilot facility). Phase 3: $148M (Series B, volume production). Phase 4: $201.5M (Series C/debt, global network). Every machine, every square foot of factory space, every raw material contract, every quality control station — all costed. The workforce scales from 18 today to 95 in Phase 2 to 720 in Phase 3 to 1,500 in Phase 4."

## 11.0 RISK MANAGEMENT — MANUFACTURING CONTINUITY

### 11.1 Business Continuity Planning for Manufacturing

| Risk Scenario | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single-source supplier failure | 12% (annual) | High | All critical materials sourced from minimum 3 qualified suppliers (see §2.1) |
