### 1.2 The DEIA Opportunity

Defense Economic Impact Assessment (DEIA) designation under DPA Title III can make CSM a mandatory source for federal procurement of GIC-protection materials. This designation:
- Requires federal agencies to source GIC-protection materials from qualified domestic producers
- Enables direct government loans and loan guarantees for manufacturing scale-up
- Provides antitrust exemption for industry coordination on GIC standards
- Authorizes voluntary agreements for production expansion

**Williams Translation:** "The government can declare that MXene and BFRP are critical defense materials, the same way they declare that semiconductors and rare earth magnets are. Once that happens, the government has to buy from qualified domestic producers — and we're the only one qualified. That's not a sales pipeline. That's a faucet."

### 4.2 ITAR/EAR Compliance Strategy

CSM products fall under the following export control regimes:
- **MXene EMI shielding films**: EAR99 (no export license required for most destinations) — however, MXene formulations above 80 dB SE may be controlled under ECCN 1A005 (protective equipment)
- **Protonic transceiver**: ITAR Category XI(c) (military electronics) — export license required
- **Phantom robot**: EAR99 for commercial inspection variant; ECCN 0A606 for military-hardened variant
- **CNT-polymer conductors**: EAR99 (commercial material)
- **BFRP composites**: EAR99 (commercial material)
- **Ceramic bearings**: EAR99 (commercial products)

CSM maintains an ITAR compliance program with:
- Empowered Official designation
- Technology Control Plan for ITAR-controlled products
- Export compliance training for all employees
- DDTC registration (in process)
