## 6.0 NAMED EXECUTIVE ENGAGEMENT METHODOLOGY

### 6.1 The "SiblingFrequency First" Approach

Before any dossier is delivered, the targeted executive has typically been exposed to CSM through one of three channels:

1. **SiblingFrequency Radio:** 15,000+ listeners include staff-level employees at every target company. The radio program builds ambient awareness — by the time a dossier arrives, the recipient's staff has already heard the name "Carrington Storm Motors" and the concept of "Dielectric Citadel."

2. **CSMReach Diplomatic Channel:** For companies with government contracts (SpaceX, Boeing, Lockheed-adjacent prospects), the CSMReach 200-country protocol has created State Department and DOD awareness that filters through procurement and compliance departments.

3. **Insurance Industry Channel:** For companies with large property/casualty programs (Walmart, Carnival, Duke Energy), the 41-insurer dossier distribution means that the prospect's insurance broker or risk manager may already have received a CSM actuarial briefing before the commercial dossier arrives.

**Williams Translation:** "We don't cold-call. By the time a CEO sees our dossier, four of their direct reports have already heard Mork explain transformer saturation on their commute. The dossier is the third impression, not the first."

### 6.2 Executive Naming Protocol

Each dossier names specific executives based on:
- Organizational chart analysis (public filings, LinkedIn, conference speaker lists)
- Procurement authority mapping (which role can sign contracts of given dollar values)
- Technical gatekeeper identification (which VP must approve engineering changes)
- Budget holder identification (which C-suite role controls relevant capital expenditure budget)

Dossiers are updated quarterly as executive rosters change — merger, acquisition, retirement, or re-organization events trigger an immediate dossier refresh.
