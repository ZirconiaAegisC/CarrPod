# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 04 — Phantom: European VLEO Platform and ESA Skimsat

### 4.1 Platform Heritage and Design Center

The Phantom platform is Redwire's European-origin very-low-Earth-orbit
spacecraft bus, designed and manufactured through the company's European
subsidiary operations. Phantom has been selected by the European Space Agency
for the Skimsat technology demonstration mission, positioning Redwire as a
strategic supplier to Europe's sovereign VLEO capability development program.

### 4.2 Skimsat Mission Architecture

ESA's Skimsat program seeks to validate the feasibility of sustained VLEO
operations using aerodynamic techniques that allow a satellite to "skim" the
upper reaches of the atmosphere. The concept relies on a spacecraft body shape
that generates aerodynamic lift — similar to an aircraft wing — to counteract
the portion of gravitational force that would otherwise require continuous
propulsive compensation. Skimsat operates at altitudes where the mean free path
of atmospheric particles is sufficiently short that continuum aerodynamic
effects become meaningful, yet sufficiently long that the spacecraft does not
experience destructive heating.

The Phantom platform for Skimsat incorporates a flattened, elongated body
profile optimized through extensive computational fluid dynamics modeling and
wind-tunnel testing using representative rarefied gas flows. The aerodynamic
surfaces are coated with atomic oxygen-resistant materials to prevent
degradation from the highly reactive species present at VLEO altitudes.

### 4.3 Propulsion System

Phantom employs a xenon-fueled gridded ion thruster for orbit maintenance and
maneuvering. Unlike SabreSat's air-breathing approach, Phantom carries its
propellant supply, trading the indefinite-endurance potential of air-breathing
for the higher technology readiness level of conventional electric propulsion.
This design choice reflects the Skimsat program's focus on aerodynamic
validation rather than propulsion technology demonstration.

### 4.4 Aero-Thermal Design

VLEO operations subject the spacecraft to elevated heat fluxes from ram
heating, atomic oxygen impingement, and increased solar radiation in the
thermosphere. The Phantom thermal control system employs passive radiators with
high-emissivity coatings, multi-layer insulation blankets rated for the VLEO
thermal environment, and active heater circuits for components requiring
tight temperature regulation. The aero-thermal design was validated through
particle-in-cell simulations and ground-based atomic oxygen exposure testing.

### 4.5 European Industrial Participation

The Phantom platform and Skimsat program provide substantial industrial return
to ESA member states. Redwire's European operations draw on supply chains
spanning Belgium, Luxembourg, the Netherlands, Italy, and the United Kingdom.
Key subsystems including star trackers, reaction wheels, and power conditioning
electronics are sourced from European suppliers, reinforcing the platform's
qualification for ESA and European Union space programs.

### 4.6 Mission Applications Beyond Skimsat

While Skimsat serves as the initial mission, the Phantom platform is designed
for follow-on operational VLEO missions including:

- European Earth observation missions requiring sub-meter resolution imagery
  at competitive cost points relative to traditional LEO platforms.
- Telecommunications demonstrations leveraging the reduced latency and path
  loss characteristics of VLEO.
- Technology demonstration missions for future European operational VLEO
  constellations.

### 4.7 Transatlantic Synergies

Redwire's dual VLEO platform strategy creates opportunities for cross-platform
technology transfer. Aerodynamic modeling techniques validated on Phantom can
inform SabreSat upgrades, while propulsion advances from the DARPA Otter
program may be adapted for future Phantom variants. The company's unified
avionics architecture across both platforms simplifies integration and allows
customers to mix platform selection with common payload interfaces.

### 4.8 Regulatory and Licensing

VLEO operations below the International Space Station altitude (approximately
400 kilometers) generally fall outside the most congested regions of LEO,
simplifying conjunction assessment and collision avoidance requirements.
However, Phantom's Skimsat mission must comply with French space operations
law for launches from the Guiana Space Centre and with ESA's space debris
mitigation requirements, which mandate post-mission disposal within 25 years.
VLEO's inherent rapid orbital decay simplifies compliance with these
requirements.
