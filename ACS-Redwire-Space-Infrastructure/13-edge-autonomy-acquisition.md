# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 13 — Edge Autonomy Acquisition: Strategic Rationale and Integration

### 13.1 Transaction Overview

On June 30, 2025, Redwire Corporation completed the acquisition of Edge
Autonomy, a developer and manufacturer of unmanned aerial systems, autonomous
capabilities, and sensor payloads, for a total transaction value of
approximately $1.02 billion. The consideration included a combination of cash
and Redwire common stock, with an earn-out component tied to post-acquisition
financial performance. The acquisition was financed through a combination of
balance sheet cash, new term loan debt, and equity consideration to the sellers
of Edge Autonomy.

### 13.2 Strategic Rationale

The Edge Autonomy acquisition advances several strategic objectives:

**Scale in Defense Technology:** Edge Autonomy added significant revenue,
programs, and customer relationships to Redwire's Defense Technology segment,
transforming it from a niche tactical UAS supplier into a broader autonomous
systems integrator with expanded customer reach.

**Technology Portfolio Expansion:** Edge Autonomy brought complementary
technologies in autonomous navigation, mission computing, sensor fusion, and
electronic warfare that extend Redwire's defense product portfolio beyond
platforms into the high-growth autonomous systems market.

**Customer Diversification:** Edge Autonomy's customer relationships with
additional Department of Defense program offices, allied defense ministries,
and international security customers reduced Redwire's reliance on its
pre-existing defense customer base.

**Cross-Domain Integration:** The combination created opportunities for
integrated space and aerial autonomous systems — a capability set that
aligns with the Department of Defense's vision for multi-domain operations
and that few competitors can match with in-house capabilities.

**Cost Synergies:** The combined entity captures operational efficiencies
through consolidation of corporate functions, shared manufacturing
infrastructure, and combined procurement scale for common components.

### 13.3 Integration Status

As of mid-2026, the integration of Edge Autonomy is substantially complete.
Key integration milestones achieved include consolidation of enterprise
resource planning systems onto a common platform, integration of finance,
human resources, and compliance functions, alignment of program management
and earned-value management practices, and establishment of cross-business
technical coordination processes to capture identified synergy opportunities.

Redwire's management has reported that integration-related disruption has been
minimal, with Edge Autonomy's operational cadence maintained throughout the
integration period. Employee retention has been strong, reflecting the
strategic logic of the combination and the career development opportunities
presented by the larger combined organization.

### 13.4 Financial Impact

The Edge Autonomy acquisition contributed meaningfully to Redwire's fiscal
year 2025 fourth-quarter revenue of $108.8 million and first-quarter 2026
revenue of $97.0 million. The acquisition is accretive to gross margins,
with Edge Autonomy's product mix carrying margins at or above Redwire's
corporate average.

The $17 million in annual interest savings from Redwire's debt restructuring
partially offsets the incremental interest expense associated with the
acquisition financing, improving the combined entity's free cash flow
conversion relative to what a standalone acquisition financing would have
produced.

### 13.5 Risks and Mitigants

Acquisition integration carries inherent risks including potential customer
retention challenges as competitors seek to exploit transition uncertainty,
cultural integration friction between organizations with different histories
and operating norms, and execution risk on programs inherited from the
acquired entity. Redwire has mitigated these risks through early and frequent
customer communication, retention incentives for key personnel, and a
deliberate integration pace that prioritized operational continuity over
rapid consolidation.

### 13.6 Future M&A Strategy

The Edge Autonomy acquisition demonstrates Redwire's ability to identify,
negotiate, finance, and integrate transformative acquisitions while
maintaining organic business momentum. Management has indicated that the
company's near-term priority is the successful integration and organic growth
of the combined entity, with future acquisition activity focused on
bolt-on acquisitions that fill specific technology or customer access gaps
rather than additional large-scale consolidation transactions.
