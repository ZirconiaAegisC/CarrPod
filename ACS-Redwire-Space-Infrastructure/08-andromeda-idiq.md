# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 08 — Andromeda IDIQ: Large Space Infrastructure Contract

### 8.1 Vehicle Overview

The Andromeda indefinite-delivery, indefinite-quantity (IDIQ) contract
represents one of Redwire's most significant strategic assets, providing a
$1.8 billion ceiling-value contracting vehicle through which U.S. government
customers can procure advanced spacecraft, payloads, and mission integration
services without conducting separate competitive acquisitions for each
individual mission. The contract was awarded through a competitive source
selection process and positions Redwire as a prime contractor for a broad
range of national security space missions.

### 8.2 Contract Structure

The Andromeda IDIQ is structured with a base period of performance and multiple
option periods extending the ordering window. Individual task orders are issued
against the base contract on a cost-plus, fixed-price, or hybrid basis
depending on the maturity of the technology and the risk profile of the
specific mission. The IDIQ framework provides the government with flexibility
to award task orders spanning the full range of Redwire's capabilities, from
individual component procurement through complete spacecraft delivery and
on-orbit commissioning.

The $1.8 billion ceiling value represents the maximum aggregate value of all
task orders that may be issued over the contract's life, not a guaranteed
funding commitment. Actual revenue recognized under the contract depends on
specific task order awards, which are subject to government budget cycles and
programmatic priorities.

### 8.3 Scope of Services

Task orders under the Andromeda IDIQ may cover:

- Complete spacecraft design, manufacture, integration, and test across any
  of Redwire's five platform families.
- Payload integration for government-furnished and Redwire-developed sensor
  payloads.
- Launch vehicle integration and mission assurance services.
- Ground segment development including mission operations centers and
  telemetry, tracking, and command infrastructure.
- On-orbit commissioning, checkout, and operational support for mission
  durations extending to multiple years.
- Technology development and risk reduction for future mission capabilities.

### 8.4 Strategic Significance

The Andromeda IDIQ is strategically significant for several reasons beyond the
direct revenue it enables. It establishes Redwire as a prime contractor rather
than a subcontractor for U.S. national security space missions, a distinction
that carries both operational responsibility and favorable positioning for
future competitive procurements. It provides a streamlined path for government
customers to access Redwire's vertically integrated manufacturing capability,
which can reduce program schedules by eliminating the need for separate
procurements of spacecraft bus and payload. It also creates a pipeline of
task order opportunities that provides visibility into future revenue beyond
the explicit contracted backlog.

### 8.5 Program Execution

Redwire has invested in the facilities, personnel, and processes necessary to
execute multiple concurrent task orders under the Andromeda vehicle. This
includes expansion of clean room capacity, implementation of enhanced program
management and earned-value management systems, and hiring of cleared personnel
with experience in national security space programs. The company's existing
facility footprint across 28 locations provides the physical capacity to
absorb substantial Andromeda task order volume without requiring major new
capital investment.

### 8.6 Risk Management

The Andromeda IDIQ carries execution risks common to large government space
contracts, including potential for cost growth on cost-type task orders,
schedule pressure from concurrent mission demands on shared resources, and
revenue concentration risk if Andromeda task orders become a dominant portion
of Space segment revenue. Redwire mitigates these risks through its
diversified customer base spanning NASA, ESA, commercial operators, and
international defense customers, and through its commercial-derivative
platform strategy that avoids the cost structure of traditional
government-unique satellite programs.

### 8.7 Relationship to Platform Portfolio

Andromeda task orders may leverage any of Redwire's five platform families
depending on mission requirements. This flexibility allows government
customers to select the optimal platform for each mission rather than being
constrained to a single bus design, while Redwire captures economies of scope
from sharing subsystems and manufacturing processes across platforms.
