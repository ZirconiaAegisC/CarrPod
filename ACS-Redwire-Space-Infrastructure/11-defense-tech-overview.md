# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 11 — Defense Technology Segment Overview

### 11.1 Segment Formation

Redwire's Defense Technology segment was established to separate the company's
growing portfolio of defense, autonomous systems, and unmanned aerial vehicle
products from its traditional space infrastructure business. The segment
reported $125.6 million in revenue for fiscal year 2025, representing 37.5
percent of total company revenue and establishing defense as a co-equal pillar
alongside the Space segment.

### 11.2 Product Portfolio

The Defense Technology segment comprises three primary product families:

**Unmanned Aerial Systems:** The Stalker Block 30 and Penguin UAS platforms
provide tactical and operational-level intelligence, surveillance, and
reconnaissance to U.S. and allied military forces. These systems have been
deployed extensively in operational environments and have accumulated hundreds
of thousands of flight hours.

**Autonomous Systems:** Redwire develops autonomous mission computers,
navigation systems, and sensor payloads that enable unmanned platforms —
both aerial and ground — to operate with reduced human supervision in
contested and denied environments.

**Sensor Suites:** The segment includes electro-optical and infrared sensor
payloads, signals intelligence collection systems, and electronic warfare
payloads integrated onto Redwire's own UAS platforms and sold as standalone
products for integration onto third-party platforms.

### 11.3 Customer Base

The Defense Technology segment serves a customer base concentrated in the U.S.
Department of Defense, with the U.S. Army, U.S. Special Operations Command, and
the U.S. Marine Corps representing the largest individual customers. Allied
defense ministries, particularly in NATO member states and Indo-Pacific
partner nations, constitute a growing share of segment revenue as international
demand for tactical UAS and autonomous systems expands.

International sales are conducted through the Foreign Military Sales process
managed by the Defense Security Cooperation Agency, which provides the
regulatory framework for export of defense articles to approved partner
nations.

### 11.4 Competitive Environment

The Defense Technology segment competes in a market characterized by a mix of
large defense primes — including AeroVironment, Textron Systems, and Anduril
Industries — and specialized UAS manufacturers. Redwire's competitive
differentiation rests on the combination of proven operational systems with
active production lines, a track record of rapid capability insertion through
software and hardware upgrades, and the ability to offer integrated solutions
spanning the platform, sensor payload, and ground control segment.

### 11.5 Segment Synergies with Space Business

The Defense Technology segment creates strategic synergies with Redwire's
Space segment that are uncommon in the defense industrial base. The company
can offer customers integrated space-and-aerial intelligence solutions in
which data collected by UAS platforms is relayed through Redwire-built
satellites, or in which satellite tasking is cued by UAS-derived intelligence.
This cross-domain integration capability aligns with the Department of
Defense's Joint All-Domain Command and Control (JADC2) concept and provides
a differentiated value proposition in competitive procurements.

### 11.6 Program Maturity and Growth

Both the Stalker and Penguin UAS platforms are mature programs with
established production lines, trained operator cadres within multiple military
services, and active sustainment contracts providing recurring revenue.
Growth opportunities include expansion of international sales to allied
nations, development of next-generation platform variants with enhanced
endurance and payload capacity, and integration of autonomous capabilities
that reduce operator workload and enable coordinated multi-vehicle operations.

### 11.7 Edge Autonomy Contribution

The June 2025 acquisition of Edge Autonomy represents the single largest
expansion of the Defense Technology segment, contributing platforms,
technologies, personnel, and customer relationships that significantly
broaden Redwire's defense portfolio. A detailed analysis of the Edge Autonomy
transaction is provided in Document 13 of this dossier.
