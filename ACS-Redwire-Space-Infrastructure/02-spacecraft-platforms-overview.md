# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 02 — Spacecraft Platforms: Architecture Overview

### 2.1 Platform Portfolio Strategy

Redwire has developed and deployed five distinct spacecraft bus platforms
spanning orbital regimes from very low Earth orbit through geostationary and
beyond-geostationary operations. Each platform has been engineered for specific
mission profiles, customer requirements, and operational environments. The
platform family covers a continuous spectrum of size, power, delta-V
capability, and mission endurance.

The platform strategy leverages common avionics subsystems, standardized
interfaces, and heritage flight software across the product line, enabling
Redwire to compress development timelines, reduce non-recurring engineering
costs, and offer customers a progression path from technology demonstration to
operational constellation deployment.

### 2.2 Orbital Regime Coverage

The five platforms provide coverage across the full range of Earth orbits:

**Very Low Earth Orbit (100–450 km):** SabreSat and Phantom platforms are
optimized for VLEO operations, where atmospheric drag imposes stringent
requirements on propulsion, attitude control, and aerodynamic design. VLEO
offers advantages in resolution for Earth observation, reduced latency for
communications, and inherent debris mitigation due to rapid natural decay.

**Low Earth Orbit (450–2,000 km):** Hammerhead and Thresher platforms serve
the LEO regime, which hosts the majority of commercial and government satellite
constellations. These platforms balance payload capacity, power generation, and
mission life against cost constraints.

**Medium and Geostationary Earth Orbit (2,000–36,000 km):** The Mako platform
extends Redwire's reach into MEO, GEO, and beyond-GEO operations, including
rendezvous and proximity operations, satellite servicing, docking, and
refueling missions that require significant propulsion capability and radiation
tolerance.

### 2.3 Heritage and Flight Legacy

The Hammerhead platform draws on over 50 years of PROBA (Project for On-Board
Autonomy) heritage traced through Redwire's European operations. ESA's PROBA
series of small satellites has demonstrated autonomous operations, formation
flying, and precision pointing technologies that directly inform Redwire's
current platform designs.

The Mako platform builds on Redwire's experience with rendezvous, proximity
operations, and docking demonstrated on prior government programs and
commercial satellite servicing missions.

### 2.4 Common Subsystem Architecture

All five platforms share a common avionics core comprising Redwire's
internally-developed command and data handling system, star tracker, reaction
wheel assemblies, and power management and distribution unit. This commonality
enables cross-platform technology insertion, simplifies supply chain
management, and allows Redwire to offer customers incremental upgrades without
full requalification.

### 2.5 DARPA Otter Program Context

The DARPA Otter program, awarded to Redwire, seeks to demonstrate sustained
VLEO operations through a novel air-breathing electric propulsion system. The
SabreSat platform serves as the primary testbed for Otter, validating
technologies that enable persistent VLEO missions despite significant
atmospheric drag. Successful demonstration of the Otter propulsion concept
would unlock new mission classes including ultra-high-resolution imaging,
electronic intelligence at reduced standoff ranges, and responsive space
reconnaissance.

### 2.6 ESA Skimsat Program

The European Space Agency's Skimsat program, for which the Phantom platform
was selected, aims to validate aerodynamic VLEO flight techniques that allow a
satellite to "skim" the upper atmosphere using lift-generating body shapes
and efficient electric propulsion. Skimsat represents Europe's commitment to
VLEO as a strategic operational domain, and Redwire's selection validates the
Phantom platform's aero-thermal design maturity.

### 2.7 Manufacturing and Integration

Redwire performs spacecraft integration across multiple facilities including
its European center of excellence for small satellites and its U.S.
manufacturing sites. The company's vertical integration strategy — combining
internally-produced solar arrays, structures, avionics, and payloads — reduces
dependence on third-party suppliers and improves margin capture on fixed-price
programs.

### 2.8 Contracting Vehicles

The $1.8 billion Andromeda indefinite-delivery, indefinite-quantity (IDIQ)
contract provides a streamlined acquisition pathway for U.S. government
customers to procure advanced spacecraft, payloads, and mission integration
services from Redwire. The Andromeda vehicle covers the full range of Redwire's
platform and payload portfolio, eliminating the need for customers to conduct
separate competitive procurements for each mission.
