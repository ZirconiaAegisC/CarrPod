# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 09 — PIL-BOX: Microgravity Payloads and ISS Operations

### 9.1 Platform Description

PIL-BOX (Pharmaceutical In-space Laboratory — Bio-crystal Optimization
Experiment) is Redwire's microgravity research payload platform designed
for operation aboard the International Space Station and on future commercial
space stations. The platform enables pharmaceutical companies, biotechnology
firms, and academic researchers to conduct protein crystallization, cell
culture, and materials science experiments in the sustained microgravity
environment of low Earth orbit.

### 9.2 Microgravity Value Proposition

Protein crystallization in microgravity produces crystals of higher quality —
larger, more ordered, and with fewer defects — than those grown in terrestrial
laboratories. These superior crystals enable higher-resolution X-ray
diffraction studies, which in turn support more accurate determination of
protein structures. Structure-based drug design, the dominant paradigm in
modern pharmaceutical development, depends on high-quality protein structures
to identify binding pockets and optimize drug candidates.

The commercial value of space-based crystallization lies in its potential to
accelerate drug development timelines, reduce the number of synthesis-and-test
cycles required to identify lead compounds, and enable the design of drugs
targeting proteins that have proven refractory to terrestrial crystallization
efforts.

### 9.3 Payload Architecture

The PIL-BOX payload is a self-contained experiment module that integrates into
ISS EXPRESS rack facilities, which provide standard power, data, and thermal
interfaces. Each PIL-BOX unit houses multiple experiment chambers capable of
running independent crystallization or cell culture protocols with automated
temperature control, reagent mixing, and optical monitoring. Experiment
parameters are programmed from the ground and executed autonomously, with
telemetry downlinked for experiment monitoring and intervention when necessary.

Upon completion of the on-orbit experiment phase, the PIL-BOX unit or its
sample return capsules are returned to Earth via SpaceX Dragon or Northrop
Grumman Cygnus cargo vehicles for laboratory analysis.

### 9.4 Industrial Partnerships

Redwire has established partnerships with multiple pharmaceutical companies
and research institutions for PIL-BOX experiments. These partnerships follow
a service model in which Redwire provides the flight hardware, ISS integration
services, and mission operations, while the pharmaceutical partner provides
the biological materials and performs the terrestrial analysis. Redwire
retains ownership of the PIL-BOX hardware and generates revenue through
experiment service fees.

### 9.5 Beyond ISS: Commercial Space Station Applications

Redwire is actively positioning PIL-BOX for operation aboard commercial space
stations currently under development by Axiom Space, Voyager Space, and Blue
Origin. These next-generation platforms are expected to offer expanded
experiment capacity, more frequent access, and more flexible operational
cadences compared to the ISS, which operates under government crew time
allocations and constrained up-mass and down-mass availability.

The transition from ISS to commercial stations represents a strategic growth
opportunity for Redwire's microgravity business, as pharmaceutical customers
currently constrained by limited ISS access will gain the ability to fly
experiments on schedules driven by research needs rather than cargo manifest
availability.

### 9.6 Adjacent Microgravity Capabilities

Beyond PIL-BOX, Redwire's microgravity portfolio includes biological research
facilities for plant growth, tissue engineering, and advanced materials
processing experiments. These facilities leverage the company's expertise in
space-qualified thermal control, fluid handling, and autonomous operations
to provide turnkey experiment capabilities for customers without in-house
spaceflight engineering resources.

### 9.7 Revenue Model and Market Outlook

The microgravity research market remains nascent but is expected to grow as
commercial space stations reduce access costs and as pharmaceutical companies
accumulate evidence of the return on investment from space-based research.
Redwire's early-mover position through PIL-BOX, combined with the company's
vertically integrated capability to manufacture both the payload hardware and
the spacecraft that carry it, positions Redwire to capture a significant share
of this emerging market.
