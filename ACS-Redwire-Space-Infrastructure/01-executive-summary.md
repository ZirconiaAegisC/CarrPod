# ACS-Redwire-Space-Infrastructure Dossier
## Document 01 — Executive Summary & Corporate Profile

### 1.1 Company Overview

Redwire Corporation (NYSE: RDW) is a space infrastructure and defense
technology company headquartered in Jacksonville, Florida. Formed through a
consolidation strategy beginning in 2020, Redwire has grown through organic
development and targeted acquisitions to become a vertically integrated provider
of spacecraft platforms, space-qualified components, deployable structures,
sensors, payloads, and autonomous defense systems. The company operates across
28 locations in the United States and Europe, employing approximately 1,400
personnel across engineering, manufacturing, program management, and corporate
functions.

### 1.2 Corporate Leadership

Chief Executive Officer Peter Cannito leads the executive team with a
background spanning aerospace, defense, and private-equity-backed growth
companies. Cannito has driven the firm's acquisition strategy, culminating in
the transformative purchase of Edge Autonomy in June 2025. Chief Financial
Officer Chris Edmunds oversees the company's capital structure, treasury
operations, and investor relations. Under Edmunds' stewardship, Redwire
completed a debt restructuring in 2025 that generates approximately $17 million
in annual interest savings compared to the prior capital structure.

### 1.3 Business Segments

Redwire operates two reportable segments: Space and Defense Technology.

The Space segment generated $209.8 million in revenue during fiscal year 2025.
This segment encompasses spacecraft platforms spanning very low Earth orbit
through geostationary and cislunar regimes, deployable solar arrays including
the Roll-Out Solar Array (ROSA) flown on NASA's DART planetary defense mission
and the Artemis program, sensors and optical payloads, microgravity research
payloads, and large-scale space infrastructure. The Space segment benefits from
deep relationships with NASA, the European Space Agency, the Department of
Defense, and commercial satellite operators.

The Defense Technology segment generated $125.6 million in revenue during
fiscal year 2025. This segment consolidates the company's unmanned aerial
systems portfolio — notably the Stalker Block 30 and Penguin platforms —
alongside autonomous systems, mission computers, sensor suites, and the
contribution from the Edge Autonomy acquisition. The segment serves the U.S.
Department of Defense, allied defense ministries, and international security
customers.

### 1.4 Key Financial Metrics

Redwire reported fiscal year 2025 revenue of $335.4 million, representing
year-over-year growth of 10.3 percent. Fourth-quarter 2025 revenue reached
$108.8 million, a 56.4 percent increase over the prior-year quarter. The
company's positive trajectory continued into the first quarter of 2026, which
posted $97.0 million in revenue — a 57.9 percent year-over-year increase.

Gross margin expanded from 14.7 percent in the prior year to 26.6 percent in
the first quarter of 2026, reflecting improved program execution, favorable
mix shift toward higher-margin contracts, and absorption of fixed overhead
across a growing revenue base.

As of the most recent reporting period, Redwire holds a record contracted
backlog of $498.1 million, representing 21 percent sequential growth. The
company achieved a book-to-bill ratio of 1.92, indicating that new orders
nearly doubled recognized revenue during the period.

Management issued fiscal year 2026 revenue guidance of $450 million to
$500 million, reflecting confidence in the organic pipeline and contributions
from Edge Autonomy. Liquidity stood at $175.2 million at the end of the most
recent quarter, the highest level in the company's history.
