  immediately, unlike most other facility types.

  days.

  replacement-timescale regional outages (see CSMEval11's Lloyd's of London
  scenario data: transformer replacement lead times of up to 12 months).

  (defibrillation strategy, diagnostic vibrometry) referenced in the
  medical-device sector's own briefing (CSMEval26) — meaning the CSM
  research base here serves both facilities engineering and clinical
  practice simultaneously.

RISKS / CONCERNS IF UNADDRESSED
• A backup-power system sized for "hours to a few days" has no meaningful
  answer to a months-scale grid event beyond eventual fuel exhaustion —
  and unlike a data center (CSMEval16), the consequence here is measured in
  patient outcomes, not service-level agreements.
• This is direct, individual, real-time safety-of-life stakes, placing this
  sector at the same urgency tier as nuclear (CSMEval06) and grid
  (CSMEval02/10).
