
# IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP
## ACS-PFIZER-PHARMACEUTICAL-COLD-CHAIN
### 30-Month Protection Program

**CLASSIFICATION: COMMERCIAL-IN-CONFIDENCE**

| Phase | Duration | Key Activities |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| Phase 0 — Assessment | Months 1-3 | 8-site GIC survey. Power quality monitoring installed. Sterile product facility prioritization. |
| Phase 1 — Manufacturing NBC | Months 4-18 | Kalamazoo (Month 4-6), Puurs (Month 7-9), McPherson (Month 10-12), Rocky Mount (Month 13-15), Andover (Month 16-18). Installation during planned maintenance shutdowns. |
| Phase 2 — Cold Storage Protection | Months 12-24 | 15 major cold storage warehouses. Ammonia refrigeration power conditioning. 7-day fuel autonomy. Temperature monitoring integration. |
| Phase 3 — Distribution Hubs | Months 18-30 | 25 regional pharmaceutical distribution centers. Generator control hardening. GIC-hardened WMS servers. |
| Phase 4 — Last-Mile Cold Chain | Months 24-30 | GIC-hardened temperature loggers deployed to refrigerated fleet. Protonic miniature transmitters on long-haul reefer trucks. |
| Phase 5 — Protonic Network | Months 18-24 | 8-node protonic network linking major manufacturing sites (Kalamazoo, Puurs, McPherson, Rocky Mount, Andover, Groton, Singapore, China facility). |

**FDA Coordination:** All modifications to manufacturing facility power systems require change control under Current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) regulations. ACS regulatory specialists coordinate with Pfizer Quality Assurance to ensure FDA-compliant validation of power system modifications.

**Key Milestones:**
- Month 3: 8-site assessment complete. Regulatory pathway validated.
- Month 18: All 8 major manufacturing facilities protected.
- Month 24: Cold storage, protonic network, and last-mile protection operational.
- Month 30: Full program delivery.
