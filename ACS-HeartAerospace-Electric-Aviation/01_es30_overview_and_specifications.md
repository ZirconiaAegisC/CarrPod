# ES-30 Aircraft Overview & Specifications

## Program Origins

Heart Aerospace was founded circa 2019 by CEO Anders Forslund. The company
originally set out to design a 19-seat all-electric regional aircraft designated
the ES-19. Market feedback and airline consultations revealed that a larger
aircraft with hybrid capability would address a broader set of regional routes,
leading to the redesign and announcement of the ES-30 in September 2022.

The ES-30 is a clean-sheet, 30-seat hybrid-electric regional aircraft. It
targets the short-haul and thin-haul regional market, where routes of 200 to
400 kilometers dominate current turboprop operations. The aircraft is designed
for entry into service with EASA CS-23 commuter category certification, with
type certification targeted for 2031.

## Design Philosophy

The ES-30 is a purpose-built regional aircraft rather than a retrofit of an
existing airframe. Every aspect of the design was reconsidered from the ground
up to optimize for hybrid-electric propulsion. The high-wing configuration,
large-diameter propellers, and distributed electric propulsion architecture
are all native to the clean-sheet design.

The fuselage cross-section was sized specifically for 30 passengers in a
comfortable regional cabin with adequate headroom, shoulder width, and
aisle clearance. Overhead bins provide carry-on storage, and a rear galley
module supports beverage and snack service on flights up to approximately
90 minutes in the hybrid configuration.

## Mission Profile

The ES-30 operates across three primary mission profiles:

- **All-electric short missions:** Departure to arrival entirely on battery
  power. Zero in-flight emissions. Range limited to 200 kilometers with full
  passenger load. Suitable for island-hopping, short domestic legs, and
  routes connecting nearby city pairs.

- **Hybrid standard missions:** Battery power for takeoff and climb, with
  turbogenerators engaging during cruise to sustain battery charge. Range
  extends to 400 kilometers with 25 passengers. Covers the vast majority of
  regional routes currently served by ATR 42, Dash 8-100, and similar types.

- **Hybrid extended missions:** Full 30-passenger load with turbogenerator
  support. Range approximately 300 kilometers. Intended for routes where
  passenger demand is highest and all-electric range is insufficient.

## Competitive Positioning

The ES-30 occupies a market segment between all-electric eVTOL and small
commuter aircraft (9–19 seats) and conventional turboprops in the 50-to-78-seat
category. This 30-seat position has historically been underserved since the
retirement of types such as the Saab 340 and Embraer EMB 120 Brasilia.

Competitors in the electric and hybrid-electric regional space include:

- **Ampaire** — hybrid-electric conversions of existing airframes
- **Eviation Alice** — all-electric 9-passenger commuter
- **Aura Aero** — hybrid-electric 19-seat ERA
- **ZeroAvia** — hydrogen fuel-cell propulsion for retrofit

Heart Aerospace differentiates through its clean-sheet design, larger passenger
count, and independent hybrid propulsion architecture that does not rely on
conversion of legacy airframes or third-party powertrain suppliers.

## Cabin Experience

The ES-30 cabin was designed with passenger comfort as a central requirement.
The 2–1 seating configuration (or 2–2 in certain layouts) provides generous
seat width comparable to mainline narrow-body aircraft. Noise levels are
significantly reduced compared to conventional turboprops due to the electric
propulsion system, particularly during all-electric operation when the
turbogenerators are idle. Large windows, LED ambient lighting, and a
low-vibration environment contribute to a passenger experience that Heart
Aerospace describes as "game-changing" for regional travel.
