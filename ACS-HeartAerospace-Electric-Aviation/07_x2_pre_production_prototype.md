# X2 Pre-Production Prototype

## Purpose and Scope

The X2 is Heart Aerospace's pre-production prototype, distinct from the
X1 technology demonstrator. While the X1 validates the basic aerodynamic
configuration and electric-only propulsion, the X2 incorporates the full
hybrid-electric propulsion system including the two rear-fuselage
turbogenerators. The X2 is the aircraft that will conduct the type
certification flight-test program and serve as the conformity article
for production certification.

The X2 represents a significant step beyond the X1 in completeness and
fidelity to the production ES-30:

- Full hybrid propulsion integration
- Production-representative avionics and flight deck
- Complete cabin interior to certification standards
- Certified materials and processes throughout
- Production-tooled components where available

## Hybrid Propulsion Integration

The X2's defining feature is the integration of the turbogenerators. These
two turbine-driven generators, located in the aft fuselage, represent the
most technically challenging subsystem of the ES-30. The X2 test program
will validate:

### Turbogenerator Start and Synchronization

The sequence for starting the turbogenerators in flight must be reliable
under all conditions from sea level to 20,000 feet. The X2 will test cold-
soaked starts after prolonged battery-only cruise, hot starts after
rapid descent, and cross-start procedures where one turbogenerator is
used to motor the other.

### Power Sharing Between Batteries and Turbogenerators

The hybrid power-management system must seamlessly blend power from
batteries and turbogenerators while maintaining stable DC bus voltage
and frequency. Transient conditions—such as sudden motor power demand
changes during gust response or go-around—must not cause voltage sag
or generator overload.

### Turbogenerator Failure Modes

The X2 test program includes planned turbogenerator shutdowns during
flight to validate:
- Automatic load transfer to the remaining generator
- Battery-only endurance following generator loss
- Cabin pressurization and avionics power stability during generator
  transients
- Cockpit alerting and crew procedures for generator failures

### Thermal Management of Combined Systems

The turbogenerators add a significant heat load to the aircraft. The X2
thermal management system must handle battery heat rejection, motor
cooling, turbogenerator exhaust heat, and cabin environmental control
simultaneously under worst-case conditions (hot-day takeoff followed by
maximum cruise power at low altitude).

## Flight Test Program

The X2 will conduct the formal type-certification flight-test program
under EASA and FAA observation. The test program is expected to encompass:

- **300 to 500 flight hours** for initial certification
- **Function and reliability testing** of 150 hours minimum per EASA CS-23
  requirements
- **High-intensity radiated fields** susceptibility testing
- **Lightning protection** verification through both analysis and test
- **Icing certification** including flight into known icing conditions
  for the turbogenerator inlets and propeller de-ice systems

## Avionics and Flight Deck

The X2 flight deck is a production-representative glass cockpit featuring:

- Three large-format primary flight displays and multi-function displays
- Integrated engine indication and crew alerting system tailored for
  hybrid-electric powertrain monitoring
- Fly-by-wire flight control system with envelope protection
- Satellite-based augmentation system for precision approaches to
  regional airports without ILS
- Integrated battery state-of-charge and state-of-health monitoring
  with predictive range calculation

The avionics suite is designed to be single-pilot-operable under CS-23,
with workload studies and human-factors evaluations to be conducted
during the X2 test program.

## Manufacturing at Gothenburg

While the X1 was largely hand-built, the X2 is constructed using
production-representative tooling and processes at the Gothenburg pilot
plant. This allows Heart Aerospace to:

- Validate production work instructions and assembly sequences
- Train production staff on the actual aircraft
- Identify tooling improvements before committing to rate production
- Generate the production conformity documentation required for the
  production certificate application

The X2 is the aircraft against which the production certificate will
be issued—every subsequent production ES-30 must conform to the X2
type design as approved by EASA.
