# Certification Pathway — EASA & FAA

## Certification Category: CS-23 Commuter

The ES-30 is being certified under EASA Certification Specification
CS-23, specifically the commuter-category provisions. CS-23 applies
to normal, utility, aerobatic, and commuter category airplanes with
a passenger-seating configuration of 19 or fewer and a maximum
certificated takeoff weight of 8,618 kilograms (19,000 pounds) or
less. However, EASA CS-23 Amendment 5 introduced the commuter category
for multi-engine aircraft with up to 19 passengers, and the ES-30's
30-seat capacity indicates that Heart Aerospace is pursuing an
extension of the commuter-category provisions or a tailored CS-23
certification basis with special conditions for the larger passenger
count.

The alternative would be certification under CS-25 (large airplanes),
which would impose significantly more stringent requirements in terms
of structural redundancy, system safety analysis, flight-test scope,
and continuing airworthiness. Heart Aerospace's deliberate choice of
a 30-seat design—just above the traditional 19-seat threshold—suggests
that the company is working with EASA to define a certification basis
that captures the safety level of a transport-category aircraft while
leveraging the more proportionate regulatory framework of CS-23.

## EASA as Primary Certifying Authority

Heart Aerospace, founded in Sweden, has EASA as its primary certifying
authority. The EASA type-certification process proceeds through four major
phases:

### Phase 1: Certification Basis

EASA and Heart Aerospace agree on applicable CS-23 specifications, special
conditions, equivalent safety findings, and exemptions. For electric
aircraft, special conditions are expected for: high-voltage electrical
system safety, battery thermal runaway containment, lightning protection
of structures with embedded electrical systems, turbogenerator certification
as a non-propulsive power source, fly-by-wire controls in a commuter-
category aircraft, and single-pilot operation with electric propulsion.

### Phase 2: Means of Compliance

Heart Aerospace proposes, and EASA accepts, specific compliance methods
(flight test, ground test, analysis, simulation, inspection).

### Phase 3: Compliance Demonstration

Heart Aerospace executes the agreed test and analysis program—the most
resource-intensive phase, corresponding to the X2 flight test program
beginning approximately 2027–2028.

### Phase 4: Type Certificate Issuance

Upon satisfactory compliance demonstration, EASA issues the type certificate.
Target date: 2031.

## FAA Validation and Bilateral Agreement

FAA validation is essential given the U.S.-centric order book. Under the
U.S.-EU bilateral aviation safety agreement, the FAA will participate in
EASA certification as an observer, conduct a validation review of
certification data, and issue a validating type certificate for U.S.
registry. The $4.1 million FAST grant and LA headquarters relocation
facilitate this process.

## Timeline Revisions

Initial certification targets: 2028, revised to 2028–2029, then to 2031.
Extensions reflect special conditions development for novel propulsion,
unprecedented battery certification scale, expanded test program scope,
and industry-wide electric aviation timeline adjustments.

## Post-Certification Deliverables

Before first delivery, Heart Aerospace must establish: a production
organization approval (POA), instructions for continued airworthiness
including maintenance and repair manuals, a maintenance review board
report defining initial airline maintenance programs, and a flight
operations evaluation board report covering pilot type-rating
requirements and training syllabi.
