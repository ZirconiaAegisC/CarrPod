# Georgia Plant & Capacity Expansion

**Document Identifier:** ACS-RVN-TECH-002
**Date:** July 2026

---

## 1. Project Overview

Rivian's Georgia manufacturing facility represents the company's second domestic production campus and the largest single capital project in its history. Located on an approximately 1,800-acre site spanning Morgan and Walton Counties east of Atlanta, Georgia, the facility is designed to add 300,000 units of annual electric vehicle production capacity. This represents a 50% increase over the initial 200,000-unit capacity plan, reflecting Rivian's upward revision based on market demand projections and the strategic importance of the R2 and future R3 platforms.

## 2. Site Characteristics and Selection Rationale

### 2.1 Location and Logistics

The Georgia site was selected in December 2021 following a multi-state competitive evaluation process. Key locational advantages include:
- Proximity to the I-20 transportation corridor, providing direct east-west highway access linking Atlanta to major Southeastern and Texan markets
- Access to the Port of Savannah for international inbound material logistics and potential export operations
- Strategic positioning within a 500-mile radius covering substantial portions of the Sun Belt EV adoption markets
- Proximity to the Georgia electric vehicle supply chain ecosystem, including multiple battery manufacturing investments by Hyundai, SK On, and other firms

### 2.2 Land and Infrastructure

The total site encompasses approximately 1,800 acres, providing generous space for:
- Main vehicle assembly campus (Phase 1: estimated 10–15 million square feet of enclosed manufacturing space)
- On-site supplier park for sequenced tier-1 operations
- Test track and vehicle validation facilities
- Storm water management and natural buffer zones
- Future expansion capacity beyond Phase 1

The State of Georgia and local development authorities provided a competitive incentive package including tax abatements, infrastructure improvements, and workforce development commitments. Specific incentive terms remain partially disclosed but are estimated to represent a material portion of the total project economics.

## 3. Production Capacity Plan

### 3.1 Capacity Evolution

| Phase | Annual Capacity | Status | Target Timing |
|-------|----------------|--------|---------------|
| Initial Plan (2021) | 200,000 units | Superseded | Original filing |
| Revised Plan (2023+) | 300,000 units | Current active scope | Phase 1 completion |
| Future Expansion | 400,000+ units | Conceptual | Post-Phase 1 |

The 50% capacity increase from 200,000 to 300,000 units was driven by:
- Stronger-than-anticipated R2 pre-order and reservation pipeline
- Market sizing analyses indicating larger addressable demand for the mid-size EV crossover segment
- Manufacturing efficiency gains enabling higher throughput within the same Phase 1 building envelope
- Strategic decision to concentrate R2 and future R3 production in Georgia while Normal focuses on R1 and EDV

### 3.2 Product Allocation

The initial Georgia product allocation centers on the R2 platform, with tooling configured for the R2 mid-size SUV as the launch vehicle. The facility is designed with platform flexibility to accommodate R2 derivatives, potential R3 compact crossover production, and future vehicle programs. This contrasts with the Normal facility's multi-platform role (R1T, R1S, EDV, R2), positioning Georgia as the high-volume, single-platform hub.

## 4. DOE ATVM Loan Facility

### 4.1 Structure and Terms

The U.S. Department of Energy committed a $4.5 billion loan under the Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing (ATVM) Loan Program to support construction of the Georgia facility. Key financial terms:

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Total Committed | $4,500 million |
| Principal | $4,006 million |
| Capitalized Interest | $494 million |
| First Draw Timing | Early 2027 (projected) |
| Program | ATVM (Advanced Technology Vehicles Manufacturing) |
| Use of Proceeds | Facility construction, equipment procurement, production tooling |
| Repayment Structure | Long-term amortization post-construction draw period |

### 4.2 Significance

The ATVM loan represents one of the largest single-project commitments in the program's history, which previously supported Tesla, Ford, Nissan, and other domestic EV manufacturing projects. The commitment provides non-dilutive capital that reduces Rivian's reliance on equity or convertible debt financing during the peak construction period. The capitalized interest component allows Rivian to defer cash interest payments until the facility enters production, reducing near-term cash burn. The DOE's due diligence process, including environmental review and financial viability assessment, provides an implicit validation of the project's technical and economic feasibility.

## 6. Capital Investment Profile

Total project capital expenditure for Phase 1 is estimated at approximately $7–8 billion, inclusive of:
- Land acquisition and site development
- Building construction (structural, envelope, utilities, environmental systems)
- Production equipment (stamping presses, body shop robots, paint systems, general assembly conveyance)
- Battery pack and drive unit production tooling
- Supplier park infrastructure
- IT/OT systems, plant networking, and manufacturing execution systems
- Workforce training centers and administrative facilities

The capital spend profile is concentrated in FY2026–FY2028, aligning with peak construction and equipment procurement, and represents the primary driver of the company's elevated capital expenditure guidance.

## 7. Workforce and Economic Impact

The Georgia facility is projected to create approximately 7,500 direct manufacturing jobs at steady-state production, with additional indirect employment through the on-site supplier park, logistics operations, and regional service providers. The State of Georgia and the Technical College System of Georgia have committed to customized workforce training programs, including the Georgia Quick Start program, to develop the skilled manufacturing workforce required for automotive assembly, robotics maintenance, quality control, and production management.

## 8. Risk Assessment

### 8.1 Schedule Risk
- Large-scale greenfield automotive construction projects historically experience schedule variances of 6–18 months. The late-2028 production target is aggressive relative to the spring 2026 vertical construction start date.
- Weather impacts, supply chain disruptions for structural steel and production equipment, and labor availability in the Southeastern construction market represent principal schedule risks.

### 8.2 Capital Risk
- A multi-billion-dollar construction program during a period of elevated interest rates and construction cost inflation exposes the project to cost overruns.
- The DOE loan mitigates but does not eliminate financing risk; additional capital raises may be necessary if costs overrun or the ramp timeline extends.

### 8.3 Operational Risk
- First-time operation of a greenfield plant at the 300,000-unit scale introduces significant learning-curve risk.
- Simultaneous ramp of the Normal R2 line and Georgia construction/commissioning stretches management bandwidth and engineering resources across two major concurrent initiatives.

### 8.4 Market Risk
- A 300,000-unit annual capacity commitment carries demand risk: the R2 must achieve sustained order rates sufficient to absorb this volume in a competitive mid-size EV segment.
- Any softening in EV adoption rates, changes in federal or state incentive programs, or intensification of competitive dynamics would directly threaten capacity utilization.
