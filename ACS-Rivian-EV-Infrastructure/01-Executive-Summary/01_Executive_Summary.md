## 1. Purpose and Scope

The ACS-Rivian-EV-Infrastructure dossier presents a comprehensive operational, financial, technical, and strategic assessment of Rivian Automotive, Inc. as of mid-2026. The dossier serves as an integrated reference for evaluating programmatic deployment, infrastructure investment, manufacturing scale-up viability, and autonomous-mobility partnership economics. The analysis spans five interrelated domains: Normal IL manufacturing and R2 production ramp, Georgia greenfield capacity expansion, R2 platform and market strategy, autonomous technology and the Uber robotaxi fleet, and service, retail, and charging infrastructure.

## 2. Key Findings

### 2.1 Financial Trajectory

Rivian reported Q1 2026 revenue of $1.38 billion, an 11% increase over the prior-year quarter, with consolidated gross profit of $119 million. FY2025 full-year revenue reached $5.4 billion on 42,284 units produced and delivered. Gross margin improved to 9% in Q1 2026, representing measurable progress toward the company's stated goal of positive vehicle gross profit. FY2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance is $(1.80)–$(2.10) billion, with capital expenditures projected at $1.95–2.05 billion. Cash and equivalents stood at $4.83 billion as of the most recent reporting period.

### 2.2 Production and Delivery Ramp

Q2 2026 production reached 12,613 units with deliveries of 12,194 units, exceeding the 9,000–11,000 guidance range. The company raised its full-year 2026 delivery outlook to 65,000–70,000 units. The R2 platform achieved first saleable production and employee deliveries in April 2026, with external customer deliveries imminent. The Normal, Illinois facility serves as the current production hub for R1T, R1S, EDV commercial vans, and the R2.

### 2.3 Georgia Plant and Capacity Expansion

Rivian's Georgia facility represents a material capacity expansion with planned annual output of 300,000 units, an increase of 50% from the initial 200,000-unit plan. The DOE has committed a $4.5 billion loan ($4,006 million principal plus $494 million capitalized interest) with first draw expected in early 2027. Vertical construction commenced in spring 2026, targeting vehicle production by late 2028.

### 2.4 Autonomous Technology and Uber Partnership

The Uber partnership totals $1.25 billion in committed investment: an initial $300 million deployed in Q2 2026 and an additional $250 million scheduled for later in 2026. The agreement covers 10,000 R2 robotaxis with a 40,000-unit option through 2030, targeting a fully autonomous Rivian fleet operating on the Uber platform. Rivian's RAP1/ACM3 autonomy hardware platform, LDM (Language Driver Model), and LiDAR integration form the technical foundation. Universal Hands-Free driving assistance now covers 3.5 million miles across the United States and Canada, with utilization doubling after release.

### 2.5 Service, Retail, and Charging Infrastructure

Rivian operates 36 retail spaces, 97 service locations, and nearly 700 mobile service vehicles. The Volkswagen Group joint venture addresses next-generation electrical architecture and software co-development.

## 4. Strategic Assessment

Rivian's trajectory through mid-2026 demonstrates accelerating production cadence, improving unit economics, and credible multi-platform expansion. The convergence of R2 market entry, Georgia capacity construction, DOE loan capitalization, and the Uber autonomous partnership constitutes a structurally transformative period. Key risk factors include the capital intensity of dual-plant operations, execution risk on the Georgia timeline, competitive dynamics in the mid-size EV crossover segment, and the technical and regulatory pathway to SAE Level 4 autonomous deployment. The dossier that follows examines each dimension in detail to support informed decision-making.
