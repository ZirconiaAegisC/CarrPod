## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This memorandum catalogs the existing statutory authorities available to the President and federal agencies for geomagnetic disturbance (GMD) preparedness, mitigation, response, and recovery. It identifies more than 50 specific statutory provisions across 18 titles of the United States Code, organized by functional area, with section-level citations and brief applicability analysis. This compendium serves as the definitive legal reference for all federal GMD activities and demonstrates that existing law provides robust — though not always well-coordinated — authority for comprehensive GMD preparedness and response.

Key findings: (a) the Defense Production Act, Stafford Act, and Federal Power Act provide the core legal framework for grid hardening and response; (b) numerous additional statutes — from the Communications Act to the Public Health Service Act to the Social Security Act — provide independent authority for specific aspects of GMD response; (c) significant gaps exist in (i) statutory authority for mandatory private-sector grid hardening, (ii) statutory authority for insurance backstop mechanisms, and (iii) statutory authority for international grid coordination; (d) these gaps should be addressed through the legislative proposals in the COVENANT-Congress bill package.

## PART I — CORE NATIONAL EMERGENCY AND DISASTER RESPONSE AUTHORITIES

### 1.1 National Emergencies Act (NEA)
**Citation:** 50 U.S.C. §§1601-1641

| Section | Citation | Description | GMD Applicability |
|---------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Declaration authority | 50 U.S.C. §1621 | President may declare national emergency; proclamation must be transmitted to Congress and published in Federal Register | Primary authority for G5 GMD emergency declaration (COVENANT-EO-001, COVENANT-EO-002) |
| Congressional termination | 50 U.S.C. §1622 | Congress may terminate national emergency by concurrent resolution | Provides congressional oversight mechanism; preauthorized declaration subject to potential termination |
| Presidential reports | 50 U.S.C. §1641 | President must report to Congress on emergency expenditures every six months | Applies to all expenditures under declared GMD emergency |
| Record-keeping | 50 U.S.C. §1641(c) | President must maintain records of emergency expenditures | Fiscal accountability requirement |

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** The NEA is the on/off switch for emergency powers. President flips it on with a declaration; Congress can flip it off with a vote. The NEA doesn't itself give the President specific powers — it activates powers in other laws that only work during declared emergencies.

## PART XII — GAPS AND RECOMMENDED LEGISLATIVE ACTION

### 12.1 Identified Gaps

| Gap | Description | Recommended Fix |
|-----|-------------|----------------|
| DPA reauthorization | Title I and Title III provisions expired 2025 | COVENANT-BILL-002: DPA Reauthorization and GIC Protection Amendment |
| Mandatory private-sector hardening | No existing statute requires investor-owned utilities to harden against GMD | COVENANT-BILL-004: Critical Infrastructure Electromagnetic Hardening Act |
| Insurance backstop | No federal insurance mechanism for GMD-related losses | COVENANT-BILL-005: Federal Geomagnetic Insurance Backstop Act |
| Strategic Transformer Reserve | No statutory authority for dedicated transformer reserve | COVENANT-BILL-003: Strategic Transformer Reserve Authorization Act |
| International coordination treaty | No existing treaty specifically addresses GMD coordination | COVENANT-UN/CONVENTION-001: International Convention on Geomagnetic Disaster Coordination |
| Stafford Act amendment | Gubernatorial request incompatible with GMD characteristics | COVENANT-BILL-005 companion provisions: Stafford Act GMD amendments |

### 12.2 Implementation Priority

1. **Immediate (via Executive Order — completed or in process):** EO-001 through EO-006
2. **Legislative Priority 1:** DPA reauthorization (COVENANT-BILL-002) — existing EOs dependent on DPA authority
3. **Legislative Priority 2:** Stafford Act amendments, Strategic Transformer Reserve, mandatory hardening (COVENANT-BILL-003, BILL-004, BILL-005)
4. **Legislative Priority 3:** Insurance backstop, veteran mobilization (COVENANT-BILL-005, BILL-006)
5. **International:** Convention on Geomagnetic Disaster Coordination (COVENANT-UN/CONVENTION-001)
