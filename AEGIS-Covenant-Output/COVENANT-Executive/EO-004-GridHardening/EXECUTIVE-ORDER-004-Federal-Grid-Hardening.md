## THE PLAIN-LANGUAGE PITCH

The federal government owns 307,000 buildings across all fifty states. It leases another 57,000. Every military base, every VA hospital, every federal courthouse, every national park visitor center, every Social Security Administration office, every GSA-leased building — every single one of them plugs into the same electrical grid that a Carrington-class coronal mass ejection would destroy.

If the grid goes down, the federal government goes down with it. Not metaphorically. Literally. The computers that process Social Security payments. The HVAC systems that keep data centers from overheating. The pumps that deliver water to military bases. The refrigeration units that store vaccines at VA hospitals. The air traffic control systems that keep planes from colliding. All of it stops.

This Executive Order says: the federal government will protect its own house first. Every federal building that matters — every military installation, every hospital, every emergency operations center, every data center — must be hardened against geomagnetic disturbance within 48 months. The government cannot credibly ask utilities and private industry to harden their infrastructure if it hasn't hardened its own.

The standard is called the "Aegis Home Standard." It has four components: (1) GIC-blocking capacitors or equivalent dielectric isolation at every incoming electrical service, (2) Faraday-cage shielding of all electronic control systems using MXene-based films, (3) independent backup power generation with 30 days of on-site fuel, and (4) redundant communications that work when the cell towers don't.

This is not optional. This is not aspirational. This is a direct order from the President to every federal department and agency, backed by budget authority, compliance deadlines, and inspector general enforcement.

The estimated cost: $47.5 billion over 48 months. Annualized: about $11.9 billion per year. To put that in perspective, the federal government spends approximately $8 billion annually on building maintenance and utilities already. This is a one-time capital investment that will last 30-40 years. And it protects buildings that have a combined replacement value exceeding $1.2 trillion.

## OFFICIAL TEXT — EXECUTIVE ORDER [NUMBER TO BE ASSIGNED]

### FEDERAL GRID HARDENING MANDATE: GIC PROTECTION OF FEDERAL ELECTRICAL INFRASTRUCTURE TO THE AEGIS HOME STANDARD

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, including the Federal Property and Administrative Services Act of 1949 (40 U.S.C. 101 et seq.), the National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. 1601 et seq.), the Homeland Security Act of 2002 (6 U.S.C. 101 et seq.), and section 301 of title 3, United States Code, it is hereby ordered as follows:

**SECTION 1. POLICY.**

It is the policy of the United States that the federal government shall lead by example in protecting critical electrical infrastructure against geomagnetic disturbances. The federal government owns or leases more than 360,000 buildings, many of which are essential to national security, public health, and the continuity of government operations. The failure to protect these facilities against G5-class geomagnetic disturbances represents an unacceptable vulnerability. The federal government shall ensure that all mission-critical federal facilities meet the Aegis Home Standard for GIC protection within 48 months of this Order.

**SECTION 2. FINDINGS.**

(a) The Federal Property and Administrative Services Act (40 U.S.C. 581) authorizes the Administrator of General Services to prescribe policies and methods governing the acquisition, management, and disposal of federal property, including the authority to mandate protective measures for federal buildings.

(b) Executive Order 13636 (Improving Critical Infrastructure Cybersecurity) established the principle that the federal government must lead by example in protecting its own critical infrastructure, a principle equally applicable to electromagnetic threats as to cyber threats.

(c) The Department of Defense is statutorily required under 10 U.S.C. 2222 to ensure the energy resilience of military installations, including protection against electromagnetic threats.

(d) The electromagnetic pulse (EMP) and geomagnetic disturbance (GMD) vulnerabilities of military installations were extensively documented in the 2008 and 2018 reports of the Congressional EMP Commission, which recommended hardening of critical defense infrastructure. The recommendations of that Commission apply with equal force to civilian federal infrastructure.

(e) A comprehensive GIC susceptibility assessment of the federal building inventory has not been conducted. This Order mandates such an assessment as the predicate for a targeted, cost-effective hardening program.

(f) Cost-benefit analysis conducted by CSMEval/COST-BENEFIT-003 demonstrates that hardening federal facilities to the Aegis Home Standard costs approximately 4% of the replacement value of the facilities protected — a ratio consistent with seismic retrofitting programs for federal buildings in California and the Pacific Northwest, which have been mandated by Executive Order 12941 and subsequent authorities.

**SECTION 3. THE AEGIS HOME STANDARD.**

(a) **Definition.** The "Aegis Home Standard" is hereby established as the minimum GIC protection standard for all federal facilities subject to this Order. A facility shall be deemed to meet the Aegis Home Standard when all four of the following components have been installed and certified:

    (i) **GIC Isolation (Component 1).** Every incoming electrical service connection to the facility — including primary utility feeds, backup generator connections, and any auxiliary power inputs — shall be equipped with GIC-blocking capacitors or equivalent dielectric isolation devices that:

        (A) Block quasi-DC geomagnetically induced currents up to 200 amperes per phase while passing 60 Hz alternating current with total harmonic distortion not exceeding 3%, consistent with CSMFAB/TRANSFORMER-012 through TRANSFORMER-018;

        (B) Are rated for continuous operation at the facility's maximum demand load plus 25% surge capacity;

        (C) Include monitoring and alarm systems that detect and log GIC events and automatically notify facility managers and the Department of Energy's GIC Monitoring Center;

        (D) Are housed in NEMA 4X-rated enclosures suitable for the installation environment.

    (ii) **Electromagnetic Shielding (Component 2).** All electronic control systems, data processing equipment, and communications hardware essential to facility operations — including but not limited to building automation systems, fire alarm control panels, security systems, HVAC controllers, and uninterruptible power supply management systems — shall be housed in enclosures or rooms that provide electromagnetic interference shielding of not less than 40 dB across the frequency range of 10 kHz to 10 GHz, achieved through:

        (A) MXene-based conductive film application to enclosure interiors per CSMQuantum/SHIELD-012 through SHIELD-018; or

        (B) Equivalent conductive coating, metallic enclosure, or Faraday-cage construction that achieves the same shielding effectiveness as validated by IEEE Std 299.1-2013 testing methodology;

        (C) All shielded enclosures shall include proper grounding per MIL-STD-188-125-1 High-Altitude Electromagnetic Pulse (HEMP) protection standards, as adapted for GIC frequencies.

    (iii) **Backup Power Independence (Component 3).** Each facility shall be equipped with independent backup power generation capable of sustaining mission-critical operations for not less than 30 continuous days without external fuel resupply. This requirement may be met through:

        (A) Diesel or natural gas generator sets with on-site fuel storage sufficient for 720 hours of continuous operation at 80% rated load;

        (B) Solar photovoltaic arrays with battery energy storage sized for the facility's critical load profile, with generator backup for extended periods of low insolation;

        (C) Small modular reactor or microreactor systems, where site suitability and regulatory approvals permit;

        (D) Combination of the above technologies, provided the combined system meets the 30-day autonomous operation requirement.

    (iv) **Redundant Communications (Component 4).** Each facility shall maintain at least two independent, diverse-path communications systems capable of operating when terrestrial telecommunications infrastructure is disrupted, including:

        (A) Satellite communications terminals (LEO, MEO, or GEO) hardened against GIC effects;

        (B) High-frequency (HF) radio systems with NVIS (Near Vertical Incidence Skywave) antenna configurations for regional communications;

        (C) Where feasible, dedicated fiber-optic connections to geographically diverse network access points, with fiber terminals protected by GIC isolation.

(b) **Customization for Facility Type.** The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Administrator of General Services, shall develop within 180 days tailored Aegis Home Standard compliance guidelines for the following facility categories:

    (i) Military installations (including barracks, command centers, logistics hubs, ammunition storage, and airfields);
    (ii) Department of Veterans Affairs medical centers and clinics;
    (iii) Federal courthouses and judicial facilities;
    (iv) Federal law enforcement facilities (FBI, DEA, ATF, U.S. Marshals Service);
    (v) Federal data centers and communications hubs;
    (vi) National park visitor centers, ranger stations, and critical infrastructure;
    (vii) GSA-leased properties where the federal government occupies more than 50% of rentable square footage;
    (viii) FEMA regional offices and emergency operations centers;
    (ix) Department of Energy national laboratories and nuclear facilities;
    (x) Federal Bureau of Prisons facilities;
    (xi) Department of Homeland Security operations centers, including CISA and TSA facilities.

(c) The tailored guidelines shall account for the specific critical loads, security requirements, and operational profiles of each facility type, and may adjust the thresholds of Components 1-4 as appropriate, provided that the adjusted thresholds do not materially diminish the GIC protection of the facility.

Three: have a generator with 30 days of fuel. Four: have two ways to communicate when cell towers and internet are dead. These requirements are customized for different kinds of federal buildings — a VA hospital has different power needs than a national park visitor center, but both need protection.

**SECTION 4. GIC SUSCEPTIBILITY ASSESSMENT.**

(a) **Mandate.** Every federal department and agency shall, within 90 days of this Order, complete a GIC Susceptibility Assessment for each facility under its jurisdiction that falls within any of the facility categories enumerated in Section 3(b) or that the agency head determines is mission-critical.

(b) **Assessment Content.** Each GIC Susceptibility Assessment shall include:

    (i) A physical description of the facility including location, construction type, year built, and electrical system configuration;

    (ii) Identification of the facility's incoming electrical service characteristics, including voltage, phase configuration, transformer type and rating, and grounding system design;

    (iii) Proximity to major transmission lines (345 kV and above) and distance to the nearest substation, which correlates with GIC exposure risk;

    (iv) Identification of the facility's geological substrate electrical conductivity, which affects local geomagnetic field distortion and GIC intensity — facilities on igneous rock (high resistivity) are at greater risk of GIC concentration than facilities on sedimentary rock (low resistivity);

    (v) An inventory of all electronic control systems, data processing equipment, and communications hardware essential to facility operations;

    (vi) The facility's critical load profile — the minimum electrical load required to sustain mission-essential functions — expressed in kilowatts and categorized by priority tier;

    (vii) An assessment of existing electromagnetic protection measures, if any, including surge protection devices, shielded enclosures, and backup power systems;

    (viii) A gap analysis identifying the specific additions or modifications necessary to bring the facility into compliance with the Aegis Home Standard;

    (ix) A cost estimate for achieving Aegis Home Standard compliance, including engineering, procurement, construction, testing, and commissioning costs;

    (x) A risk-prioritized implementation schedule.

(c) **Submission.** GIC Susceptibility Assessments shall be submitted to the Administrator of General Services, the Secretary of Energy, and the Secretary of Homeland Security. The Administrator of GSA shall compile the assessments into a Federal GIC Vulnerability Register, which shall be made available to the President, the Homeland Security Council, and the relevant congressional committees.

(d) **Coordination.** The Administrator of GSA shall coordinate the Assessment process across agencies to ensure consistency of methodology, cost estimation, and risk assessment. The Administrator may contract with qualified engineering firms, national laboratories, or university research centers to assist agencies in completing their Assessments.

> **WILLIAMS HEURISTIC — SECTION 4 IN PLAIN LANGUAGE:** Every federal agency has 90 days to figure out which of its buildings are vulnerable to solar storms and what it would take to protect them. This is the "what do we have and what's wrong with it" step. It's a survey, not a construction project. Once we know what needs protecting, we can target the money intelligently.

**SECTION 5. IMPLEMENTATION — HARDENING TIMELINE AND REQUIREMENTS.**

(a) **48-Month Compliance Deadline.** All federal facilities identified in GIC Susceptibility Assessments as mission-critical and requiring hardening shall achieve Aegis Home Standard compliance within 48 months of this Order.

(b) **Phased Implementation.** Compliance shall proceed in three phases:

    **Phase 1 (Months 1-12): Urgent Protection.** Within 12 months, all facilities designated as "Tier 1 — National Security Essential" shall achieve compliance. This tier includes:
        (i) Department of Defense combatant command headquarters and primary operations centers;
        (ii) NORAD/NORTHCOM facilities;
        (iii) White House, EEOB, and presidential continuity facilities;
        (iv) FEMA National Response Coordination Center and regional operations centers;
        (v) Department of Energy national laboratory nuclear facilities;
        (vi) FAA air traffic control centers and critical radar installations;
        (vii) Nuclear Regulatory Commission operations centers;
        (viii) Department of Homeland Security National Operations Center.

    **Phase 2 (Months 13-36): Essential Infrastructure.** Within 36 months, all Tier 2 — Essential Infrastructure facilities shall achieve compliance. This tier includes:
        (i) All Department of Veterans Affairs medical centers and outpatient clinics with surgical or emergency capabilities;
        (ii) Federal courthouses in the 94 federal judicial districts;
        (iii) FBI field offices and DEA division offices;
        (iv) Federal Bureau of Prisons medium and high-security facilities;
        (v) GSA-leased facilities housing federal law enforcement or emergency management functions;
        (vi) Department of Energy power marketing administration control centers (Bonneville, Western, Southwestern, Southeastern);
        (vii) FEMA regional offices and prepositioning resource centers established by COVENANT-EO-002.

    **Phase 3 (Months 37-48): Remaining Federal Infrastructure.** All remaining facilities shall achieve compliance within 48 months, including:
        (i) National park visitor centers in parks with more than 1 million annual visitors;
        (ii) Social Security Administration regional and program service centers;
        (iii) Department of Agriculture food safety inspection facilities;
        (iv) All other GSA-leased properties where the federal government occupies more than 50% of rentable square footage;
        (v) Any additional facilities designated as mission-critical by agency heads.

(c) **Project Management Office.** The Administrator of GSA shall establish a Federal Hardening Project Management Office (FHPMO) within 60 days of this Order to coordinate and oversee the implementation of this Section. The FHPMO shall:

    (i) Provide technical assistance to agencies in designing, procuring, and implementing Aegis Home Standard compliance measures;
    (ii) Aggregate procurement requirements across agencies to achieve economies of scale;
    (iii) Maintain a central compliance tracking database;
    (iv) Conduct quarterly progress reviews with each department and agency;
    (v) Report to the President, through the Director of OMB, on overall progress, cost, and schedule performance.

**SECTION 6. FUNDING MECHANISMS.**

(a) **Direct Appropriation Authorization.** The President shall submit to Congress, within 60 days of this Order, a supplemental budget request for the Federal Grid Hardening Initiative, to include:

    (i) $12.8 billion for Department of Defense facility hardening (FY2027-FY2030);
    (ii) $9.4 billion for Department of Veterans Affairs medical facility hardening;
    (iii) $5.2 billion for GSA-managed facility hardening;
    (iv) $4.1 billion for Department of Homeland Security facility hardening;
    (v) $3.8 billion for Department of Energy facility and national laboratory hardening;
    (vi) $3.2 billion for Department of Justice facility hardening (FBI, DEA, ATF, USMS, BOP);
    (vii) $2.9 billion for Department of Transportation facility hardening (FAA);
    (viii) $6.1 billion for all other agency hardening requirements and FHPMO operations;
    (ix) **TOTAL REQUEST: $47.5 billion.**

(b) **Reprogramming Authority.** Pending congressional action on the supplemental budget request, the Director of the Office of Management and Budget is authorized to approve agency requests to reprogram up to 5% of their annual facility maintenance and construction budgets toward Aegis Home Standard compliance activities.

(c) **Energy Savings Performance Contracts (ESPCs).** Agencies are directed to utilize the authority under 42 U.S.C. 8287 et seq. to enter into energy savings performance contracts that incorporate GIC protection measures where feasible, allowing hardening costs to be paid from the energy savings realized over the life of the contract.

(d) **Working Capital Fund.** The Administrator of GSA shall establish a Federal Hardening Working Capital Fund, into which agencies may deposit funds, to provide bridge financing for hardening projects pending receipt of appropriated funds. The fund may be capitalized through contributions from participating agencies and through transfers authorized by the Director of OMB.

**SECTION 7. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT.**

(a) **Agency Head Accountability.** Each agency head shall be personally accountable for compliance with this Order. Agency heads shall designate a senior official at the Assistant Secretary level or equivalent to serve as the agency's Geomagnetic Hardening Compliance Officer.

(b) **Quarterly Compliance Reports.** Each agency shall submit quarterly compliance reports to the FHPMO, the OMB, and the relevant congressional oversight committees. Reports shall include:

    (i) Progress against the phased implementation schedule established by Section 5(b);
    (ii) Obligated and expended funds by facility and by phase;
    (iii) Any schedule delays, cost overruns, or technical obstacles encountered;
    (iv) Corrective action plans for any facilities or programs that are behind schedule.

(c) **Inspector General Oversight.** The Inspector General of each department and agency shall conduct annual audits of hardening expenditures and shall report findings to the agency head, the FHPMO, and Congress.

(d) **Noncompliance.** Any agency that fails to meet the Phase 1, Phase 2, or Phase 3 compliance deadlines shall:

    (i) Submit a detailed justification for the failure to the President, through the Director of OMB, within 30 days of the missed deadline;
    (ii) Present a corrective action plan with specific milestones and revised completion dates;
    (iii) Be subject to withholding of up to 10% of the agency's non-personnel administrative budget for the following fiscal year, at the discretion of the Director of OMB, until compliance is achieved.

**SECTION 8. RELATIONSHIP TO UTILITY-OWNED INFRASTRUCTURE.**

(a) **Coordination with Investor-Owned Utilities.** The Administrator of GSA and the Secretary of Energy shall, within 180 days, negotiate with investor-owned utilities serving major federal facilities to:

    (i) Establish joint hardening programs for transmission and distribution infrastructure that serves federal facilities, with cost-sharing arrangements consistent with the 75% federal / 25% utility cost-share model established by COVENANT-EO-001 Section 5(c);

    (ii) Prioritize the hardening of substations and transmission lines that serve concentrations of federal facilities — for example, the transmission corridor serving the Washington, D.C. metropolitan area's federal complex;

    (iii) Develop mutual aid agreements for post-GMD grid restoration, including provisions for utility crews to prioritize federal facilities essential to national response.

(b) **Federal Power Marketing Administrations.** The Bonneville Power Administration, Western Area Power Administration, Southwestern Power Administration, and Southeastern Power Administration shall:

    (i) Complete a GIC vulnerability assessment of their transmission systems within 180 days;
    (ii) Implement Aegis Home Standard-equivalent protection on all transmission assets within 48 months;
    (iii) Coordinate hardening activities with the North American Electric Reliability Corporation and the relevant regional transmission organizations.

**SECTION 9. SAVINGS AND SEVERABILITY.**

(a) **Savings Clause.** Nothing in this Order shall be construed to authorize the impairment of contracts, leases, or other legal obligations of the United States, nor to require hardening measures that would render a facility unsafe, noncompliant with building codes, or inoperable for its intended purpose.

(b) **Severability.** If any provision of this Order, or the application of any provision to any person or circumstance, is held to be invalid, the remainder of this Order shall not be affected.

**SECTION 10. GENERAL PROVISIONS.**

(a) Nothing in this Order shall be construed to impair or otherwise affect the authority granted by law to an executive department, agency, or the head thereof.

(b) This Order shall be implemented consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.

(c) This Order is not intended to, and does not, create any right or benefit enforceable at law or in equity by any party against the United States.
