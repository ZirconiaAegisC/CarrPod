## THE PLAIN-LANGUAGE PITCH

The National Response Framework is the master playbook for how America responds to disasters. It has annexes for hurricanes, earthquakes, floods, biological incidents, nuclear incidents, and cyber incidents. It does not have an annex for geomagnetic disturbances. This Executive Order writes that annex.

The unique challenge of a geomagnetic disaster is that it is, simultaneously, a power outage, a communications blackout, a water system failure, a fuel supply collapse, a food distribution crisis, a medical emergency, a transportation shutdown, a financial system paralysis, and a public order crisis — all happening at the same time, across the entire continent, with no unaffected neighboring regions to provide mutual aid. Every disaster response framework assumes there are unaffected areas that can send help. For a G5 geomagnetic storm, there are no unaffected areas. Everyone is in the impact zone.

The Geomagnetic Annex therefore defines a new set of Emergency Support Functions — the organizational structure through which federal agencies coordinate during disasters — specific to the unique characteristics of electromagnetic catastrophe. Where the standard National Response Framework defines 15 Emergency Support Functions, the Geomagnetic Annex adds 12 GMD-specific ESFs, each of which is designed for a world where the electrical grid is offline, telecommunications are dead, and all regions are simultaneously affected.

This is not about making people afraid. It is about giving emergency managers — the people who actually have to coordinate response when things go wrong — a clear, pre-written plan so they don't have to invent one at 3 AM during a continent-wide blackout. Plans reduce panic. Plans enable action. Plans save lives. This is the plan.

## OFFICIAL TEXT — EXECUTIVE ORDER [NUMBER TO BE ASSIGNED]

### ESTABLISHMENT OF THE GEOMAGNETIC DISASTER ANNEX TO THE NATIONAL RESPONSE FRAMEWORK

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, including the Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emergency Assistance Act (42 U.S.C. 5121 et seq.), the Homeland Security Act of 2002 (6 U.S.C. 101 et seq.), the Post-Katrina Emergency Management Reform Act of 2006 (6 U.S.C. 701 et seq.), and section 301 of title 3, United States Code, it is hereby ordered as follows:

**SECTION 1. POLICY.**

It is the policy of the United States to maintain a comprehensive, pre-established operational framework for the coordination of federal, state, local, tribal, territorial, private-sector, and non-governmental response to geomagnetic disturbances of G5 classification. The Geomagnetic Disaster Annex to the National Response Framework shall serve as the Nation's principal operational doctrine for GMD response, and all federal departments and agencies shall integrate the Annex into their emergency operations plans, continuity of operations plans, and exercise programs.

**SECTION 2. FINDINGS.**

(a) The National Response Framework, as mandated by Homeland Security Presidential Directive 5 and the Post-Katrina Emergency Management Reform Act, establishes the coordinating structures for federal disaster response. The Framework's 15 standard Emergency Support Functions (ESFs) and their supporting annexes are designed for geographically bounded, single-hazard events; they are not optimized for continent-scale, multi-hazard, infrastructure-cascading events characteristic of severe geomagnetic disturbances.

(b) The Congressional Research Service (CRS Report R45210), FEMA's 2024 National Preparedness Report, and the National Infrastructure Advisory Council's 2024 report on space weather preparedness have each identified the absence of a GMD-specific response annex as a critical gap in national preparedness.

(c) The unique characteristics of GMD disasters — simultaneous continent-scale impact, destruction of the very communications and transportation infrastructure required for response coordination, multi-year recovery timelines, and the absence of unaffected neighboring regions to provide mutual aid — require a fundamentally different operational doctrine than traditional disaster response frameworks.

(d) The 12 Emergency Support Functions specific to GMD response, defined in this Order, are designed to function in a degraded operational environment where normal communications, transportation, and logistics chains are disrupted, and where all responders — federal, state, local, and private-sector — are simultaneously operating under extreme resource constraints.

> **WILLIAMS HEURISTIC — SECTION 2 IN PLAIN LANGUAGE:** The existing disaster playbook works great when one state gets hit by a hurricane and 49 other states can send help. It doesn't work when all 50 states get hit at once and the radios are dead. We need a different playbook for that scenario. This Order writes it.

**SECTION 3. THE GEOMAGNETIC DISASTER ANNEX — ESTABLISHMENT AND INCORPORATION.**

(a) **Establishment.** There is hereby established the Geomagnetic Disaster Annex to the National Response Framework, which shall be developed and maintained by the Administrator of FEMA in coordination with the Federal Geomagnetic Disaster Coordination Group established by COVENANT-EO-002.

(b) **Incorporation into the NRF.** The Geomagnetic Annex shall be incorporated into the National Response Framework as a formal Incident Annex, with the same legal and operational status as the Biological Incident Annex, the Nuclear/Radiological Incident Annex, the Cyber Incident Annex, and the Catastrophic Incident Annex.

(c) **Effective Date.** The initial Geomagnetic Disaster Annex shall be published by the Administrator of FEMA within 365 days of this Order and shall be reviewed and updated biennially thereafter.

**SECTION 4. GEOMAGNETIC DISASTER EMERGENCY SUPPORT FUNCTIONS (ESF-G).**

The Geomagnetic Disaster Annex shall establish and define the following 12 Emergency Support Functions specific to geomagnetic disaster response. Each ESF-G shall be led by a designated Coordinating Agency and supported by designated Cooperating Agencies as specified below.

---

**ESF-G1 — GRID RESTORATION AND ENERGY ASSURANCE.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of Energy.
**Cooperating Agencies:** FERC, DOD (U.S. Army Corps of Engineers), DHS (CISA), NERC (advisory).

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Coordinate the assessment, prioritization, and restoration of the electrical grid, beginning with the highest-priority loads: hospitals, water treatment, emergency communications, military installations, and fuel distribution infrastructure.
(b) Direct the deployment of prepositioned emergency generators, mobile substations, and the Strategic Transformer Reserve assets established by COVENANT-EO-003.
(c) Coordinate with investor-owned utilities, municipal utilities, and rural electric cooperatives to establish unified command for grid restoration operations.
(d) Manage the allocation of limited generation and transmission capacity during the restoration period through a Priority Load Restoration Order consistent with the National Infrastructure Protection Plan.
(e) Coordinate with ESF-G2 (Fuel Distribution) to ensure restoration crews, mobile generators, and emergency vehicles have adequate fuel.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G1 is the "turn the lights back on" team. DOE leads. They decide which hospitals get power first, deploy spare transformers, and coordinate all the utility companies into a single unified command. Without this, every utility acts independently and the grid doesn't come back as a system — it comes back as disconnected islands that can't help each other.

---

**ESF-G2 — FUEL DISTRIBUTION AND LOGISTICS.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of Energy (Office of Cybersecurity, Energy Security, and Emergency Response — CESER).
**Cooperating Agencies:** DOD (Defense Logistics Agency), DOT, FEMA, EPA.

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Coordinate the distribution of emergency fuel supplies — diesel, gasoline, aviation fuel, propane, and natural gas — to critical infrastructure operators, emergency responders, and the public.
(b) Manage the prepositioned fuel reserves established by COVENANT-EO-002 Section 5.
(c) Coordinate with fuel refiners, pipeline operators, and fuel distributors to restore fuel supply chains, including emergency repair of pipeline pumping stations and fuel terminal electrical systems.
(d) Establish emergency fuel rationing, allocation, and pricing controls to prevent hoarding, price gouging, and inequitable distribution.
(e) Coordinate with the Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) to supplement civilian fuel supplies with military fuel reserves where necessary.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G2 makes sure there's fuel for generators, ambulances, fire trucks, food delivery trucks, and people who need to evacuate or get to work. Without power, gas station pumps don't work. This team manages the emergency fuel supply — including military fuel reserves if needed — and prevents price gouging.

---

**ESF-G3 — EMERGENCY COMMUNICATIONS.**

**Coordinating Agency:** DHS (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency — CISA).
**Cooperating Agencies:** FCC, DOD (U.S. Cyber Command, DISA), FEMA, NOAA, Department of Commerce (NTIA).

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Establish and maintain emergency communications networks using satellite, high-frequency radio, and any surviving terrestrial infrastructure to connect federal, state, local, and tribal responders.
(b) Deploy the prepositioned satellite communication terminals and HF radio relay stations established by COVENANT-EO-002 Section 5(b)(iv).
(c) Coordinate with commercial telecommunications providers to prioritize restoration of emergency responder communications networks.
(d) Manage the Federal Emergency Communications Grid — a dedicated, GIC-hardened communications backbone connecting all federal emergency operations centers — as specified in COVENANT-EO-004 Section 3(a)(iv).
(e) Coordinate public information dissemination through ESF-G10.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G3 is the "how do we talk to each other when cell towers are dead" team. They deploy satellite phones and HF radios, prioritize restoring emergency responder communications, and maintain the federal government's own hardened communications network.

---

**ESF-G4 — WATER, FOOD, AND AGRICULTURE.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of Agriculture.
**Cooperating Agencies:** FEMA, HHS (FDA), EPA, DOD, DOI (Bureau of Reclamation).

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Coordinate the assessment and restoration of municipal water treatment and distribution systems, prioritizing hospitals and population centers.
(b) Deploy prepositioned mobile water purification units from FEMA's prepositioning resource centers.
(c) Coordinate the distribution of emergency food supplies, including FEMA's prepositioned shelf-stable meal inventory and USDA commodity stocks.
(d) Assess and address impacts on agricultural production, including livestock watering and feeding systems, crop irrigation, cold storage, and food processing facilities that depend on grid power.
(e) Coordinate with the private-sector food supply chain — including grocery distributors, food manufacturers, and agricultural cooperatives — to restore food distribution infrastructure.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G4 keeps people fed and hydrated. Water treatment plants need power. Food distribution needs refrigeration and trucks with fuel. This team deploys mobile water purifiers, distributes emergency food stocks, and works with grocery stores and farms to keep the food supply chain moving.

---

**ESF-G5 — PUBLIC HEALTH AND MEDICAL SERVICES.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of Health and Human Services (Assistant Secretary for Preparedness and Response — ASPR).
**Cooperating Agencies:** DOD (Defense Health Agency), VA, FEMA, CDC, FDA.

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Deploy and operate prepositioned field hospital modules from FEMA's prepositioning resource centers to supplement hospital capacity where grid-dependent hospitals are unable to operate.
(b) Coordinate with the Department of Veterans Affairs to utilize hardened VA medical facilities — which are required to meet the Aegis Home Standard under COVENANT-EO-004 — as regional medical coordination centers.
(c) Manage pharmaceutical and medical supply distribution, including cold-chain medical products (vaccines, insulin, blood products) that require temperature-controlled storage.
(d) Conduct public health surveillance to detect and respond to disease outbreaks associated with disrupted sanitation, water supply, and healthcare access.
(e) Coordinate behavioral health and psychological first aid services — recognizing that prolonged blackout, economic collapse, and social disruption will produce a mental health crisis exceeding anything in modern U.S. experience.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G5 keeps people alive. They deploy field hospitals when regular hospitals lose power. They use VA hospitals — which this Order requires to be hardened — as command centers. They manage vaccine and medicine distribution. And they plan for the mental health crisis that comes with months of blackout — because that's not a secondary concern, it's a primary cause of death in prolonged disasters.

---

**ESF-G6 — TRANSPORTATION.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of Transportation.
**Cooperating Agencies:** DOD (U.S. Transportation Command), FEMA, GSA, state DOTs.

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Coordinate the restoration of transportation infrastructure, including assessment and repair of traffic control systems, rail signaling, pipeline pumping stations, and airport navigation aids that depend on grid power.
(b) Manage emergency transportation resources — including prepositioned fuel for emergency vehicles, coordination of military and civilian airlift for critical supplies, and prioritization of road clearance for emergency corridors.
(c) Coordinate with the DOD's U.S. Transportation Command (USTRANSCOM) to supplement civilian transportation capacity with military airlift, sealift, and ground transportation assets.
(d) Establish emergency transportation corridors — designated interstate highways, rail lines, and waterways given priority for fuel, maintenance, and security — to ensure the movement of critical supplies and personnel.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G6 keeps things moving. Traffic lights, rail signals, airport navigation — all need power. This team fixes them, designates priority transportation corridors, and coordinates military airlift and sealift when civilian transportation isn't enough.

---

**ESF-G7 — PUBLIC ORDER AND SECURITY.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of Justice (Federal Bureau of Investigation).
**Cooperating Agencies:** DOD (National Guard Bureau), DHS (Federal Protective Service, CBP), Department of Justice (U.S. Marshals Service, Bureau of Prisons).

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Coordinate federal support to state and local law enforcement agencies to maintain public order during prolonged grid-down conditions.
(b) Protect critical infrastructure sites — including hospitals, water treatment facilities, fuel depots, food distribution points, and emergency communications centers — from civil disturbance, looting, or attack.
(c) Coordinate the security of federal facilities, including the hardened federal buildings required by COVENANT-EO-004.
(d) Manage the National Guard activation authorized by COVENANT-EO-001 Section 3(d), including coordination between active-duty military, National Guard forces under state control (Title 32), and National Guard forces under federal control (Title 10).
(e) Provide security for grid restoration crews and equipment, who will be operating in potentially hostile environments during prolonged blackouts.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G7 keeps order. In a prolonged blackout, people get desperate. This team protects hospitals, fuel depots, and food distribution points. It coordinates the National Guard. It provides security for the crews trying to fix the grid — because linemen with tools and fuel are prime targets in a grid-down world.

---

**ESF-G8 — FINANCIAL SERVICES AND ECONOMIC CONTINUITY.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of the Treasury.
**Cooperating Agencies:** Federal Reserve System, FDIC, SEC, CFTC, OCC, SBA.

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Coordinate the restoration and operation of critical financial infrastructure — including payment systems, securities clearing and settlement systems, and banking operations — that depend on grid power and telecommunications.
(b) Manage emergency liquidity provisioning to financial institutions, including Federal Reserve discount window operations, to prevent cascading financial system collapse.
(c) Coordinate emergency cash distribution — recognizing that in the absence of functioning electronic payment systems, physical currency becomes essential for all transactions, from food purchases to fuel to medical supplies.
(d) Administer emergency economic support programs, including expanded unemployment insurance, emergency small business loans, and direct cash transfers to affected populations — using authorities under the Social Security Act, the Stafford Act, and the Federal Reserve Act.
(e) Coordinate with the insurance industry — through ESF-G9 (International Coordination) and the Federal Insurance Office — regarding the activation of the Geomagnetic Insurance Backstop established by COVENANT-BILL-005.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G8 keeps money working. When ATMs go dark and credit card machines die, the economy freezes even if people have money. This team manages emergency cash distribution, keeps the banking system functioning through backup systems, and coordinates unemployment and small business support. In a disaster where the economy stops for months, this team is as important as the doctors.

---

**ESF-G9 — INTERNATIONAL COORDINATION.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of State.
**Cooperating Agencies:** DOD, DHS, USAID, Department of Commerce, USTR.

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Implement the bilateral mutual assistance agreements with Canada and Mexico negotiated under COVENANT-EO-005, including coordination of cross-border grid restoration, personnel deployment, and equipment transfers.
(b) Coordinate international humanitarian assistance — both U.S. assistance to affected foreign nations and the reception of international assistance offered to the United States under the mutual assistance protocols of existing treaties and the bilateral agreements established by COVENANT-EO-005.
(c) Manage diplomatic communications with foreign governments regarding the status of U.S. response efforts, the safety of foreign nationals in the United States, and the coordination of global supply chain restoration.
(d) Coordinate with the United Nations Office for Disaster Risk Reduction, the International Energy Agency, NATO Civil Emergency Planning, and other international bodies as specified in COVENANT-EO-005.
(e) Manage visa and customs processing for international response personnel, consistent with the fast-track protocol established by COVENANT-EO-005 Section 3(e).

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G9 handles the fact that this disaster doesn't stop at borders. They coordinate with Canada and Mexico under the agreements we negotiated earlier. They manage international aid coming in and going out. They keep the diplomatic channels open so allies can coordinate rather than compete for scarce resources.

---

**ESF-G10 — PUBLIC INFORMATION AND COMMUNICATION.**

**Coordinating Agency:** FEMA (Office of External Affairs).
**Cooperating Agencies:** White House Office of Communications, FCC, DHS Office of Public Affairs, Department of Education.

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Develop and disseminate clear, accurate, calm public information regarding the disaster, response efforts, safety instructions, and resource availability — consistent with the Williams Heuristic (plain-language communication) and the El Segundo Heuristic (calm, measured, competence-focused messaging).
(b) Coordinate with broadcast media (radio and television where operational), social media platforms, and emergency alert systems to reach all segments of the population, including non-English speakers, individuals with disabilities, and those without internet access.
(c) Establish and operate a National Geomagnetic Disaster Information Hotline and public website providing real-time status updates, resource location information, and safety guidance.
(d) Combat misinformation and disinformation — which historically proliferates during infrastructure disruptions — through proactive fact-based communication and coordination with social media platforms.
(e) Coordinate with ESF-G3 (Emergency Communications) to ensure public information dissemination channels remain operational through redundant, GIC-hardened systems.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G10 tells people what's happening, what to do, where to get help, and when to expect things to improve. In a blackout, rumors spread faster than facts. This team's job is to make sure facts win. They use every communications channel that still works — radio, satellite, loudspeakers on police cars if that's all that's left — to reach people with clear, calm, useful information.

---

**ESF-G11 — VULNERABLE POPULATIONS PROTECTION.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of Health and Human Services (Administration for Community Living).
**Cooperating Agencies:** FEMA, HUD, VA, Department of Education, SSA, USDA (Food and Nutrition Service).

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Identify, locate, and provide assistance to populations with heightened vulnerability during prolonged grid-down conditions, including: elderly individuals dependent on electrically powered medical equipment; individuals with disabilities requiring mobility assistance, communications support, or life-sustaining medical devices; individuals dependent on dialysis, oxygen concentrators, or other electrically powered medical treatments; residents of nursing homes and assisted living facilities; homeless populations; incarcerated populations; and individuals in geographically isolated communities with single-transmission-line electrical service.
(b) Coordinate with the Social Security Administration and the Department of Veterans Affairs to maintain continuity of benefit payments to vulnerable populations during financial system disruptions.
(c) Coordinate with ESF-G4 (Water/Food) and ESF-G5 (Medical) to prioritize vulnerable populations in the allocation of emergency resources.
(d) Develop and maintain pre-disaster registries of individuals with electrically dependent medical equipment, coordinated with electric utilities so that utility crews conducting grid restoration are aware of the locations and needs of medically vulnerable customers.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G11 protects the people who will suffer most when the power goes out. People on dialysis, oxygen, or powered wheelchairs. Elderly people in nursing homes with no backup power. Homeless people with no way to get information or resources. This team makes sure they're not forgotten. This isn't charity. This is the most efficient use of resources — because medical emergencies among vulnerable populations will overwhelm healthcare capacity even faster than the blackout itself.

---

**ESF-G12 — SUPPLY CHAIN CONTINUITY.**

**Coordinating Agency:** Department of Commerce.
**Cooperating Agencies:** DOD (Defense Logistics Agency), DOT, FEMA, DHS (CISA), GSA.

**Responsibilities:**
(a) Assess and coordinate the restoration of critical supply chains, including: pharmaceutical manufacturing and distribution; medical device and personal protective equipment supply; semiconductor and electronics manufacturing (essential to grid restoration equipment itself); food processing and distribution; fuel refining and transportation; and critical mineral extraction and processing.
(b) Manage the allocation of scarce supply chain resources through the Defense Production Act priorities and allocations authorities activated by COVENANT-EO-003, including prioritizing contracts for materials essential to grid restoration and emergency response.
(c) Coordinate with the private sector through the Critical Infrastructure Partnership Advisory Council (CIPAC) and sector-specific Information Sharing and Analysis Centers (ISACs) to share threat information, resource availability status, and restoration priorities.
(d) Develop and maintain a National Critical Supply Chain Registry identifying manufacturing facilities, distribution centers, and logistics nodes essential to national survival and recovery, with requirements for those facilities to maintain GIC-hardened backup power per the Aegis Home Standard.

**WILLIAMS PLAIN-LANGUAGE:** ESF-G12 fixes the supply chains. When factories lose power, the things America needs stop being made — medicines, semiconductors, food packaging, water treatment chemicals. This team identifies the bottlenecks and fixes them first. It uses the DPA authorities from EO-003 to tell factories "make this critical item first." It's the industrial triage team.

**SECTION 5. IMPLEMENTATION REQUIREMENTS.**

(a) **Agency Integration.** Each Coordinating Agency identified in Section 4 shall, within 270 days of this Order:

    (i) Develop and publish an ESF-G Operations Plan detailing the agency's internal procedures, resource allocations, personnel assignments, and coordinating mechanisms for fulfilling its ESF-G responsibilities;

    (ii) Designate a Geomagnetic Operations Coordinator at the Senior Executive Service level or equivalent, who shall serve as the agency's primary point of contact for ESF-G activities and shall participate in the Federal Geomagnetic Disaster Coordination Group (FGDCG) established by COVENANT-EO-002;

    (iii) Conduct a tabletop exercise for its ESF-G responsibilities, with participation from its designated Cooperating Agencies, and submit an after-action report to the FGDCG within 30 days of the exercise;

    (iv) Integrate ESF-G responsibilities into the agency's Continuity of Operations Plan (COOP) and Continuity of Government (COG) planning.

(b) **State and Local Integration.** The Administrator of FEMA shall, within 365 days:

    (i) Publish a Model State Geomagnetic Disaster Annex that states may adopt to integrate their emergency operations plans with the federal Geomagnetic Disaster Annex;

    (ii) Develop and deliver training to state, local, tribal, and territorial emergency managers on the Geomagnetic Disaster Annex and the ESF-G structure;

    (iii) Conduct no fewer than three regional GMD response exercises annually, rotating through FEMA regions to ensure nationwide familiarity with the Annex.

(c) **Private-Sector Integration.** The Administrator of FEMA, in coordination with the Sector Risk Management Agencies designated under Presidential Policy Directive 21 (Critical Infrastructure Security and Resilience), shall:

    (i) Engage private-sector owners and operators of critical infrastructure — particularly electric utilities, telecommunications providers, fuel distributors, and food supply chain entities — in the development and testing of the Geomagnetic Disaster Annex;

    (ii) Establish memoranda of understanding with critical infrastructure owners and operators regarding their roles, responsibilities, and access to federal resources during a GMD event;

    (iii) Develop pre-negotiated contracts and mutual aid agreements that can be activated immediately upon declaration of a G5 geomagnetic storm warning.

**SECTION 6. FUNDING.**

(a) The Administrator of FEMA is authorized to utilize funds appropriated for the National Preparedness Directorate to develop, publish, exercise, and maintain the Geomagnetic Disaster Annex.

(b) The President shall submit to Congress, within 120 days, a budget request for $1.8 billion over five years to fund:

    (i) ESF-G exercises, training, and personnel ($420 million);
    (ii) State and local GMD preparedness grants ($650 million);
    (iii) Private-sector integration and information-sharing systems ($380 million);
    (iv) GMD-specific emergency communications infrastructure ($350 million).

**SECTION 7. RELATIONSHIP TO EXISTING STRUCTURES.**

The Geomagnetic Disaster Annex and its ESF-G structure shall complement, not duplicate, the existing ESF structure of the National Response Framework. During a GMD event, ESF-G1 through ESF-G12 shall operate in parallel with standard ESFs 1-15, with the FGDCG resolving any conflicts of authority or resource allocation between the two structures.

**SECTION 8. SEVERABILITY AND GENERAL PROVISIONS.**

(a) Severability applies. (b) Subject to appropriations. (c) No private right of action.

## APPENDIX B — PUBLIC COMMUNICATION TEMPLATE

Today the President established the Geomagnetic Disaster Annex to the National Response Framework — America's master disaster playbook. For the first time, the federal government has a pre-written, pre-coordinated plan for responding to a severe solar storm.

The Annex creates twelve specialized emergency response teams, each with a clear leader and clear responsibilities: one for restoring the grid, one for distributing fuel, one for emergency communications, one for food and water, one for medical care, one for transportation, one for public safety, one for the financial system, one for international coordination, one for public information, one for protecting the most vulnerable, and one for keeping supply chains working.

This is not alarmism. This is professionalism. The same kind of planning that goes into hurricane response, earthquake response, and pandemic response — applied to a threat that science tells us is real, is coming, and is protectable against.

The cost of developing and maintaining this Annex — approximately $1.8 billion over five years — is roughly what FEMA spends on general preparedness in about two weeks. It is one of the highest-return investments in the federal budget.
