## SHORT TITLE

This Act may be cited as the "Critical Infrastructure Electromagnetic Hardening Act of 2026."

## SECTION 1. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE.

(a) Congress finds that the electrical grid is essential to national defense, economic stability, and public health, and that geomagnetic disturbances pose a credible threat to grid integrity.
(b) Purpose: To establish mandatory hardening standards for critical electrical infrastructure, authorize federal cost-sharing, provide liability protections, and grant eminent domain authority for essential grid protection projects.

## SECTION 2. MANDATORY HARDENING STANDARDS.

(a) The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with FERC and NERC, shall promulgate mandatory reliability standards requiring all large power transformers (345 kV and above) to incorporate GIC protection meeting the Aegis Home Standard within 48 months of enactment.
(b) Standards shall be developed with industry consultation and shall include cost-recovery mechanisms through rate-based amortization.
(c) FERC shall enforce compliance under its existing authority at 16 U.S.C. §824o.

## SECTION 3. FEDERAL COST-SHARING.

(a) The Secretary of Energy may enter into cost-sharing agreements with owners of large power transformers under which the federal government provides up to 75% of the capital cost of GIC hardening.
(b) Utilities may recover remaining costs through rate-based amortization over not less than 30 years.
(c) Authorization of appropriations: $20,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027-2031.

## SECTION 4. LIABILITY PROTECTIONS.

(a) **Government Contractor Defense.** Any person who manufactures, installs, or maintains GMD protection equipment in compliance with technical specifications adopted by the Secretary of Energy or the Director of NIST shall be immune from civil liability for claims arising from the failure of such equipment during a GMD event, provided that the person did not engage in willful misconduct, gross negligence, or knowing violation of federal law.
(b) **Federal Preemption.** No State may impose liability inconsistent with this section.
(c) **Certification.** The Secretary shall establish a certification program for GMD protection equipment.

## SECTION 5. EMINENT DOMAIN AUTHORITY.

(a) The Secretary of Energy may acquire by condemnation any real property or interest therein necessary for the installation of GMD protection equipment on critical grid infrastructure, upon payment of just compensation.
(b) Eminent domain shall be exercised only after good-faith negotiations and only for projects determined by the Secretary to be essential to grid protection.
(c) Just compensation shall be determined in accordance with the Uniform Relocation Assistance and Real Property Acquisition Policies Act (42 U.S.C. 4601 et seq.).

## SECTION 6. SAFETY ACT EXPANSION.

The SAFETY Act (6 U.S.C. 441 et seq.) is amended to include "Qualified Geomagnetic Protection Technologies" as a category eligible for liability protections, administered by the Secretary of Homeland Security.

## SECTION 7. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

Total authorization: $25,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027-2036.
