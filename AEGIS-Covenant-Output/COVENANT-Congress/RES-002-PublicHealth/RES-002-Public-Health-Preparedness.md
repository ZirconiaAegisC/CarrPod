## CONCURRENT RESOLUTION

Expressing the sense of Congress regarding public health preparedness for prolonged electrical grid failure resulting from geomagnetic disturbances.

Whereas prolonged electrical grid failure would disrupt hospital operations, pharmaceutical cold-chain storage, water treatment, and sanitation systems, with severe public health consequences;

Whereas individuals dependent on electrically powered medical equipment — including dialysis machines, oxygen concentrators, ventilators, and powered wheelchairs — face immediate life-threatening risk during grid failure;

Whereas the Strategic National Stockpile, the National Disaster Medical System, and the Public Health Service Act provide existing statutory frameworks for medical emergency response that can and should be extended to GMD scenarios;

Whereas mental health consequences of prolonged societal disruption — including increased rates of depression, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder, substance abuse, and suicide — must be anticipated and addressed as part of comprehensive GMD preparedness;

Whereas the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the Assistant Secretary for Preparedness and Response, and the Department of Veterans Affairs possess expertise and infrastructure essential to GMD health response coordination:

Now, therefore, be it Resolved by the House of Representatives (the Senate concurring), That it is the sense of Congress that—

(1) The Secretary of Health and Human Services should develop a Geomagnetic Health Emergency Annex to the National Health Security Strategy, addressing medical facility backup power, pharmaceutical supply chain continuity, mass casualty surge capacity, and behavioral health response;

(2) The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention should develop public health surveillance protocols specific to prolonged grid-down conditions, including surveillance for waterborne disease, foodborne illness, carbon monoxide poisoning from improper generator use, and heat- and cold-related mortality;

(3) The Department of Veterans Affairs should ensure that all VA medical facilities meet the Aegis Home Standard for GIC protection and backup power independence as directed by COVENANT-EO-004;

(4) The Department of Health and Human Services should, in coordination with the Federal Communications Commission, develop public health communication templates for GMD emergencies that address medication management, water safety, food safety, and mental health resources — consistent with the Williams Heuristic requiring plain-language, actionable guidance;

(5) State and local health departments should be encouraged through federal preparedness grants to develop GMD-specific public health annexes to their emergency operations plans;

(6) Research into the public health impacts of prolonged grid failure — including epidemiological studies, behavioral health research, and health systems resilience analysis — should be funded through the National Institutes of Health, the CDC, and the National Academies.
