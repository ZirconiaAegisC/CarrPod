## SHORT TITLE

This Act may be cited as the "Geomagnetic Preparedness and Defense Production Act Reauthorization Act of 2026."

## SECTION 1. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE.

(a) **Findings.** Congress finds that:
(1) The Defense Production Act of 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4501 et seq.) provides essential authorities for the prioritization of contracts and the expansion of productive capacity necessary for national defense.
(2) The authorities under Titles I and III of the Defense Production Act expired on September 30, 2025, and require reauthorization.
(3) The electrical grid of the United States is vulnerable to geomagnetic disturbances caused by coronal mass ejections, which national security, economic continuity, and public health depend upon.
(4) The domestic industrial base for the materials and products necessary to protect the electrical grid against geomagnetic disturbances is insufficient to meet national requirements, and private capital markets have not, without federal intervention, provided the investment necessary to expand productive capacity.
(5) The provision of loan guarantees and the exercise of priorities and allocations authority under the Defense Production Act are necessary to establish a domestic industrial base for GIC-blocking capacitors, MXene-based electromagnetic shielding films, carbon nanotube-doped dielectric polymer composites, basalt fiber reinforced polymer transformer enclosure materials, silicon nitride ceramic bearings, and large power transformers.

(b) **Purpose.** The purpose of this Act is to:
(1) Reauthorize Titles I, III, and VII of the Defense Production Act of 1950 for a period of ten years.
(2) Expressly confirm that the protection of critical electrical infrastructure against natural electromagnetic threats constitutes a "national defense" activity within the meaning of the Act.
(3) Authorize appropriations for the Defense Production Act Fund for geomagnetic protection industrial base activities.

## SECTION 2. REAUTHORIZATION OF DPA TITLES I, III, AND VII.

(a) Title I of the Defense Production Act of 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4511 et seq.) is reauthorized through September 30, 2036.
(b) Title III of the Defense Production Act of 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4531 et seq.) is reauthorized through September 30, 2036.
(c) Title VII of the Defense Production Act of 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4551 et seq.) is reauthorized through September 30, 2036.

## SECTION 3. GEOMAGNETIC PROTECTION AS NATIONAL DEFENSE.

Section 702 of the Defense Production Act of 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4555) is amended by adding at the end the following:

"(18) GEOMAGNETIC DISTURBANCE PROTECTION. — The term 'national defense' includes activities related to protecting critical electrical infrastructure against geomagnetic disturbances, as defined by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration's Space Weather Prediction Center geomagnetic storm scale, including the manufacture, installation, and maintenance of equipment, materials, and systems designed to block, attenuate, or mitigate geomagnetically induced currents."

## SECTION 4. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS FOR DPA FUND.

There is authorized to be appropriated to the Defense Production Act Fund established under section 304 of the Defense Production Act of 1950 (50 U.S.C. 4534) $3,000,000,000 for fiscal years 2027 through 2031, of which:

(1) $2,400,000,000 shall be available for loan guarantees under Title III for the expansion of productive capacity for GIC protection materials and products, consistent with the determinations and allocations set forth in Executive Order COVENANT-EO-003;
(2) $350,000,000 shall be available for direct purchases and purchase commitments under Title III for the Strategic Transformer Reserve;
(3) $250,000,000 shall be available for technical assistance, supply chain analysis, and administration of the DPA geomagnetic protection program.

## SECTION 5. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS.

The President shall submit to Congress, not later than March 1 of each year, a report detailing all actions taken under the authorities of the Defense Production Act for GMD protection purposes, including the status of loan guarantees, production capacity achieved, employment data, and an assessment of whether domestic productive capacity is sufficient to meet national requirements.

## SECTION 6. SUNSET.

The amendments made by this Act shall cease to have effect on September 30, 2036.
