## SHORT TITLE

This Act may be cited as the "Strategic Transformer Reserve Authorization Act of 2026."

## SECTION 1. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that:
(1) The United States electrical grid relies on approximately 2,000 large power transformers, each of which is a custom-engineered asset with manufacturing lead times of 12 to 24 months.
(2) The United States imports approximately 80 percent of its large power transformers, primarily from South Korea, Germany, and Japan.
(3) A severe geomagnetic disturbance could simultaneously destroy 300 or more large power transformers, overwhelming global manufacturing capacity of approximately 100 units per year.
(4) The absence of a strategic reserve of large power transformers represents an unacceptable vulnerability to national security and economic continuity.
(5) A strategic reserve of not fewer than 75 large power transformers, pre-positioned at geographically distributed hardened storage facilities, is necessary to ensure rapid grid backbone restoration following a catastrophic GMD event.

## SECTION 2. ESTABLISHMENT OF STRATEGIC TRANSFORMER RESERVE.

(a) **In General.** The Secretary of Energy shall establish and maintain a Strategic Transformer Reserve consisting of not fewer than 75 large power transformers (345 kV and above).
(b) **Domestic Manufacturing Requirement.** Not fewer than 50 transformers shall be manufactured in the United States.
(c) **Allied Procurement.** Up to 25 transformers may be procured from allied nations under international coordination agreements.
(d) **GIC Hardening.** All transformers in the Reserve shall incorporate GIC protection meeting or exceeding the Aegis Home Standard.
(e) **Storage.** Reserve transformers shall be stored at geographically distributed, GIC-hardened facilities maintained in a state of deployable readiness.

## SECTION 3. DEPLOYMENT AUTHORITY.

(a) The Secretary of Energy may deploy Reserve transformers upon:
(1) Declaration of a National Geomagnetic Disaster Emergency under COVENANT-EO-001; or
(2) Determination by the Secretary that a grid emergency requires Reserve deployment.
(b) Priority shall be given to restoring power to hospitals, military installations, water treatment facilities, and emergency operations centers.

## SECTION 4. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

There is authorized to be appropriated $4,200,000,000 for fiscal years 2027-2031 for procurement, storage, maintenance, and deployment of the Strategic Transformer Reserve.

## SECTION 5. REPORTING.

The Secretary shall report annually to Congress on the status of the Reserve, including the number and location of transformers, readiness assessments, and deployment exercises conducted.
