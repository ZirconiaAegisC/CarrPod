## SHORT TITLE

This Act may be cited as the "Veteran Geomagnetic Response Corps Act of 2026."

## SECTION 1. FINDINGS.

Congress finds that:
(1) The United States has approximately 18 million veterans, many of whom possess skills directly applicable to grid restoration, emergency response, and disaster logistics.
(2) The Department of Veterans Affairs and the Department of Labor administer programs that can be leveraged to recruit and train veterans for GMD preparedness and response roles.
(3) Veterans bring to emergency response a level of discipline, training, and mission focus that is particularly valuable in degraded operational environments.
(4) The establishment of a dedicated Corps provides veterans with meaningful post-service employment in national service while addressing a critical national security workforce shortage.

## SECTION 2. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE VETERAN GEOMAGNETIC RESPONSE CORPS.

(a) The Secretary of Energy, in coordination with the Secretary of Veterans Affairs and the Secretary of Labor, shall establish the Veteran Geomagnetic Response Corps (VGRC) within 365 days of enactment.
(b) The VGRC shall recruit, train, and deploy veterans for grid restoration, emergency power operations, fuel distribution, communications restoration, and related GMD response functions.
(c) The VGRC shall consist of not fewer than 5,000 trained personnel within five years.

## SECTION 3. TRAINING AND CERTIFICATION.

(a) The Secretaries shall develop standardized training curricula including: advanced grid restoration, GIC protection equipment installation, emergency generator operations, and incident command system qualifications.
(b) Training shall be conducted at community colleges, technical schools, and DOD facilities.
(c) VGRC certification shall be recognized by FEMA and state emergency management agencies for disaster response credentialing.

## SECTION 4. DEPLOYMENT.

(a) The Secretary of Energy may deploy VGRC personnel upon declaration of a National Geomagnetic Disaster Emergency under COVENANT-EO-001.
(b) VGRC personnel shall be compensated at rates not less than the prevailing wage for equivalent civilian positions.
(c) Service in the VGRC shall not affect eligibility for VA benefits.

## SECTION 5. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.

There is authorized to be appropriated $750,000,000 for fiscal years 2027-2031 for recruitment, training, equipment, and administration of the VGRC.
