**[BRAKE]**

Every Carrington-scale event in history has hit a world with more electronic infrastructure than the one before.

1859: Telegraph lines. Maybe 200,000 km of wire globally. The electrical infrastructure consisted of wires, batteries, and the operators who connected them.

1921: Telegraph plus telephone. The US had started electrification — cities had power grids, but they were local DC grids, not interconnected AC transmission networks. The grid vulnerability was nascent.

1989: Mature power grid. Long-distance transmission. Six million people in one interconnected blackout. But no internet (as we know it). No GPS (only the military's experimental NAVSTAR). No cell phones. No data centers. No cloud. No autonomous vehicles. No precision agriculture. No digital financial markets.

2003: The internet exists. GPS is operational. Cell phones are widespread. But smartphones don't exist yet (iPhone launched 2007). Social media is in its infancy. Cloud computing is a concept, not an infrastructure. E-commerce is a fraction of retail. The digital transformation hasn't happened.

2024 (Gannon Storm): Every single one of those things has happened. The world runs on electronics. The grid is the backbone, but GPS is the nervous system, data centers are the brain, and the internet is the circulatory system. And the Gannon Storm disrupted agriculture without even touching the grid. Five hundred million dollars of crop losses from GPS degradation alone. That's a new loss mechanism — one that didn't exist in 2003 or 1989 or 1921 or 1859.

2026 and beyond: More electronics. More GPS dependence. More cloud dependence. More IoT devices. More autonomous systems. More everything. The vulnerability escalates every year, regardless of where the Sun is in its cycle. The Sun doesn't have to get more active to produce a larger catastrophe — our infrastructure just has to get more dependent on the things the Sun can disrupt.

That's the timeline argument. It's not just that the Sun is active now. It's that our vulnerability is at an all-time high and increasing. The product of solar activity and infrastructure vulnerability — the "risk product" — is at a historic maximum. It's not going down. It's going up. Every year we wait, the risk product increases.

**[BURST → HOVER]**

And here's the other side of the timeline argument, the defiant optimism side: we've never been better positioned to do something about it.

In 1859, we didn't know what a CME was. The concept didn't exist. Carrington saw the flare and suspected a connection to the aurora, but it was just a hypothesis. The physics of MHD, CME propagation, magnetospheric reconnection, ionospheric currents, geoelectric fields, and GIC — none of it existed. We didn't know what hit us.

In 1921, we had a better understanding of geomagnetism but no predictive capability. No satellites. No magnetospheric models. No SWPC alerts. No GIC monitoring. No transformer protection devices. We understood the storm after it happened, not before.

In 1989, we had warning capability. Solar observation satellites. Magnetometers on the ground. SWPC in Boulder. But the grid wasn't designed with GIC in mind, and the protective technology was rudimentary. The Hydro-Québec blackout happened because the grid was vulnerable and the operators had no time to react.

Today, in 2026, we have: real-time solar observation (SDO, SOHO, STEREO, Parker Solar Probe, Solar Orbiter). Real-time CME tracking and arrival prediction (NOAA SWPC, ESA SSA, WMO space weather coordination). Real-time magnetometer networks (INTERMAGNET, USGS, SuperMAG). Real-time GIC monitoring (EPRI SUNBURST, commercial systems). Transformer neutral blocking technology that is proven and commercially available. AEGIS-C composite shielding — a new class of EMI shielding materials that didn't exist five years ago. Parametric insurance products that can be designed and launched within 12-18 months. An insurance industry that is finally, belatedly, starting to pay attention.

The window is open because the Sun is active. The window is open because our technology is ready. The window is open because the insurance industry has the capital and the motivation. The window is open because the alternative — waiting until after the event — is not a strategy, it's an abdication.
