### 1. SOLAR CYCLE FUNDAMENTALS

#### 1.1. The 11-Year Solar Cycle
- **Solar (Schwabe) Cycle**: Approximately 11-year quasi-periodic variation in solar magnetic activity, measured by sunspot number.
- **Physical mechanism**: Solar dynamo — interaction of differential rotation (equator rotates faster than poles), meridional circulation, and magnetic field generation/reconnection in the solar convection zone.
- **Cycle phases**: Minimum → Rising Phase → Maximum → Declining Phase → Minimum. Average duration: 11 years (range: 9-14 years).
- **Current Cycle**: Solar Cycle 25 (December 2019 minimum — predicted minimum ~2030-2031).

#### 1.2. Sunspot Number (SSN) — Measurement
- **International Sunspot Number**: Daily count of sunspots, compiled by the Solar Influences Data Analysis Center (SIDC) in Brussels.
- **Smoothed SSN**: 13-month running mean to filter daily/weekly variation. Standard metric for comparing cycle strengths.
- **Cycle 25 current (July 2026)**: Smoothed SSN approximately 137 (NOAA SWPC).
