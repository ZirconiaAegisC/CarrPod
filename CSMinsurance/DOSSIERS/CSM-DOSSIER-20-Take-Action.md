### 6. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (EXPECTED)

**Q: Is this real?**
A: Yes. The AEGIS-C composite shielding system exists. It has been tested at independent accredited laboratories. The shielding effectiveness of 148-165 dB across 1 kHz to 10 GHz is measured, not modeled. Patents are filed. Publications are peer-reviewed. Advisory board members are credentialed and verifiable. The risk (Carrington-class CME) is well-documented in the scientific literature and in the insurance industry's own publications (Lloyd's Futureset, Swiss Re sigma). The mitigation (NBDs, GIC monitoring, strategic spares, DCP architecture) is based on established electrical engineering principles. Everything in these dossiers is verifiable. We encourage verification.

**Q: Why should the insurance industry fund materials science research?**
A: Because the insurance industry is the largest financial beneficiary of the research. The potential loss ($0.6-2.6 trillion) is an insurance industry loss. The investment in hardening ($30-50 billion) is trivial compared to the loss. And no other industry has the same incentive — governments move slowly, utilities operate on regulated returns. The insurance industry has the capital, the incentive, and the business model that profits from loss prevention. See Dossier 08 for the full funding argument.

**Q: What if we wait for the government to do it?**
A: Governments have been "assessing" space weather risk for decades. The NERC TPL-007 standard requires assessment, not hardening. The SHIELD Act has been introduced in Congress multiple times and never passed. FERC Order 830 was remanded. Government is not going to solve this in time for Solar Cycle 25. The insurance industry can — and should — lead. See Dossier 09 for the full argument.

**Q: What if a Carrington event doesn't happen during my career?**
A: The probability of a Carrington-class event is 1-2% per year, compounding to 10-22% per decade. That is not negligible. That is not "black swan." That is a quantified, peer-reviewed probability that exceeds many perils the insurance industry models and prices routinely. But even if the event doesn't happen during your career: are you willing to bet your company's solvency on that probability? Are you willing to explain to your shareholders, your policyholders, and history why you knew about the risk and chose not to mitigate it? See Dossier 11 for the probability analysis and Dossier 16 for the timeline argument.

**Q: Can I visit the lab?**
A: Yes. Tier 2 and Tier 3 partners receive annual lab visits. Tier 1 partners can request a visit on a space-available basis. We encourage in-person due diligence. Seeing the SPS machine produce a ZrB₂-SiC panel, seeing the MXene deposition chamber in operation, seeing the SE measurement setup in the anechoic chamber — there is no substitute for seeing it with your own eyes.

**Q: Is my contribution tax-deductible?**
