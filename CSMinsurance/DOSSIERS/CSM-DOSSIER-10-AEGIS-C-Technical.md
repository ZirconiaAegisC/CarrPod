**Layer 2: The Magic Layer — MXene EMI Shield, 45 Microns**

MXene. Pronounced "max-een." Two-dimensional transition metal carbides and nitrides. Discovered at Drexel University in 2011. One of the most extraordinary materials to emerge in the last quarter-century and almost nobody outside materials science has heard of it.

MXene is a family of compounds with the general formula Mₙ₊₁XₙTₓ, where M is an early transition metal (titanium, in our case — Ti₃C₂Tₓ), X is carbon or nitrogen, and T represents surface terminations (oxygen, hydroxyl, fluorine). It forms sheets that are one atom thick. Stack a few of those sheets together and you get a material with electrical conductivity comparable to copper — up to 15,000 siemens per centimeter — but in a form factor that can be deposited as a thin film on almost any substrate.

Here's why MXene is the electromagnetic shielding material of the gods: when an EM wave hits MXene, three things happen. First, some of the wave reflects — that's the simple part, the part any conductor does. Second, the rest enters the material and gets absorbed — that's where the magic happens, because MXene's layered structure, with its alternating conducting sheets and dielectric interlayers, creates internal reflections that trap the wave energy and convert it to heat. Third, the tiny amount that makes it through is attenuated by 120-165 decibels depending on frequency and thickness.

At 45 microns — that's 45 millionths of a meter, about half the width of a human hair — our MXene layer achieves shielding effectiveness of 148-165 dB across 1 kHz to 10 GHz. That's enough to take a field that would fry your electronics and reduce it to a field that your electronics wouldn't even notice. ## SECTION 2: CORE DELIVERABLE — AEGIS-C TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

### 1. MATERIAL COMPOSITION AND ARCHITECTURE

#### 1.1. Layer Structure (Cross-Section, Application Order)

| Layer | Material | Thickness | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (Outer) | ZrB₂-SiC Ceramic Matrix Composite | 2-5 mm | Thermal protection, mechanical integrity, first-stage EMI reflection |
| 2 (EMI Core) | MXene (Ti₃C₂Tₓ) Thin Film | 45 μm (±2 μm) | Primary EMI absorption, broadband shielding |
| 3 (Substrate) | Application-specific (ceramic, polymer, or metallic) | 1-10 mm | Structural support, form factor adaptation |
| 4 (Inner) | Proprietary passivation coating | 10-50 μm | Environmental sealing, oxidation protection, moisture barrier |

#### 1.2. ZrB₂-SiC Ceramic Matrix Composite — Detailed Specification
- **Composition**: 70-80 vol% ZrB₂, 20-30 vol% SiC
- **Sintering**: Spark plasma sintering (SPS) or hot pressing at 1,900-2,100°C, 30-50 MPa
- **Density**: 5.5-6.0 g/cm³
- **Flexural strength**: 500-800 MPa (room temperature); 300-500 MPa (1,500°C)
- **Fracture toughness**: 4-6 MPa·m½
- **Melting point**: 3,246°C (ZrB₂); dissociation temperature >3,000°C
- **Thermal conductivity**: 60-100 W/m·K at room temperature
- **Coefficient of thermal expansion (CTE)**: 6-8 × 10⁻⁶ /K
- **Oxidation resistance**: Passive oxidation (SiO₂ protective layer) up to 1,800°C. Active oxidation (SiO volatilization) above 1,800°C.
- **Electrical conductivity**: 10⁷ S/m (ZrB₂ is electrically conductive — contributes to first-stage EMI reflection)

#### 1.3. MXene (Ti₃C₂Tₓ) EMI Layer — Detailed Specification
- **Composition**: Ti₃C₂Tₓ, where T = O, OH, F surface termination groups
- **Synthesis**: Selective etching of Al from Ti₃AlC₂ MAX phase precursor using LiF/HCl etchant
- **Deposition method**: Spray deposition or electrophoretic deposition (EPD) with controlled thickness
- **Film thickness**: 45 μm (±2 μm), verified by SEM cross-section or profilometry
- **Electrical conductivity**: 10,000-15,000 S/cm (4-point probe measurement)
- **EMI shielding mechanism**:
  - SEA (absorption): 120-140 dB (dominant mechanism)
  - SER (reflection): 30-45 dB (secondary, from layer interfaces)
  - SEM (multiple internal reflections): 3-8 dB (tertiary, from MXene layered structure)
  - SET (total) = SEA + SER + SEM = 148-165 dB
- **Specific EMI SE (SSE/t)**: SE divided by density and thickness. SSE/t for 45 μm MXene: 32,000-38,000 dB·cm²/g, exceeding copper (10,000 dB·cm²/g) and aluminum (15,000 dB·cm²/g) by 2-3x.

### 3. MANUFACTURING PROCESS OVERVIEW

#### 3.1. Step 1: ZrB₂-SiC Substrate Fabrication
1. Powder preparation: ZrB₂ powder (99.5% purity, 1-5 μm particle size), SiC powder (99% purity, sub-micron)
2. Ball milling: ZrB₂ + SiC mixed in ethanol with SiC milling media, 24-48 hours
3. Drying: rotary evaporator, 80°C, vacuum
4. Die filling: graphite die for SPS or hot press
5. Sintering: SPS at 1,950°C, 50 MPa, 5-10 minute dwell, vacuum atmosphere
6. Post-processing: surface grinding, dimensional verification, quality inspection

#### 3.2. Step 2: MXene (Ti₃C₂Tₓ) Synthesis
1. Ti₃AlC₂ MAX phase precursor synthesis (or sourced from supplier)
2. Etching: Ti₃AlC₂ + LiF/HCl etchant solution, 35°C, 24 hours, magnetic stirring
3. Washing: deionized water centrifugation cycles until pH ≈6
4. Delamination: sonication or manual shaking in deionized water to produce few-layer MXene colloidal suspension
5. Concentration: centrifuge to achieve target concentration for deposition process

#### 3.3. Step 3: MXene Deposition on Substrate
1. Substrate preparation: surface cleaning, plasma treatment for adhesion
2. Deposition: spray coating or electrophoretic deposition at controlled rate
3. Thickness control: in-line optical profilometry or spectroscopic ellipsometry with feedback loop
4. Drying: controlled atmosphere (N₂ or Ar), 60-80°C, 2-4 hours

#### 3.4. Step 4: Passivation Coating
1. Proprietary passivation chemistry applied to MXene surface
2. Curing: UV or thermal cure per formulation
3. Quality verification: SE re-measurement, adhesion testing (cross-hatch per ASTM D3359), environmental testing sample
