## SECTION 2: CORE DELIVERABLE — INFRASTRUCTURE RESILIENCE INVESTMENT FRAMEWORK

### 1. THE VULNERABILITY GAP — DEFINITION AND MEASUREMENT

#### 1.1. Vulnerability Gap Definition
- **Vulnerability Gap**: The difference between the electromagnetic threat environment produced by a Carrington-class CME and the electromagnetic withstand capability of existing critical infrastructure.
- **Quantified for power grid**: North American grid can withstand GMD events up to approximately Dst = -300 nT (G3-G4 range) without significant damage. A Carrington-class event produces Dst = -850 to -1,050 nT. Vulnerability gap = 550-750 nT (factor of 3-4x beyond design basis).

#### 1.2. Protection Gap Definition
- **Protection Gap (standard insurance definition)**: Economic loss minus insured loss.
- **Carrington protection gap**: Estimated economic loss $4.5-7.8T. Estimated insured loss $0.6-2.1T (assuming standard policy language applies). Protection gap = $2.4-5.7T (53-73% of total economic loss uninsured).
- **Worst case**: If coverage disputes result in widespread denial of space-weather-related claims, insured losses could be as low as $0.1-0.3T, making the protection gap 95-98%.

### 3. INSURANCE INDUSTRY HARDENING FRAMEWORK — FIVE ACTION AREAS

#### 3.1. Action Area 1: Risk-Based Pricing Integration
- **Develop space weather risk score**: multi-factor score incorporating:
  - Geographic location (latitude, ground conductivity for GIC)
  - Grid region (ISO/RTO, transformer population, NERC TPL-007 assessment results)
  - Asset-specific hardening status (NBD installed: -50% risk; GIC monitoring: -20%; AEGIS-C shielding: -70%)
  - Transformer age (0-20 years: baseline; 20-40 years: +50% risk; 40+ years: +150% risk)
  - Backup power capability (generator with 7+ days fuel: -30%; <72 hours fuel: no credit)
- **Integrate into rating algorithms** for: commercial property, energy/utility, marine, aviation, BI, cyber.

#### 3.2. Action Area 2: Premium Credit Program
- **Grid hardening premium credits**:
  - Neutral blocking device installed on insured transformers: 5-15% premium credit on property and BI.
  - AEGIS-C control room shielding: 10-20% premium credit on electronic equipment and BI.
  - GIC monitoring system installed: 3-8% premium credit.
  - Space weather contingency plan (documented, tested annually): 5-10% premium credit.
  - Combined hardening program (NBD + GIC monitoring + contingency plan + spare transformer contract): 20-35% premium credit.

#### 3.3. Action Area 3: Risk Engineering Protocol
- **New risk engineering checklist items for space weather**:
  1. Transformer inventory: age, MVA rating, oil test results (DGA), previous GMD experience.
  2. Neutral grounding configuration: GIC flow path analysis.
  3. Backup power: generator capacity, fuel storage volume (hours/days at full load), refueling contract, fuel type.
  4. Control system hardening: AEGIS-C or equivalent EMI shielding on protection relays, SCADA, and communications equipment.
  5. GPS/GNSS dependence: list of systems dependent on GPS. Non-GNSS backup available (eLORAN, INS, etc.)?
  6. Contingency plan: documented, tabletop-tested, hard copy available off-site.
  7. Spare equipment: on-site spare transformer? Contract for priority replacement from supplier?

#### 3.4. Action Area 4: Accumulation Modeling
- **Required inputs for space weather accumulation model**:
  1. Portfolio exposure data: location, industry, TIV, BI values for every policy.
  2. GIC vulnerability mapping: ground conductivity model (USGS), transmission line network, transformer population.
  3. Event scenarios: 100-year, 200-year, 500-year, 1,000-year Carrington-class scenarios.
  4. Damage functions: transformer failure probability as function of GIC magnitude, transformer age, design type.
  5. Cascade modeling: dependency mapping from grid failure to water, telecom, data center, fuel, supply chain, healthcare.
  6. Output: probable maximum loss (PML) by line of business, geography, and total portfolio.

#### 3.5. Action Area 5: Research and Advocacy Funding
- **Insurance industry consortium for space weather resilience**:
  - Modeled on IBHS (Insurance Institute for Business & Home Safety) structure: industry-funded, non-profit, applied research.
  - Initial budget: $10-25 million per year (funded by 20-50 carriers at $200K-$1M each).
  - Research agenda: GIC hardening technology validation, transformer failure probability refinement, economic impact modeling, insurance product development.
  - Advocacy: NERC/FERC engagement, IEEE standards development, building code integration for EMI shielding.
