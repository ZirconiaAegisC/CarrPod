## WHY BERKSHIRE MUST ENGAGE NOW

Berkshire Hathaway's insurance operations represent the world's largest insurance float — over $170 billion in premiums held for future claims. Warren Buffett has repeatedly warned about "super-cat" events that could trigger simultaneous claims across multiple lines. A Carrington-class CME would do exactly that: property (transformer fires, electronics destruction), business interruption (grid-down losses), marine (navigation system failures), aviation (satellite loss), and cyber (cascading system failures) — all from one solar event.

Additionally, Berkshire owns BNSF Railway, Berkshire Hathaway Energy (including PacifiCorp and MidAmerican Energy), and significant utility holdings. The company is doubly exposed: as insurer AND as infrastructure owner. This dual exposure makes them uniquely motivated to fund mitigation.

**Our value proposition:** Unlike pure insurers who only see the claims side, Berkshire owns the infrastructure that would need the shielding. They can simultaneously be our development partner, our first customer, and our distribution channel through their insurance operations.
