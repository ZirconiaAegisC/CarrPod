## 3. LAUNCH FACILITY ANALYSIS

### Cape Canaveral / Kennedy Space Center (Florida)
- **SLC-40 (CCSFS):** Primary Falcon 9 launch pad. 100+ launches from this pad. CRS, commercial, NSSL missions. 15 MW peak during countdown.
- **LC-39A (KSC):** Falcon Heavy / Crew Dragon pad. Apollo/Saturn V historic pad. Crew launch infrastructure (crew access arm, white room, emergency egress). 18 MW peak.
- **Geomagnetic Risk:** 28°N latitude — moderate GIC exposure. Coastal location enhances coupling.

### Vandenberg Space Force Base (California)
- **SLC-4E:** Polar/orbit launch pad. NRO, Iridium, Earth observation missions. 14 MW peak.
- **Geomagnetic Risk:** 35°N latitude — moderate-high GIC. Pacific coastal coupling.

### Starbase (Boca Chica, Texas)
- **Starship/Super Heavy:** The world's largest launch vehicle. Orbital launch mount, orbital tank farm (methane/oxygen). 30-50 MW estimated peak during full-stack operations.
- **Geomagnetic Risk:** 26°N latitude — Gulf of Mexico coastal effect. The site's novel methane/oxygen cryogenic systems have not been tested during grid disturbances.

### Starlink Ground Stations
- **100+ gateways globally.** Each: 8-32 phased-array antennas, fiber backhaul, local power (often rural utility feeds with long lines). High GIC vulnerability due to long rural power feeds.
