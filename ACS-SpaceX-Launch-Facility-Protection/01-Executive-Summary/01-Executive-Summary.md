
# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
## ACS-SPACEX-LAUNCH-FACILITY-PROTECTION
### Launch Infrastructure Dielectric Hardening Program

**CLASSIFICATION: COMMERCIAL-IN-CONFIDENCE**

---

## 1. STRATEGIC PROBLEM

SpaceX launch facilities operate some of the most electrically intensive and electronically sensitive industrial systems on Earth. A Falcon Heavy launch draws up to 15 MW during pre-launch operations through transformers that share the same GIC vulnerability as any industrial facility — but with exposed outdoor cable runs that act as GIC antennas, cryogenic propellant systems that require continuous pump operation, and flight termination systems that cannot tolerate a millisecond of ambiguity.

**The Starlink Ground Station Dimension:** 100+ Starlink gateway ground stations are typically in rural locations with long unprotected power feeds — ideal GIC collection antennas. If ground stations lose power, the Starlink constellation is disconnected from the terrestrial internet, degrading service for 2+ million subscribers precisely when satellite connectivity is most needed.

**National Security Context:** SpaceX is the sole U.S. crew launch provider and the dominant NSSL provider. The nation depends on SpaceX launch infrastructure continuing to function — GMD event or not.

## 2. ACS SOLUTION

NBC installation at pad substations. GIC-rated power distribution. MXene-shielded outdoor cable trays. Dedicated GIC-hardened FTS power. Protonic backup communications between all launch sites and mission control. Starlink ground station hardening prioritized by geomagnetic latitude.
