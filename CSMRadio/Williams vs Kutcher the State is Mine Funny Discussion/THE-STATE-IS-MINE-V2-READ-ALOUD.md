# THE STATE IS MINE — READ-ALOUD EDITION (V2)

## A California Duel Between the Williams Heuristic and the El Segundo Heuristic
### In the Style of "The Girl Is Mine" by Michael Jackson & Paul McCartney
### SCREEN-READER FRIENDLY — Speakers Identified by Silent Punctuation Tokens
### Legend: ★ = Robin Williams (Williams Heuristic) | ● = Ashton Kutcher (El Segundo Heuristic)

---

**OPENING — BOTH, TRADING LINES OVER A G-FUNK BEAT**

★ Every night, she walks right in my dreams...

● Dude, what? Are you... are we singing now?

★ Since I met her, I'm so proud to say...

● Okay no, we're singing. Sweet. I'm in. *(clears throat)* ...and then?

★ The state is mine!

● Whoa. Whoa whoa whoa. Back up the truck. The state is *yours*?

★ The state is MINE! I don't understand the way you think, saying that she's yours not mine!

● Okay, first of all — that falsetto was *aggressive*. I felt that in my chest. Second of all — 

★ *sending roses...*

● — sending ROSES? To who? THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA? That's 163,696 square miles, man. That's a lot of roses. You know how much water that would take? We're in a drought, Robin. We're always in a drought.

★ Oh, OH! The drought! The drought expert! Mister "I-surf-the-Pacific" over here — tell me, do you even know what the Imperial Valley LOOKS like from 30,000 feet? I've DONE shows in Bakersfield, my friend! BAKERSFIELD! In AUGUST! *(Scottish accent)* Ya don't know heat 'til ya done stand-up in a room where the air conditioner is just a FAN with a DREAM, laddie!

---

● Okay okay okay. Bakersfield. Respect. But let me ask you something.

● Have you ever sat on the pier in Santa Monica at 5:47 AM, when the fog is still sitting on the water like a blanket that forgot to get up?

★ *(immediately switching to a Santa Monica fog voice)* Oooooh, look at me, I'm FOG, I'm MYSTERIOUS, I can't decide if I'm ocean or sky because I contain MULTITUDES!

● See? See. You can't just BE with the fog. You have to BE the fog. You have to perform the fog. The fog is not a stage, Robin. The fog is the fog.

---

★ *(switching — now a gravelly old prospector)* Well, WELL! Mister So-Cal over here thinks he OWNS the coastline! Son, have you ever been to the REDWOODS? I'm talking about trees so tall you have to lean BACK — *(cranks neck up)* — BACK — BACK — and you STILL can't see the top! Trees that were ALIVE when Julius Caesar was having a BAD DAY in the Senate! "Et tu, Brute?" — "No, but et tu, REDWOOD?" Those trees were already 900 years old!

● Dude. The redwoods. Okay. That's fair. That is a fair point. One of those trees is called Hyperion, right? 380 feet. That's taller than the Statue of Liberty standing on the Statue of Liberty. I know this because I read a plaque once.

★ YOU READ A PLAQUE ONCE! Ladies and gentlemen, the El Segundo Heuristic has READ A PLAQUE! *(to imaginary audience)* Give him a hand! No really — *(claps theatrically)* — READ-ING! It's what separates us from the DOLPHINS!

---

● Alright. Plaque guy. Yeah. That's me. But let me tell you something about plaques, since we're doing that now.

● You ever been to the Salton Sea? Not the Instagram Salton Sea. The real one. The one where the fish used to be, and then there were no fish, and now it's just... bones. Fish bones. Miles of them. And the air smells like sulfur and history. And there's an old sign that says "SALTON CITY — POPULATION 15,000" but the actual population is like, 200, and half of them are artists who went there on purpose.

★ *(switching — now a Salton Sea artist)* I went there on PURPOSE! I make sculpture out of dead TILAPIA! My piece is called "THE GHOST OF PROGRESS" and it sells for zero dollars but I have COMPLETE ARTISTIC FREEDOM!

● Yes! Exactly! THAT is my California, man. The one where you can just... go somewhere and be weird and no one stops you because everyone else is also being weird. That's not in a guidebook. You can't rose-petal that. That's just — that's just what happens when you let people cook for long enough.

---

★ *(dropping the character, suddenly sincere)* Okay. Can we be real for a second? Just — just one second.

● Always, man. Always.

★ I drove up Highway 1 at sunset once. San Francisco to — well, to wherever you stop because you're crying and you don't know why. And I pulled over at this little turnout, no sign, just gravel and a guardrail. And the ocean was... it was doing that thing where the sun hits it at exactly the right angle and it turns into — I don't know — liquid copper? Liquid gold? Some metal that doesn't have a name because poets haven't gotten to it yet. And I stood there and I thought... *this entire coastline exists independently of every joke I've ever told.* It doesn't need me. It's just — HERE. It's been here. And I'm the one who's been missing it while I was busy being loud.

● *(long pause)* Dude.

● That's the whole thing. That's literally the whole thing right there.

● You don't own it. I don't own it. It just... is. And we're lucky enough to be standing on it while it's happening.

---

★ BUT HAVING SAID THAT —

● Oh here we go.

★ — I HAVE spent more time in TRAFFIC on the 405, voluntarily, than most people spend with their EXTENDED FAMILY! And that is not a joke! I have had three Thanksgivings on the 405! THREE! I ate a TURKEY SANDWICH in the Diamond Lane! The car next to me was playing football on a TABLET! We passed a BALL back and forth through the WINDOWS! Touchdown called back because we were both technically out of bounds but the SPIRIT of the play was MAGNIFICENT!

● Okay but — and I mean this — that's the same thing! You're not arguing against me! You just described a community! A community that formed spontaneously at 12 miles per hour between the 10 interchange and the Getty! That's beautiful! That's California! Nobody's road-raging, they're passing a FOOTBALL!

★ *(Scottish golfer voice)* Aye, but unlike SOME people, I actually stayed on the 405 long enough to see the EXIT signs! Do you know how many El Segundo exits there are? TWO! And you named an ENTIRE HEURISTIC after the SECOND one! That's like calling yourself "The West Covina Paradigm"!

● *(genuinely laughing)* Okay the West Covina Paradigm is actually a really good name.

★ WRITE THAT DOWN! That's FREE! I'm giving you West Covina Paradigm for FREE because I am a GENEROUS California spirit!

---

● Alright. Let me tell you about Death Valley.

★ *(immediately)* DEATH VALLEY! Finally! A place that SOUNDS like my last DIVORCE! *(Mrs. Doubtfire voice)* Oh yes, dear, the DEATH part was the giveaway!

● Stop. Wait. Hold on. Mrs. Doubtfire in Death Valley is a crossover I was not ready for.

★ *(Mrs. Doubtfire voice, now melting)* It's a HOT FLUSH, dearie! Just a HOT FLUSH! Help me find some SHADE before I turn into a SCONE!

● Okay you're doing the thing again. Come back. Come back to me. Death Valley. Lowest point in North America. 282 feet below sea level. Badwater Basin. You stand there and you look up at the cliff and there's a little sign way up high that says "SEA LEVEL" and you're like — oh. I am literally below the ocean right now. I am in the basement of the continent.

★ *(back to real voice)* I've been there. It's... it's quiet in a way that isn't peaceful. It's quiet like a held breath. Like the entire earth is waiting for something and it's been waiting for ten thousand years and it's going to keep waiting whether you show up or not.

● Yeah. Yeah, that's exactly it. And in that silence, you can hear your own heartbeat. And you realize — you're the only living thing making sound for miles in any direction. And that's not scary. That's just — true. That's just a true thing about where you are.

---

★ OKAY! OKAY! Emotional nadir ACHIEVED! We're in the basement of the continent and I'm Mrs. Doubtfire and we're both crying! BUT LET ME TELL YOU ABOUT LAKE TAHOE!

● Oh no.

★ OH YES! Lake Tahoe! 1,645 feet DEEP! So clear you can see a dinner plate at 75 feet! Water so blue it looks like someone photoshopped the sky INTO a hole in the ground! And what do we DO with this natural wonder? We put CASINOS on one side of it! "You know what this pristine alpine lake needs? SLOT MACHINES!" *(slot machine sound effects)* DING DING DING — oh, I think I won a BEAR!

● *(cracking up)* The Nevada side! The Nevada side has the casinos! The California side has — 

★ — has people looking at the Nevada side going "Should we...?" and then their California friends going "NO. We have KAYAKS. Get in the KAYAK."

● EXACTLY. You get it! You completely get it and you're still fighting me!

★ BECAUSE THE FIGHT IS THE POINT! *(Mork voice)* Nanu Nanu, confrontation is LOVE!

---

● Okay well if we're doing high altitude now — 

★ *(immediately — as if climbing a mountain)* *gasp gasp* The OXYGEN is THIN! I can see my PAST LIVES!

● Mount Whitney. 14,505 feet. Tallest peak in the contiguous United States. You can stand on top of California, literally, and look east and see the lowest point in America — Death Valley — from the HIGHEST point. That's a 14,787-foot difference across like, 80 miles. You know what that's called?

★ A METAPHOR! That's what it's called! THE ENTIRE STATE IS A METAPHOR!

● Yes! From the highest high to the lowest low, and you can stand on one and see the other! THAT is California! That's not my California or your California! That's just... the topography of being human, expressed in granite and salt!

★ *(quiet)* Okay. That one. That one was really good.

● Thanks man.

★ *(still quiet)* No, I mean really good. You're... you're actually paying attention. Most people who "own" California have never been east of Palm Springs.

---

● Dude, I drove the 395 once. All the way from Mojave up to Susanville. And that road goes through like... five different planets. The desert, then the mountains, then the high desert, then more mountains, then suddenly it's forest and you're like "wait, are we in Oregon? Did I miss a sign?"

★ YOU WENT TO SUSANVILLE?! Nobody goes to Susanville! Susanville doesn't even know it's in California! It thinks it's a small, confused piece of Idaho that got lost during the gold rush and just... stayed!

● The people there were SO nice though! This one guy at a gas station — I asked for directions and he gave me a full ten-minute breakdown of every road within 50 miles AND offered me his sister's casserole! I didn't even know directions came with casserole!

★ THAT'S THE CENTRAL VALLEY! That's the thing nobody tells you! Everyone thinks California is Hollywood and Silicon Valley and beach bodies, but the REAL California — the SPINE of the state — is people who will give you directions AND a casserole AND ask about your mother AND mean it!

---

● Okay. One more. One more and then you can go.

● Joshua Tree. 2 AM. New moon. No clouds. You lie on your back on a rock that's been warm all day and is now slowly giving that warmth back to the universe. And you look up. And there's... so many stars. So many stars, man. You can see the Milky Way. Not a picture of it. Not a screensaver. The actual Milky Way. The galaxy edge-on. And you think about how every single one of those dots is a sun, and some of them have planets, and some of those planets might have oceans, and somewhere out there maybe some OTHER guy is lying on his back on a warm rock looking up at HIS sky and wondering if anyone's out there. And you're both right.

★ *(long pause)*

★ I proposed to my first wife under a sky like that. Different desert. New Mexico. But same sky. Same stars. Oh... okay. We're — we're going there now. We're going to the real place.

● Where else would we go? That's where California lives, man. It lives in the spaces between the cities. It lives in the dark. It lives in the quiet. That's where you go to find out what you actually think, when no one's watching and no one's listening and you're just... you.

---

★ You know what I think? I think maybe... maybe the state isn't mine. And maybe it isn't yours. Maybe — *(voice cracking just slightly)* — maybe we're both just... guests. Guests who got lucky. Guests who showed up at the right cosmic dinner party and got seated next to the ocean and the mountains and the desert and the sequoias and the fault lines and the fog and the tacos and the traffic and... all of it. All of it.

● Yeah. Yeah, that's it. That's the thing.

● *(long pause)* Hey Robin?

★ Yeah?

● You ever had a California burrito? The kind with the french fries inside?

★ HAVE I HAD A CALIFORNIA BURRITO?! I INVENTED the California burrito! I was putting french fries in tortillas when you were still a PUNK'D concept! *(back to normal)* Yes. Yes I have. They're magnificent. They make absolutely no culinary sense. Carbs inside carbs. And yet — PERFECTION.

● Sweet. Let's go get one.

★ *(Mork voice)* YOU'RE the California burrito, Nanu Nanu!

● ...I don't know what that means but I'm taking it as a compliment.

★ It was! It absolutely was! NOW LET'S EAT!

---

**CLOSING — BOTH, TRADING LINES OVER A FADING G-FUNK BEAT**

● The state is mine...

★ No, the state is MINE...

● Dude, I think maybe it's... just a state.

★ *(quiet)* Just a state where anything can happen.

● Just a state where everything already did.

★ Just a state that was here before us.

● And will be here after.

★ And in between...

● ...in between, we got to stand on it.

★ And that's enough.

● That's more than enough.

● ★ The state is ours.

★ But mostly mine.

● DUDE.
