# I PAID FOR THIS STATE (IN FINES)

## A California Fiscal Throwdown Between the Williams Heuristic and the El Segundo Heuristic
### In the Tradition of "The Girl Is Mine" — "The State Is Mine" Edition
### NAMED SPEAKER EDITION — V1

---

**OPENING — A COMPTROLLER'S OFFICE, SOMEWHERE IN SACRAMENTO. TWO MEN STAND AT THE COUNTER, EACH HOLDING A THICK STACK OF CITATIONS.**

**ROBIN:** *(slamming a folder onto the counter)* THE STATE IS MINE!

**ASHTON:** Whoa. Dude. You brought paperwork. You actually brought paperwork.

**ROBIN:** I brought RECEIPTS! The state is MINE because I've paid more in FINES than you've paid in RENT! *(riffling through pages)* Look at this! 1987 — speeding ticket on the 101! Forty-seven dollars! That's not a fine — that's an INVESTMENT! I was BUILDING HIGHWAYS with my ACCELERATOR!

**ASHTON:** *(pulling a crumpled receipt from his wallet)* 1987? Dude, I wasn't even born yet and I already got a parking ticket. *(squinting at receipt)* No wait — this is from In-N-Out. This is a Double-Double receipt.

**ROBIN:** *(snatching it, examining it)* Animal style? *(genuinely impressed)* Okay that's still a contribution to the state economy but it doesn't count toward FINES!

**ASHTON:** The state is mine though. Like, spiritually. Because of all the fines. So many fines.

---

**ROBIN:** *(BURST)* Let's GO THEN! Let's COMPARE! I'll show you fines! I have more fines than the Library of Congress has BOOKS! *(sings)* THE STATE IS MINE — the STATE IS MINE — I've been paying since '79!

**ASHTON:** Okay. Okay okay okay. *(pulls out a shoebox)* This is just from 2003 to 2005.

**ROBIN:** *(staring at the shoebox)* That's a SHOEBOX. You keep your FINES in a SHOEBOX?

**ASHTON:** The state is mine. *(shrugs)* I don't make the rules. Actually I probably broke the rules, that's why the fines are in the shoebox.

**ROBIN:** THAT'S A TERRIBLE FILING SYSTEM!

**ASHTON:** The state of California doesn't care how you file. They just want the check. The state is mine.

---

**ROBIN:** *(flipping through a citation)* Okay, first of all — 1992. Golden Gate Bridge. I did a bit where I was a seagull. Just — me, on the railing, being a SEAGULL. "MINE! MINE! MINE!" — you know, like in Finding Nemo, but I did it FIRST, the seagulls STOLE IT!

**ASHTON:** You got FINED for being a seagull?

**ROBIN:** PUBLIC DISTURBANCE! Fifty dollars! The seagull was LOUD! The seagull was COMMITTED to the bit! And you know what that fifty dollars BOUGHT? It bought this STATE! The state is MINE!

**ASHTON:** *(nodding slowly)* Sweet. Okay. My turn.

---

**ASHTON:** 2004. El Segundo. I parked on the wrong side of the street on street sweeping day. Not the FIRST Thursday. The THIRD Thursday. And then? I didn't move the car. And then? Sixty-eight dollar ticket. And then? I appealed it.

**ROBIN:** You APPEALED a street sweeping ticket?

**ASHTON:** I lost. And then? They added a twenty-five dollar processing fee for the appeal. So I paid ninety-three dollars for a sixty-eight dollar ticket. That's negative twenty-five dollars of justice. The state is mine.

**ROBIN:** *(staring at him in genuine awe)* That is the most California financial story I've ever heard. You LOST money filing an appeal against yourself.

**ASHTON:** The state is mine. *(beat)* And then?

---

**ROBIN:** *(ROAR)* 1985! I was on the set of MORK AND MINDY! And I did a scene where Mork was supposed to be "sleeping" — but I IMPROVISED! I made Mork sleepwalk through a WALL! A real wall! I went THROUGH the drywall! The studio billed me for the drywall! The STUDIO billed ME! That's not even a government fine — that's a PRIVATE fine, which is WORSE!

**ASHTON:** Wait — you paid to break a wall that you weren't supposed to break?

**ROBIN:** THE STATE IS MINE! I PAID FOR THE WALL! I PAID FOR THE REPAIR! I PAID FOR THE REPAINT! I paid for the COLOR MATCHING because they couldn't find the original paint code from 1978! I have a receipt from a PAINT STORE in BOULDER, COLORADO — that's not even the right STATE!

**ASHTON:** *(genuinely laughing)* Dude, Colorado's fine system doesn't even count toward this.

**ROBIN:** IT COUNTS IN MY HEART!

---

**ASHTON:** Okay. Okay. Pulling out the big one. 2011. I got a ticket for... *(checks shoebox, pulls out a citation)* ...feeding a parking meter that wasn't mine.

**ROBIN:** *(stopping dead)* Feeding. A parking meter. That wasn't yours.

**ASHTON:** There was this old lady, right? She was digging through her purse for quarters. And I had quarters. So I just — *(mimes dropping a quarter in)* — and then the parking enforcement guy was RIGHT there. And he gave ME a ticket. For "unauthorized meter feeding." That's a real thing. That's on the books. Section 22500, subdivision e, subsection 4. I looked it up.

**ROBIN:** *(WHISPER)* He LOOKED IT UP.

**ASHTON:** The state is mine. The fine was seventy-two dollars. I paid it. I didn't appeal this one.

**ROBIN:** *(still whispering)* You PAID seventy-two dollars for feeding a STRANGER'S meter...

**ASHTON:** And then? She came back. The old lady. She said "thank you." And she gave me a cookie.

**ROBIN:** A COOKIE! *(back to ROAR)* A SEVENTY-TWO DOLLAR COOKIE!

**ASHTON:** It was a really good cookie. The state is mine.

---

**ROBIN:** *(BURST — 400wpm)* OH! OH! You want to talk about ABSURD fines? I got a NOISE COMPLAINT in 1998 for LAUGHING too loud at a restaurant in Venice Beach! Not performing! Not doing stand-up! Not even TALKING to anyone else! I was sitting ALONE — reading a MENU — and I laughed at the DESCRIPTION of the CLAM CHOWDER! "A symphony of the sea!" That's what it said! A SYMPHONY OF THE SEA! And I laughed, and a guy at the next table called the MANAGER, and the manager called the POLICE, and the police wrote me a citation for DISTURBING THE PEACE!

**ASHTON:** *(wheezing)* You got a noise complaint for laughing at chowder...

**ROBIN:** THE STATE IS MINE! I paid a HUNDRED AND FIFTEEN DOLLARS for laughing at a SOUP DESCRIPTION! That's not a fine — that's a PHILOSOPHICAL STATEMENT! That's me saying "I believe in this state so much that I will pay for the right to find its menu items amusing!"

**ASHTON:** *(still laughing)* Dude... DUDE...

**ROBIN:** *(suddenly calm, HOVER 140wpm)* The state is mine. And the chowder was actually pretty good.

---

**ASHTON:** *(wiping tears)* Okay. Okay. I got one. 2016. Beach bonfire citation.

**ROBIN:** Bonfire? That's a CLASSIC! Everyone gets a bonfire citation! That's a California RITE OF PASSAGE!

**ASHTON:** No no no — hear me out. I wasn't AT the bonfire. I was watching the bonfire from my car. With binoculars.

**ROBIN:** *(long pause)* *(then, very quietly)* ...binoculars.

**ASHTON:** I was parked a quarter mile away. On a cliff. Looking at someone else's bonfire. And a ranger rolled up and said "you're aiding and abetting an unauthorized beach fire by providing surveillance." SURVEILLANCE. I was SURVEILLING a bonfire.

**ROBIN:** *(Mork voice)* You were a BONFIRE SPY! Nanu Nanu, the bonfire has been COMPROMISED!

**ASHTON:** The state is mine! I paid TWO HUNDRED DOLLARS for being a bonfire surveillance operative! That's a line item on my taxes now! "Bonfire reconnaissance — unreimbursed."

**ROBIN:** *(collapsing against the counter)* I CAN'T! I PHYSICALLY CANNOT! You got fined for LOOKING at a fire from TOO FAR AWAY!

**ASHTON:** The state is mine, man. The state is SO mine.

---

**ROBIN:** *(pulling himself together)* Alright. Alright. If we're doing RIDICULOUS fines — 2002. San Francisco. I got a ticket for... *(dramatic pause)* ...jaywalking. While dressed as a crossing guard.

**ASHTON:** No.

**ROBIN:** YES! I was in FULL crossing guard uniform! Whistle! Vest! Little STOP sign paddle! I had just come from a charity event where I was the celebrity crossing guard! And on the way back to my car, I crossed against the light — IN THE UNIFORM — and a real crossing guard, an ACTUAL crossing guard, wrote me a ticket!

**ASHTON:** The crossing guard wrote you a ticket for jaywalking while dressed as a crossing guard...

**ROBIN:** THE STATE IS MINE! I paid EIGHTY-SIX DOLLARS to be fined BY MY OWN COSTUME'S PEER GROUP! That's like getting pulled over by a police officer while you're wearing a police Halloween costume! It's a RECURSIVE CITATION! It's a CITATION within a CITATION!

**ASHTON:** Citation-ception. Sweet.

**ROBIN:** *(breaking character for a second)* Did you just — did you just "Citation-ception" me?

**ASHTON:** The state is mine. I copyrighted that word in my mind.

---

**ASHTON:** 2018. I got a ticket for an "unsecured load" on the 405.

**ROBIN:** Unsecured load! What were you hauling? Lumber? Mattresses? A TRAMPOLINE?

**ASHTON:** A single beach ball. It blew out of the back of my truck. I pulled over. I ran into traffic. I rescued the beach ball. And when I got back to my truck, there was a CHP officer writing me a ticket for "unsecured recreational inflatable device." That's the exact wording. Unsecured recreational inflatable device.

**ROBIN:** *(alternating between laughing and crying)* THE STATE IS MINE! No wait — THE STATE IS YOURS! I can't compete with "unsecured recreational inflatable device"! That's NOT EVEN A REAL CRIME!

**ASHTON:** It's Vehicle Code 23114, subdivision a. Transported load must be covered or secured. The beach ball was neither. The state is mine.

**ROBIN:** *(muttering in disbelief)* He knows the VEHICLE CODE now...

**ASHTON:** I looked it up after the bonfire thing. I started looking up ALL of them. It's kind of a hobby now. The state is mine.

---

**ROBIN:** *(grabbing the counter with both hands)* 1995. Burbank. I paid a fine for LEAVING A PERFORMANCE — no, leaving an AUDITION — by going out a WINDOW.

**ASHTON:** An audition window exit. Okay. Go on.

**ROBIN:** It wasn't my audition! I was there supporting a FRIEND! And he was terrible — just TERRIBLE — and I started laughing — and he saw me laughing through the glass — and he started crying — and I felt SO bad that I climbed out the bathroom window to avoid him! And the casting director saw me! Halfway out the window! One leg in, one leg out, like a VERY guilty flamingo!

**ASHTON:** And they fined you for...?

**ROBIN:** TRESPASSING! Because technically the bathroom window led to a ROOF, and the roof was PRIVATE PROPERTY, and I was on it WITHOUT PERMISSION! I paid a hundred and forty dollars to stand on a roof I didn't want to be on in the first place!

**ASHTON:** The state is yours. That's a strong entry. Strong.

**ROBIN:** THE STATE IS MINE! I paid for the roof I never wanted to see!

---

**ASHTON:** 2009. I got a noise complaint for playing a didgeridoo at 3 AM.

**ROBIN:** A didgeridoo?! At 3 AM?! *(dropping into an Australian accent)* G'DAY, it's the MIDDLE OF THE NIGHT, MATE!

**ASHTON:** It wasn't even MY didgeridoo. It was my neighbor's didgeridoo. He left it on my porch with a note that said "learn this." And I thought — okay, sure, the universe wants me to learn the didgeridoo at 3 AM.

**ROBIN:** *(staring blankly)* "Learn this." That was the whole note.

**ASHTON:** "Learn this." Two words. No signature. And then? I tried to learn it. And then? I was bad at it. And then? The police came. And then? The police ALSO tried the didgeridoo.

**ROBIN:** THE POLICE TRIED THE DIDGERIDOO?!

**ASHTON:** One of them was pretty good, actually. He got suspended from circular breathing. The state is mine — and somewhere in the LAPD there's an officer who can play a didgeridoo thanks to my fine.

**ROBIN:** *(face in hands)* You MENTORED law enforcement in ABORIGINAL INSTRUMENTS through your own CITATION...

**ASHTON:** The state is mine. *(beat)* And then?

---

**ROBIN:** *(lifting his head, eyes wild)* OKAY. 2006. Marin County. I got a jaywalking ticket for crossing the street while TALKING.

**ASHTON:** All jaywalking involves being in the street. That's what jaywalking IS.

**ROBIN:** No! I was on the SIDEWALK! I was yelli ng to a friend on the OTHER sidewalk, and my VOICE crossed the street! And the officer said my VOICE constituted "distracted pedestrian behavior" because I was "projecting across a traffic lane"!

**ASHTON:** *(cracking up)* Your voice jaywalked! Your VOICE got a ticket!

**ROBIN:** THE STATE IS MINE! Fifty-five dollars for ACOUSTIC JAYWALKING! My vocal cords have a CRIMINAL RECORD! If I clear my throat in Palo Alto I have to register with the county!

**ASHTON:** Dude. Acoustic jaywalking. That's... that's art. That's performance art that you paid the state to perform.

**ROBIN:** *(with deep satisfaction)* The state is mine. And my voice is a known traffic hazard.

---

**ASHTON:** 2013. Malibu. I got fined for "misuse of a public beach shower."

**ROBIN:** *(immediately suspicious)* ...what were you doing in the beach shower?

**ASHTON:** I was washing a burrito.

**ROBIN:** *(long, long pause)*

**ASHTON:** It fell in the sand. It was a really good burrito. Carnitas. Guac. The whole thing. And I thought — I can save this. I was WRONG. But I tried. And a lifeguard saw me. And now there's a rule. At that specific beach. About burritos in the shower. I'm the reason for that rule.

**ROBIN:** *(voice cracking)* You are the PATRON SAINT of BAD DECISIONS that become CALIFORNIA STATE ORDINANCES!

**ASHTON:** The state is mine. I paid seventy-five dollars to try to save a burrito. I'd do it again.

**ROBIN:** *(Mork voice)* Mork calling Orson — the burrito is dead, but the STATE survives!

---

**ROBIN:** *(BURST)* 2019! Library fine! San Francisco Public Library! I checked out "The Grapes of Wrath" in 1983 and returned it in 2019!

**ASHTON:** That's... thirty-six years. Did you read it?

**ROBIN:** I read it SEVEN TIMES! I MEMORIZED it! I can do the whole book as a ONE-MAN SHOW! *(launching into Tom Joad)* "I'll be there... I'll be there in the way the guys yell when they're mad..." *(breaking off)* The library wanted FOUR HUNDRED DOLLARS!

**ASHTON:** Four hundred dollars for a book you could buy for twelve?

**ROBIN:** IT WAS THE PRINCIPLE! I paid it! I wrote the check right there at the counter! The librarian CRIED! She said no one had ever paid a thirty-six year late fee before! She put my check in a FRAME! It's on the WALL of the San Francisco Public Library! THE STATE IS MINE! My late fee is INSTALLATION ART!

**ASHTON:** *(slow nodding)* Okay. That's... that's a good one. That might be the best one.

**ROBIN:** *(bowing)* Thank you. The state is mine. I have LITERALLY funded the library system for DECADES.

---

**ASHTON:** 2020. I got a ticket for "operating a personal watercraft without a license" on Lake Tahoe.

**ROBIN:** Jet ski? You got a JET SKI ticket?

**ASHTON:** No. Inflatable flamingo. The big kind. The one with the cup holders. I was paddling it with my hands. And someone called the Coast Guard Auxiliary because they thought I was a "navigation hazard."

**ROBIN:** *(Mrs. Doubtfire voice)* Oh DAERIE, a PINK BIRD in the WATER! Sound the ALARM!

**ASHTON:** The Coast Guard Auxiliary showed up. In a real boat. They boarded my flamingo. They tried to write me a ticket but they didn't have a code for "unlicensed flamingo operation." So they wrote me a ticket for "unlicensed personal watercraft" and then they made a NEW code. For flamingos. I'm the flamingo code guy. At Lake Tahoe. Forever.

**ROBIN:** *(roaring)* THE STATE IS MINE! No wait — THE STATE IS YOURS! NO ONE has ever been the INFLATABLE FLAMINGO PIONEER of the U.S. COAST GUARD AUXILIARY!

**ASHTON:** The state is mine. There's a checkbox now. On a form. Because of me.

---

**ROBIN:** *(WHISPER — 60wpm)* Can I tell you something? Something I've never told anyone?

**ASHTON:** *(quieting)* Always, man.

**ROBIN:** In 1997, I got pulled over for speeding on the PCH. Big Sur. The most beautiful road in the world. And the officer walked up to my window, and I was in FULL makeup — I was filming "Flubber" at the time, and I looked INSANE — and he said "Do you know how fast you were going?" And I said *(tearing up a little)* "I was going exactly the speed of someone who just realized the ocean is RIGHT THERE and it's been there the WHOLE TIME and I've been in a SOUNDSTAGE for THREE MONTHS." And he didn't write me a ticket. He just said "slow down, Robin." And he knew my NAME. And I drove away at 35 miles an hour and I cried for twenty miles because the state KNEW me. California KNEW me. And it let me go. Just that once.

**ASHTON:** *(long silence)* Dude.

**ASHTON:** That's not a fine. That's... grace.

**ROBIN:** *(nodding)* The state is mine. Not because I paid for it with money. Because I paid for it with... everything. With every joke. Every mile. Every bad decision that became a good story. The state is mine because I showed up. Over and over. Even when I messed up. ESPECIALLY when I messed up.

---

**ASHTON:** *(quiet)* You know what the fines actually are?

**ROBIN:** What?

**ASHTON:** They're receipts. That's it. They're receipts that say "you were here. You did a thing. The thing was technically not allowed. But you were HERE while you did it." Every fine I've ever paid — that's just California signing a piece of paper that says I EXISTED. On a Thursday. In El Segundo. Next to a beach ball that got away. And the state is mine because I have the paper to prove I was there.

**ROBIN:** *(HOVER)* ...that's beautiful, man.

**ASHTON:** Thanks. I thought of it while I was paying the flamingo ticket. You get a lot of thinking done in the Tahoe municipal courthouse.

**ROBIN:** *(snapping back)* THE STATE IS MINE! ...I mean, OURS! The state is — look, the state is whoever SHOWS UP and PAYS!

**ASHTON:** The state is mine. And also yours. But mostly mine because I have the flamingo citation and you don't.

**ROBIN:** I HAVE ACOUSTIC JAYWALKING!

**ASHTON:** Flamingo navigation hazard beats acoustic jaywalking.

**ROBIN:** I PAID FOR A ROOF I NEVER WANTED TO BE ON!

**ASHTON:** I created a new DMV subcategory for inflatable birds! THE STATE IS MINE!

---

**ROBIN:** *(ROAR — 300wpm+)* 1993! I got a TICKET for an illegal U-turn on Sunset Boulevard while dressed as MRS. DOUBTFIRE! In FULL costume! Prosthetics! Wig! Dress! The officer pulled me over and said "Ma'am, you can't make a U-turn here" and I said *(Doubtfire voice)* "Oh, I'm so sorry, Officer, I was just trying to get to the GROCERY, the sale on MINCE PIE ends at FOUR!" AND HE LET ME GO! Because Mrs. Doubtfire is a RESPECTABLE WOMAN who makes an HONEST MISTAKE! But I STILL got the ticket in the MAIL! Because the CAMERA got my LICENSE PLATE! The camera didn't care about Mrs. Doubtfire! Mrs. Doubtfire means NOTHING to the DMV!

**ASHTON:** *(on the floor now)* The camera doesn't recognize Academy Award-winning prosthetics...

**ROBIN:** THE STATE IS MINE! Mrs. Doubtfire has a TRAFFIC VIOLATION! On her PERMANENT RECORD! Which is also MY permanent record! She RUINED my driving abstract! A fictional Scottish nanny has POINTS on my LICENSE!

**ASHTON:** *(gasping from the floor)* Dude — DUDE — I can't — 

**ROBIN:** *(switching to Doubtfire)* A DRIVE well, dearie! Heeeeeere's your FINE!

---

**ASHTON:** *(pulling himself up, wiping tears)* Okay. Okay. Last one. 2022. I got a ticket for "exceeding the posted noise limit" with a musical instrument.

**ROBIN:** The didgeridoo again?!

**ASHTON:** No. Kazoo. At a protest. I wasn't even AT the protest. I was walking past the protest. And someone handed me a kazoo. And I just... *(mimes playing a kazoo)* ...joined in. For like, four seconds. And a noise monitor — they had a DECIBEL METER, Robin — they measured my kazoo and it was "point seven decibels over the amplified sound permit threshold."

**ROBIN:** Point seven. You got cited for POINT SEVEN decibels.

**ASHTON:** I asked the officer if she knew how quiet point seven decibels was. She said "it's the principle." I said "the principle of what?" She said "acoustics." And I paid it. Two hundred and ten dollars. For point seven decibels of kazoo.

**ROBIN:** *(utterly defeated and deeply admiring)* The state is yours. I yield. I yield the state. The kazoo decibel conviction is the greatest fine in California history.

**ASHTON:** The state is mine.

**ROBIN:** *(quietly)* The state is yours.

**ASHTON:** *(pause)* ...and then?

**ROBIN:** *(quietly)* And then we both paid more fines than any two people should ever pay.

**ASHTON:** And then we both got stories that no one else has.

**ROBIN:** And then we both stood on this ground, and messed up on this ground, and paid for the privilege of messing up on this ground.

**ASHTON:** And then the state... *(looks up)* ...the state isn't really mine.

**ROBIN:** And it isn't really mine.

**ASHTON:** The state is just the place where we happened.

**ROBIN:** The state is the receipt for a life lived.
