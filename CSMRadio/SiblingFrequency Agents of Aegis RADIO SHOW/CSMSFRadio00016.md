Status report. DRAVEN here. Haiku Tank. The communications preparation is simpler and cheaper than most people assume. You need three things. Thing one: a battery-powered AM/FM radio. During a Carrington event, AM radio may be the only broadcast medium still functioning — long-wave AM signals propagate via ground wave, not ionospheric skip, and many AM stations have backup generators and are designated emergency broadcast outlets. An analog AM radio costs fifteen to thirty dollars. Buy one. Put batteries in it. Store extra batteries. Thing two: a handheld ham radio.
