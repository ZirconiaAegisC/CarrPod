### 2.1 Curie Temperature Physical Basis

The Curie Temperature (T_C) is the temperature above which a ferromagnetic material loses spontaneous magnetization and becomes paramagnetic. This transition point is fundamentally linked to:
- The material's magnetic permeability (μ_r → 1 above T_C)
- The material's electrical behavior (anomalous resistance increase at T_C)
- The thermal destruction or "death" of the material's magnetic properties

**Derivation of Color from Curie Temperature:**

The thermal vibration frequency (kT/h) at T_C:

$$\nu_{thermal} = \frac{k_B T_C}{h}$$

Where k_B = 1.38065×10⁻²³ J/K (Boltzmann constant) and h = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ J·s (Planck constant).

For Iron (T_C = 1043 K):
$$\nu_{thermal} = \frac{1.38065 \times 10^{-23} \times 1043}{6.626 \times 10^{-34}} = 2.17 \times 10^{13}\text{ Hz} = 21.7\text{ THz}$$

This thermal frequency lies in the infrared range (14 μm). To bring it to the visible range (400-700 THz), we apply **octave scaling** — doubling the frequency 14 times:

$$\nu_{visible} = 21.7\text{ THz} \times 2^{14} = 355\text{ PHz} → 845\text{ nm (NIR)}$$
