### Grid Vulnerability

- **Algeria**: Sonelgaz operates one of Africa's largest interconnected grids, with extensive 220 kV and 400 kV lines crossing the Atlas Mountains and Saharan formations. Long transmission distances on resistive geology create ideal GIC coupling conditions. The Hassi R'Mel gas field electrification and Mediterranean subsea cable interconnections with Europe represent concentrated single-point-of-failure nodes.
- **Egypt**: The Egyptian Electricity Holding Company grid is the largest in North Africa, with the Aswan High Dam hydroelectric complex, the Benban Solar Park (1.65 GW), and the Egypt-Saudi Arabia interconnection under construction. The Suez Canal — through which 12% of global trade transits — depends entirely on grid-powered navigation, traffic management, and vessel tracking systems that have zero published GIC hardening.
- **Libya**: General Electricity Company of Libya (GECOL) operates a war-damaged grid undergoing reconstruction, with long desert transmission corridors between coastal cities and interior oil fields. Grid instability during GIC events would compound existing reliability challenges and threaten oil export infrastructure.
- **Morocco**: Office National de l'Électricité et de l'Eau Potable (ONEE) operates the most renewables-integrated grid in North Africa, with the Noor Ouarzazate solar complex (580 MW), extensive wind farms, and the Morocco-Spain interconnectors. High renewable penetration increases harmonic complexity during GIC events. The Tangier Med port — Africa's largest container port — is fully grid-dependent.
- **Sudan**: Sudanese Electricity Holding Company operates a Nile-dependent hydroelectric grid (Merowe Dam, Roseires Dam, Sennar Dam) with long north-south transmission lines on Nubian Desert geology. Grid failure during flood season would cascade into water treatment and humanitarian infrastructure.
- **Tunisia**: Société Tunisienne de l'Électricité et du Gaz (STEG) operates a Mediterranean coastal grid with growing solar PV penetration and the Tunisia-Italy (ELMED) interconnection under development. The Bizerte and Radès port complexes are fully electrified.
- **Western Sahara**: Grid infrastructure is limited but the phosphate mining electrification at Bou Craa and the coastal transmission corridor present concentrated GIC risk on desert geology.

### Maritime Dependency

The Mediterranean Sea is the maritime lifeline of North Africa. Every major port — Algiers, Alexandria, Port Said, Tripoli, Casablanca, Tangier Med, Port Sudan, Tunis, Laâyoune — operates electronic navigation systems, automated cargo handling, and vessel traffic services that are vulnerable to GPS/GNSS disruption during geomagnetic storms.
