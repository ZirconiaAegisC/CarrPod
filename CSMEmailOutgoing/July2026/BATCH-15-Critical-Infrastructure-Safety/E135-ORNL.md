**4. SHIELD Posture**

Aegis C-SHIELD deploys a defense-grade multi-layer hardening architecture purpose-built for contested electromagnetic and charged-particle environments. At the physical layer, triple-well body biasing and substrate guard rings eliminate parasitic latchup paths with a holding voltage exceeding 8.5V. At the materials layer, our MXene-reinforced silicon carbide composite — characterized through neutron diffraction, inelastic neutron scattering, and small-angle neutron scattering at ORNL's SNS and HFIR — delivers dielectric strength exceeding 45 kV/mm at 2,000°C with no phase transformation or outgassing detected to 10⁻⁸ Torr. At the logical layer, our proprietary Dual-Interlocked-Storage-Cell (DICE) latch design achieves a single-event upset cross-section below 1×10⁻¹⁰ cm²/bit at LET of 40 MeV·cm²/mg with zero multi-cell upset propagation across word-line or bit-line neighbors. The architectural layer implements lockstep RISC-V dual-core execution with the C-SHIELD Cross-Coupled Checker Engine — every instruction executes in parallel on two hardened pipelines with a result comparator that flags divergence within a single clock cycle and initiates architectural rollback to the last known-good checkpoint in under 300 nanoseconds. At the system layer, a hardened supervisory microcontroller monitors all voltage rails, clock sources, and thermal sensors with independent watchdog timers that assert failsafe reset if any monitored parameter deviates from its triple-voted reference window. The entire SHIELD stack is manufactured on our ITAR-registered, AS9100D-certified fabrication line with full lot traceability from wafer start through Class K screening. Every C-SHIELD device ships with a Certificate of Conformance documenting measured TID, SEE, ELDRS, and ceramic substrate performance for the specific diffusion lot — not a datasheet typical, but the actual test results for the devices in your supply chain.

**9. ORNL-Specific Personalization**

Oak Ridge National Laboratory occupies a singular position in the American research ecosystem: it is simultaneously the nation's largest multiprogram science and energy laboratory, the home of the world's fastest supercomputer, and the institutional keeper of a materials science legacy stretching from the Manhattan Project to the Exascale Computing Project. The partnership Aegis proposes leverages three ORNL capabilities that no other institution can replicate in combination. First, the Spallation Neutron Source and High Flux Isotope Reactor provide neutron-based characterization — diffraction, spectroscopy, and imaging — that resolves the unit-cell-level strain state, phase distribution, and defect population of MXene-SiC composites during simulated operational thermal cycling from cryogenic to 2,200°C. No X-ray or electron-beam technique can deliver the bulk-averaged, through-thickness, statistically representative data that neutron scattering provides for ceramic matrix composites. Second, the Center for Nanophase Materials Sciences offers atomic-resolution STEM with monochromated EELS and 4D-STEM strain mapping that complements the neutron data, providing the multi-scale materials characterization pyramid from atom probe tomography through synchrotron X-ray computed tomography. Third, the Manufacturing Demonstration Facility can transition our laboratory-scale plasma spray process — currently producing 150mm × 150mm coupons — to production-scale capability supporting 300mm wafer-scale and larger substrates, with in-line process monitoring, robotic handling, and statistical process control infrastructure already in place from ORNL's additive manufacturing programs. We respectfully request the Technology Transfer Office initiate a Strategic Partnership Project (SPP) or CRADA framework for this multi-year materials validation and manufacturing scale-up program, with joint publication rights and a clear intellectual property demarcation preserving Aegis's background IP in MXene-SiC formulation while assigning jointly developed process IP under mutually agreeable terms that enable rapid commercial deployment.
