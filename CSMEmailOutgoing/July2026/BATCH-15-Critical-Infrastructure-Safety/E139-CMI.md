**9. CMI-Specific Personalization**

The Critical Materials Institute, anchored at Ames Laboratory and spanning four national laboratories, seven universities, and over thirty industry partners, has been the institutional engine of America's response to rare-earth supply chain vulnerability since its founding in 2013. CMI's achievements are genuinely remarkable: rare-earth-free magnets that match neodymium-iron-boron performance without a gram of dysprosium; ionic liquid extraction processes that recover neodymium from recycled hard disk drives with 95% efficiency; and the Critical Materials Assessment methodology that now underpins DOE, DOD, and Department of Commerce criticality determinations. What CMI has not yet addressed — and what Aegis proposes as a focused collaborative project — is the critical mineral dependency of high-voltage, high-temperature capacitor technology for grid resilience and defense electronics applications. The global tantalum capacitor market is approximately $2.8 billion, with 85% of tantalum ore originating from the DRC and Rwanda, processed through China into capacitor-grade powder. The niobium capacitor market adds another $1.2 billion, with Brazil's CBMM controlling 80% of global niobium supply. The MXene capacitor technology developed at Aegis substitutes domestically sourced titanium, aluminum, and carbon into a 2D transition metal carbide structure that delivers superior dielectric performance at one-third the materials cost and zero the geopolitical supply risk. We propose a CMI collaborative project — structured through Ames Laboratory's Technology Transfer framework — to conduct an independent, peer-reviewed techno-economic assessment of MXene capacitor technology as a critical materials displacement pathway. The project would leverage CMI's Materials Genome Initiative computational infrastructure for MXene property prediction, Ames Lab's electron microscopy and atom probe tomography capabilities for MXene nanostructure characterization, and CMI's industry-standard supply-chain criticality modeling. The deliverable: a joint CMI-Aegis technical publication quantifying the tonnage of tantalum, niobium, and rare-earth elements displaceable from the American capacitor supply chain through MXene substitution — data that directly supports DPA Title III, IRA 48C, and DOE Loan Programs Office investment decisions.
