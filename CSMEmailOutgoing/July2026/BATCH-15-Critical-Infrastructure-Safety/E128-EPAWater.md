First, it is geographically indiscriminate — a major GMD event affects entire regions simultaneously, meaning that mutual aid agreements between water utilities, which are the backbone of emergency response for localized disasters, may not be available because neighboring systems are affected by the same event. Second, it targets long-conductor electrical infrastructure — transmission lines, transformers, and the motors and drives that power treatment and pumping operations — which are precisely the components that water systems depend on and that have the longest replacement lead times.
