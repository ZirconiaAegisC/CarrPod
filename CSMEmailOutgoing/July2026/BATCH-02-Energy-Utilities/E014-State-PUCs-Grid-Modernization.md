State PUCs determine whether utility capital investments are prudent and recoverable. GMD hardening falls into a regulatory gap: recognized risk, limited precedent for cost recovery. Carrington Storm Motors provides the engineering data PUCs need.

KEY FACTS:
1. Avoided cost analysis: for a typical mid-sized utility service territory, the avoided restoration cost from a G5-equivalent event exceeds the hardening investment by a factor of 3-6x, depending on transformer fleet age and GIC vulnerability.
2. Federal cost-sharing eligibility: FEMA BRIC (Building Resilient Infrastructure and Communities) grants and DOE grid resilience programs may offset hardening costs — reducing the rate-base impact.
3. NERC compliance support: TPL-007-4 requires GMD vulnerability assessment. Utilities that implement physical hardening demonstrate compliance beyond the planning-only requirement.
4. Ratepayer protection: the 99.3% protection gap means nearly all GMD losses fall on ratepayers through extended outages rrather than insured recovery. Hardening reduces that exposure.
