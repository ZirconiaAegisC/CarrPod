**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE DISCOVERY-TO-COMMERCIALIZATION PIPELINE**

The 2011 discovery of Ti₃C₂Tₓ MXene — published by Naguib, Kurtoglu, Presser, Lu, Niu, Heon, Hultman, Gogotsi, and Barsoum in Advanced Materials — demonstrated that selective etching of the aluminum layer from the Ti₃AlC₂ MAX phase produces a 2D material with metallic conductivity (2,400 S/cm), hydrophilicity (water contact angle 35°), and a surface chemistry that can be tuned via the etchant and delamination process. Fifteen years later, ACS has translated that discovery into 46 commercial products: EMI shielding coatings (92 dB at 45 μm), thermal interface materials (45 W/m·K through-plane), piezoelectric electrode inks (10⁹ cycle fatigue life), transparent conductive coatings (42 dB SE at 88% visible transmission), and MXene-polymer composites for structural, thermal, and electromagnetic applications across consumer electronics, defense, aerospace, infrastructure, and biomedical markets. The Drexel-ACS partnership formalizes this pipeline: Drexel discovers new MXene compositions (out-of-plane ordered double-transition-metal MXenes, high-entropy MXenes, nitrogen-based MXenes); ACS characterizes them for commercial applicability; Drexel PhD students rotate through ACS's California facility for industry experience; ACS engineers guest-lecture at Drexel and co-advise PhD students. The result: a direct pathway from MXene discovery at Drexel to MXene product at ACS, with Drexel retaining IP ownership of the discovery (licensed to ACS on pre-negotiated terms) and ACS providing the commercialization engine.
