**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE SATELLITE GIC SURVIVAL PROBLEM**

Geostationary orbit (GEO, 35,786 km altitude) is the harshest electromagnetic environment in the Earth system. During a G5 geomagnetic storm, the GEO satellite fleet — approximately 550 active satellites — experiences: (a) surface charging to -20 kV on the shadowed side and +2 kV on the sunlit side (differential charging that can cause electrostatic discharge arcs between satellite components); (b) internal dielectric charging from high-energy electrons (1–10 MeV) that penetrate the spacecraft skin and accumulate in ungrounded dielectric materials, eventually discharging with energy densities up to 10 J/g (sufficient to vaporize PCB traces and destroy integrated circuits); and (c) geomagnetically induced currents in the satellite's conductive structure — the aluminum honeycomb panels, the copper power bus, the silver-coated RF waveguides — that can reach 10–50A during a G5 event, saturating the satellite's power system magnetics and causing the battery charge controller to enter an uncontrolled state. The SOLARIS power satellites — each a square-kilometer-scale structure of solar panels, microwave transmitters, and power management electronics — are especially vulnerable because their physical size (1 km × 1 km) means the induced voltage across the satellite structure during a G5 event can reach 20 kV (20 V/km × 1,000 km effective conductor length in the satellite's aluminum frame). The MXene satellite bus shielding approach replaces the aluminum structural panels with BFRP panels (fully dielectric — zero GIC), the aluminum honeycomb core with MXene-coated Nomex honeycomb (the MXene coating provides 92 dB SE for the internal electronics cavities while the Nomex substrate remains dielectric — the honeycomb acts as a waveguide-below-cutoff array that attenuates EMP by an additional 40 dB above the MXene coating's 92 dB), and the silver-coated RF waveguides with MXene-coated BFRP waveguides (the MXene coating's 2.4 Ω/sq sheet resistance is 6× higher than silver at 0.4 Ω/sq — a 1.5 dB increase in insertion loss at 5.8 GHz, the SOLARIS microwave transmission frequency — but the MXene waveguide conducts zero GIC, while the silver waveguide would carry 180A during a G5 event, melting the waveguide and destroying the microwave transmission system).
