**[PARAGRAPH 5 — KEY FACT 2: Solar Charge Controller Hardening]**

Overland vehicles increasingly depend on roof-mounted solar panels and MPPT charge controllers to maintain auxiliary battery banks that power refrigerators, communications equipment, lighting, and medical devices. The solar charge controller is a microcontroller-based device connected directly to an external antenna — the solar panel — that is optimized for surface area. During an EMP event, the solar panel couples significant electromagnetic energy directly into the charge controller's input stage. A failed charge controller means no auxiliary power — which means no refrigeration for insulin, no communications, no lighting, no way to recharge the devices that the overlander depends on. Aegis-C shielding installed around the charge controller enclosure, combined with MXene conformal coating on the charge controller circuit board, provides the protection layer that keeps the auxiliary power system operational. The basalt-fiber solar panel mounting frame — replacing aluminum — eliminates the coupling pathway from the panel frame to the vehicle chassis.

**[PARAGRAPH 7 — KEY FACT 4: Basalt-Fiber Underbody Protection]**

Overlanders invest heavily in underbody protection — steel and aluminum skid plates, rock sliders, differential covers — to protect mechanical components from trail damage.
