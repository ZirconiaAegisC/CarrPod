**[PARAGRAPH 2 — BAKER STREET ADJUSTER: EMAC architecture, precise and operational]**

The Emergency Management Assistance Compact enables governors to request and receive resources from other states during declared emergencies. The compact's operational architecture relies on: (1) EMAC Advance Teams that deploy to the affected state to assess needs and coordinate resource delivery. (2) Resource typing standards that ensure deployed assets are interoperable across state lines. (3) State EMAC coordinators who manage the request and deployment process. (4) Pre-positioned assets at state depots that can be deployed rapidly when requests are received. A Carrington-class CME introduces a specific challenge to each of these elements: advance teams cannot deploy if roads and airports are affected; resource typing standards do not include space-weather-specific asset categories; EMAC coordinators in every state will be simultaneously overwhelmed; and pre-positioned assets that depend on grid power and cellular communications will be degraded by the same event they are supposed to respond to. Carrington Storm Motors' product suite addresses each of these challenges directly: non-conductive shelter containers provide deployable, hardened command posts that do not conduct GIC. LoRa mesh networks provide off-grid communications that do not depend on cell towers or the ionosphere. Ascension drone backpacks provide aerial situational awareness when ground reconnaissance is impossible. Each product is designed for EMAC resource typing compatibility. Each is deployable by standard transport. Each is pre-positionable at state EMAC depots.
