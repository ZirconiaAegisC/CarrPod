**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE COMMUNITY-ARCHITECTURE GAP**

Plant Prefab manufactures individual homes. The homes are sustainable, factory-built, and delivered with remarkable efficiency — but they arrive on a site one at a time, connected to whatever infrastructure (or lack of infrastructure) exists at that site. A Lonsdaleite Outskirt Community is different: it is a pre-designed, pre-engineered, hexagonal-grid community of 15 structure types that works backward from the community level — the roads, the power, the water, the communications, the food production — to the individual dwelling. The hexagonal grid provides natural clustering, efficient road-to-dwelling ratios, and inherent defensible space for wildfire protection. The 15 structure types — from the 18m² Micro-Haven studio to the 144m² Clan-Hold family compound — share a common BFRP panel construction system, a common Grid-Seed solar power backbone, and a common Dew-Catcher atmospheric water network. A Lonsdaleite Outskirt Community of 50 dwellings can be delivered as 700 flat-packed pallets, assembled in 90 days by a crew of 20 with no heavy equipment, and operated entirely off-grid indefinitely.

Baker Street, let's get precise.
