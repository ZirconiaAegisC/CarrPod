**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE FOOD PRESERVATION PROBLEM**

WFP moves 4.4 million metric tons of food annually. Up to 30% of that food — particularly fresh produce, dairy, and protein — spoils before reaching beneficiaries due to the absence of cold chain infrastructure in the last-mile distribution environment. In refugee camps like Kakuma, Rohingya camps in Cox's Bazar, and IDP settlements in South Sudan, fresh food often arrives spoiled or is distributed immediately without any buffering capacity, forcing families to consume all perishables within 24–48 hours or lose them. The Cold-Root containerized food preservation system — a 200L capacity, off-grid, solar-powered refrigeration and controlled-atmosphere storage unit — extends produce life to 2 weeks without grid power, providing exactly the buffering capacity that WFP's last-mile cold chain currently lacks.

**PARAGRAPH 3 — BAKER STREET PRECISE SPECS**

Baker Street, let's get precise. The Cold-Root is a Testa Fleet Tier 5 container variant with 200L internal storage volume, maintaining 2–8°C via a dual-mode cooling system: a 400W thermoelectric cooling array powered by integrated 1.2 kWh LiFePO₄ battery and 600W folding photovoltaic panel, with a passive backup phase-change material (PCM) thermal battery that maintains safe storage temperature for 72 hours without any electrical input. The controlled-atmosphere system reduces internal oxygen to 4–6% and elevates CO₂ to 8–12%, suppressing ethylene production and microbial growth — the same principle used in commercial container shipping but applied to a 200L unit that weighs 62 kg and deploys from a flat-pack in 90 minutes. Produce life extension: 14–18 days for leafy greens, 21–28 days for root vegetables, and 14–21 days for fresh fruit — compared to 2–5 days without cold storage in ambient heat conditions typical of WFP operations in Sudan, Somalia, and Yemen.

The N-Node neighborhood hub ($28,000) complements the Cold-Root by providing the distribution infrastructure: a 36m² insulated container shell with integrated Grid-Seed 5kW solar array, Dew-Catcher atmospheric water generator producing 5L/day of potable water, Carr-Library tablet terminal for WFP SCOPE biometric beneficiary registration, and 4 Cold-Root docking bays. The N-Node serves as a neighborhood-scale food distribution point where beneficiaries collect rations, fresh produce is stored on-site, and WFP's digital beneficiary management system operates without grid power or cellular connectivity — using FEATHER mesh for camp-wide data synchronization.
