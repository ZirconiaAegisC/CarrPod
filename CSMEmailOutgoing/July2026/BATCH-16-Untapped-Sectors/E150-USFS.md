**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE WILDLAND FIREFIGHTER SAFETY GAP**

Thirty-four USFS wildland firefighters have died in entrapment incidents since 1990 — South Canyon 1994 (14), Thirtymile 2001 (4), Yarnell Hill 2013 (19 Granite Mountain Hotshots). Every single one of those firefighters was carrying an M2002 fire shelter. Every single one deployed it. None of them survived direct flame contact. The M2002 is rated for 500°F radiant heat — the temperature of a campfire at 10 feet. A chaparral crown fire burning through 40-year-old fuel loading produces flame temperatures exceeding 2,000°F at ground level. The Mana Fire-Veil survival pod — a ZrB₂-SiC ultra-high-temperature ceramic capsule rated for 2,200°C direct flame contact for 45 minutes — is designed to close the gap between the M2002's radiant-heat-only protection and the reality of direct flame overrun. For the USFS, this is not a fire shelter replacement — it is a fire shelter complement for the scenarios where the M2002 cannot succeed.
