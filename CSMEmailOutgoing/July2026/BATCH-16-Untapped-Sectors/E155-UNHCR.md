UNHCR's current shelter strategy relies heavily on three solutions: tents (UNHCR family tent, 16m², 1-year lifespan), tarpaulins (plastic sheeting, 6-month lifespan), and transitional shelters (semi-permanent, 3–5 year lifespan). The average refugee displacement now lasts 10–26 years. Families spend a decade living under plastic sheeting — in the heat of Azraq, the wind of Cox's Bazar, the cold of eastern Ukraine. The Testa Fleet container system addresses the fundamental mismatch between displacement duration and shelter durability by providing a modular, flat-packed container architecture that deploys in 4 hours, lasts 20+ years, and comes in 55 specialized variants covering every function a refugee camp requires — from the Tier 1 Share-Pod medical power sharing unit ($195) to the Tier 5 Cold-Root food preservation container (200L, 2-week produce life) to the Tier 4 dwelling unit that provides 18m² of insulated, ventilated, lockable family shelter.

The Testa Fleet container system is constructed from dielectric composite panels — BFRP (basalt fiber-reinforced polymer) facesheets with a structural foam core, total panel thickness 48mm, providing R-12 insulation without thermal bridging. Unlike steel ISO containers — which weigh 2,300 kg empty, conduct GIC, rust in humidity, and require a crane to position — the Testa Fleet flat-packed container ships as six 48mm panels on a standard pallet (187 kg total), assembles with 16 interlocking cam-lock connectors requiring only a rubber mallet, and achieves full structural rigidity in 4 hours with two people and no power tools. The 55 variants span five tiers: Tier 1 — personal power/communications (Share-Pod $195, Carr-Library $165 education tablet, 1-kWh solar battery); Tier 2 — small community infrastructure (Dew-Catcher 5L/day atmospheric water generator, Grid-Seed 5kW solar station); Tier 3 — medical/education (clinic container, classroom container); Tier 4 — dwelling units (18m² family shelter, 36m² communal shelter); Tier 5 — camp infrastructure (Cold-Root 200L food preservation, N-Node $28K neighborhood hub, Grey-Wash containerized water recycling). All containers are fully dielectric — zero GIC coupling, zero EMP vulnerability — and can be disassembled, flat-packed, and relocated as camps evolve or populations return.
