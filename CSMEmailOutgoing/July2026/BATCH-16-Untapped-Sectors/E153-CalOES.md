**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE INTEGRATED RESPONSE GAP**

CalOES coordinates the largest state-level emergency response apparatus in the United States — 58 county operational areas, mutual aid compacts with every Western state, and the California Fire and Rescue Mutual Aid System that moves thousands of firefighters across the state within hours. But that coordination depends on three things that wildfire systematically destroys: communications infrastructure (cell towers burn), situational awareness (smoke columns obscure satellite and aerial observation), and last-resort survival options for firefighters and civilians when evacuation routes collapse. The Mana Fire-Veil, Drone Backpack, and FEATHER mesh address those three gaps as an integrated suite — each product reinforcing the others in the exact sequence that wildfire response demands: detect the fire, communicate the response, survive the overrun.

The entire suite is manufactured in California, qualifies for FEMA BRIC/HMGP reimbursement, and aligns with the California Wildfire & Forest Resilience Action Plan's equipment modernization objectives.
