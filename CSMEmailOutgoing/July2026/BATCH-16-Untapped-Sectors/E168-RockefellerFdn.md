**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE PHILANTHROPIC SUPPLY CHAIN PROBLEM**

The Rockefeller Foundation's 100 Resilient Cities initiative identified infrastructure resilience as the core challenge of the 21st century — but the global supply chain for infrastructure products (solar panels, water purifiers, cold storage, communications equipment) is built on a "design in the Global North, manufacture in China, ship to the Global South" model that concentrates value creation, creates transportation emissions, and leaves recipient communities dependent on imported spare parts and manufacturer goodwill. When a water pump breaks in a village in Malawi, the village waits 6–12 weeks for a replacement part from a manufacturer in Germany that may have discontinued the model. The Carr-Forge platform inverts that model: the design files for every CSM product — N-Node hubs, Cold-Root refrigerators, Dew-Catcher water generators, Share-Pod power units, Carr-Library tablets, FEATHER mesh nodes — are published under the CERN Open Hardware Licence Version 2 — Strongly Reciprocal (CERN-OHL-S 2.0), which means anyone can manufacture, modify, and sell these products anywhere in the world, provided they share their modifications under the same license. The Carr-Forge $48/month subscription provides access to the complete manufacturing documentation package — CAD files, BOMs (bill of materials), assembly instructions, quality control checklists, and supply chain guides for sourcing BFRP panels, MXene coatings, electronics, and solar components from regional suppliers.
