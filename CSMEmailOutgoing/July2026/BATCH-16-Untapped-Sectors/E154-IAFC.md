**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE TRAINING & CERTIFICATION GAP**

The IAFC is the authoritative voice on fire service training standards in the United States. When the fire service adopted the M2002 fire shelter in 2002, it was the IAFC — through its Wildland Fire Policy Committee and Safety, Health & Survival Section — that drove the training and deployment protocols that made the shelter standard equipment on every wildland engine. Today, there is no equivalent training standard for a last-resort survival option that protects against direct flame contact. The Mana Fire-Veil is a new category of firefighter equipment — a ZrB₂-SiC UHTC survival pod that is not a fire shelter replacement but a fire shelter complement for the scenarios where the M2002's 260°C rating cannot protect against 1,200–2,000°C direct flame overrun. The IAFC is the organization that can — and should — establish the training, certification, and deployment standard for this new category.

**PARAGRAPH 3 — BAKER STREET PRECISE SPECS**

Baker Street, let's get precise on what needs to be trained and certified. The Mana Fire-Veil: 2.4m × 1.2m × 1.1m internal volume, 187 kg mass, two-occupant capacity. The deployment sequence — pull-handle release, aeroshell expansion from collapsed transport configuration, ingress, hatch seal, KO₂ activator pull — takes 30 seconds with a trained firefighter, 45 seconds with a civilian. The training curriculum ACS proposes for IAFC certification includes: (1) Fire-Veil deployment and ingress under simulated fire-ground stress; (2) KO₂ oxygen generator activation and life-support duration management; (3) integrated 406 MHz beacon operation and FEATHER mesh node pairing; (4) post-deployment extraction and pod recovery protocols; (5) crew-level decision-making on when to transition from M2002 to Fire-Veil. The proposed training standard is 8 hours — one shift day — including 4 hours classroom and 4 hours hands-on with training pods. ACS will supply training pods (identical to operational pods but without live KO₂ generators) and a train-the-trainer program for IAFC-member departments.
