**PARAGRAPH 5 — THE FIRE-VEIL PROPOSAL**

The ACS AEGIS Fire Safety Division proposes a three-phase California deployment program. Phase Alpha — CalFire-controlled pilot deployment of 50 Mana Fire-Veil units across the highest-risk wildland-urban interface zones in Butte, Sonoma, Los Angeles, and Riverside counties, with firefighter training on deployment and recovery protocols, 90-day delivery, $3.74M. Phase Bravo — expansion to 500 units across all CalFire operational units, with civil-defense positioning at known evacuation choke points and community fire-safe council coordination, 180-day delivery, $28.2M. Phase Charlie — full-scale California Wildfire Survival Infrastructure Program integrating Mana Fire-Veil pods, FEATHER mesh fire-zone communications, and Drone Backpack aerial fire-front monitoring into a unified wildland firefighter protection architecture, $93.6M over 24 months. All units are manufactured at ACS's California facility, deploy within CalFire's existing logistics framework, and require no new training infrastructure beyond the 4-hour familiarization module.
