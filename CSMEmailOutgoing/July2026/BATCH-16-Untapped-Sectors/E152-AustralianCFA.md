**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE BLACK SUMMER LESSON**

Black Summer 2019–2020: 33 people killed directly, an estimated 445 killed by smoke inhalation, 3,094 homes destroyed, 18.6 million hectares burned, and billions of animals lost. The New South Wales Rural Fire Service documented 66 firefighter entrapments across that single season. The 2009 Black Saturday fires in Victoria killed 173 civilians — the highest civilian fire death toll in Australian history — with 113 of those deaths occurring inside their own homes as people attempted to shelter in place. The lesson from both events is identical: when an Australian eucalyptus fire front moves at 20 km/h with 30-meter flame lengths driven by 90 km/h winds, the time between "we should leave" and "we cannot leave" is measured in seconds. The Mana Fire-Veil is a ZrB₂-SiC ultra-high-temperature ceramic survival pod designed to be the last-resort option for that exact moment — for firefighters, for civilians, for anyone trapped between a eucalyptus flame front and no way out.

**PARAGRAPH 3 — BAKER STREET PRECISE SPECS**

Baker Street, let's get precise for Australian conditions. The Mana Fire-Veil is constructed from the same ZrB₂-SiC ultra-high-temperature ceramic composite used in hypersonic vehicle thermal protection — a material that does not melt, does not oxidize below 2,200°C, and actively radiates heat away from the structure via an emissivity of 0.92 in the infrared. Wall thickness: 22mm total — 14mm ZrB₂-SiC outer shell, 5mm MXene vacuum-gap insulation, 3mm BFRP inner liner. The thermal performance is critical for Australian eucalyptus fires, which produce sustained flame temperatures of 1,200–1,600°C — far above the M2002 fire shelter's 260°C rating — and can maintain those temperatures for 8–12 minutes as the flame front passes. The Fire-Veil's 45-minute rated survival time at 2,200°C provides a 4× safety margin over the worst-case Australian bushfire overrun duration. Pod mass: 187 kg — lighter than a CFA pumper's auxiliary pump. Internal life support: KO₂ chemical oxygen generator for 55 minutes, lithium hydroxide CO₂ scrubbing, integrated 406 MHz emergency beacon. The pod can be pre-positioned at known entrapment zones — ridge-top staging areas, dead-end valley roads, remote helispots — and deployed from any CFA tanker or medium-lift helicopter.

**PARAGRAPH 5 — THE PROPOSAL**

ACS proposes a CFA-specific pilot program for the 2027–2028 Victorian fire season: Phase Alpha — deployment of 50 Mana Fire-Veil pods across the 10 highest-risk CFA districts (East Gippsland, North East, Goulburn, Yarra, Grampians, Barwon South West, Loddon, Central, Hume, West Gippsland), with CFA volunteer and career firefighter training, 90-day delivery, $3.74M AUD. Phase Bravo — integration of FEATHER mesh communications for fire-zone connectivity independent of Telstra/Optus cellular infrastructure (which burned out during Black Summer), 500-node deployment across CFA regions, $4.2M AUD. Phase Charlie — full Victoria-scale Fire-Veil + FEATHER + Drone Backpack deployment covering all CFA districts, $38.2M AUD over 36 months. Each Fire-Veil unit is rated for 20 deployments before aeroshell refurbishment, and ACS will establish a Melbourne-area service center for pod maintenance, KO₂ canister replacement, and crew retraining.
