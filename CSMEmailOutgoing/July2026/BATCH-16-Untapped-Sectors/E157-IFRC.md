**PARAGRAPH 2 — THE IFRC DEPLOYMENT MODEL**

The IFRC's Disaster Response Emergency Fund (DREF) releases funds within 24 hours of a disaster. The Emergency Response Units (ERUs) deploy within 48–72 hours. But the physical infrastructure those ERUs depend on — field hospitals, logistics bases, relief distribution points — still relies on tents that last 6–12 months and steel ISO containers that require cranes to position. The Testa Fleet container system is designed for the IFRC deployment timeline: 48mm BFRP-foam composite panels flat-packed on standard pallets, assembled in 4 hours by two people with a rubber mallet, and deployable to any National Society's pre-positioned disaster preparedness stockpile. When Cyclone Mocha hits Bangladesh or an earthquake flattens infrastructure in Turkey, the Testa Fleet containers arrive with the ERU — not weeks after it.
