# STRATEGIC PIVOT: FROM EXOSKELETONS TO DEFENSE AI

## THE GUARDIAN ERA (2015-2023)

Sarcos Robotics, the predecessor entity to Palladyne AI, was founded on the premise that
full-body powered exoskeletons would revolutionize industrial labor and military logistics.
The Guardian product family encompassed:

- **Guardian XO** — Full-body, battery-powered industrial exoskeleton rated for up to 200
  pounds of lift assistance. Targeted at manufacturing, warehousing, and construction.

- **Guardian XO Max** — Higher-capacity military variant designed for logistics and
  sustainment operations in theater.

- **Guardian S** — Teleoperated, serpentine inspection robot for confined spaces, hazardous
  environments, and defense applications including EOD support.

- **Guardian XT** — Upper-body exoskeleton for overhead work in aviation maintenance and
  shipbuilding.

These products generated significant media attention and early-adopter interest but failed to
achieve commercial escape velocity. The fundamental challenges included: unit costs exceeding
$100,000 per system, limited battery endurance constraining operational utility, lack of
clear regulatory frameworks for workplace deployment, and insufficient near-term ROI for
industrial customers accustomed to conventional automation at lower price points.

## THE DECISION TO PIVOT

By mid-2024, Palladyne management — under Benjamin Wolff's leadership — made the explicit
decision to discontinue all Guardian product lines. In public statements, the company cited
"longer-than-anticipated customer adoption cycles" and a strategic judgment that the
autonomous systems and defense AI markets offered superior near-term revenue growth
opportunities with more established procurement pathways.

The pivot was not merely a product discontinuation but a fundamental reorientation of the
company's identity, engineering talent base, investor narrative, and capital allocation
strategy.

## RATIONALE FOR DEFENSE FOCUS

Several macro conditions supported the pivot:

1. **Proven Demand** — The Ukraine conflict had demonstrated the operational effectiveness
   of loitering munitions and autonomous systems at scale, creating immediate and
   sustained demand from U.S., NATO, and allied defense procurement agencies.

2. **Congressional Tailwinds** — The FY2024 and FY2025 National Defense Authorization Acts
   included specific funding lines for attritable autonomous systems, loitering munitions,
   and counter-UAS capabilities. The DoD Replicator initiative further signaled intent to
   procure autonomous systems in quantities measured in thousands of units.

3. **Favorable Competitive Dynamics** — While the prime defense contractors dominate
   large-scale weapons systems, the attritable autonomous systems space remains
   fragmented, with opportunities for well-capitalized new entrants to secure production
   contracts before market consolidation.

4. **Technology Transferability** — Palladyne's expertise in actuation, control systems,
   and power management from the exoskeleton programs provided a foundation for autonomous
   system development, particularly in flight control and servo-actuated systems.

## DISCONTINUITY RISKS

The pivot introduced discontinuity risks that persist in the investment thesis:

- Loss of the Guardian customer pipeline, which had required years of relationship building
- Organizational churn as exoskeleton-focused engineers were reassigned or departed
- Skepticism from legacy investors who had bought into the exoskeleton narrative
- Brand confusion during the transition from Sarcos to Palladyne

The 21.5% short interest in PDYN shares partly reflects market uncertainty about whether an
acquisition-driven transformation can produce sustainable organic growth, or whether the
company is assembling disparate assets without a durable competitive moat.
