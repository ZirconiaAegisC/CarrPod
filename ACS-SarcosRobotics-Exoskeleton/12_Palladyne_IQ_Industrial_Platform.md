# PALLADYNE IQ INDUSTRIAL AUTONOMY PLATFORM

## PLATFORM OVERVIEW

Palladyne IQ represents the sole remaining legacy product line from the former Sarcos
Robotics era, now repositioned as an edge-deployed software platform for industrial robot
autonomy. Unlike the exoskeleton hardware that defined the company's earlier identity,
Palladyne IQ is a software-only product — an important distinction that reflects the
company's broader pivot from hardware-centric to software-defined value creation.

## CORE CAPABILITIES

Palladyne IQ provides a software layer that retrofits autonomous capabilities onto existing
industrial robot fleets. The platform's stated capabilities include:

- **Perception and Sensing** — Camera and sensor-based perception enabling robots to detect,
  classify, and localize objects in dynamic environments without pre-programmed waypoints
  or fixed infrastructure.

- **Motion Planning and Control** — Real-time trajectory generation and collision avoidance
  that adapts to changes in the environment, including human co-workers, moving obstacles,
  and variable work piece positioning.

- **Task-Level Autonomy** — Rather than requiring scripted, position-by-position programming,
  Palladyne IQ enables operator specification of task objectives (e.g., "pick this part and
  place it on that conveyor") with the system autonomously handling grasp planning, path
  planning, and error recovery.

- **Fleet Management** — Centralized management and monitoring of multiple heterogeneous
  robot platforms across a facility, with performance analytics and predictive maintenance
  capabilities.

- **Edge Deployment** — The software runs on edge computing hardware at the robot or facility
  level, minimizing latency and eliminating dependence on cloud connectivity for real-time
  control functions.

## TARGET MARKETS

Palladyne IQ addresses three principal industrial verticals:

### Manufacturing
Discrete manufacturing operations involving machine tending, assembly, material handling,
and inspection. The value proposition is reducing the integration cost and programming time
required to deploy industrial robots, particularly for high-mix, low-volume production
environments where traditional automation is uneconomical.

### Warehousing and Logistics
Order fulfillment, sortation, palletizing, and depalletizing. Competes with established
players including Symbotic, Berkshire Grey, and RightHand Robotics in piece-picking and
autonomous material handling. Palladyne IQ's differentiation is its retrofit capability: it
can be deployed on existing robot hardware rather than requiring proprietary hardware
platforms.

### Aviation and Aerospace Maintenance
Automated inspection, coating application, and assembly assistance for aircraft maintenance,
repair, and overhaul (MRO) operations. This vertical leverages the company's Salt Lake City
heritage (proximity to Hill Air Force Base, a major MRO depot) and alignment with DoD
maintenance modernization initiatives.

## COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

The industrial robot autonomy software market is competitive and consolidating:

| Competitor | Offering | Differentiation |
|---|---|---|
| Intrinsic (Alphabet) | Robot-agnostic AI software | Google-scale resources, Flowstate platform |
| Symbotic | End-to-end warehouse automation | Proprietary hardware+software stack |
| Covariant | AI robotic picking | Focused on piece-picking, strong logistics |
| Ready Robotics | Forge/OS platform | Hardware-agnostic, drag-and-drop programming |

Palladyne IQ's competitive challenge is the same one facing all robot-agnostic software
platforms: the most valuable industrial customers (automotive OEMs, large logistics
operators) tend to standardize on a single robot OEM and use that OEM's proprietary
software ecosystem. The addressable market for a third-party middleware layer between
robot hardware and end-user applications has proven narrower than many venture-backed
startups anticipated.

## REVENUE MODEL AND TRACTION

Palladyne IQ operates on a software-as-a-service (SaaS) model with recurring license
revenue, plus professional services for integration and customization. The company has
not disclosed specific IQ revenue figures separate from total corporate revenue, making
it difficult to assess the platform's stand-alone commercial traction.

Given that FY2025 total revenue was approximately $5.2 million and the FY2026 guidance
of $24-27 million is driven primarily by the defense acquisitions, it is likely that
Palladyne IQ contributes a relatively small portion of current revenue, with the defense
vertical representing the dominant growth driver.

## STRATEGIC ROLE IN THE PORTFOLIO

Despite its modest revenue contribution, Palladyne IQ serves several strategic functions:

1. **Technology Synergy** — The perception, planning, and control algorithms developed for
   industrial robots have direct applicability to autonomous vehicle guidance, creating
   a shared technology base with Palladyne Defense.

2. **Commercial Diversification** — IQ provides exposure to non-defense revenue, reducing
   the company's dependence on federal procurement cycles.

3. **Talent Retention** — The IQ platform retains engineering talent from the legacy
   Sarcos business whose skills transfer to defense autonomy programs.

4. **Optionality** — If the industrial autonomy market develops more rapidly than expected,
   Palladyne IQ provides an option on that growth without requiring material incremental
   investment.
