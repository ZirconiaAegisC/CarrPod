# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & CORPORATE OVERVIEW

## CORPORATE IDENTITY

Palladyne AI Corp. (Nasdaq: PDYN), formerly Sarcos Technology and Robotics Corporation, is
headquartered in Salt Lake City, Utah. The company completed its corporate rebranding in
2024-2025, deliberately shedding the "Sarcos" brand associated with wearable exoskeleton
technology in favor of a defense-forward AI and autonomous systems identity. CEO Benjamin Wolff,
who assumed the role in 2023, has been the principal architect of the company's transformation
from an industrial robotics and human augmentation firm into a defense technology integrator.

## TRANSFORMATION ARC: 2022-2026

The company's pivot constitutes one of the more dramatic repositionings in the small-to-mid-cap
defense sector. Prior to 2024, Palladyne (then Sarcos) derived market attention primarily from
its Guardian line of full-body powered exoskeletons — the Guardian XO, XO Max, Guardian S, and
Guardian XT. These systems were aimed at industrial logistics, military logistics, and
construction applications. All Guardian product lines have since been discontinued as the
company concluded the exoskeleton market lacked near-term commercial viability at scale.

In their place, Palladyne has assembled a defense technology portfolio through three strategic
acquisitions closed in November 2025, capped by an exclusive licensing agreement with Israel
Aerospace Industries (IAI) in June 2026. The resultant entity operates across three primary
verticals: autonomous defense systems (Palladyne Defense), precision defense manufacturing
(Crucis Companies), and industrial robot autonomy software (Palladyne IQ).

## ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

Palladyne AI Corp. operates through the following divisions and subsidiaries:

1. **Palladyne Defense** — Autonomous systems, swarm software, and loitering munitions.
   Includes the GuideTech operations (Gremlin-X loitering munition, avionics) and the IAI
   partnership for HARPY, HAROP, and Mini HARPY loitering munitions.

2. **Crucis Companies** — Comprising Warnke and MKR manufacturing operations. Produces
   precision components for F-16, F-35, Tomahawk, and Harpoon weapons systems. Provides
   the manufacturing backbone and defense supply chain positioning.

3. **Palladyne IQ** — Edge-deployed software platform for industrial robot autonomy. The sole
   remaining legacy product line. Targets manufacturing, warehousing, and logistics end-users
   seeking to retrofit autonomous capabilities onto existing robot fleets.

4. **Palladyne Pilot** — Multi-domain autonomous platform software designed to integrate and
   coordinate heterogeneous unmanned systems across air, ground, and maritime domains.

## MARKET CONTEXT

The company occupies a unique position at the intersection of several defense megatrends:
autonomous systems proliferation, loitering munition adoption driven by demonstrated
effectiveness in modern conflict, onshoring of precision defense manufacturing, and the
Department of Defense's Replicator initiative emphasizing attritable autonomous systems at
scale. The decline from a $66.72 all-time high to approximately $6.72 reflects the market's
skepticism about the sustainability of an acquisition-driven pivot, particularly given the
company's limited organic revenue history and the 21.5% short interest.
