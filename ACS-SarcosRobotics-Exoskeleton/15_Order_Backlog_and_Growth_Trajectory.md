# ORDER BACKLOG & GROWTH TRAJECTORY

## BACKLOG COMPOSITION

As of July 2026, Palladyne AI reported an aggregate order backlog of approximately $24
million. This backlog is the single most important forward-looking metric for the company,
as it represents contracted but undelivered revenue. The composition of the backlog across
the company's business segments has not been disclosed with granularity, but the following
attribution is analytically inferred:

| Segment | Estimated Backlog Contribution | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Palladyne Defense (Loitering Munitions) | $10-14 million | Gremlin-X and IAI-licensed systems |
| Crucis Manufacturing | $6-8 million | Ongoing component deliveries to primes |
| USAF Contract | $3-5 million | Remaining unearned portion of $13.8M contract |
| Palladyne IQ | $1-3 million | Software license commitments and services |

## FY2026 GUIDANCE ANALYSIS

Palladyne's FY2026 revenue guidance of $24-27 million represents 357-415% growth over FY2025.
This guidance is aggressive by any standard and requires assessment of achievability.

### Guidance Achievability Factors

**Supporting Factors:**
- Q2 2026 preliminary revenue of ~$5.8 million (480% YoY growth) provides a credible
  first-half foundation
- The $24 million backlog provides booked-and-funded visibility into the second half
- Crucis manufacturing revenue is relatively predictable given multi-year purchase orders
  from defense primes
- The IAI partnership creates a channel for loitering munition orders that was not
  available in FY2025

**Risk Factors:**
- Revenue recognition for defense hardware depends on delivery and acceptance, which
  can be delayed by supply chain issues, testing requirements, or customer acceptance
  procedures
- The midpoint of guidance ($25.5 million) implies second-half revenue of approximately
  $13.9 million (assuming H1 of ~$11.6 million), requiring meaningful production ramp
- Government contracting revenue can be subject to continuing resolutions that delay
  contract awards and funding obligations
- The company has a limited track record of delivering against guidance, making the
  forecast inherently less reliable than for more mature defense contractors

### Comparative Context

For perspective, 357-415% growth is achievable from a small base but becomes progressively
more difficult to sustain as the revenue base grows. The "law of large numbers" is not yet
a constraint at $5 million of revenue, but the company will need to demonstrate the ability
to convert backlog into recognized revenue consistently over multiple quarters to build
credibility with the investment community.

## BACKLOG QUALITY ASSESSMENT

Not all backlog is created equal. Key quality factors include:

1. **Funding Certainty** — Is the backlog funded through appropriated government budgets or
   subject to future funding decisions? Funded backlog (appropriated) is substantially more
   reliable than unfunded backlog (subject to future appropriations).

2. **Delivery Timeline** — Backlog that converts to revenue within 12 months is higher
   quality than multi-year backlog that may be subject to change orders, program
   cancellation, or descoping.

3. **Customer Diversification** — Backlog concentrated in a single program or customer
   carries higher risk than a diversified backlog. Palladyne's backlog concentration is
   not disclosed but is likely skewed toward a small number of defense customers.

4. **Cancellation Terms** — Government contracts typically include termination for
   convenience clauses, which allow the government to cancel without penalty. Commercial
   contracts may have more rigorous cancellation provisions.

## GROWTH BEYOND FY2026

The trajectory beyond FY2026 depends on several conversion events:

### Near-Term Catalysts (Next 6-12 Months)
- Full-rate production authorization for Gremlin-X against specific DoD programs of record
- IAI HARPY/HAROP initial U.S. government evaluation and potential procurement
- Additional USAF or other service contracts expanding Palladyne Pilot's funded backlog
- Crucis manufacturing contract renewals and expansions as prime production rates increase

### Medium-Term Catalysts (12-24 Months)
- International loitering munition sales through FMS channels (IAI partnership leverage)
- SwarmOS integration contracts with U.S. or allied unmanned systems programs of record
- Potential follow-on acquisitions to expand manufacturing capacity or software capabilities
- Palladyne IQ achieving meaningful commercial adoption with reference customers

### Long-Term Value Drivers
- Establishment as a qualified supplier for the Replicator initiative's production phase
- International expansion of both loitering munition and autonomy software sales
- Potential platform acquisition by a larger defense prime seeking to internalize
  autonomous systems capabilities

## BACKLOG-TO-REVENUE CONVERSION TRACKING

Investors and analysts should monitor the following metrics to track backlog conversion:
- Quarterly book-to-bill ratio (new orders / recognized revenue), with a ratio above 1.0
  indicating backlog growth
- Backlog coverage ratio (backlog / trailing twelve-month revenue), with declining
  coverage indicating a need for new order intake
- Revenue recognition against prior backlog disclosures, providing evidence of conversion
  reliability
