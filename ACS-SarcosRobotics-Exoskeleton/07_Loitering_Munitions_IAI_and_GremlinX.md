# LOITERING MUNITIONS: IAI PARTNERSHIP & GUIDETECH GREMLIN-X

## MARKET CONTEXT

Loitering munitions — unmanned aerial vehicles designed to loiter over a target area, conduct
surveillance, and engage targets through a terminal strike — have emerged as one of the most
significant procurement categories in modern defense. Combat experience in Ukraine, Nagorno-
Karabakh, and the Middle East has validated the operational concept at scale, driving demand
from the U.S. Department of Defense, NATO allies, and Indo-Pacific partners. The global
loitering munition market is projected to reach $3.0-3.5 billion by 2028, growing at a
compound annual rate of approximately 15-18%.

Palladyne AI entered this market through two distinct pathways: the acquisition of GuideTech
and its Gremlin-X platform (November 2025), and an exclusive U.S. licensing agreement with
Israel Aerospace Industries (June 2026).

## IAI STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP (JUNE 2026)

The June 2026 agreement with Israel Aerospace Industries granted Palladyne Defense exclusive
rights to market, manufacture, and support IAI's loitering munition family within the United
States. The covered systems represent some of the most combat-proven loitering munitions in
existence:

### HARPY

The HARPY is a long-endurance, autonomous loitering munition designed for suppression of enemy
air defenses (SEAD). First deployed operationally in the 1990s, HARPY has undergone multiple
block upgrades. The system loiters over a target area for extended periods, autonomously
detects radar emissions, and engages emitting targets through a terminal dive with a high-
explosive warhead. HARPY addresses the counter-anti-access/area-denial (A2/AD) mission that
the U.S. Indo-Pacific Command has identified as its highest-priority operational requirement.

### HAROP

Often designated "HARPY 2," HAROP adds electro-optical/infrared (EO/IR) seeker capability and
man-in-the-loop control to the basic HARPY concept. This enables engagement of non-emitting
targets, moving targets, and targets of opportunity identified by the human operator during
the loitering phase. HAROP has been extensively combat-proven and is in service with multiple
IAI export customers.

### Mini HARPY

The Mini HARPY is a smaller, tactical loitering munition designed for company and battalion-
level operations. It provides organic precision-strike capability to maneuver units without
requiring coordination with higher-echelon fires assets. The Mini HARPY competes most directly
with AeroVironment's Switchblade 300/600 family.

## THE GUIDETECH GREMLIN-X

The Gremlin-X is Palladyne's internally developed loitering munition, acquired through the
GuideTech transaction. Unlike the IAI-licensed systems, which come with an existing
international customer base and combat heritage, the Gremlin-X is a newer entrant that must
establish its own market position.

Key features of the Gremlin-X include:

- Tube or rail-launched man-portable configuration
- Multi-sensor payload (EO/IR with optional signals intelligence capability)
- SwarmOS-native integration for coordinated multi-unit operations
- Modular warhead architecture supporting multiple mission profiles
- Designed for integration with Palladyne Pilot command-and-control framework

The Gremlin-X is positioned to compete for U.S. Army and Marine Corps programs of record for
company-level loitering munitions, as well as Foreign Military Sales (FMS) opportunities
through IAI's existing customer relationships.

## BANSHEE AND SWARMSTRIKE

In addition to the named Gremlin-X and IAI platforms, Palladyne has referenced two additional
system concepts:

- **Banshee** — A multi-role loitering munition system described in limited public
  disclosures. Likely represents either a larger-category loitering munition or a
  derivative of existing GuideTech/IAI technology.

- **SwarmStrike** — A coordinated swarming strike capability that leverages SwarmOS to
  enable multiple loitering munitions to collaborate on target acquisition, defense
  suppression, and coordinated terminal engagement. SwarmStrike is the operational
  application that connects Palladyne's autonomy software to its munition hardware.

## REGULATORY AND EXPORT CONSIDERATIONS

Loitering munitions fall under the Missile Technology Control Regime (MTCR) Category I when
they exceed certain range and payload thresholds. Category I systems face strict export
controls. Palladyne's tactical systems (Mini HARPY, Gremlin-X) are designed to fall below
MTCR thresholds, enabling broader exportability. The HARPY/HAROP systems may face more
restrictive export treatment. The IAI partnership provides an existing export compliance
framework, as IAI has decades of experience navigating U.S. technology transfer and ITAR
requirements through its U.S. subsidiary structure.
