## 2. Critical Material Analysis V2.0

### 2.1 Iron Nitride (Fe16N2) — Niron Magnetics Update 2025

**Status (V2.0, June 2026):** Production-scale samples at 27 MGOe — a 12.5% improvement
from V1.0's 24 MGOe demonstration value.

**2025 advances:**
- Process: modified nitrification of iron powder in NH3/H2 atmosphere
- Grain alignment: magnetic field alignment during compaction improved texture coefficient
  from 0.65 (V1.0) to 0.83 (V2.0) → explains most of the BHmax improvement
- Stability: accelerated aging tests (150°C × 1000 h): <2% BHmax loss → acceptable for automotive

**Why Fe16N2 matters for CSM:**
Zero rare earth content → zero DFARS/CRMA compliance issue.
Zero Dy/Tb → immune to Chinese export controls.
Fe + N from air → essentially unlimited domestic supply.

**V2.0 CSM recommendation:** Fe16N2 is now viable as a replacement for NdFeB in
applications < 100°C service temperature. Direct substitution for SmCo at high temp:
NOT yet viable (Fe16N2 Tc = 540°C but commercial Tc currently limited;
further optimization expected 2027-2028).

### 2.2 European Critical Raw Materials Act (CRMA, 2024) — New V2.0

The EU CRMA entered into full effect March 2024:
- Requires 40% of RE annual consumption from EU or allied sources by 2030
- Mandates 25% of RE demand met from recycling by 2030
- Establishes "Strategic Projects" list for fast-permitting RE mines

**Impact on CSM materials sourcing:**
- YInMn Blue: Indium CRMA "Strategic Raw Material" list → Phoenix Protocol recycling
  program is now CRMA-compliant re-use pathway
- NdFeB magnets: Nd/Dy on CRMA list → US-Europe sourced only (Lynas, Energy Fuels)
- V2.0 action: document CRMA compliance pathway for all critical mineral uses in CSMFAB portfolio

### 2.3 MXene-Enhanced NdFeB (Drexel 2025) — New V2.0

Drexel University 2025: Ti3C2Tx MXene flakes (5 wt%) admixed in NdFeB green compact → sinter:
- Coercivity Hci increase: +12% (MXene acts as grain boundary pinning agent)
- Remanence Br: unchanged (MXene doesn't dilute magnetic phase)
- Curie temperature: +25°C (reduces intergranular demagnetization coupling at high temp)
- Result: NdFeB can now partially replace SmCo in applications up to 175°C

**CSM Application:** Where SmCo was previously required (high-temp motor control coils),
MXene-NdFeB now viable with equal rare earth content at 50% lower cost.
