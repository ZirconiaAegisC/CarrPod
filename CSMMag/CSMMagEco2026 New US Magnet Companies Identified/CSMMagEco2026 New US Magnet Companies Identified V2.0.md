## 1. Macroeconomic Context — V2.0 Updated

### 1.1 DFARS 2027 Deadline: Implementation Status

The Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (DFARS) provision banning Chinese-origin magnets in US weapons systems becomes effective **January 1, 2027** — 6 months from this document's date. V2.0 updates:

**Q1 2026 DoD implementation guidance:** Prime contractors must submit magnet supply chain disclosure forms by September 2026. Non-compliant systems will require design waivers costing an estimated $15–80M per platform.

**Current domestic production gap:**
- US DoD annual magnet requirement: ~600 metric tons NdFeB equivalent
- Current domestic production capacity: ~85 metric tons (14% of requirement)
- Target 2027 production: 280 metric tons (47% of requirement — partial exemption threshold)
- Gap: 315 metric tons requiring immediate scale-up or allied-nation sourcing

### 1.2 China Concentration Risk — Updated

China's 2025 Export Control Amendment (ECA) restricts dysprosium (Dy) and terbium (Tb) — heavy rare earths essential for high-temperature NdFeB magnets used in EV traction motors and F-35 actuators. V2.0 adds:
- Dy price spike: $2,450/kg (Jan 2025) → $4,100/kg (May 2026) — 67% increase
- Tb price: $3,800/kg → $6,200/kg — 63% increase
- EV motor magnet cost impact: +$180–400 per vehicle

## 2. Post-2014 US Magnet Startup Ecosystem — V2.0 Updated

### 2.1 Technology Pathway A: Rare-Earth-Free Magnets

**Niron Magnetics (Minneapolis, MN — founded 2022):**
- Technology: Iron Nitride (Fe₁₆N₂) permanent magnets
- V1.0 performance: BHmax = 35 MGOe (theoretical); 24 MGOe demonstrated
- V2.0 update (2025): **BHmax 27 MGOe demonstrated** on production-scale samples
- Advantage: zero rare earth content; raw material cost $0.60/kg (Fe basis)
- GIC resilience relevance: Fe₁₆N₂ motors have no rare earth supply vulnerability; 100% domestic feedstock

**Electron Energy Corporation (Landisville, PA — founded 1970, rechartered 2019):**
- Technology: Samarium-Cobalt (SmCo) for high-temperature applications
- SmCo relevant for Aegis Embark applications requiring magnets above 150°C
- V2.0: SmCo5 BHmax 20 MGOe at 200°C — only magnet type viable in post-Carrington engine environments

### 2.2 Technology Pathway B: Advanced Recycling and Circular Supply

**Urban Mining Company (Austin, TX — founded 2018):**
- Technology: Hydrogen Processing of Magnet Scrap (HPMS) for NdFeB recycling
- V1.0: 500 kg/month processing
- V2.0 update: **12,000 kg/month facility online Q2 2026** at Pflugerville TX
- Recovery: 92% Nd, 95% Fe, 88% Dy from end-of-life EV motors
- DoD certified: DFARS-compliant source (magnets from US-sourced scrap)
- CSM relevance: primary source for recycled NdFeB for Aegis non-motor applications

**Cyclic Materials (Kingston, ON / US expansion 2025):**
- Opened Denver CO facility 2025 for US market
- Processing: 2,000 kg/day REE extract from EV magnets using aqueous selective dissolution

### 2.3 Technology Pathway C: Domestic NdFeB Vertical Integration

**MP Materials (Las Vegas, NV — founded 2017):**
- Mountain Pass mine: only operating US REE mine
- V1.0: mining only; magnet manufacturing partnership announced
- V2.0 update: **Fort Worth TX magnet facility online Q4 2025** — first US-made sintered NdFeB in 30+ years
- Production 2026: 1,000 metric tons/year → scales to 3,000 t/y by 2028
- Customers confirmed: GM, Apple, DoD

**Noveon Magnetics (San Marcos, TX — founded 2019):**
- Technology: solid-state sintering for isotropic bound magnets
- V2.0: **DFARS qualification complete Q1 2026** — now certified for DoD procurement
- BHmax: 12 MGOe (isotropic) vs 45 MGOe (sintered anisotropic) — lower performance but faster production ramp

## 3. Carrington Resilience Angle — New V2.0 Section

### 3.1 Magnet Demand from GIC-Immune Motor Designs

The Stellar-Aegis Protocol mandates eliminating copper-wound induction motors (Section CSMFAB000000000020). Replacement technologies drive magnet demand:

- **Ultrasonic piezo motors:** No magnets required — eliminates REE dependency entirely for small motors (<5 kW)
- **Reluctance motors (SynRM):** No permanent magnets — copper rotor only, GIC-immune if using ceramic-coated windings
- **SmCo permanent magnet motors:** Viable for high-temperature (>150°C) applications; DoD-grade SmCo now available domestically (Electron Energy Corp)
- **Fe₁₆N₂ motors:** REE-free permanent magnets; Niron 2025 performance enables direct substitution for NdFeB in <100°C applications

**CSM Recommendation for Aegis Motor Designs (V2.0):**
Prioritize ultrasonic piezo motors (CSMFAB000000000020) and SynRM where possible. Where permanent magnets required (high torque density applications), specify Niron Fe₁₆N₂ (REE-free) or Electron Energy SmCo (REE-efficient, high-temperature).

### 3.2 MXene-Enhanced Magnet Nanocomposites (Emerging V2.0)

2025 research (Drexel University): Ti₃C₂Tₓ MXene flakes admixed in NdFeB green compact before sintering show:
- Coercivity increase: +12% (MXene acts as grain boundary pinning agent)
- Operating temperature: +25°C (MXene reduces intergrain demagnetization coupling)
- This may enable NdFeB to qualify for higher-temperature Aegis applications where SmCo was previously required
